        <h1 class="entry-title">
         This Whole Grain Should Be in Your Breakfast Bowl
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2015-01-14T00:00:16+00:00">
          January 14, 2015
         </span>
         By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/author/blogging" rel="author">
            Karen
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/2015/01/whole-grain-breakfast-bowl.html#comments">
           2 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           RecentlyI mentioned how challenging breakfast was getting for me. Im a huge believer in the benefit ofstarting the day with breakfast, but somewhere along the way it began to not happen in my morning routine.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           So, I decided to revisit some old habits that I had somehow lost, and revived some of my favorite fast, simple, and nutritious breakfast meals, like
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/2014/12/get-nutrient-packed-breakfast-one-bowl-no-cereal-required.html" target="_blank">
            this fruit, seed, and cheese studded yogurt
           </a>
           .
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           On the chilly mornings, I usually want something to warm me up, and this homemade version of cream of rice cereal from
           <a href="http://www.suegregg.com" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.suegregg.com', 'Sue Greggs Breakfasts cookbook']);" target="_blank">
            Sue Greggs
            <em>
             Breakfasts
            </em>
            cookbook
           </a>
           is perfect.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           I remember growing up and eating cream of rice cereal from a box. I dont know for sure, but I would guess it was probably made from white rice  not much nutritional benefit there.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           When rice is refined, the outer layers are stripped away, the grain is polished, and the pretty white product is nearly devoid of any nutrition. Thats why its enriched with synthetic vitamins and iron, which are not absorbed and used by our bodies in the same way natural nutrients are.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Unrefined  or brown rice  has only had the outer hull removed, thus retaining the nutrients our bodies need and can use effectively. Its more than 3 times higher in fiber than refined rice, and is rich in selenium, manganese, and antioxidants. Its also a slow-release sugar so your body isnt bombarded with extra insulin aswith refined rice.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Homemade-Cream-of-Rice-Cereal.jpg">
            <img alt="Homemade Cream of Rice Cereal" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1973" height="1339" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Homemade-Cream-of-Rice-Cereal.jpg" width="1000"/>
           </a>
           This homemade version is made from unrefinedrice,and is fast, nutritious, and filling. It does take a little planning ahead, but youll only invest 5 or 6 minutes in the evening for a steamy bowl of goodness the next morning.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Whole-Grain-Rice.jpg">
            <img alt="Whole Grain Rice" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1967" height="1339" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Whole-Grain-Rice.jpg" width="1000"/>
           </a>
           For a single serving, youll need just 3 tablespoons of rice.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           In a small saucepan, bring 3/4 cups of water and salt to taste to a boil.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Whole-Grain-Rice-in-Blender.jpg">
            <img alt="Whole Grain Rice" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1968" height="1339" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Whole-Grain-Rice-in-Blender.jpg" width="1000"/>
           </a>
           While you wait for the water to boil, put the rice into a blender and blend it until you have a powder. Dont worry if you have some small pieces left that arent blended.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Whole-Grain-Rice-Powder.jpg">
            <img alt="Whole Grain Rice Powder" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1969" height="1339" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Whole-Grain-Rice-Powder.jpg" width="1000"/>
           </a>
           Measure 1/4 cup cold water into a small measuring cup and add the powdered rice, stirring until well blended.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Once the water begins to boil, add the cold water-rice mixture and blend well. Remove from the heat, cover, and let stand overnight. This is why you need not worry about any remaining pieces. After soaking all night, it will cook up soft and creamy.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           The next morning, warm the rice over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it thickens, about 5 minutes. You can add a little extra water if youlike your cereal thinner.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Add a pat of butter if you like and enjoy your delicious, creamy, nutritious breakfast.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h4>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            Do you enjoy hot cereals? Whats your favorite breakfast recipe?
           </span>
          </strong>
         </h4>
