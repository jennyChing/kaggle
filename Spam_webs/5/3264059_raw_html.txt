         </h1>

          <p>
           Nowadays, keeping your kiddos clean and healthy is a tough job. As kids tend to play a lot, explore the surroundings, and mingle with friends, it is of utmost importance that these kids should be taught about this matter. If you are a new parent, how can you teach personal hygiene practices?
          </p>
          <p>
           You should take note that kids tend to imitate what they have observed from the oldies. In view of this, be a good model to your kiddos. The time is just ripe to show them how important a particular hygienic practice is.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="kids playing" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-19" height="359" imageholder="http://hnfguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/kids-playing-600x399.jpg" src="http://hnfguide.com/wp-content/plugins/advanced-lazy-load/shade.gif" width="540"/>
          </p>
          <h3>
           Proper Hand Washing
          </h3>
          <p>
           Your kids will not know how to do proper hand washing, unless you show them. Take time to teach your kiddos in this aspect and allow them to do it alone. Remind them to do hand washing before and after meals, or after contact with dirty materials.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Regular Bathing routine
          </h3>
          <p>
           If you want your kids to take a bath on a regular basis, then impose a regular schedule on when they can bathe. Also, do not forget to teach your kids on how to do self-cleaning using soap, shampoo, and sponge.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Hair Care
          </h3>
          <p>
           This thing is pretty much important especially for girls with long hair. To prevent tangled locks, encourage your child to brush their hair regularly. As for prevention of head lice, discourage your kids from sharing hair accessories such as hair clips and hats.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Cover Mouth and Nose when Sneezing or Coughing
          </h3>
          <p>
           It cannot be avoided, but at some times, your kids may sneeze or cough. In view of this, teach your kids to cover their nose or mouth whenever they do so.
          </p>
          <p>
           Teaching your kids about personal hygiene is one step forward for health protection. Though you teach them, always keep an eye on whether they do it regularly. Once you see it has been part of their routine, then you can be less worried on this matter.
          </p>
