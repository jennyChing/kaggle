        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Batmobile Cozy Coupe Refashion
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="11769572" data-link="http://sweetcsdesigns.com/batmobile-cozy-coupe-refashion/" data-summary="" data-title="Batmobile Cozy Coupe Refashion">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://cdn2.sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/batmobile-from-a-cozy-coupe.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5058" height="800" src="http://cdn2.sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/batmobile-from-a-cozy-coupe.jpg" title="batmobile from a cozy coupe" width="533"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sweetcsdesigns.com/batmobile-cozy-coupe-refashion/&amp;media=http://sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/batmobile-from-a-cozy-coupe.jpg&amp;description=Batmobile Cozy Coupe Refashion')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         What little kid doesnt want to be a super hero? The cool capes, the rad theme song, a trusty sidekick- and some awesome wheels. If youre Batman, you get the best of all- the Batmobile. And what kid doesnt want a kids Batmobile? Luckily with a cozy coupe kids car, you cab get your little caped crusader swinging around the block in style in just about no time at all with this awesome way to make your own Batmobile.
        </p>
        <p>
         All it takes is a little paint, spray paint or vinyl and a printer, exacto knife or a cricut and the Batman cartridge and youre on your way!
        </p>
        <p>
         Now, since there was no tutorial for this wonderful gem I saw on Pinterest (after hours and hours of searching), I came up with my own- and while cursing under my breath at my method, I came up with a few other ideas as well that would save you from a visit by the f-bomb fairy. She and I are tight after this logo, let me tell you.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even though I had a few stumbles, the whole project took less than 45 minutes, minus drying time. If you use vinyl, it would be an under 20 minute craft- you really can make it quickly!!
        </p>
        <p>
         First off, I took this funky cozy coupe my mom found at a thrift shop for $7.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://cdn2.sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/photo-6.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-5061" height="717" src="http://cdn3.sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/photo-6-e1345518691539-768x1024.jpg" title="turn a thrift store cozy coupe like this into an awesome Batmobile! " width="538"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sweetcsdesigns.com/batmobile-cozy-coupe-refashion/&amp;media=http://sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/photo-6-e1345518691539-768x1024.jpg&amp;description=Batmobile Cozy Coupe Refashion')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          (note my sons relaxed driving apparel)
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         I spray painted the entire thing using 2 cans of Krylon fusion spray paint- it goes on smoothly and sticks to plastic without priming or sanding. I did try another brand, but it dripped pretty badly. Overall, spraypainting the car black took like 5 minutes and was super easy.
        </p>
        <p>
         After letting it dry for 30 minutes, I thought it was done, and my son excitedly ran to grab it. Big mistake- and major catastrophe. Im writing about the aftermath tomorrow.
        </p>
        <p>
         A few hours later, it really was completely dry, and I cut out a batman logo shape from the
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Cricut-Cartridge-Batman-Brave-Bold/dp/B003A2IOKS">
          Cricut Batman Cartridge
         </a>
         with my cricut onto some plain white paper. I used the inverse of the shape and the outline to use as a spraypaint stencil that I taped to the car.
        </p>
        <p>
         I wanted the logo to be spraypainted on- but while I love the final results, it was a major pain. More on that in a bit- but heres how the stencil looked taped on:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://cdn.sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/photo-7.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-5062" height="461" src="http://cdn.sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/photo-7-1024x768.jpg" title="How to make a batmobile stencil using the Batman Cricut cartridge" width="614"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://sweetcsdesigns.com/batmobile-cozy-coupe-refashion/&amp;media=http://sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/photo-7-1024x768.jpg&amp;description=Batmobile Cozy Coupe Refashion')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I then carefully painted the logo in yellow, trying to avoid drips. Unfortunately, drips happened. They happened something fierce, and the stencil lines got pretty funky.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          If you get drips, or uneven edges, you can do what I did and fix it by hand.
         </strong>
         I sprayed a bunch of paint in a paper cup and painted it on by hand, which worked great- but it wasnt the easiest or least stressful way to do it. You have to be careful when handpainting with spraypaint to not add too thick a layer- or it all scrapes off- kind of like what happens when you add nail polish on too thickly.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Instead of spraying on the logo, you could either make some out of tape or vinyl and adhere it to the car- or you could spray yellow paint into a paper cup and stencil it on with a stencil brush or sponge. You could also use yellow enamel paint- but acrylic might not fare as well outside as the plastic spray paint or an enamel.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         To finish it up, I painted the wheels a metallic color to look like chrome every little boy needs a chromed out car, right?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I still need to do a slight retouch to the lower left hand side (I am waiting for another night of dry time as the more I painted it, the more paint peeled off since it was still wet), but I love it!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Little man loved how it turned out, too:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://cdn.sweetcsdesigns.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/phonto-2.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">

           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         What do you think?
        </p>

            First off, to paint the car black I ended up using almost 2 full cans of the Krylon spray paint.  Seems like it took quite a bit to cover up all of the existing color.  We also noted that the paint seemed to scrape off easily (as noted above), but it should be fine for Christmas morrning (and after a few days in our backyard ot would look like that anyway).
           </p>
           <p>
            For the Batman symbol, instead of getting the yellow spray paint (which $5 a can for the Krylon at Walmart), I bought a flexible yellow plastic Yard Sale sign at Lowes for $1.29 (Check out Lowes.com and look for item number 61491).  I found an outline of the Batman symbol online (just do a Google search for batman symbol outline).  I copied the symbol into a Microsoft Word document and sized it out for the symbol to be about 7 wide.  After printing, I cut out the oval symbol, then the interior bat outline from the printout.  I used this cutout as a guide to cut out 2 oval shapes from the sign, then traced the bat symbol within both ovals using a pencil.  Lastly, I used a black permanent Sharpie to fill in the bat symbol on both ovals.  I plan on using a hot glue gun to put them on the car doors (not sure how well they will hold, but they can always be glued back on).
           </p>
           <p>
            Thanks for the awesome idea  my 2 y/o is going to love it Christmas morning !!
           </p>
