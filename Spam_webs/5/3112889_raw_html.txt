           <p>
            Cleveland State University more popularly known as CSU is a state (public-grant) university situated in Cleveland County in North-East Ohio. It was incorporated in 1964. Presently it is a division of the University System of Ohio'. Over 17,500 students are enrolled in the academe's more than 200 study programs offered at the UG, PG, and doctoral levels. The university also proffers a string of certificate, diploma, and professional degree courses. CSU has a sprawling flagship campus in downtown Cleveland with a total area of 85 acres. The university has got another campus in Westlake in the Cuyahoga County.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             Schools of Studies and Courses Offered
            </b>
            <br/>
            CSU has nine affiliated colleges offering 70 undergraduate programs (with majors), 27 master's degree courses, two law degrees and six doctoral degrees. It also offers state-of-the-art research facilities in select academic disciplines. The colleges that are part of the CSU system are:-
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             College of Graduate Studies
            </li>
            <li>

            </li>

            </li>

            </li>

            </li>
            <li>
             Monte Ahuja College of Business
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>

           <p>
            <b>
             Basic Requirements for Admission
            </b>
            <br/>
            Freshman students who have successfully completed high school education are eligible to apply to undergraduate programs of CSU provided they have:-
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Composite high school GPA of 2.3
            </li>
            <li>
             Successfully completed 13 prescribed main or fundamental academic disciplines (4 years of English and 3 years each of Mathematics, Natural and Social Sciences)
            </li>
            <li>
             An ACT score of 16 or a SAT tally of 700
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            CSU generally reviews the sections on essay and writing for both ACT and SAT. Students who completed their high school education over 5 years ago do not have to furnish their ACT or SAT scores. Some specific colleges like Fenn College of Engineering, College of Education and Human Services, and Department of Music have their own specific admission requirements over and above the basic requirements mentioned above.
           </p>
           <p>
            <b>
             An applicant must submit the following before he or she can be considered for admission:-
            </b>
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Filled up admission application form
            </li>
            <li>
             Transcripts of ACT or SAT scores
            </li>
            <li>
             An application fee of $30 that is non-refundable
            </li>
            <li>
             Official copies of high school pass certificate or GED scores
            </li>
            <li>
             Financial Verification Form along with TOEFL scores (for overseas students)
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            <b>
             Examinations
            </b>
            <br/>
            CSU follows three academic sessions-the spring session, summer session and fall session. Exams for each session commence a few days after the last day of class. For instance, for the Fall Semester 2013, 6th December is last class day. Exams will commence from 9th December and last till 14th December.
