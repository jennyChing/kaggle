        <h1>
        </h1>
        <h1>
         Bolatta Silis-Hegh: Evoking Greenlands Past and
Future
        </h1>
        <h2>
         Born in Qaqortoq,
         <a href="http://theculturetrip.com/europe/greenland/">
          Greenland
         </a>
         in 1981,
         <a href="http://bolatta.com">
          Bolatta Silis-Hegh
         </a>
         is a contemporary artist with a fierce and fresh voice who challenges viewers
conception of modern reality. Shelton Lindsay looks at her work, which engages with Greenlands cultural and ecological identity.
        </h2>
       </div>
       <div class="imageWrapper inline-image imageFull">
        <figure>
         <img alt="Bolatta Silis-H&#xF8;egh" src="http://static.theculturetrip.com/images/56-195877-dukkehus.jpg"/>
         <figcaption>
          <em>
           <a class="imageTextLink" href="http://bolatta.blogspot.co.uk/p/curriculum-vitae.html">
             Bolatta Silis-Hegh
           </a>
          </em>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Art is clearly the life blood of Silis-Heghs family. Bolatta Silis-Hegh's brother is acclaimed modern artist
         <a href="http://www.greenland.com/en/about-greenland/kultur-sjael/kunst/inuk-silis-hoeegh.aspx" id="">
          Inuk Silis-Hegh
         </a>
         , her father is famed
photographer and videographer
         <a href="http://www.silis.gl/" id="">
          Ivars Silis
         </a>
         and her mother is
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aka_Hoegh" id="">
          Aka Hoegh
         </a>
         , one of
         <a href="/europe/greenland/" id="">
          Greenland
         </a>
         s most prominent artists who is often cited as being one
of the originators of the countrys independent artistic identity.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         A graduate of the Aarhus Art Academy, Silis-Hegh began exhibiting her work in 2003 with a solo exhibition called
         <em>
          Intaglio
         </em>
         . From then on she has continued to exhibit regularly, and has produced several works for exhibitions with her talented family members.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         Much of the artist's work explores
         <a href="/europe/greenland/">
          Greenland
         </a>
         s identity on the world stage as well as its relationship
with its own past. By attempting to marry these two discordant principles,
         <a href="http://bolatta.com">
          Bolatta Silis-Hegh
         </a>
         is looking for a way to forge new avenues for discourse on
the issues of modernisation and distasteful Americanisation which she says is occurring all over Greenland, from the small and cosy cafs to the giant shopping centres.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="imageWrapper inline-image float-image-right imageHalf">
        <figure>
         <img alt="Bolatta Silis-H&#xF8;egh" src="http://static.theculturetrip.com/images/56-195874-niels-bohr.jpg"/>
         <figcaption>
          <em>
           <a class="imageTextLink" href="http://bolatta.blogspot.co.uk/p/curriculum-vitae.html">
             Bolatta Silis-Hegh
           </a>
          </em>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         Silis-Hegh' sees this state of affairs to indicate a great identity crisis. Concrete buildings are springing up everywhere; an otherwise splendid cultural centre becomes a multiplex cinema with a cafeteria that serves coca cola in plastic cups, or the cosy bakery replaced by charm abandoned supermarkets  this is why so many people are looking for the original culture of
         <a href="/europe/greenland/" id="">
          Greenland
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         It is this search for original culture that first
motivated Bolatta Silis-Hegh as an artist. Her early work is very much
informed by the desire to reach out and understand the past while exploring
ways to connect history to the present. She does this through a mixture of multi-media presentations involving
everything from collages which inter-splice Disney graphics with more
traditional imagery, to installations which ask viewers to question how the
world will view the present time through the lens of history.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         This hunt for original culture, though it remains a vital interest for the artist, is an inquiry that
she has departed from in recent years. The sense of national romanticism inherent to these projects prevented Bolatta
Silis-Hegh from being able to see the larger picture surrounding her work and
how it relates
         <a href="/europe/greenland/">
          Greenland
         </a>
         to contemporary culture at large.This
shift in focus can be attributed to the time she spent away from Greenland in
         <a href="http://theculturetrip.com/europe/denmark/">
          Copenhagen
         </a>
         . It was upon coming back to
         <a href="http://theculturetrip.com/europe/greenland/">
          Qaqortoq
         </a>
         that she began to view her
society and its unique context in a new light. By exploring the positive and
negative aspects of her own community, she was able to approach her investigation from a broader point of view and thus more radically analyse how society forms and functions.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="imageWrapper inline-image float-image-left imageHalf">
        <figure>
         <img alt="Bolatta Silis-H&#xF8;egh " src="http://static.theculturetrip.com/images/56-195866-installation-haveforeningen-sisimiut-2068.jpg"/>
         <figcaption>
          <em>
           <a class="imageTextLink" href="http://bolatta.blogspot.co.uk/p/curriculum-vitae.html">
             Bolatta Silis-Hegh
           </a>
          </em>
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         These particular musings helped Bolatta Silis-Hegh to produce what may be her
most famous work,
         <i>
          Haveforeningen
Sisimiut 2068
         </i>
         . A Complex and often humorous meditation on the global
warming crisis, this installation piece acts as a window into the future and takes the form of a mock garden allotment from the year 2068. Bolatta Silis-Hegh replaces the traditional aesthetic
of
         <a href="/europe/greenland/">
          Greenland
         </a>
         s natural spaces with a lush and dense jungle of tropical plants,
palm trees, coconuts and butterflies. Articles from Greenlands rich cultural
past, such as the
         <a href="http://www.holstebrogruppen.dk/ukweb/ukulo.htm">
          Ulo
         </a>
         , or traditional womans knife, an old dog sled, and an
anorak can be seen lying about the installation as casual throw away reminders
of a past lost to greed and human negligence. Yet for its harsh critique of contemporary
reality, the almost hyper real presentation of
         <em>
          Haveforeningen Sisimiut 2068
         </em>
         asks viewers to comically engage with a
vision of the future. The proposed scenario is not a barren and
apocalyptical world, but one nevertheless different from dominant assumptions about the
future; it forces one to consider what it is that defines culture, history and progress.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="text-container">
        <p>
         It is this playful melding of assumption, history and
social commentary that makes Bolatta Silis-Hegh's work so refreshing and topical. She speaks with a
modern voice that is uniquely her own. Informed by the artistic heritage of her
family and her country, she strives to create her own methods to bring topics close
to her heart before the global stage.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          By Shelton Lindsay
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
