            <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
             12 Adjustments to Make in the New Year
            </h1>
            <div class="entry-meta entry-meta-above">
             Posted on
             <time class="entry-date published updated" datetime="2011-12-31T20:59:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
              December 31, 2011
             </time>
             by
             <span class="entry-author vcard" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
              <a class="author-link fn nickname url" href="http://themamazone.com/author/admin/" itemprop="url" title="View all posts by Anitra Elmore">
               <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
                Anitra Elmore
               </span>
              </a>
             </span>
             
             <a class="entry-comments" href="http://themamazone.com/12-adjustments-to-make-in-the-new-year/#comments" title="12 Adjustments to Make in the New Year  Comments">
              0 Comments
             </a>
            </div>
            <!-- .entry-meta -->
           </header>
           <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
            <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="5274093" data-link="http://themamazone.com/12-adjustments-to-make-in-the-new-year/" data-summary="" data-title="12 Adjustments to Make in the New Year">
            </div>
            <p>
             I dont make resolutions.
            </p>
            <p>
             I used to, but I discovered that in most cases, I was setting myself up for failure.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/barkbud/3970043720/">
              <img align="left" alt="Making new year adjustments" border="0" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/lh6.ggpht.com/-vwSQvqTYif0/Tv93tEPexRI/AAAAAAAADPw/FWSEBQ1V12M/adjustments_thumb%25255B21%25255D.jpg?resize=175%2C177" title="Show tapes by bark, on Flickr"/>
             </a>
             You see, I know
             <em>
              me
             </em>
             well, and Ive found that I work better when I focus on making small adjustments instead of the huge changes I come up with in the heat of the resolution-making-moment. Creating resolutions turned out to be an exercise in self torture. Life throws enough challenges my wayit doesnt need my help.
            </p>
            <p>
             So, no more resolutions. Instead, I work on making small adjustments, not just at the turn of the year, but throughout the year as well. In this way, I set myself up for small successes that keep me motivated to keep trying. Here are 12 for the coming year that you might also find worth pursuing.
            </p>
            <ol>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Increase your concern for others.
              </strong>
              Needs are rarely matched by the amount of good deeds required to address them. Make it your mission to decrease the ratio between the two, and do more to help others.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Decrease your concern for self
              </strong>
              . Wanna know how? See #1.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Increase your output.
              </strong>
              This can apply to so muchgiving more, doing more, working more, even exercising more. You know the areas in your life that need more output, and even minor adjustments can make a difference.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Decrease your consumption.
              </strong>
              Make an effort to eat less, use less, shop less and consume less across the board. Your body and the environment will thank you.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Increase your influence.
              </strong>
              Who needs what you have? Find that person or group of people and let em have it. Theres never an excess of positive influence, so pass yours on freely.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Decrease any tendency to follow the crowd.
              </strong>
              Resist peer pressure. Go your own way. Dance to the beat of a different drummer. Recall every clich youve ever heard to remind yourself not to always follow the crowd. Its okay to blaze a trail. In fact, theres probably a crowd waiting for you to do just that.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Increase order in your life.
              </strong>
              Clean up, organize, repair, replace. Put things in order and work to keep it that way.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Decrease chaos.
              </strong>
              While youre working on #7, focus on de-cluttering and getting rid of the crap that surrounds you. No need to merely apply these rules to house cleaningthere is chaos and the need for order all around us.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Increase your peacefulness
              </strong>
              . Simply make a commitment to maintaining peacefulness and watch things begin to change. Follow through on that commitment, and youll be surprised at the results.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Decrease your anger.
              </strong>
              More importantly, adjust how you respond when youre angry. See #9 for help.
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Increase your commitment to love.
              </strong>
              Its the only thing that theres just too little of
             </li>
             <li>
              <strong>
               Decrease life-draining relationships.
              </strong>
              For the most part, we know when its time to let go. We know the signs of toxic relationships but we cling to them anyway for all sorts of ridiculous reasons. Continue to give yourself to toxic people, and there will be less of you to give. Consider that and begin to make the necessary adjustments.
             </li>
            </ol>
            <p>
             Easy peasy.
            </p>
            <p>
             Well, at least easier than 12 resolutions that require major changes. If youre with me on making small adjustments or have a few of your own to add, drop me a comment. In the meantime, Im working myself through the list, which Im posting on the bathroom mirror. Heres hoping the new year finds you well adjusted. Happy 2012!!
            </p>
