      <h1>
       To Katharine:  At Fourteen Months
      </h1>
     </div>
     <div class="tab-content" id="about-top">
      <div class="tabs-poem">
       <h1>
        <a class="blue" href="#poem">
         To Katharine:  At Fourteen Months
        </a>
       </h1>
      </div>
     </div>
     <span class="author">
      By
      <a href="/bio/joelle-biele">
       Joelle  Biele
      </a>
     </span>
     <span class="fullname_search">
      Joelle Biele
     </span>
     <div class="tab-content active" id="poem">
      <div class="poem">
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        All morning, youve studied the laws
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        of spoons, the rules of books, the dynamics
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        of the occasional plate, observed the principles
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        governing objects in motion and objects
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        at rest. To see if it will fall, and if it does,
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        how far, if it will rage like a lost penny
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        or ring like a Chinese gongbecause
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        it doesnt have toyou lean from your chair
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        and hold your cup over the floor.
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        It curves in your hand, it weighs in your palm,
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        it arches like a wave, it is a dipper
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        full of stars, and youre the wind timing
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        the pull of the moon, youre the water
       </div>
       <div style="text-indent: -1em; padding-left: 1em;">
        measuring the distance from which we fall.
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="credit">
       <p>
        Poem copyright 2007 by Joelle Biele, whose most recent book of poetry is
        <em>
         White Summer
        </em>
        , Southern Illinois University Press, 2002.  Poem reprinted from
        <em>
         West Branch
        </em>
        , Fall/Winter, 2007, by permission of Joelle Biele.
        <br/>
        <br/>
       </p>
      </div>

       <div class="bio image">
        <h3>
         <a href="/bio/joelle-biele">
          Biography
         </a>
        </h3>
        <p>
         Joelle Biele is an essayist, a playwright, and a poet. Her first collection of poetry,
         <em>
          White Summer
         </em>
         (2002), won the Crab Orchard Review First Book Award. Anne Leahy, reviewing the book for the
         <em>
          Journal,
         </em>
         described Bieles vivid and careful writing style as full of images and full of alternative images. The language is at once sure of itself and slipping out of surety into lushness.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Biele edited
         <em>
          Elizabeth Bishop and The New . . .
         </em>
        </p>
