       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-02-07T07:00:35+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 7, 2014
         </time>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.jennaburger.com%2F2014%2F02%2Fmake-your-own-chalk-finish-paint%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.sasinteriors.net%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F02%2FBefore-After-Chalk-Finish-Desk.jpg&amp;description=Before%20and%20After%20Desk%20Makeover%20using%20Make%20Your%20Own%20Chalk%20Finish%20Paint.%20Transformation%20by%20%40Jenna_Burger%2C%20www.sasinteriors.net" rel="nobox">
          <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         OK, so let me start off by saying, THIS. IS. MY. FAVORITE. FURNITURE. TRANSFORMATION. yet
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="sample-permalink" tabindex="-1">
         </span>
         And the kicker is, I have a lot of furniture purging planned for the Spring and this desk was on the
         <strong>
          to-go list
         </strong>
         . Can you believe I was going to sell this beauty?!
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres the before and after + a few closeup shots. Ill share the details of the transformation on the other side
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Before-After-Chalk-Finish-Desk.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10251" height="937" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Before-After-Chalk-Finish-Desk-658x1024.jpg" width="602"/>
         </a>
         Pretty crazy that its the same piece, right?! And I am in love with the color.
        </p>
        <p>
         So this months
         <a href="http://www.lowes.com" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           Lowes
          </strong>
         </a>
         challenge for the Creative Team was to create / transform
         <em>
          something
         </em>
         using
         <strong>
          make your own chalk finish paint
         </strong>
         . Since Im in the midst of
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/2014/02/master-makeover-plan-of-attack/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           my master bedroom makeover
          </strong>
         </a>
         and this desk was about to go curbside, I thought it was the perfect opportunity to jazz it up. If the result wasnt right, nothing lost, nothing gained.
        </p>
        <p>
         The desk was purchased years ago from the Bombay company
         <em>
          (during my dark-furniture-all-over days
         </em>
         ) and its served its purpose well, but I was ready to say adios.
        </p>
        <p>
         The making of my own chalk paint made me a bit nervous, since Ive only used the premade mix by Annie Sloan and that took a bit of getting used to (
         <em>
          more for the wax application than the actual paint
         </em>
         ). But, I decided to jump right in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-12-formula.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10263" height="542" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-12-formula-1024x925.jpg" width="601"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium; color: #993366;">
          Here are the supplies I used:
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Latex flat paint (I used Valspar Signature latex)
         </li>
         <li>
          Plaster of Paris
         </li>
         <li>
          Stir sticks
         </li>
         <li>
          Plastic bucket
         </li>
         <li>
          Water (cool, not warm)
         </li>
         <li>
          Measuring Cup ( do not reuse for food)
         </li>
         <li>
          Sealer  wax or polyurthetane
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-23.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10270" height="1319" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-23.jpg" width="583"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium; color: #993366;">
          Here is the formula I used:
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          In a mixing cup, add 1 cup of latex paint
         </li>
         <li>
          Then add 1/3 cup Plaster of Paris + 1/3 cup cold water
         </li>
         <li>
          Mix all together until the Plaster of Paris is fully dissolved
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         The mix will make enough chalk paint to apply about one coat to an average-sized 6 drawer dresser. I ended up using only 1/4 of the mix and apply 2-3 coats, so it makes a lot. Chalk paint goes a lot further than typical paint.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once the formula was mixed, I was ready to paint!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-16.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-10265" height="800" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-16-535x800.jpg" width="535"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its important not to have too much paint on the brush, especially when painting furniture. Its better to apply thinner coats. When applying too much paint at once, drips and runs could develop in the corners and crevices of the furniture.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-17.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10266" height="530" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-17-640x676.jpg" width="501"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         If that happens, no worries. Get as much paint off the brush as possible and drag out the puddled paint so it thins out and covers more of the furniture surface.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-181.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10273" height="595" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-181-640x759.jpg" width="501"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I forgot to mention this neat tid-bit about the desk it actually opens up to a writing surface.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/DSC_1029-001.jpg">
          <img alt="Before Desk" class="aligncenter wp-image-10275" height="440" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/DSC_1029-001-640x546.jpg" width="515"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The difficult part was deciding where to start and stop the paint because I did not want to paint the inside  too difficult.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the end, I chose a start and stop point for where the paint would go. At first I was only going to paint the legs, but wasnt thrilled with the look. Here is a peak
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-20.jpg">
          <img alt="Before Desk" class="aligncenter wp-image-10268" height="625" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-20-535x800.jpg" width="418"/>
         </a>
         <em>
          painting on the counter is so much easier than on the floor!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         So in the end, I decided to paint the top as well, leaving only the line of the desk that opens, the original stained finished. It actually makes for a more interesting piece 
         <em>
          well, I think.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Heres the top with one coat of chalk finish paint.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-21.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10269" height="380" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-21-640x428.jpg" width="571"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         2 coats later (3 in some spots), the desk was complete
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-7.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10258" height="697" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-7-590x800.jpg" width="514"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         And ready to display in my room
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-3.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10254" height="599" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-3-640x599.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I sealed the entire desk with wax that I had on-hand. Others that have used their own chalk finish paint have used
         <a href="http://www.lowes.com/pd_45898-24-78500000_0__?productId=3078969" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           clear finishing wax
          </strong>
         </a>
         (Lowes link) or polyurethane.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         If you want to read more on applying wax, head over to
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/2012/08/my-first-annie-sloan-experience/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           this post
          </strong>
         </a>
         
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/2012/08/my-first-annie-sloan-experience/">
          <img alt="Applying Clear Wax" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-10285" height="428" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/DSC_0664-640x428.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The revamped desk fits perfectly next to my existing dresser with a new gallery wall above (
         <em>
          more details on that coming soon  Im still finishing it up
         </em>
         )
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10252" height="733" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-1-602x800.jpg" width="551"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/2011/01/spray-painting-in-snow/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           The chair
          </strong>
         </a>
         wasanother furniture transformation from a few years ago and I just recently added a new cushion using black and white striped fabric from The Online Fabric Store. The furry looking throw draped over the chair back is actually fabric that I plan on making some fun pillows with.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Check out what this space looked like before
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/IMG_3197.jpg">
          <img alt="Master Before" class="aligncenter wp-image-10276" height="584" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/IMG_3197-600x800.jpg" width="438"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         BIG DIFFERENCE!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Let me show you a few more after shots
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-8.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10259" height="591" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-8-640x684.jpg" width="553"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          What do you think?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Whats your favorite part of my desk? The new color? The lines of the desk? Or the secret compartment of the writing desk? For me, its all fun, but I think I love the paint color the best. By the way, the color is Benjamin Moore Martini Olive. Lowes color matched it for me and used their Valspar Signature Latex paint.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-131.jpg">
          <img alt="Benjamin Moore Martini Olive" class="aligncenter wp-image-10272" height="397" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Chalk-Paint-Desk-131-640x501.jpg" width="507"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Collage.jpg">
          <img alt="Before and After Desk Makeover using Make Your Own Chalk Finish Paint.  Transformation by @Jenna_Burger, www.sasinteriors.net" class="aligncenter wp-image-10271" height="800" src="http://www.sasinteriors.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Collage-607x800.jpg" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <span style="font-size: x-large; color: #993366;">
          My thoughts on making my own Chalk Finish Paint.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I really like it! Comparing it to other chalk finish paints, it is very similar and obviously the cost is a lot less AND you can make any color youd like. With other chalk finish paints there are only about 30-40 different colors to choose from. When making your own, your color choices are limitless. The cost is also significantly less because the Plaster of Paris is about $6 and a quart of paint is about $15-20. Since only one cup is needed, a sample pod / tester most likely would have been enough for this project. Also, I could have used a latex color that I already had on hand (
         <em>
          in my stash
         </em>
         ). Comparing the application process, its very similar and the finish result is about the same. A dull, chalky (but not grainy) finish is what you will get. Waxing is important when using any chalk finish paint because it seals the color and finish. Ive also heard of people using polyurethane as a sealer, but I have never tried that.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: x-large; color: #993366;">
          Why chalk finish paint?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I feel the advantage of using chalk finish paint is the elimination of sanding, prepping, and priming the existing wood. When using chalk finish paint, you can simply start the painting process directly over the stained or painted finish. *If a piece of furniture has a hi-gloss, then I would recommend sanding and possibly priming first so you have a thorough base to paint.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          I hope you enjoyed my adventure of making my own chalk finish paint. I love trying out new things and this is a winner! Have you used chalk finish paint? Have you made your own or have you used a premade mix? Would you try this on a piece of furniture?
         </strong>
        </p>
