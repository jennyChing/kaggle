        <p>
         <em>
          Full Disclosure: I received compensation for this post, but all thoughts and opinions expressed are solely mine.
          <br/>
         </em>
         <br/>
         <a data-pin-color="red" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" data-pin-height="28" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.highheelsandgrills.com%2F2015%2F04%2F.html%20%E2%80%8E&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.highheelsandgrills.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2015%2F04%2FRoasted-Garlic-and-Parmesan-Hummus-Collage.png&amp;description=Roasted%20Garlic%20Parmesan%20Hummus%20-%20This%20hummus%20is%20great%20for%20a%20party%20appetizer%20or%20a%20side%20dish%20at%20dinner.%20Dip%20vegetables%2C%20tortilla%20chips%2C%20pita%20crackers%2C%20or%20pretzels%20for%20the%20perfect%20snack!">
          <img alt="" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_red_28.png"/>

         <br/>
         <strong>
          This Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Hummus has a strong garlic taste, with just a hint of parmesan. It is great for dipping veggies, tortilla chips, pita chips, and pretzels. Makes for a great appetizer at parties or just a side dish for dinner!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Hummus 1" class="aligncenter wp-image-1128 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Roasted-Garlic-and-Parmesan-Hummus-1.png" width="600"/>
         I am really having
         <em>
          way
         </em>
         too much fun with my Blendtec, guys. First,
         <a href="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/2015/04/skinny-raspberry-frozen-yogurt.html">
          Skinny Raspberry Frozen Yogurt
         </a>
         , then
         <a href="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/2015/04/bananas-foster-frappe.html">
          Bananas Foster Frappes
         </a>
         , and now this amazing Roasted Garlic Parmesan Hummus. I just cant stop! Nor do I want to. I just hope you guys dont get sick of blender recipes anytime soon, because I have a feeling that they arent going toslow down quite yet. #sorrynotsorry
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Hummus 2" class="aligncenter wp-image-1132 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Roasted-Garlic-and-Parmesan-Hummus-2.png" width="600"/>
         This was my first time making hummus and I must say that Im surprised at how easy it is and how much better it tastes than the kind you buy at the store. In fact, Im wondering why anybody buys it at the store these days, with how ridiculously easy it is to make at home. Especially if you cheat like me and use canned chickpeas. Dont judge. Sometimes, you need a snack and you need it now. I dont have the patience to wait 2 days for fresh chickpeas to be ready! I do want to try it one day though. Youll be the first to know when I do. 
        </p>

         <img alt="Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Hummus" class="aligncenter wp-image-1130 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Roasted-Garlic-and-Parmesan-Hummus.png" width="600"/>
         One of my favorite things about hummus {besides its deliciousness and the fact that it is
         <em>
          packed
         </em>
         with protein and fiber} is how many different things go well with it. Vegetables, tortilla chips, pita crackers, pretzels, you name it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Roasted Garlic and Parmesan Hummus 4" class="aligncenter wp-image-1129 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Roasted-Garlic-and-Parmesan-Hummus-4.png" width="600"/>
         In fact, I use hummus as a tool against Addi, quite frequently, to get her to eat vegetables. She loves it with broccoli and tortilla chips the most, but will also eat carrots, peas, and peppers with it too. Hummus is one of those snacks that I can give to her and walk away feeling like a rocking mom, because she eats it so well and its super good for her.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Dabbling Duck Boutique 1" class="aligncenter wp-image-1139 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Dabbling-Duck-Boutique-1.png" width="600"/>
         My one and only gripe with hummus though, is how stinking messy it can be in little toddler hands. Every time Addi eats it, you can guarantee it will be all over her face, hands, clothes, hair, etc. I feel like I have to change her clothes and bathe her after every time she eats it, which is a huge deterrent to giving it to her at all. So annoying. We tried out a new bib from
         <a href="http://www.dabblingduckboutique.com">
          Dabbling Duck Boutique
         </a>
         , while snacking on it this week, and Im pretty sure Im going to need about 5 more of these babies.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Dabbling Duck Boutique 2" class="aligncenter wp-image-1133 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.highheelsandgrills.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Dabbling-Duck-Boutique-2.png" width="600"/>
         Theyre all handmade, super cute and stylish, but more importantly  theyre completely functional! Here are a few of my favorite features:
        </p>
        <p>
         1  They have little sleeves that help keep food off your kids clothes.
         <br/>
         2  The neckline comes up high enough that it will catch that extra drool that always comes when a toddler eats, again preventing the clothes from getting messy.
         <br/>
         3  It ties in the back, so your child cant rip it off. {Biggest pet peeve ever!}
         <br/>
         4  Its made of super soft fabric, so it doesnt rub uncomfortably!
        </p>

          Facebook

         and
         <a href="https://instagram.com/dabblingduckboutique/">
          Instagram
         </a>
         to see their newest prints. They sell leather moccasins as well, so its a one-stop shop! Also, Adelaide totally approves of this message. #modelbaby
        </p>

         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 large head garlic, roasted {Click
            <a href="http://www.food.com/recipe/how-to-roast-garlic-303247" target="_blank">
             here
            </a>
            for roasting instructions}
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup fresh parmesan, grated
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tablespoons tahini
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            5 tablespoons olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            14 cup warm water

            12 teaspoon sea salt
           </li>

            {Optional} Paprika, for garnish
           </li>
          </ul>

          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Squeeze the garlic cloves out of its shell into a food processor.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the rest of the ingredients to the food processor and blend until completely smooth.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Scrape down sides, as needed, and then continue blending to make sure all of the ingredients are blended evenly. If hummus is too thick, add extra olive oil, 1 tsp. at a time, and blend in between additions.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Sprinkle paprika over hummus and serve with vegetables, crackers, or pretzels.
