       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Sew a Shopping Bag
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2015" style="width: 197px">
         <a href="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/sbcol.jpg">
          <img alt="fabric shopping bag pattern" class="size-full wp-image-2015" data-lazy-src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/sbcol.jpg" height="230" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="187"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="fabric shopping bag pattern" class="size-full wp-image-2015" height="230" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/sbcol.jpg" width="187"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Home Sewn Shopping Bag
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          How to Sew a Fabric Shopping Bag
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         It seems that every time I turn around, Im being handed another silly plastic shopping bag. I have lots of places to recycle them (reuse them, actually)  the library takes them, etc., I still get tired of dealing with them. Invariably, I collect several bags stuffed with other bags before I remember to take them to the library, and thats just wrong.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know a lot of people carry their own tote bags, and I have a couple of them, but I often end up with five or six bags worth of groceries, and tote bags arent that convenient to load. So I looked more closely at one of the ubiquitous plastic bags here at Silver Seams World Headquarters, and realized it would be really, really easy to sew.
        </p>
        <p>
         A serger (overlocker, for non-USAns) helps here, but isnt necessary.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="sb (6K)" class="alignleft" data-lazy-src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/images/sewing/sb.jpg" height="230" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="297"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="sb (6K)" class="alignleft" height="230" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/images/sewing/sb.jpg" width="297"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Take a piece of fabric, 37 1/2 wide by 21 1/2 long.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fold it in half long-ways, and stitch the side closed.
        </p>
        <p>
         I assume 1/4 seams here, but its not critical.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you sewed it right sides together (RST), turn it now  but you can just leave the seam inside the fold.
        </p>
        <p>
         These are utilitarian, not beautiful.
        </p>
        <div style="float: right; padding: 10px; margin: 10px;">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- MidContent -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-0721241293252684" data-ad-slot="5252895686" style="display:inline-block;width:300px;height:250px">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="sb2 (5K)" class="alignleft" data-lazy-src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/images/sewing/sb2.jpg" height="240" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="206"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="sb2 (5K)" class="alignleft" height="240" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/images/sewing/sb2.jpg" width="206"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Now fold in the sides slightly over 3.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="sb3 (6K)" class="alignright" data-lazy-src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/images/sewing/sb3.jpg" height="236" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="206"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="sb3 (6K)" class="alignright" height="236" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/images/sewing/sb3.jpg" width="206"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Now is where youd tuck that seam in, but it doesnt matter.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now sew across the bottom. This seam goes on the outside, no doubt about it.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre using a conventional machine, you might want to zigzag stitch the seam allowance to cut down on fraying.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then cut the bag opening out.
        </p>
        <p>
         Youll want to catch the inside fold too, otherwise your handle wont have a hole in it.
        </p>
        <div class="clear">
        </div>
        <p>
         <img alt="sb4 (5K)" class="alignleft" data-lazy-src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/sewing/images/sb4.jpg" height="240" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/dev/wp-content/plugins/speed-booster-pack/inc/images/1x1.trans.gif" width="218"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="sb4 (5K)" class="alignleft" height="240" src="http://www.allfreecrafts.com/sewing/images/sb4.jpg" width="218"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Its probably best to finish the raw edges of the handle area first, before sewing the shoulder seams of the handles. You can use an overlock stitch, zig-zag stitch (multiple times is best) or go all-out and put bias tape on them. Then, keeping the folds in place (youre sewing through four layers), stitch across the handle seams. You may want to open up the handles, press the seam allowance to one side, and sew it down. This seam takes a lot of weight, remember.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to get really fancy, you can sew buttonholes and thread your bags onto the metal arms just like the plastic bags go, but thats four buttonholes (dont buttonhole-stitch your handles closed!) when you could just hang the bag on the handles instead.
        </p>
        <p>
         All done now when they ask Paper or plastic? you can answer Fabric!
        </p>

          Silver Seams.com
         </a>
        </p>
