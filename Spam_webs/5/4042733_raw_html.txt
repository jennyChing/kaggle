           <p>
            The first batch of old Atari games excavated from a New Mexico landfill went up for auction last week and the highest seller went to a Canadian bidder.
           </p>
           <p>
            Some 800 old games were dug up in Alamogordo, N.M., in April as part of a documentary exploring an urban legend that the Atari Corporation had buried thousands of unsold games in 1983 after a disastrous fiscal year.
           </p>
           <p>
            A few copies of the games were donated to museums, but the rest ere being sold on eBay, with the proceeds going to the city the Tularosa Basin Historical Society.
           </p>
           <p>
            The game that fetched the highest price was a copy of E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial, which went to a Canadian buyer for $1,537.
           </p>
           <p>
            Based on the Steven Spielberg movie, it is one of the biggest commercial failures in video game history.
           </p>
           <p>
            The historical society's vice-president, Joe Lewandowski,
            <a href="http://www.alamogordonews.com/alamogordo-news/ci_26943116/atari-games-net-alamogordo-over-37-000-ebay" target="_blank">
             told Alamogordo News
            </a>
            he's thrilled with the auction's outcome.
           </p>
           <p>
            "I keep getting messages from people around the world asking me if there's anymore left. It's crazy," Lewandowski said.
           </p>
