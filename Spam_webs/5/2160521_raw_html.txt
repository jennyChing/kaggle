       </h1>

        <div style="float:left;padding-right:12px;">

        <div style="text-align: justify;">
         <b>
          <i>
           "If the world seems cold to you, kindle fires to warm it"
          </i>
          . - Lucy Larcom (American Poet).
         </b>
         <a href="http://twitter.com/home?status=If the world seems cold to you, kindle fires to warm it. Lucy Larcom http://www.techcinema.com/2015/01/how-to-choose-best-space-heater.html" rel="nofollow" target="new">
          <b>
           Tweet This
          </b>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         While if your room feels cold to you, just let the best space heater warm you.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         But how can you choose the right heater for your room when the market are filled with thousands of choices???
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Don`t be confused, the following guide will help you to choose wisely, as follows:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: x-large;">
          <b>
           1. Forms
          </b>
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         In general, space heaters are fall into 2 forms, Radiant &amp; Convection.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Radiant is the most common, safe, and effective model in the market but it only provides instant warmth to the immediate area. This model also often very hot to the touch.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         While if you need to warm the entire room quickly then you should buy Convection model but it not as powerful as a radiant room heater.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: x-large;">
          <b>
           2. Design
          </b>
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
           Baseboard
         </b>
         - low-profile and can be installed in the corner of your room without taking up any space. This model tend to work best in drafty rooms.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         It pulls cold air in and pushes warm air out. The warmth produced might take longer to spread.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         A low-profile baseboard heaters also dont have fans at all, so they are going to be totally silent.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-u8BnEG2Q9kg/VL0wtRyPBBI/AAAAAAABeEw/XpSbIPlb9CA/s1600/Best%2BSpace%2Bheater.jpg" imageanchor="Best Space Heater" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-u8BnEG2Q9kg/VL0wtRyPBBI/AAAAAAABeEw/XpSbIPlb9CA/s1600/Best%2BSpace%2Bheater.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <b>
           Wall heater
         </b>
         - great space savers and cost only pennies to operate. This model is also a safe option if you have pets or children since you can install it out of their reach.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
           Electric fireplace heater
         </b>
         - most of these models can warm up to 400 sq. ft. of space and offer attractive appearances.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
           Freestanding portable heater
         </b>
         - consist of oil-filled radiator heaters and ceramic heaters.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Oil-filled heaters takes a little bit longer to warm a space but once it's warm it'll stay that way longer, even when the heater's turned off. Great choice to warm larger areas for a longer period of time.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         While the ceramic heaters pull the cold air and warmed over ceramic plates then the blower fan spread the heat to warm your room quickly. This model also tend to make less noise than other styles of heaters.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
           Non-electric heater
         </b>
          mostly fueled by propane, natural gas, or kerosene which are made for small spaces.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: x-large;">
          <b>
           3. Heating Method
          </b>
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
           Conduction
         </b>
         - will maintain a constant temperature after it has had a chance to warm up but it will take a long time for heat to spread to the rest of the room.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
           Convection
         </b>
         - creates heat by moving the air with a fan that blows air across a hot coil or panel. Since it uses a fan, it may be noisy or produce drafts.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <b>
           Radiation
         </b>
         - aims infrared waves to specific molecules that absorb the waves and are warmed by the energy. Great choice to provide warmth to a targeted area.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: x-large;">
          <b>
           4. Coverage Area
          </b>
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         The amount of watts produced by the space heater determines the coverage area. 10 watts will warm a 1 sq. ft. area. Some heater use measure output in BTUs (British Thermal Units). 1 BTU/hour is about 3.41 watts.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: x-large;">
          <b>
           5. Safety Certificate
          </b>
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         For safety reasons, it is wise if you choose a unit that carry Underwriter's Laboratory (UL) label or ETL, or CSA.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: x-large;">
          <b>
           6. Features To Consider
          </b>
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
          Timer  useful feature to set the heater to go off before you go to sleep. Its not a good idea, from a safety point of view, to sleep with a heater running in your bedroom.
         <br/>
          Automatic heat sensor that shuts the heater off when they overheat.
         <br/>
          Handle  remember that some heaters are too hot for the touch so a handle is very useful when you unit to remove the heather safely.
         <br/>
          Oxygen depletion sensor or Low-oxygen shutoff  to shut down the unit when the sensor senses a reduction in the amount of oxygen in the air due to the buildup of carbon dioxide.
         <br/>
          Thermostat or heat setting to maintain your desired temperature.
         <br/>
          Tilt cut off - to turns the heater off when they are knocked over, this prevents the carpet from being burnt.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: x-large;">
          <b>
           7. Safety Tips
          </b>
         </span>
         <br/>
          Make sure you always unplug your heater when not in use.
         <br/>
          Never leave the heater unattended.
         <br/>
          Plug the heater directly into the outlet.
         <br/>
          Do not put cords under heavy objects or in walkways.
         <br/>
          Only place your heater on a dry &amp; flat surface where children and pets can't reach it.
         <br/>
          Keep your heater at least 3 feet away from walls, furniture, and draperies.
         <br/>
          Always use a heater upright.
         <br/>
          Be sure that a plug is secure and tight.
         <br/>
          Replace missing guards or controls before using further.
         <br/>
          Do not use the high setting for long periods of time.
