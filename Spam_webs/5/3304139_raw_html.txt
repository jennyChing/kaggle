      <h3>
       You'd Rather be English: an Irish Fan's Saturday Night
      </h3>
      <p>
       <em>
        <img alt="Cowman" border="0" src="http://shinymedia.blogs.com/photos/uncategorized/2007/09/18/cowman.jpg" style="FLOAT: left; MARGIN: 0px 5px 5px 0px" title="Cowman"/>
        'Ultimate Irishman' Andy Cowman is embedded for the duration of the World Cup in an off licence in Wimbledon
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Well, I got lucky as I was able to pass on a dinner offer, thus allowing my wife to go out. The children are in bed and I have the large screen to myself. OK, pre-game checks:
      </p>
      <p>
       * kids asleep or as dammed near: check
       <br/>
       * beers cold: check
       <br/>
       * comfy seat: check
       <br/>
       * nibbles: check
       <br/>
       * anti-ITV drone, aka Radio 5 (I find it helps kids sleep then I don't have to scream at the crap ITV coverage): check, check
       <br/>
       * anti-depressants (beers), just in case: check
      </p>
      <script type="text/javascript">
       &lt;!--

      </script>

      <p>
       There I was, hoping against hope that the nightmare of the Namibian game would be a distant, nay expunged, memory! Oh NO. First half sees Ireland overcomplicate everything and get muscled off the park by a strong Georgian eight. The ball acts like this: overturns, spills, knocks on, passes badly and in the lineout is erractic. Not the fault of the Irish players, mind you. Georgia get the first points, by which time I'm on beer number two! The first half plays out with me using all my restraint to keep the noise down. 7-3! WTF is going on? Am I watching the Triple Crown champions or the Triple Clown champions?!Reality check tells me that it's the latter. I need a half time break as I'm on number 4 beer already and the ITV's half time analysis is so typically pants that it may force me to take even more anti-depressants.
      </p>
      <p>
       OK, back. I know EOS has given the boys a pep talk; it will all change. It appears not as the game opens for the second half with Georgia still up for it. I can only think that in many an Irish household rosary beads are being worked overtime. Here we go on the attack. Stringer gets third phase ball and looks to skip a pass to BOD... well, that's the plan. If Thomas Edison was alive he would be proud to see the use of the telegraph in the game of rugby. Georgia open the telegraph to read TRY. Wonderfully taken 60-odd-metre run in. Yes, I can lip-read and that was BOD calling Stringer a 'complete f***ing **nt'! The game continues with Georgia putting on the display of their lives. On come the Irish replacements, who fare no better. Each and every move of Ireland's is countered. OK, "Gir the Swerve" does get a consolation try, but this hides the real quality of the performance.
      </p>
      <p>
       By the last quarter, all beers are gone and I am officially depressed. I get the sweats. Georgian pressure mounts and they look like they could score. A few drop goal attempts go astray; they mount a fierce attack on the Irish line... again, and again. They get over, the video ref gets called in... Jesus Christ, WTF? Leamy just got his hand under it. I'm screaming by this point. The stadium is urging on the Georgians... I have to pinch myself that I am not dreaming this!
      </p>
      <p>
       14-10 Ireland as the whistle goes. In the post-match interview, Stringer says: "We set ourselves the goal of winning our pool games. That's what we have done." I'll kill the scranny tw** when I see him next! Eoin Reddan: he's the man, plus a sensible hairstyle. We are not just marginally, but by Grand Canyon dimensions, more shoite than England.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       I'm holding my head in abject misery. Capped by a returning wife, who rubs more salt in the open wounds: "You won't need to go see the Argentina v Ireland game now, so sell that ticket!". I wander off in search of the Relate number.
      </p>
