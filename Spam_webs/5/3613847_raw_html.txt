         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           Throughout the world the use of new psychoactive substances has increased by 11%
          </strong>
          between 2013 and 2014. A strong growth that worries
          <a href="https://www.incb.org/incb/en/news/AR2014/annual_report_2014.html" target="_blank">
           the International Narcotics Control Board (INCB)
          </a>
          of the United Nations, which counted 388 substances in circulation last October. The increasing traffic of new psychotropic substances that are beyond the limits of the law has become a public health problem of global significance, said the INCB a problem that has become more serious in recent years. The primary reason is the ability of drug dealers to
          <span style="color: #333333; font-style: normal; line-height: 24.375px;">
           quickly
          </span>
          place on the market perfectly legal goods, especially through the Internet, while authorities still leave empty spaces in the case of legal highs. China is among the leading producers of new psychotropic substances, the latest annual report
          <span style="color: #333333; font-style: normal; line-height: 24.375px;">
           warns.
          </span>
