         <span class="posttitle">
          <a href="http://scriptwrecked.com/2011/03/27/surprise-your-audience/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Surprise Your Audience">
           Surprise Your Audience
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="postauthor">
          Author:
          <a href="http://scriptwrecked.com/author/scriptwrecked/" rel="author" title="Posts by Trevor Mayes">
           Trevor Mayes
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="date_m">
          Mar
         </span>
         <span class="date_d">
          27
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry">
         <p>
          <img alt="Ros goes down the elevator shaft" class="alignright size-full wp-image-4219" height="267" src="http://scriptwrecked.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/LALaw-Elevator.jpg" style="margin-left: 20px; margin-right: 20px;" title="Ros goes down the elevator shaft" width="200"/>
          A friend sent me this excerpt from a recent interview with
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0005082/" target="_blank">
           David E. Kelley
          </a>
          (
          <em>
           Harrys Law, Boston Legal, Boston Public, The Practice, Ally McBeal, Chicago Hope,L.A. Law
          </em>
          ). It highlights an important mindset to have when crafting scenes:
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <span style="color: #333399;">
           <strong>
            L.A. Law is where we first got a taste of what would be your trademark, those surprise, odd twists, like
            <a href="http://www.ew.com/ew/gallery/0,,20321301_20426924_20849188,00.html" target="_blank">
             Roz going down the elevator shaft
            </a>
            . Where do those ideas come from?
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          <span style="color: #333399;">
           I promise it isnt drugs. You know, you sort of get smarter through the years, but thats the one question Im really still unable to answer. I do subscribe to the theory that it is entertainment, and when people sit down in their La-Z-Boy chair at the end of the night, they maybe should be able to see something that theyre not going to see in everyday life. So arguments of mine [between characters] will tend to be more melodramatic, and some of the eccentricities will be heightened. Thats just kind of what I like to do. Also, I loved The Twilight Zone as a kid, and Outer Limits and shows like that, which went in directions that you just never imagined. I do do that. I do say, Okay, this is the scene, this is the normal way it would go. Is there another way it could possibly go that fits within the context of the show that you may not see coming?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          So what are the important takeaways? Give the audience:
         </p>
         <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
          A) something that theyre not going to see in everyday life.
          <br/>
          B) something that they may not see coming.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Bonus
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you have 4 1/2 minutes, I highly recommend watching the following animated short that I found recently, created by
          <a href="http://grickle.com/" target="_blank">
           Graham Annable
          </a>
          . It has several moments that I hadnt seen before and definitely didnt see coming.
         </p>
         <p>
          Pay special attention to how the slow pacing (especially in one particular scene) is brilliantly utilized for humorouseffect.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="390" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/tuhZuMl9StM" title="YouTube video player" width="640">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Full David E. Kelley interview via
          <a href="http://nymag.com/daily/entertainment/2011/03/david_e_kelley_interview_wonde.html?mid=373214&amp;rid=422568499" target="_blank">
           Vulture
          </a>
          .
         </p>
