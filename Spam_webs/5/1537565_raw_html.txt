    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Simon the Sorcerer
       </b>
      </i>
      is a series of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Point-and-click" title="Point-and-click">
       point-and-click
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Adventure_game" title="Adventure game">
       adventure games
      </a>
      created by
      <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Soft" title="Adventure Soft">
       Adventure Soft
      </a>
      . The series follows the adventures of an unwilling hero of the same name and has a strong
      <a href="/wiki/Fantasy" title="Fantasy">
       fantasy
      </a>
      setting similar to
      <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Entertainment" title="Sierra Entertainment">
       Sierra
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Quest" title="King's Quest">
        King's Quest
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Westwood_Studios" title="Westwood Studios">
       Westwood
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Kyrandia" title="The Legend of Kyrandia">
        The Legend of Kyrandia
       </a>
      </i>
      series. The game varies in style, however, as it is more poised to be a parody of the fantasy genre than a member of the genre itself, with many renowned folklore characters appearing differently from what they are generally presumed to be.
     </p>
     <p>
      The first two games are often compared with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monkey_Island_series" title="Monkey Island series">
       <i>
        Monkey Island
       </i>
       series
      </a>
      in terms of style and humour,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Terry_Pratchett" title="Terry Pratchett">
       Terry Pratchett
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Discworld" title="Discworld">
        Discworld
       </a>
      </i>
      novels and derivative
      <a href="/wiki/Discworld#Merchandise" title="Discworld">
       games
      </a>
      . Unlike many older adventure games, the entire series is still available for purchase new.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The first and second games in the series are also playable using
      <a href="/wiki/ScummVM" title="ScummVM">
       ScummVM
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="About_Simon">
       About Simon
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: About Simon">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Simon the Sorcerer is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teenager" title="Teenager">
       teenager
      </a>
      transported into a fantasy world as a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sorcerer_(person)" title="Sorcerer (person)">
       sorcerer
      </a>
      dressed in a cloak and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pointy_hat" title="Pointy hat">
       pointy hat
      </a>
      ; his cloak and hat are purple in the first game, but change to red for the rest of the series (aside from possible magical colour changes in the third game). He must use his logic and magical skills to solve puzzles as he progresses through the games.
     </p>
     <p>
      Simon is rude and insulting to many of the characters he meets. This tends to interfere with the success of his quests, forcing him to go to ridiculous lengths in order to complete puzzles. Simon also breaks the
      <a href="/wiki/Fourth_wall" title="Fourth wall">
       fourth wall
      </a>
      with his comments about adventure games.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_the_Sorcerer">
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Simon the Sorcerer">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_the_Sorcerer" title="Simon the Sorcerer">
       Simon the Sorcerer
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      <i>
       Simon the Sorcerer
      </i>
      was released by Adventure Soft on 2 January 1993 in
      <a href="/wiki/DOS" title="DOS">
       DOS
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Amiga" title="Amiga">
       Amiga
      </a>
      formats.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The story begins with the protagonist, Simon, as an ordinary teenager. His dog, "Chippy", discovers a chest in the loft of his house containing a spellbook titled "Ye Olde Spellbooke". Simon throws the book onto the floor in contempt, but a portal opens above it. Chippy goes through the portal and Simon follows.
     </p>
     <p>
      After entering the portal, Simon finds himself in another world. After escaping from some goblins who intended to eat him, he discovers that he has been brought on a quest to rescue the wizard
      <i>
       Calypso
      </i>
      from the evil sorcerer
      <i>
       Sordid
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The game includes parodies of various popular books and
      <a href="/wiki/Fairy_tale" title="Fairy tale">
       fairy tales
      </a>
      , including
      <a href="/wiki/Rapunzel" title="Rapunzel">
       Rapunzel
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="The Lord of the Rings">
        The Lord of the Rings
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia" title="The Chronicles of Narnia">
        The Chronicles of Narnia
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jack_and_the_Beanstalk" title="Jack and the Beanstalk">
       Jack and the Beanstalk
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Three_Billy_Goats_Gruff" title="Three Billy Goats Gruff">
       Three Billy Goats Gruff
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Simon is voiced in this game by
      <a href="/wiki/Chris_Barrie" title="Chris Barrie">
       Chris Barrie
      </a>
      , known for his roles in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Red_Dwarf" title="Red Dwarf">
        Red Dwarf
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Brittas_Empire" title="The Brittas Empire">
        The Brittas Empire
       </a>
      </i>
      . This game marks the first appearance of two evil, if somewhat inept, demons, who are recurring characters throughout the remainder of the series.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_the_Sorcerer_II:_The_Lion.2C_the_Wizard_and_the_Wardrobe">
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer II: The Lion, the Wizard and the Wardrobe
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Simon the Sorcerer II: The Lion, the Wizard and the Wardrobe">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_the_Sorcerer_II:_The_Lion,_the_Wizard_and_the_Wardrobe" title="Simon the Sorcerer II: The Lion, the Wizard and the Wardrobe">
       Simon the Sorcerer II: The Lion, the Wizard and the Wardrobe
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      <i>
       Simon the Sorcerer II: The Lion, the Wizard and the Wardrobe
      </i>
      , commonly known as
      <i>
       Simon the Sorcerer II
      </i>
      , was released by
      <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Soft" title="Adventure Soft">
       Adventure Soft
      </a>
      in 1995. The title is a parody of the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia" title="The Chronicles of Narnia">
        Chronicles of Narnia
       </a>
      </i>
      book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Lion,_the_Witch_and_the_Wardrobe" title="The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe">
        The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe
       </a>
      </i>
      . The wizard, Simon, the Wardrobe, and the lion all feature in the game.
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Bowles_(voice_actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brian Bowles (voice actor) (page does not exist)">
       Brian Bowles
      </a>
      plays the voice of Simon, making him sound older, presumably in his late-teens.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the game, the father of a peasant boy, Runt, burns his book of magic and throws it into the centre of a
      <a href="/wiki/Pentagram" title="Pentagram">
       pentagram
      </a>
      on the floor, inadvertently bringing Sordid back to life as a spirit. He transfers his spirit to a robotic body, and sends a magical
      <a href="/wiki/Wardrobe" title="Wardrobe">
       wardrobe
      </a>
      to retrieve Simon from his own dimension. The wardrobe mistakenly brings Simon to the door-step of Calypso's Magic Shop. Upon returning to consciousness Simon learns that, in order to return home, he must find the wardrobe's power source, called
      <i>
       mucusade
      </i>
      (a pun on
      <a href="/wiki/Lucozade" title="Lucozade">
       Lucozade
      </a>
      ).
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_the_Sorcerer.27s_Puzzle_Pack">
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer's Puzzle Pack
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Simon the Sorcerer's Puzzle Pack">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A
      <a href="/wiki/Puzzle_video_game" title="Puzzle video game">
       puzzle game
      </a>
      released in 1998 by Adventure Soft for Microsoft Windows. It contains three games:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <i>
        Swampy Adventures
       </i>
        The player must help the Swampling through 50 levels of puzzles as he attempts to rescue his children from the evil Sordid.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        NoPatience
       </i>
        A set of four
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solitaire" title="Solitaire">
        solitaire
       </a>
       card games, featuring a unique
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer
       </i>
       themed deck.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        Jumble
       </i>
        A twist on a
       <a href="/wiki/Sliding_puzzle" title="Sliding puzzle">
        sliding puzzle
       </a>
       featuring a constantly changing animation.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      The game also features a desktop
      <a href="/wiki/Digital_pet" title="Digital pet">
       virtual pet
      </a>
      known as "
      <i>
       Demon in My Pocket
      </i>
      " as well as three
      <i>
       Simon the Sorcerer
      </i>
      desktop themes.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_the_Sorcerer_3D">
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer 3D
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Simon the Sorcerer 3D">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_the_Sorcerer_3D" title="Simon the Sorcerer 3D">
       Simon the Sorcerer 3D
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The third adventure game in the series, Simon the Sorcerer 3D was initially designed fully in 2D and was intended to be released as a high quality 2D adventure. Due to publisher hesitation in picking up a 2D title, the developers later decided to switch it to a 3D adventure game.
     </p>
     <p>
      The game follows on directly after
      <i>
       Simon the Sorcerer II'
      </i>
      s open ending, with a cut-scene at the beginning telling the player a connecting story of how Simon's body was rejoined to his soul by a new character, Melissa.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_the_Sorcerer_4:_Chaos_Happens">
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer 4: Chaos Happens
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Simon the Sorcerer 4: Chaos Happens">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_the_Sorcerer_4:_Chaos_Happens" title="Simon the Sorcerer 4: Chaos Happens">
       Simon the Sorcerer 4: Chaos Happens
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The fourth adventure game marks a return to the heralded
      <a href="/wiki/Point_and_click" title="Point and click">
       point-and-click
      </a>
      interface and features high quality pre-rendered backgrounds. The characters are in 3D.
     </p>
     <p>
      The German version of this game was released 23 February 2007,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and was developed by
      <a href="/wiki/Silver_Style_Entertainment" title="Silver Style Entertainment">
       Silver Style Entertainment
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The English version of this game was released on 17 October 2008,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and was published by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Playlogic" title="Playlogic">
       Playlogic
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_the_Sorcerer_5:_Who.27d_Even_Want_Contact.3F.21">
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer 5: Who'd Even Want Contact?!
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Simon the Sorcerer 5: Who'd Even Want Contact?!">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_the_Sorcerer_5" title="Simon the Sorcerer 5">
       Simon the Sorcerer 5
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      In the fifth adventure game, aliens threaten the fantasy world. The German version was released for PC on 26 March 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was released for digital download in English on 17 February 2010.
     </p>
     <p>
      <br/>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Simon_the_Sorcerer_6:_Between_Worlds">
       <i>
        Simon the Sorcerer 6: Between Worlds
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_the_Sorcerer_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Simon the Sorcerer 6: Between Worlds">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In this game Simon returns to his British, slacker roots and is forced to deal with a convergence of two worlds. As portals to another dimension begin to open up, he is forced to come face-to-face with his more hard-working, respectful and competent counterpart Simone with whom he must work to bring balance back to their universes.
     </p>
     <p>
      The game harkens back to the original two Simon the Sorcerer games and will feature fully hand-drawn 2D animated worlds and characters. It is currently in development with StoryBeasts, an Irish company based in Dublin, Ireland.
     </p>
