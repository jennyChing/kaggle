                <h1 class="entry-title">
                 The Ultimate Guide to Creating an Eco Home
                </h1>
                <div class="entry-meta entry-meta-above">
                 Posted on
                 <span class="entry-date published" title="2014-02-20T17:56:09+00:00">
                  February 20, 2014
                 </span>
                 by
                 <a class="author-link fn nickname url" href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/blog/author/lelahi/" title="View all posts by Laura Davis">
                  Laura Davis
                 </a>
                 
                 <a class="entry-comments" href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/blog/ultimate-guide-eco-home/#comments" title="The Ultimate Guide to Creating an Eco Home  Comments">

                </a>
                <div class="entry-content">
                 <p>
                  They say home is where the heart is, so are you caring for it? Find out in our ultimate guide to eco-proofing your home tips and advice on how to lower your carbon footprint room by room.
                  <span id="more-5158">
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <b>
                   Why Bother Being Green?
                  </b>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  Living green is about reducing the impact your day to day actions have on the environment. Changing small habits such as turning off the light when you leave a room to larger community efforts like recycling all makes a difference to support a healthier living space for people, nature and wildlife. It also contributes to the worldwide fight against global warming that affects all of us.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Lets take the example of recycling that we just mentioned. Did you realise that the key aim of recycling is to lessen the amount of raw materials being manufactured. Not only does the use of raw materials destroy natural habitats, the effort to harness raw resources means energy is spent and carbon dioxide (C02) is created, which is harmful to our ozone layer. By reusing materials which have already been manufactured, like cardboard, glass and plastic, less energy is exhausted. According to
                  <a href="http://www.recyclenow.com/">
                   Recycle Now
                  </a>
                  the UK currently saves 18 million tonnes of C02 each year by recycling.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  As for the bigger picture climate change is something which is causing increasing concern with us all. Greenhouse gases which are created by human activity like factory production and transportation inhibit the suns radiation from being reflected into the atmosphere and it is instead left to heat the earths surface. Its like the WWF says: The good news is, if we accept that were a major cause of climate change, we can choose to do something about it.
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <b>
                   How Much Money Could You Save?
                  </b>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  In addition to its green merits employing eco-friendly tactics at home and at work can impact your cash savings for the good. Sustainable living is after all about making the most of resources which already exist so it makes sense that the result will be expenses kept to a minimum.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Efforts for the greater good like recycling will not have a direct impact on your bills, however smart thinking like carpooling to work, boiling just enough water to make a cuppa and insulating your loft will all help you to spend less. In the room by room guide below, we also recommend renewable technologies which excel in being cost effective!
                 </p>
                 <h1 style="text-align: left;">
                  <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                   How to Make Your Home Eco-friendly One Room at a Time
                  </span>
                 </h1>
                 <p>
                  Weve taken time to explore some of the busiest areas in the home to see where you can save on money and make changes to help out mother-nature.
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <strong>
                   Savings in the Kitchen
                  </strong>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Kitchen-interior.jpg">
                   <img alt="Kitchen interior" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-5171" height="682" src="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Kitchen-interior-1024x682.jpg" width="1024"/>
                  </a>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The kitchen is a hive of activity. You use it for cooking, cleaning, storage and to socialise. Out of all rooms in the home, this is the place packed with energy guzzling appliances and devices. Think about the items you have. Does it include a toaster, kettle, microwave oven, dishwasher? How about a washing machine? These items are each guilty of wasting you electricity and we havent even begun to explore water wastage!
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Energy Saving|
                   <span style="color: #808080;">
                    Kitchen Tips
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  For the modern family home white goods are a necessity and a convenience. To save on money and energy wastage then this means its up to the user/buyer to take control of the situation. When replacing or buying a new electrical appliance always opt for an energy rated product. Keep an eye out of the
                  <a href="http://www.energysavingtrust.org.uk/Take-action/Find-Energy-Saving-Trust-Recommended-products">
                   Energy Saving Trust Recommended
                  </a>
                  label, an indication that a product will save you energy at home based on research by this charitable Foundation.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  How you use appliances will also make a big difference. Think before you act. Have you filled the dishwasher up completely before switching it on to maximise the amount you can clean in one cycle? Have you placed too much water into the kettle therefore wasting electricity unnecessarily?
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Food Waste
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      | Kitchen Tips
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Cooking food and eating it is what your kitchen revolves around but many of us pay little attention to how we dispose of it. Again, how you act here could help to save a few quid. Firstly, is it really worth throwing away? With a little effort things like animal bones can be made into stock for casseroles and soups, stale bread can be transformed into treacle tarts and chicken nuggets, too much rice can be adapted into a Chinese style takeaway; and if you think that this sounds like too much effort or complicated, there is in fact a wealth of chefs and supermarkets creating advice and tips on how to make the most of unloved leftovers typically destined for the bin. We recommend heading over to good old Jamie Olivers website to check out his
                  <a href="http://www.jamieoliver.com/savewithjamie/">
                   Money Saving Meals
                  </a>
                  .
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  As for the rest, vegetable peel, onion skin, coffee grounds and egg shells, all this and more can be composted for use in the garden. Composting is easy to do, relatively low maintenance and its well liked by wildlife. Armed with a
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/catalog/Kitchen_Recycling_Caddies/5731.html">
                   kitchen caddy
                  </a>
                  and
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/catalog/Composters/5734.html">
                   composter
                  </a>
                  you can give food scraps new purpose. Caddies can be kept inside kitchen cabinets or on top of worktops to give you somewhere dedicated to place your food waste thats also odour free and hygienic. Once full up, your caddy contents can be thrown into the composter in your back garden.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Additional information on how and what to compost can be found
                  <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/gardening/basics/techniques/soil_makecompost1.shtml">
                   here
                  </a>
                  .
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  For a dash more inspiration on the subject of food, here are some tips on saving on food bills:
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/CLsVLUGfeds?feature=oembed&amp;wmode=transparent" width="500">
                  </iframe>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Recycling
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       | Kitchen Tips
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Packaging from cleaning products and food and drink quickly mount up. Most of these items can be recycled and producers are making a greater effort to label their products with helpful information concerning the correct disposal.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Cardboard, paper, aluminium, glass and plastics can all be used again. Thankfully, with the introduction of recycling collections from the local council much more of us are familiar with what can and cannot be recycled and incorporate this into our disposal routine as second nature.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  A few ways to make the task less tasking is to invest in reliable
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/catalog/Multipurpose_Recycling_Boxes/5732.html">
                   kerbside recycling boxes
                  </a>
                  . Boxes which are colour coded are great for separating materials, a sturdier design with holes provides protection against the elements like the wind and rain, and a lid will keep the contents within and unwanted pests out!
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  To find out what happens to the goods you recycle, watch this video by Northumberland County Council:
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/uPOgeR9AGlg?feature=oembed&amp;wmode=transparent" width="500">
                  </iframe>
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <strong>
                   Savings in the Living Room
                  </strong>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/modern-interior-1113tm-pic-100.jpg">
                   <img alt="Living room" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-5113" height="682" src="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/modern-interior-1113tm-pic-100-1024x682.jpg" width="1024"/>
                  </a>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  A place to kick back and relax, the living room is generally a calmer place to be. Just dont be fooled into a false pretense that it isnt costing you a small fortune!
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  There are two things which came to mind when we researched how to make a living room more eco-friendly  heating and electricity.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Switch it Off!
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       | Living Room Tips
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The television, laptop, phone charger, blu ray player and so on, if its not being used switch it off. Doing so will instantly reduce electricity bills. Worryingly, the average UK household can waste as much as 80 a year by leaving appliances in standby mode (research by the Energy Saving Trust).
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  If youre in need of help remembering to switch off your electronics then try these
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/catalog/Energy_Saving_Devices/5739.html">
                   energy saving devices
                  </a>
                  which will both cut and monitor your power usage.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   LED Lighting
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        | Living Room Tips
                       </strong>
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Another tip that will significantly cut the cost of your electricity consumption is to
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/catalog/LED_Lighting/5567.html">
                   change your light bulbs to LEDs
                  </a>
                  . LED bulbs come in a range of styles so theres something to fit your ceiling fixtures, table lamps, floor lamps and so on. These bulbs look and operate in a similar manner to ordinary bulbs but can save a whopping average of 80 per cent off your electricity bills.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Home Heating
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        | Living Room Tips
                       </strong>
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  When youre sat down on the sofa in front of the telly youre more likely to feel the cold because of the reduced activity. The result is a heavier dependence on your heating weapon of choice be it radiators, a log burner or fireplace. None of these methods are helping the environment or your bank balance. Statics from the consumer group Which? says that energy prices are a big concern for 84 per cent of consumers. Furthermore, over 60 per cent of a households energy bills are spent on heating; the statistic was recently published by the Energy Saving Trust.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Philip Sellwood, chief executive of the Energy Saving Trust, said: In recent years weve seen a marked increase in interest from householders seeking information about energy efficiency. With householders taking a much keener interest, were now giving more detailed information as we know there is a genuine appetite for it.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Our figures now show potential monetary savings for five different home types from a detached house to a mid-floor flat, and a range of options depending on whether someone is topping up their loft or insulating for the first time, or switching from a G-rated boiler to an A-rated, or a D-rated to an A-rated.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Solar Panels
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        <strong>
                         <strong>
                          | Living Room Tips
                         </strong>
                        </strong>
                       </strong>
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  An alternative method to consider for home heating is solar power. Solar panels are a type of renewable technology that can be placed on your roof where it gathers the energy from the sun and converts into a free and useable source. There are two types; solar PV panels create electricity which can be used for running electric heating appliances like radiators, fan heaters or underfloor heating. Solar thermal panels heat your homes water system like radiators.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   More Alternative Heating
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        <strong>
                         <strong>
                          | Living Room Tips
                         </strong>
                        </strong>
                       </strong>
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  If youre fond of a roaring fire than a biomass heater may be more preferential. This type of heater burns sustainable pellets to produce heat. A greater device still is the
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/catalog/Heat_Pumps/5548.html">
                   air source heater
                  </a>
                  which uses the air around you to gather heat and utilises it to run your central heating system.
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <strong>
                   <strong>
                    Savings in the
                   </strong>
                   Bedroom
                  </strong>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/bedroom-1113tm-pic-64.jpg">
                   <img alt="Bedroom" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-5115" height="746" src="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/bedroom-1113tm-pic-64-1024x746.jpg" width="1024"/>
                  </a>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The bedroom does not seem like an obvious site for an eco-makeover, nether the less there are changes you can make. The bedroom is a place where you can be creative with your interior design skills so where possible try to upcycle textiles, decorative pieces or furniture. Pinterest is a great source for ideas on
                  <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/trueshopping/tried-tested-diy-tips/">
                   tried and tested DIY tips
                  </a>
                  so head on over if youre feeling up for a spot of do it yourself.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Layer up
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        <strong>
                         <strong>
                          <strong>
                           | Bedroom Tips
                          </strong>
                         </strong>
                        </strong>
                       </strong>
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Retain heat in your bedrooms with some eco-savvy dcor like thick curtains, plush carpet, quilts and pillows for you to feel warm and snug.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Insulate
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        <strong>
                         <strong>
                          <strong>
                           <strong>
                            | Bedroom Tips
                           </strong>
                          </strong>
                         </strong>
                        </strong>
                       </strong>
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Warmth is naturally drawn upwards so think beyond the bedroom and up into the loft to prevent this much desired energy from getting away. With a properly insulated loft you can save around 20 per cent on your energy spending. Whats more, there are some fantastic eco-friendly insulation materials.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  British manufacturer Thermafleece have design their Eco Roll using sheeps wool. This sustainable resource combines the benefits of natural design with manmade innovation. The wool is blended with recycled fibres and then compressed into wheels of insulation. This can then be used not only in lofts but cavity walls and floors too to keep homes efficient. To discover more about Eco Roll and other eco insulation click
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/catalog/Insulation/5748.html">
                   here
                  </a>
                  .
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  <strong>
                   Savings in the Bathroom
                  </strong>
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/home-bathroom.jpg">
                   <img alt="Bathroom" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-5177" height="840" src="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/info/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/home-bathroom-1024x840.jpg" width="1024"/>
                  </a>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Yet another busy area, your bathroom endures showering, bathing, toilet flushing and tap running on a regular daily basis. All this activity puts water in great demand and prone to wastage.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Water Wastage
                   <span style="color: #888888;">
                    <strong>
                     <strong>
                      <strong>
                       <strong>
                        <strong>
                         <strong>
                          <strong>
                           <strong>
                            | Bathroom Tips
                           </strong>
                          </strong>
                         </strong>
                        </strong>
                       </strong>
                      </strong>
                     </strong>
                    </strong>
                   </span>
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  As well as employing common sense by taking shorter showers and only running the tap when needed you can now purchase water saving devices. A dual flush toilet will have a light flush mechanism to reduce the quantity of water used. Similarly you can get
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/product/Trueshopping_Flow_Regulator_for_use_with_any_shower_hose_option/611/55346.html?gcsct=0ChMI0MydyoHbvAIVSAy1Ch3pYgAAEAM">
                   flow regulators
                  </a>
                  which can be placed on taps and shower hoses to keep the maximum flow rate down to six litres a minute.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  And for a bit of fun how about this
                  <a href="http://www.trueshopping.co.uk/product/H2O_Waterproof_Power_Shower_Radio/5739/87421.html?gcsct=0ChMI0MydyoHbvAIVSAy1Ch3pYgAAEAQ">
                   H20 powered shower radio
                  </a>
                  . This nifty device fits to the water feed of your shower and uses the flow to charge an internal battery.
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  Conclusion
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  Now that youre armed with a wealth of eco-friendly and money saving tips for around the home you can start to enjoy their benefits. Naturally trying all the mentioned methods may not work for you so consider which rooms in your household are the busiest and then decide if there are any habits, appliances or products which you can change, purchase or replace in the step towards living greener.
                 </p>

                   eco technologies and products
                  </a>
                  which we hope you will find useful.
                 </p>
