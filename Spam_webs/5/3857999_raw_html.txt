     <p>
      This is a
      <b>
       list of Star Wars species (AE)
      </b>
      . This list contains the names of
      <a href="/wiki/Sentience" title="Sentience">
       sentient
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">
       species
      </a>
      from the
      <a href="/wiki/Fictional_universe" title="Fictional universe">
       fictional universe
      </a>
      of Star Wars beginning with the letters A through E.
      <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">
       Star Wars
      </a>
      is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Epic_film" title="Epic film">
       epic
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Space_opera" title="Space opera">
       space opera
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Film_series" title="Film series">
       film series
      </a>
      created by
      <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">
       George Lucas
      </a>
      . The first film in the series was originally released on May 25, 1977, under the title Star Wars, by
      <a href="/wiki/20th_Century_Fox" title="20th Century Fox">
       20th Century Fox
      </a>
      , and became a worldwide
      <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">
       pop culture
      </a>
      phenomenon, followed by five sequels and three prequels. Many species of alien creatures (often humanoid) are depicted.
     </p>

They are known to look for conflict and rarely show restraint.      <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Aleena">
       Aleena
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aleena">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      This short-bodied alien race was native to the planet Aleen. These beings were characterized by their thick bodies and short limbs. They waddle when they walk, and were ungainly to look upon. Their heads were dominated by a long, thin plate of body which extended back from the skull, and their faces were flat. Two small eyes sat over a wide mouth which was filled with short, sharp teeth. Aleena were known for their quick-change metabolism, which helped them escape from sagcatchers on their home-world. Individuals were bred for their color variation, another way they avoided becoming prey. As a people, the Aleena had a strong warrior tradition. They possessed exceptional reflexes as well as a fast metabolism which allowed them to convert food into energy very rapidly in order to escape predators on their harsh home planet.

      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Mandalorian_Armor" title="The Mandalorian Armor">

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Xizor" title="Prince Xizor">

      <a href="/wiki/Boba_Fett" title="Boba Fett">

      </a>
      in a plot to destroy the Bounty Hunter Guild. Boba Fett's firsthand account of the Assembler homeworld can be found in the
      <i>
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Essential_Guide_to_Alien_Species&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Essential Guide to Alien Species (page does not exist)">
        Essential Guide to Alien Species
       </a>
      </i>
      . The main Assembler form is a large spider-like creature that is mentally linked to smaller versions of itself. Each "node", as the mini-Assemblers are called, is delegated a specific task. For instance, "lookouts" act as surveillance cameras, and "pilots" navigate the immense cocoon that an Assembler constructs so that it and its nodes can survive in space. Each node is intelligent, and capable of growing up to become a full Assembler. The main Assembler must therefore be wary enough to kill the nodes before they become mature enough to arrange the Assembler's demise directly or indirectly, thereby taking its place. However, since nodes become more useful as they mature and grow, the main Assembler always faces a dilemma as to when to kill its nodes. It seems probable that this trait is somehow beneficial to the population as a whole, possibly because it ensures that an Assembler is crafty and clever; it would not have become an Assembler or remained an Assembler for long if it were not. Kud'ar Mub'at was a node himself and killed his parent in a time "long before Boba Fett was the most feared bounty hunter in the galaxy".

     <p>
      This was a race of near-humans, noted for the retractable antenepalps atop their heads. Native to a planet also known as Balosar, they were generally regarded as shifty cheaters and scoundrels by other races. This is most likely derived from the fact that they produce and sell death sticks, addictive and eventually fatal drugs distilled from balo mushrooms, which are farmed in large quantities underground. The drug's main black market competitor is the Ryloth drug ryll. As a people, the Balosar seemed to be content to allow their homeworld to be overrun by offworld corporations and criminal syndicates, taking bribes and other payments in return for land grants or other rights. The lowest classes of Balosar were paid poorly for their work in sweatshops and factories, despite the best efforts of the Old Republic.

This is because in their distant history, a wandering Jedi had settled a bloody dispute between two major clans over prime hunting grounds.      <p>
      <b>
       Besalisks
      </b>
      are a race of four-armed humanoids from the icy planet of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_planets_(O-Q)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="List of Star Wars planets (O-Q) (page does not exist)">
       Ojom
      </a>
      , though female Besalisks can have up to eight arms. Besalisks evolved from large flightless avians, though they are often mistaken for a reptilian species. They are bulky, fleshy beings able to survive for long periods without food or water. They tend to have a bony crest surrounded by short feathers, and a wide mouth with an elastic sack dangling from it. Because of their size, they are often seen as gluttonous, though this is a misconception. Another misconception is that they are nervous or in poor shape because they sweat profusely, though this is in fact due to the normally freezing temperatures on their home planet. The average Besalisk stands approximately 1.8 meters tall. Their language consists of grunts, growls, and barks, and their written language has a simple alphabet and short words, although Besalisks can speak
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galactic_Basic" title="Galactic Basic">
       Galactic Basic
      </a>
      with ease.
     </p>
     <p>
      Although Besalisks have contributed very little to the galaxy in terms of resources and technology, they are sociable, gregarious, and keen-witted, are found easily in the galactic community and can readily use what other species have to offer. During the reign of the
      <a href="/wiki/Galactic_Empire_(Star_Wars)" title="Galactic Empire (Star Wars)">
       Galactic Empire
      </a>
      , Besalisks narrowly avoided slavery by calling in favors with various influential underworld connections. Many are still working off their freedom, especially besalisks that were foolish or desperate enough to seek aid from the Hutts. Besalisks have never sought official representation in the Galactic Senate and generally seem content to go about their business and leave Galactic affairs to politicians and bureaucrats of other species. Communities on their homeworld are sparsely populated, and few offworld colonies of Besalisks are found in any era.
     </p>

       </a>

     <p>
      <b>
       Boltrunians
      </b>
      are a large and muscular reptilian near-Human species with hairless heads, craggy faces, wide noses, and heavy brow ridges. Boltrunians could live to be over 700 years old. Well-known Boltrunians included Warto and the Dark Jedi
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maw_(Jedi)" title="Maw (Jedi)">
       Maw
      </a>
      . Often, a Boltrunian would be mistaken for a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trandoshan" title="Trandoshan">
       Trandoshan
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="B.27omarr_monks">
       B'omarr monks
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: B'omarr monks">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       B'omarr
      </b>
      are not actually a species; rather they were an order of
      <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">
       monks
      </a>
      whose
      <a href="/wiki/Monastery" title="Monastery">
       monastery
      </a>
      eventually became
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jabba%27s_Palace" title="Jabba's Palace">
       Jabba's Palace
      </a>
      . They believe that cutting themselves off from civilization and all corporeal distractions leads to
      <a href="/wiki/Enlightenment_(spiritual)" title="Enlightenment (spiritual)">
       enlightenment
      </a>
      and to that end undergo surgery to separate their
      <a href="/wiki/Brain" title="Brain">
       brains
      </a>
      from their bodies and continue their existence as a brain in a jar. They learn to communicate telepathically and by controlling technology attuned to their abilities. On the rare occasions when they need to move, they are able to use a spider-like walking apparatus. The B'omarr were still in Jabba's Palace at the time it appears in
      <a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Jedi" title="Return of the Jedi">
       Return of the Jedi
      </a>
      , and indeed, as C-3PO enters Jabba's palace, a spiderwalker can be seen. The monks inhabit the lower part of the palace and allow the various smugglers and crime lords like Jabba to use the upper levels, as long as they add another section to the lower levels for use by the B'omarr. When the current ruler of the upper levels is deposed they drive out the stragglers and add the brains of those who do not leave to their order. The infamous
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bib_Fortuna" title="Bib Fortuna">
       Bib Fortuna
      </a>
      himself eventually (unwillingly) became a monk, as he had sought power through the monks' teachings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The animated film "The Clone Wars" establishes the B'omarr order maintained monasteries on several planets. The facility on Teth, built on the top of a steep mountain, served as the hideout for Separatist forces who kidnapped Jabba the Hutt's son Rotta. The architecture was very similar to Jabba's palace. According to dialogue in the film, many of the abandoned monasteries are used as hideouts for smugglers.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Bothan">
       Bothan
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Bothan">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Bothans hail from the industrial Mid Rim planet of Bothawui, though they also populate various colony worlds like
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kothlis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kothlis (page does not exist)">
       Kothlis
      </a>
      and Torolis. Most Bothans stand about 56ft. tall. They have tapered pointed ears, elongated heads, and a very few from both sexes have beards. Bothans are manipulative, crafty, suspicious, curious by nature, loyal, brave, and very smart. They developed their technology to travel among the stars millennia ago and are longstanding members of the galactic civilization.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Bothans, in the galactic war, play the role of spies from the Rebel Alliance.
     </p>
     <p>
      To the Bothans, information is the galaxy's most valuable currency, as well as its most potent weapon. As a culture, they believe the direct confrontation with foeswhether economic, political, or martialis pointless. In an open conflict, the object both sides desire is often damaged or destroyed. This attitude has made espionage a natural line of work for Bothans. Information and spies are their greatest exports. The famed Bothan spynet is the largest independent intelligence-gathering organization the galaxy has ever known. Beings of power everywhere find Bothans irritating because they have a tendency to use every bit of information they can to acquire leverage or money, but no one wants to be the only person without access to the spynet, so everyone continues to deal with the Bothans.
     </p>
     <p>
      The most renowned Bothans from Chaos Squadron are Jorec Thebe, Giddic Ryne and Relluf Xander, the three surviving Bothans who provided the Death Star II plans in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Return_of_the_Jedi" title="Return of the Jedi">
        Return of the Jedi
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Bouncer">
       Bouncer
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Bouncer">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <b>
       Bouncers
      </b>
      are furry green floating orbs with thin black tails that live on the planet
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ruusan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ruusan (page does not exist)">
       Ruusan
      </a>
      . They also appear in the novels and audio dramatizations of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Dark_Forces" title="Star Wars: Dark Forces">
        Star Wars: Dark Forces
       </a>
      </i>
      upon the planet
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sulon_(Star_Wars)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sulon (Star Wars) (page does not exist)">
       Sulon
      </a>
      . Morgan Katarn, father of soon-to-be Jedi Master
      <a href="/wiki/Kyle_Katarn" title="Kyle Katarn">
       Kyle Katarn
      </a>
      , discovers the creatures in the Valley of the Jedi in Ruusan, naming them appropriately. Bouncers apparently move with the wind, lifting flaps of skin to use them as sails to guide their bodies. They use rocks and other formations to bounce them high, gliding them through the air for travel. Bouncers are said to be attuned with the Force and can communicate with those who can manipulate it. They can also become hostile when exposed to massive amounts of energy from the dark side of the Force, such as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Kaan" title="Lord Kaan">
       Lord Kaan
      </a>
      's thought bomb. They communicate with other species by speaking into their minds. They can attempt to predict the future.
     </p>

The Chiss are noted for their lack of droids, or any kind of artificial intelligence. Baron Soontir Fel attempted to change this during the Yuuzhan Vong invasion, by introducing self-thinking spacecraft. Any progress on this is more or less unknown. While their history has been marked by nearly continual warfare and conquest of other worlds, the Chiss are generally not personally violent: indeed, one of the major laws of their society (the breaking of which resulted in Thrawn's exile from their space) is that the Chiss cannot launch preemptive attacks, even against obvious aggressors.

     <p>
      <b>
       Devaronians
      </b>
      are a horned humanoid species from the world of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_planets_(C%E2%80%93D)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="List of Star Wars planets (CD) (page does not exist)">
       Devaron
      </a>
      in the Expansion Region, a planet of low mountains and deep valleys linked by thousands of rivers. It is believed that they are descended from a race of primates that lived in the mountains of Devaron, and that their horns were a genetic mutation that proved useful in fending off predatory birds.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Devaronians are one of the unique races in the galaxy, as their male and female sexes are so vastly different in appearance and temperament. The males are docile, non-aggressive creatures, hairless, with red-tinted skin and a pair of large horns growing from their heads. They take great pride in their horns, and groom them quite regularly. Female Devaronians are covered in thick calico fur that ranges in color from brown to white and are hornless. They are aggressive by nature, and tend to dominate their culture. In addition, the teeth of the males are all
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incisors" title="Incisors">
       incisors
      </a>
      , but the females also have
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Molars" title="Molars">
       molars
      </a>
      and prominent canines.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Devaronians were one of the first species in the galaxy to develop
      <a href="/wiki/Interstellar_travel" title="Interstellar travel">
       interstellar travel
      </a>
      , and the males of the species have been common sights in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spaceports" title="Spaceports">
       spaceports
      </a>
      throughout the galaxy for thousands of years. Devaronian males are driven by an urge to wander, usually taking the first opportunity to move on from one place to another. As such, they are often found traveling the galaxy as tramp freighter captains and scouts. Female Devaronians, on the other hand, are content to remain in a single location, and, as such, raise the young and run the government of Devaron. The males send as much money as they can manage back to their home world to support their families, but otherwise hardly ever return once they have begun to travel the stars. The females are content with this arrangement, as they tend to view the restless males as disruptive to home life.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">

          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_IV:_A_New_Hope" title="Star Wars Episode IV: A New Hope">

      <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Alliance" title="Rebel Alliance">

      <a href="/wiki/Boba_Fett" title="Boba Fett">

      </a>

     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Echani
      </b>
      are a mysterious race of well taught fighters, whose most prominent appearance was in
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Knights_of_the_Old_Republic_II:_The_Sith_Lords" title="Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords">

      <a href="/wiki/Jedi" title="Jedi">

      </a>

      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Telos_(Star_Wars)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Telos (Star Wars) (page does not exist)">
       Telos
      </a>
      . These Echani were trained by Atris to construct barriers in their minds in order to resist
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Force_(Star_Wars)" title="Force (Star Wars)">
       force
      </a>
      -based powers. The Echani race possess natural martial art skill and wear light armour in battle, although they prefer to wear very little when training to restrict their movements less. The Echani maidens were often used to hunt down rogue
      <a href="/wiki/Jedi" title="Jedi">
       Jedi
      </a>
      and subdue them for the Order. Human in appearance, they often have silver hair and closely resemble their parents, and therefore their siblings. As long as they have the same parents, Echani siblings are identical in appearance.
     </p>
     <p>
      The best known of the Echani in the Star Wars universe was Brianna, the last of Atris' Handmaidens. She served with her Mistress on Telos until the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jedi_Exile" title="Jedi Exile">
       Jedi Exile
      </a>
      arrived. After speaking with the Exile, she stowed away on board his ship, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebon_Hawk" title="Ebon Hawk">
       Ebon Hawk
      </a>
      . Eventually, it is revealed that her mother was a Jedi Knight and she herself starts on the path of the Jedi. This fact is the reason that Brianna looks different from her sisters - she does not share the same mother. This story depended on the player of the game choosing a male character. If the player chose a female character, the above story would not occur as so.
     </p>
     <p>
      It is known that the Echani held a traditional rivalry with the Mandalorians. Echani and Mandalorians both saw each other as worthy opponents, therefore causing a war between them to prove whose skills were the better.
     </p>
     <p>
      Due to the combat training of the Echani on a daily level, their bones become tougher, making them more durable. A version of the Echani style of unarmed combat is taught to members of Palpatine's Imperial Guard and Sith guard. This style has been adapted to be more brutal and efficient and less flashy. Echani were often seen on snowy/cold planets such as Hoth and Eshan. They made their bases underground since they would have been made out of durable materials.
     </p>
     <p>
      Though not much is known about their aging process it is presumed that they age at a much slower rate than, say, a human and their life expectancy is around four hundred years.

      </a>

      <a href="#Elom">
       Eloms
      </a>
      , shared the same planet with them, most Elomin refused to acknowledge that their underground brethren even existed.
     </p>
     <p>
      When
      <a href="/wiki/Palpatine" title="Palpatine">
       Emperor Palpatine
      </a>
      rose to power, the Elomin were enslaved and were forced to mine lommite. Some Eloms learned of this and deliberately caused "mining accidents" in order to free a number of Elomin.

         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Epicanthix">
       Epicanthix
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=57" title="Edit section: Epicanthix">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Epicanthix are tall, muscular, near-humans native to the planet Panatha. They were known as warriors, having conquered many bordering planets such as
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_planets_(A%E2%80%93B)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="List of Star Wars planets (AB) (page does not exist)">
       Bunduki
      </a>
      . They live longer than humans, with a life-span of around 130 years. They possess a rare genetic immunity to force assisted mental tricks, influence, domination and mind reading. Although Epicanthix appeared very similar to baseline human, they were distinct enough that they could be recognized by sight, as in the case of Epicanthix Imperial Agent Ula Vii who was so recognized by a pair of Imperials and ridiculed for his non-Human ancestry.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Er.27Kit">
       Er'Kit
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=58" title="Edit section: Er'Kit">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Er'Kit are usually skinny and grayish-blue, with long ears. The only well-known one is
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ody_Mandrell" title="Ody Mandrell">
       Ody Mandrell
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Ewok">
       Ewok
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=59" title="Edit section: Ewok">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Ewok" title="Ewok">
       Ewok
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      <b>
       Ewoks
      </b>
      are a primitive tribal species from the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forest_Moon_of_Endor" title="Forest Moon of Endor">
       Forest Moon of Endor
      </a>
      . They are approximately half the size of humans and covered with fur. The Ewoks are best known for their aid to the
      <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Alliance" title="Rebel Alliance">
       Rebel
      </a>
      mission to remove the shield protecting the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_Death_Star" title="Second Death Star">
       Second Death Star
      </a>
      known as the battle of Endor. While peaceful by nature, they are extremely territorial and have been known to fight ferociously in protection of their society. Though they are known as Ewoks, they are never actually called that. In fact they are never called anything in Episode VI.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Evocii">
       Evocii
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Star_Wars_species_(A%E2%80%93E)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=60" title="Edit section: Evocii">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Evocii
      </b>
      are a primitive humanoid race from
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nal_Hutta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nal Hutta (page does not exist)">
       Nal Hutta
      </a>
      , formerly Evocar. When the
      <a href="/wiki/Hutt_(Star_Wars)" title="Hutt (Star Wars)">
       Hutts
      </a>
      arrived on Evocar, the Evocii were amazed by the technology these large, fat slug-like aliens brought with them and began swapping areas of their own home world for the technology that the Hutts had. Soon they realized that the Hutts owned almost their entire world. Then the Hutts began treating the Evocii like slaves. The Evocii appealed to the
      <a href="/wiki/Galactic_Republic" title="Galactic Republic">
       Old Republic
      </a>
      for help but unfortunately, the laws of the Republic favored the Hutts. The Hutts then brutally relocated the Evocii to Evocar's fifth and largest moon
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Moons_of_Star_Wars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Moons of Star Wars (page does not exist)">
       Nar Shaddaa
      </a>
      where they were used as slaves to build Nar Shaddaa into a spaceport moon. Most of the Evocii eventually died from the various technologies practiced on that moon while the very few survivors mutated into savages.

         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
