         <div class="article-copy--container">
          <p>
           Its the middle of Milan Fashion Week, a whirlwind of bouncy, hot-rolled curls, bright prints, and the sound of clicking stilettos masterfully navigating the cobbled streets. With their legendary sensuality and immaculate sense of femininity, Italian women seem to be guided by one simple principle:
           <em>
            fare una bella figura
           </em>
           make a beautiful impression.
          </p>
          <p>
           And who wasnt captivated by the sight of
           <strong>
            Gina Lollobrigida
           </strong>
           s raven curls, fiery lips, and dramatically arched brows as Esmeralda in
           <em>
            The Hunchback of Notre Dame
           </em>
           ? Or Fellini muse
           <strong>
            Claudia Cardinale
           </strong>
           s gravity-defying bouffant and eyelash-grazing fringe in
           <em>
            8 
           </em>
           ? Consider
           <strong>
            Monica Vitti,
           </strong>
           who, with the help of her plush lips, rumpled blonde bob, and thick, feline flick, seduced
           <strong>
            Gabriele Ferzetti
           </strong>
           without hardly uttering a word in Antonionis masterpiece,
           <em>
            LAvventura
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Fashion, too, has seen its fair share of
           <em>
            belle donne
           </em>
           . Before becoming Frances first lady, Turin-born supermodel
           <strong>
            Carla Bruni
           </strong>
           ruled the runway with her piercing stare and high cheekbones, while
           <strong>
            Mariacarla Boscono
           </strong>
           s wide-set eyes and alabaster skin brought a haunting, alien allure to the pages of
           <em>
            Vogue
           </em>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Today, fresh-faced beauties like
           <strong>
            Margherita Missoni
           </strong>
           have helped usher in a more natural aesthetic, while ageless stunners like
           <strong>
            Isabella Rossellini
           </strong>
           and
           <strong>
            Sophia Loren
           </strong>
           seem to only gain appeal. Here, a look at the most striking Italian beauties of all time.
          </p>
