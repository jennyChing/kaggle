    <p>
     The mission to Europa has been a dream for many planetary scientists for a long time and now
     <a href="http://www.nasa.gov/press/2014/july/nasa-seeks-proposals-for-europa-mission-science-instruments/#.U8sv9Y1dVnI">
      NASA is asking for proposals
     </a>
     for what science instruments should be on board for the mission. One of the
     <strong>
      main reasons Europa
     </strong>
     is an excellent prospect to send an orbiter or spacecraft is the ocean of water beneath its thick icy crust. As far as we know water is a great indicator for potential life and with a combination of tidal heating it makes Europa a suitable candidate for life.
    </p>

     Before we jump into the details of the mission lets take a look at
     <strong>
      why Europa is insanely cool!

    <p>
     Its still quite mind boggling that not only do we live in a galaxy that
     <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
      may support from 100-400 billion exoplanets
     </span>
     but now moons may be capable of harbouringlife as well! Here are some fun and basic facts about Europa to tell your friends:
    </p>
    <ul>
     <li>
      <strong>
       Size: 1560 km in radius
      </strong>
       just a tad smaller than our own moon which has a 1737 km radius
     </li>
     <li>
      Primarily made of silicate rock with iron core 
      <em>
       thin oxygen atmosphere
      </em>
      (too thin to breathe!)
     </li>
     <li>
      Surface is made up of water ice  one of the smoothest surfaces in the solar system
     </li>
     <li>
      Water plumes on Europa
      <strong>
       eject
      </strong>
      <strong>
       twice as high as Mt. Everest!
      </strong>
      (200 km out)
     </li>
     <li>
      1 complete rotation on Europas axis (1 day)
      <em>
       takes3.5 Earth days
      </em>
      !
     </li>
     <li>
      Outer layer of water is
      <strong>
       100 km thick
      </strong>
     </li>
     <li>
      Average surface temperature is about a frosty
      <em>
       -160 C
      </em>
      (-260 F)
     </li>
     <li>
      Tidally heated by Jupiter  which is part of the reason why life may exist in Europas ocean  it could be warm enough!
     </li>
     <li>
      Since the radiation from solar winds are caught by Jupiters immense magnetic fields Europa is basically bathing in it.
      <strong>
       Just spending 15 minutes
      </strong>
      on Europa would
      <strong>
       kill a human being!
      </strong>
     </li>
    </ul>

    <p>
     Now that we have a nice idea of why Europa is an interesting candidate we can talk about the future missions NASA and the ESA has planned. One mission that
     <a href="http://www.lpi.usra.edu/opag/jul2013/presentations/Clipper_Summary.pdf">
      NASA recently updated
     </a>
     plans for is called
     <strong>
      <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europa_Clipper">
       Europa Clipper
      </a>
      .
     </strong>
     The Clipper is a flyby mission that will determine its habitability and locate a suitable landing site for future missions. This mission will be one of the first missions to make it out specifically for Europa which is aimed to get there for 2025. Another mission called
     <a href="http://solarsystem.nasa.gov/europa/docs/ES%202012%20Report%20B%20Orbiter%20-%20Final%20-%2020120501.pdf">
      Europa Orbiter
     </a>
     is to study the extent of the ocean and its relation to the deeper interior. If that wasnt enough for you theres yet another missions called the
     <a href="https://solarsystem.nasa.gov/europa/docs/ES%202012%20Report%20D%20Lander%20-%20Final%20-%2020120501.pdf">
      Europa lander
     </a>
     which will determine the moons habitability and see if there are any astrobiological organisms on Europa. The European Space Agency is also getting in on some of this action with their mission called the
     <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/science-environment-17917102">
      Jupiter Icy Moon Explorer
     </a>
     . Its a mission not dedicated to Europa but it will do a flyby and primarily study Ganymede.
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Its only 2014 and we still have a while to go before we see any of these missions make it out to Jupiter. However, 2020-2030 will be a very interesting time for us. Well have a manned mission to Mars, missions to Europa, Ganymede, and well have our lovely New Horizons mission  which will be studying Pluto sometime next year. Well if you are still itching for some more Europa material then you should check out this

      great Reddit AMA
     </a>
     with Bill Nye and some other amazing scientists as they give you some intimate details about Europa and the mission.
