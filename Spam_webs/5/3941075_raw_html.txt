        <article class="et_pb_post post-3803 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-tips" id="post-3803">
         <h1>
          Eat a Home Cooked Meal with Locals
         </h1>
         <p class="post-meta">
          by
          <a href="http://thetravelsisters.boardingarea.com/author/m/" rel="author" title="Posts by Matilda">
           Matilda
          </a>
          | Jan 5, 2015 |
          <a href="http://thetravelsisters.boardingarea.com/category/tips/" rel="category tag">
           Tips
          </a>
          |
          <span class="comments-number">
           <a href="http://thetravelsisters.boardingarea.com/meal-sharing-websites-eat-home-cooked-meals-with-locals/#respond">
            10 comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <img alt="Eat a Home Cooked Meal with Locals" height="675" src="http://thetravelsisters.img.boardingarea.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/dining.png" width="1080"/>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           I love street food as much as the next traveler but nothing beats an invite for a home-cooked meal from a local. Sometimes the invite happens organically. Sometimes it is a friend of a friend of a friend. Sometimes, it is an introduction from your awesome opthamologist (not kidding).
          </p>
          <p>
           Apparently, Im not the only one looking for that cultural immersion. While researching an upcoming trip, I ran across the concept of meal sharing websites. Basically, hosts offer a specific home-cooked meal served in their home for a set price. Hosts are pre-vetted and guests are encouraged to post reviews.
          </p>
          <p>
           Intrigued by the idea, I decided to try it out in my hometown first. I chose an enchilada and flan meal with a $12 chip-in fee on
           <a href="https://www.mealsharing.com" target="_blank">
            mealsharing
           </a>
           and was not disappointed. Although the food was tasty, the real draw was the company. The crowd included an eclectic mix of locals and tourists, most of them well-traveled. Hosts specify whether they will serve the meal only or dine with guests. Our host dined with us and made sure the conversation kept flowing.
          </p>
          <p>
           I wouldnt hesitate to book a similar experience while on the road or perhaps even host a meal myself. Its also worth trying if you just moved to a new city and are looking to meet people. A number of sites currently offer the service in varying locations worldwide.
          </p>
