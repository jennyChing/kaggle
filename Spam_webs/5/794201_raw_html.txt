          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <a href="http://issuesurgery.com/do-not-bother-to-take-daily-aspirin-if-you-havent-had-heart-attack/aspirin/" rel="attachment wp-att-1308">

          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           Taking a
           <strong>
            daily
           </strong>
           aspirin is
           <strong>
            not
           </strong>
           necessary for people with no history of heart problems. People, who
           <strong>
            havent had heart
           </strong>
           problems, even if they have a family history of it, will not benefit if they
           <strong>
            take
           </strong>
           a daily
           <strong>
            aspirin
           </strong>
           . People who have undergone a heart
           <strong>
            attack
           </strong>
           or have other heart-related problems can consider taking a daily aspirin, but everyone should consult with their physician over whats the best option for them.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           If you in possession of
           <strong>
            heart
           </strong>
           disease, as well as a heart
           <strong>
            attack
           </strong>
           , recent surgery or stents, you should
           <strong>
            take
           </strong>
           a
           <strong>
            daily
           </strong>
           <strong>
            aspirin
           </strong>
           (about 81 mg). However, you shouldnt take it if your doctor declared that you have a high bleeding risk. People who
           <strong>
            havent had
           </strong>
           heart attack is
           <strong>
            not
           </strong>
           recommended in taking aspirin because there is a possibility a bleeding in the brain or stomach occurred because of the daily aspirin use.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           The patient who usually gets benefit from
           <strong>
            daily
           </strong>
           <strong>
            aspirin
           </strong>
           consumption is someone who has almost every risk factor: high blood pressure, strong family history, high cholesterol, smoking, and diabetes. Patients with these risk factors will be treated with aspirin and the other medication.
           <strong>
            Not
           </strong>
           <strong>
            take
           </strong>
           self-medication is a good idea. So, if you
           <strong>
            havent had heart attacks do
           </strong>
           not bother yourself to take a daily aspirin.
          </p>
