        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          A great wayto
          <a href="http://www.carolamato.com/engagement-superstar-adrienne-smith-releases-build-a-blog-community-course/" target="_blank">
           get yourself known on the Internet
          </a>
          is to comment on blog posts that have helped you, and thosewhich you have an opinion on. Imexcited when someone leaves their feedbackas a comment. It lets me know Im reaching and helping you, and its a big encouragement.
         </p>
         <p>
          Other bloggersfeel the same way  we get to know what youre thinking and have more of an opportunity to help in whatever way we can.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="get out of the shadows and make your presence known - help for lurkers" class=" aligncenter" height="408" src="http://cdn.carolamato.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/help-for-lurkers.jpg" width="408"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Forums and blogs can be intimidating  especially if youre a newcomer to the community of like-minded bloggers.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, taking that first step to comment and let others know when youve been taught a valuable lesson, entertained or received helpfrom the post can help brand your name and/or companyandproduct online.
         </p>
         <div class="content-box content-box-blue">
          <strong>
           Your comment could inspire others to comment and get you noticed by industry influencers, which could bea powerful benefit.
          </strong>
         </div>
         <p>
          Here are a few ways that posting comments on a blog can help you with your own online business and also help the person who publishes the content:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Helps to build community, sostart a conversation about the topic.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Helps your name become known to others.
           </strong>
          </li>
          <li>
           Discoverwhat others are thinking.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Build relationships with others who comment and the blogowner.
           </strong>
          </li>
          <li>
           Lets you and the blog ownerknow more about what youd like to see and read about.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Share insight with others.
           </strong>
          </li>
          <li>
           Inspire others (a great beginning to branding).
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Seeing comments on my blog posts make me feel like the Energizer Bunny.
         </p>
         <p>
          After all, one of the purposes of my blog is to create a highly interactive community where people who get inspiration and knowledge from my posts will reveal their points of view and carry on a lively conversation. When I see your comments, Im inspired.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://twitter.com/home/?status=How+to+make+your+presence+known+and+stop+lurking.%20via%20@Carol_Amato_%20http://carolsentme.com/help-for-lurkers" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://twitter.com/home/?status=How+to+make+your+presence+known+and+stop+lurking.%20via%20@Carol_Amato_%20http://carolsentme.com/help-for-lurkers', '']);" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Click to Tweet - Get Out of the Shadows and Make Your Presence Known (Help for Lurkers)" class=" aligncenter" height="48" src="http://carolamatocdn.carolamato.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/tweet-carol-amato.jpg" width="538"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          My business is oriented to help others find their way to online success  and unless I know what youre having trouble with, find exciting or want to share  I might have trouble knowing exactly what you need.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           A Little Advice  Help for Lurkers
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          When you leave comments on blogs, try and make them interesting and well thought out. Avoid leaving one-liners which wont serve to open a conversation about the subject matter.
         </p>
         <p>
          Never ignore any of the comments you receive,and be sure to sign your name at the end so readers can find your site or blog and learn what youre all about.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres a video tutorial to help you
          <a href="http://www.carolamato.com/how-to-save-time-commenting-online-and-keep-your-sanity/" target="_blank">
           save time when commenting
          </a>
          on blogs.
         </p>
         <p>
          The amount of blog comments received on a post is important, but so is the meatiness of the comment. Its the key which separates a great blogger from one who simply throws a post online with no real thought of it helping or making an impact on others.
         </p>
         <p>
          Many comments Ive received over the years have helped me to improve  not only as a business person, but as a viable online presence.
         </p>
         <p>
          Anything I can do to improve what I offer to my community helps me, and those who follow me.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Be passionate about your blog (if you have one) and passionate about the comments you leave. When your words are exciting and well put together to help others, everyone wins.
          </strong>
          <br class=" sb-br"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Bloggers have the ultimate goal of building a community where ideas can be tossed around and new and unique opportunities realized.
         </p>
         <p>
          No question is too basic or too complicated  your comments, questions (and even criticisms) are welcomed and appreciated.
         </p>
         <p>
          So,
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           get out of the shadows
          </span>
          ,
          <em>
           comment
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           <strong>
            make your presence known
           </strong>
          </em>
          . I look forward to hearing from you.
         </p>
