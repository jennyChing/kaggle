         <header class="entry-header">
          <h2 class="entry-title">
           Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America
          </h2>
          <!-- .entry-title -->
         </header>
         <div class="entry-meta-bar clearfix">
          <div class="entry-meta">
           <span class="author">
            <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/author/ranjitsanyal/">
             Ranjit Sanyal - Blog Admin
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="date">
            <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/" title="3:36 pm">
             August 7, 2014
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="category">
            <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/category/campfire/" rel="category tag">
             Hobos We Admire
            </a>
           </span>
           <span class="comments">
            <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/#comments" title="Comment on Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America">
             3 Comments
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
          <!-- .entry-meta -->
         </div>
         <div class="entry-content clearfix">
          <p>
           What happens when a hobo by heart meets another hobo by chance? Fireworks! And an impromptu, life-changing trip that lasts over a year. In this case, 372 days in South America. No itinerary. No return ticket. Light backpacks that contained the barest of necessities. Robbed. Blackmailed. The months between March 2013 and April 2014 werent easy for Sara and JAAC. But then, easy wasnt the point.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Antartida-Peninsula.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="Hobo-Panama-Hats-Ecuador" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-276" height="683" src="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Panama-Hats-Ecuador.jpg" width="1024"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/&amp;media=http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Panama-Hats-Ecuador.jpg&amp;description=Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h4>
           <strong>
            5.SO WHO ARE THESE GUYS?
           </strong>
          </h4>
          <p>
           Sara, from a small Italian town of Jesi, was fascinated by the Ruins of Pompeii in her childhood. JAAC from Madrid, on the other hand, took travel seriously only after hed started working.
          </p>
          <p>
           I grew up in Madrid, Spain, and my parents have never been big travellers, he says ruefully. We only went to the beach in summer and I was not really excited about it.
          </p>
          <p>
           In Madrid, they worked as a Brand Manager and an IT engineer respectively, taking off to see interesting sights every now and then for trips that lasted a couple of weeks.
          </p>
          <p>
           Those trips werent bad, but somehow it felt like we were missing the whole point, Sara says. We didnt get to know the people, or hear their stories, or relate to their lives. It felt horribly superficial.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Pan-de-Azucar-From-Corcovado-Brazil.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="Hobo-Pan-de-Azucar-From-Corcovado-Brazil" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-275" height="683" src="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Pan-de-Azucar-From-Corcovado-Brazil.jpg" width="1024"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/&amp;media=http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Pan-de-Azucar-From-Corcovado-Brazil.jpg&amp;description=Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h4>
           <strong>
            4. SO WHERE DID THEY GO?
           </strong>
          </h4>
          <p>
           Tired of mundane routines and everyday worries, Sara and JAAC impulsively picked South America and decided to tour that continent  unscripted. Leaving their jobs was hard, but not nearly as hard as leaving their friends and families. Their closest and dearest didnt all react well. According to their blog, SantaConmigo.com, there were negotiations, offers, counteroffers, petitions, guilt, misunderstanding and family rejection.
          </p>
          <p>
           It helped that we were not the first to go through this, JAAC says. We know many who have taken the plunge and have had their dream fulfilled. Some of our struggles were similar, others different. Somewhere, the work situation was not the same, or the costs were different. Some actually had understanding loved ones, but its never a bed of roses.
          </p>
          <p>
           So they booked the cheapest flight available into Buenos Aires in Argentina, and kickstarted their trip. It would be a year before they saw home again.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Easter-Island-Chile.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="Hobo-Easter-Island-Chile" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-274" height="683" src="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Easter-Island-Chile.jpg" width="1024"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/&amp;media=http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Easter-Island-Chile.jpg&amp;description=Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h4>
           <strong>
            3. SO WHAT WAS IT LIKE?
           </strong>
          </h4>
          <p>
           The backpack is our friend. Improvisation is our rule.
          </p>
          <p>
           That mantra has served them well. It stripped away everything that was superficial in their lives, and at the end of it, they found that the world is a kinder place than they could have ever believed.
          </p>
          <p>
           Each new city bought with it countless adventures and unforgettable stories. Brazil touched their hearts with its hospitality. Ecuadors volcanoes held them spellbound with its glorious red rages. St. Augustins ancient culture gave them a head rush. The dizzying climb atop the majestic Mt.Roraima revealed an unparalleled panorama that struck with awe. Sunrise over the Amazon might have been the most romantic moment of their lives, though it competed with the overwhelmingly beautiful journey over the Carretera Austral Highway in Chile, one that passed galloping waterfalls, snow-capped mountains and glistening lakes. The horizon-less, mirror-like salt flats of Uyuni humbled them. They also crossed the incredible 6000 year old Marble rocks at Patagonia that make the General Carrera lake look like an art gallery with exotic sculptures set against fluorescent blue waters.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
            <img alt="Antartida-Peninsula" class="aligncenter wp-image-258 size-full" height="683" src="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Antartida-Peninsula.jpg" width="1024"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/&amp;media=http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Antartida-Peninsula.jpg&amp;description=Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Copyright: SaltaConmingo.com
           </em>
          </p>
          <h4>
           <strong>
            2. SO WHO DID YOU MEET?
           </strong>
          </h4>
          <p>
           But if you spoke to Sara and JAAC, theyd tell you that the sights arent the important part. The people are.
          </p>
          <p>
           Every journey is different thanks to the people you meet, and we were lucky to meet a lot of great, friendly and welcoming people. It would be impossible to mention them all. That is the greatest thing you discover by travelling: people are good. If we had to decide, we would say that Brazilian people are the most welcoming we have ever met, and our best couchsurfing experiences were with Brazilian hosts.
          </p>
          <p>
           Couch surfing meant sleeping on strange couches in the homes of people who sometimes didnt even understand what Sara and JAAC said.
          </p>
          <p>
           We hitchhiked; we slept in the most varied places  from a military quarter to a hammock in the jungle. We were robbed and blackmailed, but we never stopped feeling like we had to keep travelling.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Quilotoa-Volcano-Ecuador.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="Hobo-Quilotoa-Volcano-Ecuador" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-278" height="683" src="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Quilotoa-Volcano-Ecuador.jpg" width="1024"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/&amp;media=http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Quilotoa-Volcano-Ecuador.jpg&amp;description=Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <h4>
           <strong>
            1. SO HOW DID YOU DO IT?
           </strong>
          </h4>
          <p>
           Sara and JAAC travel light. They made sure that both their minds and their hands were devoid of unnecessary baggage. Our backpack for the entire one-year journey never weighed more than 7 or 8 kilos.
          </p>
          <p>
           We always bring a cap, an army knife and a torch and we usually do not like buying souvenirs. The best souvenirs are memories and pictures, and they weigh nothing at all. (I wonder if they listen to Cliff Richards.)
          </p>
          <p>
           The small luxury we do always allow to ourselves is going to a good restaurant, says JAAC. Sara sees no point to travelling if youre not indulging your taste buds.
          </p>
          <p>
           Everything was decided week-to-week, day-to-day. The most pressing concerns were always where to stay and where to go next.
          </p>
          <p>
           We did not expect anything in particular and we got surprised everyday, positively or, sometimes, negatively, says Sara. The best way to face it: travel slowly and enjoy wherever it is you are before thinking about the next.
          </p>
          <p>
           On the last leg of their incredible tour, Saara and JAAC visited Antarctica. Mighty glaciers and icebergs, excruciating cold  for JAAC, its was like a Jules Verne world come to life.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Perito-Moreno-Glaciar-Argentina.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="Hobo-Perito-Moreno-Glaciar-Argentina" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-277" height="683" src="http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Perito-Moreno-Glaciar-Argentina.jpg" width="1024"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://blog.triphobo.com/sara-and-jaac-372-days-through-south-america/&amp;media=http://blog.triphobo.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Hobo-Perito-Moreno-Glaciar-Argentina.jpg&amp;description=Sara And JAAC  372 Days Through South America')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The hardest part was coming home, Sara says. Wed changed too much. Everyday life wasnt real anymore, not like the past one year had been real. I cant explain it. So many things arent important anymore. I cant imagine living at home as I used to.
          </p>
          <p>
           JAAC tries to verbalise Saras feelings. To travel is to really explore yourself. Youre as fascinated at your own evolution, as at the landscapes. You begin to trust people more, to have faith. We believe that, in the future, different forms of collaborative travelling will grow and more and more people will be able to visit the world. We would not advise anything in particular: just travel and do not be scared.
          </p>
          <p>
           They are now (un)planning their South-east Asia vacation.
          </p>
