         <div class="article-meta visible-xs visible-xxs">
          <p class="byline">
           <span class="by">
            By
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" data-omtag="news:article:author:top" href="http://www.realtor.com/author/houselogic/">
             HouseLogic
            </a>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p class="posted-on">
           <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2014-02-14T16:41:15+00:00">
            4:41 pm ET
            <br/>
            February 14, 2014
           </time>
           <time class="updated hidden" datetime="2014-02-14T16:41:15+00:00">
            February 14, 2014
           </time>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="image-wrapper no-caption">
          <figure>
           <a href="http://www.realtor.com/advice/how-to-claim-your-energy-tax-credits/" title="Energy Tax Credits You Can Claim">
            <img alt="l16215e44-m7o" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="480" src="http://rdcnewscdn.realtor.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/l16215e44-m7o.jpg" width="640"/>
           </a>
           <figcaption>
            <p class="credit">
            </p>
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </div>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.realtor.com/advice/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/02/1765619051.jpg">
           <img alt="176561905" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-144205" height="231" src="http://www.realtor.com/advice/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2014/02/1765619051-300x231.jpg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          If you upgraded one or more of the following systems last year, you may be eligible to take a tax credit  up to $500  on your return.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.houselogic.com/home-advice/taxes-incentives/tax-credits-installing-biomass-stoves/">
            Biomass stoves
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.houselogic.com/home-advice/taxes-incentives/tax-credits-replacing-heating-and-cooling-systems/">
            Heating, ventilation, air conditioning
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.houselogic.com/home-advice/taxes-incentives/tax-credits-adding-or-replacing-insulation/">
            Insulation
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.houselogic.com/home-advice/taxes-incentives/tax-credits-replacing-your-roof/">
            Roofs (metal and asphalt)
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.houselogic.com/home-advice/taxes-incentives/tax-credits-non-solar-water-heaters/">
            Water heaters (non-solar)
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.houselogic.com/home-advice/windows-doors/window-replacement-tax-credit/">
            Windows, doors, and skylights
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          The energy tax credits are small, but at least a credit is better than a deduction:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Deductions just reduce your taxable income.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           With a credit, you get a dollar-for-dollar reduction in your tax liability: If you get the $500 credit, you pay $500 less in taxes.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Limits on IRS energy tax credits besides $500 max
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Credit only extends to 10 percent of the cost (not the 30 percent of yesteryear), so you have to spend $5,000 to get $500.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           $500 is a lifetime limit. If you pocketed $500 or more in past years combined, youre not entitled to any more money for energy-efficient improvements in the above categories. But if you took $300 back then, for example, you can get up to $200 now.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           With some systems, your cap is even lower than $500.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           $500 is the max for all qualified improvements combined.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="half" id="rdc-newsletter">
          <div class="nonform hide">
           <p class="msg hide">
           </p>
           <i class="fa fa-spinner fa-pulse hide">
           </i>
          </div>
          <div class="form">
           <div class="left">
            <h4>
             Please, Mr. Postman
            </h4>
            <p>
             Send me news, tips, and promos from realtor.com and Move.
            </p>
           </div>
           <div class="right">
            <input id="signup_call" type="hidden" value="rdc:news:engagement:newsletterSignup-Article"/>
            <input class="email" placeholder="Enter your email address" required="" type="text"/>
            <button type="button">
             Sign Up
            </button>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Certain systems capped below $500
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          No matter how much you spend on some approved items, youll never get the $500 credit  though you could combine some of these:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           New windows:$200 max cap (and no, not per window  overall)
          </li>
          <li>
           Advanced main air-circulating fan:$50 max cap
          </li>
          <li>
           Qualified natural gas, propane, or oil furnace or hot water boiler:$150 max cap
          </li>
          <li>
           Approved electric and geothermal heat pumps; central air-conditioning systems; and natural gas, propane, or oil water heaters:$300 max cap
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="ad-rectangle visible-xs visible-xxs">
          <div id="div-gpt-ad-MRECT3">
           <script type="text/javascript">
            googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display("div-gpt-ad-MRECT3"); });
           </script>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          And not all products are created equal in the feds eyes. Improvements have to meet IRS energy-efficiency standards to qualify for the tax credit. In the case of boilers and furnaces, they have to meet the 95 AFUE standard.
          <a href="http://www.energystar.gov" target="_blank" title="EnergyStar.gov">
           <span class="external">
            EnergyStar.gov
           </span>
          </a>
          has the details.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Tax credits cover installation  sometimes
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Rule of thumb: If installation is either particularly difficult or critical to safe functioning, the credit will cover labor. Otherwise, not. (Yes, youd have to be pretty handy to install your own windows and roof, but the feds put these squarely in the not covered category.)
         </p>
         <p>
          Installation covered for:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Biomass stoves
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           HVAC
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Non-solar water heaters
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Installation not covered for:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Insulation
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Roofs
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Windows, doors, and skylights
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           How to claim the energy tax credit
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Determine if the system you installed is eligible for the credits. Go to
           <a href="http://www.energystar.gov/index.cfm?c=tax_credits.tx_index">
            Energy Stars website
           </a>
           for detailed descriptions of whats covered; then talk to your vendor.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Save system receipts and manufacturer certifications. Youll need them if the IRS asks for proof.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <ul>
          <li>
           File IRS
           <a href="http://www.irs.gov/uac/Form-5695,-Residential-Energy-Credits" target="_blank">
            Form 5695
           </a>
           with the rest of your tax forms.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <em>
           This article provides general information about tax laws and consequences, but isnt intended to be relied upon as tax or legal advice applicable to particular transactions or circumstances. Consult a tax professional for such advice, and remember that tax laws may vary by jurisdiction.
          </em>
         </p>
