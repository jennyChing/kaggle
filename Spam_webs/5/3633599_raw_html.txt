       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Homemade Laundry Soap
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://wayfaringartist.com/2010/03/31/homemade-laundry-soap/" rel="bookmark" title="10:56 pm">
         <span class="entry-date">
          March 31, 2010
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://wayfaringartist.com/author/anna/" title="View all posts by Anna">
          Anna
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-828" height="375" src="http://wayfaringartist.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Blog-Pic-125.jpg" title="Blog Pic-1" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Tonight I experimented with making my own laundry soap. In about a half an hour I produced 8 cups of laundry soap that will wash 64 loads of clothing for about $4.00. Its environmentally friendly, good for you, and cheap! How can you lose? I owe this recipe to my friend
         <a href="http://lifegetsmessy.com" target="_blank">
          Shari
         </a>
         . I actually called her as I was driving to the grocery store How much of what again?? cause me, like a doofus, hadnt written it down. She set me straight, and heres the result:
        </p>
        <p>
         The ingrediants and recipe are this:
        </p>
        <p>
         1 part Borax, 1 part Washing Soda, 2 parts grated laundry soap (about 3 bars).
        </p>
        <p>
         Use 2 tablespoons per load of laundry (start the water, add the soap, swish, add clothes).
        </p>
        <p>
         So for the recipe I made, I did 2 cups of Borax, 2 cups of Washing Soda, and 4 cups of grated Fels Naptha, all of which I got at Publix, and Shari has also found at Kroger, so its not hard to get your hands on the stuff.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-828" height="375" src="http://wayfaringartist.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Blog-Pic-125.jpg" title="Blog Pic-1" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         NOTE: buy a grater just for this you dont want to grate your cheese after the soap, it can lead to soap residue in your food, which can lead to well shall we say general unpleasantness and runs to the bathroom. Nuff said, just spend the $2-3 dollars on a new grater, k? Okay.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         So this isnt hard, I measured 2 cups each of the Borax and Washing Soda, and then started grating my soap.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-826" height="375" src="http://wayfaringartist.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Blog-Pic-218.jpg" title="Blog Pic-2" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Ive been told by Shari that this whole grating process goes faster if you leave your soap out for a couple days and let it get dry. I was eager to see how it worked, so I only left it out for one day. I will say that it was easier to grate the dry part, and it also grates finer when dry, although this should dissolve fine as well.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         And. were done! Pretty painless, if you ask me, and its about 1/4 the price of the Seventh Generation detergent I had been using, which I love, but this is so much cheaper
         <img alt=":-P" class="wp-smiley" src="http://wayfaringartist.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_razz.gif"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://wayfaringartist.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Blog-Pic-316.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-829" height="375" src="http://wayfaringartist.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Blog-Pic-316.jpg" title="Blog Pic-3" width="560"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The left over bits of three bars of Fels Naptha.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-827" height="375" src="http://wayfaringartist.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/Blog-Pic-413.jpg" title="Blog Pic-4" width="560"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         And thats all she wrote tonight, folks.
        </p>
