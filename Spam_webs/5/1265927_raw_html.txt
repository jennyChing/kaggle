         <p>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers1.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear cucake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6074" height="350" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers1.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers1" width="525"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          I loved making the
          <a href="http://cakejournal.com/tutorials/fondant-cupcake-topper-with-easter-motif/">
           fondant cupcake toppers for Easter
          </a>
          so much. So I came up with these sweet teddy bear cupcake toppers. I have made them in pink and blue and they are just perfect for a baby shower.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-6073">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Tools-for-cupcake-toppers.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6078" height="406" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Tools-for-cupcake-toppers.jpg" title="Tools for cupcake toppers" width="525"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           This is what I use:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Rolled fondant icing
          <br/>
          Tylo powder
          <br/>
          Rolling pin
          <br/>
          Tylo glue
          <br/>
          Small brush
          <br/>
          Gel paste colors
          <br/>
          Small blossom cutter with ejector (PME)
          <br/>
          Teddy bear embosser from the Cradle 3D set (PatchWork Cutters)
          <br/>
          Round cookie cutter (40mm)
          <br/>
          Round scallop cookie cutter 2 1/4 (58mm)
          <br/>
          Round Piping tip no 2
          <br/>
          Round piping tip with a 10mm opening
          <br/>
          A little cornstarch for dusting and a small knife (not showing) if you want to make the tiny leaves on the girl cupcake toppers
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Start adding some tylo powder to your white base. This will help the cupcake toppers to hold the shape better when they are placed in the frosting.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 1:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Roll out the white fondant to a 3 mm thickness and cut out circles with the scallop cookie cutter. Place them on a lightly cornstarch dusted tray as you go.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-A.jpg">
           <img alt="Teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6081" height="374" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-A.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers A" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 2:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Take the round piping tip and cut out a small hole in each of the scallops.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-B.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6082" height="350" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-B.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers B" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 3:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Use another color of rolled fondant for the center of the cupcake toppers and roll out the fondant to a 2mm thickness. Cut out circles with the 40mm cookie cutter.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-C.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6083" height="350" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-C.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers C" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 4:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Use the large teddy bear embosser from the Patchwork cutter Cradle set and emboss the center circle.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-D.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6084" height="350" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-D.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers D" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 5:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Next brush a little tylo glue on the back of the embossed circle and place it in the middle of the white base.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-E.jpg">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6085" height="358" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-E.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers E" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 6:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          If you like, you can glue on a couple of small blossoms and tiny leaves for the girl cupcake toppers.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           TIP!
          </strong>
          I make the leaves out of tiny pieces of rolled fondant. Roll them into a small tear drop shape and make a little vein with a small knife.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-F.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6087" height="350" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-F.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers F" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 7:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          For the boys, I have made a small ball. Roll out your fondant thinly. Use the round piping tip with the 10mm opening and cut out some dots. Use the end of the no 2 piping tip and emboss the middle of the ball.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-H.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6089" height="350" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-H.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers H" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Step 8:
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Attache the small ball with a little brush of tylo glue.
          <br/>
          <a href="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-I.jpg">
           <img alt="teddy bear fondant cupcake toppers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6091" height="350" src="http://cakejournal.cakejournalllc.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/How-to-make-embossed-teddy-bear-cupcake-toppers-I.jpg" title="How to make embossed teddy bear cupcake toppers I" width="525"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Let the cupcake toppers dry thoroughly before use.
         </p>
         <p>
          Happy Caking!
         </p>
         <p>
          Louise
         </p>
