                    <meta content="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_Y_0bfxTV1Y/Tya-9pH-a9I/AAAAAAAABCA/GtvbtPzcvZY/s400/Pretty+City+Things+Glider+Reupholstery+Progress.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     How To Recover A Nursery Glider
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4444716656941487640" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     I have a little confession: it's not completely finished. But the back and bottom cushions are slip-covered and, since a lot of gliders don't even have arm cushions, I'll explain how I did these and then update this post when I do get the arms completed. Here's how she's looking at the moment...
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_Y_0bfxTV1Y/Tya-9pH-a9I/AAAAAAAABCA/GtvbtPzcvZY/s1600/Pretty+City+Things+Glider+Reupholstery+Progress.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_Y_0bfxTV1Y/Tya-9pH-a9I/AAAAAAAABCA/GtvbtPzcvZY/s400/Pretty+City+Things+Glider+Reupholstery+Progress.jpg" width="257"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Even without arm cushions, I still think it's a vast improvement on the boring blue that is hiding underneath the pretty new covers, don't you?
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yuTaa_oWsJ8/Tyan0I31rPI/AAAAAAAABAA/c7z3-T-w5Bs/s1600/Pretty+City+Things+Glider+Reupholstery+Before.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-yuTaa_oWsJ8/Tyan0I31rPI/AAAAAAAABAA/c7z3-T-w5Bs/s400/Pretty+City+Things+Glider+Reupholstery+Before.jpg" width="268"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <b>
                      You Will Need:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     - An ugly glider
                     <br/>
                     - 2-3 yards of fabric (I used Waverly Cross Section in Charcoal)
                     <br/>
                     - Sew-onVelcroand/or sew-on snap closures
                     <br/>
                     - Needle &amp; thread
                     <br/>
                     - Sewing machine (I have a Singer Simple - it's the most basic, cheapest model Singer makes, and it's great for a proper beginner like me. Also, if you buy one from Jo-Ann Fabrics, they do a free "How to Use Your Machine" class which was
                     <i>
                      really
                     </i>
                     helpful.)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      The Back Cushion:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     I actually started on the arms, made a big mess and gave up,
                     <i>
                      then
                     </i>
                     did the back cushion. But I'll skip that first bit and start with the successful part instead. ;)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I should point out, in case you're not aware, that I don't really know how to sew. This is the first thing I have ever made, so this tutorial is designed for beginners. I'm sure there are better ways of doing things but this is what worked for me. :) I chose slip-covers over just recovering the cushions, which I think is probably more difficult (if you picked the original covers apart, you'd have a pattern to work from at least), but slip-covers are removable and washable, which is what you want for a piece of furniture that's probably going to get spit up on on a regular basis.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     I began by removing the back cushion from the glider, laying it out on my new fabric and cutting two pieces of fabric big enough to make a slip cover.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VC9TOuI-7Mw/TyazEX7g8_I/AAAAAAAABAQ/Ok9tRUNnJWU/s1600/DSC_0002.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="317" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VC9TOuI-7Mw/TyazEX7g8_I/AAAAAAAABAQ/Ok9tRUNnJWU/s400/DSC_0002.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      With the two pieces of fabric pattern to pattern (if they'd been sewed together already, the cover would be inside out),I tacked all the way around the edges. Tacking, in case you are as much of a beginner as me, is very loose, hand-sewed temporary stitching designed to hold the seam until it's properly sewn.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Xu34avMJnLk/Tya1JLquFKI/AAAAAAAABAw/LAR9HpocrzI/s1600/DSC_0003.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="233" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Xu34avMJnLk/Tya1JLquFKI/AAAAAAAABAw/LAR9HpocrzI/s400/DSC_0003.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     (Apologies for the horrendously out of focus pictures - hopefully you still get the idea!)
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Xrt1v1K8L-g/Tya1JuqBNzI/AAAAAAAABA4/iloABPzB5Ks/s1600/DSC_0004.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="266" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Xrt1v1K8L-g/Tya1JuqBNzI/AAAAAAAABA4/iloABPzB5Ks/s400/DSC_0004.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="text-align: left;">
                      You could probably pin instead, but I thought this was a more fool-proof way of making sure that when I sewed it on the machine, it would be a good fit.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      Keeping the cover inside out, I removed the cushion and used my machine to sew all around the sides and top, leaving the bottom open. When that was done, I turned it the right way around and put the cushion back in to test the fit...
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-nFKkR7dnXaw/Tya1yRpKTGI/AAAAAAAABBA/eRjD9glve2A/s1600/IMG_7536-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-nFKkR7dnXaw/Tya1yRpKTGI/AAAAAAAABBA/eRjD9glve2A/s400/IMG_7536-1.jpg" width="356"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      ...and felt very proud of myself and started to think that this might not be as impossible
                      <a href="http://prettycitythings.blogspot.com/2012/01/possibly-impossible-project.html">
                       as I originally thought
                      </a>
                      ! (If you get to this point and it's not perfect, it's easy to fix - see how I made alterations on the seat cushion below.)
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      To close the bottom of the cushion, I folded the fabric under and ironed along the seams...
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wdOuQqz15So/Tya8xfccDlI/AAAAAAAABBw/FPU1S_AwOH4/s1600/IMG_7543.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wdOuQqz15So/Tya8xfccDlI/AAAAAAAABBw/FPU1S_AwOH4/s400/IMG_7543.JPG" width="381"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     ...then sewed over the ironed seams with the machine:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-l4EAm4xZq-s/Tya8x226ihI/AAAAAAAABB4/QlFO8vaeTRw/s1600/IMG_7544.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="237" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-l4EAm4xZq-s/Tya8x226ihI/AAAAAAAABB4/QlFO8vaeTRw/s400/IMG_7544.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I used sew-on snap closures to hold the opening together (like
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Dritz-R-Sew-On-Snaps-Nickel/dp/B000A8BMDQ">
                      these
                     </a>
                     ), but if I did this again, I'd useVelcrolike I did on the seat cushion (see below) because I think it looks tidier when the slip cover is on the cushion. Sorry, no picture of this step, but I promise it's very straight-forward - just sew a few on at intervals along the bottom edges of the opening. I also sewed the edges (a couple of inches on either side) with the machine. I did a better job on the seat cushion than the back cushion for this step, so I'll explain this part better later on.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      Next, I made ties to secure the cushion to the glider frame. I copied what was on the original cushion by first ironing two long strips of fabric so they'd be easy to sew like so...
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-UMCwBKoHggw/Tya7K1uL9QI/AAAAAAAABBI/fdrV_BsCiv8/s1600/IMG_7539.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="283" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-UMCwBKoHggw/Tya7K1uL9QI/AAAAAAAABBI/fdrV_BsCiv8/s400/IMG_7539.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-_vPhMx2P3ME/Tya7LJs8GOI/AAAAAAAABBQ/uzlkl4Z2k_8/s1600/IMG_7540.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;">
                       <img border="0" height="252" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-_vPhMx2P3ME/Tya7LJs8GOI/AAAAAAAABBQ/uzlkl4Z2k_8/s400/IMG_7540.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      Then I sewed them on the machine...
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-NZ7apexKTo4/Tya8k3N7VVI/AAAAAAAABBo/9kGXY9G0zFI/s1600/IMG_7541.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-NZ7apexKTo4/Tya8k3N7VVI/AAAAAAAABBo/9kGXY9G0zFI/s400/IMG_7541.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      Then I hand-sewed them onto the back of the slip-cover so that they'd be in the right place to tie on to the chair back.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-uimq0tTfWes/Tya_1T0aelI/AAAAAAAABCI/QVCCQBUe3NY/s1600/IMG_7545.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-uimq0tTfWes/Tya_1T0aelI/AAAAAAAABCI/QVCCQBUe3NY/s400/IMG_7545.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-xWbWkboqaYo/Tya_2bPQZmI/AAAAAAAABCQ/pdHyV_nZefw/s1600/IMG_7548-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="298" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-xWbWkboqaYo/Tya_2bPQZmI/AAAAAAAABCQ/pdHyV_nZefw/s400/IMG_7548-1.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     And with that, the back cushion was done. Just the seat and arms left to do...
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      The Seat Cushion:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     The seat cushion went pretty much the same way as the back cushion. The only difference was that because it was thicker, I had to make a few little adjustments.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Again, I began by removing the seat cushion from the glider and laying it out on the floor between two wrong-way-round pieces of fabric. I then pinned along the back on the cushion in a straight line. I hadn't needed to do this for the back cushion because it was easier to see that the pattern was straight with the thinner cushion. Obviously, if you're using a solid, it wouldn't be so important.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AcRzsrS5l0s/TyaunzeybaI/AAAAAAAABAI/FgZosl0ak_8/s1600/IMG_7597.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="273" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-AcRzsrS5l0s/TyaunzeybaI/AAAAAAAABAI/FgZosl0ak_8/s400/IMG_7597.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I tacked all the way around the cushion again, aiming to keep the seam at the bottom of the pillow rather than in the middle - I thought it would be easier that way because I'd only have to deal with one lot of corner folds.
                     <br/>
                     Speaking of corner folds (which I'm sure is not the technical term), I pinned them in place as I tacked the edges:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3J9yqMC2tCU/TybOSZMYYyI/AAAAAAAABCg/bfNhXg0YjYI/s1600/IMG_7599.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3J9yqMC2tCU/TybOSZMYYyI/AAAAAAAABCg/bfNhXg0YjYI/s400/IMG_7599.JPG" width="380"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     All tacked and pinned, it looked like this:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-n-M64ZMSRgc/TybOSoIBZCI/AAAAAAAABCo/mXQXOssU8bE/s1600/IMG_7601.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="340" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-n-M64ZMSRgc/TybOSoIBZCI/AAAAAAAABCo/mXQXOssU8bE/s400/IMG_7601.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then I removed the pins, took the cushion out, and sewed the sides and top on the machine. When that was done, I put the cushion back in to test the fit:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-H-gYxsJglcs/TybOTNsqh0I/AAAAAAAABCw/Rs6Dz37uOs8/s1600/IMG_7605.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="391" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-H-gYxsJglcs/TybOTNsqh0I/AAAAAAAABCw/Rs6Dz37uOs8/s400/IMG_7605.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="text-align: -webkit-auto;">
                       I trimmed the excess fabric:
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Ij4yOHqMBaE/TybO5dxC3tI/AAAAAAAABC4/A6xSRWuzLoQ/s1600/IMG_7606.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="378" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Ij4yOHqMBaE/TybO5dxC3tI/AAAAAAAABC4/A6xSRWuzLoQ/s400/IMG_7606.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Then turned it the right way round to admire my handiwork. This way round, though, it was easier to see that the fit wasn't as tight as I'd have liked.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-W_A6IQr8vOw/TybO6DUcmHI/AAAAAAAABDA/ZY1HsOSIf2A/s1600/IMG_7608.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="327" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-W_A6IQr8vOw/TybO6DUcmHI/AAAAAAAABDA/ZY1HsOSIf2A/s400/IMG_7608.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     See how the left and right sides aren't even? One was too loose, and the whole seam along the top could have been tighter too (it's not that obvious in the picture, but it was enough to bug me). Thankfully, this is an easy problem to solve - I turned it all inside out again,re-pinnedit for a better fit, and then sewed along the pins, removing them as I went:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-X09YMwWgUA8/TybQBEzIsGI/AAAAAAAABDQ/TxHI_wjmqGc/s1600/IMG_7610-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="228" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-X09YMwWgUA8/TybQBEzIsGI/AAAAAAAABDQ/TxHI_wjmqGc/s400/IMG_7610-1.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DJtWAG5jnRk/TybQM9kvViI/AAAAAAAABDY/16dVToOh4Ug/s1600/IMG_7612.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="338" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-DJtWAG5jnRk/TybQM9kvViI/AAAAAAAABDY/16dVToOh4Ug/s400/IMG_7612.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     This time when I tested it, it was perfect. :)
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-h4_W_q_Z7_o/TybQNQUvzHI/AAAAAAAABDg/HA1BzUAltgE/s1600/IMG_7613.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-h4_W_q_Z7_o/TybQNQUvzHI/AAAAAAAABDg/HA1BzUAltgE/s400/IMG_7613.JPG" width="393"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Now I just had to finish the bottom. I started by folding the fabric inward and ironing along the seam.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UpLbbA5TvG0/TybQN0q7pgI/AAAAAAAABDo/WAPIOPbfxMk/s1600/IMG_7615.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="247" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UpLbbA5TvG0/TybQN0q7pgI/AAAAAAAABDo/WAPIOPbfxMk/s400/IMG_7615.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I used sew-onVelcrothis time. The top row of pins in this picture was my guide to make sure I left enough fabric for a neat closure, then I pinned theVelcroon underneath, removed the cushion again, and sewed around the edges of theVelcroon the machine:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-OR9JuhrVvwM/TybQQIXC8jI/AAAAAAAABDw/8e9Ltp8HwOA/s1600/IMG_7617.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-OR9JuhrVvwM/TybQQIXC8jI/AAAAAAAABDw/8e9Ltp8HwOA/s400/IMG_7617.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I tested it when I was done, and it was a good fit - the pattern even matched up pretty well:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LE2_S_QIc4E/TybTiGuV2QI/AAAAAAAABEA/luk4-VEXsCs/s1600/IMG_7626.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="273" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-LE2_S_QIc4E/TybTiGuV2QI/AAAAAAAABEA/luk4-VEXsCs/s400/IMG_7626.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     To finish the corners, I folded the fabric inwards and pinned it on the outside.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-SVoQO0I6Kv0/TybTinKDSqI/AAAAAAAABEI/kBZya5MkIy0/s1600/IMG_7631.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="291" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-SVoQO0I6Kv0/TybTinKDSqI/AAAAAAAABEI/kBZya5MkIy0/s400/IMG_7631.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I removed the cushion again and sewed along the seams I'd pinned from the outside, removing the pins as I went:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wLyQIV9KaNo/TybTlTmmwoI/AAAAAAAABEQ/8_7gC6Uyr7E/s1600/IMG_7633.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="300" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wLyQIV9KaNo/TybTlTmmwoI/AAAAAAAABEQ/8_7gC6Uyr7E/s400/IMG_7633.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I put the cushion back in again and was happy with the fit again - yay!
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AWThgi3LyIA/TybTn6XTLiI/AAAAAAAABEY/dUbmwaX4RIQ/s1600/IMG_7638.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="355" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AWThgi3LyIA/TybTn6XTLiI/AAAAAAAABEY/dUbmwaX4RIQ/s400/IMG_7638.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I just had to make one more tie to hold this cushion to the chair frame. I used the same method as for the seat back ties - ironing the seams the way I wanted them to sit before sewing them on the machine - only this one was shorter and wider and used Velcro.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DOUee4DqWz0/TybX3eJZQwI/AAAAAAAABEg/QDJcjh6vqgc/s1600/IMG_7642.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="200" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DOUee4DqWz0/TybX3eJZQwI/AAAAAAAABEg/QDJcjh6vqgc/s400/IMG_7642.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I pinned it to the seat cover where I wanted it, then sewed it on the machine. I went over it three times to make it extra strong.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-IiWhdjQh0Q8/TybX3h6m9zI/AAAAAAAABEo/1EiuqbEhpcM/s1600/IMG_7644.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="327" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-IiWhdjQh0Q8/TybX3h6m9zI/AAAAAAAABEo/1EiuqbEhpcM/s400/IMG_7644.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     With the seat cover finished, it looked like this:
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UcpIes6hvaw/TybX4GDE0kI/AAAAAAAABEw/bndbbp8-d1w/s1600/IMG_7646.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-UcpIes6hvaw/TybX4GDE0kI/AAAAAAAABEw/bndbbp8-d1w/s400/IMG_7646.JPG" width="341"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     And with that, the seat cushion was done!
