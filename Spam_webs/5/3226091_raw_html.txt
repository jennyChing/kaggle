                    <h1 class="entry-title cb-entry-title cb-single-title" itemprop="headline">
                     Amsterdams Orange Madness with KLM
                    </h1>
                    <div class="cb-byline">
                     <div class="cb-date cb-byline-element">
                      <i class="fa fa-clock-o">
                      </i>
                      <time class="updated" datetime="2015-05-05">

                  <section class="entry-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
                   <h2>
                    A bit of Orange Madness, or oranjegekte, to the Dutch, went globalin reverse.
                   </h2>
                   <h3>
                    Rather than spreading The Netherlands Kings Day celebrations to the corners of the world, KLM visited Shanghai, Cape Town, Rio, Los Angeles and Oslo and invited an adventurer from each city to fly back with them to Amsterdam to join in the festivities.
                   </h3>
                   <p>
                    <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="390" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/MlDEgqMwm8Y" width="640">
                    </iframe>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    So what would you do if, on ordering a Heineken at a bar, you were invited to take part in an 
                    <a href="http://po.st/KLMOrangeExperience" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/po.st/KLMOrangeExperience');">
                     orange experience
                    </a>
                    ? I reckon a lot of us would back away thinking it might involve coating in some citrusy fluid. In this case however, those bold enough to accept had their embarassment factors further tested by being dressed in orange and challenged to sing Oranje Boven, a classic Dutch folk song, in front of the packed bar.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    In this case the rewards for being uninhibited were big. The first contenders shameless enough to perform, got to collect a suitcase, a friend and a passport I presume, before being smuggled to the airport. Only when in departure where they told they were about to be rewarded with a monster trip to Amsterdam courtesy of the Dutch national airline.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Would you have got that far? Or if you were that friend  asked unannounced to hop on a plane and travel across the world  what would you do? Not even to mention all those things that would get in the way even if you actually felt the urge.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    So I have no idea how long it took the two Dutch icons to get their five winners! But check out the video, and youll see none of them are shrinking violet types. Arriving in Amsterdam with Kings Day celebrations swathing the city in orange, they rode bikes amongst music festivals and street markets and played games before boarding one of the famous canal boats for the main event  a private party hosted by DJ Hardwell  the worlds current superstar DJ.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    So maybe next time you get asked a suspiciously odd kind of question in a public place, think twice before running a mile. You never know you could find yourself partying in Amsterdam!
                   </p>
