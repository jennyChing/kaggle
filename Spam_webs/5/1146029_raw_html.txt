       <h1 class="posttitle">
        Accessible Frank Lloyd Wright House To Make Public Debut
       </h1>
      </div>
      <div class="metaspost">
       <span class="postbyAuthor">
        By
       </span>
       <span class="postAuthor">
        <a href="http://www.disabilityscoop.com/author/michelle-diament/" rel="author" title="Posts by Michelle Diament">
         Michelle Diament

       </span>
       <p>
        <span class="postDate">
         April 14, 2014
        </span>
        <span class="textsize">

        <div class="entry">
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 585px">
          <img alt="The Kenneth &amp; Phyllis Laurent House, a one-of-a-kind accessible home designed by Frank Lloyd Wright, will open to the public in June. (Laurent House Foundation)" height="383" src="http://pcdn.disabilityscoop.com/wp-content/uploads/ds140414_laurent_house.jpg" width="575"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The Kenneth &amp; Phyllis Laurent House, a one-of-a-kind accessible home designed by Frank Lloyd Wright, will open to the public in June. (Laurent House Foundation)
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The only fully-accessible home ever designed by famed architect Frank Lloyd Wright is set to open to the public as a museum.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Kenneth &amp; Phyllis Laurent House in Rockford, Ill. will open June 6, just days ahead of what would have been Wrights 147th birthday.
         </p>
         <p>
          Wright was commissioned in the late 1940s to conceive the one-of-a-kind home for Kenneth Laurent who experienced a spinal cord injury in World War II that left the lower half of his body paralyzed.
         </p>
         <p>
          Decades before the Americans with Disabilities Act became law, the home was designed to be appreciated aesthetically from a seated position and included switches, built-in desks as well as other features and furnishings, all accommodating an individual in a wheelchair.
         </p>
         <p>
          Laurent and his wife Phyllis lived in the home from 1952 until early 2012 when local preservationists
          <a href="http://www.disabilityscoop.com/2011/12/16/accessible-wright-house-museum/14661/" target="_blank">
           acquired
          </a>
          the residence and its original furnishings designed by Wright at auction.
         </p>
         <p>
          The home was
          <a href="http://www.disabilityscoop.com/2012/10/19/accessible-home-historic-register/16695/" target="_blank">
           added
          </a>
          to the U.S. Department of the Interiors National Register of Historic Places in 2012.
         </p>
         <p>
          The building is unique in that it has been continually occupied by the original owners, and contains not only furnishings designed by the architect, but many personal items of the owners. In other words, it is a complete work of art, said restoration architect John Eifler of Eifler &amp; Associates Architects who worked with the Laurent House Foundation to restore the home and prepare it for public viewings.
         </p>
         <p>
          Starting in June, tours will be held regularly during the first and last weekend of each month and by appointment, the foundation said.
         </p>

Confronting problems directly, with all issues observed to proffer a solution, the solution itself would become apparent. Take what is given through study, add to it an ability to prepare wisely, in material and method, and you will take away deepening, lasting beauty. I have admired Mr. Wright my entire life, all fault my own. His inspiration to see beyond the obvious and take his mind further into a subject, brought  forward a lasting comfort in life found in everything accomplished for his clients. Arrogant, he was, while certainty was his faith in orchestrating our environment.
         </p>
