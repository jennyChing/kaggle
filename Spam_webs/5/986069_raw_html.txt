           <header class="entry-header">
            <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
             A Tea Party Birthday
            </h1>
            <p class="entry-meta">
             <time class="entry-time" datetime="2013-10-21T11:33:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
              October 21, 2013
             </time>
             By
             <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
              <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.733blog.com/author/blogging/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
               <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
                Kim Conner
               </span>
              </a>
             </span>
             <span class="entry-comments-link">
              <a href="http://www.733blog.com/2013/10/a-tea-party-birthday/#comments">
               1 Comment
              </a>
             </span>
            </p>
           </header>
           <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
            <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/tea-party.png"/>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             My oldest is turning 13 this week so this past weekend we held a Tea Party Birthday Party for 5 of her friends.
            </div>
            <p>
             <a name="more">
             </a>
            </p>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/tea-table.png"/>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             I picked up tea cups and saucers, a tea pot, and sugar and creamer containers at Goodwill.
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             I love the way the mismatch china looked!
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/tea-party-decorations.png"/>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             Most of the decorations came from
             <a href="http://www.orientaltrading.com/api/search?Ntt=vintage+collection" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              Oriental Tradings Vintage Collection
             </a>
             .
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             Oriental Trading sent me the hanging fans, flag banner, lace fans, snake bracelet chains, tea charms and rose beads, the suitcase centerpiece, pin wheel picks, dinner napkins, table runner and coordinating 1212 paper. All of that only totals $88, but I found a promo code online and received $10 off.
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             The table runner is a double sided coated paper. It came rolled up in a tube so the edges did curl up a bit, but it was very pretty and added the right touch to the table. I used some of the sheets of scrapbook paper as placemats. The garland came in 8 strands so I cut them in half to better fit my purposes.
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ktTUS1S1rno/UmLLU3xIlQI/AAAAAAABYEE/pCumNDA7XDY/s1600/tea-party-table.png"/>
             <br/>
             The table cloths were flat bed sheets I had in my linen closet.
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/handpainted-tea-pot.png"/>
             <br/>
             The tea pot that I found at Goodwill was plain white so I spruced it up with a little paint.
             <br/>
             The girls had ice tea and water for beverages.
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/rosette-cake.png"/>
             <br/>
             The gorgeous Ombre Rosette cake came from a local baker,
             <a href="https://www.facebook.com/CakeIstryVT" target="_blank">
              Cakeistry
             </a>
             .
             <br/>
             If you are local, I definitely recommend them.
             <br/>
             They also put together the cake pops that I added to the favor boxes the girl devoured the cake pops that I had on the dessert table too. There was only one left after the party so my younger two kids and I split it. And let me tell you, it was DELICIOUS! They keep asking me if we can buy some more, so thank you to Cakeistry! If you want to try their cake pops and are in St. Albans, you can find that at The Traveled Cup, the new coffee shop on Main Street.
             <br/>
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/place-setting.png"/>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/dessert-table.png"/>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             For food I put together cucumber sandwiches. First I soaked the thinly sliced cucumber in a bit of lemon juice and salt. I then buttered the bread, and added a slice or two of cucumber. I also made cinnamon crescent rolls and star shaped peanut butter and jam sandwiches.
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             My daughter requested fruit kabobs and I also put out the left over cucumber slices.
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             The favor boxes I made using the scrapbook paper from
             <a href="http://www.orientaltrading.com/" target="_blank">
              Oriental Trading
             </a>
             and my Silhouette.
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/food-table.png"/>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             One of the games we played was Grammy Dress Up. I share the rules
             <a href="http://www.733blog.com/2013/10/mini-foam-top-hat-tutorial.html" target="_blank">
              here
             </a>
             . I ended up adding a twist: they had to wear my husbands size 13 shoes! I was almost rolling on the ground with laughter. All of the girls were really good sports with this one! The group that won received mini-nail polish and lip gloss.
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/grammy-race.png"/>
            </div>
            <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             After cake I got out the bracelets and beads that I received from Oriental Trading and the girls made tea party bracelets:
            </div>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <img border="0" src="http://733blog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/bracelet-favor.png"/>
             <br/>
             The last game I had them play was Cracker Whistle. I gave them a small cup of Goldfish crackers.
             <br/>
             I told them to start eating and counted down from 5 and that was their signal to try to whistle.
             <br/>
             Of course crackers dry your mouth out and make this whole task very, very hard and very funny!
            </div>
            <p>
             <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="710" scrolling="no" src="//instagram.com/p/fp9pmQOaGn/embed/" width="612">
             </iframe>
            </p>
            <div>
             <center>
              The rest of the time the girls had free time to hang out and chat.
              <b>
              </b>
             </center>
             <center>
              Overall it was a great time. I cant believe my girl is almost 13! Where does time go?
             </center>
