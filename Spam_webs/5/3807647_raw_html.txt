     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        A Jihad for Love
       </b>
      </i>
      (also known by the working title
      <i>
       <b>
        In the Name of Allah
       </b>
      </i>
      ) is a 2007 documentary film on the coexistence of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islam_and_homosexuality" title="Islam and homosexuality">
       Islam and homosexuality
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-invite_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-invite-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The film is directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Parvez_Sharma" title="Parvez Sharma">
       Parvez Sharma
      </a>
      , and produced by Sharma and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Trembling_Before_G-d" title="Trembling Before G-d">
        Trembling Before G-d
       </a>
      </i>
      director
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sandi_DuBowski" title="Sandi DuBowski">
       Sandi DuBowski
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Jihad_for_Love&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       A Jihad for Love
      </i>
      is produced by Halal Films, in association with the Sundance Documentary Fund,
      <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">
       Channel 4
      </a>
      Television (UK),
      <a href="/wiki/ZDF" title="ZDF">
       ZDF
      </a>
      (Germany),
      <a href="/wiki/Arte" title="Arte">
       Arte
      </a>
      (France-Germany),
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Logo_(TV_channel)" title="Logo (TV channel)">
       Logo
      </a>
      (US) and
      <a href="/wiki/Special_Broadcasting_Service" title="Special Broadcasting Service">
       SBS
      </a>
      (Australia).
     </p>
     <p>
      The documentary was filmed in 12 different countries and in nine languages.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Miami_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Miami-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartley_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hartley-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Sharma conducted interviews throughout North America, Europe, Africa, Asia and the Middle East. Countries included
      <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">
       Saudi Arabia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">
       Iran
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">
       Iraq
      </a>
      , Pakistan,
      <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">
       Egypt
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">
       Bangladesh
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">
       Turkey
      </a>
      , France, India, South Africa, the United States and the United Kingdom.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartley_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hartley-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He found many of his interviewees online, and received thousands of emails.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NY-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The film premiered at the
      <a href="/wiki/Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="Toronto International Film Festival">
       Toronto International Film Festival
      </a>
      in September 2007, and has been screened to great acclaim at several film festivals around the world. It was the Opening film for the prestigious Panorama Dokumente section of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Berlin_Film_Festival" title="Berlin Film Festival">
       Berlin Film Festival
      </a>
      in February, 2008. The U.S. theatrical release was May 21, 2008 at the
      <a href="/wiki/IFC_Center" title="IFC Center">
       IFC Center
      </a>
      in New York City. The film screened at the
      <a href="/wiki/Frameline_Film_Festival" title="Frameline Film Festival">
       Frameline Film Festival
      </a>
      in San Francisco on June 28, 2008, and the
      <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_International_Lesbian_%26_Gay_Film_Festival" title="Tokyo International Lesbian &amp; Gay Film Festival">
       Tokyo International Lesbian &amp; Gay Film Festival
      </a>
      on July 13, 2008.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Significance_of_the_title">
       Significance of the title
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Jihad_for_Love&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Significance of the title">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The title
      <i>
       A Jihad for Love
      </i>
      refers to the Islamic concept of
      <a href="/wiki/Jihad" title="Jihad">
       jihad
      </a>
      , as a religious struggle. The film seeks to reclaim this concept of personal struggle, as it is used in the media almost exclusively to mean "holy war" and to refer to violent acts perpetrated by extremist Muslims.
     </p>
     <p>
      The film has gone by several titles, beginning with the official working title,
      <i>
       In the Name of Allah
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-official-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Among Muslims, the phrase (
      <i>
       bismillah
      </i>
      in Arabic) may be used before beginning actions, speech, or writing. Its most notable use in
      <a href="/wiki/Al-Fatiha" title="Al-Fatiha">
       Al-Fatiha
      </a>
      , the opening passage of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qur%27an" title="Qur'an">
       Qur'an
      </a>
      , which begins
      <i>
       Bismillahi r-Rahmani r-Rahim.
      </i>
      All
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sura" title="Sura">
       surahs
      </a>
      of the Qur'an begin with "Bismillahi r-Rahmani r-Rahim," with the exception of the
      <a href="/wiki/At-Tawba" title="At-Tawba">
       ninth
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Producer DuBowski's previous film,
      <i>
       Trembling Before G-d
      </i>
      , on Orthodox and Hasidic Jews, also included the name of
      <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">
       God
      </a>
      , written with a hyphen
      <a href="/wiki/Names_of_God_in_Judaism#Laws_of_writing_divine_names" title="Names of God in Judaism">
       as in Jewish tradition
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Allah" title="Allah">
       Allah
      </a>
      is the name of
      <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">
       God
      </a>
      in Islam and
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      , and it is often used among
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">
       Muslims
      </a>
      residing in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslim_countries" title="Muslim countries">
       Muslim countries
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotheists" title="Monotheists">
       monotheists
      </a>
      in Arabic speaking countries.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy_and_problems">
       Controversy and problems
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Jihad_for_Love&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy and problems">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Sharma's making of the film has not been without criticism.
     </p>
     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
        About every two weeks I get an e-mail that berates me, condemns me to hell and, if they are nice, asks me to still seek forgiveness while there is still time.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_4-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-NY-4">
          <span>
           [
          </span>
          4
          <span>
           ]
          </span>
         </a>
        </sup>
       </td>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <p>
      Sharma refuses to associate homosexuality with shame, but recognizes the need to protect the safety and privacy of his sources, by filming them in silhouette or with their faces blurred. In one case, the family of an Afghan woman he interviewed "would undoubtedly kill her" if they found out she was lesbian. In another example, one of the associate producers, an Egyptian gay man, chose not to be listed in the credits for fear of possible consequences.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NY_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-NY-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The film was banned from screening at the 2008
      <a href="/wiki/Singapore_International_Film_Festival" title="Singapore International Film Festival">
       Singapore International Film Festival
      </a>
      "in view of the sensitive nature of the subject that features Muslim homosexuals in various countries and their struggle to reconcile religion and their lifestyle," Amy Chua,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Singapore_Board_of_Film_Censors&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Singapore Board of Film Censors (page does not exist)">
       Singapore Board of Film Censors
      </a>
      chairwoman was quoted as saying by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Straits_Times" title="The Straits Times">
        The Straits Times
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ban_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ban-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
