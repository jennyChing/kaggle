      <h1>
       A Wine for a Dreadfully Awkward Meal in A.S. Byatts Possession
      </h1>
      <div class="meta">
       <em>
        Posted on:
       </em>
       September 19th, 2013
       <em>
        by
       </em>
       <a href="/about" rel="author">
        Jameson
       </a>
       <!--&lt;a href="http://jamesonfink.com/a-wine-for-a-dreadfully-awkward-meal-in-a-s-byatts-possesion/#comments" class="comments-link"  title="Comment on A Wine for a Dreadfully Awkward Meal in A.S. Byatt&#8217;s Possession"&gt;12 Comments&lt;/a&gt;-->
      </div>
      <div class="entry">
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.randomhouse.com/book/22383/possession-by-a-s-byatt">
         <img alt="a.s. byatt posession a romance" class="size-full wp-image-6740 alignleft" height="450" src="http://jamesonfink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/possession.jpg" width="294"/>
        </a>
        Ugh. You ever read something that so encapsulates, with painful accuracy, a relationship on the decline? Or one that possibly never had an apex? This is my thought when I read
        <a href="http://www.asbyatt.com/" target="_blank">
         <strong>
          A.S. Byatts
          <em>
           Possession: A Romance
          </em>
         </strong>
        </a>
        . (Spoiler alert: the romance is not about these two, Val and Roland, who live in a dingy,
        <strong>
         cat-piss
        </strong>
        scented flat.) Heres the passage:
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Val put before him grilled marinated lamb, ratatouille, and hot Greek bread. He said, Shall I get a bottle of wine? and Val said, disagreeably and truthfully, You should have thought of that some time back; itll all go cold. They ate at a card-table, which they unfolded and folded again, after.
        </em>
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_6736" style="width: 200px">
        <a href="http://jamesonfink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/mwwc.jpg">
         <img alt="monthe wine writing challenge" class="wp-image-6736 " height="192" src="http://jamesonfink.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/mwwc-298x300.jpg" width="190"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         I possess the ability to take part.
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        I was inspired to pick up this book again after reading about the
        <strong>
         Monthly Wine Writing Challenge
        </strong>
        on
        <strong>
         <a href="http://mycustardpie.com/2013/08/26/monthly-wine-writing-challenge-3/" target="_blank">
          My Custard Pie
         </a>
        </strong>
        . This months theme is 
        <strong>
         Possession
        </strong>
         and, as you can tell, I took it quite literally.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, OK, theres the possession but wheres the wine? (Im sure Val and Roland were wondering the latter as well.) Well when youve got lamb and ratatouille (and, uh, Greek bread) do yourself a favor and
        <strong>
         transport from a dank, dark London flat to Provence in the south of France
        </strong>
        . If Val and Roland wanted to perk up their chilly environs, what could be better than
        <strong>
         ros
        </strong>
        ? Im thinking something from
        <strong>
         Bandol
        </strong>
        or
        <strong>
         Tavel
        </strong>
        , two southern French regions that produce a ros that can match with both the lamb and the vegetables. Or if this duo wants to
        <strong>
         embrace the darkness
        </strong>
        , a
        <strong>
         Mourvedre
        </strong>
        -based red wine from Bandol would do the trick.
       </p>
       <p>
        And theres something about the unfolding and folding of the card table that makes it extra sad.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 510px">
        <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/spdebump/8252219371/" title="card table 05 by spdebump, on Flickr">
         <img alt="card table 05" height="375" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8223/8252219371_4c8623e4f2.jpg" width="500"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         The sign of a doomed relationship?
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.bloglovin.com/blog/3624877/?claim=2h84qqdcbq9">
         Follow my blog with Bloglovin
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Card table photo courtesy
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/spdebump/8252219371/">
          Chris Hourcle
         </a>
         .
        </em>
       </p>
