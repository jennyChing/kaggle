       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4662" height="400" src="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake.jpg" title="Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://ohmyveggies.com/recipe-pumpkin-spice-breakfast-shake/&amp;media=http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake.jpg&amp;description=Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Even though fall doesnt start until the end of September, somehow as soon as August ends, it feels like fall to me. Maybe its wishful thinking? Being ready for cooler weather and apple picking? Of course, it doesnt help that as soon as Labor Day ends, stores are full of pumpkin-flavored
         <em>
          everything
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         So I figured that even if it isnt fall yet, I could do a Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake. I was going to post this later in the month, but deliciousness of this magnitude cannot wait. It cannot!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake_in_glass.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake [with spoon]" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4663" height="545" src="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake_in_glass.jpg" title="Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake [with spoon]" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://ohmyveggies.com/recipe-pumpkin-spice-breakfast-shake/&amp;media=http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake_in_glass.jpg&amp;description=Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Like my
         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/recipe-mexican-chocolate-breakfast-shake/">
          Mexican Chocolate
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/recipe-banana-chai-breakfast-shake/">
          Banana Chai
         </a>
         overnight breakfast shakes, this one has three main components: oats, banana, and milk. Adding oats to a smoothie makes it thicker and more filling; allowing them to soak in the milk overnight makes the resulting shake smoother and less gritty, although you can definitely make this without the overnight part if youre in a time crunch. This shake is a complete breakfast in handy drink form!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake_on_newspaper.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake [top]" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4664" height="400" src="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake_on_newspaper.jpg" title="Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake [top]" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://ohmyveggies.com/recipe-pumpkin-spice-breakfast-shake/&amp;media=http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/pumpkin_spice_breakfast_shake_on_newspaper.jpg&amp;description=Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <br/>
         The first time I tested this recipe, I used a whole banana, but the shake was too banana-y. When I took it out altogether, the shake wasnt creamy enough. A half banana was the perfect balance. Theres still a little banana flavor in this shake, but the creamy consistency it adds is definitely worth it!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/cinnamon_sticks.jpg">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Cinnamon Sticks" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4665" height="400" src="http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/cinnamon_sticks.jpg" title="Cinnamon Sticks" width="600"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://ohmyveggies.com/recipe-pumpkin-spice-breakfast-shake/&amp;media=http://ohmyveggies.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/cinnamon_sticks.jpg&amp;description=Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
         <br/>
         What really makes this pumpkin-y is the addition of pumpkin pie spice. The flavor we often associate with pumpkin isnt pumpkin at all; its that homey combination of cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger and allspice that makes pumpkin pies and lattes so irresistible.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is officially my favorite fall breakfast. (Even if its still summer.)
         <br/>
        </p>

             <p class="summary italic">
              With the addition of oatmeal, this Pumpkin Spice Breakfast Shake will keep you full all morning long.
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 c. pumpkin puree
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tbsp. maple syrup or sweetener of choice (optional)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             3/4 tsp. pumpkin pie spice
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
             3/4 c. plain or vanilla almond milk (any milk is fine though!)
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-4" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 c. old fashioned oats
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-5" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 small banana, frozen
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-6" itemprop="ingredients">
             sprinkle of cinnamon (optional)
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Whisk together pumpkin puree, maple syrup, pumpkin pie spice, and almond milk in a resealable container. Stir in oats, seal, and refrigerate overnight.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             In the morning, combine frozen banana and pumpkin mixture in blender or food processor. Process until smooth; add more milk for a thinner consistency if desired. Pour into glass and sprinkle with cinnamon.
            </li>
           </ol>
