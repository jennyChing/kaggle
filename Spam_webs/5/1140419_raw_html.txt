         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          50 Incredible Alphabet Activities for Preschoolers
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p class="entry-filing">
          <span class="entry-categories">
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/kids-activities/learning-activities/literacy/" rel="category tag">
            Literacy &amp; ABCs

          </span>
          <span class="entry-ages">
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/activities/preschool-activities/">

           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-tags">
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/abcs/" rel="tag">
            ABCs
           </a>
           <br/>
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/letter-sounds/" rel="tag">

           <br/>
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/letters/" rel="tag">

         </p>
         <p>
          Its almost time for back to school and these alphabet activities will come in handy!
         </p>
         <p>
          As a preschooler, I dont really think a lot is expected for them to know. Some basics are good though, like
          <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/colors/" title="Colors Activities">
           colors
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/shapes/" title="Shapes Activities">
           shapes
          </a>
          ,
          <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/numbers/">
           numbers
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/abcs/" title="ABC Activities">
           letters
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          To refresh your preschoolers memory of letter recognition in general, as well as knowing the uppercase and lowercase alphabet and getting to know some of the letters sounds, Ive been collecting ideas for activities that Henry can do to get him back on track of school. (Check some more out my
          <a href="https://www.pinterest.com/handsonaswegrow/abcs-learning-more/">
           ABC Learning &amp; More
          </a>
          Pinterest Board.)
         </p>
         <p>
          Not to mention itll be great to get me back into school mode too!
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <form action="#">
           Getactivities forpreschoolers(andtoddlers) in your inbox, sign up for our free weekly newsletter and get the free weekly activities planner with it!
           <input class="planner" type="submit" value="Click Here"/>
          </form>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          As a non-teacher, I have never known the order of when kids are expected to learn something, or whats easiest for them to learn first. Living Montessori has all the answers according to the Montessori curriculum.
          <a href="http://livingmontessorinow.com/2010/09/07/in-what-order-should-you-introduce-letters-to-your-preschooler/">
           Including letter groups to learn together and the order.
          </a>
          Plus which is better to learn first, recognizing the letters or letter sounds.
         </p>
         <p>
          Lets get the kids learning!
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="50 Alphabet activities for preschoolers" class="alignnone wp-image-52267 size-large" height="650" src="http://handsonaswegrow.com/wp-content/uploads/50-alphabet-activities-for-preschoolers-20150222-9-433x650.jpg" title="50 Alphabet activities for preschoolers" width="433"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Here are 50 alphabet activities preschoolers.
         </p>
         <h1>
          RecognizingLetters of the Alphabet
         </h1>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          Identifying the letters if the alphabet can be a fun activity for preschoolers to learn while playing!
          <strong>
           25 Alphabet activities to recognize the letters of the alphabet.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Alphabet activities to recognize Letters" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9045" height="427" src="http://handsonaswegrow.com/wp-content/uploads/2011-05-31_2859.jpg" title="Alphabet activities to recognize Letters" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           An ABC Mat is super handy to have on hand when your child is learning the alphabet! Try an
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/matching-upper-lowercase-letters/" title="Matching Upper and Lowercase Letters on ABC Road">
            activeABC mat learning game
           </a>
           to get them really involved!
          </li>
          <li>
           By singing the ABCs the child can find his way through an
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/alphabet-maze-learning-activity/" title="Alphabet Maze Learning Activity">
            alphabet maze
           </a>
           ! Use vehicles or a ball, or even a doll to walk their way through the maze.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/learning-letters-lineup-squirt/" title="Learning Letters Lineup. Squirt!">
            Squirt the Letter.
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Make learning magical with
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/magic-letter-learning/" title="Magic Letter Learning!">
            magic letters
           </a>
           that the kids reveal as they paint over them.
          </li>
          <li>
           Do a
           <a href="http://www.littlehandsbigwork.com/2009/02/letter-search.html">
            letter search and find
           </a>
           and match the pieces back together. (Little Hands, Big Work)
          </li>
          <li>
           Make
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/i-spy-sensory-bag/" title="I Spy Sensory Bag for Sight Words &amp; Letters">
            a sensory bag to find the letters
           </a>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           Get creative and have her
           <a href="http://www.littlefamilyfun.com/2012/05/alphabet-monster-game.html">
            feed the monster letters
           </a>
           as you call them out. (Little Family Fun)
          </li>
          <li>
           Use
           <a href="http://activity-mom.com/2009/11/24/paper-towel-tube-letter-matching/">
            sticker letters to match to letters
           </a>
           that you write on a paper towel tube! (Activity Mom)
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.makeandtakes.com/learning-letters-with-pipe-cleaners">
            Make letters from pipe cleaners
           </a>
           . (Make and Takes)
          </li>
          <li>
           Have fun
           <a href="http://stayathomeeducator.com/abc-letter-stack-game/#">
            stacking letter tiles
           </a>
           ! The catch? Name the letter before you can add it to your tower! (Stay At Home Educator)
          </li>
          <li>
           Try a magic trick like Playdough to Plato does with a
           <a href="http://www.playdoughtoplato.com/abc-or-sight-word-cup-hunt/">
            ABC cup hunt
           </a>
           game! Which one is the pom pom under?
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.motherhoodonadime.com/kids/preschool-basics-trash-can-alphabet/">
            Find the letter and trash it!
           </a>
           A fun idea from Motherhood on a Dime.
          </li>
          <li>
           Playing House does a
           <a href="http://playinghouseinmaryland.blogspot.com/2011/02/abc-fun-letter-b.html">
            bean bag toss
           </a>
           into a letter tub that you call out.
          </li>
          <li>
           A twist on musical chairs,
           <a href="http://www.momto2poshlildivas.com/2012/04/play-to-learn-musical-alphabet-game-for.html">
            play musical alphabet
           </a>
           and identify the letter you stop on! Mom to 2 Posh Lil Divas can make this more difficult for older kids too!
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.firstpalette.com/Craft_themes/Alphabet_and_Numbers/Fishing_for_Letters/Fishing_for_Letters.html">
            Fish for Letters
           </a>
           from First Palette. Can you identify your catch?
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://a-heart4home.blogspot.com/2012/06/letter-i-spy-with-game-tiles.html">
            Letter I Spy
           </a>
           with Grown Up Board Games from A Heart for Home.
          </li>
          <li>
           Make these simple building blocks so your child can
           <a href="http://www.simplyrealmoms.com/posts/diy-letter-builders/">
            build a letter
           </a>
           and then tell you what it is! (Simple Real Moms)
          </li>
          <li>
           Write the
           <a href="http://www.toddlerapproved.com/2012/06/watering-alphabet-garden.html">
            alphabet on the sidewalk
           </a>
           and water the garden. (Toddler Approved)
          </li>
          <li>
           Magnetic letters matching from NutureStore
           <a href="http://nurturestore.co.uk/alphabet-games">
            http://nurturestore.co.uk/alphabet-games
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://frugalfun4boys.com/2011/02/01/alphabet-running-game/">
            Simply make it a race!
           </a>
           Lay out some letter cards and shout out a letter, have your child run as fast as they can to find it and bring it back to you. (Frugal Fun 4 Boys)
          </li>
          <li>
           A classic from my childhood. When in the
           <a href="http://teachmama.com/on-road-abc-hunt/">
            car and traveling, do an alphabet hunt
           </a>
           . (Teach Mama)
          </li>
          <li>
           Let the children
           <a href="http://www.letthechildrenplay.net/2011/07/typewriter-in-preschool-classroom.html">
            play on a typewriter
           </a>
           , or old computer keyboard. (Let the Children Play)
          </li>
          <li>
           NurtureStore reinforces alphabet identification by
           <a href="http://nurturestore.co.uk/teach-the-alphabet-with-dough">
            baking the alphabet
           </a>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           Another use for the ABC Mat is to
           <a href="http://learnersinbloom.blogspot.com/2012/06/15-activities-with-alphabet-puzzle-mat.html">
            play twister
           </a>
           ! (Learners in Bloom)
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.learning4kids.net/2011/08/16/alphabet-sponge-letters-bath-time-fun-2/">
            Homemade letter sponges
           </a>
           for the bath from Learning 4 Kids are a fun learning addition to bath time!
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Additionally, these products are excellent to have on hand when learning letters.

         <h2>
          LearningUppercase &amp; Lowercase Letters
         </h2>
         <p>
          Lowercase letters tend to be harder for preschoolers to identify as well as match up with their uppercase counterparts.
          <strong>
           9 alphabet activities to help distinguish between upper and lower case letters.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Alphabet activities to learning uppercase and lowercase letters" height="427" src="http://handsonaswegrow.com/wp-content/uploads/2011-11-10_1913.jpg" title="Alphabet activities to learning uppercase and lowercase letters" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Got on a
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/letter-number-scavenger-hunt/" title="Letter &amp; Number Scavenger Hunt">
            letter hunt and match
           </a>
           the found letters to a set of lowercase letters!
          </li>
          <li>
           Match uppercase and lowercase hearts using
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/abc-abc/">
            a free printable
           </a>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           Write
           <a href="http://www.icanteachmychild.com/clothespin-capital-lowercase-matching-activity/">
            lowercase letters on clothespins
           </a>
           and have your child match and clip them onto an uppercase letter thats printed (or written) out. (I Can Teach My Child)
          </li>
          <li>
           Bring out the
           <a href="https://accounts.google.com/ServiceLogin?service=blogger&amp;hl=en&amp;passive=1209600&amp;continue=http://www.blogger.com/blogin.g?blogspotURL%3Dhttp://erinphill.blogspot.com/2011/04/91365.html%26zx%3D1qr0qn39scvxq&amp;ltmpl=private">
            Easter Eggs
           </a>
           . Label each side with corresponding upper and lowercase letters! Can your child put the eggs back together? (The Hill Family)
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.notimeforflashcards.com/2012/04/learning-letters-on-the-go.html">
            Learn letters on the go
           </a>
           with two paper plates, one with lowercase, one with uppercase letters. No Time for Flash Cards has the how to.
          </li>
          <li>
           Make learning the ABCs a big event! Turn the floor into
           <a href="http://www.filthwizardry.com/2009/03/lowercase-and-uppercase-floor-alphabet.html">
            a mega doodle of letters
           </a>
           and then match corresponding letters to it! (Filth Wizardry)
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/sidewalk-learn-upper-lowercase-letters/" title="Learning Upper and Lowercase Letters on the Sidewalk">
            Match upper and lowercase letters
           </a>
           on the sidewalk.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.teachpreschool.org/2012/04/upper-and-lower-case-letters-in-the-salt-tray/">
            Practice writing
           </a>
           while also learning both upper and lowercase letters by using a tray of salt. (Teach Preschool)
          </li>
          <li>
           Play a game of
           <a href="http://teachmama.com/build-your-own-bingo-uppercase-and-lowercase-letters/">
            alphabet bingo
           </a>
           . (Teach Mama)
          </li>
         </ol>
         <h2>
          Letter Sound Activities
         </h2>
         <p>
          Many of the activities that are listed for recognizing the letters of the alphabet can also be adjusted for learning the sounds of the letters too!
          <strong>
           16 alphabet activities to work on the sounds of the letters!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Alphabet Activities to learn sounds Letters Make" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-9051" height="427" src="http://handsonaswegrow.com/wp-content/uploads/2012-03-15_5467.jpg" title="Alphabet Activities to learn sounds Letters Make" width="640"/>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Go on ahunt for toys that start with the letters and then got to punch through for a prize in our
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/letter-sound-punch-game/" title="Energetic Activity: Letter Sound Punch Game">
            letter sound punch game
           </a>
           .
          </li>
          <li>
           Make a game to
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/beginning-letter-sounds-sort-game/" title="Beginning Letter Sounds Sort Game">
            sort by beginning letter sounds.
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           An active way for the kids to learn their letters is simply with a ball and
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/preschooler-game-alphabet-ball/">
            shouting out words that start with a letter
           </a>
           ! The Pleasantest Thing gives us many variations of this game in her guest post!
          </li>
          <li>
           Take learning outside with an
           <a href="http://www.iammommahearmeroar.net/2012/03/alphabet-scavenger-hunt.html">
            sidewalk letter sound scavenger hunt
           </a>
           likeI Am Momma  Hear Me Roar.
          </li>
          <li>
           Another version of this would be to
           <a href="http://www.learnplayimagine.com/2012/05/letter-sounds-and-sight-word-spray.html">
            spray the letter
           </a>
           that makes the sound from Train Up a Child.
          </li>
          <li>
           Indoors, set out some paper and letters on them and have the kids
           <a href="http://www.athoughtfulplaceblog.com/2012/06/summer-saver-abc-scavenger-hunt.html">
            find as many toys as they can
           </a>
           that start with each letter. (A Thoughtful Place)
          </li>
          <li>
           Simple. Kids love to pretend to work. Give them tees and a hammer and
           <a href="http://happybrownhouse.com/2012/06/20/pound-the-sound-a-sensory-sound-game-for-preschoolers/">
            pound the sound
           </a>
           idea from Happy Brown House.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add letters to muffin tins and toss a small object. Where it lands the child has to tell you what
           <a href="http://www.abcand123learning.com/2012/06/letter-toss.html">
            sound that letter makes
           </a>
           . This ideas found at ABC &amp; Learning by Playdough to Plato.
          </li>
          <li>
           Do
           <a href="http://handsonaswegrow.com/beginning-letter-sounds-hunt-toy-wash/" title="A Beginning Letter Sounds Hunt for Toys to Wash">
            a beginning letter sound toy wash!
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           Climb
           <a href="http://amomwithalessonplan.com/letter-of-the-week-week-letter-sound-games/">
            up the stairs when you get the right letter sound
           </a>
           from A Mom with a Lesson Plan.
          </li>
          <li>
           Make a
           <a href="http://carrotsareorange.com/letter-c-and-letter-k-sound-map/">
            collage from magazine cutouts
           </a>
           for letter sounds. (Carrots are Orange)
          </li>
          <li>
           Get active with a
           <a href="http://theimaginationtree.com/2012/06/noisy-letter-jump-phonics-game.html">
            letter sound jumping game
           </a>
           from The Imagination Tree.
          </li>
          <li>
           A
           <a href="http://www.havingfunathome.com/2010/04/run-n-spell.html">
            Run N Spell game
           </a>
           from Having Fun At Home gets the kids moving and learning starting letter sounds.
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://notjustcute.com/2012/07/30/baking-the-abcs-a-fun-alphabet-game-for-hot-summer-days/">
            Baking the Alphabet
           </a>
           on a hot day from Not Just Cute. Hungry for a banana? Fill up the letter b-b-b-B!
          </li>
          <li>
           <a href="http://www.toddlerapproved.com/2011/01/alphabet-bowling.html">
            Alphabet bowling
           </a>
           with letter pins from Toddler Approved
          </li>
          <li>
           Use
           <a href="http://drjeanandfriends.blogspot.com/2011/10/letter-pops.html">
            letter pops
           </a>
           (sticks with a letter on the end) to have the kids identify objects from around the room or house that start with the same letter.(Dr. Jean &amp; Friends)
          </li>
         </ol>
