            <h2 style="text-align: left;">
             <span style="color: #3a4759;">
              <img alt="Sport-Systems-Knowledge-base" src="//cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/319534/file-641070022-png/SSCI_knowledge_base_logog.png?t=1436873730680&amp;width=190" style="width: 190px;" title="Sport-Systems-Knowledge-base" width="190"/>

            </h2>
            <div>
             <img alt="gym-mats-and-padding-faqs" src="//cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/319534/file-702904124-png/gym-mats-and-padding-101.png?t=1436873730680&amp;width=400" style="width: 400px; display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="gym-mats-and-padding-faqs" width="400"/>
            </div>
            <h3>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
              How Gym Mats &amp; Padding 101 Works
             </span>
            </h3>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
              For the past 35 years, we at Sport Systems have dedicated ourselves to designing, manufacturing and installing industry-leading athletic infrastructure and equipment. Over time, we have fielded thousands of questions from our wonderful clientele and have found that in order to find the proper system, we typically have to answer the same core group of questions.
             </span>
            </h4>
            <h4>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
              Here they are along with some other helpful information:
             </span>
            </h4>
            <h2>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
              Wall Padding
              <br/>
             </span>
            </h2>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #374a66;">
              <strong>
               <span style="color: #374a66;">
                What Gym Mats do I use for Removable Wall Padding?
               </span>
              </strong>
             </span>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
              We use the Varsity style mats for any removable wall padding application. They feature the lightest foam which in turn allows these mats to hang much more effectively on the installed Velcro...
              <a href="http://blog.sportsystemscanada.com/blog/choosing-wall-padding-for-your-athletic-facility" target="_blank">
               Click to Read More
              </a>
              <br/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #374a66;">
              <strong>
               <span style="color: #374a66;">
                What areas of my gym require wall padding?
               </span>
              </strong>
             </span>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #080808;">
              <span class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text" id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body">
               <span style="color: #080808;">
                <span style="font-size: 16px;">
                </span>
                The amount of
                <a href="http://www.sportsystemscanada.com/en/products/custom-and-wall-padding" target="_blank">
                 wall padding
                </a>
                a gymnasium needs has long been a topic of debate among facility operators and managers. The difficulty in assessing the right answer to this question lies in the fact that there is no regulating body on the matter, and that it can end up being very dependent on the unique needs of any particular gymnasium...
                <a href="http://blog.sportsystemscanada.com/blog/which-areas-of-your-gymnasium-require-wall-mats" target="_blank">
                 Click to Read More
                </a>
                <br/>
               </span>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #374a66;">
              <strong>
               What is involved in the installation of Wall Padding?
               <br/>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #050505;">
              A basic wall padding installation requires only a drill and some drywall screws. If the wall pads are being installed into concrete, brick or cement, we would recommend using Tapcon screws to secure them. The installation can usually be handled by your maintenance department or by any general contractor.
              <br/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #374a66;">
              <strong>
               How much Wall Padding do I need behind my facility's Basketball Goals?
               <br/>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #374a66;">
              <span style="color: #080808;">
               While there is no regulatory body on this, we do recommend that which the OPHEA authority states; allowing for a minimum of 16' of width and 6' of height for safety padding behind each basketball net, and not measuring more than 4" from the floor.
              </span>
              <br/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #374a66;">
              <span style="color: #374a66;">
               <strong>
                What is the standard height for Safety Wall Padding?
                <br/>
               </strong>
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #374a66;">
