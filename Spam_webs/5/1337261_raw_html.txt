       <p>
        Director
        <a href="http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0088955/">
         Neill Blomkamp,
        </a>
        best known for the dystopian masterpiece "
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002SJIO5E/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B002SJIO5E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boingboing06-20&amp;linkId=ETPL3AEXWQMN7LMC">
         District 9
        </a>
        ", was apparently set to be part of a new film project in the world of Ridley Scott's
        <em>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00498VS1E/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00498VS1E&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=boingboing06-20&amp;linkId=VLVDTXFAKSQ6FJJX">

         </a>

       </p>
       <p>
        Was working on this,
        <a href="http://instagram.com/p/xVUvaEKhEq/">
         he wrote
        </a>
        . Don't think I am anymore. Love it though. The Instagrammed images include Ellen Ripley (Sigourney Weaver)  and  Corporal Dwayne Hicks (Michael Biehn).
       </p>
       <p>
        "Woulda rocked,"
        <a href="http://instagram.com/p/xVV4e2qhHK/">
         Blomkamp said
        </a>
        . "Was a mental stroll into the world Ridley Scott created."
       </p>
       <p>
        Blomkamp's take on the Xenomorph looks awesome, faithful, but different. Below, some of the images he shared, which include work by artist Geoffroy Thoorens.
       </p>
       <p>
        It'll be interesting to see what more, if anything, comes out about a project that presumably won't happen. What a loss for all fandom.
