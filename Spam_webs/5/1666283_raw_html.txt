          <div class="text_wrapper article-blurb">
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.autoguide.com/auto-news/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/Mazda-Romeo.jpg">
            </a>
            <strong>
             Its no secret that Mazda and Alfa-Romeo are collaborating on a new roadster, and AutoGuides spy photographers have managed to snap a few pictures of an engineering mule undergoing rigorous testing.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <span id="more-219543">
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
            This upcoming car ought to deliver the driving fun and reliability of a Miata with the style and passion of an Italian exotic.
            <strong>
            </strong>
            And here she is, in all of her crudity, complete with miss-fitting panels and other curious add-ons. This tester of the Mazda-Romeo/MX-
            <i>
             Cinque
            </i>
            /MiAlfa is clearly based on the
            <a href="http://www.autoguide.com/new-cars/2014/mazda/mx-5-miata/index.html">
             MX-5
            </a>
            but it seems longer and wider than todays car with a greater dash-to-axle ratio, which should result in more interior space. There are certainly numerous other changes in places you cant see.
           </p>
           <p>
            The shared architecture on which these roadsters will be built is rear-wheel drive. Each car is expected to feature unique design, which is not unexpected, though they will still have those classic roadster proportions meaning a long hood and short rear end.
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <strong>
             SEE ALSO:
             <a href="http://www.autoguide.com/auto-news/2013/01/mazda-fiat-finalize-deal-to-produce-alfa-romeo-roadster.html">
              Mazda and Alfa-Romeo Finalize Partnership
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            The powertrain(s) that will motivate this fun-loving duet is still unknown at this point, but the two companies could end up sharing some of Mazdas Skyactiv technology, meaning theyll be efficient and enjoyable.
           </p>
           <p>
            Its widely expected that these vehicles will enter production sometime in 2015 and will be built in Hiroshima, Japan. The
            <a href="http://www.autoguide.com/new-cars/mazda/index.html">
             Mazda
            </a>
            version of the car should hit the market first, followed by the Alfa six months or a year later.
           </p>
