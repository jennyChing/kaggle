         Lifecycle of a Deleted File Infographic
        </h1>
       </header>
      </div>
      <div class="section content">
       <section>
        <p>
         As more and more of our lives become digitally formatted, deleting files is just as important as creating them. You might be using your PC for filing tax documents, storing credit reports, maintaining medical information or a host of other documents that could contain sensitive information that needs to be kept secure. If youre finished with a document and want to delete it, you need to make sure that its truly gone from your system. Likewise, if youre looking to sell, donate or recycle a used PC, you want to know that every trace of your personal data has been thoroughly deleted.
        </p>
        <p>
         Simply moving a document to the recycle bin (deleting) is only the first step. Even if you follow up by emptying the recycle bin, recovery programs are still able to scan the hard drive and piece back purged files. There are three effective methods for securely deleting your files:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1. Secure deletion: Specific software can ensure that it becomes impossible to recover files by overwriting them between three and 35 times each.
         </li>
         <li>
          2. Degaussing: This method alters a hard drives magnetic field, which erases the firmware and renders it useless. This way, any data contained within is scrambled and can no longer be recovered.
         </li>
         <li>
          3. Physical destruction: You can destroy a hard drive with a sledgehammer, drill, grinder or industrial shredder. If the hard drive can no longer be used in any physical way, then files cannot be restored. However, dont attempt to destroy any equipment by using acid, fire, microwave or thermite. Doing so can release toxic elements.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Software like AOL Computer Checkup can permanently delete files securely and quickly using various combinations of the above methods. Refer to the infographic below for more detail about how these methods work and the amounts of time they take. There is no other way to ensure that your personal information remains protected besides securely deleting it from your hard drive; this is 100% necessary if theres a possibility that anyones eyes other than your own might have access to your PC. Even if youre simply having routine computer maintenance performed, you should use file-specific data removal to protect your most important assets from prying eyes. Also, be sure to use it any time you have your hard drive replaced at a service center, or if you plan to sell, recycle or donate a computer.
        </p>
