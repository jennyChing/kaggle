       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         The Importance of Doggy Dental Care
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-02T22:59:32+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          June 2, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://kellysthoughtsonthings.com/author/ronnkelly3/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Kelly R
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://kellysthoughtsonthings.com/importance-doggy-dental-care/#respond">
           Leave a Comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          If your canine companion has stinky breath, it may be time to invest in a good pet care regime to ensure that they remain in tip top condition. While we love our pampered pooches, but their breath can be less than desirable. Ensuring that your pup has a good dental regime in place will result in a number of health benefits. Your dogs dental hygiene should be as important as their diet and regular exercise. Making sure that your dog has a good dental regime in place is imperative to their health:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
            <a href="http://i1.wp.com/kellysthoughtsonthings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/FireShot-Screen-Capture-057-The-Importance-of-Doggy-Dental-Care-Google-Docs-docs_google_com_document_d_11pipC4EnbX7JfBlDxU-7dj1KhKxOVt1XZBbHxjmpac0_edit.png">
             <img alt="FireShot Screen Capture #057 - 'The Importance of Doggy Dental Care - Google Docs' - docs_google_com_document_d_11pipC4EnbX7JfBlDxU-7dj1KhKxOVt1XZBbHxjmpac0_edit" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-24747" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/kellysthoughtsonthings.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/FireShot-Screen-Capture-057-The-Importance-of-Doggy-Dental-Care-Google-Docs-docs_google_com_document_d_11pipC4EnbX7JfBlDxU-7dj1KhKxOVt1XZBbHxjmpac0_edit.png?resize=619%2C517"/>
            </a>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/phyrephox/" rel="nofollow">
           Source
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <b>
           Inspect Those Gums:
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          If you want your pups breath to be minty fresh, you will need to start inspecting their gums on a regular basis. Check for any signs that may indicate potential problems. Check their teeth, lips and tongue to ensure that they are healthy. If there is anything unusual, or suspect, invest in
          <a href="http://www.365vet.co.uk/" rel="nofollow">
           pet medications
          </a>
          specifically aimed at dental hygiene. You should take your dog to the vet at least twice per year to ensure that your pooch is fit and well, so do ask that they inspect their teeth during their check up.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <b>
           What to Look Out For:
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Watch out for the following signs:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Halitosis
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Reluctance to chew
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Pain when chewing
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Crying or whimpering while eating
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Red puffy gum
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Tartar build up
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Loose teeth
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <b>
           The Dangers of Poor Dental Hygiene:
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          As plaque builds on the teeth, the affected areas will be more prone to bacteria. As the bacteria builds in the mouth, other vital organs will be affected too, such as the heart and kidneys. This will result in your pooch experiencing a lot of pain. The simplest way to avoid this is to address any dental issues immediately and ensure that you prevent dental problems from occurring by investing in doggy dental
          <a href="http://www.dogstrust.org.uk/az/d/dentalcare/#.U4xb2vldWSo" rel="nofollow">
           products
          </a>
          .
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <b>
           Keeping Teeth Healthy:
          </b>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          There are a number of things that you can do to make sure that your dog has healthy teeth and gums. Implementing a strict dental regime is important. You can purchase doggy toothbrushes and toothpaste to keep their teeth pearly white. However, do make sure that you purchase specially designed canine products, as human toothpaste can make dogs ill.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          If your dog is reluctant to have his teeth brushed, you can invest in oral washes to ensure that your beloved hounds teeth remain in good condition. There is also a plethora of dental treats available to ensure that your puppys dental hygiene is as it should be. If only it was this easy to get the kids to brush their
          <a href="http://dogs.about.com/od/dogandpuppyhealth/qt/dentalcare.htm" rel="nofollow">
           teeth
          </a>
          !
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          <strong>
           <strong>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
          Of course, your dog is your best friend, so you may feel a little guilty about taking him to the vet. It is important that you take your pooch to the vet for a thorough dental clean once per year. Professional dental cleaning will ensure that your dogs dental hygiene is as it should be. This usually involves a full scale and polish, as well as tooth extraction for loose teeth. Follow your vets recommendations and ensure that your beloved pooch is healthy and happy.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
