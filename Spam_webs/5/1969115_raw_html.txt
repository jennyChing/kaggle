            So we have an HSA with a local bank. It's a standard savings account as far as the bank is concerned.
           </p>

               <p class="post-signature">

                 Knightly Computing
                </a>
                is an IT service provider.
               </p>
               <div class="post-body">
                <p>
                 Just sent thisto my bank using their contact form. We'll see if they get back to me. What do you think?
                </p>
                <p>
                 "Your online banking portal has a severe security flaw in that it has access to and sends the users password via email (to the user) over an unencrypted connection when the user uses the "Forgot Password" form. This is unacceptable! Your system should not be storing passwords in such a way that it can be retrieved and most definitely should not be sending it to the user via email (which is unencrypted).
                 <br/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span>
                  Here is an excerpt taken from an FDIC document titled "FIL-69-2001: Authentication In An Electronic Banking Environment".
                 </span>
                 <a href="http://www.fdic.gov/news/news/financial/2001/fil0169a.html" rel="nofollow" title="Link: http://www.fdic.gov/news/news/financial/2001/fil0169a.html">
                  http://www.fdic.gov/news/news/financial/2001/fil0169a.html
                 </a>
                 <br/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span>
                  The last sentence is most important.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span>
                  "Password secrecy. The security provided by password-only systems depends on the password being kept secret. If another party obtains the password, he or she can perform the same transactions as the intended user. Passwords can be compromised because of customer behavior or techniques that capture passwords as they travel over the Internet. Attackers can also use well-known weaknesses to gain access to a financial institution's (or its service providers) Internet-connected systems and obtain password files. Because of these vulnerabilities, passwords and password files should be encrypted when stored or transmitted over open networks such as the Internet.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span>
                  Financial institutions need to emphasize to customers the importance of protecting the password's confidentiality, cautioning customers against writing down passwords and preventing others from observing the entry of their passwords. Customers should log-off unattended computers that have been used to access on-line banking systems and invoke password protection over their screen savers. Passwords should be encrypted wherever stored or transmitted over open systems such as the Internet, and the system should prohibit any user, including the system or security administrator, from printing or viewing unencrypted passwords."
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span>
                  I would like to be contacted regarding this breach in security and informed as to when this will be fixed. I have been a happy customer with your bank thus far and would like to give you a chance to correct this before my business is taken elsewhere.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span>
                  Thanks for your time on this matter."
                 </span>
                 <br/>
