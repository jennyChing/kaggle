          <p>
           Keeping your teeth and gums healthy isnt just a question of vanity. Sure, fresh breath and a sexy smile are great social assets. But oral health also has a lot to do with overall wellness.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.mayoclinic.org/healthy-living/adult-health/in-depth/dental/art-20047475?pg=2" target="_blank">
            According to the Mayo Clinic
           </a>
           , dental disease may be linked to health issues such as premature birth and low birth weight, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, endocarditis (infection of the inner lining of the heart), osteoporosis and even Alzheimers.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <a class="solutions-center-inline" href="/solutions/">
             <i class="fa fa-star fa-fw">
             </i>
             Find help for common financial problems in our Solutions Center!
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           A twice-annual cleaning and exam can help you stay healthy. But dental care doesnt have to break the bank.
           <a href="http://www.moneytalksnews.com/author/stacy/" target="_blank">
            Money Talks News founder Stacy Johnson
           </a>
           explains the tricks of the trade some dentists use to get you to spend more than you need to. Check out thevideo tips, then keep reading to learn more about how to reduce the cost.
          </p>
          <div class="video-container">
           <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="559" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/bPWkU4l2bzk?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="940">
           </iframe>

          <p>
           Dentists often advertise some super deals through social buying sites such as Groupon and LivingSocial, local shopper newspapers and even the famous blue envelope from Valpak. While living in Seattle, I redeemed a Valpak coupon: $29 for cleaning, X-rays and a free teeth-whitening kit custom-made for me in the office.
          </p>
          <p>
           Read such deals carefully, especially when it comes to expiration dates. If a voucher expires, you can still redeem it for the amount paid but you wont get the same price on the deal. Suppose you spent $29 on a Groupon deal for cleaning and X-rays but forgot to book the appointment in time. You can still apply the $29 toward those services, but itll cost a
           <em>
            lot
           </em>
           more than $29.
          </p>
          <p>
           Remember, too, that deep discounts, whether through social buying networks or a coupon in a local paper, are generally for new customers. You wont get nearly as good a price on future visits.
          </p>
          <p>
           Also keep in mind that this office might not be the most cost-effective game in town for later work. If the exam turns up an issue, write down the specific treatment needed and the total cost. Say youll get back to them regarding future appointments.
          </p>
          <p>
           Next, use a list of average dental costs
           <a href="http://pressroom.consumerreports.org/pressroom/2012/01/what-you-can-expect-to-pay-for-a-dental-procedure-whether-youre-insured-or-not-yonkers-ny-the-biggest-reason-for-dela.html" target="_blank">
            compiled by Consumer Reports
           </a>
           to see if the charges seem reasonable. If not, ask friends and co-workers which dentists they use and call to compare fees for the same service.
          </p>

          <h3>
           <strong>
            Boosting the bottom line
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           About that free teeth-whitening kit: Apparently the dentist would have made his money back by selling me additional bleaching solution. Fact is, I used it for a while and then lost interest, so I never had to buy replacements.
          </p>
          <p>
           Teeth whitening is just one of the unnecessary treatments offered at many offices.
           <a href="http://pressroom.consumerreports.org/pressroom/2012/01/what-you-can-expect-to-pay-for-a-dental-procedure-whether-youre-insured-or-not-yonkers-ny-the-biggest-reason-for-dela.html" target="_blank">
            Nancy Metcalf of Consumer Reports said
           </a>
           , Many dentists are trained to push for procedures, both cosmetic and non-cosmetic, that will boost their bottom line.
          </p>
          <p>
           We were struck by the finding that nearly a half of those [readers surveyed] who had had a cosmetic procedure had been prompted to do so by their dentist, Metcalf says.
          </p>
          <p>
           Among the procedures that could be unnecessary are teeth whitening, amalgam filling replacement, X-rays (more on those in a minute), precautionary removal of wisdom teeth and temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder surgery.
          </p>
          <p>
           Regarding X-rays: Most adults dont need them at every visit.
           <a href="http://www.webmd.com/oral-health/dental-x-rays?page=3" target="_blank">
            According to WebMD.com
           </a>
           , you might need them only every two to three years depending on your age and the current state of your mouth. If your dentist wants X-rays at every visit, ask why.
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            Taking a second look
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           Suppose the dentist says you need deep treatment for serious gum disease plus a crown to fix serious upper-molar decay. Youll probably deny it, wondering just how your teeth could have gotten that bad even though its been a year since your last checkup.
          </p>
