       <p class="author">
        Posted by the Mass Office of Travel and Tourism
       </p>
       <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
       <p>
        In March, the Huffington Post published an
        <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/03/19/western-mass-food_n_6895330.html" target="_blank">
         article
        </a>
        sharing 15 reasons why western Massachusetts deserves some serious credibility when it comes to food.
       </p>
       <p>
        For any locals worth their salt, and for visitors, too, this doesnt come as breaking news, given the
        <a href="http://visithampshirecounty.com/eat/restaurants/" target="_blank">
         variety
        </a>
        of
        <a href="http://berkshires.org/business_category/food-wine/" target="_blank">
         excellent
        </a>
        <a href="http://www.mohawktrail.com/dining.html" target="_blank">
         ways
        </a>
        to experience the food scene in the Pioneer Valley and the Berkshires.
       </p>
       <p>
        No doubt, Western Mass is getting a lot more acknowledgement of late as a culinary powerhouse. But still not as much as it deserves.
       </p>
       <p>
        Here are 7 reasons why.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Atkins-Farm-Doughnuts.jpg">
         <img alt="Atkins Farm Doughnuts" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7514" height="2048" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Atkins-Farm-Doughnuts.jpg" width="1365"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Doughnuts from Atkins Farm are pretty much reason enough by themselves
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1. Atkins Farm in Amherst, MA
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.atkinsfarms.com/" target="_blank">
         Atkins Farm
        </a>
        , which got a nice mention in the March article, is pretty much everything thats great about local farms.
        <a href="http://www.atkinsfarms.com/departments/freshproduce/" target="_blank">
         Fresh produce
        </a>
        is just the beginning, and that great start is complemented by a
        <a href="http://www.atkinsfarms.com/departments/bakery/" target="_blank">
         bakery
        </a>
        , a
        <a href="http://www.atkinsfarms.com/departments/deli/" target="_blank">
         deli
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.atkinsfarms.com/departments/cheese/" target="_blank">
         cheese
        </a>
        and much more, including
        <a href="http://www.atkinsfarms.com/departments/beerandwine/" target="_blank">
         beer and wine
        </a>
        . The farm is available for special events, and during the fall, theres also apple picking. For those who are unfortunately too far from Atkins farm to stop in, theres always the
        <a href="http://www.atkinsfarms.com/onlinestore/" target="_blank">
         online store
        </a>
        , too.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Franklin County CiderDays in Franklin County
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        What started as an idea around 20 years ago is now a tradition, and theres no mystery as to why.
        <a href="http://www.ciderdays.org/" target="_blank">
         Franklin County CiderDays
        </a>
        is an annual November celebration of cider (both fresh and hard) in western Mass.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Franklin-County-CiderDays.jpg">
         <img alt="Franklin County CiderDays" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7515" height="1363" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Franklin-County-CiderDays.jpg" width="2048"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         An absolute prerequisite to attending CiderDays
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Along with tastings, farm tours and classes, guests can sample some delectable fare from the stable of local farms over the course of a weekend. Oh, and the fact that CiderDays takes place in western Mass during autumn definitely doesnt hurt either, because the scenery is gorgeous to boot.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3. Northampton Farmers Market in Northampton, MA
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        The
        <a href="http://www.amherstfarmersmarket.com/" target="_blank">
         Amherst Farmers Market
        </a>
        is always worth checking out, but just a town over, Northampton boasts a very fine selection of its own. The
        <a href="http://www.farmfresh.org/food/farmersmarkets_details.php?market=122" target="_blank">
         Northampton Saturday Market
        </a>
        runs from around the beginning of spring to the onset of November, supplying passersby with grass-fed beef, lamb, dairy products, medicinal herbs and all the produce imaginable, to name just a few offerings. During the chillier months, theres the
        <a href="http://www.nohowintermarket.com/" target="_blank">
         Noho Winter Market
        </a>
        , which keeps everyone well fed until longer, sunnier days return.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Northampton-Farmers-Market.jpg">
         <img alt="Northampton Farmers Market" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7516" height="1365" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Northampton-Farmers-Market.jpg" width="2048"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Fresh Maple Syrup at the Northampton Farmers Market from Justamere Tree Farm by Lynne Graves
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         4.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Berkshire Farm &amp; Table
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.berkshirefarmandtable.org/" target="_blank">
         Berkshire Farm &amp; Table
        </a>
        bills itself as having unique and delicious culinary collaborations, and anyone whos experienced one would be hard-pressed to disagree. For a truly memorable dining experience, try
        <a href="http://www.berkshirefarmandtable.org/outstanding-in-the-field-2/" target="_blank">
         Outstanding in the Field
        </a>
        ; it gives diners the chance to eat meals from top regional chefs outdoors, as a way of drawing attention and appreciation to the local farms that source the ingredients.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Outstanding-in-the-Field.jpg">
         <img alt="Outstanding in the Field" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7517" height="365" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Outstanding-in-the-Field.jpg" width="660"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Sitting down at Outstanding in the Field by Angela Cardinali
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Outstanding in the Field tickets can be mighty hard to come by, though, as theyre very sought-after items and the event takes place there just once per year. Fortunately, Berkshire Farm &amp; Table has also created three
        <a href="http://www.berkshirefarmandtable.org/taste-trails/" target="_blank">
         Taste Trails
        </a>
        (one for beer and cider, one for cheese, another for charcuterie), which will treat you well year round.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         The Red Lion Inn in Stockbridge, MA
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Any discussion of
        <em>
         the
        </em>
        top restaurant in the Berkshires tends to spark a lively debate (
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/blog/2013/11/10-tasty-farm-to-table-restaurants-in-western-mass/" target="_blank">
         Exhibit A
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/blog/2014/01/8-outstanding-farm-to-table-restaurants-in-western-ma/" target="_blank">
         Exhibit B
        </a>
        ), simply because theres so much thats so good to choose from.
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/business/4120/" target="_blank">
         The Red Lion
        </a>
        is well known as a historic inn, with more than two centuries of hospitality to its name, and its
        <a href="http://www.redlioninn.com/berkshire-dining/?__utma=155751224.89570392.1428690726.1428690726.1428690726.1&amp;__utmb=155751224.2.10.1428690726&amp;__utmc=155751224&amp;__utmx=-&amp;__utmz=155751224.1428690726.1.1.utmcsr=google%7Cutmccn=(organic)%7Cutmcmd=organic%7Cutmctr=(not%20provided)&amp;__utmv=-&amp;__utmk=121879627" target="_blank">
         restaurant
        </a>
        has become nearly as famous in its own right.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Red-Lion-Inn-Stockbridge.jpg">
         <img alt="Red Lion Inn Stockbridge" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7518" height="1363" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Red-Lion-Inn-Stockbridge.jpg" width="2048"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Out front at the Red Lion Inn
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Executive Chef Brian Alberg keeps the best kind of contemporary cuisine on the menu, and is also an avid supporter of the local food movement, making the Red Lion an excellent destination for travelers who like good food and good accommodations. The inn is located on Main Street as well, making it ideally situated for exploring around town.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         6.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         North County Eats Restaurant Week in the northern Berkshires
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Winter in New England can be unforgiving at times, but
        <a href="http://northcountyeats.com/" target="_blank">
         North County Eats
        </a>
        gives Berkshires residents and visitors something to look forward to during the heart of it. Dishes from restaurants in North Adams and Williamstown are featured at prix fixe prices, which means some of the best meals around can be had for a tidy discount. The North County Eats website breaks down the choices by price, meal and cuisine as well, making it easy for diners to plot their next move.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         7.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Herrells Ice Cream in Northampton, MA
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/business/6666/" target="_blank">
         Herrells Ice Cream
        </a>
        is a Massachusetts institution in the best sort of way. Started in the early 70s, the ice creamery now stocks more than two hundred flavors, ranging from classics like vanilla and chocolate to more creative pursuits, such as Jalapeno and Cheesecake Cream.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Herrells-Ice-Cream.jpg">
         <img alt="Herrell's Ice Cream" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-7519" height="900" src="/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Herrells-Ice-Cream.jpg" width="1347"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <em>
         Serving up something good at Herrells Ice Cream
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Herrells frozen treats can be found at their Northampton flagship store,
        <a href="http://www.herrells.com/index.php?option=com_content&amp;view=article&amp;id=54&amp;Itemid=86" target="_blank">
         as well as
        </a>
        Williamstown, Deerfield and Shelburne Falls. The hot fudge sauce, which is made from scratch, is also sold in online and in stores, giving guests something to bring back with them after theyre done wolfing down the ice cream.
       </p>
       <p>
        Given all the great food that there is western Massachusetts, this is only a sample of it all. To learn more about the culinary scene in western MA, click
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/eat/" target="_blank">
         here
        </a>
        . You can also find plenty to do in between meals
        <a href="http://www.massvacation.com/explore/" target="_blank">
         here
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Do you have a favorite food spot in western Mass that more people should know about? Tell us below in the comments!
       </p>
