         <h1 class="entry-title">
          The Ultimate Blogger Standing Desk
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="byline">
           by:
           <span class="author vcard">

         <h2>
          The Standing Desk Transition
         </h2>
         <p>
          Today, I share with you how
          <b>
           Im going from sitting to stylishly standing
          </b>
          . Were going to talk about my experiment with an adjustable standing desk, why you need one and how awesome they are!
         </p>
         <p>
          But first, a little back story on why an adjustable standing desk is so important for my work/life style.
         </p>
         <p>
          I dont think Ive ever divulged this information but Im a workaholic. I love to work. Not busy bee work, but completing tasks and activities that add up to a larger picture, accomplishments. Reaching these milestones in projects drives me. Dont worry though, I wont die at my desk because Peter convinces me to set aside time to recharge, relax, and not do anything, which is odd because Peter enjoys working as well.
         </p>
         <p>
          I recently took the
          <a href="http://strengths.gallup.com/110440/About-StrengthsFinder-20.aspx" target="_blank" title="StrengthsFinder 2.0">
           Gallups Strengths Test
          </a>
          and I discovered that
          <i>
           focus
          </i>
          and
          <i>
           achiever
          </i>
          are two of my strengths. So basically, when Im very focused at achieving things I feel the most enjoyment because Im exercising my strengths. This ties directly into my work, both good and bad. Good because lots of things get done. Bad because I dont take a break. Its hard for me to pull away from the computer.
         </p>
         <p>
          Ive always been extremely focused on achieving my goals. In college, I always took the maximum units and was the type of student that even took summer classes to get more of my courses completed. I finished my bachelors, a minor and certificate in 4 years. Some people have told me that this was crazy and you might think its a bit too much, but its just me.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now, this focus is on my responsibilities as a blogger and digital agency President, which has me on my computer for majority of my day.
         </p>
         <p>
          Sometimes 10+ hours a day.
         </p>
         <p>
          For most, being on a computer generally means sitting. Sitting without getting up and taking a break can be very bad for your health. These prolonged periods of computer usage also attributes to the stress you may feel in your shoulders, like I do.
         </p>
         <p>
          So this brings me back to the emphasis of todays post. I want to share with you how Im addressing this sitting problem.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Problems with Sitting For Too Long
         </h3>
         <p>
          Sitting around for long periods of time is generally not good for your energy, health, and overall productivity. There has been plenty of research to show that
          <b>
           prolonged sitting can lead to many negative effects on our health.
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          So you may be wondering whats so bad about sitting?
         </p>
         <p>
          If your day has you sitting for 6 hours or longer your risk of dying of heart disease increases by 64%. Your overall risk of prostate or breast cancer increases 30%. You have an increased risk of obesity and type 2 diabetes. If the extended sitting continues youre shaving off valuable years of your life. (
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/04/17/magazine/mag-17sitting-t.html?_r=0" target="_blank" title="NYTimes">
           Source
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          This is scary stuff.
         </p>
         <p>
          When you sit your body uses very little energy, the electrical activity in your muscles flat line. Youre basically powering down your body. Your body no longer produces lipoprotein lipase and other molecules that are released when youre up and walking around. Youre heart rate, levels of good cholesterol, and calorie burn all drop. When these vital molecules drop your body suffers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Not to mention, slouching in your chairs, decreased interested and focus.
         </p>
         <p>
          So how can all of these terrible consequences from sitting in a chair be stopped? Switch to a standing desk or, what I prefer, an adjustable standing desk.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Positive Impacts of a Standing Desk
         </h3>
         <p>
          Besides the reduced risk of obesity, diabetes, cancer, and cardiovascular disease there are also some other positive side effects.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           More energy!
          </b>
          You cant get lazy at your desk while youre standing! Youre mind is more alert, your body is moving and your blood flowing. Also, with your muscles engaged youre more alert.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Your posture will improve
          </b>
          since your back muscles are engaged. Your shoulders will be less tense (after 2 weeks mine are) and since you cant slouch while standing youll have terrific posture!
         </p>
         <p>
          Since youll have
          <b>
           increased focus
          </b>
          on your work youll also become more productive.
         </p>
         <h3>
          The Awesome and Easily Adjustable Vertdesk
         </h3>
         <p>
          Let me state this clearly,
          <b>
           Im not saying you need to stand all day.
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          That is hard, I know. While I was in high school I took my first summer job at a local pharmacy and I stood for over 8 hours. I was dead tired by the end of the day. Like sitting, you shouldnt stand all day. Making the transition to a full-time standing desk is a challenge, your knees or feet will get fatigued.
         </p>
         <p>
          Therefore, you need to find your perfect middle ground of time spent standing vs sitting.
         </p>
         <p>
          Find the balance that is right for you. It takes some getting used to but youll find yourself getting more comfortable standing for longer and longer periods of time.
         </p>
         <p>
          To get the best of both worlds an adjustable standing desk is your best bet.
         </p>
         <p>
          I use the
          <a href="http://www.beyondtheofficedoor.com/vertdesk.php" target="_blank" title="VertDesk">
           VertDesk
          </a>
          Electric Adjustable Height Desk and love it. With a click of a button, I can stand in the morning and sit in the afternoon or vise-versa. This is how I started out and I recommend you do the same.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are a couple of things to keep-in-mind when searching and investing in a standing desk.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            I recommend an adjustable standing desk
           </strong>
           , youll then have the option to sit and stand whenever you want. With a push of a button its up or down! Its fantastic!
          </li>
          <li>
           For the
           <strong>
            best ergonomic positioning your arms need to be at 90 degrees
           </strong>
           . Adjust the desk so its perfect for your height. For example, check out my arm position in the standing and sitting photo.
          </li>
          <li>
           Consider getting a soft pad to stand on for extra cushioning so your feet wont hurt either. As you can see in the photos, I stand with my shoes off instead of my heels.
          </li>
          <li>
           Standing takes getting used to so dont give up,
           <strong>
            commit to the change
           </strong>
           . Getting an adjustable desk doesnt mean you should only stand for an hour and sit for the rest  dont cheat yourself.
          </li>
          <li>
           Just like investing in a quality handbag or essential pair of shoes.
           <strong>
            Invest in your standing desk!
           </strong>
           It is something youre going to use every work day! If you blog on your spare time and have a full-time job youll use it even more so! More than a handbag, a standing desk is an investment in yourself! In your health! So dont make some DIY makeshift thing that youll give up on. Take a stand for your health.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          I hope this has given you light into the dangers of sitting your life away.
         </p>
         <p>
          Sitting too much is a topic that we as active busy people can easily overlook because it doesnt feel like its
          <i>
           that
          </i>
          bad. But as it builds up over time and can have some serious effects on the quality of your life.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           I would love to hear from you! Have you thought about getting a standing desk, or the adjustable VertDesk?
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          Let me know if you have any questions  Im happy to answer any of them!
         </p>
         <p>
          Stand Stylishly,
          <br/>
          -V

          P.S.
          <em>
           Im not doctor, so make sure standing is right for you by consulting your primary care physician. Everyone is different.
