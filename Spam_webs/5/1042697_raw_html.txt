                     Bands I Want To See One More Time - GEORGIE JAMES
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1063013469092040192">
                     <div align="left">
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center">
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <b>
                        GEORGIE JAMES
                       </b>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <img src="http://therslweblog.readyhosting.com/photos/GeorgieJames-3.jpg"/>
                      <iframe frameborder="no" height="166" scrolling="no" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fapi.soundcloud.com%2Ftracks%2F27642664&amp;color=000000&amp;auto_play=false&amp;show_artwork=false" width="500">

                     <div align="left">
                      <br/>
                      <b>
                       If You Could See One Great Band just one more time, which would it be?
                      </b>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      I have several names to add to that list and that's the spirit behind this music series. There are so many great bands and some go far too quickly but blazed a white hot trail... Washington DC's
                      <b>
                       GEORGIE JAMES
                      </b>
                      was
                      <b>
                       John Davis
                      </b>

                      <a href="https://www.facebook.com/titletracksdc" target="_blank">

                      <a href="http://www.blogger.com/blogger.g?blogID=29345538" https:="" target="_blank" themynabirds="" www.facebook.com="">
                       The Mynabirds
                      </a>
                      ) and in 2007 they released one of the best pop records I have ever heard called
                      <i>
                       <b>
                        "Places"
                       </b>
                      </i>
                      under the name Georgie James. It was pretty much perfect and the music was just right. As luck would have it, they were one and done.
                      <br/>
                      <i>
                       <b>
                        <br/>
                       </b>
                      </i>
                      <i>
                       <b>
                        "Places"
                       </b>
                      </i>
                      is one of my favorite records ever. And one you should definitely own...
