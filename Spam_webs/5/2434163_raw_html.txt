                     <a href="http://arctida.blogspot.se/2015/01/diy-honey-lavender-salve-tutorial-how.html">
                      DIY Honey Lavender salve tutorial. How to make a healing ointment recipe
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                      <div class="post-share-buttons goog-inline-block">

                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7195194570514538046" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      I love using natural products for all of my personal care so I thought Ill share with you my favorite recipe of Honey salve with Lavender that Ive been making for my family for years.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.arctida.com/">
                        <img alt="http://www.arctida.com/" border="0" height="495" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-PNGMXpMLXkc/VMRyFaQOxII/AAAAAAAADBU/KxIDdytNhdg/s1600/Salva_honey_05.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Ingredients:
                      <br/>
                      Olive Oil ~ 2 oz (57 ml)
                      <br/>
                      Cocoa Butter ~ 0.5 oz (15 g/ 15 ml)
                      <br/>
                      Beeswax ~ 0.7 oz (19 g/20 ml)
                      <br/>
                      Honey, preferably raw honey ~ 1 tsp (5 ml)
                      <br/>
                      Vitamin E ~ 1/2 tsp (3 ml)
                      <br/>
                      Lavender essential oil (or your own choice) ~ 10-20 drops
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.arctida.com/">
                        <img alt="http://www.arctida.com/" border="0" height="536" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-MHbHagBXhb4/VMRyHiBUu2I/AAAAAAAADB8/Yq493g8_lXY/s1600/Salva_honey_01.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      1. Combine Olive oil, Cocoa butter and Beeswax into a mixing bowl and melt in a double boiler or in a microwave oven.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.arctida.com/">
                        <img alt="http://www.arctida.com/" border="0" height="554" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3tmUKfs6XXY/VMRyHricPpI/AAAAAAAADBg/khv9B_aZL14/s1600/Salva_honey_02.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      2. Allow salve to cool on the counter. When the mixture has cooled down to approximately 100 degrees Fahrenheit (40C degree Celsius) you can start adding Honey and Vitamin E. The essential oils should be last. Blend the whole mixture well with rubber spatula, spoon or with the electric hand mixer.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.arctida.com/">
                        <img alt="http://www.arctida.com/" border="0" height="564" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-KyDZFobvTFc/VMRyHiILigI/AAAAAAAADBc/_C5HYjkdN7g/s1600/Salva_honey_03.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       3. Transfer the finished salve into the smaller containers of your choice. Recipe yields approximately 100 ml (~ 3,5 oz) of product.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.arctida.com/">
                        <img alt="http://www.arctida.com/" border="0" height="544" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-TOc0PSwP-qs/VMRyIA_G-TI/AAAAAAAADBk/1zuYf6ycUVE/s1600/Salva_honey_04.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.arctida.com/">
                        <img alt="http://www.arctida.com/" border="0" height="494" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/--ri9Uk-zH7k/VMRyIYaD4MI/AAAAAAAADBo/d_ysVpJIFag/s1600/Salva_honey_05.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Notes:
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                       Honey is a known source of the bacteria spores that cause botulism. The spores are not nearly enough to hurt an adult and have never been known to cause harm by applying to wounds. Still, to be safe, dont use honey or honey salve on children under two years old unless the honey has been sterilized.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                       Heating raw honey past the maximum hive temperature of 95 degrees Fahrenheit changes honeys essential composition and degrades its quality. It partially destroys honeys beneficial enzymes and boils off volatile compounds that account for the unique, delicate floral aroma of the honey. If you wish to preserve as much of the honey properties as possible, wait until the mixture has cooled off to 95 degrees Fahrenheit (35C degree Celsius). Most commercial honey is already heated to 160-180 degrees Fahrenheit (70-80 C degree Celsius) so if you are using commercial honey, you can add honey right after melting your oils/butters/beeswax together.

I have tons of new projects in progress and in my sketchbook but I have not had the time to photograph any new pices yet. So instead I'm gonna show you the latest jewelry set that I've made for myself :)
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      The delicate free-formed focal was made by me using Fine Silver Precious Metal Clay (PMC) and decorated with Cubic Zirconia in the center. The chain has been hand-crafted using Rock Crystal Clear Quartz faceted round beads and Argentium sterling silver (High tarnish resistance). The matching earrings are simple high quality Rock Crystal Clear Quartz briolettes wire wrapped in  Argentium sterling silver.
