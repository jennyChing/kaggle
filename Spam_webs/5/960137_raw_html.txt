         <p>
          Reverse crunches are great for working the lower abdominal muscles and a part primodial of your
          <strong>
           <a href="http://exerciseforsixpackabs.com/">
            ab training exercises
           </a>
          </strong>
          to get a6 pack abs.
         </p>

         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          Steps to properlydoreverse crunches
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          (targeting the
          <a href="http://www.innerbody.com/anatomy/muscular/lower-torso-male">
           lower abdominal
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <strong>
           Ready, Set:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li style="text-align: left;">
           Lie on your back with your head and neck relaxed and your hands behind your ears (orarms outstretched to the sides).
          </li>
          <li style="text-align: left;">
           You want your lower body to form two right angles: Your thighs should be perpendicular to your upper body, with your lower legs parallel to the floor (A).
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong>
           GO:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Use your lower abs to raise your hips off the floor and toward your rib cage (B).
          </li>
          <li>
           Then, in a controlled motion, slowly lower your hips back to the starting position.
          </li>
          <li>
           As your hipslightly touch the floor, repeat (C). look over our program of
           <a href="http://exerciseforsixpackabs.com/exercise-for-six-pack-abs-6-week-program/">
            exercises that work the core
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          a video where we see how its done the reverse crunch:
         </p>

         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          ReverseCrunchPerformance Tips
         </h2>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Make sure your lower abs do the work. If you rock up and down, you are using momentum to aid you in the exercise, taking work away from your lower abs.
          </li>
          <li>
           Do not rest your hips on the floor at the bottom of the movement or let your lower legs drop down.
          </li>
          <li>
           Keep constant tension on your abs.
          </li>
          <li>
           Use your hands for balance, Do not use them to push off.
          </li>
          <li>
           Keep your head and neck relaxed.
          </li>
         </ul>
