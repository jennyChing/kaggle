      <h2>
       Red Velvet Blondies
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta">
       <em>
        posted by
       </em>
       Dorothy Kern
       <em>
        under
       </em>
       <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/brownies/" rel="category tag">
        brownies
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/holidays/valentines-day/" rel="category tag">
        valentine's day
       </a>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style ">
      <a class="addthis_button_tweet" tw:count="horizontal" tw:via="crazyforcrust">
      </a>
      <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:layout="button_count" style="margin-right:15px;">
      </a>
      <a class="addthis_button_stumbleupon_badge" style="margin-right:10px;">
      </a>
      <a href="javascript:void((function(){var%20e=document.createElement('script');e.setAttribute('type','text/javascript');e.setAttribute('charset','UTF-8');e.setAttribute('src','http://assets.pinterest.com/js/pinmarklet.js?r='+Math.random()*99999999);document.body.appendChild(e)})());">
       <img alt="Pin It" onclick="doPinIt();" src="/images/pinit.png" style="margin: 0 0 0 10px;"/>

     <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
     <p>
      Would you call them red velvet blondies or red velvet brownies? Im calling them blondies because (1) theyre not brownies,
      <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/2015/01/easy-double-fudge-brownies-amazing-frosting-ever/" target="_blank" title="Easy Double Fudge Brownies with the most amazing frosting ever.">
       these
      </a>
      are brownies and (2) I used my
      <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/2013/11/caramel-crunch-blondies/" target="_blank" title="Caramel Crunch Blondies {and a care package}">
       blondie
      </a>
      base.
     </p>
     <p>
      Its settled: theyre red velvet blondies.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Red Velvet Blondies" class="aligncenter wp-image-11041 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Red-Velvet-Blondies-6-of-11w.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Last week Jordan was home sick for a few days. This made me appreciate several things:
     </p>
     <p>
      1. Zicam. (Mine wasnt as bad as hers. Seriously, buy a vat of the stuff.)
     </p>
     <p>
      2. Headphones. (Because after 8 hours of Disney Channel all I want to do is say GO TURN ON NETFLIX WHERE I DONT HAVE TO HEAR IT.)
     </p>
     <p>
      3. Dance Moms. (See #2. And yes, #horribleparenting)
     </p>
     <p>
      4. Moms who homeschool. (We did school work one day, so she wouldnt get behind. OMG, am I supposed to know what an ecosystem is?
      <em>
       Why
      </em>
      , exactly?)
     </p>
     <p>
      5. A work-at-home dad. (So I could still exercise. Its the only thing that kept me sane.)
     </p>
     <p>
      6. Red Velvet Blondies. (Isnt the saying
      <em>
       feed a cold
      </em>
      ? Orsomething?)
     </p>
     <p>
      <span id="more-11038">
      </span>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Red Velvet Blondies - turn the best blondie recipe into red velvet!" class="aligncenter wp-image-11039 size-full" height="1617" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Red-Velvet-Blondies.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Red Velvet isnew box of chocolates for Valentines Day. Im not sure why; as far as I know red velvet has always traditionally been a southern holiday treat. But I like it for this time of year. January is bleak, dreary, and full of skinny this and low-fat that.
     </p>
     <p>
      February? Is about
      <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/2012/02/peanut-butter-football-dip/" target="_blank" title="Peanut Butter Football Dip">
       dip
      </a>
      and hearts. And all things
      <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/tag/red-velvet" target="_blank">
       red velvet
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Like blondies.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Red Velvet Blondies" class="aligncenter wp-image-11040 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Red-Velvet-Blondies-1-of-11.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      I just love how rich and fudgy these look. Thats what I love about my
      <a href="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/2014/10/butterscotch-blondie-gooey-bars/" target="_blank" title="Butterscotch Blondie Gooey Bars">
       blondie recipe
      </a>
      : how its moist in the center and crisp around the edgesjust like a blond brownie should be. I think that has something to do with 2 cups of brown sugar
      <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Who knew it would be so easy to turn them into
      <span style="color: #ff0000;">
       red velvet
      </span>
      ?
     </p>
     <p>
      So what makes something red velvet? Well, it kind of depends on what youre making. The hallmark of anything velvet is the addition of some
      <span style="color: #ff0000;">
       cocoa
      </span>
      , but not enough to make it chocolate. The entire pan of these blondies only have 1 tablespoon.
     </p>
     <p>
      Often youll see vinegar or buttermilk added to red velvet recipes, but I didnt add any to this one. I didnt want to undermine the way the recipe normally bakes: fudgy and perfect.
     </p>
     <p>
      And, well, obviously you need
      <span style="color: #ff0000;">
       red food coloring
      </span>
      to make red velvet. I used 2 tablespoons of dye for these. Dont bother buying the little 4-pack of squeeze bottles; there isnt enough red in that bottle. Buy the actual bottles of red food coloring instead. Then measure out 2 tablespoons. Carefully. Otherwise your kitchen will look like a crime scene. Ahem.
     </p>
     <p>
      These red velvet blondies are the perfect anytime cookie bar. Dont just eat these on Valentines DayId eat them any day!
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Red Velvet Blondies" class="aligncenter wp-image-11042 size-full" height="900" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Red-Velvet-Blondies-8-of-11.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Now, if only they taught history of red velvet in 4th grade. Then I wouldnt have had to frantically search up what a predicatewas.
      <em>
       Whats the point???
      </em>
     </p>
     <div class="recipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">

      <h2 itemprop="name">
       Red Velvet Blondies
      </h2>
      <div class="time">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Yield:
        </strong>
        <span itemprop="recipeYield">
         24 bars
        </span>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="summary" itemprop="description">
       <p>
        It's the marriage of red velvet cake and blondies! These are like cookies but thick and rich, almost fudgy. And they' are purely red velvet!
       </p>
      </div>
      <img align="right" class="photo nopin" height="125" itemprop="image" nopin="nopin" src="http://www.crazyforcrust.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Red-Velvet-Blondies-6-of-11w-200x200.jpg" width="125"/>
      <h3 id="ingredients">
       Ingredients:
      </h3>
      <div class="ingredients">
       <ul>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 cups packed brown sugar
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/2 cup unsalted butter
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 eggs
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 tablespoon vanilla extract
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1 teaspoon baking powder
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/2 teaspoon salt
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 cups flour
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         2 tablespoons red food coloring
        </li>
        <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
         1/2 cup white chocolate candy melts (or white chocolate chips), optional
        </li>
       </ul>
      </div>
      <h3 id="directions" style="clear:left">
       Directions:
      </h3>
      <div class="instructions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
       <ol>
        <li>
         Preheat oven to 350F. Line a 9x13 pan with foil and spray with cooking spray.
        </li>
        <li>
         Cream brown sugar and butter in the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with a paddle attachment. Add eggs and vanilla and beat until combined. Add cocoa, baking powder, salt, and flour and mix. Carefully mix in red food coloring.
        </li>
        <li>
         Press into prepared pan (dough is sticky, spray your hands with cooking spray to avoid it sticking to your hands).
        </li>
        <li>
         Bake or about 23-25 minutes.(Be careful not to overcook - the center will still be jiggly when you take these out of the oven and they will finish cooking and firming up as they cool.) Cool completely before slicing into squares.
        </li>
        <li>
         Optional garnish: melt white chocolate almond bark or white chocolate chips according to package directions. Drizzle over bars. Note: Ive had trouble melting white chocolate chips lately, so I highly recommend using almond bark, Candiquik, or candy melts for this step.
        </li>
       </ol>
      </div>
      <div class="notes">
       <h4>
        Notes:
       </h4>
       <p>
        Insread of drizzling the tops with white chocolate, add 2 cups of white chocolate chips (or milk/semi-sweet or a combo) into the batter in step 2. Then bake as normal.
       </p>
