                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://365catladies.blogspot.com/2011/07/tutorial-how-to-make-desktop-diorama.html">
                      Tutorial: How To Make a Desktop Diorama
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1041351598155067035" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      Today I'm going to show how I create my fanciful desktop dioramas, like those pictured here.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/SusanFaye?section_id=6082034" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629390962874285250" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ElR8FRqcuXA/Th-dQUPqfMI/AAAAAAAAIZw/rj7PBsz0Xbk/s320/Etsy%2BDiorama%2BGrouping%2BLR.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 378px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 355px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      A diorama is a "
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%; font-style: italic;">
                      three-dimensional miniature or life-size scene in which  figures, stuffed wildlife, or other objects are arranged in a  naturalistic setting against a painted background
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      ".
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/71753314/garden-cats-desktop-diorama-mini-shadow" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629390968901000690" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-N1KjHB9-eRk/Th-dQqsi3fI/AAAAAAAAIZ4/WWFgbo85N3o/s320/Etsy%2BBellaMario%2BLarge%2BDesktop%2BLR.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 176px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 169px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      For this project you will need to find a small box with a "window" in the lid.  I found these stainless steel spice boxes online. Make sure that it is deep enough for several layers of images.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-DcD-Cg8G7Cc/Th-qY89BY7I/AAAAAAAAIcQ/EmznPkVFXns/s1600/tut0.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629405404892062642" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-DcD-Cg8G7Cc/Th-qY89BY7I/AAAAAAAAIcQ/EmznPkVFXns/s320/tut0.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 271px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 272px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       This little box is 3 inches square and 2 inches deep.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-9FjVnMnxTeI/Th-qYuemiwI/AAAAAAAAIcI/t7osI7HOS4E/s1600/tut2.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629405401006377730" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-9FjVnMnxTeI/Th-qYuemiwI/AAAAAAAAIcI/t7osI7HOS4E/s320/tut2.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 232px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 282px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       You could also use a wooden shadow box like this one that I found at a local craft store.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-kHsc2hfqY4E/Th-pn0sGfHI/AAAAAAAAIb4/Mzda9FYsiWE/s1600/tut5.jpg">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629404560860019826" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-kHsc2hfqY4E/Th-pn0sGfHI/AAAAAAAAIb4/Mzda9FYsiWE/s320/tut5.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 314px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 320px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       You can draw and paint your own motifs, use pictures from books or magazines,  use rubber stamp art, or clip art from your files. I'm using one of my own illustrations for this project.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/76042471/the-garden-shed-8-x-10-whimsical-art" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629407853889234610" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Vw4YdkUgpsE/Th-sngLcprI/AAAAAAAAIcg/wQWmgFXet0s/s200/EtsyThumb%2BPrint-GK03%2B%2B8x10.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; height: 160px; margin: 0pt 0pt 10px 10px; width: 200px;"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      I've taken motifs from the illustration and reduced them in size on my computer so that they will fit into a 3" square space.  Then I printed them on white card stock.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      If you are using art that is on lightweight paper, such as images from a book or magazine, use a good paper glue and glue them onto card stock or other sturdy, heavyweight paper.  I'm going to use that flower patch square that's in the right hand corner for the background, so I made sure it is at least 3" square to cover the back of the box.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-P2j6joCPmmY/Th-qYghE7KI/AAAAAAAAIcA/0DNV5srQtxs/s1600/tut4.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629405397258661026" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-P2j6joCPmmY/Th-qYghE7KI/AAAAAAAAIcA/0DNV5srQtxs/s320/tut4.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 320px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 289px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       <br/>
                       Next I place the box on top of the background art and trace around it with pencil.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-kSKIzJjyHO4/Th-pnhjLWuI/AAAAAAAAIbw/h8gY2e__cxw/s1600/tut6.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629404555722316514" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-kSKIzJjyHO4/Th-pnhjLWuI/AAAAAAAAIbw/h8gY2e__cxw/s320/tut6.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 306px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 320px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-gIt6kD3HG_g/Th-pnVZhxxI/AAAAAAAAIbo/3lNYWD1xkzc/s1600/tut7.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629404552460617490" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-gIt6kD3HG_g/Th-pnVZhxxI/AAAAAAAAIbo/3lNYWD1xkzc/s320/tut7.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 302px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 320px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       Then I cut it out just inside of the lines.  I cover the back of it with a thin film of glue (I have used both Aleene's Tacky Glue and E6000 Craft Glue and either one works fine) and then place it inside on the back wall of the box,  pressing it firmly to make sure it lays flat.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fGKBBVB1gTc/Th-pnBji_pI/AAAAAAAAIbg/z-EDoV3rMyE/s1600/tut8.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629404547133931154" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fGKBBVB1gTc/Th-pnBji_pI/AAAAAAAAIbg/z-EDoV3rMyE/s320/tut8.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 265px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 320px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      I
                     </span>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      MPORTANT STEP: Each piece (except for the background square) is going  to need a little 1/4" tab at the bottom for gluing.  I always draw it in  with a pencil so that I don't forget it when I'm cutting out the  pieces.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                        <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                         <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                          <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                           <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-1rNXg4JZ0-I/Th-qZuYjRUI/AAAAAAAAIcY/99707lztrts/s1600/tut.jpg">
                            <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629405418160866626" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-1rNXg4JZ0-I/Th-qZuYjRUI/AAAAAAAAIcY/99707lztrts/s320/tut.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 286px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 320px;"/>
                           </a>
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       Next I cut out the other pieces that I plan to use.  I use small, sharp scissors that make it easy to maneuver around tiny details.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <a href="http://www.blogger.com/blogger.g?blogID=3340102777160116009" o="">
                      <span style="font-style: italic;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </a>
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-EAv5Dv3DUJ4/Th-owqHP4sI/AAAAAAAAIbQ/i4jIr3Z_-KI/s1600/tut10.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629403613128286914" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-EAv5Dv3DUJ4/Th-owqHP4sI/AAAAAAAAIbQ/i4jIr3Z_-KI/s320/tut10.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 319px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 320px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       I fold back each tab and crease it really well, then fold it back out half-way so that it is at a 90 degree angle from the front.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-d5MTINmfTcw/Th-owcNpwTI/AAAAAAAAIbI/MHz3-HOCipg/s1600/tut11.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629403609397051698" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-d5MTINmfTcw/Th-owcNpwTI/AAAAAAAAIbI/MHz3-HOCipg/s320/tut11.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 320px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 290px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      <br/>
                      I put a nice glob of glue on the bottom of the tab of the first element, a flower, and place it about two-thirds of the way into the box.  I tamp it down with my finger to make sure it is firm against the bottom.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4Fxf1EjOVtE/Th-owN_ZQ3I/AAAAAAAAIbA/7gXe_fjuQ3s/s1600/tut12.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629403605579154290" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4Fxf1EjOVtE/Th-owN_ZQ3I/AAAAAAAAIbA/7gXe_fjuQ3s/s320/tut12.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 320px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 314px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       <br/>
                       I add the wheelbarrow about one-third of the way from the front...
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-qVea124V-o8/Th-ova13q9I/AAAAAAAAIa4/K8OTN1sLcLQ/s1600/tut13.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629403591848995794" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-qVea124V-o8/Th-ova13q9I/AAAAAAAAIa4/K8OTN1sLcLQ/s320/tut13.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 320px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 286px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       <br/>
                       And I add the watering can at the very front of the box.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-9iP5Y0-ayvk/Th-ovVaE5aI/AAAAAAAAIaw/l0LT5lMpsLg/s1600/tut14.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629403590390244770" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-9iP5Y0-ayvk/Th-ovVaE5aI/AAAAAAAAIaw/l0LT5lMpsLg/s320/tut14.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 320px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 310px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       Next, I round off the edges of the tab on the piece that will decorate the top so that it will have a nice finished look to it.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Y-oNZPdCfHw/Th-oDhKHaKI/AAAAAAAAIag/c9-g88wltU4/s1600/tut15.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629402837630281890" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Y-oNZPdCfHw/Th-oDhKHaKI/AAAAAAAAIag/c9-g88wltU4/s320/tut15.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 211px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 210px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       <br/>
                       I place the lid on the box...
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-zSplMHK78YI/Th-oDUsMu-I/AAAAAAAAIaY/cVOzI2UD3ac/s1600/tut16.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629402834283576290" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-zSplMHK78YI/Th-oDUsMu-I/AAAAAAAAIaY/cVOzI2UD3ac/s320/tut16.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 263px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 271px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       <br/>
                       ...and then glue the top piece right behind the lid and center it.  This is where a ruler comes in handy.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Xg7NRG8QYG0/Th-oDP4FBtI/AAAAAAAAIaQ/iPgU0GBJNKw/s1600/tut18.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5629402832991225554" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Xg7NRG8QYG0/Th-oDP4FBtI/AAAAAAAAIaQ/iPgU0GBJNKw/s320/tut18.jpg" style="cursor: pointer; display: block; height: 320px; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; width: 294px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size: 130%;">
                       And voila!  A little 3-D miniature world to enjoy!
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/77929202/wheelbarrow-cat-desktop-diorama-mini" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">

                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size: 130%;">

                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7015869458301450424" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       <a href="http://illustrationfriday.com/" style="font-weight: bold;">

                       </a>
                       <br/>
                       Theme: perennial
                       <br/>
                      </span>

                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/SusanFaye" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">

                      <br/>

                     </span>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     <a href="http://365catladies.blogspot.com/2011/07/my-all-time-favorite-top-secret.html">
                      My All-Time Favorite Top Secret Watercolor Technique
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6143353575514318209" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       Impress your friends and family and give your watercolors the WOW factor with my  All-Time Favorite Watercolor Technique.  To create intricately mottled textures in your backgrounds, foliage, and landscapes as pictured below, you need go no further than your kitchen for the top secret ingredient.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ntquVJ8AAIA/TiotZuj7pUI/AAAAAAAAIgo/lUiq-Z6w9lU/s1600/SaltTut08.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632364203998946626" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ntquVJ8AAIA/TiotZuj7pUI/AAAAAAAAIgo/lUiq-Z6w9lU/s400/SaltTut08.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 390px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       It's SALT!
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       Here's how I created the foliage texture in my latest painting.  Give it a try... it's fun!
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       First I dampen my watercolor paper (Arches 140 lb. Cold Press).  Then I start plopping down washes of watery, but color-dense watercolor paint.  You will need lots of rich color--remember, watercolor paint fades a bit as it dries...I start out with yellow and a bit of rose.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XlMfcURaBbU/TiosA-9wqPI/AAAAAAAAIgY/6RmYmKLEq9k/s1600/SaltTut01.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632362679393888498" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XlMfcURaBbU/TiosA-9wqPI/AAAAAAAAIgY/6RmYmKLEq9k/s400/SaltTut01.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 294px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size:130%;">
                      Next I plop in some thalo blue and let it mix and run and bleed into the yellow to make varying shades of green.  Letting colors mix right on the paper will make the greens much more interesting than if you mix one flat color on your palette.  It's important to work fairly fast so that the area you are working on stays damp.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-TLVZEHqAiYE/TiosAwOVC4I/AAAAAAAAIgQ/SX4PYAgT3d8/s1600/SaltTut02.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632362675436850050" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-TLVZEHqAiYE/TiosAwOVC4I/AAAAAAAAIgQ/SX4PYAgT3d8/s400/SaltTut02.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 293px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size:130%;">
                      Once I have lots and lots of deep colors on the paper and I've let them all run together in interesting ways, I take common table salt and sprinkle it here and there.  You can experiment with sea salt and other types of salt--larger grains will produce different effects.  Also experiment with the amount-- sometimes less is more.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-GR2QYot61Uo/TiosAgekFEI/AAAAAAAAIgI/0biVLLfdvB8/s1600/SaltTut03.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632362671209976898" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-GR2QYot61Uo/TiosAgekFEI/AAAAAAAAIgI/0biVLLfdvB8/s400/SaltTut03.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 280px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       <br/>
                       Now it's time to sit back and relax.  Don't touch anything; just let the salt work its wonders.  Here's a close-up.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-UqKQdCGknYo/TiomYywYv4I/AAAAAAAAIgA/T7Q-7IYGKVw/s1600/SaltTut03closeup.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632356491363663746" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-UqKQdCGknYo/TiomYywYv4I/AAAAAAAAIgA/T7Q-7IYGKVw/s400/SaltTut03closeup.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 306px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <span style="font-size:130%;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       Here it is after 5 minutes.  You can see the salt chemically reacting with the paint and creating white "stars" and concentrated speckles of paint
                      </span>
                      :
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-6zj6arit33U/TiomYnQZYFI/AAAAAAAAIf4/HgNOM8nFAhg/s1600/SaltTut04.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632356488276697170" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-6zj6arit33U/TiomYnQZYFI/AAAAAAAAIf4/HgNOM8nFAhg/s400/SaltTut04.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 284px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       Here it is after 10 minutes.  Fun, eh?
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-XVG117HvFbU/TiomYf40r0I/AAAAAAAAIfo/_MB3eSKIk2U/s1600/SaltTut06.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                      <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632356486298775362" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-XVG117HvFbU/TiomYf40r0I/AAAAAAAAIfo/_MB3eSKIk2U/s400/SaltTut06.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 288px;"/>
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       Now wait until it is
                       <span style="font-weight: bold;">
                        completely
                       </span>
                       dry and brush off any remaining salt crystals before resuming painting. (You do not want to get salt on your paint brushes or transfer the salt back to your palette-- it will make
                       <span style="font-weight: bold;">
                        all
                       </span>
                       of your paint come out blotchy!)
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       I used this technique to create the background in my latest watercolor illustration. The painting is almost complete and I'll share it when it's done.  In the mean time, here is a peek at one corner that I've finished...
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-WW3ygvtanfM/TiosBFwAqiI/AAAAAAAAIgg/MZ2XNXmRqs4/s1600/SaltTut07.jpg" onblur="try {parent.deselectBloggerImageGracefully();} catch(e) {}">
                       <img alt="" border="0" id="BLOGGER_PHOTO_ID_5632362681215265314" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-WW3ygvtanfM/TiosBFwAqiI/AAAAAAAAIgg/MZ2XNXmRqs4/s400/SaltTut07.jpg" style="display: block; margin: 0px auto 10px; text-align: center; cursor: pointer; width: 400px; height: 394px;"/>
                      </a>
                      <span style="font-size:130%;">
                       Now go forth and have some watercolor fun!
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
