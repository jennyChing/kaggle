       Long Time Lucas Producer Talks Star Wars: Underworld TV Show
      </a>
     </h1>
     <div class="authmetasingle">
      Posted
      <abbr class="timeago" title="2012-06-05 22:00:10Z">
       Tuesday, June 5th, 2012 06:00 pm GMT -4
      </abbr>
      by
      <a href="http://sciencefiction.com/author/ben-silverio/" rel="author" title="Posts by Ben Silverio">
       Ben Silverio
      </a>
      <span class="sharetwhm">
       <span class="st_twitter_hcount" st_title="Long Time Lucas Producer Talks Star Wars: Underworld TV Show" st_url="http://sciencefiction.com/2012/06/05/long-time-lucas-producer-talks-star-wars-underworld-tv-show/" st_username="ScienceFiction" st_via="ScienceFiction">
       </span>
       <span class="st_facebook_hcount" st_title="Long Time Lucas Producer Talks Star Wars: Underworld TV Show" st_url="http://sciencefiction.com/2012/06/05/long-time-lucas-producer-talks-star-wars-underworld-tv-show/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_sharethis_hcount" st_title="Long Time Lucas Producer Talks Star Wars: Underworld TV Show" st_url="http://sciencefiction.com/2012/06/05/long-time-lucas-producer-talks-star-wars-underworld-tv-show/">
       </span>
      </span>
     </div>
     <div style="clear:both;">
     </div>
     <p style="text-align: center;">
      <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-65207" height="363" src="http://cdn1.sciencefiction.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/vader-and-bounty-hunters-thumb-550x363-81302.jpeg" title="Vader banner" width="550"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      For the longest time, weve been hearing about a live action Star Wars television series, but things like finances and time just havent been aligning for Lucasfilm. Despite these setbacks, producer Rick McCallum has informed Den of Geek (via
      <a href="http://geektyrant.com/news/2012/6/4/star-wars-tv-series-described-as-deadwood-in-space.html" target="_blank">
       Geek Tyrant
      </a>
      ) that they have 50 scripts completed and would be ready to go whenever the time came. However, he feels that theres a bigger problem that could hinder production further. He says:
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       Our biggest problem is that these stories are adult. I meanthese are like Deadwood in space. It so unlike anything youve ever associated with George before in relation to Star Wars. These arent for kids. I mean, we hope theyll watch, but its not being targeted at 8-to-9 year old boys.
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      You know what? Excellent. The 8-to-9 year old boys have Star Wars: The Clone Wars on Cartoon Network already. Im definitely ready to see a more adult look at the Star Wars universe. Theres a whole galaxy filled with smugglers, killers, and rogues that needs to be explored, and I think the world is ready for it.
     </p>
     <p>
      McCallum did go on to mention that whenever they got the green light to go ahead with the show, it would probably take a year of prep before shooting would start, but thats because theyre all very complex. Yeah, we wont be getting this show any time soon, but when we do, Im very confidant that it will be legen wait for it DARY!
     </p>
