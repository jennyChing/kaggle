        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Hyperloop: Elon Musk and His Plan to Revolutionize Transit
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard fn">
          The Burns &amp; McDonnell Team
         </span>
         on
         <abbr class="published" title="2013-08-22">
          August 22, 2013
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14544634" data-link="http://www.burnsmcdblog.com/2013/08/22/hyperloop-elon-musk-and-his-plan-to-revolutionize-transit/" data-summary="" data-title="Hyperloop: Elon Musk and His Plan to Revolutionize Transit">
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.burnsmcdblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Hyperloop.jpg">
          <img alt="hyperloop" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2467" height="103" src="http://www.burnsmcdblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Hyperloop-300x103.jpg" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Elon Musk is no stranger to invention. His latest idea?
         <a href="http://blog.ted.com/2013/08/12/elon-musk-unveils-his-plans-for-the-hyperloop/" target="_blank">
          Hyperloop, a high-speed transit system
         </a>
         that will carry passengers from Los Angeles to San Francisco (a distance of 382 miles) in about 35 minutes.
        </p>
        <p>
         The brains behind
         <a href="http://www.paypal.com" target="_blank">
          PayPal
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.spacex.com/" target="_blank">
          Space Exploration Technologies Corp.
         </a>
         (SpaceX) and
         <a href="http://www.teslamotors.com/" target="_blank">
          Tesla Motors
         </a>
         , Elon is a serial entrepreneur whos not afraid to think outside the box. Case in point? Hyperloop, which works similarly to the canister tubes at drive-up banks.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Hyperloop system is comprised of long tubes on top of pylons. The aboveground system, designed to run alongside highways, includes capsules that hold 28 passengers and depart every two minutes. The capsules feature compressor fans in their nose that form a cushion of air beneath them. Theyre also mounted on a pair of skis made from iconel  a metal used by SpaceX to handle high heat and pressure. The capsules are then powered by magnets and an electromagnetic field, creating levitation with little drag  the ideal circumstances for high-speed travel.
        </p>
        <p>
         Inside the pod, passengers would likely feel a large initial thrust  similar to what you feel when a plane takes off.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then once youre there, according to Musk,
         <a href="http://www.wired.com/autopia/2013/08/hyperloop-elon-musk/" target="_blank">
          as interviewed by
          <em>
           Wired
          </em>
          magazine
         </a>
         , theres no sense of speed.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sound too good to be true? Hyperloop already has its fair share of detractors who have found various faults in the plan.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sam Jaffe, a senior research analyst at clean technology firm Navigant Research,
         <a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/money/cars/2013/08/13/hyperloop-elon-musk-tesla-space-x/2646969/" target="_blank">
          shared his concerns in a
          <i>
           USA Today
          </i>
          article
         </a>
         . The biggest hurdle would be dealing with the temperature of air compression, says Jaffe, who has launched a startup business in compressed-air energy storage.
        </p>
        <p>
         Other red flags like
         <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2013/aug/16/elon-musk-hyperloop-going-nowhere" target="_blank">
          land acquisition and costs
         </a>
         have been raised as deterrents to the creation of Hyperloop. And although those are valid concerns, Elon might be able to tap into the power of crowdsourcing to solve these challenges. He
         <a href="http://www.teslamotors.com/blog/hyperloop" target="_blank">
          published the entire Hyperloop plan online,
         </a>
         giving anyone whos interested the chance to learn more about his vision.
        </p>
        <p>
         What are your thoughts on Hyperloop? Could this be the invention that revolutionizes our countrys high-speed transit? Or are there too many obstacles to make this plan a reality?
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Image via Tesla Motors
         </em>
        </p>
