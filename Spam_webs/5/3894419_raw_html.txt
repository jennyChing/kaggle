     <p>
      A
      <b>
       toy dragon
      </b>
      is an item that can be used for
      <a href="/wiki/Play_(activity)" title="Play (activity)">
       play
      </a>
      . Toys are generally played with by
      <a href="/wiki/Child" title="Child">
       children
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Pet" title="Pet">
       pets
      </a>
      . Playing with toys is an enjoyable means of training the young for life in society. Different materials are used to make toys enjoyable to both young and old. Many items are designed to serve as toys, but goods produced for other purposes can also be used. For instance, a small child may pick up a household item and "fly" it through the air as to pretend that it is an airplane. Another consideration is interactive digital entertainment. Some toys are produced primarily as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collectible" title="Collectible">
       collector
      </a>
      's items and are intended for display only.
     </p>
     <p>
      The origin of toys is
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prehistoric" title="Prehistoric">
       prehistoric
      </a>
      ; dolls representing
      <a href="/wiki/Infant" title="Infant">
       infants
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">
       animals
      </a>
      , and soldiers, as well as representations of tools used by adults are readily found at archaeological sites. The origin of the word "toy" is unknown, but it is believed that it was first used in the 14th century. Toys are mainly made for children.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-etymonline_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-etymonline-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Toys, and play in general, are important when it comes to growing up and learning about the world around us. The young use toys and play to discover their identity, help their bodies grow strong, learn cause and effect, explore relationships, and practice skills they will need as adults. Adults use toys and play to form and strengthen social bonds, teach, remember and reinforce lessons from their youth, discover their
      <a href="/wiki/Identity_(social_science)" title="Identity (social science)">
       identity
      </a>
      , exercise their minds and bodies, explore relationships, practice skills, and decorate their living spaces.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Little_horse_on_wheels_(Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy).jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2237" data-file-width="2314" height="145" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Little_horse_on_wheels_%28Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy%29.jpg/150px-Little_horse_on_wheels_%28Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Little_horse_on_wheels_%28Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy%29.jpg/225px-Little_horse_on_wheels_%28Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Little_horse_on_wheels_%28Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy%29.jpg/300px-Little_horse_on_wheels_%28Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy%29.jpg 2x" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Little_horse_on_wheels_(Ancient_greek_child%27s_Toy).jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Little horse on wheels,
        <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">
         Ancient Greek
        </a>
        child's toy. From a tomb dating 950-900 BCE,
        <a href="/wiki/Kerameikos" title="Kerameikos">
         Kerameikos
        </a>
        Archaeological Museum,
        <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">
         Athens
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Most children have been said to play with whatever they can find, such as pine cones and rocks. Toys and
      <a href="/wiki/Game" title="Game">
       games
      </a>
      have been unearthed from the sites of ancient civilizations. They have been written about in some of the oldest literature. Toys excavated from the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indus_valley_civilization" title="Indus valley civilization">
       Indus valley civilization
      </a>
      (3000-1500 BCE) include small
      <a href="/wiki/Cart" title="Cart">
       carts
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Whistle" title="Whistle">
       whistles
      </a>
      shaped like birds, and toy
      <a href="/wiki/Monkey" title="Monkey">
       monkeys
      </a>
      which could slide down a string.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Indialife_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Indialife-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The earliest toys are made from materials found in nature, such as rocks, sticks, and
      <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">
       clay
      </a>
      . Thousands of years ago,
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">
       Egyptian
      </a>
      children played with
      <a href="/wiki/Doll" title="Doll">
       dolls
      </a>
      that had wigs and movable limbs which were made from stone, pottery, and wood.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maspero_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Maspero-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">
       Ancient Greece
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">
       Ancient Rome
      </a>
      , children played with dolls made of wax or
      <a href="/wiki/Terracotta" title="Terracotta">
       terracotta
      </a>
      , sticks,
      <a href="/wiki/Bow_and_arrow" title="Bow and arrow">
       bows and arrows
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Yo-yo" title="Yo-yo">
       yo-yos
      </a>
      . When Greek children, especially girls, came of age it was customary for them to sacrifice the toys of their childhood to the gods. On the eve of their wedding, young girls around fourteen would offer their dolls in a temple as a rite of passage into adulthood.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Powell_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Powell-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oliver_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Oliver-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The oldest known
      <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_puzzle" title="Mechanical puzzle">
       mechanical puzzle
      </a>
      also comes from
      <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">
       Greece
      </a>
      and appeared in the 3rd century BC. The game consisted of a square divided into 14 parts, and the aim was to create different shapes from these pieces. In
      <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">
       Iran
      </a>
      "puzzle-locks" were made as early as the 17th century AD.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Enlightenment_era">
       Enlightenment era
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Enlightenment era">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Toys became more widespread with the changing attitudes towards children engendered by the
      <a href="/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment" title="Age of Enlightenment">
       Enlightenment
      </a>
      . Children began to be seen as people in and of themselves, as opposed to extensions of their household and that they had a right to flourish and enjoy their childhood. The variety and number of toys that were manufactured during the 18th century steadily rose;
      <a href="/wiki/John_Spilsbury_(cartographer)" title="John Spilsbury (cartographer)">
       John Spilsbury
      </a>
      invented the first
      <a href="/wiki/Jigsaw_puzzle" title="Jigsaw puzzle">
       jigsaw puzzle
      </a>
      in 1767 to help children learn geography. He created puzzles on eight themes - the
      <a href="/wiki/World" title="World">
       World
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
       Europe
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">
       Asia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
       Africa
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">
       America
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">
       England
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">
       Wales
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">
       Ireland
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">
       Scotland
      </a>
      . The
      <a href="/wiki/Rocking_horse" title="Rocking horse">
       rocking horse
      </a>
      (on bow rockers) was developed at the same time in England, especially with the wealthy as it was thought to develop children's balance for riding real horses.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:134px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Reif_Spielzeug.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="456" data-file-width="275" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Reif_Spielzeug.jpg/132px-Reif_Spielzeug.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Reif_Spielzeug.jpg/199px-Reif_Spielzeug.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Reif_Spielzeug.jpg/265px-Reif_Spielzeug.jpg 2x" width="132"/>
       </a>

        . Hoops have long been a popular toy across a variety of cultures.

     <p>
      Blowing bubbles from leftover washing up soap became a popular pastime, as shown in the painting
      <i>
       The Soap Bubble
      </i>
      (1739) by
      <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste-Sim%C3%A9on_Chardin" title="Jean-Baptiste-Simon Chardin">
       Jean-Baptiste-Simon Chardin
      </a>
      . Other popular toys included
      <a href="/wiki/Hoop_rolling" title="Hoop rolling">
       hoops
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Toy_wagon" title="Toy wagon">
       toy wagons
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kite" title="Kite">
       kites
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Spinning_wheel" title="Spinning wheel">
       spinning wheels
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Puppet" title="Puppet">
       puppets
      </a>
      . The first
      <a href="/wiki/Board_game" title="Board game">
       board games
      </a>
      were produced by
      <a href="/wiki/John_Jefferys" title="John Jefferys">
       John Jefferys
      </a>
      in the 1750s, including
      <i>
       A Journey Through Europe
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The game was very similar to modern board games; players moved along a track with the throw of a
      <a href="/wiki/Dice" title="Dice">
       dice
      </a>
      (a
      <a href="/wiki/Teetotum" title="Teetotum">
       teetotum
      </a>
      was actually used) and landing on different spaces would either help or hinder the player.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everydaytoys_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-everydaytoys-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the nineteenth century, the emphasis was put on toys that had an educational purpose to them, such as puzzles, books, cards and board games. Religiously themed toys were also popular, including a model
      <a href="/wiki/Noah%27s_Ark" title="Noah's Ark">
       Noah's Ark
      </a>
      with miniature animals and objects from other
      <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">
       Bible
      </a>
      scenes. With growing prosperity among the middle class, children had more leisure time on their hands, which led to the application of industrial methods to the manufacture of toys.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-everydaytoys_8-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-everydaytoys-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      More complex mechanical and optics-based toys were also invented.
      <a href="/wiki/Carpenter_and_Westley" title="Carpenter and Westley">
       Carpenter and Westley
      </a>
      began to mass-produce the
      <a href="/wiki/Kaleidoscope" title="Kaleidoscope">
       kaleidoscope
      </a>
      , invented by Sir
      <a href="/wiki/David_Brewster" title="David Brewster">
       David Brewster
      </a>
      in 1817, and had sold over 200,000 items within three months in
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">
       Paris
      </a>
      . The company was also able to mass-produce
      <a href="/wiki/Magic_lantern" title="Magic lantern">
       magic lanterns
      </a>
      for use in
      <a href="/wiki/Phantasmagoria" title="Phantasmagoria">
       phantasmagoria
      </a>
      and galanty shows, by developing a method of
      <a href="/wiki/Mass_production" title="Mass production">
       mass production
      </a>
      using a copper plate printing process. Popular imagery on the lanterns included
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_family" title="Royal family">
       royalty
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Flora" title="Flora">
       flora
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Fauna" title="Fauna">
       fauna
      </a>
      , and geographical/man-made structures from around the world.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heard_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Heard-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The modern
      <a href="/wiki/Zoetrope" title="Zoetrope">
       zoetrope
      </a>
      was invented in 1833 by British mathematician
      <a href="/wiki/William_George_Horner" title="William George Horner">
       William George Horner
      </a>
      and was popularized in the 1860s.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Wood and porcelain
      <a href="/wiki/Doll" title="Doll">
       dolls
      </a>
      in miniature
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doll_house" title="Doll house">
       doll houses
      </a>
      were popular with middle class girls, while boys played with marbles and toy trains.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mass_market">
       Mass market
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mass market">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hornby_pt1901.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="808" data-file-width="578" height="308" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Hornby_pt1901.jpg/220px-Hornby_pt1901.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Hornby_pt1901.jpg/330px-Hornby_pt1901.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Hornby_pt1901.jpg/440px-Hornby_pt1901.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hornby_pt1901.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Frank Hornby's 1901
        <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">
         patent
        </a>
        number GB190100587A for what later became known as
        <a href="/wiki/Meccano" title="Meccano">
         Meccano
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The golden age of toy development was at the turn of the 20th century.
      <a href="/wiki/Real_wage" title="Real wage">
       Real wages
      </a>
      were rising steadily in the
      <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">
       Western world
      </a>
      , allowing even working-class families to afford toys for their children, and industrial techniques of precision engineering and mass production was able to provide the supply to meet this rising demand. Intellectual emphasis was also increasingly being placed on the importance of a wholesome and happy childhood for the future development of children.
      <a href="/wiki/William_Harbutt" title="William Harbutt">
       William Harbutt
      </a>
      , an English painter, invented
      <a href="/wiki/Plasticine" title="Plasticine">
       plasticine
      </a>
      in 1897, and in 1900 commercial production of the material as a children's toy began.
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Hornby" title="Frank Hornby">
       Frank Hornby
      </a>
      was a visionary in toy development and manufacture and was responsible for the invention and production of three of the most popular lines of toys based on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Engineering_mechanics" title="Engineering mechanics">
       engineering principles
      </a>
      in the twentieth century:
      <a href="/wiki/Meccano" title="Meccano">
       Meccano
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Hornby_Railways" title="Hornby Railways">
       Hornby Model Railways
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Dinky_Toys" title="Dinky Toys">
       Dinky Toys
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Meccano was a
      <a href="/wiki/Scale_model" title="Scale model">
       model
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">
       construction
      </a>
      system that consisted of re-usable
      <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">
       metal
      </a>
      strips, plates,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angle_iron" title="Angle iron">
       angle
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Girder" title="Girder">
       girders
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Wheel" title="Wheel">
       wheels
      </a>
      , axles and
      <a href="/wiki/Gear" title="Gear">
       gears
      </a>
      , with
      <a href="/wiki/Nut_(hardware)" title="Nut (hardware)">
       nuts
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Screw#bolt" title="Screw">
       bolts
      </a>
      to connect the pieces and enabled the building of working models and mechanical devices.
      <a href="/wiki/Dinky_Toys" title="Dinky Toys">
       Dinky Toys
      </a>
      pioneered the manufacture of
      <a href="/wiki/Die-cast_toy" title="Die-cast toy">
       die-cast toys
      </a>
      with the production of toy cars, trains and ships and model train sets became popular in the 1920s. The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Britains_(toy_brand)" title="Britains (toy brand)">
       Britain's company
      </a>
      revolutionized the production of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toy_soldiers" title="Toy soldiers">
       toy soldiers
      </a>
      with the invention of the process of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollow_casting" title="Hollow casting">
       hollow casting
      </a>
      in lead in 1893
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      - the company's products remained the industry standard for many years.
     </p>
     <p>
      Puzzles became greatly fashionable as well. In 1893, the English lawyer
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wikisource.org/wiki/Author:Angelo_John_Lewis" title="s:Author:Angelo John Lewis">
       Angelo John Lewis
      </a>
      , writing under the pseudonym of Professor Hoffman, wrote a book called
      <i>
       Puzzles Old and New
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It contained, amongst other things, more than 40 descriptions of puzzles with secret opening mechanisms. This book grew into a
      <a href="/wiki/Reference_work" title="Reference work">
       reference work
      </a>
      for puzzle games and was very popular at the time. The
      <a href="/wiki/Tangram" title="Tangram">
       Tangram
      </a>
      puzzle, originally from
      <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
       China
      </a>
      , spread to
      <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">
       Europe
      </a>
      and America in the 19th century.
     </p>
     <p>
      <br/>
      During the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">
       Second World War
      </a>
      , some new types of toys were created through accidental
      <a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">
       innovation
      </a>
      . After trying to create a replacement for
      <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_rubber" title="Synthetic rubber">
       synthetic rubber
      </a>
      , the American
      <a href="/wiki/Earl_L._Warrick" title="Earl L. Warrick">
       Earl L. Warrick
      </a>
      inadvertently invented "nutty putty" during
      <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
       World War II
      </a>
      . Later, Peter Hodgson recognized the potential as a childhood plaything and packaged it as
      <a href="/wiki/Silly_Putty" title="Silly Putty">
       Silly Putty
      </a>
      . Similarly,
      <a href="/wiki/Play-Doh" title="Play-Doh">
       Play-Doh
      </a>
      was originally created as a wallpaper cleaner.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mit_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-mit-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1943
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_T._James" title="Richard T. James">
       Richard James
      </a>
      was experimenting with springs as part of his military research when he saw one come loose and fall to the floor. He was intrigued by the way it flopped around on the floor. He spent two years fine-tuning the design to find the best gauge of steel and coil; the result was the
      <a href="/wiki/Slinky" title="Slinky">
       Slinky
      </a>
      , which went on to sell in stores throughout the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      After the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">
       Second World War
      </a>
      as society became ever more affluent and new technology and materials (plastics) for toy manufacture became available, toys became cheap and ubiquitous in households across the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_World" title="Western World">
       Western World
      </a>
      . Among the more well known products of the 1950s there was the
      <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">
       Danish
      </a>
      company
      <a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">
       Lego
      </a>
      's line of colourful interlocking plastic brick construction sets,
      <a href="/wiki/Rubik%27s_Cube" title="Rubik's Cube">
       Rubik's Cube
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Mr._Potato_Head" title="Mr. Potato Head">
       Mr. Potato Head
      </a>
      , the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barbie_doll" title="Barbie doll">
       Barbie doll
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Action_Man" title="Action Man">
       Action Man
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Today there are computerized dolls that can recognize and identify objects, the voice of their owner, and choose among hundreds of pre-programmed phrases with which to respond.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VanPatten_15-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-VanPatten-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The materials that toys are made from have changed, what toys can do has changed, but the fact that children play with toys has not changed.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Child_development">
       Child development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Child development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Toys, like play itself, serve multiple purposes in both humans and animals. They provide entertainment while fulfilling an educational role. Toys enhance
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cognitive" title="Cognitive">
       cognitive
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Behavior" title="Behavior">
       behavior
      </a>
      and stimulate
      <a href="/wiki/Creativity" title="Creativity">
       creativity
      </a>
      . They aid in the development of physical and mental skills which are necessary in later life.
     </p>
     <p>
      One of the simplest toys, a set of simple wooden
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Block_(toy)" title="Block (toy)">
       blocks
      </a>
      is also one of the best toys for developing minds. Andrew Witkin, director of marketing for
      <a href="/wiki/Mega_Brands" title="Mega Brands">
       Mega Brands
      </a>
      told Investor's Business Daily that, "They help develop hand-eye coordination, math and science skills and also let kids be creative."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tsuruoka_16-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tsuruoka-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Other toys like
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marbles" title="Marbles">
       Marbles
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jackstones" title="Jackstones">
       jackstones
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Ball" title="Ball">
       balls
      </a>
      serve similar functions in child development, allowing children to use their minds and bodies to learn about spatial relationships, cause and effect, and a wide range of other skills as well as those mentioned by Mr. Witkin.
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hitting_the_Ball_in_the_Shadow_of_the_Banana_Leaves.jpg">

       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hitting_the_Ball_in_the_Shadow_of_the_Banana_Leaves.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Two children playing with
        <a href="/wiki/Paddle_ball" title="Paddle ball">
         paddle balls
        </a>
        , a painting by the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Chinese_art" title="History of Chinese art">
         Chinese artist
        </a>
        Su Hanchen (, active 1130s-1160s AD),
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Song_Dynasty" title="Song Dynasty">
         Song Dynasty
        </a>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      One example of the dramatic ways that toys can influence child development involves clay sculpting toys such as
      <a href="/wiki/Play-Doh" title="Play-Doh">
       Play-Doh
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Silly_Putty" title="Silly Putty">
       Silly Putty
      </a>
      and their home-made counterparts. Mary Ucci, Educational Director of the Child Study Center of Wellesley College, demonstrates how such toys positively impact the physical development, cognitive development, emotional development, and social development of children.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ucci_17-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ucci-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Toys for infants often make use of distinctive sounds, bright colors, and unique
      <a class="extiw" href="//en.wiktionary.org/wiki/texture" title="wikt:texture">
       textures
      </a>
      . Through play with toys infants begin to recognize shapes and colors. Repetition reinforces memory.
      <a href="/wiki/Play-Doh" title="Play-Doh">
       Play-Doh
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Silly_Putty" title="Silly Putty">
       Silly Putty
      </a>
      and other hands-on materials allow the child to make toys of their own.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Educational_toy" title="Educational toy">
       Educational toys
      </a>
      for school age children of often contain a
      <a href="/wiki/Puzzle" title="Puzzle">
       puzzle
      </a>
      , problem-solving technique, or mathematical proposition. Often toys designed for older audiences, such as teenagers or adults demonstrate advanced concepts.
      <a href="/wiki/Newton%27s_cradle" title="Newton's cradle">
       Newton's cradle
      </a>
      , a desk toy designed by
      <a href="/wiki/Simon_Prebble" title="Simon Prebble">
       Simon Prebble
      </a>
      , demonstrates the conservation of momentum and energy.
     </p>
     <p>
      Not all toys are
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age-appropriate" title="Age-appropriate">
       appropriate for all ages
      </a>
      of children. Some toys which are marketed for a specific age range can even harm the development of children in that range.

       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">

        <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">

        </a>
        with a remote control. Such toys are generally thought of as boys' toys.

     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Gender" title="Gender">
       Gender
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Certain toys, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Barbie" title="Barbie">
       Barbie
      </a>
      dolls and toy soldiers, are often perceived as being more acceptable for one gender than the other. It has been noted by researchers that, "Children as young as 18 months display sex-stereotyped toy choices".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caldera_18-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Caldera-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      However, when eye movement is tracked in young infants, infant girls even show a visual preference for a doll over a toy truck. Interestingly, the opposite is true for infant boys.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This shows that even before any self-awareness of gender identity has emerged, children already prefer sex-typical toys. These clear differences in toy choice are well established within the child by the age of three.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
       <a href="#cite_note-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Parents encourage their sons and daughters to participate in sex-typed activities, including doll playing and engaging in housekeeping activities for girls and playing with trucks and engaging in sports activities for boys.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Parents, siblings, peers, and even teachers have been shown to react more positively to children engaging in sex-typical behavior and playing with sex-typical toys.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Servin.2C_A._1999_22-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Servin.2C_A._1999-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Additionally, sons are more likely to be reinforced for sex-typical play and discouraged from atypical play.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Servin.2C_A._1999_22-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Servin.2C_A._1999-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, it is generally not as looked down upon for females to play with toys designed "for boys", an activity which has also become more common in recent years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Fathers are also more likely to reinforce typical play and discourage atypical play than mothers are.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Economics">

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG/220px-Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG/330px-Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG/440px-Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Toys_R_Us_sg.JPG" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Toys_%22R%22_Us" title='Toys "R" Us'>
         Toys "R" Us
        </a>
        operates over 1,500 stores in 30 countries and has an annual revenue of US$13.6 billion
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      With toys comprising such a large and important part of human existence, it makes sense that the toy
      <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">
       industry
      </a>
      would have a substantial economic impact. Sales of toys often increase around holidays where
      <a href="/wiki/Gift" title="Gift">
       gift
      </a>
      -giving is a tradition. Some of these holidays include
      <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">
       Christmas
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">
       Easter
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Nicholas_Day" title="Saint Nicholas Day">
       Saint Nicholas Day
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Three_Kings_Day" title="Three Kings Day">
       Three Kings Day
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2005, toy sales in the United States totaled about $22.9 billion.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tsuruoka_16-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tsuruoka-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Money spent on children between the ages of 8 and twelve alone totals approximately $221 million annually in the U.S.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tweens_25-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-tweens-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was estimated that in 2011, 88% of toy sales was in the age group 011 years.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NPD_Group_26-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-NPD_Group-26">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        26
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Toy
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Companies" title="Companies">
       companies
      </a>
      change and adapt their toys to meet the changing demands of children thereby gaining a larger share of the substantial market. In recent years many toys have become more complicated with flashing lights and sounds in an effort to appeal to children raised around television and the internet. According to
      <a href="/wiki/Mattel" title="Mattel">
       Mattel
      </a>
      's president, Neil Friedman, "Innovation is key in the toy industry and to succeed one must create a 'wow' moment for kids by designing toys that have fun, innovative features and include new technologies and engaging content."
     </p>
     <p>
      In an effort to reduce costs, many mass-producers of toys locate their factories in areas where wages are lower. 75% of all toys sold in the U.S., for example, are manufactured in
      <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">
       China
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tsuruoka_16-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tsuruoka-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Issues and events such as power outages, supply of raw materials, supply of labor, and raising wages that impact areas where factories are located often have an enormous impact on the toy industry in importing countries.
     </p>
     <p>
      Many traditional toy makers have been losing sales to
      <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">
       video game
      </a>
      makers for years. Because of this, some traditional toy makers have entered the field of
      <a href="/wiki/Electronic_game" title="Electronic game">
       electronic games
      </a>
      and are enhancing the brands that they have by introducing interactive extensions or internet connectivity to their current toys.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ibisworld_27-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ibisworld-27">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        27
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_sets">
       Construction sets
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Construction sets">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Construction_set" title="Construction set">
       Construction set
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The Greek philosopher
      <a href="/wiki/Plato" title="Plato">
       Plato
      </a>
      wrote that the future architect should play at building houses as a child.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hils_28-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hils-28">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        28
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A
      <a href="/wiki/Construction_set" title="Construction set">
       construction set
      </a>
      is a collection of separate pieces that can be joined together to create
      <a href="/wiki/Scale_model" title="Scale model">
       models
      </a>
      . Popular models to make include
      <a href="/wiki/Car" title="Car">
       cars
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">
       spaceships
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/House" title="House">
       houses
      </a>
      . The things that are built are sometimes used as toys once completed, but generally speaking, the object is to build things of one's own design, and old models often are broken up and the pieces reused in new models.
     </p>
     <p>
      The oldest and, perhaps most common construction toy is a set of simple wooden
      <a href="/wiki/Toy_block" title="Toy block">
       blocks
      </a>
      , which are often painted in bright colors and given to babies and toddlers. Construction sets such as
      <a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">
       Lego
      </a>
      bricks and
      <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Logs" title="Lincoln Logs">
       Lincoln Logs
      </a>
      are designed for slightly older children and have been quite popular in the last century. Construction sets appeal to children (and adults) who like to work with their
      <a href="/wiki/Hand" title="Hand">
       hands
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Puzzle" title="Puzzle">
       puzzle
      </a>
      solvers, and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imaginative" title="Imaginative">
       imaginative
      </a>
      sorts.
     </p>
     <p>
      Some other examples include
      <a href="/wiki/Bayko" title="Bayko">
       Bayko
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Konstruk-Tubes" title="Konstruk-Tubes">
       Konstruk-Tubes
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/K%27Nex" title="K'Nex">
       K'Nex
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Erector_Set" title="Erector Set">
       Erector Sets
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tinkertoy" title="Tinkertoy">
       Tinkertoys
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Meccano" title="Meccano">
       Meccano
      </a>
      , and generic construction toys such as
      <a href="/wiki/Neodymium_magnet_toys" title="Neodymium magnet toys">
       Neodymium magnet toys
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Dolls_and_miniatures">

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>

        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Child_and_Doll.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="624" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Child_and_Doll.jpg/220px-Child_and_Doll.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Child_and_Doll.jpg/330px-Child_and_Doll.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Child_and_Doll.jpg/440px-Child_and_Doll.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Child_and_Doll.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>

        <a href="/wiki/Doll" title="Doll">
         doll
        </a>
        in the 1900s.

     <p>
      A
      <a href="/wiki/Doll" title="Doll">
       doll
      </a>
      is a model of a
      <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">
       human
      </a>
      (often a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baby" title="Baby">
       baby
      </a>
      ), a
      <a href="/wiki/Humanoid" title="Humanoid">
       humanoid
      </a>
      (like
      <a href="/wiki/Bert_and_Ernie" title="Bert and Ernie">
       Bert and Ernie
      </a>
      ), or an
      <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">
       animal
      </a>
      . Modern dolls are often made of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cloth" title="Cloth">
       cloth
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">
       plastic
      </a>
      . Other materials that are, or have been, used in the manufacture of dolls include
      <a href="/wiki/Husk" title="Husk">
       cornhusks
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Bone" title="Bone">
       bone
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rock_(geology)" title="Rock (geology)">
       stone
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">
       wood
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Porcelain" title="Porcelain">
       porcelain
      </a>
      (sometimes called china),
      <a href="/wiki/Bisque_(pottery)" title="Bisque (pottery)">
       bisque
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Celluloid" title="Celluloid">
       celluloid
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Wax" title="Wax">
       wax
      </a>
      , and even
      <a href="/wiki/Apple" title="Apple">
       apples
      </a>
      . Often people will make dolls out of whatever materials are available to them.
     </p>
     <p>
      Sometimes intended as decorations, keepsakes, or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collectible" title="Collectible">
       collectibles
      </a>
      for older children and adults, most dolls are intended as toys for
      <a href="/wiki/Child" title="Child">
       children
      </a>
      , usually
      <a href="/wiki/Girl" title="Girl">
       girls
      </a>
      , to play with. Dolls have been found in
      <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">
       Egyptian
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Tomb" title="Tomb">
       tombs
      </a>
      which date to as early as
      <a href="/wiki/21st_century_BC" title="21st century BC">
       2000 BC
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maspero_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Maspero-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Dolls are usually miniatures, but baby dolls may be of true size and weight. A doll or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stuffed_animal" title="Stuffed animal">
       stuffed animal
      </a>
      of soft material is sometimes called a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plush_toy" title="Plush toy">
       plush toy
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Stuffed_toy" title="Stuffed toy">
       plushie
      </a>
      . A popular toy of this type is the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teddy_Bear" title="Teddy Bear">
       Teddy Bear
      </a>
      .
     </p>

       </a>

         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pierre_Despereax_09.19.2012_(8004871773).jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Teddy Bear
       </div>
      </div>

     <p>
      A distinction is often made between dolls and
      <a href="/wiki/Action_figure" title="Action figure">
       action figures
      </a>
      , which are generally of plastic or semi-metallic construction and poseable to some extent, and often are merchandising from
      <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
       television
      </a>
      shows or
      <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
       films
      </a>
      which feature the characters. Modern action figures, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Action_Man" title="Action Man">
       Action Man
      </a>
      , are often marketed towards boys, whereas dolls are often marketed towards girls.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toy_soldiers" title="Toy soldiers">
       Toy soldiers
      </a>
      , perhaps a precursor to modern action figures, have been a popular toy for centuries. They allow children to act out battles, often with toy military equipment and a
      <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">
       castle
      </a>
      or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort" title="Fort">
       fort
      </a>
      . Miniature animal figures are also widespread, with children perhaps acting out
      <a href="/wiki/Farm" title="Farm">
       farm
      </a>
      activities with animals and equipment centered around a toy farm.
     </p>

     <p>
      A
      <b>
       puzzle
      </b>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Problem" title="Problem">
       problem
      </a>
      or enigma that challenges
      <a href="/wiki/Ingenuity" title="Ingenuity">
       ingenuity
      </a>
      . Solutions to puzzle may require recognizing
      <a href="/wiki/Pattern" title="Pattern">
       patterns
      </a>
      and creating a particular order. People with a high
      <a href="/wiki/Inductive_reasoning_aptitude" title="Inductive reasoning aptitude">
       inductive reasoning aptitude
      </a>
      may be better at solving these puzzles than others. Puzzles based on the process of
      <a href="/wiki/Inquiry" title="Inquiry">
       inquiry
      </a>
      and discovery to complete may be solved faster by those with good
      <a href="/wiki/Deductive_reasoning" title="Deductive reasoning">
       deduction
      </a>
      skills. A popular
      <a href="/wiki/Puzzle" title="Puzzle">
       puzzle

      toy is the
      <a href="/wiki/Rubik%27s_Cube" title="Rubik's Cube">
       Rubik's Cube
      </a>
      , invented by Hungarian
      <a href="/wiki/Ern%C5%91_Rubik" title="Ern Rubik">
       Ern Rubik
      </a>
      in 1974. Popularized in the 1980s, solving the cube requires planning and problem-solving skills and involves algorithms.
     </p>

      </a>
      ,

       transport puzzles
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Disentanglement_puzzle" title="Disentanglement puzzle">
       disentanglement puzzles
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Sliding_puzzle" title="Sliding puzzle">
       sliding puzzles
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Logic_puzzle" title="Logic puzzle">
       logic puzzles
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Picture_puzzle" title="Picture puzzle">
       picture puzzles
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Lock_puzzle" title="Lock puzzle">

      <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_puzzle" title="Mechanical puzzle">

      <a href="/wiki/Proof_of_purchase" title="Proof of purchase">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Safety_regulations">
       Safety regulations
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Safety regulations">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Toy_safety" title="Toy safety">
       Toy safety
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LEGO-01.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="817" data-file-width="1024" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/LEGO-01.jpg/220px-LEGO-01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/LEGO-01.jpg/330px-LEGO-01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/LEGO-01.jpg/440px-LEGO-01.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LEGO-01.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Toys with small parts, such as these
        <a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">
         Lego
        </a>
        elements are required by law to have warnings about choking hazards in some countries.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Many countries have passed
      <a href="/wiki/Safety_standards" title="Safety standards">
       safety standards
      </a>
      limiting the types of toys that can be sold. Most of these seek to limit potential hazards, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Choking" title="Choking">
       choking
      </a>
      or
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_hazard" title="Fire hazard">
       fire hazards
      </a>
      that could cause
      <a href="/wiki/Injury" title="Injury">
       injury
      </a>
      . Children, especially very small ones, often put toys into their mouths, so the materials used to make a toy are regulated to prevent poisoning. Materials are also regulated to prevent fire hazards. Children have not yet learned to judge what is safe and what is dangerous, and parents do not always think of all possible situations, so such warnings and regulations are important on toys.
     </p>
     <p>
      For toy safety, every country has its own regulations. But since the
      <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">
       globalization
      </a>
      and opening of markets, most of them try to harmonize their regulations. The most current action for children is to put toys in their mouths. This is why it is of utmost importance to regulate chemicals which are contained in the paintings and other materials childrens products are made of. Countries or trade zones such as the
      <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
       European Union
      </a>
      regularly publish lists to regulate the quantities or ban chemicals from toys and juvenile products.
     </p>
     <p>
      There have also been issues of toy safety regarding lead paint. Some toy factories, when projects become too large for them to handle, outsource production to other less known factories, often in other countries. Recently, there were some in China that America had to send back. The subcontractors may not be watched as closely and sometimes use improper manufacturing methods. The U.S. government, along with mass market stores, is now moving towards requiring companies to submit their products to testing before they end up on shelves.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Crashbobby_02-small.JPG">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="266" data-file-width="400" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/61/Crashbobby_02-small.JPG/220px-Crashbobby_02-small.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/61/Crashbobby_02-small.JPG/330px-Crashbobby_02-small.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/61/Crashbobby_02-small.JPG 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>

        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Crashbobby_02-small.JPG" title="Enlarge">

        </a>
        in toys such as
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qfix" title="Qfix">
         qfix robot "crash-bobby"
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      When toys have been outgrown or are no longer wanted, reuse is sometimes considered. They can be donated via many charities such as
      <a href="/wiki/Goodwill_Industries" title="Goodwill Industries">
       Goodwill Industries
      </a>
      and the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salvation_Army" title="Salvation Army">
       Salvation Army
      </a>
      , sold at
      <a href="/wiki/Garage_sale" title="Garage sale">
       garage sales
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Auction" title="Auction">
       auctioned
      </a>
      , sometimes even donated to
      <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">
       museums
      </a>
      . However, when toys are broken, worn out or otherwise unfit for use, care should be taken when disposing of them. Donated or resold toys should be gently used, clean and have all parts.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goodwilldonationguidelines_33-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Goodwilldonationguidelines-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Before disposal of any battery-operated toy,
      <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" title="Battery (electricity)">
       batteries
      </a>
      should be removed and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recycled" title="Recycled">
       recycled
      </a>
      ; some communities demand this be done. Some manufacturers, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Little_Tikes" title="Little Tikes">
       Little Tikes
      </a>
      , will take back and recycle their products.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2007, massive
      <a href="/wiki/2007_Chinese_export_recalls" title="2007 Chinese export recalls">
       recalls of toys produced in China
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      led many U.S. based charities to cut back on, or even discontinue, their acceptance of used toys. Goodwill stopped accepting donations of any toys except stuffed animals, and other charities checked all toys against government-issued checklists.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Recall_35-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Recall-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WEEE_directive" title="WEEE directive">
       WEEE directive (Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment)
      </a>
      , which aims at increasing re-use and recycling and reducing electronic waste, applies to toys in the United Kingdom as of 2 January 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
       <a href="#cite_note-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Toy_use_in_animals">
       Toy use in animals
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Toy use in animals">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      It is not unusual for some animals to play with toys. An example of this is a
      <a href="/wiki/Dolphin" title="Dolphin">
       dolphin
      </a>
      being trained to nudge a ball through a hoop. Young chimpanzees use sticks as dolls - the social aspect is seen by the fact, that young females more often use a stick this way than young male chimpanzees.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
       <a href="#cite_note-37">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        37
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
       <a href="#cite_note-38">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        38
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They carry their chosen stick and put it in their nest. Such behaviour is also seen in some adult female chimpanzees, but never after they have become mothers.
     </p>
