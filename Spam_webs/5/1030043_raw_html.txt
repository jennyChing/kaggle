                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Every Day is President's Day in Panem
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <div style="float:right;margin:4px;">
                      <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" scrolling="no" src="http://www.facebook.com/plugins/like.php?href=http://www.hungergameslessons.com/2013/02/every-day-is-presidents-day-in-panem.html&amp;layout=standard&amp;show_faces=false&amp;width=100&amp;action=like&amp;font=arial&amp;colorscheme=light" style="border:none; overflow:hidden; width:400px; height:27px;">
                      </iframe>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Sv38EOZH45k/USHzfBiDi_I/AAAAAAAAGk4/-EtiV_Ar-n8/s1600/PresidentSnowDayEveryDay.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Every day is &quot;President's Day&quot; in Panem. www.hungergameslessons.com" border="0" height="504" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Sv38EOZH45k/USHzfBiDi_I/AAAAAAAAGk4/-EtiV_Ar-n8/s640/PresidentSnowDayEveryDay.jpg" title="Every day is &quot;President's Day&quot; in Panem. www.hungergameslessons.com" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <center>
                      <div>
                       <a href="javascript:void((function(){var%20e=document.createElement('script');e.setAttribute('type','text/javascript');e.setAttribute('charset','utf-8');e.setAttribute('src','http://assets.pinterest.com/js/pinmarklet.js?r='+Math.random()*99999999);document.body.appendChild(e)})());">
                        <img alt="Pin It" class="aligncenter" src="http://www.clickinmoms.com/cmprodaily/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/pinit.jpg"/>
                       </a>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </center>
                     Every day is President's Day in Panem. Or, at least President Snow would like to be honored each day, it seems.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     You can take this opportunity to discuss with your students the purpose of having a day to honor the office of the presidency and how that relates to
                     <i>
                      The Hunger Games
                     </i>
                     ,
                     <i>
                      Catching Fire
                     </i>
                     , and especially
                     <i>
                      Mockingjay
                     </i>
                     .
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      CLASSROOM CONNECTIONS
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="float: right; text-align: center;">
                      <tbody>
                       <tr>
                        <td style="text-align: center;">
                         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-sMr29BH84cA/USH2R4rApvI/AAAAAAAAGmQ/JbgyLQ8FuiY/s1600/3g12934u.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;">
                          <img border="0" height="224" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-sMr29BH84cA/USH2R4rApvI/AAAAAAAAGmQ/JbgyLQ8FuiY/s320/3g12934u.jpeg" width="320"/>
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                       <tr>
                        <td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">
                         From:
                         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:3g12934u.jpg">
                          http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:3g12934u.jpg
                         </a>
                        </td>
                       </tr>
                      </tbody>
                     </table>
                     <b>
                      To start the conversation, ask your students:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     1. Why do we have a "Presidents Day"? What are its origins? Who declared the holiday and why?
                     <br/>
                     2. How do many Americans celebrate "Presidents Day"?
                     <br/>
                     3. Would "Presidents Day" be celebrated the same way in Panem? Why or why not.
                     <br/>
                     4. In what ways do the citizens of Panem "honor" their current and past leaders? Give examples from the novel(s).
                     <br/>
                     5. Do you think the citizens of the Capitol have respect for and honor President Snow, or do so for another reason? Explain.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      What additional questions would you ask your students? Post in the comments below.
                     </i>
