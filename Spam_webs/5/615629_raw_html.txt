       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Baking Tip: How to Get Perfectly Softened Butter Fast
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2012-02-21T07:42:42+00:00">
         February 21, 2012
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://musingssahm.com/author/erika/" rel="author">
           Erika
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">

         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div style="margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 0px;">
        </div>
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         Have you ever decided to bake cookies and then realized that you need room-temperature
         <em>
          softened
         </em>
         butter? Of course you didnt pull the butter out ahead of time because you
         <em>
          just
         </em>
         decided to bake cookies.
        </p>
        <p>
         This happens to me all the time. I used to try to microwave the butter but it would either almost melt or not be soft enough.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then I heard this awesome tip for how to quickly soften butter. Its one of those this-is-so-easy why-didnt-I-think-of-this-myself kind of tips.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="how to get perfectly softened butter fast" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-34509" height="423" src="http://musingssahm.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Softened-Butter1.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Put your stick of butter with the wrapper still on in the microwave.
         <br/>
         (Just make sure its not a foil wrapper!)
        </p>
        <p>
         Heat for
         <strong>
          4 seconds on high
         </strong>
         .
         <br/>
         Flip it to the next side and heat for 4 more seconds.
         <br/>
         Do this for each side for a total of 16 seconds. The time ma vary ever-so-slightly depending on your microwave power.
        </p>
        <p>
         The result will be a slightly squishable stick of butter that is softened
         <em>
          just
         </em>
         right!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
