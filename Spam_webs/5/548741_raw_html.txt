       <article class="post-122408 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-news tag-ebola-virus tag-medical-science">
        <header>
         <h1 class="title entry-title">
          You Are Not Nearly Scared Enough About Ebola
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="post-meta">
         <span class="small">
          By
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://disinfo.com/author/majestic/" rel="author" title="Posts by majestic">
            majestic
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="small">
          on
         </span>
         <abbr class="date time published updated" title="2014-08-17T08:00:21-0400">
          August 17, 2014
         </abbr>
         <span class="small">
          in
         </span>
         <span class="categories">
          <a href="http://disinfo.com/category/news/" title="View all items in News">
           News
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments comments">
          <a href="http://disinfo.com/2014/08/nearly-scared-enough-ebola/#comments">
           <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="122408 http://disinfo.com/?p=122408">
            <i class="icon-comment">
            </i>
            30
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <script class="lockerdome-js-lite" data-box="box-off" data-follow_up="share" data-href="http://disinfo.com/2014/08/nearly-scared-enough-ebola/" data-owner="7565172005012737" data-size="custom-medium" data-size_specifics="24" data-style="text" data-width="fixed-width" src="//cdn2.lockerdome.com/_js/widget_interest_1_0.js">
        </script>
        <section class="entry">
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 395px">
          <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:2014_West_Africa_Ebola_virus_outbreak_situation_map.jpg#mediaviewer/File:2014_West_Africa_Ebola_virus_outbreak_situation_map.jpg">
           <img alt="2014 West Africa Ebola virus outbreak situation map.jpg" height="454" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f3/2014_West_Africa_Ebola_virus_outbreak_situation_map.jpg" width="385"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           2014 West Africa Ebola virus outbreak situation map by Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) via Wikimedia Commons.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Experimental drugs and airport screenings will do nothing to stop this plague. If Ebola hits Lagos, were in real trouble, says Laurie Garrett at
          <a href="http://www.foreignpolicy.com/articles/2014/08/14/you_are_not_nearly_scared_enough_ebola_vaccine_west_africa_outbreak" target="_blank">
           Foreign Policy
          </a>
          :
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p style="color: #231f20;">
           Attention, World: You just dont get it.
          </p>
          <p style="color: #231f20;">
           You think there are magic bullets in some rich countrys freezers that will instantly stop the relentless spread of the Ebola virus in West Africa? You think airport security guards in Los Angeles can look a traveler in the eyes and see infection, blocking that jet passengers entry into La-la-land? You believe novelist Dan Browns utterly absurd
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Inferno-Dan-Brown/dp/0385537859" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            description
           </a>
           of a World Health Organization that has a private C5-A military transport jet and disease SWAT team that can swoop into outbreaks, saving the world from contagion?
          </p>
          <p style="color: #231f20;">
           Wake up, fools. Whats going on in West Africa now isnt Browns silly
           <i>
            Inferno
           </i>
           scenario  its Steven Soderberghs movie
           <a href="http://contagionmovie.warnerbros.com/dvd/#/trailer" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            <i>
             Contagion
            </i>
           </a>
           , though without a modicum of its high-tech capacity.
          </p>
          <p style="color: #231f20;">
           Last week, my brilliant Council on Foreign Relations colleague John Campbell, former U.S. ambassador to Nigeria,
           <a href="http://www.cfr.org/diseases-infectious/ebola-outbreak/p33318" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            warned
           </a>
           that spread of the virus inside Lagos  which has a
           <b style="font-style: inherit;">
           </b>
           population of 22 million  would instantly transform this situation into a worldwide crisis, thanks to the chaos, size, density, and mobility of not only that city but dozens of others in the enormous, oil-rich nation. Add to the Nigerian scenario civil war, national elections, Boko Haram terrorists, and a countrywide doctors strike  all of which are real and current  and you have a scenario so overwrought and frightening
           <b style="font-style: inherit;">
           </b>
           that I could not have concocted it even
           <b style="font-style: inherit;">
           </b>
           when I
           <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2011/09/13/opinion/garrett-contagion-virus/" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            advised
           </a>
           screenwriter
           <a href="http://time.com/3079518/ebola-outbreak-pandemic-fear/" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            Scott Burns
           </a>
           on his
           <i>
            Contagion
           </i>
           script.
          </p>
          <p style="color: #231f20;">
           Inside the United States, politicians,
           <a href="http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/donald-trump-says-ebola-doctors-must-suffer-the-consequences-9646445.html" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            gadflies
           </a>
           , and much of the media are focused on wildly experimental drugs and vaccines, and equally wild notions of 
           <a href="http://www.anncoulter.com/columns/2014-08-06.html" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            keeping the virus out
           </a>
            by barring travelers and 
           <a href="http://www.nwitimes.com/news/local/govt-and-politics/article_6cd84d69-3909-50d7-aa64-cc211b491795.html" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            screening at airports
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pull-quote" style="font-style: inherit;">
            Lets be clear: Absolutely no drug or vaccine has been proven effective against the Ebola virus in human beings.
           </span>
           To date, only one person  Dr. Kent Brantly  has apparently
           <a href="http://news.yahoo.com/us-doctor-ebola-released-hospital-soon-174919099.html" style="font-style: inherit; color: #eb1414;" target="_blank">
            recovered
           </a>
           after receiving one of the three prominent putative drugs, and there is no proof that the drug was key to his improvement
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          [continus at
          <a href="http://www.foreignpolicy.com/articles/2014/08/14/you_are_not_nearly_scared_enough_ebola_vaccine_west_africa_outbreak" target="_blank">
           Foreign Policy
          </a>
          ]
         </p>
