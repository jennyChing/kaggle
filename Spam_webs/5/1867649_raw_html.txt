         <div class="entry-inner">
          <h3 class="entry-header">
           Star Decoration
          </h3>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <div class="entry-body">
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e7f3970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0441" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669e7f3970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e7f3970c-500wi" title="IMG_0441"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             I designed these little hanging decorations to add some colourful,  homespun Hooky Goodness to my Christmas Tree this season, but there's no  reason why they can't be used all year round and in all sorts of ways.  I've had suggestions for using them as baby rattles, baby mobiles, as  tags on wrapped gifts, or simply just as little decorative gifts to pass  on to friends and family.
            </p>
            <p>
             These are quick and satisfying little things to work up, ideal to use  up those little itty bits of stash yarn that you might have lurking  somewhere. I personally prefer to use cotton yarn for making decorations  (here I've used
             <a href="http://www.first4yarns.co.uk/attic-24-colours-of-rico-creative-cotton-771-c.asp" target="_blank">
              Rico Creative Cotton
             </a>
             ),  but any yarn would be fine. I also tend to switch down a hook size for  making decorations which keeps the stitches quite tight and prevents the  polyester stuffing showing through the gaps. So for example where the  Rico cotton suggests a 5mm hook size, I switch down to a 4mm hook.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e9c5970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0366" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669e9c5970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e9c5970c-500wi" title="IMG_0366"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Before we begin, a little summary of the basic stitches. I'm writing using UK crochet terms ::
            </p>
            <p style="color: #347d7e; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <span style="color: #111111;">
              <strong>
               sl st
              </strong>
              [slip stitch] :: insert hook, yarn over, pull the loop back through the stitch, then through the loop on your hook.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="color: #347d7e; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <span style="color: #111111;">
              <strong>
               dc
              </strong>
              [double crochet] ::  insert hook, yarn over, pull the loop back through  the stitch (two  loops on hook), yarn over and pull through both loops  on hook (
              <em>
               note :: this is equivalent to the US sc stitch
              </em>
              )
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="color: #347d7e; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <span style="color: #111111;">
              <strong>
               htr
              </strong>
              [half treble]:: yarn over, insert hook, yarn over, pull the loop back  through the stitch (three loops on hook), yarn over and pull through
              <em>
               all three
              </em>
              loops on hook (
              <em>
               note :: this is equivalent to the US hdc stitch
              </em>
              )
             </span>
            </p>
            <p style="color: #347d7e; font-family: Trebuchet MS;">
             <span style="color: #111111;">
              <strong>
               tr
              </strong>
              [treble] :: yarn over, insert hook, yarn over, pull the loop back  through the stitch (three loops on hook), yarn over and pull through
              <em>
               two
              </em>
              loops on hook (two loops left on hook), yarn over and pull through remaining two loops (
              <em>
               note :: this is equivalent to the US dc stitch
              </em>
              )
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e0604e1c970b-popup" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0409" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e0604e1c970b-500wi" title="IMG_0409"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             First of all you will need to make two flat circles, as described in
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/weblog/bauble-decoration.html" target="_blank">
              this tutorial for Bauble Decorations
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e06075a8970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0427" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340147e06075a8970b" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e06075a8970b-500wi" title="IMG_0427"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Round 5
             </strong>
             [the pointy triangle bits] ::
            </p>
            <p>
             *WORK THROUGH THE BACK LOOP ONLY for this round*
            </p>
            <p>
             Join in a new colour by tying a knot close to the stitches. Insert your hook into the stitch directly to the left of your knot, and pull a loop through to the front.
            </p>
            <p>
             Slip Stitch in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch (picture above)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669dd56970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0428" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669dd56970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669dd56970c-500wi" title="IMG_0428"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             You will be working a total of FOUR stitches all out of the next stitch, as follows ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Htr, Tr (that's two stitches worked)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e10a970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0429" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669e10a970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e10a970c-500wi" title="IMG_0429"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Now chain 2 (as above)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e3e1970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0430" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669e3e1970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e3e1970c-500wi" title="IMG_0430"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             To make the Pointy Bit :: insert hook into the second chain from hook, going through both loops as pictured above. Now yarn over and make a slip stitch by pulling the yarn through all loops on hook. Please note that this can be quite a tricksy manoeuvre and takes a bit of practice...you will need to hold onto your work very close to the hook and twist the hook down and away from you to ease the yarn through.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e641970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0431" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669e641970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669e641970c-500wi" title="IMG_0431"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Now still in the same stitch as before, work the following ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Tr, Htr.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the picture above, you can see that there are 4 stitches in total coming out of that one stitch (htr, tr, tr, htr), with the little picot-point sitting in the middle of the two tr's).
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669eaf6970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0432" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669eaf6970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669eaf6970c-500wi" title="IMG_0432"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Work a Sl St into each of the next FOUR stitches (as above).
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e06087fa970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0433" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340147e06087fa970b" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e06087fa970b-500wi" title="IMG_0433"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the next stitch work the following, same as before ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Htr, Tr, chain 2, insert hook in 2nd chain from hook and sl st, Tr, Htr.
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sl St into each of next FOUR stitches. (Second triangle made)
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the next stitch work the following, same as before ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Htr, Tr, chain 2, insert hook in 2nd chain from hook and sl st, Tr, Htr.
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sl St into each of next THREE stitches. (Third triangle made)
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the next stitch work the following, same as before ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Htr, Tr, chain 2, insert hook in 2nd chain from hook and sl st, Tr, Htr.
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sl St into each of next FOUR stitches. (Fourth triangle made)
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the next stitch work the following, same as before ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Htr, Tr, chain 2, insert hook in 2nd chain from hook and sl st, Tr, Htr.
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sl St into each of next FOUR stitches. (Fifth triangle made)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e0608cbe970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0434" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340147e0608cbe970b" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e0608cbe970b-500wi" title="IMG_0434"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             You should now be almost back round to where you started, can you count FOUR remaining stitches (picture above)?? Good...OK, last bit as follows ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             In the next stitch work the following, same as before ::
            </p>
            <p>
             Htr, Tr, chain 2, insert hook in 2nd chain from hook and sl st, Tr, Htr.
            </p>
            <p>
             Dc in the next stitch.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sl St in the last stitch. (Sixth triangle made)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e0608df6970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0435" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340147e0608df6970b" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e0608df6970b-500wi" title="IMG_0435"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Slip Stitch into the first chain and fasten off. Looking good?? Six points? very Star-like and pretty?? Yippee!!
            </p>
            <p>
             Ok, moving on, Assembly time....make two identical stars and stitch a button onto the centre of each one.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e06090f4970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0436" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340147e06090f4970b" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340147e06090f4970b-500wi" title="IMG_0436"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             You'll be stitching the two stars together, so hold them with right sides to the outside, and thread a darning needle with a long length of yarn. Start in the space between two triangles and begin over sewing the two stars together, stitching through the inner loops only (picture above)
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669f719970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0437" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669f719970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669f719970c-500wi" title="IMG_0437"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             When you reach the first point of the star you can thread on a small bead or bell.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669f8a4970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0438" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669f8a4970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669f8a4970c-500wi" title="IMG_0438"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Continue stitching round, attaching your beads/bells to the points as you go. After the fifth one, pause and put in the stuffing, then continue to sew up the remainder of the star.
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669f9ca970c-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false" style="display: inline;">
              <img alt="IMG_0439" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00e551101c5488340148c669f9ca970c" src="http://attic24.typepad.com/.a/6a00e551101c5488340148c669f9ca970c-500wi" title="IMG_0439"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             Lastly you will need to make and attach a hanging loop.....I'm so sorry I completely forgot to photograph this for you, but it's easy enough.
            </p>
            <p>
             Crochet a chain of 30, leaving tails at each end which are long enough to sew with. Bring the two ends of the chain together and tie them in a firm knot, then thread both tail ends onto a darning needle and stitch the chain loop securely to the star, as pictured above.
            </p>
            <p>
             I really hope you'll be inspired to give this a go, they are so much fun to make. You can see and read more about my Decorations in this
             <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/weblog/2010/12/crochet-christmas-decorations.html" target="_blank">
              Ta-dah post
             </a>
             .....have fun with it!!
            </p>
