         <p>
          <strong>
           Every mother has a preference to how they prefer to feed their child.
          </strong>
          <strong>
           Some prefer to breastfeed, while others find the bottle easier.
          </strong>
          <strong>
           There are pros and cons to each that every mother-to-be should be aware of.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_833" style="width: 310px">
          <a href="http://zoolababy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/breastfeeding.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
            <img alt="Breastfeeding a Baby" class=" wp-image-833 " height="225" src="http://zoolababy.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/breastfeeding.jpg" title="Breastfeeding a Baby" width="300"/>

            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Breastfeeding a Baby
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <strong>
            Breastfeeding
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Pros
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          There are
          <strong>
           MANY pros to breastfeeding
          </strong>
          . Some woman say that there is no better way to bond than to breastfeed. Thats completelytrue! Especially with full skin to skin contact, breastfeeding within the first 2 hours are crucial for immediate bonding. Many mothers enjoy the feeling of the child laying on them and feel much closer breastfeeding rather than using a bottle. Also,
          <strong>
           pumpedbreast milkcan be stored for months and is free!
          </strong>
          Lets not forget how convenient it is! Theres no bottles to wash or preparation!
         </p>
         <p>
          Theres also many health benefits to breastfeeding. For the mother, engorgement becomes less troublesome. Though even breastfeeding mommies still pump, the need to pump is a lot less. There is less pain and swelling since so much of the milk goes to the baby. Many mothers also claim that they got back to their pre-pregnancy weight thanks to breastfeeding.
          <strong>
           For the baby, there are a lot of health benefits!
          </strong>
          <strong>
           Due to secretory immunoglobulin A (IgA) the immune system is much stronger than a bottle fed child
          </strong>
          . There is no existing formula that can provide lgA. Only breastmilk, specifically colostrum, has this immune aid factor. Many illnesses such as meningitis and ear infections occur less in breastfed children.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Cons
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          When it comes to breastfeeding, there really isnt many cons. the only cons that exist are usually medically for the mother. Some woman fear that they do not produce enough milk to feed their child, though thats uncommon. Many woman feel frustrated when getting into a cycle, especially during the first few tries. Working moms find breastfeeding troublesome, since they have to store and others have to feed with a bottle. Youre diet can effect the breast milk! Mind you a healthy diet with plenty of fruit, veggies and lean meats is just perfect for a healthy milk and a healthy mommy!
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <strong>
            Bottle Feeding
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Pros
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          With bottle-feeding, there arent as many pros as there are breastfeeding.
          <strong>
           Though pros do exist!
          </strong>
          Many working mothers find that using formula is easier for the caregiver while she is away, along with your significant other being able to feed the little one as well. Also
          <strong>
           infants who are formula fed tend to eat less, since the formula stays longer in their digestive system.
          </strong>
          Though it doesnt contain lgA, formula is healthy and provides plenty of nutrients for the infant. Many women also find that bottle-feeding isnt embarrassing to do in public. In these times it is considered more socially acceptable to bottle-feed rather than breastfeed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Cons
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Though not necessarily harmful to the infant, there are quite a few cons to bottle-feeding. Many mothers complain that they dont feel as much of a bond when bottle-feeding. As said before, no formula contains lgA. Though children arent considered to be at a higher risk of illnesses. Obviously there is a cost situation.
          <strong>
           Formula price can really stock up after a while!
          </strong>
          Along with buying and having to constantly clean those pesky bottles!
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Whether you decide to breastfeed or bottle-feed, remember that it is your choice! Think hard about the pros and cons, along with how it will effect you and your little ones life!
          </strong>
         </p>
