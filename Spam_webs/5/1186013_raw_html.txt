      <article class="post-6345 post type-post status-publish format-standard category-farmers-market category-fruits-and-vegetables category-low-carb category-recipe category-uncategorized entry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Spinach and Parmesan Souffles
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-05-25T05:34:32+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 25, 2011
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://preventionrd.com/author/nrmintuos/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Nicole Morrissey
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://preventionrd.com/2011/05/spinach-and-parmesan-souffles/#comments">
           34 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://cf.preventionrd.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_0697.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6363" height="374" src="//preventionrd.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_0697-1024x667.jpg" title="DSC_0697" width="576"/>
         </a>
         Its been a lot of fun and games around the blog, hasnt it? Pepperoni Pizza Puffs, Coconut Banana Bread, and Baked Buffalo Chicken Pastamy, oh my, its been swell.
         <a href="http://cf.preventionrd.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_0738.jpg">
          <br/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         But there are vegetables in my life. I promise. Lots ofem. I puffy heart the green thingsnearly as much as baked doughnuts and all things buffalo saucy.
         <em>
          Nearly.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The farmers markets opened last weekend and while there was no question in my mind that
         <strong>
          I
         </strong>
         would be stopping by, it was Mr. Prevention who suggested it as a Saturday morning activity. We ended up with organic, local, grass-fed cheeses (one low-fat and low-salt that it amazingand the other is a garlic cheddar  yum!), a loaf of cranberry walnut bread that Mr. Prevention has all but polished off (I was good for a nibble here and there  Ive been enjoying the Coconut Banana Bread I made on Saturday
         <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="//preventionrd.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
         ), and a HUGE bag of spinach (nearly a pound) for $2!! And this was sure some fine-looking spinach.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="../wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_0738.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="392" src="../wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_0738-1024x697.jpg" title="DSC_0738" width="576"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         We came home to have spinach salads and grilled chicken for lunch and the following morning I knew this recipe from
         <em>
          Cooking Light
         </em>
         would help put to good use all that fresh spinach. These are packed with spinach and are a protein-rich, nutrient-dense, low-calorie start to your day. They also reheat really well in the microwave, so theyre a perfect make-ahead breakfast for busy weekday mornings!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://cf.preventionrd.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_0691.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6362" height="658" src="//preventionrd.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/DSC_0691-697x1024.jpg" title="DSC_0691" width="448"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         [print_this]
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Spinach and Parmesan Souffles
         </strong>
         from
         <a href="http://www.myrecipes.com/recipe/spinach-parmesan-souffles-50400000112999/">
          <em>
           Cooking Light
          </em>
          June 2011
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Cooking spray
         <br/>
         1 1/2 Tbsp dry breadcrumbs
         <br/>
         1 (6-ounce) package fresh baby spinach
         <br/>
         2/3 cup fat-free milk
         <br/>
         2 Tbsp all-purpose flour
         <br/>
         1/8 tsp salt
         <br/>
         1/8 tsp ground nutmeg
         <br/>
         1/8 tsp freshly ground black pepper
         <br/>
         1/2 cup (2 ounces) freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
         <br/>
         2 large egg yolks
         <br/>
         4 large egg whites
         <br/>
         1/4 tsp cream of tartar
        </p>
        <p>
         Directions:
        </p>
        <p>
         Place a baking sheet in the oven. Preheat oven to 425 F.
        </p>
        <p>
         Coat 4 (6-ounce) ramekins with cooking spray; sprinkle evenly with breadcrumbs, tilting and turning dishes to coat sides completely.
        </p>
        <p>
         Heat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Lightly coat pan with cooking spray. Add spinach; cook for 2 minutes or until spinach wilts, tossing constantly. Place spinach in a colander; let stand 5 minutes. Squeeze excess liquid from spinach. Coarsely chop spinach.
        </p>
        <p>
         Combine 2/3 cup milk and the next 4 ingredients (through black pepper) in a small saucepan over medium-high heat, stirring with a whisk until smooth. Cook for 2 minutes or until mixture is thick and bubbly, stirring constantly. Spoon mixture into a large bowl, and let stand for 10 minutes. Stir in spinach, cheese, and egg yolks.
        </p>
        <p>
         Combine egg whites and cream of tartar in a large bowl, and let stand at room temperature for 15 minutes. Beat with a mixer at high speed until medium peaks form (do not over-beat). Gently stir one-fourth of egg whites into spinach mixture, and gently fold in the remaining egg whites. Gently spoon mixture into prepared dishes. Sharply tap dishes 2 or 3 times on counter to level. Place dishes on preheated baking sheet; return baking sheet to 425 F oven. Immediately reduce oven temperature to 350 F; bake souffles at 350 F for 21 minutes or until puffy and golden brown. Serve immediately. Yield: 4 souffles.
        </p>
        <p>
         Nutrition Information (per souffle): 163 calories; 6 g. fat; 115 mg. cholesterol; 405 mg. sodium; 14.6 g. carbohydrate; 1.4 g. fiber; 13.2 g. protein
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Result:
         </strong>
         These were very good and perfect for the spinach-lover! Mr. Prevention commented that there was a lot of spinach, but in a good way. There is the perfect balance between the savory cheese and hearty spinach and eggs. They werent exactly a breeze to makein that, I dirtied a lot of dishes! Worth it! Enjoy!
        </p>
