       <div class="entry-content editorial" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>
         The Night VI wowed everybody last year with their breakout single, Thinking Of You, and then, after retreating back into the studios for a spell, they released two more tracks a couple months ago, eliminatingany doubts about the bands legitimacy. Their sound is surely refined, but the band takes sonic risks that will keep your ears locked on the speakers when the music is playing, and their ability to fuse technically flawless instrumentation with modern pop is, at times, shockingly smooth. Its like theyve just given you a music lesson without you even knowing it. You didnt sign up for one, and it may be subliminal, but you got the lesson anyway, and now youre a more well-rounded music junkie because of it.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Night VIs newest single, Wonderlust is much more upbeat than their other work, and yes, those synths wear their 80sheritage on their sleeve with pride, but the song is still very much the technical masterpiece that the bands other pieces are. The guitar riffs and bassline are relatively simple, but thats not a product of the song lacking complexity; rather, The Night VI hastrimmed all the fat, and whats left is a track that doesnt loseits shine no matter how many times you hit the replay button.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thematically, Wonderlust is a lot different then Sienna or Thinking Of You, and to be honest, the new sound threw us for a loop at first. However, once you get acclimated, the quality of the music really sinks in, and its becoming more apparent that The Night VI is not only exceptional-sounding, but highlyversatile, too.
        </p>
