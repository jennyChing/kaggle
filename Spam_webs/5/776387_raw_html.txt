       </h1>

        <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1422053137102" id="item-54c2caf6e4b03dfa2a2b1beb">
         <div class="row sqs-row">
          <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-77911ff62099e089f9d5">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Nobody wants stained, discolored teeth. A bright smile can make you more confident, and makes anyone appear more youthful. Though darkening teeth is a natural part of aging, some of the things you eat and drink every day can also be staining your teeth. So what foods should you avoid, and what can you eat instead?
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                The first thing to know is that acid isnt something you want hanging out in your mouth very long. Your teeth are covered by hard tissue called enamel, which keeps them strong. Acids eat away at the enamel and expose little holes in it, making it look more like the surface of a sponge. When your enamel is porous, it absorbs stains more easily and can make you more prone to cavities. Something acidic
               </span>
               <em>
                and
               </em>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                brightly colored, like cherries, are doubly dangerous. They can damage your enamel
               </span>
               <em>
                and
               </em>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                stick to your teeth. Our saliva naturally neutralizes acids, which is great, but it takes about 20 minutes for our mouths to reach a neutral state after eating.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Your best defense against acidic, staining foods and drinks? Water! Not only does it keep you hydrated, but it also rinses away damaging acids and staining particles. You might be tempted to brush your teeth right after eating something acidic, but you may be better off drinking and rinsing with water right after you enjoy that glass of wine or cup of Earl Grey to minimize and neutralized the acids first. Then, 20 to 30 minutes later, brush with a fluoride toothpaste to help strengthen the enamel and help undo the damage caused by the acids.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Here are 6 foods that stain your teeth, and some suggestions for how to keep your teeth white:
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <span>
               <strong>
                1. Black Tea (and Coffee)
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_19336">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below ">
              <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:700.0px;">
               <div class="image-block-wrapper has-aspect-ratio" data-description='&lt;p&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;em&gt;Photo: &lt;/em&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/laszlo-photo/3873543219"&gt;Laszlo Ilye&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;' style="padding-bottom:66.57142639160156%;">
                <noscript>
                 <img alt="Photo: Laszlo Ilye" src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2cbf0e4b0dec48bbfb4e1/1422052337972/"/>
                </noscript>
                <img alt="Photo: Laszlo Ilye" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2cbf0e4b0dec48bbfb4e1/1422052337972/" data-image-dimensions="700x466" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="54c2cbf0e4b0dec48bbfb4e1" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2cbf0e4b0dec48bbfb4e1/1422052337972/" data-type="image"/>
               </div>
               <div class="image-caption-wrapper">
                <div class="image-caption">
                 <p>
                  <span>
                   <em>
                    Photo:
                   </em>
                   <em>
                    <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/laszlo-photo/3873543219">
                     Laszlo Ilye
                    </a>
                   </em>
                  </span>
                 </p>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_19563">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Believe it or not, black tea stains your teeth worse than coffee! Thats because black tea contains more of a natural vegetable acid called tannic acid, or tannins. Tannins stick to your teeth because theyre attracted to the protein in enamel. (Coffee contains tannins too; just less.)
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               <strong>
                Solution:
               </strong>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Drink green or white tea, as they dont contain as much tannins. Or lighten your tea or coffee with milk to reduce its stain potential. As a bonus, some green tea has fluoride in it, and mint tea can freshen your breath.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <span>
               <strong>
                2. White Wine
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Like tea, wine contains teeth-staining tannins. Red and white wine are both hard on your teeth, but white wine actually has more acid than red! So drinking white wine makes your teeth more likely to absorb stains from anything else youre eating at the same time.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               <strong>
                Solution:
               </strong>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Rinse your mouth with water after that glass of wine. Or
               </span>
               <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/04/04/why-wine-stains-teeth_n_844181.html">
                <span style="font-size:15px">
                 eat a protein like cheese
                </span>
               </a>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                along with it, which will help protect your teeth. If possible, brush your teeth half an hour later.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <span>
               <strong>
                3. Carbonated, Energy, and Sports Drinks
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_23283">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-below ">
              <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:691.0px;">
               <div class="image-block-wrapper has-aspect-ratio" data-description='&lt;p&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;em&gt;Photo: &lt;/em&gt;&lt;a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/fivehanks/6138640703"&gt;&lt;em&gt;Five Hanks&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;' style="padding-bottom:68.01736450195312%;">
                <noscript>
                 <img alt="Photo: Five Hanks" src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2cc72e4b02f4c0b408c64/1422052469162/"/>
                </noscript>
                <img alt="Photo: Five Hanks" class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2cc72e4b02f4c0b408c64/1422052469162/" data-image-dimensions="691x470" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="54c2cc72e4b02f4c0b408c64" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2cc72e4b02f4c0b408c64/1422052469162/" data-type="image"/>
               </div>
               <div class="image-caption-wrapper">
                <div class="image-caption">
                 <p>
                  <span>
                   <em>
                    Photo:
                   </em>
                   <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/fivehanks/6138640703">
                    <em>
                     Five Hanks
                    </em>
                   </a>
                  </span>
                 </p>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_23513">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Soda, Gatorade, Monster energy drinks -- these all are bad news for your teeth. They not only have a high acid content, making your teeth vulnerable, but theyre also high in sugar, so cavities are practically inevitable.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               <strong>
                Solution:
               </strong>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Try to drink less of these drinks, or drink water afterwards. If youre an athlete using sports drinks for electrolyte replacement, consider a natural alternative, like coconut water. If you cant quite kick the habit, sip acidic drinks through a straw to keep them away from your teeth.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <h2>
              <span>
               <strong>
                4. Berry Pie
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
             <p>
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Berries -- as well as red wine and brown soda -- contain chromogens. What are those? Theyre vivid pigments that stick to your teeth. This means that big slice of cherry pie can leave your pearly whites not so white. The same goes for similar fruits like cranberries, blueberries, blackberries, grapes, and pomegranates, as well as fruit juice.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              <span>
               <strong>
                Solution:
               </strong>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Continue to eat berries, just in moderation (hey, theyre still good for you). Try fruits and veggies like pineapple, pears, cauliflower, apples, and cucumbers, which help keep teeth white by
               </span>
               <a href="http://www.care2.com/greenliving/12-best-and-worst-foods-for-your-teeth.html">
                <span style="font-size:15px">
                 boosting saliva production
                </span>
               </a>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                .
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block image-block sqs-block-image sqs-col-6 span-6 float float-right" data-block-type="5" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_29103">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <div class="image-block-outer-wrapper layout-caption-hidden ">
              <div class="intrinsic" style="max-width:283.0px;">
               <div class="image-block-wrapper has-aspect-ratio" data-description="" style="padding-bottom:149.8233184814453%;">
                <noscript>
                 <img src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2ccd3e4b0a740d54dceb0/1422052565128/"/>
                </noscript>
                <img class="thumb-image" data-image="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2ccd3e4b0a740d54dceb0/1422052565128/" data-image-dimensions="283x424" data-image-focal-point="0.5,0.5" data-image-id="54c2ccd3e4b0a740d54dceb0" data-load="false" data-src="http://static1.squarespace.com/static/52aa12a3e4b0291b4d27a2f4/t/54c2ccd3e4b0a740d54dceb0/1422052565128/" data-type="image"/>
               </div>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_37383">
            <div class="sqs-block-content">
             <h2 id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_36967">
              <span>
               <strong>
                5. Curries and Tomato Sauce
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
             <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_34649">
              <span id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_35666">
               <span id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_35665" style="font-size:15px">
                Sad news for anyone who likes pizza, spaghetti, or lasagna. Not only are curries and tomato sauce highly saturated, color-wise, but theyre also acidic (especially tomato sauce). Sounds like a recipe for stained teeth, right?
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_34650">
              <span>
               <strong>
                Solution:
               </strong>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                If possible, try alfredo or white wine sauce instead. If you cant stay away from that yellow curry or Italian dish, eat spinach beforehand, which can help counteract the acid. Or pop in some sugarfree (or, better yet, Xylitol) gum afterwards.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <h2 id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_34651">
              <span>
               <strong>
                6. Balsamic Vinegar
               </strong>
              </span>
             </h2>
             <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_34652">
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Before you drizzle that balsamic vinegar on your tomatoes and mozzarella, know that it too can discolor your teeth. According to
               </span>
               <a data-cke-saved-href="http://www.prevention.com/health/health-concerns/25-foods-dentists-wont-eat?s=21" href="http://www.prevention.com/health/health-concerns/25-foods-dentists-wont-eat?s=21">
                <em>
                 Prevention
                </em>
               </a>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                , In one recent study, teens who ate vinegar-heavy foods had a 30 to 85% increased risk of enamel erosion.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_34653">
              <span>
               <strong>
                Solution:
               </strong>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                Veggies! Lettuce is one food that can counteract the effects of vinegar. So save vinegar for a salad dressing. Or munch on
               </span>
               <a data-cke-saved-href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/photogallery/12-foods-that-stain-teeth.aspx#10" href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/photogallery/12-foods-that-stain-teeth.aspx#10">
                <span style="font-size:15px">
                 broccoli
                </span>
               </a>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                before. Like spinach, it coats your teeth, protecting them from vinegars effects.
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p id="yui_3_17_2_1_1422052049642_34654">
              <span>
               <span style="font-size:15px">
                The take-home message? Eat the foods you love, just know the risks. I could never tell anyone not to enjoy their morning coffee. Just try to drink it all in one sitting, rather than sipping all day long, to keep the acid and staining (and therefore the damage to your teeth) to a minimum. The same goes for staining foods. Water is your best bet when it comes to neutralizing acids and rinsing away stain. If youre still noticing your teeth are not as bright as you would like them to be, talk to your dentist!
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
