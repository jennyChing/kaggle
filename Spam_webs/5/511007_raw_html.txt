          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0828.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6769" height="578" src="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0828-680x1024.jpg" title="morning sickness tea remedy" width="384"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           I dont have terrible morning sicknessmeaning, I havent ever thrown upbut I do get sick to my stomach, dont want to eat a thing even though Im hungry, curl up in a ball and WISH I was throwing up. Sometimes I try drinking sprite or ginger ale and that helps a little, but I recently found the BEST REMEDY EVER. (
           <em>
            no, this post is in NO way sponsored, haha)
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           First, start with some
           <a href="http://www.earthmamaangelbaby.com/pregnancy/morning-wellness-tea.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.earthmamaangelbaby.com/pregnancy/morning-wellness-tea.html', 'Morning Wellness Tea']);" target="_blank">
            Morning Wellness Tea
           </a>
           from Earth Mama Angel Baby. Fill a cup with water and steam itI just put it in the microwave for a couple of minutes. Throw in the tea bag and then get your RAISINS!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           <a href="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/morning-sickness-remedy.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6770" height="427" src="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/morning-sickness-remedy.jpg" title="morning sickness remedy" width="544"/>
           </a>
           <br/>
           Yea, I said raisins. Is that normal to add to your tea?! Ive never heard of it before, but I am NOT a tea drinker. My husband threw them into my cup and I was all, what are you doing?!?! gross! but it wasnt gross at all.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0825.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6771" height="607" src="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0825-680x1024.jpg" title="raisins in tea" width="403"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           THEN as I said, Im not usually a tea drinker. Its just not appealing to me. But I was determined to make this work. Not that the tea + raisins was bad I actually enjoyed it (especially because my stomach was feeling better), but then.. I added this:
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0827.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6772" height="578" src="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0827-680x1024.jpg" title="coffee creamer" width="384"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Mmmm hmmm. Vanilla Caramel coffee creamer. YUM. Just a bit though!! Too much and itll be too sweet and gross. I add no more than a tablespoon, Id guess.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0831.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-6773" height="607" src="http://babydickey.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/DSC_0831-680x1024.jpg" title="morning sickness recipe" width="403"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: left;">
           Now I have to get up and go make some, yummm. Will you try it?! Does it sound/look good?! IT IS. And it really does put my stomach at ease. Did you have any creative morning sickness remedies?
          </p>

          <p>
           My accupuncurist had me eat Congee every morning. At first I did- and never felt sick so I thought I just didnt have morning sickness so I stopped- I went right back to eating Congee every morning.
          </p>
          <p>
           Congee-
          </p>
          <p>
           1 cup of Millet
           <br/>
           6 cups of water
          </p>
          <p>
           Bring to boil and simmer for an hour stirring occassionally.
          </p>
          <p>
           It ends up like a thick oatmeal.  I flavored with butter, dried cranraisins, sugar.  Anything you like on your oatmeal.  Just 1/4 cup every day kept the morning sickness away.
          </p>
