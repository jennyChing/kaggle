        <p>
         You might have seen StumbleUpon has expanded their share feature to allow you to send a stumble to all your friends.
        </p>
        <p>
         At first this looks cool, obvious and useful  but I wonder if StumbleUpon will regret it?
         <span id="more-11141">
         </span>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          To send a share message to all your friends you need to go to your own profile.
         </li>
         <li>
          Go to your favorites and hover over one of the stories.
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 500px">
           <img alt="StumbleUpon Share to All Friends" height="240" src="//img.skitch.com/20090311-trrfw6pdpb96sh9g1rnd8s5thu.jpg" width="490"/>
           <p class="wp-caption-text">
            StumbleUpon Share to All Friends
           </p>
          </div>
         </li>
         <li>
          A share button will appear over the image thumbnail.
         </li>
         <li>
          Hit the share link and a new sidebar will appear.
         </li>
         <li>
          From there you can click individuals or select them all.
         </li>
         <li>
          You can also enter a message to go out to all the share requests.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         The problem is, does this make it TOO easy?
        </p>
        <p>
         Before you had to think about who you were sharing with, now you can just blast them all.
        </p>
        <p>
         I just tried it (with someone elses story, not a chrisg.com article!) and it look seconds. You would have thought this would get abused heavily and in short order.
        </p>
        <p>
         What do you think? A feature too far?
        </p>
