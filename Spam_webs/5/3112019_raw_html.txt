     <p>
      <br/>
      <b>
       Pacifica Graduate Institute
      </b>
      is an accredited American
      <a href="/wiki/Graduate_school" title="Graduate school">
       graduate school
      </a>
      with two campuses near
      <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara,_California" title="Santa Barbara, California">
       Santa Barbara, California
      </a>
      . The institute offers masters and doctoral degrees in the fields of clinical psychology, counseling, mythological studies, and the humanities.
     </p>
     <p>
      The institute is affiliated with the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC), specifically the Accrediting Commission for Senior Colleges and Universities. The institute first gained accreditation on June 30, 1997, and continues to be accredited through WASC.

     <p>
      Pacifica traces many of its central ideas to the heritage of ancient story tellers, dramatists, and philosophers from all lands who recorded the workings of the imagination. The legacies of these early men and women have evolved in multiple cultural contexts and perspectives including the systematic explorations of the unconscious by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freud" title="Freud">
       Freud
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jung" title="Jung">
       Jung
      </a>
      , and other scholars of the inner life.
     </p>
     <p>
      The concepts of
      <a href="/wiki/Depth_psychology" title="Depth psychology">
       depth psychology
      </a>
      resulting from this long development are at the core of Pacifica's orientation. These ideas, such as the importance of symbol and metaphor in personal and cultural imagery and the recognition of the dynamic interplay between the natural world and the world of the human psyche, are articulated in the institute's programs.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_statement_and_core_values">
       Mission statement and core values
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacifica_Graduate_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mission statement and core values">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The mission of Pacifica Graduate Institute is to foster creative learning and research in the fields of psychology and mythological studies, framed in the traditions of depth psychology.
     </p>

        Clinical Psychology
       </a>
      </li>

       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph.D." title="Ph.D.">

        Clinical Psychology
       </a>
      </li>
      <li>
       M.A./ Ph.D. in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Depth_Psychology" title="Depth Psychology">
        Depth Psychology

      </li>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacifica_Graduate_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Pacifica Graduate Institute dates its life as an educational institution from the 1976 inauguration of a nine-month para-professional Counseling Skills Certificate program offered by the Human Relations Center.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-history-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The institute's original name was the Human Relations Institute.
     </p>
     <p>
      The M.A. in Counseling Psychology was initiated in 1982.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-history-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1984 the institute announced a new M.A. Counseling Psychology program with an emphasis in
      <a href="/wiki/Depth_psychology" title="Depth psychology">
       depth psychology
      </a>
      . The program was launched in 1984 by Stan Passy, who drew on his doctoral work in archetypal psychology with
      <a href="/wiki/James_Hillman" title="James Hillman">
       James Hillman
      </a>
      at the University of Dallas. Faculty and visiting lecturers have included
      <a href="/wiki/Marion_Woodman" title="Marion Woodman">
       Marion Woodman
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Moore" title="Thomas Moore">
       Thomas Moore
      </a>
      , Robert A. Johnson, and
      <a href="/wiki/Marija_Gimbutas" title="Marija Gimbutas">
       Marija Gimbutas
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-history-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The name of the school was changed to the Pacifica Graduate Institute in 1989.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_3-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-history-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      One of the supporters of the institute's vision in the early years was the late mythologist
      <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Campbell" title="Joseph Campbell">
       Joseph Campbell
      </a>
      . He offered guidance to the school's founders and appeared many times as a guest speaker in the institute's public conference series. After his death, his widow,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Erdman_Campbell" title="Jean Erdman Campbell">
       Jean Erdman Campbell
      </a>
      , felt that Pacifica would be able to carry Campbell's work into the future and, thus, would be the most appropriate home for his archives. She donated his 3,000-book library and archival collection to the Center for the Study of Depth Psychology, an independent non-profit organization housed at the Pacifica campus. The Joseph Campbell Archives and Library were installed in the school's Seminar Building and the home of the collection was formally dedicated in 1992.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The mythological studies program was created in 1994 by
      <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Young_(psychologist)" title="Jonathan Young (psychologist)">
       Jonathan Young
      </a>
      , building on his work as founding curator of the Joseph Campbell Archives.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The program combines folklore, literature, creative studies, and archetypal psychology.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LaderaCampus.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1500" data-file-width="2250" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bf/LaderaCampus.jpg/220px-LaderaCampus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bf/LaderaCampus.jpg/330px-LaderaCampus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bf/LaderaCampus.jpg/440px-LaderaCampus.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LaderaCampus.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Ladera Lane Campus
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Reaching full capacity for Santa Barbara County permitted operations at the Lambert Road campus in 1999, Pacifica began offering additional sections of existing programs at La Casa de Maria's newly established Ladera Lane Retreat Center (site of a former Jesuit novitiate). Pacifica became a two-campus school by purchasing the Ladera Lane center from La Casa de Maria in September 2005.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Engaged Humanities &amp; Creative Life program, along with the Jungian &amp; Archetypal Studies specialization of the M.A./Ph.D. program are the two online/hybrid programs Pacifica offers, with the majority of coursework accomplished online by students. The online studies are combined with quarterly four-day visits either on campus or at the New York Open Center. All other degrees are completed in monthly three-day or four-day retreats and one summer week annually.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Public_programs">
       Public programs
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacifica_Graduate_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Public programs">

     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:WoodmanPublicProgram.jpg">

     <p>
      Pacifica's public programs explore the fields of psychology and mythological studies through the lens of depth psychology. Programs cover a wide range of topics from humanities and culture to counseling and clinical psychology. Most of the public programs offer continuing education units for professionals in the field. These events are open to the general public with the intention of bringing Pacifica's educational resources to the wider community. Past presenters include Joseph Campbell,
      <a href="/wiki/Jean_Houston" title="Jean Houston">
       Jean Houston
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_J._Meade" title="Michael J. Meade">
       Michael J. Meade
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Huston_Smith" title="Huston Smith">
       Huston Smith
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Malidoma_Patrice_Som%C3%A9" title="Malidoma Patrice Som">
       Malidoma Patrice Som
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Sonu_Shamdasani" title="Sonu Shamdasani">
       Sonu Shamdasani
      </a>
      , Thomas Moore,
      <a href="/wiki/Marion_Woodman" title="Marion Woodman">
       Marion Woodman
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/James_Hillman" title="James Hillman">
       James Hillman
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bly" title="Robert Bly">
       Robert Bly
      </a>
      .

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">
       Campuses
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacifica_Graduate_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Campuses">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Pacificas two campus locations are located within three miles of each other in the foothills of the Santa Barbara suburbs. The 13 acre Lambert Road Campus is the former Max Fleishman estate. The Ladera Lane campus is 35 acres (14ha) with views of the Pacific Ocean and Channel Islands. Each campus contains multiple gardens, while the Lambert Campus hosts the larger Organic Garden. The Organic Garden, consisting of 7 acres (2.8ha), is farmed year round, producing vegetables and fruits available to the community.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Alumni_Association">
       Alumni Association
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacifica_Graduate_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Alumni Association">

     <p>
      Pacifica Graduate Institute Alumni Association (the Association or PGIAA) was formed on 17 April, 2013. PGIAA is a California 501(c) Nonprofit Corporation for the purposes of developing an operational and charitable membership association that supports both the alumni (Alumni or alums) of Pacifica Graduate Institute and the wider community in pursuit of developing their intellectual, spiritual, altruistic, and professional capabilities.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The PGIAA pursues and embodies eight vision statements emphasizing community, Gaia responsibility, ambassadorship, collective memory, sponsorship, sustainability, gratefulness, and groundedness.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Joseph_Campbell_Collection_at_OPUS">
       Joseph Campbell Collection at OPUS
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pacifica_Graduate_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Joseph Campbell Collection at OPUS">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:OpusStacks2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3400" data-file-width="5100" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/0b/OpusStacks2.jpg/220px-OpusStacks2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/0b/OpusStacks2.jpg/330px-OpusStacks2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/0b/OpusStacks2.jpg/440px-OpusStacks2.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:OpusStacks2.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Archival stacks of the OPUS Archives and Research Center
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CampbellLibrary.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1500" data-file-width="1500" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/24/CampbellLibrary.jpg/220px-CampbellLibrary.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/24/CampbellLibrary.jpg/330px-CampbellLibrary.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/24/CampbellLibrary.jpg/440px-CampbellLibrary.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CampbellLibrary.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Joseph Campbell Library on Lambert Campus
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Campbell" title="Joseph Campbell">
       Joseph Campbell
      </a>
      's papers and collections have been entrusted to the OPUS Archives and Research Center on the campuses of Pacifica Graduate Institute. The renowned author, scholar, and mythologist was a long-time friend of Pacifica and a frequent guest lecturer. After Campbell's death,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_Erdman_Campbell" title="Jean Erdman Campbell">
       Jean Erdman Campbell
      </a>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Campbell_Foundation" title="Joseph Campbell Foundation">
       Joseph Campbell Foundation
      </a>
      donated his papers, books and other effects to the Center for the Study of Depth Psychology at Pacifica. The center became OPUS Archives and Research Center and is the home of the collection.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-opus_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-opus-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The founding curator,
      <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Young_(psychologist)" title="Jonathan Young (psychologist)">
       Jonathan Young
      </a>
      , worked closely with Jean Erdman Campbell to gather the materials from Campbell's homes in
      <a href="/wiki/Honolulu" title="Honolulu">
       Honolulu
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Village" title="Greenwich Village">
       Greenwich Village
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York City
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Campbell Collection features approximately 3,000 volumes and covers a broad range of subjects including anthropology,
      <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">
       folklore
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">
       religion
      </a>
      , literature, and psychology. The collection also includes audio and video tapes of lectures, original manuscripts, and research papers. The mission of Opus Archives and the Research Center is to preserve, develop, and extend to the world the archival collections and libraries of eminent scholars in the fields of depth psychology, mythology and the humanities. The center is a living archive, supporting interdisciplinary dialogue, education, grants, research opportunities and public programs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-opus_10-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-opus-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The director of the OPUS Archives and Research Center is Safron Rossi. The current special collections librarian is Richard Buchen. OPUS Archives &amp; Research Center is free and open to the public and also hosts the Joseph Campbell Mythological RoundTable, a monthly discussion group in Santa Barbara.
     </p>

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
