       <div class="headline_area">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes  Gluten-free and Dairy-free
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           Beth @ Tasty Yummies
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/tastyyummies/13288033074/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/tastyyummies/13288033074/', '');" title="Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes - Gluten-free and Dairy-free by Tasty Yummies, on Flickr">
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
           <img alt="Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes - Gluten-free and Dairy-free" height="640" src="http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3686/13288033074_8fdb81a816_z.jpg" width="426"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://tasty-yummies.com/2014/03/20/grain-free-cardamom-vanilla-bean-cupcakes-gluten-free-dairy-free/&amp;media=http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3686/13288033074_8fdb81a816_z.jpg&amp;description=Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes  Gluten-free and Dairy-free')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I feel like I am constantly reiterating how aloof I am about most sweets, but here I am again. I honestly just dont have much of a sweet tooth, never have. Yet, as of late, I feel like I am constantly creating and sharing sweets and baked good recipes.I was thinking about why that might be  as someone who is much more into savory cooking. I started to realize that it was a subconscious decision I was making because of how much happiness sweets bring people and how intimidating baking and desserts can be with a restricted diet.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
          <img alt="Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes - Gluten-free and Dairy-free" class="aligncenter" height="640" src="http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3834/13287826083_a81b29d10f_z.jpg" width="426"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://tasty-yummies.com/2014/03/20/grain-free-cardamom-vanilla-bean-cupcakes-gluten-free-dairy-free/&amp;media=http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3834/13287826083_a81b29d10f_z.jpg&amp;description=Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes  Gluten-free and Dairy-free')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         More specifically when I say people  besides all of my lovely readers, I really mean my husband, Mark. He is a self-professed sweets junkie. He loves any and all sweets and has been known to eat a slice of cake for breakfast, on occasion. So, whenever I can create a healthier sweet that not only works for my dietary restrictions, but also makes him very happy, it is a HUGE win, in my book. I have been on a roll lately, so I am just going with it.
         <span id="more-5384">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
          <img alt="Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes - Gluten-free and Dairy-free" class="aligncenter" height="421" src="http://farm3.staticflickr.com/2818/13288033254_160cee89ba_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://tasty-yummies.com/2014/03/20/grain-free-cardamom-vanilla-bean-cupcakes-gluten-free-dairy-free/&amp;media=http://farm3.staticflickr.com/2818/13288033254_160cee89ba_z.jpg&amp;description=Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes  Gluten-free and Dairy-free')">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         I had been dreaming of a subtly sweet vanilla bean infused grain-free cupcake of some sort for a while now and I realized that one of my favorite spices, cardamom would be the perfect pairing. These cupcakes have a taste similar to carrot cake  subtle, comforting, spicy and not overly sweet. The creamy coconut cream frosting has complimentary flavors with a bit more of the spicy ground cardamom and two whole vanilla beans, plus a bright pop of citrusy flavor from the orange zest.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         I hope you enjoy these as much as we do!

         <strong>
          <div class="print-this-button-shell">
           <button class="print-this-button" onclick="parent.location='http://tasty-yummies.com/2014/03/20/grain-free-cardamom-vanilla-bean-cupcakes-gluten-free-dairy-free/?printthis=1&amp;printsect=1'" type="button">

          <!-- Print This Section 1 Start -->
          <div class="print-this-content">
           Grain-free Cardamom Vanilla Bean Cupcakes  Gluten-free and Dairy-free
           <br/>
           makes 12 cupcakes
           <p>
             3 cups blanched almond flour (I prefer this brand: http://amzn.to/1j0fGQ4)
            <br/>
             1/4 cup coconut flour
            <br/>
             2 teaspoons baking soda
            <br/>
             1/2 teaspoon sea salt
            <br/>
             1/2 cup coconut sugar
            <br/>
             1 tablespoon ground cardamom
            <br/>
             4 large eggs, room temperature
            <br/>
             1/4 cup honey (I used raw)
            <br/>
             1/2 cup coconut oil, melted
            <br/>
             1/2 cup coconut milk
            <br/>
             1 tablespoon vanilla extract
            <br/>
             seeds scraped from 2 vanilla beans
           </p>
           <p>
            Orange Cardamom Vanilla Bean Frosting (Vegan)
           </p>
           <p>
             1 1/2 cups chilled full fat coconut cream (either from a can of coconut cream or separated from 2+ cans of coconut milk, that has been chilled)
            <br/>
             seeds scraped from 1 or 2 vanilla beans
            <br/>
             2 tablespoons maple syrup or honey
            <br/>
             2 teaspoons vanilla extract
            <br/>
             1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
            <br/>
             zest from one orange, leaving a little aside for garnish
           </p>
           <p>
            Preheat the oven to 350 F. Position your rack in the middle of the oven. Line a muffin tin with paper cupcake liners.
           </p>
           <p>
            In a large mixing bowl add the almond flour, coconut flour, baking soda, salt, coconut sugar and ground cardamom and whisk together until well incorporated. In a medium sized bowl add the eggs, honey, coconut oil, coconut milk, vanilla extract and seeds from the vanilla beans. Whisk until well combined.
           </p>
           <p>
            Add the wet ingredients to the dry. Using a large spatula, gently mix to combine all of the ingredients together. Fold until well incorporated. Using a large scoop (about 3 tablespoons), distribute the batter between 18 muffin wells, filling each nearly all the way full.
           </p>
           <p>
            Bake 25-30 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Let cool 30 minutes in the pan on a cooling rack. Then remove the cupcakes from the pan and allow them to finish cooling on the cooling rack.
           </p>
           <p>
            While cooling, make your frosting. Add the chilled coconut cream and seeds from the vanilla bean(s) to a bowl and using handheld mixer, beat until smooth, creamy, fluffy and whipped. Add in the sweetener, vanilla extract, ground cardamom and orange zest. Whip another 30 seconds to a minute to incorporate and add to your piping bag. I keep this in the freezer for about 15 or 30 minutes while I am waiting for the cupcakes to cool, it allows the frosting to really set up and get thick.
           </p>
           <p>
            Frost the cupcakes and serve immediately or place in the fridge until you are reader to serve. I find storing these cupcakes covered and in the fridge to be best  especially once frosted.
