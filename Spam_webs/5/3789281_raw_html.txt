      <strong>
       DVD video to iPod Touch
      </strong>
      /Nano/Classic/Video and watch PDF or electronic book on iPod.
     </p>
     <p class="page_height3">
      Nowadays, Apple unveils a new music player called the iPod Touch, a new version of its popular media player with wireless internet access and other touch screen features found on the iPhone. With this new
      <strong>
       iPod Touch
      </strong>
      , all of
      <strong>
       iPod shuffle, iPod nano and iPod classic
      </strong>
      go for a version.
     </p>
     <h2 class="title_con">
      How to watch PDF or electronic book on iPod Touch/Nano/Classic/Video
     </h2>
     <p class="page_height3">
      iPod cannot directly display PDF files, the only way is to convert them to plain text and put them in the Notes folder on your iPod. Mind you, the Notes folder only allows a max of 1000 files with none of the files being larger than 4kb.and then copy the *txt file to the 'Note' folder on your iPod. Here are the detailed steps:
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span>
       1
      </span>
      Run iTunes
     </h3>
     <p class="page_height3">
      Enable your iPod as a portable hard drive, plug in your iPod to computer and run iTunes, in the summary tab, check the option named 'enable iPod disk mode'
     </p>
     <p class="page_height3">
      <img alt="How to watch PDF or electronic book on iPod Touch/Nano/Classic/Video " height="111" src="../article-image/iphone/3.jpg" width="295"/>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span>
       2
      </span>
      Download and install iGadget
     </h3>
     <p class="page_height3">
      Download and install iGadget, it can convert and cut your files to 4kb .txt files. Here is the download link:
      <a href="http://www.youtubetoipod.biz/software/igadget.html" target="_blank">
       http://www.youtubetoipod.biz/software/igadget.html
      </a>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span>
       3
      </span>
      start to transfer the files
     </h3>
     <p class="page_height3">
      Connect your iPod to computer, open 'My Computer', open the iPod drive (the new drive with your iPod name), and copy the txt file to the 'Note' folder, done. Then you can read the text file on iPod note.
     </p>
