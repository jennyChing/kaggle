           <p class="page-large-introduction">
            A daredevil dog sparked a major rescue operation when he took a cooling dip in the River Devon.
           </p>
           <p class="page-introduction">
           </p>
           <!--PSTYLE=WB Web Text-->
           <p>
            Around 40 firefighters rushed to Rumbling Bridge in Kinross-shire after Toby the golden labrador tumbled 20ft down a gorge. The 12-year-old pet had been enjoying a walk with his family on Thursday afternoon when he suddenly disappeared from view.
           </p>
           <p>
            Owner Hannie Heyes, of Dunfermline, told The Courier how their day out almost ended in tragedy.
           </p>
           <p>
            We were walking along the path when we realised Toby wasnt there, she said.
           </p>
           <p>
            We discovered him way back down the river, having been carried down by the current and even fallen down a couple of waterfalls. We managed to get him on to a ledge and kept him there until the fire engines arrived.
           </p>
           <p>
            The 19-year-old student looked on as firefighters abseiled down to Toby and secured her beloved pet in a harness.
           </p>
           <p>
            She said: Hes a bit old for all of this, but luckily hes a strong swimmer and loves the water.
           </p>
           <p>
            A Scottish Fire and Rescue Service spokesperson confirmed 11 appliances attended at Rumbling Bridge just after 3pm.
           </p>
           <p>
            They got Toby back on to dry land within an hour, thanks to the efforts of officers.
           </p>
