        <br/>
        Now I want to amaze you a bit. This is the Astrodea Celestial Watch designed by Hideo Uehara and made by the famous watch brand Citizen Japan in a limited edition.
        <a href="http://www.techpin.com/wp-content/citizen-watch-astrodea-celestial-watch-thinkgeek.jpg" title="Citizen Watch">
         <img alt="Citizen Watch" src="http://www.techpin.com/wp-content/citizen-watch-astrodea-celestial-watch-thinkgeek.jpg" style="width: 465px;"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        As you have already guessed, this cool gadget displays 90% of the whole stars visible at 35 North latitude, meaning 1,109 in number and 19 galaxies.It is made of high-quality materials including stainless steel bracelet and 42.5-mm case, a blue dial, and crystal made of anti-reflective Sapphire. There is also the gold platted version with yellow light display.
        <br/>
        Both have a time accuracy of +/-20 seconds and are waterproof.
        <a href="http://www.techpin.com/wp-content/citizen-watch-astrodea-celestial-watch-thinkgeek-3.jpg" title="Citizen Watch">
         <img alt="Citizen Watch" src="http://www.techpin.com/wp-content/citizen-watch-astrodea-celestial-watch-thinkgeek-3.jpg" style="width: 465px;"/>
        </a>
        Not all of us know how to read the astronomical diagram but Citizen provides it together with an English instruction manual to teach the users how to find out Equinox Indications, Sunrise/Sunset, Daily Duration of Sunshine, Meridian, Ecliptic, Celestial Equator, Boundaries of Constellations, and so on.
        <br/>
        In the $599.99 box you receive also a 10x magnifier for close-up viewing.
        <a href="http://www.techpin.com/wp-content/citizen-watch-astrodea-celestial-watch-thinkgeek-2.jpg" title="Citizen Watch">
         <img alt="Citizen Watch" src="http://www.techpin.com/wp-content/citizen-watch-astrodea-celestial-watch-thinkgeek-2.jpg"/>
        </a>
        It is available at
        <a href="http://www.dpbolvw.net/click-2853882-10356324" target="_blank">
         ThinkGeek.com
