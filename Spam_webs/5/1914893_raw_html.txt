       <p>
        <a href="http://thetechjournal.com/tag/t-mobile" target="_blank">
         T-Mobile
        </a>
        CEO John Legere has made a lot of noise since he launched his company on an Un-carrier path. Now, the carrier has unveiled a new deal, offering to pay the early-termination fees of such customers who decide to switch to T-Mobile.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span id="more-170576">
        </span>
        <br/>
        <a href="http://thetechjournal.com/tech-news/industry-news/t-mobile-will-pay-early-termination-fees.xhtml/attachment/t-mobile-ceo" rel="attachment wp-att-170577">
         <img alt="T-Mobile CEO John Legere" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-170577" src="//thetechjournal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/t-mobile-ceo.jpg"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        T-Mobile may not be a part of the big league when it comes to US-based wireless carriers, but the company has reinvigorated since last year and has been launching such deals and offers which are too enticing to ignore. As a result, other carriers have felt the heat and launched similar deals, trimming down the fees for the customers.
       </p>
       <p>
        The latest move from
        <strong>
         T-Mobile
        </strong>
        is one which is sure to put all others such as
        <a href="http://thetechjournal.com/tag/at-t" target="_blank">
         AT&amp;T
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://thetechjournal.com/tag/verizon" target="_blank">
         Verizon
        </a>
        on the defensive. Announced at
        <a href="http://thetechjournal.com/tag/ces-2014" target="_blank">
         CES
        </a>
        , the new deal from T-Mobile is this: go to a
        <strong>
         T-Mobile
        </strong>
        location, trade-in your handset and sign up for T-Mobile wireless service. You will receive credit based on the value of the handset you have traded in and if you have to pay any early-termination fees to the carrier you are switching from, T-Mobile will take care of that.
       </p>
       <div style="width: 800px; height: 20px; padding: 6px 6px 6px 0; float: center; margin-left: 0; margin-right: 0px;">

       <p>
        All you need to do is when you receive the final bill, including the early-termination fees from the carrier you are switching from, mail the bill to
        <strong>
         T-Mobile
        </strong>
        or upload it to
        <em>
         www.switch2tmobile.com
        </em>
        . T-Mobile will then pay the fee for you.
       </p>
       <p>
        Apart from this brilliant offer, T-Mobiles CES session packed a lot more. The company stated that it can finally claim the honor of having the fastest
        <strong>
         4G LTE
        </strong>
        network in the U.S. And although T-Mobile currently has a rather small user base when compared to big-wigs such as AT&amp;T, the company is growing rapidly, thanks to its customer-friendly deals.
