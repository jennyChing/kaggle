     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Bugatti EB110
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Mid-engine_design" title="Mid-engine design">
       mid-engine
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sports_car" title="Sports car">
       sports car
      </a>
      produced by
      <a href="/wiki/Bugatti#Bugatti_Automobili" title="Bugatti">
       Bugatti Automobili S.p.A.
      </a>
      from 1991 to 1995, when the company went bankrupt. It was the only production model made by
      <a href="/wiki/Romano_Artioli" title="Romano Artioli">
       Romano Artioli
      </a>
      's Italian incarnation of
      <a href="/wiki/Bugatti" title="Bugatti">
       Bugatti
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugatti_EB110&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Bugatti EB 110 was unveiled on 15 September 1991, in both
      <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Versailles" title="Palace of Versailles">
       Versailles
      </a>
      and in front of the
      <a href="/wiki/Grande_Arche" title="Grande Arche">
       Grande Arche
      </a>
      de la
      <a href="/wiki/La_D%C3%A9fense" title="La Dfense">
       Dfense
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">
       Paris
      </a>
      , exactly 110 years after
      <a href="/wiki/Ettore_Bugatti" title="Ettore Bugatti">
       Ettore Bugatti
      </a>
      's birth.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1992, a lighter and more powerful model with 450 kW (612 PS) at 8250rpm, the
      <b>
       EB110 SS
      </b>
      (SuperSport) was introduced. This car is capable of 216mph (348km/h) and 0-100km/h (62mph) in 3.2 seconds.
     </p>
     <p>
      Early in 1994
      <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">
       Formula One
      </a>
      driver
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">
       Michael Schumacher
      </a>
      purchased a yellow EB 110 SS, giving the company a great deal of publicity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-schumis-ss_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-schumis-ss-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Schumacher retained the car until 2003.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Derek_Hill_(driver)" title="Derek Hill (driver)">
       Derek Hill
      </a>
      , son of American
      <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">
       Formula One
      </a>
      Champion
      <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">
       Phil Hill
      </a>
      , was one of the three drivers on a team that competed with an EB 110 in the United States at the 1996
      <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Daytona" title="24 Hours of Daytona">
       24 Hours of Daytona
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Hard times hit the company in 1995 and, as result of chairman Artioli's over-ambitious purchase of
      <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Cars" title="Lotus Cars">
       Lotus
      </a>
      in addition to the company's quest to develop the EB112 four door car, the company was bankrupt. Dauer Racing GmbH of
      <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">
       Nuremberg
      </a>
      , Germany, bought the semi-finished EB 110 cars in the assembly plant plus the parts inventory through the bankruptcy trustee. The remaining chassis and a version of the engine were later developed by
      <a href="/wiki/B_Engineering" title="B Engineering">
       B Engineering
      </a>
      into their
      <a href="/wiki/Edonis" title="Edonis">
       Edonis
      </a>
      sports car.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">
       Specifications
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugatti_EB110&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specifications">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_(3).jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1672" data-file-width="2500" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_%283%29.jpg/220px-Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_%283%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_%283%29.jpg/330px-Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_%283%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_%283%29.jpg/440px-Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_%283%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bugatti_EB110_GT_-_Flickr_-_edvvc_(3).jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        View of the V12 through the glass engine cover
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The car has a
      <a href="/wiki/Multi-valve" title="Multi-valve">
       60-valve
      </a>
      , quad-
      <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">
       turbo
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/V12_engine" title="V12 engine">
       V12
      </a>
      powering
      <a href="/wiki/AWD_(vehicle)" title="AWD (vehicle)">
       all four wheels
      </a>
      through a six-speed
      <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">
       gearbox
      </a>
      . The 3.5L (3499cc) engine has a bore of 81mm (3.2in) and a stroke of 56.6mm (2.23in) and is capable of 560 PS (412 kW) at 8000rpm. Acceleration to 100km/h (62mph) takes 3.2 seconds, and the GT has a top speed of 213mph (343km/h).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The car uses a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Double_wishbone" title="Double wishbone">
       double wishbone
      </a>
      suspension, with the chassis built by
      <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale" title="Arospatiale">
       Arospatiale
      </a>
      , an aircraft company, and made from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber" title="Carbon fiber">
       carbon fiber
      </a>
      . Equipped with Gandini's famous lifting scissor doors, it has a glass engine cover that provides a view of the V12 engine along with a speed-sensitive electronic rear wing that can be raised at the flick of a switch. The shift-knob is placed close to the driver to reduce shift times.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cars_based_on_the_EB110">
       Cars based on the EB110
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugatti_EB110&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cars based on the EB110">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="B_Engineering_Edonis">
       B Engineering Edonis
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugatti_EB110&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: B Engineering Edonis">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Edonis" title="Edonis">
       Edonis
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/B_Engineering" title="B Engineering">
       B Engineering
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Edonis" title="Edonis">
       Edonis
      </a>
      is based on the Bugatti EB110
      <i>
       Super Sport
      </i>
      but has been extensively re-engineered, retaining little more than the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graphite-reinforced_plastic" title="Graphite-reinforced plastic">
       carbon-fiber
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Chassis" title="Chassis">
       chassis
      </a>
      from the original Bugatti. Both the exterior and interior of the car have been completely redesigned. The 3.5 liter Bugatti engine has had its
      <a href="/wiki/Engine_displacement" title="Engine displacement">
       displacement
      </a>
      increased from 3500cc to 3760cc. The original four small IHI
      <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">
       turbochargers
      </a>
      have been replaced by two larger units from the same manufacturer. Engine power has been boosted from 450kW (603hp) and 650Nm (480lbft) of
      <a href="/wiki/Torque" title="Torque">
       torque
      </a>
      to 500kW (671hp) at 8000rpm and 735Nm (542lbft).
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/4WD" title="4WD">
       4WD
      </a>
      triple-differential drivetrain from the original Bugatti has been replaced with a much simpler and lighter
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Front_engine,_rear-wheel_drive_layout" title="Front engine, rear-wheel drive layout">
       RWD
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Transaxle" title="Transaxle">
       transaxle
      </a>
      , thus saving approximately 70kg (154lb) from total weight. These power figures give the 1,500kg (3,307lb) Edonis a
      <a href="/wiki/Power-to-weight_ratio" title="Power-to-weight ratio">
       power-to-weight ratio
      </a>
      of 480bhp/ton. In addition, the engine's specific power output is an unprecedented 181bhp/liter. The brand claims a maximum speed of 365km/h (227mph), while accelerating from 0 to 100km/h (62mph) in only 3.9 seconds.
     </p>
     <p>
      B Engineering plans to build only 21 vehicles from chassis originally built for Bugatti by
      <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale" title="Arospatiale">
       Arospatiale
      </a>
      (Most of the remaining chassis delivered to Bugatti prior to their bankruptcy were delivered to Jochen Dauer when he purchased the assets of the company).
     </p>
     <p>
      As well as the
      <a href="/wiki/Edonis" title="Edonis">
       Edonis
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/B_Engineering" title="B Engineering">
       B Engineering
      </a>
      also provide spare parts and service for the EB110.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Dauer_EB110">
       Dauer EB110
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugatti_EB110&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dauer EB110">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg/220px-Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg/330px-Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg/440px-Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Black_Bugatti_EB110.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Dauer EB110.
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Dauer_Sportwagen" title="Dauer Sportwagen">
       Dauer Sportwagen
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">
       Nuremberg
      </a>
      , Germany, bought the remaining stock of EB110 parts from the
      <a href="/wiki/Bugatti" title="Bugatti">
       Bugatti
      </a>
      factory.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A complete spare parts
      <a href="/wiki/Parts_book" title="Parts book">
       catalogue
      </a>
      , with exploded
      <a href="/wiki/Diagram" title="Diagram">
       diagrams
      </a>
      and part numbers is now available from Dauer Sportwagen. The company has used the few remaining incomplete chassis to produce the limited edition Dauer EB110.
     </p>
     <p>
      The revised car weighs 970 pounds (440kg) less than the 4,233 pounds (1,920kg) of the original US-spec Bugatti. The European version weighed 4,145lb (1,880kg), but the Dauer car weighs only 3,263lb (1,480kg), despite adopting the longer US-style bumpers. The crash beams behind the bumper skins are also made from carbon fibre.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Bugatti engine is a 3.5-liter, quad-cam, five-valves-per-cylinder, 60-degree V12 with four IHI turbos. The Dauer engine is rated at 865bhp (645kW) at 8,250 rpm, a boost of 305bhp (227kW) from the GT and 262bhp (195kW) from the SS.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercars.net_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-supercars.net-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The four-wheel-drive car can reach 60mph (97km/h) in just 3.35 seconds,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-supercars.net_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-supercars.net-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      achieve a standing kilometer in under 19 seconds and has an estimated maximum speed of 230mph (370km/h), some 7mph (11km/h) faster than the original. The company Dauer Sportwagen went bankrupt in 2008. All original Bugatti parts especially the high performance parts of the EB110SS and the equipment were bought in 2011 by the company
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Toscana-Motors_GmbH&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Toscana-Motors GmbH (page does not exist)">
       Toscana-Motors GmbH
      </a>
      (Kaiserslautern/Germany).
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Motorsport">
       Motorsport
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bugatti_EB110&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Motorsport">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The EB110 participated in the
      <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">
       24 Hours of Le Mans
      </a>
      in 1994. The car qualified a very competitive 17th overall and 5th in the GT1 class but did not finish the race. The car is now on display at the
      <a href="/wiki/Loh%C3%A9ac" title="Lohac">
       Lohac
      </a>
      Automobile Museum.
     </p>
