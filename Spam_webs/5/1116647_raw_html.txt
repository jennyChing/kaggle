       <h1>
        Ninth day of the eruption
       </h1>
       <h2 class="subtitle">
        Fimmvruhls: eruption in decline?
       </h2>
       <p class="meta">
        <span class="date">
         29.3.2010
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        The eruption in the Eyjafjallajkull volcanic system has been on a small scale so far, and
        <a href="http://hraun.vedur.is/ja/Katla2009/gosplott.html" target="_blank">
         tremor signals
        </a>
        are diminishing, at least for the time being. The
        <a href="http://hraun.vedur.is/ja/skjalftar/silstn.html" target="_blank">
         location of seismographs
        </a>
        at Goabunga (god), Eystri-Skgar (esk), Mimrk (mid) and Lgu-Hvolar (hvo) can be found as black triangles on the map (click to enlarge), additionally Haukadalur (hau) is located just northwest of the map's borders.
       </p>
       <p>
        Even so, the scene has been spectacular and still is. Lava has flowed north into two gullies, see
        <a href="http://www.jardvis.hi.is/Apps/WebObjects/HI.woa/swdocument/1015754/Hraun+21-28+mars+2010.pdf" target="_blank">
         map of extent
        </a>
        (pdf 1,4 Mb) made at the University of Iceland, i.e. the
        <a href="http://earthice.hi.is/" target="_blank">
         Institute of Earth Sciences
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        It has been predicted that lava might reach the river Kross within a few days. Kross flows due west, north of Eyjafjallajkull, and into the larger river Markarfljt. For easy orientation, watch
        <a href="http://www.lmi.is/Files/Skra_0040866.wmv" target="_blank">
         a video
        </a>
        from the
        <a href="http://www.lmi.is/en/" target="_blank">
         National Land Survey of Iceland
        </a>
        , simulating flight from the south. The popular hiking trail is shown in green and the eruptive fissure in red.
       </p>
       <p>
        Some 25,000 people have visited the area in spite of various dangers. Sudden steam explosions, caused by boiling of melt-water from trapped snow, can throw rock and lava high up in the air and quite far away from the source. Volcanoes can emit poisonous gases, some of which are heavy and move along the ground, possibly pooling in hollows and lows in the landscape, e.g. in Hrunagil and Hvannrgil gullies.
        <a href="http://almannavarnir.is/displayer.asp?cat_id=413" target="_blank">
         Civil Protection Department
        </a>
        is on the alert.
       </p>
       <p>
        Over the last few days the air temperatures at Fimmvruhls, 900-1100 m above sea level, have been from -6C down to -14C with strong northeasterly winds and gusts over 20 m/s; in these circumstances
        <a href="/weather/articles/nr/1827">
         wind chill
        </a>
        can be considerable. For weather outlook, please check IMO's
        <a href="/weather/forecasts/text/">
         text forecasts
        </a>
        (preferably) or station forecasts (
        <a href="/weather/forecasts/areas/south/">
         south
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/weather/forecasts/areas/southeast/">
         south-east
        </a>
        ). Remember, that the forecasts are meant for low-land areas. At higher elevations the weather is expected to be much more severe.
       </p>
       <p>
        Numerous
        <a href="http://www.icesar.com/category.aspx?catID=279" target="_blank">
         rescue personnel
        </a>
        assist and guide the visitors and also restrict tourist access. Some areas have been
        <a href="http://almannavarnir.is/displayer.asp?cat_id=416" target="_blank">
         closed intermittently
        </a>
        , especially to the north and west of the eruption.
       </p>
       <p>
        Below are various photos from Fimmvruhls, taken over the last few days.
       </p>
       <p>
        Einar Kjartansson, 27th March 2010:
        <img alt="Eldgos  Fimmvruhlsi" src="/media/jar/myndsafn/medium/EKj2010_0327_202508b.jpg" title="the crater"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        lafur Sigurjnsson, 25th March 2010:
        <img alt="Eldgos  Fimmvruhlsi" src="/media/jar/myndsafn/medium/OlSi-25.03-DSC_4341.jpg" title="lava to the left, water vapour to the right"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        rni Sigursson, 24th March 2010:
        <img alt="svart flatlendi, svrt h, glittir  snj  sprungu" height="333" src="/media/jar/myndasafn/frodleikur/medium/snjosprunga_AS.jpg" title="melt-water pathway in ash-covered snow" width="541"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        rni Sigursson, 24th March 2010:
        <img alt="Vatnsrs" src="/media/jar/myndsafn/medium/AS_2403_906d2_b.jpg" title="melt-water pathway in ash-covered snow"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        rni Sigursson, 24th March 2010:
        <img alt="Bombugat" src="/media/jar/myndsafn/medium/AS2403_6593bebe07_b.jpg" title="ash-covered snow pierced by a lava bomb"/>

        <img alt="Gosstvarnar  Fimmvruhlsi" src="/media/jar/myndsafn/medium/GG_P1000579.JPG" title="blue fumes are aerosols, ash particles and sulphur compounds"/>

        Einar Kjartansson, 22th March 2010:

        See photographer and date above each photo.
       </p>
