                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Summers End Hair Week: The Easy Knotted Updo
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <center>
                      <span class="post-comment-link">
                      </span>
                     </center>
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5769892288027403937" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        And we're back with Thursday's tutorial! I think its always a bonus to look like you've spent much more time on your hair then you actually have, while at the same time having your style look strangely effortless and at ease, and I think this hairstyle does just that!
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-G33orwg57XM/Ug04jHo3cpI/AAAAAAAAM4o/xf6avNzXI-E/s1600/Freckled_fox_hair_tutorial_hairstyle_easy_knotted_updo_pin.jpg"/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="background-color: white;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <span style="line-height: 20px;">
                         Like all the other styles I've been posting this week, this is one of those that can be worn with sunglasses and a pair of jeans during the day, or with a littlesequineddress and some heels for the evening. Those are the best because we busy ladies may not have time to do our hair more then once in a day! haha I really hope you give this one a try, because it will leave you feeling very proud of yourself:) here we go:
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <img border="0" height="342" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-CcV6LS_-Ti0/Ug1GMOZOhJI/AAAAAAAAM5g/xnO0L5U6PxA/s640/Freckled_fox_hair_tutorial_hairstyle_easy_knotted_updo_8.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <b>
                         Supplies:
                        </b>
                        <br/>
                        - brush
                        <br/>
                        - hair serum (or hair pomade, etc)
                        <br/>
                        - two hair ties
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       - bobby pins
                       <br style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;"/>
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        <br/>
                        <b>
                         Method:
                        </b>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <img border="0" height="212" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-7PqH9S6uRhQ/Ug08oxAxwYI/AAAAAAAAM44/xWkkPiIemMw/s640/Freckled_fox_hair_tutorial_hairstyle_easy_knotted_updo_1.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        <b>
                         Step 1:
                        </b>
                        Start by brushing your hair smooth, then add a bit of smoothi
                       </span>
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        ng serum to the outside for some extra shine. Draw a line from the top of your ear to your part and separate that section of hair.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <img border="0" height="418" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-HLovc7CCFow/Ug08wMDXujI/AAAAAAAAM5A/0_-AsnZ-zE0/s640/Freckled_fox_hair_tutorial_hairstyle_easy_knotted_updo_4.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        <b>
                         Step 2:
                        </b>
                       </span>
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        Split it in two, and tie it in a half knot, then again into a full knot at right about below your ear. Separate another section of hair 2-3 inches wide and add it to the knot strand closest to it. then simply tie another half knot!
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <img border="0" height="212" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-FV1BznVKK_8/Ug083_oCFUI/AAAAAAAAM5I/J596b_zyjzI/s640/Freckled_fox_hair_tutorial_hairstyle_easy_knotted_updo_6.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        <b>
                         Step 3:
                        </b>
                       </span>
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        Repeat this step, moving around your head, pulling 6-8 more sections in, until you reach your other ear. Take the last section of hair and, twisting it slightly over itself back towards your head(clockwise), and combining it with the closest knot strand. Finish it all off
                       </span>
                       <span style="background-color: white;">
                        <span style="line-height: 20px;">
                         by continuing to knot all the way to the end and securing it with a hair-tie.
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <img border="0" height="212" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-omUoMRjzR44/Ug08-xi5YzI/AAAAAAAAM5Q/JpYSyo_UmLo/s640/Freckled_fox_hair_tutorial_hairstyle_easy_knotted_updo_7.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="line-height: 20px;">
                        <b>
                         Step 4:
                        </b>
                       </span>
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        Fluff your hair on your scalp a bit for more volume and fullness. Twist the leftover knotted hair back towards the other side of your head, and pin it on top of theoriginalknotted trail, making sure to hide the hair-tie underneath, give your whole head a good spritz, and you're all done!
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                        <img border="0" height="370" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ai7J2w0_p4k/Ug1JAjV-kCI/AAAAAAAAM5w/3HqL2u9htfU/s640/Freckled_fox_hair_tutorial_hairstyle_easy_knotted_updo_9.jpg" width="640"/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="background-color: white;">
                        <span style="line-height: 20px;">
                         So again I just have to add the disclaimer that just because there are a lot of pictures does NOT mean that it is any more complicated.haha If you've been reading for a while then you know that I just like to be extra through with my steps, because there's nothing worse then anincompletetutorial that leaves you wondering what the heck happened between one picture and the next!
                        </span>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="color: #666666; font-family: Georgia, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="background-color: white;">
                        <span style="line-height: 20px;">
                         <br/>
                         I really hope you give this a try for that new class or that party, and then come back and let me know how it went, and how many people complimented you! Its one of those styles that looks great on everyone, and I know you'll love it:)
                        </span>
                       </span>
                       <span style="background-color: white; line-height: 20px;">
                        Thanks so much for reading, and I hope you have a great Thursday!!
                       </span>
                      </span>
