    <h1 style="margin-left: 5px; margin-bottom: 10px; text-align: center;">
     <span itemprop="headline">
      Learn To Read Guitar Sheet Music -
      <br/>
      Free Course
     </span>
    </h1>
    <!--end #playerListTitle-->
   </div>
   <div id="authorName">
    <p style="text-align: right; margin: 5px;">
     <em>
      by Ben Dressen
     </em>
    </p>
   </div>
   <div class="playerList">
    <h2>
     <span itemprop="name">
      About This Course
     </span>
    </h2>
    <section itemprop="articleBody">
     <p>
      <img alt="How To Read Guitar Sheet Music" height="600" src="uploads/19/002_read_guitar.jpg" width="784"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      This course is designed to take you from zero to 'hero'... or as close as possible. It is the result of thousands of hours of teaching students how to read guitar sheet music. Best of all,
      <strong>
       it is FREE
      </strong>
      . Not partly free, not somewhat free... but
      <strong>
       absolutely FREE
      </strong>
      . The only requirement is your willingness and discipline in taking it.
     </p>
     <p>
      The course is brought to you by a few fellow classical guitarists with years of stage and teaching experience. The idea is to spread the knowledge of music notation to as many people as possible and teach them proper reading skills, no matter what style of music they play.
     </p>
     <p>
      We were surprised to find many guitarists didn't have even rudimentary reading skills. Compare this to most other instruments like piano, violin, cello and trumpet where knowing notation is taught early on. Unfortunately for the most part, guitarists are illiterate. This not only impedes development as a musician but also restricts severely the pursuit of advanced concepts like music theory and composition.
     </p>
     <p>
      The hope is that by doing this we will be able to promote solid musical skills that will help guitarists stand out in a musical world that is getting more and more saturated. We believe that learning how to properly read sheet music can help a beginner stand head and shoulders above the rest.
     </p>
     <p>
      Thanks to a partnership with Rezzonator, we're able to offer this course 100% free.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       There's A Reason Why A Pro Knows How To Read Sheet Music
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="Beethoven Mozart Bach Read Sheet Music" height="300" src="uploads/19/001_mozart_beethoven_bach.jpg" width="800"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      At some point, guitarists that don't know how to read music will wonder: Why bother? Can't I just use tabs? It's the same, isn't it?
     </p>
     <p>
      Absolutely not! Many guitarists live with this myth and take it for granted but there's a reason why Beethoven, Mozart, Bach or Tarrega didn't write tabs.
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Tab Gives You Less Than 8 % Of The Information Notation Tells You.
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      This isn't an exaggeration. If you need more information why this is so, please go
      <a href="post/19/Classical_Guitar_Sheet_Music_And_Tabs">
       here
      </a>
      and read
      <a href="post/19/Classical_Guitar_Sheet_Music_And_Tabs">
       this article
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <strong>
       Notation Tells You The Whole Story
      </strong>
     </p>
     <p>
      Learn how to avoid the biggest mistakes that beginners make when they try to learn to read guitar sheet music. These mistakes keep you grounded and make you feel frustrated. If you've already made your first steps reading music you might painting yourself into a corner without even knowing. Ask yourself: Is sheet music reading fun or a real joy-killer?
     </p>
     <p>
      Learn why knowing how to read sheet music is way more important than you might imagine.
     </p>
     <p>
      Most people think that learning how to read guitar sheet music will help them to get access to more material. This is true, but did you know that there are a lot more benefits to knowing how to read notation than you could imagine:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Master The Guitar Neck - No Longer Stuck In Just One Part Of The Neck
       </strong>
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Improve Your Ear - Pick Up Music By Ear More Easily And Better
       </strong>
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Strong Rhythm Skills
       </strong>
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Know How To Play In Time, No Matter What The Style
       </strong>
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Skyrocket Your Music Writing Skills - (Can't Be A Writer Without Knowing The Alphabet)
       </strong>
      </li>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Ability To Adapt And Arrange Music - Bend Music To YOUR Will
       </strong>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      These are just a few. But to put it simply, knowing how to read guitar sheet music properly means you are a Pro. After all, do you think a Pro is diligent about his notation reading skills just so he could brag to his friends that he can read, or do you think there's something in it for him? He understands that knowing how to read music very well means that his skills on the instrument just skyrocketed.
     </p>
     <p>
      This is often why some amateurs struggle on a piece for years whereas a Pro comes in and has the "magic" touch instantly.
     </p>
     <p>
      Notation allows you insight into the music itself, into the mind of the composer and subtleties of the composition itself. Sheet music provides a blueprint of how things work in the smallest detail. But it does more than that, it show you WHY they work the way they do.
     </p>
     <p>
      This knowledge is invaluable for learning, performing and understanding the material on a deeper level. It is also crucial for better understanding technique and how it can be used, adapted and improved.
     </p>
     <p>
      ALL the great things around you, from a tall skyscraper, to your computer, to your car... They all require the ability to read a blueprint from the person that creates them. Without the ability to map out a plan and comprehend what it means, you are at a loss.
     </p>
     <p>
      Without the ability to properly and quickly read a map, your classical guitar playing will be spinning around in circles and you'll be wondering what the problem is.
     </p>
     <p>
      Whenever asked, I always tell students that the ability to read music is crucial. You should pursue this by going to a professional teacher, a music school or take a free course like the one we're offering. You'll be glad you did
     </p>
    </section>

     </span>
    </h2>
    <section itemprop="articleBody">
     <ul>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Is this course free... really free?
       </strong>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Yes, absolutely.
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Does the fact that it's free mean it has little value?
       </strong>
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      Quite the opposite. The only reason it is free is because we're passionate professional classical guitarists and teachers that want to spread the word about the importance of reading music.
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <strong>
        Why do you need my email?
       </strong>
      </li>
     </ul>
