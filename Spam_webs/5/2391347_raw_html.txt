     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mochizuki_Chiyome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Chiyome, a descendant of the 15th century ninja Mochizuki Izumo-no-Kami () of the
      <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dga-ry%C5%AB" title="Kga-ry">
       Kga-ry
      </a>
      , was the wife of Mochizuki Moritoki, a
      <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">
       samurai
      </a>
      lord of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shinano_province" title="Shinano province">
       Shinano
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Saku_District,_Shinano" title="Saku District, Shinano">
       Saku District
      </a>
      and himself a distant relative of Izumo-no-Kami. After Moritoki was killed in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fourth_Battle_of_Kawanakajima" title="Fourth Battle of Kawanakajima">
       Fourth Battle of Kawanakajima
      </a>
      in 1561, Chiyome was left in the care of the
      <a href="/wiki/Daimyo" title="Daimyo">
       daimyo
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Takeda_Shingen" title="Takeda Shingen">
       Takeda Shingen
      </a>
      , an uncle of her late husband and leader of the
      <a href="/wiki/Takeda_clan" title="Takeda clan">
       Takeda clan
      </a>
      . It was then when Shingen approached her and gave her an important mission to recruit women and create an underground network of
      <a href="/wiki/Kunoichi" title="Kunoichi">
       kunoichi
      </a>
      (female ninja) and employ them against the rival warlords. Takedas plan was to have fully trained female operatives who could act as spies and agents used to gather information and deliver coded messages to his allies; Chiyome was the best candidate for this, since she came from a long line of Kga ninja. She accepted the task, set up her operation in the village of Nezu (
      <a class="extiw" href="//ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%A5%A2%E6%B4%A5%E6%9D%91" title="jp:">
       jp:
      </a>
      ) in the
      <a href="/wiki/Shinano_Province" title="Shinano Province">
       Shinshu
      </a>
      region (the present-day city of
      <a href="/wiki/T%C5%8Dmi,_Nagano" title="Tmi, Nagano">
       Tmi, Nagano
      </a>
      ), and began her search for potential candidates for training.
     </p>
     <p>
      Chiyome recruited prostitutes and other wayward women, victims of the civil wars of the
      <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">
       Sengoku period
      </a>
      , and young girls who were either orphaned, lost or abandoned. Many people believed that she was helping these women and giving them an opportunity to start up a new life, but in reality they were trained to become information gatherers and verifiers, seductresses, messengers and, when necessary, assassins. The women were taught all the skills of a
      <a href="/wiki/Miko" title="Miko">
       miko
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Shinto" title="Shinto">
       Shinto
      </a>
      shrine maiden or a wandering priestess), which allowed them to travel virtually anywhere without suspicion, receiving religious education to complete their disguise. Over time, Chiyome's kunoichi learned to effectively use more disguises such as actresses, prostitutes or
      <a href="/wiki/Geisha" title="Geisha">
       geisha
      </a>
      , which allowed them to move freely within villages, towns, castles and temples, and get access to their targets. Eventually, Chiyome and her kunoichi had set up an extensive network of some 200-300 agents that served the Takeda clan and Shingen was always informed of all activities, putting him one step ahead of his opponents at all times until his mysterious death in 1573, after which she disappeared from historical records.
     </p>
