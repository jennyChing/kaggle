         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Homemade Passata di pomodoro: Step by step
         </h1>
         <p class="p-meta">
          <span>
           In
           <a href="http://memoriediangelina.com/category/reference/" title="View all posts in: reference">
            reference
           </a>
           by Frank
          </span>
          <span>
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-08-27T11:15:39+00:00">
            27 August 2014
           </time>
          </span>
          <span>
           <a class="meta-comments" href="http://memoriediangelina.com/2014/08/27/passata-di-pomodoro-step-by-step/#comments" title="Leave a comment on: Homemade Passata di pomodoro: Step by step">
            30 Comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content content">
         <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Homemade Passata di pomodoro: Step by step" data-url="http://memoriediangelina.com/2014/08/27/passata-di-pomodoro-step-by-step/">
         </div>
         <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <strong>
           <em>
            Passata di pomodoro
           </em>
          </strong>
          (or
          <em>
           passata
          </em>
          for short) is the multi-purpose tomato pure that every aspiring Italian cook shouldlearn how to make. Fortunately, its really quick and easy, but you do need one special piece of equipment: a food mill.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The method is simplicity itself: You take ripe tomatoes, simmerthem just long enough so theyve softened a bit, and sieve them though a food mill. Youre done!
          <em>
           Passata
          </em>
          has a million and one uses in Italian cooking, starting with
          <a href="http://memoriediangelina.com/2012/04/22/tomato-sauce-101/" title="Tomato Sauce 101">
           homemade tomato sauce
          </a>
          . The taste of tomato sauce made with freshly made
          <em>
           passata
          </em>
          is incomparable. But passata can be used in any recipe calling for tomatoesthe kind Italians refer to as
          <em>
           in rosso
          </em>
          and as we all know, the are a whole lot of those in Italian cookery.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          You can buy
          <em>
           passata di pomodoro
          </em>
          injars at fancy supermarkets and Italian delis, but its so quick and simple to make at home, why spend the money?And withtomatoes at the height of their season, nows the time to get started.
         </p>
         <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
          Ingredients
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Makes enough for one mason jar
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1 kilo (2 lbs) fresh tomatoes, preferably plum tomatoes
          </li>
          <li>
           A pinch of salt
          </li>
          <li>
           A basil leaf or two (optional)
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h4>
          Directions
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Cut your tomatoes in half and put them all in a saucepan.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-8.jpg">
           <img alt="Passata di pomodoro-8" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9964" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-8.jpg?resize=720%2C481"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Sprinkle with a pinch of salt, cover the saucepan and let the tomatoes simmer over moderate heat, stirring from time to time. After about 5-10 minutes, they should have softened and just begunto melt, like so:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-7.jpg">
           <img alt="Passata di pomodoro-7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9963" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-7.jpg?resize=720%2C481"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Pour the tomatoes into a food mill positionedover a large mixing bowl. Heres my set up:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-6.jpg">
           <img alt="Passata di pomodoro-6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9962" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-6.jpg?resize=481%2C720"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Let the tomatoescool off for a couple of minutes. By this time, depending on your tomatoes, there will be either a little or quite a bit of liquid that will have drained into the bowl. Discard it before proceeding.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Re-position the food mill on top of the bowl androtate the handle until all you have left in the food mill areskins and seeds:
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://i1.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-5.jpg">
           <img alt="Passata di pomodoro-5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9961" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-5.jpg?resize=720%2C481"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Youll want to throwthis stuff away, of course. What youll have in the mixing bowl is your
          <em>
           passata
          </em>
          :
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://i0.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-4.jpg">
           <img alt="Passata di pomodoro-4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9960" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-4.jpg?resize=720%2C481"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Transfer the pureinto a mason jar or any other container you want. If you like, nestle a basil leaf or two in the
          <em>
           passata
          </em>
          . Let itcool off completely before closing the container. Passata is good for about a week in the fridge, and it freezes successfully. You can also put it up for out of season enjoyment (see Notes).
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://i2.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-1.jpg">
           <img alt="Passata di pomodoro-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9957" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/memoriediangelina.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Passata-di-pomodoro-1.jpg?resize=481%2C720"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h4>
          Notes
         </h4>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Afood mill is essential for making
          <em>
           passata di pomodoro
          </em>
          and, in fact, its essential equipment for any Italian kitchen in general. Food mills are very affordable and you can buy them most anywhere that sells cookware or
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_ss_c_0_9?url=search-alias%3Dgarden&amp;field-keywords=food+mill&amp;sprefix=food+mill%2Caps%2C334" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/s/ref=nb_sb_ss_c_0_9?url=search-alias%3Dgarden&amp;field-keywords=food+mill&amp;sprefix=food+mill%2Caps%2C334', 'online');">
           online
          </a>
          . They may be a bit old-fashioned, but technologyhasnt managed to replace them yet. I love blenders and food processors, but they wont do for some jobs. The beauty of afood mill is that it filters out the stuff you dont want while it pures, like tomatoskins and seeds. The alternative would be skinning and seeding the tomatoes before you pure and thats tedious work. Food mills usually come with 2 or 3 disks that produce finer or rougher pure. I usually opt for the medium disk, it lets a fewof the seeds through but it produces a slightly roughtexture that I like. The finest disk will produce a perfectly smooth, seedless
          <em>
           passata
          </em>
          , close to the kindyou will usually find commercially. Avoid anydisk with very large holes, as it will let all the seeds through and bits of skin as well.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The best tomatoes for making
          <em>
           passata
          </em>
          are the oval-shaped plum variety. These babies are
          <em>
           meant
          </em>
          for sauceand indeed plum tomatoesare sometimes called sauce tomatoes for that very reason. They tend to be less watery, with more fleshand fewer seeds, than other tomatoes. But, in a pinch, almost any type of tomato will do, so long as they are ripe and full of flavor. You may need to let the tomatoes drain a bit longer if youre working with larger, more watery varieties.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          If you want to put up your
          <em>
           passata
          </em>
          for enjoying out of season, you should proceed just as if you were putting up whole peeled tomatoes: sterilize your mason jars before filling them, seal them tight and then boil the jars for a good 45 minutes. (Perhaps one day soon Ill do a post on that, too.)
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The only slightly tricky part of making
          <em>
           passata di pomodoro
          </em>
          is the brief simmering step. Youll be simmering your tomatoes dry, i.e. without any oil or other cooking medium, so you need to be a bit careful to avoid scorching. A fewtips will help here: Add a pinch of salt to draw out the tomatoes own liquid, use moderate heat and stir from time to time. Oh, and make sure you dont get distracted and walk away from your tomatoes. They will burntrust me, I know from experience!
         </p>

             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Re-position the food mill on top of the bowl and rotate the handle until all you have left in the food mill are skins and seeds. You'll want to throw this stuff away, of course. What you'll have in the mixing bowl is your passata.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Transfer the pure into a mason jar or any other container you want. If you like, nestle a basil leaf or two in the passata. Let it cool off completely before closing the container.
             </li>
            </ol>

           <section class="x-comment-content">
            <p>
             V useful post. Like you say, the flavour is always better than shopbought. Ive been using a similar recipe from a Barbara Kafka book for a few years now, as you might expect with Kafka she says use a microwave for the softening process, mill, then back in the wave to reduce. I found this tends to spatter the inside of the oven a lot so now I do the same as when I make stock: pressure cook for a few minutes on high, mill, then reduce slowly (just a simmer) on the stovetop in a wide skillet until its a puree like consistency. Freeze it in an icecube tray and keep the cubes in a bag in the freezer (takes a lot less space and is less likely to spoil than in a jar). Like you, Ive found the medium plate is best. Reconsitutes easily with hot water for soup or tomato sauce for stuff al pomodoro and a cube or two added to a tomato sauce made with tinned tomatoes improves the flavour significantly. Two kilos of tomatoes will give you about 400 ml (the size of a standard ice cube trayt) or 24 cubes of puree. Im stocking up for winter so Ive got a batch on the stove right now.
            </p>
            <p>
             And the food mill is something every cook should have, far easier than sieving stuff.

    jQuery(document).ready(function($) {

    //
    // Scroll top anchor.
    //
    // 1. Get the number of pixels to the bottom of the page.
    // 2. Get the difference from the body height and the bottom offset.
    // 3. Output the adjusted height for the page for acurate percentage parameter.
    //

    var windowObj            = $(window);
    var body                 = $('body');
    var bodyOffsetBottom     = windowObj.scrollBottom();             // 1
    var bodyHeightAdjustment = body.height() - bodyOffsetBottom;     // 2
    var bodyHeightAdjusted   = body.height() - bodyHeightAdjustment; // 3
    var scrollTopAnchor      = $('.x-scroll-top');

    function sizingUpdate(){
      var bodyOffsetTop = windowObj.scrollTop();
      if ( bodyOffsetTop &gt; ( bodyHeightAdjusted * 0.75 ) ) {
        scrollTopAnchor.addClass('in');
      } else {
        scrollTopAnchor.removeClass('in');
      }
    }

    windowObj.bind('scroll', sizingUpdate).resize(sizingUpdate);
    sizingUpdate();

    scrollTopAnchor.click(function(){
      $('html,body').animate({ scrollTop: 0 }, 850, 'easeInOutExpo');
      return false;
