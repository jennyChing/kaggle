                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     The Hostage (RC Investigations Book 3) by V.R. Marks Excerpt
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8940779265303050851" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.vrmarks.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Hostagecvr-2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://www.vrmarks.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/12/Hostagecvr-2.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     $2.99 or FREE for Kindle Unlimited Subscribers
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Hostage-RC-Investigations-Book-ebook/dp/B00AM3ZKWS">
                      Amazon
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      "The best new romantic suspense voice of 2012!" ~Debra Webb, bestselling author of the Faces of Evil series
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     What happens when the hunter becomes the hunted?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Former
 Special Forces analyst, Eva Battaglia, used to be the hunter. Now, in 
the quiet town of Haleswood, SC she blends her linguistic and computer 
skills as the team analyst for RC Investigations. When someone from her 
Army days decides to exact revenge, she goes from being the hunter to 
the hunted in a matter of days.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     J.C. (Carson) Morris is proud to 
serve his hometown of Haleswood as a sheriff's deputy. When asked to 
serve as Eva's bodyguard and keep both the threat and the assignment a 
secret from her, it's no hardship to stay close to her. But when a 
sniper shows up in Haleswood targeting Eva, the rules change.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     In 
an attempt to buy time, Carson takes Eva to a safe house, but will their
 attraction for each other mean sacrifice and death or a happily ever 
after?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Also enjoy
                     <b>
                      <i>
                       The Thief
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     and
                     <b>
                      <i>
                       The Witness
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     , the first two romantic suspense novels from V.R. Marks.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      Excerpt:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "We have solid intelligence Eva
Battaglia is being targeted by the Morcos family," Ross
Carpenter said, as soon as the sheriff's office door closed.
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Deputy Carson Morris glanced from
Carpenter to the sheriff, wondering why he'd been invited to this
private meeting.
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "Morcos?" Sheriff Cochran's
chair squeaked as he leaned back.
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "They're international developers
with sidelines in black market weapons and money laundering."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "What's their concern with Eva?"
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Ross rubbed his forehead. "Years
ago she worked a hostage rescue mission involving the owner's son and
it didn't go as planned. We're tracking down those details. I'm
hoping there's some place here in the court house where I can set up
a temporary office for RC Investigations. That's safer than having
her working alone in Columbia. It's not permanent. Just until we get
this settled."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "There's office space up on the
third floor," Sheriff Cochran said. "Let's go take a look."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Ross examined the area and paced the
entire hallway, noting the elevator, main stairs, other offices, and
fire stairs. "This can work."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Deputy Morris listened while Ross and
the sheriff discussed Eva's schedule and responsibilities and managed
to keep a straight face when Ross volunteered Eva's IT expertise to
seal the deal.
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "One last thing," Carpenter
said. "I'd like someone assigned to Eva full time."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      The sheriff groaned. "You know I
don't have the manpower for that."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "Preferably Deputy Morris."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "Me?"
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "She likes you," Carpenter
said. "That's not easy to discern with Eva, I'll grant you
that."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Carson held his peace, baffled by the
statement. The woman was as prickly as a porcupine. From what he'd
seen, getting shot last month had wounded her pride more than
anything else.
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "What do you expect out of him?"
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "I expect you to treat this like
undercover work, but you just have to be yourself. I need you to make
sure she gets to and from work safely by either direct or indirect
observation. If Rick or I hover, she'll bust us for it. And I expect
you to inform me of any threats or suspicious contact."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      He understood what might qualify as a
threat, but suspicious contact? "Like what?"
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "Morris, you were born and raised
here in Haleswood," Carpenter said. "You'll recognize any
strangers snooping around here, or at her room at the motel."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "True," the sheriff admitted.
"But undercover is a tough thing to pull off in this town."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Ross grinned and clapped Carson on the
shoulder. "You'll come up with something. It's not long term, I
promise."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Carson glanced from one man to the
other. He'd gone to high school with Ross. The sheriff had lived in
the area all his life. All three of them knew there were no secrets
in Haleswood. Undercover would be a unique challenge.
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "It's not a typical assignment,
that's for sure, deputy," Sheriff Cochran said. "But seeing
as you're single, you're probably the person best suited to keep an
eye on her without causing more problems."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      "Stay close, be friendly,"
Carpenter said. "I'll let you know the minute we have the threat
contained."
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      Want to read more? Pick up your kindle
copy today!
                     </div>
                     <div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
                      -V.R. Marks
