   </h1>

     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Atheist Bus Campaign
      </b>
      aims to place "peaceful and upbeat" messages about
      <a href="/wiki/Atheism" title="Atheism">
       atheism
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Bus_advertising" title="Bus advertising">
       transport media
      </a>
      in Britain, in response to evangelical Christian advertising.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc-sherine-heather_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-abc-sherine-heather-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      It was created by comedy writer
      <a href="/wiki/Ariane_Sherine" title="Ariane Sherine">
       Ariane Sherine
      </a>
      and launched on October 21, 2008, with official support from the
      <a href="/wiki/British_Humanist_Association" title="British Humanist Association">
       British Humanist Association
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dawkins" title="Richard Dawkins">
       Richard Dawkins
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The campaign's original goal was to raise 5,500 to run 30 buses across London for four weeks early in 2009 with the slogan: "There's probably no god. Now stop worrying and enjoy your life."
     </p>
     <p>
      Richard Dawkins, author of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_God_Delusion" title="The God Delusion">
        The God Delusion
       </a>
      </i>
      , agreed to match all donations up to a maximum of 5,500, providing a total of 11,000 if the full amount were to be raised. The campaign reached that target by 10:06AM on 21 October and had raised 100,000 by the evening of 24 October. The campaign closed on 11 April 2009, having raised a total of 153,523.51.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-justgiving-abc_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-justgiving-abc-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The first buses started running on 6 January 2009  800 are running around the whole of the UK and it is also planned to place 1,000 adverts on the London Underground featuring quotations from famous atheists. Subsequently, two large LCD screens were placed on
      <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Street" title="Oxford Street">
       Oxford Street
      </a>
      , central London.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph-beckford-streets_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-telegraph-beckford-streets-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Initial_proposal">
       Initial proposal
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Atheist_Bus_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Initial proposal">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Sherine first proposed the campaign in June 2008 in a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guardian.co.uk" title="Guardian.co.uk">
       guardian.co.uk
      </a>
      Comment is Free blog post,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-sherine-five_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-guardian-sherine-five-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Atheists  gimme five
      </i>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">
       <i>
        Guardian
       </i>
      </a>
      . She expressed her frustration that the Christian organisation JesusSaid.org
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was allowed to use bus advertising to promote the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uniform_Resource_Identifier" title="Uniform Resource Identifier">
       web address
      </a>
      of a website that said that all non-Christians would burn in hell for all eternity. Sherine called on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atheist" title="Atheist">
       atheists
      </a>
      to counter this kind of evangelical advertising by donating five pounds towards a positive philosophical advert. Her idea was taken up by political blogger Jon Worth,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worth-face_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-worth-face-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      who went on to set up a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PledgeBank" title="PledgeBank">
       PledgeBank
      </a>
      page. The PledgeBank page closed on 31 July 2008, having received 877 of the 4,679 pledges necessary for the original target of 23,400.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pledge_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-pledge-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This event attracted some limited comment in the mainstream media
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times-parris_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-times-parris-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph-moore_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-telegraph-moore-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      early in August. Sherine then wrote a follow-up
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guardian.co.uk" title="Guardian.co.uk">
       Comment is free
      </a>
      article,
      <i>
       Dawkin 'bout a Revolution
      </i>
      , detailing events since the original piece.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-sherine-revolution_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-guardian-sherine-revolution-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In response, the British Humanist Association offered to lend the campaign its official support and undertook to administer all donations. Sherine then asked Richard Dawkins for a quote for the campaign, at which point he offered to match the first 5,500 raised.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_the_campaign">
       History of the campaign
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Atheist_Bus_Campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History of the campaign">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Atheist Bus Campaign's donation phase launched on Tuesday 21 October 2008 with another article by Sherine,
      <i>
       All aboard the atheist bus campaign
      </i>
      , on Comment is free.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-sherine-allaboard_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-guardian-sherine-allaboard-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      To the surprise of the organisers the fundraising target was broken within hours of the launch, raising almost 48,000 by the end of day one.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-butt_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-guardian-butt-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2056" data-file-width="3648" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg/220px-Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg/330px-Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg/440px-Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ariane_Sherine_and_Richard_Dawkins_at_the_Atheist_Bus_Campaign_launch.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Sherine and Dawkins at the campaign launch
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      After four days the campaign had raised more than 100,000. There have been donations to the Justgiving page every day since the campaign's launch, and by 9 January 2009 the total had surpassed 140,000. The BHA has reported a flood of interest in its activities and the Atheist Bus Campaign
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Facebook_group" title="Facebook group">
       Facebook group
      </a>
      has been growing rapidly since the launch. Many atheists
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2012)">
          who?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      feel the campaign has given them a voice and represented them in a way they have long hoped for.

     <p>
      The final phase of the campaign challenged the idea that children should be labelled with their parents' religion. In November 2009, an ad appeared on billboards, not buses, in London, Edinburgh, Cardiff and Belfast, displaying a young girl's picture with the caption "Please don't label me" followed by "Let me grow up and choose for myself". The background displays phrases ascribing various labels to the child"
      <a href="/wiki/Libertarianism" title="Libertarianism">
       Libertarian
      </a>
      child", "Catholic child", "Sikh child", "Capitalist child", "Atheist child", "Protestant child", and so forth.

       Daily Telegraph

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelical_Christian" title="Evangelical Christian">
       Evangelical Christian
      </a>
      family. The British Humanist Association stated that the photographs had been sourced from a photographic agency website, and that it was unaware of the religious background of the models.
