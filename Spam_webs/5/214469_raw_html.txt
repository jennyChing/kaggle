       <h2>
        How to Filter Video and Audio Podcasts in iTunes
       </h2>
       <time datetime="2009-01-26">
        <span>
         Jan
        </span>
        <span>
         26
        </span>
        <span>
         2009
        </span>
       </time>
      </header>
      <p>
       I love podcasts, but I do notice that iTunes lacks an easy way to filter between audio and video podcasts in iTunes, particularly when youre making a smart playlist. Smart playlists are, obviously, great for setting up a playlist just based on your available, unheard podcasts, but audio and video, they just dont mix.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://wandrly.me/kickstarter">
        <img alt="Dig the Free  Download? Glad I could help!  Care to  return the favor?  Check out my project on Kickstarter" class="alignright size-full wp-image-3875" height="389" src="http://48jwh53mcwj217jvx11dcwjj.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/10/kickstarter.png" title="kickstarter" width="203"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       I struggled with this for awhile, but have finally figured it out: sorting by filetype. Of course, theres no
       <em>
        filetype
       </em>
       filter in iTunes smart playlists. However, you can use the
       <em>
        kind
       </em>
       filter. Most video files are given a
       <em>
        kind
       </em>
       label of
       <em>
        MPEG-4 Video File
       </em>
       or
       <em>
        Quicktime Movie File
       </em>
       , while most audio podcasts have the word
       <em>
        audio
       </em>
       in that field. So
      </p>
      <h3>
       How to Set Up a Smart Playlist with Audio-Only Podcasts in iTunes
      </h3>
      <ol>
       <li>
        In iTunes, go to
        <em>
         File &gt; New Smart Playlist
        </em>
       </li>
       <li>
        When the Smart Playlist dialogue box pops up, enter the following information (as per the image below.)
        <ol>
         <li>
          <em>
           Podcast is true
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           Play Count is less than 1
          </em>
          (to only play podcasts you havent heard yet)
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           Kind does not contain video
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           Kind does not contain movie
           <br/>
          </em>
         </li>
        </ol>
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       <img alt="audio_podcast" class="imgbd380" src="http://48jwh53mcwj217jvx11dcwjj.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/audio_podcast.jpg" title="audio_podcast"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Voila, youre good to go. Just click on that Smart Playlist and let it rip. And now for the video aspect.
      </p>
      <h3>
       How to Set Up a Smart Playlist with Video-Only Podcasts in iTunes
      </h3>
      <ol>
       <li>
        In iTunes, go to
        <em>
         File &gt; New Smart Playlist
        </em>
       </li>
       <li>
        When the Smart Playlist dialogue box pops up, enter the following information (as per the image below.)
        <ol>
         <li>
          <em>
           Podcast is true
          </em>
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           Play Count is 0
          </em>
          (to only play videos you havent watched yet)
         </li>
         <li>
          <em>
           Kind does not contain audio
          </em>
         </li>
        </ol>
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       <img alt="audio_podcast" class="imgbd380" src="http://48jwh53mcwj217jvx11dcwjj.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/video_podcast.jpg" title="audio_podcast"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       And now youve got your own personal TV station, right on your computer.
      </p>
