          Cream Cheese &amp; Jam Sandwich Swirls
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="posted-on">
           Posted on
           <a href="http://www.coffeeandvanilla.com/cream-cheese-and-jam-sandwich-swirls/" rel="bookmark">

             22 January, 2008
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="byline">

             Margot

        <div class="entry-content">
         <h3>
          INGREDIENTS
         </h3>
         <p>
          3 slices of white crust-less square bread
          <br/>
          some cream cheese (soft white cheese)
          <br/>
          some jam (I used black currant conserve)
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="fancy jam sandwich recipe" class="alignnone wp-image-28463 size-large" height="835" src="http://coffee.416studios34.netdna-cdn.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/cream-cheese-jam-swirls-556x835.jpg" title="Cream Cheese &amp; Jam Sandwich Swirls" width="556"/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          EQUIPMENT
         </h3>
         <p>
          cling film
         </p>
         <h3>
          METHOD
         </h3>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Spread generously cream cheese over the bread, then spread jam all over it.
          </li>
          <li>
           Place all 3 slices of bread one after another and roll them one by one together.
          </li>
          <li>
           Created this way log wrap in a cling film and leaf over night or for couple of hours in the fridge.
          </li>
          <li>
           Then remove cling film, slice log into 1  1 cm wide slices.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <em>
            Makes 6  7 swirls.
           </em>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h3>
          NOTES
         </h3>
         <p>
          This recipe would work really well with the chocolate spread instead of jam.
         </p>
         <p>
          Bread wrapped in cling film can be storedovernight in the fridge and then sliced in the morning for lunchboxes.
         </p>
