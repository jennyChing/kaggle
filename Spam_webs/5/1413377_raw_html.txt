              <div class="nd-region-middle">
               <div class="field field-body">
                <p class="rtecenter">
                 <img alt="saffron_protects_macular_degeneration_greenmedinfo.jpg" src="http://cdn.greenmedinfo.com/sites/default/files/ckeditor/lrossi/images/saffron-215932_640.jpg" style="width: 600px; height: 401px;"/>
                </p>
                <p>
                 <span style="font-size:18px;">
                  <em>
                   <strong>
                    Age-related macular degeneration is on the rise. This ancient spice may save your sight.
                   </strong>
                  </em>
                 </span>
                </p>
                <p>
                 As Baby Boomers mature en masse, age-related
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/disease/macular-degeneration" target="_blank">
                   macular degeneration
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 (AMD) is on the rise.
                </p>
                <p>
                 AMD is the leading cause of vision loss among people 50 and older. It affects more than 1.75 million people in the U.S. and is expected to strike as many as 3 million in the next five years. Fortunately, an ancient spice may provide just the protection you need.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The macula is the small spot near the center of the retina. The eye needs it for central vision to see objects straight ahead. When the macula becomes damaged, it severely limits your ability to see things in your central line of vision.
                </p>
                <p>
                 As age-related
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/disease/macular-degeneration" target="_blank">
                   macular degeneration
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 progresses, a blurred area develops near the center of your vision. In addition, objects may not appear as sharp or bright as they used to. Colors seem faded. That loss of central vision can interfere with daily activities like recognizing faces, reading, driving, working, cooking, or watching TV.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Besides age, the biggest risk factor for AMD is
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/disease/smoking" target="_blank">
                   smoking
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 . Other risk factors include sun exposure,
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/disease/blood-pressure-high" target="_blank">
                   high blood pressure
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 and obesity.
                </p>
                <p>
                 But we also know that people who exercise and eat green leafy vegetables and fish have a lower risk of developing the disease.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Recent studies have found certain nutrients protect against the progression of AMD. Researchers at the National Eye Institute conducted two Age-Related Eye Disease Studies (AREDS and AREDS2).
                 <a href="/blog/saffron-protects-against-macular-degeneration-1?page=2#_edn1" name="_ednref1">
                  [i]
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 The first AREDS study showed that a combination of
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/blog/200-reasons-love-vitamin-c1" target="_blank">
                   vitamin C
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 , vitamin E, beta-carotene, zinc, and copper reduce the risk of late-stage AMD by 25%.
                </p>
                <p>
                 AREDS2 found that a combination of lutein and zeaxanthin with vitamin C, vitamin E,
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/substance/zinc" target="_blank">
                   zinc
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 , and copper further reduced the risk of late-stage AMD.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Other studies show that
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/slide/rosemary-protects-against-macular-degeneration">
                   rosemary may protect against macular degeneration
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 .
                </p>
                <p>
                 And a study from Australian and Italian researchers shows the
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/blog/ancient-saffron-magical-healing-powers-confirmed-science" target="_blank">
                   Mediterranean spice saffron
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 helps slow progression of AMD and improve vision.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The researchers conducted a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study of people with early stage AMD. One group in the study supplemented with 20 mg per day of saffron. After just 90 days the researchers saw significant improvement in the saffron group.
                 <a href="/blog/saffron-protects-against-macular-degeneration-1?page=2#_edn2" name="_ednref2" title="">
                  [ii]
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 Then the researchers tested a group of 29 patients aged 55 to 85 with early-stage AMD. The patients received 20 mg per day of saffron as a supplement for about 14 months.
                 <a href="/blog/saffron-protects-against-macular-degeneration-1?page=2#_edn3" name="_ednref3" title="">
                  [iii]
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p>
                 The researchers observed improvements in their clinical measurements of the disease after saffron supplementation.
                </p>
                <p>
                 In addition, all of the patients reported an improvement in their quality of vision. They experienced improvements in contrast and color perception, reading ability, and vision in low lighting. All of that added up to a substantial improvement in the patients' quality of life.
                </p>
                <p class="rtecenter">
                 <img alt="saffron_macular_degeneration_greenmedinfo.jpg" src="http://cdn.greenmedinfo.com/sites/default/files/ckeditor/lrossi/images/saffron-325205_640.jpg" style="width: 600px; height: 399px;"/>
                </p>
                <h1>
                 <span style="font-size:18px;">
                  <strong>
                   What's in saffron that protects the eyes?
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </h1>
                <p>
                 The researchers noted that saffron from the crocus flower contains chemical compounds called crocin and crocetin. These are antioxidant derivatives of carotenoids.
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/article/crocin-may-be-useful-agents-prevention-renal-ischemia-reperfusion-ir-induced-oxidative" target="_blank">
                   Crocin
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 protects photoreceptors from light-induced death. Crocetin increases the availability of oxygen to the cells.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Saffron also contains over 150 volatile and aromatic compounds that contribute to a long list of other
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/blog/ancient-saffron-magical-healing-powers-confirmed-science" target="_blank">
                   saffron health benefits proven by science
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 .
                </p>
                <h1>
                 <span style="font-size:18px;">
                  <strong>
                   Saffron beats Aricept for Alzheimer's Disease
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </h1>
                <p>
                 A 22-week randomized, double-blind controlled trial showed that
                 <strong>
                  <a href="/article/saffron-compares-favorably-donepezil-treatment-mild-moderate-alzhemiers-disease" target="_blank">
                   15 mg of saffron twice a day was as effective as donepezil (Aricept) in the management of mild-to-moderate Alzheimer's disease
                  </a>
                 </strong>
                 .
                </p>
