     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        American Wedding
       </b>
      </i>
      (known as
      <i>
       <b>
        American Pie 3: The Wedding
       </b>
      </i>
      or
      <i>
       <b>
        American Pie: The Wedding
       </b>
      </i>
      , in some countries) is a 2003 American
      <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy_film" title="Romantic comedy film">
       romantic comedy film
      </a>
      and a
      <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">
       sequel
      </a>
      to
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/American_Pie_(film)" title="American Pie (film)">
        American Pie
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/American_Pie_2" title="American Pie 2">
        American Pie 2
       </a>
      </i>
      . It is the third (originally intended final) installment in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Pie_(series)#Theatrical_films" title="American Pie (series)">
       <i>
        American Pie
       </i>
       theatrical series
      </a>
      . It was written by
      <a href="/wiki/Adam_Herz" title="Adam Herz">
       Adam Herz
      </a>
      and directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Jesse_Dylan" title="Jesse Dylan">
       Jesse Dylan
      </a>
      . Another sequel,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/American_Reunion" title="American Reunion">
        American Reunion
       </a>
      </i>
      , was released nine years later. This also stands as the last film in the series to be written by Herz, who conceptualized the franchise.
     </p>
     <p>
      Though the film mainly focuses on the union of Jim Levenstein and Michelle Flaherty, for the first time in the series, the story centers on Steve Stifler, and his outrageous antics including his attempt to organize a bachelor party, teaching Jim to dance for the wedding, and competing with Finch to win the heart of Michelle's lovely sister, Cadence.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Wedding&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      While on a date in a fancy restaurant with Michelle Flaherty, Jim Levenstein is ready to ask her to marry him. However, his dad still has to arrive with a ring because he forgot it. He tries to stall the question, causing her to think that he actually wants oral sex. She goes under the table and gives Jim
      <a href="/wiki/Fellatio" title="Fellatio">
       fellatio
      </a>
      just as Jim's dad arrives with the ring, causing her to bump her head on the table and attract the attention of the entire restaurant. Still, Michelle accepts the proposal.
     </p>
     <p>
      High school pals Kevin Myers and Paul Finch serve as groomsmen, while the '
      <a href="/wiki/MILF_(slang)" title="MILF (slang)">
       MILF
      </a>
      ' Guys - John and Justin - proclaim themselves as ushers. Unfortunately, the gang's obnoxious friend Steve Stifler crashes the couple's engagement party. The gang tries to keep the wedding a secret from Stifler, fearing he might ruin everything but after he finds out about it, he instantly comes up with an idea to throw Jim a huge bachelor party, upsetting Jim.
     </p>
     <p>
      Jim is worried about dancing at the wedding, but salvation comes in the form of Stifler, who has taken dance lessons. Stifler agrees to teach Jim to dance on the condition he be allowed to attend the wedding and plan the bachelor party; Jim demands that Stifler tone down his obnoxious personality for Michelle's parents in exchange. Meanwhile, the wedding dress Michelle finally settles on after long hours of searching is made by only one designer working for one store, so the boys set out to find the dressmaker for her. They go to Chicago looking for "Leslie Sommers"; Stifler unwittingly walks into a
      <a href="/wiki/Gay_bar" title="Gay bar">
       gay bar
      </a>
      , and his raucous behavior gets him into a dispute with several of the bar's patrons. It turns out Leslie Sommers is actually a man, who agrees to make the dress after witnessing a dance-off between Stifler and Bear. Stifler earns the respect of Bear, with the latter even offering to provide strippers for Jim's
      <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_party" title="Bachelor party">
       bachelor party
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In the meantime, Michelle's younger sister, Cadence, flies in for the wedding. Both Finch and Stifler are attracted to her, and in an effort to win her over, they each adopt the other's personality and mannerisms. Stifler arranges the bachelor party for everyone at Jim's house except Jim, who unknowingly has arranged a 'special dinner' for Michelle's parents before the wedding to explain why he will be a good husband and finally win them over. Bear introduces the three to Fraulein Brandi and Officer Krystal, who play
      <a href="/wiki/Dominance_and_submission" title="Dominance and submission">
       submissive and dominant
      </a>
      roles with them.
     </p>
     <p>
      The party is abruptly halted by the unexpected return of Jim, Harold and Mary. With assistance from Bear, who poses as a butler named "
      <a href="/wiki/Mr._Belvedere" title="Mr. Belvedere">
       Mr. Belvedere
      </a>
      ", Jim nearly succeeds in keeping the activities a secret, until Michelle's mother opens a closet door and is shocked to find Kevin inside, stripped to his boxers and tied to a chair (following a kinky game with the strippers). The boys explain that it was an attempt to make Jim seem like a hero that went horribly wrong, and Michelle's parents accept this explanation, and tell him that if he puts that much effort into the upcoming marriage, she can give him her blessing.
     </p>
     <p>
      As the ceremony draws near, a series of mishaps occur, including Jim's grandmother being displeased that Michelle is not
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">
       Jewish
      </a>
      , Stifler accidentally feeding the ring to the dogs, and Jim shaving his
      <a href="/wiki/Pubic_hair" title="Pubic hair">
       pubic hair
      </a>
      , then disposing of it too close to a vent that causes it to be set loose all over the wedding cake. On the night before the wedding, Stifler inadvertently disrupts the walk-in refrigerator's power supply while retrieving a bottle of champagne, essentially turning it into an oven and killing the many flowers put together for the ceremony. Previously, Stifler, unaware of Cadence's presence, had revealed his true rude and obnoxious personality. Angered and stunned, Jim asks him to leave, and all the others, including Cadence, support Jim's decision.
     </p>
     <p>
      Feeling guilty for his thoughtless behavior, Stifler convinces the local florist to put together a new batch of flowers, and he enlists the help of his football players and Bear. As a gesture of remorse, he also gives a rose to Cadence, much to the amazement of Jim and Michelle. Moved by his actions, Cadence agrees to have sex with him before the ceremony, but Stifler's presence is delayed by a brief thank-you meeting Jim calls among his groomsmen, citing how he is grateful to have friends like them to back him up when he is in need. Quickly returning to the hotel, Stifler hears someone in the supply closet and steps inside, but due to the closet's poor lighting, it is only when he gets inadvertently walked in on that Stifler realizes he's actually having sex with Jim's grandmother (who becomes pleasant, especially towards Stifler after the act), who was placed in the closet by John and Justin to stop her constant complaining.
     </p>
     <p>
      Despite the chaotic events leading up to it, Michelle and Jim eventually get married. At the reception, the newly married couple dances while Stifler dances with Cadence. Meanwhile, Finch is sitting by himself when Stifler's mom arrives. Although agreeing they are over each other, Stifler's mom mentions having a double suite and invites Finch to join her. The film ends with John and Justin spying on Stifler's mom and Finch in her suite's couple-size bathtub, having oral sex.
     </p>

      <a href="/wiki/Mrs._Robinson" title="Mrs. Robinson">
       Mrs. Robinson
      </a>

      <i>
       American Pie
      </i>
      films not to feature a
      <a href="/wiki/Blink-182" title="Blink-182">
       blink-182
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/The_Wallflowers" title="The Wallflowers">
       The Wallflowers

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Release">
       Release
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Wedding&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       American Wedding
      </i>
      was released in the United States on August 1, 2003 and opened at #1 with $33,369,440 before dropping 53.7% the next weekend, landing at #3 behind the new releases of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/S.W.A.T._(film)" title="S.W.A.T. (film)">
        S.W.A.T.
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Freaky_Friday_(2003_film)" title="Freaky Friday (2003 film)">
        Freaky Friday
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Closing about 3.5 months later (November 20, 2003), the film had grossed a domestic total of $104,565,114 and $126,884,089 overseas for a worldwide total of $231,449,203, based on a $55 million budget.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boxoffice_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-boxoffice-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Despite being a huge box office success, it is the lowest-grossing film in the series, making roughly $2 million less than
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/American_Reunion" title="American Reunion">
        American Reunion
       </a>
      </i>
      would in 2012.
     </p>
     <p>
      <i>
       American Wedding
      </i>
      grossed $15.85 million on DVD and was the number seven DVD rental in 2004.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Wedding&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      <i>
       American Wedding
      </i>
      received mixed reviews from critics.
      <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">
       Rotten Tomatoes
      </a>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">
       review aggregator
      </a>
      , gave the film a rating of 55%, based on 154 reviews, with an average rating of 5.8/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "Raunchier and even more gross than the first two American Pies,
      <i>
       American Wedding
      </i>
      ought to please fans of the series."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On
      <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">
       Metacritic
      </a>
      , the film has a score of 43 out of 100, based on 34 critics, which indicates "mixed or average reviews".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Robert Koeler of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">
        Variety
       </a>
      </i>
      compared it to the works of
      <a href="/wiki/John_Waters" title="John Waters">
       John Waters
      </a>
      and called it a "strong finish" for the franchise.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">
       Roger Ebert
      </a>
      rated it 3/4 stars and wrote that the film "is proof of the hypothesis that no genre is beyond redemption."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Elvis Mitchell of
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">
        The New York Times
       </a>
      </i>
      wrote that the film "struggles so hard to be tasteless that it's almost quaint."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Mick_LaSalle" title="Mick LaSalle">
       Mick LaSalle
      </a>
      of the
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">
        San Francisco Chronicle
       </a>
      </i>
      rated it 2/5 stars and called it strained and desperate to find jokes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
