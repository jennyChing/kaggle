          <p>
           The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) is concentrating its industry discussions on how to cut red tape for superannuation and has publicly asked the sector to share its views.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           ATO acting second commissioner Alison Lendon said as the regulator it was important for the tax office to ensure the health and security of the sector, including self-managed super funds (SMSF).
           <br/>
           <br/>
           We think from the ATOs perspective that its in good shape and that this is, in no small part, due to the positive and productive working relationships that we have with the industry and the professional organisations, and individual SMSF professionals, Lendon said at the 2014 SMSF Professionals Association of Australia National Conference in Brisbane last week.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           A major focus for our discussions at the moment is how to reduce red tape.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Were really open and looking for ideas around that so if you have any ideas, please let us know as were keen to hear them.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           She said as part of the commissioners review of the ATOs consultation forums, it was now consulting in a much more targeted and effective way.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Protecting retirement savings is our shared purpose and we are trying to make things easier for everyone by improving the way that we consult and also by improving our support services, she said.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           We have a superannuation industry advisory group, which meets three times a year and met for the first time in December, and has senior level representatives from the industry as well as representatives from Treasury, ASIC and APRA (Australian Prudential Regulation Authority).
           <br/>
           <br/>
           We also have the superannuation industry relationship network, which held its first meeting [recently], and that consists primarily of representatives registered on our ATO consultation register, as well as industry experts with whom weve established a relationship.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           The ATO was looking to lift its support for the SMSF sector, she said.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           We do think that we can do a lot more for SMSFs and their advisers; more thats in keeping with the new ways that people are looking for and consume information, she said.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Were making it easier for users to find information on our website and were looking at new ways to present that information for trustees to make it easier for them to understand and meet their obligations.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           For example, we are working on putting up SMSF information to go on our ATO app.
          </p>
