       <h1 class="entry-title">
        The Human Slaughter-House
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
         Posted on
        </span>
        <a href="http://www.simonbeattie.kattare.com/blog/archives/1839" rel="bookmark" title="10:57 am">
         <span class="entry-date">
          21 April 2015
         </span>
        </a>
        <span class="meta-sep">
         by
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.simonbeattie.kattare.com/blog/archives/author/simonbeattie" title="View all posts by Simon Beattie">
          Simon Beattie
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <!-- .entry-meta -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Were only charging machines. And the machine triumphs into our flesh. And the machine drinks the blood from our veins, guzzling it by the bucketload. These lines come from the extraordinary novella
         <em>
          Das Menschenschlachthaus
         </em>
         by Wilhelm Lamszus (18811965), which I read recently.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.simonbeattie.kattare.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Lamszus-2.jpeg">
          <img alt="Lamszus 2" class="alignnone wp-image-1840" height="709" src="http://www.simonbeattie.kattare.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Lamszus-2-569x800.jpeg" width="504"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ill admit Id never heard of it until I saw it among the items in the
         <a href="http://www.dhm.de/ausstellungen/archiv/2014/der-erste-weltkrieg.html" target="_blank">
          DHMs First World War exhibition
         </a>
         . Published in 1912, it uncannily predicts the mechanised nature of war which was to come. Though perhaps forgotten now, over a century later, at the time itsold 100,000 copies in three months, was translated into eight different languages, and was then banned in Germany in 1915. The dust-jacket for the first American edition (New York, 1913) sums it up for us:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.simonbeattie.kattare.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Lamszus-1.jpeg">
          <img alt="Lamszus 1" class="alignnone wp-image-1841" height="724" src="http://www.simonbeattie.kattare.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Lamszus-1-557x800.jpeg" width="504"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
       </div>
