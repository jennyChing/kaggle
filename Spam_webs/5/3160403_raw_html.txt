                 <h1 class="title">
                  IUVA Panel Addresses UVGI for Healthcare Facilities Disinfection
                 </h1>
                 <div class="content-content" id="content-content">
                  <div class="node odd full-node node-type-news fusion-float-imagefield-left" id="node-1186">
                   <div class="inner">
                    <div class="meta">
                     <span class="submitted">
                      Submitted by IntlUVAssn on September 20, 2013 - 12:01pm
                     </span>
                    </div>
                    <div class="content clearfix">
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       <em>
                        September 21, 2-5 p.m.
                       </em>
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       <em>
                        Mirage Resort and Casino, Las Vegas
                       </em>
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="https://www.iuva.org/event/panel-discussion-ultraviolet">
                       <strong>
                        <em>
                         Panel Discussion on UV Disinfection for Infection Prevention: Healthcare Opportunities and Application Trends
                        </em>
                       </strong>
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      There are many issues facing the healthcare industry today but one of the most pressing is the rise of Hospital Associated Infections (HAIs). Many HAIs are transmitted by airborne bacteria and have proved to be impervious to traditional antibiotic treatments and sterilization methods. The answer clearly lies in the effective use of Ultraviolet Germicidal Irradiation (UVGI) technology, which has been shown to greatly reduce bacteria and viruses in the air and on the surface in hospital settings. Todays UVGI technology is low cost to operate and easy to use.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      The ultimate challenge for scientists, engineers and product manufacturers who build UVGI devices is to convince healthcare leaders to embrace the technology in facilities to better ensure patient, staff and visitor safety from HAIs.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      This is why Mary Clancy, President of
                      <a href="http://www.healthy-interiors.com/">
                       Healthy Interiors
                      </a>
                      and colleague
                      <a href="http://www.iuva.org/sites/default/files/peter_e._gordon_iuva_biography.pdf">
                       Peter Gordon
                      </a>
                      , co-founder of Germgard Lighting LLC, have organized a panel discussion on UV scheduled for September 21, 2012, the day prior to the World Congress in Las Vegas Nevada, at the Mirage Resort and Casino. Panelists will speak from various perspectives to discuss UVGI usage in healthcare settings. The question is, asks Clancy, Will the healthcare industry be willing to give the technology a prominent place as a strategic weapon in the infection control arsenal to reduce HAIs, staff illness, and save lives?
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-left:48pt;text-align:center;">
                      <img alt="Mary Clancy, RN, CIEC, HHS, President/CEO  Secure-Environmental Healthy-Interiors &amp; IUVA Healthcare Working Group Co-Chair" src="/sites/default/files/hp_dsc08457.jpg" style="width:200px;height:150px;"/>
                      <img alt="Peter Gordon, IUVA Healthcare Working Group Co-Chair" src="/sites/default/files/hp_dsc08440.jpg" style="width:200px;height:150px;"/>
                     </p>
                     <p style="margin-left:48pt;text-align:center;">
                      <em>
                       (Left to Right) Mary Clancy, RN, CIEC, HHS, President/CEO  Secure-Environmental Healthy-Interiors &amp; IUVA Healthcare Working Group Co-Chair; Peter Gordon, IUVA Healthcare Working Group Co-Chair
                      </em>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      This panel discussion will be an excellent opportunity to find answers to questions about UVGI and how it can improve patient outcomes.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Panelists have different professional backgrounds related to UVGI disinfection of healthcare facilities.
                      <a href="http://www.iuva.org/shelly-miller">
                       Dr. Shelly Miller
                      </a>
                      of the University of Colorado will offer a more refined look at research on this topic from an academic point of view.
                      <a href="http://www.iuva.org/cheryl-perkins">
                       Cheryl Perkins
                      </a>
                      , founder of Innovations Edge, helps make new technologies into successful companies, and will help attendees understand the potentials of UVGI from a business standpoint.
                      <a href="http://www.iuva.org/sites/default/files/jimgabalski.pdf">
                       Jim Gabalski
                      </a>
                      is the North America Vice President of Marketing for Getinge Infection Control, a division of The Getinge Group. With an eye towards new technology that makes for better patient outcomes, Gabalski is currently working on new product and program offerings for UV-C room decontamination.
                     </p>
                     <p style="text-align:center;">
                      <img alt="(Left to Right) Shelly Miller, Richard Vincent, Jim Gabalski &amp; Cheryl Perkins" src="/sites/default/files/hp_dsc08459.jpg" style="width:200px;height:150px;"/>
                     </p>
                     <p style="text-align:center;">
                      <em>
                       (Left to Right) Shelly Miller, Richard Vincent, Jim Gabalski &amp; Cheryl Perkins
                      </em>
                     </p>
                     <p style="text-align:center;">
                      <img alt="Shelly Miller, Associate Professor of Mechanical Engineering at University of Colorado Boulder" src="/sites/default/files/hp_dsc08462.jpg" style="width:200px;height:150px;"/>
                      <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/hp_dsc08518.jpg" style="width:200px;height:150px;"/>
                      <img alt="Cheryl Perkins, Founder/President  Innovationedge" src="/sites/default/files/hp_dsc08483.jpg" style="width:200px;height:150px;"/>
                     </p>
                     <p style="text-align:center;">
                      <em>
                       (Left to Right) Shelly Miller, Associate Professor of Mechanical Engineering at University of Colorado Boulder; Jim Gabalski, Vice President, Marketing  North America for Getinge USA Inc.; Cheryl Perkins, Founder/President  Innovationedge
                      </em>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Panelist
                      <a href="http://iuva.org/richard-vincent">
                       Richard Vincent - MS, BS Arch, FIESNA, LEED
                       <sup>
                        
                       </sup>
                       AP - Environmental Health and Research Manager with Mt. Sinai,
                      </a>
                      also spent time in an important study on the effectiveness of UV in healthcare settings at St. Vincents Hospital in NYC. Vincent was the project manager and lighting technology specialist, for the Tuberculosis Ultraviolet Shelter Study (TUSS), (1997-2004)a multidisciplinary, multicenter epidemiological field trial of ultraviolet air cleansing effectiveness. He continues his work with the UVGI collaboration under the sponsorship of the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, NYC. Here, he has developed an advanced UVGI fixture and Computer Aided Design (CAD) tool for UVGI fixture placement. Another project undertaken through TUSS included the first field test of UVGI in-duct effectiveness in a commercial building in Manhattan, NY. Vincents exemplary experience in the field of UVGI should prove enlightening.
                     </p>
                     <p style="text-align:center;">
                      <em>
                       <img alt="Richard Vincent, Department of Medicine Administrative Manager for Division of General Internal Medicine" src="/sites/default/files/hp_dsc08507.jpg" style="width:200px;height:150px;"/>
                      </em>
                     </p>
                     <p style="text-align:center;">
                      <em>
                       Richard Vincent, Department of Medicine Administrative Manager for Division of General Internal Medicine
                      </em>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      As more professionals confer on disinfection of healthcare facilities for HAIs, healthcare facility administrators will better understand the imperative to invest in UVGI technology. There are so many applications that can be used in healthcare settings. For example, UV robotics are a unique, developing area of the technology application where robots are sent into a room to sterilize it with UV dosages, decontaminating it without risk to staff or patients. UV has been used in Tuberculosis Isolation rooms for years, says Clancy, and it is used effectively inside hospital ventilation systems to keep microbial growth under control. UV is also used in large waiting rooms and admitting areas, where there is a great deal of traffic, she added.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      As this exciting area of UV technology grows, so does the ability to measure dosages and receive correct readings from devices to gauge effectiveness of UVGI. Healthcare staff can be trained how to use UVGI devices, which are low cost to operate. For hospitals and other healthcare facilities, UVGI is a powerful alternative to the spread of HAIs, which are currently affecting an estimated one in every 20 hospital patients. Widespread use of UVGI in healthcare settings could effectively reduce these rates of infection and save lives.
                     </p>
                    </div>
