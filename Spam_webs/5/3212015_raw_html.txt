        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Interview with Christy and Kali from Technosyncratic
        </h1>
        <ul class="post-meta">
        </ul>
       </header>
       <div class="post-thumbnail">
        <a href="http://www.travelwithamate.com/interview-technosyncratic-christy-kali/">
         <img alt="Christy and Kali" class="attachment-post wp-post-image" height="427" src="http://cdn4.travelwithamate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/CK-Alabama.jpeg" width="640"/>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Meet Christy and Kali, two digital nomads who work online and travel full-time. In the summer of 2010 they sold most of their possessions and bought a motorhome to live in while exploring America. Bitten by the travel bug, they sold the rest of their possessions, and headed abroad. We caught up with them in Thailand to find out more about their travels across
         <a href="/category/places/europe">
          Europe
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="/category/places/asia">
          Asia
         </a>
         and beyond.
        </p>
        <h2>
         What first made you fall in love with travel?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.travelwithamate.com/interview-technosyncratic-christy-kali/ck-alabama/" rel="attachment wp-att-12556">
          <img alt="Christy and Kali" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-12556" height="200" src="http://cdn3.travelwithamate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/CK-Alabama-300x200.jpg" title="Christy and Kali" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Neither of us had the opportunity to travel extensively when we were younger, so by college we both had a thirst to explore. The day after graduation we hopped on an international flight and backpacked around Europe for five weeks. It was unlike anything we had done before, and the freedom and challenge were exhilirating.
        </p>
        <h2>
         What has been the most memorable moment on your journeys so far?
        </h2>
        <p>
         On that same backpacking trip we found ourselves in the most bizarre situation  abandoned in the middle of the Italian wilderness with a non-English speaking group of folks who made their own really strong wine and had an affinity for wolf howling.
        </p>
        <p>
         We had connected with a family via Servas and they suggested we spend a weekend (ostensibly with them) in the mountains at a remote cabin that lacked electricity and running water. Once we arrived they pretty much ignored us, though, so we were left to mingle with the others in Italian which we dont speak. When we werent playing with the toddlers we spent our time learning how to make felt hats and track wolves in the wild. It was the most bizarre and unforgettable weekend of our lives!
        </p>
        <h2>
         What made you pick some of the countries youve been to?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Malta" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-12555" height="200" src="http://cdn1.travelwithamate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Malta7-300x200.jpg" title="Malta" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         We rarely have an idea where were heading next; we play it by ear and see what emerges. Housesitting has taken us to a number of places we wouldnt have otherwise considered (like German suburbia), and we often peruse random Airbnb listings, which is how we unexpectedly found ourselves in Malta.
        </p>
        <p>
         We tend towards big cities (for instance, we spent a month in Berlin and loved it) because theres always a lot to keep us entertained, but mostly we look for places where we think wed be happy staying for a few weeks. Were slow travelers, and anything less than ten days in one location just isnt enough!
        </p>
        <h2>
         Where are you now? What have you done there and what do you plan to do next?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Chiang Mai" class="alignright" height="199" src="http://cdn1.travelwithamate.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/11/chiang-mai-300x199.jpg" title="Chiang Mai" width="300"/>
         Were currently in Chiang Mai,
         <a href="/category/places/asia/thailand" target="_blank">
          Thailand
         </a>
         , and weve been here for just over a month. Sadly most of our time thus far has been spent catching up on work projects and trying to recover from a nasty bout of illness, but weve also spent an inordinate amount of time in the night markets. After a full day spent in front of the computer, we love to relax in the evenings by heading to one of the many night markets around here and wandering through the stalls in search of trinkets or dinner.
        </p>
        <p>
         We still have a few months to do all the touristy stuff, though, so I know well visit the Elephant Nature Park (which is a rehabilitation center for the animals) and some of the hilltribes, go trekking in the jungle, and take a Thai cooking class before our lease expires.
        </p>
        <h2>
         What unusual destination would you recommend to everyone?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="The Cotswalds" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-12554" height="200" src="http://cdn4.travelwithamate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Cotswolds4-300x200.jpg" title="The Cotswalds" width="300"/>
         The Cotswolds. While it may not be
         <em>
          unusual
         </em>
         per se, I was very surprised recently when I learned that a lot of folks from the States havent heard of this fantastic region in the U.K. Its made up of gentle rolling hillsides and sleepy historic towns, and its so stunning that the British government has designated it an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its also probably one of our favorite places in the world, so of course we might be a little biased. :)
        </p>
        <h2>
         What locations and countries are you looking forward to visiting next?
        </h2>
        <p>
         <img alt="Canyonlands" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-12557" height="200" src="http://cdn3.travelwithamate.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/CK-Canyonlands-300x200.jpg" title="Canyonlands" width="300"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As per our standard operating procedures we still have no idea where were heading next, but there
         <em>
          are
         </em>
         a few countries weve been dying to visit. We cant wait to get back to India again and explore the country in depth, and South Korea and
         <a href="/category/places/asia/japan">
          Japan
         </a>
         have been on Kalis list for quite some time due to their modern techie vibe. Of course
         <a href="/category/places/africa/egypt">
          Egypt
         </a>
         (like almost every other kid, I was obsessed with the pyramids and Pharaohs growing up), and Sweden and
         <a href="/category/places/oceania/new-zealand">
          New Zealand
         </a>
         are on that list as well. We still have a lot of traveling to do!
        </p>
        <h2>
         And lastly, can you describe how travelling makes you feel personally?
        </h2>
        <p>
         Traveling offers a constant sense of wonderment and growth. There are so many other perspectives and cultures to learn from in the world, and we dont want our worldviews to be limited to what we can discover in just one city. The fact that were fortunate enough to travel full-time makes us feel amazingly grateful, as having complete autonomy and control over our own lives (no tyrannical bosses to deal with!) is definitely a gift.
        </p>
        <p>
         Check out their travel blog at
         <a href="http://technosyncratic.com" target="_blank">
          TechnoSyncratic.com
         </a>
         and follow them on twitter at
         <a href="http://www.twitter.com/technosyncratic" target="_blank">
          @technosyncratic
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Want to be interviewed about your travels and help promote your blog?
         <a href="/contact-us">
          Contact us
         </a>
         today.
        </p>
