        <h2>
         Burn fat, build muscle
        </h2>
        <p>
         Like most blokes, you've probably been reminded by your other half of men's inability to multi-task. Totally untrue. Watching the match,
         <a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/healthy/addictions/healthiest-beers-in-the-uk" target="_blank">
          drinking beer
         </a>
         , wolfing down pork scratchings and listening' about her day goes above and beyond multi-tasking. But it's often said that
         <a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/building-muscle/get-big/" target="_blank">
          building muscle
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/lose-weight/burn-fat/" target="_blank">
          losing fat
         </a>
         are mutually exclusive. To lose body fat you need to eat less and to add muscle you have to eat more, so it can seem downright impossible to have these two goals. Read on to find out how to tighten your sleeves and loosen your jeans.
        </p>
        <h2>
         How it works
        </h2>
        <p>
         You'll alternate between weeks of heavy weights and low repetitions to build muscle and low weights and high repetitions to burn fat. This strategy
         <a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/lose-weight/Fire-up-your-metabolism" target="_blank">
          elevates your metabolism
         </a>
         by conditioning your muscles to have both endurance and strength. Combine these efforts with the "Get ripped" meal plan (
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/food-nutrition/muscle-foods/eat-to-get-ripped-144983" target="_blank" title="Get ripped">
           click here
          </a>
         </strong>
         ) and you'll expose your body to the variables you need to hit your seemingly contradictory goals and realise the overall objective: looking your absolute best.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Get muscle workout
        </h2>
        <p>
         Do this low-repetition, high-weights programme for weeks 1,3,5,7,9. Rest for 60-90 seconds between sets to make sure you're fully recovered and constantly increase the weights you're lifting.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          EXERCISES
         </strong>
        </p>

         Wednesday: arms
