            <div class="content">
             <div class="bb_h1">
              Suffers a crippling lack of depth, variety, &amp; replay value // A bit of fun, but not enough to justify the purchase
             </div>
             <br/>
             <div class="bb_h1">
              *A gamepad is HIGHLY recommended for this title
             </div>
             <br/>
             + Saves and upgrades can be accessed and applied at any time in the arena, and effects are immediately applied
             <br/>
             + As upgrades are unlocked, combat becomes drastically more fluid and cinematic
             <br/>
             + In stark contrast to mobs that populate the waves of a given stage, boss fights are actually quite challenging, and are noticeably skill-dependant
             <br/>
             + Character models are rendered with plenty of detail and look generally adequate
             <br/>
             + Japanese voice-acting is well-done; feels very organic
             <br/>
             + Combat is generally very fun, fast-paced, and visually striking
             <br/>
             <br/>
             - Camera is sub-par and operates unreliably
             <br/>
             - Boring and uninspired environment and audio design
             <br/>
             - Game quickly becomes repetitive and a chore to slog through; this is largely because each level offers little more than the last, and it essentially boils down to 'hack, slash, rinse, repeat' with nothing to break up the pace or keep one interested
             <br/>
             - The game suffers an achievement bug; Steam achievements that have been unlocked during gameplay will remain locked in the Steam application until the game is closed (this is a problem because they will all share an idential time-stamp)*
             <br/>
             - Plot is underdeveloped to the point that it may as well have not been included; utterly forgettable
             <br/>
             - Enemy mobs are cripplingly generic and suffer from lack of variety; each stage is essentially a massive copy/paste of the previous stage, and 'upgraded mobs' share the same design with a different color scheme and slightly higher base stats
             <br/>
             - Extremely short campaign (1-2 hours) and little else in the way of replay value, save for a drive to unlock all achievements, and a narrow selection of unlockable outfits
