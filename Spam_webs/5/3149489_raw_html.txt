        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Creamy Cauliflower Soup and Creating Clarity
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2013-01-11T10:42:50+00:00">
          January 11, 2013
         </span>
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://foodformyfamily.com/author/Shaina" rel="author">
            Shaina Olmanson
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <em>
           Shallots and thyme create an aromatic cauliflower soup that is just the right amount of creamy and indulgent with this easy recipe.
          </em>
          <br/>
          <img alt="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12930" src="http://foodformyfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/creamy-cauliflower-soup-slide.jpg" title="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" width="625"/>
          <br/>
          <a class="pin-it-button" count-layout="horizontal" href="http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Ffoodformyfamily.com%2Frecipes%2Fcreamy-cauliflower-soup-and-creating-clarity&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Ffoodformyfamily.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F01%2Fcreamy-cauliflower-soup-fin.jpg&amp;description=Creamy%20Cauliflower%20Soup%20%23recipe%20via%20FoodforMyFamily.com">
           <img border="0" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/PinExt.png" title="Pin It"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Warm mittens have been pulled over tiny fingers to protect from the winter wind under a star-filled sky. Weve scurried across the frozen driveway and climbed in the car, which is now gliding down the driveway, backing out into the quiet blackness of a winters night.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Mom?
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Yep.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          So, when everyone gets in their car and turns the music on, is it the same music in all the cars that are driving? Her sweet, inquisitiveness comes from the third row seat, which faces backwards so shes staring out the back window at what were leaving behind instead of what were heading towards.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Well, no. There are different kinds of radio stations, local and satellite, and local stations are broadcast in a certain area. Think about when we drive to Grandma and Grandpa Olmansons or on the way to the cabin when the music suddenly gets fuzzy and starts jumping and blipping. There are quite a few radio stations: they play different types of music or they talk about current events or sports. All of the cars can pick what they want from the radio stations in the area. Then there are CDs and MP3 players and phones and everything else you can hook up and listen to, and finally, some people listen to nothing at all. Like how Grandma just drives in silence.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Grandma listens to the radio sometimes, offers the small voice from the center seat.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Mmm, I suppose, but its always talk radio turned down so low the words are imperceptible and it becomes noise. Everyone can choose what they listen to based on what they like and how they feel. Sometimes we listen to slow, quiet music, and other times we feel like turning it up really loud and singing along.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          I like our music.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Never forget that life is more vibrant when youre free to make your own choices and to sit as quiet or sing as loud as you want. I reach for the stereo knob and crank the music up a bit to illustrate my point. The snow shines across the landscape in the moonlight, stark white under the black blanket. Our car becomes a moving beacon of lives being lived.
          <br/>
          <img alt="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12929" height="750" src="http://foodformyfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/creamy-cauliflower-soup-florets.jpg" title="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" width="500"/>
          <br/>
          January tends to be a difficult one for me to get through. If I had to give it a color it would be grey. That light grey of nothingness that seems to stretch on for eternity, a cold color.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Theres this feeling just out of reach. I can imagine obtaining it. Its bright from sunshine streaming in through the south-facing window and warming the space as easily as it provides light for the room, casting a sense of clarity across every surface, free from clutter and crumb. Im searching for that feeling of weightlessness where things come together at the seams, clearing up all the uncertainty, washing away the grit and gravel that has collected along the edge of the snow bank.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Sometimes to find something you need to stop searching for it and just start living. That night in the car we headed to the bookstore and picked out stories to read in the comfort of our home, a home that is cluttered and chaotic, but comforting because of the people is contains.
          <br/>
          <img alt="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12927" height="750" src="http://foodformyfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/creamy-cauliflower-soup-bowl.jpg" title="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" width="500"/>
          <br/>
          The feeling Im searching for can be found in our kitchen where kids scurry through setting the table, inquiring to the smells that waft from pots on the stove, and steal bits of bacon when my back is turned. Its encased in the nightly routine of pausing and being together, of taking the time to stop even when there are piles to be dealt with all around.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Comfort comes in a bowl of simple soup, laid out in front of those you love. Cauliflower is a weird vegetable that seems to hover between broccoli and potato; admittedly, one that Im not instantly drawn to, but adore nonetheless. It fills this soup with a natural creaminess. Keep this light and forgo the splash of cream at the end, or double the richness and stir in a bit of cheese and toss a few bits of bacon on top.
          <br/>
          <img alt="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12928" height="750" src="http://foodformyfamily.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/creamy-cauliflower-soup-fin.jpg" title="Creamy Cauliflower Soup #recipe via FoodforMyFamily.com" width="500"/>
         </p>
         <div id="recipetitle">
          <table width="100%">
           <tr>
            <td>
             <h2>
              <a href="http://foodformyfamily.com/recipe-cards/creamy-cauliflower-soup" rel="bookmark" target="_blank" title="Open Creamy Cauliflower Soup in a print friendly window">
               Creamy Cauliflower Soup
              </a>
             </h2>
            </td>
            <td align="right">
             <a href="http://foodformyfamily.com/recipe-cards/creamy-cauliflower-soup" title="Click here to print this recipe card">
              Print me!
             </a>
            </td>
           </tr>
          </table>
         </div>
         <div id="recipebody">
          2 tablespoons olive oil
          <br/>
          2 shallots, minced
          <br/>
          1/4 cup diced celery
          <br/>
          6 cups vegetable broth or chicken stock
          <br/>
          1 head cauliflower, roughly chopped
          <br/>
          2 bay leaves
          <br/>
          2 sprigs thyme
          <br/>
          Sea salt
          <br/>
          Pepper
          <br/>
          1/4 cup half-and-half, optional
          <br/>
          Infused oil for garnish
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          In a large stockpot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add in the shallots and celery and cook until tender, about 5 minutes. Pour in the stock and add the cauliflower, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring to a boil, and then reduce the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes or until cauliflower is tender.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          Move the pan from the heat, and remove the bay leaves and the thyme sprigs. Using an immersion blender, blend until smooth. Season with salt and pepper. Stir in the half-and-half if desired, and serve warm. Drizzle a bit of oil over the bowls before serving as a garnish.
          <br/>
          <br clear="all"/>
          <br/>
          Makes 6-8 servings.
          <br/>

         </div>
