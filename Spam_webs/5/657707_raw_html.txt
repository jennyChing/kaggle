              <span>

              <h1>
               Simplify Your Holiday Celebrations
              </h1>
             </header>
             <aside class="disclaimer">
             </aside>
             <h2>
              The holidays can be simple and sensational. These ideas will add joy to the season without breaking the bank.
             </h2>
             <div class="bottom">
              <div class="byline">
               By
               <a href="/contributing-writers-and-editors.aspx">
                Regina Boyle Wheeler
               </a>
              </div>
              <div class="reviewedby">
               Medically reviewed by
               <a href="/medical-reviewers.aspx">
                Niya Jones, MD, MPH
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="clear">
             </div>
            </div>
            <div id="video-player-top">
            </div>
            <aside class="aside">
            </aside>
            <p>
             The Grinch learned the truth in the end  Christmas doesnt
come from a store. Small gestures or personal, homemade gifts can have
more meaning to friends and loved ones than expensive ones.
            </p>
            <p>
             Even
in difficult
economic times,
             <a href="/health-report/seasonal-guide-to-healthy-living.aspx">
              holiday celebrations
             </a>
             dont have to be out of
reach. Taking joy in the simple things wont cost you a lot and can add
richness to your holiday traditions. If you are challenged financially
and your lifestyle changes, you can still find fulfilling avenues to
celebrate, says Terry Sidford, a certified life coach in Park City,
Utah. Give a gift that is not material and costs you nothing. Remember,
the most meaningful gifts come from the heart, Sidford says.
            </p>
            <p>
             Heres how to think outside the gift box:
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Pick
up the phone.
             </strong>
             Making a phone call to someone you have
been thinking about to wish them happy holidays might just make their
day. Its also a great way to reconnect with old friends from high
school or college that you havent spoken to in years.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Count
your blessings.
             </strong>
             Make a gratitude list of all the
non-material gifts you have already been blessed with. Take time to
celebrate your family. Sit in a circle
so everyone can see each other. Take turns acknowledging what you admire
or love about each person. The person receiving can only say thank
you. There is no greater gift than an acknowledgment. This is a
wonderful activity to continue each year, says Sidford.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Listen
to music.
             </strong>
             Instead of watching TV, play inspiring music
that fills your heart and fondly reminds you of past holiday
celebrations, suggests Sidford.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Get
crafty.
             </strong>
             Make arts and crafts to give as gifts and be
sure to personalize them. Browse the aisles at an arts-and-crafts store
to get ideas, such as creating a photo album with old pictures that
bring back great memories.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Lift
up your voice.
             </strong>
             Sing out loud with friends or family for
no reason. Host a neighborhood caroling party  it could turn into a new
holiday tradition.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Volunteer
at a shelter.
             </strong>
             According to the National Alliance to End
Homelessness, economic hard times are forcing more people out of their
homes. Lending a hand at a shelter or donating to a food pantry is a
selfless way to mark the holidays. Go to
             <a href="http://www.homelessshelterdirectory.org/" rel="nofollow" target="_new">
              homelessshelterdirectory.org
             </a>
             to find a
shelter near you.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Make it
yourself.
             </strong>
             Instead of spending money on pricey holiday
greeting cards, create homemade ones with uplifting messages. Bake homemade
cookies and call in the neighbors for an old-fashioned cookie
swap.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Spread good cheer.
             </strong>
             Tell five people you know why they are special to you,
says Sidford. Perhaps your friends will pay it forward, spreading warm
holiday feelings throughout your community.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Give
the gift of food.
             </strong>
             Invite someone in need over to dinner.
An elderly neighbor or a person who is looking for work will appreciate
the gesture and the companionship.
            </p>
            <p>
             If money
is tight, finances may make it necessary for your kids to pare down
their holiday gift list. Sidford advises explaining to them that this
doesnt take away from the wealth you have as a family. Reassure your
kids that the family doesnt change, just the current spending habits.
Be sure to remind them what they have to be grateful for, she adds.
            </p>
            <p>
             Sidford also suggests making cost cutting as fun
as possible. 
             <a href="/health-report/seasonal-guide-to-healthy-living/winter-produce.aspx">
              Cook dinner at home
             </a>
             together,
             <a href="/fitness-pictures/winter-fitness-activities-for-the-whole-family.aspx">
              play games
             </a>
             , get
creative and make things. Maybe youll discover the simpler,
more meaningful lifestyle is more rewarding and youll make the changes
permanent, she says.
            </p>
