    <p>
     <!--mfunc include_once('/home/ftakj/public_html/wp-content/plugins/ad-injection/adshow.php') -->
     <!--/mfunc-->
     <br/>
     Defense is one of those positions that can often be overlooked despite how extremely important it is. Although most defensemen dont score on the same level as forwards, which leads people to place less relevance on the position, many fantasy hockey leagues require at least four starters on defense. In a standard Yahoo! public league, thats 40% of your starting skaters! If youre not careful, you could end up starting some scrubs and that could really hurt you. Here are five non-scrub blueliners that you might just find to be available in your league.
    </p>
    <strong>
     Roman Josi (NSH  71% owned)
    </strong>
    7 Goals, 20 Assists, 27 Points, +10, 12 PIM, 6 PPPStill available in a surprising 29% of fantasy hockey leagues for some reason, Josi ranks among the top fifteen fantasy defensemen in the NHL right now. His excellent +/- combined with his high point total makes him very valuable indeed. If Josi is available in your league scoop him up without a second thought.
    <strong>
     Dennis Wideman (CGY  66% owned)
    </strong>
    10 goals, 13 assists, 23 points, -4, 24 PIM, 9 PPPWideman is a free agent in 34% of all fantasy leagues despite the fact he is one of just six defensemen with ten goals scored. Perhaps the fact Wideman has 23 points in 41 games when he scored just 21 points in 46 games all of last season has some people worried his production might drop off but the idea of him suddenly not doing anything for 40 games is a little absurd. Look for him to approach the 40 point mark by seasons end.
    <strong>
     Dan Boyle (NYR  62% owned)
    </strong>
    4 goals, 5 assists, 9 points, +9, 6 PIM, 7 PPPBoyle was once a must-own fantasy asset but his value has declined as he has gotten older. Boyle missed a good chunk of time due to injury early this year so his nine points isnt actually as bad as it looks. Boyle has been pretty hot of late with seven points in his last seven games played.
    <strong>
     Nick Leddy (NYI  33% owned)
    </strong>
    6 goals, 12 assists, 18 points, +8, 10 PIM, 5 PPPLeddy has actually been having a good fantasy season as far as defensemen go with 18 points and a respectable +/- rating in 41 games. That would put him on pace for about 36 points. You cant ask for much more than that and Leddy would make a good 3rd or 4th D-man on most fantasy teams.
    <strong>
     Simon Despres (PIT  13% owned)
    </strong>
    1 goal, 10 assists, 11 points, +8, 52 PIM, 1 PPPIf your league has penalty minutes as a category then Depres can be a big help. Despres currently ranks among the top 10 at the position in that category with 52 PIM. PIM is just as important a category as any other so make sure you dont overlook it in head to head leagues.
