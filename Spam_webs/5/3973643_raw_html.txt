             <p>
              Coming upwith the Day 3rd
              <strong>
               Itinerary
              </strong>
              <strong>
               of High Sierra Trail
              </strong>
              
              <em>
               One Trekking destination you should never miss
              </em>
              in North America.
             </p>
             <h4>
              Day 3 
              <strong>
               to Moraine Lake (12.8 km) or Upper Funston Meadow (19.2 km)
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <p>
              After 2 continuousdays of hiking, the journey from Big Arroyo to Moraine Lake is relatively easy. If you have a tight schedule, you wantto bypass Moraine Lake, an extra .8 miles (1.3 km), and proceed directly to Upper Funston Meadow, at the bottom of the Kern Trench.
             </p>
             <p>
              <span id="more-9104">
              </span>
             </p>
             <h2>
              High Sierra Trail  from the day third trekking
             </h2>
             <p>
              After when you leave Big Arroyo Junction, the trail makes a moderate ascent up the north wall of the Big Arroyo, providing views of the east side of the Great Western Divide.
              <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
               Once it reaches the Chagoopa Plateau, the trail levels off, and soon reaches a junction on a tributary of Chagoopa Creek.
              </span>
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_9106" style="width: 811px">
              <img alt="moraine_lake" class="wp-image-9106" height="601" src="http://nipunscorp.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/moraine_lake.jpg" width="801"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Moraine Lake
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
              The right-hand trail branches off from the main High Sierra Trail to Moraine Lake (48 km from Crescent Meadow). The left-hand trail follows a more direct route across the Chagoopa Plateau, rejoining the Moraine Lake Trail at Sky Parlor Meadow (49.3 km). From here, it descends to the bottom of the Kern Trench. The drop is moderate at first, but concludes with a series of steep, rocky switchbacks. This stretch of the trail can be long and dry, so be sure to fill your water bottles at Sky Parlor Meadow. To reach Upper Funston Meadow (34.5 miles/55.2 km), turn south (right) upon reaching the bottom of the canyon.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <strong>
               Day 4 
              </strong>
              <strong>
               To Junction Meadow (21.9 km from Moraine Lake; 15.5 km from Upper Funston):
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <p>
              If you are coming from Moraine Lake, continue about 1 mile (1.6 km) beyond the lake to rejoin the High Sierra Trail at Sky Parlor Meadow. Descend into the Kern Trench, but turn north (left) upon reaching the bottom. If you are coming from Upper Funston, retrace your steps to the junction with the trail from the Chagoopa plateau, and continue north along the bottom of the canyon.
             </p>
             <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_9107" style="width: 737px">
              <a href="http://nipunscorp.com/2015/05/08/day-3-trekking-one-trekking-destination-you-should-never-miss-high-sierra-trail/kern-river/" rel="attachment wp-att-9107">
               <img alt="Kern RIver" class=" wp-image-9107" height="487" src="http://nipunscorp.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/kern-river.jpg" width="727"/>
              </a>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Kern River
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
              The Kern River Trail drops into a marshy area beyond the junction, then leads through a forest of Jeffrey pine and incense-cedar.
              <strong>
               Keep an eye through the trees to the west to catch a glimpse of Chagoopa Falls tumbling down from the rim of the canyon.
              </strong>
              At 58.9 km from the trailhead (excluding the side-trip to Moraine Lake), you will arrive at
              <strong>
               Kern Hot Springs
              </strong>
              , complete with a crude cement bathtub in which to soak your aching muscles. The water from the spring is 115 degree F (46 degrees C). The tub is only a few feet from the cold, rushing Kern River, and runoff from the tub mixes with river water to create a warm pool, allowing you your choice of temperatures for bathing.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               (Please avoid using soap in the river .
              </em>
              ) If youhave enough time, you may want to camp here, but you must stay in the designated camp sites, which are often heavily used.
             </p>
             <p>
              Beyond Kern Hot Spring, the trail continues along the bottom of the glaciated valley of the Kern River. This canyon runs almost due north and south for about 25 miles (40 km) along the Kern Canyon fault. The trail ascends steadily to Junction Meadow (67.2 km). Hiking along the bottom of the canyon can be hot and dry during the middle of the day. If you have camped at Kern Hot Springs, however, remember that cold air from the surrounding peaks flows down the canyon at night, and that the sun will not clear the 2000 to 5000 (610 to 1524 meter) high canyon walls until relatively late in the morning.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <strong>
               Day 5  to Crabtree Meadow (14.2 km)
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_9108" style="width: 610px">
              <img alt="Carbtree Meadow" class="wp-image-9108 size-full" height="400" src="http://nipunscorp.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Crabtree-Meadow.jpg" width="600"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Carbtree Meadow
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
              Leave the park-like Jeffrey pines of Junction Meadow and cross a steep, rocky slope covered with manzanita and currant. Soon, the trail begins to climb out of the Kern Canyon, offering increasingly impressive views of the canyon to the south, and west to the Kaweah Peaks, which were to the east of you three days ago when you came over Kaweah Gap.
             </p>
             <p>
              At 48.9 miles from the trailhead (78.2 km), you will reach the junction with the
              <strong>
               John Muir Trail
              </strong>
              , which runs from
              <strong>
               Yosemite Valley to the summit of Mt. Whitney
              </strong>
              . There are campsites here, as well as at Crabtree Meadow (85 km). Guitar Lake (56.6 miles/90.6 km) is the last campsite with water before the summit of Mt. Whitney.
             </p>
             <h4>
              <strong>
               Day 6  to the summit of Mt. Whitney
              </strong>
             </h4>
             <p>
              (To be continue)
             </p>
