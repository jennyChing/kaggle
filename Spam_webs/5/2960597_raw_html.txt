       <h1>
        <a href="/">
         100 Days of Batman
        </a>
       </h1>
       <span class="description">
        <div class="desc">
         <span style="font-style:italic;font-weight:bold">
          Brushing up on old talents and learning new tricks with my pal, the mutha@%ing Batman, one new drawing at a time.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size:13pt;font-weight:bold">
          <b>
           The Goal:
          </b>
         </span>
         to post 100 Batman drawings.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size:13pt;font-weight:bold;">
          <b>
           The Reason:
          </b>
         </span>
         Getting off my ass. Its been too long since I sketched on a regular basis. iPad apps like Sketchbook Pro make it very easy and convenient to rehabilitate a once-heralded drawing ability. They are going to be ugly and embarrassing at the start, but its all a shameless exercise in growth.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size:13pt;font-weight:bold">
          <b>
           Why Batman?:
          </b>
         </span>
         1) Batman is awesome. 2) I grew up drawing superheroes, so it feels like a natural jumping on point. Drawing something like Batman ad nauseam takes away the often paralyzing search for subject matter and just lets me cut loose.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size:13pt;font-weight:bold">
          <b>
           The Rules:
          </b>
         </span>
         I'll post 6 drawings a week. Crime rests on Sunday. Re-colours and slight variations dont count-they all have to be from scratch. The good, the bad &amp; the ugly will all be documented here. The worse the drawings are the more Ill want to bury the post with better ones.
        </div>
       </span>
