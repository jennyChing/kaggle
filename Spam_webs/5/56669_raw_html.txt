      <h1>
       Theatre for improving community outcomes
      </h1>
      <p class="author-meta">
       By
       <a href="http://stagebitz.com/author/bek-lambert/" rel="author" title="Posts by Bek Lambert">
        Bek Lambert
       </a>
       , our Content Gal
      </p>
     </hgroup>
     <img alt="Theatre companies for improving community outcomes" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="768" src="http://stagebitz.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Theatre-for-improving-community-outcomes.jpg" width="1366"/>
     <p>
      Having already explored
      <a href="http://stagebitz.com/2013/11/socially-responsible-theatre-companies/" title="More than great theatre: At risk, homeless &amp; making theatre">
       theatre for the homeless
      </a>
      and
      <a href="http://stagebitz.com/2013/11/more-than-great-theatre-telling-the-tricky-stories/" title="More than great theatre : Telling the tricky stories">
       the companies telling the tricky stories
      </a>
      , we now turn our gaze to theatre companies and groups who are making theatre for better community outcomes.
     </p>
     <p>
      These theatre companies are tackling mental health and overall wellbeing head on in order to remove the stigma, help the people involved, and to share their delicate human stories along the way. Or they are providing a vehicle for those who live in isolated areas a chance to tell their story and embrace better life outcomes through confidence, access to arts, and even on the ground support like basic literacy, equal rights or being heard.
     </p>
     <p>
      Lets have a look at the theatre companies trying to inject a little compassion and happiness in the world.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <b>
       Theatre for mental health and well being
      </b>
     </h3>
     <h3>
      <b>
       <a href="http://warehousecircus.org.au/">
        Warehouse Circus, Canberra
       </a>
       , AUS
      </b>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Using co-operation, community and involvement, Warehouse Circus Canberra uses social circus principles to provide a creative outlet for youth, especially those facing challenges with their physical or mental health. Since 1990, this troupe has been bringing circus to workshops, classes and festival participation to promote self esteem, creative expression and acceptance through performance.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <b>
       <a href="http://www.steppingouttheatre.co.uk/">
        Stepping Out, Bristol,
       </a>
       England
      </b>
     </h3>
     <p>
      One of the most recognised names in applying theatre and drama to help people with mental health issues, Stepping Out Theatre Company has been helping people tell their story since 1997. Stepping Out gives people who have dealt with mental health issues of all kinds a safe, creative and fun place to devise their own works in order to share their struggle with a wider audience.
     </p>
     <p>
      This process of creativity not only helps the participants feel better about themselves, it also helps the community at large understand what it is like living with mental health issues. Coupled with support for institutions that regular work with people who have mental health issues, Stepping Out ensures that theatre is used to remove stigma, relieve stress, and provide a possible channel to help reach people through creativity.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <b>
       <a href="http://www.artsandhealth.ie/directory/smashing-times-theatre-company/">
        Smashing Times, Dublin,
       </a>
       Ireland
      </b>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Working in the realm of mental health and suicide prevention through theatre is the aim of Smashing Times. A professional theatre company, this group works with anyone from youths in schools through to adults in community based groups to workshop, perform, and support structure to enable the exploring of suicide as an issue, and remove the stigma to create a better chance of conversation and prevention.
     </p>
     <p>
      A tough subject, suicide often sees a history of people struggling in silence. Through looking at life stressors through to being able to talk about difficulties, to looking out for signs in someone who is struggling and what to do, Smashing Times goes beyond telling the stories to remove the taboo to practical action. It also explores the impact of suicide on the people left behind through monologues and scripted work.
     </p>
     <p>
      Part awareness, part sharing the story, and part action plan, Smashing Times is taking the power of theatre to new heights in the mental health world.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <b>
       Theatre for improving outcomes
      </b>
     </h3>
     <h3>
      <b>
       <a href="http://www.atyp.com.au/education/regional">
        ATYP Kicking up Dust
       </a>
       , Katherine, NT, AUS
      </b>
     </h3>
     <p>
      High school kids in Katherine are gaining support from Sydneys Australian Theatre for Young People through Kicking Up Dust, a programme that works with local high schools to share the stories of remote outback kids. The aim is to instil confidence in the participants and share their often unique yet overlooked stories with the rest of the country.
     </p>
     <p>
      Drama workshops, film making lessons and songwriting sessions are all included in the programme that encourages the youth of the Katherine region to tell it like it is as they grow up in the Australian outback. With a play production on the way as well as a series of mini documentaries about life as a teen in some of Australias most remote country, Kicking Up Dust promises to be a unique window into the lives of Indigenous and non-Indigenous students living in the top end.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <b>
       <a href="http://artedelmundoecuador.com/">
        Fundacion Arte Del Mundo,
       </a>
       Ecuador
      </b>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In a tiny little town in Ecuador, South America is a black box theatre built by volunteers from all around the world. The aim of the theatre is to promote literacy and artistic expression in the children of the community.
     </p>
     <p>
      Using reading, storytelling and theatre, this theatre company is a not for profit operation that works to ensure kids gain freedom from poverty and improve their outcomes through education and books. FAM also provides a quasi community centre for all kinds of subjects including film screenings, yoga classes, photography, martial arts and even chess to the locals.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="http://artedelmundoecuador.com/projects">
       Volunteers
      </a>
      have the ability to work on theatre projects, help keep the theatre in working order, teach English and pitch in with the community classes.
     </p>
     <p>
      FAM uses theatre and storytelling to bring community and education to a small town. Bliss!
     </p>
     <h3>
      <b>
       <a href="http://www.amakhosiculturalcenter.com/">
        Amakhosi Theatre Productions,
       </a>
       Zimbabwe, Africa
      </b>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Taking on the ideas of poverty, social justice, inequality, health and all kinds of ideas that are bound to get you into trouble is
      <b>
       Amakhosi Theatre Productions
      </b>
      (aka
      <b>
       Amakhosi Academy of Performing Arts
      </b>
      ), aZimbabwe theatre company. So much so that the director has even been arrested on occasion for telling the sorts of stories governments dont want people to hear.
     </p>
     <p>
      What began as a karate club quickly moved towards combining sports with the art of theatre and storytelling for the empowerment of the people in a
      <a href="http://www.amakhosiculturalcenter.com/">
       community arts centre
      </a>
      . Now they have their own cultural centre and tour nationally and throughout Africa with a mix of all kinds of performance including theatre, dance,
      <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mM6A36DE8PE">
       hip hop
      </a>
      and improvisation. They aim to marry community with story and provide a place to embrace and revel in creativities power to bring womens rights, examining critical political issues, health outcomes and provide a real voice for the people in townships all around Zimbabwe.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <b>
       Improving Community Outcomes through Theatre
      </b>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Using theatre to bring people together to tell stories that are relatively hard to tell or relate to is nothing new. But what is different about these brave companies is they are taking people who feel marginalised, isolated or misunderstood and providing them with a viable platform to share who they are with a willing audience.
     </p>
     <p>
      The empowerment that is gained from being able to relate your experience whether you live in the remotest parts of the world, through to being able to share a personal journey inside ones head with others cannot be under estimated.
     </p>
     <p>
      Through bringing theatre and creative practice to people who previously felt alone, entire communities begin to benefit.
     </p>
