           <p>
            The big day has come and you are full of excitement you are finally going to adopt a cute little puppy!Its very likely that you have spent nights and days dreaming about just
            <strong>
             <a href="http://topdogtips.com/how-to-choose-a-dog-breed/" target="_blank" title="How to Choose a Dog Breed: Make the Best Choice">
              the right breed
             </a>
            </strong>
            and finding the perfect
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/best-dog-names-male/" target="_blank" title="50 Popular Best Dog Names for Male Dogs">
             name for the puppy
            </a>
            . Now it is time to bring her home.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             So how do you prepare for puppy adoption?
            </strong>
            What arrangements do youneed to go through in order to ensure a comfortable and appropriate home environment for your new family member? Lets talka little about adopting not only the right puppy, but also the right attitude towards dog adoption and howanew owner needs to prepare for the big day.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             First of all, remember that a lot of understanding will be required from your part
            </strong>
            . Your new four legged pal will most likely have gone through some stress because she was separated from the family, friends and is moving into a new place with lots of new people. Prepare to be forgiving, because your pooch will make mistakes and not always follow your directions. Its normal for a young dog, and positive reinforcement is the only way to teach a puppy how to behave in her new home.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <strong>
              RELATED:
              <a href="http://topdogtips.com/puppy-training-101-basic-walkthrough/" target="_blank" title="Puppy Training 101: Basic Walkthrough">
               Puppy training 101  Basic walkthrough
              </a>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Begin by allowing your pet to explore
            </strong>
            . Let her sniff around, relieve herself in the backyard and run around the place to get to know the surroundings. Young pups are adventurous, which is one of the cutest, most interestingthings about them. If youre a parent, have your
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/dogs-kids/" target="_blank" title="Dogs and Kids">
             pooch meet the kids
            </a>
            as soon as possible, preferably at the shelter even before you make a final decision to adopt a dog.
           </p>
           <p>
            Lets talk about preparation for arrival of a new puppy.
           </p>
           <h2 style="text-align: center;">
            Necessary dog supplies for first-time puppy owners
           </h2>
           <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_5261" style="width: 300px;">
            <a href="http://cdn.topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-1.jpg">
             <img alt="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" class="td-modal-image td-post-image-right wp-image-5261 size-medium" height="225" src="//topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-1-300x225.jpg" title="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: bullcitydogs
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            We have previouslywrote an
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/must-have-dog-supplies-every-dog-owner-should-have/" target="_blank" title="Must Have Dog Supplies Every Dog Owner Should Have">
             extensive article on
             <strong>
              must-have dog supplies
             </strong>
            </a>
            every dog owner should look into, but there are a few things that a young dog wont need and some that she will. In this post, this is exactly what were going to cover.
           </p>
           <p>
            Ideally,before the big day arrives, you should have already laid in all of the dog supplies you will need. Do not leave this until the very last moment, and especially until the dog is already home. Prepare in advance, and if you havent already, make a quick order of
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/dog-products/" target="_blank" title="Best Dog Products">
             dog products fromAmazon
            </a>
            before you go to pick up your puppy.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <strong>
              RELATED:
              <a href="http://topdogtips.com/how-to-housebreak-a-puppy/" target="_blank" title="How to Housebreak a Puppy Effectively">
               How to housebreak a puppy effectively
              </a>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Your dog supplies shopping list will depend a lot on your budget; theres always more things available for the dog than we can afford. For example,
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/best-car-seats-for-dogs/" target="_blank" title="Top 5 Best Car Seats for Dogs">
             dog car seats
            </a>
            would be a great idea for her pets first
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/car-travel" target="_blank" title="Dog Car Travel">
             car travel
            </a>
            trip from the shelter back to your house. However, this isnt a necessity yet, so if youre on a tight budget, hold off on purchasing things like dog car seats,
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/best-dog-pool/" target="_blank" title="Top 5 Best Dog Pools">
             dog pools
            </a>
            or
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/top-5-best-pet-clippers-for-dogs-grooming/" target="_blank" title="Top 5 Best Dog Clippers for Pet Grooming">
             dog grooming clippers
            </a>
            . These are all important, but not urgent.
           </p>
           <h3>
            Preparing: Dog food and bowls
           </h3>
           <figure class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_5263" style="width: 300px;">
            <a href="http://cdn.topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-3.jpg">
             <img alt="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" class="td-modal-image td-post-image-left wp-image-5263 size-medium" height="225" src="//topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-3-300x225.jpg" title="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: bullcitydogs
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            <strong>
             To start, you will need bowls for your pets food and water
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            Consider the size of your puppy before you purchase dog bowls so you can be sure shecan reach herfood and water. A lot of pet owners also grab a plastic tray to place under those pet food bowls to preventany constantwater spills or dog foodsfalling on your hardwood floor. Its true 
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/puppies" target="_blank" title="Puppies">
             puppies
            </a>
            are messy eaters, so it is best to be prepared.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Make sure you also stock up on puppy food in advance,
            </strong>
            and purchase at least a few days worth of dog food.
           </p>
           <p>
            Be aware that if you choose to feed your puppy a brand they are not used to, they may have upset stomachs; it takes a while for the dog to get used to
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/top-ten-best-dry-dog-food-brands/" target="_blank" title="Top Ten Best Dry Dog Food Brands">
             new dog food brands
            </a>
            . It may be easier for both of you if you purchase the same food she is used to eating, but you also need to consider whether that pet food is good for a young dog.
           </p>
           <h3>
            For the trip: Dog leash, collar and carrier
           </h3>
           <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_5264" style="width: 300px;">
            <a href="http://cdn.topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-4.jpg">
             <img alt="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" class="td-modal-image td-post-image-right wp-image-5264 size-medium" height="228" src="//topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-4-300x228.jpg" title="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: Cindy Higby
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            <strong>
             You will need to bring a pet collar and leash with you to pick up your puppy
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            It is best to use a regular
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/dog-leashes/" target="_blank" title="Dog Leashes">
             dog leash
            </a>
            and not a retractable one until your puppy is used to walking on a leash. When you take a puppy from the shelter, the staff will help you with the leash, so you dont have to worry about knowing how to do this in the beginning.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <strong>
              RELATED:
              <a href="http://topdogtips.com/how-to-teach-a-dog-to-walk-on-a-leash/" target="_blank" title="How to Teach a Dog to Walk On a Leash">
               How to teach a dog to walk on a leash
              </a>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             In addition, you may also want to bring a plastic carrier for the car ride
            </strong>
            . Remember, this is a new dog whos not familiar with the changing environment and the whole world in general. Sounless someone will be going with you to pick up a puppy (which is very advisable) and who can hold onto the puppy, you must have a
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/dog-carriers/" target="_blank" title="Dog Carriers">
             dog carrier
            </a>
            with you.This is for everyones safety.
           </p>
           <h3>
            For the home: Dog toys and crates
           </h3>
           <figure class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_5262" style="width: 300px;">
            <a href="http://cdn.topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-2.jpg">
             <img alt="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" class="td-modal-image td-post-image-left wp-image-5262 size-medium" height="225" src="//topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-2-300x225.jpg" title="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: Bev Sykes
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            <strong>
             At home, you pretty muchMUSThave a dog crate
            </strong>
            waiting for your puppy if you want to avoid potential
            <strong>
             <a href="http://topdogtips.com/most-common-dog-behavior-problems/" target="_blank" title="25 Most Common Dog Behavior Problems">
              behavioral problems
             </a>
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            The reason being is that using a good dog crate can help you in many ways when training, teaching and housebreaking your pooch. Also, your puppy will feel safer if he/she has a spot just for her  dogs love having their own dens they can call their actual home.
           </p>
           <p>
            For a puppy coming into a new home is scary and she will need a place to relax. Usually,
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/dog-crates" target="_blank" title="Dog Crates">
             dog crates
            </a>
            serve this purpose the best, but depending on your financial situation you can also purchase a
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/dog-beds" target="_blank" title="Dog Beds">
             dog bed
            </a>
            or even a
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/best-couch-for-dogs/" target="_blank" title="Top 5 Best Couches for Dogs">
             dog couch
            </a>
            for extra comfort and place it either into the crate, or have it as a separate spot for your puppy. If youre strapped for cash, even old blankets will workjust as well in a dog crate as they can be moved around and washed easily. You will also need the crate as a place to confine your puppy when you are gone and as you
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/housebreaking" target="_blank" title="Dog Housebreaking">
             housebreak her
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <strong>
              RELATED:
              <a href="http://topdogtips.com/top-5-best-soft-sided-dog-crate/" target="_blank" title="Top 5: Best Soft Sided Dog Crate">
               Top 5 best soft sided dog crates for puppies
              </a>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Purchase a few
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/best-puppy-toys-for-dogs-that-chew/" target="_blank" title="Top 25 Best Puppy Toys for Dogs that Chew A Lot">
             <strong>
              great puppy toys
             </strong>
            </a>
            ahead of time. Puppies love to chew a lot and unless you want your shoes and furniture to be destroyed in the matter of hours, provide the young dogwith safe alternatives that can help her with teething. Use positive reinforcement to properly
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/how-to-train-puppy/" target="_blank" title="How to Train a Puppy?">
             train your puppy
            </a>
            to switch from furniture and other bad chewing habits onto dog chew toys.
           </p>
           <h3>
            For security: Pet ID tags and microchipping
           </h3>
           <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_5265" style="width: 300px;">
            <a href="http://cdn.topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-5.jpg">
             <img alt="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" class="td-modal-image td-post-image-right wp-image-5265 size-medium" height="200" src="//topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-5-300x200.jpg" title="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: paul stumpr
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            After you brought your puppy home andhave chosena name for the dog,
            <strong>
             purchase a pet ID tag for her collar
            </strong>
            that has your name and phone number on it in case she ever runs away, gets lost or is stolen.
           </p>
           <p>
            This is an important part to ensure canines safety, and the one that must be addressed within a few days after adopting a puppy.
           </p>
           <p>
            The next step after you have purchased dog ID tags is injecting a microchip under your dogs skin. All you have to do is take your puppy to a local vet clinic(which you will do eventually anyway, and preferably as soon as possible), pay about $50 and it will take seconds to go through this process.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Louise Murray, DVM
            </strong>
            ,
            <a href="http://pets.webmd.com/features/microchipping-your-dog-or-cat" target="_blank">
             explains
            </a>
            what microchipping is:
           </p>
           <blockquote class="td_quote_box td_box_center">
            <p>
             A needle is used to place a little chip under the animals skin, usually between the shoulder blades. That chip has a unique number on it that can be picked up and read by a scanner. It can be done to lots of different animals, including horses, dogs, cats, ferrets, and most other mammals.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            According to
            <a href="http://www.sawanetwork.org/" target="_blank">
             SAWA
            </a>
            , only about 20% of lost dogs are ever returned to their rightful owners. The number is obviously not high enough, therefore, having your dog wear a
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tag/dog-collars" target="_blank" title="Dog Collars">
             dog collar
            </a>
            with pet ID tags is vital. Same goes to microchipping your puppy for extra security and safety in case of a disaster.
           </p>
           <h2 style="text-align: center;">
            Welcoming a puppy into her newhome
           </h2>
           <figure class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_5266" style="width: 300px;">
            <a href="http://cdn.topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-6.jpg">
             <img alt="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" class="td-modal-image td-post-image-left wp-image-5266 size-medium" height="201" src="//topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-6-300x201.jpg" title="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: Eric Danley
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Your poochwill probably be very excited and nervous when you get her home
            </strong>
            , so be ready to spend a lot of time with herright away.
           </p>
           <p>
            You need to make the dog comfortable, and that takes work and patience. Some puppies will want to sniff every inch of the house, while others want to just sit and take it all in before they explore. Give your puppy time to adjust, get comfortable, learn the place and then introduce him to other pets and family members.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <strong>
              RELATED:
              <a href="http://topdogtips.com/how-to-choose-the-right-dog-toys-by-age/" target="_blank" title="How to Choose the Right Dog Toys by Age">
               How to choose the right puppy toys by age
              </a>
             </strong>
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            Young dogs cant hold it in for too long, so your puppy will needto go outside often, especially as you begin to housebreak her. She will not tell when she needs to go, so you must beproactive in this and constantly monitor your pooch if you dont want to continue cleaning up after her.Take herout as soon as you get up in the morning and as often as possible during the day, especially after she eats or drinks anything. There are many techniques on housebreaking, so learning
            <a href="http://topdogtips.com/tips-on-toilet-training-puppies/" target="_blank" title="10 Tips on Toilet Training Puppies">
             effective training methods
            </a>
            will pay off big time.
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             <strong>
              The first few nights at home willbe long ones for you and your puppy.
             </strong>
            </em>
           </p>
           <figure class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_5267" style="width: 300px;">
            <a href="http://cdn.topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-7.jpg">
             <img alt="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" class="td-modal-image td-post-image-right wp-image-5267 size-medium" height="199" src="//topdogtips.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Youve-Adopted-a-Puppy-Now-What-7-300x199.jpg" title="You've Adopted a Puppy - Now What" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: Jason
            </figcaption>
           </figure>
           <p>
            Choose a spot for your puppy to sleep and place her soft dog crate there the first night. She may cry for you so be ready to go to her and comfort as best you can.
           </p>
           <p>
            Unless you plan to stay awake all night, it is best for her to sleep in a confined area until she is older, no matter how pitiful she may sound as she whines. Not only will this save you a messy house, but it is safer for the dog as well.
           </p>
           <p>
            You will need to begin your puppys training routine as soon as you bring herhome. While she may not need to know how to shake right away, you will want your puppy to begin to come when you call and to sit on command.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Again, all of this isfor hersafety
            </strong>
            . When you take herout on a leash, gently keep herbeside you and do not let your puppylead you. Start right away showing herthat you are in charge here.
           </p>
           <p>
            When you are training your puppy, remember that positive reinforcements work much better than negative ones. Every time you catch your puppy doing something right  whether it is using the bathroom outside or walking beside you  praise her and use plenty of physical reinforcement. Rubbing herbelly or scratching behind herears are great ways to show your puppy you are happy with what she did.
           </p>
           <p>
            The most important thing you need to do when you bring your puppy home is to spend plenty of time with her until shegets used to the place, new things, new people and even yourself. If at all possible, bring the puppy home when you can spend a few days with her, such as a Friday afternoon, or possibly take a couple of days off work. Always be patient with the puppy and know that her primary aim is only toplease and love you.
           </p>
