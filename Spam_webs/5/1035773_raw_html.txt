          <h1 class="blog-title">
           We just Love Exposed Wooden Beams
          </h1>
         </div>
         <div class="blog-date">
          <span class="date">
           October 29, 2014
          </span>
          <span>
           / Author: Charlie Murphy
          </span>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="post-short">
         <div class="blog-featured-image">
          <a href="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/we-just-love-exposed-wooden-beams/">
           <img alt="We just Love Exposed Wooden Beams" class="attachment-blog-listing wp-post-image" height="436" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/exposed-wooden-beams.jpg" title="We just Love Exposed Wooden Beams" width="700"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <div class="post-short-content">
          <p>
           We are lucky to live in a country where Exposed Wooden Beams are in so many everyday buildings and if you happen to live in a home with authentic exposed beams, we are certainly jealous! We just Love Exposed Wooden beams, whether its one or two peaking out from behind the plaster work or a jaw dropping ceiling full of beams.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Fall in Love with Exposed Beams
          </h2>
          <p>
           This amazing church conversion in London has the ultimate exposed beam WOW drool factor. Looking up from the lounge in cool white, the beams arch over the modern mezzanine level, reminding you that they are part of the very structure.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Exposed Beams - church conversion" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-428" height="467" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Exposed-Beams.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The mezzanine level in modern glass and stainless steel fixings allow an unobtrusive view of the exposed beams. The contrast between the beams, the glass and that amazing church window, makes this one my favorite contemporary interior blend.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Exposed Beams - Wow factor" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-429" height="432" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Exposed-Beams-1.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           This modern London home has left the beams exposed above the kitchen area creating a separation between the living and dining areas on either side, with clean white edges.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="exposed beam modern" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-430" height="440" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/exposed-beam-2.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Timber clad first floor volumes span the original walls in this modern warehouse, creating large open plan areas below that merge with three distinct outdoor spaces.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="exposed beam separate rooms" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-431" height="433" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/exposed-beam-3.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           This 700yr old London tavern provides the unstructured beams, typical of old London. This is shabby chic living at its best. If those beams could talk!
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="exposed beams 700yrs old" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-432" height="437" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/exposed-beam-4.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           This loft conversion created from a former bakery, provides a very dramatic triple height ceiling. Mixing old with new in a raw industrial feel, with exposed cast iron radiators, whilst framing the exposed beams.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="exposed beams in former bakery" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-433" height="461" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/exposed-beam-5.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           This unique Victorian barn conversion blends fresh plastered walls, exposed beam ceiling and exposed brick walls. making this an expose-es dream. Modern down lights are evenly spaced leaving the largest portion of beams exposed, and thats the whole idea.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="exposed beams converted barn" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-434" height="334" src="http://www.charliespineandoak.co.uk/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/exposed-beam-6.jpg" width="700"/>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           Have we converted you into a secret beam lover?
          </p>
