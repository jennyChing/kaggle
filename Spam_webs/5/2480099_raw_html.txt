       <h2>
        How to Make a Homemade Sail Boat
       </h2>
       <h3>
        <img src="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/themes/crisp/images/bullett.png"/>
        Supplies:
       </h3>
       <ul>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Log or Flat Piece of Wood
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Stick
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Sail (Leaf or Square Piece of Cardboard)
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000NPPAPM?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=213733&amp;creative=393185&amp;creativeASIN=B000NPPAPM&amp;linkCode=shr&amp;tag=4tunate-20&amp;qid=1351288856&amp;sr=8-2&amp;keywords=needle+nose+pliers">
           Needle Nose Pliers
          </a>
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Body of Water
         </strong>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/howtomakeahomemadeboatwithalog.jpg">
         <img alt="how to make a homemade boat with a log " border="0" height="353" src="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/howtomakeahomemadeboatwithalog_thumb.jpg" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: inline; border-left-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px" title="how to make a homemade boat with a log " width="520"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        This outdoor adventure was a lesson in simple over complex. Brad had a master plan in his head for a homemade boat made from string, sticks that were balanced perfectly, and a sail made from a giant leaf. And while it LOOKED impressive, it didnt sail long before it capsized and sunk.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/howtomakeahomemadesailboat.jpg">
         <img alt="how to make a homemade sailboat" border="0" height="353" src="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/howtomakeahomemadesailboat_thumb.jpg" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: inline; border-left-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px" title="how to make a homemade sailboat" width="520"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Our second attempt, was a tad more simplistic, made with tree bark and a similar sail.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/makeahomemadesailboatwithbarkandsticks.jpg">
         <img alt="make a homemade sailboat with bark and sticks" border="0" height="353" src="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/makeahomemadesailboatwithbarkandsticks_thumb.jpg" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: inline; border-left-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px" title="make a homemade sailboat with bark and sticks" width="520"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        However, our sail proved to be too heavy for the bark, and after a quick float, it too fell over.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/boyadventuresmakingahomemadesailboat.jpg">
         <img alt="boy adventures making a homemade sailboat" border="0" height="353" src="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/boyadventuresmakingahomemadesailboat_thumb.jpg" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: inline; border-left-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px" title="boy adventures making a homemade sailboat" width="520"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        After nearly giving up, we decided to give an even more simpler idea
        <em>
         (my own)
        </em>
        a try.
        <img alt=";)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_wink.gif"/>
        Our third and final attempt was constructed from a log, which we drilled a hole threw with needle-nosed pliers. We placed a stick
        <em>
         (a plastic yard post)
        </em>
        in the centered hole, and attached a small square of cardboard for the sail.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/homemadesailboatinstructions.jpg">
         <img alt="homemade sail boat instructions" border="0" height="353" src="http://www.4tunate.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/homemadesailboatinstructions_thumb.jpg" style="border-top-width: 0px; display: inline; border-left-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px" title="homemade sail boat instructions" width="520"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        She sailed like a dream across the pond, p
        <em>
         roving once again that it doesnt have to be complicated to be functional! =)
        </em>
       </p>

       <p>
        <em>
         * Check your materials to see if they float well first before constructing the entire boat.

        </em>

       <p>
        <em>
         * A little wind will go a long way in pushing your homemade boat across the water.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         * Making more than one boat and having a race would be another great idea!
        </em>
