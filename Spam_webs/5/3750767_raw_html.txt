     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        We Can't Go Home Again
       </b>
      </i>
      is an
      <a href="/wiki/Experimental_film" title="Experimental film">
       experimental
      </a>
      feature film directed by
      <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Ray" title="Nicholas Ray">
       Nicholas Ray
      </a>
      in collaboration with his film students at
      <a href="/wiki/Binghamton_University" title="Binghamton University">
       Binghamton University
      </a>
      . Ray and the students play fictionalized versions of themselves.
     </p>
     <p>
      The film was the major project of the last decade of Ray's life, and he and his collaborators continuously re-edited it.
      <a href="/wiki/Rough_cut" title="Rough cut">
       Rough versions
      </a>
      of the film were screened at
      <a href="/wiki/Film_festival" title="Film festival">
       festivals
      </a>
      as early as 1972 (including a
      <a href="/wiki/1973_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="1973 Cannes Film Festival">
       Cannes
      </a>
      premiere in 1973
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival-cannes.com_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-festival-cannes.com-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ), and the most well-known cut was completed in 1976.
     </p>
     <p>
      Ray was still making alterations to it at the time of his death in 1979.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=We_Can%27t_Go_Home_Again&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 1971, Nicholas Ray received an invitation to lecture at
      <a href="/wiki/Binghamton_University" title="Binghamton University">
       Binghamton University
      </a>
      's Harpur College, which had at the time just established a
      <a href="/wiki/Film_school" title="Film school">
       film department
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notcoming_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-notcoming-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This led to Ray being offered a two-year teaching position at the department, which had been founded by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Larry_Gottheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Larry Gottheim (page does not exist)">
       Larry Gottheim
      </a>
      and the experimental filmmaker
      <a href="/wiki/Ken_Jacobs" title="Ken Jacobs">
       Ken Jacobs
      </a>
      . Ray became close with his students, and together with them moved into a house off-campus where the group creating a filmmaking
      <a href="/wiki/Commune" title="Commune">
       commune
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notcoming_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-notcoming-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There they began work on
      <i>
       We Can't Go Home Again
      </i>
      , sharing all filmmaking duties.
     </p>
     <p>
      The film was made using a wide variety of equipment and shooting formats, including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_8_mm_film" title="Super 8 mm film">
       Super 8
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/16mm" title="16mm">
       16mm
      </a>
      ,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/35mm" title="35mm">
       35mm
      </a>
      and a
      <a href="/wiki/Video_synthesizer" title="Video synthesizer">
       video synthesizer
      </a>
      which had been donated to the project by
      <a href="/wiki/Nam_June_Paik" title="Nam June Paik">
       Nam June Paik
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notcoming_3-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-notcoming-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
