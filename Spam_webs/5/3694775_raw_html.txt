        <h1 class="entry-title">
         China-US Didnt Change The Climate Game; The Climate Game Changed China-US
        </h1>
        <h2 class="entry-meta">
         by
         <a href="http://anthropozine.com/author/anthropocozine/" rel="author" title="Posts by Steve Zwick">
          Steve Zwick
         </a>
         
         <time class="published updated" datetime="2014-11-12">
          November 12, 2014
         </time>
         
         <a href="http://anthropozine.com/uncategorized/how-the-warsaw-climate-talks-spawned-the-new-china-us-climate-deal/#respond">
          0 Comments
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="5025050" data-link="http://anthropozine.com/uncategorized/how-the-warsaw-climate-talks-spawned-the-new-china-us-climate-deal/" data-summary="" data-title="China-US Didn't Change The Climate Game; The Climate Game Changed China-US">
        </div>
        <p>
         <em>
          All week, weve heard how the China-US Climate Pact changed the climate game for the better, and most of what weve heard is wrong. The game has changed alright, but it didnt change this week in Beijing; it changed last year in Warsaw. If you dont understand that, then nothing that happens at the December talks in Lima will make sense.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          13 November 2014 |
         </strong>
         In the beginning was a word, and the word was commitments.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the end was a different word, and the word was contributions.
        </p>
        <p>
         OnTuesday in Beijing, we saw what a difference a word can make when US President Barack Obama and Chinese President Xi Jinping jointly
         <a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2014/11/11/fact-sheet-us-china-joint-announcement-climate-change-and-clean-energy-c" target="_blank">
          announced theyd
          <em>
           contribute
          </em>
          to the climate effort
         </a>
         by doing things that each could realistically achieve.
        </p>
        <p>
         Hailed by some as a
         <a href="http://www.motherjones.com/environment/2014/11/obama-just-announced-historic-climate-deal-china">
          game-changing bolt from the blue
         </a>
         and dismissed by others as
         <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2014/11/13/world/asia/climate-change-china-xi-jinping-obama-apec.html?_r=0">
          too little too late
         </a>
         , their announcement is the most visible test to date of a new set of rules that climate-change negotiators established in Warsaw last year rules that began with one word that changed everything.
        </p>
        <p>
         Before the Warsaw talks, we all talked about commitments,' says Peruvian Environment MinisterManuel Pulgar-Vidal, who will be presiding over Decembers talks in Lima. This year, were talking about contributions.'
        </p>
        <p>
         Gone for now is the quest for a top-down global deal setting one concrete but unworkable emission-reduction target for all countries, and it is place is a flurry of 
         <a href="http://unfccc.int/resource/docs/2014/adp2/eng/7drafttext.pdf" target="_blank">
          Intended Nationally Determined Contributions (INDC)
         </a>
         , which are bottom-up proposals that countries around the world will begin submitting to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) after Decembers talks end. Tuesdays announcement isnt just a bilateral deal, but the first indication of what the US will be putting forward:The United States will submit its 2025 target to the Framework Convention on Climate Change as an Intended Nationally Determined Contribution no later than the first quarter of 2015, the White House
         <a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2014/11/11/fact-sheet-us-china-joint-announcement-climate-change-and-clean-energy-c" target="_blank">
          said in summarizing Tuesdays announcement on its web site
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         It should come as no surprise that the US and China are providing the first big test of the INDC concept, because they were at the center of the logjam that led to their creation in the first place. Specifically, the United States, Japan, and some other developed countries refused to commit to reductions until developing countries, as well as economic powerhouses likeChina and Brazil, made commitments of their own.The shift from commitments to contributions takes the principle of 
         <a href="http://climate.diplomacy.edu/page/cbdr-principle">
          common but differentiated responsibilities
         </a>
          out of the footnotes and into the forefront of negotiations. In so doing, it turns the talks upside-down.
        </p>
        <p>
         The idea is simple: negotiators will first see what each country puts on the table, and then theyll try to incorporate these proposals into a global agreement that will keep the earths temperatures from rising more than 2C. Instead of starting with global limits and deadlines that allow for some flexibility in how theyre achieved, countries are starting with local proposals and then rolling them up into a synchronized global framework.
        </p>
        <p>
         So, yes, the game changed, but it didnt change this week in Beijing. It changed last year in Warsaw. On Tuesday, we got to see the change in action.
        </p>
        <h4>
         What is an INDC?
        </h4>
        <p>
         On December 1, negotiators will arrive in Lima for this years climate talks, and their first order of business will be to decide what constitutes a good INDC and what doesnt. Its a process that began in Warsaw and reached a fever pitch in Bonn, Germany, after
         <a href="http://unfccc.int/bodies/awg/items/7398.php" target="_blank">
          more than two dozen countries submitted their ideas
         </a>
         . In July, Kishan Kumarsinghof Trinidad and Tobago andArtur Runge-Metzgerof the European Union distilled those proposals into a
         <a href="http://unfccc.int/resource/docs/2014/adp2/eng/7drafttext.pdf" target="_blank">
          draft negotiating text on INDCs
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Its essentially a laundry list of activities that range from Technology, investment and capacity-building needs to International cooperation, including cooperative actions both of which are core to the US-China agreement and will be central to all INDCs going forward.
        </p>
        <p>
         But nothing in the draft text is etched in stone, and you can expect it to first bloat from its current form into an incomprehensible mess of phrases, half-phrases, and [bracketed text] before Kumarsinghand Runge-Metzgerboil it down into something manageable.
        </p>
        <p>
         They are co-chairs of the
         <a href="http://unfccc.int/bodies/body/6645.php" target="_blank">
          Ad Hoc Working Group on the Durban Platform for Enhanced Action (ADP)
         </a>
         , which means they, together with Pulgar-Vidal, arecharged with herding all of its cats, cattle, and canaries towards agreement onwhat makes a good INDC.
        </p>
        <p>
         For now, the only thing everyone agrees on is that INDCs must be transparent and comparable across countries, so that every country gets a fair chance to be recognized for its contributions or compensated for its losses. If most countries feel the INDCs are fair, the theory goes, theyll play the game to win, rather than simply not to lose.Only when that process is finished can the US, China, or any other country really finalize their own INDCs.
        </p>
        <p>
         Countries will then spend the first few months of 2015 uploading their INDCs to the UNFCCC web site, ramping up an iterative process designed to blend the bottom-up INDCs with top-down reality checks, culminating, we hope, with a global agreement at theParis Climate Summit (COP 21) at the end of 2015.
        </p>
        <p>
         At that point, contributions become commitments again, but theyll be commitments to specific actions that suit each countrys reality, rather than to a one-size-fits all agreement that, in reality, never quite fit anyone right.
        </p>
