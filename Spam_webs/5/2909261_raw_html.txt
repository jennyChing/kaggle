       <p>
        We all know Christina Aguilera has some serious pipes: You don't earn five Grammys and a spot as a coach on "The Voice" without proving you can sing. What we didn't know was that, with a little encouragement from Jimmy Fallon, she could show us she had other people's pipes, too!
       </p>
       <!-- partial not found -->
       <p>
        The revelation came as a part of a game she played on "The Tonight Show" Monday called Wheel of Musical Impressions. Fallon pressed a button and the wheel spun, spitting out a singer's name and a song. That led to genius pairings, including Cher with the Folgers' Coffee commercial jingle and Britney Spears with "This Little Piggy."
       </p>
       <p>
        Aguilera was awesome, sounding very much like the divas she was duplicating! And Fallon more than held up his end, doing David Bowie singing "You're a Grand Old Flag" and former Doobie Brothers singer Michael McDonald doing "The Itsy Bitsy Spider."
       </p>
       <p>
        Check it out, and be sure to stick around for the final duet of "Thank You For Being a Friend," aka "The Golden Girls" theme.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, did Aguilera raise the
        <a href="http://www.today.com/popculture/jimmy-fallon-adam-levine-bring-sinatra-azalea-more-tonight-1D80123427" target="_blank">
         high bar her fellow "Voice" coach Adam Levine set
        </a>
        when he played the same game last September? You decide!
       </p>
