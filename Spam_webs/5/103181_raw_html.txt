       Russia Mulls Privatizing ERA-GLONASS Emergency Network
       <br/>
      </span>
      <span class="BBL">
       by Staff Writers
       <br/>
      </span>
      <span class="BDL">
       Moscow (RIA Novosti) Jun 09, 2014
       <br/>
      </span>
      <table align="right" border="0" cellpadding="3" cellspacing="0" width="300">
       <tr>
        <td>
         <br/>
         <img align="right" border="0" height="250" hspace="0" src="http://www.spxdaily.com/images-lg/command-post-glonass-system-lg.jpg" vspace="2" width="300"/>
         <br clear="all"/>
         <span class="BL">
          Command post of GLONASS system. Image courtesy RIA Novosti.

      <div class="BTX KonaBody">
       <p>
        The Russian government will consider privatizing the state-owned ERA-GLONASS emergency calls network, according to the Russian newspaper Kommersant.
       </p>
       <p>
        A source in the know told Kommersant Thursday, a plan to privatize ERA-GLONASS, a real-time satellite service for reporting and responding to traffic accidents, was recently discussed at a Kremlin meeting attended by President Putin's chief of staff Sergei Ivanov.
       </p>
       <p>
        "The Transportation Ministry has proposed setting up a public private partnership to operate ERA-GLONASS as a joint-stock company based on the non-profit GLONASS partnership, and then increasing the number of shareholders through an additional stock placement," according to the paper's source in the Russian government.
       </p>
       <p>
        The state is to control the joint stock company and sell minority equity stakes to private partners, including Russia's "big three" mobile operators, Megafon, MTS and Vimpelcom, long-distance telephony provider Rostelecom and search engine Yandex.
       </p>
       <p>
        GLONASS CEO Alexander Glushko confirmed the paper's report. According to Kommersant, ERA-GLONASS infrastructure cost Russia a whopping 3.9 billion rubles (over $111 million).
       </p>
       <p>
        The system provides data from Russia's Global Navigation Satellite System to emergency responders. ERA-GLONASS is based on the European eCall/E112 standard, and allows emergency services to immediately pinpoint the location of car accidents or similar critical situations.
       </p>
       <p>
        <span class="BDL">
         Source:
         <a href="http://en.rian.ru/">
          RIA Novosti
         </a>
        </span>
       </p>
