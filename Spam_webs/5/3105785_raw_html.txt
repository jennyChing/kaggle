         <div class="thecontent" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           <img alt="trouble in chuncksvill" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-83136" height="276" src="http://unrealitymag.bcmediagroup.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/trouble-in-chuncksvill-500x276.png" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           It takes a great deal for a movie to make me feel likeretching. It is not just extreme violence, because for the most part, that doesnt bother me at all in film.But from taking note of the moments in film that made me queasy and ill, I noticed something. Almost all of them seem to attempt to merge sex and violence, which are two things that have no place coexisting side by side. While some may argue that sex and violence come from the same place and are bothinstinctualand feral in nature, I do understand how it works on a primal level, but regardless, sex and violence merging as one seems a dangerous line to tread in cinema, and a very fine one as well.
          </p>
          <p>
           Here are six scenes that attempted to merge sex and violence, and in the process, filled me with the strong urge to vomit . I think the real undertone of irony here is that I really liked most of these films, and have recommended them to you all at one point or another. Hey, just because I puked off of an odd flavor of ice cream doesnt mean it wont be your favorite flavor, know what I mean?
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-83132">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Rape Scene in Irreversible
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="THE scene" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-83133" height="334" src="http://unrealitymag.bcmediagroup.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/THE-scene-500x334.jpg" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The film is like a disease. You may only see it once, but you will never shake it.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           You may all read this site, and read some of my
           <a href="http://remycarreiro.com/cock-shots-and-crysturbation-five-incredibly-wtf-moments-from-mainstream-movies/">
            weird ass articles
           </a>
           on my site, and you may just assume I am some deviant beast who only enjoys creepy, unsettling stuff, but you would be surprised. Do you know it took me three viewings, across six months, to get all the way through Irreversible? Scoff if you like, but rape in film was not something I even allowed myself to view until I felt I was old enough to handle it. You see, rape is the ugliest act we have as creatures, even moreso than murder to me. Murder takes a life, but rape takes a soul, and then forces the person to keep living on. Jesus, I am depressing myself. I will spare you anymoreselfrighteous rape is bad speech, because we all already know that, but just setting the tone for this piece.
          </p>
          <p>
           Anyway, the scene that made me ill in question is an 8 minute long rape scene of Monica Bellucci in a subway after a night out clubbing, and director Gaspar Noe actually uses CG to make the sceneanatomicallycorrect and as soul-crushing as possible. Took me a long time to be able to digest this one, and even then, it took parts of me I wont ever get back. I can nowrecognizeGaspar as a confrontational film maker who likes to force people to see things they want to pretend dont exist, but regardless, that rape scene had me dry heaving. It is an INCREDIBLY powerful film, none the less.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Pee Pee Go Bye Bye in Teeth
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="teeeeeeeeeth" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-83134" height="168" src="http://unrealitymag.bcmediagroup.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/teeeeeeeeeth.jpg" width="300"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            This is the face I was making while watching this scene.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           I understand that Teeth is a black comedy, and I understand it plays up the rape thing a bit (so every man in this girls life, from her doctor to her brother, want to molest her. Um, ok? Yuck.) and I even know I have recommended it on one of my lists a while back, but regardless of that, there is one scene in particular, be it comedy or not, that does a close up on a young boys lap while he is clutching the oozing wound that is his half bitten-off penis, and that single scene went just a tad bit too far for me. I get it, her vagina has teeth and stuff. And I get that those teeth protect her, for the most part, but the MANY SHOTS of cut off cocks and fingers in this film seems to tap dance over the line of good taste just one too many times for me.
          </p>
          <p>
           Honestly, the film is worth a laugh at times, but that one moment will have you reaching for your puke bucket. But I do understand that this is what I get as bitter opposition to females being victimized on film, so in that sense, it is fair play and long overdue. For more cock removals, feel free to watch Hostel 2. Eli Roth LOVES cock removals for some reason.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Sex Scene from Trouble Every Day
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="dalle day" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-83135" height="266" src="http://unrealitymag.bcmediagroup.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/dalle-day.jpg" width="400"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            This is Dalle AFTER the sex scene. I am not kidding.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Most of you know French Actress Beatrice Dalle from the French horror extreme film, Inside, where she played thevillain. What most dont know is Dalle used to be an absolute sex kitten on film, taking brave, sexual roles early in her career and often throughout. If you want to look to one film that merges her old sex symbol ways with her new I will f*cking eat your soul ways, you need to see Trouble Every Day. This movie is unreal, and takes the whole sex and violence thing I discussed earlier and flips it on its head. Truly worth seeing, but be forewarned, there is one scene that REALLY made me puke.
          </p>
          <p>
           While I wont spoil anything, the best way I can describe Dalle in this film is primal. She turns sort of primal, and cannot differentiate between sex and violence. This leads to a sex scene with a young man in the middle of the film that grabbed me by my gonads and twisted. It begins sexy enough, with her seducing him. But then, in the middle, the lines blur and she starts, well, murdering him while she is banging him. Thing is, she does it with this childlike innocence, where is giggling, biting him, and riding him, all at once. You see he goes from excited to terrified real quick, and it is the moment when she begins to finger his gaping wounds is where I just started
           <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GC1P5jfpphw">
            heaving like Sweet Dee
           </a>
           . I have since gotten over that scene and really adore this film, but first time I saw it, forget about it.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Scissor Scene from AntiChrist
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="antichrist-scissors" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-83137" height="337" src="http://unrealitymag.bcmediagroup.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/antichrist-scissors.jpg" width="200"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Well, nice piece of Photoshop nightmare fuel, internet.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Okay, as gross as it is throughout, I liked Antichrist. I think it was an (at times, visually stunning) look into the psycho sexual descent of two people in the wake of loss. Yes, it is INCREDIBLY graphic (most people dont make it past that very first scene) but with all thesickeningthings that happen in this film (Willem Dafoe gets raped and has his junk smashed with a rock, and something even sicker happens after that, but I wont type it here. Well, I will type if like this: b**** **m, two words). But the final straw for me is the scene where Charlotte Gainsbourg takes scissors to her lady parts and removes a very crucial part of her womanhood.
          </p>
          <p>
           Again, I understand it was all symbolic of how her sexuality is what led to the tragedy in the film in the first place, and that she was rebelling against that and rejecting that sexuality, but still, cutting off your **** with a pair of rusty scissors? Shown in detail???
          </p>
          <p>
           Yes, I yuked.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Cat And Autopsy Scenes from Men Behind the Sun
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="men" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-83138" height="294" src="http://unrealitymag.bcmediagroup.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/men-500x294.jpg" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
             Yo dude, gimme a fist bump? Oh, come on, please!
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           A movie about the atrocities of war and torture, this is one of the few movies I will ever tell you NOT to see. Though some of the staged scenes have special effects that have not aged well, the ideas behind what is happening is enough to utterly destroy anyone who watches this film. And that is not the kicker. The kicker is they show the autopsy of a real boy, and at one point, they throw a cat into a room full ofstarvingrats and it gets ripped apart on film. While producers of the film stand firmly behind the fact that everything was simulated, the cat scene will make you think otherwise. That isDEFINITELYa real cat getting attacked by real rats at one point, and to that I say a hearty fuck you to the film makers.
          </p>
          <p>
           If I wanted to watch animals get abused, I would move next door to Chris Brown and Rihanna.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Surgery Scene in Hard Candy
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           It wasnt the idea that the perv was getting his genitals removed (OR WAS HE???!!!) that made me feel sick. It was the fact that this little girl had to go to such a dark place, willingly. That just stuck with me and made me a huge Ellen Page fan for life. But is also made me heave, ever-so-slightly.
          </p>
          <p>
           Also, one of the best movie posters of all time.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="hard candy" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-83139" height="593" src="http://unrealitymag.bcmediagroup.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/hard-candy-500x593.jpg" width="500"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The REAL Red Riding Hood metaphoric edgy reboot.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           And finally, e
           <a href="http://remycarreiro.com/the-most-soul-destroying-film-i-never-should-have-watched-a-serbian-film/">
            very single damned frame of a Serbian Film also deserves a place on this list
           </a>
           , but I dont wanna write about it and bring it any more attention.
          </p>
          <p>
           (New Addition) The hammer scene in Dogtooth, which I just watched last night. Holy sh*t is all I can say about that.
          </p>
