        <h1 class="entry-title">
         10 Best Business Books That Actually Make A Difference
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="entry-date">
          <a href="http://christinamendelsblog.com/2014/01/17/10-best-business-books-that-actually-make-a-difference/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-01-17T10:41:23+00:00">
            January 17, 2014
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="byline">
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://christinamendelsblog.com/author/chris140/" rel="author">
            Christina Mendel
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="comments-link">
          <a href="http://christinamendelsblog.com/2014/01/17/10-best-business-books-that-actually-make-a-difference/#respond">
           Leave a comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         I read a lot because I think its one of the most efficient ways to improve my entrepreneurial skills. I have made a list of the 10 best business books of all time in no particular order (includes affiliate links).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1591843170/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1591843170&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #1 The Purple Cow
          </strong>
          by Seth Godin
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1591843170/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1591843170&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books purple cow seth godin" class="size-full wp-image-303 aligncenter" height="355" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-purple-cow-seth-godin.png" width="255"/>
         </a>
         One of the ten has to be The Purple Cow by Seth Godin. Regardless of where you are at with your business, this book lists concrete questions and examples that help you to innovate, to be different, to be better, and to be remarkable. A must read for anyone who is thinking of starting a business or wants to improve an established business.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0887307280/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0887307280&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #2 The E-Myth Revisited
          </strong>
          by Michael E. Gerber
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0887307280/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0887307280&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books e myth revisited michael gerber" class="size-full wp-image-304 aligncenter" height="351" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-e-myth-revisited-michael-gerber.png" width="226"/>
         </a>
         This is without a doubt the best book for anyone who started out being good at something and went on to make a business out of it. If you have an academic background in business, this one will seem fairly obvious but it helps to read it nonetheless because it highlights both sides of the coin  street smart and book smart  which is essential for any business owner. As seen in Donald Trumps TV show The Apprentice, there is a system behind completing tasks and reaching goals successfully. This book points out those systems in a way that makes them instantly applicable.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0066620996/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0066620996&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
           #3 Good to Great: Why Some Companies Make The Leap And Others Dont
          </a>
         </strong>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0066620996/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0066620996&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          by Jim Collins
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0066620996/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0066620996&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books good to great" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-306 aligncenter" height="310" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-good-to-great.png" width="208"/>
         </a>
         Even though this book mainly analyzes and evaluates the success of large, public, and known companies and isnt necessarily tailored to small business owners that want to keep their business relatively small, its the perfect book for anyone who wants to build the next century lasting company that changes the world.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0143118757/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0143118757&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #4Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In
          </strong>
          by Roger Fisher
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0143118757/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0143118757&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books  Getting to Yes Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In" class="size-full wp-image-307 aligncenter" height="351" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-getting-to-yes-negotiating-agreement-without-giving-in.png" width="231"/>
         </a>
         Sales and negotiations are essential parts of any business. This is a book that not only helps you improve your negotiating skills but explains the underlying concepts of why people say yes or no. It teaches you to turn any negotiation to your advantage. While others find compromises, meaning both sides sacrifice something they wanted to meet in the middle, Getting To Yes shows strategies and techniques to create a win win situation. The ideal guide for long-term collaborations and valuable networks.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0307887898/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0307887898&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #5 The Lean Startup: How Todays Entrepreneurs Use Continuous Innovation to Create Radically Successful Businesses
          </strong>
          by Eric Ries
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0307887898/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0307887898&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books lean startup" class="size-full wp-image-310 aligncenter" height="351" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-lean-startup.png" width="235"/>
         </a>
         Even though this books seems to be targeting startups, its actually a guide on how to launch anything in an uncertain economy. In other words, this book is the best business book out there on making a business sustainable.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0470876417/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0470876417&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #6 Business Model Generation: A Handbook for Visionaries, Game Changers, and Challengers
          </strong>
          by Alexander Osterwalder
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0470876417/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0470876417&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books business model generation" class="size-full wp-image-311 aligncenter" height="210" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-business-model-generation.png" width="265"/>
         </a>
         This is a true business book. While touching upon best practices and classic business theories and models, this book helps you to interpret and apply those concepts to your own business.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1477800670/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1477800670&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #7 Manage Your Day-to-Day: Build Your Routine, Find Your Focus, and Sharpen Your Creative Mind
          </strong>
          by Jocelyn K. Glei
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1477800670/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1477800670&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books manage your day-to-day" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-314 aligncenter" height="353" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-manage-your-day-to-day.png" width="255"/>
         </a>
         This business book is best for business owners that feel like they would have to clone themselves to get the important things done. The book features advice, best practices, and habits of 20 extremely successful entrepreneurs, CEOs, and bestselling authors.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/081298160X/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=081298160X&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #8 The Power of Habit: Why We Do What We Do in Life and Business
          </strong>
          by Charles Duhigg
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/081298160X/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=081298160X&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books the power of habit" class="size-full wp-image-316 aligncenter" height="355" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-the-power-of-habit.png" width="235"/>
         </a>
         I am convinced that the key to success is to understand why we do what we do. Only then can we change our behavior and get what we want. This book doesnt only explain how habits develop and why we stick to them but also how to change them. An essential business book in any entrepreneurs library.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0316017930/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0316017930&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #9 Outliers: The Story of Success
          </strong>
          by Malcolm Gladwell
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0316017930/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0316017930&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books outliers" class="size-full wp-image-319 aligncenter" height="354" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-outliers.png" width="236"/>
         </a>
         This book profiles the very few individuals on this planet that are vastly more successful than the rest of the population. Its entertaining but also rich in content because Malcolm Gladwell points out what it takes to replicate or achieve similar success in life and business.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0374533555/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0374533555&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           #10 Thinking, Fast and Slow
          </strong>
          by Daniel Kahneman
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0374533555/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0374533555&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          <img alt="best business books thinking fast and slow" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-322 aligncenter" height="352" src="http://christinamendelsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/best-business-books-thinking-fast-and-slow.png" width="238"/>
         </a>
         My personal favorite and in my opinion the best book on success ever written. Besides winning the Nobel Prize in Economics, Daniel Kahneman was rewarded with countless awards for this book including but not limited to the following:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Winner of the National Academy of Sciences Best Book Award in 2012
         </li>
         <li>
          Selected by the New York Times Book Review as one of the best books of 2011
         </li>
         <li>
          A Globe and Mail Best Books of the Year 2011 Title
         </li>
         <li>
          One of The Economists 2011 Books of the Year
         </li>
         <li>
          One of The Wall Street Journals Best Nonfiction Books of the Year 2011
         </li>
         <li>
          2013 Presidential Medal of Freedom Recipient
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Even though all books in this post are extremely popular, have sold millions of copies, and were or are on countless bestseller lists, Thinking, Fast and Slow tops them all in my opinion. The book touches upon how we think, when to trust our intuition (and when not to), predicting what makes us happy in the future, and the role of confidence when it comes to corporate strategies just to name a few topics.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you only want to read one book,
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0374533555/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0374533555&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=sucliv-20" target="_blank">
          this is the one
         </a>
         .
