       <header class="article-header">
        <h1>
         How to Recognize Yeast Infection Symptoms
        </h1>
       </header>
       <ul class="inline bylines">
        <li class="byline" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         By
         <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/authors/marijke-vroomen-durning/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
          <span itemprop="name">
           Marijke  Vroomen-Durning, RN
          </span>
         </a>
        </li>
        <li class="reviewed-by" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
         Medically Reviewed by
         <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/authors/lindsey-marcellin/" itemprop="url" rel="reviewer">
          <span itemprop="name">
           Lindsey  Marcellin, MD, MPH
          </span>
         </a>
        </li>
       </ul>
       <div class="guide-deck">
        <h2 class="eh-deck">
         Most women will experience a vaginal yeast infection at some point in their lives. Recognizing the symptoms is the first step to getting treatment.
        </h2>
       </div>
      </header>
      <div class="hero-wrapper">
      </div>
      <div class="article-body" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
       <div class="eh-module-unit">
        <div class="ad-container ad-standard">
         <div class="ad-placeholder" data-adsize="300x250,300x600,300x1050" data-pos="top" data-tile="5" id="adDiv5" title="ADVERTISEMENT">
         </div>
         <noscript>
          <a href="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/jump/everydayhealth/womenshealth/general;p=37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Women's Health;page=032b7b48d0974a7c813ad5d7e46599fd;bn=;content=;;pos=top;sz=300x250,300x600,300x1050;tile=5;ord=20150624193507?" target="_blank">
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/ad/everydayhealth/womenshealth/general;37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Women's Health;page=032b7b48d0974a7c813ad5d7e46599fd;bn=;content=;;pos=top;sz=300x250,300x600,300x1050;tile=5;ord=20150624193507?"/>
          </a>
         </noscript>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Its a feeling that many women recognize right away: the vaginal itching and burning that accompanies a vaginal infection likely caused by yeast.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/yeast-infection/">
         Yeast infections
        </a>
        are a very common form of
        <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/yeast-infection/vaginitis.aspx">
         vaginal infection
        </a>
        , but even if youve had one before, how can you really be sure that you're experiencing yeast infection symptoms rather than symptoms of another type of vaginal infection altogether?
       </p>
       <h2>
        Vaginal Yeast Infection Symptoms
       </h2>
       <p>
        Not all women exhibit noticeable symptoms when they have a yeast infection, especially if the infection is mild. However, typical yeast infection symptoms include:
       </p>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Itching in the vaginal area and around the vulva (the opening of the vagina)
        </li>
        <li>
         Burning in the vaginal area
        </li>
        <li>
         Swelling of the vulva
        </li>
        <li>
         White/gray vaginal discharge that may be thick (sometimes described as looking like cottage cheese)
        </li>
        <li>
         Burning during urination
        </li>
        <li>
         Pain with sexual intercourse
        </li>
       </ul>
       <p>
        Most vaginal yeast infections do not produce a strong vaginal odor; a fishy odor is more common with
        <a href="/yeast-infection/causes/candida-albicans/">
         bacterial infections
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h2>
        Vaginal Infection: The Pitfalls of Self-Diagnosis
       </h2>
       <p>
        As straightforward as it might seem, most doctors will discourage you from diagnosing and
        <a href="/yeast-infection/treatments/home-remedies/">
         treating a yeast infection yourself
        </a>
        . This is because vaginal infections caused by bacteria, as well as some
        <a href="/yeast-infection/sex/">
         sexually transmitted infections
        </a>
        , may have symptoms very similar to those caused by yeast, but they require different treatments.
       </p>
       <p>
        Since
        <a href="/yeast-infection/treatment/">
         yeast infection treatments
        </a>
        have become available over the counter, many women simply opt to visit the closest drugstore and buy an anti-fungal cream. But according to the Centers for Disease Control and
        <a href="/yeast-infection/prevention/">
         Prevention
        </a>
        , almost two thirds of these products are being bought and used by women who think they have a vaginal yeast infection but actually dont.
       </p>
       <p>
        This misdiagnosis of vaginal infections is an important issue. Just as some bacteria are becoming resistant to certain
        <a href="/yeast-infection/causes/antibiotics/">
         antibiotics
        </a>
        , yeast that normally lives in the vagina can become resistant to
        <a href="/yeast-infection/treatments/medication/">
         anti-fungal medications
        </a>
        . If this happens, it can become very difficult to treat a yeast infection when one actually does develop.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="eh-module-unit">
        <div class="ad-container ad-standard">
         <div class="ad-placeholder" data-adsize="300x250,300x600" data-pos="bottom" data-tile="7" id="adDiv7" title="ADVERTISEMENT">
         </div>
         <noscript>
          <a href="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/jump/everydayhealth/womenshealth/general;p=37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Women's Health;page=032b7b48d0974a7c813ad5d7e46599fd;bn=;content=;;pos=bottom;sz=300x250,300x600;tile=7;ord=20150624193507?" target="_blank">
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/ad/everydayhealth/womenshealth/general;37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Women's Health;page=032b7b48d0974a7c813ad5d7e46599fd;bn=;content=;;pos=bottom;sz=300x250,300x600;tile=7;ord=20150624193507?"/>
          </a>
         </noscript>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Because of this, the American Academy of Family Physicians recommends that, for a first episode of a possible yeast infection, women
        <a href="/yeast-infection/diagnosis/">
         see a physician to get a proper diagnosis
        </a>
        . If a woman has had a physician-diagnosed yeast infection in the past and feels certain that her current symptoms are caused by a yeast infection, its reasonable to ask her doctor about self-treatment with an
        <a href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/yeast-infection/choosing-medication.aspx">
         over-the-counter medication
        </a>
        . However, if symptoms dont improve or they come back again, an office visit is warranted.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Vaginal Infection: What Doctors Look For
       </h2>
       <p>
        At your doctors office, youll be asked about your symptoms and possibly about your overall health. A gynecological exam will be done so your doctor can check for redness, swelling, discharge, and odor.
       </p>
       <p>
        "Vaginal yeast and bacterial infections are both common and difficult to diagnose based on symptoms," explained Rosanna Gray-Swain, MD, a board-certified obstetrician-gynecologist in St. Louis, Mo. "Both cause discharge and itching, plus or minus an odor. To determine the type of infection, the doctor takes a sample of discharge from the vagina and looks at it on a slide under a microscope to directly look for the yeast or bacteria. Rarely, a special culture needs to be sent to the lab."
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div class="eh-module-unit">
        <div class="ad-container ad-standard">
         <div class="ad-placeholder" data-adsize="300x250" data-pos="extrabox" data-tile="9" id="adDiv9" title="ADVERTISEMENT">
         </div>
         <noscript>
          <a href="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/jump/everydayhealth/womenshealth/general;p=37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Women's Health;page=032b7b48d0974a7c813ad5d7e46599fd;bn=;content=;;pos=extrabox;sz=300x250;tile=9;ord=20150624193507?" target="_blank">
           <img alt="" border="0" src="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/ad/everydayhealth/womenshealth/general;37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Women's Health;page=032b7b48d0974a7c813ad5d7e46599fd;bn=;content=;;pos=extrabox;sz=300x250;tile=9;ord=20150624193507?"/>
          </a>
         </noscript>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        Once the
        <a href="/yeast-infection/causes/">
         cause of your infection
        </a>
        is accurately determined, youll be on your way to getting the right treatment and, more importantly, relief.
       </p>
      </div>
