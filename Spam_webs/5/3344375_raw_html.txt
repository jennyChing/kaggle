      <h1 class="entry_title">
       German economy surges past pre-crisis levels
      </h1>
      <div class="singlepostmeta" style="font-size:14px">
       <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-68 photo" height="68" src="http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/1fe4fac83d156a9401e0967e145c56d3?s=68&amp;d=monsterid&amp;r=g" srcset="http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/1fe4fac83d156a9401e0967e145c56d3?s=136&amp;d=monsterid&amp;r=g 2x" width="68"/>
       <strong>
        About Holte Ender
       </strong>
       <br/>
       Holte Ender will always try to see your point of view, but sometimes it is hard to stick his head that far up his @$$.
       <br/>
       <a href="http://madmikesamerica.com/author/holte-ender/">
        View all posts by Holte Ender 
       </a>
       <div class="clear">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       DER SPIEGEL  The German economy grew by a surprising 1.5 percent in the first quarter of 2011 and recorded its largest year-on-year growth since reunification.
      </p>
      <p>
       Economic crisis? What economic crisis? For Germany, at least, it would appear that the dark days of 2008 and 2009 are now history. According to numbers released on Friday by the Federal
       <a href="http://www.spiegel.de/international/business/0,1518,762363,00.html">
        Statistics
       </a>
       Office, the German economy grew by a surprising 1.5 percent in the first quarter of 2011  and the countrys economic output has now exceeded the pre-crisis level of early 2008. They didnt cut pensions or healthcare to do it.
      </p>
      <p>
       Growth was not fueled entirely by exports. The Federal Statistics Office pointed to investments in the industrial and construction sectors as propelling growth. Domestic consumption, long an Achilles heel of the German economy, also drove expansion.
      </p>
      <p>
       Having said that, German exports continue to boom. Figures released earlier this week showed exports reached 98.3 billion ($140.5 billion) in March, their highest monthly figure since records starting being kept in 1950. Germany is the worlds second largest exporter, behind China.
      </p>
