               <center>
                LUCY SMILES DOWN ON US FROM THE SKY ABOVE
               </center>
               <p>
                Yesterday marked the 67th anniversary of the very first "acid trip," when Swiss scientist
                <b>
                 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Hofmann" target="_blank">
                  Dr. Albert 
Hofmann
                 </a>
                </b>
                accidentally absorbed a small amount of the drug he was synthesizing on 
April 16, 1943.  He wrote of the experience that he was
               </p>
               <p>
                <i>
                 ... affected by a remarkable restlessness, combined with a slight dizziness. At home I 
lay down and sank into a not unpleasant intoxicated-like condition, characterized by an 
extremely stimulated imagination. In a dreamlike state, with eyes closed (I found the 
daylight to be unpleasantly glaring), I perceived an uninterrupted stream of fantastic 
pictures, extraordinary shapes with intense, kaleidoscopic play of colors. After some two 
hours this condition faded away.
                </i>
               </p>
               <p>
                After further experimentation, Hofmann eventually called LSD "medicine for the soul."  It 
also happens to be one of the greatest creative catalysts music has ever experienced, 
particularly in its first major embrace by young America in the 1960s.  The direct 
corollary between LSD and rock 'n' roll is unmistakable and produced albums and songs now 
deemed "classic" by nearly every critic.  This week's Eye Candy cherry picks a few gems 
inspired by acid.  Take a gander and then hop on your bicycle and think happy thoughts 
about Dr. Hofmann!
               </p>
               <p>
                We settle in on the cool, green grass next to Steve Marriott and the boys.
               </p>
               <p>
                <object height="385" width="480">

               </p>
               <p>

               </p>

                 <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
                 </param>
                 <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/TmSlwT1xzus&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
                 </embed>
                </object>
               </p>
               <p>
                <br/>
                Even squeaky-clean mainstream acts like
                <b>
                 The Cowsills
                </b>
                got the spark in the '60s.  
Real lovely ditty.
               </p>
               <p>
                <object height="385" width="480">
                 <param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/Yi0CqIeLjkQ&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
                 </param>
                 <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/Yi0CqIeLjkQ&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
                 </embed>
                </object>
               </p>
               <p>
                <br/>
                There were, of course, less squeaky-clean lads who dabbled and came back with songs about 
what they saw and felt.
               </p>
               <p>
                <object height="385" width="480">
                 <param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/tIGdWzXhqlo&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
                 </param>
                 <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/tIGdWzXhqlo&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
                 </embed>
                </object>
               </p>
               <p>
                <br/>
                XTC side project
                <b>
                 <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Dukes_of_Stratosphear" target="_blank">
                  Dukes of the 
Stratospher
                 </a>
                </b>
                , released in the mid-80s, got the vibe of the Flower Power era just 
right.  The faux BBC documentary intro is spot-on, too.
               </p>
               <p>
                <object height="385" width="480">
                 <param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/X-
fL68DbcQ0&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
                 </param>
                 <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/X-fL68DbcQ0&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-
flash" width="480">
                 </embed>
                </object>
               </p>
               <p>
                <br/>
                Despite
                <a href="http://www.jambase.com/Artists/Artist.aspx?artistID=31188">
                 Ted 
Nugent
                </a>
                's claim that he had no idea this was about tripping, it's pretty clear this is 
a psychedelic nugget of the first order.
               </p>
               <p>
                <object height="385" width="480">
                 <param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/UN2VNFpiGWo&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
                 </param>
                 <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/UN2VNFpiGWo&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
                 </embed>
                </object>
               </p>
               <p>
                <br/>
                "My friend Jack eats sugar lumps."  Well, there you are.  A '60s cult gem from a 
performance on
                <i>
                 Beat Club
                </i>
                in 1697.
               </p>
               <p>
                <object height="385" width="480">
                 <param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/n6aVUqYBZ4U&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowFullScreen" value="true">
                 </param>
                 <param name="allowscriptaccess" value="always">
                 </param>
                 <embed allowfullscreen="true" allowscriptaccess="always" height="385" src="http://www.youtube.com/v/n6aVUqYBZ4U&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="480">
                 </embed>
                </object>
               </p>
               <p>
                <br/>
                We conclude this FAR from comprehensive salute to the LSD/rock connection with a vintage 
slab from
                <b>
                 <a href="http://www.jambase.com/Artists/Artist.aspx?artistID=30876">
                  Pink 
Floyd
                 </a>
                </b>
                in the Syd Barrett period.  This piece continues to inspire strange, often 
wonderful sounds from musicians today.  Stick around for the special commentary from a 
mustachioed Macca at the end.
               </p>
               <p>
                <object height="385" width="480">
                 <param name="movie" value="http://www.youtube.com/v/ImmPtmJ-Sac&amp;hl=en_US&amp;fs=1&amp;">
