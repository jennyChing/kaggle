      <div class="entrytextnews">
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.verbicidemagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/HarryPotter.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-16170" height="800" src="http://www.verbicidemagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/HarryPotter.jpg" title="HarryPotter" width="540"/>
        </a>
        <br/>
        The masses are happily tweeting about how theyre waiting in lines to see the final installment in the
        <em>
         Harry Potter
        </em>
        series of films, and all I can think of is Chuck Palahniuk.
       </p>
       <p>
        Chuck Palahniuk is a novelist, most famous for his book
        <em>
         Fight Club
        </em>
        , which was turned into a lovely film starting Brad Pitt, Edward Norton, Helena Bonham-Carter, and Meatloaf. His books are deliciously fucked up  he writes twisted tales that have your eyes wide and your jaw on the floor.
       </p>
       <p>
        Years ago I had friends that were bugging me to read Palahniuks work, and on the other hand I had people telling me that I should really read
        <em>
         Harry Potter
        </em>
        . I had no trouble getting a hold of the books  a friend had scooped the first three
        <em>
         Harry Potters
        </em>
        out of a dumpster for me, and another buddy was willing to lend me three novels by Palahniuk:
        <em>
         Survivor
        </em>
        ,
        <em>
         Invisible Monsters
        </em>
        , and
        <em>
         Lullaby
        </em>
        . I decided it would be fun to read both authors at once.
       </p>
       <p>
        Id read a chapter of Palahniuk, in which Id be hearing about a cult member commiting suicide by eating dirt, or a pill-popping drag queen raiding medicine cabinets at open houses. Then Id pick up
        <em>
         Harry Potter
        </em>
        and hear about muggles, Quidditch, and all the problems Severous Snape was causing Harry in potion class.
       </p>
       <p>
        Harry Potter was Oh god, Im gonna gag cute, and at some moments, Palahniuk was overwhelmingly intense and fucked up, so they went together like peanut butter and jelly. I highly recommend it.
        <strong>
         - Heather Schofner
        </strong>
       </p>
