                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Jewish Menorah Earthwork Located in Clermont County, Ohio
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1975564791073091642">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
                      <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                       Jewish Menorah Earthwork Located in Clermont County, Ohio
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Krvqje9_ZCQ/UyskJhm1zOI/AAAAAAAAQeQ/BOGFW--ZyYU/s1600/Adena-Jewish-monorah-earthwork-Nephilim-giants.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                         <img border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Krvqje9_ZCQ/UyskJhm1zOI/AAAAAAAAQeQ/BOGFW--ZyYU/s1600/Adena-Jewish-monorah-earthwork-Nephilim-giants.jpg" width="462"/>
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="background-color: black; font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19.200000762939453px;">
                       <span style="color: white;">
                        Most of the earthworks in the Ohio Valley are either a circle or a square with these shapes sometimes combined in one earthwork. The circle representing the Sun deity and the square, the Earth Mother. So what about this???
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="background-color: black; font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19.200000762939453px;">
                       <span style="color: white;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                       <span style="font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19.200000762939453px;">
                        Wikipedia "The menorah symbolized the ideal of universal enlightenment.
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19.200000762939453px;">
                        The seven lamps allude to the branches of human
                       </span>
                       knowledge
                       <span style="font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19.200000762939453px;">
                        , represented by the six lamps inclined inwards towards, and symbolically guided by, the light of
                       </span>
                       God
                       <span style="font-family: sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 19.200000762939453px;">
                        represented by the central lamp. The menorah also symbolizes the creation in seven days, with the center light representing the Sabbath.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-FJF7aIbTWe8/UysksKwj1sI/AAAAAAAAQeY/qolu-9xuVEg/s1600/Ohio+mounds-menorah-earthworks-Clermont+county.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                         <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-FJF7aIbTWe8/UysksKwj1sI/AAAAAAAAQeY/qolu-9xuVEg/s1600/Ohio+mounds-menorah-earthworks-Clermont+county.jpg"/>
                        </span>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <span style="background-color: black; color: white;">
                       This is a modern Jewish Menorah with 4 candles and a larger center candle that representing God. When the Amorite giants were expelled from the
                       <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" ginger_software_uiphraseguid="84a752b6-eeb3-447b-afa9-1bae2891abfa" id="fb0b53cc-871d-4844-a876-42bc69c7359a">
                        Canann
                       </span>
                       by Joshua, did some Hebrews follow along
                       <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" ginger_software_uiphraseguid="84a752b6-eeb3-447b-afa9-1bae2891abfa" id="e6f5396d-1801-4a09-b79b-2dd7d9ecc379">
                        to
                       </span>
                       North America. Had some of the
                       <span class="GINGER_SOFTWARE_mark" ginger_software_uiphraseguid="87185407-6ed2-46de-86f3-1993b26ceff9" id="6b7c1f15-0cf0-49a5-a4ce-1dcf70a8ee0a">
                        Amorites in
                       </span>
                       Canaan converted to Judaism?
                      </span>
                     </div>
