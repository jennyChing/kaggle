       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         one-pan roasted sweet potato burrito bowls
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-01-12T15:30:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 12, 2015
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://themuffinmyth.com/author/themuffinmyth/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Katie Trant
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://themuffinmyth.com/2015/01/one-pan-roasted-sweet-potato-burrito-bowls/#comments">
           28 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <em>
          These super simple one-pan roasted sweet potato and black bean burrito bowls are the easiest and most delicious thing to hit your dinner table this week! Want make-ahead meals? Wrap em up in a whole wheat tortilla and youve got freezer burritos!
         </em>
         <img alt="one-pan roasted sweet potato burrito bowls // themuffinmyth.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6245" height="957" src="http://themuffinmyth.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/roasted_sweet_potato_burrito_bowls.jpg" width="675"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         One of my goals for 2015 is figuring out better work-life-blog-
         <a href="http://themuffinmyth.com/services/">
          business
         </a>
         balance. I want to figure out a way to achieve more by doing less. When I photograph a recipe for the blog I tend to take a bonkers amount of photos. Take these photos for example. To choose the handful or so of photos that I thought were the best to grace this page, I had to sift through nearly 200 photos, cull 98% of them, then edit (which I am baaaad at) and resize the remaining photos before posting them here. Seriously, who takes 200 photos of a burrito bowl? I do, apparently.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="one-pan roasted sweet potato burrito bowls // themuffinmyth.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6246" height="450" src="http://themuffinmyth.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/roasted_sweet_potato_burrito_bowls_1.jpg" width="675"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Part of the issue is that I dont use a tripod, so Im compelled to take the same photo many, many times to make sure I get one that isnt blurry. But part of it is me just not being able to stop myself from taking far too many photos, which then creates a lot of work down the road. So, moving forwards Im going to challenge myself to take fewer photos per recipe. Like, 50 or so, which should make the editing process much more streamlined. Maaaaybe Ill invest in a tripod so that those 50 shots are more solid. I also need to invest some time in learning better editing skills, because since I started taking photos in RAW the editing process takes me approx 10 times as long and I think the photos end up looking crappier because I just dont know what Im doing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="one-pan roasted sweet potato burrito bowls // themuffinmyth.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6247" height="450" src="http://themuffinmyth.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/roasted_sweet_potato_burrito_bowls_2.jpg" width="675"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Another thing Im doing to keep things simple in 2015 is more make-ahead meals, and making more of those into super simple one-pot or one-pan productions. Lucky thing, these burrito bowls fit all of those criteria.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres a bit of chopping to do up front, but then your oven does the rest of the heavy lifting. Best of all, everything gets mixed into the same pan, even the black beans and lime juice and cilantro once the roasting is done. Then all youve got to do is spoon this goodness into bowls, garnish, and dinner is done.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="one-pan roasted sweet potato burrito bowls // themuffinmyth.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6248" height="454" src="http://themuffinmyth.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/roasted_sweet_potato_burrito_bowls_3.jpg" width="675"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         But wait! Want to turn these into make-ahead meals? No problem. Just grab some whole wheat tortillas, spoon some burrito filling in, roll em up, wrap em in foil, and youve totally got freezer burritos! And you know I love me some freezer burritos!
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Have you got any tips for keeping things simple in 2015? Lets share in the comments here!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="MM_Know_Icon_FINAL" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-2615" height="96" src="http://themuffinmyth.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/mm_know_icon_final.jpg" width="215"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Orange fleshed
         <em>
          sweet potatoes
         </em>
         are incredibly rich in beta-carotene, a pre-curser to vitamin A. In fact, some studies have shown that sweet potatoes contain even more bioavailable beta-carotene than leafy green vegetables. Sweet potatoes also containstorage proteins called sporamins which have unique antioxidant properties. Alsovaluable for their anti-inflammatory benefits and blood sugar regulation, sweet potatoes are loaded with fiber, which helps steady the pace of digestion and regulate glucose uptake. Theyre also a great source of vitamin C, manganese, vitamin B6, potassium, dietary fiber, niacin, vitamin B1, vitamin B2, and phosphoruscomplex carbohydrates.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="one-pan roasted sweet potato burrito bowls // themuffinmyth.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6249" height="450" src="http://themuffinmyth.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/roasted_sweet_potato_burrito_bowls_4.jpg" width="675"/>
        </p>

            <time datetime="PT45M" itemprop="totalTime">
             45 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClearLeft">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          Super-simple one-pan roasted sweet potato and black bean burrito bowls transition easily to wrapped burritos for make-ahead or freezer meals.
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            The Muffin Myth
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 medium Sweet Potatoes, peeled and cubed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 jalapeo peppers, de-seeded and diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 large red pepper, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 medium zucchini, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 red onion, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp ground cumin
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp chili powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp smoked paprika
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Salt and pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cans (400g each) black beans, rinsed and drained
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup fresh cilantro, chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Juice of one lime
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat your oven to 200C / 400F.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a roasting pan, combine sweet potatoes, jalapeos, red pepper, zucchini, and red onion. Add olive oil, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, and a touch of salt and pepper. Give this a good mix so that everything is well seasoned.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place in the oven and roast for about 20 - 30 minutes, stirring once about half way through. The sweet potato should be just tender when the vegetables have finished roasting.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove the roasting pan from the oven and add the black beans, cilantro, and lime juice directly to the pan. Stir to combine.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spoon the mixture into bowls and serve warm. Top with diced avocado, cashew cream, grated cheese, or other desired toppings.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           This also makes great burritos, wraps, or pita filling.
           <br/>
           When made as burritos, this freezes well for make ahead meals.
          </div>
