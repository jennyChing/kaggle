     <p>
      In
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assyrian_mythology" title="Assyrian mythology">
       Assyrian
      </a>
      and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Babylonian_mythology" title="Babylonian mythology">
       Babylonian mythology
      </a>
      ,
      <b>
       Pazuzu
      </b>
      (sometimes
      <b>
       Fazuzu
      </b>
      or
      <b>
       Pazuza
      </b>
      ) was the king of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mesopotamian_demon" title="Mesopotamian demon">
       demons
      </a>
      of the wind, brother of
      <a href="/wiki/Humbaba" title="Humbaba">
       Humbaba
      </a>
      and son of the god
      <a href="/wiki/Hanbi" title="Hanbi">
       Hanbi
      </a>
      . He also represented the southwestern wind, the bearer of storms and drought.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Iconography">
       Iconography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pazuzu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Iconography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Pazuzu is often depicted as a combination of diverse animal and human parts. He has the body of a man, the head of a
      <a href="/wiki/Lion" title="Lion">
       lion
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Dog" title="Dog">
       dog
      </a>
      , talons of an
      <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">
       eagle
      </a>
      , two pairs of wings, a
      <a href="/wiki/Scorpion" title="Scorpion">
       scorpion
      </a>
      's tail, and a
      <a href="/wiki/Serpentine_shape" title="Serpentine shape">
       serpentine
      </a>
      penis.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mythology">
       Mythology
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pazuzu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mythology">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Pazuzu is the demon of the
      <a href="/wiki/Wind#History" title="Wind">
       southwest wind
      </a>
      known for bringing
      <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">
       famine
      </a>
      during dry seasons, and
      <a href="/wiki/Locust" title="Locust">
       locusts
      </a>
      during rainy seasons. Pazuzu was said to be invoked in amulets, which combat the powers of his rival,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the malicious goddess
      <a href="/wiki/Lamashtu" title="Lamashtu">
       Lamashtu
      </a>
      , who was believed to cause harm to mother and child during childbirth. Although Pazuzu is, himself, an evil spirit, he drives away other evil spirits, therefore protecting humans against plagues and misfortunes.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_modern_culture">
       In modern culture
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pazuzu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In modern culture">

       </span>

     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote">
      See also:
      <a href="/wiki/Pazuzu_(The_Exorcist)" title="Pazuzu (The Exorcist)">
       Pazuzu (The Exorcist)
      </a>
     </div>
     <ul>
      <li>
       In the 1971 novel
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Exorcist_(novel)" title="The Exorcist (novel)">
         The Exorcist
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/The_Exorcist_(film)" title="The Exorcist (film)">
        the movie based on the novel
       </a>
       , Pazuzu is supposedly the evil spirit that possesses the young girl
       <a href="/wiki/Regan_MacNeil" title="Regan MacNeil">
        Regan MacNeil
       </a>
       . He reappears in the 1977 sequel
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Exorcist_II:_The_Heretic" title="Exorcist II: The Heretic">
         Exorcist II: The Heretic
        </a>
       </i>
       . In this movie, Pazuzu is both named as the demon antagonist of Regan MacNeil and the unwitting helper of Father Philip Lamont (played by Richard Burton), as he seeks to finally free Regan from his hold. In the end, Father Lamont defeats the demons.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Pazuzu appears on the album cover of the
       <a href="/wiki/Gorillaz" title="Gorillaz">
        Gorillaz
       </a>
       compilation album
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/D-Sides" title="D-Sides">
         D-Sides
        </a>
       </i>
       , as a statue in front of the band's former recording studio, in the music videos for the band's songs "
       <a href="/wiki/Dare_(song)" title="Dare (song)">
        Dare
       </a>
       " and "Rockit" and in the unmade video for "
       <a href="/wiki/Rhinestone_Eyes" title="Rhinestone Eyes">
        Rhinestone Eyes
       </a>
       ".
      </li>
      <li>
       In his author's bio in the first issue of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Warrior_(comics)" title="Warrior (comics)">
         Warrior
        </a>
       </i>
       magazine in 1982, comic book author
       <a href="/wiki/Alan_Moore" title="Alan Moore">
        Alan Moore
       </a>
       claims to be "possessed by the demon Pazuzu".
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Pazuzu is mentioned in the
       <a href="/wiki/Simon_Necronomicon" title="Simon Necronomicon">
        Simon Necronomicon
       </a>
       , a publication of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avon_Books" title="Avon Books">
        Avon Books
       </a>
       that purports to document incantations to invoke "incredible things, beings, and monsters into visible form". He is described therein as "Lord of all fevers and plagues, grinning Dark Angel of the Four Wings, horned, with rotting genitalia," and as the "brother of
       <a href="/wiki/Humbaba" title="Humbaba">
        HUMWAWA
       </a>
       ". The book claims that he is among the most fearsome of all entities, stating in part: "It is the able magician indeed who can remove PAZUZU once he has laid hold of a man, for PAZUZU lays hold unto death."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         2
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       Although the book makes the claim that Pazuzu (like all entities listed therein) was of
       <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">
        Sumerian
       </a>
       origin, critics
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=John_Wisdom_Gonce_III&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="John Wisdom Gonce III (page does not exist)">
        John Wisdom Gonce III
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Harms" title="Daniel Harms">
        Daniel Harms
       </a>
       document the erroneous nature of such a claim, stating: "Likewise, the demon Pazuzu does not appear in myth until
       <a href="/wiki/Assyria" title="Assyria">
        Assyria
       </a>
       's rise in the first millennium B.C., long after
       <a href="/wiki/Sumer" title="Sumer">
        Sumer
       </a>
       's prime ... Thus the beings of the Simon Necronomicon bear little resemblance to either Sumerian myth or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lovecraftian" title="Lovecraftian">
        Lovecraftian
       </a>
       fiction."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         3
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       Pazuzu is the demon that haunts
       <a href="/wiki/The_Extraordinary_Adventures_of_Ad%C3%A8le_Blanc-Sec" title="The Extraordinary Adventures of Adle Blanc-Sec">
        Adle Blanc-Sec
       </a>
       in
       <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Tardi" title="Jacques Tardi">
        Tardi
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Graphic_novel" title="Graphic novel">
        graphic novel
       </a>
       <i>
        The Demon of the Eiffel Tower
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        Le Dmon de la tour Eiffel
       </i>
       , 1976). Pazuzu also appears in "Mummies on Parade" ("Momies en folie", 1978).
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">

        ]
       </sup>
      </li>
