        <p>
         When you think of
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/apartments/Washington/Seattle/">
          Seattle
         </a>
         , chances are you think of rain, coffee, grunge music and the scenic beauty of the Pacific Northwest. Thats all good stuff, but when you visit or move to The Emerald City, theres much more you need to know  things about day-to-day life, the places you can work and play, the charming hideaways where you can meet with friends to escape the drizzle and enjoy a cup of joe.

        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Ballard-watermarked.png">
          <img alt="Ballard - watermarked" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63670" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Ballard-watermarked.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         With bookstores and coffee shops dotting the sidewalks and the Puget Sound forming its western border, Ballard is the top-searched Seattle neighborhood on ApartmentGuide.com  and its not hard to see why. This historic part of town has a unique charm and all sorts of attractions for all kinds of people. Boaters and windsurfers are active in the waves, while those who prefer the indoors can be found browsing bookstores, sipping coffee or enjoying the Nordic Heritage Museum. Ballard also maintains its historic charm through the Ballard Historical Society, a volunteer-run organization dedicated to preserving the character of the neighborhood.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/4-seattle-blogs-reading/">
           4 Seattle Blogs You Should be Reading
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/Capitol-Hill/">
           Capitol Hill
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/World-of-Beer-Seattle.png">
          <img alt="World of Beer Seattle" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63671" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/World-of-Beer-Seattle.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         One of the largest and most heavily populated areas of Seattle, Capitol Hill is closely associated with music  specifically the grunge era of the early 90s. Just east of the citys business district, Capitol Hill features all kind of residences, from studio apartments to stately mansions. If you like to shop, attend concerts or enjoy a pint of beer, you can find it in Capitol Hill. The neighborhood is also home to some of Seattles most famous landmarks, including the original Starbucks and Top Pot Doughnuts. Also well-known as a gay-friendly area, Capitol Hill is where the party happens  no matter what kind of party you prefer.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/settle-into-one-of-these-seattle-neighborhoods/">
           Settle Into One of These Seattle Neighborhoods
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/Queen-Anne/">
           Queen Anne
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/queen-anne-watermarked.png">
          <img alt="queen anne - watermarked" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63672" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/queen-anne-watermarked.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just across Lake Union from Capitol Hill is Queen Anne, named for the highest hill in the city. Its where youll find skate parks, 12 historic houses, the Pacific Science Center, a monorail, the scenic Kerry Park, and Seattles most famous landmark, the Space Needle. Bookworms will love the Queen Anne branch of the Seattle Public Library, which was renovated in 2007 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places. And if you love locally grown produce, youll fit right in here  the Queen Anne farmers market operates weekly from June through October.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/welcome-to-seattle/">
           Welcome to Seattle
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          4.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/University-District/">
           University District
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/u-district-watermarked.png">
          <img alt="u district - watermarked" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63673" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/u-district-watermarked.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Known to locals as the U District, you dont have to be a college student to find something to love about this neighborhood. In addition to the University of Washington, youll find many walking and biking trails, all sorts of taverns and brewpubs, and an annual street fair every May. Popular landmarks include the Blue Moon Tavern, which has been in operation since 1934, and Big Time, Seattles original brewpub.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/study-the-art-of-seattle-student-living/">
           Study the Art of Seattle Student Living
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          5.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/West-Seattle/">
           West Seattle
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Skater-Seattle.png">
          <img alt="Skater Seattle" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63674" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Skater-Seattle.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Surrounded by water on three sides  the Puget Sound to the west, Elliott Bay to the north and the Duwamish Waterway to the east  West Seattle is home to Alki Beach Park, the West Seattle Golf Course and Lincoln Park. High Point is one of the most famous areas of West Seattle, named because it encompasses the highest point in the city: the intersection of 35
         <sup>
          th
         </sup>
         Avenue and Myrtle Street, at 520 feet above sea level. The annual Seafair festival celebrates Seattles marine and boating pastimes. West Seattle is also the home of a few famous residents, including Pearl Jam lead singer Eddie Vedder.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/best-outdoor-gear-stores-in-seattle/">
           Best Outdoor Gear Stores in Seattle
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/Downtown/">
           Downtown
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Chess-Players-Seattle.png">
          <img alt="Chess Players Seattle" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63675" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Chess-Players-Seattle.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Home of the iconic Pike Place Market, the Seattle Art Museum, CenturyLink Field and the Olympic Sculpture Park, the Downtown area is also known as the central business district  but it feels like more play than work. The shopping area coincides with the main financial district (which seems apt) and the Seattle Central Library is an architectural wonder.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/staying-in-seattle-short-term-things-to-see-and-do/">
           Staying in Seattle Short-Term: Things to See and Do
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          7.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/North-Seattle/">
           North Seattle
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         An umbrella term that encompasses the neighborhoods of Olympic Hills, Victory Heights, Pinehurst, Maple Leaf and Matthews Beach, North Seattle is where youll find Jackson Park Golf Course, Victory Creek Park and the Thornton Creek natural area. Nature lovers will be captivated by the areas mature evergreens. Its famously easy to get around this area by mass transit.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/gone-fishin%E2%80%99-%E2%80%93-in-seattle/">
           Gone Fishing in Seattle
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          8.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/Northgate/">
           Northgate
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just to the west of North Seattle is Northgate, which has seen a lot of commercial and residential development in the past few years. The Northgate Mall has been recently renovated, and the area also has a new park, library and community center. Residents should expect to see much more similar development in the coming years.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/be-eco-friendly-and-shop-at-these-green-seattle-businesses/">
           Be Eco-Friendly and Shop at These Green Seattle Businesses
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          9.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/South-Lake-Union/">
           South Lake Union
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/south-lake-union-watermarked.png">
          <img alt="south lake union - watermarked" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63676" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/south-lake-union-watermarked.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Nestled between Queen Anne and Capitol Hill is where youll find South Lake Union, so named because its sits at the south end of Lake Union. This is the home of the Amazon Campus and the Center for Wooden Boats. South Lake Union is also the epicenter for healthcare facilities in Seattle, including the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center and Seattle Childrens Hospital.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/seattle-bike-shops-for-every-type-of-cyclist/">
           Seattle Bike Shops for Every Type of Cyclist
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          10.
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/neighborhoods/Washington/Seattle/Green-Lake/">
           Green Lake
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/green-lake-watermarked.png">
          <img alt="green lake - watermarked" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-63677" height="404" src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/green-lake-watermarked.png" width="607"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Just to the east of Ballard is Green Lake, a neighborhood named for the body of water in its center. Its easy to get to the Downtown neighborhood from Green Lake via Interstate 5, which is why many people have moved there in recent years. But there are plenty of other reasons to live in Green Lake  the people here love to get outside and enjoy the lake or grab a drink at one of the many local pubs.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Read more:
          <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/work-it-out-at-the-best-gyms-in-seattle/">
           Work It Out at the Best Gyms in Seattle
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <i>
          Whats your favorite Seattle neighborhood?
         </i>
        </p>

       Most Expensive Town to Rent in the U.S.
      </h3>
      <div class="textwidget">
       <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/williston-nd/">
        <img src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/confluence-at-harvest-hills-williston.jpg"/>
       </a>
       <p>
        <i>
         <a href="http://www.apartmentguide.com/apartments/North-Dakota/Williston/The-Confluence-At-Harvest-Hills/191882/">
          The Confluence at Harvest Hills
         </a>
         , Williston, ND
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        Williston, North Dakota, has the highest average entry-level rent prices in the country. We think that's fascinating ... and so do you! Here's where you can see the AG Blog in the news:
       </p>
       <center>
        <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/US/life-williston-north-dakota-expensive-place-rent-apartment/story?id=22549192">
         <img src="http://www.apartmentguide.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/abc_newsresized.jpg"/>
