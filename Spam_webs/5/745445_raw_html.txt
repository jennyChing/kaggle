     <p>
      <b>
       Zachary "Zach" L. Bonner
      </b>
      (born November 17, 1997) is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">
       philanthropist
      </a>
      and founder of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" title="Non-profit organization">
       non-profit
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">
       charity
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Little_Red_Wagon_Foundation" title="Little Red Wagon Foundation">
       Little Red Wagon Foundation
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UPI_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-UPI-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bazar_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bazar-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bonner received the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Presidential_Service_Award" title="Presidential Service Award">
       Presidential Service Award
      </a>
      in 2006.
     </p>
     <p>
      When he was seven years old, he founded the organization to aid the 1.3 million
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homeless_children_in_the_United_States" title="Homeless children in the United States">
       homeless children in the United States
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UPI_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-UPI-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bonner said that, "These kids don't have a home, they don't have a safe place to sleep at night. They're out on the streets not because they want to be, but because it's out of their control."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bazar_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bazar-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2007, Bonner began his three-stage "My House to the White House" project. The project's purpose was to raise money and awareness for homeless children. In 2007, he walked 280 miles from
      <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">
       Tampa
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">
       Tallahassee, Florida
      </a>
      , while in 2008, he covered 250 miles from Tallahassee to
      <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">
       Atlanta
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">
       Georgia
      </a>
      . In the final leg of the trip, he walked 668 miles from Georgia to Washington D.C. Upon the completion of the "My House to the White House" project, Bonner planned another project, March Across America. From March 23 to September 14, 2010, he walked 2,448 miles from Tampa to
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
       Los Angeles
      </a>
      . Starring
      <a href="/wiki/Chandler_Canterbury" title="Chandler Canterbury">
       Chandler Canterbury
      </a>
      as Bonner,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Little_Red_Wagon" title="Little Red Wagon">
        Little Red Wagon
       </a>
      </i>
      , a
      <a href="/wiki/Docudrama" title="Docudrama">
       docudrama
      </a>
      about Bonner's philanthropic work, was filmed in 2010 and released in 2012.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">
       Philanthropy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zach_Bonner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Little_Red_Wagon_Foundation" title="Little Red Wagon Foundation">
       Little Red Wagon Foundation
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="3072" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg/300px-Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg/450px-Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg/600px-Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Zach_Bonner_with_Volunteer_Florida_staff_2008.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>

        Bonner with friends and Volunteer Florida staff members
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Bonner has done a variety of
      <a href="/wiki/Volunteering" title="Volunteering">
       volunteer work
      </a>
      since he was six years old.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grossman_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Grossman-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2004, when
      <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Charley" title="Hurricane Charley">
       Hurricane Charley
      </a>
      hit neighborhoods, he collected 27 pickup trucks of water in his little
      <a href="/wiki/Toy_wagon" title="Toy wagon">
       red wagon
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stacy_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Stacy-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He established the
      <a href="/wiki/Little_Red_Wagon_Foundation" title="Little Red Wagon Foundation">
       Little Red Wagon Foundation
      </a>
      to "continue helping kids more efficiently".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bazar_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Bazar-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bonner teamed up with the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/StandUp_For_Kids" title="StandUp For Kids">
       StandUp For Kids
      </a>
      and collected 400
      <a href="/wiki/Backpack" title="Backpack">
       backpacks
      </a>
      of supplies, nicknamed "Zachpacks", for homeless children. The Zachpacks were filled with donated
      <a href="/wiki/Snack" title="Snack">
       snacks
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Toy" title="Toy">
       toys
      </a>
      , and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toiletry" title="Toiletry">
       toiletries
      </a>
      . To date he has distributed over 10,000 of the Zachpacks.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Perino_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Perino-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bonner organized
      <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">
       Christmas
      </a>
      parties for homeless children living in
      <a href="/wiki/Baker,_Louisiana" title="Baker, Louisiana">
       Baker, Louisiana
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Perino_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Perino-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and he gave Christmas presents to
      <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">
       Hurricane Katrina
      </a>
      victims.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      To mitigate the adverse effects of homelessness, Bonner host parties for children who live in shelters at
      <a href="/wiki/Build-A-Bear_Workshop" title="Build-A-Bear Workshop">
       Build-A-Bear Workshop
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Chuck_E._Cheese%27s" title="Chuck E. Cheese's">
       Chuck E. Cheese's
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Six_Flags" title="Six Flags">
       Six Flags
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newberg_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Newberg-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In April 2007, he organized 24 Hours, an event that simulated being homeless for 24 hours. During that period of time, students in
      <a href="/wiki/High_school" title="High school">
       high school
      </a>
      stayed in their own separate boxes for 24 hours.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frederick_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Frederick-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In January 2010, he launched a national campaign to end child homelessness. As keynote speaker at The Children's Philanthropy Center Annual Youth Symposium in Northern Virginia, he inspired young activists to use their voice to create change. The message, "You Matter! Let Your Voice Be Heard" became the signature anthem for their youth advocacy movement.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chandler_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Chandler-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="My_House_to_the_White_House">
       My House to the White House
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zach_Bonner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: My House to the White House">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Zach Bonner's "My House to the White House" project took place in three stages covering 1,225 miles to raise awareness and funds for homeless children. The first leg in 2007 from November 326 covered 280 miles from
      <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">
       Tampa
      </a>
      to
      <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">
       Tallahassee, Florida
      </a>
      and raised $25,000.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morelli_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Morelli-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The second leg in the fall of 2008 covered over 250 miles from Tallahassee to
      <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">
       Atlanta
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">
       Georgia
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stacy_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Stacy-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The third leg of the trip began May 11, 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ruebens_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ruebens-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The 668-mile, 59-day walk from Georgia to Washington D.C. was completed on July 10.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simon_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Simon-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
