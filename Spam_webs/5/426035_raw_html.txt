        </header>
        <!-- !Main Content -->
        <div class="region" id="content">

          <article about="/content/animal-care-and-socialization" class="node node-page article clearfix" id="node-279" role="article" typeof="foaf:Document">
           <div class="node-content">
            <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden view-mode-full">
             <div class="field-items">
              <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
               <p class="p1">
                <span style="color:#a52a2a;">
                 <span style="font-size:22px;">
                  <strong>
                   With 400-600 animals in our building
                   <br/>
                   on any given day, we rely on the support
                   <br/>
                   of our volunteers to provide socialization
                   <br/>
                   and enrichment for our animals.
                  </strong>
                 </span>
                </span>
               </p>
               <p>
                There are three distinct programs to support the needs of dogs, cats and small
                <br/>
                animals. Each program starts out in an entry level in which animals currently on
                <br/>
                the adoption floor are cared for, and you can progress through each program to
                <br/>
                support animals in our back wards. Due to the demands of this volunteer program,
                <br/>
                we do require the following commitment from volunteers:
               </p>
               <ul>
                <li class="p1">
                 All volunteers must be 18 years or age or older to participate in this program.
                 <br/>
                 Youth under 18 can participate through the Parent &amp; Me Program.
                 <br/>
                </li>
                <li class="p1">
                 We ask for a minimum commitment of two hours per week for six months.
                 <br/>
                 If you are unable to make this commitment we ask that refrain from applying
                 <br/>
                 at this time.
                 <p>
                  The reason we require this time commitment is twofold. First, we invest a
                  <br/>
                  minimum of two training sessions for each volunteer and so we ask that in return
                  <br/>
                  volunteers serve six months of volunteer time.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Secondly, due to the high demand of our volunteer program, we have limited
                  <br/>
                  capacity for volunteers. We ask that those who cannot make the required time
                  <br/>
                  commitment do not apply, so that there will be availability for an individual who
                  <br/>
                  can commit.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  The majority of our volunteer programs are drop-in based, so you will be able to
                  <br/>
                  volunteer your time based on your own schedule, be it daytime, evenings or
                  <br/>
                  weekends.
                 </p>
                </li>
               </ul>
               <p class="p4">
                <span style="color:#a52a2a;">
                 <strong>
                  How to apply
                 </strong>
                </span>
                <br/>
                Thank you for your interest in volunteering at the Edmonton Humane Society.
                <br/>
                Volunteer applications are now closed until Fall 2015.
               </p>
               <p class="p1">
                <span style="color:#a52a2a;">
                 <strong>
                  What happens next
                 </strong>
                </span>
                <br/>
                Once you have submitted your application form, we will extend an invitation to
                <br/>
                attend and orientation session to learn more about our shelter and our volunteer
                <br/>
                programs.
               </p>
               <p>
                Once you have attended the orientation session, you will sign up for
                <br/>
                program specific training, be it Cat 101, Dog 101/102, or Small Animal 101. Once
                <br/>
                you have completed your training, you will be able to begin your volunteer journey!
               </p>
               <p class="p1">
                <span style="color:#a52a2a;">
                 <strong>
                  Other things to consider
                 </strong>
                </span>
               </p>
               <ul>
                <li class="p1">
                 Applications must be submitted online. There are no hard copies available.
                 <br/>
                 Due to the volume of volunteers we oversee, we do require volunteers to
                 <br/>
                 have access to the internet and an email account.
                 <br/>
                </li>
                <li class="p1">
                 There is a one-time administrative fee of $35 cover the costs of your volunteer
                 <br/>
                 tee shirt and ID badge, as well as offset expenses in running our volunteer
                 <br/>
                 programs. This fee is payable once you have completed your volunteer training.
                 <br/>
                </li>
                <li class="p1">
                 Please note that we do not accommodate short term volunteers, such as required
                 <br/>
                 for school projects. Due to the level of training we invest in our volunteers, we
                 <br/>
                 require a minimum commitment of six months.
                 <br/>
                </li>
                <li class="p1">
                 We intake new volunteers three times a year: fall, winter and spring. Please
                 <br/>
                 always check back for information on our next intake.
                </li>
               </ul>
               <p class="p3">
               </p>
               <p class="p5">
