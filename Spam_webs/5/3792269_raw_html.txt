          <h2 style="text-align: center">
           Effects of Noise Pollution
          </h2>
          <p style="text-align: center">
          </p>
          <p class="pseudoblockquote" style="text-align:center;">
           <em>
            How does noise affect you? Why is it bad? The effects of noise on your ears, your body, your mind, your behavior.
           </em>
          </p>
          <hr style="width:40%; height:2px; border-width:0; color:#666699; background-color:#666699;"/>
          <br/>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 style="text-align:center;">
           Direct Effects of Noise
          </h3>
          The most direct and obvious effect of noise is to our ears. Loud sounds can cause physical damage to the ear, resulting in:
          <p>
          </p>
          <div class="picture picright" style="width:139px;">
           <div class="picimgandcred">
            <img alt="boy with hands over ears" height="110" src="http://www.noisehelp.com/images/133x110xeffects-of-noise-pollution-1.jpg.pagespeed.ic.a6UE6sjkJw.jpg" title="boy with hands over ears" width="133"/>
            <div class="piccredit">
             Image courtesy of
             <a href="http://www.nasa.gov" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
              NASA
             </a>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/impaired-hearing.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
            <li>
             Hearing loss
            </li>
           </a>
           <ul>
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/noise-induced-hearing-loss.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             <li>
              How noise damages the ear
             </li>
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/temporary-hearing-loss.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             <li>
              Temporary hearing loss
             </li>
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/high-frequency-hearing-loss.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             <li>
              High-frequency hearing loss
             </li>
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/degrees-of-hearing-loss.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             <li>
              Degrees of hearing loss
             </li>
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/mild-hearing-loss.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             <li>
              Mild hearing loss
             </li>
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/hearing-loss-simulation.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             <li>
              MP3 demonstration of hearing loss
             </li>
            </a>
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/presbyacusis.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             <li>
              Age-related hearing loss
             </li>
            </a>
            and
            <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/presbycusis.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
             noise
            </a>
           </ul>
           <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/ringing-in-the-ear.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
            <li>
             Ringing in the ears (tinnitus)
            </li>
           </a>
           <li>
            Painfully over-sensitive hearing (hyperacusis)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           These conditions can be either temporary or permanent.
          </p>
          <p>
           You know that jolt of adrenaline you feel when you are surprised by a loud unexpected noise? That's an example of a hormonal disturbance (albeit a fleeting one). Other physiological effects of noise include:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Increased blood pressure (hypertension)
           </li>
           <li>
            Increased heart rate and breathing rate
           </li>
           <li>
            Digestive disturbances
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           For some people, noise is a trigger for migraine headaches
           <a id="presecondary" name="presecondary">
            .
           </a>
          </p>
          <a id="secondary" name="secondary">
           <h3 class="hlev1">
            Secondary Effects of Noise
           </h3>
          </a>
          If
          <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/trouble-staying-asleep.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
           noise prevents you from sleeping,
          </a>
          that introduces a host of other problems that are associated with sleep loss, such as reduced concentration and a weakened immune system.
          <p class="pseudoblockquote sidebar">
           Sleep loss is bad enough when you are home in your own bed, but it's especially rough if you're in a hospital bed. Hospitals can be noisy places, even at night, and many patients find it difficult to get enough sleep in that 	environment. This is more than a mere annoyance, because sleep loss can dramatically slow healing and recovery.
          </p>
          If kids are trying to learn in a noisy environment, their ability to focus and concentrate is reduced, which can naturally lead to long-term learning problems.
          <p>
           Noise can cause irritability and other mood changes, which can damage your relationships and
           <a href="http://www.noisehelp.com/stress-and-sex.html" onclick="return FIX.track(this);">
            interfere with your sex life.
           </a>
           In some cases it can even contribute to an increase in aggressive behavior.
          </p>
          <p>
           Have you ever been in a bar or restaurant that was so noisy that you made yourself hoarse trying to talk loudly enough to be heard? Maybe you gave up on having a meaningful conversation altogether. Maybe you even ended up drinking more than you intended to, because it was too noisy to converse. All of these are side effects of noise.
          </p>
          <p>
           A few people find noise so intolerable that they go to extreme lengths to avoid it, staying home as much as possible and effectively becoming social hermits.
          </p>
          <p>
           From these examples, a subtle but consistent pattern emerges: Noise creates social barriers between people.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
          </p>
          <h3 style="text-align:center;">
           Bottom Line: Stress!
          </h3>
          Putting all these effects together, it's easy to see that the noise we experience adds to the stress in our lives.
