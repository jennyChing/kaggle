                MTV Goes There With 'White People' Documentary
               </a>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="separator story-name-description-separator">
             </div>
             <div class="story-description-container">
              <div class="story-description">
               <p>
                Talking about how racism and privilege affects us personally are touchy subjects to say the least. When you are White, the easiest option is always to ignore it. But when you are a Person of Color, dealing with our differences is an everyday issue that can't just be swept under the rug.
               </p>
               <p>
                And in light of events like the
                <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/pictures/charleston-south-carolina-church-shooting/">
                 heinous murders
                </a>
                in Charleston, numerous
                <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ng-interactive/2015/jun/01/about-the-counted">
                 incidents
                </a>
                of
                <a href="http://truthvoice.com/2015/06/25-shocking-facts-about-the-epidemic-of-police-brutality-in-america/">
                 police brutality
                </a>
                around the country
                <a href="http://theflama.com/politician-luis-gutirrez-schools-ann-coulter-on-immigration">
                 and many more
                </a>
                , we've seen "the dire effects racism, gone unchecked, has on our communities, Pulitzer Prize-winning journalist,
                <a href="http://joseantoniovargas.com">
                 Jose Antonio Vargas
                </a>
                said in a statement
                <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2015/06/22/jose-antonio-vargas-white-people_n_7640766.html">
                 last month
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <p>
                That's why the famed journalist, who is from the Philippines and is an undocumented immigrant, has courageously set out to bust through the guard that protects many Caucasians from talking about race, producing a film called "
                <a href="http://www.mtv.com/shows/white-people/">
                 White People
                </a>
                " with his nonprofit group
                <a href="http://www.defineamerican.com/site/splash">
                 Define American
                </a>
                , as part of MTV's Emmy-winning
                <a href="http://www.lookdifferent.org/whitepeople">
                 Look Different
                </a>
                campaign.
               </p>
               <p>
                Premiering on Wednesday, July 22nd at 8/7c on MTV, "
                <a href="http://www.lookdifferent.org/whitepeople">
                 White People
                </a>
                " centers on five one-on-one interviews with White people from varying upbringings, and includes group chats with millennials at various schools and community centers across the country.
               </p>
               <p>
                As MTV President Stephen Friedman
                <a href="http://www.tvweek.com/tvbizwire/2015/06/mtv-plays-the-race-card/">
                 puts it
                </a>
                : By shining a spotlight on whiteness, we hope White People will serve as a powerful conversation starter that encourages our audience to address racial bias through honest, judgment-free dialogue.
               </p>
               <p>
                What do you think of the documentary? Do you think it's needed or not?
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <em>
                  If you like this video, you'll also like, "
                  <a href="http://theflama.com/that-one-time-starbucks-tried-to-solve-racism">
                   That One Time Starbucks Tried To Solve Racism
                  </a>
                  ."
                 </em>
                </strong>
               </p>
