      <h1 class="title">
       <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/2014/02/21/said-funny-customer-comments/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to They said WHAT? Not-so-funny Customer Comments">
        They said WHAT? Not-so-funny Customer Comments
       </a>
      </h1>
      by
      <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/author/cscutt/" rel="author" title="Posts by Caroline Scutt">
       Caroline Scutt
      </a>
      <span class="separator">
       
      </span>
      February 21, 2014

     <div class="clearfix post-wrap post-10010 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-opinions" id="post-10010">
      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        Weve all had wide-eyed moments when we find ourselves thinking
        <i>
         Really, did this person standing in front of me actually just say that?
        </i>
        And being the customer-focused, benefit-of-the-doubt-giving, gentle bookseller souls we are, we respond with a nod and a smile and keep our sarcastic comebacks to ourselves.
       </p>
       <p>
        The good news is I wanted to come up with a top 10 list of most stupid or insensitive comments but I couldnt so here are the top 5 most insensitive, funny or otherwise out-there things said to me in the last 18 months  and how I would have liked to respond!
       </p>
       <ol start="1">
        <li>
         Really you just bought this bookstore now? I thought all bookstores were going out of business. Really? Isnt everyone going to e-readers and you cant compete with
         <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/2012/08/24/smooth-sailing-on-the-big-river-or-selling-books-on-amazon/">
          Amazon
         </a>
         can you? Umm. Good luck with it.
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <i>
           Well when you put it that way I guess I should just lock the door behind you and call it a day. But before you leave, why dont you buy something so you can proudly boast that said item was purchased from one of the last remaining bookstores.
           <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/last-bookstore.jpg">
            <img alt="last bookstore" class="size-medium wp-image-10011 aligncenter" height="225" src="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/last-bookstore-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
          </i>
         </p>
        </li>
       </ol>
       <ol start="2">
        <li>
         Youve never read (fill in the blank). Its only the best childrens book ever. How could you have never even heard of it?
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        <i>
         <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/BookWeekPoster.jpg">
          <img alt="BookWeekPoster" class="size-medium wp-image-10012 alignright" height="300" src="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/BookWeekPoster-200x300.jpg" width="200"/>
         </a>
         Maybe it is because there are millions of childrens books out there and if you arent talking about Peter Rabbit or Goodnight Moon chances are I might not be familiar with it. Id be happy to track it down and order it though. Oh, you just wanted to pick it up today. Good luck with that.
        </i>
       </p>
       <!--Ad Injection:random-->
       <div style="float:left;padding-top:5px;padding-bottom:5px;margin-right:5px;">

       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        3. This greeting card is a good price point. This one however is not but Ill buy it anyway.
        <em>
         Please
        </em>
        d
        <i>
         ont do me any favors. I can live without the $1.00 I make on that particular card if it is going to make you feel as if you are being taken advantage of in any way. And the percentage that goes to help get books into the hands of kids in third world countries, well Im sure they would agree with you, with me
         <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/grumpyoldlady.jpg">
          <img alt="grumpyoldlady" class="size-medium wp-image-10013 alignleft" height="300" src="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/grumpyoldlady-200x300.jpg" width="200"/>
         </a>
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <p>
        4. Im not a reader. I dont like books.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <i>
         Did you say you cant read because the sign over the front door says
         <strong>
          Book
         </strong>
         Garden.
        </i>
        To be fair we do sell toys, t-shirts and other items but most of them are book-related, and I have visited more than one bookstore where books are sadly in the minority.
        <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/readingfordummies.jpg">
         <img alt="readingfordummies" class="size-full wp-image-10014 aligncenter" height="207" src="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/readingfordummies.jpg" width="244"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        5. Im sure I can just order it on Amazon.
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
         Im sure you can too. Amazon has everything. And it does a great job of bringing the community together for poetry slams, author readings, writing workshops and kids book clubs. Enjoy.
         <a href="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/computersworld.jpg">
          <img alt="computersworld" class=" wp-image-10015 alignright" height="222" src="http://bookshopblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/computersworld.jpg" width="266"/>
         </a>
        </i>
       </p>
       <p>
        Not so bad, right? Im sure there are a few bookstore veterans out there who have funnier commentary to share so please do!
       </p>
