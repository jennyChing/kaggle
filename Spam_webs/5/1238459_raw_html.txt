      <h1>
       Teen Star Discusses Her Struggle with Eating Disorders
      </h1>
      <span class="post-comment-box">
       <span>
        Comments Off
       </span>
      </span>
      <div class="post-meta">
       <span class="author">
        <a href="http://blog.crchealth.com/author/crchealthgroup/" rel="author" title="Posts by crchealthgroup">
         crchealthgroup
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="date">
        March 07, 2012
       </span>
       <span class="category">
        <a href="http://blog.crchealth.com/category/eating-disorders/" rel="category tag">
         Eating Disorders
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div id="post">
      <img alt="Teen Star Discusses Her Struggle with Eating Disorders" class="featured-image" src="http://blog.crchealth.com/files/2012/03/blog-ed-lovato-demilovatosite-600x369.jpg" width="600"/>
      <div class="post-content">
       <p>
        <em>
         [Teen star Demi Lovato, center, poses with two of the Jonas Brothers to promote their 2010 concert tour. Lovato recently went public with her struggles with eating disorders and related issues.]
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Like many teenage girls her age, Demi Lovato has had her share of struggles. Unlike many of them, she is able to share her journey with millions and offer help and hope for those who find themselves on similar shaky ground.
       </p>
       <p>
        Lovato, the 19-year-old singer and actress loved by fans around the world, developed an eating disorder for which she sought treatment. Being in the public eye meant she could not have the usual amount of privacy a person wants when they go into treatment for a life-threatening condition.
       </p>
       <p>
        Lovato chose to share her story by allowing cameras from MTV to follow her, resulting in the special Demi Lovato: Stay Strong, which airs this week on MTV. The documentary follows her through treatment for her eating disorder, as well as other emotional issues, including depression and
        <a href="http://www.carolinaeatingdisorders.com/ed-and-cutters.php" target="_blank" title="Carolina House: Self-Harm">
         self-harm
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Speaking to the Today show, Lovato said, Ive decided to tell my story because there are a lot of young teens, girls and boys, that are dealing with the same issues that Ive been through. If Im able to use my voice to do good in the world then I definitely want to do that.
       </p>
       <p>
        In sharing her reason to be open in the documentary, she said I have daily challenges but so do many kids who are struggling to feel comfortable in their own skin. If opening up and sharing my story inspires even one person to stay strong or to get the help they need, Ive succeeded.
       </p>
       <p>
        Lovato has developed Stay strong as a mantra of sorts, using it to help remind herself that she has come a long way and can continue to utilize her strength as she moves past her eating disorder and other issues.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
