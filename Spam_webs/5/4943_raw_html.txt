          <div style="margin-left: 0px; margin-right: 0px; text-align: justify; line-height: 100%; font-size: 16px;">
           <p>
            <b>
             Georges Seurat
            </b>
            (December 2, 1859 - March 29, 1891) was a French painter and draftsman. His large work Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte, his most famous painting, altered the direction of modern art by initiating Neo-impressionism, and is one of the icons of 19th century painting.
           </p>
           <p>
            Seurat was born into a very rich family in Paris. His father, Antoine Chrysostom Seurat, was a legal official and a native of Champagne; his mother, Ernestine Faivre, was Parisian.
           </p>
           <p>
            Georges Seurat first studied art with Justin Lequiene, a sculptor. Seurat attended the Ecole des Beaux-Arts in 1878 and 1879.
           </p>
