      <p>
       <em>
       </em>
      </p>
      <span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="DISPLAY: inline">
       <div class="pkg embedded-image center" style="WIDTH: 500px">
        <a href="http://www.chicagonow.com/blogs/frugalista/couponsbargainbri.jpg" rel="lightbox" title="couponsbargainbri.jpg">
         <em>
          <img alt="couponsbargainbri.jpg" class="mt-image-center" height="375" src="http://www.chicagonow.com/blogs/frugalista/couponsbargainbri.jpg" width="500"/>
         </em>
        </a>
       </div>
       <p>
       </p>
      </span>
      <em>
       This post sharing some extreme couponing tipswent out as the Deals and Steals newsletter this week. Next week, the newsletter will share five more tips. You can
       <a href="http://www.chicagoshopping.com/about/newsletters/">
        subscribe here
       </a>
       .
      </em>
      <p>
       <em>
        You might want to share this list of coupon tactics with friends who want to get started in couponing. For you advanced couponers, I'd love to hear your top "secrets"!
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       The Frugalista is an extreme couponer. That means she doesn't just clip coupons, she collects coupons and uses when they yield the highest savings. By using the tricks below, the Frugalista frequently pays pennies on the dollar - or less - for staple food and household items.
      </p>
      <p>
       1. Unless you really need the item, save your coupon until it goes on sale. Everything goes on sale eventually, usually every six weeks. At full price, the store brand is probably a better deal than the name brand with a coupon. It's not unusual for a high-value coupon paired with a good sale to result in a free item.
      </p>
      <p>
       2. Smaller packages are usually a better deal when buying with a coupon. This isn't the environmentally friendly choice, but when looking at savings only it makes sense: A small $1 bottle of dish soap is 75% off when paired with a 75-cents-off coupon, but a large $3 bottle is only 25% off with the same coupon.
      </p>
      <p>
       3. When purchasing buy-one-get-one-free items, you can use two manufacturer's coupons, or one coupon good for $1 off two items, or, best of all, a buy-one-get-one-free coupon, which usually results in getting both items for free. Not all stores have this policy, but in the Chicago area I have had few problems doing this at Jewel-Osco, Dominick's, CVS and Walgreens.
      </p>
      <p>
       (Caveat: Dominick's parent Safeway specifically forbids this in its
       <a href="http://www.chicagonow.com/blogs/frugalista/2010/07/dominicks-safeway-new-coupon-policy.html">
        new coupon policy
       </a>
       , but so far my local store has not enforced it.)
      </p>
      <p>
       4. You can stack manufacturer's coupons with store coupons pretty much everywhere. At Dominick's, that means using a manufacturer's coupon along with the Super Coupons printed in the flyer. At Target, that means using an MC with
       <a href="http://coupons.target.com/mcp/pd.cfm?encp=NIoavRH10ie%2Fqf0282S5Esdtrr8thsUJlTz0hMXiacKmhZOPP8oWqZkvwtR3w78PAvktHwFHeCd1%0D%0ACHkDVsSbuYfIYHF3e7mgDapvS%2B01GdMJ7S6YzBBH9PszYKvLfQeIAlsvL%2FP%2Flppt0PAEO3hUUdeX%0D%0AtUnE1tU8jkvoEV%2FhtP74fC80vsKJSZ6ohx7IhtH2mQu6A465Ie6nUvlw84Tz9frtjSirIUgD53WI%0D%0AUHXfTN%2FaGjx92VXREC3eakUC3rTu5O%2BCPfek5YpZ2gcO%2Fy6oqbOOwiX3lGNZ5VuGL1%2Fn0XvOHbts%0D%0A5%2F9iX78Ob%2F9063kpyBNh0zs0cpwpOvoL17N%2B3cBsMQ0RIKZxy5JvpPOXquYRdSmcvdtHdJ5D%2F93%2F%0D%0AO1x6bjND%2Bh9lREomT6Jx6j4CAy%2BmSqGg0Py9o%2BmBXh43h%2BwzCb5Gqbs%2F4he9JCWzDqzEessqDI7h%0D%0A5cVwBNSZ5%2BkHqjHsQALOMwUd9a2DT0TJpweni9T9igjiPrd%2BCmcSLjD8ARLNnDjw2MCvBwbDFvL6%0D%0ApQ%3D%3D">
        store coupons printed from the Web site
       </a>
       or arriving in your mailbox.
      </p>
      <p>
       5. You can order coupons from
       <a href="http://www.hotcouponworld.com/forums/view.php?pg=coupon_clipping_sites">
        clipping services
       </a>
       in order to stock up at a great sale. These services deliver quickly, so if you check Frugalista's
       <a href="http://www.chicagonow.com/blogs/frugalista/2010/07/dominicks-deals-and-coupon-matchups---week-of-july-8.html">
        write-up of the weekly ads
       </a>
       on Wednesday, you have plenty of time to order and receive coupons for use the same week. I often find my coupons
       <a href="http://shop.ebay.com/?_from=R40&amp;_trksid=p3907.m570.l1313&amp;_nkw=coupons&amp;_sacat=See-All-Categories">
        on Ebay
       </a>
       .
      </p>
