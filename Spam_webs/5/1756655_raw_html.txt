        <p>
         In 1949, Royal Enfield motorcycles entered India. The Government was keen to bring in a decent bike particularly for army and police in the countrys borders. They approved the entry of these vehicles to India. The success story of Royal Enfield in India could be told by seeing the steady growth the company has achieved in all these years and indeed has made a big difference in cruiser bike manufacturing in India.
        </p>
        <p>
         Royal Enfield India is opening up a production plant in Oragadam, Chennai due to increase in sales and would be investing Rs. 350 crores.
        </p>
        <p>
         The chrome version of the Classic 500 could be viewed at Royal Enfields webpage. Only a few parts were chrome finished in the earlier Classic 500s. Looking at the bike now on the webpage, it gives an impression that major components have been chrome finished by the company notably the air box cover, front and rear fender and the tank.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Royal Enfield Chrome Classic 500" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2778" height="340" src="http://bikes.indiandrives.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/2011-Royal-Enfield-UCE500-Classic-Chrome-1.jpg" title="Royal Enfield Chrome Classic 500" width="630"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The new version still has the same 499 cc UCE engine that generates a power of 28 PS and a torque of 41.3 Nm. Apart from the easily distinguishable feature of chrome-finished major components, the exhaust seems to be a bit longer and straight. It is sad, however, that we still have to wait for this model in India.
        </p>
