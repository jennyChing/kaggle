         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="post-date">
           <a href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/2015/02/19/melanies-food-adventures-zebra-cake/" rel="bookmark" title="10:00 am">
            <time class="entry-date" datetime="2015-02-19T10:00:39+00:00">
             19th February 2015
            </time>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="byline">
           <span class="author vcard">
            <a class="url fn n" href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/author/wheelie38/" rel="author" title="View all posts by wheelie38">
             wheelie38
            </a>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          Drum roll please.. I would love to welcome my first guest blogger, its my new found friend and fellow Manchester United fan Melanie from
          <a href="http://melaniesfoodadventures.com/" target="_blank" title="Melanies Food Adventures">
           Melanies Food Adventures
          </a>
          . Heres her first post, enjoy!
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1466" style="width: 410px">
          <a href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image4.jpeg">
           <img alt="Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake - Cooling In The Cake tin" class="wp-image-1466" data-wp-pid="1466" height="408" src="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image4-294x300.jpeg" width="400"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake  Cooling In The Cake tin
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Thank you so much to Richard for asking me to be his first guest blogger, Im really excited to be writing for his page!
         </p>
         <p>
          My name is Melanie, and I write a food blog here in the North East of Scotland. I enjoy baking and cooking savoury dishes for my wee family and love trying out new, but not complicated, dishes. I try to find a balance of something tasty that doesnt require oodles of ingredients and that looks the business! So with that in mind.. heres my Zebra Cake.. I hope you enjoy!
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          You looking for a cake that makes a statement but involves minimal effort? Well, youve come to the right place that was lucky, wasnt it? 
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_1459" style="width: 310px">
          <a href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image5.jpeg">
           <img alt="Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake - On The Cooling Rack Cooling" class="wp-image-1459 size-medium" data-wp-pid="1459" height="202" src="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image5-300x202.jpeg" width="300"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake  On The Cooling Rack Cooling
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          I saw a video recently of someone making this cake and fell in love with it. I love the process of making it, Mr Food Adventurer just laughed when I showed him the video, saying it was too much work, but to someone who lovesthe wholebaking process, I couldnt wait to try it out. It was then one of my lovely
          <a href="http://www.facebook.com/melaniesfoodadventures">
           Facebook
          </a>
          readers pointed out that
          <a href="http://www.lorrainepascale.com/">
           Lorraine Pascal
          </a>
          had alsomade one (and upon investigation it was actually within one of her books that I had only recently bought and not noticed!) that sealed the deal to bake one! I love the fact that it looks way more complicated than it actually is, Im always onthe look out for these types of bakes  The resulting cake is gorgeously light, with the flavours of chocolate, orange and vanilla combining perfectly, with no one flavour overpowering the others.
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          <strong>
           INGREDIENTS (makes 1 cake that can be divided up into as many slices as you wish  or how generous you feel!)
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           250ml Sunflower Oil (plus extra for greasing)
          </li>
          <li>
           250g Caster Sugar
          </li>
          <li>
           100ml Semi-Skimmed Milk
          </li>
          <li>
           4 Medium Eggs (at room temperature)
          </li>
          <li>
           A few drops of Vanilla Extract
          </li>
          <li>
           300g Self Raising Flour
          </li>
          <li>
           1tsp Baking Powder
          </li>
          <li>
           25g Cocoa Powder
          </li>
          <li>
           1 LargeOrange
          </li>
         </ul>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          <strong>
           METHOD
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Preheat your oven to 180
           <sup>
            0
           </sup>
           C, 160
           <sup>
            0
           </sup>
           Fan, 350
           <sup>
            0
           </sup>
           F, Gas Mark 4. Grease the bottom of a 9 springform sandwich tin with a little oil, line with baking parchment and oil again, including up the sides of the tinbefore placing on a large baking sheet.
          </li>
          <li>
           Place 250ml Oil, 250g Caster Sugar, 100ml Milk, 4 medium Eggs and a few drops of Vanilla Extract into a large bowl and beat until well combined. If you can, use a food mixer as opposed to an electric whisk as the aim is not to add too much air and bubbles into the mixture.
          </li>
          <li>
           Pour outhalf the mixtureinto a measuring jug and sift in175g Self Raising Flour and 1/2tsp Baking Powder. Mix welland set aside.
          </li>
          <li>
           In the bowl with the remaining mixture, sift theremaining 125g Self Raising Flour, 1/2tsp Baking Powder and 25g Cocoa. Finally, using a fine grater, zest your Orange into the bowl and mix well.
          </li>
          <li>
           You now haveyour vanilla and chocolate mixtures heres where the fun begins!Put a tablespoons worth of the vanilla mixture into the middle of your tin. Then spoon a tablespoon of chocolate mixture into the centre of the vanilla one. Keep alternating between the two mixtures until youve used up both, which willform a target board effect in your tin. As you put in each spoonful, the mixture will slowly move further out to the edge of the tin.
           <p>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1463" style="width: 310px">
            <a href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Layering-Process.jpg">
             <img alt="Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake - Different Stages Of The Layering Process" class="size-medium wp-image-1463" data-wp-pid="1463" height="225" src="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Layering-Process-300x225.jpg" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake  Different Stages Of The Layering Process
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li>
           Place your tin into the oven for 35 minutes, or until a skewer comes out clean. Once done, remove from the oven and allow to cool in the tin for a few minutes. Now carefully remove from the tin and transfer to a cooling rack until completely cool.
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1457" style="width: 310px">
            <a href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image1.jpeg">
             <img alt="Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake - On The Cooling, A Top Down View Showing The Layers" class="size-medium wp-image-1457" data-wp-pid="1457" height="257" src="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image1-300x257.jpeg" width="300"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake  On The Cooling, A Top Down View Showing The Layers
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1462" style="width: 460px">
            <a href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image9.jpeg">
             <img alt="Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake - Showing The Stripes" class="wp-image-1462" data-wp-pid="1462" height="285" src="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/image9-300x190.jpeg" width="450"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Melanies Food Adventures  Zebra Cake  Showing The Stripes
            </p>
           </div>
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Melanie, Food Adventurer
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
          In true Wheelie Good Meal style heres a
          <a href="http://wheeliegoodmeals.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Zebra-Cake.pdf" target="_blank">
           downloadable pdf
          </a>
          you can print for when you make it. I know for a fact that this one wont last long especially if you have little ones running about your house.
          <strong>
           Thanks Melanie for a cracking recipe (its nice to know I have friends who can make up for my lack of expertise in the baking department)
          </strong>
