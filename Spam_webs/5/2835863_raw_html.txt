          <p>
           <strong>
            <span style="font-size:22px;">
             The top pros and cons of using granite countertops and marble countertops
            </span>
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div style="margin:0px 0px 10px 0px">
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            When you are remodeling a kitchen or designing it from scratch, it is important to pay attention to the kinds of materials you would be needed for the flooring, walls, countertops and even the backsplash. Kitchen countertops play one of the main roles in deciding the functionality and look of the kitchen and hence they must be chosen carefully. Granite and marble are the most popular choices for kitchen countertop materials and both have their benefits as well as disadvantages. You can go through the following given pros and cons of both these materials to take your decision wisely:
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            <strong>
             Granite kitchen countertops installation
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            <strong>
             Pros-
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Granite is available in a large number of colors, patterns and designs and this is one of the best reasons to choose it for kitchen countertop installation or replacement.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             <a href="http://www.merrifieldgraniteandmarble.com/backsplash-with-granite-countertops.php" target="_blank">
              <span style="color:#0000FF;">
               Backsplash with granite countertops
              </span>
             </a>
             work as a wonderful combination and this is another reason for choosing granite as a countertop material.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Granite stands very well up to heat, wear and tear of many kinds and even knife scratches.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            <strong>
             Cons-
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Granite has to be sealed quite often in order to avoid stains and this is a negative point associated with it.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Granite is very heavy and this means that you will have to have very strong cabinet boxes to be able to stand its weight. This is surely a down side of using granite as a countertop material.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            <strong>
             Marble kitchen countertop installation:
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            <strong>
             Pros-
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Installing marble in the kitchen means adding sheer elegance to your cooking space. It looks beautiful and goes well with any kind of kitchen interiors. This is one of the positive points associated with getting marble installed in the
             <a href="http://www.merrifieldgraniteandmarble.com/remodeling-kitchen-countertops.php" target="_blank">
              <span style="color:#0000FF;">
               kitchen or getting countertop reinstallation
              </span>
             </a>
             done.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Marble stands very well up to heat and the reason behind this is the fact that it always remains cool. This is another good enough reason to get it installed in your kitchen space.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            <strong>
             Cons
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <ol>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Even with sealing, marble can prove very susceptible to stains and this can be considered as one of its down sides.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size:18px;">
             Granite can easily get cracked or may chip from the edges. This reason may demoralize many not to choose it in their kitchen as a countertop material.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           <span style="font-size:18px;">
            There are many other materials that can be chosen by your for your countertop and some of them include solid surfacing, quartz, tiles, laminate, soapstone, stainless steel, paper composite, butcher block and others. So before making your choice while remodeling your kitchen, consider all the points that can help you choose the right material. Out of granite and marble, the choice can depend on the kind of look and feel you want your countertop to have and also on the overall cost and maintenance work. There are many kitchen remodeling companies in your city or town that can help you with making this choice and may also offer a huge plethora of designs and patterns to choose from. You can log on to

              Merrifield Granite and Marble.com
             </span>
            </a>
            and get a countertop installed now!
           </span>
          </p>
