            <h1 class="page-title">
             Nine Facts You Didnt Know About La Rosiere
            </h1>
            <div class="page-meta-wrapper">
             <span>
              By
              <span class="author">
               <a href="http://welove2ski.com/author/felice/" rel="author" title="Posts by Felice Hardy">
                Felice Hardy
               </a>
              </span>
              <span class="meta-sep">
               |
              </span>
              on January 15, 2015
              <span class="meta-sep">
               |
              </span>
              <a class="link-comments" href="http://welove2ski.com/where-to-ski/la-rosiere-facts#comments">
               0 Comment
              </a>
             </span>
             <div class="categories">
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/where-to-ski" rel="category tag">
               Where To Ski
              </a>
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="page-content content">
             <div class="page-featured-image">
             </div>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27445" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27445" height="482" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/long-run-PH.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo: Welove2ski.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="la-rosiere">
               La Rosiere
              </a>
              sits above Bourg St Maurice at 1850m, with the skiing going up to 2579m. It is one of Frances great but unsung resorts. Skiing has taken place here on the high Petit St Bernard pass since 1961 and it shares its area with
              <a href="/la-thuile">
               La Thuile
              </a>
              in the Italian Aosta Valley. In recent years the lift system has been substantially updated and new apartment and chalets have greatly increased its attraction as a family destination.
             </p>
             <h3>
              The Pistes are Long
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption align left" id="attachment_27443" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27443" height="429" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/la-rosiere-OT-PPG-luighi-rottier-2013-6209.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo: Luigi Rottier/ OT La Rosiere.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The 160km ski area is mainly intermediate and some of the pistes are very long indeed, with a continuous vertical drop of 1207 thigh-burning vertical metres on La Rosiere side, taking you all the way down to the hamlet of Les Eucherts. Theres a snowpark for freestylers, too. La Rosiere has tons of blue and red runs of varying gradients on a wide stretch of open mountainsides served by a series of mainly modern chairs. The long red
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/best-skiing-in-france" title="Revealed: The 10 Ski Runs That Deliver the Best Skiing in France">
               Fontaine Froide run
              </a>
              down to Ecudets is a favourite.
             </p>
             <h3>
              You Ski in Two Countries
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27423" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27423" height="429" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/la_rosiere_border.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT La Rosiere.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The frontier area around the pass is very exposed, but its easy to whisk across the border  wind permitting. Over on the other side, the linked Italian resort of
              <a href="/la-thuile">
               La Thuile
              </a>
              in the Aosta Valley has some much more demanding pistes as well as snow-sure, north-facing skiing. The area lift pass covers both La Rosiere and La Thuile, giving 160km of piste and conditions are seldom the same on either side. So if theres a white-out in La Rosiere it could be a sunny day in La Thuile  or vice-versa.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Theres Some Excellent Heli-Skiing
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27425" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27425" height="427" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/rosiereoffpisteski@philippe-royer1.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  Philippe Royer/ OT La Rosiere.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              La Rosiere is one of the only French resorts to offer heli-skiing and thats because its linked with La Thuile in Italy. There is superb
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/heli-skiing" title="Where to Go Heli-Skiing">
               heli-skiing
              </a>
              up at 3260m on the Ruitor Glacier and from a few other designated landing zones on the Italian side of the frontier  although actual drops are not allowed in France.
              <a href="http://www.evolution2larosiere.com/heliski" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.evolution2larosiere.com/heliski', 'Evolution 2');">
               Evolution 2
              </a>
              offers heli-ski trips including up to ten different drop sites, and 2000m of vertical. They also offer heli-skiing at Le Mont Freduaz to the east of the Ruitor Glacier, with a 90-minute descent amid lovely scenery and 1700m downhill.
             </p>
             <h3>
              Some Very Comfortable Places to Stay
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27429" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27429" height="482" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/Lodge-Hemera-reception.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Lodge Hemera.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              The resort originated in 1961, but much of the accommodation has been recently built. Most of it is spread along the edge of the pistes, or set slightly back. Le Lodge Hemera is just a few years old and is the type of bigger, more substantial apartments people who have skied in North America hope to find in the Alps. Le Lodge Hemera is situated in the village centre, a three-minute walk from the lifts. It has an indoor swimming-pool and a luxurious spa: the O de Cimes, with hot-tub, sauna, steam room and a range of massage treatments on offer.
             </p>
             <h3>
              You Wont Find Drunken Youths Here
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27431" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27431" height="345" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/LaRosierevillage.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT La Rosiere.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              La Rosiere hasnt been taken over by the mass market, wealthy Eastern Europeans or large groups of drunken youths. Since it was built in the 1960s  mainly in chalet-style using local wood and stone  its remained a small and pleasant family-friendly destination where people come to ski rather than shop or engage in pub crawls. Dont come here if youre looking for a wild nightlife, although there are a few bars to try  some of which hold music gigs, theme nights and DJ evenings. The hamlet of Les Eucherts, 15 minutes walk from La Rosiere, is even quieter and ideal for families with small children.
             </p>
             <h3>
              and its a Great Place for Families
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27430" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27430" height="428" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/LaRosiereOT.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  OT La Rosiere.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              La Rosiere is a
              <em>
               Famille Plus Montagne
              </em>
              destination, a national quality benchmark which pledges to provide a special welcome for families, and Les Eucherts is a hamlet that has grown to become a separate part of La Rosiere  specifically for families with small children. The nursery slope is just outside the door and the ESF and Evolution 2 both offer childcare and classes for kids here.
             </p>
             <p>
              New for 2015 are Le Petit Boarder snowboard lessons for children aged between four and seven years old who want to have a go at boarding. It is based at the childrens snow gardens in La Rosiere Centre and in Les Eucherts, with small groups of between three and five children with specially-trained instructors.
             </p>
             <h3>
              St Bernard Dogs are Bred Here
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27419" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="La Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27419" height="422" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/stbernard.png" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  Peak Retreats.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Yes, they are. The giant cuddly dogs with brandy barrels around their necks are credited with saving the lives of at least 2,000 winter travelers lost in these mountains in the 18th and 19th centuries. The rescue dogs belonged to monks at hospices mainly on the Grand  and the Petit St Bernard Passes. We hate to ruin a good story, but sadly, the brandy barrel is a myth.
             </p>
             <p>
              In the 1960s, French St Bernard breeder Jean Arpin established kennels at the Relais du Petit St Bernard in La Rosiere and the dogs are a familiar sight in the village.
             </p>
             <h3>
              The Mountain Restaurants are Fabulous
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27444" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27444" height="428" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/Antigel-Chemine-plats.jpg" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Antigel opened in 2013.
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              French cuisine is good, but
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/la-thuile" title="La Thuile, Italy">
               Italian mountain fare
              </a>
              is considerably better-priced on the La Thuile side of the mountain  and
              <a href="http://welove2ski.com/la-thuile-mountain-restaurant" title="La Thuiles Friendliest Mountain Restaurant">
               some of the restaurants are really memorable
              </a>
              . La Grotta (a converted cowshed serving classic Italian food) is reached by courtesy minibus from the foot of the pistes.
              <a href="http://www.loriondet.it/homepage.asp?l=1" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.loriondet.it/homepage.asp?l=1', 'Lo Riondet');">
               Lo Riondet
              </a>
              , up on the summer road over the pass, provides a wholesome lunch beside an open fire in authentic chalet-style surroundings.
              <a href="http://www.lantigel.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lantigel.com', 'LAntigel');">
               LAntigel
              </a>
              was new for 2013 and  is in a wood and stone chalet with an open fireplace.
             </p>
             <p>
              If you do eat on the French side, head for either LAncolie (+33 479 06 86 71) in the hamlet of Les Eucherts beside La Rosiere, or to the giant sunny terrace of
              <a href="http://www.petit-saint-bernard.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.petit-saint-bernard.com', 'Le Relais du Petit-St-Bernard');">
               Le Relais du Petit-St-Bernard
              </a>
              , the main inn of La Rosiere situated at the foot of the pistes.
             </p>
             <h3>
              and Elephants are Never Forgotten
             </h3>
             <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27432" style="width: 652px">
              <img alt="Rosiere Reasons | Welove2ski" class="size-full wp-image-27432" height="248" src="http://cdn.welove2ski.com/wp-content/uploads/hannibalalps.png" width="642"/>
              <p class="wp-caption-text">
               Photo:  Magnoliabox.com
              </p>
             </div>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Hannibal may well have passed through La Rosiere in 247BC while crossing the Alps en route to the gates of Rome. No one knows  and surely never will  but the Petit St Bernard pass is on the shortlist of routes that he might have taken.
             </p>
             <p>
              The two detachable six-pack chairs leading up towards the pass have a combined capacity of 5,400 passengers an hour and would have no problem in ferrying his 26,000 men to the Italian frontier in under five hours, but 38 war elephants might be quite another matter.
             </p>
             <p>
              The ski area is dominated by a ruined border fort. It was originally built by the French in the early 1890s and the scene of fierce fighting during the Battle of France in June 1940.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Fact Box
              </strong>
              <br/>
              Seven nights ski-drive including Eurotunnel with free Flexiplus upgrade costs from 270pp based on five people sharing an apartment in
              <a href="http://www.skicollection.co.uk/La-Rosiere/Ski-Apartments/Le-Lodge-Hemera.htm" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.skicollection.co.uk/La-Rosiere/Ski-Apartments/Le-Lodge-Hemera.htm', 'Le Lodge Hemera');">
               Le Lodge Hemera
              </a>
              , which is the resorts newest and best accommodation. Larger groups can try
              <a href="http://www.skicollection.co.uk/La-Rosiere/Ski-Apartments/Le-Refuge.htm" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.skicollection.co.uk/La-Rosiere/Ski-Apartments/Le-Refuge.htm', 'four-star Le Refuge');">
               four-star Le Refuge
              </a>
              , which has apartments sleeping eight to 14 people. Also check out
              <a href="http://www.peakretreats.co.uk/ski/la-rosiere/mgm-apartments/cimes-blanches.htm" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.peakretreats.co.uk/ski/la-rosiere/mgm-apartments/cimes-blanches.htm', 'Les Cimes Blanches');">
               Les Cimes Blanches
              </a>
              and
              <a href="http://www.peakretreats.co.uk/ski/la-rosiere/apartments/les-balcons.htm" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.peakretreats.co.uk/ski/la-rosiere/apartments/les-balcons.htm', 'Les Balcons');">
               Les Balcons
              </a>
              apartments here.
             </p>
