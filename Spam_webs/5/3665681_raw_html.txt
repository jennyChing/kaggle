           <a href="http://www.jamestnoble.com/strategy-free-advertising-and-customers-forever/" rel="bookmark">
            [Strategy] Free Advertising (and Customers) Forever
           </a>
          </h2>
          <aside class="meta">
           Feb 12, 2013
           <i class="icon-dot">
           </i>
           Posted By
           <a href="http://www.jamestnoble.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by James T Noble">
            James T Noble
           </a>
           <i class="icon-dot">

         <div class="post-content">
          <p>
           Hi there  this one is a long one but worth it so read on and let me know what you think!
          </p>
          <p>
           This is a promotion strategy I work on a LOT with my
           <a href="http://www.jamestnoble.com/private-clients/" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             private consulting clients
            </strong>
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Its a fast way to expand your prospect and customer base with very little risk  plus you get:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            Most of, all of, or MORE than your advertising costs back into your business  often before youve even paid for the ads;
           </li>
           <li>
            An almost limitless way to expand your customer list at little or no cost;
           </li>
           <li>
            A fast way to identify your best prospects.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p>
           plus it works for almost any type of business.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Heres how you do it.
           <span id="more-2652">
           </span>
          </h3>
          <ol>
           <li>
            Run a Google ad, Facebook ad or any other kind of ad that brings people to your website, where they can sign up for some free no-pitch information about your industry.
           </li>
           <li>
            Create a low-cost offer (but not free, obviously) you can make to all your incoming lead sign-ups.
           </li>
           <li>
            Clearly present this offer immediately after the visitor signs up for your information. Also send the the offer via email immediately and again 24-48 hours later to people who havent bought.
           </li>
           <li>
            The revenue from this low-cost sale repays the advertising costs you outlaid in step 1. You can often get ALL of your advertising costs covered, and sometimes even more  particularly with a little optimization of your ads and product price.
           </li>
           <li>
            Rinse and repeat to get more leads and customers from other ads.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <p>
           Not only does this give you an almost limitless ability to expand your customer base for little to no cost it also demonstrates who your best prospects are. The ones who
           <em>
            DO
           </em>
           open their wallets for the little purchase are
           <em>
            MUCH
           </em>
           more likely to buy your higher priced goods and services.
           <em>
            THESE
           </em>
           are the ones you should spend your time getting to know.
          </p>
          <p>
           Where a lot of businesses go wrong with this strategy is to assume this wont work for them because they sell high end products or services that have a longer sales process (e.g. lawyer, designer, contractor, etc.)
          </p>
          <p>
           We dont have anything low-cost that people will be happy to purchase without getting to know us they say!
          </p>
          <p>
           Fear not I have you guys covered too!
          </p>
          <h3>
           If You Offer High-End Services ONLY
          </h3>
          <p>
           Create a NEW low-end entry offer. The ones that have worked best for us and clients are evaluation/check-up offers. For example: Well evaluate your social media presence, provide specific recommendations and give you 20 topic ideas for future posts: $49.
          </p>
          <p>
           Buyers of this service are HOT prospects for social media consulting and done-for-them services.
          </p>
          <h3>
           If You Sell Information/Training/etc.
          </h3>
          <p>
           Take a small piece of your material that provides a specific (but wide appeal) outcome. For example, Learn how to install, optimize AND protect your new WordPress site: $12.
          </p>
          <p>
           Buyers of this guide are HOT prospects for design, SEO and marketing information and services.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thats it!
          </p>
          <p>
           Put this in place and youll have a constant stream of new opt-ins, prospects and BUYERS with little or zero cost to your business.
          </p>
          <p>
           For ideas and advice on the most effective entry sale item for
           <em>
            <strong>
             YOUR

           </em>
           specific business, how to optimize your promotion, where and how to advertise,
           <a href="http://www.jamestnoble.com/private-clients/" target="_blank">
            <strong>
             go here
            </strong>
            
           </a>
          </p>
