A quick and easy lunch, or dinner." data-pin-id="212724782375242153" data-pin-media="http://i1059.photobucket.com/albums/t422/AyeJayKay/From%20LandP%20to%20English%20Tea/Pinable%20Pics/silver%20beat%20mushroom%20asparagus%20stir%20fry%20Recipe._zpsvnwwqakg.png~original" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-a70ALRWzJXo/VK9hM5iSSGI/AAAAAAAAYn4/mn-fqfe8dg4/s1600/silver%2Bbeat%2Bmushroom%2Basparagus.JPG"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Lunch is my least favourite meal of the day. Mostly because I hate sandwiches. 13 years of school sandwiches made me sick of them. At work it's usually fine because I have lunch with the students of the college that my work is associated with, and that's something different everyday, as well as a normal spread of amazing salad. Perfect! But when it's not term time, lunch becomes the bane of my existence. If I'm at home, then often I just won't have lunch because that's how much I don't want to make anything.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     However, the other week I made this for lunch and it was delicious. I know it doesn't look like much, but yum! We have an infestation of silver beat in our garden at the moment, which would be terrible and traumatic if I hated silver beat. But I like it, so thumbs up, garden. Thumbs up. On this particular day we also happened to have some mushrooms and a few shoots of asparagus, so I made this stir-fry thing (for want of a better name). If you want to make this at your place, here's what you do:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      You'll need:
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Silver beat (or spinach would be fine too)
                     <br/>
                     Mushrooms
                     <br/>
                     Asparagus
                     <br/>
                     Olive oil
                     <br/>
                     Salt and pepper
                     <br/>
                     Chilli flakes
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ODnVVyxT5yY/VK9hMnEgjGI/AAAAAAAAYn8/cyPaMZKm09Y/s1600/silver%2Bbeat%2Bmushroom%2Basparagus%2B3.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Recipe :: Silver beat, mushroom and asparagus stir-fry..." border="0" data-pin-description="Recipe :: Silver beat, mushroom and asparagus stir-fry. A quick and easy lunch, or dinner." data-pin-id="212724782375242153" data-pin-media="http://i1059.photobucket.com/albums/t422/AyeJayKay/From%20LandP%20to%20English%20Tea/Pinable%20Pics/silver%20beat%20mushroom%20asparagus%20stir%20fry%20Recipe._zpsvnwwqakg.png~original" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ODnVVyxT5yY/VK9hMnEgjGI/AAAAAAAAYn8/cyPaMZKm09Y/s1600/silver%2Bbeat%2Bmushroom%2Basparagus%2B3.JPG" title="Recipe :: Silver beat, mushroom and asparagus stir-fry..."/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Chop your silver beat into decent chucks, as well as your mushrooms (I used white button mushrooms), and your asparagus. In a flat pan (or wok), put in a dash of olive oil and get that nice and hot. Throw in your mushrooms, adding a bit of crushed garlic, salt and pepper, and chilli flakes. When they start browning up and getting softer, pop in your asparagus pieces. Fry for a few minutes. Lastly, add the silver beat, as it needs the least amount of time. Fry fry fry.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     And that's basically it! So super simple, with very little ingredients, but turns out it's really yummy! Serve on a plate or in a bowl, add some salt to taste, and you're done! Eat! Great...this is making me hungry...
