             <img alt="Change Your Snacks, Change Your Body" src="http://www.yummymummyclub.ca/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/maple-leaf-protein-snacks-ymc_1.jpg?itok=-9p8s-SB" typeof="foaf:Image"/>

          <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
           <div class="field-items">
            <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
             <p>
              We all know snacks can make or break a healthy eating routine, so you wouldnt be caught dead eating a chocolate bar for an afternoon snack. Well, what about that healthy granola bar you toss in your gym bag? Or your mid-morning coffee break bagel and cream cheese habit? Does it leave you feeling satisfied? Does it help you make better eating decisions later in the day? Does it help you achieve that lean, toned, and healthy physique youre looking for?
             </p>
             <p>
              No. No, it doesnt. No matter how much you love it, or how many commercials you see with smiling, slim and smug women eating breakfast bars and low-fat crackers, that high-carbohydrate snack does not get you closer to your fitness goals. But we know what does, because its been researched and proven time and time again. When it comes to fullness, and reducing cravings, nothing beats protein. Not complex carbohydrates. Not fat. Not drinking more water. It's protein. Plain and simple.
             </p>
             <h2>
              WHY IS PROTEIN IMPORTANT?
             </h2>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               IT SUSTAINS YOU
              </strong>
              . Your body needs protein to make enzymes, hormones, and other vital chemicals.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="line-height: 1.538em; width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               IT STRENGTHENS YOU
              </strong>
              . Your body uses protein to build and repair tissue, and it speeds recovery after exercise.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="line-height: 1.538em; width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               IT CULTIVATES YOU
              </strong>
              . Protein is an important building block of bones, lean muscles, cartilage, skin and blood.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="line-height: 1.538em; width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               IT ENCOURAGES YOU
              </strong>
              . Protein helps to curb hunger, helping you maintain a healthy weight.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="line-height: 1.538em; width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               IT ENCOMPASSES YOU
              </strong>
              . Protein is a component of every cell in your body. In fact, your hair and nails are made of nearly 100% of it!
             </p>
             <h2>
              Want to reduce nighttime snacking?
             </h2>
             <p>
              Studies show eating more protein early in the day, at breakfast and lunch, as well as when snacking, reduces the urge to snack later in the day.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Want to slim down?
             </h2>
             <p>
              Research shows fat loss is faster with higher protein intake, and that a protein-rich diet, combined with a slightly reduced calorie intake, allows you to burn more fat.
             </p>
             <h2>
              Want to look healthy and toned?
             </h2>
             <p>
              Research proves that a higher protein intake helps you hang on to that youthful lean muscle mass as you age, and burn fat when you exercise instead of that all important calorie-burning, grocery-carrying, healthy-looking muscle.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Shoot for 0.5-1.0 grams of protein per day, per pound of bodyweight for optimal body composition and health.
              </strong>
              For a woman of 150 pounds, this means a daily protein intake of 75-150g.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               How can you achieve all of this? It means not just protein-rich meals, but smarter snacking:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
               Choose eggs and/or lean meats as part of your breakfast.
              </strong>
              Instead of toast and peanut butter, try a half English muffin with natural ham and scrambled eggs for a homemade breakfast sandwich. Or make a breakfast burrito with natural sausage or pepperoni, scrambled egg, avocado and salsa wrapped in a tortilla.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               Prepare lean meats on the weekend to pack for your at-work lunches
              </strong>
              or grab on hectic weekdays at home with the littles, and prepare a little extra meat at night so you have protein-rich leftovers for lunch the next day. Think outside the box; a leftover burger tastes delicious crumbled over a big, crunchy salad with oil and vinegar, and doesnt even need to be reheated. Chicken can be mixed with a little mayo, yogurt, and green onion to make a light chicken salad, then wrapped in lettuce leaves.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u39/natural-breakfast-sausage-greens.jpg" style="height: 400px; width: 400px; margin: 3px; border-width: 1px; border-style: solid; "/>
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               Stock your fridge with quick-cooking protein sources
              </strong>
              for hectic weeknight meals; natural sausages,
              <a href="http://www.mapleleafprime.ca/en/Products/Frozenselections/Pages/default.aspx" target="_blank">
               lean beef, chicken or turkey burgers
              </a>
              will hold up in your freezer, and thaw and cook as quickly as you can bake a sweet potato and toss a salad on the side. Frozen chicken breasts and chicken strips can be quickly thawed and sauted for a high-protein stir fry that cooks in minutes.
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u714/lips_icon.gif" style="width: 15px; height: 16px;"/>
              <strong>
               Build your snacks around complete protein sources.
              </strong>
              Try
              <a href="http://www.schneiders.ca/en/Products/CountryNaturals/Pages/default.aspx" target="_blank">
               natural ham or turkey
              </a>
              wrapped around shredded lettuce and/or sliced peppers, with mustard for zingy flavour. Skip the crackers and try a natural pepperoni stick with a piece of cheese for a snack that tastes like a treat but packs as much protein as 2 eggs. On the go? Try protein-rich packaged snacks like
              <a href="http://www.protinis.ca/en/" target="_blank">
               Protinis natural chicken or chicken-and-fruit combinations
              </a>
              .
             </p>
             <p>
              <img alt="" src="/sites/default/files/u39/natural-ham-sprouts.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: 400px; margin: 3px; "/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Once youve got the protein piece of this puzzle down, youll start to see changes in the way your body feels, performs and looks. But all the protein in the world cant make your muscles firmer and stronger if youre not using them regularly!
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Put that protein to good use and build some sexy, lean muscle by incorporating strength training into your weekly routine.
              </strong>
              Aim for a full-body strength training workout 2-3 times per week. On days you cant make it to the gym or squeeze in your usual home workout.
             </p>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Here are some creative ways to sneak in muscle-building, fat-burning exercise:
              </strong>
             </p>
             <ul>
              <li>
               Try a wall sit while you brush your teeth. See if you can hold a squat position against the wall while you brush, popping up only to spit and rinse!
              </li>
              <li>
               Do 10 push ups on the 3
               <sup>
                rd
               </sup>
               or 4
               <sup>
                th
               </sup>
               step of your stair case before heading upstairs each time. As they get easier, move down a stair until you are on the 1
               <sup>
                st
               </sup>
               step.
              </li>
              <li>
               Set a timer and challenge your little one to a plank contest. See how your time improves every time you play.
              </li>
              <li>
               Press your little one overhead in the air and have her count the presses  youll train your upper body and her counting skills!
              </li>
              <li>
               Try the 100 squat challenge  find 10 opportunities to do 10 squats over the course of an entire day.
              </li>
              <li>
               Choose one of your usual TV shows and devote the commercial breaks to mini-workouts; change your exercise on each commercial and cycle between squats, plank, jumping jacks, push ups and jogging on the spot.
              </li>
              <li>
               Keep a set of dumbbells in the hallway near the washroom. Every time you take a washroom break, do 10 squat presses: squat and tap the dumbbells on the floor, then curl them up to shoulder height, stand, and press them overhead.
              </li>
             </ul>
             <p>
              <strong>
               Choose foods that fuel and see how changing your snacks can change your body!
              </strong>
             </p>
