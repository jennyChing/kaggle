      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        <em>
         Married,
        </em>
        FXs latest comedy seriesstarring Nat Faxon and Judy Greer
        <em>
         ,
        </em>
        falls in line with a recent trend of a darker, more realistic sort of rom-com like the recently released
        <a href="http://www.nerdist.com/2014/04/trailer-for-obvious-child-with-jenny-slate-promises-a-true-to-life-rom-com/" target="_blank">
         <em>
          Obvious Child
         </em>
        </a>
        . Faxon and Greer play a married couple that has had three children and is, for their own sanity, absolutely not looking to have anotherone. Theyre also trying, as one would expect in this situation, to make the marriage more exciting.
       </p>
       <p>
        This may sound like a bit of a typical premise for a sitcom, but dont gloss over the wondrous pairing of Judy and Nat. In fact, itll be nice to see Faxon get to breathe more in
        <em>
         Married
        </em>
        , which definitely seems like its going for a more edgier audience than his last show, Foxs
        <em>
         Ben and Kate
        </em>
        . Greer is, of course, a delight to watch in the countless TV shows and movies shes been in. If youre anything like us, you probably still quote, Say goodbye to
        <em>
         these
        </em>
        ! as Kitty Sanchez on
        <em>
         Arrested Development
        </em>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Not many TV series get to announce that they have
        <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6RMEJpKIohc" target="_blank">
         a red band trailer
        </a>
        , but
        <em>
         Married
        </em>
        made sure theirs was one of the first trailers to be posted online, which should count in their favor leading up to the shows premiere next month. In the trailer, we also find some delightful guest appearances from
        <em>
         The Daily Show
        </em>
        sJohn Hodgman and Jenny Slate, who stars in the aforementioned
        <em>
         Obvious Child
        </em>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/6RMEJpKIohc" width="640">
        </iframe>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Married
        </em>
        is set to premiere on July 17th on FX.
       </p>
       <p>
        Are you excited for FXs
        <em>
         Married
        </em>
        and/or
        <em>
        </em>
        down on the whole institution of marriage after watching the trailer? Let us know in the comments below.
       </p>
