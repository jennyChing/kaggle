        <h1 class="entry-title single-title" itemprop="headline" rel="bookmark">
         Ultimate List of Online Public Relations Tools
        </h1>
        <p class="byline entry-meta vcard">
         posted by
         <span class="entry-author author" itemprop="author" itemptype="http://schema.org/Person" itemscope="">
          <a href="http://blog.raventools.com/author/guest-writer/" rel="author" title="Posts by Guest Writer">
           Guest Writer
          </a>
         </span>
         on
         <time class="updated entry-time" datetime="2011-01-11" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 11, 2011
         </time>
        </p>
       </header>
       <section class="entry-content cf post_typography" itemprop="articleBody">
        <div id="intro-parag">
        </div>
        <div id="post-feat-image">
         <img alt="ultimate-PR-tools" class="attachment-full wp-post-image" height="330" src="http://blog.raventools.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/recite-7121-1422220315-dcfwwc.png" width="660"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          UPDATED JULY 31,2014
         </strong>
         Online public relations is becoming a go-to strategy for Internet marketing as a whole. The power of the press builds a brand, enhances a companys reputation, drives traffic and accrues high-quality links. But to do it right, you need the right tools.
        </p>
        <h2>
         PR Heavyweights
        </h2>
        <p>
         These companies provide great overall service for press release submission, editorial calendars and brand monitoring. If you have the money, theyre worth it:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.cision.com/us/?utm_source=vocus.com&amp;utm_medium=referral&amp;utm_campaign=vocusredirect" target="_blank" title="Vocus">
           Vocus
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.prnewswire.com/" target="_blank" title="PRNewswire">
           PRNewswire
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.businesswire.com/portal/site/home/" target="_blank" title="Business Wire">
           Business Wire
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.marketwired.com/" target="_blank" title="Marketwire">
           Marketwired
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.cision.com/us/" target="_blank" title="Cision">
           Cision
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.prweb.com/" target="_blank" title="PRWeb">
           PRWeb
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         But there are a number of other services and tools out there that cost significantly less than the heavyweights and provide a great value. Here are 30 more tools that will make your online
         <abbr title="public relations">
          PR
         </abbr>
         skills cooler than everyone elses:
        </p>
        <h2>
         Press Release Submission and Placement
        </h2>
        <p>
         For the best exposure, I would go with one of the bigger submission services, such as PRWeb and PRNewswire. But if you have a tight budget and want to submit press releases as part of an integrated marketing campaign, choose one of these. Some charge less than $50.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.freepressindex.com/" target="_blank" title="Free Press Index">
           FreePressIndex
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.free-press-release.com/" target="_blank" title="Free Press Release">
           Free Press Release
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.prleap.com/" target="_blank" title="PR Leap">
           PR Leap
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.24-7pressrelease.com/" target="_blank" title="24-7 Press Release">
           24-7 Press Release
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.pr.com/" target="_blank" title="PR.com">
           PR.com
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.prlog.org" target="_blank" title="PR Log">
           PRLog
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="https://www.send2press.com/" target="_blank" title="Send2Press">
           Send2Press
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.pressat.co.uk/" target="_blank" title="Pressat">
           Pressat
          </a>
          Press release distribution service focusing on the U.K.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.pitchengine.com/" target="_blank" title="PitchEngine">
           PitchEngine
          </a>
          is one of the better resources for the social media press release.
         </li>
         <li>
          Blog Friendly PR
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Editorial Contacts and Calendars
        </h2>
        <p>
         Editorial calendars offer the easiest way to get stories in mainstream media. When you know what an editor is planning to write about, you know what to pitch. Editorial contact services provide a way to find out what journalists are looking for when theyre looking for it and discover the right media members to build a relationship with.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.helpareporter.com/" target="_blank" title="Help A Reporter Out">
           HARO
          </a>
          . Most online marketers probably know about HARO. Peter Shankman has provided a great resource for both reporters and PR pros. Sign up for HARO (Help a Reporter Out) and youll get daily e-mail queries from reporters who need experts for specific stories. Its a free service, but be warned: you will get a lot of e-mails.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.cision.com/us/" target="_blank" title="Seek or Shout">
           Seek or Shout
          </a>
          . Cisions HARO-esque content collaboration community for journalists, bloggers and PR pros. Seek content, sources, research and support for stories or shout to tell the community about your story.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.newscertified.com/" target="_blank" title="News Certified">
           NewsCertified Exchange
          </a>
          . Founded by veteran journalists, NewsCertified provides the media with a searchable database of credible, interview-ready experts and story ideas.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://muckrack.com/" target="_blank" title="MuckRack">
           Muck Rack
          </a>
          . Muck Rack lists thousands of journalists on social media in geographic- and subject area-specific lists vetted by a team of editors. Muck Rack Pro is a sort of CRM specifically for media contacts: add journalists to live media lists and add private notes to track how youve interacted with them.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="https://www.mymediainfo.com/" target="_blank" title="My Media Info">
           MyMediainfo
          </a>
          . If Vocus is like a BMW, MyMediainfo is like the Honda Accord. Both get you to the same destination; one is a lot less expensive.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         PR Monitoring Tools
        </h2>
        <p>
         One negative comment, status or review can spread quickly and tarnish a brand. Monitoring negative and positive things said about a brand online is crucial to public relations today. Aside from
         <a href="https://www.google.com/alerts" target="_blank" title="Google Alerts">
          Google Alerts
         </a>
         , there are many other services that can keep you in the loop about your brands image online. For a giant list, visit this comprehensive
         <a href="http://wiki.kenburbary.com/" target="_blank" title="Wiki">
          wiki of social media monitoring solutions
         </a>
         . Here are five to check out:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://socialmention.com/" target="_blank" title="Social Mention">
           Social Mention
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="https://www.viralheat.com/" target="_blank" title="Viral Heat">
           Viral Heat
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="https://www.twilert.com/" target="_blank" title="Twilert">
           Twilert
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://topsy.com/" target="_blank" title="Topsy">
           Topsy
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.trackur.com/" target="_blank" title="Trackur">
           Trackur
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Writing Tools
        </h2>
        <p>
         As any PR person knows, writing is a big part of it. Sometimes after smashing our fingers on the keyboard and stretching each brain cell all day, we need inspiration and guidance. There are a lot of places to get that muse again, but here are a few thatll spark that brain again:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.aminstitute.com/headline/index.htm" target="_blank" title="Headline Analyzer">
           Headline Analyzer
          </a>
          helps you write better headlines by analyzing your current headline for emotion, influence and other factors.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.generatorland.com/glgenerator.aspx?id=122" target="_blank" title="Blog Post Idea Generator">
           Blog Post Idea Generator
          </a>
          and
          <a href="http://linkbaitgenerator.com/intro.php" target="_blank" title="Linkbait Generator">
           Linkbait Generator
          </a>
          will give you catchy, and sometimes crazy, blog post ideas. For instance, for this blog post it gave me titles like 7 reasons PR tools get better with alcohol and 8 reasons PR Tools are like Satan. You may never use one of these titles, but it will get you thinking.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://blog.myntpr.com/post/1347288499/100-pr-buzzwords" target="_blank" title="100 PR Buzzwords">
           100 PR Buzzwords
          </a>
          . This blog post from MyntPR is a comprehensive list of buzzwords that each PR person needs to strike from their memory. If your press release has any of these words, please edit it. If you dont, youll be giving all of us a bad name.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.dack.com/web/bullshit.html" target="_blank" title="Bullshit Generator">
           Bullshit Generator
          </a>
          is great for those rare times when you actually want to write like your CEO. Play with it a few times and youll be addicted.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://listen.grooveshark.com/" target="_blank" title="Grooveshark">
           Grooveshark
          </a>
          . Music is the ultimate inspiration. Grooveshark is great for creating a playlist around exactly the type of music you need for the occasion. Its selection of music seems endless. And, yes, this is a critical tool for any PR pro whos writing.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h2>
         Blogger Outreach Tools
        </h2>
        <p>
         Bloggers are rapidly becoming a new arm of press, so it makes sense that new tools are being created to help sift through the many blogs out there to find the one youre looking for. Start with
         <a href="https://www.google.com/blogsearch?gws_rd=ssl" target="_blank" title="Google Blog Search">
          Google Blog Search
         </a>
         and then move on to these three services.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.blogdash.com/" target="_blank" title="Blogdash">
           Blogdash
          </a>
          . Media database that allows bloggers choose to opportunities theyre open to being pitched about and brands to find the right bloggers for their projects or campaigns.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://guestr.com/" target="_blank" title="Guestr">
           Guestr
          </a>
          . Service that helps website owners find writers and quality content and writers find guest post opportunities.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.bloggerlinkup.com/" target="_blank" title="Blogger Linkup">
           Blogger Linkup
          </a>
          . Join this service to be added to an email list filled with bloggers looking for expert sources, requests for guest posts, guest posts on offer, and PR reps and others seeking reviews of products.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.blogsynergy.com/" target="_blank" title="BlogSynergy">
           BlogSynergy
          </a>
          . Social network for the blogging community  follow, connect and share guest blogging opportunities.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.grouphigh.com/" target="_blank" title="Group High">
           GroupHigh
          </a>
          . Software that helps public relations, SEOand social media professionals reach out to bloggers.
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://postjoint.com/" target="_blank" title="Post Joint">
           PostJoint
          </a>
          . Connects advertisers and bloggers to make content marketing more productive and targeted.
         </li>
         <li>
          Copy for Bylines. Bridging the gap between writers and online publishers, this site works with both to produce better content online.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         There are a lot of PR tools out there. Any I missed? Please comment to add to the list.
        </p>
