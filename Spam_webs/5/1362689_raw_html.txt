         <h2>
          Silence is Golden
         </h2>
         <p>
          We at Overblown are full sure that all our avid readers can recall the unrivalled awesomeness of Mick Jagger and David Bowies music video for their 1985 cover of Martha and the Vandellas classic Dancing in the Street. Comprising entirely of dodgy dancing, gruesome gurning and amazing costume choices, the video has thawed many a cold hearted viewers disposition. Of course, all of this epic weirdness was for a good cause, as its recording and release was in aid of the Live Aid charity movement which was organised by Bob Geldof and Midge Ure to raise funds for the relief of the ongoing Ethiopian famine. The song was a huge hit on both sides of the Atlantic, reaching number 4 in the UK and number 2 in the US.
         </p>
         <p>
          Well now, thanks to YouTuber
          <a href="https://www.youtube.com/user/digitalofen" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.youtube.com/user/digitalofen', 'Mario Weinerroither']);" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Mario Wienerroither YouTube">
           Mario Weinerroither
          </a>
          , there is now a version of the video for Dancing in the Street withthe original audio track removedand with added choice sound effects. Inexplicably, this makes the video even more hilarious which doesnt really seem possible. Click the link below to bear witness to the spectacle.
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="480" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/_li_d_YviZ4" width="853">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          Bowie released his first new album in ten years in 2013. Entitled
          <em>
           The Next Day,
          </em>
          it receivedmasscritical acclaim upon release and entered many charts around the world at number 1.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Rolling Stones last album,
          <em>
           The Bigger Bang
          </em>
          , was released in 2005. They will tour Australia and New Zealand in October and November 2014 to perform dates that have been rescheduled from earlier on in the year due to the untimely passing of Jaggers partner fashion designer LWren Scott.
         </p>
         <p>
          Check out the tour dates below:
         </p>
