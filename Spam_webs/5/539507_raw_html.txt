        <h2 class="postitle">
         Long Term Recovery Is Contingent On A Strong Foundation
        </h2>
        <div class="single_metainfo">
         On July 11, 2014 by ASAPRehab
        </div>
        <div class="zn_post_wrap">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.asaprehab.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Long-Term-Recovery-Is-Contingent-On-A-Strong-Foundation.jpg" rel="lightbox[588]">
           <img alt="Long Term Recovery Is Contingent On A Strong Foundation" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-590" height="397" src="http://www.asaprehab.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Long-Term-Recovery-Is-Contingent-On-A-Strong-Foundation.jpg" width="619"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Recovery from alcohol or drug abuse can be a life-long struggle, and typically requires continuous maintenance. The relapse rate for addiction is high, around
          <a href="http://www.drugabuse.gov/publications/addiction-science/relapse/relapse-rates-drug-addiction-are-similar-to-those-other-well-characterized-chronic-ill" target="_blank" title="Drugabuse.gov l Addiction Relapse Rate">
           40 to 60 percent
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Although the numbers are high, the relapse rate is similar to other chronic conditions requiring maintenance, such as diabetes and heart disease.
         </p>
         <p>
          It is possible to overcome addiction and not relapse; however, one must have a strong foundation that goes beyond just detoxifying from the addicting substance, such as that provided by intensive addiction treatment programs.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Emotional And Psychological Help
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Many people turn to drugs and alcohol to minimize and reduce powerful negative emotions and feelings, including those due to a mental health condition or trauma. In order to successfully recover from addiction, these underlying emotions and feelings must be addressed.
         </p>
         <p>
          A person must learn how to manage these strong emotions without turning to drugs and alcohol. After living life with numb emotions and feelings for so long, it can be even harder to face these emotions once again.
         </p>
         <p>
          Therefore, it is important to undergo some form of counseling and therapy when overcoming addiction, especially if there is an underlying or co-occurring condition, such as depression or anxiety.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Learning Healthy Stress Management
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Chronic stress causes numerous health problems, and increases a persons risk of developing an alcohol or drug addiction. Many people will drink or use drugs to reduce stress, which can quickly become a habit, leading to addiction.
         </p>
         <p>
          Learning healthy stress management tools can reduce the risk of relapse and facilitate a persons long-term recovery. Exercise, a healthy diet, yoga, meditation and other mindfulness activities help to reduce and manage stress.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           A Strong Support Network
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          One of the most important facets of a strong foundation for long-term recovery is a support network. This should include friends and family, as well as support groups such as Alcoholics Anonymous, Narcotics Anonymous, and SMART Recovery.
         </p>
         <p>
          A person should reduce the amount of time he or she spends with those who facilitated and enabled the addiction, especially friends or family members who are still using the addicting substance. Instead, one should surround him- or herself with friends who support the recovery, and provide a positive and loving environment.
         </p>
         <p>
          Engaging in a support group willhelp a person to talk with those who understand what he or she is going through and can offer advice and support. Support groups offer a lot of experience, strength and hope in that those who are involved have experienced very similar issues, if not the exact same problems that a person who is reaching out for help has experienced.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <strong>
           Continuum Of Care For Addiction Treatment
          </strong>
         </h2>
         <p>
          To create a really strong foundation for a successful long-term recovery, it is important to undergo intensive treatment that includes a continuum of care. Going through an intensive
          <a href="http://www.asaprehab.com/addiction-directory/drug-addiction/drug-addiction-treatment/" target="_blank" title="Asaprehab.com l Drug Addiction Treatment">
           addiction treatment
          </a>
          at a dedicated facility provides a person with a strong foundation to reduce the risk of relapse and ensure long-term recovery.
         </p>
         <p>
          These programs provide many of the necessary elements for creating a strong foundation, such as counseling, learning healthy stress management, dealing with temptation and cravings, creating a healthy support system, learning coping mechanisms, finding healthy relationships, and more. They also offer a continuum of care including after care programs.
         </p>
         <p>
          There are different levels of treatment, and the level of care one needs will be dependent upon the specific circumstances. However, most people have more success in their recovery by undergoing intensive treatment at a residential or intensive outpatient addiction treatment center.
         </p>
         <p>
          Studies have also shown that entering sober living and undergoing an aftercare program after completing treatment reduces a persons risk of relapse. Addiction is a disease with which one will struggle his or her entire life. However, by building up a solid foundation during treatment, an addict will have a reduced risk of relapse and a stronger control over his or her addiction.
         </p>
