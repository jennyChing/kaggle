       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         What Is Mid-Century Modern?
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         If youve spent any time talking with other collectors, antiquers, dealers, or folks who just enjoy watching the
         <a href="http://www.inherited-values.com/tag/television-shows/" target="_blank">
          plethora of collecting shows
         </a>
         , youve been hearing an increase in the term 
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574634280&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5336482919&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2Fi.html%3F_trksid%3Dp5197.m570.l1311%26_nkw%3Dmid%2Bcentury%2Bmodern%26_sacat%3D0" target="_blank">
          mid-century modern
         </a>
         <img alt="" src="http://rover.ebay.com/roverimp/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574634280&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5336482919&amp;customid=&amp;mpt=[CACHEBUSTER]" style="text-decoration: none; border: 0; padding: 0; margin: 0;"/>
         . Loosely applied, the term can mean anything made in the middle of the last, or 20th, century,
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574634280&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5336482919&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2FVintage-Retro-Mid-Century-%2F69851%2Fi.html" target="_blank">
          usually 1940-1960
         </a>
         . But more aptly, the term applies to a design aesthetic which embraces the marriage between function and form  with a simplicity of style born of the artistic and cultural movement of
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modernism" target="_blank">
          Modernism
         </a>
         . And because of the modern in mid-century modern, the style dates back much further than the name implies.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/midcentury-modern-keck-home.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3595" height="338" src="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/midcentury-modern-keck-home.jpg" title="midcentury modern keck home" width="450"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Modernism is more than just an artistic style; its a cultural movement. The movements origins go far back as the 1880s, to Germany before the first World War. Despite, or perhaps because of, the prevailing conservatism, there was an increased interest in what the Germans considered the very American notion of usefulness  or, as
         <a href="http://www.whitman.edu/content/art-history/faculty/dennis-crockett" target="_blank">
          Dennis Crockett
         </a>
         phrased it in his book,
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0271017961/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0271017961&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=inherited-20" target="_blank">
          <em>
           German Post-Expressionism: The Art of the Great Disorder 1918-1924
          </em>
         </a>
         , the predilection for functional work.
        </p>
        <p>
         This philosophy, called
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Objectivity_%28architecture%29" target="_blank">
          Neues Bauen or New Objectivity
         </a>
         , was first or most notably employed in addressing the German housing crisis of the time. The physical design application of New Objectivity resulted in design innovations in architecture, in which the commercial need for cost-effective housing was met with a radically simplified yet dynamic functionalism, offering simplicity, health, and beauty for the occupants. This solidified the notion that mass-production was indeed reconcilable with individual artistic spirit  it meant affordability  and it was something
         <a href="http://bauhaus-online.de/en" target="_blank">
          the famous Bauhaus
         </a>
         would build upon when it was founded by Walter Gropius in 1919.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Bauhaus-Haus-am-Horn-kitchen-1923.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3590" height="589" src="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Bauhaus-Haus-am-Horn-kitchen-1923.jpg" title="Bauhaus Haus am Horn kitchen 1923" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/ceramic-containers-designed-by-Theodor-Bogler-for-Bauhaus-kitchen-1923.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3591" height="413" src="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/ceramic-containers-designed-by-Theodor-Bogler-for-Bauhaus-kitchen-1923-550x413.jpg" title="ceramic containers designed by Theodor Bogler for Bauhaus kitchen 1923" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Widely acknowledged as the the first academy for design in the world, the
         <a href="http://bauhaus-online.de/en/atlas/das-bauhaus/idee/manifest" target="_blank">
          Bauhaus manifesto
         </a>
         includes the declaration to create a new unity of crafts, art and technology. It was here students and artists would focus on the craftsmanship and the manufacture of works in a
         <a href="http://bauhaus-online.de/en/atlas/das-bauhaus/idee" target="_blank">
          collaborative setting
         </a>
         , to produce a work that is not limited to its purely aesthetic meaning, but supports and even influences the transformation of social reality and thus shapes a new society. And at this time, the transformation desired was a modern one of simplicity and functionality. (For more on the Bauhaus movement and the artists themselves, I highly recommend a book Ive been reading
         <em>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0307268365/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=inherited-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0307268365" target="_blank">
           The Bauhaus Group: Six Masters of Modernism
          </a>
         </em>
         , by Nicholas Fox Weber. Its fascinating!)
         <strong>
          <em>
           Here is where mid-century modern truly begins.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Josef-Albers-Set-of-four-stacking-tables-1927.jpg">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3592" height="424" src="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Josef-Albers-Set-of-four-stacking-tables-1927.jpg" title="Josef Albers Set of four stacking tables 1927" width="520"/>
           </a>
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Because the Bauhaus produced more than a mere decorative style, because it pushed the values and needs of a modern world, the school, the artists, and the works created would inspire many others throughout the (mainly Western) world. Spurred on by urban living and the rapid development of plastics and other materials, mid-century modernism became quite popular.
        </p>
        <p>
         Some of the most known  and collected names  in what we now call mid-century modern, were influenced by the Bauhaus and the movement. They include Americans
         <a href="http://www.eamesoffice.com/charles-and-ray/" target="_blank">
          Ray and Charles Eames
         </a>
         (who made
         <a href="http://www.kitschy-kitschy-coo.com/blog/2012/06/eames-people-play/" target="_blank">
          fabulous toys too
         </a>
         !); Brits
         <a href="http://designmuseum.org/design/robin-lucienne-day" target="_blank">
          Robin Day and his wife Lucienne
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.racefurniture.com/ernest-race/" target="_blank">
          Ernest Race
         </a>
         ; the Japanese designer and sculptor
         <a href="http://www.noguchi.org/noguchi" target="_blank">
          Isamu Noguchi
         </a>
         ; and Scandinavians
         <a href="http://www.danish-furniture.com/designers/boerge-mogensen/" target="_blank">
          Brge Mogensen
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.arne-jacobsen.com/en/arne-jacobsen/biography" target="_blank">
          Arne Jacobsen
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.finnjuhl.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/01/Finn-Juhl-manual.pdf" target="_blank">
          Finn Juhl (PDF)
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.carlhansen.com/?id=1468" target="_blank">
          Hans Wegner
         </a>
         . (Because of the latter design giants, mid-century modern overlaps with, and is often confused for, Scandinavian or Danish design. Here the date of creation and manufacture help make the final decision.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres a photo of mid-century modern designers
         <a href="http://www.georgenelson.org/" target="_blank">
          George Nelson
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.r20thcentury.com/biography_detail.cfm?designer_id=113" target="_blank">
          Edward Wormley
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.eerosaarinen.net/eero_saarinen.shtml" target="_blank">
          Eero Saainen
         </a>
         (who died not long after this photo was taken),
         <a href="http://www.harrybertoia.org/" target="_blank">
          Harry Bertoia
         </a>
         , Charles Eames, and
         <a href="http://www.dwr.com/category/designers/r-t/jens-risom.do" target="_blank">
          Jens Risom
         </a>
         for an article in
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574634280&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5336482919&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2FMagazine-Back-Issues-%2F280%2Fi.html%3F_nkw%3Dplayboy%2B1961" target="_blank">
          <em>
           Playboy
          </em>
          , July 1961
         </a>
         <img alt="" src="http://rover.ebay.com/roverimp/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574634280&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5336482919&amp;customid=&amp;mpt=[CACHEBUSTER]" style="text-decoration: none; border: 0; padding: 0; margin: 0;"/>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/mid-century-modern-furniture-designers-in-playboy-july-1961.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3586" height="368" src="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/mid-century-modern-furniture-designers-in-playboy-july-1961-550x368.jpg" title="mid-century modern furniture designers in playboy july 1961" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Furniture wasnt the only thing affected by mid-century modernist design. Other functional household objects, such as clocks, radios, and lamps (
         <a href="http://www.mathmos.com/Our-story.html" target="_blank">
          this was the start of lava lamps
         </a>
         !) were made  and are heavily collected today.
        </p>
        <p>
         Housewares, kitchenalia, and decorative items also got the
         <a href="http://www.etsy.com/treasury/NjQwMzIxNXwyNzIwNTk2ODk3/mid-century-modern" target="_blank">
          mid-century modern treatment
         </a>
         . Youll see lots of geometric yet sleek pottery and glassware with embossed patterns and lines of the mid-century mod design. While there still were the more traditional shapes and forms, some with more elaborate and fancy painted designs, made during this time too (Hey, not everyone hops on the trends!), the mid-century modern look is most readily identified by its design simplicity. The decorations seem to better fit the form and function of the piece. Look for pieces in solid colors with embossed designs which seem to flow along the lines of the piece rather than appear applied to it. And remember, one of the primary influences of the movement was purposefulness; meaning the design is wed to an item of purpose and function. When it comes to pieces of
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?icep_ff3=9&amp;pub=5574634280&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5336482919&amp;customid=&amp;icep_uq=turquoise+california+pottery&amp;icep_sellerId=&amp;icep_ex_kw=&amp;icep_sortBy=12&amp;icep_catId=&amp;icep_minPrice=&amp;icep_maxPrice=&amp;ipn=psmain&amp;icep_vectorid=229466&amp;kwid=902099&amp;mtid=824&amp;kw=lg" target="_blank">
          decorative turquoise California pottery
         </a>
         , for example, theres less usefulness and practicality than there is with a chair, lamp, or piece of refrigerator glass However, the style is often represented in these pieces more decorative than functional items and collectors do like them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/mid-century-modern-westinghouse-beige-pottery-pitcher.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3593" height="412" src="http://www.inherited-values.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/mid-century-modern-westinghouse-beige-pottery-pitcher-550x412.jpg" title="mid century modern westinghouse beige pottery pitcher" width="550"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Mid-century modern as a category of collecting dates from the 1930s through the mid-1960s. Because of the dates involved, mid-century modern overlaps, influences, and to some degree encapsulates designs from the Atomic Era, Space Age and
         <a href="http://www.ultraswank.net/design/the-world-of-googie/" target="_blank">
          Googie design
         </a>
         , California Modernism, etc. These innovative and popular designs of the 20th century not only pioneered modern furniture and industrial design, but are now the iconic pieces we think of when we think of these decades.
        </p>
        <p>
         Truly defining or identifying mid-century modern pieces may be difficult; but like
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potter_Stewart" target="_blank">
          Justice Potter said
         </a>
         of pornography, youll know it when you see it.
        </p>
        <p>

         <em>
          Playboy

        </p>
