        <h1 class="entry-title">
         "No results" page feedback
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Have gotten amazing feedback from folks on the
         <a href="http://www.calacanis.com/2007/11/21/new-no-result-page-at-mahalo-com/">
          Mahalo no results page
         </a>
         we put up quietly yesterday. I didnt realize how well this would be received. A number of folks told me they can now switch their default search engine to Mahalo because of it. Wow!
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats the wonderful thing about doing a startup you can get these great wins from things you never thought of. Its the same rush I get from playing poker and you win with a horrible hand that suddenly becomes great unexpected wins are the best wins.
        </p>
        <p>
         The tabbed interface started in a discussion about making a mashed up page of various search results. We thought, well if were going to mash it up why not just give the raw materials. We mocked it, and the designed said hey lets put the logos in and I was like ohhh. that looks amazing. Folks are going to freak that were promoting competing services.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is
         <a href="http://snarfd.com/2007/11/22/mahalo-multiplies-search-options-for-its-users/">
          my favorite quote
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Mahalo has become something of a searchers power tool.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Wow. Thats the exact opposite of what we were going for. We were going for a service for the masses, but if this feature brings in the power users, well, rocking!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://rustle.blogsome.com/2007/11/22/minde-divine-habits/">
          Rustle added
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          of course this is all about habits. calacanis wants to change yours. someone, somewhere is going to create the page you go to by default. whos it going to be?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Yes, I think thats right. The good news is that most folks use 3-4 search engines a month so we dont have to beat Googlewe just need to beat one of the other three.
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://calacanis.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif"/>
        </p>
