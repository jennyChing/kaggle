        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          #8  Lack of exercise
         </strong>
         
         <span style="color: #111111;">
          Exercise helps boost your bodys HDL good cholesterol while lowering your LDL bad cholesterol. Not getting enough exercise puts you at risk of high cholesterol.
          <em>
           (
           <a href="http://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/high-blood-cholesterol/basics/risk-factors/con-20020865" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/high-blood-cholesterol/basics/risk-factors/con-20020865', 'Source');" target="_blank">

          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          #9  Obesity
         </strong>
         
         <span style="color: #111111;">
          Having a body mass index (BMI) of 30 or greater puts you at risk of high cholesterol.
          <em>
           (
           <a href="http://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/high-blood-cholesterol/basics/risk-factors/con-20020865" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/high-blood-cholesterol/basics/risk-factors/con-20020865', 'Source');" target="_blank">
            Source
           </a>
           )
          </em>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          #10 Diet
         </strong>
          Yes. This is a factor but not in the way that is traditionally thought about. Diet plays a part because an improper diet contributes to all the above causes. So, it all starts with diet but rather than the traditional low fat diet recommended for high cholesterol, focus on a low inflammation, low sugar and high in water diet.

          #11 Hereditary
