My kids relish the time with us too. This is especially important to me now that school is back in session. One common complaint about family dinners at home is that it takes too long to make something healthy. We are all busy, and that is true of some recipes. But not this one!          <ul>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 (15 ounce) cans beans, drained and rinsed, any variety
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 cups salsa, your favorite variety
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 cup fresh or frozen corn
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           One (12 ounce) package Pamela's Cornbread and Muffin Mix
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 Tablespoon coconut sugar or sucanat
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           1 cup water
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            cup oil
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           2 large eggs, lightly beaten
          </li>
          <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           Garnishes - cilantro, cashew cream, etc.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <div class="ERSClear">

        <div class="ERSInstructions">
         <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
          Instructions
         </div>
         <ol>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Preheat oven to 400F. Have an 8.5- by 11-inch casserole dish ready.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Combine beans, salsa, and corn and spread evenly in the bottom of the casserole dish.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           In a large mixing bowl, stir together Pamela's Cornbread and Muffin Mix with sugar, water, oil, and eggs until combined. Some lumps may remain.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Pour the batter evenly over the filling making sure to get it all the way to the edges. Place in oven and bake for 30-35 minutes. The cornbread crust will be golden and cooked through, and the filling bubbling around the edges. Test for doneness by inserting a toothpick just into the cornbread. If it comes out clean, the tamale pie is done.
          </li>
          <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Let cool slightly before serving. Garnish with your favorite toppings.
          </li>
         </ol>

       <h2>
        HOW TO PICTURES
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="GF Tamale Pie Ingredients" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5228" height="429" src="http://cafejohnsonia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Tamale-Pie-Ingredients.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        You only need a few simple ingredients  canned or
        <a href="http://cafejohnsonia.com/2013/02/how-to-slow-cooker-black-beans.html" target="_blank" title="How To: Slow Cooker Black Beans">
         home-cooked beans
        </a>
        , corn (fresh or frozen), your favorite salsa, and the cornbread mix plus sugar, water, eggs, and oil.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Easy GF Tamale Pie" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5225" height="429" src="http://cafejohnsonia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Easy-Tamale-Pie_.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Mix together the filling and spread it in the casserole dish, then mix up the cornbread batter.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Quick Tamale Pie" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5219" height="429" src="http://cafejohnsonia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Easy-Tamale-Pie_-3.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Pour it evenly over the filling.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Quick and Easy Gluten-Free Tamale Pie" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5220" height="480" src="http://cafejohnsonia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Easy-Tamale-Pie_-4.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Bake for 30-35 minutes. The baking time may differ a little bit according to the type of baking dish you use. This pan is stoneware, so it took a little longer than glass or metal to bake completely. Also, the package directions cite a lower baking temperature. I use a higher one to account for the filling needing to heat up to cook the bottom of the batter. Be sure to test for doneness by inserting a toothpick into just the top part. It should come out clean. If theres any raw batter on the toothpick, bake for an additional 5-10 minutes. If the top starts to brown too quickly, cover with foil.
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Gluten-Free Tamale Pie Topped with Cornbread" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5221" height="840" src="http://cafejohnsonia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Easy-Tamale-Pie_-5.jpg" width="600"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Let it cool for a few minutes, then serve it up with your favorite toppings and garnishes!
       </p>
       <p>
        <img alt="Bean Gluten-Free Tamale Pie" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-5222" height="480" src="http://cafejohnsonia.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Easy-Tamale-Pie_-6.jpg" width="600"/>

       </p>
