     <div class="entry-header">
      <h1 class="entry-title">
       <a href="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-questions/you-ask-we-answer-any-penalty-for-selling-my-solar-house-after-getting-tax-credits/" rel="bookmark">
        You ask, we answer: Any penalty for selling my solar house after getting tax credits?
       </a>
      </h1>
     </div>
     <div class="byline">
      by Dave Llorens
     </div>
     <div class="entry-content">
      <p class="no-break">
       <span class="st_email_large" st_title="You ask, we answer: Any penalty for selling my solar house after getting tax credits?" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-questions/you-ask-we-answer-any-penalty-for-selling-my-solar-house-after-getting-tax-credits/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_facebook_large" st_title="You ask, we answer: Any penalty for selling my solar house after getting tax credits?" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-questions/you-ask-we-answer-any-penalty-for-selling-my-solar-house-after-getting-tax-credits/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_googleplus_large" st_title="You ask, we answer: Any penalty for selling my solar house after getting tax credits?" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-questions/you-ask-we-answer-any-penalty-for-selling-my-solar-house-after-getting-tax-credits/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_twitter_large" st_title="You ask, we answer: Any penalty for selling my solar house after getting tax credits?" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-questions/you-ask-we-answer-any-penalty-for-selling-my-solar-house-after-getting-tax-credits/" st_username="solarpowerrocks">
       </span>
       <span class="st_linkedin_large" st_title="You ask, we answer: Any penalty for selling my solar house after getting tax credits?" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/solar-questions/you-ask-we-answer-any-penalty-for-selling-my-solar-house-after-getting-tax-credits/">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/SPRlogo5.png">
        <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-3101 aligncenter" height="116" src="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/01/SPRlogo5-300x116.png" title="SPRlogo5" width="300"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: left">
       Rollin from North Carolina contacted us just a while ago to ask:
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        If you install a solar panel system, receive the applicable tax credits and then sell the house in two years, do you have to refund the tax credits? If not, is there any penalty?  I have a 20 year shingle roofshould I consider re-roofing prior to installing solar panels and, as a NC Outer Banks resident (Ocracoke), what is the hurricane experience? Corrosion experience? Average sunlight here in Ocracoke?Thanks, Rollin
       </p>
      </blockquote>
      <p>
       <span style="color: green;font-size: x-large">
        ANSWER:
       </span>
       Rollin, the tax credit issue is complicated and you should definitely double check our advice here with your local tax geek. Were solar geeks, so the IRS doesnt really count us as tax authorities.
       <span style="text-decoration: line-through">
        Nevertheless, it is our understanding that you will not have to refund any of the portion of the tax credits that youve received thus far. Why do I say thus far? Because your
        <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/solar-trends/how-to-calculate-the-30-federal-solar-investment-tax-credit-itc/" target="_blank">
         30% Federal Tax Credit
        </a>
        vests over 5 years. Therefore, you will only get 40% (2 years worth) of the full 30% Federal Tax credit. Again, please double check with your tax person, but thats our understanding of the law from
        <a href="http://www.ongrid.net/papers/index.html" target="_blank">
         another solar financial guru, Andy Black
        </a>
        . (Of course, Andy is a solar guru, not a tax guru.)
       </span>
       Please see below from someone who knows better for commercial and residential tax consequences.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now to the easier answers:
      </p>
      <p>
       If your roof is older than 8-10 years, you should definitely re-roof before installing the panels. That way, you wont have to go through the added headache of snapping the panels off later and having to deal with re-installing the racking system. Also, if you coordinate your roof replacement with your solar installer, both warranties will stay intact. If you take your panels off later, re-roof, then reinstall, you may be voiding the original installation warranty of either the roof or the solar installation.
      </p>
      <p>
       Regarding winds, solar racking systems have gotten so good over the years that you dont have to worry so much about them blowing off your house in a hurricane as much as you may have to worry about your entire roof being blown off. That said, after going solar, you should cover the panels with your homeowners insurance to prevent any ill feelings down the road.
      </p>
      <p>
       Corrosion can happen when installations are done by amateurs. The equation looks like: wet + electric current + salt water + exposed conduit = big problem. This is why its important to go with an experienced, licensed crew, who arent going to cut any corners. Most
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/see-your-solar-savings/">
        solid solar installation outfits
       </a>
       will warranty all their work for at least 5 years, though some of the best out there will warranty their work for 10 or more. (Btw, solar panels nowadays are warrantied to be producing at at least 80% of their original capacity at year 25, so you know theyll still be kicking out significantly more than that then because the solar panel manufacturers dont want to be shelling out new panels all over the country.)
      </p>
      <p>
       Were not Ocracoke meteorologists, but we know North Carolina gets significantly more sunshine than say, Germany, which has more solar installed per capita than anywhere else in the world. What makes solar make financial sense isnt the sunlight, insomuch as the combination of available state rebates, financing, and tax credits (which NC has in spades).
      </p>

          </p>
          <p>
           Item #6: What if I sell my house, can I still claim the credit?
          </p>
          <p>
           A: The residential credit is not recaptured if the house is later sold. However, the homeowner will have had to reduced his tax basis in the house by the amount of the credit. He is more likely to have a gain on the sale of real property.
          </p>
          <p>
           Commercial Property
          </p>
          <p>
           According to the ARRA guidelines for the Section 1063 Grant in Lieu of Tax credits Page 18 article 7. Entitled Recapture
          </p>
          <p>
           If the applicant disposes of the property to a disqualifed person or the property ceases to qaulify as a specified energy property within 5 years from the date the property is placed in service, the section 1063 payment must be repaid to the Treasury as follows:
          </p>
          <p>
           100% if disqualfiying event takes place within 1 year
          </p>
          <p>
           80% 1-2yrs  60-40-20% to 5 years.
          </p>
          <p>
           There are various disqualifying events such as the property is sold to: any Federal, state or local government, including political subdivision, agency or any organization that is a 501(c) or 501(a)
