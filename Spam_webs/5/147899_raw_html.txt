     <p>
      <b>
       The Janitor
      </b>
      is a fictional character, played by actor
      <a href="/wiki/Neil_Flynn" title="Neil Flynn">
       Neil Flynn
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Comedy-drama" title="Comedy-drama">
       comedy-drama
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Scrubs_(TV_series)" title="Scrubs (TV series)">
        Scrubs
       </a>
      </i>
      . Though he is a janitor at Sacred Heart, he is rarely referred to as
      <i>
       the
      </i>
      janitor, but rather just called Janitor.
     </p>
     <p>
      Neil Flynn was originally billed as a recurring guest star throughout Season 1, although he appeared in all 24 episodes of that season. He was promoted to a series regular beginning with Season 2 and remained a regular through to Season 8. He made his final appearance in the Season 9 premiere, "
      <a href="/wiki/Our_First_Day_of_School" title="Our First Day of School">
       Our First Day of School
      </a>
      ", as a guest star.
     </p>
     <p>
      Janitor appeared in every episode during the first eight seasons except Season 2's "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Lucky_Day_(Scrubs_episode)" title="My Lucky Day (Scrubs episode)">
       My Lucky Day
      </a>
      " and Season 8's "
      <a href="/wiki/My_Last_Words" title="My Last Words">
       My Last Words
      </a>
      ", "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Absence" title="My Absence">
       My Absence
      </a>
      " and "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Full_Moon" title="My Full Moon">
       My Full Moon
      </a>
      ".
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_character_biography">
       Fictional character biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Janitor_(Scrubs)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fictional character biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the series'
      <a href="/wiki/My_First_Day" title="My First Day">
       pilot episode
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Protagonist" title="Protagonist">
       protagonist
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/J.D._(Scrubs)" title="J.D. (Scrubs)">
       J.D.
      </a>
      sees Janitor fixing a
      <a href="/wiki/Sliding_door" title="Sliding door">
       sliding door
      </a>
      that is stuck, and suggests someone might have stuck a
      <a href="/wiki/Penny" title="Penny">
       penny
      </a>
      in the door. Janitor immediately accuses J.D. of sabotaging the door, and swears revenge.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_First_Day_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_First_Day-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      For the rest of the series, the Janitor makes it his personal mission to torment J.D. with insults, mind games and practical jokes. In the season eight finale, J.D. admits to accidentally sticking the penny in the door; the Janitor replies he saw J.D. do it and began torturing him because he failed a "test of character" by keeping silent about it.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Finale_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Finale-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Some of the Janitor's
      <a href="/wiki/Practical_joke" title="Practical joke">
       practical jokes
      </a>
      have been on the severe side, such as destroying J.D.'s bike (twice),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Way_Home_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Way_Home-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      trapping him in a
      <a href="/wiki/Water_tower" title="Water tower">
       water tower
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-his-story-3_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-his-story-3-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      stranding him in the middle of nowhere,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Last_Chance_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Last_Chance-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and tricking him into robbing an Asian couple's house.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_New_God_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_New_God-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Neil Flynn has said:
     </p>
     <blockquote class="templatequote">
      <p>
       "I think it's possible that he doesn't hate J.D. Maybe J.D. is as close to a friend as he has. For all we know, he just has poor
       <a href="/wiki/Social_skills" title="Social skills">
        social skills
       </a>
       . I think that the Janitor constantly misreads J.D.'s motives and assumes he's a young punk."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MOPNT_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-MOPNT-7">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         7
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      It is suggested in the episode "His Story III" that he is in some way responsible for a medical intern named Jill having a child.
     </p>
     <p>
      During the first six seasons he has a crush on
      <a href="/wiki/Elliot_Reid" title="Elliot Reid">
       Dr. Elliot Reid
      </a>
      , but in Season Seven he meets his future wife
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_on_Scrubs#Ladinia_Williams" title="List of characters on Scrubs">
       Lady
      </a>
      , and they get married the following season.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Soul_On_Fire_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Soul_On_Fire-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In
      <a href="/wiki/Scrubs_(season_9)" title="Scrubs (season 9)">
       the first episode of season 9
      </a>
      , a flashback is seen of the Janitor's last appearance the day after J.D. leaves Sacred Heart. Janitor believes that J.D. is simply hiding, as a practical joke, and will reappear. When Turk convinces him that J.D. has left for good, the Janitor hands over his mop to Turk and leaves, never to be seen again.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Our_First_Day_of_School_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Our_First_Day_of_School-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Personality">
       Personality
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Janitor_(Scrubs)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personality">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Concrete information about the Janitor's personal history is sparse, and confounded by his penchant for giving outrageous (and often conflicting) accounts of his past. Many of the stories that he has told J.D. are patently absurd, some bordering on the surreal or fantastical.
     </p>
     <p>
      He has claimed that his parents are also his siblings; made multiple conflicting accounts of having been married and having children; claimed that, in college, he came close to a world record in the 100m
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurdles" title="Hurdles">
       hurdles
      </a>
      race; and given an explanation for learning
      <a href="/wiki/Sign_language" title="Sign language">
       sign language
      </a>
      so convoluted that he asks to have it read back to him when J.D. asks if any part of it is true. His childhood and family life are equally mysterious. The occasional flashback sequences imply that his parents were fastidious and mildly
      <a href="/wiki/Child_abuse" title="Child abuse">
       abusive
      </a>
      . One flashback implies the loss of a stuffed animal in the "clutter" of his bedroom led him to take on a janitorial career. He also claims that his great-grandfather was the
      <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">
       Civil War
      </a>
      general
      <a href="/wiki/Ambrose_Burnside" title="Ambrose Burnside">
       Ambrose Burnside
      </a>
      (mispronounced "Burnsides").
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Janitor speaks
      <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">
       Spanish
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">
       Korean
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">
       German
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">
       Italian
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/American_Sign_Language" title="American Sign Language">
       American Sign Language
      </a>
      . When the hospital support staff wants dental coverage, he becomes their spokesman.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Coffee_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Coffee-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On occasion Janitor indicates he does little actual work. He tells J.D. that he spends only an hour and a half a day performing his duties at work. He does, however, defend his sanitary responsibilities in the hospital whenever J.D. questions him about it, and takes great pride in the cleanliness of the hospital's floors.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Drive-By_12-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Drive-By-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Although he spends most of the series harassing J.D., on occasion he will torment other characters such as
      <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Turk" title="Christopher Turk">
       Christopher Turk
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Dirty_Secret_13-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Dirty_Secret-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This is usually in response to a perceived wrong, such as another character creating a mess in the hospital or disrespecting him.
     </p>
     <p>
      Several episodes show the Janitor pretending to be a doctor ("Dr. Jn tor") and he also greatly enjoys one occasion when he is asked to assist during a medical emergency.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-his-story-3_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-his-story-3-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On one occasion, he uses a set of broken defibrillator paddles to pretend to revive a patient (in reality, a pair of pillows and a mop). He also pretends to be the chief of medicine when the real chief,
      <a href="/wiki/Bob_Kelso" title="Bob Kelso">
       Bob Kelso
      </a>
      , is away.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Road_to_Nowhere_14-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Road_to_Nowhere-14">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Name">
       Name
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Janitor_(Scrubs)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Name">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The mystery of the Janitor's name is a
      <a href="/wiki/Running_gag" title="Running gag">
       running gag
      </a>
      throughout the series. In several episodes, he calls himself "Janitor" (even in his own mind), and in many episodes he refers to himself as "Doctor Jn tor".
     </p>
     <p>
      In "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Hero_(Scrubs)" title="My Hero (Scrubs)">
       My Hero
      </a>
      ", Janitor chastises J.D. for not even knowing his name while Janitor knows many personal details about J.D.'s life (though it's later shown Janitor has been breaking into J.D.'s locker and reading his journal). When J.D. protests that he does in fact know Janitor's real name, Janitor asks him to say it aloud and quickly claps his hand over his name badge so J.D. can't read it.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the beginning of the eighth season, Sacred Heart's new Chief of Medicine, Dr. Maddox (played by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Courteney_Cox_Arquette" title="Courteney Cox Arquette">
       Courteney Cox Arquette
      </a>
      ) examines his name tag only to find it says "The Janitor."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Jerks_20-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Jerks-20">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        20
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bill Lawrence gave a hint towards the Janitor's name at a speech at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_College_of_William_and_Mary" title="The College of William and Mary">
       The College of William and Mary
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Williamsburg,_Virginia" title="Williamsburg, Virginia">
       Williamsburg, Virginia
      </a>
      on January 29, 2009. He said that the only clue he had given was that Janitor's name is the same as the janitor on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Clone_High" title="Clone High">
        Clone High
       </a>
      </i>
      (Glenn, voiced by Neil Flynn).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the
      <a href="/wiki/My_Finale" title="My Finale">
       Season 8 finale
      </a>
      he tells J.D. that his name is
      <b>
       Glenn Matthews
      </b>
      ;
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      "My Finale" was previously intended to be the series finale, in which the Janitor's real name would be revealed. When J.D. asks why the Janitor is only revealing his name now, the Janitor points out that J.D. has never before asked what his name is, and proves that J.D. has already forgotten it. However just a few seconds later, someone else walks by and calls the Janitor by another name, to which he responds.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Finale_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-My_Finale-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In a
      <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">
       Twitter
      </a>
      post on April 5, 2011, Bill Lawrence confirmed that the Janitor was telling the truth when he revealed his name as Glenn Matthews.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Figment_of_J.D..27s_imagination">
       Figment of J.D.'s imagination
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Janitor_(Scrubs)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Figment of J.D.'s imagination">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      As revealed in the
      <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">
       DVD
      </a>
      commentary on several episodes, the Janitor character was initially to be used as a figment of J.D.'s imagination if the show had been canceled during the first season or the first half of the second. This would have been revealed to the audience in the finale.
     </p>
     <p>
      The first time that Janitor interacts with another major character besides J.D. is in the season two episode "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Karma" title="My Karma">
       My Karma
      </a>
      ", in which he
      <a href="/wiki/Blackmail" title="Blackmail">
       blackmails
      </a>
      J.D. and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Turk" title="Chris Turk">
       Turk
      </a>
      . From the beginning of Season 2, Flynn joins the rest of the main cast appearing in the show's extended opening credits, but the credits were changed back due to objections by
      <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">
       NBC
      </a>
      , who wanted longer episodes instead. However, he was still acknowledged as a main cast member by the producers as of the second season. Since the start of season two, Janitor has had encounters with most of the other regular characters. He has even had an entire episode ("
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/His_Story_III" title="His Story III">
       His Story III
      </a>
      ") devoted mostly to him.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-his-story-3_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-his-story-3-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production_notes">
       Production notes
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Janitor_(Scrubs)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production notes">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Flynn is an
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Improvisational_comedy" title="Improvisational comedy">
       improv
      </a>
      comedian and, as such,
      <a href="/wiki/Ad_libitum" title="Ad libitum">
       ad-libs
      </a>
      many of his lines. Although it is often stated that Flynn ad-libs all of his lines, on several parts of the Season One DVD commentaries and special features, both Flynn and series creator
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bill_Lawrence_(producer)" title="Bill Lawrence (producer)">
       Bill Lawrence
      </a>
      say that it is generally a mix of ad-libbed lines and the original script, with Flynn usually building on the original lines. Lawrence has also said that the rest of the cast followed Flynn's lead and that he would occasionally enter the rehearsal room with no idea what scene was taking place due to its lack of resemblance to the original script. Janitor's
      <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">
       alter ego
      </a>
      , Dr. Jn tor, is a happy consequence of one such moment of inspiration.
      <a href="/wiki/Sam_Lloyd" title="Sam Lloyd">
       Sam Lloyd
      </a>
      once commented on Flynn's improvising: "I opened my script up once and it said 'Janitor: Whatever Neil says,' and I just started laughing."
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2007)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Flynn originally auditioned for the role of Dr. Cox (which ultimately went to
      <a href="/wiki/John_C._McGinley" title="John C. McGinley">
       John C. McGinley
      </a>
      ). However, Lawrence asked Flynn if he would consider another partthe mysterious custodian who makes tormenting J.D. his life's work.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MOPNT_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-MOPNT-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The role of Janitor was originally devised as a one-time gag in the series'
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilot_episode" title="Pilot episode">
       pilot episode
      </a>
      , Lawrence admitted: "When we watched the pilot, we knew instantly we had to keep this guy around."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
       <a href="#cite_note-25">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        25
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In flashback scenes of Janitor's childhood, he is played by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brandon_Waters&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brandon Waters (page does not exist)">
       Brandon Waters
      </a>
      .
     </p>
