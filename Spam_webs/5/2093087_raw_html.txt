       There are numerous landscaping ideas for small yards, ranging from trendy to practical, expensive to thrifty, simple to elaborate, and everywhere in between. All of these projects can benefit from forethought and planning, to give you the best possible outcome with the least amount of stress. Below well look at some easy lawn improvements, and the maintenance each will require.

       are the foundation of any landscaping project, and can be as small or large as you want. For those with limited space, a few flowerbeds placed as a barrier between your home and lawn take minimal room, while greatly enhancing your property. Bushes, flowers, or whichever plants you prefer will look great and be easily managed this way. Consider bushes along the perimeter of your property, to give a nice natural barrier between neighboring lawns. As always, growing conditions like direct and indirect sunlight, and average size when fully grown must be taken into account before selecting your plants. Occasional weeding and watering are the biggest maintenance tasks you have to worry about.

       </iframe>
      </noscript>
      <p>
       A simple stone path between your front, side, or back door, leading to your driveway or porch is another easy option, and cuts down on wear and tear to your lawn. Most home improvement stores sell thesein sizes of one square foot and up, for less than two dollars apiece, making this option very inexpensive.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <strong>
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Landscaping ideas for small yards aren't that different from the options available to those with more space, and can incorporate many of the same features, just to a smaller scale. Water features are still an option, and the smaller size has the added benefit of being both cheaper and easier to maintain. A
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0079GHJXY/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0079GHJXY&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=so0f5-20&amp;linkId=ODZEACXCYM7EIV7I" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B0079GHJXY/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B0079GHJXY&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=so0f5-20&amp;linkId=ODZEACXCYM7EIV7I', 'bird feeder');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        bird feeder
       </a>
       <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=so0f5-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B0079GHJXY" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
       and bird bath will help attract these beautiful creatures to your home, and help add to the garden atmosphere. Checking the food and water levels periodically are about the only maintenance these require. Artificial ponds and fountains are other options, available in a wide array of styles and sizes, but require electricity and much more frequent maintenance.
      </p>

       <strong>
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </strong>
      </p>

      <p>
       <b>
        Rental Property Options
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Living in a rental property doesn't necessarily limit the landscaping ideas for small yards that are available to you. Speak with your landlord about adding anything permanent before starting a project. Most landlords will have no issue about residents adding small enhancements to their property, as these improvements cost them nothing, and add to the value of the residence. More maintenance demanding projects such as water features will most likely not be approved, but it never hurts to ask.
      </p>
      <p>
       <script>
        // &lt;![CDATA[

       If your property manager or landlord refuses to allow these additions,
       <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00ADY6I0O/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00ADY6I0O&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=so0f5-20&amp;linkId=COPSMUISXKF2S3SX" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00ADY6I0O/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00ADY6I0O&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=so0f5-20&amp;linkId=COPSMUISXKF2S3SX', 'Above Ground Planters');" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
        Above Ground Planters
       </a>
       <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=so0f5-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00ADY6I0O" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
       and pots can be used instead. Add decorative rocks and an electric fountain to a closed bottom pot to create a miniature water feature as well. The ability to transport these potted plants from home to home makes them a great option for renters, while achieving the same aesthetic as in ground planting. A few potted plants and patio furniture will create a comfortable, personal space on balconies or back patios as well.
      </p>

      <p>
       <b>
        What Should I Do?
       </b>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       Any landscaping project will require hard work and maintenance to stay looking great. Find a project with a workload you know you can manage, and consider starting small. Be realistic about the ideas you want to incorporate and the space you have to work with. The care and attention you give your projects will be given back to you every time you step outside, so get out there and build something youll love.
      </p>
