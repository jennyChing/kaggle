      <h1 class="entry-title">
       8 Easy Steps for Planning Your Annual Easter Egg Hunt (Without Losing Your Mind)
      </h1>
      <div class="entry-meta">
       <span class="meta-prep meta-prep-author">
        Posted on
       </span>
       <a href="http://www.extraattic.net/newsletter/8-easy-steps-for-planning-your-annual-easter-egg-hunt-without-losing-your-mind/" rel="bookmark" title="7:36 pm">
        <span class="entry-date">
         March 23, 2015
        </span>
       </a>
       <span class="meta-sep">
        by
       </span>
       <span class="author vcard">
        <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.extraattic.net/newsletter/author/admin/" title="View all posts by Richmond Self Storage">
         Richmond Self Storage
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <!-- .entry-meta -->
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.extraattic.net/about-us.php">
         <img alt="Easter eggs" class="alignright wp-image-292" height="257" src="http://www.extraattic.net/newsletter/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/extra_attic_pic16.png" title="extra_attic_pic16" width="384"/>
        </a>
        Its almost that time of year again . . . the time that kids love and parents dread . . . Easter! Easter can be a very fun, family-oriented holiday, especially if you have young kids. However, as any parent of young children will tell you, it can also be highly exhausting. One activity that kids love (but parents can dread) is the annual Easter egg hunt. Sure, its always fun to watch your children search for eggs filled with hidden gems, but the actual preparation for the event can be downright exhausting. Fortunately, weve created an easy eight step guide to help you plan the annual egg hunt at your home without losing your mind!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          1. Pick a time
         </em>
        </strong>
         Choose a time that works best for you, your family, and your guests. The key here is to set a specific date and time before anyone else has a chance to schedule their egg hunt on the same day. Since some people may go away for the Easter holiday, you may choose to hold the event the week before Easter, which this year would be March 29
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        . If youre planning to hold your egg hunt outside, be sure to schedule a backup rain date as well.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          2. Choose a location
         </em>
        </strong>
         When choosing a location, consider how many guests you plan to invite. If you are inviting more than 20 people, you may want to take things outside or consider reserving a few hours at a park or area with a wide open field. However, a smaller Easter egg hunt will do just fine indoors so long as your home is large enough to support a group of children running around safely.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          3. Send Out Invites
         </em>
        </strong>
         Be sure to send out the invites no later than two weeks prior to the event to give your guests enough time to RSVP and schedule the event onto their calendars. Be sure to ask guests to respond to the invitation with the number of children they plan to bring no later than one week prior. This will ensure you have plenty of time to purchase enough supplies.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          4. Decide on prizes
         </em>
        </strong>
         Youll probably want to fill the eggs with candy and perhaps some small party toys that can be bought in bulk for cheap at stores like Party City. However, you can also get creative and include special golden eggs for your guests to search for. Limit the number of these eggs and give out a special, larger prize to the guests who find them. These could be a gift certificate to the movies or a fun restaurant or a special toy or video game. Cant find golden eggs? Dont worry! You could always paint some or simply explain that a certain number of eggs will contain special prizes.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          5. Purchase the supplies
         </em>
        </strong>
         The sooner you can purchase supplies, the better. That way, you wont have to worry about the stores running out of essentials like plastic eggs or candy. You can also buy them in bits and pieces to help budget your money each week.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          6. Get rid of the junk
         </em>
        </strong>
         You cant safely host an Easter egg hunt for children with a bunch of junk clogging up the inside or outside of your home. Whether you choose to hold your egg hunt indoors out outdoors, youll want to give yourself plenty of space. You might want to
        <a href="http://www.extraattic.net/">
         look into renting Richmond, VA self-storage units
        </a>
        to help you stash any unnecessary items around your home that are taking up too much space.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          7. Set everything up
         </em>
        </strong>
         Be sure to give yourself several hours in advance to start setting up the egg hunt. Youd be surprised by how long it can take to hide all of the eggs and get everything prepared! The last thing you want is to have to try to hide the eggs when all of the kids are there watching! If you expect to be crunched for time, you can always set some things up the night before.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <em>
          8. Plan post-hunt activities
         </em>
        </strong>
         The chances of all of the kids participating in the egg hunt and going home are slim to none. Be sure to have some post-hunt activities planned for those kids who stick around a little bit longer. Look for DVDs of kid-friendly Easter cartoons or specials they can watch. You can also search Pinterest to find hundreds of ideas for fun Easter crafts the kids can make. If you dont mind things getting a little messy, you can also boil some eggs up and have a post-hunt egg dyeing party!
       </p>
       <p>
        What steps do you take when planning an Easter egg hunt for your kids?
       </p>
      </div>
