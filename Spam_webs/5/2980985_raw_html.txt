       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Love Letter &amp; Scripted Heart Cookies
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2011-02-04T23:13:33+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          February 4, 2011
         </time>
         -
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://sweetapolita.com/2011/02/love-letters-scripted-heart-cookies/#comments">
           75 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Love Letter Cookies via Sweetapolita" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9212" height="385" src="http://sweetapolita.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/lovelettersrev.jpg" width="540"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Happy love-month, everyone! Ive been having so much fun exploring different love-themed treats, now that the Valentines spirit is upon us. I have a seemingly endless list of lovey sweetsin myblog-ideas notebook, so I hope to somehow make it through several more before Valentines Day. Ifeel, though,that so many of thesedesigns would also make for gorgeous wedding, bridal shower, or anniversary confections, because where there is love, oh please, let there be cookies!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Love Letter Cookies via Sweetapolita" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9681" height="756" src="http://sweetapolita.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/loveletters3rev.jpg" width="540"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         While browsinga recentissue of Martha Stewart Living, I was beyond inspired (as usual). This time, though, things were really clicking. Every time I turned the page, I felt creative nudge upon creative nudge, resulting in some quick scribbles and sketches in my pink Moleskine notebook (I should be buying the notebooksby the dozen these days!). One of the most inspiring images, to me, was a gorgeous set of pastel letters with wine-coloured seals, created bya fabulous paper company called
         <a href="http://www.orangeart.com/product.asp?p=1404&amp;pt=192&amp;txt=&amp;cmd=" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.orangeart.com/product.asp?p=1404&amp;pt=192&amp;txt=&amp;cmd=', 'OrangeArt']);" target="_blank">
          OrangeArt
         </a>
         .Seeing asI dontthink inpaper, butin sugar, I immediately started to design this collection oflove letter cookies.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Scripted Heart Cookies via Sweetapolita" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9209" height="540" src="http://sweetapolita.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/loveletterheartsrev.jpg" width="540"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         As a hopeless romantic, love letters get me every time. Lucky for me, Grant is an exceptional writer, and sure knows how to speak from the heart. I still have some of his first love letters to me, and I will admit that the written word affects me more than anything else (particularly his, of course!).It seems were all enchanted bythelove lettersno matter whose they are, really.I noticed thisafter Grant and I watched our third NicholasSparks(clearly a deep and sensitive author who has a real adoration for love letters) movie last weekendall ofwhich have a cameo appearance (or more) by a big stack of gorgeously tattered and heartfelt letters.
         <em>
          The Notebook
         </em>
         , of course, which is centered around the tragic pile of letters intercepted and hidden by the mother of a girl in a star-crossed love affair;
         <em>
          Dear John
         </em>
         , which also focuses on the written word of love between a young couple parted by war; and finally, our most recent watch,
         <em>
          The Last Song
         </em>
         another story of love, but with a heap of unread letters from a father to his daughter. Itall reminds mehow inspirational and beautiful words really are; even the simple aesthetic of script, to me, is so lovely and artful.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Single Heart Cookie via Sweetapolita" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9208" height="756" src="http://sweetapolita.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/singleheartcookierev.jpg" width="540"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I suppose thats why I was so taken with the script rubber stamp I found the other day. The effect when impressedupon the fondantis simple, but I think its so pretty, textural, and perfectly antiquated.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Love Letter Cookies via Sweetapolita" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9207" height="386" src="http://sweetapolita.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/lettercookierev.jpg" width="540"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         So here we have ahappy little family of scripted love cookies. You know, speaking of family and love letters, I wanted to sharea really neat book/idea with you. Shortly after our first daughter, Reese, was born, Grants mom bought me a beautiful book called
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Love-Mommy-Writing-Letters-Your/dp/1550227580/ref=sr_1_2/102-5094386-5834549?ie=UTF8&amp;s=books&amp;qid=1174427047&amp;sr=8-2" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/Love-Mommy-Writing-Letters-Your/dp/1550227580/ref=sr_1_2/102-5094386-5834549?ie=UTF8&amp;s=books&amp;qid=1174427047&amp;sr=8-2', 'Love, Mommy: Writing Love Letters To Your Baby.']);" target="_blank">
          Love, Mommy: Writing Love Letters To Your Baby.
         </a>
         The author, Judy Siblin-Librach, encourages and inspires the reader to do just that: to write heartfelt love letters to your baby. This gives them something amazing to cherish when theyre older, and they can learn all about specific, day-to-day reasons why you love them so. So, every night, for an entire year, I wrote Reese a love letter in a journal. Ive put it away so that I can wrap it up and give it to her either on her wedding day, or when the time is right. Ive also started this for Neve, and Im so thrilled that Mary Lou (my mother-in-law) gave me this fabulous book. I cant wait to sit down with the girls, years from now, and read each of their love letters again.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Love Letter Cookies via Sweetapolita" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9211" height="386" src="http://sweetapolita.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/loveletters2rev.jpg" width="540"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Once Istarted creating these cookies, Ibegan imagining endless possibilitiesthere may even be a part two to this post, if I cant resist the urge to create the next part of my vision! I hope you run with this theme. If youd like to re-create what Ive done above, here is the how-to:
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #ff99cc;">
          Love Letters:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Using large rectangular cookie cutter (these are 4.5 x 3), cut out/bake/cool desired number of letters.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Colour fondant (I use Satin Ice brand) desired colours. I used ivory fondant and added the following colours (in tiny amounts):
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pink:
         </strong>
         Sugarflair Pinkand a small amount of grey petal dust
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Mauve:
         </strong>
         AmeriColor Mauveand a small amount of grey petal dust (I originally used a drop ofElectric Purple, but Mauve is already muted, so its a better choice)
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ice Teal:
         </strong>
         AmeriColor Teal and a small amount of grey petal dust
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Burgundy (for the seal):
         </strong>
         Sugarflair Red and AmeriColor Electric Purple and bit of AmeriColor Super Black
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Roll out your fondant to about 1/8 thickness, one cookie at a time, keeping the remaining fondant covered, and cut rectangle using same cookie cutter you used for cookie. When rolling fondant for cookies, I like to sprinkle icing sugar on counter surface before rolling, and then let the cut-out sit for a moment or two before lifting up with an artists palette knife. This way the fondant doesnt get stretched in any way.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Spread a very, very thin layer of royal icing onto theinner surface of the cookiewith an offset palette knife, and gently place your fondant cut-out on top, gently smoothing with your fingers.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Cut a top flap (any style you likeI used real envelopes for reference) and adhere to your envelope with a dab of water. Using a knife, create two angled envelope lines.
        </p>
        <p>
         6. For scripted detail piece: roll out ivory fondant, then press the rubber stamp firmly down upon the fondant and lift straight up. Using a knife, cut a rectangular piece, let dry for about 15 minutes, and adhere to envelope with royal icing.
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Using a #2 tip, pipe string onto envelope.
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Roll a small ball of burgundy fondant, and emboss with wax seal, then placein centre of cookie.
        </p>
        <p>
         Let cookies dry for several hours, preferably overnight, before packaging.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #ff99cc;">
          Scripted Heart Cookies:
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         These are so simple!
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Cut out/bake/cool any heart shapes you desire.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Roll pastel coloured fondant as with letter cookies, and impress script using rubber stamp. Using same cutters as used for making cookies, cut scripted fondant hearts.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Adhere to corresponding cookie using very thin layer of royal icing and an offset palette knife.
        </p>
        <p>
         I can see these making for fabulous event favours, gifts for loved ones &amp; friends, dessert table details, and more.
        </p>
        <p>
         Good luck &amp; enjoy!
        </p>
