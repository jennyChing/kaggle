         <p>
          Cabbage  most people either love it or hate it. Regardless, it has been a staple in the Irish kitchen for centuries. Back in the early 1700s it was said that the poor and rural farmers relied on vegetables as a large part of their diet, because livestock, wheat and oats were used to sell. Cabbage was one of those main vegetables. March is such a big month with the celebration of St. Patrick in Ireland, were sharing this cabbage recipe that we believe will have everyone scooping up seconds.  While youre whipping this dish up, be sure to keep the kids engaged with this
          <a href="/st-pattys-day-fun/" title="Potato Stamp Fun">
           St. Paddys Day Craft
          </a>
          using another Irish favorite, potato.
         </p>
