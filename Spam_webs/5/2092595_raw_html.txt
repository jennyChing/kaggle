        <h1 class="post-tile entry-title" itemprop="name">
         Dont Do This If Youre Trying to Fix Your Phantom Motor
        </h1>
        <div class="mom-post-meta single-post-meta">
         <span class="author vcard">
          Posted By:
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.thedroneu.com/author/admin/">
            Drone U
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span>
          on:
          <time class="updated" datetime="2015-03-10T10:17:32+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           March 10, 2015
          </time>
         </span>
         <span>
          In:
          <a href="http://www.thedroneu.com/category/tips-tricks/" title="View all posts in Tips &amp; Tricks">

          </a>
         </span>
         <span>
          <a href="http://www.thedroneu.com/dont-do-this-if-youre-trying-to-fix-your-phantom-motor-2/#comments">

         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          We recently had a student out practicing with his drone. He had a small mishap (live and learn) and could tell there was something not quite right with one of the motors. He tried to fix or tweak it a bit, but ended up making it worse, so much so that hell have to replace the motor. This is a very short video giving you a few tips on what NOT to do in the hopes that maybe you will save yourself some time and money from minor mistakes like this. Check it out.
         </p>
         <p>
