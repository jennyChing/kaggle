/**
 * Gets the "real" position of an element
 * Courtesy Peter-Paul Koch 
 * http://www.quirksmode.org/js/findpos.html
 */         
function findPos(obj) {
    var curleft = curtop = 0;
    if (obj.offsetParent) {
        do {
            curleft += obj.offsetLeft;
            curtop += obj.offsetTop;
        } while (obj = obj.offsetParent);
        return [curleft,curtop];
    }
}

/**
 * Shows or hides the premium notice
 */
function hoverPop(mode, element) {
    var realPos = findPos(element);
    if (mode == "show") {
      getPremiumNotice().style.top = realPos[1] - 15;
      getPremiumNotice().style.left = realPos[0] + 25;
      getPremiumNotice().innerHTML="Premium Content";
      getPremiumNotice().style.display = "block"; 
    } else if (mode == "hide") {
      getPremiumNotice().style.display = "none"; 
    }  
}

               <h1 class="articleTitle" id="articleTitle">
                Police: Man kidnapped, raped 7-year-old girl in Paradise Township
               </h1>
               <!--subtitle-->
               <!--byline-->
               <div class="articleByline" id="articleByline">
                By TED CZECH, York Daily Record/Sunday News
               </div>
               <!--date-->
               <div class="articleDate" id="articleDate">

                <p>
                 A Paradise Township man is accused of abducting a sleeping 7-year-old girl through a window in June, taking her to a nearby trailer and raping her, according to charging documents.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Robert Carl Yeager, 36, was arraigned Wednesday on rape, kidnapping and other charges. He is in York County Prison and was denied bail.
                </p>
                <p>
                 "Mr. Yeager took it upon himself to commit this heinous act against a very young child," Northern York County Regional Police Lt. David Lash said Wednesday. "We were fortunate that she (the alleged victim) told her family so quickly ... When dealing with DNA, sometimes time is of the essence."
                </p>
                <p>
                 Police said that at 4:32 a.m. June 24, they were called to a trailer park in the 6800 block of Lincoln Highway for a report of a sexual assault, documents state. The park, visible from Route 30, has about 7 trailers.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Once there, they spoke with a woman who said her 7-year-old niece woke her up that morning to say that a man had just raped her.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The girl was then taken to York Hospital where evidence was collected from the child, documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Lash said Wednesday that news of the incident was not made known to the public at the time because police did not want to jeopardize the investigation.
                </p>
                <p>
                 "It was an active investigation, and releasing information at that time could have been detrimental," he said.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Police spoke with another resident at the trailer park, who said she saw two men, one of whom was Yeager, pull into the park between 3:30 and 4 a.m., documents state. Yeager was driving the vehicle, she said.
                </p>
                <div class="articleEmbeddedAdBox" style="width:336px">
                 <hr class="articleAdRule"/>
                 <div class="articleAdHeader">

                <p>
                 Investigators then spoke with the other man who was with Yeager. He said the two had been drinking at a bar and left when it closed around 2 a.m. and returned to the trailer park where they live, documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Police also spoke with another resident who said she knew Yeager and that he had not returned to the trailer park since the alleged rape occurred.
                </p>
                <p>
                 "She said Yeager had been constantly calling her phone to see what has been going on with the investigation," documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 In an interview, the girl said she was sleeping on a recliner in her home that morning when Yeager grabbed her through a window, put his hand over her mouth and walked her to another house, documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Yeager kept telling her to shut up and threatened to kill the girl's family, documents state, adding that he continued to scream at her and struck her.
                </p>
                <p>
                 The girl also noted that Yeager had tattoos on his arms. Police then arranged a photo lineup, and the girl picked Yeager's tattoos from the lineup, documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Police interviewed Yeager on Dec. 7, and he said that on June 24, he was drinking at a bar, but that he fell asleep when he returned to his trailer. He denied raping the girl and said it had been "several months, it's almost been a year. I only had sex once at the trailer," documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 When police asked Yeager about the possibility of his DNA matching the DNA found in the girl, he said, "It better not. It's not possible."
                </p>
                <p>
                 Police then had Yeager do a swab, where DNA was collected from inside the cheek.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Lash said Wednesday that if Yeager had not agreed to do the swab, police had a search warrant to get it.
                </p>
                <p>
                 "We were getting that sample one way or another," he said.
                </p>
                <p>
                 On March 25, a state police lab reported to police that DNA from Yeager matched the DNA collected from the girl. The chances of it not being Yeager's are 1 in 10 quintillion or 1:10,000,000,000,000,000,000, documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 When confronted with this information on March 28, Yeager continued to deny raping the girl, documents state.
                </p>
                <p>
                 "He says nothing adds up," Northern Regional Detective Michael Hine wrote in charging documents. "I explain that everything adds up. The time it occurred, the fact that he left the residence and never returned, the tattoos, and especially the DNA."
                </p>
                <p>
                 Yeager was charged with rape of a child, aggravated indecent assault of a child, kidnapping, burglary, indecent assault of a person less than 13, and corruption of minors.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Bail was denied Wednesday during a video arraignment by District Judge Thomas Reilly, as Yeager was in York County Prison.
                </p>
                <p>
                 No amount of bail is sufficient to guarantee the defendant's presence at future court hearings, or the safety of the community," Reilly wrote in a court document.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Yeager's preliminary hearing on the charges is scheduled for May 7, according to Reilly's office.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Yeager was already in York County prison on $30,000 bail on charges of burglary, trespass and criminal mischief in a separate case. He is also being held on a $50,000 detainer from Adams County for escape, according to York County Prison.
                </p>

                 <b>
                  Suspect's criminal history

                </p>
                <p>
                 Yeager has an extensive criminal history in York and Adams counties, dating back to 1997, according to court records.
Among those charges:
                </p>
                <p>
                 Nov. 16 in York County: Charged with criminal trespassing and DUI in Hanover. He was released on $10,000 bail.

                </p>
                <p>
                 Dec. 14 in Adams County: Charged with escape and default in required appearance. He was committed to Adams County Prison on $50,000 bail.
                </p>
