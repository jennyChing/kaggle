             <div class="blog-header">
              <h2 class="blog-title">
               <a class="blog-title-link blog-link" href="http://www.sassypiehole.com/what-i-say/the-toughest-job-youll-never-have-unless-you-try">
                The toughest job you'll never have (unless you try)!
               </a>
              </h2>
              <p class="blog-date">
               <span class="date-text">
                3/6/2014 07:08:52
               </span>
              </p>
              <p class="blog-comments">
               <a class="blog-link" data-disqus-identifier="102600898309373487" href="http://www.sassypiehole.com/what-i-say/the-toughest-job-youll-never-have-unless-you-try#comments">
                Comments
               </a>
              </p>
             </div>
             <div class="blog-separator">
             </div>
             <div class="blog-content">
              <div>
               <div class="wsite-image wsite-image-border-none" style="padding-top:10px;padding-bottom:10px;margin-left:0;margin-right:0;text-align:center">
                <a href="/uploads/1/2/9/0/12900637/7448780_orig.png?663" onclick="if (!lightboxLoaded) return false" rel="lightbox">
                 <img alt="Picture" src="/uploads/1/2/9/0/12900637/7448780.png?663" style="width:auto;max-width:100%"/>
                </a>
                <div style="display:block;font-size:90%">
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="paragraph" style="text-align:left;">
               There's nothing quite like working from home... You get to wear pajamas, shower whenever you feel like it (or not), and no one is there to interrupt your day with silly questions about
               <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fy3rjQGc6lA" target="_blank" title="">
                TPS Reports
               </a>
               or complaints about their weekend. It's a good lifeif you can handle it, but it's not for everyone.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               When I started a business back in 2001, I had no idea what to expect. I was afraid that the everyday distractions might keep me from
               <em>
                getting anything done.
               </em>
               But it didn't take long for me to figure out how to get the most bang from my non-existent buck; and before I knew it, I had a full-time job, which I
               <span>
                sorta
               </span>
               wish I still had right now. *Cha-ching* While my friends would be gearing up to
               <em>
                clock
               </em>
               <em>
                out,
               </em>
               I would be starting my second shift. There were no cigarette breaks or story swapping in the lunchroom, I was at work; and no matter how meager the task was at hand, my boss was
               <strong>
                always
               </strong>
               burning a hole in the back of my neck with her
               <em>
                strictly business
               </em>
               eyes.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               It takes a lot of discipline to be your own boss. You have to be able to close the blinds on your personal life and work twice as hard as you ever did anywhere else; but the sense of accomplishment and pride you'll exude will far outweigh any regret you could ever have from not taking a chance.
               <span style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.5;">
                There's a fine line between working from home and just being at home; and the only way that you're ever going to get where you want is by trying to get there.
               </span>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               <strong>
                Can't
               </strong>
               <span>
                <span>
                 <span>
                  lives
                 </span>
                </span>
               </span>
               on
               <strong>
                Won't
               </strong>
               Street... Where do YOU live?
              </div>
