        <div class="article-titles">
         <h1 id="title">
          Khans of Tarkir Limited Set Review: Temur and Land
         </h1>
         <h2 class="article-column-name">
          <a href="/mtg/articles/tag/Limited_Set_Review">
           Limited Set Review
          </a>
         </h2>
         <div class="social-sharing">
          <div class="fb-share-button" data-href="http://www.cardagain.com/mtg/articles/842/Khans_of_Tarkir_Limited_Set_Review:_Temur_and_Land" data-type="button_count">
          </div>
          <div class="g-plusone" data-size="medium">
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="credits-wrapper">
         <div class="credits">
          <img alt="Christopher Bezerra-Clark" class="article-author-avatar" src="http://graph.facebook.com/10105757/picture?width=100&amp;height=100"/>
          <p>
           by
           <a href="/mtg/articles/author/1601">
            Christopher Bezerra-Clark
           </a>
          </p>
          <p class="article-created-date">
           Saturday, September 27, 2014
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="article-text">
         <p>
          Hello again! I am back here at CardAgain.com to discuss the newest expansion, Khans of Tarkir. Per usual I will discuss each card and the impact I believe it will have on the Khans Limited environment. For those who do not know me my name is Christopher Bezerra-Clark, better known as CBC. I have an unhealthy love for playing decks with Basic Mountains in them. I am an Arizona PTQ grinder (Best finish is Top 16) and had the honor of participating in Arizona's First Magic Invitational. I definitely favor Limited formats, hence I am here to offer my point of view on this ever changing landscape.
         </p>
         <p>
          During the review I will rate the cards using a traditional grading scale.
         </p>
         <p>
          A: Cards that are Limited bombs, these win the game almost every time they are cast, or greatly increase your chances to win. (
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Pack Rat" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_rtr_73.jpg" href="/mtg/card/rtr_en_73/Pack_Rat" target="_blank">
           Pack Rat
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Elspeth, Sun's Champion" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ths_9.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ths_en_9/Elspeth,_Sun's_Champion" target="_blank">
           Elspeth, Sun's Champion
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Triplicate Spirits" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_m15_40.jpg" href="/mtg/card/m15_en_40/Triplicate_Spirits" target="_blank">
           Triplicate Spirits
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          B: Cards that make me want to play their color. If I am already in that color, they will 100% always make the maindeck. (
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Wingsteed Rider" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ths_36.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ths_en_36/Wingsteed_Rider" target="_blank">
           Wingsteed Rider
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Stoke the Flames" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_m15_164.jpg" href="/mtg/card/m15_en_164/Stoke_the_Flames" target="_blank">
           Stoke the Flames
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          C: Cards that are filler. Most of your deck will be C graded cards. I am happy to play these cards but they do not pull me into their color. (
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Netcaster Spider" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_m15_186.jpg" href="/mtg/card/m15_en_186/Netcaster_Spider" target="_blank">
           Netcaster Spider
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Borderland Minotaur" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ths_114.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ths_en_114/Borderland_Minotaur" target="_blank">
           Borderland Minotaur
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          D: Just on the edge of playability. Probably won't make my deck. Sideboard only cards fall in this category. (
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Lost in a Labyrinth" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ths_52.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ths_en_52/Lost_in_a_Labyrinth" target="_blank">
           Lost in a Labyrinth
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Naturalize" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_142.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_142/Naturalize" target="_blank">
           Naturalize
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          F: Not playable at all. I never see myself casting the card in the format. (
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Heroes' Podium" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_bng_159.jpg" href="/mtg/card/bng_en_159/Heroes'_Podium" target="_blank">
           Heroes' Podium
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Karametra, God of Harvests" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_bng_148.jpg" href="/mtg/card/bng_en_148/Karametra,_God_of_Harvests" target="_blank">
           Karametra, God of Harvests
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          For the finial installment I will discuss Temur (green-blue-red) and take a look at the nonbasic lands for the set. The ability word for Temur is Ferocious. It cares about you controlling a creature with power 4 or greater. If that condition is met spells and creatures with Ferocious are improved.
         </p>
         <h1>
          Multicolor
         </h1>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Avalanche Tusker
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B-
         </h2>
         <p>
          A 5 mana 6/4 is well worth it already. The only way the guy will connect for damage is if your opponent has no untapped creatures. If they have any creatures able to block, Avalanche Tusker will begin to pick them off in combat. The 6 power means it should kill most anything, but with 4 toughness it trades with more.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Avalanche Tusker" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_166.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_166/Avalanche_Tusker">
            <img alt="Avalanche Tusker" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_166.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Bear's Companion
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B
         </h2>
         <p>
          Here we get 6 power and toughness spread over 2 creatures for our 5 mana. Again, 2 creatures for 1 card is always good. The bear token that comes into play is a 4/4 which is perfect for enabling Ferocious.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Bear's Companion" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_167.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_167/Bear's_Companion">
            <img alt="Bear's Companion" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_167.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Icefeather Aven
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B+
         </h2>
         <p>
          This card is super cool. If you have to, you can play it as a 2/2 flier for 2 mana. Most often it will played as a Morph for the opportunity to set your opponent back significantly.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Icefeather Aven" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_178.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_178/Icefeather_Aven">
            <img alt="Icefeather Aven" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_178.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Savage Knuckleblade
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B+
         </h2>
         <p>
          Savage Knuckleblade is a perfect mana sink for a Temur deck. It will can be a 4/4 on turn 3 and then threaten being a 6/6 for the rest of the game. If you untap with Knuckleblade he will be very hard to remove. In response to a kill spell just return to hand and play him with haste the next turn. As long as you are not being killed by fliers the game should devolve around Knuckleblade once its cast.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Savage Knuckleblade" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_197.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_197/Savage_Knuckleblade">
            <img alt="Savage Knuckleblade" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_197.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Snowhorn Rider
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C
         </h2>
         <p>
          A 5/5 trample for 6 mana with added utility of having a Morph cost of 5 seems like a good deal. My issue here is with the 3 color costs associated. As you will see later, we can get comparable creatures for just 1 color of mana. Since these are so much easier to cast I do not think Snowhorn Rider is worth the extra colors.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Snowhorn Rider" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_201.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_201/Snowhorn_Rider">
            <img alt="Snowhorn Rider" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_201.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Surrak Dragonclaw
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B+
         </h2>
         <p>
          Another creature giving us 6 power and toughness for 5 mana. This time it comes on an uncounterable body with flash. The uncounterable clause will rarely come up in limited, but flash is always relevant. It allows you to keep up mana and decide at the last moment when it is right to cast Surrak.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Surrak Dragonclaw" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_206.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_206/Surrak_Dragonclaw">
            <img alt="Surrak Dragonclaw" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_206.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Temur Ascendancy
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C
         </h2>
         <p>
          This Ascendancy is better the earlier you play it. By giving your creatures haste it helps to ease the loss of tempo from taking a turn off to cast it. Once you begin to cast your 4+ power creatures, you start to rip through your deck. It can be argued, that just by casting these creatures you are already winning, and the Ascendancy does not add to it. You may be better suited playing a card that immediately affects the board over this.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Temur Ascendancy" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_207.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_207/Temur_Ascendancy">
            <img alt="Temur Ascendancy" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_207.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Temur Charm
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C+
         </h2>
         <p>
          Probably the weakest of the Charm cycle. The removal mode requires you to have a creature. The falter mode doesn't work against opposing Temur decks. The counter mode will probably be the most used but it requires you to leave up 3 mana for a conditional counter. Granted, Temur Charm still has enough utility to warrant inclusion in your deck, it just doesn't feel as game changing as the others.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Temur Charm" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_208.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_208/Temur_Charm">
            <img alt="Temur Charm" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_208.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Trap Essence
         </h3>
         <h2>
          D+
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Trap Essence" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_209.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_209/Trap_Essence">
            <img alt="Trap Essence" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_209.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h1>
          Green
         </h1>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Alpine Grizzly
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C-
         </h2>
         <p>
          A fine curve filler that also enables Ferocious. It receives a minus because the 2 toughness means it is trading with Morphs.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Alpine Grizzly" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_127.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_127/Alpine_Grizzly">
            <img alt="Alpine Grizzly" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_127.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Awaken the Bear
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C
         </h2>
         <p>
          A fine combat trick, but is a little expensive at 3 mana.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Awaken the Bear" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_129.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_129/Awaken_the_Bear">
            <img alt="Awaken the Bear" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_129.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Feed the Clan
         </h3>
         <h2>
          D-
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Feed the Clan" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_132.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_132/Feed_the_Clan">
            <img alt="Feed the Clan" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_132.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Heir of the Wilds
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B
         </h2>
         <p>
          This is a fantastic 2-drop for green decks. It is as good on turn 2 as it is on turn 5. The fact this creature has deathtouch means it will at worst trade with anything it can enter combat with. Creatures that stay relevant throughout many stages in the game should be picked highly. This is no different.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Heir of the Wilds" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_134.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_134/Heir_of_the_Wilds">
            <img alt="Heir of the Wilds" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_134.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Highland Game
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C
         </h2>
         <p>
          This is a way for green decks to have an early game presence, and make sure they stay alive to cast the big green spells.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Highland Game" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_135.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_135/Highland_Game">
            <img alt="Highland Game" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_135.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Naturalize
         </h3>
         <h2>
          D
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Naturalize" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_142.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_142/Naturalize">
            <img alt="Naturalize" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_142.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Pine Walker
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B
         </h2>
         <p>
          I think this card is fanastic for Temur. It is a 5/5 for 5 mana. Even though it also has a Morph cost of 5, you will still choose to cast it as a Morph most of the time. There is always an opportunity to make your opponent believe they have a free attack only to be surprised by an unmorphed Pine Walker. Once the Pine Walker does its thing your opponent will have to respect every other Morph you have.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Pine Walker" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_143.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_143/Pine_Walker">
            <img alt="Pine Walker" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_143.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Rattleclaw Mystic
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B
         </h2>
         <p>
          This is another 2-drop that stays relevant through out the game. Early on during the developing stage is where this excels. But, if drawn late you can cast this face down for the chance to have a burst of mana the next turn. Unmorphing Rattleclaw Mystic and then tapping it for mana you will net 2 mana for that turn. That being said it is still a 2/1 for 2 which is fine too.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Rattleclaw Mystic" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_144.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_144/Rattleclaw_Mystic">
            <img alt="Rattleclaw Mystic" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_144.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Roar of Challenge
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C-
         </h2>
         <p>
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Lure" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_m12_183.jpg" href="/mtg/card/m12_en_183/Lure" target="_blank">
           Lure
          </a>
          effects are only effective when the game is at parity. I do not like to include cards in my deck that are only good in specific game states. This can be a sideboard card for a Temur mirror match. Roar of the Challenge can act as straight removal with the Ferocious clause, which is why it is a C- and not a D.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Roar of Challenge" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_145.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_145/Roar_of_Challenge">
            <img alt="Roar of Challenge" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_145.jpg" target="_blank"/>

         <h3>
          Savage Punch
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C
         </h2>
         <p>
          This is the type removal that green gets now. It is sorcery speed so be sure not to cast it into a removal spell and risk getting 2-for-1ed. As you are able to trigger Ferocious this becomes closer to consistent removal.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>

          D

         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="See the Unwritten" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_149.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_149/See_the_Unwritten">
            <img alt="See the Unwritten" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_149.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          Since there is nothing truly broken to get in this set. I would rather just have another creature instead of this card.
         </p>

          Seek the Horizon
         </h3>
         <h2>
          D
         </h2>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Seek the Horizon" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_150.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_150/Seek_the_Horizon">

          Temur Charger
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C
         </h2>
         <p>
          A 3/1 for 2 is adequate filler. Unless you really need the trample there does not seem to be a reason to cast this as a Morph.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>

          Trail of Mystery
         </h3>
         <h2>
          D
         </h2>
         <p>
          This card has me stumped. I want to believe if taken early it can enable a sweet, 5 color Morph deck. In my gut though, I think it will mostly be relegated to sideboards.
         </p>

          C+

          D

          Force Away
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C+
         </h2>
         <p>
          Bounce is going to be important in this set. It is a way to peek at your opponents Morph creatures before they can surprise you. Also since Morph adds a 3 mana "tax" to some creatures Force Away allows you to set your opponent back multiple turns worth of mana. The Ferocious clause is icing on the cake.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>

          C+
         </h2>
         <p>

         </h3>
         <h2>
          C-
         </h2>
         <p>
          Neither mode of Scaldkin is exciting. The first mode is as a 2/2 flier for 4. The second is as a 7 mana
          <a class="card-reference" data-card-name="Shock" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ddn_21.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ddn_en_21/Shock" target="_blank">
           Shock
          </a>
          . Both versions are serviceable, but there is so much power in this set that we can do better.

         <h2>
          C+
         </h2>
         <p>
          If you are heavy in blue then you can start to get use out of the activated ability. With enough blue mana Scion of Glaciers can attack and block as anywhere between a 2/5 to 6/1. If blue is not one of your primary colors then you should leave this card out of your main deck.

          For 6 mana you can have a 3/3 with first strike. That is a little over priced, but the caveat is you can cast this as Morph and flip it for 5 mana. A 3/3 with first strike matches up very well against opposing Morphs. It is unfortunate this doesn't trigger Ferocious, which is why it is receiving a minus.

         <h1>
          Land
         </h1>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h3>
          Fetches
         </h3>
         <h2>
          C
         </h2>
         <p>
          The fetchlands in this set are all ally colored. This means they only fit into one clan if you want to use them as color fixing.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Bloodstained Mire" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_230.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_230/Bloodstained_Mire">
            <img alt="Bloodstained Mire" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_230.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Refuges
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B-
         </h2>
         <p>
          These dual lands will be the main way to fix your colors in the format. They all come into play tapped and gain you a life, and there is 1 for each 2 color combination. The enemy color lands will likely be picked higher, due to the fact they can go into 2 clans. The life gain from these is not insignificant, and will favor midrange and control decks in the format by giving them more time.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Bloodfell Caves" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_frf_165.jpg" href="/mtg/card/frf_en_165/Bloodfell_Caves">
            <img alt="Bloodfell Caves" src="/cardImages/en_frf_165.jpg" target="_blank"/>
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h3>
          Trilands
         </h3>
         <h2>
          B
         </h2>
         <p>
          Here we get one land for each clan that produces their 3 colors. These will be high picks if you know because they can still fix for the 2 enemy colors if you do not go into that clan. For example, If I know I am going to be green and blue, I can take Frontier Bivouac and go into Temur or Sultai. If I go into Temur it obviously much better, but if I go into Sultai I still get a dual land.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <ul class="card-img-list">
          <li>
           <a class="card-img" data-card-name="Frontier Bivouac" data-img-src="/cardImages/en_ktk_234.jpg" href="/mtg/card/ktk_en_234/Frontier_Bivouac">
            <img alt="Frontier Bivouac" src="/cardImages/en_ktk_234.jpg" target="_blank"/>
