          <p>
           British Jews across the country will fast today to call for an end to hostilities in the Middle East.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           The day-long fast, which is supported by dozens of rabbis, is planned as part of a public effort to show unity in the fight against violence in the region.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           Using the Twitter hashtag #HungryforPeace, the campaign which started in Israel has gained considerable support in England.
          </p>
         </div>

           Yachad, a UK-based pro-Israel, pro-peace group, said the fast, in which hundreds are expected to take part, was a response to the recent escalation of violence in Israel, the West Bank and Gaza.
          </p>

          </p>
          <p>
           Hannah Weisfeld, director of Yachad, said: Through the fast, British Jews want to loudly and clearly call for de-escalation, return of calm, return to the negotiating table and the creation of two states for two peoples, the only way that can guarantee stability and security in the long run.
