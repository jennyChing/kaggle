        Colleen Green is an original

        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          Colleen Green isthat person you knew in high schoolthe one whowasnt supposed to tourthe country or get a record deal. Colleen Green wasnt supposed to make it.
         </p>
         <p>
          But she did, and she did it her way: Writing herown material; creating her own t-shirts (merchandise) in her bedroom; drawing her own album art; creating her own identity. Shes a recluse, almost anti-social if you ask her friends, but shes doing it her way, with a bud-leaf-bass, a Happy Birfday Jeff guitar, and oh yeah, a Yamaha RX-11 drum machine that she uses as abackup band.
         </p>
         <p>
          Did we mention she doesnt have a band? Sure, someone along the way told Colleen Green to get a drummer and a bass player, maybe even a second singer.But she was too focused on writing fuzzy Ramones-style tunes to give a shit.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_225910" style="width: 322px">
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Colleen-Green-Cujo-cover.jpg">
           <img alt="Colleen Green - Cujo cover" class="wp-image-225910" height="241" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Colleen-Green-Cujo-cover-585x564.jpg" width="312"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           CUJO (2011) EP Cover Art by Colleen Green
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Shes now signed to
          <a href="http://www.hardlyart.com/colleengreen.html" rel="nofollow">
           Hardly Art
          </a>
          (part of the a Sub Pop family), she also had a stint with Art Fag (who helped release her early material in 2011) and for the past year shes been opening upfor every garage or punk band in Los Angelesincluding playing the final Vivian Girls show in L.A.playing all the major Burger Records festivals, and becoming the talk of the town. Everyone wants to see Colleen Green, and usually if you go out to a show in L.A. that isnt heavy metal or rap, youll see her.
         </p>
         <p>
          But shes originally from Massachusetts. Living in Boston, she wrote a few songs on her war-torn Apple MacBook. A couple homemade tapes, one of which would become
          <em>
           Milo Goes to Compton
          </em>
          , got people talking in the Bay Area (her new home, for while). After a stint in Oakland, hanging with friends like Nobunny, she moved to Los Angeles with nothing but a guitar, and keys toan apartment where she was now using that Yamaha RX-11 to appease neighbors, which she nabbed at a garage sale (nobody uses RX-11s anymore, it mostly faded away in the 80s). Leaving Massachusetts, heading westthat was risk. After all, theses no case study for a Colleen Green. She only had her spiral notebook, some funny drawings, Garage Band on her MacBook, and some pop-punk tunes inspired by Blink 182,The Ramones, Nobunny, and being uncomfortable around weird boys.
         </p>
         <p>
          Her ethereal voice, touched by the stoned angels from the kingdom of slackers, sounded different. Her music is deliciously sweet like the donuts she would eat during songwriting breaks in West L.A. When she would book a show, she would create her own mercha variety of t-shirts with artwork inspired by doodling while stoned and listening to the Go-Gos or some 90s punk band she grew up on. She also brought tapes to every show, lots of them, which she made sure looked cool enough to buy (if only for the art work) and then the music would do the real selling. Then, with her debut LP
          <em>
           Sock it to Me
          </em>
          , released last year on Hardly Art, Colleen Green, the quiet girl who was too much of a burnoutto make it, was suddenly LAs itgirl.
         </p>
         <p>
          But she would sell so many more records with a full band, something youd expect to her from a suit.She never listened. Sure, shes a bit aloof and too laid-back to be a rock star, but Colleen Green is herself. She still rents video tapes like
          <em>
           Dumb and Dumberer
          </em>
          (because she cant afford the Blu-Ray); still uses that ratchetyRX-11; and she still does her thing without a manager, a publicist, oranyone telling her what to do. Shes an original, and her new album (slated for release in 2015) should be a noise-pop, 8-bit video game escape into psychedelic noise and pop, like the Go-Gos, but way more chill.
         </p>
