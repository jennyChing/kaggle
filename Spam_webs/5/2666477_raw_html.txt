         <p>
          We may never know the answer to the eternal question, If a tree falls in the woods and no one is there to hear it, does it still make a sound?but it turns out we do know the answer to the question, If a bunch of trees bloom into the shape of a giant swastika in the woods and no one is high up enough to see it, do they still look like a swastika? The answer is, yep, they do.
         </p>
         <p>
          Decades after World War II ended, several forests around Germany quietly bloomed into giant swastikas every autumn as deciduous larch trees bloomed into yellow and eventually brown, surrounded by pine trees that stay green all year. In the spring and summer months the swastikas would fade into the background, invisible even from the air. But the question of who planted them remains an unsolved mystery.
         </p>
         <p>
          In a retrospective on Germanys swastika forests,
          <a href="http://www.spiegel.de/fotostrecke/photo-gallery-swastikas-in-the-woods-fotostrecke-98824-8.html">
           Der Spiegel
          </a>
          points out that the an undiscovered forest swastika was uncovered in 1992 by Gnter Reschke, an intern for a landscaping company. He discovered the pattern of larches during routine work, commissioned a plane to fly overhead and photograph the forest, and voilaa swastika. He was dumbfounded. Do you see what this is? he asked his coworkers.
         </p>
         <p>
          Local forester Klaus Gricke measured the trees themselves and determined that they were in fact planted in the late 1930s, as Hitler was coming to power.
         </p>
         <p>
          And this actually isnt the first time a swastika forest was discovered. American troops discovered a swastika along with 1933 planted in a similar style in a forest in Hesse in the late 1970s:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/201768/germanys-insane-swastika-forests-are-still-an-unsolved-mystery/screen-shot-2013-07-08-at-10-19-49-am/" rel="attachment wp-att-201776">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2013-07-08 at 10.19.49 AM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-201776" height="461" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Screen-Shot-2013-07-08-at-10.19.49-AM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/201768/germanys-insane-swastika-forests-are-still-an-unsolved-mystery/screen-shot-2013-07-09-at-9-03-26-am/" rel="attachment wp-att-201781">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2013-07-09 at 9.03.26 AM" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-201781" height="398" src="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/Screen-Shot-2013-07-09-at-9.03.26-AM.png" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          But the question of who planted them remains a mystery. One local man claims to have helped plant them as part of the Hitler Youth, but his claims seem unverified. Another swastika forest discovered in Kyrgystan, formerly part of the USSR, is rumored to have been planted by German POWs, but again theres no proof.
         </p>
         <p>
          Though the prominent swastika in the Uckermark region pictured above was eventually cut down in 2000, occasionally new swastika formations will show up in planting pattern, a disturbing sign of neo-Nazis, like this one, just discovered in 2010:
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/201768/germanys-insane-swastika-forests-are-still-an-unsolved-mystery/screen-shot-2013-07-08-at-10-20-40-am/" rel="attachment wp-att-201775">

          </a>
         </p>
