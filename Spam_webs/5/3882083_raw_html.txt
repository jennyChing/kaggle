         <h1 class="entry-title">
          Ethereal Woven Metal Leaf and Seed Installations by Michelle Mckinney
          <span class="post-date">
           by
           <a href="http://thisiscolossal.com/about" rel="author external" title="Visit Christopher Jobsons website">
            Christopher Jobson
           </a>
           on May 15, 2014

         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="mckinney-3" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-43934" height="2011" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/mckinney-3.jpg" width="2048"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          U.K. artist
          <a href="http://www.michellemckinney.co.uk/" target="_blank">
           Michelle Mckinney
          </a>
          examines the contrast of manmade materials with forms of nature in her ethereal installations of leaves, seeds, and butterflies formed from handcut woven metal. The artist cuts each shape from copper, brass, or steel mesh which is then colored and assembled into the forms seen here. You can see more of her work over on
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/MichelleMckinneyArtist/photos_stream" target="_blank">
           Facebook
          </a>
          and in her
          <a href="http://www.michellemckinney.co.uk/portfolio.htm" target="_blank">
           portfolio
          </a>
          . (via
          <a href="/submissions/">
           Colossal Submissions
          </a>
          )
         </p>
