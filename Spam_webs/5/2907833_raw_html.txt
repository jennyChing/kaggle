          <p>
           The days of Prohibition are long gone, but the spirit of the 1920s is alive and well at Manhattans coolest hidden watering holes.
          </p>
          <p>
           Discreet doors, password policies, and next-level mixology are the name of the game at these hush-hush hangouts. Youre not a true NYC insider until youve wet your whistle at at least one.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            1.
            <a href="http://www.2ndflooronclinton.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.2ndflooronclinton.com/', '2nd Floor on Clinton');">
             2
             <sup>
              nd
             </sup>
             Floor on Clinton
            </a>
             Lower East Side
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/2458/" rel="attachment wp-att-2458">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2458" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2nd-floor-on-Clinton.jpg?679039" title="????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????????"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px; text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="http://www.2ndflooronclinton.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.2ndflooronclinton.com/', '2nd Floor On Clinton');">
             2nd Floor On Clinton
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           When Tony Powe  owner of Clinton Streets Barramundi  decided that his LES neighbors needed a place for good conversation and good cocktails, he converted the second floor of his original bar into a small, table service-only reading room with overflowing bookshelves, plush sofas, and an exceptional cocktail menu designed by Ektoras Binikos.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Enter through Barramundi at 67 Clinton Street (between Stanton and Rivington) and make your way to the back.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Get In:
           </strong>
           Push a bell to the left of the door marked private to be granted entry. You may even be greeted by Tony himself.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            2.
            <a href="http://blindbarber.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://blindbarber.com/', 'The Blind Barber');">
             The Blind Barber
            </a>
             East Village
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/blind-barber/" rel="attachment wp-att-2457">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2457" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Blind-Barber.jpg?679039" title="Blind-Barber"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px; text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="https://www.facebook.com/TheBlindBarber" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/TheBlindBarber', 'The Blind Barber');">
             The Blind Barber
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           During the day, all the action goes down in the front of The Blind Barber, which really does offer personal grooming services (and yes, they all include a free drink). But at night, its all about the hidden back room that serves up creative cocktails. Try the Sweeney Todd, their signature concoction made of Irish whiskey, egg white, lemon juice, honey, and Creole Bitters.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           The Blind Barber is located at 339 E. 10th Street, at the north-east corner of Tompkins Square Park. Enter through the classically designed front room (mirrored wall, striped pole, black combs in blue disinfectant) and proceed through the doorway at the back. Theres also a separate, vaguely marked entrance on the street.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            3.
            <a href="http://www.apothekenyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.apothekenyc.com/', 'Apotheke');">
             Apotheke
            </a>
             Chinatown
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/apotheke/" rel="attachment wp-att-2456">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2456" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/apotheke.jpg?679039" title="apotheke"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="http://www.apothekenyc.com" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.apothekenyc.com', 'Apotheke');">
             Apotheke
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           Apotheke is, naturally, an apothecary-themed cocktail bar inside a former opium den. In keeping with the theme, the list of over 250 specialty cocktails is divided into categories like Health and Beauty, Stimulants, Pain Killers, Stress Relievers, and Aphrodesiacs.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Apotheke can be found in central Chinatown, at 9 Doyers Street. Doyers  once known as The Bloody Angle and Murder Alley  is one of Manhattans only curved roads and was once the site of severe gang violence. Look for a red sign that says Gold Flower Restaurant.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Get In:
           </strong>
           Walk-ins are accepted until 9pm, but after that reservations are a must for guaranteed admittance. On some occasions, a password is required for entry.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            4.
            <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/little-branch-new-york" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.yelp.com/biz/little-branch-new-york', 'Little Branch');">
             Little Branch
            </a>
             West Village
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/little-branch/" rel="attachment wp-att-2455">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2455" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Little-Branch.jpg?679039" title="Little-Branch"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px; text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Little-Branch/41435210381" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/pages/Little-Branch/41435210381', 'Little Branch');">
             Little Branch
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           Little Branch is the third establishment from the famed drink slinger Sasha Petraske. As befits Petraskes liquid legacy, the cocktails are divine and served by suspenders-sporting bartenders to the sounds of (sometimes live) jazz. The atmosphere of Little Branch is a warmer version of its Petraske predecessor, Milk &amp; Honey.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Little Branch is hidden underground behind an unmarked metal door at the corner of Seventh Avenue and Leroy Street.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            5.
            <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/angels-share-new-york" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.yelp.com/biz/angels-share-new-york', 'Angels Share');">
             Angels Share
            </a>
             East Village
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/angels-share/" rel="attachment wp-att-2454">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2454" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Angels-Share.jpg?679039" title="Angel's-Share"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Angels-Share/534149856600355" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/pages/Angels-Share/534149856600355', 'Angels Share');">
             Angels Share
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           The drinks at Angels Share are strong, but the rule enforcement is even stronger. No standing is allowed and only groups of four or fewer get seated, ever. But if you manage to gain entry to this elegant drink parlor, youll be treated to exotic drinks, an angelic mural, and a cant-miss selection of bar snacks.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Head upstairs at yakitori restaurant Village Yokocho (8 Stuyvesant Street) and look for the unmarked door off the dining room.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            6.
            <a href="http://bathtubginnyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bathtubginnyc.com/', 'Bathtub Gin');">
             Bathtub Gin
            </a>
             Chelsea
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/bathtub-gin/" rel="attachment wp-att-2453">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2453" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/bathtub-gin.jpg?679039" title="bathtub-gin"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="http://bathtubginnyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://bathtubginnyc.com/', 'Bathtub Gin');">
             Bathtub Gin
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           Bathtub Gin is named after the beverage of choice (though it was sometimes poisonous and often led to blindness or death) in Prohibition-era America. The faux Victorian bar offers up the easy refinement of cocktail culture surrounded by damask wallpaper, pressed tin ceilings, and silk couches. And yes, there is a copper bathtub prominently displayed in the center of the bar.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Look for the red light glowing inside the Stone Street Coffee Company (132 Ninth Avenue), a tiny Manhattan outpost of the Brooklyn-based roaster. The bar is hidden behind a camouflaged door in the wall.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            7.
            <a href="http://www.garretnyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.garretnyc.com/', 'The Garret');">
             The Garret
            </a>
             West Village
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/the-garret/" rel="attachment wp-att-2452">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2452" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Garret.jpg?679039" title="The-Garret"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/the-garret-new-york" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.yelp.com/biz/the-garret-new-york', 'Yelp');">
             Yelp
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           Like the Brooklyn Heights Tavern that preceded it, The Garret is hidden above the West Village Five Guys. Unlike the bar that preceded it, The Garret is not a sports bar. Instead, The Garret has a refined, artsy loft feel with a (non-functioning) fireplace, paintings by street artist Bradley Theodore, and eclectic knickknacks that include a collection of hourglasses.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Stop for a burger at Five Guys, then head up the staircase located beyond the tables, condiment stand, and counter.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            8.
            <a href="http://www.backroomnyc.com/the-backroom-bar" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.backroomnyc.com/the-backroom-bar', 'The Back Room');">
             The Back Room
            </a>
             Lower East Side
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/the-back-room/" rel="attachment wp-att-2451">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2451" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/The-Back-Room.jpg?679039" title="The-Back-Room"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="http://www.backroomnyc.com/the-backroom-bar" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.backroomnyc.com/the-backroom-bar', 'The Back Room');">
             The Back Room
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           The Back Room is a 20s-style speakeasy which has the distinction of being in the same spot as an
           <em>
            actual
           </em>
           speakeasy from the 1920s. The bar pays fitting tribute to its ancestry with cocktails served in teacups and beer wrapped in brown paper bags. For an even more exclusive experience, try to find the VIP lounge hidden behind a trick bookcase.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           The Back Room is cleverly disguised as an old-fashioned toy store at 102 Norfolk Street called the Lower East Side Toy Company. Pass through the gate, head down the stairs, cross the alley, and walk up a second flight of stairs to enter the bar.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            9.
            <a href="http://redroomnyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://redroomnyc.com/', 'The Red Room');">
             The Red Room
            </a>
             East Village
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/red-room/" rel="attachment wp-att-2450">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2450" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Red-Room.jpg?679039" title="Red-Room"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="http://www.marchphoto.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.marchphoto.com/', 'Oleg March');">
             Oleg March
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           The Red Room is a newly opened speakeasy that was once an intimate black box-style theatre. The sexy club space features gangster-inspired libations, antique art and objects, and nightly performances from musicians and burlesque dancers.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           The Red Room is located above East Village institution KGB Bar at 85 E. 4
           <sup>
            th
           </sup>
           Street, on the third floor.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            10.
            <a href="http://www.raineslawroom.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.raineslawroom.com/', 'Raines Law Room');">
             Raines Law Room
            </a>
             Flatiron
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/raines-law-room/" rel="attachment wp-att-2449">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2449" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Raines-Law-ROom.jpg?679039" title="Raines-Law-ROom"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/raines-law-room-new-york" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.yelp.com/biz/raines-law-room-new-york', 'Yelp');">
             Yelp
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           Stepping into the Raines Law Room feels like stepping into the pages of
           <em>
            The Great Gatsby.
           </em>
           Named for an 1896 law meant to keep New Yorkers liquor consumption in check, the windowless space is perfectly decked out in antique furniture and a few charmingly garish touches. The cocktails, crafted by Meaghan Dorman, can be ordered by pressing a buzzer that calls a waitress to your table.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           The building at 48 W. 17
           <sup>
            th
           </sup>
           Street looks like a private residence, but dont be fooled by the unassuming, unmarked door.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Get In:
           </strong>
           Ring the buzzer at the door and pass the inspection of the discerning host.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            11.
            <a href="http://www.employeesonlynyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.employeesonlynyc.com/', 'Employees Only');">
             Employees Only
            </a>
             West Village
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p>
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/employees-only/" rel="attachment wp-att-2448">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2448" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Employees-Only.jpg?679039" title="Employees-Only"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <em>
            Image:
            <a href="https://www.facebook.com/EmployeesOnlyNYC" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/EmployeesOnlyNYC', 'Employees Only');">
             Employees Only
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           This spot has long been a local favorite known for its late-night eats. Until 3:30am, guests can snack on treats like bone marrow poppers, truffled grilled cheese, Reuben croquettes, and bacon wrapped lamb chops in between throwing back exceptional cocktails and having their fortunes read by the in-house tarot card reader.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           At 510 Hudson Street youll find a red neon sign advertising a psychic. On busy nights, there might also be a bouncer who will lead you through a curtain into the bar.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            12.
            <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/attaboy-new-york" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.yelp.com/biz/attaboy-new-york', 'Attaboy');">
             Attaboy
            </a>
             Lower East Side
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/attaboy/" rel="attachment wp-att-2447">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2447" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/Attaboy.jpg?679039" title="Attaboy"/>
           </a>
           <em style="text-align: right;">
            Image:
            <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/attaboy-new-york" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.yelp.com/biz/attaboy-new-york', 'Yelp');">
             Yelp
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           When Milk &amp; Honey closed on New Years Eve 2012, two longstanding M&amp;H bartenders rechristened the space as Attaboy. Fans of Milk &amp; Honey will recognize the snappily dressed staff, the lack of menus, and the so-good-they-could-be-crafted-from-angels-tears beverages. What they wont recognize is Attaboys refreshing no-reservations policy.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Look for the steel door at 134 Eldridge Street with AB on it and a neon A in the window.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Get In:
           </strong>
           Ring the buzzer to be ushered inside this drinking den.
          </p>
          <p>
           <br/>
           And of course
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3>
           <strong>
            13.
            <a href="http://pdtnyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://pdtnyc.com/', 'PDT');">
             PDT
            </a>
             East Village
           </strong>
          </h3>
          <p style="text-align: right;">
           <a href="https://www.upout.com/blog/new-york-city/13-secret-spots-for-clandestine-drinking-in-manhattan/attachment/pdt-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-2446">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2446" src="http://www.upout.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/PDT1.jpg?679039" title="PDT"/>
           </a>
           <em style="text-align: right;">
            Image:
            <a href="http://pdtnyc.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://pdtnyc.com/', 'PDT');">
             PDT
            </a>
           </em>
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            The Deal:
           </strong>
           Yes, its super famous and barely a secret anymore, but we couldnt end this list without paying tribute to PDT. Please Dont Tell is, oxymoronically, the best known secret bar in the city (maybe its the bacon-infused cocktails?). Be sure to stop in the bathrooms for the No Opium Smoking and No Cocaine Peddling signs, and grab a hot dog on the way out to kickstart the sobering up process.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Find It:
           </strong>
           Cruise into Crif Dogs at 113 St. Marks Place. See that phone booth off to the left? Get inside it.
          </p>
          <p style="padding-left: 30px;">
           <strong>
            How To Get In:
           </strong>
           PDT takes the prize for best hidden entryway in Manhattan. Pick up the phone inside the camera-installed booth, dial one, and cross your fingers that your call/prayers are answered. If you pass inspection, the back wall will open to let you into the adjacent bar.
          </p>
