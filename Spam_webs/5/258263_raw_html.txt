     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Live Adventures of the Waterboys
       </b>
      </i>
      is a concert recording, released by
      <a href="/wiki/The_Waterboys" title="The Waterboys">
       The Waterboys
      </a>
      in 1998.
      <a href="/wiki/Mike_Scott_(musician)" title="Mike Scott (musician)">
       Mike Scott
      </a>
      refers to this
      <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">
       album
      </a>
      as an "unofficial release" or
      <a href="/wiki/Bootleg_recording" title="Bootleg recording">
       bootleg recording
      </a>
      , but praises the recording period as a "classic" period for the Waterboys
      <sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_KTB">
       <a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Live_Adventures_of_the_Waterboys#endnote_KTB">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      . Most of the live songs on
      <i>
       The Live Adventures...
      </i>
      indeed already appeared on the bootlegs
      <i>
       A Golden Day
      </i>
      (1991) and
      <i>
       Born To Be Together
      </i>
      (1992).
      <sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_KTB">
       <a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Live_Adventures_of_the_Waterboys#endnote_KTB">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is the only Waterboys album on which member
      <a href="/wiki/Guy_Chambers" title="Guy Chambers">
       Guy Chambers
      </a>
      appears.
     </p>
     <p>
      According to Scott, the album was put out by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=New_Millennium_Communications&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="New Millennium Communications (page does not exist)">
       New Millennium Communications
      </a>
      (NMC). Scott claims that New Millennium stopped paying royalties to the band but continued to sell the album. The album is not listed on the band's own discography
      <sup class="reference" id="ref_KTBa">
       <a href="#endnote_KTBa">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The Waterboys released another, entirely official, live album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Karma_to_Burn_(The_Waterboys_album)" title="Karma to Burn (The Waterboys album)">
        Karma to Burn
       </a>
      </i>
      , through Scott's own record label, Puck records, in 2005.
     </p>
     <p>
      "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Girl_Called_Johnny" title="A Girl Called Johnny">
       A Girl Called Johnny
      </a>
      " was the first Waterboys
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_single" title="Music single">
       single
      </a>
      and was originally released on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Waterboys_(album)" title="The Waterboys (album)">
        The Waterboys
       </a>
      </i>
      (1983), as was "Savage Earth Heart." "The Thrill Is Gone" was originally released on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Pagan_Place" title="A Pagan Place">
        A Pagan Place
       </a>
      </i>
      (1984). "Medicine Bow", "This Is the Sea", "Be My Enemy", "Old England", "The Pan Within", "
      <a href="/wiki/The_Whole_of_the_Moon" title="The Whole of the Moon">
       The Whole of the Moon
      </a>
      " and "Spirit" were originally released on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/This_Is_the_Sea" title="This Is the Sea">
        This Is the Sea
       </a>
      </i>
      (1985). "Fisherman's Blues" and "We Will Not Be Lovers" were originally released on the album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Fisherman%27s_Blues" title="Fisherman's Blues">
        Fisherman's Blues
       </a>
      </i>
      (1988), over two years after these live performances. A studio version of "The Earth Only Endures" was released on
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Secret_Life_of_the_Waterboys_81-85" title="The Secret Life of the Waterboys 81-85">
        The Secret Life of the Waterboys 81-85
       </a>
      </i>
      compilation of outtakes, live tracks and demos in 1994. Studio versions of "Meet Me at the Station", "The Wayward Wind" and "Saints &amp; Angels" were released in the collection "Fisherman's Box" in 2013 - studio recordings from 1986-88. "Death Is Not the End" and "Purple Rain" make their only appearances on a Waterboys album here.
     </p>
     <p>
      The album features several covers and interpolations of songs by other artists including "
      <a href="/wiki/Because_the_Night" title="Because the Night">
       Because the Night
      </a>
      " by
      <a href="/wiki/Patti_Smith" title="Patti Smith">
       Patti Smith
      </a>
      which opens
      <i>
       The Pan Within
      </i>
      .
     </p>
