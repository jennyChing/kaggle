             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/facebook/">
                Facebook
               </a>
               broke a Guinness World Record this week  and no, this doesn't have to do with the company's
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/facebook-ipo/">
                much-anticipated IPO
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               Fans of the
               <a href="http://mashable.com/category/zynga/">
                Zynga
               </a>
               game
               <em>
                FrontierVille
               </em>
               sought and set a World Record, posting over 1,001,600 comments on a single item. If nothing else gives away the string of more than 1 million comments, the name of the group  Ffgpioneers/gwr/attempt  states the purpose of the charade was to land in
               <i>
                The Guinness Book of World Records
               </i>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               Tracey Hodgson's Oct. 30, 2011
               <a href="http://www.facebook.com/groups/234321533289185/" target="_blank">
                post
               </a>
               received comments from 107
               <em>
                FrontierVille
               </em>
               fans over the last three months, more than doubling the previous record set by Italy's Roberto Esposito, according to
               <a href="http://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/news/2012/1/frontierville-fan-sets-facebook-record-for-most-comments/" target="_blank">
                Guinness World Records
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               The record setting post read, "hi all!!! , hope everyone is ok today , this is 'the one', please talk about anything you like here, whatever you want to talk about, change topic whenever you like also, just have fun , . so how are you all today?( please someone speak quick or i'll have no nails left!!!) ;-) x x x."
              </p>
              <p>
               Hodgson says she took up
               <em>
                FrontierVille
               </em>
               after her daughter started playing on her Facebook account. She wanted to set the record to show the strength of the
               <em>
                FrontierVille
               </em>
               community, with which she had become deeply involved.
              </p>
              <p>
               "I began to play myself, and then got involved in helping with the game's community groups as an administrator," she told Guinness. "My friend Nattie Murphy saw the previous record and suggested I try to break it  it sounded like a great idea for our group to show what we are capable of when we work together"
              </p>
              <p>
               According to Hodgson, the record was set largely due to four of the 107 commenters.
              </p>
              <p>
               What do you think, is this a proud display of the
               <em>
                FrontierVille
               </em>
               community's unity or a big waste of time? Let us know in the comments.
              </p>

                    <i>
                     Wolfenstein 3D
                    </i>
                    and
                    <i>
                     Doom
                    </i>
                    , also had a mega hit with
                    <i>
                     Quake
                    </i>
                    in 1996.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Quake's online community remains passionate to this day, and id breathed new life into the aging title with Quake LIVE, a free, web-based portal where fans can frag each other online to their hearts' content.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>

                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   7.
                   <a href="http://mashable.com/2011/10/10/facebook-dungeons-and-dragons/">
                    Dungeons &amp; Dragons: Heroes of Neverwinter
                   </a>
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    <i>
                     Forgotten Realms
                    </i>
                    is a longstanding game world for tabletop
                    <i>
                     Dungeons &amp; Dragons
                    </i>
                    that found its digital legs in 2002's
                    <i>
                     Neverwinter Nights
                    </i>
                    from BioWare.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    After a successful sequel and the birth of a strong modding community, the franchise has made the leap to Facebook with an experience more reminiscent of the board games than the PC versions.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="512798f497b2f81f552ab8a8" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzAxLzIxLzEzXzMwXzM4XzE4N19maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/e237836a.jpg">

                  <img alt="3aa32939" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzAxLzIxLzEzXzMwXzM4XzE4N19maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDg1MD4KZQlqcGc/3aa32939.jpg"/>
                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   8.
                   <a href="http://mashable.com/2011/06/24/team-fortress-2-free-valve/">
                    Team Fortress 2
                   </a>
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    Beloved multiplayer FPS
                    <i>
                     Team Fortress 2
                    </i>
                    was made free-to-play by Valve last summer, and new players flocked to the addictive title.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    The game is supported by downloadable content: hats and other accessories that let you customize your character, but don't affect the competitive balance.
                   </p>
                  </div>
                  <div class="credit">
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </li>
                <li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="512798f497b2f81f552ab8a9" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzAxLzIxLzEzXzMwXzM4XzIzNl9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/f7c06ff7.jpg">

                 </figure>
                 <div class="meta">
                  <h2 class="title">
                   9.
                   <a href="http://www.atari.com/arcade/arcade/asteroids-online" target="_blank">
                    Asteroids Online
                   </a>
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    Atari has been busy getting its classic back catalog onto the web. A free portal lets you play updated versions of the old console standbys, including the inertia-bending
                    <i>
                     Asteroids
                    </i>
                    .
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    You can also check out the free iPhone and iPad app,
                    <i>
                     <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/id474572720" target="_blank">
                      Asteroids: Gunner
                     </a>
                    </i>
                    .
                   </p>

                    World of Warcraft
                   </a>
                  </h2>
                  <div class="caption">
                   <p>
                    Perhaps a "modern classic," depending on your perspective (the game was released in 2004, after all), Blizzard announced last summer that new adventurers could play for free up to level 20.
                   </p>
                   <p>
                    Tread with caution, though. It may not be so easy to quit when it comes time to pay-up for a subscription.
