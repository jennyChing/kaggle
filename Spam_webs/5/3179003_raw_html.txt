        <div class="body" itemprop="description">
         <strong class="db" itemprop="headline">
          Turn a pretty Barbie doll into a brain-eating zombie.
         </strong>
         <p itemprop="text">
          Did you ever want to turn your pretty Barbie doll into a brain-eating zombie? No? Well, now you're going to learn how anyway. The cool guys at CO+K asked me to write a little guide on how i went about making my Barbie of The Undead. Im not going to claim Im the first to do this, youll find lots of them on Google. But for some reason people seemed to like my zombie, when they really should be scared to death. Really.
         </p>

        <div class="span7">
         <ul class="steps" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ItemList">
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34775/full_1_1253800852.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 1" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34775/normal_1_1253800852.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body small">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 1
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The doll
               <br/>
               First you'll need to get hold of a suitable doll. The cool thing to do is stealing it from a sister or cousin or something, but if you want to be dull, you can buy one. Youll find them in all toy stores, and also find tons of them on ebay, flea markets etc.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34776/full_2-scalp_1253800899.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 2" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34776/normal_2-scalp_1253800899.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body small">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 2
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The plan
               <br/>
               When youve got your doll you should make a plan. Ive seen quite a few zombie Barbies now, and most of them just end up a ugly mess. Its important to keep her looking like a pretty girl  a pretty dead girl. You do not want to cut her in pieces and smear blood all over her. Take a look at some zombie flicks for inspiration. Ill just assume your Barbie-destruction-plan is similar to mine, and explain what I did.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34777/full_3-scalped_1253800934.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 3" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34777/normal_3-scalped_1253800934.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body small">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 3
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               I wanted to give her a few wounds. One on her jaw  as if shed been making out with her zombie Ken when he suddenly decided to have a snack. The other one on her arm. I kind of imagined she took a bite of her own arm...
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="advert-well">
           <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-6287412971271285" data-ad-format="rectangle" data-ad-slot="9385019883" id="Project_Content">
           </ins>
           <script>
            (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
           </script>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34778/full_4-sculpey_1253801016.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 4" class="step-image" height="133" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34778/normal_4-sculpey_1253801016.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body small">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 4
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The face
               <br/>
               So I was going to tear part of her face off. The only problem is that barbies are pretty darned empty-headed. I needed to fill her up to get the desired effect. I used a paper knife to make a small surgical incision in the back of her head. This made a slot for me to fit a scull.
              </p>
              <p>
               I often use this clay called Super Sculpey. Its great for making all sorts of stuff. Its easy to shape, light of colour, and bakes in a regular oven in just 15 minutes.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34779/full_5-scullpey_1253801028.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 5" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34779/normal_5-scullpey_1253801028.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 5
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               Now for the skull. I wasnt sure how much of her face I was going to cut off, so I made more details to the skull than was actually visible on the finished doll.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34780/full_6-whitey_1253801067.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 6" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34780/normal_6-whitey_1253801067.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 6
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The scull was painted with a few strokes of regular acrylic paint. Luckily the scull was a perfect fit  it wouldnt be easy getting it out again.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34781/full_7-missing-cheek_1253801099.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 7" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34781/normal_7-missing-cheek_1253801099.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body small">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 7
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               After the skull was in place, I used my knife to cut up her cheek  starting with the side of her mouth, and slowly cutting off small slices of skin until i was pleased. (Now read this paragraph again, and imagine Im a psycho.)
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34782/full_4_1253801121.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 8" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34782/normal_4_1253801121.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 8
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The arm
               <br/>
               I then started cutting up her arm. You cant see it clearly on the photo, but as the arm is not hallow, i could just carve out a bone. Youll see it better on the finished doll.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="step instruction" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <div class="body small">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 9
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The paint
               <br/>
               Painting a zombie is fairly simple. Youll need to mix a few colours to get your rotten flesh tone. I used acrylics paint, and also mixed it with some water to get it very thin. I wanted the original skin colour to still be visible. Her hair was also randomly cut, and I actually took some dirt from my back yard for the finishing touch.
              </p>
              <p>
               Lastly youll want some blood. Again  the important thing is not to overdo it. And try to think how blood would be running on a real person. But dont do too much research.
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34784/full_13-new-base_1253801184.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 10" class="step-image" height="300" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34784/normal_13-new-base_1253801184.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body small">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 10
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The base
               <br/>
               When finishing up, I realized Barbies are not very good at standing. I guess theyve been walking on heels too much. I made my doll a base (again of Super Sculpey) to fit her feet. This was done in a hurry, and I wasnt pleased with it at all, so recently I made her a new one. This is actually the first time I show anybody this updated version, so I hope you like it!
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
          </li>
          <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
           <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
            <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34785/full_18-latest-photos_1253801199.jpg" itemprop="image">
             <img alt="How to decorate a Barbie doll. Barbie Of The Undead - Step 11" class="step-image" height="133" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/22619/steps/34785/normal_18-latest-photos_1253801199.jpg" width="400"/>
            </a>
            <div class="body">
             <span class="number" itemprop="position">
              Step 11
             </span>
             <div itemprop="text">
              <p>
               The end
               <br/>
               Now you should be ready to beautify some way too pretty dolls. Good luck!
              </p>
             </div>
            </div>
           </div>
