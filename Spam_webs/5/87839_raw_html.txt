      </h2>

       <p>
        Version 1.0.1 of OpenSSL introduced the vulnerability known as heartbleed, and was released on March 14, 2012.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content_2_right">
       <img src="images/content_2_2.png"/>

     <div class="content_3" itemprop="articleBody">
      <h2>
       HOW IT WORKS
      </h2>
      <div class="content_3_block_top content_3_1">
       <img src="images/content_3_1.png"/>
       <p>
        A computer that is on a secure connection to a server will send out a request to confirm that the connection is still active.
       </p>
       <div class="triangle_1">
       </div>
       <div class="triangle_2">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="content_3_block_top content_3_2">
       <img src="images/content_3_2.png"/>
       <div class="heart_beet_lines">
        <img class="beat_lines" data-1600="left: -5px;" data-3000="left: -550px;" src="images/heart_beat_lines.png"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        This secure connection (SSL/TSL), is called a heartbeat. It includes two things: a payload, and padding.
       </p>
       <div class="triangle_3">
       </div>
       <div class="triangle_4">
       </div>
       <div class="triangle_5">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="clear">
      </div>
      <div class="content_3_block_bottom content_3_3">
       <img src="images/content_3_3.png"/>
       <p>
        The server takes that request and stores the data.
       </p>
       <div class="triangle_6">
       </div>
       <div class="triangle_7">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="content_3_block_bottom content_3_4">
       <img src="images/content_3_4_bottom.png"/>
       <img class="content_3_4_blood_1" src="images/content_3_4_blood_1.png"/>
       <img class="content_3_4_blood_2" src="images/content_3_4_blood_2.png"/>
       <img class="content_3_4_blood_3" src="images/content_3_4_blood_3.png"/>
       <img src="images/content_3_4_top.png"/>
       <div class="heart_beet_lines">
        <img class="beat_lines" data-1600="left: -5px;" data-3000="left: -550px;" src="images/heart_beat_lines.png"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        Then it returns that same packet of data.
       </p>
       <div class="triangle_8">
       </div>
       <div class="triangle_9">
       </div>
       <div class="triangle_10">
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>

      <h2>
       THIS IS WHERE THE
       <span>
        BLEEDING
       </span>
       HAPPENS
      </h2>
      <p>
       Servers using the protocol do not check to confirm that the packet of data actually matches the size indicated.
      </p>
     </div>
