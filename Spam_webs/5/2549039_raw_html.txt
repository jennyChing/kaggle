        <h1>
         Forklift safety: Do not operate machinery when loads are unstable and high above the ground
        </h1>
        <p>
         What kind of accident is about to happen?
         <br/>
         Animal feed bags are being loaded onto a truck using a forklift. The forklift operator is trying to back up from the site where the feed bags were hung onto the two forks.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Can you predict whats about to happen?
        </h2>
        <p>
         Look what happened!
         <br/>
         The driver suddenly turned the steering wheel to the right while the forklift was raised. The forklift tumbled over, pinning the operator beneath it.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3669" style="width: 480px">
         <a href="http://www.tonythanh.com/construction-safety-training/forklift-safety-not-operate-machinery-loads-unstable-high-ground/attachment/the-driver-suddenly-turned-the-steering-wheel/" rel="attachment wp-att-3669">
          <img alt="The driver suddenly turned the steering wheel " class="size-full wp-image-3669" height="333" src="http://www.tonythanh.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/The-driver-suddenly-turned-the-steering-wheel.png" width="470"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The driver suddenly turned the steering wheel
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Read more:
         <a href="http://www.tonythanh.com/construction-safety-training/forklift-safety-loading-unloading-use-appropriate-loading-machinery/">
          Forklift safety of loading and unloading: Use appropriate loading machinery
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Tips for preventing similar accidents
        </h2>
        <p>
         Do not operate machinery when loads are unstable and high above the ground (loads should be carried about 15cm above the ground).
         <br/>
         Do not turn or brake suddenly while operating machinery.
         <br/>
         Use designated attachments, such as a crane arm, to transport loads.
        </p>
