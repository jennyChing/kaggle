       <h2 class="post-title">
        What is Diquat Contamination?
       </h2>
       <p align="justify">
        There are plenty of substances that can pollute the water and make it unsafe for drinking and other uses. Plenty of these substances or water contaminants comes from human activities. Industries that are necessary to sustain the modern world use materials and byproducts that, when released to the environment, can do irreversible damage. Among various industries, agriculture is one of the leading causes of water contamination. The way fertilizers, pesticides and herbicides makes it very easy for contamination to happen.
       </p>
       <figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="428" data-orig-width="640">
        <div align="center">
         <img data-orig-height="428" data-orig-width="640" src="http://41.media.tumblr.com/def8faada2e82a59067e13af29702e8f/tumblr_inline_nmrvdnBqIR1s52do1_540.jpg"/>
        </div>
       </figure>
       <p align="justify">
        Those who are using a private well and are near an agricultural land are
 prone to diquat contamination. In order to prevent contamination from 
happening, regular
        <a href="http://callidus.se/bestall-vattenanalys/" target="_blank">
         <b>
          water analysis
         </b>
        </a>
        must be conducted. The owner of the agricultural land must also provide
 a list detailing the substances used in their operations.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        Diquat is one of the many contaminants that comes from agricultural practices. A type of herbicide, diquat has been extensively used in North America and some other regions in the world for almost half a century now. It is used for controlling weeds in crops and potato plantations. It is also used for wilting and drying out silage during the harvesting of cotton and various oil seed crops. Diquat is a very useful substance to say the least.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        However, research conducted regarding consumption of water contaminated by diquat has been found to increase the likelihood of people acquiring cataract. Diquat is usually in the form of crystal with a clear or yellowish tint. If dissolved in water, the color of the solution turns to dark brownish red.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        If a water is found to be contaminated by
        <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diquat" target="_blank">
         <b>
          diquat
         </b>
        </a>
        , the use of a water filter with a granular activated carbon can remove the contaminant and make the water safe again for drinking and other uses.
        <br/>
       </p>

       <h2 class="post-title">
        Bacteria in Water
       </h2>
       <p align="justify">
        There are plenty of contaminants that are present in your drinking water. Bacteria is one of the most common contaminants. The ubiquitous nature of these microorganisms makes bacterial contamination very hard to prevent. This is also the reason why most drinking water contain bacteria, albeit within safety level.
       </p>
       <figure data-orig-height="418" data-orig-width="270">
        <div align="center">
         <img data-orig-height="418" data-orig-width="270" src="http://40.media.tumblr.com/86d3bf98790f2bab36e7ee16bc55ad7e/tumblr_inline_nm0kk68Typ1s52do1_500.jpg"/>
        </div>
       </figure>
       <p align="justify">
        Bacterial presence in water becomes a problem if the number of microbes are way above the acceptable contamination levels.
        <a href="http://www.callidus.se/Vattenproblem/Vattenproblem/Bakterier.aspx" target="_blank">
         <b>
          Bacteria in water
         </b>
        </a>
        serve as an indicator instead of being the actual threat. Public water with a high count of bacteria is a sign of a larger problem that the delivery system for the water has been compromised and that other more harmful microbes or contaminants are possibly in the water.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        Public water with high levels of bacterial contamination has to be tested to further determine the cause and extent of the problem. During this time, the public water supplier are responsible for providing households with safe water through other means.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        The problem with this type of contamination is that the detection of the unsafe condition of the water happens after people have consumed the water, increasing the chances of people acquire illness due to pathogens in the water.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        A way to avoid this problem is through the installation of a

         </b>
        </a>

       <h2 class="post-title">
        Abnormal pH Value of Water
       </h2>
       <p align="justify">
        The
        <a href="http://www.callidus.se/Vattenproblem/Vattenproblem/L%C3%83%C2%A5gtpHsurtvattengr%C3%83%C2%B6nautf%C3%83%C2%A4llningar.aspx" target="_blank">
         <b>
          pH value of water
         </b>
        </a>
        is the measure of its acidity or alkalinity. It is one of the basic indicators for water quality. The acidity or alkalinity of the water can affect its taste and smell.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <figure data-orig-height="372" data-orig-width="234">
        <div align="center">
         <img data-orig-height="372" data-orig-width="234" src="http://41.media.tumblr.com/8f6ec6b22104d249cc158154ca6acaaa/tumblr_inline_nm0k99Bhbh1s52do1_500.jpg"/>
        </div>
       </figure>
       <p align="justify">
        Abnormal pH levels can damage water pipes and cause heavy metals from the pipe to leak into the water and making it unsafe. It can also damage sensitive components of home appliances such as water heaters, dish washers or washing machines.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        An abnormal pH value indicates the presence of foreign substances or contaminants in the water. Aside from the aforementioned changes in taste and smell, water with abnormal pH often exhibit a discoloration and staining of fixtures such as faucets or sinks.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        In order to revert the pH of the water to its normal values, the contaminants that causes the abnormal change must be removed from the water. If you are using public water, the first step to dealing with this problem is by contacting the supplier. It is the suppliers responsibility to ensure that the quality of the water is suitable for drinking and other uses.
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        The problem gets trickier when you are using a private well. A well with an abnormal pH has likely been contaminated by naturally occurring minerals or compounds underground. Preventing this type of contamination is impossible. A well owner has the choice of changing the water source or to install a
        <a href="http://www.callidus.se" target="_blank">
         <b>
          water purification system
         </b>
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        It can be difficult for someone who is used to drinking water from well to adjust to the flavor of public water that contains disinfectants. In this case, the best solution is to install a home water treatment system.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p align="justify">
        Have the water tested to identify the exact cause of the abnormal pH and then install a
        <b>
         filter system
        </b>
        that removes the contaminant responsible for the problem.
       </p>
