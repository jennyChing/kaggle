          <p>
           There are thousands of people playing on different
           <a href="http://partners.fanduel.com/processing/clickthrgh.asp?btag=a_5505b_2" target="_blank" title="Play FanDuel">
            FanDuel
           </a>
           tournaments every single day. This particular article is going to focus on those who like to play in football tournaments on the one week fantasy league website. If you like playing in fantasy football
           <a href="http://partners.fanduel.com/processing/clickthrgh.asp?btag=a_5505b_2" target="_blank" title="Play FanDuel">
            FanDuel
           </a>
           leagues then you will probably want to look over the different tips Im going to provide you with in this post. If you dont already know them then I suggest you pay attention and keep them in mind the next time youre drafting a league online. It doesnt really matter which fantasy football website you play on. These tips work on
           <a href="http://partners.fanduel.com/processing/clickthrgh.asp?btag=a_5505b_2" target="_blank" title="Play FanDuel">
            FanDuel
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://partners.draftkings.com/aff_c?offer_id=124&amp;aff_id=178867" target="_blank" title="Play DraftKings">
            DraftKings
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://www.draftday.com/?affiliateRefCode=mixing" target="_blank" title="Play DraftDay">
            DraftDay
           </a>
           , or any of the others out there!
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Always Look for All Purpose Running Backs
           </strong>
           <br/>
           One important factor to remember about these one week fantasy websites is that theyre all PPR (Points Per Reception) leagues. This means you get points for every pass that gets caught, whether its a wide receiver or a running back catching the ball. With that in mind, some running backs get passed the ball more often that others. These are the kinds of running backs you want to target because not only do they get you rushing points, they get you passing points as well. Along with that, all purpose running backs usually dont share the backfield with other running backs. That means they get you more rushing yards as well! Matt Forte, Arian Foster, and DeMarco Murray would all be prime examples of
           <em>
            all purpose
           </em>
           running backs.
          </p>
          <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle" style="margin: 5px; padding: 0px;">
           <center>

             <img border="" src="http://partners.fanduel.com/processing/impressions.asp?btag=a_5505b_879" style="border:none;width:468px;height:60px;"/>

           </center>
          </div>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Look at Wide Receiver Targets
           </strong>
           <br/>
           You should always try to do a little research over different players before you add them. Dont just add a player because you see the on TV each weekend. When it comes to wide receivers you can often get good ones cheap. Your better off spending more money on good quality running backs than receivers. When youre researching different receivers you dont just want to look at their touchdowns. Touchdowns are never an absolute guarantee! Instead, you should be looking at how many targets each wide receiver is getting. A wide receiver that gets 5 targets and 3 catches per week on average is nothing compared to one that gets 8 targets and 5 catches. Wide receivers can have on and off days, especially when they are playing good pass defenses. With that in mind, looking at the number of targets can easily tell you which receiver the quarterback is connecting with the most. If they get lots of targets then youll get extra points for each pass which gets caught, and also for the touchdowns and yards as well!
          </p>
          <p>
           These two simple
           <a href="http://partners.fanduel.com/processing/clickthrgh.asp?btag=a_5505b_2" target="_blank" title="Play FanDuel">
            FanDuel
           </a>
           tips might not seam like much. They may even seam like common sense. But, youd be surprised how many people dont take this kind of information into consideration when picking their team. This gives you the upper hand every weekend!
          </p>
