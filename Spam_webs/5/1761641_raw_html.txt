       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Is Facebook Sharing Updates From Secret Groups?
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="byline">
          <i class="fa fa-user">
          </i>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.bloggingbasics101.com/author/melanie-nelson/">
            Melanie Nelson
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="posted-on">
          <i class="fa fa-calendar">
          </i>
          <a href="http://www.bloggingbasics101.com/is-facebook-sharing-updates-from-secret-groups/" rel="bookmark">
           <time class="entry-date published" datetime="2012-10-02T12:41:08+00:00">
            October 2, 2012
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="comments-link">
          <i class="fa fa-comments">
          </i>
          <a href="http://www.bloggingbasics101.com/is-facebook-sharing-updates-from-secret-groups/#comments">
           21 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
       </header>
       <div class="clearfix entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         I received an email today that has me concerned about Facebook, how Facebook shares our information, and what our expectations of privacy are.
        </p>
        <p>
         My friend received a screenshot from another friend that showed her in the Facebook widget on an ABC news page. I went to the URL in the screenshot and found the same thing:
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.bloggingbasics101.com/2012/10/is-facebook-sharing-updates-from-secret-groups/facebook-sharing-secret-group/" rel="attachment wp-att-4456">
          <img alt="Is Facebook Sharing Secret Group Updates" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4456" height="423" onerror="javascript: wp_404_images_fix = window.wp_404_images_fix || function(){}; wp_404_images_fix(this);" src="http://1pwuq82w8fex3688f429axe1.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/Facebook-Sharing-Secret-Group.png" title="Facebook-Sharing-Secret-Group" width="689"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Why is this a problem? Because my friend shared that video link in a secret Facebook group. If youre not familiar with Facebook groups, there are three types:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Open groups
          </strong>
          are just that: open. Anyone can find the group via search, they can see whos in the group, and they can see what members post.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Closed groups
          </strong>
          allow people to see the group (find via search) and whos in the group. However, if youre not in the group, you cant see what people post.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Secret groups
          </strong>
          are stealth. Only members of the group can find and see the group, the members list, or posts made by members. Anyone outside of the group cannot see a thing.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         So, if my friend posted the video link to a secret group, why is it showing up in a widget on an ABC news site? Ive tried to find an answer and Im completely stumped. The widget is pulling in updates from the Ticker and is showing me people connected with me. I know Amy, Lisa, and Deb (and obviously my friend whose name is blurred for privacy  all of whom are referenced in the image above). But that update shouldnt have shown in my Ticker OR in the ABC widget
         <em>
          because its in a private Facebook group
         </em>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         I also looked at these possibilities:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Did she or I have an app installed that tracked what we read?
          </strong>
          Many sites have these (e.g., Huffington Post), but alas, neither of us has any sort of social reader installed.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Was the friend who sent her the original screenshot in her secret group?
          </strong>
          Nope. And neither am I, yet I can see that she shared the video.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Does the friend have her Ads shown by 3rd Parties privacy set to no one?
          </strong>
          She originally had friends as the setting, but then changed it to no one. Her update still appears on the ABC news site even after the change. But even so, remember,
          <strong>
           this was a status update from a private group.
          </strong>
          It should not have been shown anywhere.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Did she share the video from ABC?
          </strong>
          When I clicked on the link in the Facebook widget, I was taken to http://abcnews.go.com/GMA/video/psys-gangnam-style-dance-takes-gma-17217294?fb_ref=abc-fb-recs where I could view the video. Except that my friend didnt share the video from ABC. Heres a screenshot of what she did share in her secret group  you can clearly see she shared the video from YouTube:
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.bloggingbasics101.com/2012/10/is-facebook-sharing-updates-from-secret-groups/facebook-secret-group-status-update/" rel="attachment wp-att-4462">
          <img alt="Facebook secret group status update shown publicly" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4462" height="220" onerror="javascript: wp_404_images_fix = window.wp_404_images_fix || function(){}; wp_404_images_fix(this);" src="http://1pwuq82w8fex3688f429axe1.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/facebook-secret-group-status-update.png" title="facebook-secret-group-status-update" width="572"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         So why is the video re-routing you to a page on ABC? Why is her status update to a private Facebook group showing to her friends on another site?
        </p>
        <p>
         Last week it came out that Facebook may be publishing private messages that you sent before 2010. Now, I know there is some debate about that. Some people havent seen those private messages on their Timelines, but I know several people who have. They see sensitive information  not just addresses and phone numbers, but friends admitting they are gay but havent come out to their family or someone admitting they cheated on a girlfriend/boyfriend. Those are hardly items that one would post on a Wall. The people who have reported seeing those kinds of items swear they were PMs at the time. It didnt happen on my Timeline, but people I trust to know the difference between a PM and a Wall post are saying they
         <em>
          do
         </em>
         see it. I was content to let it go because I dont usually share things of high intimacy on Facebook. I checked my Timeline and none of my friends had anything incriminating either, so I let it stand. I took the controversy as another warning to watch what and how I share on social media. And then I saw this issue of the wrong kind of status update showing up in the wrong place.
        </p>
        <p>
         Look, you all know Im a fan of Facebook in general. I use it every day, I wrote a book about it, I speak at conferences about it, I consult about it. Its a huge part of my career as a social media strategist. But even I dont understand whats going on here. These apparent privacy breeches need to be addressed and quick. Whats your take?
        </p>
        <h2>
         Update
        </h2>
        <p>
         Be sure to read part 2 of this issue (I explain
         <a href="http://www.bloggingbasics101.com/2012/10/is-facebook-sharing-your-secret-group-updates-no-i-dont-think-so-part-2/">
          why ABC and Facebook may
          <em>
           not
          </em>
          be sharing your information
         </a>
         !)Ive been discussing this with people on Facebook and a trusted friend brought up some excellent points. In a nutshell he points out that the video link shared on ABC isnt the same as the one on the original post and so may be unrelated to the post in the private group and somehow triggered by another action or event. He also pointed out that the timestamps dont match up (ABC says she posted it about a week ago  and that screenshot is from 10/2/12; she actually posted it on September 11). These are all excellent points. When I talked to my friend again she looked back and saw that she had Liked the video link on a friends Wall, but did not share it from there. She shared it later, and directly from YouTube.
        </p>
        <p>
         So lets forget the privacy issue for a minute. Yes, thats important, but to me its secondary. Im really mostly interested in the how and why of this situation. So here are my questions:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Is it Facebook or ABC populating the widget?
         </li>
         <li>
          How did Facebook or ABC know that she had posted a link related to Gangnam style? I understand that Facebook would have a record, and if the widget is populated by the Facebook Ticker or News Feed then it would show up that she Liked the video, not that she shared the video (because that info isnt supposed to be available outside the private group, right?). BUT if ABC coded the widget and is populating it based on info it takes from Facebook, how would ABC know that she shared the video?
         </li>
         <li>
          How did the link change from YouTube to ABC if ABC isnt populating the widget? Related: Again, I wonder how ABC would know what she was posting in private group?
         </li>
         <li>
          If her public Liking of the video is what triggered the widget info (rather than her video share in a private group), then why is the widget saying she shared something she didnt share?
          <strong>
           Is this ABC using Facebook info in any way they see as beneficial (e.g., monitoring that she Liked something, then populating with a page from their site thats similar)? And is that ethical?
          </strong>
         </li>
