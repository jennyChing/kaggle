   <div id="biodb_notes">
    <p>
     Brief notes on what bioDBnet currently offers
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      db2db
     </b>
     handles all the conversions from one database identifier to another.
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      dbWalk
     </b>
     lets you walk through your own bioDBnet path.
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      dbReport
     </b>
     reports every possible information that it can get for a particular identifier.
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      dbFind
     </b>
     finds the type of identifiers and converts all into a chosen database identifier type
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      dbOrtho
     </b>
     converts identifiers from one organism into homolog identifiers of a different organism
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      dbAnnot
     </b>
     provides different types of annotations for various biological identifiers
    </p>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      dbOrg
     </b>
     provides downloadable files of organism wide conversions
    </p>
   </div>
   <div class="biodb_notes">
    <p>
     bioDBnet also offers supporting analysis
     <a href="/tools">
      <b>
       tools
      </b>
     </a>
     to the main functions
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      orgTaxon
     </b>
     for finding the taxon ID of an organism
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      goTree
     </b>
     to look at the parents of any go accession
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      chrView
     </b>
     for visualising data on chromosomes
    </p>
    <p>
     <b>
      bioTaxon
     </b>
     for finding genes, proteins or GO accessions based on search terms
    </p>
   </div>
