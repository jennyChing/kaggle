         <div class="separator" style="color: #414141; text-align: center;">
          Greetings from Eastern Iowa!
          <strong>
           I hope you are enjoying glorious fall weather this Tuesday, the first official day of fall!
          </strong>
          Fall is definitely in the air out here on my little homestead. The days are getting shorter and the air is getting cooler  although, its supposed to be 80 on Friday. Ive been busy freezing okra, digging up the last of my potatoes and taking in all of the butternut and acorn squash I grew. Sweet potatoes will be coming out of the garden in the next few days and Im still harvesting broccoli side shoots and enough tomatoes to get a nice sized pot of sauce every few days.
         </div>
         <div class="separator" style="color: #414141; text-align: left;">
         </div>
         <div class="separator" style="color: #414141; text-align: left;">
          <em>
           <strong>
            Potatotes
           </strong>
          </em>
         </div>
         <div class="separator" style="color: #414141; text-align: left;">
         </div>
         <div class="separator" style="color: #414141; text-align: left;">
          But my big garden accomplishment this past week has to do with potatoes. Two years ago I read about the
          <a href="http://www.simplifylivelove.com/how-to-plant-potatoes/">
           no-dig potato planting method
          </a>
          in one of my favorite gardening books, the
          <a href="http://amzn.to/1v8hOqw" rel="nofollow">
           Rodale Encyclopedia of Organic Gardening
          </a>
          . This is the second year I have planted my potatoes using the no-dig method and I continue to be very pleased with the results. The first year, I grew over
          <a href="http://www.simplifylivelove.com/garden-update-results-dig-potato-planting-method/">
           200 pounds of potatoes
          </a>
          , and this year Im sure I did too even though I have not managed to weigh them. I have three big bushel baskets full of potatoes again this year, just like I got last year and Im excited to have them to eat all winter again this year. You can read how I store my potatoes and how they fared over the winter last year
          <a href="http://wp.me/p27p1n-3s4">
           here
          </a>
          .
         </div>
