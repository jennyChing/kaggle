              <h5>
               Oriental  The Proud Purrer
              </h5>
              <div class="caption">
               <p>
                Wow  make that me-WOW. This cat is called the Rainbow Cat because the breed comes in more than 300 colors and patterns. Yes, 300-plus. The
                <a href="http://www.vetstreet.com/cats/oriental">
                 Oriental
                </a>
                is loud and proud and can purr like a Mack truck. Life is good when the Oriental has plenty of playmates and attention, but this cat can get a bit cranky if left alone for long durations and may even scold you for being late. Extremely smart, the Oriental sports a heart-shaped face and large bat ears. Both the shorthaired and longhaired varieties are easy to groom.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="slide">
              <div class="image-wrap">
               <img alt="Tonkinese Cat Breed" data-size="embedded-slideshow" src="http://d21vu35cjx7sd4.cloudfront.net/dims3/MMAH/thumbnail/590x420/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fs3.amazonaws.com%2Fassets.prod.vetstreet.com%2F4f%2F4e8110a41c11e192b2005056ad4734%2Ffile%2FAP-JLKJOY_Tonkinese_Cat.jpg"/>
              </div>
              <div class="credit">
               <p>
                Tetsu Yamazaki, Animal Photography
               </p>
              </div>
              <h5>
               Tonkinese  The Tenacious Talker
              </h5>
              <div class="caption">
               <p>
                This cat loves to talk, talk, talk. The
                <a href="http://vetstreet.com/cats/tonkinese">
                 Tonkinese
                </a>
                also unleashes a powerful purr. He has been described as part puppy and part monkey because of his playful loyalty and acrobatic skills. Yes, this cat will play fetch and even an indoor game of tag, and he cant wait to rush to the front door to greet visitors and welcome them with plenty of chatter. Its hard not to fall in love with this confident cat who sports a trim, muscular physique and a short, silky coat that comes in five colors.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="slide">
              <div class="image-wrap">
               <img alt="Singapura cat breed" data-size="embedded-slideshow" height="420" src="http://d21vu35cjx7sd4.cloudfront.net/dims3/MMAH/thumbnail/590x420/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fs3.amazonaws.com%2Fassets.prod.vetstreet.com%2Fc0%2F08%2F2624097141e48b51a3035b7b118e%2FSingapura_Cats-AP-01AVWP-590lc061213.jpg" width="590"/>
              </div>
              <div class="credit">
               <p>
                Helmi Flick, Animal Photography
               </p>
              </div>
              <h5>
               Singapura  Small Body, Bold Voice
              </h5>
              <div class="caption">
               <p>
                Billed as the tiniest cat breed, the
                <a href="http://www.vetstreet.com/cats/singapura" target="_blank">
                 Singapura
                </a>
                weighs less than 6 pounds but can roar loudly to get your attention. This is a cat who likes to walk across your computer keyboard to get you to focus on him  not your work  and loves to hear the sound of his own voice. His origins remain disputed, with some claiming his roots are in Singapore while others insist he came from the United States. This outgoing, curious cat with large ears and eyes sports a short, fine beige coat that is a cinch to maintain.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="slide">
              <div class="image-wrap">
               <img alt="Maine Coon cat breed" data-size="embedded-slideshow" src="http://d21vu35cjx7sd4.cloudfront.net/dims3/MMAH/thumbnail/590x420/quality/90/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fs3.amazonaws.com%2Fassets.prod.vetstreet.com%2F2d%2Ffed320a41c11e192b2005056ad4734%2Ffile%2FAP-OXPSGX_Maine_Coon_Cat.jpg"/>
              </div>
              <div class="credit">
               <p>
                Barbara O'Brien, Animal Photography
               </p>
              </div>
              <h5>
               Maine Coon  The Gentle Giant
              </h5>
              <div class="caption">
               <p>
                Rated as one of the largest feline breeds on the planet, this native American longhaired cat gets its name from the state where it originated. Nicknamed the Gentle Giant, the
                <a href="http://www.vetstreet.com/cats/maine-coon">
                 Maine Coon
                </a>
                is quite talkative to his favorite people. But his vocals are not thunderous meows so much as melodic chirps and trills. It's almost as if the Maine Coon speaks in questions, with the inflection rising at the end of each trill. The Maine Coon is friends with the family dog, children and other cats and enjoys acting goofy and clownish to win your attention
                <em>
                 and
                </em>
                your affection. This cat appreciates  and needs  regular grooming sessions.
               </p>
