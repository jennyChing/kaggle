           <div class="td-post-featured-image">
            <a data-caption="" href="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/4a83a421998322a74a63ea37599eb628.png">
             <img alt="" class="entry-thumb td-modal-image" height="426" itemprop="image" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/4a83a421998322a74a63ea37599eb628-640x426.png" title="4a83a421998322a74a63ea37599eb628" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <p>
            Today, We all live in big homes, tiny homes and everything in between but now we can expect that in the next few years, people will like to live in the sea.
           </p>
           <p>
            A Japanese architecture firm, The Shimizu Corporation has recently revealed the plan of worlds first underwater city and the firm says it could be constructed as early as 2035. In this underwater city, there will be a lot of space for businesses, hotels and homes for up to 5,000 people off the coast of Japan. The massive, spherical structure measures more than four football fields in length, and its electricity and water supplies extend almost two miles deep into the sea.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Shimizu Corporation said in a press release, is to
            <em>
             make the most
            </em>
            of undersea space, as some neighborhoods on land face damage from rising sea levels.
            <em>
             The Ocean Spiral,
            </em>
            while not likely to be constructed anytime soon, would have sustainable fisheries, plazas and promenades for its residents.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The Ocean Spiral, a futuristic concept for a self-sufficient city in the ocean, uses the sea to run infrastructure that could support 5,000 people.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="3040294-slide-s-3-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for" class="aligncenter wp-image-11824 size-full" height="418" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/3040294-slide-s-3-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for.jpg" width="750"/>
            <em>
             huffingtonpost.com
            </em>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <strong>
             The Blue Garden sphere, 500 meters in diameter, would hold a tower with hundreds of apartments and larger homes, stores, offices, a hotel, and research facilities into the water.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="3040294-slide-s-4-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for" class="aligncenter wp-image-11825 size-full" height="395" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/3040294-slide-s-4-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for.jpg" width="750"/>
            <em>
             huffingtonpost.com
            </em>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <strong>
             A seawall would circle the sphere that keeps the large waves away.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="3040294-slide-s-5-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for" class="aligncenter wp-image-11826 size-full" height="500" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/3040294-slide-s-5-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for.jpg" width="557"/>
            <em>
             huffingtonpost.com
            </em>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <strong>
             Though building an underwater city is an obvious challenge but the company believes that it also could have multiple advantages for the environment as well as in the development.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="3040294-slide-s-6-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for" class="aligncenter wp-image-11827 size-full" height="500" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/3040294-slide-s-6-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for.jpg" width="557"/>
            <em>
             huffingtonpost.com
            </em>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <strong>
             The worlds first underwater city has also massive inner atrium.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="pic" class="aligncenter wp-image-11829 size-full" height="515" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/pic1.jpg" width="590"/>
            <em>
             huffingtonpost.com
            </em>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <strong>
             Hideo Imamura, a spokesperson for Shimizu Corporation said, The deep sea contains enormous possibilities that can possibly help ongoing environmental problems throughout the world.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="3040294-slide-s-7-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for" class="aligncenter wp-image-11830 size-full" height="401" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/3040294-slide-s-7-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for.jpg" width="750"/>
            <em>
             huffingtonpost.com
            </em>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <strong>
             A long spiraling tube would travel nine miles down to the sea floor to pull the cold water up for the citys power plant.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            <img alt="3040294-slide-s-9-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for" class="aligncenter wp-image-11831 size-full" height="500" src="http://www.bimzz.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/3040294-slide-s-9-look-at-the-crazy-designs-for.jpg" width="714"/>
            <em>
             huffingtonpost.com
            </em>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            <strong>
             The worlds first underwater city will reach almost two miles to the sea floor.
            </strong>
