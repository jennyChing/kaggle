     <p>
      <b>
       Harold Finch
      </b>
      is a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">
       fictional character
      </a>
      from the
      <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">
       CBS
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crime_drama" title="Crime drama">
       crime drama
      </a>
      television series
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Person_of_Interest_(TV_series)" title="Person of Interest (TV series)">
        Person of Interest
       </a>
      </i>
      . He is played by
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Emerson" title="Michael Emerson">
       Michael Emerson
      </a>
      , and in scenes from his past by Parker Brightman (child) and Chris Bert (teenager). Finch is a
      <a href="/wiki/Recluse" title="Recluse">
       reclusive
      </a>
      private billionaire
      <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">
       software engineer
      </a>
      who developed
      <a href="/wiki/Person_of_Interest_(TV_series)#The_Machine" title="Person of Interest (TV series)">
       a machine
      </a>
      that can isolate the Social Security numbers of people with either premeditated homicidal intent or who will be homicide victims, based on its analysis of surveillance data. He recruits
      <a href="/wiki/John_Reese_(Person_of_Interest)" title="John Reese (Person of Interest)">
       John Reese
      </a>
      , a former
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Army_Special_Forces" title="U.S. Army Special Forces">
       Green Beret
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">
       CIA
      </a>
      operative, to help him prevent crimes related to the 'people of interest' that the Machine identifies. He is also known by a series of aliases, many using various bird names as a surname. A quirky and mysterious character, Finch tends to keep his personal life a secret as he rejects Reese's numerous attempts to learn more about him.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
       Background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Harold_Finch_(Person_of_Interest)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Born in Lassiter, Iowa, Finch's real surname is unknown, though his first name has been confirmed to be Harold. A
      <a href="/wiki/Child_prodigy" title="Child prodigy">
       child prodigy
      </a>
      , he displayed early skills in the fields of
      <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">
       mechanical engineering
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">
       computer science
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      With his father suffering early onset
      <a href="/wiki/Dementia" title="Dementia">
       dementia
      </a>
      , the teenaged Finch grew increasingly interested in artificial intelligence and human-computer interaction.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Needing more memory for the machine he was trying to build, Finch hacked into the early military Internet,
      <a href="/wiki/ARPANET" title="ARPANET">
       ARPANET
      </a>
      , resulting in his becoming the subject of federal investigation and obliging him to begin living under a false identity. His father had taught him bird names, which might explain his preference for incorporating these into his aliases.
     </p>
     <p>
      As 'Harold Wren', Finch entered
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MIT" title="MIT">
       MIT
      </a>
      , where he and classmate
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Person_of_Interest_characters#Nathan_Ingram" title="List of Person of Interest characters">
       Nathan Ingram
      </a>
      co-founded the computer company 'IFT' (perhaps Ingram Finch Technologies). Ingram was the public face of the hugely successful business while Harold necessarily remained unseen, accumulating vast wealth without having a public profile. It is made clear in the early seasons that Finch's personal fortune runs into billions of dollars.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      After the
      <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">
       September 11 attacks
      </a>
      , Ingram and Finch began working to create an artificially intelligent machine that could predict terrorist attacks through processing government feeds from emails, phone calls, and surveillance cameras.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The machine also generated a list of predicted murders and violent crimes "non-relevant to national security". It was determined that this non-relevant list should have to be ignored, since passing the information to law enforcement agencies could reveal the existence and extent of constitutionally proscribed government surveillance. For this reason, and rationalizing it as done for the greater good, Finch programmed the machine to delete the non-relevant list every day at midnight. Ingram and Finch clashed when Finch discovered that Ingram had created a
      <a href="/wiki/Backdoor_(computing)" title="Backdoor (computing)">
       backdoor
      </a>
      into the machine before handing it over and was trying to warn and even save people on the "irrelevant list". Finch shut down the backdoor.
     </p>
     <p>
      Nathan's moral qualms led him to contact a journalist with the intention of exposing the surveillance program. Anxious to stop him, Finch arrived at the meeting place only in time to see Ingram murdered in a seeming terrorist bombing that as well claimed dozens of innocent lives. Badly injured himself, Finch was taken to the temporary triage. Suspecting (rightly) that the attack was a government sponsored attempt to kill Ingram, and understanding for the first time the lengths to which elements of the government would go to maintain the machine as secret, Finch allowed his fiance Grace Hendricks to believe he was killed in the bombing.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the secret library location where Ingram had created his backdoor to the machine, Finch reactivated the backdoor and began his own mission to save the non-relevant numbers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Beginning_war_on_crime">
       Beginning war on crime
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Harold_Finch_(Person_of_Interest)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Beginning war on crime">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Knowing that he cannot himself intervene, Finch recruits Rick Dillinger, a former Blackwater mercenary. However their relationship is uneasy because of ethical differences.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The machine provides Finch and Dillinger the number of Daniel Casey, a computer hacker who tests computer security systems. Dillinger observes that Casey is being pursued by multiple third parties, including Special Control and CIA agents revealed to be John Reese and Kara Stanton. Dillinger rescues Casey and takes him to Finch, who is sympathetic to Casey's predicament. Examining Casey's laptop he concludes that it should be sold to the highest bidder (at some unknown point later in the season two finale, Finch creates and installs onto the laptop a virus to "set the machine free").
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Dillinger, who secretly planted a bug to listen in their conversation, decides to go rogue by drugging Finch and stealing the laptop.
     </p>
     <p>
      Finch follows Dillinger to Central Park and watches from behind a tree as Dillinger gives the laptop to the Chinese buyers. After the trade is made, an unknown assassin (revealed to be Shaw) fires at the buyers at first, prompting them to flee, before shooting Dillinger right in the chest. Finch makes his way to Casey and Reese's encounter. Finch watches from behind as Reese lies to Kara, telling her that Casey is dead. Reese tells Casey that he doesn't deserve to die and provides a safe passage in Canada. It is at this point, seeing in Reese the requisite skill-set and a strong sense of moral duty, that Finch begins considering him as replacement for Dillinger.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Activities_with_John_Reese">
       Activities with John Reese
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Harold_Finch_(Person_of_Interest)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Activities with John Reese">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <table class="cquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
      <tr>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
       <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
        I recognize, Mr. Reese, that there's a disparity between how much I know about you and how much you know about me. I know you'll be trying to close that gap as quickly as possible. But I should tell you... I'm a really private person.
       </td>
       <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
        
       </td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
       <td class="cquotecite" colspan="3" style="border: none; padding-right: 4%">
        <p style="font-size: smaller; text-align: right;">
         <cite style="font-style: normal;">
          Harold Finch, Pilot
         </cite>
        </p>
       </td>
      </tr>
     </table>
     <p>
      When Finch first makes contact with John Reese, Reese is suffering from depression brought on by the death of his former lover and is living on the streets. Defending himself against subway thugs leads Reese to be arrested and questioned by Detective
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Person_of_Interest_characters#Joss_Carter" title="List of Person of Interest characters">
       Joss Carter
      </a>
      . Finch gets him out of police custody with the help of a costly lawyer and tells him that he needs a purpose and a job, explaining that what he can offer is a "chance to be there in time". After initially refusing, Reese begins on his first case, watching assistant district attorney Diane Hansen.
     </p>
     <p>
      In the course of this first job Reese blackmails Detective
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Person_of_Interest_characters#Lionel_Fusco" title="List of Person of Interest characters">
       Lionel Fusco
      </a>
      to be their informant in the NYPD. When Reese completes the case, Finch tells him, "The names never stop coming" (knowing that there will be infinite Social Security numbers and cases to solve, as he agrees to take on the job). As their friendship starts to evolve in the series, Reese becomes increasingly protective of Finch, particularly following Finch's kidnapping by
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Person_of_Interest_characters#Root" title="List of Person of Interest characters">
       Root
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Reese gives Finch his dog
      <a href="/wiki/Person_of_Interest_(TV_series)#Main_characters" title="Person of Interest (TV series)">
       Bear
      </a>
      as a companion, but also for protection when Reese is not around.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      For his part, Reese gives little if any indication that he sees Finch's injuries as a limitation, and has gradually drawn Finch into field work. Although reluctant at first, Finch has increasingly begun to assist Reese in the field, growing in confidence as he does.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Skills">
       Skills
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Harold_Finch_(Person_of_Interest)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Skills">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Throughout the series, Finch displays his extraordinary expertise with
      <a href="/wiki/Hacker_(computer_security)" title="Hacker (computer security)">
       computer hacking
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/High_tech" title="High tech">
       advanced technology
      </a>
      . As a result of his injury from the ferry bombing, he is unable to fully turn his head, has rigid posture, and walks with a limp. In contrast to Reese, he has lacked hand-to-hand combat techniques due to his physical limitations, but he prefers to use
      <a href="/wiki/Self-defense" title="Self-defense">
       self-defense
      </a>
      and a non-lethal weapon (such as a
      <a href="/wiki/Taser" title="Taser">
       Taser
      </a>
      when he stunned an NYPD officer inside the morgue in order to help Reese and Carter escape from HR).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
