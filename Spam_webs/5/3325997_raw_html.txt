The majority of the populationlives and work in the cities and suburbs of the world and come winter time, people think less about keeping warm than they do about preparation. They are annoyed that it&#8217;s cold and snowing and it becomes a thing that people just have to deal with. This creates a form of complacency that brings with it a very real danger.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8294" src="http://www.offgridworld.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/cabin-fireplace.jpg" alt="cabin fireplace" width="700" height="525" /&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Not a likely danger mind you, but a real one that most people are not prepared for in the case of an unexpected winter storm. Earlier this year (November, 2014) Buffalo, NY was hit by a winter blizzard that buried entire areas under7 feet of snow in less than a couple days. This caught everyone off guard and completely by surprise. It strained the city&#8217;s infrastructure and pushed it beyond its maximum capability. Power went out, streets and highways were shut down, and the city ground to a icy halt.&lt;/p&gt;
