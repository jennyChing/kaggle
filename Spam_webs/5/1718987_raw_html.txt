     <p>
      "
      <b>
       I, Roommate
      </b>
      " is the third episode of
      <i>
       <a href="/Futurama" title="Futurama">
        Futurama
       </a>
      </i>
      , the third   of the
      <a href="/Season_1" title="Season 1">
       first production season
      </a>
      and the third   of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Broadcast_season_1" title="Broadcast season 1">
       first broadcast season
      </a>
      .   It aired on
      <a href="/Infosphere:Anniversaries/6_April" title="Infosphere:Anniversaries/6 April">
       6 April
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/1999" title="1999">
       1999
      </a>
      , on
      <a href="/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">
       Fox
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/Philip_J._Fry" title="Philip J. Fry">
       Fry
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/Bender_Bending_Rodriguez" title="Bender Bending Rodriguez">
       Bender
      </a>
      move in together as roommates, and they are established as
      <a href="/Fry-Bender_relationship" title="Fry-Bender relationship">
       close friends
      </a>
      , but when Fry moves to
      <a href="/Dr._Mbutu%27s_apartment" title="Dr. Mbutu's apartment">
       another apartment
      </a>
      Bender feels lonely and stops drinking.
     </p>

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

       </span>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Act_I:_.22Somebody.27s_been_leaving_food_around..._and_it.27s_attracting_owls.21.22">
       Act I: "Somebody's been leaving food around... and it's attracting owls!"
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/index.php?title=I,_Roommate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Act I: &quot;Somebody's been leaving food around... and it's attracting owls!&quot;">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="/Hermes_Conrad" title="Hermes Conrad">
       Hermes
      </a>
      complains about
      <a href="/Philip_J._Fry" title="Philip J. Fry">
       Fry
      </a>
      living in the
      <a href="/Planet_Express" title="Planet Express">
       Planet Express
      </a>
      Headquarters, since he leaves food around, uses too much water, bathes in the emergency chemical burn shower, drys his hair in the ship's engine and is really annoying. Everyone agrees, so Fry has to find a new place to live. At the
      <a href="/Food-O-Mat" title="Food-O-Mat">
       Food-O-Mat
      </a>
      , Bender invites him to stay in his
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Apartment_number_00100100" title="Apartment number 00100100">
       apartment
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/Robot_Arms_Apartments" title="Robot Arms Apartments">
       Robot Arms Apartments
      </a>
      and when Fry goes to see the apartment, it turns out to be a tiny space only a couple of feet across. Since this doesn't work well for Fry, Leela takes Fry and Bender apartment hunting. They visit a lot of apartments, including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Underwater_House_Salesman" title="Underwater House Salesman">
       one
      </a>
      underwater,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Unique_Architecture_Apartment" title="Unique Architecture Apartment">
       one
      </a>
      built in the style of an Escher picture and
      <a href="/Suspiciously_fantastic_apartment" title="Suspiciously fantastic apartment">
       one
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">
       New Jersey
      </a>
      (that's the reason it's bad). Since they are all bad, the pair give up. They finally find a nice rent-controlled place that belonged to a friend of
      <a href="/Professor_Hubert_J._Farnsworth" title="Professor Hubert J. Farnsworth">
       the Professor
      </a>
      . Fry stays in the main apartment while Bender stays in the closet because it's perfect for him.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Act_II:_.22Fry.2C_of_all_the_friends_I.27ve_had..._you.27re_the_first..22">
       Act II: "Fry, of all the friends I've had... you're the first."
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/index.php?title=I,_Roommate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Act II: &quot;Fry, of all the friends I've had... you're the first.&quot;">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Bender and Fry get settled in at their new apartment to watch
      <a href="/All_My_Circuits" title="All My Circuits">
       All My Circuits
      </a>
      with Leela,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Amy_Wong" title="Amy Wong">
       Amy
      </a>
      , Hermes,
      <a href="/Dr._John_A._Zoidberg" title="Dr. John A. Zoidberg">
       Zoidberg
      </a>
      and the Professor, but a problem arises - the TV reception is bad in their apartment and in all the building's residents' apartments. Neighbors such as
      <a href="/Randy_Munchnik" title="Randy Munchnik">
       Randy Munchnik
      </a>
      and a
      <a href="/Huge-assed_woman" title="Huge-assed woman">
       huge-assed woman
      </a>
      come and want the problem solved so
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/Hattie" title="Hattie">
       Hattie
      </a>
      comes and looks for it. It turns out to be Bender's antenna that interferes with the entire buildings' TV reception. The neighbors want Bender and Fry out of the building but Fry doesn't want to give up the apartment, so Bender is forced to move out. He can't move back in with Fry unless he cuts off his antenna, so Bender moves back into his old place, becomes very depressed because his friend is not with him, and becomes sober.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Act_III:_.22Bender.2C_you.27re_blind_stinking_sober.21.22">

      </span>

     <p>
      Staggering around in a stupor of sobriety, Leela discovers Bender has made a mess of himself and tries to make him promise he will drink, but he doesn't hear her and runs out. At the apartment, Leela trys to explain to Fry that Bender misses him and he shows up at Fry's apartment with the idea of cutting off his antenna so he can live with Fry. He cuts it off and Fry feels guilty, so with help from
      <a href="/Smitty" title="Smitty">
       Smitty
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/URL" title="URL">
       URL
      </a>
      , they look for and reattach Bender's antenna and then move back into Bender's apartment. When Fry looks for light for the fruit tree that Leela gave him, Bender reveals there is a "closet", which is actually an entire apartment, and Fry decides to live in that part.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/index.php?title=I,_Roommate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      After having shown some initial scripts to Fox, the company asked the writers for a more down to Earth episode.  The idea of "I, Roommate" was to satisfy this need.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mgdxc-script_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-mgdxc-script-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The script, written by
      <a href="/Eric_Horsted" title="Eric Horsted">
       Eric Horsted
      </a>
      , tried to distance itself from things that had scared the Fox people off, such as
      <a href="/Suicide_booth" title="Suicide booth">
       suicide booths
      </a>
      , lobster creatures and Bender being anti-social.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mgdxc-script_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-mgdxc-script-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Unfortunately, their reaction to the script was "Worst. Episode. Ever".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mgdxc-script_1-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-mgdxc-script-1">
        [1]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      As a result of the initial criticism, the show runners decided from thereon, they wanted to do the show they wanted.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mg-theirshow_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-mg-theirshow-2">
        [2]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The story line itself was developed to establish Fry's living arrangements as well as the relationship between him and Bender, and obviously a method to get people to care for Bender, despite being anti-social.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-dxc-care_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-dxc-care-3">
        [3]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;">

       </a>

          <img alt="" height="11" src="/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        The aerial view of Bender's apartment was the way to indicate its small size,
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-bh-apartment_4-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-com-bh-apartment-4">
          [4]
         </a>
        </sup>
        since confined spaces are hard to illustrate in animation.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mg-spaces_5-0">
         <a href="#cite_note-com-mg-spaces-5">
          [5]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      <a href="/Matt_Groening" title="Matt Groening">
       Matt Groening
      </a>
      , as it will not be too unusual, had not seen the
      <i>
       <a class="extiw" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Odd_Couple" title="wikipedia:The Odd Couple">
        The Odd Couple
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mgdxc-oddcouple_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-mgdxc-oddcouple-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      that the episode slightly parodies, especially during its montage.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mgdxc-oddcouple_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-mgdxc-oddcouple-6">
        [6]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Originally though, the music over the montage was more than just an interlude.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-bw-music_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-bw-music-7">
        [7]
       </a>
      </sup>
      The episode also includes several sequences where the viewer gets a chance to figure it out before it is explicit said,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-dxc-explicit_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-dxc-explicit-8">
        [8]
       </a>
      </sup>
      as well as a slow thinker, because apparently, everybody likes those.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-mg-slowthinker_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-mg-slowthinker-9">
        [9]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      "I, Roommate" was a challenging episode to draw for the animators, especially several scenes involving the
      <a href="/Planet_Express_ship" title="Planet Express ship">
       Planet Express ship
      </a>
      were drawn before the 3D model was done and the animators had little go on, when it came to close ups of the ship.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com-gv-3d_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-com-gv-3d-10">
        [10]
       </a>
      </sup>
      Especially a scene like the
      <a href="/Food-O-Mat" title="Food-O-Mat">
       Food-O-Mat
      </a>

       <a href="#cite_note-com-bh-foodomat-11">
        [11]
       </a>
      </sup>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">
       Reception
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/index.php?title=I,_Roommate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      "I, Roommate" aired on 6 April, 1999.  And it was the first
      <i>
       Futurama
      </i>
      episode that would air in its regular spot on Fox's Tuesday line up in a block of four sitcoms, previously, it had aired during its Sunday line up, between
      <i>
       <a href="/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">
        The Simpsons
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       X-Files
      </i>
      .  As expected, the moving of
      <i>
       Futurama
      </i>
      hurt its ratings.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        [12]
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      When this episode was first shown on
      <a class="extiw" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Two" title="wikipedia:BBC Two">
       BBC Two
      </a>
      , on 11
      <a href="/Infosphere:Anniversaries#September" title="Infosphere:Anniversaries">
       September
      </a>
      <a href="/2001" title="2001">
       2001
      </a>
      (
      <a class="extiw" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_One" title="wikipedia:BBC One">
       BBC One
      </a>
      couldn't show it that day), it was interrupted by
      <a class="extiw" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_News" title="wikipedia:BBC News">
       BBC News
      </a>
      , which meant first-time viewers only got to see bits and pieces of "I, Roommate". It was finally shown complete later that month.
     </p>
