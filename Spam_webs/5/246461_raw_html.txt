     <p>
      <b>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </b>
      is a project by
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Mystery_Science_Theater_3000" title="Mystery Science Theater 3000">
        Mystery Science Theater 3000
       </a>
      </i>
      (
      <i>
       MST3K
      </i>
      ) creator and original host,
      <a href="/wiki/Joel_Hodgson" title="Joel Hodgson">
       Joel Hodgson
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Meyer-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The project involves "
      <a href="/wiki/MSTing" title="MSTing">
       riffing
      </a>
      "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-movies" title="B-movies">
       B-movies
      </a>
      , in a manner similar to that of
      <i>
       MST3K
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ellis_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ellis-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Joining Hodgson are many of the original
      <i>
       MST3K
      </i>
      cast, as well as some cast members who joined later in the show's run.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn100906_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-cnn100906-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      These include
      <a href="/wiki/Trace_Beaulieu" title="Trace Beaulieu">
       Trace Beaulieu
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/J._Elvis_Weinstein" title="J. Elvis Weinstein">
       J. Elvis Weinstein
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Frank_Conniff" title="Frank Conniff">
       Frank Conniff
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Mary_Jo_Pehl" title="Mary Jo Pehl">
       Mary Jo Pehl
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greetings_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-greetings-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was first performed live on December 7, 2007 and first aired on December 22, 2007.
     </p>
     <p>
      On February 16, 2013, it was announced that the touring portion of Cinematic Titanic was going on an indefinite hiatus. According to an
      <a href="/wiki/Email" title="Email">
       email
      </a>
      sent out to members of the site, due to "5 people living in 5 different cities with different lives and projects, it has become increasingly difficult to coordinate our schedules and give Cinematic Titanic the attention it requires to keep growing as a creative enterprise and a business."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The final tour began on September 23, 2013.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">
       Description
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematic_Titanic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Cast.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2567" data-file-width="2567" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Cast.jpg/220px-Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Cast.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Cast.jpg/330px-Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Cast.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Cast.jpg/440px-Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Cast.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">

     <p>
      Like
      <i>
       Mystery Science Theater 3000
      </i>
      , the series uses black silhouettes of the riffers placed over the films, but in the case of
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      they sit on both sides of the screen rather than just on the lower right.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewinski_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lewinski-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Visual gags are frequent (such as Beaulieu's use of a
      <a href="/wiki/Cherry_picker" title="Cherry picker">
       cherry picker
      </a>
      in
      <i>
       The Oozing Skull
      </i>
      ), and there are two or three host segments per episode, all performed in silhouette.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematic_Titanic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The actors essentially play themselves as they participate in an experiment for some unknown (possibly shadowy) corporation or military force. The story currently provided to the cast is that there is a tear in the "electron scaffolding" that threatens all digital media in the world.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ryan_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Ryan-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Their experience doing
      <i>
       MST3K
      </i>
      is key to the organization's plans. The riffing for each film is recorded to a "nanotated disc" and inserted into a "Time Tube" by Hodgson that descends into the frame at the end of every episode. The unknown organization is very firm on keeping the cast focused on their duties, providing no time frame for completion and requiring them to stay within the facilities at all times. They apparently have massive resources and an autonomous military force, which they use to keep the cast in line. As of now, the cast is inquisitive of the true purpose of the experiments but have no major problems as, aside from having to watch bad movies, they are well-treated.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Relation_to_RiffTrax">
       Relation to
       <i>
        RiffTrax
       </i>
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematic_Titanic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Relation to RiffTrax">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_J._Nelson" title="Michael J. Nelson">
       Michael J. Nelson
      </a>
      , who took over as the star of
      <i>
       MST3K
      </i>
      upon Hodgson's departure from the show, has his own movie-riff series, titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/RiffTrax" title="RiffTrax">
        RiffTrax
       </a>
      </i>
      . Nelson's project produces riffs for a wide variety of films, including many current and well-known movies, such as
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(2008_film)" title="Twilight (2008 film)">
        Twilight
       </a>
      </i>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Matrix" title="The Matrix">
        The Matrix
       </a>
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Rings_(film)" title="Lord of the Rings (film)">
        Lord of the Rings
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn100906_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-cnn100906-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He then posts the audio for sale on the
      <i>
       RiffTrax
      </i>
      website.
     </p>
     <p>
      The fact that
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      involves almost every
      <i>
       MST3K
      </i>
      writer and performer aside from Nelson,
      <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Murphy_(actor)" title="Kevin Murphy (actor)">
       Kevin Murphy
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Bill_Corbett" title="Bill Corbett">
       Bill Corbett
      </a>
      , who happen to be the regular cast of
      <i>
       RiffTrax
      </i>
      , has prompted fan speculation about a rivalry between Hodgson and Nelson surrounding the two projects, but the pair have consistently denied that such a rift exists and expressed praise for each other's projects, pointing out that they fill different niches and there is more than enough room for both of them. Speaking with
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Associated_Content" title="Associated Content">
        Associated Content
       </a>
      </i>
      in 2009, Mary Jo Pehl, who has worked in both projects, denied knowledge of any animosity between the
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      and
      <i>
       RiffTrax
      </i>
      groups. "I have no idea if there's some sort of family feud between the Joel vs. Mike factions. If there is, people ought to find better things to do with their time, like debating which way the
      <a href="/wiki/Toilet_paper" title="Toilet paper">
       toilet paper
      </a>
      should hang on the
      <a href="/wiki/Toilet_roll_holder" title="Toilet roll holder">
       spindle
      </a>
      , or if the opening of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pillow_case" title="Pillow case">
       pillow cases
      </a>
      should face the outside of the bed or the inside when placed.
      <i>
       RT
      </i>
      does their thing, and
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      does theirs."
     </p>
     <p>
      When asked about potential collaborations with those involved in
      <i>
       RiffTrax
      </i>
      , Hodgson told
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/New_York_(magazine)" title="New York (magazine)">
        New York
       </a>
      </i>
      magazine, "I dont know. I think those guys  Bill, Kevin and Mike  are really talented, obviously. I think anythings possible, but I thought it might get confusing to try to merge them together or do crossover projects. I would never rule it out because its all kind of the same universe. But
      <i>
       RiffTrax
      </i>
      , the idea of riffing on topical movies, is a different thing. And I like that the movie-riffing universe got bigger when they decided to do that, but we just do weird movies youve never seen before."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newyorkmag_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-newyorkmag-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Studio_.2F_.22Live.22_DVDs">
       Studio / "Live" DVDs
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematic_Titanic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title='Edit section: Studio / "Live" DVDs'>
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="570" data-file-width="1200" height="105" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/aa/Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg/220px-Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/aa/Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg/330px-Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/aa/Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg/440px-Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cinematic-Titanic-2011-09-24-Live.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The cast riff on the movie
        <a href="/wiki/Dynamite_Brothers" title="Dynamite Brothers">
         <i>
          East Meets Watts
         </i>
        </a>
        at a live show in NYC
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In a question and answer session at the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tivoli_Theatre_(St._Louis)" title="Tivoli Theatre (St. Louis)">
       Tivoli Theatre
      </a>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">
       St. Louis, Missouri
      </a>
      , it was announced that
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      would begin to release recordings from their live shows as "Live" DVDs in an effort to bring the energy of their on stage antics into people's living rooms and further promote the stage show. When asked if this meant the demise of the studio produced DVDs, J. Elvis Weinstein said, "No, studio releases will return at some point in the future." The first of these "Live" DVDs to be released was
      <i>
       East Meets Watts
      </i>
      , which was recorded in front of a live audience during one of the group's performances in Los Angeles.
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Release_history">
       Release history
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematic_Titanic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Release history">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The first live performance was a private show for employees of
      <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic" title="Industrial Light &amp; Magic">
       Industrial Light &amp; Magic
      </a>
      on December 7, 2007.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greetings_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-greetings-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After the live show, the cast reworked some jokes, delaying the original December 10 release date. The first episode of
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      was released on DVD to the public at midnight on December 21, via the download-to-burn company EZTakes. According to the
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      website, due to rights issues, the episode was not available for download until April 2, 2008.
     </p>
     <p>
      Both the private show and the first release feature the B-movie
      <i>
       Brain of Blood
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewinski_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Lewinski-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      One of the original film's producers, concerned that creating multiple versions of the film could create marketplace confusion, requested that
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic'
      </i>
      s version have a different name. To alleviate his concerns,
      <i>
       Cinematic Titanic
      </i>
      retitled their release
      <i>
       The Oozing Skull
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ItsOn_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ItsOn-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In June 2012, the first ten DVDs were made available for free viewing on
      <a href="/wiki/Hulu" title="Hulu">
       Hulu
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In March 2013, Cinematic Titanic sold the last of their on-hand DVD stock and ceased pressing their own discs. From then on all releases were either in digital format, or through
      <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">
       Amazon.com
      </a>
      's print-on-demand service.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DVDclearance_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-DVDclearance-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
