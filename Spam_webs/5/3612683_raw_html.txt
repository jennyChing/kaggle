       <p>
        <strong>
         Cannes: Jeremy Irons Starring in Action-Comedy Monumental
        </strong>
        <br/>
        <em>
         May 16th, 2015 || By Dave McNary
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Jeremy Irons, Virginia Madsen, AnnaSophia Robb and Rory Culkin will star in action-comedy Monumental with filming starting July 6.
       </p>
       <p>
        Producers are Eric Fischer and Brianna Lee Johnson from Dead Fish Films. Brett Simon is directing from a script by Annie Burgstede and Chad Faust.
       </p>
       <p>
        Kathy Morgan International is handling foreign pre-sales at Cannes. CAA is handling domestic rights.
       </p>
       <p>
        Culkin and Robb will portray a young couple who have accidentally killed their tyrant boss and kidnapped a wise-cracking Vietnam Veteran relative, played by Irons. Madsen will play an FBI agent hot on their trail.
       </p>
       <p>
        Executive producers are Aaron Gilbert, Robert Odgen Barnum, 3 Point Capital, Ksana Golod, James Bryant and George Parra. London Pacific Finance provided development funding.
       </p>
      </blockquote>

       <p>
        <strong>
         Casting alert: PBS drama filming in Virginia to feature Josh Radnor, Gary Cole, and AnnaSophia Robb
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Picture Ted from How I Met Your Mother in Civil War-era garb and a young Carrie Bradshaw as a corseted southern belle. Cant quite manage it? Cue the magic of TV.
       </p>
       <p>
        The actors who played those roles (Josh Radnor and AnnaSophia Robb, respectively) are part of the cast of Mercy Street, the upcoming PBS historical drama filming in Virginia, along with a slate that includes Gary Cole, Mary Elizabeth Winstead, Peter Gerety, Norbert Leo Butz, McKinley Belcher III, and Shalita Grant, the network says.
       </p>
       <p>
        Now would-be rubberneckers have an idea of who to be on the lookout for as cameras start rolling in the Richmond and Petersburg area. Set in a Civil War hospital in Alexandria and pitched as a sort of ER-in-hoop-skirts, the show will air in late 2016, just as the beloved Downton Abbey prepares to say its goodbyes.
       </p>
       <p>
        PBS also announced that directors Roxann Dawson and Jeremy Webb have joined the show, whose executive producers include heavy hitters Ridley Scott and David Zucker. The casting announcement hints at a plot heavy with North-meets-South drama, in which a wealthy familys Alexandria hotel is occupied by the Union army and converted into a hospital.
       </p>
      </blockquote>
