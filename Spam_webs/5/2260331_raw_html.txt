         In light of the warmer weather weve been blessed with in Chicago, we thought wed bring you a special free pattern from
         <a href="http://www.darngoodyarn.com/" target="_blank" title="Visit Darn Good Yarn!">
          Nicole at Darn Good Yarn
         </a>
         . We hope you enjoy! For more awesome patterns and tutorials, dont miss
         <a href="http://www.allfreeknitting.com/tag/summer" target="_blank" title="Free knitting patterns for summer ">
          the summer section
         </a>
         on our site.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Were almost there! Sunglasses weather! And with fluorescent and bold prints being all the rage this season, you need a fashionable home for your sunnies. Personally, Ive really been loving large framed glasses and I needed a case to house them because my standard hard cases arent large enough. So this pattern is perfecto for them! You can also add some cute modifications to it like a wrist strap or a closure. Make it a little large to be a mini purse, its all up to you my dears! Enjoy and love  Nicole
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.allfreeknitting.com/tag/summer" target="_blank" title="Free knitting patterns for summer">
          <img alt="sunglasses case Summer Loving: Free Sunglasses Case Pattern!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-538" height="358" src="http://d274dmtz4zj3ch.cloudfront.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/sunglasses-case.jpg" title="sunglasses-case" width="509"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h1>
         Free Knitting Pattern: Sunglasses Case
        </h1>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Materials:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          1 skein of Darn Good Yarns Recycled Resolution (it can be the classic, sparkle or unpixillated versions of this yarn!)
         </li>
         <li>
          Size US 10.5 (6.5mm) knitting needles or the size to have you obtain 4 sts per inch.
         </li>
         <li>
          A piece of fabric for lining (11x 11)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Instructions:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Cast on 40 stitches
         </li>
         <li>
          Knit 1, Purl 1 every row creating a 11 Rib.
         </li>
         <li>
          Create this until your piece measures 8 inches tall. Bind off.
         </li>
         <li>
          **Note** If you get a really long stretch of one color, cut your yarn and change it to another color to keep this small piece visually interesting and super colorful!
         </li>
         <li>
          Setting up your lining
         </li>
         <li>
          Now take your lining material. Lay it flat on a table and fold in the edges to match your knitted piece. Make the knitted piece perfectly square. Pin the edges of the knitted piece to the fabric. This is a nice process be-cause you are doing a dry blocking of your knitted project. If you would like to show off the lining like I did, just offset your lining fabric and fold it over the top of your case and throw some stitches in to hold the fabric in place and finish the edge. I put the fabric over the knitted piece and it makes a nice, thick edging.
         </li>
         <li>
          Now, if you have a needle with a large enough eye whip stitch the edge with the left over yarn. If the needle isnt that large then no worries! Just use thread and stitch closely.
         </li>
         <li>
          Then fold your piece in two and overhand stitch the side seam and the bottom seem.
         </li>
        </ol>

        </p>
