         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           Williams rewarded their staff with a bonus worth 2 million after returning to the front of the grid following a decade in the wilderness  despite the Formula One operation making a 42.5 million loss last year.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           The product of 10 years of poor performance was reflected in the sizeable loss for 2014 announced on Monday; the hangover from their awful run, according to the teams senior management.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/motorsport/formulaone/11466614/Formula-One-teams-ranked-for-2015-by-David-Coulthard.html">
            <strong>

            </strong>
           </a>
          </p>

         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           But the 660 staff at Williams base in Grove, Oxfordshire, each received 3,000 for finishing third in the constructors championship, higher than Ferrari. Their revival was aided by signing with Mercedes as engine supplier and their hire of Pat Symonds as technical director; a respected figure with a controversial past following his involvement in the crashgate affair.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           <iframe frameborder="0" height="338" id="tweetframe" scrolling="no" src="//renderer.qmerce.com/interaction/5506b5fd7fdf079b4ef1e516" width="100%">

         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           Team founder Sir Frank Williams - who will remain in charge for as long as he wants despite overseeing a decade in the midfield  has seen his team make a promising start to the year, although they have lost some ground to Mercedes and the Italian outfit.
          </p>
          <p>
           Formula Ones critics  and some of its struggling teams  will seize on the headline figure to point to the sports ills, but Williams insisted there is a more nuanced picture. The overall loss for the group, which includes an advanced engineering business, was 34.3 million as opposed to an 11.9 million profit in 2013.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img caption="" credit="Getty Images" declared-caption="" height="387" name="williams2" photographer="Clive Mason" refid="3282104" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/03282/williams2_3282104b.jpg" version="b" width="620"/>

          <p>
           But Claire Williams, deputy team principal and de facto head of the operation, said on Monday there were extenuating circumstances. Williams paid for finishing well down the order for three consecutive seasons, hitting their prize money hard. They expect to receive a 60 per cent boost in income from Bernie Ecclestones Formula One Management this season.
          </p>
          <p>
           The team also lost sponsorship revenue from Pastor Maldonado and Venezuelas state oil company, PDVSA, which is believed to be worth as much as 30 million. Maldonado went to Lotus and his money is keeping that team afloat.
          </p>
          <p>
           
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/picturegalleries/11561664/Britains-richest-sportsmen-2015-including-Lewis-Hamilton-Wayne-Rooney-and-Andy-Murray.html">

            </strong>
           </a>

          </p>

           It is very tough but history says that we have beaten these giants in Formula One nine times in the constructors championship, Williams. We have always said that we know we do not have the kind of budgets that some of our rivals have.
          </p>

           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/motorsport/formulaone/11463384/The-sport-still-takes-my-breath-away.-Its-great-to-be-a-part-of-it.html">
