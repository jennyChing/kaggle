              </div>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Many of you know Diana Vick,aka
               <span class="ljuser i-ljuser i-ljuser-type-P " lj:user="artvixn">
                <a class="i-ljuser-profile" href="http://artvixn.livejournal.com/profile" target="_self">
                 <img class="i-ljuser-userhead" src="http://l-stat.livejournal.net/img/userinfo.gif?v=17080?v=128.6"/>
                </a>
                <a class="i-ljuser-username" href="http://artvixn.livejournal.com/" target="_self">
                 <b>
                  artvixn
                 </b>
                </a>
               </span>
               ,
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               from
               <span class="ljuser i-ljuser i-ljuser-type-C " lj:user="steamcon">
                <a class="i-ljuser-profile" href="http://steamcon.livejournal.com/profile" target="_self">
                 <img class="i-ljuser-userhead" src="http://l-stat.livejournal.net/img/community.gif?v=556?v=128.6"/>
                </a>
                <a class="i-ljuser-username" href="http://steamcon.livejournal.com/" target="_self">
                 <b>
                  steamcon
                 </b>
                </a>
               </span>
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               , Norwescon and other local events and her passionate drive to promote and educate the public about all the nuances of steampunk. I was delighted she had time to sit down over coffee and grant an interview to NWSFS!
               <br/>
               <br/>
              </span>
              <a name="cutid1">
              </a>
              Steampunk seems to be evolving; as I've heard the terms dieselpunk, and atomic punk, being bandied around - not to mention more specific terms like gaslamp fantasy etc.. I'm all for evolution; but is it possible steampunk might morph into genres seemingly unrelated to their parent? While interesting to contemplate, is this a good thing?
              <br/>
              &lt;/span&gt;
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               It seems there are two distinct camps when it comes to the use of the word steampunk. Those of us who feel that steampunk refers to a genre of science fiction/fantasy occurring in Victorian or steam-age times, and those who believe steampunk is a feeling or aesthetic that refers to anything grungy, faux industrial and old i.e. earlier than the 1950s. Actually the latter camp generally decries there being any definition whatsoever, often saying its whatever you want it to be. I feel that the anything crowd are using the term incorrectly, and perhaps retro futurism might better suit the huge umbrella they are describing. Steampunk, dieselpunk, clockpunk, atomicpunk, etc would then all be found underneath that term. It makes no sense to say that dieselpunk is a child of steampunk. They are brothers with different technological levels defining them. Gaslamp fantasy was merely a term thought up so that they didnt have to call themselves steampunk.
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               The confusion here lead me to write my
               <a href="http://www.dianavick.com/?p=57" target="_blank" title="Steampunk Litmus Test">
                Steampunk Litmus Test
               </a>
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               so I could have some way of vetting all the things people wanted to call steampunk. Its not perfect, but it helps me when trying to decide if something really fits in the genre.
               <br/>
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(192,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               And do you have your own favorite niche?
               <br/>
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Creativity. I love that when I dress steampunk, no one expects me to be a particular character. I love that when I tell people about steampunk, they get a gleam in their eye and go off to do their own thing. Its inspiring to folks and thats what I love about it. It has a wonderful aesthetic. It has imagination. It has creativity.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               So tell me more about your intense schedule prior to Steamcon; I understand you have quite the itinerary going.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               RainFurrest is first; I will be showing in their art show as well as having a table. Then Vcon , which has a steampunk theme; a friend offered to go up with me and promote Steamcon, doing steampunk some good. The third con, Congaloosh, has been a dream of mine. Disneyworld used to have the Adventurer's Club which got closed. Dedicated fans started a club. Their first convention was last year, but it was too close to Steamcon, This year they moved it and I can't wait to go! The Friday night banquet is in the Indiana Jones Adventure Stunt Show. Disney's involved this year; and between the guests who have been invited and the energy of the fans, I'm really excited. Imagineers and original cast members will be there. I'm going on the Jungle Cruise, complete with a talk from one of the original cast. The name Congaloosh comes from the secret club salute: wiggle, drink, and wave  then say congaloosh.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Last is Orycon which is the weekend before our convention; attending this year was a real debate for us. But since it's local, we felt we should be there. I won't have a booth; but will be doing several panels. But most of our energy energy will be devoted to Steamcon promotion as it right around the corner.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Wow, that's one heck of a schedule. And in addition, there are steampunk events between now and the convention, aren't there?
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               There are Steamcon meetings as well as the swap meet on September 18
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 7.2pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: super; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               th
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               ..
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               We wanted to have more summertime steampunk activities, like Steampunk University; but spring worked out better. We had someone express interest in running activities this summer; but since he'd already committed to his fair circuit, he really didn't have time.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               The hardest thing for these events is finding a venue. Speakers, topics, workshop ideas are easy in comparison. Martin was looking into Magneson Park because it is large and we need ventilation for many of the workshops. So far, the Universities have tended to be academic in focus; and Martin wants to see more technical and how-to programming. Even at Steamcon we can't have demos because we're indoors and the lack of ventilation and possible damage to hotel space is a real concern. We can always used more dedicated help planning these events.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               What are some of the demos that you're really excited about?
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               The Iron Bodger contest. 'Bodgering' is a term for making. Give each person a bucket of bits and two hours to make a thing. Currently, having it would require a booth in the hotel parking lot and lots of space  which isn't practical for a fall, indoor convention.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               I didn't do classes at Dragoncon; but since I mentioned it in my costuming class, I had a lot of people come up and ask questions about gun moding. More than about costuming actually, because I was the only one who mentioned it on the panel.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               BTW, thanks for the gun modding posts on the blog. Good pictures of the entire process and easy-to-read, thorough instructions make life easier.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">

              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Gail Carriger will be there besides Cherie Priest. I've enjoyed her books; but overall, I don't know how I feel about the supernatural in steampunk. It really has no scientific explanation; and while I enjoy it, I don't feel it completely fits within the steampunk genre.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               I don't know if Blaylock's
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: italic; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Homunculus
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               will be available at the convention. I know that K.W. Jeter is being republished by Angry Robot, so perhaps they will also pick up Jims books. His work would be a great addition to the Authors Row.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Currently on my reading list is an anthology titled
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: italic; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Steampunk Reloaded
              </span>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               edited by the Vandermeers. I have also heard about an interesting collection of original Victorian SF; supposedly the illustrations are atrocious; but it still sounds interesting.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               I'll be eager to hear about that last. I know I've found a ton of Victorian-era SF on Project Gutenberg, which has been wonderful. Anything you can tell us about Steamcon III?
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               I am excited to announce the theme for Steamcon III: 20,000 Leagues Under The Sea.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Wow! How exciting!
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Yes, I was chatting with Cherie Priest about submersibles. Apparently our minds work similarly as we came up with this concept at roughly the same time.
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(255,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
               Well, I'm already looking forward to attending!
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">
              </span>
              <br/>
              <span style="BACKGROUND-COLOR: transparent; FONT-STYLE: normal; FONT-FAMILY: Times New Roman; COLOR: rgb(0,0,0); FONT-SIZE: 12pt; VERTICAL-ALIGN: baseline; FONT-WEIGHT: normal; TEXT-DECORATION: none">

               <a href="http://www.steamcon.org/" target="_blank" title="here">
                here

               as well as on LJ.
