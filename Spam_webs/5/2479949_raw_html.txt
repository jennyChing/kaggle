        <header class="article-header entry-header">
         <h1 class="entry-title single-title" itemprop="headline" rel="bookmark">
          Tunisian Crochet: A Knitting and Crochet Hybrid
         </h1>
         <p class="byline entry-meta vcard">
          <span class="by">
           Posted by
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/author/ashley/" rel="author" title="Posts by Ashley Little">
           Ashley Little
          </a>
          <time class="updated entry-time" datetime="2013-10-19" itemprop="datePublished">
           // Oct 19, 2013
          </time>
         </p>
        </header>
        <section class="entry-content cf" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="at-above-post addthis-toolbox" data-title="Tunisian Crochet: A Knitting and Crochet Hybrid" data-url="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/2013/10/what-is-tunisian-crochet/">
         </div>
         <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis-toolbox" data-title="Tunisian Crochet: A Knitting and Crochet Hybrid" data-url="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/2013/10/what-is-tunisian-crochet/">
         </div>
         <p>
          Have you ever seen those crazy hybrids of
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/knitting?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           knitting
          </a>
          needles and
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/crocheting?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           crochet
          </a>
          hooks? Theyre as long as knitting needles, but the ends look like crochet hooks and some even have a little knob on one end. Those weird little things are actually
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/class/Tunisian-Crochet-Revolutions-in-Color-and-Style/69?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           Tunisian crochet
          </a>
          hooks. So, what is Tunisian crochet?
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Crochet Hooks and Knitting Needles in Glass Jars " height="682" src="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Screen-Shot-2013-09-26-at-3.01.11-PM.png" width="479"/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          If knit and crochet had a baby, it would be named Tunisian crochet.
         </h2>
         <p>
          You might also hear some crafters refer to it as afghan crochet. Before I knew what Tunisian crochet looked like, I would see it occasionally and wonder, Is that knitting or crochet? And how did they get it to look like that? Years later, someone in my stitch group showed up wielding one of those crazy hooks and the mystery was solved.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/class/Tunisian-Crochet-Revolutions-in-Color-and-Style/69?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           <img alt="Woman Wearing Crocheted Hat " height="640" src="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/crochet.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
          <br/>
          <em>
           Photo via Craftsy instructor
           <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/user/21532/projects?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
            Jennifer Hansen
           </a>
          </em>
         </p>
         <h2>
          How is it different from knitting and crochet?
         </h2>
         <p>
          Tunisian crochet starts with chain stitcheslike the crochet youre familiar with, but then it takes on its own persona thats somewhere between knitting and crochet.
         </p>
         <p>
          Youll work back and forth with Tunisian crochet, never actually turning the work as you would with knitting or crochet. Each row in Tunisian crochet has two steps: the forward and the return. The forward is similar to what we call
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/2013/04/cast-on-knitting/?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           casting on in knitting
          </a>
          , while the return is similar to
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/2013/07/cast-off-knitting/?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           casting off
          </a>
          . The difference? Casting off in Tunisian crochet doesnt mean youre at the end of the project.
         </p>
         <p>
          Some of the Tunisian crochet stitches, like Tunisian knit stitch and Tunisian purl stitch, even sound similar to types of knitting stitches. The Tunisian knit and purl stitches look similar to the
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/techniques/knitters-handbook/basic-knitting-stitches?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           knitting knit and purl stitches
          </a>
          , but theyre created in completely different ways.
         </p>
         <p>
          Tunisian crochet looks different from any knitted or crocheted fabric. Sometimes it can actually appear woven. Take a look at this
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/project/view/spa-cloth---blue/42003?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           spa cloth
          </a>
          made by Craftsy member
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/user/267316?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           morijay
          </a>
          . You cant get a stitch like that with knitting or crochet.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Blue Spa Cloth " class="alignnone wp-image-41693" height="446" src="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/full_9952_42003_Spaclothblue_1.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Incorporating color into Tunisian crochet
         </h2>
         <p>
          Onebenefit ofTunisian crochet for you color lovers is that you can usea whole palette of colorand they weave together in a way they cant with knitting or crochet. If you prefer a crochet
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/2013/05/intarsia-knitting-tips/?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           intarsia
          </a>
          look, that can also be accomplished with Tunisian crochet. I love the colors that Craftsy member
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/user/801527/projects?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           tdkshine
          </a>
          used on this in-progress Tunisian crochet project seen below.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Tunisian Crochet Project on Needles" class="size-full wp-image-41738 alignnone" height="440" src="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/full_9251_52021_SummerWildflowerMultiGarment_1.jpg" width="600"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Creating a color palette is one ofmy favoriteparts about Tunisian crochet. You dont have to limit yourself to just one or two colors. Go nuts! Experiment and see how all the colors look when you combine them.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Creating a Pattern in Tunisian Crochet " class="size-full wp-image-41744 alignnone" height="767" src="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/full_9321_45638_TunisianCrochetMultigarment_1.jpg" width="573"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Once its worked up into a complete project, Tunisian crochet will look like nothing youve ever seen before in knitting or crochet, but rather a perfect hybrid of both. Just look at Craftsy member
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/user/653443?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           Komejan
          </a>
          s
          <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/project/view/tunisian-crochet-wrap/126718?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
           Tunisian crochet wrap
          </a>
          for proof.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Woman Modeling Crocheted Wrap " class="alignnone size-full wp-image-41760" height="768" src="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/full_4958_126718_TunisianCrochetWrap_1.jpg" width="576"/>
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           If Tunisian crochet sounds like something youd like to try, check out the Craftsy class
           <em>
            <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/class/Tunisian-Crochet-Revolutions-in-Color-and-Style/69?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
             Tunisian Crochet: Revolutions in Color and Style
            </a>
           </em>
           with Jennifer Hansen. Shell help you through foundation stitches, choosing colors, and stitches like the ripple stitch.
          </p>
          <p>
           You might also enjoy our post which gives a bit of info on the
           <a href="http://www.craftsy.com/blog/2012/11/cats-corner-a-tunisian-thanksgiving/?_ct=rbew&amp;_ctp=41551">
            history of Tunisian crochet
           </a>
           .
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Tomorrow on the Craftsy blog, well share 8 tips for weaving in those pesky ends!
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: center">
          <em>
           Have you ever tried Tunisian crochet? How does it compare to crochet and knitting?
          </em>
