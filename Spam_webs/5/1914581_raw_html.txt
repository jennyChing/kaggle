        <div class="stumbleupon-button">
         <su:badge layout="1" location="http://growingupgabel.com/handmade-christmas-gifts-easy-reversibl-handbag/">
         </su:badge>
        </div>
       </div>
       <!-- .social-media-icons -->
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Im trying to give as many handmade Christmas gifts as I can this year. This purse or handbag is perfect for the tween or teen or in your life. Use any cute fabric to turn just four simple pieces into a reversible handbag.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://growingupgabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Purse-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Easy Reversible Handbag" class="aligncenter wp-image-4488" height="673" src="http://growingupgabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Purse-1.jpg" title="Easy Reversible Handbag: Growing Up Gabel" width="604"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         After I finished making child aprons for the little kids for Christmas, I needed something for our newly minted teenager. I already have
         <a href="http://growingupgabel.com/2012/11/holiday-gifting-for-teens-daisy-red-ryder-bb-gun/" title="Holiday Gifting for Teens: A Daisy Red Ryder BB Gun (Giveaway!)">
          a Daisy BB gun
         </a>
         for her tom boy side and I wanted a handmade Christmas gift for her feminine side. I had pinned this purse tutorial from
         <a href="http://verypurpleperson.com/downloads/?did=19" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://verypurpleperson.com/downloads/?did=19', 'Very Purple Person');" target="_blank">
          Very Purple Person
         </a>
         a while ago and decided it was what I was looking for. Im a very beginner sewer and I knew from perusing the tutorial that it is written for someone with a little more skill than I had. I need to know to sew the pieces wrong sides together to start and what seam allowances to use. But it looked pretty simple so I decided to go for it anyway.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://growingupgabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Purse-3.jpg">
          <img alt="Easy Reversible Handbag" class="aligncenter wp-image-4490" height="617" src="http://growingupgabel.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Purse-3-988x1024.jpg" title="Easy Reversible Handbag: Growing Up Gabel" width="596"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The first purse I made (the pink butterfly one) took me about an hour because I made a lot of mistakes figuring out the tutorial. But the second purse (the turquoise one) only took me about 30 minutes. I think it takes longer to cut out the pieces than it did to sew it together. I did opt to leave out the darts. Ive never sewn darts before and I didnt need more to think about and I think the purses turned out really well without them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Dont worry if you dont sew! I have several other non-sewing handmade Christmas ideas like a
         <a href="http://growingupgabel.com/2011/12/family-memory-jars/" title="Holiday Gift Idea: Family Memory Jars">
          Family Memory Jar
         </a>
         and a
         <a href="http://growingupgabel.com/2011/12/handprint-christmas-trees/" title="Handprint Christmas Trees">
          Handprint Christmas Tree print
         </a>
         (which is perfect for grandparents!).
        </p>
