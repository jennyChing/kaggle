        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         The Hardwood Flooring Discussion
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-04-12T03:00:58+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 12, 2012
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://howtonestforless.com/author/howtonestforless/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            howtonestforless
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/2012/04/12/hardwood-carpet/#comments">
           31 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-config="none" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fhowtonestforless.com%2F2012%2F04%2F12%2Fhardwood-carpet%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fhowtonestforless.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2012%2F04%2Fhardwoodfloors400.jpeg&amp;description=The%20Hardwood%20Flooring%20Discussion" rel="nobox">
          <img data-pib-button="true" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Im in desperate need of new flooring throughout my home. I meant it, desperate.
        </p>
        <p>
         My hubby and I know that we want to put our house on the market in the next year or two, but we cant wait any longer to upgrade our 6 year old cheap carpeting. But to what? It seems like no one talks about carpet anymore.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          Why is that?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/hardwoodfloors400.jpeg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="436" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/hardwoodfloors400.jpeg" title="hardwoodfloors400" width="550"/>
         </a>
         <span style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.bhgrealestate.com/Live/Remodeling/Basic-Builds/Choosing-Hardwoods-for-Floors.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bhgrealestate.com/Live/Remodeling/Basic-Builds/Choosing-Hardwoods-for-Floors.html', 'source');" target="_blank" title="BHG Real Estate">
           source
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <strong>
          Flooring makes a first impression.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         If you walk into a home, one of the first things youre going to notice is the flooring. Not the windows, not the little dent in the hallway wall, not even the beige toilet. You want to grasp people at their first step when they enter your home.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Nice flooring will help sell your home faster.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         These days you can purchase a brand new home at rock-bottom pricing. People arent shopping for fixer-uppers if theyre buying resale; they want to move in and get settled.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/Hard-Wood-Floors.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5252" height="327" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/Hard-Wood-Floors.jpg" title="Hard-Wood-Floors" width="500"/>
         </a>
         Could you imagine the home in this
         <a href="http://www.bamboofloorings.org/category/hard-wood-floors/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bamboofloorings.org/category/hard-wood-floors/', 'photo');" target="_blank" title="Bamboo floorings">
          photo
         </a>
         with carpeting? It just wouldnt have the same warm feeling that you get with hardwood flooring.
        </p>
        <p>
         It seems most renovators and resellers are leaning towards hardwood flooring these days.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hardwood does not have to be replaced as often as carpeting.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Lets say you plan on selling your house two or three years down the road. You might have to replace the carpet again (yep, cranberry juice stains can happen a week after you get new carpeting or you might have some finicky buyer that doesnt want carpet that isnt brand new!). Your hardwood flooring would not need to be replaced once its installed.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hardwood flooring has a better return on investment.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Yep, hardwood flooring just seems to impress more. New buyers will tend to pay $10,000 on the price of a $200,000 home if you have hardwood flooring over carpeting. If your investment was only $4,000 for the hardwood upgrade over the cost of new carpeting, youve made $6,000! Dont go crazy-expensive though on hardwood flooring if the house is not worth it. You wouldnt add $15,000 of hardwood flooring in a $120,000 house.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hardwood flooring is cleaner than carpeting.
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Carpet is impossible to fully clean. Dirt, dust and dander can accumulate under the padding and weave over time. Gross. With hardwood flooring, I can vacuum and mopalthough it sure will show all the dirt a lot more!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/carpet_stain_removal_Brighton.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5256" height="288" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/carpet_stain_removal_Brighton.jpg" title="carpet_stain_removal_Brighton" width="460"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.brightoncarpetcleaning.co.uk/Stain-removal-tips.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.brightoncarpetcleaning.co.uk/Stain-removal-tips.html', 'source');" target="_blank" title="Photo source">
          source
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hardwood flooring does have some cons though.
         </strong>
         It can be pretty loud since it wont absorb noise like carpeting. It is also colder during the winter months, although you can cover with pretty area rugs in key spots.
        </p>
        <p>
         And yes, hardwood flooring is sleek and chic, but it can be expensive. Carpeting is much more affordable. You can even find Frieze carpeting for less than hardwood flooring.
        </p>
        <p>
         But there are other choices than hardwood flooring and carpeting.
         <a href="http://www.younghouselove.com/2012/03/ok-now-you-can-put-a-cork-in-us/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.younghouselove.com/2012/03/ok-now-you-can-put-a-cork-in-us/', 'Young House Love');" target="_blank" title="Young House Love">
          Young House Love
         </a>
         recently put cork floors in their kitchen and they look FABULOUS!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/floor-done1.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5249" height="521" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/floor-done1.jpg" title="floor-done1" width="350"/>
         </a>
         Faux wood laminate is also popular these days but dont have the durability of natural hardwood flooring.
        </p>
        <p>
         Vinyl is also a choice, although it can get dinged pretty easily. Plus you have to watch out for the grime build-up and discoloration in the seams. I have faux wood vinyl flooring in my kitchen and am ready for an upgrade.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/my-kitchen.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5250" height="427" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/my-kitchen.jpg" title="my kitchen" width="640"/>
         </a>
         And of course ceramic tile flooring. This is great in a kitchen area, but would be awfully cold in the winter months throughout your entire home!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          So, whats my choice?
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Recently Ive been hearing about engineered hardwood flooring. Its basically multiple layers of plywood covered with a layer of hardwood. These floors have been known to have a higher resistance to moisture (which can be great in high humidity). Engineered hardwood flooring can be resealed, but cannot be sanded and refinished. But average lifespan is heard to be around 20-30 years (depending on traffic)!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/engineered.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5253" height="427" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/engineered.jpg" title="engineered" width="640"/>
         </a>
         This is engineered flooring sample above, and an actual hardwood flooring sample below
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Can YOU see the difference?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/hardwood.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="427" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/hardwood.jpg" title="hardwood" width="640"/>
         </a>
         Well, I ordered a bunch of free samples this past week. Goodness! I had no idea there were so many options! Handscraped vs. smooth. Wide plank vs. varying width vs. narrow plank. And colors galore!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/hardwood-flooring-options.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="427" src="http://howtonestforless.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/hardwood-flooring-options.jpg" title="hardwood flooring options" width="640"/>
         </a>
         I still have a bunch of research to do, but at least I have a head start in the right direction.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          So, what are your thoughts on hardwood flooring?
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Any other options you prefer?
        </p>

              <div class="comment-content" itemprop="commentText">
               <p>
                This is not completely accurate information.  Whether you can refinish engineered flooring depends on the thickness of the wear layer (the real wood veneer) and this thickness varies considerably, from 0.6mm to as much as 7mm.  It would be a bad idea to attempt to sand a floor with 0.6mm veneer, but 7 mm can be sanded multiple times (as many as any solid hardwood.)  Wear layers in between in those extremes can usually be renfinished once or twice.
               </p>
               <p>
                Also, there is new floor refinishing technology which merely abrades the finish with a brush-like tool, rather than sanding to raw wood.  This allows thin veneer or sculpted floors (such as hand-scraped) to be refinished.
               </p>
               <p>
                Finally, what most people dont realize is that the vast majority of solid hardwood floors are never refinished.  Refinishing is an expensive and very inconvenient process.  Most people will replace individual planks when major damage occurs, and otherwise just touch-up scratches, even in floors which can be sanded down.  The issue of refinishing seems to unnecessarily worry prospective buyers.
               </p>

            When I used to sell flooring, we pretty much ask the following questions:
           </p>
           <p>
            House or condo?
            <br/>
            If house, we push the client towards a hardwood, if condo, we push them towards an engineered solution.
           </p>
           <p>
            Then comes the question of budget?
            <br/>
            Between 1-2$ we recommend that they use a laminate floating floor
            <br/>
            2-3$ we recommend a domestic wood in a country grade. (or floating engineered/regular engineered)
            <br/>
            3-4$ we recommend the premium grade domestic woods
            <br/>
            5$+ we try and push an exotic Brazilian floor
           </p>
           <p>
            Hope this helps!
           </p>
