                                <article class="item" id="main-story">
                                 <header class="clearfix da-all da-pad ta-all ta-pad" id="header-story">
                                  <h3 class="heading-section">
                                   <a href="/topics/categories/politics.html">
                                    Politics
                                   </a>
                                  </h3>
                                  <hgroup>
                                   <h1 class="heading-story">
                                    UK uses health workers in counter-terror plan
                                   </h1>
                                   <div>
                                    <h2 class="standfirst">
                                     Muslims in the UK say they are unfairly targeted by programme asking medical workers to identify potential 'terrorists'.
                                    </h2>
                                    <p class="meta">
                                     <span class="oryx-heading-profile">
                                      <a href="/profile/matthew-cassel.html">
                                       Matthew Cassel
                                      </a>
                                      |
                                     </span>
                                     <time datetime="10 Apr 201408:23 GMT">
                                      10 Apr 201408:23 GMT
                                     </time>
                                     |
                                     <span id="article-topics">
                                      <a data-topic-name="Politics" href="/topics/categories/politics.html">
                                       Politics
                                      </a>
                                      ,
                                      <a data-topic-name="United Kingdom" href="/topics/country/united-kingdom.html">
                                       United Kingdom
                                      </a>
                                     </span>
                                    </p>
                                   </div>
                                  </hgroup>
                                 </header>
                                 <div class="ArticleTopToolbar" id="Article-Top-Toolbar">
                                 </div>
                                 <div class="video-container" id="vdoContainer">
                                 </div>
                                 <script type="text/javascript">
                                  RenderPagesVideo('3451761637001', 0, '100%', 473, 'vdoContainer');
                                 </script>
                                 <div class="article-body" id="article-body">
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 10px;">
                                    <strong>
                                     Al Jazeera's Matthew Cassel reports on the Prevent programme from Birmingham
                                    </strong>
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Medical workers, most would agree, have one important job to do: look after the well-being of their patients. However, in the UK, employees of the National Health Service are now being assigned another task: identifying potential terrorists.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    This new directive comes from the Prevent programme, part of the UK government's counter-terrorism strategy created in the wake of the July 2005 London bombings.
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    As part of this mission, since last year Prevent has been providing mandatory trainingto employees of the National Health Service (NHS) on how to identify potential terrorists among patients, visitors and other medical staff, and report them to the authorities.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Documents given by Prevent to medical workers, copies of which were obtained by Al Jazeera, say the following:
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    "The NHS has been identified as a key player in supporting the Prevent strategy as healthcare staff are considered to be well placed to help to identify concerns and protect people from radicalisation."
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    <strong>
                                     'Government informants'
                                    </strong>
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Al Jazeera spoke to a nurse working for the NHS on condition of anonymity because she was not permitted to talk to the media. (Al Jazeera also learned of similar Prevent training being offered to educators, firefighters and others in the public sector; however, none agreed to discuss the training on record.)
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <table align="right" border="0" cellpadding="3" cellspacing="0" style="width: 180px;">
                                   <tbody>
                                    <tr>
                                     <td bgcolor="#b68809" class="MostActiveDescHeader">
                                      <span style="color: #ffffff; font-family: verdana, geneva; font-size: small;">
                                       <strong>
                                        UK counter-terror plan
                                       </strong>
                                      </span>
                                     </td>
                                    </tr>
                                    <tr>
                                     <td bgcolor="#dfd2ad" class="MostActiveDescBody" valign="top">
                                      <p>
                                       <span style="font-size: 10px;">
                                        The four elements of CONTEST, the UK government's counter-terrorism strategy, include:
                                       </span>
                                       <br/>
                                       <br/>
                                       <span style="font-size: 10px;">
                                        <strong>
                                         Pursue:
                                        </strong>
                                        to stop terrorist attacks
                                       </span>
                                      </p>
                                      <p>
                                       <span style="font-size: 10px;">
                                        <strong>
                                         Prevent:
                                        </strong>
                                        to stop people becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism
                                       </span>
                                      </p>
                                      <p>
                                       <span style="font-size: 10px;">
                                        <strong>
                                         Protect:
                                        </strong>
                                        to strengthen the UK's protection against a terrorist attack
                                       </span>
                                      </p>
                                      <p>
                                       <span style="font-size: 10px;">
                                        <strong>
                                         Prepare:
                                        </strong>
                                        to mitigate the impact of a terrorist attack
                                       </span>
                                      </p>
                                     </td>
                                    </tr>
                                   </tbody>
                                  </table>
                                  <p>
                                   <!-- PAGELOADEDSUCCESSFULLY-->
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    <!-- PAGELOADEDSUCCESSFULLY-->
                                    "The healthcare worker's job is to ultimately treat your patient," the nurse said. "It doesn't matter what they walk in the door with - you, as a healthcare professional within whatever specialty you work, you've been trained to support them."
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    The nurse was concerned by the vague characteristics presented as indicators of possible radicalisation. One of the Prevent documents listed factors such as "identity crisis", "personal crisis" and "unemployment" that could make someone vulnerable to radicalisation.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    The document also listed political views that NHS staff should look out for, such as a "rejection of UK foreign policy", "mistrust of Western media", and "perceptions that UK government policy is discriminatory [eg counter-terrorist legislation]".
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    The nurse said trainers were careful to avoid mentioning Muslims. However, medical staff were told that the main terrorist threat to the UK comes from Islamist groups, and the violent acts mentioned were mostly incidents perpetrated by Muslims.
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    She added that identifying potential terrorists was not part of her job as a health worker.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    "It's actually something that the police should be doing," she said. "Offering this training, it's almost as if we're becoming government informants."
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    <strong>
                                     Outreach
                                    </strong>
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Sir Peter Fahy, chief constable of Greater Manchester and national lead for Prevent's police programme, confirmed to Al Jazeera that medical workers and other civil servants were being given counter-terrorism training.
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <table align="right" border="0" bordercolor="white" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="border-collapse: collapse; width: 33px;">
                                   <tbody>
                                    <tr>
                                     <td>
                                      <div class="mceVideoBox" style="width: 300; height: 169; float: Right;">
                                       <div id="bc_3449601680001" style="width: 300; height: 169;">
                                        <!-- -->
                                       </div>

// ]]]] &gt;
                                       </script>
                                      </div>
                                      <p style="text-align: center;">
                                       <span style="font-size: 10px;">
                                        <strong style="text-align: center;">
                                         Sir Peter Fahy describes the goal of the Prevent programme
                                        </strong>
                                       </span>
                                      </p>

                                  </table>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    "If there are health professionals who have serious concerns that the person they're dealing with is getting involved in extremist activity and that is harming their well-being and harming their community, then yes, absolutely, it's about them being able to raise those concerns," Sir Fahy said.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    "Clearly, there is a significant terrorist threat to this country. We can understand that people can feel very strongly about international issues and other political issues, and it's trying to identify people who may be at risk of taking that concern to a level of violence."
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    On its website, Prevent says it seeks to tackle terrorist threats wherever they occur. However, it also
                                    <a class="internallink" href="https://www.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/97976/prevent-strategy-review.pdf" target="_blank">
                                     says
                                    </a>
                                    that the "most serious is from al-Qaeda, its affiliates and like-minded organisations". With the overwhelming majority of Prevent's efforts focused on British Muslims, many in the minority community believe they are being unfairly targeted.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Sir Fahy acknowledged the grievance, and said he hopes to address complaints by making Prevent's efforts more transparent to the public.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    "It's really about how we... confront the threat of terrorism, but at the same time maintaining the confidence of the Muslim community as we go along."
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    <strong>
                                     Lost confidence
                                    </strong>
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    But that confidence may already be lost.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Jahan Mahmood, a historian and former adviser to the government's counter-terrorism unit, said that while Prevent mentions possible extremism from a range of groups, "there is a disproportionate focus on Muslims, there is no doubt about that.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    And that's also one of the reasons it's failed to gain traction with the Muslim population".
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <table align="right" border="0" bordercolor="white" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="border-collapse: collapse; width: 33px;">
                                   <tbody>
                                    <tr>

                                       </span>

                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    In Birmingham's predominantly Muslim Sparkbrook neighbourhood, Mahmood pointed above his head to lampposts where in 2010, the government installed hundreds of surveillance cameras - ostensibly for monitoring crime in the area.
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    But it was
                                    <a class="internallink" href="http://www.theguardian.com/uk/2010/jun/04/surveillance-cameras-birmingham-muslims?guni=Article:in%20body%20link" target="_blank">
                                     soon exposed
                                    </a>
                                    that the counter-terrorism unit installed the cameras to monitor residents. After an outcry from the Muslim community, bags were placed on top of the cameras and they were eventually removed, with authorities assuring that they had never been turned on.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Mahmood said the incident led to a serious breakdown of trust between Muslims and the police.
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    Al Jazeera spoke to a number of British Muslims in cities like Birmingham and Manchester, who said they believe Prevent and other counter-terrorism efforts are less about preventing violence than about
                                   </span>
                                   <a class="internallink" href="http://www.aljazeera.com/indepth/features/2014/02/surveilling-uk-muslims-cradle-grave-201422575943406757.html" target="_blank">
                                    monitoring every aspect of Muslim life
                                   </a>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    . This has left many in the community feeling alienated from the rest of British society.
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    But Mahmood warned that it's not only British Muslims who should be concerned over the government's counter-terrorism laws and programmes like Prevent.
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
                                  <p>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    In recent years, Mahmood said: "We've seen draconian legislation introduced - and that means we are surrendering our civil liberties. Where will this end?
                                   </span>
                                   <span style="font-size: 14px;">
                                    The rest of Britain needs to wake up to the fact that we are sleep-walking ourselves into very serious times."
                                   </span>
                                  </p>
