       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         <a href="http://www.jjcrochet.com/blog/crochet-hexagon-blanket-is-finished/" rel="bookmark">
          Crochet Hexagon Blanket is Finished!
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="comments-link">
         <a href="http://www.jjcrochet.com/blog/crochet-hexagon-blanket-is-finished/#comments">
          15 Replies
         </a>
        </div>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://jjcrochet.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/DSC_6478.jpg">
          <br/>
         </a>
         Since
         <a href="http://jjcrochet.com/blog/crochet-hexagon-blanket-project/">
          its inception
         </a>
         on November 15, 2011, I knew this crocheted hexagon blanket was going to be breath-taking. With over 17 different colors combinations in a wonderful yarn, how could it not be? I knew this would be a one-of-a-kind crocheted afghan.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/jjcrochet.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/DSC_6485.jpg" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/downloads/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/DSC_6485.jpg');">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-549" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/jjcrochet.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/DSC_6485-1024x634.jpg?resize=614%2C380" title="Crocheted Hexagon Blanket"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          Project Details:
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Inspired by Attic 24s
          <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/weblog/2008/12/the-hexagon-blanket-tadah.html" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/attic24.typepad.com/weblog/2008/12/the-hexagon-blanket-tadah.html');">
           Crocheted Hexagon Blanket
          </a>
          (free
          <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/weblog/hexagon-howto.html" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/attic24.typepad.com/weblog/hexagon-howto.html');">
           hexagon how-to crochet pattern
          </a>
          can be found on
          <a href="http://attic24.typepad.com/weblog/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/attic24.typepad.com/weblog/');">
           her crochet blog
          </a>
          )
         </li>
         <li>
          Yarn: Rowan
          <a href="http://www.knitrowan.com/yarns/handknit-cotton.aspx" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/www.knitrowan.com/yarns/handknit-cotton.aspx');">
           Hand-Knit Cotton
          </a>
         </li>
         <li>
          Crochet Hook: Size E
         </li>
         <li>
          Time it took to make working on-and-off: 5 1/2 Months (while finishing my 8th semester and graduating from college!)
         </li>
         <li>
          Intended use: High school graduation gift for a lovely girl
         </li>
         <li>
          Worth it factor (WIF): YES! Id do it again in a heartbeat.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         As I was recently reminded, I said
         <a href="http://jjcrochet.com/blog/crochet-hexagon-blanket-in-a-day/">
          in one of my posts
         </a>
         that, and I quote, I
         <em>
          dont
         </em>
         crochet afghans. But with two crocheted afghans under my belt (this hexy one and another
         <a href="http://jjcrochet.com/blog/crochet-hexagon-blanket/">
          hexagon baby blanket
         </a>
         ), Im starting to be converted to the world of crocheted afghans.
        </p>
        <p>
         Afghans are different than crocheting hats. In a way, theyretherapeutic. Especially afghans made from motifs. Completing a large project is more satisfying. Watching something grow piece-by-piece is exciting. Its all about the thrill and theres nothing like a crocheters high. Wait, or is it a runners high? Whatever  crocheting and running are the same as far as Im concerned. (But dont listen to me  clearly
         <a href="http://fourpairsofshoes.wordpress.com/2011/04/03/and-then-there-were-three-jayna-grassel-turns-in-her-shoes/" onclick="javascript:pageTracker._trackPageview('/outgoing/fourpairsofshoes.wordpress.com/2011/04/03/and-then-there-were-three-jayna-grassel-turns-in-her-shoes/');">
          Im no runner
         </a>
         .)
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, hexy is finished and I am so very proud of her. I mailed her off a few weeks ago to live at her new home. Thought saying goodbye was bittersweet, Im overjoyed knowing shell be sharing her rainbow of joy with others for years to come.
        </p>
        <p>
         I wish you all could see the beauty of this blanket in person, but these pictures will have to do.
        </p>
