        <h3>
         Basic Considerations forAndroid Phones
        </h3>
        <p>
         It seems contradictory to call 17 different considerations
         <em>
          basic
         </em>
         , but thats what they are: the below are 17 of the most important specifications that dictate your smartphone experience. That doesnt mean theyll all be important to you, though, so as you read through, consider your life and how you use your smartphone. Focus on the things that are necessary for you.
        </p>
        <p>
         For comparison purposes, weve included each of these 17 specs in the smartphone overviews, as well as in our Android smartphone comparison chart.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          MSRP:
         </strong>
         If you have to buy a phone off-contract, the retail price can be a real surprise  instead of $99 or $199, you may be looking at serious sticker shock to the tune of $500, $600, or even $700+.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Release Date:
         </strong>
         Obviously, newer phones typically have newer technology  but thats not what release date is all about. Smartphone manufacturers typically release new models in set annual cycles, so a Q4 2013 model will likely see an update in Q4 2014.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2799" style="width: 155px">
         <img alt="HTC One M8" class="wp-image-2799 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.scratchwireless.com/pad/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/HTC-One-M8-145x300.png" width="145"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          HTC One M8
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Operating System:
         </strong>
         As this is an Android smartphone guide (with the iPhone thrown in for comparisons sake), were looking at the specific version (e.g. Ice Cream Sandwich vs. Kitkat) and release (e.g. Kitkat 4.4.3 vs. Kitkat 4.4.4). Additionally, give each phone a try since most Android smartphones (the Nexus 5 is the exception) do not run pure Android, but rather sport a version with extras thrown in. (e.g. Samsungs TouchWiz UI)
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Network:
         </strong>
         Double-check that your smartphone is compatible with your cell phone carrier network. And if you plan to do lots of mobile browsing, choose a phone that supports the lightening-fast 4G LTE technology.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Internal Storage:
         </strong>
         Internal storage capacity is either incredibly important or of very little importance. Heres the deal: 8 GB is the minimum for smartphones today, but you wont actually have 8 GB free to use; lots of that memory is used to store your operating system, settings, apps, and more. If you want to pile on music, video, photos, and other memory-eating features, youll need a lot more than 8 GB. Internal memory is an expensive but necessary add-on, if and only if your phone doesnt have an external card slot (see below).
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Card Slot:
         </strong>
         Several of the phones on our list allow you to sub in a microSD card for extra memory. Capacity runs from 32 GB to a whopping 128 GB, meaning that you can get away with purchasing a less expensive smartphone model (say, the 16 GB version) without sacrificing storage for your favorite media.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Dimensions:
         </strong>
         Your phones overall size will affect how you use it: a smaller phone is easy to stash in your pocket, while a 6 phablet (and its accompanying large screen) is an excellent choice for work on-the-go.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Display Size:
         </strong>
         If a phones dimensions dictate how easy it is to store your phone in tight spaces, its display (screen) size dictates ease of use  typing, browsing, reading, and all those other things you do everyday. Before you go small with a 4 or big with a 6, really consider how youll use your phone.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Weight:
         </strong>
         Its not surprising that the smallest (dimensions + screen size) phones are the lightest, but some smartphones within the same class (e.g. 4 phones) vary in weight. Even half an ounce can make a difference, if you spend a lot of time holding your phone to your ear, or reading on the subway, or really, handling your phone for any extended period of time.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Processor:
         </strong>
         Almost every phone on the market today is as fast as you need it to be, so when you buy the fastest processor youre basically making an investment in the future  how well your phone will work with tomorrows OS, apps, and technology.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          RAM:
         </strong>
         More RAM means faster phone speeds: faster app functionality and a generally better overall experience.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Resolution:
         </strong>
         Full HD (1080p) is the sweetheart of smartphones, and for good reason: these screens are noticeably sharper. But lower resolutions are still crisp and clear  and a lot cheaper.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2800" style="width: 310px">
         <img alt="Huawei Ascend Mate7" class="size-medium wp-image-2800" height="156" src="http://www.scratchwireless.com/pad/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Huawei-Ascend-Mate7-300x156.jpg" width="300"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Huawei Ascend Mate7
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Pixel Density:
         </strong>
         If youre after a super sharp, gorgeous display, consider pixel density as well as resolution. The rule is, the higher the ppi, the better the image. But the difference on a smartphones small screen, even the relatively large 6 displays, is negligible.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Water Resistance:
         </strong>
         If youve lost your phone to an accidental drop in the sink  or just been caught without an umbrella in a thunderstorm you know how incredible it would be to have a water-resistant smartphone. Two of the phones on our list (the Samsung Galaxy S5 and the Sony Xperia Z3) are both water-resistant for up to 30 minutes in shallow (3-5 feet) of water.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Battery Life:
         </strong>
         Battery life is an extremely important consideration for people on-the-go and without constant access to their smartphone chargers. Consider standby battery life  how long your phone can stay charged when not in use  if you keep your phone in your purse, briefcase, or pocket for hours (or days) at a time.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Talk Time:
         </strong>
         For many, talk time is even more important than standby battery life. This spec identifies the maximum minutes or hours you can talk before running your battery down to zero.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Camera(s):
         </strong>
         Megapixels are an imprecise measure of a cameras quality, but theyre the only quantifier we can list. All the phones on our list offer decent cameras, but if youre after sparkling photos, give each contender a test run before you buy.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Other Smartphone Considerations
        </h3>
        <p>
         In addition to the Big 17, there are a few additional features you may want to consider before purchasing your smartphone. This list is by no means exhaustive, but it does cover some of the most common additions to the need- or want-list.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Build:
         </strong>
         This is the material with which your phone is made. Most phones, even high-end smartphones, are made with plastic. The HTC One M8 and iPhone 6/6 Plus are exceptions, with aluminum bodies, and the Moto X is also available with a unique (and uniquely aesthetic) leather, wood or bamboo backing.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Color:
         </strong>
         If youre picky about color, check the options for your phone of choice.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Navigation:
         </strong>
         Navigation buttons are important for two primary reasons: 1) some people prefer physical buttons they can actually click; and 2) physical navigation buttons dont take up pixels, meaning your entire display is devoted to your apps.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Stylus:
         </strong>
         Any smartphone today is compatible with third-party styluses, but the Galaxy Note 4 comes with its very own stylus, designed to work perfectly with the phone and screen.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_2801" style="width: 310px">
         <img alt="iPhone 6 and 6 Plus" class="size-medium wp-image-2801" height="226" src="http://www.scratchwireless.com/pad/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/iPhone-6-Plus-300x226.png" width="300"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          iPhone 6 and 6 Plus
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Fingerprint Sensor:
         </strong>
         If youre tired of security codes, a fingerprint sensor may be the way to go. And, even better, fingerprint sensors are going somewhere: the Galaxy S5s sensor lets you login and pay with Paypal, no password required.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Heart Rate Sensor:
         </strong>
         If you like to track your exercise, consider a phone with a built-in heart rate sensor (the Galaxy G5 is the only one with this feature on our list).
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Voice Assistance:
         </strong>
         Voice-based virtual assistance is pretty standard in todays smartphones, but some assistants are better than others. If you think youll use this feature, play around to see which you like best.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Power Saving:
         </strong>
         A few phones today boast an extreme power-saving mode that lets you eek extra
         <em>
          hours
         </em>
         of use out of your remaining few percentage points of battery life. If you often find yourself at 5% battery, nowhere near a charger, and only halfway through your day, this can be a sanity-saving feature.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          IR Blaster:
         </strong>
         If youve ever wanted your phone to double as a TV remote control, look for one with in-built infrared.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Dual-LED Flash:
         </strong>
         This ones for the mobile photographers out there: dual-LED flash, available in the iPhone, LG G3, and HTC One M8, produces deeper, richer photos with less wash-out.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Speakers:
         </strong>
         Chances are you usually use headphones with your smartphone, but if you ever wish you could share your favorite music (or just a phone call) with a third party, quality speakers can make a big difference.
        </p>
