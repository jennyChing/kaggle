        <h1>
         How To Use Your Companys Most Powerful Business Tool
        </h1>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="col-xs-12 info clearfix">
       <div class="left">
        <span class="author">
         By
          David Mizne
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="social right">
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       With new software applications coming out every day, technology continues to offer a solution for any business need.
       <a href="https://slack.com/" target="_blank">
        Slack
       </a>
       is shifting the way teams communicate,
       <a href="https://hackpad.com/" target="_blank">
        Hackpad
       </a>
       allows for collaborative creation, andthis blog wouldnt exist without
       <a href="https://wordpress.com/website/" target="_blank">
        WordPress
       </a>
       . But the most powerful business tool was actually invented long ago and was only recently perfected through technology  its the question.
      </p>
      <p>
       Thats right, questions. Just think about the most successful companies. Google began as a search engine, literally your one stop resource for any answer. Apple continues to astonish the world by asking, how can we do _______ better?
      </p>
      <p class="ace-line gutter-author-p-277320 emptyGutter" data-author-initials="m" data-author-link="/ep/profile/mfnBYahaSwR" data-author-name="miz@15five.com" id="magicdomid18">
       <span class="author-p-277320">
        <a href="http://amorebeautifulquestion.com/" target="_blank">
         15Five
        </a>
        is a software company built on questions.
       </span>
       They are not only the central pillar of our product, but also the central pillar of the truly remarkable culture we have architected. Good questions have the power to spark innovation, avoid fire-drills, and help employees show-up as their best selves. Which is why we createdour newest feature, the Question Bank.
      </p>
      <p class="ace-line gutter-author-p-277320 emptyGutter" data-author-initials="m" data-author-link="/ep/profile/mfnBYahaSwR" data-author-name="miz@15five.com">
       <span id="more-5577">
       </span>
      </p>
      <h3 class='\"ace-line' data-author-initials='\"m\"' data-author-link='\"/ep/profile/mfnBYahaSwR\"' data-author-name='\"miz@15five.com\"' emptygutter="" gutter-author-p-277320="">
       The Power of Inquiry
      </h3>
      <p class="ace-line gutter-author-p-277320 emptyGutter" data-author-initials="m" data-author-link="/ep/profile/mfnBYahaSwR" data-author-name="miz@15five.com">
       It may seem silly to think that people need guidance on something as simple as asking questions. But asking a thought provoking question that sparks creativity and problem-solving takes skill. We are on a mission to teach that skill, and are undoing the damage caused by a system that kills creative thought by focusing more on answers than questions
      </p>
      <p>
       <span class="body-text-wide">
        Good questions have the power to spark innovation, avoid fire-drills, and help employees show-up as their best selves.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p class="ace-line gutter-author-p-277320 emptyGutter" data-author-initials="m" data-author-link="/ep/profile/mfnBYahaSwR" data-author-name="miz@15five.com">
       According to
       <a href="https://twitter.com/GlimmerGuy" target="_blank">
        Warren Berger
       </a>
       author of
       <a href="http://amorebeautifulquestion.com/" target="_blank">
        <em>
         A More Beautiful Question
        </em>
       </a>
       , a young child asks 300 questions a day. But by the time we graduate high school, that curiosityhas slowed to a halt. School systems are focused onpreparing students for taking tests, not on nurturing the curiosity that is so critical for entrepreneurship, innovation, and creativity.
      </p>
      <div class="video-container embed-responsive">
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/5ALlGU2GYbk?feature=oembed" width="500">
       </iframe>
      </div>
      <p class="ace-line gutter-author-p-277320 emptyGutter" data-author-initials="m" data-author-link="/ep/profile/mfnBYahaSwR" data-author-name="miz@15five.com">
       Likewise, in the business world where we are so focused on producing results, we dont always encourage the disruptive thinking that leads to success. Questioning enables us to organize our thinking around what is true and what is false. When we go deeper into that inquiry we begin to see that what exists as true can be transformed, reinvented, improved-upon or even replaced.
      </p>
      <h3>
       A Wealth of Questions
      </h3>
      <p>
       <em>
        If youre not asking the questions in a thoughtful way, youre not going to get any results that are useful or interesting.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: right;">
       <em>
        ~Tony Wagner, Harvard University Innovation Lab
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Some questions are easy, they are little more than requests for facts:How are things going in your role? What projects did you complete this week?
      </p>
      <p>
       Other questions are more complex because they are designed to instigate more critical thinking. They challenge the status quo, the accepted notions that keep business stagnant. To be effective the wording must be precise, even subtly so. For example, you could ask the question, What could we be doing better as a company? That might spark some creativity. Or you could ask, What would you change about our company/product if you had a magic wand?
      </p>
      <p>
       The latter question expands the realm of possibility, allowing the responder to take a moment and get creative. Their response might seem impossible at first, but after a brainstorm or two with the development team, it could prove to be the
       <a href="http://blog.15five.com/the-secret-path-to-innovation/" target="_blank">
        innovative product feature
       </a>
       that launches the company to a new level of success.
      </p>
      <p>
       With our question bank, we take the guess-work out of asking these questions. Simply search by keyword or category and discover the right question to inform meetings, inspire creative thinking, or maintain your company culture.
      </p>
      <p>
       Each question in the bank is tagged with a different category. Here are some examples:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <span class="body-text-intro">
         High Performance
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <span class="body-text-intro">
         Better one-on-ones
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <span class="body-text-intro">
         Innovation
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <span class="body-text-intro">
         Culture Building
        </span>
       </li>
       <li>
        <span class="body-text-intro">
         Learning and Development
        </span>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://blog.15five.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Screen-Shot-2015-04-20-at-3.17.57-PM.png">
        <img alt=" " class="body-image-wide aligncenter wp-image-5584" height="417" src="http://blog.15five.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Screen-Shot-2015-04-20-at-3.17.57-PM.png" width="804"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       Asking questions can improve business outcomes and even
       <a href="http://blog.15five.com/how-i-saved-hundreds-of-hours-by-asking-four-questions-a-week/" target="_blank">
        save hundreds of hours
       </a>
       each week. The practice can boost team performance, innovation and focus. But only the right questions willinspire creativityand yieldthe results that managers desire. Consider what you are looking to achieve and then choose your words carefully. A well-worded, beautiful question can bring out the best in people. A poorly-worded one feels likea waste of time.
      </p>
