                  <span>
                   Friday, January 11, 2013
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry">

                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     <a href="http://www.mommyskitchen.net/2013/01/ranch-house-crock-pot-chicken.html">
                      Ranch House Crock Pot Chicken
                     </a>
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7NISNCl7_Uw/UYgPG7jiC2I/AAAAAAAAX2k/PiIw7c7pyYg/s1600/ranch+chicken+007mk.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7NISNCl7_Uw/UYgPG7jiC2I/AAAAAAAAX2k/PiIw7c7pyYg/s1600/ranch+chicken+007mk.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        Today's chicken recipe is simple and delicious. You literally just toss everything in the crock pot, cover and let it do it's magic.
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        I initially saw this recipe on
                        <a href="http://life-as-a-lofthouse.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">
                         Holly's
                        </a>
                        blog, but she used pork chops. Since my freezer was full of chicken breasts I adapted the recipe just a bit. One of my goals for the New Year is to use my crock pot more regularly.
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        I'm not sure why I don't, because it's such a huge time saver. Not to mention slow cooked food is so much more flavorful and tender.
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BujWORPwwkI/UO7SNz8zEPI/AAAAAAAAV4g/ApzGp2hycNI/s1600/ranch+chicken+011mk.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-BujWORPwwkI/UO7SNz8zEPI/AAAAAAAAV4g/ApzGp2hycNI/s1600/ranch+chicken+011mk.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        This
                        <span style="color: #cc0000;">
                         <b>
                          Ranch House Chicken
                         </b>
                        </span>
                        turned out perfect and tender! The combination of the chicken soup and the  ranch dressing were fabulous.
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <br/>
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        I also added a tad of sour cream which gave the sauce a nice tang.
                       </span>
                       I highly recommend this chicken dish, so file it away in your recipe box, because you will be making it more than just once
                      </span>
                      .
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-eFCBojZ-2iw/UO7SoVbyxmI/AAAAAAAAV4w/2oK49HX4EAw/s1600/PicMonkey+Collage.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-eFCBojZ-2iw/UO7SoVbyxmI/AAAAAAAAV4w/2oK49HX4EAw/s1600/PicMonkey+Collage.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       This recipe only requires a few steps. Spray a 6 quart or larger crock pot with non stick spray, place the chicken inside and add the soup mixture and dry ranch dressing. Cover and cook on low for 5-6 hours. Serve chicken and gravy over mashed potatoes, rice or pasta.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                      <a name="more">
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-IXPrKoi2lD8/UO7SDk61mXI/AAAAAAAAV4Y/TCimlFklPN0/s1600/ranch+chicken+010mk.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-IXPrKoi2lD8/UO7SDk61mXI/AAAAAAAAV4Y/TCimlFklPN0/s1600/ranch+chicken+010mk.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <center>
                     </center>
                     <center>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                       <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </div>
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                      </span>
                      <div style="text-align: left;">
                       <!-- Begin ZipList Button Code -->
                      </div>
                     </center>
                     <div class="zl-recipe-link">
                      <span style="color: #993399;">
                       <b>
                        <a class="small-butn-link" href="javascript:getZRecipe(document.getElementById('zlr-elem'),%20'mommyskitchen',%20'');" id="zlr-elem" title="Add this recipe to your ZipList, where you can store all of your favorite web recipes in one place and easily add ingredients to your shopping list.">
                        </a>
                       </b>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <!-- End ZipList Button Code -->
                     <br/>
                     <span style="color: #274e13;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       <b>
                        Ranch House Crock Pot Chicken
                       </b>
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="color: #274e13;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       {mommyskitchen.net}
                      </span>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       6 - boneless, skinless Chicken Breasts
                       <br/>
                       2 - (10 ounce) cans  Cream of Chicken soup,
                      </span>
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       undiluted
                       <br/>
                       1/4 - cup sour cream, plain or plain Greek yogurt
                       <br/>
                       1 - 1 oz packet Hidden Valley Ranch Dressing Mix (unprepared)
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Spray a 6 qt or larger crock pot with non stick spray. Place chicken breasts in the bottom of crock pot.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       In a small bowl add the sour cream or yogurt, and cream of chicken soup. Mix together to combine.
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       Pour the soup mixture over the chicken, and sprinkle with the dry ranch dressing packet.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style='font-family: Georgia,"Times New Roman",serif;'>
                       Cover and cook on low for 5 - 6  hours, or on high for 4 hours. Serve chicken and gravy over mashed potatoes or rice.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>

                        Tried this tonight. I used garlic herb chicken from Whole Foods, and I didn't defrost it in time so I just left it in kind of a frozen 'block' and it worked fine.
