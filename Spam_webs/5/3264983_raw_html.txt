     <p>
      <b>
       Corentin Louis Kervran
      </b>
      (1901 - 2 February 1983) was a French scientist. Kervran was born in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quimper,_Finist%C3%A8re" title="Quimper, Finistre">
       Quimper, Finistre
      </a>
      (Brittany), and received a degree as an engineer in 1925. In WWII he was part of the
      <a href="/wiki/French_Resistance" title="French Resistance">
       French Resistance
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Kervran proposed that
      <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_transmutation" title="Nuclear transmutation">
       nuclear transmutation
      </a>
      occurs in living organisms, which he called "biological transmutation".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Such transmutations are not possible according to the known laws of
      <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">
       physics
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">
       chemistry
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">
       biology
      </a>
      . Proponents of biological transmutations fall outside the mainstream and are not part of the scientific discourse.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tibor_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-tibor-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biological_transmutation">
       Biological transmutation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Corentin_Louis_Kervran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biological transmutation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In the 1960s,
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_Kervran" title="Louis Kervran">
       Louis Kervran
      </a>
      claimed to have conducted experiments and studies demonstrating violations of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Law_of_conservation_of_mass" title="Law of conservation of mass">
       law of conservation of mass
      </a>
      by biological systems, specifically during the formation of egg shells. As a result, he claimed that organisms can transmute potassium into calcium by
      <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fusion" title="Nuclear fusion">
       nuclear fusion
      </a>
      :
      <sup>
       19
      </sup>
      K +
      <sup>
       1
      </sup>
      H =
      <sup>
       20
      </sup>
      Ca. Since biological systems do not contain mechanisms to produce the speed, temperature and pressure necessary for such reactions, even for extremely short periods of time, this contradicts basic physical laws.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tibor_4-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-tibor-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Kervran said that his work was supported by prior studies and by reports of industrial accidents involving carbon monoxide.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Kervran said that
      <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">
       enzymes
      </a>
      can facilitate biological transmutations using the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weak_nuclear_force" title="Weak nuclear force">
       weak nuclear force
      </a>
      , by what he called "neutral currents."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His response to criticism is to claim that
      <a href="/wiki/Physical_law" title="Physical law">
       physical laws
      </a>
      do not apply to biological reactions, which contradicts the mainstream understanding that physical laws apply for all scales and conditions.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tibor_4-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-tibor-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Kervran's theories seem to be based on the philosophy of
      <a href="/wiki/Yin_and_yang" title="Yin and yang">
       yin and yang
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tibor_4-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-tibor-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 1978, philosopher
      <a href="/wiki/George_Ohsawa" title="George Ohsawa">
       George Ohsawa
      </a>
      , the founder of the
      <a href="/wiki/Macrobiotic_diet" title="Macrobiotic diet">
       macrobiotic diet
      </a>
      , said that he had himself managed to transmute carbon into iron, with oxygen being the yin and carbon being the yang.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tibor_4-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-tibor-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The alleged transmutations resemble
      <a href="/wiki/Cold_fusion" title="Cold fusion">
       cold fusion
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tibor_4-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-tibor-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      There is currently no accepted theoretical model which would allow cold fusion to occur.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1993, Kervran was awarded a parodic
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ig_Nobel" title="Ig Nobel">
       Ig Nobel
      </a>
      prize due to his "improbable research" in biological transmutation. The award description called him an "ardent admirer of alchemy."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
