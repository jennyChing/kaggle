     <h1 class="entry-title">
      What Is the Name of the Linux-based OS: A Survey
     </h1>
     <div class="entry-meta">
      <span class="byline">
       By
       <span class="author vcard">
        <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.systutorials.com/author/zma/">
         Eric Zhiqiang Ma
        </a>
       </span>
      </span>
      <span class="">
       on
      </span>
      <span class="posted-on">
       <a href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/" rel="bookmark">
        <time class="entry-date published-updated" datetime="2015-03-08T17:05:14+00:00" title="Last updated on April 1st, 2015.">
         March 8th, 2015
        </time>
       </a>
      </span>
      <span class="">
      </span>
     </div>
    </header>
    <div class="content-area" id="primary">
     <main class="site-main" id="main" role="main">
      <article class="post-136152 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-linux category-news tag-emacs tag-fedora tag-gnome tag-linux tag-os" id="post-136152">
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         You may already well know Linux and may also use the operating system based on the Linux kernel directly or indirectly (you are indirectly using it now as this site is
         <a class="internal internal-post" href="http://www.systutorials.com/3095/hosting-from-dreamhost/">
          hosted on Linux
         </a>
         ). But how should we name the OS based on Linux? You may know there is
         <a class="external external-https" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GNU/Linux_naming_controversy" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          GNU/Linux naming controversy
         </a>
         . Different people have different opinions about this. Today, lets see how do these widely used distributions call themselves by looking at their about web pages.
        </p>
        <div class="align-center image-align-center">
         <a href="http://www.systutorials.com/wp/files/2015/03/linux.jpg" rel="attachment" title="linux.jpg">
          <img alt="linux.jpg" class="lazy lazy-hidden wp-post-image" data-lazy-src="http://www.systutorials.com/wp/wp-content/plugins/blogtext/api/thumbnail/do.php?id=857451d4793a922a416e6bd0b2bd1ff48d5a15cc_730x0_resize_if_larger" data-lazy-type="image" height="365" src="http://file.systutorials.com/file/2015-03-31-9206-holder.png" title="linux.jpg" width="730"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="linux.jpg" class="wp-post-image" height="365" src="http://www.systutorials.com/wp/wp-content/plugins/blogtext/api/thumbnail/do.php?id=857451d4793a922a416e6bd0b2bd1ff48d5a15cc_730x0_resize_if_larger" title="linux.jpg" width="730"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <h2 id="redhat-enterprise-linux-.28rhel.29">
         RedHat Enterprise Linux (RHEL)
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#redhat-enterprise-linux-.28rhel.29" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a class="external external-http http" href="http://www.redhat.com/en/technologies/linux-platforms/enterprise-linux" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          http://www.redhat.com/en/technologies/linux-platforms/enterprise-linux
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          With Red Hat Enterprise
          <strong>
           Linux
          </strong>
          , a platform with unparalleled stability and flexibility, 
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         RHEL calls itself a Linux and also a platform.
        </p>
        <h2 id="fedora">
         Fedora
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#fedora" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a class="external external-https https" href="https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/Overview" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          https://fedoraproject.org/wiki/Overview
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          The Fedora Project builds open source software communities and produces a
          <strong>
           Linux distribution
          </strong>
          called Fedora.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Fedora calls itself a Linux distribution.
        </p>
        <h2 id="suse-linux-enterprise">
         SUSE Linux Enterprise
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#suse-linux-enterprise" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a class="external external-https https" href="https://www.suse.com/company/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          https://www.suse.com/company/
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Established in 1992, SUSE is the original provider of the enterprise
          <strong>
           Linux distribution
          </strong>
          and the most interoperable platform for mission-critical computing.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         SUSE also considers itself a Linux distribution and a platform.
        </p>
        <h2 id="opensuse">
         openSUSE
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#opensuse" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a class="external external-https https" href="https://en.opensuse.org/Main_Page" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          https://en.opensuse.org/Main_Page
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          openSUSE creates one of the worlds best
          <strong>
           Linux distributions
          </strong>
          , working together in an open, transparent and friendly manner as part of the worldwide Free and Open Source Software community.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         openSUSE considers itself one of the  Linux distributions.
        </p>
        <h2 id="debian">
         Debian
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#debian" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a class="external external-https https" href="https://www.debian.org/intro/about" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          https://www.debian.org/intro/about
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          This operating system that we have created is called Debian.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Debian calls itself Debian operating system.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          A large part of the basic tools that fill out the operating system come from the GNU project; hence the names:
          <strong>
           GNU/Linux
          </strong>
          , GNU/kFreeBSD, and GNU/Hurd. These tools are also free.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         It also claims GNU/Linux is a proper name for it.
        </p>
        <h2 id="ubuntu">
         Ubuntu
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#ubuntu" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a class="external external-http http" href="http://www.ubuntu.com/about/about-ubuntu" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          http://www.ubuntu.com/about/about-ubuntu
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Thats why Mark Shuttleworth gathered a small team of developers from one of the most established
          <strong>
           Linux projects
          </strong>
           Debian  and set out to create an easy-to-use
          <strong>
           Linux desktop
          </strong>
          : Ubuntu.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Ubuntu calls Debian, its upstream, a Linux project and itself a Linux desktop.
        </p>
        <h2 id="linux-mint">
         Linux Mint
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#linux-mint" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a class="external external-http http" href="http://www.linuxmint.com/about.php" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          http://www.linuxmint.com/about.php
         </a>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Linux Mint is the most popular desktop
          <strong>
           Linux distribution
          </strong>
          and the 3rd most widely used home operating system behind Microsoft Windows and Apple Mac OS.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Linux Mint also calls itself a Linux distribution.
        </p>
        <h2 id="summary">
         Summary
         <a class="heading-link" href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/#summary" title="Link to this section">
          
         </a>
        </h2>
        <p>
         As you see, Linux distribution seems the common name for the OS based on Linux. It is short and Linux is indeed a nice name.
        </p>
        <div class="align-center image-align-center">
         <a href="http://www.systutorials.com/136152/what-is-the-name-of-the-linux-based-os-a-survey/image-2/" rel="attachment" title="Linux">
          <img alt="Linux" class="lazy lazy-hidden wp-post-image" data-lazy-src="http://www.systutorials.com/wp/wp-content/plugins/blogtext/api/thumbnail/do.php?id=ed284b2334f253c5ebe510d5a264209a3118eded_730x0_resize_if_larger" data-lazy-type="image" height="546" src="http://file.systutorials.com/file/2015-03-31-9206-holder.png" title="Linux" width="730"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Linux" class="wp-post-image" height="546" src="http://www.systutorials.com/wp/wp-content/plugins/blogtext/api/thumbnail/do.php?id=ed284b2334f253c5ebe510d5a264209a3118eded_730x0_resize_if_larger" title="Linux" width="730"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         GNU tools are just a set of the tools in the OS. Including all important tool contributors, such as X11, GNOME, Apache and GNU, in the OS name may not be realistic. That said, GNU tools, such as gcc, binutils, make and Emacs, play an important and critical role in almost every Linux distributions.
        </p>
