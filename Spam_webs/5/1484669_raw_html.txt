        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Which High-Paying Careers in Nursing Will You Choose?
        </h1>
        <small>
         Posted September 15, 2014 | By csponline
        </small>

        <p>
         When you look at the numbers, its clear that a bachelors degree opens up nursing career options.
        </p>
        <p>
         By 2020, the
         <a href="http://www.iom.edu/Reports/2010/The-Future-of-Nursing-Leading-Change-Advancing-Health/Report-Brief-Education.aspx">
          Institute of Medicine
         </a>
         has called for 80 percent of practicing nurses to hold at least a bachelors degree. And by that time, the
         <a href="http://www.bls.gov/news.release/ecopro.t06.htm">
          Bureau of Labor Statistics
         </a>
         estimates that the number of employed nurses will grow by 26 percentleading all occupations.
        </p>
        <p>
         A bachelors degree in nursing also provides increased opportunity for advanced positions, since the degree is frequently required for higher-level jobsjobs that often pay more.
        </p>
        <p>
         Take a look at just a few careers in nursing that can be pursued with this degree.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="high paying nursing careers" src="http://online.csp.edu/images/uploads/blog/nursing3-highpaying-CSP.jpg" style="width: 700px; height: 280px;"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Nurse Educator
        </h3>
        <p>
         Nurse educators are needed to shape the next generation of nurses.
        </p>
        <p>
         As a nurse educator, you will teach student nurses in a classroom or clinical setting, developing a curriculum and preparing the future nurses to handle any situation. Youll use your experience in direct patient care to help students prepare for this very role.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to the
         <a href="http://www.aacn.nche.edu/media-relations/fact-sheets/nursing-faculty-shortage">
          American Association of Colleges of Nursing
         </a>
         (AACN), there is a nursing faculty shortage that is being described as a crisis. With an advanced education and clinical experience, youll be able to make a difference in the field as a leader in the health care industry.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Recommended Education:
          </strong>
          Bachelors degree required, with a masters or doctoral degree offering further growth potential
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Salary:
          </strong>
          $66,092, based on 1,277 reported salaries from
          <a href="http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Job=Nurse_Educator/Salary">
           PayScale
          </a>
          (May 2014)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Public Health Nursing
        </h3>
        <p>
         In public health nursing, opportunities abound. You can mold nursing services for diverse communities by designing, executing and evaluating public health programs. You can specialize in a field like community health nursing, where you will work, teach and consult to lead nurse generalists and provide care to underserved communities.
        </p>
        <p>
         Public health nurses focus on issues such as health and safety, disaster relief efforts and health education. Settings can include federal and state agencies, health departments and schools.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to the
         <a href="http://www.rwjf.org/en/about-rwjf/newsroom/newsroom-content/2013/07/public-health-nurses-satisfied-with-their-jobs--in-high-demand--.html">
          Robert Woods Johnson Foundation
         </a>
         , public health nurses are in high demand across the country.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Recommended Education:
          </strong>
          Bachelors degree
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Salary:
          </strong>
          $61,633, based on 65 reported salaries of Public Health Directors from
          <a href="http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Job=Public_Health_Director/Salary">
           PayScale
          </a>
          (May 2014)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Nursing Management
        </h3>
        <p>
         In nearly every nursing specialty, there is the opportunity to step into a management position. A bachelors degree will help you maximize your experience and pursue these roles.
        </p>
        <p>
         Nursing management positions often involve overseeing a staff of nurses. Youll recruit and assist nurses to achieve quality care for the hospital, care center or health care environment where you work. At times, you might work with your staff and doctors on patient care. Outside of the more direct responsibilities, you might manage finances and take care of medical records.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Recommended Education:
          </strong>
          Bachelors degree
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Salary:
          </strong>
          $79,568, based on 1,175 reported salaries from
          <a href="http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Job=Nursing_Manager/Salary">
           PayScale
          </a>
          (May 2014)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Other Opportunities
        </h3>
        <p>
         The aforementioned nursing career options only touch the surface of whats available with a BS in Nursing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Other specific careers are also available. For instance, you could become an informatics nurse, nurse advocate, nurse executive, oncology nurse or occupational health nurse with the added education.
        </p>
        <p>
         Often, a BS in Nursing enhances the same opportunities that you have as an RN. Across the United States, a bachelors degree is becoming the minimum to work as a nurse in a hospital. You might need a bachelors degree to land a great job.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Higher Pay
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you dont immediately enter into a field that requires a bachelors degree, the advanced degree frequently will lead to higher pay.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="advanced nursing degrees lead to higher pay" src="http://online.csp.edu/images/uploads/blog/nursing4-advanceddegrees-CSP.jpg" style="width: 700px; height: 280px;"/>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Diploma of Nursing:
          </strong>
          Employees with one to four years of experience and a Diploma of Nursing earned $52,458. This is based on 25 reported salaries from
          <a href="http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Degree=Diploma_of_Nursing_%28DN%29/Salary#by_Years_Experience">
           PayScale
          </a>
          (May 2014).
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Associate Degree in Nursing:
          </strong>
          Employees with one to four years of experience and an Associate Degree in Nursing earned $57,159. This is based on 430 reported salaries from
          <a href="http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Degree=Associate_Degree_Nursing_%28ADN%29/Salary#by_Years_Experience">
           PayScale
          </a>
          (May 2014).
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Bachelor of Science in Nursing:
          </strong>
          Employees with one to four years of experience and a Bachelor of Science in Nursing earned $61,157. This is based on 904 reported salaries from
          <a href="http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Degree=Bachelor_of_Science_in_Nursing_%28BSN%29/Salary#by_Years_Experience">
           PayScale
          </a>
          (May 2014).
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         According to
         <a href="http://www.payscale.com/research/US/Degree=Bachelor_of_Science_in_Nursing_%28BSN%29/Salary#by_Job">
          PayScale
         </a>
         , RNs with a bachelors degree earned nearly $5,000 more than RNs with an associate degree. The former group earns a median of $57,632.
        </p>
        <p>
         With a BS in Nursing, you can earn more money and pursue several career options in nursing. Take a look at our fully
         <a href="http://online.csp.edu/adult-undergraduate-programs/nursing/rn-to-bs-in-nursing" title="online BS in Nursing degree">
          online RN to BS in Nursing
         </a>
         program that lets you study when you have the time. It allows you to keep your shift, get your degree and advance
        </p>
       </div>

        jQuery(document).ready(function($){

					$.cookie.json = true;

					//CREATE DEFAULT VARIABLES
					defaultProgress = {
					    "show": true,
					    "one": false,
					    "two": false,
					    "three": false,
					    "four": false,
					    "five": false
					}

					var currentProgress;


					//CHECK TO SEE IF COOKIE EXISTS AND SET VARIABLES ACCORDINGLY
					if( $.cookie('progress') == undefined ){
						$.cookie('progress', defaultProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
						currentProgress = defaultProgress;
					}else{
						currentProgress = $.cookie('progress');
					}

					//SHOWS AND HIDES PROGRESS BAR
					$('.progress-icon').click(function(){
						$('body').toggleClass("show-progress");
						currentProgress.show = true;
						$.cookie('progress', currentProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
					});
					$('.progress-close').click(function(){
						$('body').toggleClass("show-progress");
						currentProgress.show = false;
						$.cookie('progress', currentProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
					});

					//SHOW OR HIDE BAR ON PAGE LOAD
					if( currentProgress.show ){
						$('body').addClass("show-progress");
					}

					//SET PROGRESS BASED ON URL
					if( window.location.href.search("/academics/") != -1 ){
						currentProgress.one = true;
						$.cookie('progress', currentProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
					}
					if( $('body').hasClass('single-program')){
						currentProgress.two = true;
						$.cookie('progress', currentProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
					}
					if( window.location.href.search("/about/") != -1 ){
						currentProgress.three = true;
						$.cookie('progress', currentProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
					}
					if( window.location.href.search("/admissions/tuition-and-financial-aid/") != -1 ){
						currentProgress.four = true;
						$.cookie('progress', currentProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
					}
					if( window.location.href.search("/thank-you/") != -1 ){
						currentProgress.five = true;
						$.cookie('progress', currentProgress, { expires: 30, path: '/' });
					}

					//OPEN REQUEST INFO DRAWER
					$('.step-five').click(function(){
						$('.request-drawer').addClass("open-drawer");
					});

					//CHANGE PROGRESS BAR BASED ON VARIABLE / COOKIE DATA
					function refreshProgress(){
						if( currentProgress.one ){
							$('.step-one').addClass("complete");
							$('.step-one').removeClass("next");
							if( currentProgress.two ){
								$('.step-two').addClass("complete");
								$('.step-two').removeClass("next");
								if( currentProgress.three ){
									$('.step-three').addClass("complete");
									$('.step-three').removeClass("next");
									if( currentProgress.four ){
										$('.step-four').addClass("complete");
										$('.step-four').removeClass("next");
										if( currentProgress.five ){
											$('.step-five').addClass("complete");
											$('.step-five').removeClass("next");
										}else{
											$('.step-five').addClass("next");
											$('.step-five .progress-info span').html("Final Step:");
										}
									}else{
										$('.step-four').addClass("next");
										$('.step-four .progress-info span').html("Next Step:");
									}
								}else{
									$('.step-three').addClass("next");
									$('.step-three .progress-info span').html("Next Step:");
								}
							}else{
								$('.step-two').addClass("next");
								$('.step-two .progress-info span').html("Next Step:");
							}
						}else{
							$('.step-one').addClass("next");
							$('.step-one .progress-info span').html("First Step:");
						}
					}

					refreshProgress();

				});
