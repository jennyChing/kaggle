          <a href="http://www.handsoccupied.com/2015/04/karbonz/">
           Review &amp; Giveaway: Knitters Pride Karbonz
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="byline">
          By
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.handsoccupied.com/author/hjg/" rel="author" title="Heidi">
            Heidi
           </a>
          </span>
          <time class="published" datetime="2015-04-22T06:00:21-05:00" title="Wednesday, April 22nd, 2015, 6:00 am">
           April 22, 2015
          </time>
          |
          <a class="comments-link" href="http://www.handsoccupied.com/2015/04/karbonz/#comments" title="Comment on Review &amp; Giveaway: Knitters Pride Karbonz">
           73 Responses
          </a>
         </div>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Click through for a review of Knitter's Pride Karbonz Interchangeable Circular Knitting Needles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12113" height="650" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/karbonz-review-1-sq.jpg" width="650"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.handsoccupied.com/2015/04/karbonz/&amp;media=http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/karbonz-review-1-sq.jpg&amp;description=Review and Giveaway: Knitters Pride Karbonz')">
           </span>
          </span>
          In the latest
          <a href="http://www.handsoccupied.com/2015/02/survey-results-2/" title="Survey Results!">
           reader survey
          </a>
          , you guys said youre interested in seeing what sort of tools I use to knit. By far my most-used knitting needles are interchangeable circulars. There are a few brands I like to use, depending on my mood and the project or fiber Im working with. One of those go-to brands is
          <a href="http://www.knitterspride.com/index.asp">
           Knitters Pride
          </a>
          .Lightweight and warm to the touch, the Knitters Pride
          <a href="http://www.knitterspride.com/Materialwise-Details.asp?id=16&amp;mcid=1">
           Karbonz
          </a>
          knitting needles are really easy to work with and virtually unbreakable. I always look forward to casting on projectswith these needles.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Click through for a review of Knitter's Pride Karbonz Interchangeable Circular Knitting Needles &amp; Enter to win a set of your own!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12130" height="433" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/karbonz-review-5.gif" width="650"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.handsoccupied.com/2015/04/karbonz/&amp;media=http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/karbonz-review-5.gif&amp;description=Review and Giveaway: Knitters Pride Karbonz')">
           </span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          When appropriately tightened with the keys, Karbonz interchangeable needles dont haveacatching issue between the cord and the needle tip  a common issue Ive run into time and again with other interchangeable needle sets from a variety of brands. For very observant knitters, you might noticethe slightest catch between the needle tips and the Karbonzs bodies, but I was so enamored by the smooth glide of my stitches along the Karbonzs bodies that I really didnt notice anything but smooth sailing on the needles as I worked with them.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
           <img alt="Click through for a review of Knitter's Pride Karbonz Interchangeable Circular Knitting Needles" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12115" height="975" src="http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/karbonz-review-3.jpg" width="650"/>
           <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.handsoccupied.com/2015/04/karbonz/&amp;media=http://www.handsoccupied.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/karbonz-review-3.jpg&amp;description=Review and Giveaway: Knitters Pride Karbonz')">
           </span>
          </span>
          My only realcritique of the interchangeable Karbonz is with the cables. While they broke in quickly, the first few projects I knit with my circular Karbonz were marred with a little bit of frustration at the cables wanting to stay in the shape they came in, a.k.a. at their memory. Ive run into this hurdle with other needles, though, and the memory issue is essentially moot after you break them in. The smooth handling of the tips &amp; needle bodies alone is drool-worthy, so I would absolutely not let the fact that the cables needed a bit of breaking in keep you from getting your hands on a pair of Karbonz circulars or a set of Knitters Prides interchangeable circulars.
         </p>

          Speaking of which
