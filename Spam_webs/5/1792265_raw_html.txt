          <p>
           New legislation proposed in Montana would make it illegal for police to use licence plate scanners for tracking and database creation without cause or warrant. Unfortunately, federal agents would not be under state jurisdiction and would thus be able to use licence scanners on state property if they chose to.
          </p>
          <p>
           However, state and local police departments would be prevented from using the devices for law enforcement purposes.
          </p>
          <p>
           The bill, HB 344 states that
           <em>
            an agency or employee of the state or any subdivision of the state may not use, either directly or indirectly, a license plate scanner on any public highway.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           As expected, police are not happy.
          </p>
          <p>
           This bill ties the hands of law enforcement. In advance, Larry Epstein of the Montana Association of Police Chiefs complained to reporters.
          </p>
          <p>
           The bill would allow licence plate scanners to be used for some rare situations. One example is 
           <em>
            to collect data for city planning as long as the anonymity of the vehicle, the vehicle owner, the driver of the vehicle, and any passengers in the vehicle
           </em>
            was guaranteed. However, the bill also states that information collected for city planning purposes could not later be used against suspects in court.
          </p>
          <p>
           The sponsor of the bill, Rep. Daniel Zolnikov describes himself as 
           <em>
            a strong believer in gun rights, civil rights concerning our freedoms and liberties, limited government, a simplified tax code, economic freedom and a competitive job environment.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           According to
           <a href="http://www.krtv.com/story/28073261/law-enforcement-wary-of-montana-license-plate-scanner-ban" target="_blank">
            KRTV:
           </a>
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <p>
            Zolnikov says part of the purpose behind this bill and other pieces of privacy-related legislation is to prevent future abuses of technology, and to create limits for state involvement so that federal authorities cant later use state data.
           </p>
