        <h1 class="post-title">
         OOLONG TEA  FRIENDLY FOR A HEALTHY BODY AND SOUL
        </h1>
        <p class="meta">
         <span class="posted">
          Published by HughDay
         </span>
         -Categories:
         <a href="/tag/health%20%26%20fitness/" title="Health &amp; Fitness">
          #Health &amp; Fitness
         </a>
        </p>
        <div class="body">
         <div class="ob-sections">
          <div class="ob-section ob-section-html">
           <p>
            <b>
             Author:
             <a href="https://plus.google.com/b/112068780360196393194/112068780360196393194/posts" rel="author">
              Teasy Teas
             </a>
            </b>
           </p>
           <p>
            Tea lovers are always looking for the best teas around them. No matter whether they are at work, travelling or shopping, there urge for good quality teas never ends. There is one tea which is preferred by many people not because of its flavor but also due to its health benefits. This tea is
            <strong>
             oolong tea
            </strong>
            . This tea is made from the leaves of the same plant with which the green tea and black tea are produced. The variance is in the time of fermentation of the leaves. The leaves of green tea are not fermented, while
            <a href="http://www.theindianrepublic.com/uncategorized/tea-colours-black-green-white-100048962.html">
             <strong>
              black tea
             </strong>
            </a>
            leaves are fermented completely. The variety of oolong is obtained from a partial fermentation of the leaves.
            <br/>
            Fermentation, or the lack of it, gives the various types of tea and their color and aroma. It also alters the chemical composition of tea. The most significant changes affecting a group of chemicals called Catechins. These are powerful antioxidants that can act directly on human cells.
           </p>
           <p>
            <br/>
            <img alt="oolong tea" border="0" height="250" src="http://www.taiwanteacrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/The-four-states-of-Mingjian-Charcoal-Roasted-Oolong-Tea-Lot-112.jpg" title="oolong tea" width="400"/>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             Why should you drink oolong tea?
            </strong>
            <br/>
            Tea is a caffeinated beverage. One of the main benefits related to this tea is the increase of alertness, energy level and intellectual activities. These effects are directly related to the level of caffeine. They increase with the increase of the level of caffeine.
            <br/>
            Some people drink this tea to prevent or treat obesity and diabetes. One study suggests that drinking 6 cups of
            <a href="http://www.teasyteas.com/oolong-tea/">
             <strong>
              oolong tea
             </strong>
            </a>
            a day for a month can help people suffering from type 2 diabetes, lowering blood sugar. Other studies, conducted both on humans and on animals, suggest that the intake of this tea can help you lose weight.
            <br/>
            Most of the people drink this tea as it contains lots of health benefits and helps reducing various ailments like:
            <br/>
            1. Heart disease
            <br/>
            2. Osteoporosis
            <br/>
            3. Atherosclerosis
            <br/>
            4. Hypertension
            <br/>
            5. Hyperlipidemia (increased plasma concentration of triglycerides and cholesterol)
            <br/>
            6. Dermatitis
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <a href="http://www.teasyteas.com/oolong-tea">
             <img alt="oolong tea" border="0" height="150" src="http://www.taiwanteacrafts.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/A-scoop-of-Premium-Oriental-Beauty-Oolong-Tea-Lot-122.jpg" title="oolong tea" width="300"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            However, studies in these areas have been contradictory and inconclusive.
            <br/>
            Some studies have suggested that
            <strong>
             oolong tea
            </strong>
            may offer some protection against mental decline. Although a recent study in China has supported the idea that this tea can offer such protection, the results have not been conclusive thus making it necessary to conduct further studies.
            <br/>
            The researchers are interested in the possibility of tea, especially the green tea to help treat or prevent certain types of
            <a href="http://www.encyclopedia.com/topic/cancer.aspx">
             <strong>
              cancer
             </strong>
            </a>
            . Studies in animals have revealed encouraging reports. However, the results obtained from studies on the human being for the most part are still inconclusive. Some evidence, however, suggests that women who drink 2 cups of tea (green, oolong or black) per day can significantly reduce the risk of ovarian cancer.
            <br/>
            The
            <strong>
             oolong tea
            </strong>
            has strong antioxidant properties and anti-overweight. Derived from the Camellia Sinensis tea like other greens and blacks. Quercetin and catechin contribute to the antioxidant power of tea while caffeine and catechins, which are present in moderate amounts, help you speed up your metabolism and fat oxidation. The same properties of green tea, although to a lesser extent.
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             official source :
             <a href="http://www.teasyteas.com/oolong-tea/">
              www.teasyteas.com/oolong-tea/
             </a>
            </strong>
           </p>

  function commentListener(e) {
    var data = JSON.parse(e.data)
    onMessage(data.type, data)
  }

  try {
    window.addEventListener('message', commentListener)
  } catch (e) {}

  function onMessage(type, data) {
    var owner = data.owner
    var root = owner === 0

    switch (type) {
      case 'resize':
        var iframe = document.querySelector("#ob-comments-iframe-" + owner)
        if (iframe) iframe.style.height = data.height + 'px'

          removeChild(comment.querySelector('.ob-info'))
          removeChild(comment.querySelector('.ob-message'))
        }
        obcomment.className += ' ob-comment-state-'+data.comment.state
        container.insertBefore(comment, container.firstChild)
        offsets[owner]++
        document.location.hash = 'ob-comment-' + comment.id
        if (root) {

  function renderComment(comment, replies, allowReply) {
