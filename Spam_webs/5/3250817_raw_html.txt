		var $courseScroll = $('#course-scroller');
		var $courseScrollWrap = $courseScroll.find('.wrapper');
		var $courseScrollWrapItems = $courseScrollWrap.children();
		var courseScrollWrapItemWidth = $courseScrollWrapItems.outerWidth();
		var courseScrollWrapItemCount = $courseScrollWrapItems.length;
		var $courseScrollbar = $courseScroll.find('.scrollbar');
		var courseScrollbarWidth = $courseScrollbar.width() - 22;
		var $courseScrollSeeker = $courseScrollbar.find('.seeker');
		var offsetX = 11;

		// work out scrollbar width
		$courseScrollSeeker.width( Math.round((courseScrollbarWidth * courseScrollbarWidth) / (courseScrollWrapItemWidth * courseScrollWrapItemCount)) );

		var courseScrollSeekerWidth = $courseScrollSeeker.width();
		var courseScrollbarLimit = (courseScrollbarWidth + 11) - courseScrollSeekerWidth;

		$courseScrollSeeker
			.bind('mousedown', function(e){
				e.preventDefault();
				$(document).bind('mousemove', function(e){
					
					if( offsetX &gt;= 11 &amp;&amp; offsetX &lt;= courseScrollbarLimit ){
						offsetX = (e.pageX - $courseScrollbar.offset().left) - (courseScrollSeekerWidth / 2);
					}

					// limit scrolling area
					if( offsetX &lt;= 11 ) offsetX = 11;
					if( offsetX &gt;= courseScrollbarLimit ) offsetX = courseScrollbarLimit;

					$courseScrollSeeker.css({ left: offsetX + 'px' });
					$courseScrollWrap.css({ left: '-' + (offsetX * 2 - 22) + 'px' });
				});
			});

		// disable moving seeker
		$(document).bind('mouseup', function(){
			$(document).unbind('mousemove');
		});
	});
});

         <h1 class="entry-title">
          25 Cyber Crime Raids Across the UK
         </h1>
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          The National Cyber Crime Unit (NCCU) part of the UKs National Crime Agency (NCA) instigated 25 raids last week in the UK in an attempt to crack down on increasing cyber crimes such as money laundering, theft of money and data and also to target those suspected of creating the viruses that help hackers carry out their crimes.
          <br/>
          The NCCU collaborated with the Metropolitan Police and other regional crime squads in 56 arrests in London, Leeds, Essex and elsewhere. An essential part of the raids involved ensuring that all computers at the raided sites were confiscated before data on them could be destroyed by the suspects.
         </p>
         <p>
          Some of those arrested are thought to be hackers involved in organised groups that have carried out high profile attacks. Others took part in Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, such as the ones recently carried out on Sony and Xbox Live, which aim to overload websites to the point where they are unable to cope and are shut down.
         </p>
         <p>
          The recent breach of Sony systems is thought to have involved the theft of confidential data on tens of thousands of Sony employees, the theft of unreleased film material and also the destruction of data.
         </p>
         <p>
          The Xbox Live breach was carried out by the Lizard Squad, a member of which was thought to have been arrested in the raids last week.
         </p>
         <p>
          One of those arrested is also thought to have shut down the Police Scotland systems with a DDoS attack.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <a href="to%20http:/www.net-security-training.co.uk/forensics-investigator-training/">
           Computer Hacking Forensics
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p>
          Cyber crime specialists from the police forces involved will examine the confiscated computers as part of the criminal investigation to prosecute those involved in the crimes and to glean information that could be used to disrupt hacker groups targeting the UK.
         </p>
         <p>
          Computer Forensics has in recent years become as much a part of crime investigations and more traditional forensics analysis.
         </p>
         <p>
          Some of the investigations and subsequent arrests were the result of forensic information provided by the FBI.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Educating Businesses and the General Public
         </h2>
         <p>
          The UKs National Crime Agency also visited 70 companies last week to educate them about the vulnerabilities present on their servers and the risks that they could be used by cyber criminals in future DDoS attacks, even if not actually subject to an attack themselves.
         </p>
         <p>
          All individuals need to be aware of the steps to take to prevent their computers and mobile devices from being compromised. They should be checked with the latest software to ensure they are free of known viruses and malware. Computers and other devices can be infected without the user necessarily knowing that there is anything wrong, especially if the aim is to use the device for a DDoS attack.
         </p>
         <p>
          Businesses and other organisations should ensure that their IT security professionals are up to date with the latest methods to prevent and detect cyber crime activity on their systems.
          <a href="http://www.net-security-training.co.uk/ceh-training">
           Ethical hacking training
          </a>
          programmes aim to teach IT professionals about the methods that criminal hackers employ to breach systems in order to help then strengthen their defences.
         </p>
        </div>
