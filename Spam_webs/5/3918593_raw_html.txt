          <h2>
           <a href="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/baked-arancini" rel="bookmark" title="Baked Arancini">
            Baked Arancini
           </a>
          </h2>
          <div class="postinfo">
           Posted on
           <span class="postdate">
            July 14th, 2012
           </span>
           by admin
          </div>
          <div class="entry">
           <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
           <div addthis:title="Baked Arancini " addthis:url="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/baked-arancini" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style ">
            <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:layout="button_count">
            </a>
            <a class="addthis_button_tweet">
            </a>
            <a class="addthis_button_pinterest_pinit">
            </a>
            <a class="addthis_counter addthis_pill_style">
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="pf-content">
            <p>
             I was first introduced to these by my lovely Italian neighbour and omg were they good, so I wanted to have a go at making some lower in fat baked ones and I was very pleased with the result. These were yummy served with a homemade tomato sauce and some salad.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="DSC_1207" border="0" height="652" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/DSC_1207.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1207" width="427"/>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <font face="Throw My Hands Up in the Air" size="4">
               <u>
                Baked Arancini
               </u>
              </font>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <em>
              <font face="Segoe Print">
               Slimming Eats Recipe
              </font>
             </em>
            </p>
            <p>
             Makes 10 balls
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <font color="#ff8000">
               Extra Easy
              </font>
             </strong>
              2 HEas and 2 HEbs for the entire recipe
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <font color="#008000">
               Green
              </font>
             </strong>
              2 HEas and 2 HEbs for the entire recipe (when using a no meat alternative for the filling)
            </p>
            <p>
             <span id="more-5045">
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <u>
               Ingredients
              </u>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              1 cup (240ml) of arborio rice
             </li>
             <li>
              1 small onion, finely chopped
             </li>
             <li>
              2 baby bella mushrooms, finely chopped
             </li>
             <li>
              2 cloves of garlic, crushed
             </li>
             <li>
              Italian mixed herbs
             </li>
             <li>
              chicken stock (approx 800ml)
             </li>
             <li>
              120g of whole wheat bread (lightly toasted and blitzed into breadcrumbs)
              <strong>
               <font color="#0000ff">
                (2xHEbs)
               </font>
              </strong>
             </li>
             <li>
              60g of parmesan cheese
              <strong>
               <font color="#0000ff">
                (2 HEas)
               </font>
              </strong>
             </li>
             <li>
              salt and black pepper
             </li>
             <li>
              1 egg, beaten
             </li>
             <li>
              for the filling I used some leftover bolognese from this recipe
              <a href="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/spaghetti-bolognese" target="_blank">
               here
              </a>
              (omitting the wine) and mixed in a few frozen peas. (You only need about a heaped teaspoon of bolognese per ball)
             </li>
             <li>
              Frylight or Pam spray
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             <strong>
              For the sauce:
             </strong>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              1 small onion, finely chopped
             </li>
             <li>
              2 cloves of garlic, crushed
             </li>
             <li>
              1 cup (240ml) of passata
             </li>
             <li>
              1/2 cup (120ml of chicken stock)
             </li>
             <li>
              Frylight or Pam spray
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             Method
            </p>
            <p>
             Place your stock and Italian mixed herbs into a saucepan and keep on a low heat.
            </p>
            <p>
             Spray a large frying pan with some Frylight or Pam spray, add your onion, garlic and mushrooms and fry until softened, mix in the arborio rice and start adding your stock a couple of ladles at a time. Repeat the process until rice is nice and creamy, but still has a slight bite to it.
            </p>
            <p>
             Add the rice to a bowl, mix in the parmesan and beaten egg and set aside to cool.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="DSC_1159" border="0" height="325" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1159.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1159" width="464"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Preheat oven to 200c or 400f (gas mark 6)
            </p>
            <p>
             Place your breadcrumbs in a deep dish and season with some salt and black pepper and then you can start the process of making the arancini.
            </p>
            <p>
             Get about 2 tablespoon of the risotto mixture in your hands and flatten it down so you have a kind of pancake. Place one heaped teaspoon of your bolognese mixture into the centre of the pancake, and then carefully bring the rice up and around the mixture and form into a ball. Place in the breadcrumbs and roll to evenly cover and then place on a baking tray sprayed with some Frylight or Pam spray.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="DSC_1153" border="0" height="318" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1153.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1153" width="468"/>
             <img alt="DSC_1161" border="0" height="323" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1161.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1161" width="470"/>
             <img alt="DSC_1164" border="0" height="319" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1164.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1164" width="470"/>
             <img alt="DSC_1166" border="0" height="350" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1166.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1166" width="470"/>
             <img alt="DSC_1168" border="0" height="320" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1168.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1168" width="471"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Repeat the process with the rest of the mixture. You should get approx 10 balls.
            </p>
            <p>
             When all mixture is gone, go back over all the balls just to make sure they are firmly closed up and roll them through the remaining breadcrumbs again.
            </p>
            <p>
             Place back on the tray and spray over the top with some more Frylight or Pam spray.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="DSC_1178" border="0" height="317" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1178.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1178" width="467"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Put them in the oven and bake for approx 30 mins until lightly golden all over (you will need to rotate them at least once half way through the cooking time.
            </p>
            <p>
             While they are baking you can make your tomato sauce.
            </p>
            <p>
             Spray a frying pan with some frylight or Pam spray, add your onion and garlic and fry until lightly golden. Add the passata, stock and splash of balsamic vinegar and bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer until sauce has thickened.
            </p>
            <p>
             Serve your balls with the sauce.
            </p>
            <p>
             <img alt="DSC_1203" border="0" height="336" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1203.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1203" width="468"/>
             <img alt="DSC_1217" border="0" height="344" src="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/DSC_1217.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="DSC_1217" width="469"/>
            </p>
            <p>
             Enjoy!!
            </p>
            <p>
             You can use any type of risotto you like for the rice and the filling is entirely up to you too. Check out my lentil bolognese recipe
             <a href="http://www.slimmingeats.com/blog/lentil-bolognese" target="_blank">
              here
             </a>
             , if you want to are doing the green day alternative.
            </p>
