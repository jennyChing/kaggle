         <h2>
          <a class="titreArticle" href="http://hotelineurope.over-blog.com/article-toulouse-city-and-food-guide-reviews-europe-120400294.html" title="Toulouse City and Food Guide Reviews, Europe">
           Toulouse City and Food Guide Reviews, Europe
          </a>
         </h2>
        </div>
        <div class="contenuArticle">
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <img alt="Shanghai_-_Nanjing_Road---Toulouse-city-center.jpeg" class="GcheTexte" height="178" src="http://img.over-blog.com/300x220/5/07/91/59/Shanghai_-_Nanjing_Road---Toulouse-city-center.jpeg" width="252"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are a number of things that you have to do when it comes to dining in
          <a href="http://wikitravel.org/en/Toulouse">
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            Toulouse
           </span>
          </a>
          during
          <a href="http://hotelineurope.over-blog.com/pages/HolidayVacations-5948025.html">
           getting to Europe
          </a>
          . Some of these things include going for a coffee on the Place du Capitole at a caf and also
    having a cassoulet traditional meal at one of the numerous small bistros or the restaurants that are around the Toulouse city center.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          For those that can be able to push out the boat, it is possible for them to enjoy their dinner in one of the Michelin starred restaurants that are highly fashionable in Toulouse. There are also a
    number of cafes that are above the market that is covered on the Place Victor Hugo. Here you will get to enjoy simple fare and the brilliant atmosphere of this place.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
          <img alt="europe-travel-guide.jpg" class="GcheTexte" height="195" src="http://img.over-blog.com/260x195/5/07/91/59/europe-travel-guide.jpg" width="260"/>
          When To Eat
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Cafes are the most suitable places to go to during the day for either a light snack or coffee. Usually continental breakfasts in most cafes in Toulouse are served starting from 08:00.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The small restaurants that are available above the Victor Hugo market are open only during the day. Sandwich bars and snack stalls also offer fast food during the day. Most restaurants and
    brasseries all around Toulouse have at least one day per week that they do not operate.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are those that mostly close on Sunday while others close on Saturday lunchtime. Some small dining venues close in August when the owner needs to take annual holiday.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <hr/>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
          <img alt="Toulouse-food-guide.JPG" class="GcheTexte" height="192" src="http://img.over-blog.com/300x225/5/07/91/59/Toulouse-food-guide.JPG" width="257"/>
          What To Eat
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are a number of meals to choose from in Toulouse with the most famous meal being the traditional cassoulet.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          With a
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Restaurant_rating">
            Toulouse food guide
           </a>
          </span>
          you can be sure of getting the most delicious meal that
    suits you perfectly. The cassoulet has a number of versions with the original being duck, white beans and different types of pork.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are also spicy sausages, foie gras and duck which are commonly served in most of the restaurants menu in this city.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">

          <img alt="Toulouse_Christmas_market---Europe.jpg" class="GcheTexte" height="153" src="http://img.over-blog.com/300x153/5/07/91/59/Toulouse_Christmas_market---Europe.jpg" width="253"/>
          Where To Eat
         </h3>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are many cafes, restaurants and different dining options in the Place du Capitole. There are also numerous suitable restaurants, and
          <a href="http://www.toulousehotelsstay.com/">
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            hotels in Toulouse
           </span>
          </a>
          that are on your way out of the square.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          There are also very many small restaurants that serve lunch above the Victor Hugo market. Other includes the Michelin stars which are usually highly sought after.

         <strong>
          <blink>
           SAY CHEERS TO OKTOBERFEST!!
          </blink>
         </strong>
        </span>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <span style="font-size: 10pt;">
         O
        </span>
        riginating from Munich, Bavaria Germany, this 16 day long beer festival is being celebrated in various arts of the world in its own from. So get set to say
    cheers this October.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        . Along with focusing on beer, Oktoberfest is also about adventurous rides, tents, fun and food. Various countries are introducing new interesting elements to make this celebration more special.
    Lets have a look on some of the most happening Oktoberfest around the world.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <span style="font-size: 12pt; color: #0000ff;">
         <strong>
          GERMANY:
         </strong>
        </span>
        <br/>
        All set for its 180th edition this year, Oktoberfest in Munich has everything, from tapping the first barrel to rocking at the big brass concert. This year they are introducing new rideslike
    Odyssee, the highest transportable free fall tower Sky Fall (7 meter), the Cobra looping ride and the Pirate Adventure. The traditional Oktoberfest known as Oide Wiesn is back this year. Taking
    lace in the southern art of the Theresienwiese fairground, it will be hosted in traditional tents with historical rides and a lot more. To cherish it throughout your life, you can buy some
    interesting souvenirs from the shops at the fest selling, beer mug, wheat beer glass, T-shirts, pants, USB sticks, waistcoats, etc.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <br/>
        <strong>
         When:
        </strong>
        21st September to 6th October 2013.
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Where:
        </strong>
        Theresienwiese Fairground, Munich, Germany;
        <br/>
        <strong>
         For Further Info visit us:
        </strong>
        Oktoberfest.de
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <br/>
        <span style="font-size: 12pt; color: #0000ff;">
         <strong>
          USA:
         </strong>
        </span>
        <br/>
        Celebrated by the name of Great American Beer Festival, It represents the largest collection of beer ever served. Along with having breweries, the festival will also have food pavilions,
    bookstores, homebrew marketplace, competition booth, and a lot more designated pavilions. Silent Disco is one of the most interesting part of the festival; here only the people wearing the
    headphones can listen and groove to the live DJ whereas for others it is just silence. But whether you hear the music or no, this definitely brings a smile to your face. The festival unlike the
    one in Germany, takes places only for three consecutive days.
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: justify;">
        <br/>
        <strong>
         When:
        </strong>
        10th to 12th October 2013;
        <br/>
        <strong>
         Where:
        </strong>
        Colorado Convention Centre, Denver;
        <br/>
        <strong>
         For Further Info visit us:
        </strong>
        greatamericanbeerfestival.com
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        <span style="font-size: 12pt; color: #0000ff;">
         <strong>
          LONDON:
         </strong>
        </span>
        <br/>
        Another variant of this traditional festival is on three consecutive weekends. It is celebrated in a tent accommodating around 3,300 people. The first two weekends will be celebrated in
    Kennington Park, Lambeth and the last weekend will be held at Miliwall park, Canary Wharf. Starting from 19th September 2013, it wills happen from Thursday to Sunday. Books like Albfetza and
