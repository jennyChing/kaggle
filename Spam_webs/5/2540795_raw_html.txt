        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Open Faced Baked Eggplant Parmesan Sub
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-10-09T07:00:19+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 9, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/author/lyuba" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            lyuba
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/2014/10/open-faced-baked-eggplant-parmesan-sub.html#comments">
           4 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14893145" data-link="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/2014/10/open-faced-baked-eggplant-parmesan-sub.html" data-summary="" data-title="Open Faced Baked Eggplant Parmesan Sub">
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Classic
         <strong>
          Eggplant Parmesan
         </strong>
         turned into a delightful hot sub sandwich. Perfect lunch, full of wonderful flavors and ready in less than30 minutes.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Open Faced Baked Eggplant Parmesan Sub | from willcookforsmiles.com" class="alignnone wp-image-7493 size-full" height="905" src="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Open-Faced-Baked-Eggplant-Parmesan-Sub-2-from-willcookforsmiles.com_.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-pin-color="red" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" data-pin-height="28" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.willcookforsmiles.com%2F2014%2F10%2Fopen-faced-baked-eggplant-parmesan-sub.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.willcookforsmiles.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2014%2F10%2FOpen-Faced-Baked-Eggplant-Parmesan-Sub-2-from-willcookforsmiles.com_.jpg&amp;description=Open%20Faced%20Baked%20Eggplant%20Parmesan%20Sub%20%7C%20from%20willcookforsmiles.com">
          <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_red_28.png"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <!-- Please call pinit.js only once per page -->
         <br/>
         <script async="" src="//assets.pinterest.com/js/pinit.js" type="text/javascript">
         </script>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         This was probably my favorite
         <strong>
          lunch
         </strong>
         in weeks!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         Ive been craving some
         <strong>
          Eggplant Parmesan
         </strong>
         for a while and took it as a sign when one literally fell on my head at the store. Its a funny story really. I was picking up a few things at the grocery store, just grooving along to the music in my head. (Im not crazy, I had earphones in and listening to music on my phone. I knew you were thinking that!) So, while getting a few things in the produce section, I bent down to pick out some peppers. You know how grocery stores always have produce piled on top of each other mile high? Well, apparently eggplants were right above me and a guy standing next to me needed one. He got one while a few others came crashing down down and one happened to land on my head. Naturally, I knew it wanted to be my Eggplant Parmesan. While the guy apologized profusely, I just told him thanks for the lunch suggestion!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         The
         <strong>
          eggplant
         </strong>
         was absolutely right! It made a delicious
         <strong>
          Open Faced Baked Eggplant Parmesan Sub
         </strong>
         . Fresh whole grain 12-inch sub, drizzled with some olive oil and balsamic vinegar, topped with Marinara sauce, some fresh basil, lightly breaded eggplant slices and lots of cheese. Ahhh, what can be better?
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="Open Faced Baked Eggplant Parmesan Sub 1 from willcookforsmiles.com" class="alignnone wp-image-7492" height="550" src="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Open-Faced-Baked-Eggplant-Parmesan-Sub-1-from-willcookforsmiles.com_.jpg" width="830"/>
        </p>

        <p style="text-align: left;">
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Open Faced Baked Eggplant Parmesan Sub
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img itemprop="image" src="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Open-Faced-Baked-Eggplant-Parmesan-Sub-1-from-willcookforsmiles.com_.jpg" width="205"/>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://www.willcookforsmiles.com/easyrecipe-print/7490-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            Serves 4
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            12 inch whole grain Italian sub bread
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 lb eggplant
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup Italian Bread crumbs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup dry Parmesan cheese
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            salt, pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 eggs
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            salt pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2-3 Tbsp olive oil for cooking
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Olive oil and Balsamic vinegar for bread
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            About 1 cups of Marinara sauce
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp fresh minced basil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup of shredded Mozzarella cheese
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup fresh grated Parmesan cheese
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat your oven to 350.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Peel the eggplant and cut it into  inch slices, discarding tips.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Combine bread crumbs, dry Parmesan cheese and some salt and pepper in one shallow bowl. Whisk eggs with a little salt and pepper in another wide, shallow bowl.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Batter eggplant slices by first pressing it in bread crumbs, then dip it in eggs, then in bread crumbs again, patting the breading down.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat a skillet with 2 tbs of oil. Saute eggplant slices for about 5 minutes on each side, until soft and golden.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cut sub along the side but not all the way through. Cut it in half, making two 6-inch subs, sliced open. Place bread on a baking sheet.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Drizzle open sub with some olive oil and a little balsamic vinegar.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Spread half of Marinara sauce on the bread and sprinkle minced basil, evenly.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Layer cooked eggplant slices and spread remaining sauce over eggplant.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Sprinkle Mozzarella cheese and then grate some Parmesan over the top.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Bake for 13-15 minutes.
           </li>
          </ol>
