                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Sewing With Recycled Materials
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8080971427547922261" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     Three things you need to know about me and anything crafty:
                     <br/>
                     1. I love recycling
                     <br/>
                     2. I never throw anything away
                     <br/>
                     3. I never turn down free stuff
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     That being said, I do a lot of sewing using recycled materials, stuff people have given me, and little scraps from other projects. I like to save things and wait for
                     <i>
                      just the right project
                     </i>
                     to use them on. Lately I had the opportunity to create a yoga mat bag for a friend. The yoga mat is very thick and heavy so the bag needed to be strong, and she wanted a pocket for cell phone, keys, etc. This is the same person that gave me several bags stuffed full of upholstery samples that a local furniture store was going to get rid of. So I sorted out a bunch of the upholstery samples that looked good together and went to town! I also just happened to have a navy blue shirt pocket that fit perfectly with this project. I lined the bag with cream colored canvas to make sure it was extra sturdy, and used an old pair of blue jeans to make the drawstring strap. (The only things I bought to make this project were the grommets to attach the handle, and the canvas.)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <img border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hq-5f1H6RKo/TZIJcwdXh9I/AAAAAAAAAUQ/gt9Zs7LDPSw/s320/DSC_0020.JPG" width="214"/>
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25370403@N08/5571164695/">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-J6GaNR2Jcss/TZIJbaAh2QI/AAAAAAAAAUM/OJGt-nNO7ag/s320/DSC_0018.JPG" width="214"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Pulling out my upholstery samples inspired me to create some small zippered pouches using the leftovers. I bought another bag of upholstery samples at a rummage sale a couple years ago. Most of the fabric is heavier cotton with fun prints. At this same rummage sale I picked up a big bag of assorted zippers (both the zippers and the fabric probably cost less than $2.) For these pouches I used bits of coordinating upholstery samples, some of that canvas I bought for the yoga bag as the pouch lining, and whatever zipper I could find. I also am using scraps of old button down shirts I had leftover from making purses.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25370403@N08/5571162205/">
                       <img border="0" height="214" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hT2XBIp58-I/TZIJewweHwI/AAAAAAAAAUU/lLNzsB8Ncqg/s320/DSC_0001.JPG" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     This brings me to my shirt purses. I started making these a few years ago from my own pattern. Since then I have made at least 7 of them. The bags are slouchy hobo-style purses made from old shirts and upholstery samples with necktie handles. Not only are these bags recycled, they are reversible! I try to pick fabrics and neckties that go well together, and sometimes the button down shirts I choose are really awesome fabric... but really ugly shirts.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25370403@N08/5571751494/">
                       <img border="0" height="214" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ySJsWoF32p0/TZIJnaT3DUI/AAAAAAAAAUg/msS7mOFTMn4/s320/DSC_0008.JPG" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     Speaking of ugly shirt fabric... this ipod case used to be a ridiculous lime green plaid shirt. The inside is a scrap of green fleece, and the gray portion is from the leftovers after hemming some dress pants.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/25370403@N08/5571163909/">

                      </a>
