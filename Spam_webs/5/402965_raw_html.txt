     <p>
      <b>
       Carnivore
      </b>
      was an American
      <a href="/wiki/Crossover_thrash" title="Crossover thrash">
       crossover thrash
      </a>
      band from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" title="Brooklyn, New York">
       Brooklyn, New York
      </a>
      founded by singer and bassist
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Steele" title="Peter Steele">
       Peter Steele
      </a>
      , and was formed out of the breakup of the Brooklyn metal group
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fallout_(band)" title="Fallout (band)">
       Fallout
      </a>
      in 1982.
     </p>
     <p>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnivore_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Carnivore released their self-titled debut album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Carnivore_(album)" title="Carnivore (album)">
        Carnivore
       </a>
      </i>
      in January of 1986. For this album, the band spelled their names in what they thought were "exotic" ways. In September of 1987 they released their second album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Retaliation_(Carnivore_album)" title="Retaliation (Carnivore album)">
        Retaliation
       </a>
      </i>
      . Soon after their second album was released, the band broke up and
      <a href="/wiki/Peter_Steele" title="Peter Steele">
       Peter Steele
      </a>
      formed a new band called Subzero, which would later be renamed
      <a href="/wiki/Type_O_Negative" title="Type O Negative">
       Type O Negative
      </a>
      . According to the sleeve notes of the "Top Shelf Edition" of their second album, the first Type O Negative album,
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slow_Deep_and_Hard" title="Slow Deep and Hard">
        Slow Deep and Hard
       </a>
      </i>
      was composed largely from material originally written for Carnivore.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Reformation">
       Reformation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnivore_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reformation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Despite being disbanded for many years, the band was reactivated on several occasions, including several reunion shows in the mid 1990s with the Retaliation lineup, and an appearance at the 1996 Milwaukee Metalfest, plus several shows in 2006-2007 with a new 4 piece lineup. The new lineup of the band performed at the
      <a href="/wiki/Wacken_Open_Air" title="Wacken Open Air">
       Wacken Open Air
      </a>
      in 2006.
     </p>
     <p>
      Peter Steele died April 14, 2010, thus ending the reunion.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">
       Musical style
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnivore_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Musical style">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The first Carnivore album was heavily influenced by the contemporary New York metal scene. It also drew inspiration from
      <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">
       Black Sabbath
      </a>
      and early
      <a href="/wiki/Judas_Priest" title="Judas Priest">
       Judas Priest
      </a>
      , whereas the second album had significant
      <a href="/wiki/Crossover_thrash" title="Crossover thrash">
       crossover
      </a>
      influences. The 'post-apocalyptic' theme that dominated the first album and was carried onto parts of the second album, was apparently inspired by a dream Pete Steele had and which became the basis for the lyrics of "Predator", the first song from the original album. The lyrical theme was then expanded on to describe human society (or the lack of one) between imaginary World Wars 3, 4 and possibly 5 (as referenced in the song 'World Wars 3 &amp; 4'). Other lyrical themes included
      <a href="/wiki/Nihilism" title="Nihilism">
       nihilism
      </a>
      , anti-religious sentiment,
      <a href="/wiki/Cynicism_(contemporary)" title="Cynicism (contemporary)">
       cynicism
      </a>
      , and explicit but tongue-in-cheek depictions of gore and despair. Song titles such as "Jesus Hitler", "Race War", and "God is Dead" reflect these themes.
     </p>
