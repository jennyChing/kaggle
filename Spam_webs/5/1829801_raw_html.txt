         <h2>
          Banana Empanadas
         </h2>
         <div class="node-info">
          <span class="author">
           <a href="http://www.rhodesbread.com/blog/blog/term/Mely" id="user-18" title="Mely">
            Mely
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="date">
           Mon, Jul 23 2012
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="node-body">
          <p>
           <strong>
            Bananas Empanadas
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Ingredients:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            12 Rhodes Dinner Rolls, thawed but still cold
           </li>
           <li>
            5 tbs Butter
           </li>
           <li>
             cup Brown Sugar
           </li>
           <li>
            3 Bananas, diced
           </li>
           <li>
            Oil for frying
           </li>
          </ul>
          <p class="ListParagraphCxSpLast">
           Background:
          </p>
          <p>
           This is my first summer in the mid-west and boy is it hot! I wanted to create something easy that I could serve with some ice cream. This was what I came up with. Enjoy!
          </p>
          <p>
           Directions:
          </p>
          <p>
           Melt your butter in a small saucepan over medium-high heat. Once the butter is melted, add your brown sugar and stir until it is moistened and on its way to melting. Now add your banana. The banana will immediately begin to melt.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="1" height="400" id="mobile" src="/blog/app/webroot/images/2012/072312/1.JPG" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Youre going to want to stir the banana mixture until your sauce thickens and your bananas melt down very small.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="1" height="400" id="mobile" src="/blog/app/webroot/images/2012/072312/2.JPG" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Set aside your mixture and let cool.
          </p>
          <p>
           Now take each roll and mold into a circle. I used my hands because I do not add flour to them to ensure they stick well later. You can do this by first flattening the dough and then stretching it out in a circle. Place them on a sprayed counter.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="1" height="400" id="mobile" src="/blog/app/webroot/images/2012/072312/3.JPG" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Now add your cooled banana mixture to the middle of the flattened dough.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="1" height="400" id="mobile" src="/blog/app/webroot/images/2012/072312/4.JPG" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Now take the opposite side of the dough and fold it over so that it creates a half moon shape. Roll the top dough edge under the bottom dough and then with a fork, create grooves around the edge so as to seal the dough edges.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="1" height="400" id="mobile" src="/blog/app/webroot/images/2012/072312/5.JPG" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Now make sure your oil is pre-heated to 375 degrees f. When it is ready, fry your dough until it is a dark golden brown.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="1" height="400" id="mobile" src="/blog/app/webroot/images/2012/072312/6.JPG" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This desert is meant to be served warm with ice cream. I recommend vanilla ice cream. Then again, it is my favorite. I added some chocolate syrup for good measure.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="1" height="400" id="mobile" src="/blog/app/webroot/images/2012/072312/7.JPG" width="600"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           Grade:
          </p>
          <p>
           My guests tried this dessert and loved it. They gave it an 7 out of 10. Enjoy!!!!
          </p>

           <p>

As an Adult, she worked her way up the corporate ladder to become an Executive by the time she was 30. For years her main focus was travel, work, and service in Mexico, the Katrina devastated Mississippi, the Canary Islands, and other locations. In 2009 during a vacation in Ireland, she met the Irishman who became her husband. They now live in London, OH where Mely dedicates her time to raising their toddler, and her hobbies of Genealogy and cooking. When asked, Mely will tell you that her love of cooking comes from the time she spent during the summers of her childhood on her familys ranch. She remembers the daily gathering of family to share large, wonderful meals. Her wish is to share the tastes of her childhood with everyone.
