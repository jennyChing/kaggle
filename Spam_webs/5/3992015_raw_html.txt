        <h1 class="entry-title">
         DIY Welcome Spring Decor + a Giveaway
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2015-03-09T00:00:47+00:00">
          March 9, 2015
         </span>
         By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/author/blogging" rel="author">
            Karen
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/2015/03/diy-welcome-spring-decor-a-giveaway.html#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Its getting so very close now!
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Can you hear the happy songs of birds yet? Can you see some lush, fresh green peeking out here and there? Can you feel a gentle warmth in the air yet?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           I absolutely love the arrival of spring, but these days that are on the fringe  getting closer with every passing one  fill me withgiddy anticipation.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring.jpg">
            <img alt="Spring Decor" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2331" height="785" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring.jpg" width="2592"/>
           </a>
           Its time to get some bright and colorful spring decor out and freshen up the looks indoors as things begin to color themselves outdoors.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           I had a sweet time of creating a fun little project over the weekend with a couple of my children that I want to share with you today.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           This project is super easy and a great oneto include your children in. You can follow the idea we have here, or change the colors, embellishments, or letters to customize it to your personal style.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Are you ready to get started? Heres what youll need:
          </span>
         </p>
         <h3>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Mini Canvas Spring Decor
          </span>
         </h3>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            6 mini canvas frames
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            6 mini canvas stands
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            colored scrapbook paper
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            wooden letters
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            craft paint
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            modge podge or craft glue
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
            floral embellishments
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Start by cutting the scrapbook paper to fit your canvas, allowing enough to wrap over the edges. Using modge podge, attach the paper to each canvas and allow to dry.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring-Canvas-and-Flowers.jpg">
            <img alt="Spring Canvas and Flowers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2332" height="881" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring-Canvas-and-Flowers.jpg" width="1000"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Next, paint the letters, and allow them to dry.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring-Letters-Canvas.jpg">
            <img alt="Celebrating Spring" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2334" height="717" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring-Letters-Canvas.jpg" width="1000"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Finally, attach the embellishments to the letters and attach them to the front of each papered canvas. Hannah decided to surprise me by completing this step while I was doing something else, so I have no picture.
           <img alt=":)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           While the adhesive is drying, paint the stands and let them dry.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           Finally, place each canvas on the stand and youre done!
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           <a href="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring-Indoors.jpg">
            <img alt="Celebrating Spring" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2333" height="596" src="http://www.toworkwithmyhands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Spring-Indoors.jpg" width="1000"/>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           I havent decided exactly where Ill put this yet, but I love the fresh springy colors that it adds to our warm-toned sofa table and it might just stay right where it is.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 14pt;">
           We had so much fun making these that I plan to make more to give as gifts. Would you like a set? Im giving one away to a
           <em>
            To Work With My Hands
           </em>
           reader. If youd like a chance to win, enter the Rafflecopter giveaway below. The giveaway will end Friday and Ill announce the winner in the newsletternext week.
          </span>
         </p>
         <h4>
          <strong>
           <span style="font-size: 14pt; font-family: tahoma, arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">
            What are you most looking forward to about spring?
           </span>
          </strong>
