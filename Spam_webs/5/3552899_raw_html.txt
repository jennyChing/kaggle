       <img alt="Horchata Ice Cream" class="photo" src="http://www.seriouseats.com/recipes/assets_c/2013/06/20130625-horchata-ice-cream-thumb-625xauto-335763.jpg" width=""/>
       <div class="summary" id="entry-text">
        <p class="caption">
         [Photograph: Max Falkowitz]
        </p>
        <p>
         This ice cream is especially creamy thanks to the rice starch that infuses into the base. It's worth the effort to seek out canela, Mexican or "true" cinnamon, which has a gentler, fruitier flavor than more common cassia bark. Canela can be identified by its matte tan color, thin, papery bark, and citrusy aroma. More about it
         <a href="http://www.seriouseats.com/2011/01/spice-hunting-cinnamon-canela-ceylon-saigon-cassia.html">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <small>
          <strong>
           About the author:
          </strong>
          <a href="http://www.seriouseats.com/user/profile/MFalk">
           Max Falkowitz
          </a>

          . You can follow him on Twitter at
          <a href="http://twitter.com/#!/maxfalkowitz">
           @maxfalkowitz
          </a>
          .
         </small>
        </p>

       <h3 class="fn">
        Horchata Ice Cream
       </h3>
       <div class="recipe-about">
        <h2>
         About This Recipe
        </h2>
        <table>
         <tr>
          <td>
           <span class="label">

       <div class="content-block ingredients">
        <h2>
         Ingredients
        </h2>
        <div class="ingredients-section">
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            1/2 cup roasted, unsalted almonds
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            1/2 cup long grain rice
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            1 3-inch long cinnamon stick (canela; see note above)
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            4 cups half and half
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            5 egg yolks
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            3/4 cup sugar
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span class="ingredient">
            1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
       </div>
       <!--/ingredients-->
       <div class="content-block procedure">
        <h2>
         Procedures
        </h2>
        <ol class="procedure-steps instructions">
         <li class="instruction">
          <div class="procedure-number checked">
           1
          </div>
          <div class="procedure-text">
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            In a heavy medium saucepan, toast almonds, rice, and cinnamon on high heat, tossing frequently, until almonds begin to smell aromatic, 2 to 3 minutes. Add half and half, stir to combine, and bring to a bare simmer. Remove from heat, cover, and let steep for two hours.
           </p>
          </div>
         </li>
         <li class="instruction">
          <div class="procedure-number ">
           2
          </div>
          <div class="procedure-text">
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            In a second saucepan, whisk together sugar and egg yolks until well combined and slightly thickened.
           </p>
          </div>
         </li>
         <li class="instruction">
          <div class="procedure-number ">
           3
          </div>
          <div class="procedure-text">
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Using a fine mesh strainer or a couple layers of cheesecloth, strain dairy mixture into a large measuring cup or bowl. Press down firmly on rice with a wooden spoon to release as much dairy and rice starch as possible. Strained dairy should yield about 2 1/2 cups. Discard rice and almonds.
           </p>
          </div>
         </li>
         <li class="instruction">
          <div class="procedure-number ">
           4
          </div>
          <div class="procedure-text">
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Transfer strained dairy to saucepan with egg yolks and sugar and whisk until very well combined. Put saucepan on medium heat and cook, stirring frequently, until a custard forms on the back of a spoon and a swiped finger leaves a clean line. Stir in vanilla, then salt to taste.
           </p>
          </div>
         </li>
         <li class="instruction">
          <div class="procedure-number ">
           5
          </div>
          <div class="procedure-text">
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Pour custard through a fine mesh strainer into an airtight container and chill overnight. The next day, churn according to manufacturer's instructions. Transfer ice cream to container and chill in freezer for at least 3 to 4 hours before serving.
           </p>
          </div>
         </li>
        </ol>
       </div>
