          <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
           <a href="http://www.minds-in-bloom.com/2014/03/teacher-time-savers.html">
            Teacher Time Savers
           </a>
          </h3>
          <div class="post-header-line-1">
          </div>
          <div class="post-body entry-content">
           I'm Susanna from Whimsy Workshop Teaching, and I'm thrilled to be back sharing more ideas with you today! This time I want to pass along some time-saving tips for teachers.
           <br/>
           <br class="Apple-interchange-newline"/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-faSnu64QtQo/UwaREkkhpoI/AAAAAAAAELY/WXEDllRXzmU/s1600/TeacherTimeSavers.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-faSnu64QtQo/UwaREkkhpoI/AAAAAAAAELY/WXEDllRXzmU/s1600/TeacherTimeSavers.png" width="246"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           </div>
           There are many new ideas that I come across or create myself that truly improve the efficiency and productivity in the classroom. I often wish I'd discovered them when I first started teaching - I could have saved myself so much time and stress! With that in mind, here is a list of some of my favorites:
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            1. Organizing Your Class Library
           </b>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Problem:
           </u>
           I am lucky enough to have a huge classroom library. Over the years I've collected books from stores, thrift shops, garage sales, and book fairs. The down side is that there were just too many to keep tidy, and when I wanted to find one it was almost impossible! Everything always looked messy.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Solution:
           </u>
           Number your book baskets, and put that same number on every book that goes inside. This way, if a book is on the floor, students know exactly where it goes. My little firsties never really understood how to sort by theme or author. I order the baskets from easiest to hardest, and for random collection of books, I group them by size so it looks neat.
           <br/>
           And if this
           <i>
            still
           </i>
           doesn't keep your books in order, try this trick: Give each of your students a number, and put them in charge of the basket with that same number. Whenever you feel the need, ask students to go find "their" basket and make sure all the books have the right number. No more messy books!
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-yhNsk5p8LTY/Uv5cFOEqaUI/AAAAAAAAGNE/ovdO07TvH-g/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+10.09.26+AM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="261" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-yhNsk5p8LTY/Uv5cFOEqaUI/AAAAAAAAGNE/ovdO07TvH-g/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+10.09.26+AM.png" width="400"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <br/>
           <b>
            2. Keeping Desks Clean
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <u>
            Problem:
           </u>
           <b>
           </b>
           Student desks are so messy they can't find anything! It's usually random worksheets, toys, pictures, or sometimes even old lunch!
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Solution:
           </u>
           All together desk clean up! All at once, students take everything out of their desk and put in on their chair. Then, one by one, I tell them what to put back in. I'll say "Red folders go in. Yellow journals go in. Pencil boxes go in." I keep it to only 5 or six things. Finally, everything left on their chairs has to go either in their backpacks or the garbage.
           <br/>
           Another idea is to always dismiss students with clean desks - after a while they get the idea and try to keep it clean.
           <br/>
           If those ideas don't work, there's always the elusive Clean Desk Fairy; she visits the class randomly while students are away and leaves a small gift on the very cleanest desks!
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <b>
            3. Color Coding Table or Desk Groups
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <u>
            Problem:
           </u>
           <b>
           </b>
           It can be difficult to keep track of who is being chosen for particular tasks and classroom routines.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Solution:
           </u>
           I assign a color to each table by hanging a big tissue pom pom above it. I can use this for organization in so many ways. For example: Red table lines up first. In the gym, red table vs. the blue table. When I pool supplies in the middle of each table, all the supplies are marked with that color, so if the yellow scissors are on the floor, everyone knows where to put them back. At center time, I have bins of activities that rotate around the different table colors. I have a pocket chart with the table colors, and simply move the card with each center on it through the colors each day to stay organized. It's a life saver!
           <br/>
           <span style="font-size: x-large;">
            <br/>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-7lBEEYzQj1k/Uv5393biE5I/AAAAAAAAGOM/BRH4bib1fXc/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+12.08.58+PM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="277" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-7lBEEYzQj1k/Uv5393biE5I/AAAAAAAAGOM/BRH4bib1fXc/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+12.08.58+PM.png" width="320"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <span style="font-size: x-large;">
            <br/>
           </span>
           <b>
            Absent Folder
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <u>
            Problem:
           </u>
           It can be difficult to keep track of the work students missed while absent, especially if some are away several days in a row, or many students are absent with colds on different days.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Solution:
           </u>
           The daily helper puts a "Stuff You Missed Folder" on the desk of each student who is absent. It's simply a file folder pouch to put papers in. As students hand out printables or even art projects throughout the day, they include the folder in their hand outs. When the student returns, they now have everything they missed in a neat package to take home.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-7wT5OlWAktA/Uv51-8ZHgFI/AAAAAAAAGOA/eAnr9lpo8Ls/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+11.34.43+AM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="287" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-7wT5OlWAktA/Uv51-8ZHgFI/AAAAAAAAGOA/eAnr9lpo8Ls/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+11.34.43+AM.png" width="320"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <br/>
           </div>
           <b>
            Not Finished Bag
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <u>
            Problem:
           </u>
           It's hard to keep track of partially finished projects or printables for each student, and they can often get lost inside desks or forgotten about.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Solution:
           </u>
           Tape a large ziplock bag to the side or front of each desk. Students slip their unfinished worksheets into the bag so it won't get lost inside their desk. More importantly, I can see exactly what hasn't been done with a glance, and remind the student to finish or take home to complete. If the bag gets too full, then it alerts me to the fact that this little person has a problem with work completion and I can work with the student and parent to find a solution.
           <br/>
           <span style="font-size: x-large;">
            <br/>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-I-EXOhxJYQE/UwQc7yrBDVI/AAAAAAAAGSk/f64F01-wU3Q/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-18+at+6.35.43+PM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="496" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-I-EXOhxJYQE/UwQc7yrBDVI/AAAAAAAAGSk/f64F01-wU3Q/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-18+at+6.35.43+PM.png" width="640"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <span style="font-size: x-large;">
            <br/>
           </span>
           <span style="font-size: x-large;">
            <br/>
           </span>
           <b>
            Storing Wet Paintings
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <u>
            Problem:
           </u>
           There are no flat surfaces to dry wet paintings at the end of the day! The custodian needs to wipe down the desks, and if the painting are hung up, all the paint will drip.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Solution
           </u>
           : Use stacked chairs as a drying rack! Students slip one wet painting into each slot after stacking the chairs, and when the chairs are needed again in the morning, the paintings will be dry and ready to display or store. I shared this idea recently on the
           <a href="http://whimsyworkshop.blogspot.ca/2014/02/bright-ideas-blog-hop-february.html" target="_blank">
            Bright Ideas Blog Hop
           </a>
           !
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-sEhDBXK5u7o/Uv5kXoutD5I/AAAAAAAAGNU/KPBZ5fOlly0/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+8.20.48+AM.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
             <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-sEhDBXK5u7o/Uv5kXoutD5I/AAAAAAAAGNU/KPBZ5fOlly0/s1600/Screen+shot+2014-02-14+at+8.20.48+AM.png" width="312"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <b>
            Early Finishers Folder
           </b>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Problem:
           </u>
           No matter how much we differentiate, there will always be early finishers. It's a challenge to find something for them to do - I don't like to necessarily give students more work as a reward for being focused and efficient.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <u>
            Solution:
           </u>
           I make each student a "Read, Write or Draw" folder at the beginning of the year, and they know to use that when they are finished early. Inside is a variety of learning puzzles, searches and completely open-ended activities that can be used alone or with easily accessible materials around the room. For example, I include several of these sheets to use with any building materials (lego, bead, pattern blocks, etc).
           <br/>
           <span style="font-size: x-large;">
            <br/>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://whimsyworkshop.blogspot.ca/2013/09/entire-store-giveaway-and-freebie.html" target="_blank">
             <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-V869dodc1xw/Ui0F-DgD1mI/AAAAAAAAEHg/AWjyuRhH994/s400/Screen+shot+2013-09-08+at+4.13.40+PM.png" style="-webkit-box-shadow: transparent 0px 0px 0px !important; background-image: none !important; border: none !important; box-shadow: transparent 0px 0px 0px !important; padding: 5px; position: relative;" width="240"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <br/>
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            Here is an example of how it's used:
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <br/>
           </div>
           <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
            <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-z9HMZEWZN04/Ui0Ct0jlKFI/AAAAAAAAEHU/EROUR9PGa0U/s1600/Screen+shot+2013-09-08+at+1.17.27+PM.png" imageanchor="1" style="color: #341079; margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-decoration: none;">
             <img border="0" height="250" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-z9HMZEWZN04/Ui0Ct0jlKFI/AAAAAAAAEHU/EROUR9PGa0U/s640/Screen+shot+2013-09-08+at+1.17.27+PM.png" style="-webkit-box-shadow: transparent 0px 0px 0px !important; background-image: none !important; border: none !important; box-shadow: transparent 0px 0px 0px !important; padding: 5px; position: relative;" width="400"/>
            </a>
           </div>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           When students are done early, they can move ot a quiet spot with a bin of building materials. Students enjoy building something, and then answer the questions on the recording sheet; it's a fun activity to look forward to, but still provides learning and is self-differentiating.

           <br/>
           I hope some of these ideas will save you some time and energy in your class!

You could then use the pot at the end of the rainbow for positive reinforcement or "good as gold" pats on the back.
