         <div class="thecontent" itemprop="articleBody">
          <p>
           <a href="http://savvymujer.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/pernil-pork-shoulder-SavvyMujer.jpg">
            <img alt="pernil-pork-shoulder-SavvyMujer" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1373" height="1154" src="http://savvymujer.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/pernil-pork-shoulder-SavvyMujer.jpg" width="650"/>

          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           In Puerto Rico we say that 
           <em>
            el amor entra por la cocina
           </em>
            or love comes in through the kitchen as a way to say that the girlfriend cooked her way into her mans heart. My husband grew up in a butcher family so he was raised as a meat lover. Thanks
           <em>
            suegra
           </em>
           , that made it easy for me! 
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           If you ask me, whats a traditional Christmas meal,
           <em>
            puerto rican pernil
           </em>
           or roast pork and
           <em>
            arroz con gandules
           </em>
           is the one dish thats top of mind. I remember since I was a child how my parents would teamed up to have the holiday spread ready on time.
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           My mom would work on the rice and the
           <a href="http://www.savvymujer.com/potato-salad-recipe-puerto-rican-style/" title="Recipe: Potato Salad Puerto Rican Style">
            potato salad
           </a>
           and that was pork master.And as soon as that pork was roasting in the oven the smell of Christmas would take over in our home. Those were the days!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           I told you all this because once I moved to the mainland, I wanted to recreate that sense of Christmas without making a phone call being home sick so my parents would cook and freeze pernil, overnight it just to brighten my days a bit (and they did just that more than once or twice.) Yes, in my book they are the best! When I met my husband (a year later of being by myself) the phone calls changed a bit. I was not
           <del>
            begging
           </del>
           asking for comfort food anymore.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Related Recipe:
            <a href="http://savvymujer.com/potato-salad-recipe-puerto-rican-style/" title="Recipe: Potato Salad Puerto Rican Style">

           </strong>
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           I wanted to impressed him with my cooking skills that were pretty much non-existent then. Luckily, I had my secret weapon at the other end of the line: my dad telling me step by step on how to cooka
           <em>
            pernil
           </em>
           . Once my husband saw the process a couple of times, he urged me to think outside the
           <em>
            sazn
           </em>
           and use our own spice mix.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>

          </p>

           <div class="ERSIngredients">
            <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
             Ingredients
            </div>
            <ul>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              2 tablespoon smoked paprika
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              4 cloves of garlic, minced
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 teaspoon cumin
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 tablespoon onion salt
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 tablespoon kosher salt
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              1 teaspoon Chipotle Pepper or cayenne pepper, optional
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               cup extra virgin olive oil
             </li>
             <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
              8 pounds pork shoulder, bone-in
             </li>
            </ul>
            <div class="ERSClear">
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSInstructions">
            <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
             Instructions
            </div>
            <ol>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Mix all the spices with the garlic and the olive oil.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Using a sharp knife, cut slits into the pork and rub the pork with the spices mixture.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Place the pork, skin side up, in a roaster (no need to use the rack.)
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Preferably, let the pork marinate overnight or at least 4 hours before cooking in the refrigerator.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Preheat oven at 350 degrees F.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Take the pork out of the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for 30 minutes.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Roast uncovered for 3 to 3-1/2 hours in center of the oven.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Every hour during the cooking, flip the pork over.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              The idea is to cook the pork evenly, and your last flip will have the pork skin side up again.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              At the end, the pork should be at an internal temperature of 165 degrees.
             </li>
             <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
              Let it rest for at least 15 minutes before slicing it and enjoy!
             </li>
