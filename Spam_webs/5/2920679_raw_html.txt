      <div class="text text-1 parbase section">
       <p>
        <em>
         Roman Mars terrific design podcast
        </em>
        <a href="http://99percentinvisible.org/">
         <em>
          99% Invisible
         </em>
        </a>
        <em>
         covers design questions large and small, from
        </em>
        <a href="http://99percentinvisible.org/episode/episode-81-rebar-and-the-alvord-lake-bridge/">
         <em>
          his fascination with rebar
         </em>
        </a>
        <em>
         to
        </em>
        <a href="http://99percentinvisible.org/episode/episode-78-no-armed-bandit/">
         <em>
          the history of slot machines
         </em>

        <em>

        </a>
        <em>
         . Here at The Eye, we cross-post new episodes and host excerpts from the 99% Invisible blog, which offers complementary visuals for each episode.
        </em>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text-2 text parbase section">
       <p>
        <em>
         This week's editionabout one man's mission to name an island after Busta Rhymes
        </em>
        <em>
         can be played below. Or keep reading to learn more.
        </em>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="renderHtml section">
       <iframe frameborder="no" height="166" scrolling="no" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=https%3A//api.soundcloud.com/tracks/138996249&amp;auto_play=false&amp;hide_related=false&amp;visual=true" width="100%">
       </iframe>
      </div>
      <div class="text-3 text parbase section">
       <p>
        A few years ago, reporter Sean Cole was working on a radio story and needed to interview therapper Busta Rhymes.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="tablet-ad">
       Advertisement
       <br/>
       <div class="ad " data-ad-mode="tabletPortrait" data-ad-type="midarticleflex" id="slug_midarticleflex">
       </div>
       <div class="ad " data-ad-mode="mobile" data-ad-type="mob_bigbox" id="slug_mob_bigbox">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-4 section">
       <p>
        Cole was living in Boston at the time, so he did a Google search forBusta Rhymes and Boston to see if Busta had any upcoming shows that Cole could stake out.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-5 section">
       <p>
        Google didnt return any relevant tour dates. But it did give Sean a map, centering on a tiny speck of land in a neighboring suburb called Shrewsbury, Mass.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text text-6 parbase section">
       <p>
        The tiny speck of land was labeled Busta Rhymes Island.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-7 section">
       <p>
        The
        <em>
         Boston Globe
        </em>
        confirmed that, yes,
        <a href="http://www.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2009/03/13/sound_off/">
         Busta Rhymes Island is for real
        </a>
        .
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-8 section">
       <p>
        The Google Maps listing gives a phone number to call the island. So Cole called. A guy named Kevin OBrien answered. O'Brien is a guy in his early 30s who works in tech support and DJs weddings on weekends.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-9 section">
       <p>
        O'Brien started canoeing out to the island when he and his wife moved to Shrewsbury about 10 years ago. The island, a 40-by-40 speck of land, is practically in their front yard. O'Brien planted blueberry bushes on the island, and theres a rope swing hanging from one of the trees.He doesnt just visit the island a bunch, he takes care of the islandhe cleans up after the teenagers who leave beer cans lying around. He drinks beer himself on the island, with his wife and their friends.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-10 section">
       <p>
        And when you love a place the way O'Brien loves this place, you dont want to refer to it generically. You want to give it a a name.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text-11 text parbase section">
       <p>
        O'Brien and a friend decided on Busta Rhymes Island.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="parbase image slate_image section">
       <div class="">
        <figure class="image real-width" style="width: 590px;  display: block;margin: 0 auto;float: none;">
         <img alt="140312_EYE_2" src="/content/dam/slate/blogs/the_eye/2014%20new/03/12/140312_EYE_2.jpg.CROP.promovar-mediumlarge.jpg" src_tag_name="src" title="140312_EYE_2"/>
         <figcaption class="caption">
          <span>
           One man wants this man to be an island.
           <br/>
          </span>
         </figcaption>
         <p class="credit">
          Courtesy of TopStreetWear via Flickr
          <br/>
         </p>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <script>
        requirejs(["jquery"],function($){if($(window).width()&lt;640){$(".slate_image figure").width("100%");}});
       </script>
      </div>
      <div class="text-12 text parbase section">
       <p>
        All O'Brien had to do was go on Google Maps and apply a geotag to it. But O'Brien wanted the name to stick, so he didnt stop there. He wanted Busta Rhymes Island to become the islands official name.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text-13 text parbase section">
       <p>
        O'Brien submitteda formal proposal to the
        <a href="http://geonames.usgs.gov/">
         U.S. Board on Geographic Names
        </a>
        ,which decides what the federal government is going to call a piece of land. The board turned O'Brien downbut not for the reasons you might think.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text text-14 parbase section">
       <p>
        For any body of land to be named after a celebrity in a commemorative fashion, that person has to have been deceased for five years. The thinking is that, when someone passes awayespecially someone high-profileits an emotional time. Close relatives, friends, and fans want to honor the person. And so the U.S. Board on Geographic Names instituted a five-year cooling-off period, figuring that if someone is still beloved five years after his or her death to the extent that people still want a place named for him or her then, and only then, should naming be considered.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text text-15 parbase section">
       <p>
        Also, a living celebrity can do something heinous. Like that time Busta Rhymes
        <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/04/03/busta-rhymes-homophobic-slur-cheeseburger-baby_n_3007243.html" target="_blank">
         allegedly used a gay slur
        </a>
        in a Miami cheeseburger restaurant.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="youtube section">
       <div data-video-autoplay="" data-video-bcbasepageurl="http://www.slate.com/blogs/the_eye/2014/03/13/one_man_s_mission_to_name_an_island_after_busta_rhymes_99_invisible_by_roman.html" data-video-bcenabled="false" data-video-bcexperienceid="0" data-video-bcplaylistid="" data-video-bcvideoid="" data-video-height="400" data-video-type="youtube" data-video-width="568" data-video-youtubeid="DaALSHEGLzs" id="youtube_c7f7">
       </div>
       <script type="text/javascript">
        $(document).ready(function(){requirejs(['jquery','youtube','youtubeapi'],function($,youtube){});});
       </script>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-16 section">
       <p>
        Then how do you explain Mark Sandman SquareinCambridge, Mass.?
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-17 section">
       <p>
        Mark Sandman was the lead singer of the Boston-based band Morphine. He died in 1999 after collapsing on stage during a concert in Italy. And then just a year later, a sign honoring him went up on the corner ofBrookline Street and Massachusetts Avenue.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="youtube section">
       <div data-video-autoplay="" data-video-bcbasepageurl="http://www.slate.com/blogs/the_eye/2014/03/13/one_man_s_mission_to_name_an_island_after_busta_rhymes_99_invisible_by_roman.html" data-video-bcenabled="false" data-video-bcexperienceid="0" data-video-bcplaylistid="" data-video-bcvideoid="" data-video-height="400" data-video-type="youtube" data-video-width="568" data-video-youtubeid="eTTYoN0L0MY" id="youtube_69c0">
       </div>
       <script type="text/javascript">
        $(document).ready(function(){requirejs(['jquery','youtube','youtubeapi'],function($,youtube){});});
       </script>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-18 section">
       <p>
        It turns out that the five-year rule applies only to geographic features. Public spaces in cities are a different matter entirely. Those are decided by local municipalities, not the Board on Geographic Names.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-19 section">
       <p>
        It turns out that Cambridge loves naming places after people. Theres aJohn T. Johnny Collins Square and a Commander Francis X. Buddy Foster Square. Robert E. Goodman Road.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase section text-20">
       <p>
        In fact, some intersections in Cambridge have a differentcommemorativeblack sign on each corner.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-21 section">
       <p>
        As for Busta Rhymes Island, it might be a while before it can be officially recognized. But you can help. If you find yourself in Shrewsbury, Mass., and you ask for directions to Busta Rhymes Island, that counts as local usageone factor that the U.S. Board of Geographic Names looks for when considering a naming proposal.
       </p>
      </div>
