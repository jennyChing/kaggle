     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Roasted Red Beet Hummus
     </h1>
     <img height="517" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Roasted-Red-Beet-Hummus-790x517.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         November 14, 2012
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="655" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/roasted-red-beet-hummus/roasted-red-beet-hummus-3/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/roasted-red-beet-hummus-3" rel="attachment wp-att-6406">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Nothing quite tops the rich red and burgundy of a red beet. Whether it be in raviolis or this Roasted Red Beet Hummus, beetroots just add so much to a dish, visually speaking. And theyre damn tasty too!
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 medium-sized Red Beets
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup cooked chickpeas
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Clove
            <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/ingredient/garlic/">
             Garlic
            </a>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Tablespoons Sesame Seeds
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Meyer Lemon
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Cup Extra Virgin Olive Oil
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon Sea Salt
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/roasted-red-beet-hummus/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT1H15M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            1 hour, 15 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            15 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT1H" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            1 hour
           </div>
          </div>
          <h2>
           Oven Temperature:
           <span>
            375 F
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Type:
           </h2>
           Appetizer
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Main Ingredient:
           </h2>
           Beet
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Diet:
           </h2>
           <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/diet/vegan/">
            Vegan
           </a>
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Equipment:
           </h2>
           <a class="grub-featuredProductLink" grub-data-productpostid="3815" href="#featured-product/cuisinart">
            Cuisinart Food Processor
           </a>
           ,
           <a class="grub-featuredProductLink" grub-data-productpostid="3780" href="#featured-product/fry-pan">
            Fry Pan
           </a>
           ,
           <a class="grub-featuredProductLink" grub-data-productpostid="3756" href="#featured-product/lemon-juicer">
            Lemon Juicer
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. Wash and trim 2 red beets and wrap them in tinfoil. Place them on a baking sheet and roast them for about an hour at 375 degrees until they are super tender.
        </p>
        <p>
         NOTE: If you buy your beets with the greens on them, dont toss them! Those greens are not only delicious but at one time, they were the preferred portion of the plant. Just cook them up as you would spinach or Swiss chard. I saute them with garlic and olive oil.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="587" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/roasted-red-beet-hummus/red-beets-3/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/red-beets-3" rel="attachment wp-att-6404">
          <img alt="Red Beets" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-6404" height="352" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Red-Beets-600x352.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         FACT: Did you know that beets were considered an aphrodisiac by the ancient Romans? And they were on to something. Because of their nitrates, these tasty little gems can increase blood flow all the way down there. Also, they contain high amounts of boron, which is directly related to human sex hormone production. Sounds like the only thing this hummus recipe is missing is some Barry White.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. In a small fry pan, add 2 tablespoons of sesame seeds and cook them over a medium-high heat for about 5 minutes until toasted. Toss the seeds frequently to prevent them from burning. As they cook, they will release their natural oils and begin to stick together more, so have a spatula ready.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="546" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/roasted-red-beet-hummus/toasted-sesame-seeds-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/toasted-sesame-seeds-2" rel="attachment wp-att-6407">
          <img alt="Toasted Sesame Seeds" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-6407" height="327" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Toasted-Sesame-Seeds-600x327.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. When the red beets are fully cooked (you can tell by poking them with a knife), remove them from the oven and let cool. Then peel and cube them.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Add the following ingredients to your food processor: 1 cup of chickpeas, 1 clove of garlic, the toasted sesame seeds, the roasted beets, 1/4 cup olive oil and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Puree.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Add 1/2 of the juice of 1 lemon to the mixture and puree it. Add more lemon juice as needed.
        </p>
        <p>
         FACT: Whats in a name? Well, in 1908, USDA employee Frank Nicholas Meyer brought a little fruit back from China that looked like an orange-yellow lemon but tasted much sweeter. Many believe the fruit is a cross between a lemon and a mandarin orange.
        </p>
        <p>
         Enjoy! I like to garnish mine with parsley and sesame seeds.
        </p>

         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="1450" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/roasted-red-beet-hummus/roasted-red-beet-hummus-2/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/roasted-red-beet-hummus-2" rel="attachment wp-att-6405">
          <img alt="Roasted Red Beet Hummus-2" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-6405" height="870" itemprop="image" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/Roasted-Red-Beet-Hummus-2-600x870.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
