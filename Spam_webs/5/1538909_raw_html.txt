        <h2>
         There are approximately 10 million smartphones that reach the end of their user cycle each month. When is the best time to sell your phone?
        </h2>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4456" style="width: 1610px">
         <a class="grouped_elements" href="http://www.bezilch.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Make-money-phone-2.jpg" rel="tc-fancybox-group4454">
          <img alt="Money-phone" class="wp-image-4456 size-full" height="1067" src="http://www.bezilch.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Make-money-phone-2.jpg" width="1600"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Image courtesy of
          <a href="http://www.techhive.com/article/2044034/fcc-wasting-phone-subsidy-money-study-says.html" target="_blank">
           techhive.com
          </a>
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         The used smartphone market is huge: as Kevin Ramos of
         <a href="http://www.statista.com/" target="_blank">
          Statista
         </a>
         states, its estimated that more than one billionsmartphones have been sold since 2007, and many of those devices make it onto the secondary market. With all those devices out there, youre essentially competing with other sellers to ensure you get the money you want for the device youre looking to sell. Companies like Gazelle take advantage of this fact by offering to buy your phone in a very simple way. The catch? They offer you amounts that are far below the true value of your product. So if your best bet is to sell your phone yourself, when is it the best time to do that?
        </p>
        <p>
         As Chris Mills of
         <a href="http://www.gizmodo.co.uk/2013/10/whats-the-best-time-to-sell-a-smartphone/" target="_blank">
          Gizmodo
         </a>
         points out, selling your phone more than three months after the release of its new counterpart will likely net you less money than pulling the trigger within that three month window. As Mills states, the conclusion is that it doesnt matter if you sell your phone a month before launch or a month after: as long as your listing picture isnt ridiculous, youll probably still get about the same amount of cash for it. After that three month window, though, the prices yielded tend to be much less. This is likely because of the manypeople looking to take advantage of the influx of year old devices hitting the market.
        </p>
        <p>
         As an example, lets take a look at the iPhone 5s. When the iPhone 6 was announced, Apple devotees were no doubt excited to venture out and grab the latest and greatest from the Cupertino company. Many of these people obviously had the just-replaced iPhone 5s,which led the secondary market to becomeflooded with these devices. As Mills says above, the iPhone 5s would have retained its value from September (when it was released) to December. After December, however, its price would havereduced. Using this simple time frame will help you get as much money as possible when you sell your phone.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         With all the smartphones entering the secondary market all the time, little tricks to help you maximize your sales potential can make all the difference. Smart money says that when you sell your phone, posting it within three months of the release of its replacement is ideal.
        </p>

   (function() {
				function addSubmittedClassToFormContainer(e) {
					var form = e.target.form.parentNode;
					var className = 'mc4wp-form-submitted';
					(form.classList) ? form.classList.add(className) : form.className += ' ' + className;
				}

				function hideHoneypot(h) {
					var n = document.createElement('input');
					n.type = 'hidden';
					n.name = h.name;
					n.style.display = 'none';
					n.value = h.value;
					h.parentNode.replaceChild(n,h);
				}

				var forms = document.querySelectorAll('.mc4wp-form');
				for (var i = 0; i &lt; forms.length; i++) {
					(function(f) {

						// make sure honeypot is hidden
						var h = f.querySelector('input[name="_mc4wp_required_but_not_really"]');
						if(h) {
							hideHoneypot(h);
						}

						// add class on submit
						var b = f.querySelector('[type="submit"]');
						if(b.addEventListener) {
							b.addEventListener('click', addSubmittedClassToFormContainer);
						} else {
							b.attachEvent('click', addSubmittedClassToFormContainer);
						}

					})(forms[i]);
				}
			})();
