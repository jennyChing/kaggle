       <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
        Mumsnet tips on marketing to parents: and why so many companies get it wrong
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <span class="entry-date">
         <img align="absmiddle" alt="" class="author-image" src="/wp-content/themes/jrc2014/images/author-thumb-jonny-ross.png" width="30"/>
         <a href="https://www.jonnyross.com/author/jonnyross">
          Jonny Ross
         </a>
         posted this on
         <time class="entry-date updated" datetime="%3$s" pubdate="">
          Wednesday, May 6th, 2015
         </time>
         <div style="display:none;">
          <a href="https://plus.google.com/103257102611138481756/" rel="author">
           Jonny Ross
          </a>
          posted this on Wednesday, May 6th, 2015
          <div class="vcard">
           <cite class="fn">
            <a class="url" href="https://plus.google.com/103257102611138481756/" rel="external nofollow">
             Jonny Ross
            </a>
           </cite>
          </div>
         </div>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="https://www.jonnyross.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carrie-Longton-Mumsnet-Buy-Yorkshire-2015.jpg">
          <img alt="Carrie Longton Mumsnet Buy Yorkshire 2015" class="aligncenter wp-image-4602" height="410" src="https://www.jonnyross.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Carrie-Longton-Mumsnet-Buy-Yorkshire-2015-768x1024.jpg" width="307"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Last week I attended Carrie Longtons seminar Marketing to Mums at
         <a href="http://buyyorkshire.com/" target="_blank">
          Buy Yorkshire.
         </a>
         Carrie is an online marketing expert thanks to her experience as the co-founder of
         <a href="http://www.mumsnet.com/" target="_blank">
          Mumsnet.com
         </a>
         , the UKs biggest online parent network. Mumsnet boasts more than 15 million visits per month.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         Read my
         <a href="https://www.jonnyross.com/news/buy-yorkshire-2015-round-up-of-day-one" target="_blank">
          Buy Yorkshire review here
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Carries seminar was full of helpful tips and information for anyone marketing their services or products to mums or parents, which Ill share with you in this post. But first, I wanted to explore some of the issues she highlighted with marketing to parents  and why so many companies get it wrong.
        </p>
        <h2>
         The lucrative parent demographic  misunderstood?
        </h2>
        <p>
         It is no wonder that parents are seen as such a prime target demographic for marketing campaigns. Companies are always looking for new customers  and loyal customers. Targeting parents opens the doors to their children too  and a potential lifetime of brand loyalty. And once parents find a brand their kids like, they are much more likely to stick with it.
        </p>
        <p>
         But so many companies get it wrong  why?
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3694" style="width: 474px">
         <a href="https://www.jonnyross.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/family-watching-tv.jpg">
          <img alt="courtesy of jms-group.com" class=" wp-image-3694 " height="240" src="https://www.jonnyross.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/family-watching-tv.jpg" width="464"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          courtesy of jms-group.com
         </p>
        </div>
        <h2>
         3 mistakes advertisers make when marketing to mums
        </h2>
        <h3>
         (1) Playing on parents guilt
        </h3>
        <p>
         This is a pet hate of Carries. A guilt marketing strategy is compelling for parents. Tapping into parents insecurities or feelings of inadequacy is an easy way for marketers to push products that claim to make a childs life happier, healthier or more comfortable. The your kids deserve more approach is difficult to ignore. But this type of marketing promotes competitive parenting and worry  with parents making purchases out of fear. Mumsnet does not support this kind of advertising, and instead promotes positive marketing, whereby parents are supported, and instead feel inspired and encouraged, rather than guilty.
        </p>
        <h3>
         (2) Not understanding parents
        </h3>
        <p>
         In her talk, Carrie quoted a survey in which mums were asked if they thought marketeers actually understood them (i.e. did mums believe that a real mum had been involved in putting together any part of the marketing). Only 19% said they did.
        </p>
        <p>
         There is as much variety within a mums and parents group as there is in the general population. They should not be seen as homogenous. Age is a big factor: The baby boomer generation have kids at college or university, and many now have grandchildren too;
         <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/bw/stories/2004-04-14/the-abcs-of-selling-to-generation-x" target="_blank">
          Generation X parents
         </a>
         may often choose to buy products that offer simple solutions that give them more quality time with their children; while
         <a href="https://www.bcgperspectives.com/content/articles/marketing_center_consumer_customer_insight_how_millennials_changing_marketing_forever/?chapter=3" target="_blank">
          Millenials are more responsive to emotional advertising
         </a>
         . Within each of those group, there are the usual innovators who are first to try new products and the key influencers who will actively share recommendations with their friends on Facebook or via word-of-mouth. To understand parents, marketers need to do their homework and understand the current networks and relational complexities within the group in order to effectively engage with them to try new products and build brand loyalty.
        </p>
        <h3>
         (3) Calling them Mum
        </h3>
        <p>
         According to research cited by Carrie in her talk, only 23% of mothers are happy with people other than their family calling them mum. Mum is a very personal family term and advertisers who wade in and use the word without first being invited can be seen as intrusive or irritating.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.jonnyross.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Mumsnet-logo.png">
          <img alt="Mumsnet logo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4637" height="201" src="https://www.jonnyross.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Mumsnet-logo.png" width="251"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Mumsnet: 5 tips on marketing to mums / parents
        </h2>
        <p>
         Carrie firmly believes in positive marketing; if you buy a product and feel positive about it youll want to tell people about it, and its very important to think about this in your marketing and communications strategy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Carrie explained that when Mums where surveyed about how companies engage with them and their online marketing strategy they received the following results:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Only 19% believed mums had been involved in the marketing campaigns planning process
         </li>
         <li>
          Only 23% were happy with anyone other than their family calling them mum
         </li>
         <li>
          79% bought a product after seeing it recommended on Mumsnet and 80% would look on Mumsnet before buying a product
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Here are Carries top five tips for engaging mums and parents in your online digital marketing:
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Listen, engage, get to know and understand your audience before you start your marketing campaign.
         </li>
         <li>
          Dont say your product will make me a better parent, just give a better product.
         </li>
         <li>
          Write genuinely funny or useful content only, entertainment is the key to successful marketing and will catch the eyes of those parents looking for some light relief. Its fine to entertain them!
         </li>
         <li>
          Help them celebrate the fun times; having fun with kids makes a mum feel like a better parent.
         </li>
         <li>
          Do a good deed in a dark world and tell your brands story.
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Carries final thought of that day was: stop, think, empathise, enfranchise, engage.
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <em>

          </em>

         <li>
          <em>
           Are you a parent? Marketeer? Id love to know your views.
          </em>
         </li>
