    <h2 class="blogheader">
     Identity Guidelines for Wavepulse Acoustics Logo
    </h2>
    <div class="clear big">
    </div>
    <div class="two_thirds">
     <article class="post-4354 post type-post status-publish format-aside has-post-thumbnail hentry category-flickr category-identity-guidelines category-logo-design-process category-logo-specifications category-portfolio tag-brand-identity tag-client tag-fonts tag-guidelines tag-identity-2 tag-identity-guidlines tag-logo-4 tag-logo-design tag-logos tag-pdf tag-wavepulse post_format-post-format-aside">
      <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4368" data-src="http://imjustcreative.com/wp-content/uploads/wavepulse-identity-header.png" height="313" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAAAAACH5BAEKAAEALAAAAAABAAEAAAICTAEAOw==" style="margin-bottom: 20px;" title="wavepulse-identity-header" width="470"/>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4368" height="313" src="http://pull1.thelogosmith.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/wavepulse-identity-header.png" style="margin-bottom: 20px;" title="wavepulse-identity-header" width="470"/>
      </noscript>
      <h2>
       Identity guidelines for Wavepulse Acoustics logo
      </h2>
      <h4>
       View a larger size over at my
       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/grahamsblog/4502041725/" target="_blank" title="Flickr logo portfolio">
        Flickr
       </a>
       account.
      </h4>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Read more on the development of this logo design 
        <a href="http://imjustcreative.com/logo-process-wavepulse-acoustics-identity-development/2010/03/30/" target="_blank">
         Logo Process  WavePulse Acoustics Identity Development
        </a>
       </strong>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This is a single page A4 identity guide for the Wavepulse Acoustics logo. Its purpose is to provide basic guidelines for the correct usage of the logo. As some projects are only providing logo designs, opposed to more complex brand identities, the amount of information needed is minimal. Its not meant to confuse or overwhelm the client, hence it being a handy A4 PDF file.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2689/4502041725_1e8d8b60b3_b.jpg" title="Identity Guidelines - Wavepulse Acoustics Logo by imjustcreative, on Flickr">
        <img alt="Identity Guidelines - Wavepulse Acoustics Logo" data-src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2689/4502041725_1e8d8b60b3_b.jpg" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAAAAACH5BAEKAAEALAAAAAABAAEAAAICTAEAOw==" style="margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;" width="470"/>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Identity Guidelines - Wavepulse Acoustics Logo" src="http://farm3.static.flickr.com/2689/4502041725_1e8d8b60b3_b.jpg" style="margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;" width="470"/>
        </noscript>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h2>
       Whats included
      </h2>
      <p>
       This guide covers the general construction of the logo, dimensions and proportions. Also includes the additional logo styles such as contained logos, portrait and landscape logos, social media profile images and website favicon. Information on the fonts used as well as a few handy dont do this with your logo examples at the foot of the guide.
      </p>
      <p>
       This is a neat way to also show the client, at a glance, the level of detail that goes into a logo, the spacing, the alignment, proportions etc. On initial glance, the logo may look simple with a word here and logomark there, there is fine level of detail to ensure the logo looks and feels right.
      </p>
      <p>
       It has been a challenge to try to fit all the information I wanted onto one page, without over crowding, whilst maintaining a clean layout design.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://imjustcreative.com/wp-content/uploads/wavepulse-acoustics-logomark1.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4271" data-src="http://imjustcreative.com/wp-content/uploads/wavepulse-acoustics-logomark1.jpg" height="313" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAAAAACH5BAEKAAEALAAAAAABAAEAAAICTAEAOw==" style="margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;" title="wavepulse-acoustics-logomark" width="470"/>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4271" height="313" src="http://pull1.thelogosmith.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/wavepulse-acoustics-logomark1.jpg" style="margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;" title="wavepulse-acoustics-logomark" width="470"/>
        </noscript>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h2>
       Created how?
      </h2>
      <p>
       This file was created in Illustrator opposed to InDesign. Not sure why, I just started fiddling in Illustrator and then I was too far done to start over. It did take some time, I was tweeting my progress whilst at various stages of the design. Finding the right amount of detail and laying it out so that is looks OK was the challenge. Lots of tweaking, redoing, rehashing, coffee and undos. This was one page, I researched corporate identity guides with over 20 pages of guidelines, so these things are not quick. One reason why they cost so much to design.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now I have one file done, I can use this as a template for all my other projects, so time should be greatly reduced. I have done ad-hoc guides before, but I wanted to create a more branded look, something consistent with my own brand. I am now working on a 2 page guide and also a 4-6 page guide.
      </p>
      <h2>
       The client will get
      </h2>
      <p>
       The file will be saved as a PDF file and also a layered Illustrator EPS file. All elements except the various logo styles will be locked. This means the client can use this to pick up the various logos for their own use without having individual logo files floating around, should help to keep everything neat and tidy.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Therefore, my aim is to provide each client with this Dummies Guide to logo usage PDF guide. The majority of clients will get this A4 guide without question, this includes logo designs with a budget of more than 500. If the project or client requires a higher level of detail in the guide, they can pay extra for a more detailed book.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <strong>
        Template for download
       </strong>
      </h3>
      <p>
       I will be making a version of this identity guide available as a download, saved in Illustrator CS 4. Any other CS versions will be available on request. This means you can benefit from all my hard work and have a template to base your own guide from. Obviously try to inject some of your own style into it, rather than just using my design. But should be useful to get some of you off your feet in doing your own guides.
       <strong>
       </strong>
      </p>
