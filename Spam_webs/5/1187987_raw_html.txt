        <p>
         Cooking a great meal off the top of your head is possible, and it can be done without any
         <em>
          Top Chef
         </em>
         -level skills. All you need are some non-
         <a href="http://greatist.com/healthy-recipes">
          recipes
         </a>
         dishes you know how to make by heartthat can be customized based on what leftovers are lurking in the fridge and what youre craving that night.
        </p>
        <p>
         You probably have at least one non-recipe already. Maybe its a stir-fry that has a different protein and veggies in it each time you make it, and you also serve over different grains. Or its something as simple as
         <a href="http://greatist.com/eat/avocado-toast-recipes">
          avocado toast
         </a>
         , which can have a surprising number of tasty variations.
        </p>
        <div class="ym" data-yieldmo="ym_966591849689717803" data-yieldmo-tablet="ym_976628814724144286" id="ym_966591849689717803">
        </div>
        <p>
         Add these five nutritionist-approved meal formulas to your regular rotation, and youll never fall into the rut of making the same recipe the same way every time you have it. These are so easy youll memorize them quickly, and soon youll be playing with your food and discovering your own delicious takes on them. See? Cooking
         <em>
          can
         </em>
         be fun.
        </p>
        <section class="slideshow list-image-full" ng-controller="SlideshowListCtrl" ng-init="init()">
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="media-g_full_width_inline_image media-element-container">
           <img alt="Meal Templates Gain Bowls" src="http://greatist.com/sites/default/files/styles/article_main/public/MealTemplates_GrainBowls_0.png?itok=aOrQavjD"/>
          </div>
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            1. Grain Bowls
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            This recipe allows you to benefit from the slow-burning energy starches provide without making them the main component of a meal, which can result in carb overload, says Cynthia Sass, M.P.H., R.D., sports nutritionist and author of the upcoming
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Slim-Down-Now-Pounds-Inches/dp/0062311832/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1423003552&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=slim+down+now">
             <em>
              Slim Down Now: Shed Pounds and Inches With Real Food, Real Fast
             </em>
            </a>
            . Mixing up the toppings creates a balanced ratio of carbs, protein, and fat that will steady your blood sugar and insulin levels, and leave you feeling simultaneously energized and satisfiednot undernourished or sluggish. Its also super easy to mix things up to create new texture and flavor combos.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="media-g_full_width_inline_image media-element-container">
           <img alt="Meal Templates Noodle Bowls" src="http://greatist.com/sites/default/files/styles/article_main/public/MealTemplates_NoodleBowls2_0.png?itok=IoQYERTf"/>
          </div>
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            2. Noodle Bowls
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Im a
            <a href="http://greatist.com/health/healthy-pasta-recipes">
             pasta
            </a>
            lover and find that adding beans and veggies to my dish helps to keep my portion in check while still filling me up, says
            <a href="http://kerigansnutrition.com/">
             Keri Gans
            </a>
            , R.D.N., author of
            <em>
             The Small Change Diet
            </em>
            . I also love a meal that doesnt require a lot of pots or pans, nor a lot of preparation or cooking time. While the pasta cooks (any shape works), saut the garlic in olive oil, then add the remaining ingredients to that pan. Toss everything together once its all ready.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="media-g_full_width_inline_image media-element-container">
           <img alt="Meal Templates Quesadilla " src="http://greatist.com/sites/default/files/styles/article_main/public/MealTemplates_Quesadilla_0.png?itok=rY-7UAR8"/>
          </div>
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            3. Quesadillas
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            Eating
            <a href="http://greatist.com/eat/new-quesadilla-combinations">
             quesadillas
            </a>
            is a great way to knock out a few food groups all at once: whole grains from corn or whole-wheat tortillas, veggies, and lean protein, says
            <a href="http://elisazied.com/">
             Elisa Zied
            </a>
            , R.D. author of
            <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Younger-Next-Week-Ultimate-Reverse/dp/0373892837/ref=redir_mobile_desktop?ie=UTF8&amp;dpID=512vXgJgdxL&amp;dpPl=1&amp;keywords=elisa%20zied&amp;pi=AC_SY200_QL40&amp;qid=1423234222&amp;ref=plSrch&amp;ref_=mp_s_a_1_1&amp;sr=8-1">
             <em>
              Younger Next Week
             </em>
            </a>
            . I also love them because they take little time to assemble and cook, and you can use so many combinations of things, even leftover chicken, steak, or shrimp.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>

          </div>
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            4. Chili
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            I love chili because it is hardy and filling yet uber healthy if done right, says Keri Glassman, R.D., founder of
            <a href="http://nutritiouslife.com/">
             NutritiousLife.com
            </a>
            . It's loaded with fiber and antioxidants from the veggies and the beans, and protein from the beans and meat, making it a super proportioned and satisfying meal. Heat 2 teaspoons olive oil in a pot, then add the vegetables and saut until vegetables are tender. Add meat (if using) and cook until done. Add beans, tomatoes, and spices, and cover. Let simmer over medium-low heat for 1 hour.
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="slide">
          <div class="media-g_full_width_inline_image media-element-container">
           <img alt="Meal Templates Squash Boats" src="http://greatist.com/sites/default/files/styles/article_main/public/MealTemplates_SquashBoats.png?itok=QZR6hmEV"/>
          </div>
          <div class="title-wrapper">
           <h4>
            5. Squash Boats
           </h4>
          </div>
          <div class="slide-body">
           <p>
            This is a fantastic way to sneak in extra veggies, which we can all benefit from, says
            <a href="https://instagram.com/nutritionstripped/">
             McKel Hill
            </a>
            , R. D., creator of the blog
            <a href="http://nutritionstripped.com/">
             Nutrition Stripped
            </a>
            . Its also lower in carbohydrates, higher in fiber, and a good source of vitamins A, K, and beta-carotene. All you have to do is cut a
            <a href="http://greatist.com/health/gourd-squash-pumpkin-recipes">
             squash
            </a>
            in half, remove the seeds, roast it, and then stuff it with a tasty entre-like filling.
           </p>
          </div>
