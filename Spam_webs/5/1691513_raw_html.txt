            Why You Shouldnt Be a Part of the 90% Of Americans Who Still Use Microwaves

          </h1>

          </p>
          <div class="fb-like" data-action="like" data-font="" data-href="http://www.wakingtimes.com/2015/04/16/why-you-shouldnt-be-a-part-of-the-90-of-americans-who-still-use-microwaves/" data-layout="standard" data-send="false" data-width="450" show_faces="false" style="margin: 0px 0px 5px 0px; height:25px; overflow:hidden;">
          </div>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.wakingtimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Microwave-Oven.jpg">
            <img alt="Microwave Oven" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-56527" height="200" src="http://www.wakingtimes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Microwave-Oven-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
           Mark De Nicola,
           <em>
            <a href="http://www.collective-evolution.com/2015/04/06/why-you-shouldnt-be-a-part-of-the-90-of-americans-that-still-use-microwaves/" target="_blank">
             Collective Evolution
            </a>
           </em>
           <br/>
           <a href="http://www.wakingtimes.com/2015/04/16/why-you-shouldnt-be-a-part-of-the-90-of-americans-who-still-use-microwaves/" target="_blank" title="Waking Times">
            <strong>
             Waking Times
            </strong>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Theyre fast, convenient, and easy to use. Manufacturers even produce packaged foods specifically for their use.What Im referring to are of course, microwave ovens, the household appliance that is still regularly used as a part of meal preparation and re-heating in approximately 90% of American homes.
          </p>
          <p>
           I say still in the previous statement because the dangers associated with microwave use have been documented for quite some time, dangers that I will once again outline in this article. Ultimately, arguments can always be made for the use of anything harmful if done in moderation, but given the hectic natureofmost of our lives, the convenience the device offers makes little to moderate use seem very unlikely to be practiced.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Microwaves Alter Your Foods Chemical Structure
          </h3>
          <p>
           Microwaves heat food by causing the water molecules contained within to vibrate at very high frequencies, eventually turning those moleculesinto steam. It is this reaction which increases the temperature of your food. The type of heating microwavesproduce is known as dielectric heating, which bounces around the device and is absorbed by the food that you put into it.
          </p>
          <div align="center" style="margin-top: 0em; margin-bottom: 1em;">
           <script type="text/javascript">
            // &lt;![CDATA[
google_ad_client = "ca-pub-7393699293279853"; /* WT_InContent_468x60 */ google_ad_slot = "8357301278"; google_ad_width = 468; google_ad_height = 60;
// ]]&gt;
           </script>
           <script src="http://pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
            // &lt;![CDATA[&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;// ]]&gt;
           </script>
          </div>
          <p>
           Heating your food in general is known to deprive itof at least a portion of its nutrients, but many believe that opting to heat foodvia a microwave also subjectsitto what is referred to as the microwave effect. Many still argue that the microwave effect is a myth, but a number of studies have come out suggesting otherwise. Most notably, a study released byPenn State University in 2001 stated that conventional and microwave heating appear the same at the macro level but are very different at the molecular level. The study also referenced a 1995 study in which a scientist successfully demonstrated that microwaves  unlike other heating devices  are capable of destroying viral DNA.
           <a href="http://articles.mercola.com/sites/articles/archive/2010/05/18/microwave-hazards.aspx" target="_blank">
            (1)
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Another study conducted in 2003 examined broccoli prepared in the microwave and found that the vegetable had lost 97% of its valuable antioxidant content through the cooking process.Given that we live in a world dominated by processed food, and much of thefresh producewe do eatis grown in soil heavily depleted of its naturally occurring nutrients, the idea of losing even more nutrition seems almost like a knockout punch to our health. To most of us, myself included, eating completely raw is definitely not overly enticing, so opting for the best possible cooking method certainly does seem important.
          </p>
          <p>
           The best possible cooking method also extends into the oil you choose to prepare your food with, since many oils are found to turn rancid when heated past a certain point. You can find out more about that
           <a href="http://www.collective-evolution.com/2012/05/02/a-truth-about-olive-oil-we-should-all-know/" target="_blank">
            HERE
           </a>
           and see one better alternative
           <a href="http://www.collective-evolution.com/2013/03/27/coconut-oil-an-affordable-alternative-packed-with-benefits/" target="_blank">
            HERE
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <h3>
           Container Chemicals &amp; Food Fats Leak Into Your Food
          </h3>
          <p>
           Were all familiar with the Microwave Safe label found on a number of food containers, but this label is entirely misleading. Despite what many people think, itonly refers to the containers ability to be used in a microwave safely without melting or starting a fire. What it doesnt refer to is the containers safety in terms of the numerous toxic chemicals itcan leak into your food when heated  chemicals which you then ingest.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://wakingtimes.com/ads/aquaponics4you.html" target="_blank">
            <img alt="" class=" alignright" src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/_qLAIskTQXUc/TaemSibTUYI/AAAAAAAAIO8/gjRLdqlnvPg/ad5.jpg"/>
           </a>
           Chemicals such as polyethylene terpthalate (PET), benzene, toluene, xylene, the now well-known
           <a href="http://www.collective-evolution.com/2013/03/15/bpa-shown-to-negatively-impact-brain-development/" target="_blank">
            BPA
           </a>
           , and even its so-called safer alternative
           <a href="http://www.collective-evolution.com/2015/02/04/study-shows-regularly-used-bpa-alternative-bps-could-be-just-as-dangerous/" target="_blank">
            BPS
           </a>
           . Many of these chemicals have been linked tothe impairment of female reproductive elements and
           <a href="http://www.wakingtimes.com/2013/09/05/neurosurgeon-speaks-vaccines-harm-child-brain-development/" title="Neurosurgeon Speaks Out On How Vaccines Harm Child Brain Development">
            brain development
           </a>
           , along with diabetes, prostate disease, and more. The microwaving of fatty foods in microwave safe plastic containers has also been found to lead to the release of the known carcinogen dioxins.
           <a href="http://articles.mercola.com/sites/articles/archive/2010/05/18/microwave-hazards.aspx" target="_blank">
            (1)
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Oven-safe glass is widely recognized as one of the best alternatives when it comes to heating your food, and even though glassware can often be more expensive than many of its counterparts, it seems to be a worthy investment.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Even aNew &amp; Safety Inspected Microwave Can Leak Radiation
          </h3>
          <p>
           Microwave manufacturers do deserve a lot of credit for the heightened safety standards theyve put into practice when developing the newest microwave ovens. Thanks to their hard work, a number of previous concerns have been rectified, however there still seems to be some exposure, especially when within a certain radius of the appliance when in use.
          </p>
          <p>
           Even a brand new, perfectly functioning and locking microwave can expose us to up to 400 milliGauss of unwanted radiation. To put that into perspective, a mere 4 milliGauss has been linked to leukemia.
           <a href="http://articles.mercola.com/sites/articles/archive/2010/05/18/microwave-hazards.aspx" target="_blank">
            (1)
           </a>
           Steering clear of microwaves when in use is certainly advisable for all but is particularly critical for both pregnant women and children. Given that microwaves are most often used in the midst of the cooking process, steering clear of them can often be quite difficult, further exemplifying why they should be avoided altogether if possible.
          </p>
          <h3>
           How To Move On Without A Microwave
          </h3>
          <p>
           There are certainly no shortage of alternatives when it comes to heating food. The one key difference between microwaves and the other available options, however, is time. As with so many other worthy changes we feel inspired to make in our lives, they take a level of commitment in order to be implemented properly.
          </p>
          <p>
           A simple step such as planning ahead can go a long waytowardsavoiding the need for a microwave. Taking frozen ingredients out of the freezer well in advance of meal preparation time will give them the opportunity to naturally thaw out in time for when they need to be cooked.
          </p>
          <p>
           I believe that the more we become consciously present with the meals we prepare, the more likely we are to naturally steer away from the need for a quick prep option. Both a
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=toaster%20oven&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=wakitime09-20&amp;linkId=JW6ZQC6ZPWORFXLM" target="_blank" title="Find on Amazon">
            toaster oven
           </a>
           and conventional oven may take substantially longer to prepare a meal, but the health concerns that they successfully avoid is certainly worth the wait.
          </p>
          <p>
           
          </p>
          <p>
           I personally havent had a microwave in any of the houses Ive lived in since the age of 12 and I cant say that I miss it. What are your thoughts on the health concerns related to microwaves? Do you still use one regularly? And if so, why? Wed love to know via the comment section below.

This is NOT a steam/microwave combo.  It toasts, convection bakes, steam bakes, broils, steam broils, steams, super steams, bakes and keeps warm.  It is a counter top oven, I have mine on a shelf over my regular stove.  Between that a pressure cooker and bamboo steamers I rarely use my big oven.  About 10 years ago I purchases a Scan brand wok, semi-nonstick, non coated surface, can be cleaned with a stainless steel chore boy type scrubber but food doesnt stick like it does to stainless steel pans. Best piece of cookware I own after my cast iron skillets.  I live at 7500 ft elevation so just cooking rice takes way to long(water boils at about 190 degrees here).             <p>
             I havent owned a microwave in 5 years and I havent even missed it once. I moved and left it behind and have had small kitchen counters so I didnt want it taking up all that space. I read the article on microwaves damaging the antioxidant content in food, so thats another thing thats keeping me from buying another one.
            </p>
            <p>
             A major reason that I havent missed it is that I have turned away from all processed food. If you make your own food and freeze it, all you have to do is plan a little and you dont need the microwave  figure out what youre going to want for dinner and take it out of the freezer in the morning. Its that simple.
            </p>
            <p>
             What Ive done is purchase a convection toaster oven. It heats your food about 30% faster and gets things nicely brown (unlike a microwave).
            </p>
            <p>
             So, that little thing takes up too much valuable space for your returns, and its probably damaging your food anyway, so its not worth the money or effort to have.
            </p>
            <p>
             Basically, if you make your food choices healthier and eat more vegetables and raw food,  you will naturally eliminate the need for a microwave. I wouldnt buy one now even if it didnt damage my food or send plastics into it.
