        <h2 class="limitdesc" id="title" rel="title|articles|198">
         Where did I put my keys? 12 ways to improve memory
        </h2>
        <div class="description limitdesc" id="description" rel="description|articles|198">
         <span class="small">
          By Nicole W.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <p>
          How many of us have wasted hours searching for our keys or glasses? Occasional feelings of forgetfulness or memory loss are fairly common and fortunately are not usually a cause for concern. However, as the brain has to store such an immense amount of data, its worth investing in a little brain training every now and again. Follow these 12 simple steps to improve your brain power and prevent the dreaded brain fog.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align:center">
          How to improve memory
         </h2>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           1. Take a break
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          When you feel stressed the brain struggles to retain new information, which affects short-term memory. Stress also prevents the production of new neurons and reduces brain power. To de-stress try switching to something else, like taking a nature walk or reading a book. Many people also find meditation or deep breathing exercises effective in relieving stress and tension.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           2. Laugh as much as possible
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Laughter is not only the best medicine, it may also be an effective memory enhancer, particularly when it comes to age-related memory loss. Laughing acts as a natural stress reducer by stimulating several different areas of the brain and releasing endorphins. A research team at Loma Linda University in California found that watching funny videos can significantly improve memory recall and learning ability, whilst reducing levels of the stress hormone cortisol. These findings suggest that humour is key to reducing stress levels.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           3. Engage in brain games
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Word games like Scrabble or crosswords can help to train the memory and protect against memory loss. These exercises cause the brain to think critically, problem solve and recall information. Scientists have found that playing brain games for just half an hour every day results in structural changes to areas of the brain associated with critical thinking, reasoning, and memory.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" src="http://healthylife.simplysupplements.net/library/article_graphics/chess-mind-games.jpg" style="width:70%"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           4. Exercise daily
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Regular exercise increases the production of neurons in the brain and releases feel-good hormones that protect cells from damage. Researchers at Cambridge University have shown that aerobic exercises, such as jogging, help the brain to stay mentally sharp and energised. Dancing is also effective as it engages coordination, planning, and muscle function. However, exercise doesnt need to be overly demanding to be rewarding. Walking is an easy yet effective way to boost brain power, with the added benefits of clearing and relaxing the mind. Keeping physically active (at any age!) keeps the brain active and can even reverse brain shrinkage. So don your trainers or walking boots this evening and get moving.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           5. Eat sea food
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Can foods really make you smarter? The answer is yes - a regular supply of certain brain foods can improve brain function and memory. Oily fish is the best example as it contains high levels of the omega 3 fatty acids EPA and DHA, which are frequently associated with bigger and healthier brains. 60% of the fats in the brain are omega 3s, with DHA being the main type. Researchers in Sweden have found that people with high blood levels of DHA had a 47% lower risk of developing dementia. To further reinforce these findings, the Mediterranean diet, which is particularly rich in omega 3 fats, can increase lifespan by up to 15 years.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           6. Go nuts
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Revising for an important exam? Snack on a handful of nuts whilst studying. Scientists have shown that eating nuts before an exam can result in higher test scores. Nuts are rich in omega 3s, magnesium and vitamin E, which are known to enhance brain function and protect it against damage. Nuts may also help to boost the brains serotonin levels. For best results, choose walnuts or almonds.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" src="http://healthylife.simplysupplements.net/library/article_graphics/nuts-healthy-snack.jpg" style="width:70%"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           7. Sip Green Tea
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Researchers at the University of Basel have found that Green Tea enhances cognitive functions, in particular working memory. They discovered that participants supplementing with Green Tea experienced increased connectivity between different areas of the brain and tested significantly better for working memory tasks. So, next time you hit the mid-afternoon energy slump, opt for a mug of green tea in place of your regular cuppa.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           8. Top up with B Vitamins
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Although a healthy and balanced diet is crucial to good health, adding a high quality
          <strong>
           <u>
            <a href="http://www.simplysupplements.net/product/81/vitamin-b-complex/">
             Vitamin B Complex supplement
            </a>
           </u>
          </strong>
          to your daily routine offers added protection and vitality. B vitamins are involved in the production of brain chemicals such as dopamine and serotonin. MRI scans have shown that a combination of folic acid (B9),vitamin B6 and vitamin B12, can have a protective effect on the brain; reducing brain shrinkage associated with dementia and Alzheimers by up to 90%.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           9. Stay hydrated
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          The brain is around 80% water, and it requires water for the electrical energy it uses to perform everyday functions. When the brain is dehydrated it releases the stress hormone cortisol, which impairs its ability to store information. Being thirsty may also distract the brain from cognitive task and reduce energy and concentration. As the brain cant store water, it requires a regular supply throughout the day.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" src="http://healthylife.simplysupplements.net/library/article_graphics/drink-more-water.jpg" style="width:70%"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           10. Listen to music
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Music has an extraordinary power over memory and brain function. Classical music in particular has been found to improve recall and emotional intelligence, and is often referred to as the Mozart effect. This is based on findings from several large-scale studies that showed a whopping 80% improvement in intelligence scores when revising students listened to Mozart. Songs can also help to trigger memories, and music therapy is becoming an increasingly useful tool in dementia care.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           11. Be creative
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Painting sparks creativity and can be an effective way to boost brain power by engaging new parts of the brain. Even if youve never tried painting before, give it a go and see if you can unlock your inner Da Vinci. Writing can also help by stimulating thought processes, which enhance brain function.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size:20px">
           12. Get some Zzzzs
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Sleep helps the brain to consolidate and organise information, and clears out all the mental clutter. There is extensive evidence to show that a lack of sleep impairs creativity, problem solving, and memory. Try to get eight hours of sleep per night for optimal brain function during the day. Feeling tired during the day? Taking a nap can be an effective refresher, by improving task performance and thinking abilities.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="color:#FFFFFF">
           ...
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Use these simple steps to boost your brain power. Do you have any quick and easy tips?
         </p>
