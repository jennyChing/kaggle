        Ben + Jerrys to go full non-GMO by mid-year
       </h1>

      <div class="entry-content pbl ">
       <p>
        Premium ice cream maker Ben + Jerrys plans to soon pull any genetically modified (GMO) ingredients from its supply lines in both Canada and the U.S.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Vermont-based company, which makes and sells ice cream in over two dozen countries through major retailers and its chain of ice cream shops, and last year pledged to transition to non-GMO worldwide, recently updated its deadline to sourcing non-GMO ingredients for all our products everywhere by mid-year 2014.
       </p>
       <p>
        In Canada and the U.S., 80 per cent of Ben + Jerrys ingredients by volume are today sourced non-GMO, while its products made in Europe are already fully non-GMO, the company said.
       </p>
       <p>
        Beginning now, and throughout the coming year, we will transition packaging so that all Ben + Jerrys products will be labeled with respect to GMO by the end of 2014.
       </p>
       <p>
        The company last fall publicly supported Washington states proposed I-522 ballot initiative, to require mandatory labelling of foods for GMO content.
       </p>
       <p>
        Unfortunately I-522 did not pass, but the work in Vermont is still going full steam ahead converting our own ingredients and flavours to Fairtrade-certified and non-GMO sourced, the company said in a December report.
       </p>
       <p>
        The company, which has previously lobbied for labelling of products made from milk produced by cows fed recombinant bovine growth hormone (rbGH), is proud to stand with the growing consumer movement for transparency and the right to know whats in our food supply by supporting mandatory GMO labeling legislation.
        <em>
          AGCanada.com Network
        </em>
       </p>

               There is a big, as in huge, difference between selective breeding/hybrids and GMOs. Selective breeding and hybridizing is using what nature already put in the apples to yield a more desirable result. GMOs pull bits of genetic material out of completely different organisms and add it into the genetic material of another organism. For example, BT toxin was originally produced by certain bacteria, but thanks to GMO tech, it is now produced by potatoes, corn, etc. So every time you eat that GMO corn, you are ingesting a heavy dose of insecticide that cant be washed off because it is actually part of the corn. Yum, yum!
              </p>
              <p>
               It is well understood that individuals choose their mates, in part, for the best potential offspring- the characteristics that are found to be attractive have a certain survival value. That doesnt mean that its okay to tamper with the DNA so that human children will have gills or fins!
              </p>
              <p>
               Please take some time to research the differences if you are going to state opinions such as this.
