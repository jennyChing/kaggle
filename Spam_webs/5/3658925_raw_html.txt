        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2014/02/06/bush-is-back-baby/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to Bush is back, baby">
         Bush is back, baby
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p class="metadata">
        <span class="date updated">
         February 6, 2014
        </span>
        in
        <span class="categories">
         <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/category/popcult/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Pop Culture">
          Pop Culture
         </a>
        </span>
        |
        <a class="comments-link" href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2014/02/06/bush-is-back-baby/#respond">
         <span class="dsq-postid" rel="675 http://www.thelasttriangle.com/?p=675">

      </div>
      <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
       <p>
        I started this blog a few years back as part of my graduate school thesis project. If you had told me then that in 2014 pubic hair would be turning up regularly in the headlines, I would have laughed.
       </p>
       <p>
        But its doing just that. Bush, it would seem, is back.
       </p>
       <p>
        Or at least thats what I keep hearing.
       </p>
       <p>
        Evidently,
        <a href="http://http://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2014/jan/19/year-of-the-bush-female-body-hair-cameron-diaz-pubic" target="_blank">
         The Guardian has declared 2014 to be the Year of the Bush.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        On December 31st, actress Cameron Diaz published a health guide called The Body Book in which she
        <a href="http://www.thefrisky.com/2014-01-02/cameron-diaz-shares-her-detailed-thoughts-in-praise-of-pubes/" target="_blank">
         sings the praises of cultivating our mighty pubes
        </a>
        (though not in those exact words).
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Unknown.jpeg">
         <img alt="Unknown" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-676" height="259" src="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Unknown.jpeg" width="194"/>
        </a>
        And of course, clothing chain American Apparel, famous for their eyebrow-raising marketing schemes,
        <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/women/womens-life/10578880/American-Apparel-pubic-hair-mannequins-shock-New-Yorkers.html" target="_blank">
         recently dressed their front window mannequins in pubic hair on a New York City street
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        I dont feel particularly surprised about any of this, of course. Theres nothing the media loves more than finding some aspect of the female anatomy to make a fuss over, especially if its a topic that has been traditionally been a little taboo.
       </p>
       <p>
        But hearing that 2014 will be pubic hair friendly makes me laugh. Oh, thanks great fashion and body care advisors  you mean its OK for me to wear my body hair au naturel this year and not face scorn or shame from or anyone else? We shall see.
       </p>
       <p>
        Since there is so much going on the pubic-realm these days, I will do my best to keep my commentary fresh on ye ol blog. The fact is, however, that I worry about getting repetitive. Once youve spent a lot of time writing about a particular issue, it can get challenging to find fresh avenues to travel down.
       </p>
       <p>
        There are a lot of smart women letting their voices be heard in the media these days. Im certainly glad were having these conversations in public. But Im taking it all with a grain of salt.
       </p>
       <p>
        I look forward to hearing your thoughts.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>

         Pubes on (web) TV
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p class="metadata">
        <span class="date updated">
         November 19, 2013
        </span>
        in
        <span class="categories">
         <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/category/media-2/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Media">
          Media

        |
        <a class="comments-link" href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2013/11/19/pubes-on-web-tv/#comments">

      <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
       <p>
        Hello, fair readers!
       </p>
       <p>
        I know I havent exactly been keeping on top of the blog, but thoughts of body hair are never too far from my mind. I hope youre all thinking critically about the stuff you see depicted out there in the world, and the ways in which you regard your own body and the bodies of others. Theres always lots of good material for discussion flying on ye ol internet. When it comes to this blog, however, I sometimes feel like I run out of stuff to say. I dont really want to get to the point where Im just posting stuff and going SEE!? and then repeating the same rants over and over again. I hope that explains something of my recent online silences. Im also finding myself busy with work, new extracurricular activites (Im learning spanish!) and with a recent move to a different city.
       </p>
       <p>
        BUT: I did want to let you know that Im taking the pubic hair discussion back into the world later today. Im going to be making an appearance later this afternoon on
        <a href="live.huffingtonpost.com" target="_blank">
         HuffPost Live
        </a>
         the live streaming network on The Huffington Post.Ill be part of a panel of guests (so far Ive been told the others will be an esthetician and someone who has had a bad experience with waxing) who will be chatting with host Caitlyn Becker via webcam.
       </p>
       <p>
        Our discussion will be based on a recent Huffington Post article that garnered a whole lot of feedback and discussion called 
        <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/11/04/down-there-grooming_n_4212945.html" target="_blank">
         Waxing on your period &amp; more down there grooming questions you were too afraid to ask
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Apparently our segment starts just after 5:30pm EST. I hope youll be able to tune in and maybe throw a question my way!
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/women/womens-life/10452327/Pubic-hair-is-back-ladies.-The-men-dont-care-and-the-women-cant-be-bothered.html?fb" target="_blank">
         (oh- and have you heard the good news? Pubic hair is back! An article in the Telegraph told me so!)
        </a>

         Brazilian waxes, ball ironing and other summer fun
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p class="metadata">
        <span class="date updated">
         June 20, 2013
        </span>
        in

      <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2013/06/20/brazilian-waxes-ball-ironing-and-other-summer-fun/brazilian-wax/" rel="attachment wp-att-653">
         <img alt="" class="alignleft wp-image-653" height="180" src="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/06/brazilian-wax.jpg" title="brazilian-wax" width="300"/>
        </a>
        Well, with summers start tomorrow I know were all freaking out about getting ready for bathing suit season right? What, no? Ok, youre right: bathing suit season is low on the list of things competing for space in my mind right now. This is the time of year, however, when the mainstream media like to post helpful tips and tricks for getting ready for the aforementioned season  and for women, that usually involves some kind of well-intentioned piece about how to whisk away unwanted hair.
       </p>
       <p>
        Thats why I was amused (and sort of pleased) to see an article in the Toronto Star called 
        <a href="http://www.thestar.com/life/fashion_style/2013/06/19/my_first_brazilian_wax.html" target="_blank" title="My First Brazilian Wax ">
         My First Brazilian Wax
        </a>
        . Written by Victoria Ptashnick, the article charts her first foray to the salon for a full Brazilian  ie. a wax after which very little is left to the imagination. Victoria doesnt break any ground with her tale  its charming and funny and pretty much exactly what you would expect  but I did think it was refreshing to see a young woman opting out of what appears to be the dominant pubic-grooming practice of her age. In
        <a href="http://www.thestar.com/life/fashion_style/2013/06/19/my_first_brazilian_wax.html" target="_blank">
         a video added to the site yesterday
        </a>
        , Victoria answers reader questions about her article and experience. She is very clear about one thing: her future will not include intimate waxing.
       </p>
       <p>
        Speaking of intimate procedures, I recently learned about ball ironing, sometimes known as tightening the tackle (apparently) in
        <a href="http://nymag.com/thecut/2013/06/ball-ironing-a-cosmetic-procedure-for-men.html">
         a brief article on an offshoot of the NY Magazines website called The Cut
        </a>
        . According to the article, the thin testicular skin gets wrinkly and discoloured with age, so men can now pay good money (and lots of it) for a procedure that involves using lasers to remove hair, erase wrinkles, and correct discoloration on the scrotum. Will we ever run out of things to worry about?
       </p>

        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2013/04/26/hairless-beavers-and-other-controversies/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to Hairless Beavers (and other controversies)">
         Hairless Beavers (and other controversies)
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p class="metadata">
        <span class="date updated">
         April 26, 2013
        </span>
        in
        <span class="categories">
         <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/category/interviews/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Interviews">
          Interviews
         </a>

         </a>
        </span>
        |
        <a class="comments-link" href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2013/04/26/hairless-beavers-and-other-controversies/#respond">

          No comments
         </span>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2013/04/26/hairless-beavers-and-other-controversies/beaverwindow/" rel="attachment wp-att-642">
         <img alt="" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-642" height="84" src="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/beaverwindow.jpg" title="beaverwindow" width="150"/>
        </a>
        The other day a video popped up on a friends Facebook feed. It featured a small image of what appeared to be a t-shirt on a mannequin. There was a big, blurry circle in the mannequins crotch area.
       </p>
       <p>
        Next to it was this headline: controversial window display in Halifax, N.S. turns heads. Figuring it might involve pubic hair, I could resist clicking the link.I wasnt wrong.
        <a href="http://www.ctvnews.ca/video?clipId=909286&amp;playlistId=1.1245899&amp;binId=1.810401&amp;playlistPageNum=1" target="_blank">
         (you can watch it for yourself here).
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        It took me to a news segment that aired earlier this week on CTV news in Halifax. Before running the item, host Steve Murphy looks at the audience and says we caution you that some of you may find this offensive, and it may not be appropriate for younger viewers.
       </p>
       <p>
        Which is what made the item so extra hilarious. In short: its a very short news item about an art installation thats getting mixed reviews in a store-front window on Barrington Street in downtown Halifax. Apparently a whole whack of people are incredibly offended by it and theres a petition circulating to have the work taken down. The news item, which is shabbily put together, clips a couple of people talking about how terrible they find it, along with a few who shrug their shoulders and say they dont really know what its about. While the item fails to credit the artist entirely, it does clip the curator, Scott Saunders, who makes a couple of valid points about how there is much more offensive stuff going on in the windows of the sex shops down the street. He says the work is about Canadian identity, sexual politics, and the idea of Canadiana.
       </p>
       <p>
        At no point do we ever get to see what everyone is so upset about because of the HUGE, BLURRY CIRCLE shielding us from the displays horrors.
       </p>
       <p>
        Well here, ladies and gentlemen, is what the news needed to protect you from:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2013/04/26/hairless-beavers-and-other-controversies/imagejpeg_2-1/" rel="attachment wp-att-643">
         <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-643" height="600" src="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/imagejpeg_2-1.jpg" title="imagejpeg_2-1" width="448"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Thats right: a mannequin wearing a T-shirt reading Canada: Go Beavers! and a furry, pubic-hair-esque loin cloth. Though she is not mentioned in the controversy, it didnt take me long to figure out that the artist is Bonita Hatcher, a NSCAD grad.
       </p>

        Heres a wider view of the entire window:

       <p>
        <a href="http://instagram.com/p/YQm8eCOTg7/" target="_blank">
         (Sorry its so small! Halifaxs alt weekly, The Coast, posted a great photo to Instagram, too).
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Curious to know more (and so thoroughly dissatisfied by the news item), I booked a call with the artist.
       </p>
       <p>
        Our chat was wonderful  hilarious, energizing, and informative. Bonita immediately filled in some of the blanks around the window work for me.Firstly, the news item omitted some significant details: most significantly, that the beaver featured in the lower left hand corner of the window IS COMPLETELY SHORN! Thats right  in what sounds like a painstaking process, she SHAVED A TAXIDERMY BEAVER. She then cut up a bit of an old (beaver) fur coat, adorned a particularly phallic piece of it with red ribbons, and turned it into a loin cloth. She had the T-shirt created especially. Though its hard to read at first, the beaver in the image is hairless.
       </p>
       <p>
        Bonita, who is in her early 40s with a background in marine biology, was as appalled as anyone by the upset her piece had elicited among Haligonians. She said she was most fascinated by the fact that there was nothing ACTUALLY offensive in what she had created  a shorn beaver, a furry merkin, and a printed t-shirt. It was, she pointed out, up to viewers to make connections if they wanted to  and in order to do so, one had to be properly equipped (a shorn beaver, for example, has no meaning in itself unless you know that beaver is a slang term for a womans genitalia).
       </p>
       <p>
        While Bonita told me she hadnt sought out to make a statement about pubic hair (in fact, the shorn beaver was originally part of a larger piece addressing Canadiana), normalized pubic hair removal among women has fascinated her for some time. She described re-entering the dating scene after the end of her marriage and coming to terms with what a Brazilian was (it took me awhile to figure out what it was, she recalled, I remember going oh-my-god, are you kidding me? In my mind, if I had a hair caught in the elastic of my underwear I would cry.
       </p>
       <p>
        For Bonita, who only started making art as an adult has always been interested in feminist art and performance. In one of her first performances at art school, she cut off her clothes and painted herself with latex. I got that stuff out of the way, she laughs, later wearing a wedding dress 24-hours a day for a week in a bid to explore the idea that traditional female wedding garb serves to cover the body from head to toe and restrict movement.
       </p>
       <p>
        I dont even think that Bonita has a precise grasp on what she was trying to say to the world with her controversial window display  but she definitely managed to make a statement. The shorn beaver is visually clever, the phallic merkin, provocative. Its not entirely clear whether we are supposed to celebrate the hairless beast and lament the loss of his fur, or whether we should be re-embracing our own Canadian short-n-curlies, but in a sense it really doesnt matter. The work does (as Bonita intended) start a conversation that is definitely worth having.
       </p>
       <p>
        Bonita also let slip that shes intended to start selling her go beavers T-shirts. I cant wait to stroll the streets of my city in it this summer. No blurry circle required.
       </p>

        </a>

       </p>

        alternative newspaper in Halifax wrote me out of the blue and asked me to contribute something about the demise of pubic hair to their annual Back-to-School issue.
       </p>
       <p>
        (wondering what pubic hair has to do with heading back to school?
        <a href="http://www.thecoast.ca/halifax/grooming-101/Content?oid=3331438" target="_blank">
         You can read the whole article here
        </a>
        )
       </p>
       <p>
        The second thing to happen is that Ive been invited to be a guest speaker at the
        <a href="www.nedic.ca/" target="_blank">
         National Eating Disorder Information Centre
        </a>
        s 2013 conference. They want me to talk about (surprise, surprise!) body hair, body politics and popular culture.
       </p>
       <p>
        So: its time for me to starting thinking about all this stuff again. And there is, of course, lots of stuff to think about. So again  apologies for not being here when you needed a place to rant. More updates coming soon.
       </p>
       <p>
        Really.

       <h2 class="title entry-title" id="post-611">
        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2012/04/13/this-well-runs-deep/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to This well runs deep">
         This well runs deep
        </a>
       </h2>
       <p class="metadata">
        <span class="date updated">
         April 13, 2012
        </span>
        in
        <span class="categories">
         <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/category/capitalism-2/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Capitalism">
          Capitalism
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/category/media-2/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Media">

         ,
         <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/category/popcult/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Pop Culture">

         </a>
        </span>
        |
        <a class="comments-link" href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2012/04/13/this-well-runs-deep/#respond">
         <span class="dsq-postid" rel="611 http://www.thelasttriangle.com/?p=611">
          No comments
         </span>
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
       <p>
        Yes, thats right: when you spend your spare time thinking about the political significance of public hair, you never run out of fodder for party small talk. Nor do you ever run out of things to make you mad. Heres the latest thing  send to me by a number of friends/readers/allies.
       </p>
       <p>
        Seems besides having intimate bits that are too hairy, its also possible to have genitals that are too brown. At least thats what the people who produce a new product aimed at Indian woman. Evidently, the product is some kind of intimate wash that also helps to make your vulva many shades fairer. Curious? Heres the television ad:
       </p>
       <p>
        http://youtu.be/9Tx9vVVMWw0
       </p>
       <p>
        Predictably, our protagonist is sad with her ordinary vagina, but is radiantly happy once shes doused it in chemicals. Ive written before about the notion of the vagina as being dirty until all its protective hair is whisked away. This is a product that actually exactly embodies your vagina is dirty mentality. That graphic, with the product lightening an animated groin seems to say it all. Sigh.
       </p>
       <p>
        Huffington Posts got a good article on the top. Have a read here:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/2012/04/12/vagina-brightener-indian-feminine-hygiene-product-promises-to-make-genitals-many-shades-fairer_n_1420052.html?ref=canada-living&amp;ir=Canada+Living" target="_blank">
         http://www.huffingtonpost.co.uk/2012/04/12/vagina-brightener-indian-feminine-hygiene-product-promises-to-make-genitals-many-shades-fairer_n_1420052.html?ref=canada-living&amp;ir=Canada+Living
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        And as Ive written before, I love it when readers get in touch. I had a nice little note from Emily recently, who got in touch to tell me about a recent experience she had reading Cosmopolitan magazine.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres what she wrote:
       </p>
       <div>
        <em>
         I was at a friends house the other day and found something in a magazine that seemed relevant to The Last Triangle.
        </em>
       </div>
       <div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <em>
         Looking at Cosmo is like looking at a car crash. I know Im going to be horrified by what I see, but if there is one there, I still look. The image I attatched is a snap of 99 sex questions answered. I know not to expect much from Cosmo, but it was still upsetting to see that when ones lover has a preference for pubes, this publication is encouraging her to leave him the bare minimum. Oh, the implications.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
        </em>
        She also sent along this snapshot:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/2012/04/13/this-well-runs-deep/cosmo-page-from-emily/" rel="attachment wp-att-616">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-616" height="298" src="http://www.thelasttriangle.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/04/cosmo-page-from-emily--1024x612.jpg" title="cosmo page from emily" width="500"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        As Emily points out, the problem with this is, of course, that at no point is the poor woman told that her boyfriend would probably be just fine with nothing more than a little trim. But the only option this mainstream mag can offer up is the landing strip: a (probably) salon-driven grooming practice requiring pain, money, time, effort.
       </p>
       <p>
        As Ive said a million times: I believe women should be allowed to do whatever they want with their bodies, as long as they know what the options are. Magazines like this would have all young women believe there are only a few ways of being in the world.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
