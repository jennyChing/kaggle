        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Homemade Toner  Step2
        </h1>
        <div class="post-info">
         <span class="date published time" title="2013-05-13T10:21:03+00:00">
          May 13, 2013
         </span>
         - By
         <span class="author vcard">
          <span class="fn">
           <a href="http://www.savvyhomemade.com/author/angela/" rel="author">
            Angela

         <span class="post-comments">
          <a href="http://www.savvyhomemade.com/homemade-toner-step2/#respond">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
         <p>
          A good homemade toner will tighten your skin as it closes the pores, and it will remove any leftover traces of your cleanser. It is both cooling and refreshing, and should be the second step in your daily facial routine.
         </p>
         <table align="center" cellpadding="6" cellspacing="6" style="width: 100%;">
          <tbody>
           <tr align="center">
            <td class="ThumbnailLink" valign="top">
             <h4>
              Step1
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.savvyhomemade.com/home-remedies-for-clear-skin.html#Step1">
               <img alt="homemade cleanser" class="aligncenter size-thumbnail wp-image-4613" height="150" src="http://738081880.r.lightningbase-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/clear-skin-cleanser-150x150.jpg" title="Homemade Toner" width="150"/>
               <span class="Caption">
                Homemade Cleanser
               </span>
              </a>
             </p>
            </td>
            <td class="ThumbnailLink" valign="top">
             <h4>
              Step3
             </h4>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.savvyhomemade.com/homemade-moisturizers.html">
               <img alt="homemade moisturizer" class="aligncenter size-thumbnail wp-image-4615" height="150" src="http://738081880.r.lightningbase-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/moisturizer-vitamin-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
               <span class="Caption">

             </p>

         <p>
          Most store bought toners are alcohol based, and unfortunately this can lead to irritation or drying of the skin, especially when it is used on sensitive or already partially dry skin.
         </p>
         <p>
          But worry not; as you can easily make your own skin toner skin toner using just natural ingredients that are kind and gentle to your skin.
         </p>
         <p>
          So have a go at making one of these refreshing homemade spray toners, or try your hand at good old fashioned (yet rejuvenating) flower water!
         </p>
         <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
         <div style="float:none;margin:25px 0 25px 0;text-align:center;">

           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});

         </div>
         <h3>
          All Purpose Spray Toner (suitable for all skin types)
         </h3>
         <p>
          This homemade toner is perfect for either using as a conventional toner after cleansing, or as a quick fix sprizer for a wonderful and refreshing skin boost.
         </p>
         <p>
          Once this spray is made up it needs to be kept in a cool place (preferable in the refrigerator) and used within 1 month.
          <br/>
          <img alt="Homemade Toner" class="aligncenter" height="262" src="http://738081880.r.lightningbase-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/skin-toner-all.jpg" title="Homemade Toner" width="350"/>
         </p>
         <h4>
          What you will need
         </h4>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1 teaspoon (5ml) chopped
           <a href="http://edge.affiliateshop.com/public/AIDLink?AID=100943&amp;Redirect=/bulkherb/l.php#h_l_b" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Lemon Balm
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           3 fl oz (75ml) boiling water
          </li>
          <li>
           2 tablespoons (30ml)
           <a href="http://edge.affiliateshop.com/public/AIDLink?AID=100943&amp;Redirect=/bulkmisc/bulkmisc.php#bu_w_hz" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Witch Hazel
           </a>
          </li>
          <li>
           A pinch of boric acid
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h4>
          Method
         </h4>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Place the lemon balm in a bowl and pour the boiled water over it, cover and leave to cool.
          </li>
          <li>
           Once the lemon balm infusion has cooled, strain the liquid into a fresh bowl.
          </li>
          <li>
           Mix the boric and witch hazel together, then add it to the lemon infusion.
          </li>
          <li>
           Transfer into a sterile glass bottle before storing in the refrigerator.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <div class="CalloutBox">
          <h4>
           Tip
          </h4>
          <p>
           If you would prefer to use a spray toner thats more suited to your skin type, you can replace the lemon balm with one of the following herbs.
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <b>
             Normal skin
            </b>
             Camomile  parsley  dandelion
           </li>
           <li>
            <b>
             Dry skin
            </b>
             Elderflowers  linden flowers  calendula  marshmallow
           </li>
           <li>
            <b>
             Oily skin
            </b>
             Horsetail  sage  yarrow  peppermint
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="CalloutBox">
          Save money and find the best quality essential oils and other organic natural ingredients
          <a href="http://www.savvyhomemade.com/natural-ingredients.html">
           Natural Ingredients  Best Price On The Best Quality
          </a>
         </div>
         <h3>
          Flower Water Homemade Toner
         </h3>
         <p>
          <img alt="Homemade Skin Toner" class="aligncenter" height="264" src="http://738081880.r.lightningbase-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/skin-toner-flower.jpg" title="Homemade Skin Toner" width="350"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Toners were originally the by-products of essential oils distillation, rose and lavender being the most popular.
         </p>
         <p>
          By making an infusion with herbs, and adding a few drops of essential oils you can easily create a refreshing homemade toner to suit your own skin.
         </p>
         <h4>
          What you will need
         </h4>
         <ul>
          <li>
           1 tablespoon (15 ml) fresh herbs or 1teaspoon (5ml) dried herbs (see below)
          </li>
          <li>
           3 fl oz (75ml) boiling water
          </li>
          <li>
           1 drop of
           <a href="http://www.savvyhomemade.com/essential-oil-use-chart.html">
            essential oil
           </a>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h4>
          Method
         </h4>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Make an herbal infusion by placing the herbs into a bowl and pouring the boiling water over them.
          </li>
          <li>
           Allow the infusion to cool, before straining.
          </li>
          <li>
           Add the essential oil and transfer into a sterile bottle.
          </li>
          <li>
           Store in the refrigerator and use within a month.
          </li>
          <li>
           Dampen a cotton ball with the flower water, and gently smooth over the face, giving extra attention to the nose and cheeks area.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <div class="CalloutBox2">
          <div align="center">
           Herbs And Oils For Flower Water Toner
          </div>
          <p>
           The herbs below can all be found at
           <a href="http://edge.affiliateshop.com/public/AIDLink?AID=100943&amp;Redirect=/bulkherb/bulkherb.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Mountain Rose Organic Herbs
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           And the oils from
           <a href="http://edge.affiliateshop.com/public/AIDLink?AID=100943&amp;Redirect=/aroma/ess.php" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Mountain Rose Essential Oils
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Dry skin:
           <br/>
           <b>
            Essential oils
           </b>
            Camomile  neroli  rose  sandalwood
           <br/>
           <b>
