        <p>
         Who doesn't need life to be made a little bit easier? Below are 25 tips to do precisely that. Readers are invited to post their own life hacks below in comments.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1. Take a photo with your phone of where you park your car at the mall.
         </strong>
         That way, when you misplace the ticket you can at least show the nice security guard where your car is.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          2. Take a cell photo of a list of all the medications used by relatives in your care.
         </strong>
         It's handy to have it in case of an emergency or when you take your mother to see a new doctor.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          3. To get rid of your turkey neck in photos, photographers recommend that you stick your neck out and tuck your chin down.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          4. To make your smile look bigger in photos, squint your eyes just a little.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          5. Use nail polish to paint a colored dot on the end of your mom's keys to help her remember which one unlocks what.
         </strong>
         Green for front door; red for back door; etc.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          6. Use a hanging shoe rack to store cleaning supplies inside the pantry door.
         </strong>
         It eliminates having to crawl under the sink to find the ones you use most frequently.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          7. Can't hear the alarm clock? Set the alarm on your smartphone and put it in an empty glass near your bed.
         </strong>
         The sound will be amplified.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          8. When a friend borrows a tool or book, snap a photo of them holding it so you'll remember later where it went.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          9. Mark the side of your water bottle by hours so that you can keep track of what you've had to drink and will stay hydrated.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          10. Take a photo of the inside of your refrigerator to help you remember what you need at the grocery store.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          11. Use the clasps of an old skirt hanger to keep your cords from tangling and being tripped over.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          12. When you downsize your house, consider buying a well-made foldout cot instead of a pullout sofa.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         Your guests will thank you.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          13. Use two-sided tape on area rugs to keep them in place.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          14. Replace your aging parents' area rugs with wall-to-wall carpeting to minimize falls.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <br/>
         <strong>
          15. Use cold cucumbers to reduce eye puffiness.
         </strong>
         <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/10/16/puffy-eyes-undereyes-cucumbers-cures-remedies_n_1964329.html" target="_hplink">
          Cucumbers contain antioxidants that are thought to reduce irritation, while the cooling effect reduces swelling.
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          16. Carry a big scarf with you 24/7.
         </strong>
         It's as good as a shawl in overly air-conditioned restaurants, movies and airplanes and can double for sun protection at beaches and outdoor cafes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          17. Give charitable donations as gifts.
         </strong>
         Most charities notify the honoree only that a donation was made in their name from you and leave the amount out. You can appear to be a bigger spender and do some good in the meantime.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          18. Have a big event and want to look glamorous? Make an appointment at a department store makeup counter for a makeover with their products.
         </strong>
         Sure they'll try to sell you stuff, but buy the least expensive item and tell them you'll think about the rest.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          19. Feet making your shoes a bit odoriferous?
         </strong>
         Try sticking a dry teabag in each shoe overnight to reduce the smell.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          20. Have trust issues with your GPS? Highway sign tip:
         </strong>
         If the exit sign is on the left of the sign, the exit itself will be on the left. If it's on the right, the exit will be on the right.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          21. In a rental car, look at the little icon for the gas pump.
         </strong>
         The direction the gas handle is pointing is the side where you put your gas in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          22. Carry a nightlight with you when you stay in hotels.
         </strong>
         Sleeping in unfamiliar territory and trying to find your way to the bathroom in the dark can be a challenge for anyone.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          23. Attach a small vial of hand sanitizer to the outside of your purse and use it liberally when you are out in public places.
         </strong>
         Escalators, elevator buttons, door handles are all germ territory.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          24. Make your own emergency first aid kit by stuffing into an old prescription bottle: two bandages, a tiny sample of antibiotic ointment and -- if you are caregiving for a heart patient -- some nitroglycerin tablets.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          25. A frozen wet sponge in a Ziploc bag makes for a great icepack that won't leak.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <br/>
        </p>
