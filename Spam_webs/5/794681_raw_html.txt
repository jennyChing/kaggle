                      <p>
                       Ive noticed a foodie trend that I
                       <strong>
                        MUST
                       </strong>
                       share with you!!! One that I am indeed quite fond of. Its called One Pan Baking! In a large nutshell throwing everything onto one pan, tossing it with seasoning and/or sauce and baking it off until finished. Thats it.you are done. Talk about a total weeknight no-brainer! I happened to be browsing through Redbook Magazine and saw some simple &amp; tasty looking dinner finds. The Tofu With Eggplant really caught my mind. Since eggplant wasnt in the kitchen that night, I used carrots instead and additionally added some sliced shallots to the dish. However, my absolute favorite part of this recipe is the peanut butter sauce..are you surprised? Just a drizzle of this creamy deliciousness with the crunchy peanuts was the PERFECT final touch!
                      </p>

                       <em>
                        Adapted from Redbook Magazine January 2014
                       </em>
                      </p>

                        Ingredients

                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1-16 oz block firm light tofu completely drained, patted with all excess liquid removed. (Wrap snuggly for 20-30 minutes in a paper towel prior to slicing.)
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       2 tablespoons olive oil
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1 shallot sliced thinly
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1 carrot peeled and julienne
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1 pound string beans cleaned &amp; trimmed
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1/4 cup smooth reduced-fat peanut butter
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1 teaspoon honey
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       2 tablespoon reduced sodium soy sauce
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1 tablespoon sesame oil
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       2 tablespoon rice wine vinegar
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       1/4 cup crushed roasted peanuts for garnish
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        Directions
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <div>
                       1.) Line a medium-sided baking pan or sheet with sprayed aluminum foil and preheat oven to 350 degrees.
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       2.) In a small bowl whisk together peanut butter, honey, soy sauce, sesame oil and rice wine vinegar.
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_8680" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3660" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/IMG_8680-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       3.) Slice tofu into cubes. (Using a sharp knife, slice width first into four and then each piece lengthwise into four again and dice into 1-inch cubes)
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       4.) Place all veggies onto the baking sheet and toss with the olive oil. Evenly spread and add tofu. Bake for about 20-25 minutes until just brown.
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_2312" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5840" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_2312-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       5.) When finished remove from oven and drizzle the peanut sauce all over the top and toss.
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_2317" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5842" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_2317-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                       6.) Garnish with crushed peanuts and serve.
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <img alt="IMG_2323" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-5843" height="200" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/IMG_2323-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </div>
                      <div>
                      </div>
