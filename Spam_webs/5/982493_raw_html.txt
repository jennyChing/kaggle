           <p>
            Sodastream International Ltd (
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/company/soda/" target="_blank">
             SODA
            </a>
            ) buyout rumors just keep on coming, and fizzing, over and over again. This time around, the source is The Independent, which has reported there is a probability that SABMiller plc (
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/company/sbmry/" target="_blank">
             SBMRY
            </a>
            ) and Diageo might be interested in the company. The rumor comes at the heels of another news a couple of days back, according to which an
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/25695-sodastream-international-ltd-up-5-on-buyout-rumors/" target="_blank">
             unidentified British private equity fund was interested
            </a>
            in the company. The British fund was reportedly looking to buy the carbonated soda machine manufacturer for $840 million, or $40 per share. However, according to rumors, SABmiller may seal the deal at $46, which would represent a premium of more than 40% over Sodastreams current stock price.
           </p>
           <p>
            As this article is written, Sodastream has already started to pare gains. This is again, not a very unusual sight. Investors have grown wary of rumors of buyout interest in the company time and again, which eventually amount to nothing. Sodastream stock jumped 5% just this Tuesday over buyout rumors but gave back most of its gains before trading seized that day.
           </p>
           <p>
            Just last year, beverage behemoths like PepsiCo, Inc. (
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/company/pep/" target="_blank">
             PEP
            </a>
            ) and The Coca-Cola Company (
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/company/ko/" target="_blank">
             KO
            </a>
            ) were reportedly willing to buy stake in Sodastream for more than $90 per share. Pepsi denied the rumors and Coca-Cola went for another company; Keurig Green Mountain Inc (
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/company/gmcr" target="_blank">
             GMCR
            </a>
            ). Sodastream stock never really recovered from that point onward; after making a multi-year high of $77.8 in June last year, it went on to lose
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/22495-sodastream-stumbles-to-new-52week-low/" target="_blank">
             more than 60%
            </a>
            of its market cap. But the
            <a href="http://www.bidnessetc.com/23232-sodastream-fizzes-on-leveraged-buyout-rumors/" target="_blank">
             rumors kept on coming
            </a>
