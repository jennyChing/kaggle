      <p>
       <span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;">
        <table border="0" class="image right" width="200">
         <tbody>
          <tr>
           <td>
            <a href="http://www.truecrimereport.com/Cyberstalking.jpg" onclick="window.open('http://www.truecrimereport.com/Cyberstalking.jpg','popup','width=300,height=450,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0'); return false">
             <img alt="Cyberstalking.jpg" height="300" src="http://www.truecrimereport.com/assets_c/2009/12/Cyberstalking-thumb-200x300.jpg" width="200"/>
            </a>
           </td>
          </tr>
          <tr>
           <td class="caption">
            It's one of those cases where you want both sides declared losers
           </td>
          </tr>
         </tbody>
        </table>
        
       </span>
       The internet, as we all know, has a way of bringing out people's inner ferocity when they get to unleash it from the safety of their own computer. So the following beef out of North Carolina shouldn't come as a surprise.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Jeff Martin, who writes the
       <a href="http://fecundstench.com/WordPress/">
        Vie Di Malchance
       </a>
       blog -- that would be "life of misfortune" for those not fluent in French -- was growing increasingly pissed at Dr. Mary Johnson, who authors the
       <a href="http://drjshousecalls.blogspot.com/">
        Dr. J's HouseCalls
       </a>
       blog. Over the past few years, he claims the pediatrician has left 400 comments on his site, many not particularly complimentary of Martin's views, it's safe to assume.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Their duel grew more heated lately in a beef over a dam project. So Martin went batshit, choosing to send the good doctor 27 harassing emails --- in one hour...
       <br/>
      </p>
      <a name="more">
      </a>
      <p>
       Among his better lines: "Everyone knows your crazy, but no one has exhibited the courage to deal with it. I'm not afraid of you." He also admitted telling her he knows where she lives.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       How courage relates to threatening a woman over email, we're not sure. But Martin, a man clearly in possession of a romantic self-image, sees himself as the victim here. He's launched "The Justice League" -- no, we're not making this up -- to raise money for his defense fund. For a $25 donation, "members of The Justice League will receive the password to the
       <em>
        27 e-mails
       </em>
       ,
       <em>
        The Real Story
       </em>
       and any new intel I may receive."
       <br/>
       <br/>
       He also comically sees his fight in World War II proportions:  "Would Rommel
       <strong>
       </strong>
       have been chased from North Africa if Montgomery
       <strong>
       </strong>
       hadn't taken the battle to him?"
       <br/>
       <br/>
       Johnson, meanwhile, has chosen the traditional route of defending herself with barely cogent references to victimhood, and blaming the media for misrepresenting her side of the beef.
       <br/>
       <br/>
       In the end, it seems like one of those fights where you just want the judges to declare both combatants losers.
