         How to Make Homemade Soap  The Easy Way!

        <p>
         Christmas time is here! Its one of my favorite times of the year. I love the true meaning of Christmas  JESUS- and the whole spirit of those around me. Its such a magical time. Whats I dont really care for is stressing over what to get someone and finding that perfect gift. This year were going
         <a href="http://allthingsmamma.com/2013/12/give-a-life-saving-gift-this-holiday-season-with-heifer-international/" target="_blank">
          back to basics
         </a>
         and giving simple homemade gifts. What can show you really care and put love into picking out a gift like making it yourself?!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/homemadesoap1.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13185" height="533" src="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/homemadesoap1.jpg" title="How to Make Homemade Soap - The Easy Way!" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Today I want to share my simple semi-homemade recipe for making soap! Yes, soap! Forget the lye and gloves and fumes, were making soap the easy way by buying a readymade soap base and adding ingredients to it to make it special. (and affordable!)
        </p>
        <p>
         Making soap couldnt be easier and it really doesnt take much time at all. First you need to buy a quality soap base. I bought a 5lb block of a shea butter base. It doesnt have additives or extra chemicals that arent needed, so it should be allergy friendly to most people.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/soap11.jpg">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13187" height="518" src="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/soap11.jpg" title="How to Make Homemade Soap - The Easy Way!" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         The first step is to cut off a bunch of 2 inch chunks and put them in a microwave safe bowl. Heat on medium high for only 20 secs at a time and stir each time until melted. Thats it!
        </p>
        <p>
         Once its melted, let it sit out on the counter until its thickened up a bit if you want to add in ingredients. That way they dont all just sink to the bottom of the bowl and soap mold youll be pouring the soap into.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/soap2.jpg">
          <img alt="How to Make Homemade Soap - The Easy Way!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13183" height="533" src="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/soap2.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I added coconut oil  because everything is better with coconut oil, vegetable glycerin  because coconut oil cuts down on the lather in the soap and I like a good lather and vegetable glycerin provides that, vitamin E oil as a preservative and for its moisturizing properties,
         <a href="http://allthingsmamma.com/2013/08/how-to-use-essential-oils/" target="_blank">
          essential oils
         </a>
         for smell and healing properties and ground up oatmeal for an added scrub benefit. (just give your oatmeal a whirl in the blender to grind it up a bit  it will mix into the soap easier)
        </p>
        <p>
         You can add all these things, just some or none at all! Its really up to you to personalize the way YOU want to!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/soap3.jpg">
          <img alt="How to Make Homemade Soap - The Easy Way!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13182" height="533" src="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/soap3.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         So, once the soap has cooled and thickened a bit, you mix in your add-ins and pour into a mold of your choice. It can be as simple as a bread pan lined with parchment paper or as fancy as a silicone mold. I chose to go with an aluminum cake bar pan from Wilton and it worked great! I greased my pan with a little coconut oil before putting the soap in, just to make sure it didnt stick. I didnt have any trouble getting it out though!
        </p>
        <p>
         Once the soap has hardened for about 45 minutes, you can pop your soap right out by turning over you mold and gently tapping on the bottom. If it doesnt come out easily, stick the whole pan in the freezer for about 5-8 minutes and it will shrink up a bit and come right out!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/homemadesoap2.jpg">
          <img alt="How to Make Homemade Soap - The Easy Way!" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13179" height="533" src="http://allthingsmamma.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/homemadesoap2.jpg" width="800"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Thats it! See how easy it was?! Now youre ready to package it up in a pretty cellophane bag, mini tins, or wrap it with pretty festive paper right around it!
        </p>
        <p>
         Homemade soap is sure to be a welcomed and loved gift by anyone you chose to give it to!
        </p>
        <p>
         Note: Check out my Amazon widget in the sidebar  theres lots of items there for you to make this soap with! (affiliate link, also!)
        </p>
