&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;From my first day teaching to mothering in Nicaragua, living my best life means embracing &lt;a href="http://wp.me/p30hEa-oV"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;growth&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/a&gt; in all it&#8217;s subtle forms.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;I first felt like a grown-up&#8230;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;em&gt;when I started teaching, back in early 1991.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://jenniferwolfe.net/?attachment_id=2755" rel="attachment wp-att-2755"&gt;&lt;img class="size-medium wp-image-2755 aligncenter" alt="Jen first day teaching" src="/~jenni131/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Jen-first-day-teaching1-300x400.jpeg" width="300" height="400" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;College wasn&#8217;t easy for me, and I spent a lot of time trying to figure out who I was and where I was going. Ultimately, I found myself in a credential program teaching 7th grade. To my great surprise, I loved the kooky age group, and when I was offered a job teaching 7th grade English at a brand new school, I was thrilled. I remember my first day so clearly-what I wore (purple, because it was the school colors &#8211; ugh!), my nervousness, the over-planning, the exhaustion, the adrenaline, the absolute weirdness of being called &#8220;Miss Mason&#8221; by groups of kids not much smaller than me! Over the 22 years I&#8217;ve been teaching, I&#8217;ve experienced tremendous growth, but still get that first-day-of-school tingle when I know I&#8217;m meeting kids for the first time!&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;If I could make anything grow on trees, it would be&#8230;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;strong&gt; &lt;/strong&gt;time.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://jenniferwolfe.net/?attachment_id=2753" rel="attachment wp-att-2753"&gt;&lt;img class="size-medium wp-image-2753 aligncenter" alt="Cameron age 2" src="/~jenni131/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Cameron-500x376.jpg" width="500" height="376" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;I&#8217;d like to be able to pick a few minutes or hours or days off branches right outside my study window. I&#8217;d love to be able to go back to those lazy summer days of my childhood, when it seemed like all I had was time and couldn&#8217;t wait to go back to school to fill up my days. I&#8217;d love to stop time and buy those minutes back from my babies&#8217; childhoods, those times when they were screaming and fussing and I didn&#8217;t know what to do, just that I wanted it to stop. Their growth as humans measures my time as their mother; now that they&#8217;re plugged in and heading into their lives independent of me, I&#8217;d love to have more time to be with them before they head off into the world without me.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;My last growing pain was&#8230;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;em&gt; this summer, when I had to stretch outside my proverbial box and trust that everything would be OK.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;img class="size-medium wp-image-2751 aligncenter" alt="Nicaragua Lily and Cameron" src="/~jenni131/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/13-7-24-Nicaragua-Casas-Viejas-2-002-50-300x400.jpg" width="300" height="400" /&gt;In Nicaragua, I had to trust that my kids were strong, smart, and &lt;a href="http://wp.me/p30hEa-F"&gt;capable of traveling in a foreign country&lt;/a&gt; without me hovering all the time. I needed to let myself grow into trust, knowing that all would be well, and that they were learning valuable lessons right along with me.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;I use my &#8220;green thumb&#8221; to&#8230;&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;em&gt;bring beauty into the world.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;img class="size-medium wp-image-2750 aligncenter" alt="garden" src="/~jenni131/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/13-8-Jens-iPhone-014-500x373.jpg" width="500" height="373" /&gt;I can&#8217;t remember a time when I didn&#8217;t have a garden-first it was at my parent&#8217;s one-acre lot, where my dad would plant rows and rows of vegetables. I remember spending so many summer days happily moving hoses and scampering around in the dirt. My grandma Flossie was the consummate gardener &#8211; so much so that she carried around pruning shears in her purse to take slips of plants that she found on her daily walks. When I finally purchased my first house, the first thing we did was plant and landscape a blank plot of dirt into a beautiful Monet-inspired garden, complete with a brick patio and retaining wall built by hand. Today, as I write, I look out my second-story window and see hummingbirds feasting on Buddleia bushes and Stargazer lilies, old-fashioned roses blooming alongside gorgeous, droopy lavender Russian sage. My &lt;a href="http://wp.me/p30hEa-15"&gt;morning blooms&lt;/a&gt; make me happy, and digging in the dirt centers my soul.&lt;/p&gt;
