     <p>
      "
      <b>
       Thank You (Falettinme Be Mice Elf Agin)
      </b>
      " is a 1969 song recorded by
      <a href="/wiki/Sly_and_the_Family_Stone" title="Sly and the Family Stone">
       Sly and the Family Stone
      </a>
      . The song, released as a
      <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side#Double_A-side" title="A-side and B-side">
       double A-side
      </a>
      single with "
      <a href="/wiki/Everybody_Is_a_Star" title="Everybody Is a Star">
       Everybody Is a Star
      </a>
      ", reached number one on the soul single charts for five weeks, and reached number one on the
      <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">
       <i>
        Billboard
       </i>
       Hot 100
      </a>
      in February 1970.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <i>
       Billboard
      </i>
      ranked the record as the No. 19 song of 1970.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      "Thank You" was intended to be included on an in-progress album with "Star" and "
      <a href="/wiki/Hot_Fun_in_the_Summertime" title="Hot Fun in the Summertime">
       Hot Fun in the Summertime
      </a>
      "; the LP was never completed, and the three tracks were instead included on the band's 1970
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Sly_and_the_Family_Stone_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Sly and the Family Stone album)">
        Greatest Hits
       </a>
      </i>
      LP. "Thank You" and "Star", the final Family Stone recordings issued in the 1960s, marked the beginning of a 20-month gap of releases from the band, which would finally end with the release of "
      <a href="/wiki/Family_Affair_(Sly_and_the_Family_Stone_song)" title="Family Affair (Sly and the Family Stone song)">
       Family Affair
      </a>
      " in 1971.
     </p>
     <p>
      The song's length on the original hit single and the
      <i>
       Greatest Hits
      </i>
      LP is 4:48 and was
      <a href="/wiki/Duophonic" title="Duophonic">
       re-channeled to simulate stereo
      </a>
      on the popular
      <i>
       Greatest Hits
      </i>
      LP. The previously unreleased full-length version (6:18) was mixed by Bob Irwin in true stereo and its only issue was on a 1990 Columbia promotional CD
      <i>
       Legacy: Music for the Next Generation
      </i>
      . On the subsequent (and currently available as of 2013)
      <i>
       The Essential Sly &amp; The Family Stone
      </i>
      2-CD set, the track is in stereo but is the standard 4:48 length hit version.
     </p>
     <p>
      The song was ranked number 402 on
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">
        Rolling Stone
       </a>
      </i>
      magazine's "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/500_Greatest_Songs_of_All_Time" title="500 Greatest Songs of All Time">
       500 Greatest Songs of All Time
      </a>
      ".
     </p>

     </p>

      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
       Overview
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thank_You_(Falettinme_Be_Mice_Elf_Agin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Overview">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The title is an intentional
      <a href="/wiki/Mondegreen" title="Mondegreen">
       mondegreen
      </a>
      for "thank you for letting me be myself again." The third verse contains specific references to the group's previous hit songs, "
      <a href="/wiki/Dance_to_the_Music_(song)" title="Dance to the Music (song)">
       Dance to the Music
      </a>
      ", "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Everyday_People_(Sly_and_the_Family_Stone_song)" title="Everyday People (Sly and the Family Stone song)">
       Everyday People
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/Sing_a_Simple_Song" title="Sing a Simple Song">
       Sing a Simple Song
      </a>
      ", and "You Can Make It If You Try". The song features co-lead vocals from
      <a href="/wiki/Sly_Stone" title="Sly Stone">
       Sly Stone
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Rose_Stone" title="Rose Stone">
       Rose Stone
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Stone" title="Freddie Stone">
       Freddie Stone
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Larry_Graham" title="Larry Graham">
       Larry Graham
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_versions">
       Cover versions
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thank_You_(Falettinme_Be_Mice_Elf_Agin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cover versions">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Sly and the Family Stone revisited "Thank You (Falettinme Be Mice Elf Agin)" in a slower pace as "Thank You for Talkin' to Me, Africa", the closing track of 1971's
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/There%27s_a_Riot_Goin%27_On" title="There's a Riot Goin' On">
        There's a Riot Goin' On
       </a>
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">
       Magazine
      </a>
      covered the song in 1980 on the album
      <i>
       The Correct Use of Soap
      </i>
      . The same recording was issued as the A-side of a single, and a live version was included on the 1981 album
      <i>
       Play
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Soundgarden" title="Soundgarden">
       Soundgarden
      </a>
      performed this song numerous times on their 1989 tour as well as during their
      <a href="/wiki/John_Peel" title="John Peel">
       John Peel
      </a>
      session that year.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Dave_Matthews_Band" title="Dave Matthews Band">
       Dave Matthews Band
      </a>
      performed the song numerous times on their 2008 summer tour. It was often featured as the final song of the encore.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">
       In other media
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thank_You_(Falettinme_Be_Mice_Elf_Agin)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In other media">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The song was used as the outro for the 2007 animated film
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Shrek_the_Third" title="Shrek the Third">
        Shrek the Third
       </a>
      </i>
      , with vocals performed by the characters of Donkey (
      <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Murphy" title="Eddie Murphy">
       Eddie Murphy
      </a>
      ) and Puss in Boots (
      <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Banderas" title="Antonio Banderas">
       Antonio Banderas
      </a>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Janet_Jackson" title="Janet Jackson">
       Janet Jackson
      </a>
      's 1989 song "
      <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_Nation" title="Rhythm Nation">
       Rhythm Nation
      </a>
      " samples the groove from the song in between verses.
     </p>
