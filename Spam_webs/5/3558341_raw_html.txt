     <h1 class="title">
      <a href="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/blog/cal-stomps-huskies-tied-for-pac-12-lead/" title="Cal Stomps Huskies, Tied for Pac-12 Lead">
       Cal Stomps Huskies, Tied for Pac-12 Lead
      </a>
     </h1>
     <div class="post_body">
      <div class="entry">
       <div class="post_meta">
        <div class="post_author">
         <img src="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/themes/netscouts/images/common/icon_author.png"/>
         Carl Berman
        </div>
        <div class="post_date">
         <img src="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/themes/netscouts/images/common/icon_date.png"/>
         15 Jan 2014
        </div>
        <div class="post_category">
         <img src="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/themes/netscouts/images/common/icon_category.png"/>
         <a href="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/category/blog/" rel="category tag">
          Blog
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="post_comments">
         <a href="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/blog/cal-stomps-huskies-tied-for-pac-12-lead/#respond">
          <img src="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/themes/netscouts/images/common/icon_comments.jpg"/>
          0 comments
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <a href="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CalWash1.jpg">
         <img alt="CalWash1" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-6262" height="224" src="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CalWash1-300x224.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <i>
         Berkeley, CA
        </i>
         This is our third view of the
        <b>
         California Golden Bears
        </b>
        in the last week. We came away very impressed after Cal won two hard fought games last Thursday and Saturday at Oregon and Oregon State.
        <b>
         Justin Cobbs
        </b>
        came up clutch in both games and was named the
        <b>
         NetScouts Basketball National Player of the Week.
        </b>
        It was even more impressive that the Golden Bears won both games with their star freshman
        <b>
         , Jabari Bird,
        </b>
        out with an ankle injury.
       </p>
       <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_6263" style="width: 261px">
        <a href="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CalJustinCobbs.jpg">
         <img alt="Justin Cobbs" class="size-medium wp-image-6263" height="300" src="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CalJustinCobbs-251x300.jpg" width="251"/>
        </a>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Justin Cobbs
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Bird returned to action in limited duty (two points, five assists) tonight as Cal faced a resurgent
        <b>
         Washington Huskies
        </b>
        squad. The Huskies, coming off some mediocre play in their non-conference slate, including a loss at home to Cal-Irvine, are starting to play much better. Washington comes into the game off a home sweep of Utah and Colorado. In the Colorado game,
        <b>
         C.J. Wilcox
        </b>
        took advantage of the unfortunate injury to
        <b>
         Spencer Dinwiddie
        </b>
        to score 31 points. One of the main factors of the game would be whether Cal could make it difficult for Wilcox to score.
       </p>
       <p>
        Washington got off to an 8-2 lead but then Cal took control with a 28-6 run to take a 30-14 lead. They took a 13-point lead (30-17) into the halftime break, holding
        <b>
         Wilcox
        </b>
        to six first half points with zero made three-pointers. Cal did not let the Huskies get into the game in the second half. With
        <b>
         Richard Solomon
        </b>
        (13 points, 12 rebounds) and
        <b>
         David Kravish
        </b>
        (11 points, 11 rebounds, four blocks) dominating the glass, and getting another strong performance off the bench by
        <b>
         Jeff Powers
        </b>
        (12 points, 3-4 3PT) California ran away with the 82-56 win to go to 4-0 in the Pac-12.
        <b>
         Cobbs
        </b>
        (11 points, six assists) didnt need to score much but he played great defense against Wilcox.
        <b>
         Wilcox
        </b>
        finished with 18 points but most of his points came after the game was decided.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CalWash2.jpg">
         <img alt="CalWash2" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-6264" height="206" src="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/CalWash2-300x206.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
        <b>
         Richard Solomon
        </b>
        has come into his own this year. The 610 senior center is one of two players from a major conference (the other being Kentuckys
        <b>
         Julius Randle
        </b>
        ) to be averaging a double-double (12.1 ppg, 10.2 rpg). Solomon is very active and runs the floor well. Hes decent stepping out to 15-18 feet but does much of his damage in the paint. Hes a good shot-blocker (1.4 pg) but uses his length to disrupt other inside shots as well as deflect passes and generate steals (1.2 pg).
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://netscoutsbasketball.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/Calwashhuddle.jpg">

        </a>
        Combined with
        <strong>
         Kravish
        </strong>
        , Cal has two strong inside players in a conference where most teams (with the exception of Arizona) might have only one. Combined with the heady point guard play of
        <b>

        </b>
        , the energy of
        <b>
         Tyrone Wallace
        </b>
        and the emerging
        <b>
         Bird, Cal
        </b>
        is the surprise team of the
        <b>
         Pac-12
        </b>
        and a solid NCAA Tournament team.
