        <p>
         <strong>
          Top Time and Money saving HOT Tips and Ideas
         </strong>
         that YOU can use to maintain and protect your home right away, even if you are a DIY novice, well show you how!
        </p>
        <p>
         All info sourced from the public domain and hand edited by a surveyor, this article is sure to impress, AND
         <strong>
          save you money.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         You are cordially invited to browse through this, our very special collection of really
         <strong>
          great ideas, tips and unique products
         </strong>
         all to do with painting and maintaining your home the easy and cost effective way.
        </p>
        <p>
         You will find loads of massive time and labour saving snippets of
         <strong>
          information
         </strong>
         that will save you a serious amount of precious time, money and loads of un-necessary hard work!
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          If you have any comments or questions about this page,
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           please DONT email us,
          </span>
          post a comment at the foot of the page. thanks
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Unlike other websites similar in nature to ours, we arent selling you anything, well, not on this page at least! If you have a read through, or maybe print it off for later reference (there is a print button at the foot of the article), whatever has brought you to this page, I guarantee if you READ it through, youre gonna learn something!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          First up, we look at one of our top topics: painting
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Top DIY and painting tips.
        </h2>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Tip: How to keep your paint roller tray perfectly clean while painting
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Before you load your paint roller tray, wrap a plastic bag all around and over it, so that when youre finished painting or even changing colour, all you have to do is throw away the bag (safely) and your tray is spotless!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          In fact looking at my plastic bags from going to our local co-op supermarket yesterday, they have some writing down the side explaining exactly what uses the discarded shopping bag can be used for and using it to cover your paint roller tray is one of them so I guess they read this article too LOL!
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: The Easy Way to freshen up those glossed or varnished doors &amp; skirting boards
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Normally, to paint over previously glossed or varnished surfaces such as doors, architraves, skirtings etc, you would need to
          <strong>
           sand the surface first
          </strong>
          , then undercoat and finally top coat. Three tedious &amp; off-putting operations. Heres the best trick yet for a
          <strong>
           quick lick of paint
          </strong>
          or varnish to brighten your place up a bit.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <script type="text/javascript">
          // &lt; ![CDATA[
    google_ad_client = "ca-pub-1493207334839549";
    google_ad_slot = "7662837315";
    google_ad_width = 468;
    google_ad_height = 60;
// ]]&gt;
         </script>
         <br/>
         <!-- small banner one advert -->
         <br/>
         <script src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
          // &lt; ![CDATA[&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;// ]]&gt;
         </script>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          All you need to do is clean the wood with some
          <strong>
           warm water and sugar soap,
          </strong>
          and allow to dry.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Apply ESP, using a clean, lint-free cloth and leave it to cure for about two hours (at normal room temp). All you need to do then is apply your topcoat directly to the surface and the jobs done.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          For best results, if you are using oil based gloss, satinwood, varnish etc, add in some
          <strong>
           Owatrol oil
          </strong>
          as it makes it flow out nicer and gives a finish your neighbours will hate you for! If you choose to apply water based gloss etc, consider adding in some Floetrol paint conditioner for a similar result.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          With this method you could totally repaint or varnish a few doors all inside one day, and at a very cheap price.
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_31912" style="width: 440px">
         <img alt="floetrol-paint-conditioner" class="size-full wp-image-31912" height="473" src="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/floetrol-paint-conditioner.jpg" width="430"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Unfortunately I dont get any commission for recommending this (LOL!) but its good stuff.
         </p>
        </div>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: How to prevent your paint peeling
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Insufficient key or grip, is usually the cause of
          <strong>
           paint peeling or flaking
          </strong>
          .
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          This can manifest itself easily on most walls, and example of flaking masonry paint

         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Paint too thick to brush or roll.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Paints in general terms, are manufactured to work at their best in given temperature ranges, &amp; when temperature drops or rises, paint can become more difficult to brush or roll.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Solution  Emulsions can be made more workable by adding in about 10% or so paint conditioner like Floetrol. All oil-based paints can be loosened up by adding about 5% to 10% (15%) oil based conditioner like Owatrol Oil. Unlike water or white spirit thinning, the above mentioned additives, actually increase paint hiding power and coverage, and so do not damage the inherent good qualities of the paint.
         </span>

          TIP: Paint Skinning.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          A skin will always form on paint once the tin has been opened and the lid left off.
         </span>
        </p>

         <span style="color: #000000;">

          <strong>
           white spirit
          </strong>
          on the paint, just enough to cover the surface. This will prevent a skin forming for up to a week. Other people suggest storing the tin upside down, but unless you have sealed the lid tighter than the factory, I personally would not entertain that!

        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Sand down the area with some 320 Grit wet &amp; dry paper saturated with some D1 &amp; wipe off any residues. Then wipe off with a cloth saturated with D1. After that just apply a coat or two of D2 for a perfect finish. This system is the preferred choice of boat &amp; yacht owners Worldwide.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          <em>
           Tip: To remove dents in wood, especially from a hammer, cover with a wet cloth and hold a warm iron on the dent. Its remarkable how the dent disappears when the steam swells the fibres.
          </em>
         </span>
        </h3>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Need a lovely, easy-to-apply, clear coat on exterior wood?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Apply two coats of TEXTROL, wet on wet end of story. Textrol is a clear (but tintable) deep penetrating, anti UV, matte finish, which will not peel or flake. Shows up the grain beautifully. After a year or two, just apply one coat to keep the wood topped up
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Used extensively on decks, this product has a multitude of uses, such as wooden garden furniture etc. Will not flake or peel. Available in a few tones also.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Lovely Rustic look for Interior Wood.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          A beautiful rustic look can be achieved on indoor wood with a coat or two of Owatrol Oil used neat. Apply by brush or roller, wet on wet. Leave about 40 minutes between coats and wipe off excess to avoid shiny spots. Stain it if you wish to darken or whatever. A great idea, yet so simple.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Cement / plaster burn marks on wooden windows / sills.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Caustic Soda, acts as a whitener on cement burn marks. Mix the caustic soda with water as per instructions and apply to the wood. The caustic soda will bring them all back to one colour. Wash off. You can then proceed to stain or varnish as normal.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Note: Follow the usual safety precautions when using caustic soda.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Painting a lot of woodwork.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Perhaps you should take a look at the TURBINAIRE HVLP Sprayers. These wonder tools are capable of applying most types of paint to the highest possible finish. After a bit of practice, you can spray finish panel doors, frames, skirting, stairs, spindles in a fraction of the time it takes to do with brush or roller. Also great for applying Fleck type paints. (For pros or serious diyers only)
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Rust
         </span>
        </h3>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: 5 Steps to the Proper treatment of badly rusted steel or Iron.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="rusting cars in a field" class="aligncenter wp-image-24615 size-full" height="375" src="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/rusting-cars.jpg" title="rusty old car" width="500"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          You wont beat OWATROL Oil on rust FACT! It is a very simple, yet powerful treatment that lasts years longer than any other treatment. Heres how you do it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           No 1. Paint the entire surface including rust lumps and seemingly rust-free areas.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           No 2. After an hour or so, get a scraper and de-scale (the loosened rust lumps) with minimal effort. You dont need to hammer or wire brush the steel. Brush off loose dust &amp; rust particles with a dry paint brush or similar.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           No 3. Top up any hungry areas with another drop or two of Owatrol Oil and leave for 24 hours approx. to dry to a tough but flexible finish.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           No 4. Apply a coat of oil based metal primer with about 25% Owatrol Oil mixed in and apply.
          </span>
         </li>
         <li style="text-align: justify;">
          <span style="color: #000000;">
           No 5. Apply top coat of oil based paint / whatever colour you like, with about 10% or so OWATROL Oil added in to help flow.
          </span>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <script type="text/javascript">
          // &lt; ![CDATA[
    google_ad_client = "ca-pub-1493207334839549";
    google_ad_slot = "7662837315";
    google_ad_width = 468;
    google_ad_height = 60;
// ]]&gt;
         </script>
         <br/>
         <!-- small banner one advert -->
         <br/>
         <script src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/show_ads.js" type="text/javascript">
          // &lt; ![CDATA[&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;// ]]&gt;
         </script>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Painting outside steel fire escapes.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          If steel has severe rust, prepare with Owatrol Oil first. After that apply 2 coats of OWATROL ALUMINIUM RA85. This is a highly pigmented, tight-gripping, yet FLEXIBLE paint which will give years of service. If surface only has tight rust, apply 2 coats of Owatrol Aluminium RA85 direct. Or see other Rust option above.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Rusty Nail Heads.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          If you ever come across rusted nail heads in facias etc all you have to do is, get a clean old paint brush, dip it in Owatrol Oil and dab it on to the nail head liberally. This will seal up the nail fully, and fix the problem for years. Paint over with paint (oil) containing Owatrol Oil.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Painting rusted wire mesh, chain link fencing.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          This is a very easy and useful tip, which can save the day in many situations. Because of the awkward shape of this type of material, it was always a problem to treat or maintain. All you have to do is get some OWATROL ALUMINIUM RA85, a reasonably longhaired paint roller and apply.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Dont be too surprised if the Owatrol Aluminium crawls or creeps right around the rear of the fence because it gets everywhere. It is truly a great product for all this type of work. It is also very flexible when cured, which allows it to move with the fence without cracking etc.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Rust stains on concrete.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Next time you have to remove ugly rust stains from a concrete wall or stone, use NET-TROL, an environmentally friendly, oxalic based cleaner. Ideal, where you have aerial brackets, balustrades, nails etc. Wet surface completely, apply NET-TROL (dilute up to 4:1) Leave for Up to half an hour and rinse, or power wash off end of story.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Bringing Fibreglass back to life.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Dull faded Fibreglass need not present a problem any more! Clean first with NET-TROL and when that dries apply Polytrol or Owatrol Oil and buff to a shine. You will be astonished with the result!
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Wallpaper
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          If your rolls of
          <a href="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/blog/exterior-wallpaper/" target="_blank" title="Ever heard of exterior wallpaper? No? then check this out!">
           wallpaper
          </a>
          dont have exactly the same batch code number on them, then there may possibly be slight differences in shade, as they will have been printed at different times. Buy your paper all from the same batch if at all possible. No harm to have a roll or two left over in case you need to do a repair.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: How you can remove Mildew and Mould.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Remove mildew with a 50:50 mix of bleach and warm water.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <img alt="mould on a wall can be cured by never paint again" class="aligncenter wp-image-20356 size-full" height="600" src="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/mould-on-a-wall.jpg" title="Mould on a wall" width="450"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Mildew needs moisture to survive, so identify
          <a href="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/blog/waterproofing-and-weatherproofing-your-house/" target="_blank" title="Read more about damp and mould here">
           and fix the moisture problem
          </a>
          before redecorating. A common cause of mildew is insufficient ventilation, bad insulation and or lack of heating.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          To prevent the mildew or mould coming back, just add a fungicidal paint additive like VC175 Mould Stop to subsequent paint coats to help prevent a return of the problem. Also very good on outdoor walls near trees, bushes etc.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         NOTE: Some people suggest that to cure mildew inside, water should NOT be used, nor should household bleach, which actually contains water! If in doubt, use a special mould and mildew remover, of which I would recommend the ASTONISH range of products, which are also really cheap.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Wood pigment stains.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Some wood extracts, usually brown in colour, bleed through some paint finishes. Wash with soapy water, prime with a primer sealer like Aqualock and re-coat.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Filler sanding. If you apply too much filler, it makes your job harder.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          After applying filler, smooth it off with a damp sponge or wet brush to remove the sharp edges. Some powder fillers (Polyfilla for example) and most ready-mixed fillers dry very hard so apply as smoothly as possible and save yourself the grief.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Greasy walls and ceilings.
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Painting kitchen areas can often be tricky due to all the greasy deposits all over the place. Paint as we all know will not stick to that, and one good solution is to wash thoroughly with sugar soap &amp; warm water and allow to dry prior to any painting.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Strippers or blowtorch?! Whats the best way to remove paint?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          A blowtorch is cheaper and very effective but it can be dangerous and it is not suitable in many cases. A hot air gun is useful for small jobs where time is not important. Intricate detailed woodwork is best coated with a liquid or semi-paste chemical as burning can cause damage to fine detail. If wood gets a burn stain from a stripper, you can usually restore the natural colour very easily with Net-Trol Cleaner and Restorer.
         </span>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          If you want to know even more about methods of paint removal, take a look at
          <a href="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/blog/npas-removal-tips-for-paint-and-wall-coatings/" target="_blank" title="Learn all about how to safely remove paint">
           our dedicated paint removal page here.
          </a>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          TIP: Is that wet wall damp or is it Condensation?
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Not sure if your wall is letting in
          <a href="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/npa-clearshield-2-cure-penetrating-damp/" target="_blank" title="The product on this link cures damp">
           damp or if its condensation?
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Cut out about 20 square inches of household cooking silver foil and tape it to the wall with good masking tape or duct tape. If its damp coming in, the inside of the foil will gather drops of water after a time.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          If condensation it will remain dry on the inside. A good product to stop moisture or water coming through your wall is THE NPA EXTERIOR WALL SYSTEM. This amazing process is applied by NPAs own expert teams, after doing all necessary repairs to your home.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         This differs from a clear coating system as it is a very thick, weatherproof and durable coloured wall covering that gets sprayed on to the outside walls of your house with a specialist machine.
        </p>

         <img alt="bridlington house after wall coatings" class="wp-image-31792 " height="283" src="http://www.neverpaintagain.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/bridlington-house-after-wall-coatings.jpg" width="501"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Phone us for a house painting quote on 0800 970 4928
         </p>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Not only that, but the walls are fully repaired and brought back to life by the expert teams at NPA. For a price far less than you think, you can the exterior of your house sorted out, well and truly, PLUS a great, brand new exterior finish, AND nothing needs to be done to it at all for up to 25 years, AND that comes with an insurance backed guarantee!
        </p>
