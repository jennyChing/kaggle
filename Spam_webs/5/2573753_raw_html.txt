                        <div itemprop="articleBody">
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          INNISFIL - Coyotes  there seems to be more of them this year, and theyre much more brazen.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Thats not news to Innisfil farmers like Brian Bowman, who has lost about 20 lambs to the crafty critters.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Ive had some killed right in the barn, Bowman told the Journal. Thats the bigger issue. Theyre much bolder. Ive been 25 feet away from them and they just stand there looking at you.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Bowman has put up a five-foot high predator proof fence to protect his sheep, but the coyotes are jumping over the fence or digging underneath it.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          I havent seen any deer around. Last year we had four fawns in the field and theres nothing this year, Bowman said. When the natural food isnt there, they come looking. For me its the sheep, but other fellas around here are losing calves.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          The Ministry of Agriculture reports that about 15 cattle and 162 sheep have been killed by coyotes in Simcoe County since April. But that is only the amount farmers have claimed under the ministrys financial compensation program.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Coun. Peter Kmet is calling for local action to manage the coyote population after receiving calls from farmers in his ward looking for help.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Years ago there used to be a bounty on them for $100 a piece, Kmet said. Maybe its time we looked at that again. There have been a lot of sightings and a lot of sheep killed. Ive been getting a lot of calls on this.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Bounties  financial compensation for killing or trapping  have been illegal in Ontario since 1972. However, some municipalities have been given special permission from the province using professional hunters authorized by the Ministry of Natural resources.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          This authorization has conditions on targeting specific problem coyotes such as those that are killing livestock, the ministrys website states. These authorizations are for relatively short periods and are confined to specific geographic areas where problem coyotes have been identified.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Research by the ministry shows bounties do little to reduce coyote populations from one year to the next. Most killed coyotes are juveniles that take risks while trying to stake out new territory.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Within each territory, one breeding pair of coyotes produces a single litter of pups each year, the ministrys website says. These breeding males and females tend to have relatively high survival rates because of their familiarity with their territory and their reduced tendency to take risks.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Bowman, whose farm is on the 9th line near Sideroad 10, hunts the coyotes whenever he can to keep them away from his livestock.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          The nuisance ones are usually the easiest to get because theyre standing out there in the morning waiting for the sheep to come out, he said.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          The Fish and Wildlife Conservation Act allows landowners to kill coyotes, wild dogs or wolves to protect their livestock.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          However, Bowman has found himself in a unique position ever since the City of Barrie annexed land from Innisfil last January. Part of his farm is in Innisfil and some is within city limits.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          That means Bowman cant shoot coyotes on the Barrie land because hunting is banned within the citys boundaries.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Im fine in the Innisfil area, and I have shot a couple there, but I cant hunt in Barrie, he said.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Still, he credited the citys clerk department for helping him get financial compensation for his lost livestock. Under the program, farmers must go through municipal clerks departments to make a claim with the Ministry of Agriculture.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          The city has been excellent. They havent had to deal with this sort of thing for 15 years, but theyve been very good, he said.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          While killing coyotes is somewhat helpful, Bowman knows there are still more out there.
                         </p>
                         <p class="printable-text">
                          Every night my dog is all bent out of shape. And the sheep are leery about going out in the field in the morning.
                         </p>
