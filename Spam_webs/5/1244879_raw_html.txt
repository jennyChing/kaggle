            A Trip To Union Square, San Francisco
           </h1>

             <p>
              If you plan on moving to a luxury apartment in San Francisco, you have already begun to search for things to do and places to visit. You will have studied the best neighborhoods. You will know what they are noted for. You will have heard of many in a historic or other context. Among the various districts you may have come across and noted is Union Square.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <strong>
               Union Square, San Francisco
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Union Square sits firmly in the heart of Downtown San Francisco. The name of Union Square is derived from the big pro-Union rallies held there during the American Civil War (1861-1865). The intent of them was to increase support, financially, militarily and politically for the Northern side of this conflict. Today, it no longer serves as a rallying point. Instead, it is the place to which a well-off individual may head from his or her luxury apartment or residence. The purpose is see and be seen and, of course, to shop.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              What was once a balanced Square with two spectacular buildings, the City of Paris and Fitzhugh were actually demolished in the 1970s to make way for commercial giants Saks and Neiman Marcus. This preceded an influx of commercial enterprises that makes the Union Square what it is today. Yet, forced steps to make sure the razing of buildings of historical significance would not continue and that a maximum height was established.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <strong>
               What to See and Do in Union Square
              </strong>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              In and around Union Square you can amuse yourself in many ways. From your nearby luxury apartment, it is easy to attend a concert at the Bill Graham Civic Auditorium. You can also visit the Theater District adjacent to Union Square including the Curran Theater (1922) and the Geary Theater (1909). You can also visit museums and, as is often the case, stroll along Maiden Lane and elsewhere to wine, dine and shop, shop, shop. Among the popular choices are:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              1. Saks Fifth Avenue
              <br/>
              2. Neiman Marcus
              <br/>
              3. Macys
              <br/>
              4. Bloomingdale's
              <br/>
              5. Barneys New York
              <br/>
              6. Gumps
              <br/>
              7. Tiffany &amp; Co
              <br/>
              8. Cartier
              <br/>
              9. Nordstrom
              <br/>
              10. Stuart Weinstein Gallery
              <br/>
              11. Lang Antiques
              <br/>
              12. Ferrari Store
              <br/>
              13. Louis Vuitton
              <br/>
              14. Gucci
              <br/>
              15. Chanel
              <br/>
              16. Burberry
              <br/>
              17. Bottega Veneta
              <br/>
              18. Herms
              <br/>
              19. Prada
              <br/>
              20. Giorgio Armani
              <br/>
              21. Lacoste
              <br/>
              22. Bulgari
              <br/>
              23. Saint Laurent Paris
              <br/>
              24. Marc Jacobs
              <br/>
              <br/>
              When it comes to shopping, you do need to consider the capabilities of Union Square to satisfy your needs and wants. This is why
              <span style="text-decoration:underline;">
               <strong>
                <a href="http://www.ownbridgewater.com/home/">
                 living in a luxury apartment in San Francisco
                </a>
               </strong>
              </span>
              can make a shopaholic very happy, very easily.
             </p>
