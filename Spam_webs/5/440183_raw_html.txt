        function ShowMenuSelection() {
            var currlink;
            var currhref = window.location.href;
            delimArray = new Array("?", "#");
            for (c = 0; c &lt; delimArray.length; c++) {
                if (currhref.indexOf(delimArray[c]) &gt; -1) {
                    currhref = currhref.substring(0, currhref.indexOf(delimArray[c]));
                }
            }
            if (currhref.substring(currhref.length, currhref.length - 1) == "/") currhref += "default.aspx";
            $(".tree li a").each(function () {
                currlink = $(this).attr('href');
                if (currlink.substring(currlink.length, currlink.length - 1) == "/") currlink += "default.aspx";
                if (currhref.substring(currhref.length, currhref.length - currlink.length) == currlink) {
                    $(this)
                    .parents("li").addClass("expanded").children("a").addClass("selected");
                    return false;
                }
            });

       <h1>
        Tips for Women in the Male-Dominated Tech Field
       </h1>
       <p class="mobile-hidden">
        These strategies can help women climb the corporate ladder as high as they want to go
       </p>
       <hr/>

       <div class="article">
        <span id="ctl00_body_articleCopy">
         <span class="article-image">
          <img alt="Tips for Women in Male-Dominated Jobs" src="/images/articleimages/tips-for-women-in-male-dominated-fields_online_250x167.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          Tips for Women in Male-Dominated Jobs
         </span>
         <p>
          Although reports of women crashing through the so-called glass ceiling occur more and more frequently, theres one career area where women remain woefully underrepresented: technology.
         </p>
         <p>
          Consider these numbers:
         </p>
         <p>
           Only 10% of high-growth tech company founders are women, according to The New York Times.
         </p>
         <p>
          Women make up only about 25% of the workforce at Apple, Google, Facebook, Oracle and Microsoft.
         </p>
         <p>
           Only 14% of computer science graduates are women, according to Dice.com. It was 37% in the mi
          <br/>
         </p>
         <div class="usn-widget-square">
          <dl class="widget widget-square widget-novertpadding widget-h widget-h-large widget-h-dark widget-h-taut promo widget-first widget-last widget-only widget-odd">
           <dt>
            Find a Program for You
           </dt>
           <dd class="subwidget text-only subwidget-first subwidget-last subwidget-only subwidget-odd">
            <div>
             <p class="t-larger t-tight">
              <a class="btn btn-heavy btn-right btn-wide" href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com/multistepform.aspx?ver=22&amp;creativeid=ArticleFindProgram" style="margin: 5px 0 10px;" target="_blank">
               <font color="white">
                Get Matched
               </font>
              </a>
              <a class="" href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com/multistepform.aspx?ver=22&amp;creativeid=ArticleFindProgram" target="_blank">
               U.S. News University Directory
              </a>
              can match you with online programs that meet your criteria in a few simple steps.
             </p>
             <a class="right-top" href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com" target="_blank">
              <img alt="" border="0" src="http://www.bestalliedhealthprograms.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/usnud-logo-widget.png"/>
             </a>
            </div>
           </dd>
          </dl>
         </div>
         d-1980s.
         <p>
          Just 3% of venture capitalists are women.
         </p>
         <p>
           Women in Silicon Valley make 49 cents per every dollar made by men, according to the Social Science Research Council.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Changes in Tactics Can Help Women
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          As is clearly evident,
          <a href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com/articles/women-in-stem_13867.aspx">
           women in technology
          </a>
          and other male-dominated field can face long odds, but that doesnt mean the situation is hopeless. Successful women have a few tips on how to best proceed.
          <br/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           [Read:
           <a href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com/articles/soft-skills-employers-are-looking-for_13979.aspx">
            Soft Skills: What Are They and Why Do They Matter?
           </a>
           ]
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Mashable.com offered a few ideas, starting with this one: find like-minded colleagues.
         </p>
         <p>
          Surround yourself with people that have the
          <a href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com/articles/massively-open-online-courses_13525.aspx">
           skills and experience
          </a>
          you do not have. In this process, honesty is the key: Know what you do not know and look for people that can fill the gaps where you lack, said Joanna Weidenmiller, co-founder and CEO of 1-Page, on Mashable.
         </p>
         <p>
          The ultimate goal is to create a dream team where people can learn from each other and where each persons contribution is unique and invaluable to hitting the common targets.
         </p>
         <p>
          Fran Hauser, president of the digital, style and entertainment group at Time, Inc., suggested being strategic about time management and embracing hard work  a time-tested strategy for getting ahead at any job.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Trusting Intuition and Challenging Yourself
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Shauna Mei, founder and CEO of AHALife.com, told women to trust their intuition (instead of following industry standards) and build something unique.
         </p>
         <p>
          Deanna Varshavskaya, founder and CEO of Wanelo, counseled women to embrace curiosity.
         </p>
         <p>
          One way to do this is to ask yourself: What if the success of the entire company depended on me? What would I do then? What problems would I need to solve and what opportunities would I pursue? she said.
         </p>
         <p>
          And one of the most important things is to use mentors and support networks.
         </p>
         <p>
          Entrepreneurs will face many obstacles, and it's this group of trusted advisors that can help provide outside ideas, advice and guidance on
          <a href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com/articles/military-to-college-hurdles_13992.aspx">
           overcoming challenges
          </a>
          , as well as help build confidence as you move forward, said Ingrid Vanderveldt, founder of the Dell Center for Entrepreneurs.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           Networking for Women in Tech Fields
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          The number of support networks is growing quickly. They include Girls in Tech Los Angeles and Lesbians Who Tech in the San Francisco Bay area (they also have had events in Chicago and New York. Tech LadyMafia and XX in Tech operate in both Washington, D.C. and New York.
         </p>
         <p>
          <b>
           [Read:
           <a href="http://www.usnewsuniversitydirectory.com/articles/top-tech-disciplines-to-learn_13908.aspx">
            Top Tech Disciplines to Learn Now
           </a>
           ]
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          Some of the many other organizations include Girls Who Code, Skillcrush, Black Girls Code and TechGirlz.
         </p>
         <p>
          For more information about support groups near you, visit
          <a href="http://witi.meetup.com/">
           <span style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #1155cc; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: underline; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap; ">
            http://witi.meetup.com/
           </span>
          </a>
          <span style="font-size: 15px; font-family: Arial; color: #0; background-color: transparent; font-weight: normal; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap; ">
           .
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Nobody ever said a tech career would be easy, but the resources are there to succeed.
         </p>
         <p>
          More importantly, signs are pointing to improvement. The federal Bureau of Labor Statistics reported in September 2013 that there were 60,000 new employees working in the computer industry over the past year 36,000 of them (or 60%) being women.
         </p>
         <p>
          Thats admittedly a relatively small step, but still a promising one.
         </p>
        </span>
