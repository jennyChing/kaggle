        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29801945253/tartine">
          Tartine
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <span>
           <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m9172ag7j71rndx62.jpg"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Location:
          </strong>
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=Tartine&amp;hl=en&amp;ll=37.765015,-122.424088&amp;spn=0.034469,0.063086&amp;sll=37.787267,-122.415333&amp;sspn=0.008615,0.015771&amp;gl=us&amp;hq=Tartine&amp;t=m&amp;z=14">
           600 Guerrero Street, San Francisco, CA
          </a>
         </p>
         <div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <span class="pp-headline-item pp-headline-phone">
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          <span>
           People who queue up at
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.tartinebakery.com/">
           <span>
            Tartine Bakery
           </span>
          </a>
          <span>
           have all the trappings of geeks lining up at an iPhone launch: wide eyes, fevered anticipation, and copious drooling. Theres a reason for that. The perma-packed Mission patisserie infuses its collection of baked goods (everything from decadent eclairs to succulent sandwiches) with communal flavor, using locally grown organic flour, eggs, and meat. The ingredients are ultra-fresh, while breads and pastries always spring piping hot from the oven.
          </span>
          <br/>
          <span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          <span>
           For Hidden Menus visit, Tartines Sierra Zumwalt opened our eyes to a spartan (but none the less tasty) off-menu option.
          </span>
          <br/>
          <span>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m9173dCMjg1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Tartine Toast
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          <span>
           Tartines
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.tartinebakery.com/bread.html">
           <span>
            famous in-house made country bread
           </span>
          </a>
          <span>
           roasted and served with sweet butter and orange marmalade.
          </span>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          <span>
          </span>
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/1918/arrested-development-operation-hot-mother">
           <span>
            Its one hot mother.
           </span>
          </a>
          <span>
           The country bread has a slightly tart twang; its like a subtle sourdough. We suspect it would be good with some steamy soup. Two slices of toast and some coffee? Theres the ideal snack on a cold rainy afternoon.
          </span>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          <span>
           Tartines bread springs fresh from the oven Wednesday through Sunday after 5:00pm. You can typically score bread (baked on a radiant stone hearth no less) infused with walnuts, sesame seeds, and olives.
          </span>
          <br/>
          <span>
          </span>
          <br/>
          <span>
           Tartines toast is available everyday. Whats not available on a daily basis are the following clandestine gems that we didnt have the pleasure to nosh on.
          </span>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <span>
            Savory Bread Pudding
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span>
            Apple on Puff Pastry
           </span>
          </li>
          <li>
           <span>
            The staff also snacks on ham and cheese croissants with apricot jam and sandwich makings stuffed in a gougere cheese. Tartine doesnt sell this unholy yet delicious sounding monstrosity to customers but that doesnt mean you cant try making it at home.
           </span>
          </li>
         </ul>

        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29801953961/turtle-tower">
          Turtle Tower
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            <img alt="image" src="http://31.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m916u1QQ3F1rndx62.jpg"/>
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong id="internal-source-marker_0.41009368328377604">
           <span>
            Location:
           </span>
          </strong>
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=Turtle+Tower&amp;hl=en&amp;ll=37.787267,-122.415333&amp;spn=0.008615,0.015771&amp;sll=37.787556,-122.414603&amp;sspn=0.008615,0.015771&amp;gl=us&amp;hq=Turtle+Tower&amp;t=m&amp;z=16">
           631 Larkin Street, San Francisco, CA
          </a>
         </p>
         <div>
          <div>
           Just eating at Turtle Tower makes you feel like youre in the know. Set in the midst of Little Saigon, the Pho shop even has hipster-ready t-shirts and a secret room in the back (you enter through a dirty alley!) that add to its exclusive mystique.
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          The northern-style pho tastes like it was prepped in Hanoi. Featuring lighter broth and wide rice noodles, the soup comes unencumbered by hoisin sauce or any of the usual garnishes typically found in South Vietnamese versions of the dish. Turtle Towers main objective is to keep it simple. Well keep it simple and keep it fresh.
         </p>
         <p>
          Chef and owner Steven Nghia is uncompromising about the quality of his ingredients. Nghia buys produce from local farmers markets, uses only free-range chicken, and the noodles are handcrafted every day. Steven, on that note, we have an idea for your next t-shirt: Turtle Tower Keeps It Fresh.
         </p>
         <p>
          Even though the menu and food are uncomplicated, team Turtle Tower (the restaurant gets its name from the Thap Rua, one of Hanois most prominent landmarks) presented HM with a clutch of off menu dishes. Heres what they cooked up for us.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            <img alt="image" src="http://31.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m916s62kLb1rndx62.jpg"/>
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            Pho Tai Ga
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span>
            What it is:
           </span>
          </strong>
          You dont have to choose between rare beef and chicken. You can get both in the same bowl.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           <span>
            What we think:
           </span>
          </strong>
          A collision of flavors thats at first a bit jarring then settles into a symphony of noodle-y goodness.
          <strong>
           <br/>
           <span>
            Inside Info
           </span>
           <span>
            :
           </span>
          </strong>
          You can get Turtle Towers pho pretty much any way you want it. That can mean generous helpings of rare beef, extra tripe, or a bowl teeming with giblets.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
           </span>
           <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m916v0R61Y1rndx62.jpg"/>
           <br/>
           <span>
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            Bun (No. 12)
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span>
            What it is:
           </span>
          </strong>
          Grilled pork patty and sliced pork loin suspended in a tangy broth served with cold vermicelli noodles, lettuce, mint, and cilantro.
          <strong>
           <br/>
           <span>
            What we think:
           </span>
          </strong>
          A lovely cool contrast to a heaping bowl of hot soup. Great for putting out fires in your mouth when youve shoveled in too much Pho and Sriracha sauce.
          <strong>
           <br/>
           <span>
            Inside Info:
           </span>
          </strong>
          While technically on the menu, you can can get the dish with larger portions of patties or just the sliced pork. We opted for more sliced pork and were not even remotely disappointed.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m916voZ5xP1rndx62.jpg"/>
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            Custom Side Dishes
           </span>
           <br/>
           <span>
            What it is:
           </span>
          </strong>
          If you want ANYTHING, all you need to do is ask. Customers often order mini sized portions of the side dishes. We were tickled by the prospect of ordering mini dishes of egg, giblets, and liver.
          <strong>
           <br/>
           <span>
            What we think:
           </span>
          </strong>
          We ordered a mini portion of the liver, because lets be real, theres nothing wrong with a little extra iron in the diet.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           <span>
            Inside Info:
           </span>
          </strong>
          Egg yolk with soup, a popular off-menu item was just recently added in January.
         </p>

        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29801930555/the-american-grilled-cheese-kitchen">
          The American Grilled Cheese Kitchen
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m917b4zlkL1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Location:
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=The+American+Grilled+Cheese+Kitchen,+San+Francisco,+CA&amp;hl=en&amp;ll=37.79249,-122.392673&amp;spn=0.034456,0.063086&amp;sll=37.765015,-122.424088&amp;sspn=0.034469,0.063086&amp;oq=American+Grilled+Cheese+&amp;gl=us&amp;hq=The+American+Grilled+Cheese+Kitchen,+San+Francisco,+CA&amp;t=m&amp;z=14&amp;iwloc=A">
           1 South Park Street, San Francisco, CA
          </a>
         </p>
         <div>
          Grilled cheese  a snack that came into popularity during the
          <a href="http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=RM&amp;p_theme=rm&amp;p_action=search&amp;p_maxdocs=200&amp;p_topdoc=1&amp;p_text_direct-0=0EB4ECE74A867F9E&amp;p_field_direct-0=document_id&amp;p_perpage=10&amp;p_sort=YMD_date:D&amp;s_trackval=GooglePM">
           Great Depression
          </a>
          , and has since been banished to the kids menu at diners, is all grown up and headlining at SOMAs The American Grilled Cheese Kitchen.
         </div>
         <p>
          In its traditional form, the beauty of the grilled cheese lies in its simplicity - just two slices of Wonderbread and a couple Kraft singles. Buttered. Grilled. Perfect. But at TAGCK, all sandwiches are made using fresh rustic levain bread from San Franciscos Pinkies Bakery, and theyre accessorized with ingredients like basil-lavender pesto and rosemary butteringredients more synonymous of haute California cuisine than your mom kitchen.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          The constant line spilling out the restaurants doors and on to the sidewalk has proven that there is no lack of enthusiasm for the gussied up grilled cheese. And fortunately for fans, loyalty has its rewards. The masterminds behind this pilgrimage spot have cooked up a secret menu for regulars only.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Owner Nate Pollack gave Hidden Menu the passwords for three variations of the Kitchens signature sandwich The Mousetrap and prepared us a mystery drink that he refused to give use the code word for.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Here are the passwords that will get you access to three variations of the Kitchens flagship dish. The Mousetrap usually comes with three kinds of the cheese (Tillamook sharp cheddar, creamy havarti, and monterey jack) on artisan sourdough.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          For the drink, youre on your own. But HM likes a good challenge and were pretty sure our resourceful readers will figure out what its called and how to order it. And when you do, hit us up at tips@hiddenmenu.com.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong id="internal-source-marker_0.7561411736533046">
           <strong>
            <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m917im1lDO1rndx62.jpg"/>
           </strong>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            Tell them you want it 
           </span>
           <span>
            Kiddie Style
           </span>
           <span>
            .
           </span>
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Tillamook sharp cheddar grilled between two pieces of bread.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          The closest thing The American has to its namesakes traditional form. Just add tater tots and tomato soup, and youll be transported back to a rainy day in 4th grade.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          According to Nate, this is most often ordered by kids (hence the name), or adults who eat like kids (ahem, Danny).
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m917ht8Rtx1rndx62.jpg"/>
           <br/>
           <span>
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            Tell them you want 
           </span>
           <span>
            The Works
           </span>
           <span>
            .
           </span>
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          A Mousetrap with applewood-smoked bacon, roasted tomato and bread nbutter pickles.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Sweet Zombie Cheesus! The bacon and sweet pickles work better together than Hall and Oates.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          The Americans bread n butter pickles are proudly made in house.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <strong>
            <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m917h0t4241rndx62.jpg"/>
           </strong>
           <br/>
           <span>
           </span>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <span>
            Tell them you want a 
           </span>
           <span>
            Purist
           </span>
           <span>
            .
           </span>
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          A Mousetrap straight up.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Technically, a grilled cheese purist would probably lean toward the no-frills Kiddie Style.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Using the lingo just lets them know youre a regular.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <strong>
            <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m917eeu0yO1rndx62.jpg"/>
           </strong>
           <br/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The Drink That Must Not Be Named
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Though we dont know what its called, we do know this fizzy concoction contains real fruit, house made syrup, and real sugar.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Tastes kind of like a mixed berry Crystal Geyser but better. Were thinking its the lack of brain damaging corn syrup typically found in most sodas.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Nate didnt tell us the drinks moniker or how to order it. If one of you enterprising grilled cheese aficionados has this intel tell us in the comments or better yet drop us an email.
         </p>

        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29801980062/rye">
          Rye
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <strong>
           <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m915myGgWB1rndx62.jpg"/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Location:
          </strong>
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=Rye,+Geary+Street,+San+Francisco,+CA&amp;hl=en&amp;ll=37.787556,-122.414603&amp;spn=0.008615,0.015771&amp;sll=37.779738,-122.393253&amp;sspn=0.008615,0.015771&amp;oq=Rye,+San&amp;gl=us&amp;hq=Rye,&amp;hnear=Geary+St,+San+Francisco,+California&amp;t=m&amp;z=16">
           688 Geary Street, San Francisco, CA
          </a>
         </p>
         <div>
          <div>
          </div>
          <span class="pp-headline-item pp-headline-phone">
          </span>
         </div>
         <p>
          The staff at San Franciscos Rye arent bartenders  theyre highly trained mixologists  cocktail nerds who delight in libation innovation and experimentation. But like so many SF-based establishments theyre also hardcore locavores, mixing in as many seasonal, homegrown components as possible. Think of Rye as a bookend to
          <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29371112100/farm-table">
           Farm:Table
          </a>
          ; you can start your day off with expertly crafted coffee at one location and wind it down with exquisitly formulated cocktails at the other.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          We pulled up a stool (next to Farm:Tables Shannon Amitin no less) and spoke to professional spirit-slingers Jon Gasparini and Doug Williams. They concocted a quartet of off-menu drinks for us using ingredients like bergamots, blood oranges, and meyer lemons along with some highly specialized liquors. Heres what they blended that left us feeling stirred.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://31.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m916c9nuje1rndx62.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           International Sour
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          A medley of spirits originating from France, Italy, Trinidad, the US, and China mixed with Navan (a cognac-based vanilla liquer), house made kumquat syrup, a dash of port, then topped with foamy egg white, lemon peel, and served in a tall glass (phew, got all that?).
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          This extremely labor intensive reinterpretation of the whiskey sour is both masculine and delicate. One gastronaught was overheard exclaiming, its a manly, foo-foo drink.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside info:
          </strong>
          When Rye threw a mixology contest, the International Sour ended up being the winning entry.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m915rf7gTB1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Blood Orange Blood and Sand
           <br/>
          </strong>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Glenrothers reserve scotch blended with fresh squeezed blood orange juice, Dolin Rouge vermouth, and Cherry Herring.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          According to Gasparini, People are often apprehnesive about scotch cocktails. For us, there was nothing to be bashful about. This easily approachable, earthy, zesty, and aromatic drink is as frothy as it is refreshing.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside info:
          </strong>
          The drinks namesake comes from
          <a href="http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0012952/">
           a 1922 film
          </a>
          starring Rudolph Valentino and even uses era appropriate ingredients 
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cherry_Herring">
           Cherry Herring
          </a>
          was a popular staple in pre-prohibition bars.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m915qjrJ5E1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The Unnamed Cocktail
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Fresh grapefruit, lime, and green apple muddled together with a 100% agave tequila, canton ginger liquor then garnished with a slice of green apple, and topped with crushed ice.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Tart and tingly. It hits like an exceptionally smooth margarita with a lip smacking finish.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          This yet-to-be-named libation is typically produced with house-made ginger syrup (Rye had just run out when we visited.) Oh and if any of you clever commenters can think of a pun-tastic name for it, let us know; well pass it on to the folks at Rye.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m915sbdr5q1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Dougs Drink
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Pikesville rye whiskey, Leopold Bothers Georgia Peach flavored whiskey, Antica Carpano vermouth,
          <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amaro_%28drink%29">
           Amaro
          </a>
          , fresh bergamot juice, topped with frothy egg white.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          It takes a few sips to fully appreciate this libation. But after a few tastes, youll be treated to a full bodied and spicy yet also elegant and herbal experience.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Due to its strong floral hints, Doug typically likes to make this cocktail in the spring. But get some before the last batch of Leopold Brothers runs out. The
          <a href="http://www.leopoldbros.com/Georgia_Peach_Whiskey.html">
           painstakingly slow
          </a>
          process of blending whiskey with Georgia Peaches yields just under 20 cases a year.
          <br/>
          <br/>

           <span>
            Written by:

            Kat Manalac

            Danny Dumas

            Jon Snyder

        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29373786783/solstice-restaurant-and-lounge">
          Solstice Restaurant and Lounge
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <strong id="internal-source-marker_0.3045049700886011">
           <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pzjgGx8R1rndx62.jpg"/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Location:
          <a href="http://Solstice%20Restaurant%20and%20Lounge,%20California%20Street,%20San%20Francisco,%20CA">
           2801 California Street, San Francisco, Ca
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Main entry: Solstice
         </p>
         <p>
          solstice
          <br/>
          Pronunciation: \sl-sts, sl-, sl-\
          <br/>
          Function: noun
          <br/>
          1: Either of the two times of the year when the sun is at its greatest distance from the celestial equator.
          <br/>
          2: A restaurant in San Franciscos NOPA district serving up easy sipping drinks, a wide selection of small plates, and several off menu items smoother than Miles Davis on a hot buttered slip and slide.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Yeah, we prefer the second definition too.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          To celebrate the shortest day of the year, we hitched up our one-horse-open-sleigh and headed to
          <a href="http://www.solsticelounge.com/">
           Solstice
          </a>
          for some live music and secret eats. HiddenMenu snuggled into a booth with Solstices Alison Winters where she stoked us with a trifecta of off menu drinks and a scattering of unlisted dishes.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pzmfxOLk1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Last Word (Green)
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Gin, Chartreuse, lime juice, maraschino cherry liqueur, served in a chilled glass with a sugared rim.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Sweet but not overpowering. The Last Word finishes with a note more dulcet than a scene from
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rw_R9SS3kQ8">
           Willy Wonka
          </a>
          .
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside info:
          </strong>
          According to Ted Saucier in his 1951 bartenders guide Bottoms Up, The Last Word is a prohibition-era drink that originated at the Detroit Athletic Club.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pzrnztJb1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Cosmo-Not (Red)
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Raspberry vodka, lime juice, Cointreau, raspberry puree.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Tastes like a grownup glass of raspberry Kool-aid. (Thats good!)
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside info:
          </strong>
          The Tang infused Cosmonaut cocktail has actually been putting people in orbit for years. But Solstices Cosmo-Not jettisons the space-friendly powder for a raspberry puree payload.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pzsb81xP1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Flying Sidecar (Yellow)
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          4 Roses Bourbon, triple sec, lemon juice.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Hopefully someone forgot to service the brakes on this sidecar  we never want to get off.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside info:
          </strong>
          Sidecars typically have a brandy or cognac base. The Flying Sidecar subs in bourbon for a decidedly stiffer punch.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pzq37j2Q1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Chicken Pesto Pizza
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          1 cheese pizza - tomato sauce + pesto + marinated chicken
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Pizza purists may decry the addition of pesto on anything. Nuts to them. This pie was handily obliterated by our four hungry gastronauts.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside info:
          </strong>
          The cooks are more flexible than a spring of well cooked asparagus. You can ask for virtually any kind of pizza and theyll do their best to make it for you.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pzr0Oopc1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Ahi with Lime, Apples and Jalapeno
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Ahi poke with a south-of-the-border twang.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Delicious and light with ample spiciness. We were dubious about the apples + ahi pairing, but theyre a surprisingly delicious combination.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside info:
          </strong>
          This one gets the ubiquitous staff favorite badge. We can see why. Its easier to kick a smack habit than to cease consumption of the buttery blue corn chips, and the rich, tasty ahi.
         </p>

        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29373044415/little-star-pizza">
          Little Star Pizza
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pzaolJ5i1rndx62.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          Location:
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=Little+Star+Pizza&amp;hl=en&amp;sll=37.777499,-122.438164&amp;sspn=0.137851,0.267792&amp;hq=Little+Star+Pizza&amp;t=m&amp;z=14">
           846 Divisadero Street, San Francisco, CA
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you consider thin crust blasphemy, make pilgrimages to Chicago for Giordanos (or Lou Malnatis, or Ginos East, everyone has an opinion), and kneel before the altar of deep dish, then Little Star should be your West Coast temple of worship.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          The pizzeria specializes in pie that hits like a wrecking ball of flavor: Chicago style, loaded with toppings, crowned with thick tomato sauce, and encased in a buttery cornmeal crust. If you leave hungry then you should probably get yourself checked for tapeworm.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          We rolled in to the
          <a href="http://www.littlestarpizza.com/">
           Little Stars
          </a>
          Mission street location where they whipped up two off menu pizzas.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://31.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pz755gn21rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Brass Monkey
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          In the burbs a Brass Monkey is both malt liquor mixed with OJ and one of the best songs from Licensed to Ill. At Little Star its their namesake deep dish pizza (spinach bended with ricotta, feta, mushrooms, onions and garlic) with a sausage upgrade.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Get a knife and fork ready, you cant really eat a slice of this stuff with one hand. Its a veritable lasagna pie. The sausage gives each slice a sweet, almost maple-y quality and nicely balances out the cornbread crust which  despite the dog-pile of ingredients  never gets soggy.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          The Brass Monkey was featured in
          <a href="http://www.7x7.com/content/eat-drink/2009-big-eat-sf-100-things-try-you-die">
           7x7s 100 Things to Try Before You Die 2009.
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://31.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pz98llfs1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The Abba Zabba aka The Half Baked
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Any Little Star Pizza (we opted for the Mediterranian) cooked 50% through and tossed in a to-go box.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UzEio_1trro">
           Dave Chappelle
          </a>
          would approve.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Take home, bake at 375 on top rack for 25 minutes. For better results, consider
          <a href="http://gadgets.boingboing.net/2009/05/01/how-to-build-the-ult.html">
           constructing a DIY home pizza oven.
          </a>
          Its seriously not hard!
         </p>

        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29372416415/little-skillet">
          Little Skillet
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pyqrEbyt1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Location:
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=Little+Skillet,+Ritch+Street,+San+Francisco,+CA&amp;hl=en&amp;sll=40.697488,-73.979681&amp;sspn=0.52892,1.071167&amp;oq=Little+Skillet+Sa&amp;hq=Little+Skillet,+Ritch+Street,+San+Francisco,+CA&amp;t=m&amp;z=14">
           330 Ritch Street, San Francisco, CA
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          One glance at the plaid hipsters sitting atop their fixies on Ritch Street, and youd swear
          <a href="http://www.littleskilletsf.com/">
           Little Skillet
          </a>
          was in a Mission enclave, not an alley in SOMA.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          But it is indeed south of Market. Planted by the farm-fresh soul foodists at
          <a href="http://www.farmerbrownsf.com/location.html">
           Farmerbrown
          </a>
          , Little Skillet is in itself something of a hidden foodie hotspot. At lunchtime, scores of people materialize at the to-go window of
          <a href="http://www.google.com/search?q=330+Ritch&amp;ie=utf-8&amp;oe=utf-8&amp;aq=t&amp;rls=org.mozilla:en-US:official&amp;client=firefox-a">
           330 Ritch
          </a>
          to order up hearty helpings of Southern comforts like chicken &amp; waffles and bacon wrapped waffledogs.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Hidden Menu braved the wind and rain and huddled under Little Skillets bright canopy while we watched chef Christian Ciscle and his team fry us up some of their favorite off-menu grub. If you can bear deprive yourself from the house fried chicken and waffles dig into these:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pytcX0jI1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Grits With Pulled Pork, Tillamook Cheddar and Scallions
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What is is:
          </strong>
          See above.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Frakkin delicious. The creamy grits and cheddar are the best duo since Hall and Oates.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          The pulled pork is co-opted from the on-menu breakfast po'boy. If youre feeling adventurous, try any of the po'boy ingredients on top of grits. (Were ordering Creole shrimp and grits on our next visit.)
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pz12LbYg1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Wing-Wings
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Sweet and spicy chicken wings dabbled with whole grain mustard.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Nicely balanced  not too sweet, not too spicy.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Its not always available, but check the Little Skillet
          <a href="http://twitter.com/littleskillet">
           twitter feed
          </a>
          to see when it is.
          <br/>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pyv8w3yM1rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Eggs McMahon
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          A waffle floating on a river of sausage gravy then topped scallions, cheddar cheese,
          <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/35047/the-simpsons-jolly-fat-man">
           and a fried egg
          </a>
          .
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Hard on the heart and heavenly in your mouth, its like a deep south interpretation of Eggs Benedict.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Got a hangover? Ingest one Eggs McMahon, chase with two Advil and feel your headache dissipate.
          <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ji-cT58rgNc">
           Hi-yo!
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pyvxQGN41rndx62.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Brown Sugar Black Pepper Biscuits
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          The well known and voraciously consumes southern biscuit with a sweet, piquant kick.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          The sweet/savory combo gave us the same delightful surprise we had when poured maple syrup on fried chicken for the first time.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Though theyre off-menu, the biscuits are one of Little Skillets signature staples.
         </p>

        <h2>
         <a href="http://hiddenmenu.com/post/29372017335/five-happiness">
          Five Happiness
         </a>
        </h2>
       </header>
       <section>
        <div class="caption">
         <p>
          <strong id="internal-source-marker_0.18307293066754937">
           <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pylgT70f1rndx62.jpg"/>
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Location:
          <a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=Five+Happiness,+4142+Geary+Blvd,+San+Francisco,+CA+9411&amp;hl=en&amp;sll=37.781228,-122.463953&amp;sspn=0.008615,0.016737&amp;hq=Five+Happiness,+4142+Geary+Blvd,+San+Francisco,+CA+9411&amp;t=m&amp;z=14">
           4142 Geary Blvd, San Francisco, CA 94118
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The problem with Chinese food in America is that its, well, very American. Noodles slathered in sweet-and-sour-meets-napalm sauce or deep fried dough injected with overcooked mystery meat is often standard issue. Dishes like these are about as close to authentic Chinese food as Taco Bell is to genuine Mexican cuisine. But when youve got an uncontrollable hankerin for some
          <a href="http://www.shanghai-chefs.com/?p=238">
           intestines with blood tofu
          </a>
          , there are ways to get it. You just have to know where to go and what to ask for.
         </p>
         <p>
          One place to go is
          <a href="http://www.yelp.com/biz/five-happiness-san-francisco">
           Five Happiness
          </a>
          . One thing to ask for is their traditional Chinese menu.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Unless youre a regular at Five Happiness, youll most likely receive their standard menu filled with the requisite Kung Pao chicken and broccoli beef. However if you press them for unlisted/more traditional items theyll (somewhat reluctantly) produce a single pink laminated placard. On this unassuming hidden menu are 25 authentic Taiwanese and Shanghai dishes that you wont find in many Americanized Chinese restaurants. And yep, they even have the aforementioned intestines with blood tofu.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          We asked the staff what some of their most beloved dishes were. This is what they recommended to us:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pyly9WHk1rndx62.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Spicy Dumplings
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Fresh wonton dumplings cooked in pungent red pepper oil.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          These slippery little morsels slide down the pipe fast  we didnt so much eat them as inhale them.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          The dumplings arent even on the special hidden menu. You have to order them separately.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="image" src="http://38.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pymh6BxI1rndx62.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Shanghai Rib with Spinach
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          Small hunks of baked short ribs lightly sprinkled with spicy savory gravy and served over fresh spinach.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          The meat is so tender; it literally melts from the bone. The delectable sauce mixes perfectly with the spinach and has a hint of red wine and pan drippings.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          Shanghai ribs are usually cooked to taste very sugary. But Five Happiness take on the meat skews less sweet, more savory.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          <img alt="image" src="http://33.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m8pyn2hAGG1rndx62.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Eight Treasure Delight
          </strong>
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What it is:
          </strong>
          A medley of pork, shrimp, beef, tofu, soybeans, mushrooms, green onions, and peanuts wok seared in a tangy spicy sauce.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           What we think:
          </strong>
          Richer than Paul Allen and more complex than theoretical physics, this dish is best cut with an ample dollop of sticky rice.
          <br/>
          <strong>
           Inside Info:
          </strong>
          A favorite of the staff. When we ordered it their eyes lit up with collective approval. It was almost as if it granted us instantaneous street cred.
          <br/>
          <br/>
          Theres 23 more dishes on the hidden menu ranging from braised pork shank with tendon to Hakak style shredded dry bean cake. Are we going to list them all? Nope. But thatll give you cause to head over and see what the rest are. Maybe even try a couple. Even the blood tofu.
         </p>
