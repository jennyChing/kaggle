       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Project Camping: How to Plan a CampOut
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-07-01T07:00:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          July 1, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/author/todays-post/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Todays Post
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-categories">
          Filed Under:
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/category/lifestyle/travel/" rel="category tag">
           Travel
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pf-content">
         <div style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-meals.png">
           <img alt="camping meals" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-27571" height="720" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-meals.png" width="507"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Todays feature is good enough to be split up into several posts and made into a workshop! Angie has a whole series of posts on how to plan a campout. If you dont have any camping adventures planned anytime soon, bookmark this page and come back to it next year. And if youre like my husband and prefer to camp at the Holiday Inn, maybe these ideas will change your mind and youll find yourself longing to become one with nature in your sleeping bag just to experience the yumminess of the following posts! If nothing else, it could spark your creativity to plan an outdoor BBQ, picnic or Bon Fire with some friends. Take a look at this:
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: x-large;">
           Camping Cuisine {Preparations}
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Before I get to the recipe portion of this post, I want to mention how food prep for camping is a girls best friend. I start with a menu plan for each meal that will be required for our stay. And I create a grocery list based on my meal plan. Pretty basic planningbut it helps the trip go smoothly.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-preparations.jpg">
           <img alt="camping preparations" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20878" height="640" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-preparations.jpg" width="427"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          Tips:
         </h2>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-food.jpg">
           <img alt="camping food" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20879" height="600" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-food.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           I make sure to wash and prep all the vegetables and store in ziploc bags. On theleft are vegetables for dipping for snacks. On theright are vegetables for chicken kebabs.
          </li>
          <li>
           Here are onions and mushrooms prepped for our steak night.
          </li>
          <li>
           I also take containers of other meal ingredients that we will need.
          </li>
          <li>
           Fruit washed and cut up for quick camping snacks.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-food-preparations.jpg">
           <img alt="camping food preparations" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20880" height="600" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-food-preparations.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           I dont waste money buying small sizes of condiments from the grocery store. I just scoop outdifferent things fromthe big sizes already in our fridgeand store in small containers to put in the cooler. AndI am sure to label, label, label!!
          </li>
          <li>
           One of my favorite things in our camping kitchen- a handy, dandy screen to keep those pesky flies off of the salads.
          </li>
          <li>
           The other must-have is our cutlery caddy to keep everything handy at the table, so I dont have to get up a million times searching for things.
          </li>
          <li>
           To be clear, we do have hamburgers sometimes.everyone in our family likes them. I just make sure that I make the burgers before we leave home so that my husband just has to put them on the grill.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h2 style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: x-large;">
           Camping Cuisine {Recipes}
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
          Along with our hamburgers and hotdogs (after all, what is a camping trip without roasting weenies on a campfire?) here is a list of a few other favorites:
         </p>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <b>
            <span style="font-family: 'Courier New',Courier,monospace;">
             Picnic Sandwich
            </span>
           </b>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-veggies.jpg">
           <img alt="camping veggies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20883" height="450" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-veggies.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/picnic-sandwich.jpg">
           <img alt="picnic sandwich" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20885" height="450" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/picnic-sandwich.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
          Not only is this a fabulous sandwich, but it is the ultimate in make ahead food. It tastes better the day after its made.after the flavours have had time to mingle. This sandwich is the perfect lunch for the first day of camping. Afterarrivingat the campsite and spending a few hours putting up tents, inflating mattresses and getting things in order, everyone is usuallly tired and hungry. Thats when I am so happy to pull out the picnic sandwich for an instant lunch. It makes 8 sandwich wedges and each piece is very filling and satisfying. This sandwich requires a filling, some meat &amp; cheeses and a round loaf of bread. To see how to make it, click
          <a href="http://applevalleygirl.blogspot.com/2010/07/menu-plan-monday-camping-food-part-1.html">
           HERE
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <b>
            <span style="font-family: 'Courier New',Courier,monospace;">
             Chicks on Sticks
            </span>
           </b>
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/chicks-on-sticks.jpg">
           <img alt="chicks on sticks" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20884" height="450" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/chicks-on-sticks.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
          (Seriously. That is the name of the recipe.) Simply gather your ingredients and do the preparations listed
          <a href="http://applevalleygirl.blogspot.com/2010/07/camping-food-part-2-chicks-on-sticks.html">
           HERE
          </a>
          and your meal is ready to go into the cooler for your camping trip.When preparing this meal, simply thaw the chicken by placing it on the top of the cooler. Thread the chicken pieces with the washed and ready-to-go-veggies on skewers. Grill each side about 5-6 minutes or until chicken looks cooked and juices run clear.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <b>
            <span style="font-family: 'Courier New',Courier,monospace;">
             Blueberry and Cream Cheese Dessert
            </span>
           </b>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-dessert.jpg">
           <img alt="camping dessert" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20881" height="450" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-dessert.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          This is SUPER easy to make and you only need 4 ingredients and a fire iron or camp cooker. Get the recipe and step by step instructions
          <a href="http://applevalleygirl.blogspot.com/2010/08/camping-cuisine-part-4-blueberry-cream.html">
           HERE
          </a>
          Its a pocket of warm, yummy &amp;creamy goodness! You dont have to limit yourself to blueberry jam. Here are some other suggestions: Strawberry jam &amp; cream cheese, marmalade &amp; cream cheese, peanut butter and banana slices, nutella &amp; banana slices or any combination you like. For some meal suggestions for fire irons or camp cookers, try: pizza sauce, mozarella and pepperoni. Or how about smoked salmon and cream cheese? The possibilities are many!
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <b>
            <span style="font-family: 'Courier New',Courier,monospace;">
             For Goodness Steak
            </span>
           </b>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-steak.jpg">
           <img alt="camping steak" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20882" height="450" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-steak.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
          This recipe requires you to mix up a quick &amp; easy marinade to toss on steak in a ziploc bagto freeze for later use on the camping trip. Recipe and prep instructions
          <a href="http://applevalleygirl.blogspot.com/2010/08/camping-cuisine-part-3-for-goodness.html">
           HERE
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           <b>
            <span style="font-family: 'Courier New',Courier,monospace;">
             Dont Forget Your Veggies
            </span>
           </b>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-veggies.jpg">
           <img alt="camping veggies" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-20883" height="450" src="//www.somewhatsimple.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/camping-veggies.jpg" width="600"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          On our last camping trip, I took along a bean salad which included lots of veggies. I chose this recipe because its a marinated salad and it only gets better with age, therefore, it makes a great camping recipe. I modified the original recipe a bit because my husband is not crazy about too many beans. {And everyone has to sleep in a tent togetherso enough said about that.}The recipe made so much that it actually fed 3 families and we still had some leftover! I served the salad as a side to our hamburgers one night for dinner. It was so good. This salad travels really well. I highly recommend it! Recipe
          <a href="http://applevalleygirl.blogspot.com/2010/08/camping-cuisine-part-5-eat-your-veggies_12.html">
           HERE.
          </a>
         </p>
         <h2 class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <span style="font-size: x-large;">
           Camping Cuisine {Wrap Up}
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
          Of course, the whole reason for camping.the happy pink glow in the childrens cheeks. Here is my sous chef enjoying his morning fried egg sandwich with bacon.made by Dad.
         </p>
         <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
          <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_gaOlYHheIP0/TGIsyplgRRI/AAAAAAAAB2g/VWRg6xD8Sz8/s1600/IMG_0828.JPG" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
           <img alt="" border="0" height="640" src="//3.bp.blogspot.com/_gaOlYHheIP0/TGIsyplgRRI/AAAAAAAAB2g/VWRg6xD8Sz8/s640/IMG_0828.JPG" width="428"/>
          </a>
         </div>
         <p class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
          Did I mention that while I do all the food prep for camping, my husband then takes over and does all of the actual cooking while we are camping? Yes. I
          <i>
           love
          </i>
          him. Food eaten in the fresh air is so very good! I like to think that I have inspired some campers to mix it up a little. I hope so. I hope on your next camping trip you feel inspired to try something new and fun!
         </p>
