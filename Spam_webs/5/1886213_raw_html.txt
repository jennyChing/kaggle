        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         5 Things No One Told Me About Working From Home
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-12-04T11:07:09+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          December 4, 2014
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://wahadventures.com/author/mirandag55225" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Miranda Grimm
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://wahadventures.com/2014/12/5-things-no-one-told-me-about-working-from-home.html#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertAbove" style="margin: 5px; padding: 0px;">
         <script type="text/javascript">

        <h2>
         <img alt="I can't believe no one told me working from home would be like this!" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-11431" height="450" src="http://www.wahadventures.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/no-one-told-me.png" width="400"/>
         I Cant Work With My Kids Around
        </h2>
        <p>
         I decided to pursue a work at home career after having kids. I couldnt take the idea of dropping my baby off to a daycare full of strangers. However, every work at home job Ive done was nearly impossible to do with my kids around. Even blogging. They needed my attention and even now that they are older, they are very distracting. Ive had to get pretty creative and sacrifice things like sleep in order to make working from home work for me.
        </p>
        <p>
         When I worked as a sales rep for a virtual call center, I had to be sure to find a flexible company that would allow me to schedule my own hours, around my husbands schedule. Although we didnt get a lot of quality time together, at least I was able to still work while he was home to care for the children. It was hard  but we survived.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive done other work at home jobs that did not require the complete silence that a phone job does, but I couldnt accomplish much with them around. I had to limit my work time to after they were in bed. Now, my kids are in school and I have a lot of time alone.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         I Have No Idea What Im Doing
        </h2>
        <p>
         There is very little support for those of us who work from home. Even the companies that Ive worked with that had chat systems and forums in place to help guide me through and provide support, it never felt like enough. When I worked brick and mortar jobs, I was able to get direct confirmation and correction during training.
        </p>
        <div class="wpInsert wpInsertInPostAd wpInsertMiddle" style="margin-top: 15px; margin-bottom: 15px; padding: 10px; float: right;">
         <div class="pf_ads" id="pf_36722">
          <script src="//load.passionfruitads.com/a/fec60c5de7bf3265fb585905e39c04a3dbfb7bdf.js" type="text/javascript">
          </script>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         Working from home, however, I have been faced with many times when I felt as if I had no idea what I was doing.
        </p>
        <p>
         Specifically when I began working as a freelance writer. I over-thought every step of the way.With some encouragement from members at WorkPlaceLikeHome.coms forum- I pushed through and just did it to the best of what I knew and everything turned out okay.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Theres Still No Time For Dishes
        </h2>
        <p>
         I thought working from home would open up some time for me to better keep up with the house work. Although, I do save time on commuting, I feel the need to work in every possible moment I have alone. Plus, I am now making more of a mess since I am home all day.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have dropped the idea that my house would become better organized. Now I am just more comfortable with my mess.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         I Am Not a Highly Self-Motivated Person
        </h2>
        <p>
         If you would have asked me 8 years ago, before I began working from home, if I was a highly self-motivated person, I would have said YES with complete confidence.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now that I have the freedom to either walk to my computer or walk to my fridge.I realize I am not naturally self-motivated. It takes a lot of mental effort to keep myself on track.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Jeans Became Part of My Dress Up Wardrobe
        </h2>
        <p>
         Long gone are the days I wore slacks, dress socks, leather shiny shoes and curled my hair daily. Jeans were weekend wear before I began working from home. Now, my dress attire is a cotton t-shirt, a pair of pajama pants and I throw my hair up in a pony tail. Jeans are for going out! This is not a complaint! I love it!
        </p>
