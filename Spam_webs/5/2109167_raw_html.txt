       <h1 class="title">
        Humanae Portraits Match People of Different Ethnicities With Their Pantone Color
       </h1>
       <div class="postmeta">
        March 10, 2014 by
        <strong>
         <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/author/laura-barisonzi/" rel="author" title="Posts by Laura Barisonzi">
          Laura Barisonzi
         </a>
        </strong>
        |
        <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/2014/03/angelica-dass/#comments">
         <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="65141 http://www.featureshoot.com/?p=65141">
          3 comments
         </span>
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="content">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/2014/03/angelica-dass/#!zjYcW">
          <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66220" height="517" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Angelica-Dass-Humanae.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.featureshoot.com/2014/03/angelica-dass/#!zjYcW">
          <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66221" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/angg.jpg" width="620"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Brazilian fine art photographer
         <a href="http://www.angelicadass.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.angelicadass.com/', 'Angelica Dass']);" target="_blank">
          Angelica Dass
         </a>
          series
         <em>
          Humanae
         </em>
         identifiesportrait subjects from around the world using the Pantone color system.
         <span id="more-65141">
         </span>
         Using an1111 pixel swatch from her subjects faces, Dass matches them to corresponding Pantone colors, creating an abundant and unique catalog of skin tones that reflects the worlds diversity beyond the categorizations we have long been confined to.We recently asked her more about the ongoing project.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-65384" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Angelica_Dass_81.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-65382" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Angelica_Dass_51.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What was your inspiration for using Pantone colors to represent humans?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         If what I wanted was to destroy the concepts of colors associated with race, such as red, yellow, white and black, it would not be logical to use a color scale that works with percentages of these colors. Thats why I chose not to use CMYK or RGB. Pantone works on a neutral scale, where a color has no more importance than another. Its a very identifiable scale for those in the world of design, but also easilyunderstood by anyone. It provides a way to look objectively at the human object.'
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-65381" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Angelica_Dass_41.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-65383" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Angelica_Dass_61.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How do you go about finding subjects for your work and what are the criteria you are looking for?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         There is no selection criteria. I make public announcements through social networks. To ensure diversity in the project, I work at spaces that are not only in the art world, too. The 2000 images in the project have been made in galleries and art fairs, but also in urban favelas, in NGOs, at the headquarters of UNESCO, and in cooperatives that work with the homeless.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not only are there a mix of faces and colors, but a mix of social classes, religions, sexual orientations, political elections, economic statustogether in
         <em>
          Humanae
         </em>
         we cannot be confined to these codes.So far I have taken portraits in seven cities: Madrid, Barcelona, Winterthur, Rio de Janeiro, So Paulo, Paris, and Chicago. My minimum goal is to make portraits on all the five continents.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66219" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/ang.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66222" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/humanae.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          By identifying your subjects by Pantone color rather than ethnicity/color of origin/name/occupation, etc., what commentary do you hope to make about their identities and the relationship between them?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         It is a kind of game for subverting our codes. The ultimate goal is to provoke discussion about ethnic identity, creating images that lead us to identify each other independent from factors such as nationality, origin, economic status, age, or aesthetic standards. The most important thing for me is the dialogue
         <em>
          Humanae
         </em>
         has generated outside of the existing conversation. For example,
         <em>
          Humanae
         </em>
         has been used in educational textbooks by teachers who use it as a tool to talk about equality, appropriation for new artists who are interested in the physiognomic variety in the project, or scientists who use it to illustrate their research.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Since the Pantone system is a highly structured and ordered system, how does your display of the work relate to this system?
         </strong>
         <br/>
         The project is exhibited in different ways, but always messy numerically. The aim isnt to make a color scale from light to dark. The intention is to make it like real lifeall together, and mixed. Each time I exhibit, I always try to make new portraits, to continue adding images to a work in progress.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-65386" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Angelica_Dass_101.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Angelica Dass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-66218" height="620" src="http://www.featureshoot.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/AN.jpg" width="620"/>
        </p>
