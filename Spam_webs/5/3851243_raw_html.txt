              <p>
               During this season of giving, its easy togift the child in your life something with more longevity than a toy or gadget.
              </p>
              <p>
               A deposit as small as $25 can be made into a (partner)
               <a href="http://lajollamom.com/scholarshare-529-college-savings-plan/" target="_blank">
                ScholarShare 529 college savings account
               </a>
               .It is often done aspart of alargergift, too, if youd like to also givethe child something immediate and tangible.
              </p>
              <p>
               How? Contribute toan existing ScholarShare account or open up a new one with the child as beneficiary. Its actually much easier than it sounds.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Gift to an Existing ScholarShare Account
              </h2>
              <p>
               If the account is existing, its easiest to download a
               <a href="https://www.scholarshare.com/documents/ca_gift_form.pdf" target="_blank">
                ScholarShare gift deposit form
               </a>
               . Ask the account owner for the account number. Write a check to ScholarShare College Savings Plan. Be sure to include the account number on the check and then mail it in with the deposit form.
              </p>
              <p>
               Alternatively, you can present the recipient with a check and gift deposit form and let them fill in the account number.This is what I would likely do.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Gift a New ScholarShare Account
              </h2>
              <p>
               If the child doesnt yet have a
               <a href="http://lajollamom.com/saving-for-college-scholarshare/" target="_blank" title="Must Read: Your 529 College Savings Plan FAQs Answered">
                ScholarShare account
               </a>
               , then you would complete the application process which can be done online in about 5 minutes. Or, request a paper application. You will need to know the childs social security number, however. And, remember that a beneficiary can have more than one ScholarShare account.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Print a Special Gift Certificate for the Occasion
              </h2>
              <p>
               Download a ScholarShare gift certificate to accompany your contribution. Gift certificates are themed by occasion from baby showers to birthdays to any occasion. This is a great way to let the account owner know that you send a gift, if the check didnt pass directly through their hands.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Drop the Hint to Friends and Relatives
              </h2>
              <p>
               Friends and relatives wont know to gift money to a 529 college savingsaccount without parents or the child mentioning it. Send them an email and include the link to the gift deposit form. Let them take it from there. No pressure.
              </p>
              <h2>
               Other Things to Know
              </h2>
              <p>
               The generous folks who send in gifts to existing accounts dont have any access to your account. They cant change investment allocations or see balances. Gifted funds are invested according to the accounts pre-determined investmentallocation.
              </p>
              <p style="text-align: center;">
               <strong>
                Education opens the doors to future successwhich iscertainly an important gift!
               </strong>
              </p>
