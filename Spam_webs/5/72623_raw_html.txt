         </a>
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XaG6Oxr0QTA/Uw6LAw4y9PI/AAAAAAAAD9E/vuIhgb7sHWI/s1600/PicMonkey+Collage.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XaG6Oxr0QTA/Uw6LAw4y9PI/AAAAAAAAD9E/vuIhgb7sHWI/s1600/PicMonkey+Collage.jpg', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XaG6Oxr0QTA/Uw6LAw4y9PI/AAAAAAAAD9E/vuIhgb7sHWI/s1600/PicMonkey+Collage.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="400" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="400"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-XaG6Oxr0QTA/Uw6LAw4y9PI/AAAAAAAAD9E/vuIhgb7sHWI/s1600/PicMonkey+Collage.jpg" width="400"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Love these ? Me too. Here is my semi-successful attempt.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          <br/>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a name="more">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Let me premise by saying I would love to learn to weld. Until then, I  will have to stick to a faux metal finish.
        </p>
        <p>
         I began by drawing my desired shape on a piece of OSB (Only because I happened to have it on hand. In retrospect I would had DEFINITELY used a smooth surface board ie: plywood, or paneling).
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SEWKjhdMRro/Uw6K2QHu43I/AAAAAAAAD8o/mDR9YcW6itw/s1600/IMG_3097+sign.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SEWKjhdMRro/Uw6K2QHu43I/AAAAAAAAD8o/mDR9YcW6itw/s1600/IMG_3097+sign.jpg', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SEWKjhdMRro/Uw6K2QHu43I/AAAAAAAAD8o/mDR9YcW6itw/s1600/IMG_3097+sign.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="240" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="320"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SEWKjhdMRro/Uw6K2QHu43I/AAAAAAAAD8o/mDR9YcW6itw/s1600/IMG_3097+sign.jpg" width="320"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Using a jigsaw and a circular saw I cut out my shapes (Arrow and the letters E.A.T.) I also wanted mine to have a raised edge, so I cut out 1.5 wide strips that would go around the perimeter of my signs.
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Eap9tT1gvW0/Uw6LEh5lPKI/AAAAAAAAD9Q/mT6H_IjrkCw/s1600/drill+holes+.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Eap9tT1gvW0/Uw6LEh5lPKI/AAAAAAAAD9Q/mT6H_IjrkCw/s1600/drill+holes+.jpg', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Eap9tT1gvW0/Uw6LEh5lPKI/AAAAAAAAD9Q/mT6H_IjrkCw/s1600/drill+holes+.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="240" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="320"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="240" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Eap9tT1gvW0/Uw6LEh5lPKI/AAAAAAAAD9Q/mT6H_IjrkCw/s1600/drill+holes+.jpg" width="320"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Next, I drilled holes in my cutouts so that I could insert short carriage bolts. I thought giving it rivots would make look more realistic. (I also think it would be really awesome to insert lights instead!)
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-MCMePK7o-Dg/Uw6NyE2nAvI/AAAAAAAAD9g/DvzgpeaIgcM/s1600/$_57.JPG" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-MCMePK7o-Dg/Uw6NyE2nAvI/AAAAAAAAD9g/DvzgpeaIgcM/s1600/$_57.JPG', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-MCMePK7o-Dg/Uw6NyE2nAvI/AAAAAAAAD9g/DvzgpeaIgcM/s1600/$_57.JPG" data-lazy-type="image" height="240" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="320"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-MCMePK7o-Dg/Uw6NyE2nAvI/AAAAAAAAD9g/DvzgpeaIgcM/s1600/$_57.JPG" width="320"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         In retrospect I would have added the carriage bolts at this point so that they would get painted at the same time. ( I added just a dab of hot glue on each one to ensure they stayed put.)
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gec5bsPMKAc/Uw6K8WLuyEI/AAAAAAAAD84/t4UYbSQw-6Q/s1600/IMG_3101%252Cm.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gec5bsPMKAc/Uw6K8WLuyEI/AAAAAAAAD84/t4UYbSQw-6Q/s1600/IMG_3101%252Cm.jpg', ' ']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gec5bsPMKAc/Uw6K8WLuyEI/AAAAAAAAD84/t4UYbSQw-6Q/s1600/IMG_3101%252Cm.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="300" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="400"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="300" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-gec5bsPMKAc/Uw6K8WLuyEI/AAAAAAAAD84/t4UYbSQw-6Q/s1600/IMG_3101%252Cm.jpg" width="400"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         This next part was a little bit of trial and error, I started by giving the wood a base coat of Metallic Aluminum spray paint.
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
        </div>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lbikIH_VVaQ/Uw6K5NzfCDI/AAAAAAAAD8w/8xNmQrDK42k/s1600/IMG_3099lkh.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lbikIH_VVaQ/Uw6K5NzfCDI/AAAAAAAAD8w/8xNmQrDK42k/s1600/IMG_3099lkh.jpg', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lbikIH_VVaQ/Uw6K5NzfCDI/AAAAAAAAD8w/8xNmQrDK42k/s1600/IMG_3099lkh.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="240" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="320"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-lbikIH_VVaQ/Uw6K5NzfCDI/AAAAAAAAD8w/8xNmQrDK42k/s1600/IMG_3099lkh.jpg" width="320"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Once the silver layer was dry I used a matte black and did some eratic dusting all over the signs (edging strips included) in order to give it some dimension.
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--qzruX2zPgU/Uw6MxAzZbPI/AAAAAAAAD9Y/XyI9kiNztKc/s1600/index.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--qzruX2zPgU/Uw6MxAzZbPI/AAAAAAAAD9Y/XyI9kiNztKc/s1600/index.jpg', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--qzruX2zPgU/Uw6MxAzZbPI/AAAAAAAAD9Y/XyI9kiNztKc/s1600/index.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="320" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="320"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--qzruX2zPgU/Uw6MxAzZbPI/AAAAAAAAD9Y/XyI9kiNztKc/s1600/index.jpg" width="320"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         I then repeated the same process with Metallic Oil Rubbed Bronze. ( I pretty much repeated the erratic dusting method with any metallic spray paint I had in my arsenal),
         <br/>
         * Like I said earlier, in retrospect I would have added the bolts (rivets) before the painting process.*
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-t8H5GsVj2V0/Uw6K_n2a--I/AAAAAAAAD9A/snkINZVKr-k/s1600/IMG_3102l%252Cm.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-t8H5GsVj2V0/Uw6K_n2a--I/AAAAAAAAD9A/snkINZVKr-k/s1600/IMG_3102l%252Cm.jpg', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-t8H5GsVj2V0/Uw6K_n2a--I/AAAAAAAAD9A/snkINZVKr-k/s1600/IMG_3102l%252Cm.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="400" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="300"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="400" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-t8H5GsVj2V0/Uw6K_n2a--I/AAAAAAAAD9A/snkINZVKr-k/s1600/IMG_3102l%252Cm.jpg" width="300"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Using a finish nailer I fastened the 1.5 wide slats of wood and tacked it to the cutout. I just liked that it gave it dimension and created the look of a vintage metal sign.
        </p>
        <div style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-zAzesRBlRZU/UxKgWosLFeI/AAAAAAAAD90/cPRT2O9DdQs/s1600/DSC_5269.jpg" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-zAzesRBlRZU/UxKgWosLFeI/AAAAAAAAD90/cPRT2O9DdQs/s1600/DSC_5269.jpg', '']);" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
          <img alt="" border="0" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-zAzesRBlRZU/UxKgWosLFeI/AAAAAAAAD90/cPRT2O9DdQs/s1600/DSC_5269.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="267" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="400"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="" border="0" height="267" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-zAzesRBlRZU/UxKgWosLFeI/AAAAAAAAD90/cPRT2O9DdQs/s1600/DSC_5269.jpg" width="400"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         Once everything was secured I took a rag with a little bit of stain and added it to the creases to give it more depth.
        </p>
        <p>
         Overall I dont think it is half bad for $4.00 in materials.
        </p>
        <p>
         ( Like I stated earlier, if I were to do it over I would have used a smooth board.)
        </p>
