        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
         Brian Williams Suspended From Nightly News For Six Months
        </h1>
        <div class="meta-info">
         <div class="td-post-author-name">
          By
          <a href="http://www.techmalak.com/author/maqabyah7613/" itemprop="author">
           Matthew Barnes
          </a>
          -
         </div>
         <div class="td-post-date">
          <time class="entry-date updated td-module-date" datetime="2015-02-11T00:07:17+00:00" itemprop="dateCreated">

           <div class="td-post-content td-pb-padding-side">
            <p>
            </p>
            <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Brian Williams Suspended From Nightly News For Six Months" data-url="http://www.techmalak.com/brian-williams-suspended-nightly-news-six-months/">

            <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">

            <p>
             Brian Williams is being suspended by NBC for the Nightly News following revelations of his
             <a href="http://www.techmalak.com/can-brian-williams-even-trusted-anymore/" title="Can Brian Williams Ever Be Trusted Anymore?">
              fabricated Iraq war story
             </a>
             , leaving Lestor Holt to fill in. Williams has been temporarily relieved from his position as Anchor and managing editor for NBCs Nightly News without pay yesterday.
            </p>
            <p>
             Many viewers of the program have lost trust in Americas most popular anchor who was one of the top ranking trusted news reporters in the nation, but now has seen his
             <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/cmo/2015/02/10/brian-williams-perception/" target="_blank">
              rating drop to 835
             </a>
             . There are some who are calling for his resignation from the network saying he just simply cannot be trusted anymore.
            </p>
            <p>
             Less than a week ago, Williams had apologized for lying about his time in Iraq while in a war helicopter, saying the crew he was traveling with was hit by a rocket-propelled grenade. The soldiers that were present that day called out Williams on his story, saying it was in fact another helicopter which was hit and not the one Brian Williams was traveling in.
            </p>
            <p>
             NBCs president, Deborah Turness addressed staff in a letter stating:
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              While on Nightly News on Friday, January 30, 2015, Brian misrepresented events which occurred while he was covering the Iraq War in 2003
              <br/>
              It then became clear that on other occasions Brian had done the same while telling that story in other venues. This was wrong and completely inappropriate for someone in Brians position
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             <a href="http://techmalak.techmalak.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/brian-williams-nightly-news.jpg">
              <img alt="Brian Williams Nightly News" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-2943" height="300" src="http://techmalak.techmalak.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/brian-williams-nightly-news-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             There is an ongoing investigation into more of Brians work as a result of the latest news. Brian has a net worth of roughly $40 million with an annual salary of $13 million dollars. A six month suspension without pay may not be enough for those who want to see a harsher punishment for him. Mr. Williams has stated that he is deeply remorseful of his actions and plans to win back his viewers trust.
            </p>
            <p>
             Some are speculating if he will ever return to NBC after the temporary banishment. If more evidence is found about Brian Williams lying on other stories, the network may have no other choice but to let him go permanently.
            </p>
