        <p>
         <i>
          Gardner won the gold medal in Greco-Roman wrestling at the 2000 Olympics by beating Russian Alexander Karelin, who had been undefeated for thirteen years. In 2007, Gardner survived a small-plane crash, swimming in 44-degree water for more than an hour.
         </i>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Is courage
         </b>
         something that you choose? Or is it something that's forced upon you?
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          If a steer
         </b>
         is more than four or five months old, he's too big to wrestle.
        </p>
        <div class="embedded-recirc--container norewrite">
         <a class="embedded-recirc--link link">
          <div class="embedded-recirc--image-container">

        <p>
         <b>
          The first time
         </b>
         I ever beat my brother was the end of my junior year. At the end of the match, he tried to throw me and I took him to his back and pinned him. Instead of simply losing, he bit me in the chest. I got up and told the referee, "He bit me!" The ref was like, "You won. Get off the mat." It's just the way it is when you're the youngest of nine kids. Every day was a battle just to get to where I was at.
        </p>
        <div class="ad-gpt-sharethrough">
         <div class="gpt-tag" id="ad-gpt-sharethrough">
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         <b>
          My favorite
         </b>
         part of Wyoming is as soon as I hit the border. I'm home, where life is good.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Everything
         </b>
         is work on the farm.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          I was snowmobiling
         </b>
         and I got stranded. It definitely went past what I knew as cold. There's a way I could demonstrate how cold it was. Imagine yourself in a T-shirt, sweatshirt, and fleece top, then dipping yourself in ice water, then going into a deep freeze for eighteen hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          I remember
         </b>
         having a vision, seeing a light, and the light came toward me. Jesus was on the right, and God was on the left. But then what really caught my focus was my brother -- who had passed away in 1979. When I saw him, I was like, "This isn't good. Because this means I'm about to die, and I don't want to die. I'm just starting to get good and master myself."
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          I keep my
         </b>
         amputated toe in the refrigerator to remind me of everything I went through. You know, don't be stupid anymore.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          You gotta
         </b>
         live to live.
        </p>
