       <section class="article-content">
        <p>
         Capping off their set of twelve awkwardly assembled Christmas presents, Pottermore leaves us this holiday season with a funeral. It may not be open casket (thank Rowling), but you can see a cloak-wrapped bundle in Hagrids arms. Mercifully, the Pottermore team decided not to animate it, unlike
         <a href="http://thenosemilk.com/news/entertainment/books/want-pottermore-christmas-innocence-back/">
          yesterday
         </a>
         . (And yes, I did move my mouse over it just to check, judge me if you will.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Id be lying if I said the funeral and
         <del datetime="2014-12-23T18:27:47+00:00">
          eulogy
         </del>
         new piece of writing from JK Rowling werent anticlimactic. While I realize that its logically the only scene Pottermore could have ended their Christmas event with, I was still hoping for something a little less cathartic and a little more vengefulsay, giving chase to the sunnuvanewt murderer. (And yes, I realizespoiler alert, but seriously, read the books already!hes not really.)
        </p>
        <p>
         Once I stopped expecting special and started accepting the moments as normal additions to the Pottermore site trussed up in shiny wrapping and ribbon, I enjoyed the whirlwind revisitation of
         <em>
          Half-Blood Prince
         </em>
         . There were plenty of moments that were skipped that I wish would have been included, though I suppose stuffing an entire book into a mere twelve moments means even more gets cut than even stuffing a book into 153 minutes of film. I just hope that the team goes back in and starts filling in the rather large gaps. (And yes, I realize they probably wont, but come on, a girl can dream for some Harry-Draco bathroom action*, cant she?)
        </p>

        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          *Not what you think. READ THE BLOODY BOOKS ALREADY!
         </em>
        </p>
