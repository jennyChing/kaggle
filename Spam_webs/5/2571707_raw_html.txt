      <h1>
       <a href="http://www.thecrepesofwrath.com/2012/01/09/mexican-rice/">
        Mexican Rice
       </a>
      </h1>
      <h2>
      </h2>
     </hgroup>
     <div class="entry">
      <p>
       Kramer and I had a pretty relaxing weekend. We had some friends over, went to dinner at our new favorite Italian restaurant in Brooklyn,
       <a href="http://ptbrooklyn.com/" target="_blank">
        PT
       </a>
       , and had a few drinks. For the most part, though, we stayed in. Kramer put together some of the new bedside tables that we got at Ikea last weekend, and I did a bunch of cooking. I also got a chance to play around in
       <a href="http://www.adobe.com/products/photoshoplightroom/" target="_blank">
        Lightroom
       </a>
       , which is a program that I should already know how to use, but, of course, dont. Kramer and I took at class at
       <a href="http://www.3rdward.com/" target="_blank">
        3rd Ward
       </a>
       last Tuesday so that we could both learn how to use it, and its been so much easier to work on my photos now that I am comfortable using it. I am the kind of person who needs hands-on instruction, so taking a class was extremely helpful. Maybe I will finally get around to taking a photography class there soon  who knows.
      </p>
      <p>
       This is the most simple, delicious Mexican rice that I have had in a long, long time. Its not too spicy, but its still bold, flavorful, and bright. You dont need any special ingredients to make Mexican rice at home, so its worth it to try it out for yourself. All you have to do is puree tomatoes and shallots, saute your rice with some garlic and jalapeo peppers, and cook the rice in a large pot in the oven until tender. Kramer and I had a hard time not eating this rice while we stood in the kitchen getting the rest of dinner ready, and you probably will, too. We ate this with some roasted pork, which I will post the recipe for soon, but the rice is great with eggs, in tacos, or as a side to any of your favorite entrees. Instead of your usual white or fried rice, try out some fresh Mexican rice tonight!
      </p>

      Puree them until smooth.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_7.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_7.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Rinse and dry your rice.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_8.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_8.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Toast your rice in a large pot or pan until golden.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_9.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_9.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Mince your garlic and jalapeo peppers.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_10.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_10.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Stir in the garlic and peppers until fragrant, about 5 minutes.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_11.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_11.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Add in the pureed tomatoes and shallots, chicken stock, tomato paste, and salt. Bring to a boil, cover, and place in a 325 degrees F oven for 15 minutes.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_12.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_12.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      After 15 minutes, take the rice out, give it a stir, and place it back in the oven for 15-20 minutes, until tender and cooked through.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_13.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>
      <noscript>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_13.jpg"/>
      </noscript>
      <br/>
      Serve alongside your favorite meal and enjoy.
      <p>
       <img alt="Mexican Rice" class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="/images/2012/01/Mexican_Rice/Mexican_Rice_14.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs="/>
      </p>

       <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
        Mexican Rice
       </div>
       <div class="ERSClear">
       </div>
       <div class="ERSDetails">
        <div class="ERSAuthor">
         Author:
         <span itemprop="author">
          The Crepes of Wrath
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
        <div class="ERSTimes">
        </div>
        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
        <div class="ERSHead">
         Serves:
         <span itemprop="recipeYield">
          4-6
         </span>
        </div>
        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="ERSIngredients">
        <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
         Ingredients
        </div>
        <ul>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 large tomatoes, cored and roughly chopped
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 large shallots, peeled and roughly chopped
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 cups long grain rice (such as basmati), rinsed and drained
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
           cup olive oil
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          6 cloves garlic, minced
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2-3 large jalapeo peppers, washed and minced
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          2 tablespoons tomato paste
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          1 teaspoon kosher salt
         </li>
         <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
          cilantro, for garnish
         </li>
        </ul>
        <div class="ERSClear">
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="ERSInstructions">
        <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
         Instructions
        </div>
        <ol>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Preheat your oven to 325 degrees F. Core and quarter your tomatoes, roughly chop your shallots, and add them to your food processor. Process your tomatoes and shallots until pureed and smooth, about 30 seconds. Set aside.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          In a large, heavy bottomed pot, add your  cup of olive oil and heat over medium-high heat. Rinse your rice in a mesh sieve with cold water until the water runs clear, then shake out as much excess water as possible. Add the rice to the pot and cook, stirring often, until the rice is golden and lightly toasted. Add in the minced garlic and jalapeo peppers, and stir for 5 minutes or so, until fragrant. Add in the pureed tomatoes and shallots, stock, tomato paste, and salt. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          When the mixture is boiling, stir to be sure nothing is burning to the bottom, then cover and place in the 325 degree F oven for 15 minutes. Remove the rice from the oven, stir, and return to the oven for another 15-20 minutes, until cooked and tender. Taste, adjust seasonings as needed, and serve with some fresh cilantro and lime wedges.
         </li>
        </ol>

         <p>
          Looks yummy! I am mexican and as someone already said, this is a VERY popular rice dish in Mexico, and a typical side dish for almost any meal. Its not called Mexican Rice in Mexico, of course. We call it ARROZ ROJO (Red rice) and we usually add a small handful of fresh cilantro before covering the pot and we add a few (whole) serrano chiles instead of jalapenos which are too big. In Mexico, Arroz a la mexicana (Mexican rice) is basically just arroz rojo with diced potatoes, carrots and peas and is also extremely popular.
         </p>
