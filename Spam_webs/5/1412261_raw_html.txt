         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1549" height="307" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_00111.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="Fresh Coconut Milk Tom Yam Soup of Grilled Banana Blossom and Chicken " width="460"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          It will not be an overstatement to say that banana trees accompany Thai people from their birth to the afterlife. Starting with the decorative objects made out of banana leaves newborns receive to invite protective spirits, and continues their entire life with the endless uses for banana leaves, trunks and fruits; finally ending with the female spirit ghost,
          <em>
           maae praai taanee ()
          </em>
          , who resides in banana trees and Thai beliefs.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1562" height="374" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/maae-praai-taanee.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="maae praai taanee, The banana tree ghost " width="300"/>
          On the menu today is tender, artichoke like, heart of an unopened banana flower, the
          <em>
           huaa bplee ()
          </em>
          , high in calcium, iron and potassium its a great dish for maternal diet.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1563" height="350" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/Bannan-blossom-botanical-drawing-antique.jpg" title="Banana blossom, Antique botanical drawing" width="176"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The rich coconut milk based soup is offset with the combined acidic tartness of the lemon grass, galangal, tamarind paste, kaffir lime leaves and limejuice, while the roasted garlic and shallots donate charred sweetness essence to the soup.
         </p>
         <p>
          As for the banana blossom, we will grill it, charred completely from the outside, eliminating its unripe, sharp and somewhat harsh taste, resulting in a magic nutty sweetness, which lends an exotic fragrant note to the dish without overpowering it.
         </p>

          <div class="ERSInstructions">
           <div class="recipe_section">
            Method
           </div>
           <ol>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             On relatively high flame, grill the banana blossom until deeply charred
             <br/>
              
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1570" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3014.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="Grill the banana blossom" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Peel the charred layers, exposing the whitish meat and cut into bite size pieces.
             <br/>
             ,    
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1571" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3059.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="grill the banana blossom" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Roast peeled garlic on low heat with constant stirring until charred, just before done, add dry chilies and roast to fragrance.
             <br/>
               
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1574" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3078.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3078" width="435"/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1573" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3114.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="banana blossom" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Roast the shallots on low heat with constant stirring until charred
             <br/>
              
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1575" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3081.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3081" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Cut skinless chicken breasts into
             <em>
              even bite size pieces
             </em>
             to assure even cooking. Cut at 45 across the meat fibers. To cut the chicken into beautiful pieces, place the chicken on the cutting board with the
             <em>
              skin side down
             </em>
             .
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1577" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3090.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3090" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Prepare fresh coconut cream and milk as describes
             <a data-mce-href="../recipes/basic_techniques/623" href="../recipes/basic_techniques/623">
              here
             </a>
             or use canned coconut cream.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Bring the coconut milk into a boil, (if using canned coconut cream, dilute couple tablespoon of canned coconut cream with water to substitute).
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1578" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3121.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3121" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add chicken and
             <em>
              briefly
             </em>
             cook for couple of minutes, or until only just
             <em>
              bit more than half done
             </em>
             . Immediately remove the chicken and set aside. (The meat will cook further from the residual heat and the chicken will remain moist and tender).
             <br/>
              
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1579" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3134.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3134" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Bring the coconut cream mixed the water to a boil.
             <br/>
               
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1580" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3141.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3141" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add roasted dry chili, roasted garlic and roasted shallots, add sliced galangal and chopped lemon grass. Keep simmering for 2 to 3 minutes on medium-low heat until fragrant.
             <br/>
                   
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1582" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3154.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3154" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add the grilled banana blossom
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1583" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3159.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3159" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add the oyster mushroom and keep simmering for extra 3 to 4 minutes on medium-low heat until both the mushrooms and banana blossoms are cooked.
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1584" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3168.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3168" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Return the half-cooked chicken to the pot
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1585" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3184.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3184" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Season with tamarind paste
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1586" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3194.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3194" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Season with fish sauce
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1587" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3197.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3197" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add hand teared kaffir lime leaves
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1588" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3201.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3201" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             TURN OF THE HEAT
             <br/>
             
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Squeeze the juice of half a lime
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1589" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3204.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3204" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add crushed birds eye chilies
             <br/>
             
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1590" height="290" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_3209.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_3209" width="435"/>
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Serve hot garnished with fresh and dry chilies.
             <br/>
              
             <br/>
             <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1591" src="http://www.thaifoodmaster.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/10/IMG_00112.jpg" style="margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;" title="IMG_0011" width="435"/>
            </li>
           </ol>
