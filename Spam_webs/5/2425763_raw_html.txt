        <p>
         In this week's fashion Q&amp;A, Cosmopolitan.com's style editor, Charles Manning, answers your questions about how to dress and where to shop when you're under 5-foot-4.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Q: I'm 5-foot-2. Is there a way for me to wear jumpsuits without looking ridiculous and extra short?
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          A:
         </strong>
         Look for something fitted and tailored  no loose, flowy shapes or gathered elastic waistbands  or, instead of a full-length jumpsuit, try a  romper. The shorter it is, the longer your legs will look and the taller you will look.
        </p>

        <p>
         <strong>
          Q: Is there a way to look taller without wearing high heels?
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          A:
         </strong>
         Flats and strappy sandals, especially in light or nude colors, that show a lot of skin will help elongate your legs instead of just cutting them off at the ankles. When it comes to boots, look for styles that end just above your ankle, at the narrowest part of your leg, so that as much of your leg is visible as possible. You can also try a fitted over-the-knee boot, but make sure to wear it with something extra short and fitted through the waist to make your legs look as long as possible.

        <p>
         <strong>
          Q: Is there a trick to wearing long sleeves and pants when you are short? I'm only 5-foot-1, and I feel like anything long just makes me look even shorter.
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          A:
         </strong>
         Avoid anything too loose or boxy and stick to cropped styles  ankle-length pants,  sleeves, etc. Seeing that extra inch or two of leg or forearm will trick the eye into thinking that your limbs are just sooooooo long they don't fit into normal clothes. It's subtle, but it works.  In a pinch, you can just roll your sleeves or cuff your pants, but it's a good idea to have clothes tailored to fit whenever possible.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Q: I hate shopping in the "petite" section, but I'm only five feet tall. Are there any normal brands that are good for petite women?
          <br/>
         </strong>
         <strong>
          A:
         </strong>
         Aritzia and BCBGeneration cut a lot of their pieces extra short, which makes them great for petites. The same thing goes for a lot of Australian brands like Cameo and Keepsake, which specialize in shorter, sexier clothes and cropped styles. And Topshop and ASOS have great petite collections, which are way more fashion-forward than standard "petite" fare.
         <br/>
         In general, though, you want to focus on brands that are a little sexier  Nasty Gal, Bebe, Missguided, etc.  since something that looks especially  short on a 5-foot-10 model could be just right on you. For this reason, sites that feature on-model shots, instead of just flat-styled product shots, are going to be especially helpful to you.
