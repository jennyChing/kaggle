                        <img alt="addtext_com_MTMwNDIzNzYyMjM_2_crop" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8137" height="713" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/addtext_com_MTMwNDIzNzYyMjM_2_crop.jpg" width="876"/>
                       </a>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       I could still smell those Black &amp; White Cookies peaking through the cracks of the small white bakery box. So neatly packaged up with a blue and white string made into neat little bow. You see every so often as a little girl, I would have Shabbat Dinner at my friend, Melissas house, where her father would bring home these yummy treats for the two of us. Ahhhhh.the warm buttery cookie with the smooth vanilla and chocolate glaze was like pure heaven. Now, so many years later, I still do adore this classic dessert and would love to try baking them myself. However, just no time. (You know me and dessertsthe faster and easier the better!!) Hence, I decided to go a simpler route with these guys! The kids and I had a blast making these Black &amp; White OREOS As you can say they are not perfection.but who really cares! They taste fantastic and make quite the show stopper at a PARTY!
                      </p>
                      <p>
                      </p>
                      <p>
                       <strong>
                        <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                         Ingredients
                        </span>
                       </strong>
                      </p>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       <ul dir="ltr">
                        <li>
                         <div>
                          20OREO cookies (DoubleStuff or Regular)
                         </div>
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         <div>
                          8 oz. milk chocolate chips OR any type of plain milk chocolate candy pieces formelting
                         </div>
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         8 oz. white chocolate candy or white chocolate chunks
                        </li>
                        <li>
                         vegetable oil (optional for melting chocolate)
                        </li>
                       </ul>
                       <p style="text-align: center;">
                        <a href="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5940.jpg">
                         <img alt="IMG_5940" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8139" height="682" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5940-1024x682.jpg" width="1024"/>
                        </a>
                       </p>
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       <p>
                        <strong>
                         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                          Directions
                         </span>
                        </strong>
                       </p>
                       <p>
                        1.) Create an Oreo dipping bar with all of your ingredients lined up. (Its so much fun!)
                       </p>
                       <p style="text-align: left;">
                        2.) Melt chocolate in a double boiler or microwave adding a little vegetable oil if too clumpy.
                       </p>
                       <p style="text-align: left;">
                        <a href="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5937.jpg">
                         <img alt="IMG_5937" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8138" height="682" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5937-1024x682.jpg" width="1024"/>
                        </a>
                       </p>
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       3.) Using tongs dip oneOreo into chocolate a little more than halfway covered. Lay on wax paper lined baking sheet.
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5945.jpg">
                        <img alt="IMG_5945" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8141" height="682" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5945-1024x682.jpg" width="1024"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       4.) Immediately place in refrigerator to chill and harden fully.
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       5.) In a seperate bowl melt white chocolate candy until smooth.
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       6.) Dip the other side of the cookie into the white chocolate so there is a slight overlap. (If the white seems too thin on the cookie let it harden bit and dip again.)
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5948.jpg">
                        <img alt="IMG_5948" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-8142" height="682" src="http://www.nibblesbynic.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_5948-1024x682.jpg" width="1024"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       7.) Place in fridge again to harden again until cookies are set.
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       8.) Chip away with a very sharp knife at the extra chocolate that spilled onto wax paper.
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                      </div>
                      <div dir="ltr">
                       Nics Tips  As you can seethey all do not turn out very neat! No worries.They still taste great and remember practice makes perfect!!!
                      </div>
