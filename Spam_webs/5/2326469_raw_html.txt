     <article class="hentry category-cakespy-recommends tag-candy tag-oregon tag-portland author-cakespy post-type-text" data-item-id="553b2829e4b0eb3719c75553" id="article-553b2829e4b0eb3719c75553">
      <!--POST HEADER-->
      <header>
       <h1 class="entry-title">
        <a href="/blog/2011/5/22/sweet-love-a-bakery-crush-on-willamette-valley-confectionery.html">
         Sweet Love: A Bakery Crush on Willamette Valley Confectionery
        </a>
       </h1>
       <div class="meta">
        <span class="date">
         <time class="published" datetime="2011-05-22">
          May 22, 2011
         </time>
        </span>
        <span class="author">
         <a href="/?author=551886ece4b0c77ffa0b06d7" rel="author">
          Cakespy
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
      </header>
      <!--SPECIAL CONTENT-->
      <!--POST BODY-->
      <div class="body entry-content">
       <div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" id="item-553b2829e4b0eb3719c75553">
        <div class="row sqs-row">
         <div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12">
          <div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-553b2839e4b0eb3719c7d584">
           <div class="sqs-block-content">
            <div data-src="v5">
             <p>
              <span class="full-image-block ssNonEditable">
               <span>
                <img alt="" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/553b26fde4b08ceb08a4242c/553b2823e4b0eb3719c6d635/553b2839e4b0eb3719c7d582/1305851838877/1000w/5716979166_4a419d6b81.jpg"/>
               </span>
              </span>
              A couple of weeks ago, at Crafty Wonderland, I met the two men of my dreams.
             </p>
             <p>
              They were at a booth surrounded by cake pops, homemade ptes de fruits, chocolates, and toffee. There was a dreamy sign that said
              <a href="http://www.willamettevalleyconfectionery.com/" target="_blank">
               "Willamette Valley Confectionery".
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Of course, they also appeared to be a couple, but that was ok with me, because I really just wanted their candy.
             </p>
             <p>
              And oh, what candy it was.
             </p>
             <p>
              <span class="full-image-block ssNonEditable">
               <span>
                <img alt="" src="https://static1.squarespace.com/static/553b26fde4b08ceb08a4242c/553b2823e4b0eb3719c6d635/553b2839e4b0eb3719c7d583/1305851958207/1000w/293552911.jpg" style="width: 500px;"/>
               </span>
              </span>
              Let's start with the cake pops. They really, really wanted to upsell the fact that they were gluten-free, and that is fine by me, and it made me happy for people who can't eat gluten, but it really wasn't of great importance to me.
             </p>
             <p>
              But happily, devoid of gluten as they might have been, they were full of flavor: very moist, rich, and extremely--like, your tooth might fall out but it would fall out so good--sweet.
             </p>
             <p>
              The pte de fruitwas a bit more subtle, still very sweet but made with local Marionberries. And shaped like a heart!
              <strong>
               Love.
              </strong>
             </p>
             <p>
              Willamette Valley also boasts a full line of confections, such as barks, chocolates, and tiny cakes.
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               Happily, if you are in Portland or the environs, you can pick up their goods at Whole Foods markets; it is slightly pricier but still delicious to have the goods shipped, which you can do via their website,
               <a href="http://www.willamettevalleyconfectionery.com/" target="_blank">
                willamettevalleyconfectionery.com
               </a>
               .
              </em>
             </p>
            </div>
           </div>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
