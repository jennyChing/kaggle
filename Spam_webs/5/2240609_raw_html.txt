        <h1 class="entry-title">
         5 Reasons to Stop Fearing Your Credit Report
        </h1>
        <p class="author-top">
         <span class="inline-icon-user">
          Written By:
          <a href="http://blog.quizzle.com/author/brice/" rel="author" title="Posts by Bill Rice">
           Bill Rice
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <a class="home-thumb boxed" href="http://blog.quizzle.com/2011/11/5-reasons-to-stop-fearing-your-credit-report/" title="5 Reasons to Stop Fearing Your Credit Report">
         <img alt="Are You Afraid of Your Credit Report?" class="attachment-large wp-post-image" height="683" src="http://blogquizzlecom.c.presscdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/scared-man.jpg" width="579"/>
        </a>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content boxed">
        <p>
         <img alt="scared-man" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18624" height="683" src="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/scared-man.jpeg" width="579"/>
         Its pretty cool that Quizzle gives away a
         <a href="http://quizzle.com/">
          trulyfreecreditreport
         </a>
         . Naturally, all of us here at Quizzle love to tell friends and family about that awesomeness. The ironic thing is most still dont get their credit report.
        </p>
        <p>
         This has always puzzled us 
         <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2009/07/a-free-credit-report-and-free-credit-score-you-say-okay-whats-the-catch/">
          its free!?
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Last week, we dug a little deeper and did an informal survey: Why dont you want to get your free credit report? Ironically, the answers were all very similar. Almost everyone was afraid they might find something bad on their credit report.
        </p>
        <p>
         Isnt that the point of getting your credit report? Thats a rhetorical question. Yes it is!
        </p>
        <p>
         Your credit report always needs to be in tip-top shape. If there are blemishes on it, you want to know about them as soon as possible so you can start your
         <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2011/10/10-ways-to-improve-your-credit-score-the-good-the-bad-and-the-ugly/">
          credit improvement plan
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you need more motivation? Here are
         <strong>
          <em>
           5 reasons to stop fearing your credit report:
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Usually Its Not that Bad
        </h3>
        <p>
         There are tons of
         <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2010/04/10-credit-score-facts-and-fictions/">
          myths surrounding credit reports and scores
         </a>
         . As a result, most people imagine scenarios and reasons why their credit score must be bad.
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Boy, I dont know I was late on my rent back in college.
         </li>
         <li>
          Ugh! No, my credit stinks. I dont make a lot of money.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         These are very typical excuses. The truth is, these kinds of things arent even part of your credit report. Youre probably imagining the worst, because youve never seen a credit report or have been misled about what is really tracked on your credit report.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="https://www.quizzle.com/get-started/quizzle-wire?qls=QZL_Getstrtd.QuizleBlog">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-16473" height="40" src="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/check-my-credit-score.png" title="check-my-credit-score" width="263"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Getting Your Credit Report Doesnt Hurt
        </h3>
        <p>
         Another common fear is that getting a copy of your credit report will somehow lower your credit score.
        </p>
        <p>
         Fear no more. This is false.
        </p>
        <p>
         You, as the consumer, are entitled to request a copy of your credit report and score whenever you want one  its your right. When you request your own credit report and score, it is called a
         <em>
          soft inquiry
         </em>
         and does not get reported or used in the calculation of your credit score.
        </p>
        <p>
         In fact, in 2003 the federal government made it your right to know whats on your credit report, passing the
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fair_and_Accurate_Credit_Transactions_Act">
          FairandAccurateCreditTransactionsAct
         </a>
         (FACTA). This guarantees you one free credit report a year, which you can get by telephone, mail, or online at
         <a href="http://annualcreditreport.com">
          AnnualCreditReport
         </a>
         <a href="http://annualcreditreport.com">
          .
         </a>
         <a href="http://annualcreditreport.com">
          com
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres the difference
        </p>
        <p>
         When you apply for a credit card, auto loan, or a
         <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2011/02/home-loans-101-what-you-need-to-qualify-for-a-mortgage-today/">
          mortgage
         </a>
         , your creditor or lender will make a
         <em>
          hard inquiry
         </em>
         on your credit report.This simply means that they are requesting your credit report and score from one or more of the credit bureaus and results in the bureau recording that inquiry and considering it in the calculation of your credit score.
        </p>
        <p>
         The difference is easy to understand. When youre checking on your personal credit report, there is no risk to a creditor or lender that you are about to get more debt. In contrast, if youre applying for more credit cards, getting a new loan, or buying a home, the additional debt you may be taking on may make it a little more likely that you wont pay all of your bills.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Mistakes Are Common
        </h3>
        <p>
         Creditors are processing millions of consumer records every day  mistakes will happen. If you ignore your credit report, theres a really good chance a mistake will land on your credit report eventually.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whats worse is reporting mistakes almost always have a big negative impact on your score. Here are a few common errors Ive seen over the years:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          A family members credit card ends up getting reported on your credit report.
         </li>
         <li>
          A creditor incorrectly reports a late payment or closes an old account.
         </li>
         <li>
          A credit card or loan is opened in your name by an
          <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2010/05/5-ways-to-protect-yourself-from-identity-theft/">
           identity thief
          </a>
          .
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         [
         <strong>
          Free Resource:
         </strong>
         Check your
         <a href="http://quizzle.com/?qls=QZL_kaleidic.creditblog">
          free credit report and score
         </a>
         ]
        </p>
        These mistakes can easily lower you credit score 10 to 20 points instantly.
        <p>
         True story here
        </p>
        <p>
         Before I was checking my credit report every month, an identity thief opened a credit account in my name. He cleverly had all of the correspondence go to his address and then after using the account, let it go into collections before I even knew about it. What a mess to unwind! In the interim, my credit score went from excellent to
         <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2011/09/why-is-your-credit-score-stuck/">
          marginal
         </a>
         overnight.
        </p>
        <p>
         Check your credit score!
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Bad Credit Only Gets Worse
        </h3>
        <p>
         If you have blemishes on your credit report, theres a problem. Whether its a creditors reporting mistake or your personal mistake, its going to impact your life.
        </p>
        <p>
         I say this over and over again, but it bears repeating:
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <em>
           In tough economic times, credit reports rule your life.
          </em>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         We all know they are used in approving credit cards, auto loans and mortgages. But, did you also know they are used in determining:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Your insurance rates,
         </li>
         <li>
          If you qualify for cell phone plans,
         </li>
         <li>
          If you have to pay security deposits for utilities, and
         </li>
         <li>
          Even are increasingly common in job screenings?
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2011/03/confessions-of-a-credit-score-modeler/">
          Credit modelers
         </a>
         and bureaus are continually
         <a href="http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052970203687504576655182086300912.html">
          tryingtomodelandpredictyourbehavior
         </a>
         . Experian attempts to predict your income, Equifax is confident they can predict your discretionary spending and FICO even claims they can predict if you will take your medication.
        </p>
        <p>
         All of these predictions are turned into models that are used by businesses. As a result, credit report information is used to:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Determine if and how they will work with you,
         </li>
         <li>
          What they will charge you, and
         </li>
         <li>
          What level of service they will offer you.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         If you want the best opportunities, the lowest price or rate, and the best service, youd better be working toward an
         <a href="http://www.quizzle.com/blog/2011/08/secrets-of-the-800-credit-score-club-2/">
          excellent credit score
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Credit Improvement Is Not that Hard
        </h3>
        <p>
         Even if there is bad stuff on your credit report, you can fix it. Credit improvement is easier than you think.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are only
         <a href="http://www.myfico.com/crediteducation/whatsinyourscore.aspx">
          fivemajorfactorsthatgointoyourFICOscore
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Payment history
         </li>
         <li>
          Amounts owned
         </li>
         <li>
          Length of credit history
         </li>
         <li>
          New credit
         </li>
         <li>
          Types of credit used
         </li>
        </ol>
        <p>
         You can see which of these you are having trouble with and start working to improve it.
        </p>
