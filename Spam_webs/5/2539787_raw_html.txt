        <h1 id="titlebar-title">
         Puente del Alamillo
        </h1>
        <div class="titlebar-text-content">
         <p>
          Santiago Calatravas Tribute to Seville
         </p>
        </div>

            <p>
             <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
              <img alt="Puente del Alamillo Seville" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2934" height="551" src="http://spainattractions.es/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/alamillo-bridge.jpg" width="1000"/>
              <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://spainattractions.es/puente-del-alamillo/&amp;media=http://spainattractions.es/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/alamillo-bridge.jpg&amp;description=Puente del Alamillo')">
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             Puente del Alamillo is a striking bridge over the Guadalquivir River in Seville, connecting the citys old quarter with La Cartuja island. It was designed by Spanish architect and engineer Santiago Calatrava (author of
             <a href="http://spainattractions.es/city-arts-sciences/">
              Valencias City of Arts and Sciences
             </a>
             ) and completed in 1992, just in time for the Universal Exposition of Seville.
            </p>
            <p>
             Despite its practical meaning, the innovative structure attracts visitors due to its architectural beauty and ingenuity. The harp-shape bridge consists of a single pylon angled at 58 degrees, counterbalancing a 200 m portion with thirteen lengths of cables. Moreover, it is the only bridge in the world whose designdoesntrequire a back anchorage.
            </p>
            <p>
             Puente del Alamillo has been originally built as a road bridge, but a pedestrian walkway was added recently to give passers the opportunity to admire Seville from above and to visit the Alamillo Park at the northern end of the bridge.
            </p>
