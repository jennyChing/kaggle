       <h2 class="post-title">
        <span class="cat">
         <a href="/category/general">
          General
         </a>
         
        </span>
        <a href="http://blog.lulus.com/general/a-warm-welcome-homemade-chai-tea/">
         A Warm Welcome: Homemade Chai Tea
        </a>
       </h2>
       <div class="post-content">
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <div style="text-align: center">
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            <img alt="" height="333" src="/images/blog/IMG_4352(1).jpg" width="500"/>
           </span>
          </span>
         </div>
         <div style="text-align: center">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           One of the best things about my hometown is the rich Indian culture brought over by a wonderfully large population of Punjabi immigrants.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           In rural California, I was able to grow up with the flavors of spiced samosas, pakoras and vibrant platters of pink tandoori chicken everywhere from local parties to elementary school functions. We had nearly a dozen local Indian markets, and at age eighteen I worked as a waitress at an Indian restaurant called The Bombay Oven.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           Cleaning off menus and ringing up orders behind a large case of mixed Indian sweets was a rare pleasure, but the real magic happened in the kitchen. A broiling tandoori oven flamed furiously just around the corner from an entire sweets making area, where the sweets cook would make each Masala Chai to order.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           Dark, sweet cups of hot chai would be boiled quickly and straine
          </span>
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           d barely a minute before hitting the patrons table. This is the chai I fell in love with; milky, heavily sweetened and potent!
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           Now, you dont have to get this serious, but try experimenting a little, and soon you will have your own personalized recipe!
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           Make your own Chai base and whizzy up a cup any time !
           <br/>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           <img align="left" alt="" height="372" src="/images/blog/masala-chai.jpg" style="margin-right: 10px" width="250"/>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: center">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           <strong>
            Ingredients:
           </strong>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Water
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            4 C.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Cinnamon
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            4 sticks
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Green Cardamom Pods
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            20 or so
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Whole Cloves
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            30 or so
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Bay Leaves
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            4
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Black Peppercorns
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            30 or so
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Star Anise
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            10
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Fresh Ginger
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            A piece about as long as your thumb, sliced in big chunks
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Black Tea
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            1/3 C. loose, or 8-10 bags
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Sugar
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            To taste (I like lots!)
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Milk
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Substitute any type of milk you please!
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
        </div>
        <div style="text-align: left">
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           <strong>
            Directions for Base:
           </strong>
           In a medium saucepan, heat water and spices. Bring to a simmer. Simmer for 5-10 mins. Turn off heat and add tea. Cover and let steep 5 mins. Strain well and store base in fridge for 2-4 weeks.
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <div>
         <strong>
          Preparing a cup of Hot Chai:
         </strong>
         Mix equal parts of base and milk, and heat over medium heat. Add sugar to taste, and bring to a boil. Immediately remove from stove and serve!
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </div>
        <div>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: small">
          <span style="font-family: Verdana">
           <strong>
            Wondering where to get spices?:
           </strong>
           If you dont have a local indian market, many natural foods stores carry bulk spices which are often far more economical than buying the little plastic jars at the supermarket. Also, try the ethnic section at your local market, you might be surprised!
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: small">
           <span style="font-family: Verdana">
            Try this, or tell us about you favorite Chai!
           </span>
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
       </div>

function PopupCenter(url, title, w, h) {
    // Fixes dual-screen position                       Most browsers      Firefox
    var dualScreenLeft = window.screenLeft != undefined ? window.screenLeft : screen.left;
    var dualScreenTop = window.screenTop != undefined ? window.screenTop : screen.top;

    var left = ((screen.width / 2) - (w / 2)) + dualScreenLeft;
    var top = ((screen.height / 2) - (h / 2)) + dualScreenTop;
    var newWindow = window.open(url, title, 'scrollbars=yes, width=' + w + ', height=' + h + ', top=' + top + ', left=' + left);

    // Puts focus on the newWindow
    if (window.focus) {
        newWindow.focus();
    }
}
