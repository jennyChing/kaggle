             <h1 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name headline">
              <a href="http://www.jadegamingnews.com/2015/02/jeffs-top-3-dnd-classes.html" itemprop="url">
               Jeff's Top 3 DnD Classes
              </a>
             </h1>
             <ul class="entry-meta">
              <li class="">
               <img src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4LNV9IgnYRg/Ujr7crKK_UI/AAAAAAAABdY/FB3BRdVzclI/s1600/user.png" style="display: inline;width: 14px;margin-right: 1px;margin-top: -3px;"/>
               By
               <a class="tooltip">
                Jade GamingNews
               </a>
              </li>
              <li class="">
               <img src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-Zvz7OCzeUEc/Ujr9DwHJ_1I/AAAAAAAABdk/rOv0N5r0XvI/s1600/date.png" style="display: inline;width: 14px;margin-right: 1px;margin-top: -3px;"/>
               2/22/2015 04:48:00 p.m.
              </li>
              <li class="">
               <img src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-xtxIqXWYuqQ/Ujr9LT4va5I/AAAAAAAABds/mdXWR4KxNhk/s1600/comments.png" style="display: inline;width: 14px;margin-right: 1px;margin-top: -3px;"/>
               <a>
               </a>
               <a class="comment-link" href="http://www.jadegamingnews.com/2015/02/jeffs-top-3-dnd-classes.html#comment-form" onclick="">
                0 comments
               </a>
              </li>
              <li class="">
               <img src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DydYuQGpSfI/Ujr9LRFmjnI/AAAAAAAABdw/JEo7bFd33NU/s1600/tungs.png" style="display: inline;width: 14px;margin-right: 1px;margin-top: -3px;"/>
               <a href="http://www.jadegamingnews.com/search/label/Game%20Reviews" rel="tag">
                Game Reviews
               </a>
              </li>
             </ul>
             <div class="post-header">
              <div class="post-header-line-1">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-4030136245494648751" itemprop="articleBody">
              <div class="moz-text-html" lang="x-western">
               <div dir="ltr">
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-DFuu2PtJabc/VOo4TeTNJBI/AAAAAAAADMg/UDnGwumadGc/s1600/Top%2Bthree%2Bcharacters%2Bmain%2Bimage.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" height="280" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-DFuu2PtJabc/VOo4TeTNJBI/AAAAAAAADMg/UDnGwumadGc/s1600/Top%2Bthree%2Bcharacters%2Bmain%2Bimage.jpg" width="320"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  So you want to role-play
do ya? You want to assume the mantle of the Hero or villain who
tirades across the country side saving damsels or murdering them
without recourse? Is that what you want? Well gud on ya bai.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  You can
talk the talk, but how ya gonna walk it son? In an RPG with an endless
array of classes and sub-classes, character traits, and personality
quirks available to pump up your precious immersion, you
can cast yourself as just about any character in the story of your
life.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  With all the choices, it is hard tyo know where to begin. So allow me, Jeff, and your fine friends at JADE to help
make your character development a little more guided and a little
less like a gaping chasm of soul-sucking tedium. Here are, what see as, the three best
classes in D &amp; D:
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-w8X9KRQKs6M/VOo4wOOknAI/AAAAAAAADMo/ndajEMe-JUY/s1600/druid.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-w8X9KRQKs6M/VOo4wOOknAI/AAAAAAAADMo/ndajEMe-JUY/s1600/druid.jpg" width="276"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <h3>
                  <span style="font-size: small;">
                   Druid
                  </span>
                 </h3>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  If you consider
yourself a classic hippie at heart, (And lets be honest with ourselves who doesn't) then you love nature. But
instead of spending all day hugging trees, which can of course take a
venerable toll on your arms, why not channel your enthusiasm for the
woodland realm into a badass RPG experience?
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  You can assume the identity of
one of nature's fiercest protectors and gain access to a host of cool
spells that prove just how sexy Gaia really is. From the ability to
summon animals, commune with them, ride them, touch them, or simply
smile at them politely... You can do a shitload of cool things
with animals. Couple this fact with being able to poison, regenerate
limbs, foresee imminent danger, and interact, communicate with, and
manipulate many elements, and you've got yourself a class that gives
the player a chance to interact with the surrounding world in subtle
yet interesting ways.
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2RXmZ9r6O0U/VOo5ehtTxdI/AAAAAAAADM0/KSUQzEQpGmw/s1600/Bard.bmp" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" height="320" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2RXmZ9r6O0U/VOo5ehtTxdI/AAAAAAAADM0/KSUQzEQpGmw/s1600/Bard.bmp" width="249"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <h3>
                  <span style="font-size: small;">
                   Bard
                  </span>
                 </h3>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Who doesn't love
being the passionate story-teller of the group? Seriously who, i'll
fight you. Few thrills compare to donning the mask of the charming
and witty aficionado who can charm their way out of the stingy
dungeon and into even stingier bed chambers if you know what I mean.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Bards are the shit not only because of a pungent charisma that grants
the player social skills allowing them to be a central component of a
lot of the party's major scenes of intrigue, but because a lot of
their spells do badass things under the pretense of smoke and
mirrors. It's the ability to jedi mind trick people into calling you
daddy, disguise yourself as the King's long lost cousin Henry (with documents to prove it), and pretty much
elicit valuable information from a monkey's anus, that can lead the
Bard even so far as entering Rogue territory.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Planning a heist? The
player fielding the bard can experience a cool multi-class high when
their powers allow them to play the socialite and the thief if a more
specialized class of criminal isn't readily available. Hell, they can
even roll high on a decked out persuasion roll and have the queen for
dessert. To some, this is the closest they will ever come  to knowing
the touch of a woman in real life. Don't take that away from them.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                  <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-5e_XAusytHE/VOo6px8WBTI/AAAAAAAADNA/44rWwIzs4mM/s1600/Sha%27ir.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                   <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-5e_XAusytHE/VOo6px8WBTI/AAAAAAAADNA/44rWwIzs4mM/s1600/Sha'ir.jpg" width="241"/>
                  </a>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <h3>
                  <span style="font-size: small;">
                   <b>
                    Sha'ir
                   </b>
                  </span>
                 </h3>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Though this class
is only available in 2
                  <sup>
                   nd
                  </sup>
                  edition D and D (and a 3rd edition redo in Dragon Magazine issue 315, 321), it comprises all
magic classes on the scale of badassery for the sole reason that
these magic users cast spells using a F@#$ing genie, and this genie
can cast ANY spell from ANY level providing you make an adequate roll
on the percentile die.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  But before you go breaking out your
collector's edition Blu-ray copy of Aladdin, know this: The higher the spell level, the higher
the margin for error on your dice roll. But that is what makes role-play fun, the risk. And if you have
horseshoes implanted deep in your rectal cavity, then
playing a Sha'rir could put you on a different level. So much so that, when local
authorities come a knockin' on your door, investigating the recent
string of magical incidents that have ruined their
town, and proceed to ask you your name, you can look at them
slowly and say Goddd.
                 </span>
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <br/>
                </div>
                <div style="margin-bottom: 0cm;">
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Role-play is no joke. In
fact it's more f@#$ing serious than just about every major
Geo-politcal crisis currently facing the Earth... Ok maybe not. But if
you want to do well, building yourself a cool character that can aid
the party in very strategic ways while keeping you wholesomely
engaged is critical. The above classes are the ones that can do the most
and not just hack and slash at meat like it's some divine calling.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Now while the classes mentioned in this article are super
and all, there is still an endless amount of character kits available
that expand on these basics to yield an even deeper level of
immersion, but if you thought I felt like writing about those too
then I want you to know that I'm moved by your blind faith. But, go explore
the material yourself, create a character that makes you feel all
tingly, and as always Happy Gaming.
                 </span>
                 <br/>
                 <br/>
                 <span style="font-size: small;">
                  Written by: Jeff Clive
