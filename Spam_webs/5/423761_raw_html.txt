        <p>
         Are you trying to
         <strong>
          build a successful blog
         </strong>
         ? Does it feel like an uphill battle?
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you wonder how to create a blog that captivates, motivates and inspires your audience?
        </p>
        <p>
         When I first started blogging,I had so many questions. I suspect that you dotoo.
        </p>
        <p>
         Last week I was lucky enough to join Denise Wakeman on her show,
         <a href="http://denisewakeman.com/adventures-in-visibility-free-hangouts/" target="_blank">
          Adventures in Visibility
         </a>
         where we talked about blogging and its power when leveraged effectively.
        </p>
        <p>
         Below are
         <strong>
          forty things you wish you knewbefore you started your blog
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether youre new to blogging or looking to improve your results,these tips will help you boost your blog performance andeffectiveness!
        </p>
        <h1>
         What You Wish You Knew
        </h1>
        <h4>
         1. Your Purpose
        </h4>
        <p>
         If I asked you why you were blogging, could you answer? You have to know why youre using a blog to market your business before you can make it a success.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ask yourself why you write, what purpose it serves and how your blog will support your overall marketing goals?
        </p>
        <h4>
         2.Commitment Matters
        </h4>
        <p>
         Get clear on your blogging commitment. Know
         <a href="http://rebekahradice.com/profitable-blog-strategy/" target="_blank" title="Successfully Fuel a Profitable Blog Strategy">
          how often you can blog
         </a>
         and stick with it. Knowing this will allow you tostay consistent. Becauseconsistencycan be a deal killer.
        </p>
        <p>
         Think about it. When was the last time you revisited a blog that wasnt recently updated? Probably never. Dont shoot yourself in the foot by making this big mistake.
        </p>
        <h4>
         3. Take a Look at theCompetition
        </h4>
        <p>
         Do your competitive research and learn whats working for your competitionand whats not. Find holes within their strategy and fill them. Theres a great big audience out there dying to hear what you have to say.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to
         <strong>
          leverage your competition
         </strong>
         , read this post from Debra Trappen, a Business Consultant with D11 Consulting and find
         <a href="Here%20are SIX ways to fire up your thinking around and leverage your competition:" target="_blank">
          six ways
         </a>
         you can get to know themfast!
        </p>
        <h4>
         4. Research Keywords
        </h4>
        <p>
         Research the top keywords and phrases that your audience is using to find you. Thats valuable insight you dont want to overlook. Take the guesswork out of your research and use tools like
         <a href="https://adwords.google.com/KeywordPlanner">
          Google Keyword Planner
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.semrush.com/">
          SEMRush
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h4>
         5. Clearly Identify YourTopic
        </h4>
        <p>
         Get focused on your topic. What are you passionate about? If you dont
         <strong>
          write what fires you up and gets you excited
         </strong>
         , youll burn out quickly.
        </p>
        <h5>
         Tool Tip
        </h5>
        <p>
         Once you identify your topic, use a tool like
         <a href="https://app.buzzsumo.com" target="_blank">
          BuzzSumo
         </a>
         to find top performing content. Identify whats resonating within your industry and then lend your voice to that topic. Figure out how you can write about it, but with your unique point of view.
        </p>
        <h4>
         6. Write Down Your Goals
        </h4>
        <p>
         What doesnt get written down doesnt get done. Write down your goals, identifyyour desired outcome and then celebrate your milestones.
        </p>
        <h4>
         7. Get Crystal Clear onYour Audience
        </h4>
        <p>
         If I asked you who your
         <a href="http://rebekahradice.com/4-simple-steps-content-marketing-success/" target="_blank" title="4 Simple Steps to Content Marketing Success">
          target market
         </a>
         is, would you know the answer?(Hint: the answer is not everyone) To succeed, you need to know who youre talking to.
        </p>
        <p>
         Have a deep understanding of who your market is and what theyre looking for. Dont assume you have the answers.
         <strong>
          Do your homework.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h5>
         Tool Tip
        </h5>
        <p>
         <a href="http://socialmention.com/" target="_blank" title="social mention">
          Social Mention
         </a>
         is a nifty tool with a ton of functionality that will help you get to know your audience. Dissect, analyze and research content based on keyword, phrase or Twitter handle.
        </p>
        <p>
         Begin by taking a look at your industry specific keywords and then drill down into the blogs, tweets and posts that pull up in the stream of most recent content shared.
        </p>
        <h4>
         8. Niche Down
        </h4>
        <p>
         As the saying goes, the riches are in the niches and its absolutely true. If you want to grow your blog, you need to niche down. As I mentioned above, get crystal clear about your target market.
        </p>
        <p>
         Talking to everybody means youre speaking to nobody.Show your readers how much you care by researching who they are and what it is that matters most to them.
        </p>
        <h4>
         9. Speak to an Audience of One
        </h4>
        <p>
         Makeyour message targeted and designed for your perfect buyer, client or consumer. Identify your core reader and understand what theyre looking for when they land on your blog.
        </p>
        <h5>
         How to Identify Your Core Reader
        </h5>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Put yourself in the shoes of your target market. What challenges do they face, what questions do they have and what problems do you solve?
         </li>
         <li>
          What is your competition talking about? Does that seem relevant to your audience?
         </li>
         <li>
          Now look to the Internet. Research keywords and phrases that your audience would type in if they were looking for your product or service.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h4>
         10. Know Your Limitations
        </h4>
        <p>
         What is it youre willing to talk about and what are taboo topics? Knowing youre limitations will make it easy to stick within those boundaries.
        </p>
        <p>
         For many, there are moral or religious reasons for staying away from certain topics. Whatever the case is for you, know them before you find yourself in an uncomfortable situation.
        </p>
        <h4>
         11. Create a Content Calendar
        </h4>
        <p>
         I live and die by my
         <a href="http://rebekahradice.com/create-and-manage-an-editorial-calendar/" target="_blank">
          editorial calendar
         </a>
         . I am a checklist girl and what isnt on the list, doesnt get done. That holds true in my business and flows through to my blog and social media. Create a calendar and map out specific topics like:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Company milestones
         </li>
         <li>
          Upcoming events
         </li>
         <li>
          Hot topics
         </li>
         <li>
          Industry news
         </li>
         <li>
          Core values
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h4>
         12. Never Stop Learning
        </h4>
        <p>
         Never stop learning and growing as a blogger. Become an avid reader and find ways to improve your blogging abilities.
        </p>
        <p>
         Write dailyand know that
         <strong>
          every word better connects you toyour audience
         </strong>
         .Be a student of your craft and learn from others who are succeeding within in your industry or field. Then take that knowledge and make it your own, unique to you.
        </p>
        <h4>
         13. Edit Ruthlessly
        </h4>
        <p>
         Take your time to spell check and review your article before you post. Eliminate any needless words or phrases. Keep it short and to the point, removing any adjectives or adverbs that only belabor a sentence.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you struggle with grammar, then pass off the review to a colleague or
         <a href="http://rebekahradice.com/grow-your-business/" title="How to Grow Your Business Beyond You With Virtual Assistants">
          Virtual Assistant
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <h4>
         14. Make Your Content Easy to Read
        </h4>
        <p>
         While it might be typical to talk in industry jargon within your office, dont make it a habit on your blog. Using fancy words or terminology doesnt make you sound smarter, it leaves your readers scratching their heads.
        </p>
        <p>
         Speak their language and make your content easier to readwith a tool like
         <a href="http://www.hemingwayapp.com/" target="_blank">
          Hemmingway
         </a>
         . Simply copy and paste your post and then look to your score for guidance. Update the misspellings, grammatical errors or complexities in a matter of moments.
        </p>
        <h4>
         15. Rework Your Opening Paragraph
        </h4>
        <p>
         You have one chance to grab your readers attention. Dont miss the opportunity to craft an opening to your blog post that seizes your audiences attention from the moment they land on your blog.
        </p>
        <p>
         Take these tips from
         <span class="hiddenSpellError">
          Copyblogger
         </span>
         to
         <a href="http://www.copyblogger.com/5-simple-ways-to-open-your-blog-post-with-a-bang/">
          open your blog post with a bang
         </a>
         :
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Ask a question.
         </li>
         <li>
          Share an anecdote or quote.
         </li>
         <li>
          Invoke the minds eye.
         </li>
         <li>
          Use an analogy, metaphor or simile
         </li>
         <li>
          Cite a shocking statistic.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h4>
         16. Walk Away
        </h4>
        <p>
         Ever find yourself staring at the screen, longing for inspiration? Yep, me too. At that point, its time to take a break. Get up, stretch, walk around the block, grab a cup of coffee. Whatever you do, relax your mind and your body for a few minutes. It will do wonders for your creative juices, I promise!
        </p>
        <h4>
         17. Offer an Enormous Amount of Value
        </h4>
        <p>
         Dont be afraid to give away your knowledge and years of experience.. Take the advice ofPauline Cabrera, the savvy mind behind
         <a href="http://www.twelveskip.com/marketing/content-marketing/278/7-easy-but-proven-ways-on-how-to-create-content-that-goes-viral" target="_blank">
          TwelveSkip
         </a>
         ,
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          An article will spread naturally if people can learn something from it. If the content contains information that readers can use in their lives, then the article will be read by a lot of people. When you write your articles, make sure that youre providing useful, well-researched, and credible information.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <h4>
         18. Give a Sneak Peek Into Your World
        </h4>
        <p>
         Let people into your day. Share what you do to help customers and how youve solved their problems. Give unique insight into your industry or niche that allows your readers to make an educated decision.
        </p>
        <p>
         We all love to see behind-the-scenes. Your readers are no different and will appreciate the chance to get to know you better.
        </p>
        <h4>
         19. Build Relationshipswith Fellow Bloggers
        </h4>
        <p>
         Build relationships with other like-minded bloggers. Go beyond the four walls of your blog and create conversations across other networks, including social media.
        </p>
        <p>
         Join blog groups that have created Tribes of individuals who share the same interests and will support spreading your message. My top resource is
         <a href="http://triberr.com/">
          Triberr
         </a>
          an amazing community designed to amplify your message and expand your reach. Im also a big fan of
         <a href="http://socialbuzzclub.com/">
          Social Buzz Club
         </a>
         . You find/connect with your fav bloggers, add your blog and start sharing others content!
        </p>
        <h4>
         20. Build Relationships, Not Numbers
        </h4>
        <p>
         I understand that your blog purpose is to draw potential clients in, but bashing them over the head with boring stats and constant promotional ads is not the way to
         <strong>
          build relationships
         </strong>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Your blog audience wants you to woo them. They want to get to know you and understand how you can solve their problems. Share your value, but do it in a conversational way.
        </p>
        <h4>
         21. Build Your List
        </h4>
        <p>
         One of the biggest mistakes I made initially was not building my list. I had a list Id built within my business, but not one specifically centered around my blog. Dont make that same mistake.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to get the word out about your blog, building a list is a great way to do it. Write amazing content that goes out to just your email subscribers. Get them excited and involved in your marketing efforts.
        </p>
        <h4>
         22. Schedule Your Marketing Time
        </h4>
        <p>
         Promotion is almost a bigger part of your blog than writing your posts. Commit time to getting the word out.Create separate images for all social networks to match the size requirements for each.
        </p>
        <h4>
         23. Use Eye-Catching Graphics
        </h4>
        <p>
         Use images to create brand awareness and as an instant identifier. Be recognizable! Use the same layout, font, and design to better connect people to your company. Allow people to connect with you and that essence that makes your business special.
        </p>
        <h4>
         24. Create Graphics Specific to the Social Network
        </h4>
        <p>
         I create separate images for all social networks to match the requirements for each. For example, if youre using images on Twitter, ensure that youre creating them correctly.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://pegfitzpatrick.com" target="_blank">
          Peg Fitzpatrick,
         </a>
         a Social Media Strategist whoconsistently uses images in a creative and highly effective wayoffers this advice,
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          The best size to use for Twitter graphics and images is 1024 pixels by 512 pixels. Batch your images and create a design template.This makes it easy toquickly create a series of tweet graphics that are ready to use when you need them.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <h4>
         25. Find Content Everywhere
        </h4>
        <p>
         One of the top questions Im asked is, where do I find blog topics? My answer? Content is everywhere! Its wherever you go, whatever you do, and in every conversation you have. Begin to look at everything aspotential content for your blog.
        </p>
        <p>
         Did a client ask a question that your readers might find interesting? Answer it and then offer advice on what best to do. It willposition you as an expert, andan authority on the topic.
        </p>
        <h4>
         26. Use What You Have
        </h4>
        <p>
         Look around at all of your currentmarketing collateral. Everything from old newsletters to emails can be
         <a href="http://rebekahradice.com/breathe-new-life-into-old-blog-content/" target="_blank" title="5 Tips to Breathe New Life into Old Blog Content">
          repurposed to become fresh content
         </a>
         . Why reinvent the wheel when you have everything you need right at your fingertips?
        </p>
        <h4>
         27. Create Content by Listening
        </h4>
        <p>
         Listen to your readers. Comb through their questions and find ways to solve problems. Offering a
         <strong>
          substantial amount of value
         </strong>
         builds trust and will establish you as the industry authority. Remember, trust takes time and will not happen overnight.
        </p>
        <h4>
         28.Find Your Own Groove
        </h4>
        <p>
         While understanding your competition is smart, watching their every step can be unhealthy. Stop mimicking everything your competition is doing. Every blog is different and every audience is unique.
        </p>
        <p>
         Do what feels right to you.
         <strong>
          Test, tweak and find your own rhythm.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h4>
         29. Stop Blasting Only Promotional Stuff
        </h4>
        <p>
         Stop promoting and instead focus on being an invaluable resource. While traffic and views matter, people arent going to take an interest inyour blog until you show that you care about them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Neil Patel,co-founder of
         <a href="http://www.crazyegg.com/">
          Crazy Egg
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.hellobar.com/">
          Hello Bar
         </a>
         , and
         <a href="http://www.kissmetrics.com/">
          KISSmetrics
         </a>
         offers advice every blogger should take,
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          These days, I do make money, and lot more than I used to when I first started blogging, but money is a side effect of solving problems and helping people. If I had to put a dollar value on my responses to comments and emails, I probably would be in red. But how could you put a price on helping people?
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <h4>
         30. Share Your Blogeverywhere
        </h4>
        <p>
         Add your
         <a href="http://rebekahradice.com/revive-old-blog-content/" title="How to Instantly Revive Old Blog Content">
          blog post
         </a>
         to all of your marketing including postcards, email signature, newsletter and other promotional items.
        </p>
        <p>
         Share your latest blog post across all of your social networks and tweet it multiple times throughout the week. Change up the verbiage to test what works and what doesnt. A common misconception is to tweet or post once. Never assume everyone in your audience saw it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Share, share, share!
        </p>
        <h4>
         31. Track Your Stats
        </h4>
        <p>
         Install
         <a href="https://www.google.com/analytics/web" target="_blank">
          Google Analytics
         </a>
         and track your stats. Not reviewing your statistics is like driving at night without your headlights on. You may get to your destination, but the ride wont be pretty.
        </p>
        <h4>
         32. Make Your Title Powerful
        </h4>
        <p>
         Dont forget the power of your blog post title. Take the time to create an attention grabbing, descriptive, SEO friendly headline. Tips to create a great blog post title:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Evoke emotion
         </li>
         <li>
          Personalize the content
         </li>
         <li>
          Solve a problem
         </li>
         <li>
          Simplify a process
         </li>
         <li>
          Encourage an action
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Use
         <a href="http://www.aminstitute.com/cgi-bin/headline.cgi">
          Advanced Marketing Institutes Headline Analyzer
         </a>
         to test your title. Submit your blog title and see how it ranks based on their Emotional Marketing Value analysis.
        </p>
        <h4>
         33. Get Commenting
        </h4>
        <p>
         A promotional strategy often overlooked is commenting on other blogs within your niche. It builds relationships and reciprocity. Someone who makes this look effortless is Adrienne Smith. If you want to become a
         <a href="http://www.adriennesmith.net/how-to-blog-commenting-superstar/" target="_blank">
          blog commenting superstar
         </a>
         , she is the one to follow!
        </p>
        <h4>
         34. Let Others Shareeasily
        </h4>
        <p>
         Make sure your share buttons are prominent on your blog. Dont make your readers hunt and peck to find a way to share your content.
        </p>
        <h4>
         35. Know Your Best Times to Share on Social Media
        </h4>
        <p>
         Are you testing and analyzing when its optimal to share across social media? From
         <strong>
          Google Analytics to Facebook Insights,
         </strong>
         use the tools available to you to understand when your fans and followers are online.
        </p>
        <h4>
         36. Make Your Blog a Destination
        </h4>
        <p>
         Share your knowledge and expertise by giving readers and subscribers the content they really want. Make your blog a destination and one they cannot live without.
        </p>
        <h4>
         37. Use a Call to Actionalways
        </h4>
        <p>
         Youve written a great post and youre ready to go live. Dont hit that share button quite yet! Make sure youve added your compelling
         <a href="http://rebekahradice.com/instantly-improve-website-credibility/" target="_blank" title="10-Step Checklist to Instantly Improve Website Credibility">
          call to action
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Your call to action (CTA) is the exactnext-step youre asking your readers to take. Whether its a subscription or download, give clear instructions. You never want to assume they know what you want them to do.
        </p>
        <h4>
         38. Join the Conversation
        </h4>
        <p>
         The single most effective way to build loyalty and trust is through conversation. A blog allows readers to get to know you on a very intimate level. If you let comments sit without responding or even acknowledging them, you are giving away a valuable opportunity.
        </p>
        <h4>
         39. Have Fun
        </h4>
        <p>
         Nothing is worth doing if its not fun, right? Have fun with your blog. Just because blogging is a commitment, doesnt mean it cant bean enormous amount of fun.
        </p>
        <h4>
         40. Stop Selling and Start Serving
        </h4>
        <p>
         Stop selling and start serving your audience. When you create content from the heart of a servant, amazing things begin to happen.
        </p>
        <h4>
         Final Thoughts
        </h4>
        <p>
         Its no secret that
         <em>
          your blog is a powerful too
         </em>
         l. Its the foundation of your online presence. Knowing best practices and mistakes to avoid can save a lot of time and energy.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to succeed at blogging, you need to bestrategic, focused, and totally zeroed in on your audience. So what are you waiting for?
        </p>
        <p>
         What changes can you make today to build a blog that your audience looksforward to coming back to time and again?
        </p>
