      Celebrating Holidays At United States Of America
     </p>
     <p>
      Every year United States of America has been entertaining a large crowd of tourists round the year. It does cost a whole lot of fortune but owing to the facilities and enjoyment the trips to The States are indeed made equally memorable! You are sure to have an album full of photographs and string of happy memories attached, but to help you to have a holiday with maximum enjoyment you must consult a tour guide offering
      <a href="http://www.tours-usa.com/best-west-coast-usa-tours">
       west coast tours USA
      </a>
      assistance. As you would hear from your tour guide, that visiting regions of The States at certain times will provide you with a chance to be a part of some of the grand celebrations held there. The top three holiday festivals celebrated at United States of America are:
      <br/>
      <br/>
      1. Christmas on the River
      <br/>
      <br/>
      This festival was started in 1972 when only at night time the parade was seen and followed by a magnificent firework show. However with changing trends, now this is a week-long celebration of Christmas held with a lot of pomp and show at Demopolis, Alabama. Several events are lined up for the much awaited week and at night a parade of boats take place. These special boats that sail on the Tombigbee River are specially lighted up displaying various Christmas themed decorations.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      As a part of the grand celebration there are several events held, the main three events held on big scale are, The Alabama Barbecue Championship (involving over 8000 participants from 9 different states and 75 judges), Christmas in the Canebrake (held in two halls and three homes night before the finale) and Jingle Bell Run (a 5k race for men and women held on the final day). So whether it is the west coast USA tours or a place of your choice, enjoy to the hilt.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      2. New Orleans Mardi Gras
      <br/>
      <br/>
      Mardi Gras meaning Fat Tuesday is an annual carnival celebration held at New Orleans. This is considered to be a celebration welcoming the Catholic season of Lent that is said to start after the twelfth night, on Epiphany (January 6th). It is a time when the full city congregates and shares its happiness by means of grand balls, beautiful parades and king cake parties. These celebrations go on for about two weeks before the Ash Wednesday.
      <br/>
      <br/>
      3. Spirit of America Festival
      <br/>
      <br/>
      A two day, outdoor festival celebrated at Point Mallard Park in Decatur, Alabama is one of the largest free Fourth of July celebrations in the Southern United States and is held in the summer season. Main events include the presenting of two awards, announcing a pageant and the most awaited largest aerial firework show of North Alabama.
