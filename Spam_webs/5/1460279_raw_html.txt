          <p>
           Three-quarters of seasonal and pandemic influenza infections never caused symptoms, a population-based comparison of serologic and weekly symptom monitoring showed.
          </p>
          <p>
           Only
           <a href="http://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanres/article/PIIS2213-2600%2814%2970034-7/abstract" target="_blank">
            23% of unvaccinated people with serologic evidence of infection reported respiratory or flu-like symptoms
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="https://iris.ucl.ac.uk/research/personal?upi=ACHAY31" target="_blank">
            Andrew Hayward, MD
           </a>
           , of University College London, and colleagues found in the
           <a href="http://www.fluwatch.co.uk/Content.aspx?ContentName=Overview" target="_blank">
            Flu Watch study
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-left desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           Just 17% of the symptomatic cases confirmed by nasal swab analysis saw a doctor for their flu, the researchers reported online in
           <em>
            Lancet Respiratory Medicine.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           "Reported cases of influenza represent the tip of a large clinical and subclinical iceberg that is mainly invisible to routine surveillance systems," they wrote.
          </p>
          <p>
           That has big implications for flu prevention, commented
           <a href="http://depts.washington.edu/daid/directory/duchin.html" target="_blank">
            Jeffrey S. Duchin, MD
           </a>
           , of the University of Washington in Seattle and chair of the Infectious Diseases Society of America's public health committee.
          </p>
          <p>
           "We've known for many years that you can transmit infection before you become symptomatic," he told
           <em>
            MedPage Today
           </em>
           . "What we didn't know was the extent to which this study documents that people can be asymptomatic."
          </p>
          <p>
           Guidance to keep people from spreading the virus, such as stay home when sick or cover a cough, is typically only aimed at symptomatic flu, Duchin pointed out.
          </p>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-left tablet-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           While it's not clear just how infectious asymptomatic flu is, it's likely that a large number of seemingly well, but mildly infectious people, account for a substantial amount of transmission in the community,
           <a href="http://www.ndm.ox.ac.uk/principal-investigators/researcher/peter-horby" target="_blank">
            Peter William Horby, MBBS
           </a>
           , of the University of Oxford, England, suggested in an accompanying editorial.
          </p>
          <p>
           The findings emphasize the importance of recommendations that everyone get vaccinated against the flu every year, Duchin concluded.
          </p>
          <p>
           The study, which followed hundreds of households across England during six flu seasons from 2006 through 2011, was unique in giving a true picture of the severity of the 2009 pandemic, he noted.
          </p>
          <p>
           Despite the "enormous international concern, expense, and disruption" at the time, the rates of infection and symptoms weren't significantly different than for seasonal influenza, Hayward's group pointed out.
          </p>
          <p>
           On average, 18% of the total population of unvaccinated people caught the flu each winter based on preseason versus postseason serology.
          </p>
          <div class="tablet-ad-wrap-container">
           <div class="ad-slot ad-right tablet-ad">
           </div>
           <div class="ad-slot ad-right tablet-ad">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right desktop-ad">
          </div>
          <div class="ad-slot ad-right mobile-ad">
          </div>
          <p>
           Of the quarter of those with polymerase chain reaction-confirmed disease, "people infected with the 2009 pandemic strain had markedly less severe symptoms than those infected with seasonal H3N2," the researchers pointed out.
          </p>
          <p>
           Heightened concern about the pandemic flu likely made people more likely to see a physician if they thought they had the flu and thus led to more reported cases, distorting the apparent burden of disease, the group suggested.
          </p>
          <p>
           "Measurement of the proportion of serologically-confirmed infections that are asymptomatic should be an early priority for any emerging infection of pandemic potential," they recommended. "This provides an additional index of severity complementing population level data on admission to hospital and deaths."
          </p>
          <div style="clear:both;">
          </div>

           </p>

            Hayward, Duchin, and Horby disclosed no relevant financial relationships with industry.
           </p>
