	&lt;div class="entry-content"&gt;
		&lt;p&gt;&lt;script src="http://video.foxnews.com/v/embed.js?id=3678647674001&amp;w=466&amp;h=263"&gt;&lt;/script&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;noscript&gt;Watch the latest video at &lt;a href="http://video.foxnews.com"&gt;video.foxnews.com&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/noscript&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Fox News&#8217; Host of &#8220;The Five,&#8221; Eric Bolling came out and declared himself an &#8220;Anarcho-capitalist,&#8221; the radical libertarian school of thought which declares the primacy of the individual over the collective.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;In a segment discussing Millenials and their stance on socialism, Bolling stated:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;Let&#8217;s talk about the difference between socialism and capitalism, OK? You may not like the extent to which we are capitalists. We may be overly capitalists, anarcho-capitalist. I know I am. I&#8217;m proud of it. That&#8217;s why I wore this today.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;Difference is profit motive. The profit motive makes you work harder. The profit motive makes you risk your own capital and labor. And the profit motive enables you to innovate and come up with new ideas that will help end up creating profits for yourself.&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Bolling has been making great strides towards libertarianism in recent years. Despite being hostile to libertarians over foreign policy disputes in the past, Bolling&lt;a href="http://video.foxnews.com/v/2839165931001/republican-party-infighting-threatening-gop-unity/#sp=show-clips" target="_blank"&gt;openly embraced libertarian ideas&lt;/a&gt; as the &#8220;wave of the future.&#8221; He has alsohas been on the recordsympathizing withEdward Snowden against the NSA, as well as rejecting the more establishment Chris Christie&#8217;s policies in favor of those of Senator Rand Paul.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;It&#8217;s an exciting thing to watch unfold on national television as a prominent news anchor becomes more and more libertarian.
