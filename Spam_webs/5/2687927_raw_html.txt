                     <p>
                      <span style="color: #000000;">
                       <strong>
                        On a full-moon day of May, when the trees were blossoming, Queen Mahamaya gave birth to a son. In the Lumbini grove, under the shade of a flowering Sal tree, The Buddha, founder of the philosophy of Buddhism was born. He was known as Siddhattha. (
                       </strong>
                       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gautama_Buddha" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gautama_Buddha', 'Siddhartha');">
                        <span style="color: #000000;">
                         <strong>
                          Siddhartha
                         </strong>
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       <span style="color: #000000;">
                        <strong>
                         = one whose purpose has been achieved). This is part 3 in my continuing series of Buddhist quotes from the Dhammapada. Each photo links directly to the original source.
                        </strong>
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </p>

                      <span style="color: #000000;">
                       <strong>
                        Buddhistverses from Siddhartha  The Enlightened One
                       </strong>
                      </span>
                     </p>

                      <span style="color: #000000;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </p>
                     <h5>
                      <span style="color: #000000;">
                       <a href="http://vi.sualize.us/view/1a773ccc4073ceb5c66655f37c03f972/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://vi.sualize.us/view/1a773ccc4073ceb5c66655f37c03f972/', '');">
                        <span style="color: #000000;">
                         <img alt="Boy with map of Africa on his face" class="size-medium wp-image-3482 alignleft" height="160" src="http://enlightenyourday.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/Boy-with-map-of-Africa-on-his-face-300x200.jpg" title="Boy with map of Africa on his face" width="240"/>
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       <span style="color: #000000;">
                        The World
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </h5>
                     <h5>
                      <span style="color: #000000;">
                      </span>
                     </h5>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Do not live thoughtlessly, in distraction and with deluded aims, outside the universal law.  The Buddha verse 167
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Rouse yourself and follow the enlightened way through the world with energy and joy. The Buddha verse 168
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Swans rise and fly through the air. How magical they are! So do the resolute conquer the armies of delusion and rise above the world. The Buddha verse 175,
                       <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhammapada" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhammapada', 'The Dhammapada');">
                        The Dhammapada
                       </a>
                       <span id="more-3480">
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <h5>
                      <strong>
                       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/ecstaticist/1048502108/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/ecstaticist/1048502108/', '');">
                        <img alt="Catch the Sun by ectaticist" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3483" height="240" src="http://enlightenyourday.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/Catch-the-Sun-by-ectaticist-300x300.jpg" title="Catch the Sun by ectaticist" width="240"/>
                       </a>
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <h5>
                      <strong>
                       The Awakened One
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       It is hard to be born as a human being, and hard to live the life of one. It is even harder to hear of the path; and harder still to awake, to rise and to follow. verse 182
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Yet the teaching of the Buddha is simple: Cease to do evil, learn to do good. And purify your mind. verse 183
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Whoever reveres the awakened one and the noble path, will live in measureless serenity and peace. 
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       The Buddha, verse 196, The Dhammapada
                       <br/>
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <h5>
                      <strong>
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <h5>
                      <strong>
                       <a href="http://vi.sualize.us/view/a36997c45b45cee81b4b4e22f517b290/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://vi.sualize.us/view/a36997c45b45cee81b4b4e22f517b290/', '');">
                        <img alt="enjoying the sunlight" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3485" height="240" src="http://enlightenyourday.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/Amazing-Enlightened-women-enjoying-the-sunlight-276x300.jpg" title="enjoying the sunlight" width="221"/>
                       </a>
                       Happiness
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Live happily, without hating even those who hate you. Be free from hatred among those who hate. 197
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Having tasted the sweetness of inner solitude and calmness, he who lives by the Law is free from fear and suffering. 205
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Follow the wise, the intelligent and the awakened. Follow them as the moon follows the path of the stars. 
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       <a href="http://www.ancientindia.co.uk/buddha/home_set.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ancientindia.co.uk/buddha/home_set.html', 'The Buddha');">
                        <span style="color: #003300;">
                         The Buddha
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       verse 208, The Dhammapada
                       <br/>
                      </strong>
                     </p>

                       </a>
                       Pleasure
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       The good man who speaks the truth and lives it is respected wherever he goes. verse 217
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Just as the man returned home from a journey is met by this relatives and friends verse 219
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       So the man of good deeds will be welcomed in his new life by those good deeds. The Buddha verse 220, The Dhammapada.
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <h5>
                      <strong>
                       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/chalkbass/2217876395/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/chalkbass/2217876395/', '');">
                        <img alt="Angry stone" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3487" height="159" src="http://enlightenyourday.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/Angry-stone-300x199.jpg" title="Angry stone" width="240"/>
                       </a>
                       Anger
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Overcome anger with kindness, evil with goodness, meanness with generosity and lying with truth. Verse 223
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Let a man be truthful and calm, and give to those who are in want. By these means he will perfect himself. Verse 224
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       Those who meditate and are watchful day and night will overcome their harmful thoughts and approach Nirvana.
                       <a href="http://books.google.ca/books?q=The+Buddha+books&amp;source=in&amp;ei=EntWS4ajAY3-NeDM-dME&amp;sa=X&amp;oi=book_group&amp;ct=title&amp;cad=bottom-3results&amp;resnum=11&amp;ved=0CC4QsAMwCg" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://books.google.ca/books?q=The+Buddha+books&amp;source=in&amp;ei=EntWS4ajAY3-NeDM-dME&amp;sa=X&amp;oi=book_group&amp;ct=title&amp;cad=bottom-3results&amp;resnum=11&amp;ved=0CC4QsAMwCg', 'The Buddha ');">
                        <span style="color: #003300;">
                         The Buddha
                        </span>
                       </a>
                       verse 226, The Dhammapada
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <h5>
                      <strong>
                       <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/msgreenclean/3417836827/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.flickr.com/photos/msgreenclean/3417836827/', '');">
                        <img alt="water droplets" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3489" height="240" src="http://enlightenyourday.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/12/water-droplets-175x300.jpg" title="water droplets" width="140"/>
                       </a>
                       Impurity
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <h5>
                      <strong>
                      </strong>
                     </h5>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       As a silversmith removes dust from silver, so the wise man rids himself gradually of all his impurities. verse 239
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       He who envies the food or gifts given to others will never know peace of mind for himself. verse 249
                      </strong>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       There is no fire like hatred, no rushing river like craving, and no snare like illusion. The Buddha verse 251,
                       <span style="color: #003300;">
                        The Dhammapada
                       </span>
                      </strong>
                     </p>
