         <h1>
          <a href="http://phenomena.nationalgeographic.com/2013/12/19/an-up-to-date-neanderthal-genome-fits-into-the-web-of-humanity/">
           An Up-to-Date Neanderthal Genome Fits Into the Web of Humanity
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="post-author">
          <span class="by">
           by
          </span>
          Carl Zimmer
         </div>
        </header>
        <div class="article-text">
         <p>
          Theres more news on the ancient human DNA front: as I report in
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/19/science/toe-fossil-provides-complete-neanderthal-genome.html?pagewanted=1">
           my new Matter column in the
           <em>
            New York Times
           </em>
          </a>
          , scientists have now reconstructed the genome of a Neanderthal with exquisite accuracy. Their genome sequence is as good as what youd get if you had your own genome sequenced with the finest equipment available today. And yet the DNA comes from a fossil thats approximately 130,000 years old.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2013/12/19/science/toe-fossil-provides-complete-neanderthal-genome.html?pagewanted=1">
           You can read more about this remarkable feat
          </a>
          and what it impliesin my column. But theres something more that I didnt have room to discuss that I found really intriguing. Heres the tree of human evolution that scientists have generated from the Neanderthal genome in comparison with other human DNA:
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_160513" style="width: 581px">
          <a href="http://phenomena.nationalgeographic.com/files/2013/12/mixing-tree.jpg">
           <img alt="Nature " class="size-full wp-image-160513" height="320" src="http://phenomena.nationalgeographic.com/files/2013/12/mixing-tree.jpg" width="571"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Nature
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Now zoom out to the tree of all living things*:
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_160514" style="width: 610px">
          <a href="http://phenomena.nationalgeographic.com/files/2013/12/nrg1000-i1.gif">
           <img alt="Brown, Nature Reviews Genetics http://www.nature.com/nrg/journal/v4/n2/abs/nrg1000.html" class="size-full wp-image-160514" height="353" src="http://phenomena.nationalgeographic.com/files/2013/12/nrg1000-i1.gif" width="600"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Brown, Nature Reviews Genetics http://www.nature.com/nrg/journal/v4/n2/abs/nrg1000.html
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Evolution is a mixture of flowthe cascade of genes from parents to offspring, and the criss-cross movement between populations and species. It has made us who were are, over just the past 60,000 years and over the past four billion.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           [Note: The image at the top of this post comes from the
           <a href="http://www.neanderthal.de">
            Neanderthal Museum
