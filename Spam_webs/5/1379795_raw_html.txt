        <h1>
         Suspended Strength Training in the Martial Art
        </h1>
        <h1>
         And Who is Ernie Pyle?
        </h1>
        <p>
         Before I get into a rather interesting discussion
         <br/>
         of real strength in Martial Arts,
         <br/>
         and offer a different viewpoint
         <br/>
         and training method
         <br/>
         for strength training in the martial arts,
         <br/>
         let me offer an anecdote of good wishes
         <br/>
         and quality of life for this Thanksgiving.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Outlaw-Karate-Secret-Year-Black/dp/1495421430/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1391872357&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=outlaw+karate">
          <img alt="how long does it take to get a black belt" class="alignleft wp-image-3025" height="315" src="http://freemartialartsonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/4-outlaw-karate-cover-orig-186x280.jpg" width="210"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/How-Kill-Ex-wives-politicians%C2%A0-everything/dp/1503207560/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1415996927&amp;sr=8-1&amp;keywords=how+to+kill+al+case">
          <img alt="how to kill book" class="alignright wp-image-3075" height="315" src="http://freemartialartsonline.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/how-to-kill-people-186x280.jpg" width="210"/>
         </a>
         Not many people know who Ernie Pyle was,
         <br/>
         but he was a war correspondent during WW2.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now,
         <br/>
         try this one,
         <br/>
         Ernst Hemingway.
         <br/>
         Everybody knows who he is.
         <br/>
         and many even know
         <br/>
         that he was
         <br/>
         a war correspondent during WW2.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now,
         <br/>
         bear with me,
         <br/>
         Ernst Hemingway
         <br/>
         wrote about drinking ancient wine
         <br/>
         in hallowed corridors,
         <br/>
         invading
         <br/>
         and living the life.
         <br/>
         Midnight samplings,
         <br/>
         caviar,
         <br/>
         a conquerors dreams come true.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ernie Pyle wrote about people.
         <br/>
         Hed talk about lone figures in a line,
         <br/>
         marching into the dust,
         <br/>
         half of whom wouldnt come back.
         <br/>
         Hed talk about starving soldiers
         <br/>
         sharing their last ration with him
         <br/>
         sitting in a foxhole,
         <br/>
         heating the ration
         <br/>
         in a steel helmet
         <br/>
         with a candle.
         <br/>
         Rubbing his hands together to keep warm
         <br/>
         and seeing the look
         <br/>
         in the eyes of the soldier with him
         <br/>
         just glad to be alive
         <br/>
         and with another human being.
        </p>
        <p>
         So
         <br/>
         caviar dreams,
         <br/>
         or humanity.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well,
         <br/>
         Hemingway blew his brains out with a shotgun.
        </p>
        <p>
         But the soldiers Pyle wrote about
         <br/>
         went on to create
         <br/>
         the greatest country
         <br/>
         in the history of this planet,
         <br/>
         a country that believed in and shared
         <br/>
         freedom.
        </p>
        <p>
         And,
         <br/>
         for all my friends in other countries,
         <br/>
         bushwah aside,
         <br/>
         thats all us United Statians want,
         <br/>
         freedom for all,
         <br/>
         not because were better,
         <br/>
         but because we can all be better,
         <br/>
         and I hope that this thought,
         <br/>
         my Thanksgiving thought for this yar,
         <br/>
         can be shared by the world,
         <br/>
         next year.
        </p>
        <p>
         So lets talk about martial arts.
         <br/>
         and Suspended Strength.
        </p>
        <p>
         The guy who can lift the most
         <br/>
         is the strongest.
         <br/>
         Grrr!
         <br/>
         He wins.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well,
         <br/>
         uh
         <br/>
         have you ever heard of time?
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets say a guy lifts a hundred pounds.
         <br/>
         Hes a young kid,
         <br/>
         and this is a good lift for him.
         <br/>
         But,
         <br/>
         when you measure the time it took him to lift
         <br/>
         he lifted 100 pounds in two seconds.
         <br/>
         One second up and one second down.
         <br/>
         so,
         <br/>
         200 pounds.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now,
         <br/>
         lets take his twin brother
         <br/>
         give him 20 pounds,
         <br/>
         and have him lift it 12 times.
        </p>
        <p>
         Hmm.
         <br/>
         240 pounds.
         <br/>
         Hes stronger.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh,
         <br/>
         you dont like my math?
         <br/>
         Well,
         <br/>
         Im just starting.
        </p>
        <p>
         Lets take that evil twin brother,
         <br/>
         you know,
         <br/>
         the one who cheated to become stronger by lifting less,
         <br/>
         and lets have him lift 20 pounds,
         <br/>
         but take take 30 seconds to do it.
         <br/>
         15 seconds up and 15 seconds down.
         <br/>
         Do the math
         <br/>
         15 times 20
         <br/>
         300 pounds.
         <br/>
         Hes stronger.
        </p>
        <p>
         Not only is he stronger,
         <br/>
         but because he didnt stress his body
         <br/>
         there is less chance of injury,
         <br/>
         pulled muscle,
         <br/>
         or even fatigue.
        </p>
        <p>
         And,
         <br/>
         Im going to tell you something weird,
         <br/>
         his strength is going to improve on a sharper curve
         <br/>
         than the two second boy.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now,
         <br/>
         you think Im talking Tai Chi here,
         <br/>
         right?
        </p>
        <p>
         Well,
         <br/>
         sort of,
         <br/>
         but sort of not.
         <br/>
         Other arts than Tai Chi go slow.
         <br/>
         And,
         <br/>
         hate to say it,
         <br/>
         guys who get old or injured
         <br/>
         slow their stuff down.
        </p>
        <p>
         The point here is that
         <br/>
         power comes in different speeds.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes,
         <br/>
         there is explosive power,
         <br/>
         great stuff,
         <br/>
         recommended if you want your art to work.
         <br/>
         But there is also suspended power.
         <br/>
         You wont lift the most,
         <br/>
         but youll lift longer,
         <br/>
         last longer,
         <br/>
         not have injuries,
         <br/>
         be able to rehabilitate injuries.
        </p>
        <p>
         I use to work out at karate hard,
         <br/>
         an hour felt like eight.
         <br/>
         But,
         <br/>
         I cant do that anymore.
         <br/>
         But,
         <br/>
         I can work out for eight hours
         <br/>
         and make it feel like one.
        </p>
        <p>
         I just do my forms slower,
         <br/>
         and work on suspended strength.
        </p>
        <p>
         Oh,
         <br/>
         WARNING!
         <br/>
         be careful when doing kicks or extreme positions slowly,
         <br/>
         suspending the leg because you could throw out your back.
        </p>
        <p>
         When you explode,
         <br/>
         you see,
         <br/>
         you only use part of your leg,
         <br/>
         part at the beginning and part at the end (to stop it).
         <br/>
         When you go slow you use all your leg,
         <br/>
         and this puts stress on body parts
         <br/>
         that never knew stress before.
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay,
         <br/>
         if you want to learn slow,
         <br/>
         get stronger than an ox whos been eating good,
         <br/>
         then check out the Five Army
         <a href="http://monstermartialarts.com/martial-arts/five-army-tai-chi-chuan/" target="_blank" title="strength training martial arts">
          Tai Chi Chuan
         </a>
         ,
        </p>
        <p>
         Actually,
         <br/>
         theres three systems on the course.
         <br/>
         An easy basics system,
         <br/>
         an intermediate system that just sort of explodes on the cerebral cortex,
         <br/>
         and then theres the classical eight animals.
        </p>
        <p>
         Think about it,
         <br/>
         three systems,
         <br/>
         a book,
         <br/>
         the ability to age gracefully,
         <br/>
         a complete art,
         <br/>
         only ten bucks a disk.
         <br/>
         Whoa,
         <br/>
         thats a Fng deal!
        </p>
        <p>
         Okay,
         <br/>
         guys and gals,
         <br/>
         you have the greatest week ever,
         <br/>
         and remember
         <br/>
         every time you work out
         <br/>
         its one step closer
         <br/>
         to the truth of you.
         <br/>
         And,
         <br/>
         man,
         <br/>
         that truth is grand.
        </p>
        <p>
         Later
        </p>
        <p>
         Al
        </p>
