       <div class="post-title">
        <h2>
         <a href="https://blog.profitbricks.com/cloud-downtime-cloud-reboots-cloud-providers-forcing-customers/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Cloud Downtime and Cloud Reboots: Why Some Cloud Providers Are Forcing Them on Customers">
          Cloud Downtime and Cloud Reboots: Why Some Cloud Providers Are Forcing Them on Customers
         </a>
        </h2>
       </div>
       <!--/post-title -->
       <div class="post-date">
        <em>
         On 10.01.14, In
         <a href="https://blog.profitbricks.com/cloud-computing-news/" rel="category tag">
          Cloud Computing
         </a>
         , by
         <a href="https://blog.profitbricks.com/author/william/" rel="author" title="Posts by William Toll">
          William Toll
         </a>
        </em>
       </div>
       <p>
        Amazon Web Services, Rackspace and other Cloud Providers havethis weekannounced cloud downtime and cloud server reboots affecting hundreds of thousands of customers. The Xen hypervisor security alert (XSA-108)forcedthese cloud computing providers to notify their customers,at very short notice (sometimes less than 48 hours), that their virtualserverswouldbe restarted.For many companies, downtime and reboots completelyhalt normal business operations or at least hinder them.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         What are the consequences?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        Amazons sprawling cloudfeatures many availability zonesand due to the design of their cloud platform, most customers that are affected will see their cloud servers experience downtime. The time window
        <a href="http://www.zdnet.com/aws-users-fret-over-downtime-ahead-of-amazons-massive-ec2-reboot-7000034041/" target="_blank">
         Amazon
        </a>
        gaveEC2 customerswassometime between
        <span data-term="goog_1560745421">
         7pm
        </span>
        September 25
        <sup>
         th
        </sup>
        and September 30th,leaving users to ensure they were adequately prepared torestart their applications, check database synchronizations and review log files.
       </p>
       <p>
        An undefined period of downtimerequires system administrators, network engineers and database administrators to be on standby ready and waiting to restart applications and databases. Complicating matters is that the only way to avoid the downtime is to follow Amazons best practice guideline of running applications and databases in multiple availability zones.This drives up costsand creates complicated cloud environments that need expert design, configuration and management skills.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         The cloud was supposed toabstracthardware  what happened?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        First generation cloud providers like Amazons AWS EC2 and Rackspace werent designed using modern hardware and cloud provisioning software, The result of which has their users suffering when they email customers individually or en masse like they have done this week. ProfitBricks is a second generation cloud computing platform and we architected our cloud computing IaaS platform expressly to avoid downtime resulting from hardware updates and reboots.
       </p>
       <p>
        ProfitBricks Cloud Computing IaaS platform features a Live Migration routine, allowing us to evacuate a physical server and move customers cloud servers to a
        <a href="https://blog.profitbricks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Clouds.jpg">
         <img alt="Clouds" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-4174" height="222" src="https://blog.profitbricks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Clouds-300x222.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
        separate server.We accomplish this at
        <a href="https://www.profitbricks.com/technical-info#section=under-the-hood" target="_blank">
         InfiniBand
        </a>
        speed which means thatcustomersapplications and databases experience zero downtime or performance loss while our engineers repair, replace or update physical servers.Halted and hindered business operations are not part of our discourse.
       </p>
       <p>
        We believe that Cloud computings IaaS should, as it was always intended,enable users to simply focus on their cloud servers and the applications and databases on them, and not worry about physical hardware, data center cooling, power and so on.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <strong>
         What can Amazon EC2 and Rackspace cloud users do to avoid downtime in the future?
        </strong>
       </h2>
       <p>
        To prevent disruptions caused by downtime and reboots, look for a provider like ProfitBricks that has implemented a Cloud Computing 2.0 architecture. Consider taking advantage of cloudcomputings option to run applications and databases in a multi-cloud environment.Whichever method you choose, be sure toask before you make a commitment that may cause you to have downtime and headaches at a later time.
       </p>
       <p>
        What do you think about these announcements? How would they affect your business?
       </p>
