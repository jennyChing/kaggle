    <p align="justify">
     <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2008/11/18/science/18obroach.html?_r=1&amp;partner=rss&amp;emc=rss" target="_blank">
      Apparently cockroaches have the ability to plan out escape routes
     </a>
     . Not to the point that we could predict which route they might take, but their movement is not necessarily random.
    </p>
    <p align="justify">
     In other words, we're probably not that far off from them being able to plan out how to rebel against humans. It will probably involve a good amount of disease, rock and roll, and sticky floors that we get trapped on (The last one as a matter of revenge).
    </p>
    <p align="justify">
     - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
    </p>
    <p align="justify">
     On a side note,
     <em>
      Y: The Last Man
     </em>
     by Brian K. Vaughan is a pretty good investigation of the contagion angle, as is
     <em>
      World War Z
     </em>
     by Max Brooks. Be forewarned that
     <em>
      World War Z
     </em>
     will be disturbing in how accurately it could depict such a sudden outbreak running rampant and how our world leaders would react. Also be forewarned that
     <em>
      Y: The Last Man
     </em>
     can drag in the middle, but finishes with one of the best series endings possible.
    </p>
