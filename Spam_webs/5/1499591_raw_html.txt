        Goat Cheese and Sun-dried Tomato Stuffed Chicken Thighs

       <div class="span6 body bottom-padding">
        <p>
         <span style="line-height:1.45em;">
          I assume everyone in America has been to Carrabbas at least once. And I also assume everyone has tried Chicken Bryan, because as far as Im concerned its the only thing on the menu.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you somehow have never had it, its grilled chicken topped with goat cheese, sun-dried tomatoes, basil and a lemon butter sauce. There are about a million copycat recipes circulating the Internet, and I even have the recipe from Carrabbas.
        </p>
        <p>
         The thing is, when you make Chicken Bryan yourself, it just doesnt taste as good as at the restaurant. So, I stopped trying. This recipe is inspired by Chicken Bryan, without trying to be it.
        </p>
        <p>
         I originally was going to stuff chicken breasts with goat cheese and sun-dried tomatoes. But after browsing through the Epicurious app on my
         <a href="http://www.t-mobile.com/cell-phones.html" target="blank">
          windows phone on T-mobile
         </a>
         , I was inspired by an awesome recipe for
         <a href="http://www.epicurious.com/recipes/food/views/Chicken-Thighs-Stuffed-with-Plantain-and-Serrano-Ham-238286" target="blank">
          stuffed chicken thighs
         </a>
         . Crispy chicken skin AND goat cheese? I may have outdone Carrabbas afterall. I used a ton of chicken thighs so I could have a lot leftover, but you can easily adjust  just use 1 oz of goat cheese, 1/2 tbsp sun-dried tomatoes and one basil leaf per chicken thigh.
        </p>
        <p>
         I could only make meals that contain goat cheese for the rest of my life and be perfectly happy.
        </p>

        </p>
        <p>
         Serves 4-6
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ingredients
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         8 oz goat cheese
        </p>
        <p>
          cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
        </p>
        <p>
         6-8 basil leaves (one for each chicken thigh)
        </p>
        <p>
         6-8 bone-in, skin on chicken thighs
        </p>
        <p>
         2 tbsp olive oil
        </p>
        <p>
         Salt and pepper to taste
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Instructions
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>

        </p>
        <p>
         Mix together goat cheese and sun-dried tomatoes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Gently lift skin from chicken thighs and spoon about 1 ounce of the goat cheese mixture under skin. Place a basil leave on top of the goat cheese, then pat the skin down to evenly distribute the goat cheese.
        </p>
        <p>
         Repeat with remaining chicken.
        </p>
        <p>
         Place chicken in a 9 x 13 baking dish. Coat well with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
        </p>
        <p>
         Bake chicken for 45-55 minutes, or until the juices run clear.
        </p>
