		&lt;h1 class="entry-title"&gt;&#8220;My Problem with the World&#8221; by Brenda Paro&lt;/h1&gt;
				&lt;div class="entry-meta"&gt;
			&lt;span class="byline"&gt;August 17, 2009&lt;/span&gt;		&lt;/div&gt;&lt;!-- .entry-meta --&gt;
		
	&lt;/header&gt;&lt;!-- .entry-header --&gt;

		&lt;div class="entry-content"&gt;
		&lt;p style="padding-left: 0px;"&gt;&lt;em&gt;Brenda Paro&lt;/em&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
&lt;strong&gt;&lt;br /&gt;
MY PROBLEM WITH THE WORLD&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="padding-left: 0px;"&gt;Occurs because pregnant women&lt;br /&gt;
wear signs on their bellies&lt;br /&gt;
that say theyve spread their legs.&lt;br /&gt;
Because the smell of sewers&lt;br /&gt;
rises hot and thick from below&lt;br /&gt;
sidewalks, beneath the feet&lt;br /&gt;
of suited business men.&lt;br /&gt;
Because wherever people congregate&lt;br /&gt;
(in office meetings, grocery stores),&lt;br /&gt;
it means a room full of genitalia&lt;br /&gt;
that would all fit together&lt;br /&gt;
if they tried.&lt;br /&gt;
Because toilets are pipe ends&lt;br /&gt;
sticking out of floors,&lt;br /&gt;
inescapable as fact, and&lt;br /&gt;
because the handsome boy who&lt;br /&gt;
holds the door for the girl&lt;br /&gt;
behind him still&lt;br /&gt;
makes a sticky mess&lt;br /&gt;
on the bed without a condom.&lt;br /&gt;
Because the bookstore clerk beams&lt;br /&gt;
shyly and holds out Fitzgerald&lt;br /&gt;
with a small hand, then&lt;br /&gt;
jerks off in the employee bathroom&lt;br /&gt;
later, thinking about your ass&lt;br /&gt;
while you walked out.&lt;br /&gt;
Because your parents had sex&lt;br /&gt;
on dirty sheets one day and out&lt;br /&gt;
you slid, flaked with wax and wet&lt;br /&gt;
with membrane, in a hospital&lt;br /&gt;
where someone died messily,&lt;br /&gt;
one floor below,&lt;br /&gt;
an hour earlier.&lt;br /&gt;
Because flies linger around&lt;br /&gt;
graveyards, and because&lt;br /&gt;
a poem cant hide forever&lt;br /&gt;
what is really happening.&lt;br /&gt;
Because the surface&lt;br /&gt;
is thin&lt;br /&gt;
as human skin,&lt;br /&gt;
pulsing with the blood, black&lt;br /&gt;
tar and grease&lt;br /&gt;
that keep the body&lt;br /&gt;
beating and limping along:&lt;br /&gt;
each thing pressed&lt;br /&gt;
against the next,&lt;br /&gt;
like a strangers arm&lt;br /&gt;
youre forced to lean against,&lt;br /&gt;
without looking at each other,&lt;br /&gt;
the entire bus ride home.&lt;/p&gt;
