         <h2>
          <a href="http://www.howtobeadad.com/2013/20093/how-to-give-gifts-actually-for-you" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to How to Give Gifts that Are Actually for You">
           How to Give Gifts that Are Actually for You
          </a>
         </h2>
         <p class="author">
          Posted by
          <a href="http://www.howtobeadad.com/author/andy" rel="author" title="Posts by Andy">
           Andy
          </a>
          , under
          <a href="http://www.howtobeadad.com/category/equipment" rel="category tag">

         <div class="comments-social-single">
          <span class="st_facebook_hcount" displaytext="share" st_title="How to Give Gifts that Are Actually for You" st_url="http://www.howtobeadad.com/2013/20093/how-to-give-gifts-actually-for-you">
          </span>
          <span class="st_twitter_hcount" displaytext="share" st_title="How to Give Gifts that Are Actually for You" st_url="http://www.howtobeadad.com/2013/20093/how-to-give-gifts-actually-for-you" st_via="HowToBeADad">
          </span>
          <span class="st_pinterest_hcount" displaytext="share" st_title="How to Give Gifts that Are Actually for You" st_url="http://www.howtobeadad.com/2013/20093/how-to-give-gifts-actually-for-you">

          <span class="st_sharethis_hcount" displaytext="share" st_title="How to Give Gifts that Are Actually for You" st_url="http://www.howtobeadad.com/2013/20093/how-to-give-gifts-actually-for-you">

         <br/>
         <p>
          <img alt="How to Give Gifts that Are Actually for You" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-20118" height="250" src="http://www.howtobeadad.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/give-yourself-gifts-header.jpg" width="575"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Every holiday season, we shop for our kids, our family and our friends, but lets admit it, were pretty frigging interested in what we might get. There are so many rad things! Gotta look out for number one, even if were not supposed to say that. Hmmmm Ah ha!
         </p>
         <p>
          What if there were a way to magnify the effect of your gift giving, maximize your money, and ensure you got amazingly rad gifts yourself?
         </p>
         <p>
          Imagine if you bought your family the things YOU wanted! Two birds, one stone! But YOURE one of the birds and the stone might be something like, lets sayyyy oh I dont knowwww an XBOX! Go with me on this
         </p>
         <h2>
          Give the Gifts that GIVE BACK
         </h2>
         <p>
          Its probably best if you give presents that the recipient probably wont want at all, but youd cross a landfill of dirty diapers to get for yourself. Heck! You may even luck out and get it immediately given back to you if they see you staring at it with your mouth open, drooling in adoration.
         </p>
         <p>
          Get Grandma an iPod Touch that she cant even see let alone operate and maybe shell even say something sweet as she hands it back to you like, Its the thought that counts, sweetie. Youll already be on level 5 of Angry Birds as you thank her. Win/win, right?
         </p>
         <h2>
          Keep it in the Family
         </h2>
         <p>
          Get these incompatible gifts for your darling, your kids or anyone who
          <em>
           lives with you
          </em>
          . This last part is
          <em>
           really
          </em>
          important. Its super hard to properly appreciate a gift that you gave to someone (that you actually wanted for yourself) when its in another home entirely. At least try to limit it to your own zip code. Gas is pricey these days.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Convincing &amp; Explaining
         </h2>
         <p>
          You may encounter stern looks of disapproval from your spouse while holiday shopping using this devilishly clever self-Santa-Clausing. Youll need to prepare your strategy when they pull the formidable No Way Jos roadblock tactic.
         </p>
         <p>
          Be prepared to explain yourself. Gaming consoles have great educational games for the kids, and enhance a vital life skill: hand-eye coordination. Without which wed all jab ourselves in the eye with our toothbrush every morning, probably. Try to finish with something dramatic like, Were practically being terrible parents if we DONT get her a gaming station or What if he wants to become an astronaut?
         </p>
         <h2>
          The Little Ones
         </h2>
         <p>
          Be ready for confused looks on Christmas morning. If you get little Johnny that sweet red mixer, that can turn concrete into whipped cream, instead of the remote control car hes been constantly begging for, be ready to confront a healthy dose of quivering lips or wailing.
         </p>
         <p>
          If theyre little enough, maybe youll hit the jackpot and theyll wind up tumbling around in the box and wrapping paper! Like they always do anyway, right? Then you wont even have to deal with teary eyes and looks of betrayal.
         </p>
         <p>
          <del>
           If
          </del>
          <em>
           When
          </em>
          things do go bad or
          <del>
           if
          </del>
          <em>
           when
          </em>
          it becomes a worst-case scenario, explain to them how entitled and bratty theyre being with a long tedious story about how when you were a kid, you didnt have things like fitness tracking wristbands and they should just be grateful
         </p>
         <p>
          Alright.
         </p>
         <p>
          I admit it.
         </p>
         <p>
          This is an awful idea and would make you a terrible, terrible person on a day youre supposed to be your most wonderful. So, go gift the ones you love with the rad stuff they want! Maybe the Christmas karma will result in the GoPro or kareoke machine youve been dying for.
         </p>
