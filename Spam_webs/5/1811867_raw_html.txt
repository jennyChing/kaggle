               <span class="single-comments-link">
                <a class="commentsNameLink" href="http://www.thecannabist.co/2014/03/24/pot-friendly-tv-show-feel-good-cooking-battle-medicated-chef-returns/7366/#disqus_thread" title="Comments on this article">

              <p class="byline" title="Written by">
               By
               <strong>
                <span class="author">
                 <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.thecannabist.co/author/rbaca/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Ricardo Baca">
                  Ricardo Baca
                 </a>
                </span>
               </strong>
               <em>
                , The Cannabist Staff
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               Famed management consultant Peter Drucker once said, The best way to predict the future is to create it. And thats a sentiment Steve Nelson Jr., a.k.a. Thurlow Weed, understands.
              </p>
              <p>
               Nelson is the kind of entrepreneur who thinks big and often. His year-old
               <a href="http://the24hourpipeandtobaccoshop.com/">
                iBake Denver
               </a>
               in unincorporated Adams County is the only head shop around that allows the on-site consumption of marijuana  a concept he plans on licensing elsewhere in Colorado and Washington. He hosts his Internet radio
               <a href="http://the24hourpipeandtobaccoshop.com/events/event/entrepotneur-show-live-ibake-denver/">
                Entrepotneur Show
               </a>
               five days a week. He throws parties and events, including the upcoming
               <a href="https://www.facebook.com/MedicatedChef?ref=br_tf">
                Medicated Chef competition
               </a>
               that takes a cannabis spin on the Iron Chef model. And hes an independent distributor for a number of marijuana-related businesses, from grow accessories to glass companies.
              </p>
              <p>
               Nelson has been pursuing all of these endeavors, some of them questionably legal, for a long while in a state that only legalized recreational marijuana in late 2012.
              </p>
              <hr/>
              <p>
               <a href="http://www.thecannabist.co/tag/event/">
                <em>
                 <strong>
                  420-friendly speed dating, job fairs and painting classes:
                 </strong>
                 Learn about more marijuana events right here
                </em>
               </a>
              </p>
              <hr/>
              <p>
               I feel like were winning a battle, Nelson said earlier this week. I know its not completely won, because there are still people locked up. But were heading in the right direction, especially in Colorado and Washington. It makes me feel good to fight for more change here and everywhere else.
              </p>
              <p>
               Like other activists, part of Nelsons fight involves integrating marijuana into mainstream culture  and thats where Medicated Chef comes in. After a number of preliminary rounds at iBake Denver, winning chefs cooking with cannabis will be pushed to the Sept. 20 finale at a venue to be determined. The Medicated Chef winner will receive the title and $5,000  $2,500 when they win, and another $2,500 after Nelson and his colleagues have wrapped a 13-episode Medicated Chef season to be broadcast on Hulu and other platforms, he said.
              </p>
              <hr/>
              <p>
               <a href="http://www.thecannabist.co/category/food/recipes/">
                <em>
                 <strong>
                  Quinoa, deviled eggs and mac n cheese:
                 </strong>
                 See our library of marijuana-infused recipes here
                </em>
               </a>
              </p>
              <hr/>
              <p>
               We want a chef who knows his stuff and who will look good on camera, because its more about the TV show than it is the event, said Nelson. The five top chefs from the preliminary rounds will compete Iron Chef-style at the finale, and they wont know until 24 hours before what theyll be cooking with. Each person will have something different.
              </p>
              <p>
               Yes, its a bold vision  a plan that will require everything falling into place, including a significant amount of funding and a large venue that is open to all types of on-site marijuana consumption.
              </p>
              <p>
               Last years competition at the Oriental (Theatre) was great, but the Oriental wont be big enough for this years show, Nelson said. When I first started the show, G4 and Spike TV had shown interest, but I wasnt ready to get a demo ready and together.
              </p>
              <hr/>
              <p>
               <em>
                The first of three preliminary contests for Medicated Chef will be March 27 at iBake Denver, 6125 Washington St., Denver.
                <a href="http://medicatedcheftv.com/">
                 Get more info here
                </a>
               </em>
              </p>
