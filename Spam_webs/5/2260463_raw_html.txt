     <div class="article-content" data-swiftype-name="body" data-swiftype-type="text">
      <p class="image-container" style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/11/22/chromecast-chrome-os-nasa/">
        <img alt="" data-credit="Google" data-mep="576285" src="http://o.aolcdn.com/hss/storage/midas/96e8b5be0b4f2f711eb5390f493913d8/201131713/chromecast-nasa-backdrop.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       After
       <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2014/09/15/chromecast-google-drive-video/">
        a few months of testing
       </a>
       , the feature that allows Chrome OS users to stream videos from Google Drive storage -- like the free 1TB allotted to new owners -- to a Chromecast
       <a href="http://googlechromereleases.blogspot.com/2014/11/stable-channel-update-for-chrome-os.html">
        is now available to (almost) everyone
       </a>
       . An update on the stable channel this week pushed it to most people, with the exception of a few devices: the Dell Chromebook 11, HP Chromebook 14, Acer C720 and the Toshiba Chromebook. One thing everyone with the Chromecast dongle can appreciate are additional backgrounds, this time
       <a href="https://plus.google.com/u/0/100585555255542998765/posts/KYwPhkx3z6L">
        provided by NASA
       </a>
       . To access them, pop open the Chromecast app on your mobile device, select "Backdrop", go to settings and choose NASA.
      </p>
