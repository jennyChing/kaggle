						&lt;h1&gt;ISOBEL CAMPBELL AND MARK LANEGAN-Hawk&lt;/h1&gt;


					&lt;/div&gt;


					&lt;div class="post-content clearfix"&gt;


						

						

						&lt;p&gt;&lt;img class="alignleft" src="http://www.brooklynvegan.com/img/music2/campbelllanegan2.jpg" alt="" width="423" height="309" /&gt;Isobel Campbell gave up her role as &#8220;Belle&#8221; in Belle and Sebastian almost ten years ago, going a wholly different route as a solo artist and a collaborator with Mark &#8220;Screaming Trees&#8221; Lanegan. Their latest album of duets, Hawk, includes two numbers by Townes Van Zandt and contribution from Willy Mason. As with the duo&#8217;s other release, &#8220;Hawk&#8221; is uneven but largely a success. There are many powerful, moving songs like &#8220;Come Undone&#8221;, but there a few missteps that are so slow that they become dull (&#8220;We Die and See Beauty Reign&#8221;). These two are at their best when they have a lot of give and take, and when the tempos change, because both of them have fairly even delivery. Nobody&#8217;s belting out a scream or a big Aretha wail here. Creepy, atmospheric blues is the best use of their talents. Like Lanegan&#8217;s extreme, vibrating bass against Campbell&#8217;s hopeful, airy quality on &#8220;Time of the Season&#8221; (not a Zombies remake). And when they hit their marks, like on &#8220;No Place to Fall&#8221; and &#8220;Get Behind Me,&#8221; it&#8217;s as touching as a Tom Waits role in a Jim Jarmusch film. It&#8217;s worth having on your shelf, for sure.&lt;/p&gt;
