     <h1 class="article-headline wordy no-hero">
      Shape-Shifting Liquid Metal Motor Behaves Like T-1000
     </h1>
     <div class="article-content" id="article-content">
      <div class="article-subhead">
       <div class="article-share-count-container">
        <span class="icon icon-sharrow">
        </span>

        </span>

       "The soft machine looks rather intelligent."
      </div>
      <span class="article-byline icon-pointy">
       By
       <a href="http://people.ign.com/rh236110">
        Rachel Haas
       </a>
      </span>
      <aside class="ign-ad ign-ad-300x250 no-hero">
       <div class="sugarad" id="sugarad-300x250">

      <p>
       A new self-powered liquid metal motor could be bringing us all one step closer to a real-life T-1000.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.newscientist.com/article/dn27127-liquid-metal-brings-shapeshifting-robot-a-step-closer.html#.VP9_dsZNtRl" rel="nofollow">
        New Scientist
       </a>
       explains the device: "just a drop of metal alloy made mostly of gallium  which is liquid at just under 30C  with some indium and tin mixed in. When placed in a solution of sodium hydroxide, or even brine, and kept in contact with a flake of aluminium for 'fuel', it moves around for about an hour. It can travel in a straight line, run around the outside of a circular dish, or squeeze through complex shapes."
      </p>
      <p class="iframe-container">
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/ErCOTdIruoc" width="420">
       </iframe>
      </p>
      <p>
       Jing Liu from Tsinghua University in Beijing, China said, "The soft machine looks rather intelligent and [can] deform itself according to the space it voyages in, just like [the] Terminator does from the science-fiction film. These unusual behaviours perfectly resemble the living organisms in nature."
      </p>
      <p>
       Through a series of experiments, Liu and his colleagues revealed that two mechanisms caused the drop to move: a charge imbalance across the drop, which creates a pressure differential and pushes it forward, and the reaction between the aluminium and the sodium hydroxide, which releases hydrogen bubbles that drive the drop even faster.
      </p>
      <p>
       This work is all part of a long-term effort to create intelligent, non-rigid robots that can be reshaped on the fly, like the fictional T-1000. Liu said that a robot based on their device could soon be used to deliver materials within pipes and blood vessel, as well as to monitor the environment.
      </p>
