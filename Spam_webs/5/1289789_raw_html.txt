       <p>
        Thousands of restaurants open across the country each yearbut only a few truly make a splash. Which were the most important restaurant openings of 2013? Here are our top 5.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Trois Mec: Los Angeles
        </strong>
        . Theres no bill at this unusual restaurant fromLudo Lefebvre, in partnership with Jon Shook &amp; Vinny Dotolo of Animaljust tickets to buy in advance, and whatever 5-course menu the chef wants to serve.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Serpico: Philadelphia
        </strong>
        .
        <strong>
        </strong>
        Peter Serpico, once a higher-up at Momofuku, comes to Philly in collaboration with Stephen Starr at this well-reviewed, truly globally-influenced restaurant, where pastas share space with kimchi stew or sliced pig head.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         qui: Austin
        </strong>
        . The food world hung on eagerly for the immensely talented Paul Quis flagship opening, and by all accounts it was worth the wait.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Betony: NYC
        </strong>
        . Its rare that a restaurant appears so polished from the moment it opens, but the elegant Betony nails the food, cocktails, serviceeverything.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Peche: New Orleans
        </strong>
        . Donald Link can do no wrong, and his seafood-focused Peche, where many fish are grilled over a live fire, is as exciting and creative as Cochon was before it.
       </p>
