     <p>
      <b>
       Miss Teen USA
      </b>
      is a
      <a href="/wiki/Beauty_pageant" title="Beauty pageant">
       beauty pageant
      </a>
      run by the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miss_Universe_Organization" title="Miss Universe Organization">
       Miss Universe Organization
      </a>
      for girls aged 1419. The reigning titleholder is
      <a href="/wiki/K._Lee_Graham" title="K. Lee Graham">
       K. Lee Graham
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">
       South Carolina
      </a>
      . Unlike its sister pageants
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe" title="Miss Universe">
       Miss Universe
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA" title="Miss USA">
       Miss USA
      </a>
      which are broadcast on the
      <a href="/wiki/Reelz" title="Reelz">
       Reelz
      </a>
      Channel,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tadena1_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Tadena1-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the teen pageant is
      <a href="/wiki/Webcast" title="Webcast">
       webcast
      </a>
      on the Miss Teen USA website and simulcast on
      <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">
       Microsoft
      </a>
      's
      <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live" title="Xbox Live">
       Xbox Live
      </a>
      service.
     </p>
     <p>
      The pageant was first held in 1983 and has been broadcast live on
      <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">
       CBS
      </a>
      until 2002 and then on
      <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">
       NBC
      </a>
      from 20032007. In March 2007 it was announced that the broadcast of the Miss Teen USA pageant on NBC had not been renewed, and that
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Teen_USA_2007" title="Miss Teen USA 2007">
       Miss Teen USA 2007
      </a>
      would be the final televised event.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eonline_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-eonline-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Since 2008, the pageant is held at the
      <a href="/wiki/Atlantis_Paradise_Island" title="Atlantis Paradise Island">
       Atlantis Paradise Island
      </a>
      Resort, located in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nassau,_The_Bahamas" title="Nassau, The Bahamas">
       Nassau
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">
       Bahamas
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Notable pageant winners include actresses

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Teen_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Miss Teen USA pageant was created in 1983 as a sister pageant to the Miss USA system. The first pageant was held in
      <a href="/wiki/Lakeland,_Florida" title="Lakeland, Florida">
       Lakeland, Florida
      </a>
      in August 1983 and was won by
      <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Zakarian" title="Ruth Zakarian">
       Ruth Zakarian
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_New_York_Teen_USA" title="Miss New York Teen USA">
       New York
      </a>
      . The pageant moved to April the following year, and then was held in January the next two years. It was held in July in 19881990 and has been held in August since 1991. The most common host city was
      <a href="/wiki/Biloxi,_Mississippi" title="Biloxi, Mississippi">
       Biloxi, Mississippi
      </a>
      which hosted the pageant from 1990 till 1994, which caused problems in 1992 when the conclusion of the live broadcast was delayed because of
      <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">
       Hurricane Andrew
      </a>
      . Other frequent host cities have been
      <a href="/wiki/South_Padre_Island,_Texas" title="South Padre Island, Texas">
       South Padre Island, Texas
      </a>
      (1997, 20012002),
      <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">
       Shreveport, Louisiana
      </a>
      (19982000) and
      <a href="/wiki/Palm_Springs,_California" title="Palm Springs, California">
       Palm Springs, California
      </a>
      (20032004, 2006).
     </p>

     </h2>
     <p>
      Prior to the final telecast the delegates compete in the preliminary competition, which involves private interviews with the judges and a presentation show where they compete in swimsuit and evening gown.
     </p>
     <p>
      During the final competition, the semi-finalists are announced and go on to compete in swimsuit and evening gown. From 1983 to 2002 all semi-finalists also competed in an interview competition as well as both swimsuit and evening gown, followed by one or two final interview questions. In
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Teen_USA_2003" title="Miss Teen USA 2003">
       2003
      </a>
      , a new format was introduced where the top fifteen competed in evening gown, the top ten competed in swimsuit and the top five competed in the final question. In
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Teen_USA_2006" title="Miss Teen USA 2006">
       2006
      </a>
      , the order of competition was changed where the top fifteen competed in swimsuit and the top ten in evening gown. The latest competition format was used since
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Teen_USA_2008" title="Miss Teen USA 2008">
       2008
      </a>
      the final not broadcast on TV, where the top fifteen both competed in swimsuit and evening gown, and the top five competed in the final question who all signed up by a panel of judges.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting_of_the_pageant">
       Broadcasting of the pageant
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Teen_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Broadcasting of the pageant">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1983.E2.80.932007:_Viewership_and_later_decline">
       19832007: Viewership and later decline
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Teen_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 19832007: Viewership and later decline">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The pageant's viewership peak was hit in 1988, when the pageant averaged over 22 million viewers on
      <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">
       CBS
      </a>
      . Even as recently as 1999, the show managed to bring in over 10 million viewers. The 2006 airing was the second lowest rated in the pageant's 23-year history, with only 5.6 million viewers watching the live broadcast (the lowest: 2004, with 5.34 million).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The 2007 telecast (25th Anniversary, scheduled for August 24, 2007) was the last time Miss Teen USA aired live on television.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2008:_Dark_pageant">
       2008: Dark pageant
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Teen_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2008: Dark pageant">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The 2008 pageant was held, untelevised, on August 16, 2008. One factor that prevented NBC from broadcasting was its prime time commitment to the
      <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">
       2008 Summer Olympics
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">
       Beijing
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.93present:_Internet_pageant">
       2009present: Internet pageant
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Teen_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2009present: Internet pageant">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Since 2009, it is broadcast over the
      <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">
       Internet
      </a>
      on the Miss Teen USA website. It can be viewed worldwide, without any region restrictions via
      <a href="/wiki/Geolocation" title="Geolocation">
       geolocation
      </a>
      . Currently, it is hosted by
      <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">
       YouTube
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2012, the pageant began to simulcast on Microsoft's Xbox Live service, expanding accessibility to
      <a href="/wiki/Xbox" title="Xbox">
       Xbox
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Video_game_console" title="Video game console">
       consoles
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">
       Windows
      </a>
      branded devices. Unlike the website, it has been available in a limited number of countries due to the
      <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live#Availability" title="Xbox Live">
       service's availability
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Miss_Teen_USA_at_Miss_USA">
       Miss Teen USA at Miss USA
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Teen_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Miss Teen USA at Miss USA">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In the early years of Miss Teen USA, three winners represented the title "Miss Teen USA" at
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA" title="Miss USA">
       Miss USA
      </a>
      . The three queens were: Ruth Zakarian (1983), Cherise Haugen (1984), and Allison Brown (1986). This also made history because the Miss USA 1984 pageant had 53 delegates, the most ever in a Miss USA pageant. In 1987, Miss Teen USA 1986, Allison Brown participated in the 1987 Miss USA pageant. All three failed to make the cut at their respective Miss USA pageants.
     </p>
     <p>
      Only in the three years mentioned above did Miss Teen USA winners automatically gain representation at
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA" title="Miss USA">
       Miss USA
      </a>
      . Starting in 1988, if the Miss Teen USA winners want to compete at Miss USA, they first must win a Miss USA state crown. A total of seven Miss Teen USA winners have participated at Miss USA, with four winning their state Miss USA crowns first. The first of these was Kelly Hu, Miss Teen USA 1985, who won the
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Hawaii_USA" title="Miss Hawaii USA">
       Miss Hawaii USA
      </a>
      1993 title and represented Hawaii at Miss USA 1993. She finished as a finalist. The next was
      <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Solinger" title="Jamie Solinger">
       Jamie Solinger
      </a>
      , Miss Teen USA 1992. She took the Miss Iowa USA 1998 title but failed to make the cut at the Miss USA pageant. Only two Miss Teen USA winners have made the top 3 of Miss USA.
      <a href="/wiki/Brandi_Sherwood" title="Brandi Sherwood">
       Brandi Sherwood
      </a>
      , Miss Teen USA 1989 won the
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Idaho_USA" title="Miss Idaho USA">
       Miss Idaho USA
      </a>
      1997 title. Succeeding Kelly, she took 1st runner up and later inherited the Miss USA crown, when
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brook_Mahealani_Lee" title="Brook Mahealani Lee">
       Brook Mahealani Lee
      </a>
      of Hawaii won
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Universe" title="Miss Universe">
       Miss Universe
      </a>
      1997. She is the only Miss Teen USA winner to hold the title Miss USA.
      <a href="/wiki/Shauna_Gambill" title="Shauna Gambill">
       Shauna Gambill
      </a>
      was close in winning the Miss USA 1998 title, but placed 1st runner up to
      <a href="/wiki/Shawnae_Jebbia" title="Shawnae Jebbia">
       Shawnae Jebbia
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Massachusetts_USA" title="Miss Massachusetts USA">
       Miss Massachusetts USA
      </a>
      1998. However, to date it has never happened that the same woman had won the 2 separate pageants officially.
     </p>
     <p>
      There have been two years when two Miss Teen USA winners participated at Miss USA. The first was 1984, when Miss Teen USA 1983 and Miss Teen USA 1984 participated, the second was 1998, when Jamie Solinger competed as
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Iowa_USA" title="Miss Iowa USA">
       Miss Iowa USA
      </a>
      but went unplaced, and
      <a href="/wiki/Shauna_Gambill" title="Shauna Gambill">
       Shauna Gambill
      </a>
      competed as
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_California_USA" title="Miss California USA">
       Miss California USA
      </a>
      and placed first runner-up.
     </p>
     <p>
      The first Miss Teen USA not to win a Miss USA state pageant on her first attempt was
      <a href="/wiki/Christie_Lee_Woods" title="Christie Lee Woods">
       Christie Lee Woods
      </a>
      , Miss Teen USA 1996, of Texas, who placed third runner-up in the
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Texas_USA" title="Miss Texas USA">
       Miss Texas USA
      </a>
      2002 pageant. She would also become the first Miss Teen USA to compete for, but never win a Miss USA state title (she also placed as a semi-finalist in 2003 and 2004). The second Miss Teen USA who did not win a Miss USA state title on her first attempt was
      <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Coleman" title="Ashley Coleman">
       Ashley Coleman
      </a>
      , Miss Teen USA 1999, of Delaware, who competed in the
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_California_USA" title="Miss California USA">
       Miss California USA
      </a>
      2006 pageant and finished third runner-up. She is the first Miss Teen USA winner to compete in a different state from that where she won her Miss Teen USA crown.
      <a href="/wiki/Tami_Farrell" title="Tami Farrell">
       Tami Farrell
      </a>
      , Miss Teen USA 2003, competed at
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_California_USA" title="Miss California USA">
       Miss California USA
      </a>
      2009 as Miss Malibu USA, but failed to win the crown, placing first runner-up to
      <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Prejean" title="Carrie Prejean">
       Carrie Prejean
      </a>
      . Farrell competed at Miss Teen USA as
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Oregon_Teen_USA" title="Miss Oregon Teen USA">
       Miss Oregon Teen USA
      </a>
      , and was one of the few Miss Teen USA winners to try for the state crown outside of the state she represented for Miss Teen USA. In a span of only two years later,
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_Teen_USA_2006" title="Miss Teen USA 2006">
       Miss Teen USA 2006
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Katie_Blair" title="Katie Blair">
       Katie Blair
      </a>
      also competed at
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_California_USA" title="Miss California USA">
       Miss California USA
      </a>
      2011. Representing the state of Montana at Miss Teen USA 2006, Blair is only the third Miss Teen USA to compete in a state other than the state she competed in Teen. Similarly to Coleman and Farrell, Blair placed 1st runner up in the pageant, to
      <a href="/wiki/Alyssa_Campanella" title="Alyssa Campanella">
       Alyssa Campanella
      </a>
      , who also competed in Miss Teen USA representing another state. Campanella went on to win
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA_2011" title="Miss USA 2011">
       Miss USA 2011
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Miss_Teen_USA_state_delegates_at_Miss_USA">
       Miss Teen USA state delegates at Miss USA
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miss_Teen_USA&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Miss Teen USA state delegates at Miss USA">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      A large number of Miss Teen USA state delegates have won Miss USA state titles and competed at Miss USA. Most held both titles in the same state, but a number have held titles from two states. From 1996, many former Miss Teen USA delegates have gone on to win the
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA" title="Miss USA">
       Miss USA
      </a>
      crown. Out of the eight former teens which won the crown outright, seven competed for Miss USA in the state they won their Teen title and one represented a different state.
     </p>
     <p>
      They are:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Ali_Landry" title="Ali Landry">
        Ali Landry
       </a>
       , Miss USA 1996 (
       <a href="/wiki/Miss_Louisiana_Teen_USA" title="Miss Louisiana Teen USA">
        Miss Louisiana Teen USA
       </a>
       1990,
       <a href="/wiki/Miss_Louisiana_USA" title="Miss Louisiana USA">
        Miss Louisiana USA
       </a>
       1996)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Kimberly_Pressler" title="Kimberly Pressler">
        Kimberly Pressler
       </a>
       , Miss USA 1999 (Miss New York Teen USA 1994, Miss New York USA 1999)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Lynnette_Cole" title="Lynnette Cole">
        Lynnette Cole
       </a>
       , Miss USA 2000 (Miss Tennessee Teen USA 1995, Miss Tennessee USA 2000)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Susie_Castillo" title="Susie Castillo">
        Susie Castillo
       </a>
       , Miss USA 2003 (Miss Massachusetts Teen USA 1998, Miss Massachusetts USA 2003)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Cooley" title="Chelsea Cooley">
        Chelsea Cooley
       </a>
       , Miss USA 2005 (
       <a href="/wiki/Miss_North_Carolina_Teen_USA" title="Miss North Carolina Teen USA">
        Miss North Carolina Teen USA
       </a>
       2000,
       <a href="/wiki/Miss_North_Carolina_USA" title="Miss North Carolina USA">
        Miss North Carolina USA
       </a>
       2005)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Tara_Conner" title="Tara Conner">
        Tara Conner
       </a>
       , Miss USA 2006 (Miss Kentucky Teen USA 2002, Miss Kentucky USA 2006)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Smith" title="Rachel Smith">
        Rachel Smith
       </a>
       , Miss USA 2007 (Miss Tennessee Teen USA 2002, Miss Tennessee USA 2007)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a href="/wiki/Alyssa_Campanella" title="Alyssa Campanella">
        Alyssa Campanella
       </a>
       , Miss USA 2011 (Miss New Jersey Teen USA 2007, Miss California USA 2011)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA_2009" title="Miss USA 2009">
       Miss USA 2009
      </a>
      was the first Miss USA pageant since
      <a href="/wiki/Miss_USA_1994" title="Miss USA 1994">
       1994
      </a>
      where no former Miss Teen USA state titleholders made the semi-finals.
     </p>
