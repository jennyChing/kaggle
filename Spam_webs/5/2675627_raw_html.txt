        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SO1K3WMH2MYHNQK" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FRH/JAW3/H2TIFWYF/FRHJAW3H2TIFWYF.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FRHJAW3H2TIFWYF" data-notes="" href="/file/FRHJAW3H2TIFWYF" id="photoset-link-F86M4P6H2WET5W7-0" style="">

       <div class="txt step-body">
        If you want to be able to see in the dark you can pick up a maglite and shine it every where you want. But if you don't want other people to know you are there you have to figure out an other way to be able to see using a lightbeam others can't see. in otherwords build your own night vision scope.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        what do you need:
        <br/>
        - a viewfinder from an old camera.
        <br/>
        - an old 380 line black and white camera
        <br/>
        - six Infrared LED's
        <br/>
        - three 15 ohm resistors

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 1: The viewfinder
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SI5I0J2H2MYHNQN" data-entry-url="/id/How-to-See-in-The-Dark/step1/The-viewfinder/" id="photoset-SI5I0J2H2MYHNQN">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SI5I0J2H2MYHNQN" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FLM/Z3DK/H2TIFWYL/FLMZ3DKH2TIFWYL.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FLMZ3DKH2TIFWYL" data-notes="" href="/file/FLMZ3DKH2TIFWYL" id="photoset-link-FSS3II9H2TIFWZT-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="Picture of The viewfinder" class="photoset-photo id_FLMZ3DKH2TIFWYL " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FLM/Z3DK/H2TIFWYL/FLMZ3DKH2TIFWYL.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;height:203px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SI5I0J2H2MYHNQN" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/F50/J5CN/H2TIFWYK/F50J5CNH2TIFWYK.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/F50J5CNH2TIFWYK" data-notes="" href="/file/F50J5CNH2TIFWYK" id="photoset-link-F5DXW3JH2TIFWZU-1" style="margin-left:10px">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:295px;height:203px;">
           <img alt="open-viewfinder.jpg" class="photoset-photo id_F50J5CNH2TIFWYK lazyphoto" data-notes="" data-original="http://cdn.instructables.com/F50/J5CN/H2TIFWYK/F50J5CNH2TIFWYK.MEDIUM.jpg" src="/static/img/pixel.png" style="width:295px;"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="open-viewfinder.jpg" class="photoset-photo" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/F50/J5CN/H2TIFWYK/F50J5CNH2TIFWYK.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:295px;"/>
           </noscript>
          </div>
         </a>
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SI5I0J2H2MYHNQN" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FRC/35LI/H2TIFWYM/FRC35LIH2TIFWYM.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FRC35LIH2TIFWYM" data-notes="" href="/file/FRC35LIH2TIFWYM" id="photoset-link-F9ZP5QRH2TIFWZV-2" style="margin-left:10px">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:295px;height:203px;">
           <img alt="viewfinder-cable.jpg" class="photoset-photo id_FRC35LIH2TIFWYM lazyphoto" data-notes="" data-original="http://cdn.instructables.com/FRC/35LI/H2TIFWYM/FRC35LIH2TIFWYM.MEDIUM.jpg" src="/static/img/pixel.png" style="width:295px;"/>
           <noscript>
            <img alt="viewfinder-cable.jpg" class="photoset-photo" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FRC/35LI/H2TIFWYM/FRC35LIH2TIFWYM.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:295px;"/>
           </noscript>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        The viewfinder I used for this night vision scope. Was taken from a CANON UC4000 V8 Camcorder. The Camera was broke but the viewfinder is stil very usefull.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        The viewfinder used to be connected to the camera with 5 cables.
        <br/>
        When I started measuring them I noticed that I just needed the first three cables those where.
        <br/>
        - +5 volt
        <br/>
        - Ground
        <br/>
        - video-in
        <br/>
        <br/>
        ATTENTION:
        <br/>
        The viewfinder contains a 0.7 inch picture tube, a high voltage power supply and some calibration pots. The size of this module is only 2,5 x 5 cm.
        <br/>
        Therefore the high voltage power supply could for the picture tube could cause serious injury if you touched it.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        This is why in attached new cables to those connection points en put the viewfinder back together.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Now our output device is ready.

       <h2 class="step-title">
        Step 2: The camera
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SVOGSPMH2MYHNQR" data-entry-url="/id/How-to-See-in-The-Dark/step2/The-camera/" id="photoset-SVOGSPMH2MYHNQR">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SVOGSPMH2MYHNQR" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FFL/Y4OQ/H2TIFWYZ/FFLY4OQH2TIFWYZ.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FFLY4OQH2TIFWYZ" data-notes="" href="/file/FFLY4OQH2TIFWYZ" id="photoset-link-FXZ7SMQH2WET5WD-0" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="width:600px;">
           <img alt="Picture of The camera" class="photoset-photo id_FFLY4OQH2TIFWYZ " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/FFL/Y4OQ/H2TIFWYZ/FFLY4OQH2TIFWYZ.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>
          </div>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        After some searching I found a 380 line black/white camera in my garage which was solderd on a piece of stripboard and used a 5 volt power supply (thats nice the same votage as my view finder). This camera cannot see in the dark but it can see IR light (which is invisible for the humans eye). So fore the record this night vision scope is not an image intensifier this scope needs an Infrared light source.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        (Sometimes manufactors put a filter in the lens which blocks the infrared light when you work carefull jou can remove it as described in my Instructable
        <a href="http://www.instructables.com/id/Turning-an-old-webcam-into-a-Night-vision-cam/">
         Turning an old webcam into a Night vision cam

         Just as a guide.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Infrared LEDs in the 850nm range do give the red glow.  The 940 nm do not.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         10 degree viewing angle gives the longest distance, while a wider viewing angle gives less distance but a broader field of view.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Try to keep the forward tip of the LED behind the lens of the camera to prevent a glow across your lens.
