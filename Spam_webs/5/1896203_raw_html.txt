         Four Actions I Took to Finally Follow My Dream
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn" href="http://gogirlfinance.com/author/carriesmith/">
           Carrie Smith
          </a>
         </span>
         on
         <abbr class="published" title="2015-04-17">
          April 17, 2015
         </abbr>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <!-- google_ad_section_start -->

        <p dir="ltr">
         <a href="http://27w1sc3tefw4199hoi4yp1j18i7.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/dream-pin.jpg">
          <img alt="Woman walking alone along the beach as the tide comes in" class="alignright size-full wp-image-8770" height="424" src="http://27w1sc3tefw4199hoi4yp1j18i7.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/dream-pin.jpg" width="283"/>
         </a>
         After 10 years of working at my accounting job, I finally walked out the door for the last time. Like many people who are dedicated to their employers, it took several years to finally work up the courage to 1) admit that I wanted to quit (leaving a secure paycheck is tough) and 2) tell my boss that I was indeed quitting.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Now I couldnt be happier! Heres the four steps I took to follow my dream and finally quit the daily grind.
        </p>
        <h2 dir="ltr">
         Determine Your Dealbreakers
        </h2>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Once I finally had enough, I wrote down a few non-negotiables that I no longer wanted to sacrifice. One of those things was weekends  I was tired of giving up my weekends and I wanted my life back.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         The key to following your dream is to finalize what you can no longer live with (or without). Are you tired of sacrificing your time, your loved ones, your sanity? Understanding these dealbreakers will help you be resolute in your decision to follow your dream.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Digital Publishing Coach,
         <a href="http://danasitar.com">
          Dana Sitar
         </a>
         shares how shes finally living a life without compromises. Ive left behind several day jobs at various points, because they never allow me the time or brain space to pursue my own work. Thats a dealbreaker for me. Now I get to call the shots! It allows me to only do work I love, and to do everything else I love in life without compromises.
        </p>
        <h2 dir="ltr">
         Create Your Escape Plan
        </h2>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Once I decided it was time to take action, the first thing I did was
         <a href="http://www.carefulcents.com/invest-in-yourself/">
          hire a business coach
         </a>
         . She helped me create weekly action items, and bite-sized goals so I could work up the courage to finally quit. Even with all the financial knowledge I had, I still needed someone to be in my corner to encourage me to turn my dream into a reality.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         The first thing I did while creating my plan was to work out how much income I needed to survive without my jobs paycheck. I also used the time while still working at my job to save extra money for making that leap.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         I think Raven Delana, of
         <a href="http://www.verandalane.com">
          Veranda Lane
         </a>
         says it best, Make money first! Whether you save up from your current job or start with some freelance work to pay the bills, it helps to have a steady flow of money to take care of the essentials. It doesnt make you a failure or a quitter, if you pick up an outside job to pay the bills. In fact, it makes you brilliant. Be your own investor!
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         The hardest part is taking the first step, but once youve got that momentum going, its easy to keep putting one foot in front of the other.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         <a href="http://www.lisalandtroop.com">
          Lisa Landtroop
         </a>
         , encourages anyone following their dream to, Create an escape plan, like Jonathan Mead says. Do not allow yourself to be stuck indefinitely. Writing down your plan will help you to visualize a way to make it a reality.
        </p>
        <h2 dir="ltr">
         Plan Ahead For Ups and Downs
        </h2>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Being your own boss is liberating, and I can truly say its one of the best decisions Ive ever made  even if I dont make near the amount of money I used to. But whats the point of making a lot of money if you never have time to spend it? Now I value my income a lot more, and am very thankful for every penny I make.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Although there are a lot of things to love about working for yourself, there are still a few cons. One of the main considerations is the inconsistent income. When youre
         <a href="http://gogirlfinance.com/money/paying-down-debt-how-i-paid-off-my-credit-cards-on-one-income/">
          responsible for paying the bills
         </a>
         and put food on the table, it can be stressful. Just keep reminding yourself of WHY youre doing this, and try to focus on the fact that it will be worth the sacrifice.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         Stephanie Halligan, a
         <a href="http://www.empowereddollar.com/consultant/">
          Financial Empowerment Consultant
         </a>
         , says its natural to worry about the uncertainty in being your own boss. Im a VERY risk averse person, and wondering where the money will come from each month is nerve-racking. But remember this: nothing is ever 100%. There will come a point where all you can do is jump. And its the greatest, scariest moment of your life.
        </p>
        <h2 dir="ltr">
         Remember One Thing
        </h2>
        <p>
         No matter how scary or intimidating your dream might be, remember not to let your fear of the unknown paralyze you into inaction. This was the biggest mistake I made, and it cost me dearly. You shouldnt sacrifice your dream for something safer or less risky, because youll always regret not pursuing it.
        </p>
        <p dir="ltr">
         <a href="http://www.antonrasmussen.com">
          Anton Rasmussen
         </a>
         sums it up nicely, Take action. Just do it . . . today.
        </p>

           Hi Carrie! Your post is inspiring. I like the part where you said that one should create a plan first. When I decided to become a freelance writer, I didnt have a solid direction right away. I was content with having a single client and doing the things I wasnt passionate about for a long time. I took the job because I thought it was the safe choice for a freelancer. But its like I didnt leave the office cubicle at all.
          </p>

           Having a plan made a differencefor the things I really want to do (the kind of writing services I want to offer), for my professional development, for our bills, and even for leisure activities. With a solid plan, Im able to enjoy the kind of freedom that I missed when I was a 9-5 employee.
          </p>
