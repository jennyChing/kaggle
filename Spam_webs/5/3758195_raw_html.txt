           <h3>
            IIM Lucknow summer Placements Class 2014-16: Process gets over in 4.5 days
           </h3>
           <p class="date">
            MBAUniverse.com | 27, Oct 2014 0133 IST

           <p class="medium">

        <div class="genericModule">
         <div class="articleFull">
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The competitive environment among the B-schools of  being The Best strives them to attract more recruiters every year and make  themselves possible to be in the sight of the aspirants and corporate sector.  This has been noticed in the Summer Placements 2014-16 of the prestigious  IIM-Lucknow where the whole gets of Internship placements ended in just 4.5  days.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Record  Summer Placements
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            Indian Institute of Management Lucknow (IIML) broke  its own placement record and registered 100 per cent summer placements in a  time of 4.5 days only. The institute had created history last year by placing  the entire batch in 5.5 days during summer Placements and 5 days during Final  Placements", an IIM release issued here said.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Participation  of Domestic and International Companies
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The summer  recruitment drive of Premier Management Institute of India saw the  participation of 164 domestic and international recruiters. A total of 464  students had participated in the recruitment drive.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The  highlight of the placement season was participation of firms like JP Morgan,  Alvarez and Marsal, an international mutual fund company and 4 Indian private  equity players.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Roles  offered in the Drive
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The top four segments based on roles offered were  sales and marketing, finance, consulting and business development, including  some extremely sought after and niche profiles in the finance domain.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The rest of the offers came from domains such as  operations/supply chain, general management, systems/IT, market research,  analytics and HR.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Sectoral  Placements
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             FMCG/Consumer  Goods -
            </strong>
            Coke, Colgate Palmolive, Dabur, GSK Consumer,  Hindustan Unilever, ITC, Kellogg's, Mondelez, Marico, Mars, P&amp;G, Pepsi,  Reckitt Benckiser. P&amp;G, which was  the top recruiter in this segment, opened the highest number of profiles this  year
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Telecom  Industry
            </strong>
            - The telecom giants Airtel and Vodafone  participated and made offers in the recruitment drive. Paints Industry leader  in India Asian Paints has shown its presence in the drive.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Consulting  Domain
            </strong>
            - Accenture, Alvarez &amp; Marsal, AT Kearney The  Boston Consulting Group, Deloitte and McKinsey made a total of 37 offers with  McKinsey and BCG being the top two recruiters.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Pharmaceuticals  / Healthcare Industry
            </strong>
            - Firms such as Bristol-Myers Squibb,  Boehringer Ingelheim, Cipla, GSK Pharma, J&amp;J Pharma and Novartis recruiting  in large numbers across functions. The roles were primarily in sales and  marketing, finance, operations, market research and general management. One of  these firms also opened its prestigious internship for leadership roles in  Asia-Pacific, Europe/Middle East/Africa or Latin America.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             Banking  Financial Services and Institution (BFSI)
            </strong>
            - BFSI saw the  participation of JP Morgan this year which hired IIML students for its  'Front-End Markets' profile.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             IT/E-commerce/Technology-
            </strong>
            The  companies participated were Amazon, Capgemini, Cognizant, eBay, Flipkart, HCL,  Taxiforsure.com and Wipro. These firms opened not only IT/systems profiles but  also roles such as sales enablement, business development, supply chain  management, product marketing, strategic alliances, program manager and  analytics.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            This year IIM-L attracted the social impact and  education sector with a total of 14 firms, offering roles in sales and  marketing, market research, business development, finance, IT, policy research  and project implementation. It is one of the niche additions to the portfolio  of the IIM-L.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            The institute also secured the highest number of  international offers from diverse sectors such as Retail, FMCG and real estate.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            <strong>
             IIM-Lucknow
            </strong>
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>
          <p style="text-align: justify;">
           <font size="2">
            IIM-L was established in 1984 as the fourth Indian  Institute of Management (IIM) by the Government of India after IIM Calcutta,  IIM Ahmedabad and IIM Bangalore. It is recognized as an "Institution of  Excellence" by the India's Ministry of Human Resource Development. IIM-L  has a 100 per cent track record of placements.
           </font>
          </p>
          <font size="2">
          </font>

           <font size="2">
            <em>
             Stay  tuned to MBAUniverse.com for more updates on IIM Lucknow
            </em>
           </font>
