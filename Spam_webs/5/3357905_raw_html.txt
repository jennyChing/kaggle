                      </div>
                     </li>
                     <li class="hs-blog-social-share-item hs-blog-social-share-item-google-plus">
                      <!-- Google +1 -->
                      <div class="g-plusone" data-href="http://blog.globalbasecamps.com/best-travel-blogs-foxnomadcom-travel-smarter-blog-professionally" data-size="medium">

                  <div class="section post-body">
                   <div class="post-body-bg">
                    <span class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text" id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" style="">
                     <p>
                      <img align="left" alt="Anil of FoXnoMad.com" src="//cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/392642/file-931936867-jpg/images/foxnomad.jpg?t=1436221208285" style="margin: 0 10px 10px 0;" title="Anil of FoXnoMad.com"/>
                      If you empathize at all with my new must-get-to-Turkey-now plan, youll have no problem understanding what drew me to Anil of FoXnoMad.com (Hes traveled there extensively). Even if you arent, the fact that he is sponsoring a contest that could get you 6 nights stay and airfare to almost anywhere of your choosing, is enough reason to read on. Beyond that, Anil earns the proud distinction of Best in Travel Blogs because hes serious about blogging and it shows. Not to mention, that fact that he is a formally educated anthropologist and self proclaimed former computer hacker (the good kind) gives a nifty twist to his travel insights.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: When and how did you get hooked on travel?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      Ive been traveling my entire life and suppose my curiosity to do so is strongly influenced by my parents, both of whom are journalists.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: You are running an amazing Live the Backpacker Life contest. Can you tell us a bit more about it?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      I want to give the
                      <a href="http://www.foxnomad.com/2010/09/07/live-the-backpacker-life-most-anywhere-in-the-world-contest" target="_blank" title="Win airfare &amp; 6 nights accommodation to practically anywhere!">
                       opportunity
                      </a>
                      for one of my readers to take a backpacking trip to most anywhere they want in the world. Its a bit of a role-reversal. [Before October 5th] create a 3-5 minute travel video guide about a specific aspect of a given city and be the one traveling.
                      <!--more-->
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: I loved your post on Compelling Reasons Americans Shouldnt Make Their First International Trip to Europe. You did indeed come up with some compelling reason, care to share some of them with us?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      <a href="http://www.foxnomad.com/2010/01/11/compelling-reasons-americans-shouldnt-make-their-first-international-trip-one-to-europe/" target="_blank" title="Read FoXnoMads post: Compelling Reasons Americans Shouldnt Make Their First International Trip to Europe">
                       For one
                      </a>
                      its expensive but also its relatively similar to the US and can give many first-time travelers the sense that much of the world resembles back home. That said, international travel anywhere, including Europe, can be an enlightening experience and one that cannot be recreated at domestically; no matter where the first trip happens to be.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: Youve traveled extensively in Turkey. What are the off-the-beaten-path things we shouldnt miss in that part of the world?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <img align="right" alt="Ankara, Turkey" src="//cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/392642/file-928483191-jpg/images/foxnomadturkey.jpg?t=1436221208285" style="margin: 10px 10px 10px 10px;" title="Ankara, Turkey"/>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      Go east! Cities like Trabzon, Van, and Gaziantep are almost always overlooked by tourists coming to
                      <a href="http://www.globalbasecamps.com/turkey" title="Explore Turkey with Global Basecamps">
                       Turkey
                      </a>
                      . Aside from varied histories and cultures, they each have wonderful local cuisines.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: Have you had any experience with sustainable travel?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      I havent personally but its an interesting trend. The unfortunate thing is that many companies claim to be eco-friendly but arent and its tough for consumers to recognize the differences. I hope that ultimately all travelers consider the environmental impact of their actions both at home and whilst traveling.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: Out of all the places youve traveled, which destination do you think is the most overlooked or underrated?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      Im not sure about places (although there are many that get a bad rap) but simple experiences in different places are often underrated. Shopping in a local grocery store, walking through a small town, and eating street food are some that come to mind.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: Whats your favorite travel gadget?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      Lately its been my e-Reader the Nook.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: Which country has the best street food or most memorable meal?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      Tough one! One of my most recent favorite foods is from
                      <a href="http://www.globalbasecamps.com/sample-trip/chile-argentina-explorer" title="Explore Chile &amp; Argentina with GlobalBasecamps">
                       Chile
                      </a>
                      , a bean dish called "porotos granados". It's a wonderful (vegetarian) blend of cranberry beans, squash, onions, some spices and a few other ingredients I am no doubt forgetting. Another new taste that recently made it on my list of personal favorites is
                      <a href="http://www.globalbasecamps.com/sample-trip/highlights-ecuador-peru" title="Experience the Highlights of Ecuador &amp; Peru with Global Basecamps">
                       Ecuadorian
                      </a>
                      ceviche. That's raw fish or shrimp marinated in citrus juice; throw in some
                      <a href="http://www.globalbasecamps.com/blog/top-5-markets-world" title="See what else you can munch on at our blog on Quitos Oltavalo Market">
                       plantain chips
                      </a>
                      on the side and it makes for a refreshing afternoon meal.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: You are living the full-time travel blogger dream. What advice to you for those who want to follow in your footsteps?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <img align="left" alt="La Basilica Church, Quito, Ecuador" src="//cdn2.hubspot.net/hub/392642/file-931936882-jpg/images/foxnomadecuador.jpg?t=1436221208285" style="margin: 0 10px 10px 0;" title="La Basilica Church, Quito, Ecuador"/>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      Work hard, read everything you can about travel blogging, and come up with a strategy to turn your desire into a reality. It takes a long time (for me nearly 4 years) to make it possible and it typically means more work (not less) in the long run. If you want to make money blogging so you dont have to work anymore youve got things backwards.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      A very good resource and place to begin is Nomadic Matts ebook,
                      <a href="http://www.nomadicmatt.com/travel-tips/make-money-with-your-travel-blog/" target="_blank" title="Read Nomadic Matts ebook: How to Make Money With Your Travel Blog">
                       How To Make Money With Your Travel Blog
                      </a>
                      .
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: What's your number one travel tip?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      If I had to narrow it down to one thing I'd say don't worry about the little things.
                     </p>
                     <p class="question">
                      Global Basecamps: We couldnt agree more! What else would you like the online travel community to know about you?
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <strong>
                       FoXnoMad:
                      </strong>
                      I can't think of anything but if anyone has any questions I'm always available!
                      <a href="http://foxnomad.com/contact" target="_blank" title="Contact Anil of FoXnoMad.com">
                       Contact Anil of FoXnoMad.com
                      </a>
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <em>
                       To get better acquainted with Anil of FoXnoMad, follow him on
                       <a href="http://twitter.com/foxnomad/" target="_blank" title="Follow FoXnoMad on Twitter">
                        Twitter
                       </a>
                       and visit his other sites:
                       <a href="http://howtotravelwithpets.com" target="_blank" title="howtotravelwithpets.com">
                        How to Travel with Pets
                       </a>
                       ,
                       <a href="http://travelblogadvice.com" target="_blank" title="travelblogadvice.com">
                        Travel Blog Advice
                       </a>
                       , and
                       <a href="http://techguidefortravel.com" target="_blank" title='techguidefortravel.com"'>
                        Tech Guide for Travel
                       </a>
                       .
                      </em>
                     </p>
                    </span>
                   </div>
