         <p>
          Have you ever noticed the irony that when you want to shut down your PC, you have to go to the Start menu?  Well I found an easy way to make a PC shutdown button which sits on the desktop along with a reboot button and an abort button which cancels the shutdown or reboot if you change your mind.     You dont have to be a top-notch programmer to do it and the buttons actually prove quite useful.
         </p>
         <p>
          Just follow the instructions here. Normally I would post screenshots but since I use a German computer, I figured it was pretty useless showing German language screenshots if you dont know German. But I think the following instructions are clear enough :
         </p>
         <ul>
          <strong>
           1)
          </strong>
          Right-click on the desktop and choose
          <em>
           New
          </em>
          then
          <em>
           Shortcut
          </em>
          .
          <p>
           <strong>
            2)
           </strong>
           When the box comes up, browse to the file
           <em>
            C:\Windows\System32\Shutdown.exe
           </em>
           , click
           <em>
            Next
           </em>
           , name the shortcut, and click Finish.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            3)
           </strong>
           Now right-click on your new shortcut and choose
           <em>
            Properties
           </em>
           .  In the Target box (the box which shows the location of the
           <em>
            Shutdown.exe
           </em>
           program), attach one of the following commands at the end:
           <em>
            -l
           </em>
           (to log off),
           <em>
            -s
           </em>
           (to shut down), or
           <em>
            -r
           </em>
           (to reboot).
          </p>
         </ul>
         <p>
          If you also add the command
          <em>
           -t xx
          </em>
          (where xx is a number of seconds), your shutdown procedure will display a warning and countdown before activating. I chose 10 seconds which is more than enough time.
         </p>
         <p>
          You can also specify a comment to be displayed with the warning by adding the command
          <em>
           -c Your text
          </em>
          . This is quite amusing as you can give yourself a personalised shutdown message such as Goodbye Mark!
         </p>
         <p>
          Once the shutdown / reboot button is activated, the only way to stop the computer from shutting down / rebooting is to launch the shutdown program again with the
          <font>
           -a
          </font>
          (for abort) command in the target box of the shortcut. So it would be best to repeat the above process and also make an abort button for your desktop too.
          <br/>
          I even gave my new buttons nice South Park icons :
         </p>

              After numerous email queries, I just want to clear something up that I wasnt too clear on in the article.    When you are writing the command line in your shortcut, there has to be a space between the target link and the command.   So for example, it has to be something like :
             </p>
             <p>
              C:\Windows\System32\Shutdown.exe -s
             </p>
             <p>
              If you dont leave a space between the exe and the -s then the computer thinks that shutdown has the file format exe-s which is of course invalid.   So make sure you leave a space between the target link and the command for it to work.
