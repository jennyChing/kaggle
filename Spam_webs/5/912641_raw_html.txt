         <p>
          For those of you who know me, it may come as no surprise that Im a bit of a type-A personality. This is especially true when it comes to being organized for my travel. Its not that I want to run us ragged, but because I want us to enjoy the trip. And as a result of my years of travel, Ive come up with some pretty simple ways to stay organized while on the road.
          <strong>
           One way I do this is by creating a special travel itinerary template, which I also refer to as my cheat sheet for the trip.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: center;">
          <img alt="Cheat Sheet icon" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8044" height="411" src="http://www.peanutsorpretzels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Cheat-Sheet-icon.jpg" style="border: 0px;" width="307"/>
          <span id="more-8027">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          A Cheat Sheet is More Than Just an Itinerary Template
         </h2>
         <p>
          Keeping track of all the details related to a vacation can be overwhelming. Especially if it is a long or busy trip filled with hotel changes, transportation, and activities. After you have made your travel arrangements, you are bound to have endless emails and confirmation numbers to sort through. That combined with your research and notes about the things you want to see and do on your trip can make being organized even more complicated.
          <strong>
           You need all this information nicely organized where you can access it easily when you need it.
          </strong>
          And that is why I came up with my travel cheat sheet.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.shareasale.com/r.cfm?b=162172&amp;u=1063040&amp;m=19982&amp;urllink=&amp;afftrack=" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.shareasale.com/r.cfm?b=162172&amp;u=1063040&amp;m=19982&amp;urllink=&amp;afftrack=', '']);" target="_blank">
           <img alt="Book Sightseeing Tours, Day trips, Activities and Things to do with City-Discovery.com" border="0" class=" aligncenter" src="http://static.shareasale.com/image/General_728x90_1.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Sometimes Nothing Beats a Good Ol Fashioned Piece of Paper!
         </h2>
         <p>
          Technology, especially mobile devices, have really helped travelers stay organized while on the road. In fact, one of our
          <a href="http://www.peanutsorpretzels.com/best-travel-apps/" title="Best Travel Apps  Our Favorites!">
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             favorite travel apps
            </strong>
           </span>
          </a>
          that we use is called TripIt. This tool helps us consolidate a lot of our travel information and confirmation numbers into one place. This allows us to access it online or save it for viewing offline on our mobile devices.
          <strong>
           However, I have learned from my previous travel experience that using technology doesnt always make things easier or quicker.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is especially true when traveling overseas. I dont always have my mobile devices powered on, and often they are not connected to a data network. Other times I may not want to draw attention to myself and my fancy technology while Im walking down the street.
          <strong>
           In these cases Ive discovered that its easier to go old school, with an actual piece of paper!
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Unfortunately, all those confirmation emails and travel planning documents tend be long and include a lot of information that you may not need. In fact, if you were to print all of that information out, you would probably be walking around with a small book in your pocket! This defeats the whole point of a cheat sheet because you would be fumbling around trying to find what you need. My cheat sheet is only 1 page!
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_8042" style="width: 497px">
          <a href="http://www.peanutsorpretzels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Travel-Itinerary-Cheet-Sheet.png">
           <img alt="stay organized while on vacation, travel planning tools, trip organizer, itinerary template, travel itinerary" class="wp-image-8042 size-full" height="609" src="http://www.peanutsorpretzels.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Travel-Itinerary-Cheet-Sheet.png" width="487"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           A travel cheat sheet should be concise. Mine is only one page. The text looks small, but when its printed it can be read fine. Just fold it into quarters and stuff it in your pocket!
          </p>
         </div>
         <h2>
          Items to Include on Your Travel Cheat Sheet
         </h2>
         <p>
          There are all kinds of things you may find useful to put on your travel Cheat Sheet. But the most critical pieces of information to include are only those that you may need to reference quickly while you are on the go.
          <strong>
           So dont overdo it by putting too much information. Keep it concise and easy to read.
          </strong>
          Some of the items I include in my cheat sheet include:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Rough Itinerary
           </strong>
            Dont plan each minute! But its helpful to plan out major activities on each day, especially days when you are traveling.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Hotel Information
           </strong>
            confirmation numbers, addresses, contact information, prices &amp; deposits paid (so you know what you owe on arrival), and possibly directions.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Transportation Information
           </strong>
            flight numbers, train / bus schedules, how to use local transportation and buy tickets, etc.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Attractions
           </strong>
            if there are certain sights that you absolutely want to see, make a note of their operating hours and days (because that might impact your itinerary), addresses, and prices (which will help with budgeting).
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Activities
           </strong>
            anything that you have pre-booked, along with the confirmation numbers.
          </li>
          <li>
           <strong>
            Language Phrases
           </strong>
            include a few simple phrases or notes about the language or even local customs.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <h2>
          Bring a Hard Copy of Your Cheat Sheet &amp; Keep It Handy
         </h2>
         <p>
          My cheat sheet template is only one page and itis grouped into 4 sections so that I can easily fold it into quarters and stuff it in my pocket.
          <strong>
          </strong>
          Within the document itself, I group the information within each quarter section. This way when it is folded, each side has a table of similar information. For example, one side might have my overall itinerary for each day, while another side contains all my hotel information. I also color code the header of each page for each section.
          <strong>
           This makes it easy for me to find the page I need for whatever it is that Im looking for.
          </strong>
         </p>
