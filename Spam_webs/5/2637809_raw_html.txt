        The Worlds Most Beautiful Watery Wonders
       </h1>

        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          As you know 71 percentage of our Earth surface is covered by water. From space the living planet looks blue in color because the water bodies reflects blue in light spectrum. At same time our world also blessed with beautiful rivers, lakes, spectacular waterfalls, stunning glaciers and icecaps. Fortunately these stunning watery wonders can experience by every one of us.
         </span>
         <span id="more-427">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Victoria Falls, Zimbabwe-Zambia
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Victoria waterfalls is the largest waterfall in the world and it is also one of seven natural wonders of the world. It is located in Zambezi river between the border of Zambia and Zimbabwe. Victoria waterfall is one mile wide and it measures falling of water at the rate of five hundred million cubic meter per minute. The water falls to the depth of 93 meters and makes strong spraying of water. Due to this Victoria waterfalls can be spotted from 50 kilometers away from the site.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/victoria-falls-1.jpg">
          <img alt="victoria-falls-1" class="aligncenter wp-image-429" height="672" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/victoria-falls-1.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          The strong spraying of water also makes 24*7 rain in nearest rain forest. Surprisingly visitors can swim at edge of Victoria falls safely and fearlessly. Becausethe natural rock block you from falling down and that pool is known as Devils pool. During full Moon Victoria falls also make one of magnificent natural phenomenon known as Moonbow, beautiful rainbow will form across the waterfalls as light refracted by water.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/victoria-falls-2.jpg">
          <img alt="victoria-falls-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-431" height="658" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/victoria-falls-2.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Great Barrier Reef, Australia
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Great barrier reef is the largest coral reef in the world, one of seven natural wonders of the world. It link together 900 islands and stretching over 2300 kilometers. It is large enough to seen from outer space, also became national icon of Australia. Great barrier reef contains 3000 individual reefs created by micro organisms in millions of years. It became UNESCOs world heritage site in 1981.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-barrier-1.jpg">
          <img alt="great-barrier-1" class="aligncenter wp-image-434" height="713" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-barrier-1.jpg" width="950"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Great barrier reef support wide variety of marine life. There are 1500 species of fishes, 3000 species of mollusks, 500 species of worms, 133 species of sharks and rays and 30 species of whales and dolphins lives within great barrier reef. Nowadays great barrier reef attracts 2 million visitors every year. Glass-bottomed boat tours, snorkeling and kayaking are popular tourist activities in great barrier reef.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-barrier-2.jpg">
          <img alt="great-barrier-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-437" height="642" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-barrier-2.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Niagara Falls, United States
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Niagara falls located in international border between Canada and United States. Niagara combines three waterfalls, American falls, Bridal Veil falls and Horseshoe falls. These three waterfalls together makes average water flow of 85000 ft/s. It is the highest water flow rate than any other waterfalls in the world. Horseshoe falls is the largest one among three water falls of Niagara and most part of it situated in the side of Canada. American falls and smaller Bridal veil falls entirely situated in US region.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/niagara-falls-1.jpg">
          <img alt="niagara-falls-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-440" height="639" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/niagara-falls-1.jpg" width="989"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Niagara was formed 10000 year ago by Wisconsin glaciation. The brilliant green coloration of water of Niagara is caused by mixing up of salt and rock flavor with water at higher rate. The whirlpool that created by Niagara fall will stretches over an area of 1.2 kilometers. The depth of this section same as that of height of Niagara, that is 52 meters. The water from Niagara flows to lake Ontario in the Canadian province.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/niagara-falls-2.jpg">
          <img alt="EC3107-001" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-442" height="512" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/niagara-falls-2.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          The Maldives, Republic of the Maldives
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <div id="stcpDiv" style="position: absolute; top: -1999px; left: -1988px;">
         The Maldives is one of the most beautiful and relaxing places in the world with crystal clear water, white sandy beaches and beautiful resorts, placing you right in the heart of the warm Indian Ocean. It should come as no surprise that the Maldives has a reputation as being one of the best island destinations in the world.  See more at: http://www.flightsite.co.za/blog/?p=1924#sthash.KE56tzZP.dpuf
        </div>
        <div id="stcpDiv" style="position: absolute; top: -1999px; left: -1988px;">
         The Maldives
        </div>
        <div id="stcpDiv" style="position: absolute; top: -1999px; left: -1988px;">
         The Maldives
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          The Maldives is one of the most beautiful and relaxing places in the world with crystal clear water, white sandy beaches and beautiful resorts, placing you right in the heart of the warm Indian Ocean. It should come as no surprise that the Maldives has a reputation as being one of the best island destinations in the world.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/maldives-1.jpg">
          <img alt="maldives-1" class="aligncenter wp-image-443" height="640" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/maldives-1.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          The Maldives consists of 26 atolls. The islands offer crystal clear waters, exotic coral reefs, white sandy beaches and beautiful endemic vegetation. The Islands that make up the Maldives are as close to paradise as you can find. The beaches, clear water and exotic sea life all contribute to making this place the ultimate relaxation destination, where visitors can unwind and switch off. It is a great spot for diving and snorkelling, along with other water based activities where you can explore the unspoiled exotic underwater world that the Maldives has to offer.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/maldives-2.jpg">
          <img alt="maldives-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-445" height="634" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/maldives-2.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         <strong>
          Canals Of Venice, Italy
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Venice is a group of 117 small islands separated by canals and lined through bridges. In other words the 117 canals of Venice divided the city into 117 different Islands. Interestingly these canals served as main transportation network in Venice. Grand canal, the main waterway of the city is the largest canal of Venice, have a length of 3.8 km and width of 60  90 meter.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Venice-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Venice-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-447" height="640" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Venice-1.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Grand canal boat tour becomes the best way to explore Venice, to get deep knowledge about historical importance of the city. Gondolas, traditional flat bottomed boats and motorized boats are mainly used for grand canal tour. Such tours bring close view of historical buildings, palaces, churches and centuries old rialto bridge of Venice.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Venice-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Venice-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-448" height="667" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Venice-2.jpg" width="1000"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Death Sea, Jordan
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Dead sea is the second saltiest bodies of water in the world, located between borderline of Israel and Jordan. The salinity of death sea averages between 34.2 -35 percentage. It is almost ten times greater than that of normal salinity of sea water. This higher percentage of salinity makes lack of aquatic life, thats why this lake called as The dead sea. It is also situated 423 meters below sea level, makes lowest elevation on land.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/death-sea-1.jpg">
          <img alt="death-sea-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-450" height="638" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/death-sea-1.jpg" width="961"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          This high concentration of salt also let the visitors to float in dead sea. The water of dead sea also benefits human health as it contains high volume of minerals like potassium, calcium, sulphur and bromine. This water can heal different skin conditions and will flush out toxins. The unusual concentration of minerals also cause discharging of asphalt in dead sea. It is said to be huge amount of asphalt imported from dead sea to Egypt for during mummification processes.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/death-sea-2.jpg">
          <img alt="death-sea-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-451" height="600" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/death-sea-2.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          The Harbour of Rio de Janeiro, Brasil
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Rio de Janeiro is to Brazil what Machu Picchu is to Peru, the countrys primary tourist attraction. It receives more visitors annually than any other city in all of South America. With approximately 80 kilometres of unspoiled beaches and the famous Sugarloaf and Corcovado mountains overlooking the city and harbour, it is one of the most recognisable and iconic cities in the world.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/harbour-rio-1.jpg">
          <img alt="harbour-rio-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-453" height="768" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/harbour-rio-1.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          The Harbour of Rio, which was originally known as Guanabara Bay, is one of the seven natural wonders of the world as well as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Surrounded by gorgeous granite mountains the harbour has some of the most beautiful and busiest beaches in the world. The area around the harbour is scattered with islands, such as the Snake and Fundao Islands.
         </span>
         <br/>
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/harbour-rio-2.jpg">
          <img alt="harbour-rio-2" class="aligncenter wp-image-454" height="683" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/harbour-rio-2.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Lake Baikal, Siberia
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          Lake baikal is the oldest and deepest lake in the world. It is located in Southeastern Siberia. The lake have a depth of 1700 m and it formed 25 million years ago. The 20 percentage of total fresh water in the world also contain within lake baikal. The lake is also surrounded by scenic areas that are protected by the government. It is also listed within UNESCOs world heritage sites.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/lake-baikal-1.jpg">
          <img alt="lake-baikal-1" class="aligncenter wp-image-455" height="643" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/lake-baikal-1.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          There are 1200 monuments in total situated within baikal region, have many cultural, archaeological and historical values. It is also home for 1340 species of animals. Many of them endemic to lake baikal region. The surrounding mountains, boreal forest and islands also makes lake baikal region to one of most biodiverse site in the world.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/lake-baikal-2.jpg">
          <img alt="lake-baikal-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-456" height="706" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/lake-baikal-2.jpg" width="960"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Great Blue Hole, Belize
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          It is a large natural underwater sinkhole located 70 kilometers from main land and at center of Belize barrier reef. This sinkhole have a depth of 120 meter and it is 300 meter across. It formed in ice age, 150000 years ago by the opening of a dry cave. The melting of ice and rising of seas level causes the formation of great blue hole
         </span>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-Blue-Hole-1.jpg">
          <img alt="great-Blue-Hole-1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-459" height="768" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-Blue-Hole-1.jpg" width="1156"/>
         </a>
         <span style="color: #000000;">
          The great blue hole became a world heritage site in the year 1997. It is also home for 500 rare form of animals and plant life. Every year this natural sink hole attracts more than 2 lacks of international tourists. scuba diving becomes main activity in great blue hole.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-Blue-Hole-2.jpg">
          <img alt="great-Blue-Hole-2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-460" height="667" src="http://traveling-earth.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/great-Blue-Hole-2.jpg" width="1000"/>
         </a>
        </p>
