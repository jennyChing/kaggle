      <p>
       <a href="http://www.thejealouscurator.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/nathan1.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16511" height="900" src="http://www.thejealouscurator.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/nathan1.jpg" title="nathan1" width="584"/>
       </a>
       <br/>
       <a href="http://www.thejealouscurator.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/nathan2.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16512" height="1533" src="http://www.thejealouscurator.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/nathan2.jpg" title="nathan2" width="584"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       I write about embroidery and crochet
       <em>
        a lot
       </em>
       , mainly for two reasons. No.1, I am in complete awe of anyone that can master these techniques. No.2, I absolutely love it when an artist is able to push this once craft-only medium into the world of fine art. Well, not only did New York based artist
       <a href="http://nathanvincent.com/" target="_blank">
        Nathan Vincent
       </a>
       do that, he also managed to cross a few gender roles along the wayare these not the most masculine doilies that youve ever seen? And, if the tighty-whities arent man enough for you, how bout these bad boys:
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.thejealouscurator.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/nathan3.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-16513" height="819" src="http://www.thejealouscurator.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/nathan3.jpg" title="nathan3" width="584"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       Bang.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.thejealouscurator.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/nathanvincent31.jpg">
       </a>
       <em>
        {Nathan is one of an amazing group of artists represented by
        <a href="http://www.murielguepingallery.com/" target="_blank">
         Muriel Guepin Gallery in NYC
        </a>
        }
       </em>
      </p>
