       <div class="entry-content clearfix">
        <p>
         Of course many businesses would like to have a personal profile rather than a Facebook business page because it makes it easier for you to approach potential customers.  Friends can talk to other 'friends'.  So businesses who have set up a personal profile go around asking people to friend them.
        </p>
        <p>
         However, that's against Facebook's terms and conditions.  They specifically state that you can only have one log in to Facebook and that must be a personal profile.
        </p>
        <p>
         People have friends and businesses have fans.
        </p>
        <p>
         So businesses have to create a personal profile for the business owner and then
         <a href="http://lollipoplocal.co.uk/create-a-facebook-business-page" target="_blank" title="create a Facebook business page">
          create a Facebook page
         </a>
         for the business when they are logged in as a person.
        </p>
        <p>
         This is because Facebook don't want you to be able to talk to your customers whenever you want.  That's why they keep cutting back on the number of fans that you an reach with your status updates.  They want you to pay to promote them.  The more you pay, the more of your content your fans will see.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Why businesses should have Facebook pages not personal profiles
        </h2>
        <p>
         The truth is that it's not just about the money that Facebook will lose in advertising revenue.
         <span id="more-13294">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         Protecting individuals on Facebook
        </h3>
        <p>
         It's also about protecting individuals on Facebook.  It's imposing to be asked to be a friend of a business.  Some people will feel intimidated into accepting a request that they might not otherwise have sought out.
        </p>
        <p>
         And it's about privacy.  Many people will change their settings to ensure that only friends can see their status updates.  A business masquerading as a personal profile will have everything set to public.  This means that if the business likes, comments or shares anything by someone who has their settings to friends only - that status update suddenly goes out into the public domain.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Private Messages from potential customers who are not yet your friend
        </h3>
        <p>
         <img alt="facebook business page private message" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-13317" height="44" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/business-page-private-message.png" width="295"/>
         Of course, the down side of being a personal profile as a business is that you don't have the private message button.
        </p>
        <p>
         Potential customers who are outside of your network cannot message you direct for information about your business.  Well not without paying to ensure that the message goes into your Inbox and not the Other inbox spam filter.  Not sure how many potential customers would be prepared to do that.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sure, they can ask to friend you and change that but it may take time for you to notice and accept the request.  The potential customer has suddenly moved on to another local supplier who can accept their message.
        </p>
        <h3>
         Why you should not accept a friend request from a business
        </h3>
        <p>
         These businesses are trying to get ahead of all the others who play by the rules.  As private individuals, we should not be encouraging them.  They put us and our friends at risk of exposure to strangers.  As explained in another post about
         <a href="http://lollipoplocal.co.uk/facebragging-and-fake-profiles-on-facebook-and-social-media" target="_blank" title="Facebragging">
          Facebragging
         </a>
         .  These businesses are not interested in vetting their new 'friends'.  They just want the numbers.
        </p>
        <p>
         They don't care if the people they friend, who look as if they are in the right location and demographic, are actually burglars or scammers waiting to prey on other people who have agreed to be friends with that business.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          That means, if you accept a friend request from a business, you are putting all YOUR friends at risk.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h3>
         What can other businesses who are playing by the rules do?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Other business owners who notice what is going on are well within their rights to
         <a href="http://lollipoplocal.co.uk/report-a-facebook-page" target="_blank" title="report a facebook page or profile">
          report the transgression to Facebook
         </a>
         .  It's a simple button to press - and many do.
        </p>
        <p>
         Facebook are becoming a lot stricter about closing down accounts and banning persistent offenders.
        </p>
        <p>
         Find out
         <a href="http://lollipoplocal.co.uk/welcome-to-facebook" target="_blank" title="how to use Facebook properly">
          how to use Facebook properly
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3>
         What can you do if you have set up a business as a personal profile by mistake?
        </h3>
        <p>
         Give us a call on 01702 476517.  It is possible to migrate your personal profile into a business page and still keep your fans.  We cannot guarantee that we will be able to achieve this - because of the mercurial nature of Facebook - but we do have a 100% record of success so far.
        </p>
       </div>
