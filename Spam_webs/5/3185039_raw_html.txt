            <div class="content narrow-rule ">
             <p class="content-excerpt">
              Contextually-driven actions, empowered by mobile sensor tech, are reconditioning the phone into a contributive shopping companion
             </p>
             <div class="post-index-meta clearfix">
              <a class="author" href="/author/psfk-labs" title="PSFK Writer PSFK Labs">
               PSFK Labs
              </a>
              <ul class="data">
               <li class="date">
                26 february 2015
               </li>
               <li class="category">
                <a class="" href="/category/sponsored-2">

             <p>
              <a href="http://www.psfk.com/2015/02/braintree-contextually-driven-actions-retail-urbanon-placeus.html/uo_rialto_womens" rel="attachment wp-att-269154">
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>

             <p>
              <em>
               The
              </em>
              <a href="http://bit.ly/18mUVdH">

               <em>

               </em>
              </a>
              <em>
               .
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              We all have that friend with whom we love to go shopping. We may cringe at how honest their opinions can be, but receiving timely recommendations can make all the difference when weighing a purchase decision.
             </p>
             <p>
              Today, shoppers can lean on their mobile devices as in-the-know buying companions. By delivering relevant information and communications, a new breed of services are helping transform the mobile phone from a passive device into an active agent that helps shoppers along their purchase path. Mobile sensor technologies such as geo-location and in-store beacons are offering new delivery mechanisms to provide consumers with more personalized and immersive experiences each time they enter a store.
             </p>
             <p>
              In a trend we are calling
              <em>
               Contextually-Driven Actions
              </em>
              , we have noticed that mobile devices are beginning to serve as a portal for serving up information at key moments along a customers purchase path, supporting them in their decision making. This idea is supported by the CMO Councils findings, which indicate that when customers dont receive customized, relevant content and offers, 54 percent of them are willing to end their relationship with a retailer. Below are some of the most compelling examples of how we see this trend entering the marketplace.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.psfk.com/2015/02/braintree-contextually-driven-actions-retail-urbanon-placeus.html/urbanon" rel="attachment wp-att-269155">
               <img alt="urbanon" class="alignnone wp-image-269155" height="563" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/urbanon.jpg" width="1000"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              With its finger on the pulse, clothing retailer Urban Outfitters has been increasing shopper engagement by serving up relevant notifications to loyal customers at key moments during their store visit. Its native app,
              <a href="https://www.urbanoutfitters.com/urban/on/urbanon.jsp">
               Urban On
              </a>
              , prompts shoppers to log in through social media when entering any Urban Outfitters; inside the store users receive relevant mobile messages and promotions.
             </p>
             <p>
              UOs contextually rich experience doesnt end there. When customers are in the changing room, the app nudges them to take selfies while wearing store productsin essence, transforming what once was a personal moment into a social one. When in line to pay, users can shake their smartphones to uncover their loyalty card and/or earn a digital badge. Urban On also lets those shoppers listen to UO Radio while browsing clothes, find nearby UO stores, and send personalized e-gift cards to friends in their networks.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.psfk.com/2015/02/braintree-contextually-driven-actions-retail-urbanon-placeus.html/placeus" rel="attachment wp-att-269153">
               <img alt="placeus" class="alignnone wp-image-269153" height="647" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/placeus.jpg" width="1000"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              In another example of this trend,
              <a href="http://company.alohar.com/placeus/">
               PlaceUs
              </a>
              is a tracking app built on the premise that users tend to repeatedly visit the same locations, which may be of interest to their inner circle of friends. The app is tailored for on-the-go families, couples and friends to inform them where their loved ones are at any given time. In the companys own words, Its about enjoying deeper connections, having peace of mind and simplifying everyday communication.
             </p>
             <p>
              To accomplish this, PlaceUs uses artificial intelligence and ambient-sensing technology to determine the patterns and motions of its users. As the app learns a users routines, it can do things like automatically send a message to a spouse when he or she is headed home from work or stuck in traffic. In stores, it can recognize recurring purchases such as a coffee order at a caf and automatically place that order when the user arrives. By using AI as a predictive force, PlaceUs is able to understand not only where a user is, but also why they are there, and when to lend a helping hand.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://www.psfk.com/2015/02/braintree-contextually-driven-actions-retail-urbanon-placeus.html/aros_quirky_03-17-13_side_ac_b-2" rel="attachment wp-att-269152" style="line-height: 1.5;">
               <img alt="AROS_Quirky_03-17-13_Side_AC_B-2" class="alignnone wp-image-269152" height="563" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/AROS_Quirky_03-17-13_Side_AC_B-2.jpg" width="1000"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              In another example,
              <a href="http://www.quirky.com/">
               Quirky
              </a>
              has developed weather-contextual ads for its app-controlled AC unit. The innovative advertisements come included in weather alerts supplied by weather service
              <a href="http://poncho.is/">
               Poncho
              </a>
              . The ads are delivered whenever temperatures are forecasted to be above 75 degrees. The weather-relevant advertisements are able to effectively time product suggestions to the moment when consumers are most able (and willing) to make a purchase.
             </p>
             <p>
              Contextually-driven actions are elevating the place of the phone to the very foundation upon which brands must build their approach. By delivering timely, accurate recommendations where and when we need them, our mobile devices may soon ensure we are never without an able and willing companion at our sides.
             </p>
