        <p>
         Printmakers
         <a href="http://www.flickr.com/people/pullup_theroots/">
          Todd Freeman
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://megperec.com/">
          Meg Perec
         </a>
         of Freeman &amp; Perec recently created
         <a href="http://www.artprize.org/artist/id/4156">
          Sixty Foot Ghost
         </a>
         , a site specific art installation that recreates a 60 giant squid. The project was created for
         <a href="http://www.artprize.org/">
          ArtPrize 2009
         </a>
         and is currently on display at
         <a href="http://www.artprize.org/venue/id/261">
          47 Commerce SW
         </a>
         in Grand Rapids, MI.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Large animals captivate like few other beings can. They are deified, hunted, consumed and catalogued. While our culture has seemingly amassed a working knowledge of all living species on the planet, one of the worlds giants has successfully eluded the scientific community for centuries. Save for a few partially decomposed specimens virtually nothing is known of Architeuthis dux, the Giant Squid. Architeuthis is a 60 foot ghost, moving unnoticed through deep and dark.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our representation of the giant squid was conceived out of a need to see the animal for ourselves, beyond pale museum subjects or small renderings in books. At life size, the true scale of Architeuthis becomes clear, a massive, unfamiliar animal deserving of the same fascination and wonder owed to any whale, elephant or dinosaur. Our intent is to bring the myth into a gratifying real space, and give the viewer a chance to be confronted with one of the largest and most secretive animals to ever live.
         </p>
        </blockquote>
