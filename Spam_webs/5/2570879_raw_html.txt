           <h1 class="entry-title margin-bottom-30">
            Turkey Sized Vegetarian T-Rex Discovered
           </h1>
           <div class="entry-meta margin-bottom-30 hidden-print" style="display:none">
            <span class="posted-on">
             <time class="entry-date published updatedpull-left" datetime="2015-04-28T04:43:01+00:00">
              April 28, 2015
             </time>
            </span>
            <span class="byline">
             <span class="author vcard">
              <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.zmescience.com/author/osamik/" rel="author">
               Mihai Andrei
              </a>
             </span>
            </span>
           </div>
           <!-- .entry-meta -->
          </header>
          <!-- .entry-header -->
          <div class="entry-content">
           <p>
            A seven year old has discovered the fossil of a turkey-sized dinosaur that roamed South America over 140 million years ago. The tiny dinosaur was related to T-Rex, but had few similarities to it; aside for its size, the dinosaur was a vegetarian, munching on plants instead of terrorizing other creatures.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_38186" style="width: 1034px">
            <a href="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Chilesaurus_2015.jpg">
             <figure class="hammy-responsive size-large wp-image-38186" data-media="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/Chilesaurus_2015-1024x641/3612257075.jpg" data-media300="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/Chilesaurus_2015-1024x641/3962506845.jpg" data-media460="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/Chilesaurus_2015-1024x641/178090511.jpg" data-media600="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/Chilesaurus_2015-1024x641/2393010694.jpg" data-media800="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/Chilesaurus_2015-1024x641/1675971285.jpg" title="">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" height="641" src="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Chilesaurus_2015-1024x641.jpg" title="" width="1024"/>
              </noscript>
             </figure>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Chilesaurus diegosuarezi grew up to ten feet long Photo: Reuters
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            At the end of the Jurassic period, the Earth was very different from what it is today. Average temperatures were a whopping 3 degrees Celsius higher than today, there was virtually no frozen landmass, and of course, dinosaurs ruled the planet. But while most dinosaurs were huge and terrifying
            <em>
             Chilesaurus diegosuarezi
            </em>
            was nothing of sorts.
           </p>
           <p>
            It had short arms and handswith two fingers, like T-Rex, but feet that resembled long-necked dinosaurs. It could grow up to ten feet, but the discovered specimen was about as big as a turkey. It was part of the same group asVelociraptor and Tyrannosaurus, from which modern birds evolved, but its just strange.
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             Chilesaurus is so unexpected, so drastically different than anything else weve seen before. Its an anatomical Frankenstein, says Lindsay Zanno of the North Carolina Museum of Natural Sciences in Raleigh.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_38187" style="width: 630px">
            <a href="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/platypus1_3283123b.jpg">
             <figure class="hammy-responsive size-full wp-image-38187" data-media="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/platypus1_3283123b/1017710948.jpg" data-media300="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/platypus1_3283123b/3128875600.jpg" data-media460="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/platypus1_3283123b/3093829094.jpg" title="">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" height="230" src="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/platypus1_3283123b.jpg" title="" width="620"/>
              </noscript>
             </figure>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Photo: Reuters
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            The species really riddled paleontologists, who have rarely seen such a hybrid and enigmatic dinosaur. So strange was the dinosaur that initially, scientists believed they were dealing with several different species.
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             Its like a T. rex thats been shrunk but not shrunk equally, says Pete Makovicky, an associate curator and dinosaur specialist at The Field Museum in Chicago, who was not involved in the new find. This animal wouldve looked more silly than fearful  more similar to an ostrich than to T. rex.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            The fossil was actually discovered 11 years ago, whenDiego Suarez was only 7 and he was accompanying his geologist parents on an expedition, and the little boy playing actually discovered the dinosaur, who was named in his honor  a remarkable case of beginners luck.
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             Out of nowhere, two small things appeared.  They were fossils, Suarez says by email. He ran to show his mother. His parents, he says, were amazed. You (can) imagine it was, to them, like wining the lottery. The family sent the fossils to dinosaur specialists in Argentina.
            </p>
            <p class="entry-stuff clear margin-bottom-15 hidden-print single-post-ads-between center-block text-center">
             <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
             </script>
             <!-- ZME Science -->
             <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-2424323874644721" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="5307795920" style="display:block">
             </ins>
             <script>
              (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
             </script>
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_38188" style="width: 399px">
            <a href="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/635657339585552171-diego.png">
             <figure class="hammy-responsive wp-image-38188" data-media="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/cache/2015/04/635657339585552171-diego/3835682491.png" title="">
              <noscript>
               <img alt="" height="274" src="http://cdn.zmescience.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/635657339585552171-diego.png" title="" width="389"/>
              </noscript>
             </figure>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Diego Suarez has a passion for fossils. (Photo: Manuel Suarez)
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            In terms of evolutionary significance, Chilesaurus seems to throw a curveball. Its ancestors were meat eaters, its living relatives were meat eaters, but it ate plants.It had vertebrae and front limbs like T-Rex and velociraptor, butits feet, ankle and some of its pelvis looked like they belonged to a completely different branch of plant-eating animals. In a way, its like a platypus  a creature that embodies distinctive traits from entirely different species.
           </p>
           <p>
            Martin Eczurra, a PhD student at Birmingham University said:
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             Chilesaurus can be considered a platypus dinosaur because different parts of its body resemble those of other dinosaur groups due to mosaic convergent evolution.In this process a region or regions of an organism resemble others of unrelated species because of a similar mode of life and evolutionary pressures.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
           <p>
            It might also provide some insight into evolution itself.
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             Chilesaurus provides a good example of how evolution works in deep time and it is one of the most interesting cases of convergent evolution documented in the history of life.It comes as false relationship evidence may arise because of cases of convergent evolution  such as that present in Chilesaurus.
            </p>
           </blockquote>
