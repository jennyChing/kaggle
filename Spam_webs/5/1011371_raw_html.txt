     <p>
      If we told you you could get a great European-style beer in the heart of Texas, youd probably think we were crazy.
     </p>
     <p>
      Meet Adelberts Brewery, just outside of downtown Austin. They adhere to the principle that good beer should be a shared experience, and theres nothing like a good Belgian to bring people together.
     </p>
     <p>
      Established in 2012, Scott Hovey started Adelberts to capture and celebrate the fun-loving spirit of his late brother, George Adelbert HoveyDel for short. We here at
      <em>
       The Craft
      </em>
      spoke with the General Manager of the brewery, Sarah Zomper Haney, to find out what it is about Adelberts that makes them unique, makes them love Austin, and makes them stand out in such a craft-crazed city.
     </p>
     <p>
      At Adelberts its all about making Belgian style bottle-conditioned beer, true to form. Ninety-nine percent of their ingredients come directly from Europe, and you wont find a force-carbonated beer in the placeits all done right in the 750mL bottle youre drinking from. Why 750s? Because, as Sarah says its the sexiest bottle format that exists!
     </p>
     <p>
      Just like Dels stories, the bottles are meant to be shared (though we wouldnt blame you finishing off an entire bottle of their Philosophizer Saison Aleit won the Gold Medal Saison from Best of Craft Beer Awards and the 2013 Craft Beer Awards Gold Medal).
     </p>
     <p>
      Hoveys brother, Del, spent much of his life traveling the world, and his life experience helps provide names for each of the 10 year-round beers that the 20 person team at Adelberts brews. Hovey started out as a business guyhe graduated from Syracuse in 1984 and had a long-standing interest in brewing. He spent eighteen years in the semiconductor business before making the jump to brewing full time and bringing a few of his friends with him. In a 2013 interview, Hovey told
      <a href="http://www.businessworld-magazine.com/business-profiles-and-featured-articles/food-beverage-industry-articles/adelberts-brewery/" target="_blank">
       <em>
        Business World Magazine
       </em>
      </a>
      :
     </p>
     <blockquote>
      <p>
       I always envisioned myself starting a business of my own, he said. I didnt know that it would be at midlife and it would have to do with my love for homebrewing, but it started to make more sense the more I became a fan. My skill set was always more in operations. Id be the the guy fixing things and then breaking them again, just to fix them. This fits my personality.
      </p>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      Hovey started Adelberts when his brother passed awayas a way to honor his memory. It was Del who first introduced Hovey to the world of craft beer and, according to Sarah, Del was a generous guy who loved to share great beer with great people. It was a natural solution.
     </p>
     <p>
      Del is such a part of Adleberts that on the back of each year-round brew youll find a story he once told. Take the Tripel B, for example. One of Dels college girlfriends suggested Scott try making a Bad Boy Brew to commemorate his not-so-quiet college days. What beer to make for a triple B title? A Belgian Tripel. Kismet.
     </p>
     <p>
      The city of Austin maintains a hyper-localized mentality according to Sarah. People are proud of what the Austin scene has to offer, and Austinites are more than happy to support their local breweries. Sarahs advice on the must-sees of Austin: the fantastic culinary offerings of Chinatown, Town Lake Trail, and the great craft-beer-heavy and speakeasy-style bars that populate the citys vibrant nightlife are not to be missed.
     </p>
     <p>
      And of course, if youre around the outskirts of the city where most of the breweries reside, pop in for a tour. Its all about the experience, after all. Stop by, raise one to Del, and thank your lucky stars youre in Texas.
     </p>
     <p>
      For more on Adelberts, check them out
      <a href="http://adelbertsbeer.com/" target="_blank">
       here
      </a>
      .
     </p>
