          <span style="color: #000000;">
           Overview
          </span>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           The Hornet is not a high-end gaming computer by any means. However, if youre looking to build an affordable entry-level system that will allow you to play most games on high settings on a 1080p monitor, then this build is a great option for you.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           The FX-6300 is a solid CPU for gaming and the GTX 750 Ti is plenty powerful enough for you to max out popular games like World of Warcraft, League of Legends, Dota 2, and even higher-end games like Battlefield 4, Skyrim, etc.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           The Hornet also has upgrade potential. The power supply it comes with is pretty powerful as itis capable of powering a fairly high-end video card without any extra adapters.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 75px;">
           You can also easily upgrade to 8GB of RAM. And, the motherboard in this build will support an AMD FX-8320 or 8350, though you would probably want to upgrade the motherboard if you want to do any kind of overclocking.
          </p>

          </p>

           Not only that, but this build offers a good entry point into 25601440 gaming. With the GTX 970, this system can handle most games on a 1440p monitor fairly well. Though, if you want to play more demanding games on that resolution, you might want to look at some more powerful cards.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           However, this build has a lot of upgrade potential. The power supply can handle a video card as big as a GTX 980, GTX 980 Ti, or R9 390x, and your motherboard will allow you to jump up to an i7-4790 as well.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 75px;">

           Overview
          </span>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           The Lion is a high-end gaming computer that wont come with a huge price tag. Yes, $1,000 is a lot of money any way you look at it but compared to a pre-built machine of the same caliber, The Lion seems like a steal
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           With a i5-4670K and aGTX 970,The Lion will handle just about anything you throw at it. It will easily max out anything on a 19201080 monitor and it can be used to max out most games on a 25601440 monitor as well. So, if youe looking for a balance between affordability and the ability to play games at 1440p well, then this build is a great option.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           This build can easily be upgraded in the future as well, as the 550W PSU can easily handle the biggest video cards on the market. Though, if you want to add a second GTX 970later down the road, you will need to upgrade the power supply.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 75px;">
           Ultimately, The Lion is an exceptional gaming computer and for the majority of gamers, it offers more performance than they could ever need.

A divine being, fathered by Poseidon, and of the same blood as Medusa, Pegasus holds a high place amongst the Gods. And, if Zeus trusts the noblest of all horses with the burden of carrying his own thunderbolts, then imagine what you can do with Pegasus by your side!

          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           With two GTX 980s, this build is designed specifically for 4K gaming. Aside from the incredible graphics processing power, Pegasus has an i7-4790K, a 240GB SSD, and 16GB of DDR3 memory, making it perform like a workstation as well as a powerful gaming computer.
          </p>
          <p class="overview-content-p" style="margin-bottom: 27px;">
           Also, this build comes with a top-of-the line motherboard, a big power supplythat willeasily support both GTX 980sand overclocking, and a sleek full tower computer case to house your ridiculously awesome build.
          </p>
