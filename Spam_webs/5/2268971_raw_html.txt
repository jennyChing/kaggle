           <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
            <span>
             The essence of baby boomer memories and events in poetry
            </span>
           </h1>
           <div class="entry-meta">
            <span class="date">
             <i class="fa fa-clock-o">
             </i>
             <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-02-21T02:00:30+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
              February 21, 2014
             </time>
            </span>
            <span class="categories-links">
             <i class="fa fa-folder-open">
             </i>
             <a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/category/books-baby-boomer/" rel="category tag">
              Books
             </a>
            </span>
            <span class="comments-link">
             <i class="fa fa-comments-o">
             </i>
             <a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/2014/02/21/essence-baby-boomer-memories-events-poetry/#comments">
              5 Comments
             </a>
            </span>
           </div>
           <!-- .entry-meta -->
          </header>
          <!-- .entry-header -->
          <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
           <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="14890128" data-link="http://www.boomercafe.com/2014/02/21/essence-baby-boomer-memories-events-poetry/" data-summary="Oh how we love baby boomers' memories, especially when they're articulated the way award-winning poet Harriet Shenkman expresses them." data-title="The essence of baby boomer memories and events in poetry">
           </div>
           <p>
            <br/>
            <strong>
             We baby boomers all have memories of key events in our lives, sometimes even mementos of everything from crises like wars and assassinations to the good ol days of the Beatles, of muscle cars, of the first landing on the moon. Well, BoomerCafs award-winning Poet-In-Resident Harriet Shenkman of Westchester, New York, whose newest published work is 
             <a href="https://finishinglinepress.com" target="_blank">
              Teetering
             </a>
             , (
             <em>
              you can get it by clicking on
              <a href="https://finishinglinepress.com" target="_blank">
               this link
              </a>
              , and then on Bookstore
             </em>
             ) has written of her own memories and mementos in this poem entitled The Sapphire, The Muff and Monopoly.
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            <strong>
             The Sapphire, the Muff, and Monopoly
            </strong>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_13238" style="width: 255px">
            <a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Shankman_Harriet.jpg">
             <img alt="Harriet Shenkman" class=" wp-image-13238 " height="183" src="http://www.boomercafe.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/Shankman_Harriet-350x262.jpg" width="245"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Harriet Shenkman
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            I was thrilled with a rabbit muff
            <br/>
            for my sixth birthday.
           </p>
           <p>
            I stroked the silky white fur,
            <br/>
            plunged my hands into its warmth,
            <br/>
            felt like Jackie O in muff
            <br/>
            matched to fur trimmed boots.
           </p>
           <p>
            I passed Go on the Monopoly board
            <br/>
            for my next birthday,
           </p>
           <p>
            collected two hundred dollars,
            <br/>
            acquired tiny houses and hotels,
            <br/>
            a gloating Donald Trump.
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://www.boomercafe.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Shenkman_Harriet_Cover.jpg">
             <img alt="Shenkman_Harriet_Cover" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-21090" height="350" src="http://www.boomercafe.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Shenkman_Harriet_Cover-226x350.jpg" width="226"/>
            </a>
            And I flaunted a sapphire ring
            <br/>
            for one more September birthday,
           </p>
           <p>
            Kate in the halls of Windsor,
            <br/>
            Nefertiti tempting Charlton Heston.
           </p>
           <p>
            The sapphire was pure glass,
            <br/>
            the fur muff a fake, the pastel money
            <br/>
            useless for the rent.
           </p>
           <p>
            Still, I keep the fur in a cedar closet,
            <br/>
            the paper money in a bank vault,
            <br/>
            the glass jewel in a velvet-lined box
           </p>
           <p>
            to remind me of when I was
            <br/>
            the center of someones universe.
            <br/>
           </p>

            <div class="comment-content">
             <p>
              I wrote the following poem in honor of baby boomers.  All rights reserved.
             </p>
             <p>
              Ode to Baby Boomers
             </p>
             <p>
              Boomers starting to mature
              <br/>
              Stock pile anti-age brochures
              <br/>
              Before we go back to the dust
              <br/>
              Write bucket list and living trust
             </p>
             <p>
              Once was active, young and trim
              <br/>
              Now eyesight is growing dim
              <br/>
              No more are we lads and lasses
              <br/>
              Forced to wear bifocal glasses
             </p>
             <p>
              Those spicy foods we loved to eat
              <br/>
              Now were loving cream of wheat
              <br/>
              Blood pressure is too high or low
              <br/>
              Daily check for new skin moles
             </p>
             <p>
              Cant walk a block or much less run
              <br/>
              Climbing stairs, our goose is done
              <br/>
              Enjoyed the little walks to town
              <br/>
              Now we own a Hoveround
             </p>
             <p>
              Youthful skin is heading south
              <br/>
              Can barely hear unless you shout
              <br/>
              Gaining weight, no longer thin
              <br/>
              Now weve grown an extra chin
             </p>
             <p>
              Teeth arent real, nor is the hair
              <br/>
              We use Depends for underwear
              <br/>
              Pain in joints wont be ignored
              <br/>
              Aleve and Motrin will restore
             </p>
             <p>
              We should be planning to retire
              <br/>
              Want to work, but cant get hired
              <br/>
              Employers say we have the skills
              <br/>
              Wont admit were over the hill
             </p>
             <p>
              As boomers we have set the pace
              <br/>
              Just lack the wind to run the race
              <br/>
              We may be leaving center stage
              <br/>
              But looking good in our old age
             </p>
            </div>
