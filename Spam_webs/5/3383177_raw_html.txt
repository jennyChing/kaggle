                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     KITCHEN REMODEL: FINAL PLANNING
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-3530921532300103328" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     Today we went to IKEA with the goal of walking out with the final plans so next time we are there all we have to do is pay, pick and pack, and load the truck.
                     <br/>
                     <div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      We b-lined to the kitchen section with a few side eye glances at some pretty things. At hte Kitchen section they have computers to work on your kitchen model. I logged into my IKEA 3D Kitchen builder I have been fine tuning since the summer. I had a few questions about fillers.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      My first question was, since my dishwasher is next to a corner (where the breakfast bar will be) I need a filler space to make sure it opens. So How much filler is necessary so when you open the dishwasher it doesn't hit the breakfast bars cabinet's handles. The lady Emily recommended 2 inches filler space next to the dishwasher. Here is an example of a 2 inch filler space.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-AzJEHLa5yAQ/VDs4DoK-R-I/AAAAAAAAAzI/Xh3y2sMzTrE/s1600/photo%2B5.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="400" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-AzJEHLa5yAQ/VDs4DoK-R-I/AAAAAAAAAzI/Xh3y2sMzTrE/s1600/photo%2B5.JPG" width="300"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div>
                      <br/>

                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6H51nag7YCs/VDs4iuopr9I/AAAAAAAAAzQ/8D3guLZnA-E/s1600/overhang.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6H51nag7YCs/VDs4iuopr9I/AAAAAAAAAzQ/8D3guLZnA-E/s1600/overhang.jpg" width="298"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       while I won't have the breakfast bar elevated it will be just as high as the base cabinets this still gave me an idea of what is an acceptable, and feasible over hang.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       While I definitely saved time building my kitchen on the IKEA 3D Kitchen Builder online at home it did take over an hour to finalize all details. This included making sure all cabinet doors had knobs, all draws had handles, estimated the square footage of the counter tops, confirming which pieces are in stock and which ones I have to wait for.
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">

Thery are coming out with new kitchen cabinet fronts in February but I can't take the chance I might not even like the new fronts so now is the time and we are purchasing everything next Saturday!! Instead of Nov first since stock is an issue. While we are not in rush it's better to get all the pieces we need before December. My goal is to have everything done by 2nd week in December just in time for my birthday and Christmas!!!
                      </div>
