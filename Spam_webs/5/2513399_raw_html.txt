           <div>
            <div>
             Bella Notte loves her treats. By treats, I mean anything edible. Or sometimes, things that arent edible but contain edible things- weve gone through
             <em>
              so many
             </em>
             snack cups lately because Zac leaves them all over the place and Bella Notte chews her way through them.
            </div>
            <div>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div>
             In an attempt to discourage all the food stealing, I make sure Bella always has food in her bowl {since she is good about pacing herself- at least with her
             <em>
              own
             </em>
             food} and I make sure to give her plenty of treats! {Which we keep in the
             <a href="http://www.notquitesusie.com/2014/11/diy-craft-idea-dog-treat-jar.html">
              DIY dog treat jar
             </a>
             I made, which is super cute if I do say so myself.} One of her favoritesthatsperfect for the summer are those ice cream cups made just for dogs!
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div>
            </div>
            <div>
             The only problem with those cups? Theyre pretty pricy. I dont give them to her every day or anything, but they still add up quickly! So when a friend of mine told me that she makes her OWN, I just had to get the recipe!
            </div>
            <div>
            </div>
            <p>
            </p>
            <div style="text-align: center;">
             <img alt="DIY Frozen Dog Treats Recipe- a copycat Frosty Paws Recipe with just two ingredients! This is the perfect way to spoil your dog in the summer and will cost WAY less than the store bought ones!" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-13367" height="1049" src="//www.notquitesusie.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/DIY-Frozen-Dog-Treats-Recipe-a-copycat-Frosty-Paws-Recipe-with-just-two-ingredients-This-is-the-perfect-way-to-spoil-your-dog-in-the-summer-and-will-cost-WAY-less-than-the-store-bought-ones-700x1049.jpg" width="700"/>
            </div>
            <div>
            </div>
            <p>
             <strong>
              Ingredients:
              <br/>
             </strong>
             -1 tub plain yogurt, 32 oz
             <br/>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
              -About 1 cup chicken bits*
             </span>
            </p>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
           *These were the pan drippings and bits from a whole chicken I cooked. I poured the pan drippings into a container and tossed them into the refrigerator until I was ready to use. You can also freeze them until ready to use. If you dont have chicken bits, you can always use any other meat you might have- just be sure it contains no onions, garlic or other dangerous items dogs should not eat. The third option is to use whole pieces of chicken or any other meat. Just make sure its cooled before processing.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Directions:
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          1. In a
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00004S9EM/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00004S9EM&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=notquisushom-20&amp;linkId=VNDOIQ4JYIPNEB32">
           food processor
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="//ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=notquisushom-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00004S9EM" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          , add yogurt and chicken bits.Process about 1 minute or until smooth.
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         2. Place
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00COWLXJ4/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00COWLXJ4&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=notquisushom-20&amp;linkId=QYUYFAALX6FPAWDI">
          silicone baking cups
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="//ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=notquisushom-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00COWLXJ4" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
         onto a cutting board or flat tray that is freezer safe.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. Fill with yogurt mix and freeze overnight. Pop out and store in abaggiein the freezer!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="FrostyPawCup1" class="aligncenter wp-image-13369" height="871" src="//www.notquitesusie.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/FrostyPawCup1-700x1028.jpg" width="593"/>
        </p>
