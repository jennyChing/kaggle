      <h1 class="asset-headline" itemprop="headline">
       Stafford's fiance blasts NFL for Suh suspension
      </h1>
     </section>
     <section class="storytopbar-bucket priority-asset-module" id="module-position-OQnFLVRdIAQ">
     </section>
     <section class="storytopbar-bucket story-byline-module" id="module-position-OQnFLVQ6KbU">
      <div class="asset-metabar" itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
       <span class="asset-metabar-author asset-metabar-item" itemprop="name">
        <img class="asset-metabar-avatar" height="26" src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/-mm-/9cab5db1961bebf7e22abf6efe60e11cd034f4c4/c=0-0-120-120&amp;r=1024x1024&amp;r=26&amp;c=26x26/local/-/media/DetroitFreePress/None/2014/10/16/635490675055580024-brian.jpg" width="26"/>
        Brian Manzullo, Detroit Free Press

        5:18 p.m. EST December 30, 2014
       </span>

      <div class="story-asset story-leadin-asset" id="module-position-OQnFLWt22oQ">
       <h2 class="lead-in">
        Kelly Hall says because Stafford isn't Rodgers, no one noticed him being stepped on or cleated
       </h2>
      </div>
      <div class="story-asset story-metadata-asset" id="module-position-OQnFLWsl5wY">
       <div class="article-metadata-wrap">
        <section class="storymetadata-bucket expandable-photo-module" id="module-position-OQnFLVTyOyg">
         <aside class="single-photo expandable-collapsed" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">

           <img alt="Stafford 2" class="expand-img-horiz" data-mycapture-src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/media/2014/12/30/DetroitFreePress/DetroitFreePress/635555533590153130-UST-APC-PACKERS-vs-LION.jpg" itemprop="url" src="http://www.gannett-cdn.com/-mm-/0c7ba20c535b5f4c7e23894c0a5a50f0538bf405/c=103-65-1577-1173&amp;r=x404&amp;c=534x401/local/-/media/2014/12/30/DetroitFreePress/DetroitFreePress/635555533590153130-UST-APC-PACKERS-vs-LION.jpg"/>

           </span>

          </div>
          <p class="image-credit-wrap">

            <span class="cutline js-caption">
             The Green Bay Packers' Sam Barrington hits the Detroit Lions' Matthew Stafford in the second half.
            </span>

      <p>
       <span class="-newsgate-macro-cci-drop-initial-">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Kelly Hall made it loud and clear what side she was on Tuesday on Instagram.
      </p>
      <p>
       Hall, the fiance of Lions quarterback Matthew Stafford, blasted the NFL
       <a href="/story/sports/nfl/lions/2014/12/29/ndamukong-suh-suspension/21004933/" title="http://www.freep.com/story/sports/nfl/lions/2014/12/29/ndamukong-suh-suspension/21004933/">
        for its one-game suspension
       </a>
       of defensive tackle Ndamukong Suh after he stepped on Packers quarterback Aaron Rodgers' leg in the Lions' 30-20 loss Sunday.
      </p>
      <p>
       Hall claimed Stafford was stepped on, too, but no one noticed because Stafford is "not Aaron Rodgers." She
       <a href="http://instagram.com/p/xPUFL9qd61/?modal=true" title="http://instagram.com/p/xPUFL9qd61/?modal=true">
        included this photo
       </a>
       of Stafford from Sunday's game with his left sock torn:
      </p>
      <div class="story-asset oembed-asset" id="module-position-OQnFLWsvKsA">
       <div class="story-oembed-wrap ">

.oembed-asset-photo-author-name{color:#009BFF;}
         </style>

           <span>
            Instagram

          <p class="oembed-asset-photo-caption">
           Thank you. Pretty sure Matthew's leg looks a lot damn worse right now from when he got stepped on/cleated in the same game. But it wasn't Suh.. And he's not Aaron Rodgers, so no one would think twice about it. #nflbias #givemeabreak #DETROITvsEVERYBODY
          </p>

      <p>
       "Pretty sure Matthew's leg looks a lot damn worse right now from when he got stepped on/cleated in the game game," Hall wrote. "But it wasn't Suh... And he's not Aaron Rodgers, so no one would think twice about it. #nflbias #givemeabreak #DETROITvsEVERYBODY."
      </p>
      <p>
       This came after ex-NFL vice president of officiating Mike Pereira
       <a href="/story/sports/nfl/lions/2014/12/30/mike-pereira-ndamukong-suh/21050017/" title="http://www.freep.com/story/sports/nfl/lions/2014/12/30/mike-pereira-ndamukong-suh/21050017/">
        disputed the NFL's decision
       </a>
       , saying Suh's reputation, not the actual step, led to the suspension.
      </p>
      <p>
       Though it's certainly debatable whether Suh should be suspended for a playoff game, Hall brings up a point: Players get stepped on and cleated in NFL games all the time. Obviously, reputation played a part, for Suh and for Rodgers.
      </p>
