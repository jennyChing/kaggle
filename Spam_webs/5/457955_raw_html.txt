         Bentonite Purifying Face Mask
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">

          February 9, 2015

         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

        <p>
         <img alt="Bentonite Clay" class="aligncenter wp-image-7941 size-large" data-lazy-src="http://i1.wp.com/cravethegood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Bentonite-Clay.jpg?resize=1024%2C703" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/cravethegood.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif"/>
        </p>
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Bentonite Clay" class="aligncenter wp-image-7941 size-large" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/cravethegood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Bentonite-Clay.jpg?resize=1024%2C703"/>
        </noscript>
        <p>
         Anyone who knows me knows that I have had troubled skin for the past couple of years. Its oily in some spots, dry in some spots, and I almost always have a handful of blemishes.
        </p>
        <p>
         When my acne was at its worst (September), I dreaded leaving the house in the morning partly because of the pain and partly because I was so embarrassed!! Since then, I have gotten it somewhat under control through a major change in diet. I found out that I am allergic to eggs, gluten, almonds (and other nuts), and all dairy. Avoiding these foods has helped my acne considerably.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://i0.wp.com/cravethegood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Bentonite-Clay-Mask-Ingredients.jpg">
          <img alt="Bentonite Clay Mask Ingredients" class="aligncenter wp-image-7945 size-large" data-lazy-src="http://i0.wp.com/cravethegood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Bentonite-Clay-Mask-Ingredients.jpg?resize=1024%2C834" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/cravethegood.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Bentonite Clay Mask Ingredients" class="aligncenter wp-image-7945 size-large" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/cravethegood.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/Bentonite-Clay-Mask-Ingredients.jpg?resize=1024%2C834"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I am always up for removing toxins from my body and this face mask is the perfect way to purify and soothe my sensitive skin. Anti-inflammatory, high in antioxidants, healing, and detoxifying this face mask is the perfect addition to your beauty routine.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Bentonite Clay
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Well known as healing clay, Bentonite clay has many benefits when used both internally and externally. It helps to expel heavy metal toxins such as lead, mercury, cadmium, and benzene. The clay naturally has a negative charge, which binds to the positively charged heavy metals and removes them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Green Tea
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I decided to add matcha green tea powder for several reasons: it is extremely high in antioxidants, the high levels of chlorophyll are detoxifying, and it is very anti-inflammatory need I say more?
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Activated Charcoal
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Activated charcoal has benefits when applied topically and taken internally. It is extremely porous which allows it to absorb a large amount of toxins out with the old!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            Ingredients
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/2 cup bentonite clay
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             8 capsules of activated charcoal
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 tbsp matcha green tea
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-3" itemprop="ingredients">
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            Instructions
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Measure and combine all ingredients in a metal-free bowl. You will have a large batch (roughly 2/3 cup) of this for several applications. Store in an air-tight container, in a cool and dry place.
            </li>
           </ol>
           <div class="ziplist-recipe-plugin" style="display: none;">
            3.1
           </div>
           <a href="http://cravethegood.com/2015/02/bentonite-purifying-face-mask/" id="zl-printed-permalink" title="Permalink to Recipe">
            http://cravethegood.com/2015/02/bentonite-purifying-face-mask/
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p style="line-height: 18.0pt;">
         <span style="font-size: 12.0pt; font-family: Georgia; color: #333333;">
          For the mask: using a small bowl and plastic or wooden utensil, mix 2 tbsp with around 4tbsp of water or enough to give the mask a mustard-like consistency. Apply to your face and leave on for 15-20 minutes. I suggest that you mist your face or pat water on the mud every 5 minutes or so in order to prevent excessive drying and discomfort. Remove with a damp, warm facecloth. Follow up with your favourite toner and moisturizer.
          <em>
           <span style="font-family: Georgia;">
            I simply use witch hazel for a toner and argan oil with YLLavenderand Purification for mymoisturizer
           </span>
          </em>
          . Your skin may be red after you remove the mask but it should go away in about an hour depending on how sensitive your skin is!
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         After I removed the mask, I noticed that some of my cystic acne came to the surface. The hard sebum simply popped through and I was able to remove it with the swipe of my finger.
        </p>
        <p>
         When you are mixing the clay, it is important to remember
         <strong>
          not
         </strong>
         to mix it with any metal utensils or use bowls that have metals in them because the metal will interact with the charge of the clay.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         *****
        </p>
        <p>
         As an added bonus, I filled several vegetable capsules with the mask for an internal detox and made up a small container for overnight spot treatments on-the-go.
        </p>
