         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          My Wine Page: #Wine
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-byline">
          by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://findingourwaynow.com/author/findingourway" itemprop="url" rel="author" title="Posts by Susan P. Cooper">
            <span itemprop="name">
             Susan P. Cooper
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>

           Mar 15, 2015
          </time>

           <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="1008 http://findingourwaynow.flywheelsites.com/?p=1008">

           </span>
          </a>

         <!-- .entry-byline -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          Ive little time of late, to do any kind of research on the many fun new wines Ive come across. That said, I thought you might enjoyhearing about how my
          <a href="http://findingourwaynow.com/wine">
           wine page
          </a>
          came about on my blog.
         </p>
         <h2>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           My Wine Page
          </span>
         </h2>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           To set the record straight, I am, by no means, a wine expert. Like so many, I just know what kind of wine I enjoy.
           <img alt="My Wine Page, findingourwaynow.com" class="alignright wp-image-14631" height="347" src="http://findingourwaynow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Screen-Shot-2013-03-05-at-12.29.52-PM.png" title="My Wine Page, findingourwaynow.com" width="229"/>
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           My wine page all started innocently enough. I have a dear friend, Elizabeth, who shares my interest in wine. When Elizabeth visitswe go to the wine shop and select a wine we know, and for fun, one we dont. A few months after I started the blog, I had mentioned a specific wine we had shared in a blog post. Elizabeth suggested I share our many wine finds on my blog. She had an ulterior motive. It was so she could keep track of what we liked and what I recommended. I did as she suggested at the very next opportunity. At first it was just a mention of the wine, tasting notes and an image. Over time, it has involved into researching and sharing a bit of history and information about each winery/vintner along with various pictures and tasting notes.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Elizabeth and I still reminisce at the many times weve shared some really great wines. We also laugh about the wines we didnt like (really hated),throwing them down the drain. Those wines never get posted LOL. We still rememberthe fun we had in selecting wine when we were in need of a serious attitude adjustment; many good wines fit the bill very nicely indeed. Besides, it would be a sin to pass up anopportunity to enjoy a good glass of wine regardless of the reason; dont you think?
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://findingourwaynow.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Red-Or-White-Wine.jpg" target="_blank">
           <img alt="My Wine Page, findingourwaynow.com" class=" alignleft wp-image-1386" height="315" src="http://findingourwaynow.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Red-Or-White-Wine.jpg" title="My Wine Page, findingourwaynow.com" width="237"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           That is how my wine page got started and what it is today.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           To this day, Elizabeth still looks up wines on my blog when shes out at the wine shop looking for a particular wine she knows she will enjoy. How cool is that?
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           My wine page has continued to evolve. No surprise; I have found it to be a great deal of fun sharing my many discoveries withanyone who finds it of interest.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span style="line-height: 1.5;">
           Im very fortunate to be living in and around the wine country. I never take that for granted. In addition to my wine discoveries, I love to research a bit about the winery/vintner who produces these delights. Through the years, I found myself partnering with multiple marketing and PR firms that bring new additions to what I now share on my blog. Having all this access has allowed me the opportunity to share information Iuncover about the various wines, from boutique wineries to national brands. Regardless, I do enjoy sharing some of the delightful surprises Ive found along the way.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Irrespective of all that, I do enjoy taste-testing various wines and then sharing some of the pleasant surprises I have enco
          <img alt="My Wine Page, findingourwaynow.com" class=" alignright wp-image-14629" height="368" src="http://findingourwaynow.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Screen-Shot-2013-03-05-at-12.22.10-PM.png" title="My Wine Page, findingourwaynow.com" width="244"/>
          untered. Besides, a good glass of wine, especially with a friend, can always ease a frustrating and hectic day or help us celebrate whatever moves us.
         </p>
         <p>
          I invite you to join in the fun. If you find a bottle of wine you greatly enjoy, let me know. It would be great fun to pass it along on my wine blog, after taste-testing it, of course :-).
         </p>
         <p>
          As a side note, manyof the wines I mention, most often, can be found on
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search/ref=as_li_qf_sp_sr_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=aps&amp;keywords=wine&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=fin0aa-20&amp;linkId=IAHQUFMXGFZZELOF">
           amazon.com
          </a>
          , bevmo.com, totalwine.com, wine.com, or on the winerys website. Many times these sites will offer free shipping, and theyre fast at delivering what you desire directly to you. So give them a look.
         </p>
         <p>
          Last of all and for a bit of fun, check out a few of
          <a href="http://findingourwaynow.com/wine">
           my wine page posts
          </a>
          . Who knows, you might just find some great wines to give a try. Enjoy.
         </p>
