        <p>
         Its a time-honored New York City tradition to go completely apeshit at any hint of inclement weather, and raiding the nearest Trader Joes/Whole Foods/Citarella is always the first order of business.
        </p>
        <p>
         In most parts of the country where winter exists, people go through this ritual as well, but they are practical in their purchasingdecisions:Bread and milk gofirst, followed by all the salty snacks, beer, wine, and liquor.
         <strong>
          New Yorkers, on the other hand, emergency shop as if they are getting stocking up for the worlds longest dinner party,
         </strong>
         beelining for the artisan cheeses, throwing bows in thegluten-free aisles, and making sure theres enough high-grade bacon on hand to tackle
         <a href="http://luckypeach.com/recipes/bacon-dashi/" target="_blank">
          Dave Changs pork dashi recipe
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         If you need one single piece of evidence to reflect the current state of NYCculture, its that it is all but impossible to get your hands on any kale right now in the city. Thats right:
         <strong>
          New Yorkers are bracing for#Snowmageddon2015by
          <i>
           buying all the kale
          </i>
          (as well as the kombucha, natch).
         </strong>
