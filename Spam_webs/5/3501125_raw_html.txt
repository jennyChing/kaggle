        <p>
         More than half of Americans believe that they or others are better off with Obamacare, a new poll shows.
        </p>
        <p>
         The CNN poll
         <a href="http://i2.cdn.turner.com/cnn/2014/images/07/22/rel7c.pdf" target="_blank" title="released">
          released
         </a>
         Wednesday found that 18 percent of respondents said they or their family had benefited from the health care law, while an additional 35 percent said while they may not be better off, the lives of others have improved.
        </p>

         Forty-four percent say no one has benefited from Obamacare.
        </p>
        <p>
         (
         <a href="http://www.politico.com/story/2014/07/obamacare-democrats-health-care-moment-109247.html" target="_blank">

         </a>

        <p>
         The polls results come a day after a pair of contradictory federal appeals court decisons  one saying insurance subsides cannot be used in federal-run exchanges, while the other ruled that people can use subsidies in both federal and state health care exchanges.
        </p>
        <p>
         Despite the ongoing legal challenges the law has faced, support for the law has remained relatively consistent since CNNs March poll at 40 percent, while 59 percent said they oppose Obamacare.
        </p>
        <p>
         The CNN poll surveyed 1,012 adults and was conducted on July 18-20. It has a margin of error of plus or minus 3 percentage points.
