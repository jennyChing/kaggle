                 <h1 class="entry-title contentheading">
                  <span>
                   Side Effects of Abuse Trick You Into Staying
                  </span>
                 </h1>
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="articledivider">
               </div>
               <div class="contentpaneopen clearfix">
                <div class="article-info-surround ">
                 <div class="entry-meta article-tools article-info-surround2">

                  <span class="entry-date createdate">

                  </span>
                  <span class="author createby vcard">
                   <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.healthyplace.com/blogs/verbalabuseinrelationships/2011/01/about-kellie-holly/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Kellie Jo Holly">
                    Kellie Jo Holly

                <div class="entry-content">
                 <div addthis:title="Side Effects of Abuse Trick You Into Staying " addthis:url="http://www.healthyplace.com/blogs/verbalabuseinrelationships/2012/07/side-effects-abuse/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style ">
                  <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:layout="button_count">

                 <p>
                  Staring at this blank space, waiting for me to write something, reminds me of my days of abuse. Each day could seem so clean and full of promise  the trick was, I had to expend energy to make it a great day, use my will to write the day full of actions that led me toward my goals and toward a new and better day.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  But, unfortunately, I didnt have the energy to create a new day. I didnt have the ability to break the days chains and move forward. All I could do was sit there and wait for the day to write itself.Slowly, just as the sun moved across the sky, my blank day moved from bright to dark with no help from me.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Id go to bed that night knowing Id created nothing grand, never mind improved myself in any way. Never mind improving my thoughts, my plans, or the ability to live any dream. No, my days in abuse were blanks. Unwritten opportunities that
                  <em>
                   could have
                  </em>
                  been something grand if only Id had the energy needed to progress.
                  <span id="more-960">
                  </span>
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  At night, exhausted from nothing, Id berate myself for not fulfilling my potential. I hated myself for allowing my dreams to constantly sit on the tip of my tongue, excited but silent. I blamed myself, my Depression, my laziness, my lack of stick-to-it-ive-ness and inability to find my motivation.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <img alt="living with abuse saps motivation" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-961" height="180" src="http://www.healthyplace.com/blogs/verbalabuseinrelationships/files/2012/07/abuse-on-shoulders-153x180.jpg" title="living-with-abuse" width="153"/>
                  The silent oppressor, Abuse, laughed at my struggle and relished my willingness to add to its goals with my constant self-abuse. No, Abuse slept well at night, hooked to my energy through a feeding tube, sucking down my life force and gaining weight for tomorrow.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  All of this happened without my knowledge. I didnt consider that my relationship could be a factor in my depressed state or the self-talk I listened to and never refuted. I am a firm believer in each individuals ability to create life as they want it to be. I didnt often think to blame anyone or anything but myself. I would put such thoughts aside and berate myself for being weak or selling-out to an excuse instead.
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  Process of Realizing Abuse Was My Weakness
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  When I first realized that my husband abused me, I was angry!
                  <em>
                   He
                  </em>
                  angered me to the point of dizziness. I couldnt believe the things he said and did. I couldnt believe that someone I loved would seek to suppress me and not give a hoot about how
                  <em>
                   he made me feel
                  </em>
                  .
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  I allowed myself to blame him. I allowed myself to be angry at him. And I set sail on a course to
                  <em>
                   prove to him
                  </em>
                  that he abused me.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  If any of you have tried that tact, then you already know the outcome. Inflammatory fights, outright denial, and
                  <em>
                   increased abuse
                  </em>
                  of ALL types. It wasnt until I separated my husband from the Abuse that I was able to carry on, but I do not regret
                  <em>
                   feeling the anger
                  </em>
                  .
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Under the weight of abuse, I also denied myself the right to feel anger. Anger wasnt helpful, it didnt solve anything, and he told me I had no right to be mad and listed a million reasons to back his case. So I quit feeling angry. I was a regular Polly Anna. So reversing my position and becoming a raving lunatic banshee felt really, really good.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  But, as I said before, anger
                  <em>
                   toward him
                  </em>
                  wasnt helpful and it certainly didnt solve anything. I began seeing Abuse as a separate entity (specifically a demon). Abuse was strong, uncaring, and evil to the core with no remorse. And as long as Abuse and my husband were attached, there was no place for me. Their unholy alliance would stop at nothing to control me.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  I deserved better. I ended up leaving, and it was the best thing I could have done.
                 </p>
                 <h2>
                  Life After Abuse
                 </h2>
                 <p>
                  Im not going to lie and say the day after I left him life became rosy; it certainly did not. I mourned the relationship, I doubted my decision. I thought about grovelling at his feet and garnering another chance. Fear for my future filled me so full at times that I wondered if I would live another day. I wished I wouldnt wake up the next morning some nights when I went to sleep. The stress felt overwhelming.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  But
                  <em>
                   I was feeling the stress
                  </em>
                  ! It was a miracle. I felt the stress, and I did not die. Despite the doubt and pain, I had the energy to deal with it. There was no one riding me about my inability to cope with the real world or to insert doubt into my mind.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  I clearly understood what I was going through for the first time in years. It was as if walking through that door knocked 500 pounds I didnt know I carried clean off my shoulders. Five hundred pounds of Abuse demon and its attached man fell right off, and I was able to use my energy to raise up my soul, reclaim my self, and reach my goals.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Snap. Just like that.
                 </p>
