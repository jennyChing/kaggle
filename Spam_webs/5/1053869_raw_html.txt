         Norfolk Firm Becomes First Carbon Balanced Publication Printer
        </h3>
        <h4>
         Aylsham-based Barnwell Print is Norfolks first Carbon Balanced Publication Printer
        </h4>
        <p>
         Ever conscious of environmental issues, Barnwell Prints responsible approach to the environment has seen a 90% reduction in the amount of waste sent to landfill  waste that is now recycled instead. And recent investment in electricity usage has resulted in a 20 tonne reduction in carbon dioxide emissions.
        </p>
        <div class="columns">
         <div class="column col-1-3">
          <figure class="full-width-mobile " style="width: 115px;">
           <img alt="" class="responsive wp-image-1748" data-src="wp-content/uploads/2012/04/biomass-energy-logo.png" src="/"/>
          </figure>
         </div>
         <div class="column col-2-3 last">
          <p>
           A further 24 tonne reduction in carbon emissions has been achieved by installing a brand new biomass heating system at our printing premises in Aylsham near Norwich. Were one of the first printers in the UK to utilise a biomass heating system which is fuelled by highly efficient wood pellets.
          </p>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         Barnwell Print has now taken a step further in becoming the countys first Carbon Balanced Publication printing pioneer, where the carbon impact of the whole production of a printed item is estimated and then offset. Verified by the Edinburgh Centre of Carbon Management and facilitated by their key paper supplier, these carbon emissions are then balanced, or offset, by the World Land Trust through the purchase and preservation of endangered areas of natural forest.
        </p>
        <p>
         The World Land Trust is an international conservation charity based in Suffolk, which protects the worlds most biologically important and threatened habitats. Since its foundation in 1989 the WLT has funded partner organisations around the world to create reserves and give permanent protection to wildlife and their habitat. To date they have assisted in the purchase of more than half-a-million acres of critical land.
        </p>

         Sir David Attenborough supports the World Land Trust

         Why Use Carbon Balanced Paper?
        </h2>

         <li>
          Reduces your carbon footprint

          Adds credibility to your brand by use of a unique logo
         </li>
         <li>

         </li>
         <li>
          We can produce certificates &amp; management information to show your carbon savings and the area of land you have preserved
         </li>
         <li>
          Our house sheet, Regency offsets 0.85 tonnes of carbon and saves 67m of forest per tonne of paper used
         </li>
         <li>
          Carbon balancing (Offsetting) is done through the World Land Trust who are an environmental charity
         </li>
         <li>
          The World Land Trust buy areas of critically threatened tropical forests to preserve and cultivate
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Director, Julian Barnwell explainsCarbon Balanced Publication is one of the highest statements of integrity and credibility for users of paper-based communications, because the carbon impacts of the whole production of the printed item has been estimated and offset. We are extremely proud that we are using Carbon Balanced papers as our house sheet and equally proud to be leading the way.
        </p>
        <p>
         Barnwell Prints contribution makes impressive reading. Their commitment to the environment has enabled the Norfolk based printer to offset 525 tonnes of carbon and help the World Land Trust preserve 62,000 square metres (15.2 acres) of critically threatened tropical rain forest. This carbon balancing is equivalent to taking 189 average petrol cars off the road each year, or 292 return flights from London to New York.
        </p>
