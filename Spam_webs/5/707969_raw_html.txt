     <div class="post-content-container container" itemprop="articleBody">

       <div class="col-md-8 col-md-offset-2 post-content-page-wrapper">
        <div class="post-content-page single">
         <p>
          I've finally decided to chronicle the most unsettling sequence of events from my life. I've been asked about/told this story so many times, it made sense to put it all down in a hard copy. The following all took place over the first quarter of my freshman year in college in the mid-00s.
         </p>
         <p>
          So I'm eighteen years old and me and one of my best girlfriends (K) are moving into our dorm room. Our room is on the first floor of a sought after coed dorm on campus. We're moving our things in and everyone is extremely friendly, people are coming in and out of every room, it felt like the opening scene out of a 90s teen movie or something.
         </p>
         <p>
          K and I barely unpack and are swept away by the adrenaline rush of meeting and drinking with all the new faces of the floor. We end up in T and S's room, two girls that live down the hall. Their room is identical to ours, but K notices a strange protrusion on the side of the wall, like a water bulge in a ceiling, but it had a unique design instead of being one big lump. T and S are like, "Oh great, watch it be mold. I really don't want to move off this floor." They decide they'll tell the RA and have him take a look at it at some point.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our first week goes by and I've never felt closer to a group of people in such a short amount of time. So when something strange happened, everyone on the floor knew about it. Like when B, another girl on the floor, had such an extreme night terror shedropped out of school for the quarter. Apparently B had a flashback to an incident of sexual assault from her past. Very, very unfortunate, but nothing utterly bizarre.
         </p>
         <p>
          A few days later, T and S are knocking on my door asking if I have any batteries. I find a pack and head over to their room to go hang out. Our RA asked them to take a photo of the bulge so he could send it to maintenance. T and S show me how their entire pack of batteries must be defunct. They put a fresh set from their pack in T's camera, take a photo of me standing in their room, show me the photo, and then they point the camera at the bulge. The camera dies. They tell me they've used numerous sets of batteries from their relatively new pack. They try the ones I brought over. The same thing happens.
         </p>
         <p>
          We're nervously laughing at this point, "It must be the camera. It HAS to be the camera." I grab my camera, bring it over, take a test photo, then point it at the protrusion. It dies. WHAT THE FUCK.
         </p>
         <p>
          There was an all outfrenzy on the floor over this fucking bugle. It became a nightly routine to show someone new. No matter what you did, you couldn't take a photo of it. We must have tried 25 different cameras. It was strange, but the "My friend's Dad knows this priest, and should we call some reality show" type banter was entertaining initially.
         </p>
         <p>
          There was a flood of unusual occurrences over the next two months. People saying they felt like they were being watched when they slept. Lights going out right after they had been replaced. Items and belongings moved around when roommates would leave for a weekend. Most people that heard about it brushed it off as everyone on the floor going stir crazy or wanting attention by adding some fuel to the fire, but all these instances were happening weeks after the initial hype died off.
         </p>
         <p>
          I told my parents about the whole ordeal and they'd ask about it every so often when I'd call home. One of my Dad's close friends is a private investigator who caught word of everything. He was more than willing to do some research on the building to see if something abnormal had happened there. I kept declining the offer, honestly I just wanted the whole thing to be over.
         </p>
         <p>
          Then I heard a story from my RA. He was up late writing a paper and left his single to grab some snacks. He swears he locked the door, and I always believed him since he was the one having to deal with the headaches of reporting the incidents, getting new bulbs, on and on. When he returned, a directory had fallen from a bookshelf above his desk. This is where everyone calls bullshit, but after seeing the cameras with the bugle, I still believe my RA. He said two highlighters also fell on top of the open directory. The first sat on one page and the second sat across from it on the other. They pointed precisely to a single name in the directory. My RA did not tell me the name, he said she was a current female student at the school, and he was not going to contact her because 1. how the fuck do you start that conversation and 2. he did not want to be committed.
         </p>
         <p>
          Chills. Fuck it. I told my Dad to call his PI friend. A couple weeks later I heard back. A female freshman was raped and murdered in T and S's room in the 70s. Also, her killer was months away from being up for parole for the first time ever.
         </p>
         <p>
          I told very few people about the info. I also did not want to be committed.
         </p>
         <p>
          I have no idea if there was any correlation between B's night terror and everything else, I have not spoken to her since she left. I know what I saw, I believe what my friends told me. That's where it ends for me. Adding it all up and trying to find a solution is the type of thing that makes people go crazy.
         </p>
         <p>
          And yeah, K and I moved the fuck out of there.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>

	//Note, we should refactor below once the messaging system is out.

	var ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION, LINK_DETECTION_REGEX, MARKSAFE_FUNCTION, emojify, formatTextForHTML, linkify;

	//below regex is from: http://stackoverflow.com/questions/6927719/url-regex-does-not-work-in-javascript
	LINK_DETECTION_REGEX = /(([a-z]+:\/\/)?(([a-z0-9\-]+\.)+([a-z]{2}|aero|arpa|biz|com|coop|edu|gov|info|int|jobs|mil|museum|name|nato|net|org|pro|travel|local|internal))(:[0-9]{1,5})?(\/[a-z0-9_\-\.~]+)*(\/([a-z0-9_\-\.]*)(\?[a-z0-9+_\-\.%=&amp;]*)?)?(#[a-zA-Z0-9!$&amp;'()*+.=-_~:@/?]*)?)(\s+|$)/gi;

	ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION = Handlebars.Utils.escapeExpression;

	MARKSAFE_FUNCTION = function(str) {
	  return new Handlebars.SafeString(str);
	};
	linkify = function(safeContent) {
	  return safeContent.replace(LINK_DETECTION_REGEX, function(url) {
	  	brEnd = false;
	  	if(url.indexOf("\n")) {
	  		brEnd = true;
	  	}
	  	url = url.trim();
	    url = url.replace(/(?:\r\n|\r|\n)/g, '');
	    var address;
	    address = /[a-z]+:\/\//.test(url) ? url : "http://" + url;    
	    url = url.replace(/^https?:\/\//, '');
	    link = "&lt;a href='" + address + "' target='_blank' rel='nofollow'&gt;" + url + "&lt;/a&gt;";
	    if(brEnd) {
	    	return link + '&lt;br/&gt;';
	    } else {
	    	return link;
	    }
	  });
	};

	formatTextForHTML = function(content) {
	  var safeContent;
	  safeContent = ESCAPE_EXPRESSION_FUNCTION(content);
	  safeContent = linkify(safeContent);
	  safeContent = safeContent.replace(/(?:\r\n|\r|\n)/g, '&lt;br /&gt;');

	  return MARKSAFE_FUNCTION(safeContent);
