        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.bakingqueen74.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_1380.jpg">
          <img alt="Slow Cooker Black Forest Fudge" class="alignnone size-full" src="http://www.bakingqueen74.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_1380.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I actually made this fudge back in August but have been saving it until now, so am making up for that now as it is fudge season at last!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Recipe:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         400 g chocolate (I used Dairy Milk)
         <br/>
         397 g can of condensed milk
         <br/>
         1 tsp vanilla extract
         <br/>
         100 g Black Forest flavour icing sugar from Sugar and Crumbs
         <br/>
         100 g glac cherries, mostly chopped, a few halved
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Method:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Melt the chocolate in the slow cooker with the condensed milk and vanilla extract.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once it is smooth and silky you can take it out. It only took half an hour on high in my slow cooker, which runs very hot.
        </p>
        <p>
         Sieve in the icing sugar and beat or whisk well. Stir in the chopped cherries.
        </p>
        <p>
         Pour the mixture into a lined baking tray. Press the halved cherries on top.
        </p>
        <p>
         Put the tray in the fridge to chill for a couple of hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove and chop it up, enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         If you want to keep it, it will last for 2 weeks in the fridge in an airtight container.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.bakingqueen74.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_1381.jpg">
          <img alt="Slow Cooker Black Forest Fudge" class="alignnone size-full" src="http://www.bakingqueen74.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/IMG_1381.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         For more fudge, check out this Eton mess fudge from The Crafty Larder, which looks delicious:
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.thecraftylarder.co.uk/2014/09/eton-mess-fudge.html">
          http://www.thecraftylarder.co.uk/2014/09/eton-mess-fudge.html
         </a>
        </p>
