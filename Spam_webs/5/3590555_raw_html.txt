         <span style="font-family: arial black,avant garde; font-size: 16px; color: #0000ff;">
          By Jason Curlee
         </span>

        <p>
         I dont worry a lot about per post reach. I think its a futile chase. You shouldnt think about it like that either. If youre creating content that serves your audience and engages with them your reach will take care of itself.
        </p>
        <p>
         To say that, I really do want to show you some exciting things Ive been seeing with Facebook videos.
        </p>
        <p>
         Luckily, Ive been creating videos for quite a while. I also had been in the midst of posting some daily videos on Facebook when Facebook decided to tweak its wonderful algorithms. I noticed that the per post reach on most of my posts dropped down some. Even certain types of posts that used to do excellent were really low.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well that doesnt scare me since Im always testing. What I noticed surprisingly though, was where text posts were lower, video posts had sky-rocketed. Facebook videos were now getting sometimes five times the per post reach or more.
        </p>

         As you can see it reached 2,261 people and was played 463 times. Thats HUGE.

         Again we did nothing more that posting an engaging video. Our page at this time had 1,042 likes. So thats over double the likes and the video plays equaled almost half the likes.
        </p>
        <p>
         This has huge potential if you can use Facebook videos right, so lets look at three ways.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="color: #0000ff;">
          How To Use Facebook Videos
         </span>
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Short Facebook Videos Work -
          </strong>
          Remember this is Facebook. Its about
          <a href="https://www.facebook.com/jasoncurlee.me/posts/436520353117642" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.facebook.com/jasoncurlee.me/posts/436520353117642', 'micro-content']);" title="Micro Content">
           micro-content
          </a>
          . People dont have time to stop and watch a 5 to 10 minute video, but most have time for 30 sec to a minute and 30 seconds. Dependent on the potential engaging content they may stay longer dependent on what time of the day.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Post One or Two Per Day -
          </strong>
          SEE POINT ONE!!!! Dont go overboard now that you found an answer to getting some more reach. Dont start trying to chase reach by posting videos all the time. Make it a part of you overall strategy. Remember also that they need to be engaging. Thats what Facebook uses to gauge what to get more of to your fans.
         </li>
         <li>
          <strong>
           Use Videos of You
          </strong>
           This is a powerful way to connect with your audience on a totally different level. The more you use video of you, your audience begins to read in your voice. That doesnt mean you cant use other types of videos. Videos will help you take an extra step of being the expert in your niche.
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <strong>
          So get out there and start using Facebook videos. Experiment and test them within your story and see if you dont get results.
         </strong>
        </p>
