     <h1 class="nodetitle">
      Starting a traffic jam
     </h1>
    </div>
    <form action="/title/Starting+a+traffic+jam" enctype="multipart/form-data" id="pagebody" method="post" name="pagebody">
     <input name="node_id" type="hidden" value="1056861"/>
     <div class=" item">
      <div class="contentinfo contentheader">
       <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
        <tr class="wu_header">
         <td class="wu_type">
          <span class="type">
           (
           <a href="/user/KrazyIvan/writeups/Starting+a+traffic+jam">
            idea
           </a>
           )
          </span>
         </td>
         <td class="wu_author" style="text-align:left">
          by
          <a name="KrazyIvan">
          </a>
          <strong>
           <a class="author" href="/user/KrazyIvan">
            KrazyIvan
           </a>
          </strong>
         </td>
         <td class="wu_dtcreate" style="text-align:right">

           Wed May 23 2001 at 5:32:58
          </small>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
      </div>
      <div class="content">
       First of all, you must understand the underlying
       <a class="populated" href="/title/principle" title="principle">
        principle
       </a>
       s of how a
       <a class="populated" href="/title/traffic+jam" title="traffic jam">
        traffic jam
       </a>
       works.
       <br/>
       Traffic jams are essentially the result of delayed action and/or slow
       <a class="populated" href="/title/human" title="human">
        human
       </a>
       reflexes.
       <p>
        For example, when you are
        <a class="populated" href="/title/driving" title="driving">
         driving
        </a>
        and the person ahead of you slows down for a bit before
        <a class="populated" href="/title/speeding" title="speeding">
         speeding
        </a>
        back up again, how long does it take for you yourself to also tap the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/brakes" title="brakes">
         brakes
        </a>
        to slow down then speed up again?
        <br/>
        Clearly, you and the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/driver" title="driver">
         driver
        </a>
        ahead of you do not slow down and speed up again in unison. The usual sequence
of events usually follows this pattern:
        <br/>
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         The driver ahead of you slows down.
        </li>
        <li>
         You see the driver ahead slow down.
        </li>
        <li>
         A second later you press down on your brakes to slow down.
        </li>
        <li>
         The driver ahead of you speeds up.
        </li>
        <li>
         You see the driver ahead of you speed up.
        </li>
        <li>
         A second later, you press down on your
         <a class="populated" href="/title/accelerator" title="accelerator">
          accelerator
         </a>
         to speed up.
        </li>
       </ol>
       Now what of the driver behind you? And the driver behind that driver? All those precious seconds will add up! Each and every
       <a class="populated" href="/title/car" title="car">
        car
       </a>
       behind the original
driver who slowed down must follow the sequence of events described above. Some drivers may take more
time to
       <a class="populated" href="/title/respond" title="respond">
        respond
       </a>
       and make the
       <a class="populated" href="/title/situation" title="situation">
        situation
       </a>
       nastier. Can you see how all this can be a problem?
       <p>
        Every time a driver
        <a class="populated" href="/title/stomps" title="stomps">
         stomps
        </a>
        on the brakes, he is creating a zone of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/compression" title="compression">
         compression
        </a>
        on the road. Cars in this zone will likely squish closer together and slow down to avoid hitting each other. This is the traffic jam. Now for every zone of compression, there is also a zone of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/rarafiction" title="rarafiction">
         rarafiction
        </a>
        , where cars are more sparse and tend to speed up to take advantage of all that open road ahead of them.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Now here's the fun part.
        </strong>
        To start a traffic jam, gently press down on the brakes and slow down on the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/freeway" title="freeway">
         freeway
        </a>
        . Make sure the car behind you does not attempt to switch to another lane to pass you up. Speed up again and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/weave" title="weave">
         weave
        </a>
        between
        <a class="populated" href="/title/lanes" title="lanes">
         lanes
        </a>
        and slow down again if you have to. It helps to have a friend help you out here. What you are trying to do is to get the cars behind you to slow down enough to get the cars behind them to slow down, and the cars behind those cars... etc. Now use that zone of rarafiction to speed up and make your
        <a class="populated" href="/title/getaway" title="getaway">
         getaway
        </a>
        . If you have a few friends to help you out, weave between lanes like
        <a class="populated" href="/title/crazed+ferrets" title="crazed ferrets">
         crazed ferrets
        </a>
        , slowing down and speeding up at
        <a class="populated" href="/title/random" title="random">
         random
        </a>
        . Drivers behind you will slow down to avoid you. Of course, if you or any of your friends gets into a
        <a class="populated" href="/title/really+nasty" title="really nasty">
         really nasty
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/car+accident" title="car accident">
         car accident
        </a>
        at this point, the car wreck alone will be enough to start a big traffic jam, but that was what you were trying to accomplish all along, right?
       </p>
       <p>
        Upon sucessfully initiating a traffic jam that would no doubt infuriate countless
        <a class="populated" href="/title/commuters" title="commuters">
         commuters
        </a>
        ,
        <a class="populated" href="/title/soccer+moms" title="soccer moms">
         soccer moms
        </a>
        , and
        <a class="populated" href="/title/delivery+boys" title="delivery boys">
         delivery boys
        </a>
        , it is important that you make it known that your traffic jam was a result of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/planned+and+deliberate+actions" title="planned and deliberate actions">
         planned and deliberate actions
        </a>
        as opposed to other traffic jams which are caused by mere
        <a class="populated" href="/title/random+stupidity" title="random stupidity">
         random stupidity
        </a>
        or
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Acts+of+God" title="Acts of God">
         Acts of God
        </a>
        . Call your local traffic
        <a class="populated" href="/title/news+radio" title="news radio">
         news radio
        </a>
        station and tell them something to the effect of  "
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Interstate" title="Interstate">
         Interstate
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/666" title="666">
         666
        </a>
        is backed up from Somewhereville to Someotherville because the
        <a class="populated" href="/title/hypocritical+swine" title="hypocritical swine">
         hypocritical swine
        </a>
        of this county have failed to do their part to
        <a class="populated" href="/title/Save+the+Whales" title="Save the Whales">
         Save the Whales
        </a>
        . We hope this traffic jam will offer then them opportunity to contemplate and reevaluate their stance on
        <a class="populated" href="/title/saving+our+planet" title="saving our planet">
         saving our planet
        </a>
        !" You could also declare your traffic jam a piece of
        <a class="populated" href="/title/abstract" title="abstract">
         abstract
        </a>
        <a class="populated" href="/title/performance+art" title="performance art">
         performance art
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <hr/>
       <a class="populated" href="/title/Existentialism" title="Existentialism">
        Existentialism
       </a>
       is
       <a class="populated" href="/title/that+girlfriend+who+cheated+on+you" title="that girlfriend who cheated on you">
        that girlfriend who cheated on you
       </a>
       .
       <a class="populated" href="/title/Post-modernism" title="Post-modernism">
        Post-modernism
       </a>
       is who she was with.
      </div>
