      <header class="entry-header">
       <h1 class="name name-page">
        7 Hands-on Ways to Teach Kids the Value of Money
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-meta">
        <a href="https://www.supermoney.com/author/pamela-britton-baer/">
         Pamela Britton-Baer
        </a>
        on
        <time datetime="2014-09-08T11:38:51+00:00" pubdate="">
         09/08/2014
        </time>
       </div>
       <div id="sm-share">
        <div class="vicon-2x vicon-twitter" data-text="7 Hands-on Ways to Teach Kids the Value of Money" data-title="Tweets" id="twitter">
        </div>
        <div class="vicon-2x vicon-facebook" data-title="Likes" id="facebook">
        </div>
        <div class="vicon-2x vicon-google-plus" data-title="Google+" id="googleplus">
        </div>
        <div class="vicon-2x vicon-linkedin" data-title="Shares" id="linkedin">
        </div>
       </div>
      </header>
      <!-- / entry-header -->
      <style>
       .responsive-banner { width: 320px; height: 50px; }

      </style>
      <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
      </script>
      <!-- Responsive_Banner -->
      <ins class="adsbygoogle responsive-banner" data-ad-client="ca-pub-6309077162818058" data-ad-slot="3708707322" style="display:inline-block">

      <script>
       (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
      </script>
      <div class="entry-content">
       <p>
        It slips through your fingers as quickly as sand. Money. Kids seem to believe theres a magic fairy that dispenses dollar bills to mommys and daddys everywhere. It doesnt matter how often you explain that it doesnt grown on trees, kids just dont seem to understand.
       </p>
       <p>
        Related article:
        <a href="http://www.supermoney.com/2014/08/coach-family-good-personal-finances/" target="_blank" title="How to coach your family to be good with personal finances">
         How to coach your family to be good with personal finances
        </a>
        .
        <a href="http://www.supermoney.com/2014/08/coach-family-good-personal-finances/" target="_blank" title="How to coach your family to be good with personal finances">
         <br/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        To help, weve come up with seven unique and interesting ways to not just teach kids the value of money, but to let them see firsthand how it flows.
       </p>
       <h2>
        1. Play Classic Money Games
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="Game Money" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-23954" height="317" src="http://www.supermoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Game-Money-478x317.jpg" width="478"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Theres no better way to make a point than teaching by example. Pull out that old Monopoly board and have them be the bank. Let them do the math when you hand them dozens of dollars for taxes, rent, and to purchase property.
       </p>
       <p>
        We also love
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Learning-Resources-Right-Money-Game/dp/B000062SRU/ref=sr_1_22?s=toys-and-games&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1408666948&amp;sr=1-22&amp;keywords=money" rel="nofollow">
         Buy It Right
        </a>
        , a game that helps kids learn the value of money, how to make change, even how to set prices. And a long-time favorite is from
        <em>
         Rich Dad, Poor Dad
        </em>
        author Robert Kiyosaki, the
        <a href="http://www.richdad.com/apps-games/cashflow-classic.aspx" rel="nofollow">
         Cashflow
        </a>
        game which teaches kids about how to invest, and let their money work for them. You can also play the game online for free.
       </p>
       <h2>
        2.Get Them a Real Live Bank Account
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="Kids Bank Account" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-23953" height="300" src="http://www.supermoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Kids-Bank-Account.jpg" width="448"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Dont be afraid to teach them the value of saving money young. A piggy bank is a great start, but open a real savings account with your bank for them.
       </p>
       <p>
        When you pay them an allowance, insist they put a portion of it into the account. Show them the statement from the bank every month, and how it grows just like yours does. Let them see with their own eyes how quickly the balance can grow, or shrink if its dipped into.
       </p>
       <h2>
        3.Have Them Raise a Farm Animal
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="FFA Animals" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-23952" height="323" src="http://www.supermoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/FFA-Animals-478x323.jpg" width="478"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Relax. Were not talking about raising a steer in your backyard.
        <a href="http://www.4-h.org/about/youth-development-research/" rel="nofollow">
         Groups like 4-H and FFA
        </a>
        help kids to raise everything from livestock to tomato plants. The truly awesome thing is that most of the time what theyre raising has to be sold. They are told to track their expenses and their time. Not only does raising a plant or an animal teach responsibility, theyll get to see how much time and money goes into raising their project.
       </p>
       <h2>
        4. Have Them Start an In-House Business
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="Kids Chores" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-23950" height="267" src="http://www.supermoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Kids-Chores-478x267.jpg" width="478"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Though a lemonade stand is a great option, get creative. Have your child (or children) become the business owner(s) of a cleaning service, or gardening service, or of a dog grooming business. You become the first customer. Have them work every night for a week. Pay them an actual wage. Use fake money, like the
        <a href="http://www.amazon.com/The-Learning-Journey-Kids-Money/dp/B001Y66JU4/ref=sr_1_5?s=toys-and-games&amp;ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1408666948&amp;sr=1-5&amp;keywords=money" rel="nofollow">
         play money set we found on Amazon
        </a>
        , but give them the current minimum wage. Then ask them to pay for their meals. Its all for fun, of course, but itll provide a powerful lesson.
       </p>
       <h2>
        5.Let Them Do the Grocery Shopping
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="M" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-23949" height="318" src="http://www.supermoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/Grocery-Shopping-Kids-478x318.jpg" width="478"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Relax your grip on your sacred grocery list and give it to your kid, along with cash. Maybe even tell them they can have the leftover money if they find ways to save. Dont be afraid. Help them add up the purchases and watch the light bulb go off over their head. Dont be surprised if they go from wanting everything in sight to picking the bargain basement discount items. Itll teach them to be frugal and smart with their money.
       </p>
       <h2>
        6. Show Them The Fake Money
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="monopoly-money" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-23575" height="317" src="http://www.supermoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/monopoly-money-478x317.jpg" width="478"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Using your fake money from above, withdraw your monthly salary. Show all that money to your child  then start paying the bills. Use actual figures. Let them see firsthand how quickly your monthly income dwindles away. Here are some more great ways to help you teach your children
        <a href="http://life.familyeducation.com/money-and-kids/personal-finance/34481.html" rel="nofollow">
         how to manage money from Family Education
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <h2>
        7.Let Them Run a Business on Their Own
       </h2>
       <p>
        <img alt="Kids and Money" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-22579" height="485" src="http://www.supermoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Kids-and-Money-e1406165036158-478x485.jpg" width="478"/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Of course, theres always the tried and true technique to teach your children about saving and spending money. They could sell crafts at fairs or mow lawns around the neighborhood. You might help them hold a monthly bake sale or car wash. Just be sure to deduct all their expenses, too. Heck, maybe even create a profit and loss statement, using
        <a href="http://www.scholastic.com/parents/resources/free-printable/math-printables/profit-and-loss-essential-business-math-skills" rel="nofollow">
         these worksheets from Scholastic
        </a>
        to explain the concept. Make sure they do all the calculations themselves. Its one thing to tell them how much money theyve earned, another thing to have them figure it out on their own.
       </p>
       <p>
        However you decide to do it, theres no underestimating the importance of teaching your child the value of money. Theyre never too young to learn, either. So go on. Get creative and teach your kids that money truly doesnt grow on trees.
       </p>
