             <div style="text-align: center">
              <img align="middle" alt="The Toronto Chocolate Festival is Back, Flickr: ayngelina" height="340" src="/image.axd?picture=2013%2f10%2fToronto+Chocolate.jpg" title="The Toronto Chocolate Festival is Back, Flickr: ayngelina" width="450"/>
             </div>
             <p align="center">
              <strong>
               Go Chocolate Crazy!
              </strong>
             </p>
             <br/>
             <p>
              Dark chocolate, milk chocolate, white chocolate. Theres
              <strong>
               <a href="/tag/chocolate/default.aspx">
                chocolate
               </a>
              </strong>
              with chili pepper, with fleur de sel, and with goji berries. Whatever flavour you like, you can be pretty sure youll be able to find it in chocolate form.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I used to be a tried and true milk chocolate fan, but over the years Ive grown rather fond of dark chocolate. Not jut any old dark chocolate though. After many years of taste testing, Ive determined that Lindt makes the creamiest, tastiest dark chocolate thats available in almost any drugstore. Im sure there are some more exclusive and handmade varieties that are as good if not better.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The
              <a href="http://www.torontochocolatefestival.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
               Toronto Chocolate Festival
              </a>
              is now in its eighth year and happens from Oct. 12-Nov. 3, so theres plenty of time to visit (and re-visit) and taste all sorts of delicacies that make the most of the raw material (CHOCOLATE!)
              <br/>
              <br/>
              First on the menu is the Chocolate Ball. Its a big dress up party to celebrate the festival and its main player, chocolate. Therell be dinner and dancing and yes, a whole lot of chocolate tasting.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Who doesnt love a high tea? Check out the Omni King Edward Hotel wholl be offering a chocolate inspired high tea during the festival dates. The traditional high tea has been popular since the early 1900s in this particular hotel, but the chocolate one has line ups out the door so best make a reservation. There will be chocolate infused teas, chocolate desserts and even chocolate inspired lunch items on the menu!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              The Chocolate Show is a one-day event on the very last day of the festival to come and meet the makers. There will be demos, a chocolate eating contest, wine and chocolate pairings, tastings (of course) and chocolate shopping opportunities from vendors from larger brands to home spun purveyors.
              <br/>
             </p>
