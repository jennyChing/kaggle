           <p>
            As a student of history, Ive found that women have always participated in and led revolutions, transcending societys assigned roles and yet they are always pushed to the bottom along with their issues when they are not needed anymore. I wholeheartedly agree with this video adding that women have always been the ones to force the hands of others, especially men to acknowledge their issues because without their voices their issues will never be given the time of day that they deserve. Women have always been told that to include their issues might threaten the focus of the movement, and to me, that is a trap that has been set to divide us. Issues like rape and sexual assault have been stereotyped to the point where rape myths and rape apologia dominate the discourse. Im glad that you are fighting back and changing the narrative because it is solely needed. Movements like Occupy need to broaden the focus to acknowledge that there are forms of oppression that also need to be addressed. Instead of worrying about whether we are too unfocused, the movement needs to become more inclusive and more receptive to the voices it unwittingly or not muzzles itself.
           </p>
           <p>
            As a man, I can never fully understand what it is like to be under the oppression of patriarchy but seeing this blog and the facebook page can at least help me to grasp a little of the voices that are coming out.
