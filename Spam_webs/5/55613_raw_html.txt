         <h1>
          Bestselling children's author Dav Pilkey talks Captain Underpants
         </h1>
         <div class="ct-meta-details">
          <dl>
           <dt>
            First published:
           </dt>
           <dd>
            Wednesday 11 June 2014 4:29PM
           </dd>
           <dt>
            By:
           </dt>
           <dd>
            <strong>
             Alex McClintock
            </strong>
           </dd>
          </dl>
         </div>
         <div class="section rich-text">
          <div class="inline-content photo full">
           <a href="/radionational/programs/lifematters/captain-underpants-in-action/5505456">
            <img alt="Captain Underpants in action" src="http://www.abc.net.au/radionational/image/5505456-3x2-700x467.jpg" title="Captain Underpants in action"/>
           </a>
           <a href="/radionational/programs/lifematters/captain-underpants-in-action/5505456">
            <strong>
             Image:
            </strong>
            <em>
             Captain Underpants and the Revolting Revenge of the Radioactive Robo-Boxers
            </em>
            is the 11th book in the series.
            <span class="source">
             (Supplied)
            </span>
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           <em>
            Dav Pilkeys 'Captain Underpants' books have sold more than 70 million copies and are more controversial than '50 Shades of Grey', but the author says that his hero is fighting for truth, justice and all that is pre-shrunk and cottony.
            <strong>
             Alex McClintock
            </strong>
            reports on a class clown made good.
           </em>
          </p>
          <iframe frameborder="no" height="166" scrolling="no" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=https%3A//api.soundcloud.com/tracks/153637695&amp;color=0a3a0e&amp;auto_play=false&amp;hide_related=false&amp;show_artwork=true&amp;show_comments=true&amp;show_user=true&amp;show_reposts=false" width="100%">
          </iframe>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            The Adventures of Captain Underpants
           </em>
           have been translated into 20 languages and have sold more than 70 million copies. Perhaps even more impressively, they have topped the list of the
           <a class="external" href="http://www.ala.org/bbooks/frequentlychallengedbooks/top10">
            most banned books
           </a>
           in America two years running.
          </p>
          <p>
           Not bad for a character created by an eight-year-old with dyslexia and ADHD.
          </p>
          <div class="inline-content quote right">
           <blockquote class="pullquote">
            <div class="quote">
             <p>
              It started with my grade two teacher, who was talking to the class one day and she happened to say the word 'underwear' and everyone just burst out laughing.
             </p>
            </div>
            <div class="cite">
             Dav Pilkey, author
            </div>
           </blockquote>
          </div>
          <p>
           It started with my grade two teacher, who was talking to the class one day and she happened to say the word 'underwear' and everyone just burst out laughing, says the series creator, Dav Pilkey, now in his forties.
          </p>
          <p>
           That was an amazing, transforming moment, because I realised that underwear was a very powerful word, it makes everybody laughso I started drawing Captain Underpants that day.
          </p>
          <p>
           Pilkey had been diagnosed with attention problems and learning difficulties at an early age, and didnt always enjoy school. He remembers spending a lot of time sitting in the hallway.
          </p>
          <p>
           I remember being very, very frustrated at school. I would come home crying a lot of times and I would say to my mum all the teachers hate me, and its terrible out there, and my mother would always say that sometimes things happen for a reason, and maybe something good will come out of this.
          </p>
          <p>
           I feel like something good has come out of it because it really helped to inspire the
           <em>
            Captain Underpants
           </em>
           series, and now I have the opportunity to talk to children all over the world and tell them about my problems, and many of those children have the same problems.
          </p>
          <p>
           Pilkey started writing professionally at the age of 19 after a college English teacher saw that his notebook was filled with cartoon characters. She held him back after class, but far from getting him in trouble, she encouraged him to pen a childrens book. He finished his first manuscript that year.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Listen:
            <a href="/radionational/programs/booksandarts/diary-of-a-wombat-author-jackie-french-new-children27s-laureate/5130542" target="_self" title="">
             Jackie French named Children's Laureate
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The teacher characters in the
           <em>
            Captain Underpants
           </em>
           series, however, are rarely nice. In fact, theyre downright nasty, with school principal Mr Krupp playing the role of villain (along with Professor Tinkletrousers and many others).
          </p>
          <p>
           Most of the teachers I had in elementary school, or primary school, and in high school were very vicious and cruel people, says Pilkey. However, there are some good grownups in the
           <em>
            Captain Underpants
           </em>
           series and thats the parental figures.
          </p>
          <p>
           I dont have too many teachers complaining about that kind of thing, its done in fun in the books and so usually teachers are very, very supportive and they love the books.
          </p>
          <div>
           <h3>
           </h3>
           <div class="article-index section ct-teaser-view">
            <ul class="article-index">
             <li>
              <div class="cs-teaser ct-teaser-feature">
               <div class="figure">
                <a href="/radionational/programs/lifematters/captain-underpants/5505424">
                 <img alt="Captain Underpants" src="http://www.abc.net.au/radionational/image/5512028-1x1-80x80.jpg" title="Captain Underpants to the rescue"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <div class="header">
                <h3 class="title">
                 <a href="/radionational/programs/lifematters/captain-underpants/5505424">
                  Captain Underpants to the rescue
                 </a>
                </h3>
                <ul class="cs-audio-links">
                 <li>
                  <a class="ico ico-download" href="http://mpegmedia.abc.net.au/rn/podcast/2014/06/lms_20140609_0920.mp3" title="Download this item as MP3(7.74MB)">
                   Download
                  </a>
                 </li>
                </ul>
                <p class="time">
                 Monday 9 June 2014
                </p>
               </div>
               <div class="summary">
                <p>
                 Listen to
                 <em>
                  Life Matters
                 </em>
                 to hear the full interview with
                 <em>
                  Captain Underpants
                 </em>
                 creator Dav Pilkey.
                </p>
                <p class="goto-more">
                 <a href="/radionational/programs/lifematters/captain-underpants/5505424">
                  More
                 </a>
                </p>
                <p class="cs-has-media cs-has-media-icons">
                 <span class="hide">
                  This [series episode segment] has
                 </span>
                 <span class="ico ico-image" href="#" title="This Story has an image">
                  image,
                 </span>
                </p>
               </div>
              </div>
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
          </div>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           He does field complaints, however, about the spelling mistakes in the sections of
           <em>
            Captain Underpants
           </em>
           that purport to be written by the books young protagonists, George Beard and Harold Hutchins. Pilkey has no plans to change, though, and neither do George and Harold.
          </p>
          <p>
           They will never grow up,' says Pilkey. 'I think theyll always be nine and 10 years old.'
          </p>
          <p>
           I hope I never grow up either. Im getting older and Im almost 50 now but I still feel and think like a kid.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="/radionational/programs/lifematters/" target="_self" title="">
            <strong>
             Life Matters
            </strong>
           </a>
           <strong>
            <em>
             charts and analyses contemporary Australian life, with a special focus on social policy, personal stories, and listener contributions.
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
