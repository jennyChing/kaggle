      <b>
       Colleen M. Fitzpatrick
      </b>
      (born April 25, 1955) is an
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      forensic genealogist.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Colleen_M._Fitzpatrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Fitzpatrick was born April 25, 1955
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">
       New Orleans, Louisiana
      </a>
      . She received her BA in physics (1976) from
      <a href="/wiki/Rice_University" title="Rice University">
       Rice University
      </a>
      , and her MA (1983) and PhD in nuclear physics (1983) from
      <a href="/wiki/Duke_University" title="Duke University">
       Duke University
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      She worked for 25 years on high resolution optical measurement techniques at
      <a href="/wiki/Rockwell_International" title="Rockwell International">
       Rockwell International
      </a>
      and Spectron Development Laboratories, after which she founded the high technology optics company, Rice Systems.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      She is a Fellow of the Society of Photoinstrumentation Engineers (
      <a href="/wiki/SPIE" title="SPIE">
       SPIE
      </a>
      ).
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      She currently is a forensic genealogy consultant with the firms of Yeiser &amp; Associates, Huntington Beach, California and with Identifinders, also of
      <a href="/wiki/Huntington_Beach,_California" title="Huntington Beach, California">
       Huntington Beach, California
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_cases">
       Recent cases
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Colleen_M._Fitzpatrick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent cases">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      She was a member of the AFDIL team that identified the remains found in the wreckage of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northwest_Flight_4422" title="Northwest Flight 4422">
       Northwest Flight 4422
      </a>
      that crashed in
      <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">
       Alaska
      </a>
      in 1948,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      worked on the
      <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Earhart" title="Amelia Earhart">
       Amelia Earhart
      </a>
      Project,
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      and was a member of the team that identified the remains of
      <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Leslie_Goodwin" title="Sidney Leslie Goodwin">
       the Unknown Child
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">
       RMS Titanic
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      She also worked with
      <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Sergeant" title="Sharon Sergeant">
       Sharon Sergeant
      </a>
      on the exposure of two false Holocaust memoirs, the
      <a href="/wiki/Misha_Defonseca" title="Misha Defonseca">
       Misha Defonseca
      </a>
      hoax, and the
      <a href="/wiki/Herman_Rosenblat" title="Herman Rosenblat">
       Herman Rosenblat
      </a>
      ,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Angel_at_the_Fence" title="Angel at the Fence">
        Angel at the Fence
       </a>
      </i>
      fraud. rence for identifying remains found on
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gardner_Island" title="Gardner Island">
       Gardner Island
      </a>
      in the Pacific Ocean.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
