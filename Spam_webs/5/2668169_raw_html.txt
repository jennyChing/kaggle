        <h1 class="post-title">
         <a href="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/2013/02/16/elephants-toothpaste-fun-with-catalysts/" title="Elephants Toothpaste  Fun With Catalysts">
          Elephants Toothpaste  Fun With Catalysts
         </a>
        </h1>
        <p>
         <img alt="elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide homeschool science fun.jpg" border="0" height="600" src="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/elephant-toothpaste-hydrogen-peroxide-homeschool-science-fun.jpg" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide homeschool science fun.jpg" width="454"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Elephants toothpaste is a wonderful illustration of the power of a catalyst to speed up a reaction. Its also
         <em>
          very
         </em>
         cool, and anything that shows kids how cool science is worth doing, right?
        </p>
        <h3>
         What You Need
        </h3>
        <ul>
         <li>
          6% Hydrogen peroxide (1/2 cup)
         </li>
         <li>
          Yeast (1 tsp)
         </li>
         <li>
          Hot water (2 tbsp approx) in a small dish
         </li>
         <li>
          Food colouring
         </li>
         <li>
          Washing-up liquid (dish soap)
         </li>
         <li>
          Empty soda/water bottle (small)
         </li>
         <li>
          Tray to stand the bottle on to catch the foam
         </li>
         <li>
          Funnel (optional)
         </li>
        </ul>
        <h3>
         <img alt="elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide and yeast homeschool science" border="0" height="450" src="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/elephant-toothpaste-hydrogen-peroxide-and-yeast-homeschool-science.jpg" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide and yeast homeschool science.JPG" width="600"/>
        </h3>
        <h3>
         What You Do
        </h3>
        <ol>
         <li>
          Pour the hydrogen peroxide into the bottle
         </li>
         <li>
          Mix the yeast into the water
         </li>
         <li>
          Add the washing up liquid and food colouring to the hydrogen peroxide in the bottle
         </li>
         <li>
          Add the yeast mixture to the bottle
         </li>
         <li>
          Stand back and admire the reaction!
         </li>
        </ol>
        <h3>
         <a href="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/elephant-toothpaste-hydrogen-peroxide-and-yeast-homeschool-science1.jpg">
          <img alt="elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide yeast science fun" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2460" height="600" src="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/elephant-toothpaste-hydrogen-peroxide-and-yeast-homeschool-science1.jpg" width="482"/>
         </a>
        </h3>
        <h3>
         What Happened
        </h3>
        <p>
         The photos say it all! We had enough
         <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Care-Hydrogen-Peroxide-Solution-200ml/dp/B003VV70J4/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361005308&amp;sr=8-1" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.co.uk/Care-Hydrogen-Peroxide-Solution-200ml/dp/B003VV70J4/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361005308&amp;sr=8-1', 'hydrogen peroxide']);">
          hydrogen peroxide
         </a>
         in our 2.74 bottle to have two goes. Afterwards I gave the children a bowl each to mix up the red and green foam.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide yeast" border="0" height="600" src="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/elephant-toothpaste-hydrogen-peroxide-yeast1.jpg" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide yeast.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         The Scientific Explanation
        </h3>
        <p>
         Hydrogen peroxide naturally breaks down over time into waterand oxygen. Catalase, the enzyme in yeast, acts as a catalyst to speed up the reaction.
        </p>
        <p>
         The washing-up liquid joins with the oxygen to make an abundance of foam. And  as with so many of our homeschool science projects  the food colouring just makes the whole thing look even cooler!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide yeast 2" border="0" height="198" src="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/elephant-toothpaste-hydrogen-peroxide-yeast-2.jpg" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" title="elephant toothpaste hydrogen peroxide yeast 2.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <h3>
         What We Might Do Differently Next Time
        </h3>
        <p>
         I get so excited doing experiments like this that there are always a few things I forget to mention to the kids. (But thats ok because then we have an excuse to do it again. :-))
        </p>
        <p>
         I would point out next time that during the reaction, the bottle gets warm and steam is produced from the water, because this is an
         <em>
          exothermic
         </em>
         (heat-producing) reaction.
        </p>
        <p>
         I would also mention that although a catalyst speeds up a chemical reaction, it is left unchanged itself: the dissolved yeast is left in the bottle.
        </p>
        <h3>
         The Book and the Hat-Tip
        </h3>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Science-Experiments-Robert-Winston/dp/1405362863/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361005812&amp;sr=8-1" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.co.uk/Science-Experiments-Robert-Winston/dp/1405362863/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361005812&amp;sr=8-1', '']);">
          <img alt="Elephant Toothpaste hydrogen peroxide yeast science" class="alignright wp-image-2468 size-thumbnail" height="150" src="http://www.navigatingbyjoy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/02/Science-Experiments-Robert-Winston-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
         </a>
         Elephant toothpaste is well set-out in our favourite
         <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Science-Experiments-Robert-Winston/dp/1405362863/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361005812&amp;sr=8-1" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.co.uk/Science-Experiments-Robert-Winston/dp/1405362863/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361005812&amp;sr=8-1', 'Science Experiments']);">
          Science Experiments
         </a>
         book, but I followed theinstructions I found at another mums blog (unfortunately her blog is no longer live). Where theres a choice, I prefer to follow a mums personal experience when it comes to learning fun with kids!
        </p>
        <p>
         Do you notice our
         <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Lux-Optical-Safety-Glasses--VISILUX/dp/B0041S0TUI/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361006926&amp;sr=8-2" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.co.uk/Lux-Optical-Safety-Glasses--VISILUX/dp/B0041S0TUI/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1361006926&amp;sr=8-2', 'safety glasses']);">
          safety glasses
         </a>
         ? Not strictly necessary here, but they definitely remind us were Real Scientists!
        </p>
