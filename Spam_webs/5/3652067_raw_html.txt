        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Windows to the Soul
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="sep">
          Posted on
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.turtlecontacts.com.au/blog/windows-to-the-soul/" rel="bookmark" title="2:36 am">
          <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-12-09T02:36:54+00:00" pubdate="">
           December 9, 2014
          </time>
         </a>
         <span class="by-author">
          <span class="sep">
           by
          </span>
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.turtlecontacts.com.au/blog/author/admin/" rel="author" title="View all posts by admin">
            admin
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Eyes. They are the first thing most people notice about another. Beautiful eyes, whether natural or enhanced with colour contact lenses, are captivating.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to an old English proverb, which actually had its origins in the New Testament of the Bible, the eyes are the windows to the soul. This quote has existed in one form or another since at least as far back as Cicero, who lived well before the time of Christ. The philosophy has since been frequently applied to poetry and prose. But what does it mean?
         <span id="more-44">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         The concept basically states that, by looking deeply into anothers eyes, we can see who they truly are  with no artifice. One can fake a smile, but one cannot fake what the eyes truly say.
        </p>
        <p>
         We all know that by looking at someones eyes, we can often tell how they are feeling. Without needing to smile or grimace, eyes give away whether someone is happy, sad, appreciative, fearful, angry, or any of a myriad of other emotions. If a person is warm and accepting, the eyes say it all. If someone has dead eyes, most of us know intuitively to steer well clear. The eyes can give the first sign of ones intentions, and whether they are friend or foe. Even when wearing
         <a href="http://www.turtlecontacts.com.au/all-coloured-contact-lenses.html" title="Coloured Contact lenses">
          colour contact lenses
         </a>
         , the inner light shines out from the eyes.
        </p>
        <p>
         There are even scientific studies that prove that they eyes really are the windows to the soul and not simply in the sense of our inner self shining out from them.
        </p>
        <p>
         Swedish scientists from Orebro University compared the irises of over four hundred volunteers, to assess the structures in the iris as compared with their personality traits. Those subjects who were more warm hearted, trusting, and empathetic had significantly different iris structures than those who were neurotic and impulsive. The scientists found that the genes responsible for the development of the iris were also responsible for the development of the frontal lobe of the brain  which strongly influences personality traits.
        </p>
        <p>
         In another study, Yale University research psychologists determined that most people feel intuitively that their self resides in the region of the eyes. Three separate experiments saw researchers question both adults and preschoolers about where in the body they felt their self or soul to most reside. The majority of both preschoolers and adults identified the eyes as the seat of the soul  even when shown cartoon pictures of alien creatures, whose eyes were on the chest, the eyes were determined to be the seat of the soul. (Interestingly, not in the brain).
        </p>
        <p>
         Additionally, the part of the brain which is behind the eyes is thought to be that part from which self-awareness arises.
        </p>
        <p>
         Whether looking at eyes scientifically or metaphysically, the eyes are by far the most expressive feature we have  whether human or animal, the eyes say it all.
        </p>
        <p>
         Looking deeply into the eyes of another, one engaged on a whole other level. Rather than listening to what their voice says, look at what the eyes say. Eyes dont lie.
        </p>
