       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Eggless Milkless Chocolate Cake with Caramel Frosting
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">

         October 3, 2014
        </span>
        <span class="post-comments">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <em>
          The perfect rich, dark chocolate cake for any day or any celebration. This cake contains no milk or eggs yet is absolutely decadent, especially when topped with a delicious caramel frosting. This Eggless Milkless Chocolate Cake with Caramel Frosting is one not to be missed!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-5993">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Eggless Milkless Chocolate Cake with Caramel Frosting | Melanie Makes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5997" height="913" src="http://melaniemakes.com/images/2014/10/eggless-milkless-chocolate-cake-mm.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive had this delicious cake waiting in the wings to share with you since August. Nearly two months ago! If youre like me, chances are you have one or two community cookbooks in your collection. You know the kind. Spiral bound and full of recipes from friends, family and acquaintances. Off the top of my head, I know I have one from my childhood church, Andys childhood church and one from my moms workplace. Theres always a gem or two to be found and this happens to be one of them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Eggless Milkless Chocolate Cake with Caramel Frosting | Melanie Makes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5996" height="900" src="http://melaniemakes.com/images/2014/10/eggless-milkless-chocolate-cake-5.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         At our house, this cake has been deemed anniversary cake because I make it each year for our wedding anniversary. Andy and I got married on what wouldve been my dads birthday and this was the cake he always requested my mom make for him. So each year, this cake is made and I add candles to adorn the top in the number of years weve been married.
        </p>
        <p>
         As its name implies, this cake contains no eggs or milk. The cake itself is a dark chocolate thats absolutely decadent. You could go the simple route and simply sprinkle some powdered some sugar on top, but obviously I am my fathers daughter as he chose an out of this world caramel frosting instead. The richer the cake the better, right? Pour me a glass of milk!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Eggless Milkless Chocolate Cake with Caramel Frosting | Melanie Makes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-5998" height="900" src="http://melaniemakes.com/images/2014/10/eggless-milkless-chocolate-cake-3.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>

           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons baking soda
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup vegetable oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons vanilla
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 teaspoons vinegar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 cups water
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Caramel Frosting
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup milk
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup butter
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup brown sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2-1/2 cups powdered sugar
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             teaspoon vanilla
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Eggless Milkless Chocolate Cake with Caramel Frosting
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a large bowl, sift together flour, sugar, cocoa, salt and baking soda. Set aside.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a blender, add oil, vanilla, vinegar and water. Blend until well combined.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Stir together wet and dry ingredients.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Pour batter into ungreased 9x13 pan and bake 35-45 minutes or until toothpick inserted in the middle comes out clean.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Let cool completely before frosting.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSSectionHead">
           Caramel Frosting
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In a small saucepan over medium heat, add milk, butter and brown sugar. Stir together and bring to a boil.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Remove from heat and stir in powdered sugar and vanilla.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Frost cake immediately.
           </li>
          </ol>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
          <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
           Notes
          </div>
          <div class="ERSNotes">
           Although this cake is traditionally made in a 9x13 pan, I also like to make it as a bundt cake. To do so, simply increase your baking time to approximately 50 minutes. To make the frosting so that it is more of a glaze consistency, simply stir in enough milk to allow it to run down the side of the cake.
          </div>
