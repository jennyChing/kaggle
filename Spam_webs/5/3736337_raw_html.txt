                 <h2 class="date-header">
                  <span>
                   11/6/14
                  </span>
                 </h2>
                 <div class="date-posts">
                  <div class="post-outer">
                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qgIfv3IDFiY/VFtyJ1FQ9PI/AAAAAAAADVk/8MWkvyxzkFM/s1600/Termessos_tomb_with-a-view.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>
                    <meta content="7454013720252386618" itemprop="blogId"/>
                    <meta content="5256107536628691435" itemprop="postId"/>
                    <a name="5256107536628691435">
                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Bodies (5)
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5256107536628691435" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qgIfv3IDFiY/VFtyJ1FQ9PI/AAAAAAAADVk/8MWkvyxzkFM/s1600/Termessos_tomb_with-a-view.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="228" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-qgIfv3IDFiY/VFtyJ1FQ9PI/AAAAAAAADVk/8MWkvyxzkFM/s1600/Termessos_tomb_with-a-view.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Oh, thats beautiful! Who sings this? I ask, leaning forward to increase the volume of the music.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      The narrow, dusty road is winding through the hinterland with its scrub, its holm oaks, strawberry trees, japes, junipers, buckthorns and pine trees. To our right, craggy peaks and rugged rocks cut into the light, transparent sky. To the left, the sea shimmers in silvery reflections, but far below us. I let my arm hang out through the open car window, trying to catch some of the cool mountain air with my fingers.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Murat has kept his promise, providing not only a car, but also someone to drive me around. I dont know if its a random decision or if Murat has noticed his bodyguards timid smile, yesterday. A smile I might only have imagined, to be honest.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      But whatever. My personal tourist guide today is the young, lean, melancholic Hazim.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Half an hour ago, he knocked on my door as I was finishing my solitary breakfast on the balcony. I was rather surprised to see him when I opened. When I offered coffee and orange juice, he declined with a mute sign of his head. When I went into the bathroom to brush my teeth, he didnt even want to sit down.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      And he hasnt said much ever since. His face an unreadable mask of polite neutrality, he led me through the hotel park in silence, then across the parking lot. He opened the back door of a black Audi, but I ignored him, walked around the car and sat down on the passenger seat.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Where you want to go? was the only thing he muttered without looking at me when he started the engine.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Choose a place. I dont care, I answered.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      And that was it. End of our thrilling conversation.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      He switched on the car audio system and inserted a CD as soon as we left the hotel premises, though.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      And now theres this woman singing. A strange melody that all those florid Turkish vowels and consonants render almost surreal. Shes got a rich and throaty voice, the voice of an old woman whos seen it all. Yet Im pretty sure shes only in her twenties or thirties. Turkish female singers all seem to have a rare quality: to put a whole lifetime of experiences, pleasant and unpleasant, into their voices. Even those whove barely entered adulthood.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Hazim! The singerwhats her name? I repeat, noticing that my voice is harsher than planned. But Im thrown off by his behaviour. It doesnt feel hostile, but wary, as if he didnt want an invisible barrier to disappear between us.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Despite my irritated tone, he keeps staring at the road, blocking me out. I start to wonderis he deaf? Or doesnt he speak English?
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Finally, when Ive given up hope to get an answer, Hazim clears his voice and says, Burcu Gne. Her name is Burcu Gne.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Oh, you do have a tongue, then, I comment drily, drumming my fingers on the door.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      We listen to the song for a while. When I can stand it no longer, I say, I dont understand whats going on. Are you, I dont know,
                      <i>
                       mad
                      </i>
                      at me for something? I wouldve thought that having a day off was nicer than walking through Antalya at your bosss side. Even if it means you are forced to spend the day with me.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Another silence.  Then he replies, Its not that bad. He shoots me an odd glance.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Then talk, for Gods sake!
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      I have nothing to say.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      That may be so, but you barely answer my questions! I cant stop you from sulking, but to be honest, if I had known that I would spend the day in broody silence, I wouldve stayed all alone. I do have a driving licence, after all. I dont need a chauffeur.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Im sorry, my English is not good.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Cut it out, your English is good enough! I dont ask you to explain the complexity of the universe, I merely ask for some superficial small talk.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      He mulls this over. Then he surprises me by saying, You notice we both wear black clothes? Which, indeed, has nothing to do with the complexity of the universe.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Hey, I guess youre right! What a coincidence!, I coo in a mocking tone.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      You always wear black clothes. I have noticed, in Istanbul, last time I saw you. I only wear black clothes, too.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Great. We have something in common then.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      You look good in black, sir.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      You do, too. And dont sir me, please. My names Marc.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      The song is over, another one starts.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      You want me to play the song again, um Marc? Hazim asks.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Oh. Yes, please. If you dont mind.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      The woman, Burcu Gne, starts again. 
                      <i>
                       Blblm gel de dile
                      </i>
                      , she sings.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Whats she singing about? I want to know.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Difficult. I dont know if I can translate.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Give it a try, come on.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      She sings 
                      <i>
                       My song-bird, start talking even sing with me make your voice heard to strangers Sorrow, oh my sorrow bird
                      </i>
                       Its a traditional Turkish song called 
                      <i>
                       ile blblm
                      </i>
                      .
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Beautiful, I say, trying out the sound of the song titel. 
                      <i>
                       ile blblm
                      </i>
                      . My sorrow bird. Really beautiful. I look at Hazim. Thank you.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      What for?
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      For the translation. And for, you know, making an effort.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      He gazes back and seems about to answer something. Yet he doesnt. Just closes down his invisible shutters and drives on.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      I wonder what Ive said that makes him brood again.
                     </div>
