        <p>
         Five-year-old Rilee was born with Achondroplasia, a form of short-limbed dwarfism. However, that's hardly going to stop her from dancing around.
        </p>
        <p>
         Watch as the adorable little girl dances to Taylor Swift's "Shake It Off." You won't be able to stop smiling. Seriously, my face actually hurts right now from all the grinning.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="394" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/5RMoLhfal5Y?rel=0" width="700">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         (Source:
         <a href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC-jnKWixh4sXZQ6TA0PTx_Q" target="_blank">
          Angela Thurber
         </a>
         )
        </p>
        <p>
         Rilee's mom Angela originally uploaded this video in hopes of helping people understand dwarfism. She also wanted to show how her spunky five-year-old is no different from any other little girl. After the video went viral, Angela created a Facebook page called "
         <a href="https://www.facebook.com/smilesfromRilee?fref=nf" target="_blank">
          Smiles from Rilee
         </a>
         " that posts more videos of her daughter, as well as educational articles on dwarfism. Way to go, Rilee and Angela!
