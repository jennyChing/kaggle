           <h2 class="graf--h2 graf--first" id="title" name="title">
            This Is How the World Could Have Ended
           </h2>
           <h4 class="graf--h4" id="subtitle" name="subtitle">
            World War III would have kicked off with hundreds of nukes
           </h4>
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="section-captionContent u-ignoreBlock">
         <div class="section-inner sectionLayout--caption">
          <label class="graf--sectionCaption graf--last" data-default-value="Type caption for image (optional)" id="coverCaption" name="coverCaption">
           Polish plan to invade The Netherlands during World War III. Via Frans De Jonge
          </label>

        <div class="section-content">
         <div class="section-inner layoutSingleColumn">
          <p class="graf--p graf--first" id="b1fd" name="b1fd">
           This is a plan for the end of the world, dated 1970.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="98ce" name="98ce">
           The arrows are armies and the red vertical symbols are nuclear bombs, all part of a part of Cold War contingency plan crafted by the Soviet Union and its Warsaw Pact allies in case of war.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="48d1" name="48d1">
           War that would have destroyed civilization.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="fb7d" name="fb7d">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 625px; max-height: 393px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 62.9%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-height="393" data-image-id="1*FZNgxewT6Nj49TXu96NUOg.jpeg" data-width="625" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*FZNgxewT6Nj49TXu96NUOg.jpeg"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption class="imageCaption">
            Map depicting a planned Warsaw Pact offensive. Via Third Republic blog
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
          <h4 class="graf--h4" id="f8ce" name="f8ce">
           War plan
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="db45" name="db45">
           The map and other documents,
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://translate.google.com/translate?sl=pl&amp;tl=en&amp;js=n&amp;prev=_t&amp;hl=en&amp;ie=UTF-8&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fdziennik3rp.blogspot.com%2F2012%2F02%2Fpolska-szykowaa-zachodowi-atomowa.html" href="http://translate.google.com/translate?sl=pl&amp;tl=en&amp;js=n&amp;prev=_t&amp;hl=en&amp;ie=UTF-8&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fdziennik3rp.blogspot.com%2F2012%2F02%2Fpolska-szykowaa-zachodowi-atomowa.html" rel="nofollow">
            discovered in Poland
           </a>
           , show how the Warsaw Pact aimed to put tanks on the shores of the Atlantic within 14 days of the first shot being fired.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="3618" name="3618">
           Among Warsaw Pact armies, Polands was second in size only to the Soviet Unions. It had a peacetime strength of 361,000 troops and could expand to 865,000 upon mobilization. It had 15 combat divisions, versus the U.S. Armys 10 divisions today. The Poles had 2,880 tanks, 2,750 armored personnel carriers and more than 2,000 artillery pieces.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="e072" name="e072">
           In the event of war, the Polish and Soviet armies would have marched west, invading West Germany, Denmark, The Netherlands and Belgium. The attack was meant to overrun NATOs northern ports on the Atlantic, preventing the arrival of reinforcements from the U.S. and Canada. Polish marines and airborne troops would invade Denmark on day five, knocking the tiny NATO country out of the war.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="08aa" name="08aa">
           All of this was to be accompanied by the use of
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            hundreds
           </em>
           of nuclear weapons.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="4bdc" name="4bdc">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 625px; max-height: 455px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 72.8%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-height="455" data-image-id="1*KO7s8qDSg29cMar8TpeJLg.jpeg" data-width="625" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*KO7s8qDSg29cMar8TpeJLg.jpeg"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption class="imageCaption">
            Warsaw Pact plan for overrunning Denmark, including nuclear strikes. Via Third Republic blog
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
          <h4 class="graf--h4" id="0ef6" name="0ef6">
           The atomic battlefield
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="8f73" name="8f73">
           All the Warsaw Pact war plans released, or leaked, to the public after the Cold War feature the liberal use of nuclear weapons. According to documents unearthed in the Czech Republic, Soviet generals fully expected Western imperialists to make the first nuclear strike. The Soviets and their allies determined they should stage preemptive atomic attacks if war were imminent.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="e745" name="e745">
           The Polish maps make it clear just how many nukes the Soviets would have dropped. Large-yield nuclear weapons would have wipe out economic and political targets. The West German cities of Hamburg and Hanover and the ports of Wilhemshaven and Bremerhaven all would have been nuked.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="525e" name="525e">
           In The Netherlands, The Hague, Rotterdam, Utrecht and Amsterdam were on the nuke list. Belgium would have lost the port city of Antwerp and Brussels, the site of the main NATO headquarters.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="be66" name="be66">
           Even tiny Denmark, with a population of just under five million at the time, would have been hit with no fewer than five nuclear weapons, including two dropped on the capital city of Copenhagen.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="9691" name="9691">
           The Warsaw Pact would have used many more smaller tactical nukes against NATO command posts, army bases, airfields, equipment depots and missile and communications sites.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="71df" name="71df">
           Radiation would have contaminated farmland and water supplies. Refugees fleeing the fighting would have been particularly hard hit. Radioactive fallout would have affected a far larger area than the bomb blasts themselves.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="a75e" name="a75e">
           In all, Warsaw Pact plans called for 189 nuclear weapons: 177 missiles and 12 bombs ranging in yield from five kilotonsroughly a quarter the size of the bomb dropped on Hiroshimato 500 kilotons.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="41ec" name="41ec">
           And that was just for the Northern Front. There were two other fronts, Central and Southern, covering the rest of Germany down to the Adriatic. Atomic bombs factored into Soviet plans for those areas, too. According to the Hungarian Cold War archive,
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/austria/1364037/Vienna-was-top-of-Soviet-nuclear-targets-list.html" href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/austria/1364037/Vienna-was-top-of-Soviet-nuclear-targets-list.html" rel="nofollow">
            Vienna was to be destroyed with two 500-kiloton nuclear bombs
           </a>
           , Munich one.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="fa66" name="fa66">
           Escalation to all-out, global nuclear warfare would have been practically inevitable.
          </p>
          <figure class="graf--figure" id="7c9b" name="7c9b">
           <div class="aspectRatioPlaceholder is-locked" style="max-width: 700px; max-height: 469px;">
            <div class="aspect-ratio-fill" style="padding-bottom: 67.10000000000001%;">
            </div>
            <img class="graf-image" data-action="zoom" data-action-value="1*Qkwcke3QL0MDSMz_jIv0KA.jpeg" data-height="1140" data-image-id="1*Qkwcke3QL0MDSMz_jIv0KA.jpeg" data-width="1700" src="https://d262ilb51hltx0.cloudfront.net/max/800/1*Qkwcke3QL0MDSMz_jIv0KA.jpeg"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption class="imageCaption">
            Warsaw Pact T-72 tanks on maneuvers. File photo
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
          <h4 class="graf--h4" id="8aef" name="8aef">
           Apocalypse now?
          </h4>
          <p class="graf--p" id="fe20" name="fe20">
           But would any of this actually have happened? Just because the Warsaw Pact planned for atomic war didnt mean the war was any more likely than if it
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            hadnt
           </em>
           planned at all, right? Surely this was a case of thinking about the unthinkable.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="611c" name="611c">
           Some historians insist that the Soviet Union had a
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/1563692/Soviet-plan-for-WW3-nuclear-attack-unearthed.html" href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/1563692/Soviet-plan-for-WW3-nuclear-attack-unearthed.html" rel="nofollow">
            fairy-tale mindset
           </a>
           when it came to a hypothetical war in Europe and the use of nuclear weapons. Its also worth noting that the United States has done all the same insane planning. Americas
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://nsarchive.wordpress.com/2011/11/08/u-s-war-plans-would-kill-an-estimated-108-million-soviets-104-million-chinese-and-2-3-million-poles-more-evidence-on-siop-62-and-the-origins-of-overkill/" href="http://nsarchive.wordpress.com/2011/11/08/u-s-war-plans-would-kill-an-estimated-108-million-soviets-104-million-chinese-and-2-3-million-poles-more-evidence-on-siop-62-and-the-origins-of-overkill/" rel="nofollow">
            Single Integrated Operational Plan
           </a>
           had thousands of nukes annihilating the Soviet Union, Eastern Europe and China.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="16a7" name="16a7">
           But its possible to believe that the Soviet leadership
           <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">
            did
           </em>
           think realistically about atomic weapons and had no illusions about their dangers. Consider that the Soviets never exported nuclear weapons, except to China, and
           <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="http://www.businessinsider.com/soviets-almost-left-100-nukes-on-cuba-2012-10" href="http://www.businessinsider.com/soviets-almost-left-100-nukes-on-cuba-2012-10" rel="nofollow">
            kept nukes out of the hands of Fidel Castro
           </a>
           when they decided he was unstable.
          </p>
          <p class="graf--p" id="b400" name="b400">
           Its possible that in formulating these plans, the Soviets caught a brief glance at the realities of atomic combatand that may have made them even more determined to avoid all-out war.
          </p>
