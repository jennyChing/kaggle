         <h2>
          1) Peanut Worm
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/182/cache/marine-worms01-peanut-worm_18258_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Peanut worms are mainly found at the lowest levels of water. They burrow in the mud or sand between tide levels or in oozes of the deepest ocean trenches.
         </p>
         <h2>
          2) Spaghetti Worm
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via megamarinesurvey" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hiD5hMoWr6M/UKtUYYB966I/AAAAAAAA6uc/Vg6QYPNaxyc/s1600/reneong3.jpg" title="Via megamarinesurvey"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via megamarinesurvey
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The tentacles of this fish are the most extraordinary things about it. The Spaghetti Worm typically lies hidden in sediment or rocky crevices while its tentacles are used to grab food. The tentacles are six times its body length and new ones grow if one is lost.
         </p>
         <h2>
          3) Flower Hat Jelly
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via Scienceblogs.com" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://scienceblogs.com/shiftingbaselines/wp-content/blogs.dir/425/files/2012/04/i-6bfe33fc568f84b05968385f99d9a0ce-flower.jpg" title="Via Scienceblogs.com"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via Scienceblogs.com
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          This is a rare creature and although its name sounds innocent, the flower hat jellys lilac-tipped fringe can deliver one painful sting.
         </p>
         <h2>
          4) Ancient Lobe Coral
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/178/cache/corals01-lobe-coral_17847_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          This coral is 500 years old and can be foundon Kingman Reef in the South Pacifics remote Line Islands. It is part of the lobe coral species  usually found in tropical parts of the Indian and Pacific oceans.
         </p>
         <h2>
          5) Sea Pens
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via Seaphotos.com" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://www.seaphotos.com/bc/Sea_Pens_08BC5w.jpg" title="Via Seaphotos.com"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via Seaphotos.com
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          With a name that was given to it because of its resemblance quill pens, Sea Pens protrude from the surface of the mud and can grow more than 2m long. They can also deflate or expand by taking in or releasing water.
         </p>
         <h2>
          6) White Anemone and Sea Star
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/174/cache/colorful01-sea-anemone_17425_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          In the waters off British Columbia you can find this white sea anemone (which is one of a more than a thousand sea anemone species) that keeps company with a sea star.
         </p>
         <h2>
          7)Multicolored Nudibranch
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/181/cache/nudibranchs09-nudibranch_18179_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          This is one of the many types ofNudibranchs which are a group of soft-bodied marine mollusks thatshed their skin after the larval stage. They are very well known for their bright and extraordinary colours.
         </p>
         <h2>
          8) Giant Spider Crab
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/184/cache/giant-spider-crab-doubilet_18480_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Usually found in Japanese waters, this species of crabhas the largest leg span of any anthropodreaching up to 3.8 metres (12ft). Apparently, despite its unnerving appearance, the crab is known to have a gentle disposition.
         </p>
         <h2>
          9) Flamingo Tongue Snail
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via Tumblr" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://24.media.tumblr.com/tumblr_m3z2vmURdk1r5n27ro1_500.jpg" title="Via Tumblr"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via Tumblr
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          This variety of the sea snail is usually found among Caribbean and Atlantic coral reefs. The actual shell is white and it is the living body that produces these bright colours.
         </p>
         <h2>
          10) Hydromedusa
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via Jellieszone.com" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://jellieszone.com/Output/Hydromedusae/Popup190.jpg" title="Via Jellieszone.com"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via Jellieszone.com
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The abundance of this creatures tentacles is what led its name to include that of the Greek mythological figure Medusa. This jellyfish reproduce asexually and can have up to 400 tentacles.
         </p>
         <h2>
          11)Napoleon Wrasse
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/003/cache/napoleon-wrasse-palau_304_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Also known as theHumphead Wrasse, this creatureis one of the largest coral reef fish and is usually found in the Indo-Pacific region.
         </p>
         <h2>
          12) Textile Cone Snail
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via storiented.blogspot.com" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/_fnXu10W5PIY/TN8RnAGbsSI/AAAAAAAAEMc/Vwye1kl7Eog/s1600/conesnail.jpg" title="Via storiented.blogspot.com"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via storiented.blogspot.com
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Even though it looks pretty peaceful in this photo, the cone snail is one of the most venomous creatures in the world. Their hollow teeth are quickly jabbed into their prey to release their deadly venom.
         </p>
         <h2>
          13) Moray Eel
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via Flickr" class="aligncenter content-image" src="https://c1.staticflickr.com/7/6110/6412307093_fa2caba5a5_z.jpg" title="Via Flickr"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via Flickr
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Warm temperatures, deep and shallow waters are just the places for this species of eel. Although from this photo they look quite daunting, they actually spend a lot of time hiding amongst the rocks of the ocean so youll rarely see them reach the shore.
         </p>
         <h2>
          14) Wolffish Pair
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/181/cache/deep-sea03-wolffish-pair_18163_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Mostly settling in rocky coastal areasup to 1,600 feet deep, wolffish are often found in pairs and they look pretty sinister together.
         </p>
         <h2>
          15) Puffer Fish
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via true-wildlife.blogspot.com" class="aligncenter content-image" src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/-5SOLNmu63Rw/TX2rEdZm0vI/AAAAAAAABrM/7ZXxQnmJZVs/s1600/BUNTEL+DUREN.jpg" title="Via true-wildlife.blogspot.com"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via true-wildlife.blogspot.com
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          You probably recognise this fish from your favourite Pixar film about marine life  Finding Nemo. The puffer fish is most commonly known for expanding its body as a way to defend itself from potentially dangerous situations.
         </p>
         <h2>
          16) Stargazer Fish
         </h2>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_" style="width: 680px">
          <img alt="Via National Geographic" class="aligncenter content-image" src="http://images.nationalgeographic.com/wpf/media-live/photos/000/135/cache/toxic11-stargazer-fish_13501_600x450.jpg" title="Via National Geographic"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Via National Geographic
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          You would most likely stumble upon this monstrous looking creature while frolicking through the sand as thats where they bury themselves to ambush their prey. If you do happen to come across one then look out for their two large poison spines that contain venom.
         </p>
