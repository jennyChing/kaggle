          <h2>
           <span style="color: #b76e00;">
            About Marilynne Robinson
           </span>
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             Marilynnes first novel,
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Housekeeping-A-Novel-Marilynne-Robinson/dp/0312424094?SubscriptionId=AKIAJ5Q5A4WOCGIYUGIA&amp;tag=thethoughtfac-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
              <em>
               Housekeeping
              </em>
             </a>
            </span>
            <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=thethoughtfac-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=0312424094" style="font-size: 16px;" width="1"/>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             , was released in 1980. Nominated for the Pulitzer Prize and winning the PEN/Hemingway Award for Best First Novel, its tale of Ruth andLucille in the fictional town of Fingerbone, Idaho, has been hailed a classic.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             The second novel,
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Gilead-A-Novel-Marilynne-Robinson/dp/031242440X?SubscriptionId=AKIAJ5Q5A4WOCGIYUGIA&amp;tag=thethoughtfac-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
              <em>
               Gilead
              </em>
             </a>
             , came a staggering24-years later. Recounting the memories of a dying Reverend to his seven year old son, it went on to win the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction.
            </span>
           </li>
           <li>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             The third novel,
             <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Home-A-Novel-Marilynne-Robinson/dp/B003JTHSEK?SubscriptionId=AKIAJ5Q5A4WOCGIYUGIA&amp;tag=thethoughtfac-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="">
              <em>
               Home
              </em>
             </a>
            </span>
            <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.assoc-amazon.com/e/ir?t=thethoughtfac-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B003JTHSEK" style="font-size: 16px;" width="1"/>
            <span style="font-size: 16px;">
             , took the risky step of retelling the story of Gilead through the eyes of another family in town. It won Britains prestigious Orange Prize for Fiction in 2009.
            </span>
           </li>
          </ul>
