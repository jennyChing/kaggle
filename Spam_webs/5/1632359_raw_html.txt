       <div class="post-content">
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Let
         <br/>
         us speak for a moment of the choir to whom we are preaching: Somewhere along the way, we stumbled
         <br/>
         onto something that tickled our insides in that magical way that only
         <br/>
         ontological truth can  whether it was Castaneda or Ram Dass, Lao Tsu or LSD,
         <br/>
         something stirred, and we found ourselves suddenly seeking. We worked our way through the requisite
         <br/>
         spiritual curriculum, dabbling in Watts and Watson, McKenna and Melchizidek,
         <br/>
         Gurdjieff, Arguelles, Pinchbeck and Braden.
        </p>
        <p>
         We delved deeply into the realms of psychedelic exploration
         <br/>
         from, if not every which way, at least a great big bunch of em: we sipped of
         <br/>
         the sacred vine, we engaged the wisdom of the mushroom, we navigated through
         <br/>
         our astonishment in the presence of the machine elves. And even now, we dance naked on the
         <br/>
         Playa, we Ujayi breathe our way into temporary states of Samadhi, we observe
         <br/>
         sensations, retain our seed, douse our insides with ORMUS, Shilajit and Reishi
         <br/>
         spores; we speak the same language, fluently, and yet, we're still bound by the
         <br/>
         very same third dimensional shackles as those who sleep, oblivious to the
         <br/>
         magical multidimensional wonders that we know, and so soon will they, as they
         <br/>
         are we, just as you are me.
        </p>
        <p>
         "I
         <br/>
         have no choice but to stay present because the future's too horrifying and the
         <br/>
         past is too embarrassing." James
         <br/>
         Mathers
        </p>
        <div class="ad-horizontal">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- InContent468x60 -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-5265427203945338" data-ad-slot="5348743008" style="display:inline-block;width:468px;height:60px">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         And
         <br/>
         while Jimmy's words are woven with wisdom, and the present moment is all there
         <br/>
         is, it is in the prickly arms of this embarrassment that our transformative
         <br/>
         magic lies.
        </p>
        <p>
         It's
         <br/>
         precisely our fear of looking stupid, foolish and ugly that keeps us from
         <br/>
         evolving our consciousnesses as quickly as the moment now requires. Our
         <br/>
         self-consciousness is our current Permian event; our pride, our potential
         <br/>
         extinction. Now, more than ever,
         <br/>
         we must do our work  the grueling, messy, scary kind that's anything but the
         <br/>
         fluff of New Age magical mind-stuff, of intending sun-drenched sparkles and
         <br/>
         rainbow-speckled dolphin pods.
        </p>
        <p>
         I'm talking about the real-deal, down deep n' dirty, in the trenches of
         <br/>
         our darkest, ickiest, ouchiest wounds sort of inner excavation that doesn't
         <br/>
         brandish the glossy sheen of positive thinking, empowered languaging, fun fur,
         <br/>
         free love, or fire dancing, all of which have their place, and still aren't any
         <br/>
         sort of replacement for the often agonizing unearthing of core patterning that
         <br/>
         keeps us from truly loving ourselves, truly loving each other, and showing up
         <br/>
         as our biggest, brightest most authentic selves, in service to each other and
         <br/>
         the planet.
        </p>
        <p>
         I
         <br/>
         don't care how much Chi Gong you practice, how much Sanskrit you memorize, how
         <br/>
         much peyote you keep down or how closely you follow the Thirteen Moon
         <br/>
         calendar. It's our inner work
         <br/>
         that's going to bring about the experiential shifts for which we are jonesing,
         <br/>
         and The Shift we are now being called to integrate and to embody.
        </p>
        <p>
         We,
         <br/>
         of Unity Consciousness and expanded minds, of wide open hearts, visionary
         <br/>
         perspective, art and magic, we must pave the way for our others in the
         <br/>
         deliberate unraveling of our self-consciousness. Let us trip, slip, fumble and bumble our way to this mass
         <br/>
         awakening that is so near, that is here, crouching behind that oak tree,
         <br/>
         smiling, waiting for us to cut the crap, to get real, and to move up and out of
         <br/>
         our wounding so that we can float higher, higher and higher still on the
         <br/>
         collective clouds of our knowingness, our limitlessness and our every biggest,
         <br/>
         brightest imagining.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
