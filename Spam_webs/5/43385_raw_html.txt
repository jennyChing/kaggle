        <h1 class="entry-title">
         A Visit to the Nestl Kitchens
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn" href="http://cookingontheside.com/author/cots/">

        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3730" style="width: 590px">
         <img alt="Ani, me and Chris" class="size-full wp-image-3730" height="387" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Ani_Kathy_Chris-580.jpg" title="Ani, me and Chris" width="580"/>

        <p>
         These ladies with the gorgeous smiles standing with me in the photo above are Ani Simn and Chris Garboski, managers of the test kitchens at Nestl USA. I took a little road trip up to the Los Angeles area last week to visit the Nestl headquarters in Glendale, see firsthand what goes on in the test kitchens and find out what its really like to be an in-house recipe developer at a major food company.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3731" style="width: 410px">
         <img alt="In front of the Nestl headquarters in Glendale, California" class="size-full wp-image-3731" height="533" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/In_front_of_headquarters-400.jpg" title="In front of the Nestl headquarters in Glendale, California" width="400"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          In front of the Nestl USA headquarters in Glendale, California
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         I didnt realize before that Nestl is actually the worlds largest food company, with more than 25,000 employees in the United States alone. Besides Toll House morsels (aka, chocolate chips), which is the product I most often associate with the company, Nestl USA owns a slew of other well-known brands, including Buitoni, Carnation and Libbys Pumpkin. A major corporate headquarters might be the last place youd expect to encounter warm hospitality, but thats exactly what I experienced during my visit. Entering Chris and Anis workplace kitchen was like being a guest in their home.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-3729">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Finishing the pesto kale chips with a sprinkle of salt" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3735" height="193" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Making_kale_chips.jpg" title="Finishing the pesto kale chips with a sprinkle of salt" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         When I arrived, Chris was baking melt-in-your-mouth light kale chips  that she had seasoned with Buitoni pesto. Theyve got another minute  and thirty-nine seconds, she determined after peering into the oven. My  initial response was
         <em>
          Wow, she really does have this whole recipe testing thing down to a precise science!
         </em>
         She laughedand showed me the little digital timer she was reading from.
        </p>
        <p>
         After nineteen years in the Nestl kitchens, starting as a nutrition major right out of college, Chris Garboski probably does have this role down pat. Chris and I first met on Twitter, of all places, and later we had the chance to meet in person at the BlogHer Food conference in San Francisco last October. Her counterpart, Ani Simn, who specializes in developing recipes for the Emerging Markets division (e.g., La Lechera, Abuelita and Maggi brands) first started as an intern at Nestl and is now a registered dietitian.
        </p>
        <p>
         Developing simple, nutritious and economical recipes featuring the various Nestl  brands is the primary role of the kitchens. They will typically receive  a request from the marketing teams for, say, ten Valentines Day  treats. Then Chris and Ani will work on concepts in the kitchens. They take  into account the latest food trends and their understanding of Nestl  consumers tastes, and present their dishes to the marketing folks for feedback. The  recipes they create end up printed on the side of the food packing (we  like that on this blog!), as well as in various print media outlets, on  their own
         <a href="http://nestleusa.wordpress.com/" target="_blank" title="Nestle Kitchens blog">
          <strong>
           Nestl Kitchens blog
          </strong>
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.meals.com" target="_blank" title="Meals.com">
          <strong>
           Meals.com
          </strong>
         </a>
         . Its an absolute dream job for someone who enjoys getting creative in the kitchen, if you ask me!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="A well-stocked pantry" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3738" height="533" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Pantry-400.jpg" title="A well-stocked pantry" width="400"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         The first thing that struck me when I entered the kitchens was that theyre real
         <em>
          kitchens
         </em>
         . Not labs, or even lab-like. Theyre definitely spacious and pristine  way more so than at my house  but the scale of their operation is more consumer than commercial. I envisioned their pantry to be some warehouse-style food arsenal with every type of food imaginable, like on
         <em>
          Top Chef
         </em>
         . In actuality, its much more modest  well-stocked, for sure, but not in an institutional way. They use the kinds of ingredients that you or I might have at home so their efforts in the test kitchens can match the cooking experiences of the average consumer.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Just a few of the treats coming out of the test kitchen that day" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3736" height="193" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Treats-3888.jpg" title="Just a few of the treats coming out of the test kitchen that day" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Even with the sweets, Chris likes to incorporate healthful elements wherever possible. For example, the dark chocolate chip cookies she baked on the day of my visit were made with whole wheat flour and less butter than usual (I couldnt tell a difference and Im not really one for whole wheat!). The new-and-improved
         <a href="http://nestleusa.wordpress.com/2011/03/01/new-year-new-test-kitchen-better-peanut-butter-cookie-recipe/" target="_blank" title="See Chris' new-and-improved peanut butter cookie recipe">
          <strong>
           peanut butter cookie recipe
          </strong>
         </a>
         she also prepared with natural peanut butter and came out crispy and delightful, which I consider a real feat. If youve ever tried making peanut butter cookies with natural peanut butter rather than conventional Skippy or Jif you know it often doesnt work out quite right. As more of us are going for organic, all-natural ingredients its up to the test kitchens to stay current with those preferences and adapt traditional recipes as needed.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3740" style="width: 590px">
         <img alt="They have a few more props at their disposal than the average food blogger" class="size-full wp-image-3740" height="435" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Prop_room-580.jpg" title="They have a few more props at their disposal than the average food blogger" width="580"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          They have a few more props at their disposal than the average food blogger
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         While the kitchens are designed to simulate a home environment, one glance into their prop room quickly reminded me that, yes, its still a corporate operation here. There was row upon row of neatly stacked serveware, glassware, silverware
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3741" style="width: 590px">
         <img alt="Need a ramekin?" class="size-full wp-image-3741" height="435" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Ramekins-580.jpg" title="Need a ramekin?" width="580"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Need a ramekin?
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         and ramekins! Have you ever seen this many ramekins? Im thinking this section may have exploded during the crme brle craze in the late 90s.
         <img alt=":-)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/simple-smile.png" style="height: 1em; max-height: 1em;"/>
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3744" style="width: 410px">
         <img alt="The photo area" class="size-full wp-image-3744" height="533" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Photo_setup-400.jpg" title="The photo area" width="400"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          The photo area
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         In addition to their recipe development responsibilities, Chris and Ani have become food bloggers, writing frequent posts for the
         <a href="http://nestleusa.wordpress.com/" target="_blank" title="Nestle Kitchens blog">
          <strong>
           Nestl Kitchens blog
          </strong>
         </a>
         . Theyve got a photo setup in their kitchen just like I do, albeit with better equipment and no crayons, Crocs or three-year-olds around the periphery.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3732" style="width: 590px">
         <img alt="Nestl test kitchen managers Chris Garboski and Ani Simn" class="size-full wp-image-3732" height="387" src="http://cookingontheside.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Chris_Ani-580.jpg" title="Nestl test kitchen managers Chris Garboski and Ani Simn" width="580"/>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Nestl test kitchen managers Chris Garboski and Ani Simn
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         It was
         <a href="http://nestleusa.wordpress.com/2011/03/01/new-year-new-test-kitchen-better-peanut-butter-cookie-recipe/" target="_blank">
          <strong>
           a recent post that Chris wrote on their blog
          </strong>
         </a>
         about the kitchens appliance makeover that prompted me to propose a visit. It just looked like such an interesting place to work within the food industry and I was intrigued. I got in touch with Chris and within a few hours we had a date on the calendar. Three weeks later I had the chance to meet up with her and Ani and tour the kitchens. Thank you, ladies, so much again for a fantastic visit!
