             The Top 8 Things to Do In Panama City
            </a>
           </h1>

            <div class="post_cats">

         <p>
          I left Panama City kicking and screaming  in just eight days, the metropolis had joined a lofty list of my favorite urban centers around the globe. And Ive taken on spreading the word about this newly cherished destination as something of a personal cause.
         </p>
         <p>
          Far too many tourists only stay in Panama City long enough to cast a glance on the canal before hightailing it to more exotic destinations elsewhere. Dont make the same mistake! Give Panama City the time it deserves and dont dare leave without.
         </p>
         <h3>
          1. Laying Eyes on The Panama Canal
         </h3>
         <p>
          Yes, theres more to Panama City than the canal.
          <em>
           But the canal is pretty damn cool
          </em>
          ! The visitors center at
          <a href="http://www.pancanal.com/eng/acp/cvm/" target="_blank" title="Miraflores Locks">
           The Miraflores Locks
          </a>
          is without question crowded and touristy. Yet its also fantastically organized and presented. Theres a 3D theater featuring beautiful footage of the canal, a four-level interactive museum, and three observation decks from which to wave to passing ships.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_001.png">
           <img alt="The Miraflores Locks, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21597" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_001.png"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_003.jpg">
           <img alt="The Miraflores Locks, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21599" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_003.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_004.jpg">
           <img alt="The Miraflores Locks, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21600" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_004.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          It doesnt matter how you slice it  The Panama Canal is simply fascinating. Its history and politics are steeped in drama, its construction is a mind-blowing feat of human engineering, and its current impact on world trade and economics is immeasurable. My poor brain could barely keep up with all the information coming its way.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_005.jpg">
           <img alt="The Miraflores Locks, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21601" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_005.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_007.jpg">
           <img alt="The Miraflores Locks, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21603" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_007.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_006.jpg">
           <img alt="The Miraflores Locks, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21602" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_006.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_008.jpg">
           <img alt="The Miraflores Locks, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21604" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Canal_008.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          2. Strolling through Casco Viejo
         </h3>
         <p>
          Ideally, you wont just be visiting Panama Citys
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/2014/03/12/casco-viejo-panama-city/" target="_blank" title="Casco Viejo: Heart and Soul of Panama City">
           most charming neighborhood
          </a>
           youll also be
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/2014/03/18/where-to-stay-in-panama-city/" target="_blank" title="Style at Every Price in Panama City">
           staying there
          </a>
          . But regardless, dont even think about leaving Panama City without spending a few hours cruising around the Casco  a restored historic neighborhood overflowing with chic boutiques, trendy restaurants, and red hot nightlife.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_001.jpg">
           <img alt="Casco Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21605" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_001.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_002.jpg">
           <img alt="Casco Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21606" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_002.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_003.jpg">
           <img alt="Casco Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21607" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_003.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_004.png">
           <img alt="Casco Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21608" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Casco-Viejo_004.png"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          3. Getting a Taste of Panamanian Culture
         </h3>
         <p>
          Panama is a nation of diverse cultures and indigenous groups, and they have a wealth of musical, fashionable, and culinary traditions to prove it. You can get a fun  and delicious  primer on all of the above by attending
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/2014/03/14/panama-city-dinner-theatre/" target="_blank" title="Panama City Dinner Theater">
           a cultural dance performance
          </a>
          in Panama City.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3469.jpg">
           <img alt="Tinajas Panama City Dinner Theatre" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21504" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3469.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3337_1.png">
           <img alt="Panama City Dinner Theatre" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21493" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3337_1.png"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          On our visit, we sampled Panamanian specialties like
          <i>
           sancocho,
          </i>
          a country-style chicken soup,
          <i>
           ropa vieja
          </i>
          , a spicy shredded beef and the national dish, and
          <i>
           tres leches,
          </i>
          a delicious cake that I blame for the fact that I left the country with a few extra pounds for a souvenir. As we noshed, we enjoyed live music accompanied by an endless swirl of colorful costumes, passionate live music and elaborate dances.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3344.jpg">
           <img alt="Traditional Panamanian Dress" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21495" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3344.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3338-copy.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama City Dinner Theatre" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21494" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/IMG_3338-copy.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          4. Cruising Along the Amador Causeway
         </h3>
         <p>
          The Causeway, a 2km long man-made link between the mainland and four small islands dotting the Pacific entrance to the Panama Canal, is an attraction in its own right. Its a popular stretch for locals and tourists alike to get in a dose of oceanfront exercise by bicycling, jogging, or rollerblading up and down the
          <em>
           calzada
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_001.png">
           <img alt="The Amador Causeway, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21609" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_001.png"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_002.jpg">
           <img alt="The Amador Causeway, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21610" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_002.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Along the way, you can stop to take in the
          <em>
           nearly
          </em>
          finished Frank Gehry-designed
          <a href="http://www.biomuseopanama.org/" target="_blank" title="Biomuseo Panama">
           Biomuseo
          </a>
          , spot wild sloths at the Smithsonian-operated
          <a href="http://www.stri.si.edu/espanol/visitenos/culebra/la_visita.php" target="_blank" title="Visiting STRI">
           Centro de Exhibiciones Marinas
          </a>
          , and watch ships roll into the Canal from one of the many seaside restaurants. We visited the Causeway as part of a Panama City day tour with
          <a href="http://www.barefootpanama.com/panama-tours/day-tours/panama-city-tour/" target="_blank" title="Barefoot Panama">
           Barefoot Panama
          </a>
          , a trip that also took in the Miraflores Locks, Cerro Ancon hill, and Casco Viejo.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_003.jpg">
           <img alt="The Amador Causeway, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21611" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_003.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_004.jpg">
           <img alt="The Amador Causeway, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21612" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Causeway_004.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          5. Diving into Downtown
         </h3>
         <p>
          Panama Citys concrete jungle of skyscrapers sets it apart from any other Central American capital. Not only is Panama Citys downtown a major center for international banking and commerce, its also a top shopping destination for Latin Americas elite. Those who once upon a time would flock to Miami for their retail fixes have set their sights on Panama thanks to the USAs ever tightening borders and unpleasant airport experience, meaning today, a Prada bag is considered as good a Panama souvenir as any.
         </p>
         <p>
          With taxis charging an expensive Gringo Tax, we opted to cruise through downtown with a
          <a href="http://www.viator.com/tours/Panama-City/Panama-City-Hop-On-Hop-Off-Tour/d950-5694PTY?aid=prdm14" target="_blank" title="Panama City Hop On Hop Off">
           Hop On Hop Off
          </a>
          bus pass, which picked us up right in our home base of Casco Viejo. I wasnt kidding about the shopping obsession  three out of the fifteen stops are malls.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_004.jpg">
           <img alt="Downtown Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21616" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_004.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_002.jpg">
           <img alt="Downtown Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21614" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_002.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_0021.jpg">
           <img alt="Downtown Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21618" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_0021.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Not that Im talking smack about malls. I was thrilled to restock on some hard-to-find products (hello, Lush
          <a href="http://www.lushusa.com/Jumping-Juniper/00554,en_US,pd.html" target="_blank" title="Lush Solid Shampoo">
           solid shampoo
          </a>
          !), bask in air conditioning and frozen yogurt stands, and see a movie in a theatre instead of on my laptop. While those things might not seem thrilling to someone on a quick vacation, to those who have been long-term backpacking through Latin America, they are a fairly close simulation of heaven.
         </p>
         <p>
          Looking for a seriously good-value downtown date? On a Wednesday, head to
          <a href="http://www.tripadvisor.com/Restaurant_Review-g294480-d2285415-Reviews-Sukhi_Thai-Panama_City_Panama_Province.html" target="_blank" title="Sukhi Thai">
           Sukhi Thai
          </a>
          for their weekly $10 all-you can-eat curry buffet  some of the best Thai food I found in Latin America  and then waddle your way over to the
          <a href="http://www.cinepolis.com.pa/_CARTELERA/cartelera.aspx?ic=62" target="_blank" title="Multiplaza Movie Theater">
           Cinpolis Multiplaza
          </a>
          , Panama Citys plushest retail palace, to take advantage of Wednesday night half-price movie tickets.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_003.jpg">
           <img alt="Downtown Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21615" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_003.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_005.png">
           <img alt="Downtown Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21617" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_005.png"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          6. Hiking in Metropolitan Park
         </h3>
         <p>
          Every urban jungle needs a green oasis of respite within city limits  in New York City, its Central Park; in Panama City, its Parque Metropolitano.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Metropolitan-Park_001_1.jpg">
           <img alt="Metropolitan Park, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21619" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Metropolitan-Park_001_1.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          With a $4 entrance fee and five interlocking trails that total an estimated 4+ hours of hiking time, a morning at Metropolitan Park is a great value (though we found the trails took us about half the time that the maps estimated). The park is well maintained and informative signs help you choose a trail based on what youre after: an easy walk, a challenging hike, a great view, birdwatching, etc.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our wildlife encounters were limited to some eerie spiders and the rustling of a few monkeys in the distance, but we heard of others coming across anteaters, sloths, and white-tailed deer along the trails. One thing that would be impossible to miss? The views from the 150 meter high
          <em>
           mirador.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Metropolitan-Park_003.png">
           <img alt="Metropolitan Park, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21621" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Metropolitan-Park_003.png"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Metropolitan-Park_002_1.jpg">
           <img alt="Metropolitan Park, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21620" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Metropolitan-Park_002_1.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          7. Riding the Panama Railway
         </h3>
         <p>
          The historic Panama Railroad spans the entire width of the American continent  which in Panama, means just under an hour on the rails. Constructed in 1855, the Panama Railroad took five years and $8 million dollars to build  at the time, it was per-kilometer the most expensive set of tracks ever built. Today, you can
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/2014/03/17/panama-canal-railway/" target="_blank" title="Riding on the Rails of History: The Panama Canal Railway">
           ride from Pacific to Atlantic
          </a>
          for just $25, taking in views of Gatun Lake, the Panama Canal, and the countrys diverse tropical rainforest along the way.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Railway_006.jpg">
           <img alt="Historic Panama Canal Railway" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21515" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Railway_006.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Railway_001.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama Canal Railway" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21510" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Railway_001.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Railway_005.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama Canal Railway" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21514" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Railway_005.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <h3>
          8. Going Back in Time at Panama Viejo
         </h3>
         <p>
          Panama City, like many national capitals, has gone through a few phases. Currently, it is centered around the skyscraper-laden Downtown. Previously, it was based in the charming area of Casco Viejo. And originally, from 1519, it was rooted in Panama Viejo  the first European settlement anywhere along the Pacific!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_001.jpg">
           <img alt="Downtown Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21613" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Downtown_001.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_001.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21622" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_001.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_002.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21623" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_002.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Today, the site is mostly dismantled and the ruins are, well, in ruin. But the lively history makes up for the whats neglected of the Unesco World Heritage Site. For over 150 years after its founding the city thrived thanks to the gold being plundered from
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/category/destinations/Peru" target="_blank" title="Peru Posts">
           Peru
          </a>
          and siphoned back to Spain through Panama, and the natural barriers that made Panama Viejo an unstoppable fortress. So historians are still baffled as to why, in 1671, the Spanish soldiers
          <em>
           abandoned their city stronghold
          </em>
          in order to meet Captain Henry Morgains pirates, who were approaching Panama Viejo from the Atlantic side of the country. It was a fatal mistake, and Panama Viejo was plundered and burned to the ground as a result.
         </p>
         <p>
          While its certainly not the most intact ancient city Ive ever visited, Panama Viejo is a must-see in my books due to the pirate-y lore, the historical significance and the juxtaposition of looking out at a modern metropolis from the crumbling carcass of its past.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_003.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21624" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_003.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_004.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter wp-image-21625" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_004.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          While visiting the ruins is free, the onsite museum has a $3 entrance fee  dont make the same mistake as I did and visit on a Monday when its closed! To avoid high taxi prices from Casco Viejo, we visited as a stop on our Hop On Hop Off tour.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_005.jpg">
           <img alt="Panama Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21626" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_005.jpg"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_006.png">
           <img alt="Panama Viejo, Panama City" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21627" src="http://www.alexinwanderland.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Panama-Viejo_006.png"/>
          </a>
         </p>

           <strong>
            Have you been to Panama City? Have I left anything off the list?
           </strong>
          </span>
         </p>
