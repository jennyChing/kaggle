           <h1 class="post-title">
            What if Bilbo Was a Chick?
           </h1>
           <p class="post-byline">
            by
            <a href="http://dblback.com/author/brentfatig/" rel="author" title="Posts by Brent Fatig">
             Brent Fatig
            </a>
             January 2, 2014
           </p>
           <div class="clear">
           </div>
           <div class="entry share">
            <div class="entry-inner">
             <p>
              So, Im a nerd. Like, a huge nerd. I was raised on things like Dr. Who and Star Trek: The Next Generation.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thanks a lot, Dad.
             </p>
             <p>
              <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
               Thats why
               <a href="http://www.lastwordonnothing.com/2013/12/18/one-weird-old-trick/" target="_blank">
                this article by Michelle Nijhuis over at Last Word on Nothing
               </a>
               caught my eye today.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              Her 5-year-old daughter insisted that Bilbo Baggins, the main protagonist of J.R.R. Tolkiens
              <em>
               The Hobbit
              </em>
              , was female and that the book must be read to her as such moving forward. Hobbits do come across a bit whiny and emotional at times, but Id say that its more likely thatthis behavior is due to their being an allegory to old-timey English-folk instead of to the fairer sex.
             </p>
             <p>
              One thing that cant really be disputed is Tolkienscomplete lack of female representation within the plots of his stories.
             </p>
             <p>
              Thinking of Bilbo as a heroine really is an interesting twist, and it might just be worth thinking about the next time you are reading
              <em style="line-height: 1.5em;">
               The Hobbit.
              </em>
              <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
               And by reading
              </span>
              <em style="line-height: 1.5em;">
               The Hobbit
              </em>
              <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
               , I definitely mean watching Peter Jacksons dialogue-rich and action-inflated trilogy.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p>
              My only hang up? The hairy feet
             </p>
             <p>
              [
              <a href="http://www.lastwordonnothing.com/2013/12/18/one-weird-old-trick/" target="_blank">
               Last Word on Nothing
              </a>
              , Photo Credit: Warner Bros. via
              <a href="http://www.blackfilm.com/read/2011/06/first-look-from-the-hobbit/the-hobbit-an-unexpected-journey/" target="_blank">
               Black Film
              </a>
              ]
             </p>
