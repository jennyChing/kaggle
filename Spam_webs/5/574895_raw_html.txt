         <div class="body" itemprop="description">
          <strong class="db" itemprop="headline">
           Shortening bootcut jeans with an "invisible seam".
          </strong>
          <p itemprop="text">
           How many times have you left a great looking, worn down pair of denim in to the shop because the legs are too long? They just dont lok the same if you just cut them shorter...
          </p>
          <p itemprop="text">
           Denim pants with an abraded hem need to be shortened with a seam. Bootcut jeans cannot be shortened with a straight seam because the sewing lines are not the same length. In this tutorial youll see how to shorten jeans with a curved seam - no matter how flare legged your bootcut jeas are!
          </p>
         </div>

       <div class="span9">

         <div class="span7">
          <ul class="steps" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ItemList">
           <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
            <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
             <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8317/full_8317.jpg" itemprop="image">
              <img alt="How to make jeans. Shortening Bootcut Jeans Or Pants - Step 1" class="step-image" height="150" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8317/normal_8317.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
             <div class="body small">
              <span class="number" itemprop="position">
               Step 1
              </span>
              <div itemprop="text">
               <p>
                Try the denim on with both flat and hig heeled shoes. I usually mark the denim about hanf an inch (2cm) longer than my heel. If the denim is a regular bootcut the front will be about 3/4 inches (3cm)longer. If the denim is a bell-bottom it will be even longer from the front.
               </p>
               <p>
                The heel measurement is the important one! After I know how much must be taken off, I draw (usually with a ballpoint pen) a straight line 0,5cm above the hem turn. Measure how wide your leg is on this line. This will be your bottom sewing line.
               </p>
               <p>
                Then measure the shortening-measurement up from here (in picture: left legpiece). Now draw a curvy line towards the front.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
            <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
             <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8320/full_8320.jpg" itemprop="image">
              <img alt="How to make jeans. Shortening Bootcut Jeans Or Pants - Step 2" class="step-image" height="266" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8320/normal_8320.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
             <div class="body small">
              <span class="number" itemprop="position">
               Step 2
              </span>
              <div itemprop="text">
               <p>
                This curvy line is your top sewing line has to be exactly the same length as your bottom sewing line, otherwise the two sewing lines will not match. All that is between the two lines will be gone when the denim is done.
               </p>
               <p>
                In this example I will take 11cm from the back and 9cm from the front.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
            <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
             <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8321/full_8321.jpg" itemprop="image">
              <img alt="How to make jeans. Shortening Bootcut Jeans Or Pants - Step 3" class="step-image" height="150" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8321/normal_8321.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
             <div class="body">
              <span class="number" itemprop="position">
               Step 3
              </span>
              <div itemprop="text">
               <p>
                To make sewing thick denim easier, cut away extra allowences from seams. See that you do not cut the seam open.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="advert-well">
            <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-6287412971271285" data-ad-format="rectangle" data-ad-slot="9385019883" id="Project_Content">
            </ins>
            <script>
             (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
            </script>
           </li>
           <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
            <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
             <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8324/full_8324.jpg" itemprop="image">
              <img alt="How to make jeans. Shortening Bootcut Jeans Or Pants - Step 4" class="step-image" height="150" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8324/normal_8324.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
             <div class="body">
              <span class="number" itemprop="position">
               Step 4
              </span>
              <div itemprop="text">
               <p>
                Now you can start pinning the two sewing lines together. Always start from the sideseams, then pin the front-middle, then back-middle, and then the space between.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
            <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
             <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8325/full_8325.jpg" itemprop="image">
              <img alt="How to make jeans. Shortening Bootcut Jeans Or Pants - Step 5" class="step-image" height="150" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8325/normal_8325.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
             <div class="body">
              <span class="number" itemprop="position">
               Step 5
              </span>
              <div itemprop="text">
               <p>
                To get your sewing lines exactly in place, puch the needle straight down. See that it comes out the other side on the sewing line. If it doesnt, you have to adjust the fabric so it does.
               </p>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
           </li>
           <li class="instruction step with-image" itemprop="itemListElement" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ListItem">
            <div itemprop="item" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/CreativeWork">
             <a class="step-image-link" data-gallery="#blueimp-gallery" href="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8326/full_8326.jpg" itemprop="image">
              <img alt="How to make jeans. Shortening Bootcut Jeans Or Pants - Step 6" class="step-image" height="150" src="http://images.coplusk.net/projects/3053/steps/8326/normal_8326.jpg" width="400"/>
             </a>
             <div class="body">
              <span class="number" itemprop="position">
               Step 6
              </span>
              <div itemprop="text">
               <p>
                Check that especially the sideseams are exactly in-line.
               </p>
