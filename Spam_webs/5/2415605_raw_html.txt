        <a href="http://www.riverrelief.org/updates/entry/welcome-our-new-education-coordinator-kristen-schulte" title="Permalink to this entry">
         Welcome our new Education Coordinator, Kristen Schulte!
        </a>
       </h3>
       <div class="update-inset">
        <img alt="" src="http://www.riverrelief.org/image/1/200/0/uploads/news-images/website-photo_brightened-kristen.jpg"/>
       </div>
       <p>
        We are proud and so lucky to be welcoming our new full-time Education Coordinator, Kristen Schulte on board.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        Kristen has been working outdoors educating kids for six years everywhere from Yellowstone National Park to Missouri Botanical Gardens and Missouri State Parks. She's also a hardworking volunteer and board member for a Missouri nonprofit we really admire, the Ozark Trail Association.
        <br/>
        <br/>
        She's already become a great asset to the team and will be leading her first Missouri River summer camp this July with River Relief and Summers@Mizzou.
       </p>

         Refurbish Our Fleet Campaign
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4>
        UPDATE! Our Campaign was a success...but we still need help!
       </h4>
       <p>
        Thanks to the generous donations of
        <strong>
         127 people
        </strong>
        , our Indie-GoGo campaign raised
        <strong>
         $12,186!!!
        </strong>
        But we still have a ways to go to reach our goals. You can always donate on
        <a href="/donate/">
         our website here.
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        We will begin fulfilling donor perks in late June and we'll contact you with our plan. THANK YOU!!!!
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          Behind Every Healthy River is a Strong Fleet
         </strong>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Our five trucks have driven
        <strong>
         more than 1.5 million miles
        </strong>
        . Theyve been good to us, but its time to retire these guys to peaceful pastures. We need
        <strong>
         $100,000
        </strong>
        to replace them.

       <h3>
        <a href="http://www.riverrelief.org/updates/entry/film-peeled-faces-on-the-amazon-the-story-of-the-amazon-express" title="Permalink to this entry">
         Film: "Peeled Faces on the Amazon - The Story of the Amazon Express"
        </a>
       </h3>
       <h4>
        Missouri Premiere - Saturday, April 11
       </h4>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Where
        </strong>
        -
        <a href="https://goo.gl/maps/FdMkn">
         Columbia Independent School - 1804 N. Stadium Blvd, Columbia, MO
        </a>
        (please use South Parking Lot and entrance)
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         When
        </strong>
        - Door Open at 5:30 p.m. Film starts at 7:00 p.m. followed by discussion with expedition paddler West Hansen
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Tickets - $18 in advance or at the door. (15 and under FREE with paid family member)
         <a href="https://riverrelief.wufoo.com/forms/q6aw7vj1fksuio/">
          PURCHASE TICKETS HERE
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Film Length - 1 hour 42 min.
         <a href="/workspace/uploads/documents/amazon-poster-mo.pdf">
          Click here to download flier
         </a>
         (pdf)
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Refreshments donated by Be the Change Volunteers and
         <a href="http://buroakbeer.com/">
          Bur Oak Brewing
         </a>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Come see a special showing of the feature length film documenting the first descent of the Amazon River from its most distant source in 2012. This will be followed by a question and answer session with the expeditions leader West Hansen. With sponsorship from National Geographic, the Amazon Express team negotiated significant whitewater challenges in the upper headwaters and then transitioned to paddle down waters that develop into the largest river on earth.
        <a href="https://vimeo.com/79890523">
         Click here to view the trailer on Vimeo.
        </a>
       </p>
       <h4>
        This event is a fundraiser for Missouri River Relief and
        <a href="http://www.bethechangevolunteers.org/">
         Be The Change Volunteers
        </a>
        . See below for more info.
       </h4>
       <p>
        <img alt="amazon delta" height="213" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8567/16226567153_2db18328c3_n.jpg" width="320"/>
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>

       <h3>
        <a href="http://www.riverrelief.org/updates/entry/join-missouri-river-relief-for-the-2015-summers-mizzou" title="Permalink to this entry">
         Join Missouri River Relief for the 2015 "Summers@Mizzou"
        </a>
       </h3>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <img alt="" class="right" height="203" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8752/16741192527_94d91bed2c.jpg" width="194"/>

        </strong>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         University of Missouri - Columbia
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Missouri River Relief is excited to offer a very special summer camp
        <strong>
         for students aged 12-18
        </strong>
        getting up close and personal with the Big Muddy
        <strong>
         .
        </strong>
        It's called
        <strong>
         "Big River Experience"
        </strong>
        and it's part of the "Summers @ Mizzou" summer camp hosted by MU Extension and the 4-H Center for Youth Development. There are 30 slots available, first come first serve.
       </p>

        </a>
       </h4>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <img alt="" class="right" height="203" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8752/16741192527_94d91bed2c.jpg" width="194"/>

        </strong>
        <br/>
        <strong>
         University of Missouri - Columbia
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Missouri River Relief is excited to offer a very special summer camp
        <strong>
         for students aged 12-18
        </strong>
        getting up close and personal with the Big Muddy
        <strong>

        </strong>
        It's called
        <strong>

        </strong>
        and it's part of the "Summers @ Mizzou" summer camp hosted by MU Extension and the 4-H Center for Youth Development. There are 30 slots available, first come first serve.

       <h4>
        <a href="http://www.riverrelief.org/updates/entry/enjoy-the-big-muddy-speaker-series-each-month-in-three-cities" title="Permalink to this entry">
         Enjoy the Big Muddy Speaker Series each month in three cities!
        </a>
       </h4>
       <p>
        The Big Muddy Speaker Series has its own website! A one stop shop for statewide Missouri River knowledge.
        <a href="http://bigmuddyspeakers.org/">
         www.bigmuddyspeakers.org
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Big Muddy Speaker Series is held in three locations,
        <a href="http://bigmuddyspeakers.org/rocheport/">
         Rocheport
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://bigmuddyspeakers.org/kansascity/">
         Kansas City
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://bigmuddyspeakers.org/stcharles/">
         St. Charles
        </a>
        . The free monthly presentations bring experts to discuss Missouri River issues, biology, hydrology, history and more.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://bigmuddyspeakers.org/">
         <img alt="bigmuddyspeakerseries" height="47" src="http://farm9.staticflickr.com/8101/8548794827_bc12b05db8_m.jpg" width="192"/>

       <h4>
        <a href="http://www.riverrelief.org/updates/entry/big-muddy-clean-sweep-2014" title="Permalink to this entry">
         Big Muddy Clean Sweep 2014
        </a>
       </h4>
       <p>
        <a href="/updates/entry/big-muddy-clean-sweep-2014/">
         <img alt="" height="57" src="http://farm7.static.flickr.com/6085/6083097370_bca5818b56_m.jpg" style="display: block; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="137"/>
        </a>
        <strong>
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Brunswick, Jameson Island, Boonville, Cooper's Landing, Jefferson City, Hermann
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        The Big Muddy Clean Sweep is a wrap! With our final river clean-up in Hermann, MO, we finished our two month series of clean-ups and education events across the middle of Missouri.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="/updates/entry/big-muddy-clean-sweep-2014/">
         Read all about what was accomplished on this amazing journey here!
        </a>

       <h4>
        <a href="http://www.riverrelief.org/updates/entry/cleaning-up-a-missouri-river-trash-trap-video" title="Permalink to this entry">
         Cleaning up a Missouri River Trash Trap (video)
        </a>
       </h4>
       <p>
        <br/>
        As part of the 24th
        <a href="https://www.facebook.com/pages/Project-Blue-River-Rescue/189682744389036">
         Project Blue River Rescue
        </a>
        on April 5, 2014, Missouri River Relief tackled a site on the Missouri River where floating trash collects downstream of the Blue River and Kansas City. We call this the "Missouri River Trash Trap".
        <a href="/updates/entry/cleaning-up-a-missouri-river-trash-trap-video/">
         Here's a video
        </a>
        and description of the project at
        <em>
         <strong>
          Rivermile 350.9, just downstream of Kansas City.
         </strong>
        </em>
