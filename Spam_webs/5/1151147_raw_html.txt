         <h2 class="date-header">
          July 01, 2015
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-author-pamgarrisontypepadcom entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7a80de5970b">
          <div class="entry-inner">
           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://www.pamgarrison.com/2015/07/july-1.html">
             July 1
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <img src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb084c22d2970d-pi"/>
              <br/>
              Wow. June was a crazy month for me. I'm pretty sure if I counted the days, I was away from home for at least half of it, and that's a crazy crazy amount for this homebody! I'm glad all the travel is behind me now!
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I took this photo on Sunday when I arrived home from the final June excursion:
              <br/>
              <img src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7a80dd7970b-pi"/>
              <br/>
              I think I should spend all of July sitting right here. Now that I survived my lunatic June, it feels as if my summer can really start. Summer is
              <i>
               supposed
              </i>
              to feel relaxing, after all.
              <br/>
              <img src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb084c22d7970d-pi"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              And now that it's summer, a colorful garland in the hallway seems just right. I made the giant painted cardboard garland for the Creative Bug set. Man, how I love pretty things made from everyday items like a shipping box.
              <br/>
              <img src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb084c22dc970d-pi"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Here they are before I hot glued them all together. (I didn't bring the giant cardboard rose, I'll have to find a use for it here at home.) and while I'm talking about home decor...
              <br/>
              <img src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb084c22e1970d-pi"/>
              <br/>
              Have I introduced you to our new lamp? I looove it, (even though it was very expensive). I bought it from
              <a href="http://www.schoolhouseelectric.com">
               www.schoolhouseelectric.com
              </a>
              . I remember seeing it on someone's Instagram feed a year or two ago and sending a screenshot to a friend telling her I thought she needed it. And after trolling Target and HomeGoods looking for a special lamp for this spot for ages, I decided to splurge and I'm glad I did. I don't know if you can tell from this photo, but it's milk glass. I didn't really realize how much I'd appreciate the craftsmanship of the construction and details like the cloth cord and vintage type plug. It fits so perfectly and I'm trying to keep the table around it spotless despite my penchant for filling up every flat surface with a pile of some sort.
              <br/>
              <img src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7a80de0970b-pi"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I think this blooming succulent looks like spring turned to summer, with it's hues ranging from the palest apple green to salmon and pops of sunshine yellow. It's the first time that it's bloomed although it's been potted out front for at least a year or two. I don't know what the plant is called, but I'm referring to it as the
              <i>
               bells of summer i
              </i>
              n my head each time I walk by it. Ring ring ring, summer is here!
             </p>
            </div>

         <h2 class="date-header">
          June 06, 2015
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-author-pamgarrisontypepadcom entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a00d83452354569e201b8d1234390970c">
          <div class="entry-inner">
           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://www.pamgarrison.com/2015/06/saturday-greetings-1.html">
             Saturday greetings
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <br/>
              <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
               (
               <em>
                ed: pardon the repost if this one comes up twice in your blog reader, I tried to get the photos full size the second time- still getting the hang of phone blogging!
               </em>
               )
              </span>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <img alt="" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb083deb17970d-pi"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              A little something bright and happy in the works.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <img alt="" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d1234381970c-pi"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Always have something in the works.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I guess that's who I am.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I think most of us are like that- having creative pursuits in the works. Well, most that would likely read this blog, I assume.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I love my creativity, and try to nurture it everyday, but I do realize that that often adds chaos to my living. It's a choice I make regularly, but it's not without consequences, both good and bad. Like all our choices, right? Blabbering about what, Pam? I really don't know, lol. (
              <em>
               I have always disliked lol and never used it- until suddenly- now it's on repeat- who am I?)
              </em>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <img alt="" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d1234386970c-pi"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              My pal
              <a href="http://amyhanna.typepad.com/" target="_self">
               Amy
              </a>
              and I traded some art for art- had to have this pretty piece. You can find your own in her etsy,
              <a href="https://www.etsy.com/shop/amyhanna" target="_self" title="amy hanna etsy">
               amyhanna.etsy.com
              </a>
              (
              <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
               <em>
                ed: fixed!
                <span style="text-decoration: line-through;">
                 at baseball so I'm blogging from phone again, can't make hyperlink
                </span>
               </em>
              </span>
              ). Something exciting happened for Amy recently,
              <a href="http://www.thesocietyinc.com.au/blog/#.VXSgymTBzGc" target="_blank" title="sibella court">
               Sibella Court
              </a>
              (
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <em>
                do you know her work? she's quite a creative genius- here's
                <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/174196556X/pamgarrison-20" target="_self">
                 one of her books
                </a>
                , and
                <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0762779616/pamgarrison-20" target="_self">
                 another
                </a>
                , and
                <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/0062318330/pamgarrison-20" target="_self">
                 another still
                </a>
                - I'm telling you, she's a big deal!
               </em>
              </span>
              ), saw our friend
              <a href="http://mengmahar.wix.com/mengmahar" target="_self">
               Molly Meng
              </a>
              , wearing one of Amy's pieces and was smitten, and inquired about some for herself, as well as possibly carrying some of Amy's pieces in
              <a href="http://www.thesocietyinc.com.au/" target="_self">
               her Sidney, Australia shop
              </a>
              . It's a big deal, but what was most exciting to me was to watch how fun it was for Amy to be inspired creatively by the collaboration. Amy and Sibella seem very like minded: loving creativity, the hunt and feeling like pirates. Very fun to see her ideas grow due to the joint creative influence.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I've been thinking a lot about collaborations lately, how when done best, they elevate each individual participant to a greater whole. I love that.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              It's probably because I'm involved in a lot of them right now; a class at creativebug, and an e-course creation with
              <a href="http://www.dispatchfromla.com/" target="_self">
               Mary Ann
              </a>
              , both mentioned before, anda joint original artwork project with
              <a href="http://www.ccerruti.com/" target="_self" title="courtney cerruti ">
               Courtney Cerruti
              </a>
              , among others. Look at the pretty parcel from Courtney that just arrived for me to work on:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <img alt="" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d123438b970c-pi"/>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Yum! These are backgrounds she made using techniques from her new book,
              <a href="http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/1631590367/pamgarrison-20" target="_self" title="playing with surface design link">
               <em>
                Playing With Surface Design
               </em>
              </a>
              . More on that later, we are going to do a giveaway of her book and some of our original art together. I'll post it here then.
              <br/>
              <br/>
              Another pretty package that arrived was this:
              <br/>
              <br/>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c79a5e65970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="image from http://s3.amazonaws.com/hires.aviary.com/k/mr6i2hifk4wxt1dp/15060721/d6d0864a-d5da-490d-8f17-fa23fe8d3a66.png" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c79a5e65970b image-full img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c79a5e65970b-800wi" title="image from http://s3.amazonaws.com/hires.aviary.com/k/mr6i2hifk4wxt1dp/15060721/d6d0864a-d5da-490d-8f17-fa23fe8d3a66.png"/>
              </a>
              <br/>
              <br/>
              I bought one of Ali Nelson's snail mail subscriptions for myself. That glassine envelope she addressed so beautifully, talk about happy mail... You can find out more about it on her website,
              <a href="http://www.alimakesthings.comhttp://alimakesthings.com/" target="_self" title="alimakestings">
               www.alimakesthings.com
              </a>
              if you want your own pretty package.

            <a href="http://www.pamgarrison.com/2015/03/a-baby-quilt-gift.html">
             a baby quilt gift
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc374d970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG quilt for Vince" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc374d970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc374d970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG quilt for Vince"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Hey there! How goes it out there? I wish I had some good excuse for being away from the blog for so long, but the truth is, the speed with which time passes is befuddling me. It's crazy!
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1ac33970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 12" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1ac33970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1ac33970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 12"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Anyway, I'm back with one of the things I was up to in my absence; a gift for sweet baby Vincent, born to
               <a href="http://raisedbydesign.com/" target="_blank" title="Maggie raised by design">
                Maggie and James
               </a>
               , and
               <a href="http://housewrenstudio.typepad.com/" target="_blank">
                my dear friend Charlotte's
               </a>
               first grand-baby!
              </span>
             </p>
             <div class="photo-wrap photo-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1aecb970c" id="photo-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1aecb970c" style="display: inline-block; width: 700px;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1aecb970c-pi">
               <img alt="personalized quilt label " class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1aecb970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1aecb970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="personalized quilt label "/>
              </a>
              <div class="photo-caption caption-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1aecb970c" id="caption-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1aecb970c">
               a little dedication for baby
              </div>
             </div>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               After seeing
               <a href="http://raisedbydesign.com/bambinos-nursery-decor/" target="_self">
                Maggie's posts about nursery decor plans
               </a>
               for her baby to be, and being on a quilting binge, I couldn't resist a little patchwork quilt. I went to JoAnn's fabrics one night and bought a bunch of fabrics, then raided my own stash once home to come up with a selection of various prints and solids. The mix and the match, the choosing combinations, it's so much fun for me. A big reason I've always been drawn to fabric and occasionally go on purchasing binges with no specific project in mind. It's also likely why I haven't used solids alone in the past, missing an opportunity to play with design and color specifically. Just more fun things I'm noticing along the way as I continue to explore and learn about quilting.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b38a970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PGarrison baby quilt" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b38a970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b38a970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PGarrison baby quilt"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I improved a little bit with matching my corner points, but there are still many many non matching points:
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588410970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 7" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588410970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588410970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 7"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b4d4970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 1" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b4d4970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b4d4970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 1"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b4ed970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 5" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b4ed970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b4ed970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 5"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I'm totally fine with that because it's handmade with so much love. I never even used to look at things like that when looking at quilts, but the more quilting books I look at, and linking around I do, I really respect the quilting artists out there and their mad skillz, and that aspect of the art form. I am not one to pursue perfection in my creating, but I do want to pursue precision when that is something that I believe
               <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                adds
               </span>
               to the creation. And some quilt designs would definitely qualify for that category.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc40fc970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="PG baby quilt 2" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc40fc970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc40fc970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 2"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Luckily, this patchwork baby gift
               <em>
                doesn't
               </em>
               qualify for that. I don't think the journey of learning (thus the imperfect quilting) is anything to be secret about so that's why I'm sharing them up close. Not because I'm proud of them, but because it's a totally welcome part of any creative journey to me. I embrace the participation in something creative so much more than any result.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588574970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="PG baby quilt 8" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588574970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588574970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 8"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I made the binding trim out of one of the fabrics.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588639970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="PG baby quilt 6" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588639970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7588639970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 6"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I did everything on the sewing machine, except sewing the binding to the back, that I did by hand.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c75886b1970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 3" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c75886b1970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c75886b1970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 3"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I quilted it by sewing machine, sewing first in the ditch, and adding one, two, sometimes three more stitches to the inside of each square. Making things up along the way vs. following any pattern or rule.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b839970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 4" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b839970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b839970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 4"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Just enough to give it that puckering after washing. I love the way the back looks because of it, those big puffy squares:
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc4300970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 10" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc4300970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07fc4300970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 10"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b890970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 11" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b890970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b890970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 11"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I can't remember what size the finished quilt ended up to be. I
               <em>
                think
               </em>
               it was around 38x58". A baby quilt or lap quilt makes an excellent size if you're not a quilter yet but want to start exploring with it. It's not too big to easily fit under a home sewing machine. And overall it can be done pretty quickly, decreasing the chances of getting intimidated and overwhelmed, (
               <em>
                or even bored
               </em>
               ).
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b9cb970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="PG baby quilt 13" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b9cb970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0e1b9cb970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG baby quilt 13"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Baby and Mama should receive it today ;).
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               A little bit of California warmth sent to the other coast this crazy winter season.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Welcome to the world baby Vincent!!!! xo
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
             </p>

             more art made at art retreat ps #3
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c75182df970b-pi" style="display: inline;">

               </a>
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I mentioned before that I took very limited art supplies to our little Art Retreat #3 in Palm Springs. Just some art journals and sketchbooks. It felt good to limit my supplies and spend less time sorting through them to find want I wanted, and less time going from one idea to the other vs. just relaxing and really enjoying one. Okay, I consider art journaling and sketching to be different, so make that two.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               After we were finished driving around taking pictures like these:
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">

               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
               <em>
                Thanks Carol for this photo of me behind the Palm Springs sign. When I was a little girl I loved
                <a href="http://www.poetryfoundation.org/poem/173916" target="_self">
                 Henry Wadsworth Longfellow poem
                </a>
                that starts, "There was a little girl, who had a little curl, right in the middle of her forehead." Not so much the little, but the poem springs to mind when I see this. ;)
               </em>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 11pt;">
               <em>

             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               We headed back to the house for fun, and making art was the main priority. All day long outside and all night inside.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518420970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="P Garrison PS 2" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518420970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518420970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="P Garrison PS 2"/>
               </a>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f537b3970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison sketchbook lemons" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f537b3970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f537b3970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison sketchbook lemons"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               I plucked ripe lemons from the tree and tried to paint/sketch them in a manner more similar to what I learned in
               <a href="http://www.pamgarrison.com/2014/11/another-jag-in-the-road-day-1.html" target="_blank">
                the oil painting class by Leslie Duke I attended
               </a>
               . I love to explore different methods.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5378c970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison sketchbook17" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5378c970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5378c970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison sketchbook17"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c751852e970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison sketchbook 1" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c751852e970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c751852e970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison sketchbook 1"/>

               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53828970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison painting" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53828970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53828970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison painting"/>
               </a>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c75185aa970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison sketchbook  2" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c75185aa970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c75185aa970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison sketchbook  2"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Carol bought this sweet teacup and saucer at the antique store we popped into. You can see it in use
               <a href="http://talltalesfromkansas.typepad.com/tall-tales-from-kansas/2015/02/before-i-attempt-to-sketch-it.html" target="_self">
                at her home here
               </a>
               . It was too beautiful to resist sketching.
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Carol was also the inspiration for this sketching of a chair that was at the house, as she was the first to sketch it. All three of us had a go at it. Not only is it fun to sketch the same subject yourself a few times, it's even more fun to have three different people sketch the same thing and enjoy the results of how unique each one is, how different the styles are. (The Moss sisters have their renditions on their blogs too.)
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c75185f7970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison sketchbook 5" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c75185f7970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c75185f7970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison sketchbook 5"/>
               </a>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5386a970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison sketchbook 6" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5386a970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5386a970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison sketchbook 6"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daf4fa970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Pam garrison watercolor bird" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daf4fa970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daf4fa970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam garrison watercolor bird"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5427a970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Pam garrison watercoloring leaves" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5427a970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5427a970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam garrison watercoloring leaves"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5427f970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="PG watercolor experiments" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5427f970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f5427f970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="PG watercolor experiments"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f54287970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Pam garrison watercolor bird 1" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f54287970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f54287970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam garrison watercolor bird 1"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daea6a970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison sketchbook 8" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daea6a970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daea6a970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison sketchbook 8"/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Watercolored birds.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               Yep, still birds.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
                And then I some art journaling (the difference for me being:not based on looking at anything for inspiration, just imagination):
               </span>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518aca970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Pam Garrison art journal 5" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518aca970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518aca970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison art journal 5"/>
               </a>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeec6970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="P GARRISON art 2" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeec6970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeec6970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="P GARRISON art 2"/>
               </a>
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeecf970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="P GARRISON art " class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeecf970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeecf970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="P GARRISON art "/>
               </a>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               How gorgeous is this
               <a href='http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B000WMQXKW/pamgarrison-20"nofollow"' target="_blank">
                new Antietam ink
               </a>
               I bought? Dipped my brush right in it to paint with it, even though I bought it for my fountain pen.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeed5970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="P GARRISON art 1" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeed5970c img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b8d0daeed5970c-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="P GARRISON art 1"/>
              </a>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518ba2970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Pam Garrison art journal " class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518ba2970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518ba2970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison art journal "/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               A little doodly drawing using this
               <a href='http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ASIN/B004ESZ9DW/pamgarrison-20"nofollow"' target="_blank">
                Pilot Parallel pen
               </a>
               and then watercoloring fill in.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53dbe970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Pam Garrison art journal 1" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53dbe970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53dbe970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison art journal 1"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53ddc970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Pam Garrison art journal 2" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53ddc970d img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53ddc970d-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Pam Garrison art journal 2"/>
              </a>
              <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201bb07f53dbe970d-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <br/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               <a class="asset-img-link" href="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518c3f970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
                <img alt="Moss sisters in PS" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518c3f970b img-responsive" src="http://pamgarrison.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452354569e201b7c7518c3f970b-700wi" style="width: 700px;" title="Moss sisters in PS"/>
               </a>
               <br/>
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               These two. Excellent Art Retreat cohorts. Even cuter sisters.
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               It was a good trip, I highly recommend grabbing a small group and making time to create, I'm never sorry when I do!
              </span>
             </p>
             <p style="text-align: left;">
              <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
               And with that, my Palm Springs Art Retreat #3 recap concludes.
              </span>

               Well, one more:
