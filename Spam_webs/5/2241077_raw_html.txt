                    <div class="post-header">

                     </div>

                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-305524725465088682" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://www.gladstonesclinic.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/11/may31-2013-foto-stress-fatigue.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">

                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">

                     </div>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="color: cyan;">
                        <b>
                         Regardless of who we are, we have all most likely been burdened by stress and anxiety at least once in our lifetime. There are few who have to unfortunately live with this burden every day. It is for these few as well as others, who wish to learn to free themselves from this burden that this article is meant for. Below you will find a detailed analysis of Stress and Anxiety, several different effective methods to help reduce their impact on your daily life, as well as short reviews on products others have profusely recommended as being quite effective in helping them overcome both Stress and Anxiety.
                        </b>
                       </span>

                     <b id="docs-internal-guid-00c87d4b-d117-1ec6-837b-e6b0af5d58a8" style="font-weight: normal;">

                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: bold; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        Overview: Stress And Anxiety
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <b style="font-weight: normal;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        <br/>
                       </span>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        Now most people tend to use Stress and Anxiety interchangeably, and that is understood as they both share common features such as feelings of worry, nervousness, and fear. Yet, it is agreed that there is quite the distinctive difference between both emotions. The main difference is that with stress, one tends to know why they are stressing. It may be due to the pressure of meeting deadlines at work, family issues, or a plethora of other things which cause people to worry, but at the end of the day, people tend to know what it is that they are stressing about. With anxiety, this may not always be the case, for anxiety is a more internally caused emotion, whereas stress is caused from external factors.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <b style="font-weight: normal;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        Anxiety stems not so much from worry and pressure, like stress, but more so from fear. Most people who are clinically diagnosed as showing severe signs of anxiety also deal with personal phobias. It is for this reason that anxiety may be so closely related to stress. If there is anything which causes people to worry and stress, it is their fears. If anything, fears of letting others down or not being able accomplish or resolve something may just further add on to the initial stress people feel.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        Signs and Symptoms of Stress and Anxiety
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <b style="font-weight: normal;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        There is a plethora of signs and symptoms which become apparent when stress and anxiety are accumulated over time.
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <b style="font-weight: normal;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        - Memory lapses - Irritability/Agitation - Loss of Appetite - Aches/Pain
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        - Inability to Concentrate - Moodiness - Sleep Deprivation - Nausea
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        - Pessimism - Depression - Procrastination - Dizziness
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
                      <span style="background-color: transparent; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">
                       <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                        - Poor Judgment - Isolation - Alcohol/ Drug Abuse - Fevers/Colds
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">

                        Treatments and Management Techniques

It is when we all overdue it, as in find ourselves stressing more times during the day than not, that we must accept that stress and anxiety have become a problem for us. Luckily, we are not alone, for many have been in similar situations and they have come up with many tactics on how to effectively manage stress and anxiety.
