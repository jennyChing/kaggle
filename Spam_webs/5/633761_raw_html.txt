         <h1 style="text-align: center;">
          Clean Water Means Opportunity &amp; Freedom
         </h1>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Water is lifes most basic need. Yet, today there are nearly a billion people on the planet without regular access to clean drinking water.This water crisis is happening all over the world, more prevalently though, in developing nations such as sub-Sahara Africa, Southeast Asia and Latin America.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Its called a water crisis because it starts with water, but in reality, it has the trickle effect of impacting education, health and poverty. Women and children are particularly affected, many of whom spend hours each day trekking across dangerous distances in search of this prized commodity which, far too often, is not even clean. This prevents them from pursuing an education, growing food, caring for their families and performing other productive activities.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          To address the global water crisis, Pro-Surfer
          <a href="http://www.consciousconnectionmagazine.com/2013/07/jon-rose-waves-for-water/">
           Jon Rose
          </a>
          founded an organization called
          <em>
           <a href="http://www.wavesforwater.org/" target="_blank">
            Waves for Water
           </a>
          </em>
          with the mission of bringing clean water to every person on the planet who needs it. Hes enlisted the help of fellow waterman Rob Machado to organize the largest single day of water relief efforts in history. The event, called
          <a href="http://www.wavesforwater.org/project/WWD" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            100K World Water Day
           </strong>
          </a>
          , culminates on March 22nd with the goal of bringing clean water access to a minimum of 100,000 people.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Jon Rose has been a close friend for years and Ive joined him and the W4W team on many water missions, says surfing legend Rob Machado. 
          <span class="pullquote">
           When you travel the globe its shocking to see what little access many people have to clean drinking water
          </span>
          , the most simple basic human necessity. Jon is someone who takes action, a leader with a vision and deeply passionate individual in righting what is wrong. Its an honor for me to lend a hand in helping make a difference in peoples lives. 
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          To correspond with
          <strong>
           World Water Day
          </strong>
          of 2014,
          <em>
           Waves For Water
          </em>
          also teamed up with
          <em>
           <a href="http://blog.hurley.com/blog/h2o" target="_blank">
            Hurley H2O
           </a>
          </em>
          to coordinate this massive global water relief effort. Six groups of trained professionals will lead Clean Water Courier missions to six different parts of the globe: Haiti, Brazil, Philippines, Indonesia, Liberia and Nicaragua.Approximately 1,000 water filters will be installed in a single day with the capacity to help provide access to clean water for 100,000 people.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_2352" style="width: 701px">
          <img alt="W4W's first mission with the ASP World Tour Surfing took them back to the Philippines. After this trip, up to 10,000 people in the region have gained access to clean water. " class=" wp-image-2352 " height="463" src="http://cdn8.consciousconnectionmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Jon-Rose-Waves-for-Water.jpg" width="691"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Jon Rose on W4Ws first mission in the Philippines with the ASP World Surfing Tour. After this trip, up to 10,000 people in the region have gained access to clean water.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          We typically work in a very lean and covert fashion to maximize our efficiency on the ground, but since this specific project is just as much about awareness as it is action, says
          <em>
           Waves For Water
          </em>
          Founder Jon Rose. Im thrilled to have the opportunity to bring some of our longtime friends and advocates  Carissa Moore, Rosario Dawson, Rob Machado, Alex Atala, and more  along for the ride.
          <span class="pullquote">
           World Water Day is already happening, all we are doing is coordinating efforts to raise the stakes and maximize the impact.
          </span>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          The
          <a href="http://www.wavesforwater.org/project/WWD" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            100K World Water Day
           </strong>
          </a>
          awareness effort launched on January 1, 2014 and is supported by ongoing fundraising until World Water Day, March 22, 2014.The concept of
          <a href="http://www.unwater.org/worldwaterday" target="_blank">
           <strong>
            World Water Day
           </strong>
          </a>
          was born at the 1992 United Nations Conference on Environment and Development, and was first officially celebrated in 1993 as a means of bringing attention to the importance of fresh, clean water, as well as serving as an advocacy platform for the sustainable management of freshwater resources.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          Twenty years later,
          <em>
           Waves For Water
          </em>
          and
          <em>
           Hurley H2O
          </em>
          plan to bring even more global attention to World Water Day. Our goal with this initiative is to create a measurable, large-scale action, on a day when the entire world has its eyes and focus on water. We hope that our pledge to provide access to clean water for 100,000 people on this one day will help trigger the masses into taking similar action throughout the following years, said
          <em>
           Waves For Water
          </em>
          Founder Jon Rose.
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <a href="https://www.wavesforwater.org/donate">
           <img alt="Donate to waves for water" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2477" height="99" src="http://cdn5.consciousconnectionmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/03/Donate-to-waves-for-water.png" width="521"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: justify;">
          <em>
           Waves for Water
          </em>
          will equip any traveler with filters to take along on their journeys, essentially crowdsourcing globalambassadorsfor the greater good. As Jon Rose puts it, Imagine a million travelers doing this. Now were making waves.Learn more about how you can get involved or donate to the cause at
          <a href="http://www.wavesforwater.org/" target="_blank">
           www.wavesforwater.org
          </a>
          .
         </p>
