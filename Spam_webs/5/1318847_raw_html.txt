                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     The Presque Isle River, Part 2 -- The Bear Story
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-1533806085773768666">
                     <div align="left" class="MsoTitle">
                      <i>
                       <span style="font-family: Calibri, sans-serif; font-size: 11pt;">
                        Stripped down &amp; told straight
up. About 19 hours in the woods...
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoTitle">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoTitle">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gDww4IxE5Tg/U1GsJT3irVI/AAAAAAAABzk/bbwBE5rIDqg/s1600/Cover+Shot.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="340" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gDww4IxE5Tg/U1GsJT3irVI/AAAAAAAABzk/bbwBE5rIDqg/s1600/Cover+Shot.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       Bobcat Lake is nestled in the Ottawa
National Forest just a few minutes through the woods off U.S. 2 and once on
that you can get anywhere you need. The lake is lovely and at seventy-eight
acres is comfortably sized for a canoe.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-yt5lZ_EemYc/U1GsQYiACsI/AAAAAAAABzs/KuSXOlWszPM/s1600/Bobcat+Southeast.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="262" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-yt5lZ_EemYc/U1GsQYiACsI/AAAAAAAABzs/KuSXOlWszPM/s1600/Bobcat+Southeast.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       We
                       <i>
                        yearned
                       </i>
                       to be there. I suppose in part because when loose in the northwoods our spirits
seemed large, which hints of greatness were otherwise purged in the routine disinfectant
of everyday city life.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       During great years at Bobcat we caught
mighty fish, ventured into the forest beneath glistening autumn skies, then
with nightfall kicked back in wonder at the heavens and sometimes, the northern
lights.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       During not so great years we
persevered all the same. We traveled a long way specifically to take what comes
as it came and it wasn't much in us to surrender.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       Like the time it rained for five
straight days and the temperature never rose above 45.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       We mostly huddled at the picnic table beneath
a tarp, peering up to a sodden sky. Nearby ran a trench dug 'round the fire pit
to divert running water. On the sixth day, it snowed. Four inches. Which proved
about the limit of our perseverance. We bragged on that vacation for years.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       In the year of the bear, weather was
fair for late September. Eagles flew
&amp; fish jumped. We ate well and slept soundly. I suppose its simply human nature
that we sought more, even when already gifted.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       So it was that year when Heather and I
decided to explore the Presque Isle River in a canoe.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZX-isRxMBmw/U1GsXZalVnI/AAAAAAAABz0/tqcJzbQd3k0/s1600/Bridge.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="266" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZX-isRxMBmw/U1GsXZalVnI/AAAAAAAABz0/tqcJzbQd3k0/s1600/Bridge.jpg" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       We put in just downstream from the small
dam at the Presque Isle Flowage, a weedy waterfowl impoundment in whose distant
upstream reaches of flooded timber and obscure watery tendrils are said to lurk
Musky as long as your leg. Couldn't prove it by me.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       Our expedition would go with the flow of
the river until past Marenisco and the bridge at US 2, where Johnny'd
pick us up with the car. It figured to be a pleasant way to spend a gentle autumn's
day.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       Our eighteen-foot Grumman aluminum
canoe held Heather, myself and everything we might possibly need. I wore sensible
sneakers so if we went into the river, I'd not struggle to swim out of my
boots.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       We bade farewell to Johnny and pushed
off.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       Caught up by gentle current, it took
only a few paddle strokes to pass under the bridge, after which the forest
closed in on both sides of the river. It
seemed we'd traveled back in time. Dependent
only upon one another and well outside our experience, we felt as voyageurs.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">
                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>
                       After a bit, a pair of river otters
joined us.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">

                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">

an older man beside a small pickup truck. He bent over the rear of trucks covered
bed and cooked a meal. After catching no
fish and seeing as how the man was done eating, eventually I hobbled on over.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">

                     </div>

the man's face. His outfit was dirty and
torn. Maybe fifty years old maybe
eighty, this was a hardscrabble gent accustomed to time spent in out of the way
places. The trucks bed was jammed with all
manner of gear and enclosed by a rickety fiberglass top.

                       </o:p>
                      </span>

the company, though hed definitely have preferred someone closer his age. He'd come up from Detroit to hunt bear and
after a couple weeks, his tag remained unfilled.
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                     </div>

to exhausted with bitterness and being alone in the woods was a solace to him,
even without benefit of killing a bear. As his litany of complaint proved
wearisome, I focused on the old man's halo.

                       </o:p>

cloud of gnats and flies. Every now and
again hed wave a gnarled hand past his face.
The cloud dispersed a skosh then quickly reformed, drawn like strewn iron
filings toward a powerful magnet. If the
insects bothered him, I saw no sign of it.
I shuddered at his indifference.

man, on that morning near the mouth of the Presque Isle when I became mostly
indifferent to flies.

warmth as evening turned gray.

dinner and I returned to fishing, catching a northern pike of nasty
disposition. I held it aloft for the man to see. He grinned broadly but when I released the
fish, shook his head as if I were addled.

Johnny could be. Winter lurked just beyond the horizon as if to pounce, biting
down hard on the evening air. A day begun sweetly was sour by nightfall.

                       </o:p>

lot and my rescuers arrived. We loaded up
and returned to camp.

                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                     </div>

moisture, the hole in my foot was easily revealed in the bright beam from a
flashlight. I didn't recall my last
tetanus shot, so off we went to the hospital in Ironwood. It felt good to bind
my foot with the dry snugness of a fresh boot.
We piled into the car. I drove.

Bessemer, the dread lights of a state patrol car flashed behind us. Thankfully,

                       </o:p>

                       Typically, a bear hunter deposits a smelly
pile of bait at some likely spot in the woods.
Bears like the bait. Hunter hauls
in more. Bears acquire taste for bait. Then the season arrives and while
sitting over fresh bait, the hunter bags a bear.

                       </o:p>
                      </span>

across the hall had been baiting bear.

                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div align="left" class="MsoBodyTextIndent" style="text-indent: 0in;">

ready, near his bait. Daylight dimmed
and at the ready he remained. After
nightfall, when prudent men gather 'round the fire to drink bourbon from tin
cups in honor of heroics both real and imagined, the bear hunter stayed
steadfast.

to the bait. The man shot it, then climbed
down to claim what was hard earned. A bear cub lay dead at his feet.

sudden wrath of a vengeful god.

                       The bear shattered the man's arm,
broke his leg and smashed some ribs. She chewed hard on his face and shoulders.
Somehow, the bear cub killer threw two shots into her. With the second, she
tumbled off into the woods. He managed to crawl back to camp, from where his
buddies rushed him to the hospital.

                       </o:p>
                      </span>

                       As we were staying in the woods,
naturally we wondered exactly where this'd occurred. But the only person around who knew wasnt
talking.

outside. I got my shot. We went over to
Scottys and a dinner of porterhouse steak, potatoes, salad &amp; coffee. All for
$6.25, American.

affairs. A couple tables over a group of teenagers debated some dead kid's
haunting of a local abandoned hockey rink. No lie.

find a motel or head back to camp. The
Ottawa is something like zillion acres of woods and the hospital at Ironwood
the only one in the region. Odds were overwhelming that the wounded bear wasnt
anywhere near our camp.

2., lights flashing, siren silent. It whisked the hunter to Marquette and the

piled into the car for the drive back to Marenisco, followed by a short jaunt through the woods to camp at Bobcat Lake.

                      <span style='font-family: "Calibri","sans-serif"; font-size: 11.0pt; mso-ascii-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-bidi-font-family: Calibri; mso-bidi-theme-font: minor-latin; mso-hansi-theme-font: minor-latin;'>

about 9:30 every night, 'cept near the three taverns clustered at the far edge
of the main drag. It was fully 11:00 by
the time we turned off the highway and into town.

Police in the world awaited.

away from anyone who cared, we decided to gut it out. Once in the tent I placed an axe by my side.
Then I lay down in my sleeping bag, put my hand around the handle of the axe
and fell to fitful sleep.

                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </span>
                     </div>

                     </div>
