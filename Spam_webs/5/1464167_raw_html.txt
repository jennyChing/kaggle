           <p>
            (Bloomberg) -- U.S. President Barack Obama and Cuban
President Raul Castro said today they would begin normalizing
relations between the two nations, a deal brokered by Pope
Francis and aided by the generational shift in Floridas Cuban-American community.
           </p>
           <p>
            The action means not simply the opening of a U.S. embassy
in Havana but the lifting of some of the restrictions that have
limited travel and commerce and kept aficionados from legally
bringing Cuban cigars to U.S. soil.
           </p>
           <p>
            Only 90 miles from the Florida coast, Cuba has long been a
fixation -- some might say obsession -- of American imagination
and politics. Beloved by Ernest Hemingway, it underwent a
communist revolution, was the locus of perhaps the worlds
closest brush with nuclear war, and yet has remained the
spiritual home of thousands of refugees who fled the Castro
regime and who for decades drove U.S.-Cuban policy.
           </p>

&lt;p&gt;The thaw was wrapped into the release of American aid
worker Alan Gross and the exchange of a U.S. spy for three Cuban
intelligence agents. Obama and Castro made simultaneous
announcements in Washington and Havana to outline the
rapprochement. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Changed Relationship &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Today, the United States of America is changing its
relationship with the people of Cuba, Obama said at the White
House. Neither the American nor Cuban people are well served
by a rigid policy thats rooted in events that took place before
most of us were born. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The Cuban leader said the progress achieved in the
exchanges weve had shows that its possible to find solutions
to many problems. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;We have to learn the art of living together with our
differences in a civilized way, Castro said in Havana. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Cubas decision was also rooted in economic reality. Its
longtime patrons, Russia and Venezuela, have lost influence and
are being squeezed by plummeting oil prices. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The changes follow a rare private intercession by Pope
Francis, the Catholic Churchs first Latin-American pontiff,
secret meetings between Cuban and American delegations at the
Vatican and in Canada, and an extraordinary telephone
conversation lasting more than 45 minutes yesterday between
Obama and Castro. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Vatican Statement &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The Vatican, in a statement today, said, The Holy Father
wishes to express his warm congratulations for the historic
decision to normalize relations, with the aim of overcoming,
in the interest of the citizens of both countries, the
difficulties which have marked their recent history. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Obama said Cuba was on the agenda when he met with Pope
Francis at the Vatican in March. The pope played a very
important role, Obama said in an interview with ABC News,
calling the pontif the real deal, a remarkable man. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Obama said that while hes not sure whether Castro, at age
83, has suddenly changed his views, there is a generational
change in the country that holds the prospect for more democracy
and freedom. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Raul Castros older brother Fidel, a leader in the Cuban
revolution and ruled the country until stepping down in 2008,
wasnt part of the talks. Obama said he was not sure how
much the 88-year-old Castro was aware or approved of the talks. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Gross Freed &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The White House announced the steps after Cuba released
Gross on humanitarian grounds. Following high-level talks
between the governments since the spring, the U.S. and Cuba also
made a parallel prisoner exchange of three Cuban intelligence
agents for a U.S. intelligence asset who has been imprisoned for
more than 20 years, according to administration officials who
briefed reporters on condition of anonymity. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Cuba also agreed to release 53 people the U.S. considers
political prisoners, some of whom have already been released,
the officials said. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The White House plans to move swiftly. The U.S. will issue
regulations within weeks and will open an embassy as soon as
logistically possible, according to administration officials who
briefed reporters on condition of anonymity. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Obama said he would engage Congress in an honest and
serious debate about changing legislation to fully end the
U.S. embargo of Cuba. Some Cuban-American lawmakers vowed to
block the presidents plans. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Vow to Block &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;This Congress is not going to lift the embargo,
Republican Senator Marco Rubio of Florida, whose parents left
Cuba in 1956, said at a news conference in Washington today. I
intend to use every tool at our disposal in the majority to
unravel as many of these changes as possible. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;New Jersey Democratic Senator Robert Menendez, usually an
administration ally, criticized Obamas action, saying it
invites dictatorial and rogue regimes to use Americans serving
overseas as bargaining chips and will set back efforts to
bring democracy to Cuba. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;South Carolina Republican Lindsey Graham, who is set to
become chairman in January of the Senate Appropriations
subcommittee on state and foreign operations, said he would try
to block funding to open an embassy in Havana. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Punishing Cuba has fallen out of favor with the U.S.
public. In every Gallup Poll from 1999 to 2009, the last time
the question was asked, a majority of Americans favored normal
diplomatic relations between the U.S. and Cuba. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Travel Eased &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The administration will make it easier for people to go
there under any of 12 exceptions in the current law, including
for family visits, education, research, journalism, professional
meetings, religious activity, athletic competitions, information
transmission and export transactions, officials said. Purely
touristic travel will still be against the law unless Congress
acts to lift the embargo &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Those who do get to Cuba will be able to use credit and
debit cards in Cuba and Americans will be able to legally bring
home up to $100 in previously illegal Cuban cigars. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;U.S. companies will be permitted to export to Cuba
telecommunications equipment, agricultural commodities,
construction supplies and materials for small businesses. U.S.
financial institutions will be allowed to open accounts with
Cuban banks. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Financial Transactions &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Limits on Cuban-Americans remittances to relatives in
their homeland will jump to $8,000 from $2,000 annually. U.S.
companies will be permitted to export an expansive list of goods
including building materials and allowed to build
telecommunications infrastructure on the island. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Exports will mainly be permitted to Cubas emerging private
sector, including residential goods and equipment for small
businesses and agriculture, an official said. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The president also ordered the State Department to review
Cubas designation as a state sponsor of terrorism. Cuba has
been listed as a terrorism sponsor since 1982, the official
said. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Cuba also agreed to allow greater Internet access for its
citizens and easier telecommunications with the United States. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The U.S. and Cuba will begin a series of high-level visits
between the governments as they negotiate normalization of
relations with a visit to Havana in January by an assistant
secretary of state for talks on migration policy. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Cuba Visit &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Obama wouldnt rule out making a visit to Cuba sometime in
the future. I dont have any current plans to visit Cuba, he
said in the ABC interview. Lets see how things evolve. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;White House press secretary Josh Earnest said presidents
routinely stop in countries with which the U.S. has
fundamental disagreements, citing recent trips by Obama to
China and Myanmar. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The popes role in the talks included sending a letter to
Castro and Obama, urging them to resolve the dispute over Gross
and to pursue closer relations. An official said the request
from the Pope was rare and came shortly after a meeting Obama
had with Francis at the Vatican earlier this year. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The Vatican hosted delegations from the two countries for
talks, though most of the meetings were held in Canada, an
administration official said. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Return to U.S. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Gross, a 65-year-old American, left Cuba on a U.S.
government plane this morning and landed at Andrews Air Force
Base outside Washington shortly before Obama spoke. The three
Cuban agents were returned to their country this morning, an
administration official said. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Gross was arrested by Cuban officials while working to
expand Internet access for Havanas Jewish community. He was
accused of undermining the Cuban state and in December 2009 was
sentenced to 15 years in prison. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The U.S. isolation of Cuba has endured in part because of
the influence of the Cuban-American exile community in Florida,
a crucial swing state in national elections. But support for the
embargo has waned among younger Cuban-Americans, even as
opposition to the Castro regime remains. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Trade with Cuba has been severely restricted for half a
century due to a Cold War freeze. The U.S. hasnt had normal
diplomatic relations with Cuba since 1961. The two countries had
no diplomatic relations until 1977. Since then, the U.S. has
been represented by an interests section within the
Switzerlands embassy to Cuba. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h2&gt;Bay of Pigs &lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Under President John F. Kennedy, the Central Intelligence
Agency backed a failed armed invasion of the island at the Bay
of Pigs, along with repeated attempts to assassinate Cuban
President Fidel Castro, Rauls older brother. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The U.S. has maintained an embargo on most trade with the
island, a policy that periodically has come under pressure from
American business interests that see potential profit there. The
World Bank, citing 2011 data, pegs the islands gross domestic
product at more than $68 billion -- about what the U.S. produced
that year in a day and a half, according to data compiled by
Bloomberg. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Under Raul Castro, who took over from Fidel Castro as
acting president in 2006 and succeeded him two years later, the
Marxist nation has taken tentative steps away from central
planning. In July, Cuba lowered its economic growth forecast for
this year to 1.4 percent. &lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Earlier this week, Moodys said the economy on the island
about 90 miles off the coast of Florida was slowing, citing
decreased reform momentum. &lt;/p&gt;

    (Bloomberg) -- U.S. President Barack Obama and Cuban\nPresident Raul Castro said today they would begin normalizing\nrelations between the two nations, a deal brokered by Pope\nFrancis and aided by the generational shift in Florida&#x2019;s Cuban-American community. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;The action means not simply the opening of a U.S. embassy\nin Havana but the lifting of some of the restrictions that have\nlimited travel and commerce and kept aficionados from legally\nbringing Cuban cigars to U.S. soil. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Only 90 miles from the Florida coast, Cuba has long been a\nfixation -- some might say obsession -- of American imagination\nand politics. Beloved by Ernest Hemingway, it underwent a\ncommunist revolution, was the locus of perhaps the world&#x2019;s\nclosest brush with nuclear war, and yet has remained the\nspiritual home of thousands of refugees who fled the Castro\nregime and who for decades drove U.S.-Cuban policy. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;The thaw was wrapped into the release of American aid\nworker Alan Gross and the exchange of a U.S. spy for three Cuban\nintelligence agents. Obama and Castro made simultaneous\nannouncements in Washington and Havana to outline the\nrapprochement. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;h2&gt;Changed Relationship &lt;/h2&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;&#x201C;Today, the United States of America is changing its\nrelationship with the people of Cuba,&#x201D; Obama said at the White\nHouse. &#x201C;Neither the American nor Cuban people are well served\nby a rigid policy that&#x2019;s rooted in events that took place before\nmost of us were born.&#x201D; &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;The Cuban leader said &#x201C;the progress achieved in the\nexchanges we&#x2019;ve had shows that it&#x2019;s possible to find solutions\nto many problems.&#x201D; &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;&#x201C;We have to learn the art of living together with our\ndifferences in a civilized way,&#x201D; Castro said in Havana. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Cuba&#x2019;s decision was also rooted in economic reality. Its\nlongtime patrons, Russia and Venezuela, have lost influence and\nare being squeezed by plummeting oil prices. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;The changes follow a rare private intercession by Pope\nFrancis, the Catholic Church&#x2019;s first Latin-American pontiff,\nsecret meetings between Cuban and American delegations at the\nVatican and in Canada, and an extraordinary telephone\nconversation lasting more than 45 minutes yesterday between\nObama and Castro. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;h2&gt;Vatican Statement &lt;/h2&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;The Vatican, in a statement today, said, &#x201C;The Holy Father\nwishes to express his warm congratulations for the historic\ndecision&#x201D; to normalize relations, &#x201C;with the aim of overcoming,\nin the interest of the citizens of both countries, the\ndifficulties which have marked their recent history.&#x201D; &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Obama said Cuba was on the agenda when he met with Pope\nFrancis at the Vatican in March. The pope &#x201C;played a very\nimportant role,&#x201D; Obama said in an interview with ABC News,\ncalling the pontif &#x201C;the real deal, a remarkable man.&#x201D; &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Obama said that while he&#x2019;s not sure whether Castro, at age\n83, has suddenly changed his views, there is a generational\nchange in the country that holds the prospect for more democracy\nand freedom. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Raul Castro&#x2019;s older brother Fidel, a leader in the Cuban\nrevolution and ruled the country until stepping down in 2008,\nwasn&#x2019;t part of the talks. Obama said he was &#x201C;not sure&#x201D; how\nmuch the 88-year-old Castro was aware or approved of the talks. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;h2&gt;Gross Freed &lt;/h2&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;The White House announced the steps after Cuba released\nGross on humanitarian grounds. Following high-level talks\nbetween the governments since the spring, the U.S. and Cuba also\nmade a parallel prisoner exchange of three Cuban intelligence\nagents for a U.S. intelligence asset who has been imprisoned for\nmore than 20 years, according to administration officials who\nbriefed reporters on condition of anonymity. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Cuba also agreed to release 53 people the U.S. considers\npolitical prisoners, some of whom have already been released,\nthe officials said. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;The White House plans to move swiftly. Purely\ntouristic travel will still be against the law unless Congress\nacts to lift the embargo &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Those who do get to Cuba will be able to use credit and\ndebit cards in Cuba and Americans will be able to legally bring\nhome up to $100 in previously illegal Cuban cigars. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;U.S. companies will be permitted to export to Cuba\ntelecommunications equipment, agricultural commodities,\nconstruction supplies and materials for small businesses. The three\nCuban agents were returned to their country this morning, an\nadministration official said. &lt;/p&gt;\n&lt;p&gt;Gross was arrested by Cuban officials while working to\nexpand Internet access for Havana&#x2019;s Jewish community.
