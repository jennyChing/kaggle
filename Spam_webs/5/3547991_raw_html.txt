     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        The Kybalion: Hermetic Philosophy
       </b>
      </i>
      is a 1908 book claiming to be the essence of the teachings of
      <a href="/wiki/Hermes_Trismegistus" title="Hermes Trismegistus">
       Hermes Trismegistus
      </a>
      , published anonymously by a person or persons under the pseudonym of "the Three Initiates".
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="General_description">
       General description
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kybalion_Book_Cover.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="554" data-file-width="354" height="313" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Kybalion_Book_Cover.jpg/200px-Kybalion_Book_Cover.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Kybalion_Book_Cover.jpg/300px-Kybalion_Book_Cover.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1c/Kybalion_Book_Cover.jpg 2x" width="200"/>

     <p>
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      was first published in 1908 by the Yogi Publication Society and is now in the
      <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">
       public domain
      </a>
      , and can be found on the internet. The book purports to be based upon ancient
      <a href="/wiki/Hermeticism" title="Hermeticism">
       Hermeticism
      </a>
      , though many of its ideas are relatively modern concepts arising from the
      <a href="/wiki/New_Thought" title="New Thought">
       New Thought
      </a>
      movement. The book early on makes the claim that it makes its appearance in one's life when the time is appropriate and includes variations of material found in the
      <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Proverbs" title="Book of Proverbs">
       book of Proverbs
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Mental_Transmutation">
       Mental Transmutation
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mental Transmutation">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      <a href="/wiki/Mind" title="Mind">
       Mental
      </a>
      Transmutation (also described as Mental Alchemy, the Art of Mental Chemistry, and the Art of Polarization) refers to the art of changing and transforming one's own mental states and conditions, as well as influencing those of others. It is also called a form of "Mystic Psychology".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_seven_Principles">
       The seven Principles
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The seven Principles">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The book devotes a chapter to each of its seven "Principles", or axioms:
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Principle_of_Mentalism">
       Principle of Mentalism
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Principle of Mentalism">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Principle of Mentalism
      </b>
      embodies the idea that "All is Mind."
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Principle_of_Correspondence">
       Principle of Correspondence
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Principle of Correspondence">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Principle of Correspondence
      </b>
      embodies the idea that there is always a correspondence between the laws of phenomena of the various "planes" of being and life.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Hermeticism" title="Hermeticism">
       As above, so below
      </a>
      ; as below, so above. This principle states that there is a harmony, agreement and correspondence between these planes, delineated as:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       The Great Physical Plane
      </li>
      <li>
       The Great Mental Plane
      </li>
      <li>
       The Great Spiritual Plane
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Principle_of_Vibration">
       Principle of Vibration
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Principle of Vibration">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Principle of Vibration
      </b>
      embodies the idea that motion is manifest in everything in the Universe, that nothing rests, and everything moves, vibrates, and circles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This principle explains that the differences between different manifestations of Matter, Energy, Mind, and even Spirit, are the result of only different "vibrations".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The higher a person is on the scale, the higher the rate of vibration will be. Here,
      <a href="/wiki/The_All" title="The All">
       The All
      </a>
      is said to be at an infinite level of vibration, almost to the point of being at rest. There are said to be millions upon millions of varying degrees between the highest level, The All, and the objects of the lowest vibration.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Mental Transmutation is described as the practical application of this principle. To change one's mental state is to change vibration. One may do this by an effort of Will, by means of deliberately "fixing the attention" upon a more desirable state.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Principle_of_Polarity">
       Principle of Polarity
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Principle of Polarity">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Principle of Polarity
      </b>
      embodies the idea that everything is dual, everything has two poles, and everything has its opposite.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      All manifested things have two sides, two aspects, or two poles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Everything "is" and "isn't" at the same time, all truths are but half truths and every truth is half false, there are two sides to everything, opposites are identical in nature, yet different in degree, extremes meet, and all paradoxes may be reconciled.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Principle_of_Rhythm">
       Principle of Rhythm
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Principle of Rhythm">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Principle of Rhythm
      </b>
      embodies the idea that in everything there is manifested a measured motion, a to and fro, a flow and inflow, a swing backward and forward, a pendulum-like movement.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      This principle explains that there is rhythm between every pair of opposites, or poles, and is closely related to the Principle of Polarity.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It can be seen that this Principle enables transition from one pole to the other, and not necessarily poles of extreme opposites.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Principle_of_Cause_and_Effect">
       Principle of Cause and Effect
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Principle of Cause and Effect">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Principle of Cause and Effect
      </b>
      explains that there is a cause for every effect, and an effect for every cause.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It also states that there is no such thing as chance, that chance is merely a term indicating extant causes not recognized or perceived.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Principle is clarified in the chapter Causation.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Principle_of_Gender">
       Principle of Gender
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Principle of Gender">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The
      <b>
       Principle of Gender
      </b>
      embodies the idea that
      <a href="/wiki/Gender" title="Gender">
       gender
      </a>
      is manifested in everything.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The authors state that this does not relate explicitly to the commonly understood notion of
      <a href="/wiki/Sex" title="Sex">
       sex
      </a>
      , but rather "... to beget; to procreate, to generate, to create, or to produce..." in general.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Gender is manifested as the Masculine and Feminine principles, and manifests itself on all planes.
     </p>
     <p>
      <b>
       Mental Gender
      </b>
      is described as a
      <a href="/wiki/Hermeticism" title="Hermeticism">
       Hermetic
      </a>
      concept which relates to the masculine and feminine principles. It does not refer to someone's
      <a href="/wiki/Sex" title="Sex">
       physical sex
      </a>
      , nor does it suggest that someone of a certain physical sex necessarily has a matching mental gender. Ideally, one wants to have a balanced mental gender.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The concept put forth in
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      states that gender exists on all planes of existence (Physical, Mental, and Spiritual), and represents different aspects on different planes. It is also stated that everything and everyone contains these two elements or principles.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Masculine principle is always in the direction of giving out or expressing, and contents itself with the "Will" in its varied phases.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Feminine principle is always in the direction of receiving impressions, and has a much more varied field of operation than the Masculine. The Masculine conducts the work of generating new thoughts, concepts, and ideas, including the work of the imagination.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
       <a href="#cite_note-19">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        19
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <div id="paragraph">
      <p>
       It is said that there must be a balance in these two forces. Without the Feminine, the Masculine is apt to act without restraint, order, or reason, resulting in chaos. The Feminine alone, on the other hand, is apt to constantly reflect and fail to actually do anything, resulting in stagnation. With both the Masculine and Feminine working in conjunction, there is thoughtful action that breeds success, which points out that both the Feminine and the Masculine fulfill each other.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         20
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
     </div>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Authorship">
       Authorship
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Authorship">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The "Three Initiates" who authored
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      chose to remain anonymous. As a result, a great deal of speculation has been made about who actually wrote the book. The most common proposal is that
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      was authored by
      <a href="/wiki/William_Walker_Atkinson" title="William Walker Atkinson">
       William Walker Atkinson
      </a>
      , either alone or with others, such as
      <a href="/wiki/Paul_Foster_Case" title="Paul Foster Case">
       Paul Foster Case
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Elias_Gewurz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Elias Gewurz (page does not exist)">
       Elias Gewurz
      </a>
      . Atkinson was known to use many pseudonyms, and to self-publish his works.
     </p>
     <p>
      Also suggestive is that among his earliest published pseudonymous and anonymous works may have been a series entitled
      <i>
       The Arcane Teachings
      </i>
      with its first edition published prior to 1909 and the second edition published in 1909.
      <i>
       The Arcane Teachings
      </i>
      bears many superficial similarities to
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      explores seven "Hermetic Principles" while
      <i>
       The Arcane Teachings
      </i>
      examines seven "Arcane Laws",
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      claims to be an elucidation of an ancient, unpublished Hermetic text of the same name, while
      <i>
       The Arcane Teachings
      </i>
      claims to reveal the wisdom of an ancient, unpublished scroll of occult aphorisms, and both books describe three "Great Planes" of reality which are further subdivided into seven lesser planes. Both books also describe three of the lesser planes as "astral black keys" analogous to the black keys on a piano, and inhabited by elemental spirits. And, both books describe the process of "Mental Alchemy" in great detail, and in nearly complete agreement with each other. There are other similarities, and some
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (June 2015)">
          who?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      argue that the
      <i>
       The Arcane Teachings
      </i>
      might have been Atkinson's "first draft" of material which later became
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      . Atkinson also attempted to describe the workings of the universe in terms of a set of laws in his last manuscript
      <i>
       The Seven Cosmic Laws
      </i>
      , written in 1931 and published posthumously in 2011.
     </p>
     <p>
      A common theory is that Atkinson co-wrote the book with Paul Foster Case and Michael Whitty. This theory is often held by members of
      <a href="/wiki/Builders_of_the_Adytum" title="Builders of the Adytum">
       Builders of the Adytum
      </a>
      (B.O.T.A.), the Mystery School later founded by Case, though the group doesn't publicly make this claim itself. This story appears to have originated with a B.O.T.A. splinter group, the
      <a href="/wiki/Fraternity_of_the_Hidden_Light" title="Fraternity of the Hidden Light">
       Fraternity of the Hidden Light
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
       <a href="#cite_note-21">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        21
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Along these lines, much has been made about Paul Foster Case's being a Freemason, and that
      <i>
       The Kybalion's
      </i>
      publisher, the Yogi Publication Society, gave its address as "Masonic Temple, Chicago IL" on the book's
      <a href="/wiki/Book_frontispiece" title="Book frontispiece">
       frontispiece
      </a>
      . However,
      <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">
       Chicago
      </a>
      's "Masonic Temple" was also the city's first skyscraper, housing dozens of stores and small businesses without any Masonic affiliations, and named for the Masonic Lodge which financed much of its construction and met in its top few floors.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
       <a href="#cite_note-22">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        22
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Other names speculatively mentioned as co-authors of
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      include Harriet Case (Paul Foster Case's wife at the time),
      <a href="/wiki/Mabel_Collins" title="Mabel Collins">
       Mabel Collins
      </a>
      (a prominent Theosophical writer),
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claude_Bragdon" title="Claude Bragdon">
       Claude Bragdon
      </a>
      (an architect, Theosophist, and writer on "mystic
      <a href="/wiki/Geometry" title="Geometry">
       geometry
      </a>
      "), and
      <a href="/wiki/Alexander_(magician)" title="Alexander (magician)">
       Claude Alexander
      </a>
      (a well-known stage magician, mentalist, proponent of
      <a href="/wiki/Crystal_gazing" title="Crystal gazing">
       crystal gazing
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/New_Thought" title="New Thought">
       New Thought
      </a>
      author).
      <a href="/wiki/Ann_Davies_(occultist)" title="Ann Davies (occultist)">
       Ann Davies
      </a>
      , who succeeded Paul Foster Case as head of the B.O.T.A., is often mentioned as a possible
      <i>
       Kybalion
      </i>
      contributor, but she was born in 1912four years after the book's first publication.
     </p>
     <p>
      The introduction for a 2011 edition of
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      published by Tarcher/Penguin presents an argument that William Walker Atkinson was the sole author of the work, including evidence such as the 1912 edition of Who's Who in America, which attributes Atkinson as the author, and a 1917 French language edition of
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      in which the translator's introduction attributes the work to "the American psychic master W.W. Atkinson."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
       <a href="#cite_note-23">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        23
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      However The Kybalion and the Arcane Teachings have strong contradictions and even attacks between some of their core teachings, and this added to both having been published in a very close space of time (first edition of The Arcane Teachings years before 1909, second edition in 1909 and third edition in 1911) make the hypothesis of Atkinson's authorship of the Kybalion very inconsistent.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
       <a href="#cite_note-24">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        24
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Influences">
       Influences
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Influences">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The works of the English philosopher
      <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Spencer" title="Herbert Spencer">
       Herbert Spencer
      </a>
      are referred to approvingly in
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      as showing an understanding of
      <a href="/wiki/Hermeticism" title="Hermeticism">
       hermetic principles
      </a>
      ; and Spencer himself is eulogised as a reincarnation of the 5th century BCE Greek philosopher
      <a href="/wiki/Heraclitus" title="Heraclitus">
       Heraclitus
      </a>
      ; who is in turn described as being a reincarnation of a still more ancient, Egyptian philosopher. No other writers are similarly referenced or endorsed within the text.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Derivative_works">
       Derivative works
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Derivative works">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Doreen Virtue, PhD wrote a book called
      <i>
       Divine Magic: The Seven Sacred Secrets of Manifestation (A New Interpretation of the Classic Hermetic Manual The Kybalion)
      </i>
      , in 2006, presenting a modern version of
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      with companion "meditative" CD. In her view, one can learn to master one's moods, release negativity, manifest "higher levels of abundance", and attract new opportunities into one's life with the aid of the teachings of
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Another loosely
      <a href="/wiki/Derivative_work" title="Derivative work">
       derivative work
      </a>
      has emerged from an esoteric organization by the name of
      <a href="/wiki/Summum" title="Summum">
       Summum
      </a>
      which, considering
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      to be antiquated and incomplete, rewrote the text to incorporate a "grand principle", their Principle of Creation. Summum describes this as the foundation underlying the existence of
      <a href="/wiki/The_All" title="The All">
       The All
      </a>
      , and the source from which the seven Principles emanate.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">
       Languages
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kybalion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Languages">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The Builders of the Adytum consider
      <i>
       The Kybalion
      </i>
      an essential foundation of their beliefs, and have translated the original text into many
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words">
         <span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (July 2014)">
          which?
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      languages.
      <i>
