      <h2>
       Pay Attention to the center of gravity in your family.
      </h2>
      <p>
       Last night, my wife and I had the opportunity to attend the
       <a href="http://www.screamfree.com/site/PageServer">
        Scream Free Parenting
       </a>
       seminar sponsored by the GSL PA. While Im not a big fan of the name, the actual concepts and ideas presented were thought provoking and good reminders for us all as parents.
      </p>
      <p>
       The speaker, Hal Runkel, was an engaging presenter with plenty of humor and parenting anecdotes woven throughout the presentation, but what stuck with us was a visual imagine he discussed. One of the myths he tried to debunk was the common conception that Your life is over when you become a parent. While it is true that becoming a parent is an incredible, life-changing event, it does not mean that your life is over. Nor should it mean that all our attention is focused on our child(ren). The speaker reminded us to
       <strong>
        pay attention
       </strong>
       to who is in the center of our life- is it our spouse or our children?
      </p>
      <p>
       The modern American conception of parenting often has the children in the middle; they become the center of a family planetary system while the adults spin circles around them to provide them with a better life. You can imagine what this looks like- bending over backwards to ensure the childs happiness, smoothing away any obstacles or difficulties, rescuing them when homework or lunch is forgotten, and doing anything to raise their self-esteem. Weve
       <em>
        all
       </em>
       done this to some degree. When we hand them freedom at 18 or 21 or 25, the results arent so good. Theyve not had sufficient practice making tough choices.
      </p>
      <p>
       A healthier orientation would have the spousal relationship the primary relationship in the family, with the children orbiting around the adults. Ultimately, Mr. Runkel goes on to say, the goal is for the childrens orbits to grow further and further away from the parent until they spin off into their own trajectory, ready to establish their own path in the world. Sounds a lot like our updated mission statement:  responsible citizens in their communities and the world. This also has the pleasant effect of strengthening the spousal relationship so that it is ready for the day when the children have left the nest.
      </p>
      <p>
       Driving home last night, my wife and I had some interesting conversations about where the center of gravity is in our family- even how that changes from time to time. The Scream Free parenting seminar reminded us to pay attention to our relationship first; we need to make certain it is healthy and thriving, since that in turn will lead to a productive family and healthy children.  As the airlines say Put your own oxygen mask on first, then assist the passenger next to you.
      </p>
