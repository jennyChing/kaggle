           <span class="story-subtitle serif fs-1p5 lh-1p5">
            That thing you thought you liked? Its rape culture.
           </span>
           <section>
           </section>
           <p dir="ltr">
            <span class="drop">
             T
            </span>
            here are almost too many to choose from, but here are elevenof the most politically correct moments on college campuses in 2014:
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             1. Princeton University students launched a
            </span>
            microaggression-reporting
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             service.
            </span>
           </h2>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             In December, Princeton students
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/394461/princeton-students-set-microaggression-reporting-service-katherine-timpf" style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             relaunched
            </a>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             Tiger
            </span>
            Microaggressions
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             , a service that takes other students reports of
            </span>
            microaggressions
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             and publishes them on its Facebook page. According to the operators, absolutely anything can qualify as a
            </span>
            microaggression
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             since there are no objective definitions to words and phrases.
            </span>
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            2. College students invented a roofie-detecting nail polish  only to be told that thats actually also rape culture.
           </h2>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             In August, college students
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/386267/feminists-say-roofie-detecting-nail-polish-actually-also-rape-culture-katherine-timpf" style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             invented
            </a>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             a nail polish that changes color if it comes into contact with date-rape drugs  only to incite rage from feminists who insist that anything that might help women protect themselves actually promotes rape culture by acknowledging that we live in a world where rapes happen. (FYI: We do.) Or, as feminist activist Rebecca Nagle eloquently put it: I dont want to f[***]
            </span>
            ing
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             test my drink when Im at the bar. . . .Thats not the world I want to live in. The fact that fear of whether or not someone could be wearing the polish might deter potential rapists from drugging womens drinks (whether or not they themselves were actually testing their f***
            </span>
            ing
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             drinks or not) was not addressed.
            </span>
           </p>
           <h2>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             3. Students hosted an anti-rape-culture rally only to be told thats  yep  actually also rape culture.
            </span>
           </h2>
           <p dir="ltr">
            In October, an Arizona State University rally against rape culture was
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/390523/anti-rape-culture-rally-accused-promoting-rape-culture-katherine-timpf">
             slammed
            </a>
            for promoting rape culture because it encouraged men to respect women  and respect for women should be a given and not have to be encouraged. Come on, you guys!
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            4. A school campaigned against offensive language such as wuss, you guys, and derpbecause it has an oppressive impact on culture.
           </h2>
           <p dir="ltr">
            Oops. I guess I accidentally oppressed some people with the way I ended No.3  at least
            <a href="http://www.campusreform.org/?ID=5481">
             according
            </a>
            to the More Than Words: Inclusive Language Campaign launched at Macalester College this past summer. It included videos featuring student explanationsand posters covering the campus walls that dispensed politically correct instructions  such as telling students to stop using words such as crazy or derp and replacethem with person with a mental health condition or person with a learning or cognitive disability (even though those arguably kind of maybe sound even more offensive.)
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            5. Students opposed a female-to-male transgender candidatefor class diversity officer because hes a white man.
           </h2>
           <p dir="ltr">
            I thought hed do a perfectly fine job, but it just felt inappropriate to have a white man there, the anonymous student behind the so-called Campaign to Abstain at the all-womens Wellesley College
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/390425/students-transgender-woman-cant-be-diversity-officer-because-shes-white-man-now">
             said
            </a>
            .
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            6. A school told its orientation officers not to use the word freshman because it promotes rape.
           </h2>
           <p dir="ltr">
            In November, Elon University
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/392994/university-bans-word-freshman-because-its-sexist-and-promotes-rape-katherine-timpf">
             instructed
            </a>
            its orientation officers to use the term first-year instead of freshman because the term freshman is sexist and actually suggests that women might make good rape victims.
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            7. A liberal group demanded the school teacha mandatory transgender-sensitivityclass to right the wrongs of colonial America.
           </h2>
           <p dir="ltr">
            In June, more than 700 students, professors, and faculty at the University of Minnesota
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/390425/students-transgender-woman-cant-be-diversity-officer-because-shes-white-man-now">
             ordered
            </a>
            the school to admit its just a product of the evil actions of colonial Americans and must fundamentally alter its structure to make it up to marginalized communities  starting with forcing all students to take a transgender-sensitivity class.
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            8. A student newspapers editorial board wrote a whole piece about how racist bras are.
           </h2>
           <p dir="ltr">
            In September, the student editorial board at the University of Oklahoma
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/387639/bras-are-now-racist-because-they-come-nude-katherine-timpf">
             wrote
            </a>
            an article all about how bras are racist because they come in colors named nude and not everyone is that color when theyre nude. They also said that Band Aids were an example of white privilege.
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            9. The War on Tacos.
           </h2>
           <p dir="ltr">
            A sorority at California State University,Fullerton, got in serious
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/387987/sorority-big-trouble-after-taco-tuesday-event-deemed-racist-katherine-timpf">
             trouble
            </a>
            in September for hosting a Taco Tuesday event because some attendees wore culturally insensitive attire (read: sombreros and mustaches.). A similar thing
            <a href="http://www.campusreform.org/?ID=5576">
             happened
            </a>
            in April at Dartmouth University, where backlash forced a fraternity to cancel a fundraiser  yes, fundraiser  for cardiac patients because one student complained that the fiesta theme was offensive. Although the fraternity was careful to warn students to definitely not wear sombreros to the event, they did plan to serve virgin frozen drinks, salsa, guacamole, and  gasp!  burritos, so apparently Mexican food is offensive in itself.
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            10. The War on Coconut Bras.
           </h2>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             In May, the student government at the University of California,Irvine,
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.campusreform.org/?ID=5646" style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             demanded
            </a>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             that the Phi Gamma Delta fraternity be punished because they hosted a fundraiser  yes, again, fundraiser  where students wore grass skirts and coconut bras. Thats apparently racist against Pacific Islanders. A group of students even released a statement that said stuff like tell members of your organization to stop wearing our traditional/cultural attires, they dont know jack s[***] about its cultural significance.
            </span>
           </p>
           <h2 dir="ltr">
            11. Harvard University was about to stop buying water machines from the Israeli company SodaStream because they might be a microaggression.
           </h2>
           <p>
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             The schools dining services also
            </span>
            <a href="http://www.nationalreview.com/article/395027/harvard-stop-buying-sodastream-machines-because-theyre-microaggression-katherine" style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             planned to
            </a>
            remove
            <span style="font-size: 1em; letter-spacing: 0px; line-height: 1.5em;">
             the labels from all of the existing machines just to make sure no student has to see one and be traumatized. But to end the year on a good note: At the request of the university president, the school
             <a href="http://www.thecrimson.com/article/2014/12/18/HUDS-statement-sodastream/">
              is reconsidering
             </a>
             its decision.
            </span>
           </p>
           <p>
