     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Roadrunner United
       </b>
      </i>
      was a project organized by the
      <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">
       heavy metal
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">
       record label
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Roadrunner_Records" title="Roadrunner Records">
       Roadrunner Records
      </a>
      to celebrate its 25th anniversary. It culminated in an album released worldwide on October 11, 2005, entitled
      <i>
       The All-Star Sessions
      </i>
      . Four "team captains" were chosen to lead 57 artists from 45 past and present Roadrunner bands, and produce and oversee the album's 18 tracks:
      <a href="/wiki/Joey_Jordison" title="Joey Jordison">
       Joey Jordison
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Scar_the_Martyr" title="Scar the Martyr">
       Scar the Martyr
      </a>
      , formerly-
      <a href="/wiki/Slipknot_(band)" title="Slipknot (band)">
       Slipknot
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Murderdolls" title="Murderdolls">
       Murderdolls
      </a>
      ;
      <a href="/wiki/Matt_Heafy" title="Matt Heafy">
       Matt Heafy
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Trivium_(band)" title="Trivium (band)">
       Trivium
      </a>
      ;
      <a href="/wiki/Dino_Cazares" title="Dino Cazares">
       Dino Cazares
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Fear_Factory" title="Fear Factory">
       Fear Factory
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Asesino" title="Asesino">
       Asesino
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Divine_Heresy" title="Divine Heresy">
       Divine Heresy
      </a>
      and formerly of
      <a href="/wiki/Brujeria_(band)" title="Brujeria (band)">
       Brujeria
      </a>
      ; and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Flynn" title="Robert Flynn">
       Robert Flynn
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Machine_Head_(band)" title="Machine Head (band)">
       Machine Head
      </a>
      and formerly of
      <a href="/wiki/Vio-lence" title="Vio-lence">
       Vio-lence
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Forbidden_(band)" title="Forbidden (band)">
       Forbidden
      </a>
      . The unprecedented project was the brainchild of Roadrunner UK General Manager Mark Palmer and Roadrunner USA VP of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%26R" title="A&amp;R">
       A&amp;R
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Monte_Conner" title="Monte Conner">
       Monte Conner
      </a>
      . The album project was coordinated by Lora Richardson and was mixed by
      <a href="/wiki/Colin_Richardson" title="Colin Richardson">
       Colin Richardson
      </a>
      and
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Sneap&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andy Sneap (page does not exist)">
       Andy Sneap
      </a>
      .
      <i>
       The All-Star Sessions
      </i>
      spawned one single and music video ("The End"). The DVD included with the CD purchase is a documentary of the "Making Of" the songs. It features the sessions of the four team captains making their songs.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On October 13, 2008, Roadrunner Records announced on its
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MySpace" title="MySpace">
       MySpace
      </a>
      and on its official website that a two-disc DVD of a release party-concert, featuring songs from the album performed live, would be released on December 9, 2008. The DVD features the full concert and a documentary.
     </p>
