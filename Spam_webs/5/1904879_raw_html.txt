        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           Pop songs give us the happy, party side of Christmas. Bing Crosby and Judy Garland give us the nostalgic side. What does classical Christmas music give us? Nostalgia too, at times, and a cosy sense of gathering round the fire. Carols give us those things too, of course. But in classical music they're often accompanied by something else, which often seems to have vanished from the world; a feeling of hope and joy, and a quiet, awestruck awareness of the miraculous. Here are ten pieces that summon up all those feelings.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           <strong>
            JS BACH: CHRISTMAS ORATORIO
           </strong>
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           Some people have the idea Bach was always on his knees when he wrote church music. Not true. Here he stands up and shouts for joy. His Christmas Oratorio, composed in 1734, tells the entire Christmas story from the Birth right through the arrival and adoration of the Magi. This opening movement sets the mood. Its in the celebratory key of D major, with lots of royal trumpets for the Christ child, and the trilling oboes give a foretaste of the pastoral mood that comes later.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" id="player1" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:2H425XFuvuqOWPzJlW4Oue" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </p>

          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            VICTOR HELY-HUTCHINSON: CAROL SYMPHONY
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           The BBC music supremo in the 1940s, Victor Hely-Hutchinson made a point of never buying a car, and sticking to his bicycle. Which might make him seem like Scrooge, but theres nothing Scrooge-like about his music. He had a passion for nursery rhymes and childrens poems, and often set them to music. His best known piece is his Carol Symphony, which gained a whole new generation of fans in the Eighties when it was used as the title theme for a BBC adaptation of John Masefield's The Box of Delights. The first movement of the symphony kicks off in a jolly but not especially Christmassy way. But hang in there. At the 31 second mark, it all becomes clear.
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:4UrJNK7UyUvuS4VQXQriFU" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </h3>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            LISZT: CHRISTMAS-TREE SUITE
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Franz Liszt seems the last person who would write charming Christmas music. Virtuoso fireworks, desperate passion and the smell of hell-fire was more his line. But by 1881 hed turned 70, and become a mild and introspective clergyman. His music had become gentle, as this Christmas-tree Suite shows. In the Autumn he went to stay in a modest hotel in Rome, where his grand-daughter Daniela kept him company. On Christmas day he played the Suite for her and a few guests. This movement pictures the shepherds around the crib, and quotes the carol In Dulci Jubilo.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" id="player2" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:0m08rNfPa9GC1FniKqJFP6" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            TCHAIKOVSKY: WALTZ OF THE FLOWERS FROM THE NUTCRACKER
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           What could be more appropriate for a childs Christmas than a magic kingdom of sweets and presents? Thats the scene for Act 2 of Tchaikovskys last ballet The Nutcracker. The little girl Clara has helped the Nutcracker-shaped soldier defeat the army of wicked mice. He turns into a handsome prince who leads her off to the aforementioned kingdom, with its Sugarplum Fairy, chocolate from Spain, coffee from Arabia and tea from China. Then comes this wonderful Waltz of the Flowers.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" id="player3" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:0CJuLeZpCdqrqoCMXWZw8k" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            J.S. BACH: PASTORALE FROM CHRISTMAS ORATORIO
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Why another piece from Bachs Christmas Oratorio? Because its the greatest Christmas piece ever written, and ten excerpts wouldnt be enough. And also because it has a wonderful example of that quintessential Christmas genre, the pastorale. Shepherds were first on the scene of the miraculous birth of Christ, and the shepherds pipe (evoked and made more refined by oboes) has been associated with Christmas down the ages. The tradition of shepherds piping in the streets of Rome during Epiphany goes on to this day (though these days the shepherds are doctors and civil servants in shepherds dress). Bachs swaying, drowsy pastorale shows the tradition at its peak.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" id="player4" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:27zZEtXJORCDVa7AZZVQY0" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            GERALD FINZI: IN TERRA PAX
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           For an evocation of a nostalgic English Christmas, this piece cant be beaten. Finzi composed it in 1954, when he already knew he was dying of leukaemia. Thirty years previously on a freezing cold Christmas Eve, when he was probably far from sober, Finzi climbed up a church spire on a hill between Cheltenham and Gloucester. The sound of the midnight bells he heard then lingered in his memory, and inspired this piece. The words come from a poem by Robert Bridges, interwoven with St Lukes description of the angels appearance to the shepherds. Listen out for the way bell chimes and The First Nowell seem to mingle in the distance.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" id="player5" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:6lb9JznYxGe1uVxx58yiNb" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            FRANCIS POULENC: QUAM VIDISTIS PASTORES?
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           French composer Francis Poulenc was far from saintly. He liked rude jokes and kept getting crushes on unsuitable young men. But his religious feeling was as deep as any composer of the 20th century. His sacred music has a special tone, intense and aspiring but aware of human frailty. His Four Motets for Christmas are sometimes awestruck, sometimes reverent, sometimes straightforwardly joyous. This one, which asks, What did you see at the crib, Shepherds? has a lovely innocent tone which gives the answer: a miracle.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" id="player6" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:3VpFxEprRDWvMY9ealeloO" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            ARNOLD SCHOENBERG: CHRISTMAS MUSIC
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Could the grim ogre of modern music really have written a nice Christmas piece? And why would a Jewish composer do such a thing anyway? In fact Schoenberg liked to celebrate Christmas with his family, just like any other Viennese father (and he had in fact converted from Judaism to Lutheranism in 1898). He composed some little chamber pieces for the family to play at home, including this exquisite little miniature for two violins, cello, harmonium and piano. The main tune is the lovely German carol Es ist ein Ros ensptrungen, but listen out for Silent Night in the strings.
          </p>
          <p>
           <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="380" id="player7" src="https://embed.spotify.com/?uri=spotify:track:1ljy3MzZ1Uxt7zWIXrErR0" width="300">
           </iframe>
          </p>
          <p>
          </p>
          <h3 class="was-h3">
          </h3>
          <p>
           <strong>
            HECTOR BERLIOZ: SHEPHERDS FAREWELL FROM CHILDHOOD OF CHRIST
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Those pastoral oboes at the beginning drop a broad hint that this piece must be to do with the shepherds visiting the manger. Sure enough, we then hear a chorus of shepherds, bidding farewell to the baby Jesus before the Holy Family set off for Egypt. This lovely little miniature forms part of Berliozs oratorio The Childhood of Christ, but it actually started life as an organ piece written for a friends album at a party in 1850. "I thought it had a certain pastoral, naive myticism about it," the composer said later, "so I decided to put appropriate words to it."
          </p>
          <p>

           <strong>
            BENJAMIN BRITTEN: A CEREMONY OF CAROLS
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           Britten composed two sets of carols, and this second one is much the more popular one. Its partly the startlingly beautiful sound of boys voices combined with harp, partly the cunning way Britten plots the piece to give it a satisfying shape. One of the carols is Balulalow, an old Scottish carol, first published in 1567. "Oh my deir hert, young Jesus sweit, Prepare thy creddil in my hert and never mair from thee depart," says the first verse. The harp introduction, which shifts mysteriously between major and minor, casts a spell across the whole of this tiny piece.

        var readNextCurrentArticleId = "11294202";

        /*
         Read Next Widget
         */
        var readNextWidget = (function(){

            var settings = {
                articleImage: "",
                articleTitle: "",
                articleUrl: "#",
                articleId: "",
                currentArticleId: "",
                articleList: [],
                articleListToSkip: [],
                readNextCookieName: "tmg_read_next",
                openButton: "#readNextOpen a",
                closeButton: "#closeBtn a",
                articleContainer: "#readNextArticleContainer",
                widgetContainer: "#readNextWidget",
                displayThumbnail: false,
                panelOpen: false,
                autoOpen: true
            };

            var renderTemplate = function() {
                var template = '&lt;div id="readNextWidget" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextOpen"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div id="readNextOpenInner"&gt;&lt;a href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Next Story&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="icn"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextArticleContainer" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticle"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
