                 <h2 class="date-header">

                   <div class="post h-entry">
                    <a name="2622629071303034057">
                    </a>
                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title">
                     Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs for the Big Game
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content">
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Are you looking for some fun Game Time Grub? These
                      <b>
                       Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs
                      </b>
                      served with Alexia Roasted Crinkle Cut Fries with Sea Salt that I made as part of a post sponsored for
                      <span style="background-color: white; color: #333333; line-height: 16px;">
                       #CollectiveBias
                      </span>
                      will make your hungry fans happy no matter the outcome of the game.
                      <span style="background-color: white; color: #333333; line-height: 16px;">
                       <span style="font-family: inherit;">
                        #GameTimeGrub
                       </span>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fWddgFaG5U8/VLMK9AGIZtI/AAAAAAAAVTQ/79Qd3I6ySKw/s1600/super-bowl-party-food-beer-bacon-cheddar-dip-cheese-fries%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs served with Alexia Roasted Crinkle Cut Fries make great snacks for your Super Bowl Parties from HungryHappenings.com" border="0" height="640" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fWddgFaG5U8/VLMK9AGIZtI/AAAAAAAAVTQ/79Qd3I6ySKw/s1600/super-bowl-party-food-beer-bacon-cheddar-dip-cheese-fries%2B.jpg" title="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs served with Alexia Roasted Crinkle Cut Fries make great snacks for your Super Bowl Parties from HungryHappenings.com" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Whenever I make food for the sports fans in my life, I can't go wrong if I include bacon, cheese, and beer in the recipe. Last year, I served some
                      <a href="http://www.hungryhappenings.com/2014/01/bacon-cheddar-beer-bread-footballs.html" target="_blank">
                       Bacon Cheddar Beer Bread Footballs
                      </a>
                      , and they were a huge hit, so I thought it would be fun to play around with some other recipes using the same flavor combination and came up with this Beer Bacon Cheese Dip.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Of course, you know I can't just throw a dip in a bowl and serve it. I just can't. I have to make it look festive.
                     </div>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MtJE4Ubd4Qs/VLMK94uPyqI/AAAAAAAAVTY/qgcr5GkoIcw/s1600/beer-bacon-cheese-dip-footballs-served-with-Alexia-fries%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs served with Alexia Roasted Crinkle Cut Fries make great snacks for the big game from HungryHappenings.com" border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-MtJE4Ubd4Qs/VLMK94uPyqI/AAAAAAAAVTY/qgcr5GkoIcw/s1600/beer-bacon-cheese-dip-footballs-served-with-Alexia-fries%2B.jpg" title="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs served with Alexia Roasted Crinkle Cut Fries make great snacks for the big game from HungryHappenings.com"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      I found a really cool serving vessel that has three small football shaped bowls attached together with a goal post in the middle. It is the absolute perfect size for my cheese dip. One batch was divided in thirds. I served one bowl of the plain dip, another was spiced with hot sauce and the final one was spiced with jalapenos. I topped each bowl with crisp bacon and decorated each with slices of white cheddar cheese to resemble the stripes and laces on the footballs.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Of course I needed something to serve with the dip and chose fries, but not just any fries,
                      <a href="http://gametimegrub.net/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                       Alexia Fries
                      </a>
                      . I always have them in my freezer. I love the Sweet Potato Fries, my husband prefers the Crinkle Cuts which tasted great covered in the cheese dip. I baked the fries along with some Alexia Onion Rings, and served them in football shaped bowls too.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-tai9qO7DCc8/VLQ5yi61snI/AAAAAAAAVT0/da4BL3kTnEI/s1600/super-bowl-party-shopping%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="214" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-tai9qO7DCc8/VLQ5yi61snI/AAAAAAAAVT0/da4BL3kTnEI/s1600/super-bowl-party-shopping%2B.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      I found a great selection of frozen
                      <a href="http://www.alexiafoods.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                       Alexia products
                      </a>
                      in the side dish freezer section at Walmart. I had never tried their onion rings before and really enjoyed the crisp exterior and nice fresh onion flavor. The sweet potato fries will always be my favorite, but I enjoyed trying some new varieties.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      To set up my game day buffet was super easy. I simply used a plastic game day tablecloth and football shaped or themed serving dishes. If you have all this stuff on hand, and your friends decide at the last minute to stop by and watch the game, you can make a super impressive looking party buffet in no time.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-RwHDvECdOoY/VLMK-9NmKfI/AAAAAAAAVTg/llfhVnONKO4/s1600/super-bowl-party-snacks-bacon-cheddar-dip-and-fries%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Super Bowl Party Food Ideas - Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs served with Alexia Roasted Crinkle Cut Fries from HungryHappenings.com" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-RwHDvECdOoY/VLMK-9NmKfI/AAAAAAAAVTg/llfhVnONKO4/s1600/super-bowl-party-snacks-bacon-cheddar-dip-and-fries%2B.jpg" title="Super Bowl Party Food Ideas - Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs served with Alexia Roasted Crinkle Cut Fries from HungryHappenings.com"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs
                      </span>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <i>
                       Ingredients:
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     8 ounces uncooked Applewood smoked bacon (or any bacon you like)
                     <br/>
                     butter, if needed (about 1 tablespoon)
                     <br/>
                     3 tablespoons all purpose flour
                     <br/>
                     1 cup Amber Ale (or your choice of beer)
                     <br/>
                     1/4 cup heavy whipping cream
                     <br/>
                     2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
                     <br/>
                     16 ounces sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
                     <br/>
                     pinch of salt
                     <br/>
                     optional: hot sauce, cayenne pepper, jalapenos, Tabasco sauce, black pepper
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                      <i>
                       Instructions:
                      </i>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Vjnf3mGgF_I/VLRHg__sSsI/AAAAAAAAVUE/TEjW4JWC_Jg/s1600/how-to-cook-bacon%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="224" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Vjnf3mGgF_I/VLRHg__sSsI/AAAAAAAAVUE/TEjW4JWC_Jg/s1600/how-to-cook-bacon%2B.jpg" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="text-align: justify;">
                      Chop bacon into small pieces. Cook bacon in two batches in a medium saucepan set over medium heat. Remove bacon and measure the bacon fat. You need 3 tablespoons. If you have too much, remove some, if not enough add butter to make up the difference.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-aYgRojluQg8/VLRHnqY7TUI/AAAAAAAAVUM/XkSpcXIQOoo/s1600/bacon-beer-cheese-dip-recipe%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Recipe from HungryHappenings.com" border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-aYgRojluQg8/VLRHnqY7TUI/AAAAAAAAVUM/XkSpcXIQOoo/s1600/bacon-beer-cheese-dip-recipe%2B.jpg" title="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Recipe from HungryHappenings.com"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Place bacon fat back in saucepan and heat over medium heat. Add flour and stir for one minute, scraping up all the browned bits on the bottom of the pan.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Slowly pour in beer whisking until thickened. Add heavy whipping cream, then mustard and stir to combine.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <i>
                       Reduce the heat to low
                      </i>
                      and add one handful of cheese at a time, stirring until completely melted. This took me about 15 minutes. Don't rush it or your dip can curdle. You want it nice and smooth.
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Season with salt and optionally any hot sauce, or pepper.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-xLevOCucDFI/VLRHwJDB_XI/AAAAAAAAVUU/7CxQxX1KvY4/s1600/football-themed-party-food-beer-cheese-dip%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs Recipe from HungryHappenings.com" border="0" height="390" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-xLevOCucDFI/VLRHwJDB_XI/AAAAAAAAVUU/7CxQxX1KvY4/s1600/football-themed-party-food-beer-cheese-dip%2B.jpg" title="Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs Recipe from HungryHappenings.com" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: justify;">
                      Pour the cheese sauce into a football shaped bowl or in my case, a football dipping dish with three individual plastic football shaped bowls. I love this set of bowls, not only does it look cool, but I was able to fill each bowl with a different flavored beer bacon cheese dip. One was plain, one had hot sauce and another jalapenos.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Finely chop the bacon and sprinkle it over the cheese dip.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Z5EdIglYU-U/VLRH4cEGtEI/AAAAAAAAVUc/d9e4w4UU_8A/s1600/football-party-food-ideas%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img alt="How to make Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs from HungryHappenings.com" border="0" height="260" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Z5EdIglYU-U/VLRH4cEGtEI/AAAAAAAAVUc/d9e4w4UU_8A/s1600/football-party-food-ideas%2B.jpg" title="How to make Beer Bacon Cheese Dip Footballs from HungryHappenings.com" width="640"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Cut white cheddar cheese slices into strips and laces to turn each dip into a football. Set the cheese pieces over the bacon.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      This dip is good hot or cold and can be made up to a few days before your party and simply re-heated when you are ready to serve. If you use the Football Dipping Dish, it is plastic and can't be heated. I serve my dip cold and ...
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2sP3lMFEcbY/VLRJgHOPelI/AAAAAAAAVUo/GafEo-5G1qc/s1600/alexia-fries-crinkle-cut-sweet-potato-onion-rings%2B.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2sP3lMFEcbY/VLRJgHOPelI/AAAAAAAAVUo/GafEo-5G1qc/s1600/alexia-fries-crinkle-cut-sweet-potato-onion-rings%2B.jpg"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      with a variety of Alexia Fries and Onion Rings. We have a small toaster oven, and we filled plates with the baked fries, scooped some of the Beer Bacon Cheese Dip on top and heated them through before digging in.
                     </div>
