     <p>
      <b>
       Alia Baha Ad-Din Touqan
      </b>
      (

      <a href="/wiki/Hussein_of_Jordan" title="Hussein of Jordan">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">
       Background
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alia_al-Hussein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      A daughter of Baha ud-Din Toukan (or Touqan), former Jordanian ambassador to the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Court_of_St._James%27s" title="Court of St. James's">
       Court of St. James's
      </a>
      (United Kingdom),
      <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">
       Italy
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">
       Turkey
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">
       Egypt
      </a>
      , Alia was born in
      <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">
       Cairo
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">
       Egypt
      </a>
      in December 1948.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abit_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-abit-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Her father was a Palestinian from
      <a href="/wiki/Nablus" title="Nablus">
       Nablus
      </a>
      , who had settled in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salt,_Jordan" title="Salt, Jordan">
       Salt
      </a>
      , near
      <a href="/wiki/Amman" title="Amman">
       Amman
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He served King Abdullah I, helped write the Jordanian Constitution, and was Jordan's first Ambassador to
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_United_Nations" title="The United Nations">
       the United Nations
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      She spent most of her childhood years travelling with her parents during her fathers career in Jordans diplomatic corps: she lived in Egypt, Turkey, London, the United States, and
      <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">
       Rome
      </a>
      . She attended Church School in
      <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
       London
      </a>
      with her younger brothers, Alaa and Abdullah. She was educated at the
      <a href="/wiki/John_Felice_Rome_Center" title="John Felice Rome Center">
       Rome Center of Liberal Arts
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Loyola_University_Chicago" title="Loyola University Chicago">
       Loyola University Chicago
      </a>
      . She studied political science with a minor in social psychology, and public relations at
      <a href="/wiki/Hunter_College" title="Hunter College">
       Hunter College
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
       New York
      </a>
      . She was interested in sports and writing, and she wished to be a diplomat. In 1971, she moved to Jordan, where she worked for
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Jordanian" title="Royal Jordanian">
       Royal Jordanian
      </a>
      . She was asked by King Hussein bin Talal to oversee the preparations for the first International Water Skiing Festival held in
      <a href="/wiki/Aqaba" title="Aqaba">
       Aqaba
      </a>
      in September 1972.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Family">
       Family
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alia_al-Hussein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Alia married the King in a private ceremony on 24 December 1972, and was titled
      <b>
       Queen Alia al Hussein
      </b>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">
       Arabic
      </a>
      :
      <span dir="rtl" lang="ar" xml:lang="ar">
        
      </span>
      ).
     </p>
     <p>
      They had two children:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Haya_Bint_Al_Hussein" title="Princess Haya Bint Al Hussein">
        Princess Haya
       </a>
       (born 3 May 1974)
      </li>
      <li>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_Ali_Bin_Al-Hussein" title="Prince Ali Bin Al-Hussein">
        Prince Ali
       </a>
       (born 23 December 1975)
      </li>
     </ul>
     <p>
      They also adopted
      <a href="/wiki/Abir_Muhaisen" title="Abir Muhaisen">
       Abir
      </a>
      , a 5-year-old Palestinian girl whose mother had been killed by a plane crash at a refugee camp near the Amman airport.
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Queen">
       Queen
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alia_al-Hussein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Queen">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3771" data-file-width="5908" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg/220px-King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg/330px-King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg/440px-King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:King_Hussein_and_Queen_Alia_with_the_Fords.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        Queen Alia (left) with her husband following state dinner with
        <a href="/wiki/Betty_Ford" title="Betty Ford">
         Betty
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Ford" title="Gerald Ford">
         Gerald Ford
        </a>
        in March 1976
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Queen Alia founded the Office of the Queen of Jordan and gave it an active and public role. The active role she took in Jordan has been emulated by her successors. She financed social development projects, placing particular emphasis on women and children. She often made surprise visits to hospitals and national institutions, aiming to raise service standards and help people to help themselves. In her drive to ensure that children from impoverished backgrounds received their right to education, she fostered close ties with schools such as the Schneller School for Orphans, which took many of the street children that Queen Alia sent there. Her commitment to improving social services continued throughout her lifetime and was still pursued in her name after her death, when King Hussein ensured the continuation of the many educational scholarships given in her honour.
     </p>
     <p>
      Alia's love of the arts and literature inspired the establishment of libraries throughout the country, including one at the Central Bank of Jordan and another in the King Hussein Medical City. Her interest in the arts led to the founding of the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Haya_Cultural_Centre_for_Children&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Haya Cultural Centre for Children (page does not exist)">
       Haya Cultural Centre for Children
      </a>
      , the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_Folklore_Troupe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National Folklore Troupe (page does not exist)">
       National Folklore Troupe
      </a>
      and the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alia_Art_Gallery&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alia Art Gallery (page does not exist)">
       Alia Art Gallery
      </a>
      . It was also instrumental in conceiving the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jerash_Festival_for_the_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jerash Festival for the Arts (page does not exist)">
       Jerash Festival for the Arts
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1974, Alia called for women to be granted the right to vote and be elected for parliament. On 4 April 1974, a law was promulgated granting women this right; however, the suspension of parliamentary life in Jordan between 1974 and 1989 prevented its implementation.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1976, Alia received the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Decoration_for_Services_to_the_Republic_of_Austria" title="Decoration for Services to the Republic of Austria">
       Great Star of Honour for Services to the Republic of Austria
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Death">
       Death
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alia_al-Hussein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Alia died in a helicopter crash in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amman,_Jordan" title="Amman, Jordan">
       Amman, Jordan
      </a>
      on 9 February 1977.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abit_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-abit-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Amman's principal airport,
      <a href="/wiki/Queen_Alia_International_Airport" title="Queen Alia International Airport">
       Queen Alia International Airport
      </a>
      (AMM), was completed in 1983 and named in her honour. It is located 20 miles south of the city and replaced Amman Marka International Airport (now
      <a href="/wiki/Amman_Civil_Airport" title="Amman Civil Airport">
       Amman Civil Airport
      </a>
      ) as the city's main gateway.
     </p>
