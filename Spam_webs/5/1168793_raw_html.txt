        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="" border="0" src="http://cdn.stumble-upon.com/images/120x20_su_gray.gif"/>
         <br/>
         <a href="http://worththewhisk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Orange-French-Toast.jpg">
          <img alt="Orange French Toast" class="aligncenter wp-image-7063" height="450" src="http://worththewhisk.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Orange-French-Toast.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         Lately, Ive been eating challah on Fridays around sundown. My Jewish readers might think, Such a good girl, going to Shabbat. Catholic-raised me must tell the truth: I volunteer at the UCLA Ronald Reagan Hospital until 5 PM on Fridays. We have a meditation room (not to be confused with a medication room) and Shabbat services, followed by Rabbi Pearl often visiting me with any unused challah. If co-volunteer Marty is working, he gets the bread  he runs marathons and my fat ass does not. But Marty has been vacationing for over a month. So, I snack. Time to figure out something to do with this bread besides wreck dinner.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-3523">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Larry recently came up with a household Top Chef challenge. Each of us over the next few weeks would prepare a fresh idea for Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner. Challah on my mind, here was my breakfast performance,
         <strong>
          Citrus French Toast with Marmalade Drizzle
         </strong>
         , which received a thumbs-up from Judge Larry. I served it with a parfait of farmers market strawberries, Greek yogurt sweetened with a little OJ, granola.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Citrus French Toast with Marmalade Drizzle
         </em>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Juice of one fresh-squeezed orange, divided
         </li>
         <li>
          1/2 cup orange marmalade
         </li>
         <li>
          3 large eggs, room temperature
         </li>
         <li>
          3 thick slices challah, or other egg bread (if bread is super fresh, let slices set out overnight to stale slightly)
         </li>
         <li>
          2 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         In small saucepan, heat half the orange juice with the marmalade until simmering, turn off heat and set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         Slice bread into 1-inch logs, four per slice. In a medium bowl, beat eggs slightly with remaining orange juice.
        </p>
        <p>
         Melt half the butter in large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat, or electric skillet set at 300 degrees F. Dip bread into egg mixture, coating evenly.
        </p>
        <p>
         Place half the bread slices in pan, cook 2 minutes on each side or until golden brown. Remove from pan, repeat with remaining butter and slices. Stack French toast and drizzle with warm marmalade. Serves 2 with six logs each.
        </p>
