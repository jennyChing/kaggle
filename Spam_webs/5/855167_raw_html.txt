     <h2>
      Getting a Degree Without Breaking the Bank
     </h2>
     <p>
      With college costs consistently outpacing the rate of inflation, many students are thinking twice when it comes to going to college. Let's explore the numbers students are up against today and how to make higher education more affordable.
     </p>
     <h3>
      College is Expensive  and We Go Into Debt to Fund It
     </h3>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      $88,000
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Average cost of a four-year degree
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      $23,000
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Average student loan balance in 2012
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      About $1 trillion
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Total outstanding U.S. student loan debt, that's more than the national GDPs of Australia, Saudi Arabia, Taiwan, Poland and hundreds of other countries.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      $85 billion
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Student loans past due
     <br/>
     <strong>
      <em>
       Who's borrowing?
      </em>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     <em>
      Student loan borrowers by age
     </em>
     <br/>
     Under 30 	39.6%
     <br/>
     30-39 	26.9%
     <br/>
     40-49 	14.8%
     <br/>
     50-59 	11.8%
     <br/>
     60 and over 	5.3%
     <br/>
     Unknown	1.7%
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <em>
      Student loan borrowers by balance (37 million borrowers)
     </em>
     <br/>
     $1-$10,000	43.1%
     <br/>
     $10,000-$25,000	29.2%
     <br/>
     $25,000-$50,000	16.5%
     <br/>
     $50,000-$75,000	5.9%
     <br/>
     $75,000-$100,000	2.3%
     <br/>
     $100,000-$150,000	1.9%
     <br/>
     $150,000-$200,000	0.7%
     <br/>
     Greater than $200,000	0.5%
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      1 out of 3
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Graduates who wished they'd pursued more scholarships and financial aid options
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <h2>
      Starting Early
     </h2>
     <br/>
     High-schoolers are stepping it up to save money. They're adding to their already-heavy study load by taking advantage of dual-credit programs, which allow students to receive up to two years of their college for free or at a significantly reduced rate.
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      1.3 million
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Number of high school students who took classes for university credit before completing high school (2010-2011)
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      8 out of 10
     </strong>
     <br/>
     High schools that offer university-level classes
     <br/>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      67%
     </strong>
     <br/>
     How much Portland high school students save per class by taking college courses in high school though Portland State University
     <h3>
      Tips for Saving on College
     </h3>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      <em>
       Stay in state
      </em>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      $27,250
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Annual amount a Michigan resident can save attending school in Ann Arbor vs. a non-Michigan resident
     <br/>
     <strong>
      <em>
       Consider community college
      </em>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     <strong>
      $11,000
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Potential savings by taking general education courses at community college
     <br/>
     <strong>
      <em>
       Stay on track
      </em>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Don't pay for an extra semester, which can average around $10,000
     <br/>
     <strong>
      <em>
       Identify student discounts
      </em>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Many retailers, restaurants and even Amtrak offer student discounts. Apple offers up to $200 off new computers to students.
     <br/>
     <strong>
      <em>
       Textbooks
      </em>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Buy or rent used textbooks and sell last semester's books back; use e-books
     <br/>
     <strong>
      $1,168
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Average amount spent on books and supplies at public four-year colleges
     <br/>
     <strong>
      <em>
       Live cheaper
      </em>
     </strong>
     <br/>
     Become a resident adviser. Many get free room and board, which can save
     <strong>
      $7,500-$9,000
     </strong>
     per year.
