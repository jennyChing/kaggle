         <p>
          Eliaquim Mangala is Manchester City's only injury worry ahead of tomorrow's lunchtime showdown with Tottenham.
         </p>
         <p>
          Mangala,
          <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/eliaquim-mangala">
           a 32million summer signing from Porto
          </a>
          , will miss City's clash with Spurs, but should be fit for their must-win clash with CSKA Moscow on Tuesday in Russia.
         </p>
         <p>
          Pellegrini said: "The position about all of the players is good. We just have a small problem with Mangala. He has a small muscle injury but I think he will be fit for Tuesday [against CSKA Moscow] and able to play without a problem.
         </p>
         <p>
          "All the other players are okay. Of course it's not easy to say which is the best starting line-up for Saturday, with so many different players arriving at different days of the week.
         </p>
         <p>
          There was doubt over whether Yaya Toure would make it back to Manchester in time to be considered for selection against Spurs, but
          <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/yaya-toure">
           the midfielder
          </a>
          arrived back on Thursday morning and is in
          <a href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/manchester-city-fc">
           City's squad
          </a>
          for tomorrow.
         </p>
         <p>
          <figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
           <div class="image-credit-wrapper">
            <span class="image-credit">
             Clive Mason
            </span>
            <img alt="" height="409" src="http://i3.mirror.co.uk/incoming/article4134158.ece/ALTERNATES/s615/Manchester-City-v-Stoke-City-Premier-League.jpg" title="Plans: Pellegrini admits Spurs will be a different prospect this season" width="615"/>
           </div>
           <figcaption>
            Plans: Pellegrini admits Spurs will be a different prospect this season
           </figcaption>
          </figure>
         </p>
         <p>
          Pellegrini said: "Yaya Toure arrived back with Aleksandar Kolarov and Matija Nastasic, others arrived on Monday and Tuesday, so we're going to see who will play on Saturday. But all of them are involved in the squad list for Saturday."
         </p>
         <p>
          City subjected Tottenham to 6-0 and 5-1 defeats in the Premier League last season, but Pellegrini said he feels Spurs will prove tougher opponents under new boss Mauricio Pochettino this time around.
         </p>
         <p>
          "I am sure it will be a different Tottenham," said Pellegrini. "Last season, one of the best games we played was against Tottenham away.
         </p>
         <p>
          "Here [at the Etihad Stadium] we also played well, but we are not expecting we can score five or six goals every time we play Tottenham."
         </p>
