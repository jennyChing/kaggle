        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Galapagos, Espaola  one of the greatest wildlife walks on the planet
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="cat">
          <span class="entry-cat-bg main-color-bg cat126-bg">
           <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/category/americas/">
            Americas
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-cat-bg main-color-bg cat762-bg">

           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="date post-date updated">
          October 14, 2014
         </span>
         <span class="sep">
          -
         </span>
         <span class="vcard author post-author">
          <span class="fn">
           Tom Warburton
          </span>
         </span>
         <span class="sep">
          -
         </span>
         <span class="comments">
          <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/2014/10/14/galapagos-espanola-one-of-the-greatest-wildlife-walks-on-the-planet/#comments">
           1 comment
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
       </header>
       <div class="thumbnail single-post-thumbnail section">
        <img alt="Espanola-7- Ecuador" class="attachment-wt550_300 wp-post-image" height="300" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-7-Ecuador-550x300.jpg" width="550"/>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         This trip to
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/category/ecuador/" target="_blank">
          Ecuador
         </a>
         was incredible each and every day. A huge highlight whilst on the
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/tag/galapagos/" target="_blank">
          the Galapagos
         </a>
         was the short walk at Punta Suarez on the island of Espaola. We arrived offshore on the Sunday morning on
         <a href="http://www.lapintagalapagoscruise.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          La Pinta
         </a>
         and disembarked for the dry landing on a headland. Wed been told to expect something special, and how right they were.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19194" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-6-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-6- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19194 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-6-Ecuador.jpg" title="Blue Footed Boobie (Sula nebouxii excisa)" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Blue Footed Boobie (Sula nebouxii excisa)
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19189" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-1-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-1- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19189 size-full" height="1192" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-1-Ecuador.jpg" title="Galapagos Sea Lion (Zalophus wollebacki)" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Galapagos Sea Lion (Zalophus wollebacki)
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         It has got to be one of the greatest wildlife walks in the world  Galapagos sea lions, marine iguanas, lava lizards, blue footed boobies, Nazca boobies, nesting waved albatrosses, views to die for and all in about a 4 kilometre circular walk. All the animals are so lacking in fear of humans that they completely ignore you  you have to watch your step not to tread on a marine iguana or a sea lion pup. Truly awesome.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19201" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-13-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-13- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19201 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-13-Ecuador.jpg" title="Marine Iguana (Amblyrhychus critatus)  - at sea" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Marine Iguana (Amblyrhychus critatus)  at sea
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19198" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-10-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-10- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19198 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-10-Ecuador.jpg" title="Waved albatross chick (Phoebastria irrorata)" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Waved albatross chick (Phoebastria irrorata)
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Particularly worthy of note are the waved albatrosses. Espaola is the nesting site for almost the entire global population of these majestic birds (apparently a few pairs have been know to nest on Genovesa Island and Isla de La Plata). Espaola is, therefore, pretty much the only place in the world where you can see waved albatrosses nesting. Watching the adults flying by is a truly amazing sight, their 2.2 to 2.5 metre wing span silently gliding them over your head.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19199" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-11-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-11- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19199 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-11-Ecuador.jpg" title="Waved albatross (Phoebastria irrorata)  adult in flight" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Waved albatross (Phoebastria irrorata)  adult in flight
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Espaola is also the home of one of the most successful conservation stories over the past few decades. The population of the Espaola endemic tortoise dropped to 15 individuals  they were rounded up and taken into a captive breeding programme. The island was then cleared of the introduced goats (a great story in its own right), which had wreaked havoc with the endemic vegetation. Then the young tortoises from the captive breeding project were reintroduced to the island. You can read more of the wider Galapagos tortoise story in The Galapagos: A Natural History by Henry Nicholls. Pirates, whalers and even Darwin himself loved the taste of tortoise meat!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19197" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-9-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-9- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19197 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-9-Ecuador.jpg" title="Nazca Boobie (Sula granti)" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Nazca Boobie (Sula granti)
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19200" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-12-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-12- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19200 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-12-Ecuador.jpg" title="Waved albatross (Phoebastria irrorata)  adults on nest" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Waved albatross (Phoebastria irrorata)  adults on nest
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         A note on the tourism pressure on the islands and its management. I was really impressed with the level of knowledge from the La Pinta guides and their commitment to safe guarding this special environment. As I understand it the National Park licences tourist operators and guides. Each visitor site has a limited number of visits, and groups can only visit each site with the trained guides. The tourist operators are on a strict rotation and so the La Pinta only visits each site once a fortnight  so if you have a particular site or island you want to visit you should check the timetables carefully. If you step off the path the guides will be quick to point it out and ask you to get back on. 97% of the land area of Galapagos is National Park! The vast majority of the land area is not visited except if you have a licence to undertake research of the national park. And even those are very limited.
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19190" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-2-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-2- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19190 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-2-Ecuador.jpg" title="Galapagos Sea Lion and pup (Zalophus wollebacki)" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Galapagos Sea Lion and pup (Zalophus wollebacki)
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19202" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-14-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-14- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19202 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-14-Ecuador.jpg" title="Magnificent Frigate Bird (Fregata magnificens)  - female in flight" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Magnificent Frigate Bird (Fregata magnificens)  female in flight
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Although the islands are now well protected  and pirates &amp; whalers (or Darwin) no longer visit the islands to collect tortoises  they are still under pressure and if you do visit please, please respect the rules and regulations  dont be the one to introduce some plant or animal that creates more havoc. And do as the guides ask  its a bit weird being quite forcefully told what to do and what not to do when your on holiday  but please do as they say  its very, very important and crucial to the survival of these remarkable islands.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thanks to excellent
         <a href="http://www.metropolitan-touring.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          Metropolitan Touring
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.lan.com/en_uk/sitio_personas/index.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          LAN
         </a>
         for making this trip possible and making an ex-biologist very happy. Get a hold of them if you wish to have this experience, you will never ever regret it!
        </p>
        <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_19193" style="width: 805px">
         <a href="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-5-Ecuador.jpg">
          <img alt="Espanola-5- Ecuador" class="wp-image-19193 size-full" height="530" src="http://www.baldhiker.com/wp-content/uploads/Espanola-5-Ecuador.jpg" title="Blue Footed Boobie (Sula nebouxii excisa)" width="795"/>
         </a>
         <p class="wp-caption-text">
          Blue Footed Boobie (Sula nebouxii excisa)
         </p>
        </div>
