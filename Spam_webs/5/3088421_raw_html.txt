       <h1 id="printHeader" style="display:none">
        How-To Geek

       <div class="thepost" id="post-114063">
        <div style="max-width:650px;width:100%;margin-bottom:0px;margin-top:-10px;min-height:24px;">
         <div style="clear:both;">

        </div>
        <h2>
         <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/114063/how-to-use-calibre-to-correctly-order-your-ebook-series/" rel="bookmark" title="How To Use Calibre To Correctly Order Your Ebook Series">
          How To Use Calibre To Correctly Order Your Ebook Series
         </a>
        </h2>
        <div class="thecontent">
         <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-15_141543" border="0" height="300" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/650x300x2012-05-15_141543.jpg.pagespeed.ic.XTU3FeBVjz.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="2012-05-15_141543" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Trying to remember, based on the titles alone, what order a series of books goes in can be quite frustrating. Read on as we show you how to annotate and sort your book titles as theyre transferred to your ebook reader for frustration-free reading.
         </p>
         <p>
          Heres a common situation: youve transferred a series of books to your ebook reader and, once on the reader, there is no easy way to tell the books apart. Does
          <em>
           The Mysterious Ranch
          </em>
          come before
          <em>
           The Mysterious Mid-Century Modern
          </em>
          ? You could go through the enormous hassle of renaming every series book you have to include the series and series number in the title, but theres no need to do that. Thanks to a very handy function in the popular ebook management application Calibre, all it takes is a few minutes of tweaking to enjoy automatically renamed and properly numbered books on your ebook reader.
         </p>
         <div id="div-1342-1" style="margin-top:10px;padding-top:10px;border-top:1px solid #CCC;margin-bottom:10px;border-bottom:1px solid #CCC;">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           tmntag.adTag('div-1342-1');
          </script>
         </div>
         <h3>
          What Do I Need?
         </h3>
         <p>
          For this tutorial youll only need your ebook reader and free tools. Heres what were using:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <a href="http://calibre-ebook.com/download">
            Calibre
           </a>
           (a free and open-source ebook manager).
          </li>
          <li>
           A Kindle (this trick works with Nooks and other ebook readers, too).
          </li>
          <li>
           An ebook series.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          If youve never used Calibre before, wed recommend checking out
          <a href="http://www.howtogeek.com/73979/how-to-organize-your-ebook-collection-with-calibre/">
           our guide to organizing your ebook collection with Calibre
          </a>
          to familiarize yourself with the application.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Getting Started
         </h3>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-15_143510" border="0" height="261" src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-15_143510.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="2012-05-15_143510" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The most important thing is that you have the book series in Calibre. For the purposes of this tutorial we created a set of ebook files by a fabricated authorour apologies to those readers who are dying to know what happens in
          <em>
           The Mysterious Mid-Century Modern
          </em>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          The second most important thing is that youve correctly labeled the series in Calibre using the Series meta-data tag. If you dont have it done already, we promise this will be the most labor intensive (and thankfully one-time) part of the tutorial.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-15_144012" border="0" height="134" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-15_144012.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="2012-05-15_144012" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          An easy way to quickly tag all the books in a series is to highlight the books, right click on the highlighted group, and select
          <strong>
           Edit metadata individually
          </strong>
          . In the Edit Metadata menu you can enter the Series name and Number at the top of the screen. If you need help figuring out the order of the books in the series youre editing, we highly recommend you visit the helpful website
          <a href="http://www.fictfact.com/">
           FictFact
          </a>
          there you can browse by author name and book series.
         </p>
         <p>
          Youve got your books? Youve tagged them with the correct Series and number? Now its time to tie it all together.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Setting Up a Calibre Plugboard
         </h3>
         <h3>
          <img alt="2012-05-15_145403" border="0" height="182" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-15_145403.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="2012-05-15_145403" width="650"/>
         </h3>
         <p>
          Calibre has an awesome feature known as a Plugboard. The plugboard exists exclusively to allow you to, on-the-fly, edit ebook metadata during the send-to-device and save-to-disk operations. Thanks to the magic of the plugboard you dont have to do annoying and time consuming things like hand edit book titles in order to insert the series name/number or otherwise resolve formatting and ordering issues on various ebook devices.
         </p>
         <p>
          Currently our series, MysteryHouse, contains 6 books:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           <em>
            The Mysterious House
           </em>
          </li>
          <li>
           <em>
            The Mysterious Palace
           </em>
          </li>
          <li>
           <em>
            The Mysterious Mansion
           </em>
          </li>
          <li>
           <em>
            The Mysterious Bungalow
           </em>
          </li>
          <li>
           <em>
            The Mysterious Mid-Century Modern
           </em>
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          If we were to simply transfer them to our Kindle, there would be no indication which book came first or last in the series. A simple plugboard can solve that problem by, as the books are copied to the Kindle, editing the title/metadata so that we can, at a glance, see which book is which.
         </p>
         <p>
          To create your plugboard, click on
          <strong>
           Preferences &gt; Metadata plugboards
          </strong>
          (located in the Import/Export section). Youll be presented with a blank plugboard, like so:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-15_145555" border="0" height="252" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-15_145555.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="2012-05-15_145555" width="649"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The first order of business is to select the format and device. While you could run it wide-open with any format and any device selected, its wiser to set up specific plugboards for specific devices. Were going to be setting one up for a Kindle 3 (now known as the Kindle Keyboard). For the Format, well select MOBI and for the Device well select Kindle2 (the Kindle 2 and Kindle 3 use the same metadata formatting).
         </p>
         <p>
          Under the
          <strong>
           Source template
          </strong>
          you insert the the naming string youd like to use for the books. Although you can make your own by reading
          <a href="http://manual.calibre-ebook.com/template_lang.html">
           this Calibre manual entry
          </a>
          on the subject, well save you the trouble and share a few basic ones here. Our examples are arranged with the string first and the example output second.
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           {series}{series_index:0&gt;2s| #|  }{title}
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          MysteryHouse #01  The Mysterious House
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           {series}{series_index:0&gt;2s|  |  }{title}
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          MysteryHouse  01  The Mysterious House
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           {series:|| }{series_index:0&gt;2s|[|] }{title}
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          MysteryHouse [01] The Mysterious House
         </p>
         <p>
          Once youve selected the naming string youd like to use, paste the code into the Source template slot and then select Title in the
          <strong>
           Destination field
          </strong>
          <em>
           .
          </em>
          Were using the second one in the list for this tutorial. Click
          <strong>
           Save plugboard
          </strong>
          . The plugboard will appear in the Existing plugboards box like so:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-15_151631" border="0" height="130" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-15_151631.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="2012-05-15_151631" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          If you need to tweak the plugboard in the future, simply select it and click it and the variables for that plugboard will automatically load into the menu for editing.
         </p>
         <p>
          Now that we have the plugboard set up, its time to test it out. Click
          <strong>
           Apply
          </strong>
          in the upper left hand corner to exit the plugboard menu and apply your work. Close the preferences screen and return to the main Calibre menu.
         </p>
         <p>
          Make sure your device is plugged into your computer and then highlight the books in the series you wish to send to your device. Right click and select
          <strong>
           Send to device
          </strong>
          pick the storage option on the device you normally use, in our case main memory.
         </p>
         <p>
          Dismount your device and power it up. If everything went as planned you should see the book series neatly named and organized like so:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="2012-05-15_152517" border="0" height="300" onload="pagespeed.lazyLoadImages.loadIfVisibleAndMaybeBeacon(this);" pagespeed_lazy_src="http://cdn5.howtogeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/2012-05-15_152517.jpg" src="/pagespeed_static/1.JiBnMqyl6S.gif" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="2012-05-15_152517" width="650"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Success! No more wondering whether the
          <em>
           The Mysterious Palace
          </em>
          precedes
          <em>
           The Mysterious Mansion
          </em>
          ! Any time you add more devices to your stable of gizmos, you can hope back into the plugboard menu and create a new plugboard script for the device. Youll never be left trying to remember what order your books go in again.
         </p>
         <hr/>
         Have a sweet Calibre or ebook reader trick to share? Lets hear about it in the comments.
