     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Prelude to Axanar
       </b>
      </i>
      (working title:
      <i>
       Star Trek: Prelude to Axanar
      </i>
      , and long title:
      <i>
       The Four Years War Part III: Prelude to Axanar
      </i>
      )
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-film_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-film-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is a 2014
      <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">
       independent
      </a>
      American
      <a href="/wiki/Fan_film" title="Fan film">
       fan made
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">
       short film
      </a>
      , directed by
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Gossett" title="Christian Gossett">
       Christian Gossett
      </a>
      and written by
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alec_Peters_(actor)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alec Peters (actor) (page does not exist)">
       Alec Peters
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Balder_.26_Dash_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Balder_.26_Dash-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Science_Fiction_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Science_Fiction-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Crowdfunding" title="Crowdfunding">
       Funded
      </a>
      through
      <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">
       Kickstarter
      </a>
      , production sought $10,000 in funding, but in gaining $101,000, received more than ten times the amount originally sought.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It had its public debut June 26, 2014 at the
      <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con_International" title="San Diego Comic-Con International">
       San Diego Comic-Con
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wired_1_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Wired_1-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Set in the
      <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">
       Star Trek
      </a>
      universe, the film stars
      <a href="/wiki/Kate_Vernon" title="Kate Vernon">
       Kate Vernon
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tony_Todd" title="Tony Todd">
       Tony Todd
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hatch_(actor)" title="Richard Hatch (actor)">
       Richard Hatch
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Gary_Graham" title="Gary Graham">
       Gary Graham
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/J._G._Hertzler" title="J. G. Hertzler">
       J. G. Hertzler
      </a>
      , in a documentary-style film recounting the events surrounding the Battle of Axanar, a major clash between the
      <a href="/wiki/United_Federation_of_Planets" title="United Federation of Planets">
       Federation
      </a>
      and the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Klingons" title="Klingons">
       Klingons
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Inquisitr_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Inquisitr-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>

      </sup>

        <span>
         [

        9

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">
       Production
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Prelude_to_Axanar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Alec Peters began work on the Axanar series in 2010.
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paramount_Studios" title="Paramount Studios">
       Paramount Studios
      </a>
      , who owns the Star Trek franchise, traditionally allows fan-made projects to move forward just "as long as they agree not to sell anythingincluding tickets, merchandise, or copies of the finished film or series." These limitations made financing a quality fan film difficult, so Peters turned to Kickstarter and a fan funding campaign which eventually exceeded his initial funding goal of ten thousand dollars more than ten times over by raising $101,000.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wired_1_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Wired_1-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_Liberty_Voice_1_10-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian_Liberty_Voice_1-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Kickstarter campaign raised over this amount by March 31, 2014,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and the film itself was shot in two days.

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
       Plot
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Prelude_to_Axanar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The film is presented as an episode of a
      <a href="/wiki/United_Federation_of_Planets" title="United Federation of Planets">
       Federation
      </a>
      documentary pertaining to the Four Years War with the
      <a href="/wiki/Klingon" title="Klingon">
       Klingon Empire
      </a>
      , narrated by noted historian John Gill (who appeared in "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patterns_of_Force_(Star_Trek)" title="Patterns of Force (Star Trek)">
       Patterns of Force
      </a>
      ") and featuring interviews of actual participants on both sides. It begins on
      <a href="/wiki/Stardate" title="Stardate">
       Stardate
      </a>
      2241.03, two decades before
      <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_The_Original_Series" title="Star Trek: The Original Series">
       the original series
      </a>
      , with the war's opening battle at Arcanis IV, a prosperous Federation colony along the Klingon border. The Klingons, who did not consider the Federation to be a worthy adversary, maintained the initiative for the first six months of the war, with a number of victories under the leadership of their supreme commander, Kharn. The
      <a href="/wiki/Vulcan_(Star_Trek)" title="Vulcan (Star Trek)">
       Vulcan
      </a>
      diplomatic delegation under Ambassador
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_recurring_characters_in_Star_Trek:_Enterprise#Soval" title="List of minor recurring characters in Star Trek: Enterprise">
       Soval
      </a>
      (who appeared in
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek:_Enterprise" title="Star Trek: Enterprise">
        Enterprise
       </a>
      </i>
      ), overseeing negotiations with the Klingons, are left with little room to maneuver.
     </p>
     <p>
      In response to the losses suffered in the war, Starfleet appoints a new Commander-in-Chief, Admiral Marcus Ramirez, who pledges in a fleet-wide broadcast to defend "the dream of the Federation" against the Klingons' commitment to its destruction. Ramirez oversees the creation of the
      <i>
       Ares
      </i>
      -class cruisers, Starfleet's first warships, to counter the Klingons' mainstay, the D6 battlecruiser. The introduction of the
      <i>
       Ares
      </i>
      turns the tide against the Klingons, who begin to give Starfleet its due as a worthy opponent, and particularly take notice of Garth of Izar (who appeared in "
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whom_Gods_Destroy_(Star_Trek)" title="Whom Gods Destroy (Star Trek)">
       Whom Gods Destroy
      </a>
      "), the captain of the prototype U.S.S.
      <i>
       Ares
      </i>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      To counter the
      <i>
       Ares
      </i>
      cruisers, the Klingons order the construction of a newer and more advanced battlecruiser, the
      <a href="/wiki/Klingon_starships#D7-class" title="Klingon starships">
       D7
      </a>
      , that would restore the Klingons' technical and military advantage. In response, Starfleet begins developing their own next-generation heavy cruiser, the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Starfleet_starships_ordered_by_class#Constitution_class" title="List of Starfleet starships ordered by class">
       <i>
        Constitution
       </i>
       -class
      </a>
      , but construction falls behind schedule. To gain more time to finish their new heavy cruiser, Starfleet approves a plan proposed by Garth to fight the Klingons at Axanar, the planet where Kharn's spies have reported the
      <i>
       Constitution
      </i>
      prototypes (revealed to be the
      <i>
       Constitution
      </i>
      and the
      <a href="/wiki/USS_Enterprise_(NCC-1701)" title="USS Enterprise (NCC-1701)">
       <i>
        Enterprise
       </i>
      </a>
      ) are being built. The film ends on Stardate 2245.1, just before the battle at Axanar, when the first three D7s enter the war.
     </p>
