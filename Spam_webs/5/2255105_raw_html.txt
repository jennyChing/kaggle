                    <meta content="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2w3xUATcUBA/UFS2N-TuacI/AAAAAAAAGbk/i943zh42Q4c/s72-c/applecake+019-1.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Apple Bourbon Bundt Cake
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-6524612927947294757" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2w3xUATcUBA/UFS2N-TuacI/AAAAAAAAGbk/i943zh42Q4c/s1600/applecake+019-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-2w3xUATcUBA/UFS2N-TuacI/AAAAAAAAGbk/i943zh42Q4c/s1600/applecake+019-1.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      A last minute search came up with an
                      <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/11/23/dining/apple-bourbon-bundt-cake-recipe.html?_r=0&amp;pagewanted=print">
                       Apple Bourbon Bundt Cake
                      </a>
                      by Melissa Clark published by The New York Times in 2011. I wished to change things around a tad from my
                      <a href="http://foodwanderings.blogspot.com/2010/08/apple-orange-and-lemon-chiffon-cake.html">
                       favorite apple cake
                      </a>
                      I bake every year. For 5773 I upped the stakes and went all boozy and I like it!
                     </div>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-tzCLW0fCUOw/UFP8xBa57UI/AAAAAAAAGX0/2duJWqS5XUU/s1600/hatchpep+111-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-tzCLW0fCUOw/UFP8xBa57UI/AAAAAAAAGX0/2duJWqS5XUU/s1600/hatchpep+111-1.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      The original cake is more of a spice cake with candied ginger, nutmeg and a hefty dose of cinnamon and vanilla extract. I adjusted it to my sensibilities and it came out great. I think either way it is a spectacular cake and I am so happy I came across this version to change around the Rosh HaShana antics.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-hp5h-xsxNgM/UFQAq4JItrI/AAAAAAAAGY8/FoxUSdbo9EM/s1600/applecake+024-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-hp5h-xsxNgM/UFQAq4JItrI/AAAAAAAAGY8/FoxUSdbo9EM/s1600/applecake+024-1.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     I did not grate the apples and was tempted to preheat the oven at my comfort zone to 350F. I was worried I wouldn't get that puff effect of the cake rise at 325F, but the batter was different from my
                     <a href="http://foodwanderings.blogspot.com/2010/08/apple-orange-and-lemon-chiffon-cake.html">
                      apple chiffon cake
                     </a>
                     so I just went on a limb being curious of the results. The cake as you can tell does not suffer from lack of height. I could not resist it and dug into the apple bourbon cake and it is absolutely divine.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-JSDI0lTPVPc/UFS3r6zzRNI/AAAAAAAAGbs/J4LGxyTrG4E/s1600/applecake+001-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-JSDI0lTPVPc/UFS3r6zzRNI/AAAAAAAAGbs/J4LGxyTrG4E/s1600/applecake+001-1.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     Wishing all of you a healthy 5773, as I said on social media, all the rest then will fall into place! Love you all and can't wait to kick start the new year with our continued friendship and support.
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-p8swyQrf9CI/UFP6MvAUOkI/AAAAAAAAGWs/gycUHhbKwT0/s1600/hatchpep+117-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-p8swyQrf9CI/UFP6MvAUOkI/AAAAAAAAGWs/gycUHhbKwT0/s1600/hatchpep+117-1.jpg"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: right;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <i>
                      Note for the kosher crowd:
                     </i>
                     Substitute butter with margarine and use soy based yogurt instead of the sour cream. You can also bake the cake as is and have a slice for breakfast or along with your afternoon tea.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Apple Bourbon Bundt Cake
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      <span style="font-size: x-small;">
                       10 to 12 servings
                      </span>
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Adapted from A New York Times
                      <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2011/11/23/dining/apple-bourbon-bundt-cake-recipe.html">
                       recipe
                      </a>
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Ingredients
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     2 sticks unsalted butter, at room temperature, plus more to grease pan
                     <br/>
                     4 large eggs, at room temperature
                     <br/>
                     1 cup sour cream
                     <br/>
                     2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour plus more to dust the pan
                     <br/>
                     2 tablespoons bourbon
                     <br/>
                     1 1/2 cup Sugar in the Raw
                     <br/>
                     2 teaspoons baking powder
                     <br/>
                     1 teaspoon baking soda
                     <br/>
                     1/2 teaspoons cinnamon
                     <br/>
                     1/4 teaspoon salt
                     <br/>
                     1-2 teaspoons vanilla extract
                     <br/>
                     Zest from 1 medium/large lemon
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     2 medium/large Granny Smith apples peeled, cored, and finely diced
                     <br/>
                     1/4 cup Sugar in the Raw
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Bourbon Syrup
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     <i>
                      Ingredients
                     </i>
                     <br/>
                     1/2 cup Sugar in the Raw
                     <br/>
                     Juice of 1/2 lemon
                     <br/>
                     1/2 cup bourbon
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <b>
                     </b>
                     <br/>
                     Heat the oven to 325 degrees. Grease and flour a bundt pan. Dust excess flour off the pan by flipping it up side down and tapping the bottom.
                     <br/>
                     In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the 
paddle attachment, beat together the 1 1/2cups sugar and butter on
 medium-high speed, until light and fluffy, about 5 minutes. Beat in the
 eggs, 1 at a time, until incorporated.
                     <br/>
                     In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour with the baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon and salt. In
 a separate bowl, whisk together the sour cream and vanilla. Pour in the
 2 tablespoons of bourbon and whisk until 
smooth. Stir in zest.
                     <br/>
                     With the mixer on medium speed, add the dry mixture
 and sour cream mixture to the wet mixture in three additions, 
alternating between the two. Add a 1/4 cup sugar to apples, mix and fold the apples into the batter. 
Scrape the batter into the prepared pan. Bake until the cake is golden 
brown and a skewer inserted into the cake comes out dry, about 1 hour.
                     <br/>
                     While the cake cools in the bundt pan make the bourbon syrup. Combine the 1/2 cup 
granulated sugar and 1/2 cup bourbon in a small saucepan. Over low heat,
 gently stir until the sugar dissolves. Stir in the lemon juice and take
 off the heat.
                     <br/>
                     While the cake cools, make 15-20 holes on top with a skewer and pour half the bourbon-syrup on the still-warm 
cake. When the cake is fully cool run a knife around the edges of the pan, flip it and pour the rest of the bourbon syrup on the top side and serve.
