         <section class="article-body-wrap">
          <div class="body-text clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Im not much of a mix person, but I am an experimenting sort. Sometimes I just cant resist tinkering with box gluten-free flour blends and baking mixes. I havent tried them all, of course, but from what I have tried, I think
            <a href="http://www.kingarthurflour.com/glutenfree/" target="_blank">
             King Arthur Flour gluten-free products
            </a>
            perform the best. Thats just me, and I could be wrong. I definitely need to try more mixes, so that I can share my results with you, this I know. (No, this is not a paid ad for KAF, nor is it a product review. Just some thoughts on store-bought flours/blends from me and a recipe.)
           </p>
           <p>
            But, there are several reasons I really do like KAF products:
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             The Multipurpose GF Flour doesnt contain gums, which, if youve been here for a while, you know that is how I prefer to bake.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <ul>
            <li>
             The Multipurpose GF Flour behaves very similarly to my own
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.glutenfreegigi.com/gigis-everyday-gluten-free-flour-blend-gum-free/" target="_blank" title="Gigis Everyday Gluten-Free Flour Blend (Gum-Free)">
               Everyday Gluten-Free Flour Blend
              </a>
             </strong>
             , so its easy to use in recipes Ive developed for you here on the site in a 1:1 ratio if youd rather use a mix and not make your own blend.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <ul>
            <li>
             The GF Baking Mix, while it does contain
             <strong>
              <a href="http://www.glutenfreegigi.com/facts-about-gums-in-gluten-free-baking/" target="_blank" title="Facts about Gums in Gluten Free Baking">
               xanthan gum
              </a>
             </strong>
             , behaves well in recipes that call for a bisquick type blend without that gross bisquick taste.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <ul>
            <li>
             These products are produced in a dedicated GF facility. This is always a bonus. While it doesnt remove ALL the possibilities of cross-contamination, it certainly lowers the risk dramatically.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <ul>
            <li>
             In general, these products behave very well in all the baking applications where Ive tested them.
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            Thats my take on KAF products for now. How about you? Have you tried them? Love them? Hate them? Have a better brand that you use? Leave a comment after the recipe and let me know!
           </p>
           <p>
            Now, lets have cookies!
           </p>
           <p>
            I was experimenting with making a quick mix cookie. Of course, this could have been any type cookie, but if you say cookie, I say chocolate chip. And Little Chef says, chocolate chunk. In this recipe, she clearly won at our house, but you could use your favorite chocolate chip  just be sure its safe for you. I opt for a gluten-free vegan product that is not produced in a shared facility with peanuts/tree nuts since I have all these additional food allergies in addition to celiac disease. But you go with what works for you.
           </p>
           <p>
            As far as taste and texture, I am just going to share my recipe note with you. I write these to myself when Im developing/testing:
           </p>
           <p>
            <em>
             This makes a dense, sturdy chocolate chunk cookie.
            </em>
           </p>
           <p>
            That sums it up. These taste wonderful, but are more akin to a shortbread cookie texture than a traditional chocolate chip cookie (you know, chewy, ooey, gooey), so if thats what youre looking for, make these
            <a href="http://www.glutenfreegigi.com/the-perfect-gluten-free-chocolate-chip-cookie/" target="_blank" title="The Perfect Gluten Free Chocolate Chip Cookie">
             <strong>
              Perfect Gluten Free Chocolate Chip Cookies
             </strong>
            </a>
            . And if youre into the peanut butter/chocolate flavor combo, dont miss my
            <strong>
             <a href="http://www.glutenfreegigi.com/thick-chewy-bakery-style-sunbutter-chocolate-chip-cookies/" target="_blank" title="Thick &amp; Chewy Bakery Style Sunbutter Chocolate Chip Cookies">
              Thick &amp; Chewy Bakery Style Sunbutter Chocolate Chip Cookies
             </a>
            </strong>
            .
           </p>
           <p>
            BUT if youre up for a thick, non-chewy cookie (and some of you prefer this type to the chewy ones, I know because Mr. Dreamy told me its true) this may be your cookie! They are terrific with a glass of cold milk (of any variety you choose).
           </p>
           <p>
            <a href="http://3wnows1tc89q3u20hj3p1h8f18wc.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Chocolate-Chunk-Cookies-KA-Quick-Mix-Bite-Out_.jpg">
             <img alt="Gluten Free Quick Mix Chocolate Chunk Cookies" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-11090" height="300" src="http://3wnows1tc89q3u20hj3p1h8f18wc.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Chocolate-Chunk-Cookies-KA-Quick-Mix-Bite-Out_-300x300.jpg" width="300"/>
            </a>
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: center;">
            Gluten Free Quick Mix Chocolate Chunk Cookies
           </p>
           <p style="text-align: left;">
            I give the flour measurement by weight, in grams. If youre curious about baking by weight, read my thoughts on
            <a href="http://www.glutenfreegigi.com/gluten-free-baking-weight/" target="_blank">
             <strong>
              Gluten Free Baking by Weight
             </strong>
            </a>
            . (If youre measuring by volume with cups, use about 2 cups of the baking mix in this recipe.)
           </p>
