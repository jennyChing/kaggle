           <h2 class="title">
            <a href="http://blog.lifeextension.com/2013/01/truth-about-gluten-sensitivities.html">
             The Truth about Gluten Sensitivities
            </a>
           </h2>
           <div class="post-header-line-1">
           </div>
           <div class="entry">
            <p>
             <meta content=" Maylin Rodriguez-Paez, RN    For the last couple of weeks, Ive tried to completely eliminate wheat..." name="description"/>
            </p>

            <h2>
             Maylin Rodriguez-Paez, RN
            </h2>
            <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
             <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-45AFJIbWJ6k/UOHrYXrdXAI/AAAAAAAABSs/BR_AI4T1Vko/s1600/606.03IO+-+The+Truth+about+Gluten+Sensitivities.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
              <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-45AFJIbWJ6k/UOHrYXrdXAI/AAAAAAAABSs/BR_AI4T1Vko/s320/606.03IO+-+The+Truth+about+Gluten+Sensitivities.jpg" style="border: none;" width="196"/>
             </a>
            </div>
            For the last couple of weeks, Ive tried to completely eliminate
            <a href="http://lef.org/magazine/mag2011/oct2011_Wheat-The-Unhealthy-Whole-Grain_01.htm" target="_blank">
             wheat
            </a>
            from my diet. And I wont lie, it hasnt been easy
            <b>
             at all
             <i>
              .
             </i>
            </b>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            I never thought I would have a difficult time or even miss wheat at all. But now I truly understand the power that it can have on both the mind AND the body.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Why am I doing this? Im trying to rule out a gluten sensitivity, which is an issue thats becoming more common today. And for those of you who are in doubt 
            <i>
             it definitely
             <b>
              is
             </b>
             a real issue
            </i>
            .
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Gluten sensitivities can cause uncomfortable symptoms, and can even make some of us feel downright
            <i>
             miserable
            </i>
            .
            <br/>
            <br/>
            But before you give up wheat or other gluten containing foods for good, do a little investigative work. Why? Because giving it up isnt necessarily right for
            <i>
             <b>
              everybody
             </b>
            </i>
            .
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <h3>
             What is a Gluten Sensitivity?
            </h3>
            Gluten is a protein found in wheat and other grains such as rye, barley, and spelt. Its also an additive used in many processed foods.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            A gluten sensitivity is believed to be an immune related-reaction.  One suspected culprit is
            <i>
             gliadin
            </i>
            ,
            <sup>
             1
            </sup>
            which is a component of gluten.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Normally your immune system produces antibodies to fight viruses or bacteria, but in a gluten-sensitivity reaction, antibodies may be produced against gluten.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <h3>
             Should We All Get On the Anti-Gluten Bandwagon?
            </h3>
            Does everyone have to give up gluten? No. Simply put, most people dont have gluten sensitivities. However, if youre suffering from
            <a href="http://blog.lef.org/2012/02/food-allergies-mystery-symptoms.html" target="_blank">
             strange symptoms
            </a>
            with an unknown cause, you may want to give it a shot.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Some of the symptoms frequently reported and linked to gluten sensitivities include the following: abdominal pain, diarrhea, constipation, bloating, fogginess, headaches, tiredness, rashes, joint and muscle pain, leg numbness, and depression.
            <sup>
             1
            </sup>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            If this sounds like you, please read on!
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <h3>
             Removing Gluten May Help You Feel Better
            </h3>
            What are the benefits of going gluten-free? For one, you might feel healthier 
            <i>
             if you have a real sensitivity
            </i>
            .
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Some of the conditions shown to improve include IBS, autism, dermatitis,
            <sup>
             2
            </sup>
            and headaches.
            <sup>
             3
            </sup>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Not only that but, research shows that certain diseases are associated with
            <i>
             anti-gliadin
            </i>
            antibodies, showing there may be a link between them and gluten sensitivities. This doesnt mean that gluten causes these diseases, but only that it warrants further investigation.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Here are some diseases that have been associated with
            <i>
             anti-gliadin
            </i>
            antibodies:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Juvenile rheumatoid arthritis
              <sup>
               4
              </sup>
             </li>
             <li>
              Psoriasis
              <sup>
               4
              </sup>
             </li>
             <li>
              Autoimmune thyroid disease
              <sup>
               4
              </sup>
             </li>
             <li>
              Liver disorders
              <sup>
               4
              </sup>
             </li>
             <li>
              Bipolar disorder
              <sup>
               5
              </sup>
             </li>
             <li>
              Schizophrenia
              <sup>
               5
              </sup>
             </li>
             <li>
              Multiple sclerosis
              <sup>
               6
              </sup>
             </li>
            </ul>
            How does this apply to you? If you have any of these conditions, perhaps you should find out if youre gluten sensitive. After all, it cant really hurt to experiment with your diet a bit.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <h3>
             Testing for Gluten Sensitivities
            </h3>
            Theres no standard way to diagnose a gluten sensitivity, but one common approach is to perform a
            <a href="http://www.lef.org/magazine/mag2010/sep2010_Whats-Really-Making-You-Sick_01.htm" target="_blank">
             food sensitivity test
            </a>
            . This screens a sample of blood for IgG antibodies, which may be produced in response to gluten.
            <sup>
             1
            </sup>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Another way to test is to eliminate wheat and other gluten-containing foods from your diet for at least two weeks. This is probably the best way, since certain cases of gluten sensitivities are idiopathic, meaning the true cause cant be determined.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            After your trial, evaluate your health. If you feel better then you may want to say goodbye to gluten, or if you dont want to go that drastic, eat it sparingly.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <h3>
             A Life Without Gluten 
            </h3>
            A life without gluten isnt that bad. It may be hard, but its doable.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Many people object to this diet because it seems very limiting. However, there are plenty of things that
            <b>
             can
            </b>
            be eaten. You just have to be a little creative.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Here are some gluten-free foods that are worth a shot:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Almond crackers
             </li>
             <li>
              Flax crackers
             </li>
             <li>
              Millet bread
             </li>
             <li>
              Buckwheat pancakes (No wheat here! Buckwheat is actually a fruit)
             </li>
             <li>
              Rice flour muffins - for the occasional indulgence ;)
             </li>
             <li>
              Mung bean noodles
             </li>
            </ul>
            Dont forget  there are a variety of gluten-free products in the market too! Be on the lookout for them.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Do you have gluten sensitivity yourself? How has your life changed since removing it from your diet? Please share your experiences in the comments!
