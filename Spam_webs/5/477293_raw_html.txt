       <header class="entry-header">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Froothie Optimum 9400 Blender Review
        </h1>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/spacekadet/14966491335" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/spacekadet/14966491335', '']);" title="Froothie Optimum 9400 by Monica Shaw, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Froothie Optimum 9400" src="https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3917/14966491335_1f99ac1ac5_c.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         As many of you know, Ive been a devoted user of the Vitamix for years, so when the opportunity came along to try out the
         <a href="http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=67&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=67&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531', 'Froothie Optimum 9400 Blender']);" target="_blank">
          Froothie Optimum 9400 Blender
         </a>
         I felt a little bit like I was cheating on an old and very dear friend. But I decided to have an open mind and see what this new kid on the block could do. A few weeks ago I stashed away my Vitamix and replaced it with the Froothie to see how it worked for my daily blending needs.
        </p>
        <p>
         Daily? Thats right. Im a pretty heavy blender user  I make smoothies for breakfast every day and often (at least once per week) use the blender for other tasks such as
         <a href="http://smarterfitter.com/2012/09/04/avocado-tortilla-soup-in-the-vitamix/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://smarterfitter.com/2012/09/04/avocado-tortilla-soup-in-the-vitamix/', 'pureed soups']);" title="Avocado Tortilla Soup in the Vitamix">
          pureed soups
         </a>
         , custard for
         <a href="http://smarterfitter.com/2014/05/28/coffee-ice-cream/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://smarterfitter.com/2014/05/28/coffee-ice-cream/', 'ice cream']);" title="Coffee Ice Cream">
          ice cream
         </a>
         , almond milk,
         <a href="http://smarterfitter.com/2009/09/22/easy-delicious-cashew-gravy/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://smarterfitter.com/2009/09/22/easy-delicious-cashew-gravy/', 'cashew gravy']);" title="Easy Delicious Cashew Gravy">
          cashew gravy
         </a>
         and on special occasions,
         <a href="http://smarterfitter.com/2014/02/16/raw-raspberry-cheesecake/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article-int', 'http://smarterfitter.com/2014/02/16/raw-raspberry-cheesecake/', 'raw raspberry cheesecake']);" title="Raw Raspberry Cheesecake">
          raw raspberry cheesecake
         </a>
         . I need a blender that can withstand my serious usage and heavy pureeing needs.
        </p>
        <p>
         Over the past three weeks Ive been using the Froothie for those daily smoothies, and have also used it to make oat milk and banana ice cream. I have yet to try more interesting tasks such as custard or hot soups, but those items will certainly have their chance because I dont intend to stop using the Froothie anytime soon. So far its been a stellar performer and has performed at least as well as the Vitamix. If it excels in one area Id say its noise: the Froothie is noticeably quieter than my Vitamix. I also like the clear jug.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/spacekadet/14963388761" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/spacekadet/14963388761', '']);" title="Froothie vs Vitamix by Monica Shaw, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Froothie vs Vitamix" src="https://farm6.staticflickr.com/5579/14963388761_66b7ef7f85_z.jpg"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Perhaps one of the reasons I havent missed my Vitamix is because the Froothie is almost identical in terms of looks and function. Under the hood sees a few benefits, too. The Optimum 9400 has a 2,238 Watt motor (compared to Vitamixs 1,492), a 44,000 rpm speed (vs 37,000), a 6 blade assembly (vs 4) and one jug that works for both wet and dry blending (all of my flour- and spice-grinding dreams come true). At 329, its also cheaper than Vitamix which starts at about 465.
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes, its still expensive, but having spent this kind of money on a high power blender myself, I can tell you that Ive never regretted my purchase. A good blender is enabling. You could use a cheaper blender but you wouldnt get the same silky smooth results, and you wouldnt be able to blitz up great stuff like kale, broccoli and beetroot into perfectly smooth smoothies. At best, you might get smoothie salsa! A good blender also enables you to do fun things like make your own nut milk, nut butters, flour, cashew cheese and other things that are only achievable with a good motor and some strong blades.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/spacekadet/14779891318" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/spacekadet/14779891318', '']);" title="Hemp Protein Green Smoothie by Monica Shaw, on Flickr">
          <img alt="Hemp Protein Green Smoothie" height="333" src="https://farm4.staticflickr.com/3894/14779891318_e4c4daebe2.jpg" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         That word enabling brings me to another surprise plus Ive found with the Optimum 9400. The blender comes with a quaint little booklet called
         <a href="http://thesunnyrawkitchen.blogspot.co.uk/2011/09/presenting-deliciously-raw-quick-n-easy.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://thesunnyrawkitchen.blogspot.co.uk/2011/09/presenting-deliciously-raw-quick-n-easy.html', 'Deliciously Raw']);" target="_blank">
          Deliciously Raw
         </a>
         full of recipes for raw smoothies, soups, dressings, nut cheeses and desserts. The book is written by Carmella Soleil of the
         <a href="http://carmellassunnyrawkitchen.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://carmellassunnyrawkitchen.com/', 'Sunny Raw Kitchen blog']);" target="_blank">
          Sunny Raw Kitchen blog
         </a>
         and the stories shes included with her recipes are down to earth and really inspiring. I especially liked her discovery story of raw nut cheese, having been unimpressed early on, but then having her interest rekindled by Chad Sarnos
         <a href="http://chadsarno.com/say-not-cheese/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://chadsarno.com/say-not-cheese/', 'Cashew Cheese Au Poivre']);" target="_blank">
          Cashew Cheese Au Poivre
         </a>
         . I just love origin stories like these and it really helps put a commercial product like a blender in perspective. It also
         <i>
          really
         </i>
         makes me want to have a go at making raw cheeses  particularly the nacho cheeze to have with raw tostadas (with raw corn chips made in the food dehydrator).
        </p>
        <p>
         All this raw healthy vegan stuff certainly is hugely appliance heavy, and for that Im often in two minds about whether some of this stuff is the way forward. Regardless, I know from experience that smoothies and juices have had a positive impact on my own health and wellbeing, so Im willing to let these gadgets into my life. And like most gadgets, its better to get something of quality, that works well, and will last. So far the
         <a href="http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=67&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531%20%20%20%20%20%20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=67&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531%20%20%20%20%20%20', 'Optimum 9400']);" target="_blank">
          Optimum 9400
         </a>
         is ticking all of those boxes.
        </p>
        <p>
         Speaking of appliances, Im looking forward to trying out the
         <a href="http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=73&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=73&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531', 'Froothie Slow Juicer']);" target="_blank">
          Froothie Slow Juicer
         </a>
         next, particularly for its bonus abilities as a nut butter machine and a tofu maker. Stay tuned!
        </p>
        <p>
         <i>
          You can test-drive any of Froothies machines with a 30-day money-back guarantee. Check out the
          <a href="http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=66&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=66&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531', 'Froothie website']);" target="_blank">

          </a>
          to see their full line of blenders and juicers including the
          <a href="http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=67&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531%20%20%20%20%20%20" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.froothie.co.uk/index.php?dispatch=aff_banners.view&amp;bid=67&amp;sl=EN&amp;aff_id=1531%20%20%20%20%20%20', 'Optimum 9400']);" target="_blank">
