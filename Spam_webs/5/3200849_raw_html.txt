      <!-- Begin Title -->
      <h1>
       Einstein Summation
      </h1>
      <!-- End Title -->
      <!-- Begin Total Content -->
      <!-- Begin Content -->
      <p>
       Einstein summation is a
       <a href="/Notation.html">
        notational convention
       </a>
       for simplifying expressions including
       <a href="/Sum.html">
        summations
       </a>
       of
       <a href="/Vector.html">
        vectors
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/Matrix.html">
        matrices
       </a>
       , and general
       <a href="/Tensor.html">
        tensors
       </a>
       .
 There are essentially three rules of Einstein summation notation, namely:
      </p>
      <p>
       1. Repeated indices are implicitly summed over.
      </p>
      <p>
       2. Each index can appear at most twice in any term.
      </p>
      <p>
       3. Each term must contain identical non-repeated indices.
      </p>
      <p>
       The first item on the above list can be employed to greatly simplify and shorten equations involving
       <a href="/Tensor.html">
        tensors
       </a>
       . For example, using Einstein
 summation,
      </p>
      <div>
       <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="padding-left: 50px" summary="" width="100%">
        <tr>
         <td align="left">
          <img alt=" a_ia_i=sum_(i)a_ia_i " border="0" class="numberedequation" height="36" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/NumberedEquation1.gif" width="84"/>
         </td>
         <td align="right" width="3">
          <div class="eqnum" id="eqn1">
           (1)
          </div>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
      </div>
      <p>
       and
      </p>
      <div>
       <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="padding-left: 50px" summary="" width="100%">
        <tr>
         <td align="left">
          <img alt=" a_(ik)a_(ij)=sum_(i)a_(ik)a_(ij). " border="0" class="numberedequation" height="36" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/NumberedEquation2.gif" width="116"/>
         </td>
         <td align="right" width="3">
          <div class="eqnum" id="eqn2">
           (2)
          </div>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
      </div>
      <p>
       The second and third items on the list indicate that the expression
      </p>
      <div>
       <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="padding-left: 50px" summary="" width="100%">
        <tr>
         <td align="left">
          <img alt=" M_(ij)v_j=sum_(j)M_(ij)v_j " border="0" class="numberedequation" height="38" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/NumberedEquation3.gif" width="106"/>
         </td>
         <td align="right" width="3">
          <div class="eqnum" id="eqn3">
           (3)
          </div>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
      </div>
      <p>
       is valid, whereas the expressions
      </p>
      <div>
       <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="padding-left: 50px" summary="" width="100%">
        <tr>
         <td align="left">
          <img alt=" M_(ij)u_jv_j+w_i " border="0" class="numberedequation" height="17" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/NumberedEquation4.gif" width="79"/>
         </td>
         <td align="right" width="3">
          <div class="eqnum" id="eqn4">
           (4)
          </div>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
      </div>
      <p>
       and
      </p>
      <div>
       <table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" style="padding-left: 50px" summary="" width="100%">
        <tr>
         <td align="left">
          <img alt=" T_(ijk)u_k+M_(ip) " border="0" class="numberedequation" height="17" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/NumberedEquation5.gif" width="80"/>
         </td>
         <td align="right" width="3">
          <div class="eqnum" id="eqn5">
           (5)
          </div>
         </td>
        </tr>
       </table>
      </div>
      <p>
       are invalid because the index
       <img alt="j" border="0" class="inlineformula" height="14" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/Inline1.gif" width="6"/>
       appears three times
 in the first term of (), while the non-repeated index
       <img alt="j" border="0" class="inlineformula" height="14" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/Inline2.gif" width="6"/>
       in the first term
 of () doesn't match the non-repeated
       <img alt="p" border="0" class="inlineformula" height="14" src="/images/equations/EinsteinSummation/Inline3.gif" width="8"/>
       of the second term.
      </p>
      <p>
       The convention was introduced by Einstein (1916, sec. 5), who later jested to a friend, "I have made a great discovery in mathematics; I have suppressed the summation sign every time that the summation must be made over an index which occurs twice..." (Kollros 1956; Pais 1982, p.216).
      </p>
      <p>
       In practice, the convention tends to occur alongside both the
       <a href="/KroneckerDelta.html">
        Kronecker delta
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/PermutationSymbol.html">
        permutation symbol
       </a>
       . Moreover,
 the Einstein summation convention easily accommodates both superscripts and subscripts
 for
       <a href="/ContravariantTensor.html">
        contravariant
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/CovariantTensor.html">
        covariant
 tensors
       </a>
       , respectively.
      </p>
