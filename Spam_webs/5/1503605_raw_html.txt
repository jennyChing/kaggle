           <div class="entry">
            <p>
             AMDs 7950 is one of the best if not the best pound for performance card out there, most owners report at least a 20% performance gain with the temperatures being extremely efficient. HIS 7950s have been reported to easily reach 1200/1400 with temps not even reaching 60 Celsius, it will also max out the latest games such as battlefield 4.
            </p>
            <p>
             Depending on what brand of 7950 you purchase decides what software you should use. If you purchased a Sapphire, Gigabyte, Powercolor, Asus or MSI you should use MSIs afterburner program to manage overclocking, if you purchased a HIS 7950 you should use their HIS iTurbo software its also recommended to flash the firmware to a more efficient version that removes the stock boost feature which is know to cause stuttering issues. When overclocking use a benchmark program such as the Heaven Benchmark 4.0 and run it on Extreme tessellation, Ultra quality and 4x Anti Aliasing.
            </p>
            <p>
             Buying used it also something to think about as you can sometimes get a refurbished 7950 from somewhere like Dabs used stock for under 130 which is just insane value, typical new youre looking at around 180-210 depending on the brand and if any offers are on.
            </p>
            <p>
             Our pick for best 7950 was HIS boost version as once flashed it can easily reach 1200/1500 with temps running at under 65 Celsius with a custom fan profile that doesnt go over 47% on load so the card is very quiet and cool and plays Battlefield 4 with everything on Ultra with around 70 FPS on 64 player servers.
            </p>
