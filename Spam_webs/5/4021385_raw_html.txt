             <section class="article-content">
              <p>
               What keeps you up at night?
              </p>
              <p>
               This is the first question Casey Gerald asks small-business owners.
              </p>
              <p>
               As one of the founding members of
               <a href="http://mbaxamerica.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                MBAs Across America
               </a>
               , Gerald understands the hurdles faced by entrepreneurs  especially those far from the benefits of major business hubs like Silicon Valley or New York City. In addition to the struggles already facing small-business owners, a lack of professional resources in hard-to-reach locations can make it difficult for them to invest in development.
              </p>
              <p>
               One phrase I hear all the time is I spent so much time working in the business that I can't work on the business, Gerald said in a recent interview.
              </p>
              <p>
               Thats where MBAs Across America comes in. The organization, which he laughingly calls a domestic Peace Corps for MBAs, helps small businesses by connecting them with MBAs. Volunteers spend six weeks of the summer traversing the country and working hands-on with entrepreneurs, offering advice on everything from finances to marketing.
              </p>
              <p>
               Gerald and three of his fellow Harvard Business School classmates conceived the idea for MBAs Across America at a dinner party. MBAs Across America was not so much an idea of an organization, but an idea for our lives, he said.
              </p>
              <p>
               That summer, they drove 8,000 miles across the country connecting with small businesses, spending one week with each. When they couldn't find an RV, they had to use two of their own cars  which they nicknamed R and V. Weve come far from those scrappy days, laughed Gerald.
              </p>
              <p>
               The planning for their trip started months in advance, as they reached out to communities in search of entrepreneurs who wanted to take part in the program.
              </p>
              <p>
               <span class="microcontent" data-description="We look for a place that matters, with a story to tell, and entrepreneurs that are poised for growth and can have a positive impact in their community, he said." data-fragment="we-look-for-a" data-micro="1">
                We look for a place that matters, with a story to tell, and entrepreneurs that are poised for growth and can have a positive impact in their community, he said.
               </span>
               I've been overwhelmingly blown away by how folks are willing to welcome us into their businesses and their lives.
              </p>
              <p>
               The group has traveled to nearly every corner of America, from small towns to major cities like Detroit and New Orleans. Part of its strength lies in the collective experience of its members: They've now worked with people from nearly every walk of life, in communities all across the country. There are a lot more places to go, Gerald said. We have a huge spirit of exploration.
              </p>
              <p>
               The groups greatest strength, a fluid model that allows them to go anywhere, also presented its greatest challenges. Expenses like transportation, gas and lodging were all costs the nonprofit had to somehow account for. Lodging in particular was a huge concern, with projected costs running upward of $100,000.
              </p>
              <p>
               With not a lot of options and time running out, the group reached out to Holiday Inn. The hotel was moved by the similarities between its own Journey to Extraordinary program and the central mission of MBAs Across America, and offered help by covering the groups hotel costs.
              </p>
              <p>
               We're so lucky to have corporate sponsors like Holiday Inn, who really took a chance on us, said Gerald. Holiday Inn literally enabled us to exist this summer.
              </p>
              <p>
               <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/glO0wuSkpJU?enablejsapi=1&amp;" width="560">
               </iframe>
              </p>
              <p>
               And that existence has enabled small-business success from coast to coast. One of MBAs Across Americas earliest and most notable partners was Sebastian Jackson, owner of the
               <a href="http://www.tscbarber.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                Social Club Grooming Company
               </a>
               in Detroit  a barbershop that recycles hair to make compost, which is then used to plant local trees.
              </p>
              <p>
               After working with MBAs Across America, Jackson was able to increase his companys revenue by almost $12,000 a month, raise $100,000k from a local investor and even renovate his business using wood gathered from helping the community tear down an abandoned home.
              </p>
              <p>
               The early success made its mark. It told us not that we were geniuses, said Gerald, but that there were people like Sebastian all over the country.
               <span class="microcontent" data-description="We talk about the 28 million small businesses in America, but we need to do a far better job putting the wind at their backs." data-fragment="we-talk-about-the" data-micro="1">
                We talk about the 28 million small businesses in America, but we need to do a far better job putting the wind at their backs.
               </span>
               "
              </p>
              <p>
               As the success of MBAs Across America continues to grow, so does awareness of the group and its mission. It was recently announced that Gerald and his team will be featured on a new documentary series called
               <a href="http://www.hlntv.com/shows/growing-america-journey-success" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                Growing America: A Journey to Success,
               </a>
               premiering Sunday, Nov. 16 on HLN.
              </p>
              <p>
               When asked what advice he would give to entrepreneurs, Gerald said that they should always be reaching out for new connections  which he calls their greatest currency. And for small businesses around the country, that connection may be MBAs Across America.
              </p>
