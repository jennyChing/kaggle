        <em>
         Beauty Bargain pick posts
        </em>
       </a>
       <em>
        should be super helpful}
       </em>
       , there may be someone out there just getting started in couponing and feeling a bit overwhelmed.
      </p>
      <p>
       You just want to save a little money on makeup, right?
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://simplenailsandbeauty.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/CosmeticsCouponing101FeaturedImage.jpg">
        <img alt="Cosmetics Couponing 101 : where I get all the best coupons || SimpleNailsandBeauty.com" border="0" height="604" src="http://simplenailsandbeauty.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/CosmeticsCouponing101FeaturedImage_thumb.jpg" style="display: inline; border: 0px;" title="Cosmetics Couponing 101 : where I get all the best coupons || SimpleNailsandBeauty.com" width="604"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       So, in conjunction with my
       <a href="http://simplenailsandbeauty.com/category/beauty-bargains/">
        Beauty Bargains series
       </a>
       , I thought I would also start a little mini-series titled 
       <a href="http://simplenailsandbeauty.com/category/cosmetics-couponing-101/">
        Cosmetics Couponing 101
       </a>
        where I share my tips and tricks at getting the biggest savings not only at the drugstore, but on high-end cosmetics as well.
      </p>
      <p>
       And remember, I like to keep things simple. The way I coupon is a process that I have developed over a few years now and it has become the most simple way that works for me. However, there may be a different process, or a slight amendment, to the way I do something that works better for you. If so, go for it! If it works for you, and you are saving money, that is all I hope for!
      </p>
      <p>
       Also, I am going to be honestly realistic with you when it comes to couponing for cosmetics. I am not going to sit here and tell you that I get all of my makeup for free because I coupon and that I havent paid for an eye shadow in years.
      </p>
      <p>
       Have I gotten a few things for free? Yes, absolutely it is possible, every once in a while. But not EVERYTHING is going to be free like so many coupon guides tell you magically happens when you start to coupon. However, I do get pretty great deals very consistently, though, and I rarely pay full retail value on any of my cosmetics or beauty items.
      </p>
      <p>
       I thought I would start this series by showing you where I get coupons. Because without coupons, there would be no couponing to speak of!
      </p>
      <p>
       There are basically three different categories of coupons for me: 1) newspaper, 2) online, and 3) smart phone app coupons
      </p>
      <h2>
       Newspaper Coupons
      </h2>
      <p>
       Each week, the local Sunday newspaper is delivered to my door
       <em>
        {oddly enough, it arrives on Saturday, so I always feel like I am getting a peek into the future}
       </em>
       . We started subscribing to newspaper delivery because we actually wanted to read it and keep up to date with local news, so I look at the coupons as an added bonus
       <em>
        {in other words, I do not feel like I am paying for the newspaper just to get the coupons}
       </em>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       However, if we stopped reading the paper, we would certainly keep subscribing because of the coupons. Our local subscription is $11 per month and that includes a newspaper delivered 6 days a week. And do not discount the issues other than the Sunday paper! Very regularly, department stores will send a circular during the week that has a coupon in it. So, the value of coupons that I use from our newspaper far exceeds the $11 it costs us each month and getting the paper delivered to our door is actually less expensive than if we were to go buy the paper every day at the store!
      </p>
      <p>
       In my local newspaper, I usually receive the Smart Source coupon insert and, once a month, the P&amp;G coupon insert. There is also a Red Plum coupon insert, but we do not get that where I am from. If you do, score! Red Plum usually has some pretty sweet makeup coupons that I am envious of not having.
      </p>
      <p>
       Now, please do not imagine me sitting down at my kitchen table every Sunday and clipping out every single coupon and then organizing it into my huge coupon binder. I do not clip every coupon and I do not have a binder. I keep it much more
       <strong>
        <em>
         simple
        </em>
       </strong>
       , people. I only clip the coupons I know I am going to use and use them during the week.
      </p>
      <p>
       How do I know which ones I will use? I actually regularly visit a couple of couponing sites that TELL me which ones I should be using for that particular week. More on that to come later.
      </p>
      <p>
       But, this allows my couponing to be pretty straight-forward and simple. Someone tells me which coupons I need
       <em>
        {just like in my
       </em>
       <a href="http://simplenailsandbeauty.com/category/beauty-bargains/">
        <em>
         Bargain Beauty posts
        </em>
       </a>
       <em>
        },
       </em>
       I get those coupons, and I go buy the items.
      </p>
      <h2>
       Online Coupons
      </h2>
      <p>
       Okay, I am going to contradict myself just a tad when it comes to talking about online coupons. I do print out ANY and all cosmetics coupons that I think I MIGHT use in the future and do have them saved to use for whenever that brand goes on sale.
      </p>
      <p>
       The reason being is that with online coupons, one day they are here, the next day they are not
       <em>
        {unlike the newspaper coupons you receive that you can access at anytime}
       </em>
       . Manufacturers usually only let a certain number of prints per coupon take place online. So, when I see a coupon for $2 off an eye shadow come available, I print it out and save it for when that brand is on sale.
      </p>
      <p>
       But, again, please do not picture me with this massive three ring binder cutting out and sorting thousands of coupons. I have one, ONE, letter sized envelope with my cosmetic and beauty related coupons sorted in. I keep it in my purse
       <em>
        {it is that small}
       </em>
       and that way if I am ever out shopping and come across a sale or clearance items on makeup, I know I have any and all available coupons with me to use in conjunction with that sale.
      </p>
      <p>
       I plan on doing a post as a part of this Couponing 101 series that shows you how I organize this ONE envelope, so more on that to come!
      </p>
      <p>
       In the meantime, here are the online websites I check for coupons regularly. I check these sites once every week
       <em>
        {or so}
       </em>
       and just print out all of the beauty related coupons. Bookmark these links so that you can have quick and easy access!
      </p>
      <p>
       Also, you should know that you can print out each coupon on these sites two times per computer. I have two computers in my house, so I get to print each coupon out four times. Muahahahaha!
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.coupons.com/?pid=13903&amp;zid=xh20&amp;nid=10&amp;bid=1254990001" rel="nofollow">
        Coupons.com
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.redplum.com/">
        Redplum.com
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.smartsource.com">
        Smartsource.com
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       See, there are only three sites.how simple is it to check three sites, for five minutes, and print out some coupons? I waste more time on Facebook!
      </p>
      <h2>
       Smart Phone App Coupons
      </h2>
      <p>
       This is my favorite category! I actually save more money than I ever did because of my smart phone. Since I have to have the phone anyways, it is like the newspaperthe cost of the smart phone is going to be a bill I pay regardless, so why not utilize it at least somewhat to save a bit of money on that fab new lipstick color I have been eyeing up!
      </p>
      <p>
       I am going to do a more extensive post on all of these smart phone apps, as each one deserves a bit more explanation to maximize your savings, but below is a list of the smart phone apps I currently use in conjunction with my paper coupons. And yes, all of these apps are free. Paying for an app to coupon just seems counter-intuitive!
      </p>
      <p>
       Saving Star
      </p>
      <p>
       Ibotta
      </p>
      <p>
       Checkout 51
      </p>
      <p>
       Cartwheel
       <em>
        {for Target}
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       Walgreens
       <em>
        {the app to access and use your Balance Rewards}
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       <em>
        There are plenty of other apps, including those for drugstores like CVS, but I do not have one of those locally {and therefore do not shop there or need the app}. If you have a drugstore that you like to frequent, other than those listed above, check and see what apps might be available to use for that store!
       </em>
      </p>
      <h2>
       BONUS  Online Shopping Cash Back
      </h2>
      <p>
       Along with coupons themselves, there are a few ways and a few different websites you can utilize to save even more money while you are shopping online rather than when you are shopping in-store. To be honest, sometimes I find I save more money shopping from a stores website versus actually going to the physical location. Again, follow-up posts on each one of these savings sites to come, but for now start taking a peek at them to get a feel for what they have to offer!
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.ebates.com/rf.do?referrerid=sRXGl%2F5c0VHSzBlWEwrQng%3D%3D&amp;eeid=26471" rel="nofollow">
        Ebates.com
       </a>
        anytime you plan to make a purchase online, start at Ebates, click through to the stores site, and you will get a certainpercentage of your purchase back as a cash rebate
       <em>
        {it gets deposited right into your Paypal account each month, or you can request a check be sent}.
       </em>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.shopathome.com">
        Shopathome.com
       </a>
        works just like Ebates. I typically check Ebates first
       <em>
        {usually a better cash back percentage}
       </em>
       , but if I am shopping somewhere Ebates doesnt work with, I then try Shopathome.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://tracking.groupon.com/r?tsToken=US_AFF_0_202522_229570_0&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.groupon.com%2Fgoods%3Fcategory%3Dhealth-and-beauty-goods%26utm_source%3DGPN%26utm_medium%3Dafl%26z%3Dskip%26utm_campaign%3D202522">
        Groupon.com
       </a>
        it wasnt until recently that I discovered how many beauty related deals there are on Groupon!
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.hautelook.com/invite/JCassellius946" rel="nofollow">
        Hautelook
       </a>
        if you are not familiar with this site, it is a flash sale site. New items are added each day, at a huge discount, but are only available for a short period of time, or until they sell out!
      </p>
