      <h1 class="page-title">
       Reporter fired after story compared local school to Mean Girls
      </h1>
      <div class="article-content">
       <p class="article-meta">
        <img alt="avatar" class="avatar avatar-50 avatar-default" height="50" src="/wp-content/uploads/avatars/beaujon-new.jpg" style="width: 50px; height: 50px;" width="50"/>
        <span class="meta-byline">
         by
         <a href="http://www.poynter.org/author/abeaujon/" rel="author" title="Posts by Andrew Beaujon">
          Andrew Beaujon
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="meta-published">
         <strong>
          Published
         </strong>
         <span class="meta-date">
          Oct. 21, 2013
         </span>
         <span class="meta-time">
          11:49 am
         </span>
        </span>
        <span class="meta-modified">
         <strong>
          Updated
         </strong>
         <span class="meta-modified-date">
          Oct. 21, 2013
         </span>
         <span class="meta-modified-time">
          5:30 pm
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://nightlynoodlemonthly.com/post/64660658667/i-got-fired-for-being-a-journalist">
           Nightly Noodle Monthly
          </a>
         </strong>
        </em>
        |
        <em>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://nightlynoodlemonthly.com/post/64660658667/i-got-fired-for-being-a-journalist">
           North Adams Transcript
          </a>
         </strong>
        </em>
        <br/>
        <br/>
        In a piece called 
        <a href="http://nightlynoodlemonthly.com/post/64660658667/i-got-fired-for-being-a-journalist">
         I Got Fired For Being A Journalist
        </a>
        , Isaac Avilucea says Cheyanne Alcombright, the subject of
        <a href="http://www.thetranscript.com/localsports/ci_24335826/cheyanne-alcombright-latest-hornet-lead-way-scoring">
         his Oct. 18 story
        </a>
        in the North Adams Transcript, told him shed transferred to a different Massachusetts school even though it had somewhat inferior academics and athletics. Her old school, Mount Greylock, was like the movie Mean Girls.
       </p>
       <p>
        Avilucea writes that he was fired Friday. Transcript sports editor Josh Colligan read his story, he writes, but Editor-in-Chief Mike Foster was bothered by the story after fielding calls from school principals and angry parents. In an editorial published Saturday, Foster and Colligan write that they 
        <a href="http://www.thetranscript.com/sports/ci_24343413/our-opinion-editors-hope-rebuild-bridges?IADID=Search-www.thetranscript.com-www.thetranscript.com">
         offer our sincere apologies for any and all harm caused by a story in Fridays sports section
        </a>
        .
        <br/>
        <span id="more-226646">
        </span>
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         A segment of the story in question unintentionally cast aspersions on the academics of McCann and colored in a pallor the social environment at Mount Greylock. This serious editorial mistake has caused a great amount of angst for many  and rightfully so. Not only was it unjust on our part to allow this error to see the light of day, but the statements were simply wrong.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        Via e-mail, Avilucea says the somewhat inferior line was him paraphrasing Alcombright. You can make the case I should have attributed that line just to be safe, but that was never added or brought up in the editorial process, he writes. The Mean Girls passage is no longer in the
        <a href="http://www.thetranscript.com/localsports/ci_24335826/cheyanne-alcombright-latest-hornet-lead-way-scoring">
         online version of Aviluceas story
        </a>
        . The Transcript does not comment on personnel matters, Foster tells Poynter in an email.
       </p>
