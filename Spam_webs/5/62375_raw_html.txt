               <div class="posttitle">
                <h1>
                 <a data-rel="bookmark" href="http://now.snopes.com/2015/05/10/autistic-girl-removed/" title="Permalink to 15-Year-Old Autistic Girl Removed from Flight">
                  15-Year-Old Autistic Girl Removed from Flight
                 </a>
                </h1>
                <div class="line">
                 <span>
                 </span>
                </div>
               </div>
               <div class="entry-utility">
                <ul class="entry-utility">
                 <li class="user">
                  Posted by
                  <a href="http://now.snopes.com/author/snopes/">
                   David Mikkelson
                  </a>
                  /
                 </li>
                 <li class="date">
                  May 10, 2015
                 </li>
                 <li class="comment">
                  /
                  <a href="http://now.snopes.com/2015/05/10/autistic-girl-removed/#respond">
                   0 Comment

                 </li>
                </ul>

               <div class="posttags">

               <div class="entry-content">
                <div class="pw-widget pw-counter-vertical pw-horizontal" pw:image="" pw:title="15-Year-Old Autistic Girl Removed from Flight" pw:url="http://now.snopes.com/2015/05/10/autistic-girl-removed/">
                 <a class="pw-button-stumbleupon pw-look-native">
                 </a>
                 <a class="pw-button-tumblr pw-look-native">
                 </a>
                 <a class="pw-button-email pw-look-native">
                 </a>
                 <a class="pw-button-reddit pw-look-native">
                 </a>
                 <a class="pw-button-facebook pw-look-native">
                 </a>
                 <a class="pw-button-googleplus pw-look-native">
                 </a>
                 <a class="pw-button-twitter pw-look-native">
                 </a>
                </div>
                <p>
                </p>
                <center>
                 <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="315" seamless="seamless" src="http://www.katu.com/news/local/Autistic-teen-family-kicked-off-Portland-bound-plane-303146891.html?embed" width="560">
                 </iframe>
                </center>
                A mother claims United Airlines kicked her family off a Portland-bound flight after the pilot made an emergency landing in Salt Lake City due to her daughters autism.
                <p>
                 Dr. Donna Beegle and her family, including her 15-year-old daughter with autism, Juliette, were returning from Disney World on a flight from Houston to Portland at the time of the emergency landing. Beegle, a public speaker, said Juliette has flown most of her life as she often brings her along on her speaking engagements, and there had never been any issues with that form of travel.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Before boarding the Portland-bound flight, however, Beegle said Juliette had refused the food they bought and also would not eat the snacks they brought on board the plane. Juliette, like many people with autism, is clinically picky about the food she eats, and although she is high-functioning she has extreme difficulty communicating verbally. So in an attempt to prevent the situation from escalating, Beegle asked a flight attendant if they could provide any hot food her daughter:
                </p>
                <blockquote>
                 <p>
                  The family ate dinner in Houston, Beegle said, but Juliette refused to eat. Beegle brought some snacks on board for her because if her blood sugar lets go, she gets frustrated and antsy. We try to anticipate that and prevent that.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  After boarding, Beegle said she asked the flight attendant if she had any hot meals.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  Juliette refuses room-temp food, Beegle said. I had no real way to bring hot snacks in my bag.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  The attendant told her they had a hot sandwich, but the sandwich arrived cold, Beegle said, and Juliette refused to eat it.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  I could see [Juliette] getting frustrated, Beegle said, adding that she asked the flight attendant to check on meals available for purchase in first class.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  I asked if I can purchase something hot for my daughter and [the first class flight attendant] said no she said. I called him back over and I said to him, Please, help us out here,' but he again refused.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  He came back again and I said, I have a child with special needs, I need to get her something. And he said, I cant do that,' she explained. I said, How about we wait for her to have a meltdown, shell be crying and trying to scratch in frustration. I dont want her to get to that point.'
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  The attendant then brought Juliette rice and jambalaya, Beegle said, and she ate and she was fine.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  About 25 minutes later, according to Beegle, an announcement over the loudspeaker said the plane was making an emergency landing because of a passenger with a behavior issue.
                 </p>
                </blockquote>
                <p>
                 According to Beegle: Then the police said, Well, you know, were going to have to ask you to leave the plane. And I said, I dont understand why? Theres no issue. What is the problem? And he said, Well, the captain doesnt feel comfortable flying to Portland with your daughter on the plane, Beegle continued.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Another passenger on the flight maintained that officials overreacted to the situation:
                </p>
                <blockquote>
                 <p>
                  Another traveler, Jodi Smith, who was sitting three rows behind Juliette, said she heard the entire conversation with the first-class flight attendant.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  He was being totally ridiculous, Smith said.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  Then the medics came on, then the police. They went right straight to Dr. Beegle. You could hear them saying their daughter was perceived as a threat, Smith [said]. I stood up and said, Absolutely positively not.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  This was just ridiculous. She was calm, she had done nothing. Ive been on flights where kids have screamed for 4 hours and theyve never diverted a flight.
                  <br/>
                  <br/>
                  This was the epitome of discrimination, Smith said. I have never in all my years of flying seen anything like this.
                 </p>
                </blockquote>
                <p>
                 Beegle said Juliette has traveled often, accompnying her to to London, Paris and 22 states.
                </p>
                <p>
                 Weve never experienced anything like that, she said. It was horrific.
                </p>
                <p>
                 A United Airlines spokesperson wrote of the incident:
                </p>
                <blockquote>
                 <p>
                  After working to accommodate Dr. Beegle and her daughter during the flight, the crew made the best decision for the safety and comfort of all of our customers and elected to divert to Salt Lake City after the situation became disruptive. We rebooked the customers on a different carrier and the flight continued to Portland.
                 </p>
                </blockquote>
                <p>
                 Beegle is in the process of filing a lawsuit against both United Airlines and the Federal Aviation Administration. She said the purpose of the legal action was to encourage the carrier to better train their employees in understanding the needs of autistic children.
                </p>
