           <h1>
            Nick Hancock sets world records on Rockall
           </h1>
           <!-- &lt;h2&gt;&lt;/h2&gt; -->
           <div class="article-meta clearfix">

           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_119980" style="width: 879px">
            <img alt="Nick Hancock" class="wp-image-119980 size-full" height="598" src="http://cdn.grindtv.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Screen-Shot-2014-07-17-at-1.12.11-PM.png" width="869"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Nick Hancock broke two world records, having spent 43 straight days living atop Rockall, a tiny islet 286 miles west of Scotland.
             <a href="https://twitter.com/seanpglackin/status/489236638451920897/photo/1" target="_blank">
              Photo by Sean Glackin
             </a>
             is a twitpic
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Nick Hancock of Scotland broke out a tiny bottle of champagne and celebrated alone Thursday the world records he established by occupying the tiny islet of Rockall for 43 consecutive days, breaking solo and group (i.e. overall) records for inhabiting the tiny piece of rock.
           </p>
           <p>
            Rockall is a remote granite rock painted white by seabirds, the only inhabitants who could possibly enjoy the locale 286 miles westof Scotlandin the Atlantic Ocean.
           </p>
           <p>
            There can be no place more desolate, despairing, and awful, is how Lord Kennet described Rockall in 1971.
           </p>
           <p>
            Why anybody would want to step foot on this rock let alone live there for any length of time is incomprehensible. But thats what Hancock did, living among the seabirds and using a yellow RockPod as his shelter on the outcropping measuring 60 feet tall and 82 feet at its widest point.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_119981" style="width: 400px">
            <img alt="nick hancock on rockall is a twitpic" class="wp-image-119981" height="219" src="http://cdn.grindtv.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/nick-on-rock-twitpic.jpg" width="390"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Nick Hancock poses for a selfie holding a mini-bottle of champagne to celebrate his world records.
             <a href="https://twitter.com/RockallNick" target="_blank">
              Photo by a twitpic
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Its strange really, as its just another day on the rock, Hancock
            <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2696222/Nick-Hancock-spends-43-days-Rockall-island-Scotland-missing-World-Cup.html" target="_blank">
             told MailOnline
            </a>
            via satellite phone on Thursday about his record. I had a little smile to myself, but theres been no euphoria as such. I had no one to celebrate with.
           </p>
           <p>
            Other than the seabirds.
           </p>
           <p>
            Hancock, 39, broke the 40-day solo record established by former SAS soldier Tom McClean in 1985, and broke the 42-day group and overall record set by three Greenpeace members in 1997.
           </p>
           <p>
            Hancock
            <a href="http://rockallsolo.blogspot.com" target="_blank">
             posted his final blog entry
            </a>
            Thursday and indicated the boat picking him up will leave Leverburgh on Friday evening and pick him up Saturday.
           </p>
           <p>
            Another day here means another day onto my new occupation record, but having taken down the turbine yesterday, may mean that Im short on power tomorrow [Friday], Hancock wrote on his blog. Hence why Im posting this blog today rather than on my last night on the rock.
           </p>
           <p>
            Originally, Hancock was planning to remain on Rockall for 60 days but was forced to cut the visit short after losing four barrels of supplies in a storm at the beginning of July.
           </p>
           <p>
            His pod, which sits on the little habitable space available on Rockall, is firmly bolted into place. On days Hancock strays from the pod, he wears a harness attached to a strong lifeline.
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_119985" style="width: 630px">
            <img alt="Nick Hancock shot this photo of his RockPod from above. You can see there isn't a lot of room to roam on Rockall. Photo is a twitpic from Hancock" class="wp-image-119985" height="465" src="http://cdn.grindtv.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/home-on-rock-rockall-solo-blog-1024x768.jpg" width="620"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Nick Hancock shot this photo of his RockPod from above. You can see there isnt a lot of room to roam on Rockall. Photo is a
             <a href="https://twitter.com/RockallNick" target="_blank">
              twitpic from Hancock
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Hancock began his world-record attempt on June 5, before the World Cup started, and he didnt find out Germany had won until Thursday. But he didnt sound interested anyway since hes not a soccer fan.
           </p>
           <p>
            So, what does one do while sitting on a rock alone on the Atlantic Ocean?
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_119990" style="width: 655px">
            <img alt="nick hancock" class="wp-image-119990 size-full" height="374" src="http://cdn.grindtv.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/nick-hancock.jpg" width="645"/>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             Nick Hancock on his small perch atop the tiny islet of Rockall.
             <a href="https://twitter.com/seanpglackin/status/489236638451920897/photo/1" target="_blank">
              Photo by Sean Glackin
             </a>
             is a twitpic
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Hancock began talking to the homing pigeons and guillemots that landed on the rock. He read several books. He wrote blog posts. He watched shearwaters gliding centimeters above the waves. He observed two minke whales surface close to Rockall. He viewed fishing trawlers that passed by. He did housekeeping. He began taking Italian lessons. Oh, and he also raised $17,000 for the
            <a href="http://www.helpforheroes.org.uk" target="_blank">
             Help the Heroes
            </a>
            charity.
           </p>
           <p>
            Now that the end is within sight, Hancock, who has eaten nothing but army rations, has something specific in mind once he gets back home to Ratho, near Edinburgh, Scotland.
           </p>
           <p>
            Im looking forward to a glass of wine and a pint of good ale when I get back, he told MailOnline. I think a pizza is in order too.
           </p>
