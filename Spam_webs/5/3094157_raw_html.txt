       </a>
       , andalso includes a rollercoaster.
       <span id="more-683510">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Billed as "the most innovative windmill in the world", the
       <a href="http://dutchwindwheel.com/" target="_blank">
        Dutch Windwheel
       </a>
       is intended asalandmark attractionfor Rotterdam's waterside.Architecture studio
       <a href="http://www.doepelstrijkers.com/" target="_blank">
        DoepelStrijkers
       </a>
       designed the structure and is promoting itwith the help of partners
       <a href="http://www.meystersbuiten.nl/" target="_blank">
        Meyster's
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.bloc.nl/" target="_blank">
        BLOC
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Dutch Windwheel wind turbine" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-683645" height="312" src="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_468_1.jpg" width="468"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The designers imagine the 174-metre-high structure as beingframed bytwo rings  arotatingouterring made up of 40 rollercoaster cabins, and a static inner ring containing flats, a hotel and a restaurant.
      </p>
      <p>
       <!-- Inline see also text: 1-->
      </p>
      <hr/>
      <div class="inline-see-also-text cf">
       <p>
        <strong>
         Related story:
        </strong>
        <a href="http://www.dezeen.com/2014/11/23/mvrdv-markthal-rotterdam-market-hall-photographs-hufton-crow/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'See Also', 'Text', this.href]);" style="">
         MVRDV's Markthal Rotterdam photographed by Hufton + Crow
        </a>
       </p>
      </div>
      <hr/>
      <p>
       The wind turbine is at the centre but, according to the designers, it won't disturb residents or guests trying to sleep  because it won't make any noise.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Dutch Windwheel wind turbine" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-683647" height="229" src="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_468_3.jpg" width="468"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Unlike traditional turbines that generate energy through the movement of wind-propelled sails, theDutch Windwheel will require no mechanically moving parts, and will instead use a combination ofwind and water tocreate electricity.
      </p>
      <p>
       ThistechnologyisnamedEWICON, which stands for electrostatic wind energy convertor, and was developed byTU Delft researchers Johan Smit and Dhiradj Djairam.
      </p>
      <p>
       <img alt="Dutch Windwheel wind turbine" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-683646" height="229" src="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_468_2.jpg" width="468"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Itgenerates power using an electric field madeup of thin metal tubesand nozzles that spray outwater droplets.
      </p>
      <p>
       The wind causes the droplets to movetowards thepositive electrodes, increasing their potential energy  the energy that an object has due to its position  and allowing it be harnessed.
      </p>
      <div class="large-video-embed-wrapper">
       <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="263" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/tqksCHWROBU?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" width="468">
       </iframe>
      </div>
      <p>
       The processis described by researchers as being "similar to pushing a rock up a mountain against gravity". They believe it could function silently andwithout anyvibrations.
      </p>
      <p>
       "It is the first time the EWICON technique will be used in a building; it has only been tested in a laboratory," explained BLOC'sLennart Graaff."The upscaling and optimisation of the technique will be part of the research and development path."
      </p>
      <figure>
       <a href="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_4_1000.jpg">
        <img alt="Dutch Windwheel wind turbine" class="wp-image-683638 size-full" height="337" src="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_4.jpg" width="468"/>
        <figcaption>
         Sustainability diagram  click for larger image
        </figcaption>
       </a>
      </figure>
      <p>
       The team is currently in talks with the city council and a number of developers,technology and energy companies as well asuniversities to find ways of moving the project forward. But they believe it could be builtas soon as 2020.
      </p>
      <p>
       "The idea was born when we were discussing that Rotterdam is booming, but could really use an iconic attraction," said Graaff. "From the start we wanted to combine that with our ideas for a 100per centsustainable building, a platform for all kinds of technic and technologic innovations."
      </p>
      <figure>
       <a href="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_6_1000.jpg">
        <img alt="Dutch Windwheel wind turbine" class="wp-image-683642 size-full" height="357" src="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_6.jpg" width="468"/>
        <figcaption>
         Programme  click for larger image
        </figcaption>
       </a>
      </figure>
      <p>
       "We wanted to make a future landmark and a landmark for the future," he added. "The goal is to develop a building that generates more energy than it uses. We will use all the innovations that can contribute to that goal."
      </p>
      <p>
       The building is expected to attract around 1.5 million visitors per year. As well as offering impressive views over the city, the rollercoaster will include an interactivecinema that chartsthe history of Dutch water management.
      </p>
      <figure>
       <a href="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_7_1000.jpg">
        <img alt="Dutch Windwheel wind turbine" class="wp-image-683644 size-full" height="338" src="http://static.dezeen.com/uploads/2015/04/Dutch-Windwheel-wind-turbine_7.jpg" width="468"/>
        <figcaption>
         Access and parking  click for larger image
        </figcaption>
       </a>
      </figure>
      <p>
       Part of the facade will integrate digital glazed "smart walls" that add a virtual layer in front of the view, helping users to identify varioushistoric mills nearby.
      </p>
      <p>
       The hotel will include 160 rooms across seven storeys, while floors below will accommodate 72 apartments.
      </p>
