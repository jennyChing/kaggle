          <h1 class="OneLinkNoTx">
           8 tips for healthier boozing (+ 60 cocktail recipes)
          </h1>
          <p>
           posted: May 10, 2014
          </p>
          <hr/>
         </div>
         <!-- post content -->
         <div class="the-content single group">
          <div class="OneLinkNoTx">
           <p>
            <img alt="Healthy Cocktails" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-50398" height="310" src="/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Healthy-Cocktails.png" width="600"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Alcoholic drinks can be a sneaky source of calories, sugar, and even saltbut not when
            <em>
             youre
            </em>
            playing bartender. Try to get a few healthier alternatives under that (suddenly much looser) belt to impress friends and family, and so you can order with confidence when at a restaurant or bar. Our friends at
            <a href="http://greatist.com/" target="_blank">
             Greatist
            </a>
            narrowed down eight key guidelines to make our most indulgent drinks into healthier onesand created
            <a href="http://greatist.com/health/60-healthier-drinks-boozing" target="_blank">
             60 recipes
            </a>
            for kick-ass cocktails.
           </p>
           <p>
            1.
            <strong>
             Use only fresh-squeezed or 100 percent fruit juice
            </strong>
            to reduce added sugars. Fruit juice can still be a dangerfood, though, so sometimes its best to dilute it with water or ice to cut back on calories.
           </p>
           <p>
            2.
            <strong>
             Choose seltzer water
            </strong>
            over other carbonated waters to eliminate added sodium and other additives. For recipes that call for soda, skimp on portions, or opt for diet or natural (read: less added sugar) versionsthough those can have their pitfalls, too.
           </p>
           <p>
            3.
            <strong>
             Make simple syrup with honey
            </strong>
            which, though sugary, has some added benefits, like a healthy dose of antioxidants. It also gives you better control of the sugar content. Any recipe that calls for simple syrup will use the following recipe:  tablespoon honey mixed with  tablespoon warm water.
           </p>
           <p>
            4.
            <strong>
             Use whole fruit instead of sugar or flavoured syrups
            </strong>
            . The fruit itself adds fiber, natural sugars, and vitamins.
           </p>
           <p>
            5.
            <strong>
             Choose light alcohol over dark alcohol
            </strong>
            . The dark stuff contains more compounds known as congeners, which can worsen hangovers.
           </p>
           <p>
            6.
            <strong>
             Limit yourself to one shot per drink
            </strong>
            . Multi-alcohol cocktails can pack in serious calories, so keep to one shot (or 1  ounces) each. The shooters and shots we included actually contain less than one shot each to further lessen the caloric load.
           </p>
           <p>
            7.
            <strong>
             Size matters
            </strong>
            especially when it comes to consuming liquid calories. Thats why we kept all our drinks right around the 8-ounce mark.
           </p>
           <p>
            8.
            <strong>
             Choose nutritious ingredients
            </strong>
            . Ginger, coffee, and lemon star in several of our drinks. Theyve each got their own proven health benefits, even though they may not totally counteract the negative affects of alcohol. (Note: Too much alcohol and caffeine can both dehydrate, making hangovers even worse, so try to keep it at no more than one cup of Joe for the night.
            <br/>
           </p>
           <h3>
            Be a bar star! Click
            <a href="http://greatist.com/health/60-healthier-drinks-boozing" target="_blank">
             here
            </a>
            for 60 amazing cocktail recipes by Greatist.
           </h3>
