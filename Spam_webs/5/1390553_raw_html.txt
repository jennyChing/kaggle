              <img alt="Firefly" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-5787" height="250" src="http://www.giantfreakinrobot.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/firefly1.jpg" title="" width="578"/>
              A professor has been threatened with criminal charges for being a
              <i>
               Firefly
              </i>
              fan, sort of. It happened at the University of WisconsinStout, where theater professor James Miller put a
              <i>
               Firefly
              </i>
              poster on his door. The poster depicted Captain Mal Reynolds and the words, You dont know me, son, so let me explain this to you once: If I ever kill you, youll be awake. Youll be facing me. And youll be armed.
             </p>
             <p>
              The University forced him to take it down, so he put up a second poster featuring Captain tight-pants and the word, Warning: Fascism.
             </p>
             <p>
              Presumably he made this poster himself since, I havent actually managed to find such a poster for sale anywhere.  And the poster was on his door, which would seem to imply that Facism could be found in his room. That doesnt make a lot of sense, but whatever, the whole thing seems harmless.
             </p>
             <p>
              Except, well, the University had a problem with that one too and even went so far as to bring in the campus police chief who threatened to charge Miller with disorderly conduct, according to
              <a href="http://thefire.org/article/13595.html">
               The Fire
              </a>
              .
              <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
             </p>

&lt;p&gt;Universities are supposed to be a place of political expression and freedom but in practice they&#8217;re still businesses and if everyone&#8217;s running around voicing their views all the time no one will ever actually, you know, learn anything.  What I&#8217;m getting at here is that it&#8217;s not entirely unreasonable that a University might require one of its employees to refrain displaying some specific image or slogan on campus, it is after all their property.  What I don&#8217;t understand is why any university might have a problem with a silly &lt;I&gt;Firefly&lt;/I&gt; poster in particular.&lt;/p&gt;

              I get why they threw a fit about the second poster, they didnt like what it said about them.  I dont get why they had a problem with the first poster, nor do I understand why they seem to be overreacting in general. Theyve even gone so far as to turn Miller over to their campus threat assessment team.  Whatever happened to just firing a guy?  He hasnt committed a criminal offense, only a fireable one and even then only if your boss is a huge, huge dick.  If you hate

               Firefly

              that much give him the heave-ho and brace yourself for an invasion of angry Browncoats. I hear they often aim to misbehave.
