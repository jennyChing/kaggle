           <h2 class="art-postheader">
            <a href="http://smokeandmenus.com/2009/08/13/pulled-pork-for-dummies/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Pulled Pork for Dummies">
             Pulled Pork for Dummies
            </a>
           </h2>
           <div class="art-postheadericons art-metadata-icons">
            August 13th, 2009 | Author:
            <a href="http://smokeandmenus.com/author/Dad/" rel="author" title="Posts by Dad">
             Dad
            </a>
           </div>
           <div class="art-postcontent">
            <!-- article-content -->
            <div class="pf-content">
             <p>
              <img alt="pulled pork, Texas recipes, Evyl, Crockpot, BBQ" height="297" src="http://i64.photobucket.com/albums/h190/Morphthecat/808483_f520.jpg" width="462"/>
             </p>
             <p>
              Heres a recipe from a dear friend from Spur, Texas.
              <br/>
              My wife made it last weekend and let me tell you it was incredible.
             </p>
             <p>
              *Pork Roast (any size will do, depending on how much pork you want)
              <br/>
              *Vidalia Onions (1 large or two small will be fine. Slice them up)
              <br/>
              *One CrockPot
              <br/>
              *Sweet Baby Rays BBQ Sauce
             </p>
             <p>
              *Put roast in crock and toss in onions.
              <br/>
              Add enough water to almost cover the roast.
              <br/>
              Cook on Low for 6 hours.
              <br/>
              Remove roast and onions and drain water.
              <br/>
              Pull meat off the roast (should fall off) and dice onions.
              <br/>
              Add meat and onions to crockpot and add several tbls. of water.
              <br/>
              Add enough BBQ sauce of choice to cover meat (Sweet Baby Rays is preferred)
              <br/>
              and simmer for an additional 2 hours on Low.
             </p>
             <p>
              Grill some fresh buns with butter and serve with fresh coleslaw.
              <br/>
              (Add some coleslaw to the sandwich for a Carolina garnish)
              <br/>
              Enjoy!
             </p>
             <p>
              <em>
               <a href="http://smokeandmenus.com/2009/08/coleslaw/" target="_self" title="totally awesome">
                *awesome with coleslaw
               </a>
              </em>
             </p>
             <p>
              ~m
             </p>
