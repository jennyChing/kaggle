            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
               Spotify is your instant record collection online, theres more music than you can ever listen to at the click of a button!
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <img alt="Spotify" height="300" src="..//admin/images/tinymce/85//ad-800x800.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 5px;" width="300"/>
              And our friends at Spotify are offering students the chance to be a music millionaire for less than a fiver with 50% off Spotify Premium (unlimited, ad-free music on your mobile, computer and tablet).
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://seedmarketing.go2cloud.org/aff_c?offer_id=2&amp;aff_id=1016" target="_blank" title="Spotify Premium student offer">
                Click here
               </a>
              </strong>
              and subscribe with your NUS card to get Spotify Premium at 4.99 for up to 12 months.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              The next problem is what to listen to! So weve done some of that work for you. Try out these incredible debut albums for your listening pleasure.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Wu Tang Clan  Enter the Wu Tang  The 36 Chambers
               <br/>
               (1992)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              East Coast raps elite nine-man unit, Wu Tang Clan dropped this stone cold classic in 1992. With nine unique MCs, who complimented each others flow effortlessly, they sounded like nothing else. With their penchant for kung-fu samples and RZAs haunting production (all chilling piano lines and dusty soul samples) it still sounds fresh, vital and unique.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Bring Da Ruckus, C.R.E.A.M, Protect Ya Neck
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/3tQd5mwBtVyxCoEo4htGAV" target="_blank" title="Wu Tang Clan - Enter the Wu Tang">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Pink Floyd  Piper At The Gates of Dawn
               <br/>
               (1967)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Before the prog-rock drudgery and stadium filling self-indulgence, Pink Floyd were Britains greatest purveyors of mind-altering psychedelic sounds. Their debut, the only under the leadership of Syd Barrett, is playful and inventive in the best way possible. Barretts mad-genius persona is etched into every groove.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Lucifer Sam, Take Up Thy Stethoscope and walk , The Gnome
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/4Qgz29mI2wJFD1tbnXqj7V" title="Pink Floyd - Piper At The Gates of Dawn">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Elastica  Elastica
               <br/>
               (1994)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Sure, they nicked all their best riffs from Wire and The Stranglers but Elasticas debut is a flawless exercise in post-punk cool. Not only do they cram 16 songs inside 40 minutes they are all classics. The album went to number 1, spawned hit singles and saw them conquer America.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Connection, Waking Up, Stutter
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/2Eu3dJJYgcbzrhp0zyRBZz" target="_blank" title="Elastica">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               The Stone Roses  The Stone Roses
               <br/>
               (1989)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Few albums have the cultural impact that the Roses debut had on the UK. The album was birthed from the second Summer of Love and opened the door to the optimism of the nineties. A classic from start to finish, this album pretty much wrote the blueprint for the popular indie music that followed, and is so good it still reduces middle-aged men to either tears or a dancing mess of wild abandon. It ends with one of the greatest improvised jams of all time.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  I Wanna be Adored, She Bangs the Drums , I Am The Resurrection
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/0um9FI6BLBldL5POP4D4Cw" target="_blank" title="The Stone Roses">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               The Velvet Underground  The Velvet Underground and Nico
               <br/>
               (1967)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              It sold badly but changed everything. Rock music would not sound like it does without this album. While other groups were espousing peace, love and flowery imagery the Velvets delved into the dark worlds of sexual deviance and drug taking. Glowing pop moments are interspersed with experimental noise and haunting instrumentation. The most prophetic rock album of all time, everyone needs to hear it.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Sunday Morning, Venus in Furs , Im Waiting for the Man, Heroin, There She Goes Again
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/02O0Ea7JGwlLq8faAiv1MN" title="The Velvet Underground &amp; Nico">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Joy Division  Unknown Pleasures
               <br/>
               (1978)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              This magnificent debut revealed the dark underbelly of the punk movement, and laid the way for post-punk and goth to emerge as prominent music forms a few years later. Ian Curtis existential lyrics and captivating drone pierces music that sounds like it is oozing from Manchesters urban sprawl. Martin Hannetts inspired production gave the bands songs space to breath and lifted them to a near mythical level.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Day of the Lords, Shes Lost Control, Shadowplay
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/0cbpcdI4UySacPh5RCpDfo" target="_blank" title="Joy Division - Unknown Pleasures">
              <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
               <strong>
                LISTEN NOW
               </strong>
              </span>
             </a>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Chemical Brothers  Exit Planet Dust
               <br/>
               (1995)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Exit Planet Dust was a game-changer. It may not sound like it now, in the wake of what it spawned, but the duos big beat blending of huge beats and bass, psychedelia, funk, rock and electronic sounded like it had dropped from another planet.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Leave Home, Song to the Siren , Life is Sweet
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/1Bmuyq89rXZJNK1w8pauEg" title="Chemical Brothers - Exit Planet Dust">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Guns N Roses  Appetite For Destruction
               <br/>
               (1987)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              From the chiming guitar of opener Welcome To The Jungle, the debut from the most dangerous rock band in the world is a rollercoaster ride of pure rock power. Combining the emotion of blues and the power of punk with a funk-filled rhythm section, this was quite rightly one of the biggest selling rock records of the eighties and made GnR the biggest band in the world.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Welcome to the Jungle, Its So Easy , Paradise City
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/3I9Z1nDCL4E0cP62flcbI5" target="_blank" title="Guns N Rose - Appetite For Destruction">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Black Sabbath  Black Sabbath
               <br/>
               (1970)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              The first metal album emerged from the industrial smog of the Midlands with Black Sabbaths dark debut. Sure other albums had been heavy, but this brought the dark subject matter and metal imagery that acted as the foundation to the whole genre.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Black Sabbath, The Wizard, Warning
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/57CAarkoPZ13Ifbq0o2LCA" target="_blank" title="Black Sabbath">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               The Strokes  This Is It
               <br/>
               (2001)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Arguably kick-starting the noughties indie hey-day, The Strokes emerged with the style and the songs to capture the imagination of a new generation. They brought some New York garage rock swagger back to the charts and created a whole new crowd of indie-kids.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Barely legal, Someday, Last Nite, New York City Cops.
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/3EeZu1PTUY341mnNEiyiYl" target="_blank" title="The Strokes - This Is It">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               DJ Shadow  Endtroducing
               <br/>
               (1996)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Josh Davies aka DJ Shadow almost did for the turntable what Hendrix did for the guitar. Purists still dont consider the decks an instrument, but they absolutely havent heard this slice of sample-tastic brilliance. Built purely on samples from a life-time of crate digging it picks from an unbelievable number of genres and reminds us what was fun and free about hip hop in the first place.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Building Steam with a Grain of Salt, Organ Donor.
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/7N0W5QxnRMP3s1mMxfQo1p" title="DJ Shadow - Endtroducing">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               The Stooges  The Stooges
               <br/>
               (1968)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              What was the first punk album? Its a raging debate. But this debut from the Stooges is a strong contender. Some of the album is pretty standard blues rock, but other tracks like I wanna be your dog sound more punk than those that came after it. With the thrashed simple riffs and Iggy Pops menacing, wild man strutting sound intimidating today  what must it have sounded like in 1968.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  I Wanna be your Dog, No Fun, Not Right
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/3MANoCcmaHWeXSuWiO3iVo" target="_blank" title="The Stooges">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Daft Punk  Homework
               <br/>
               (1997)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Before they simply ripped off disco records, Daft Punk were dance music innovators with their fusion of techno, house, acid house and electro sounds. Homework was a funk-filled breath of fresh air creating a new kind of robotic-disco sound to fill the dancefloors, film soundtracks and home stereos. This is still the French duo at their most exciting.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Da Funk, Around the World, Indo Silver Club
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/5uRdvUR7xCnHmUW8n64n9y" target="_blank" title="Daft Punk - Homework">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Rage Against The Machine  Rage Against The Machine
               <br/>
               (1992)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Other bands had united hip hop and metal before, but the Los Angeles groups debut was a cultural earthquake. Righteous attack politics, funky rhythms and Tom Morrellos are-you-sure-thats-a-guitar-making-that-noise playing, RATM sounded like a bomb going off.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Bombtrack, Killing in the Name, Bullet in the Head.
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/71sJ7DgvfOLmxKN3kjdN2N" target="_blank" title="Rage Against the Machine">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>

             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Arctic Monkeys  Whatever People Say I Am Thats What Im Not
               <br/>
               (2006)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              While indie was a whole bunch of Libertines sound-a-likes, a band of teenagers from Sheffield drummed up their own hype releasing demos on the internet. By the time their debut hit the shelves everyone was in love with Arctic Monkeys. Alex Turners kitchen-sink, poetic observations on teen-life, coupled with rhythms more akin to hip hop than indie and massive riffs made it the most important British guitar record of the era.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  I Bet You Look Good on the Dancefloor, Fake Tales of San Francisco, When The Sun Goes Down.
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/2PXlvqTzGBNetlGct7L5e6" target="_blank" title="Arctic Monkeys">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Roxy Music  Roxy Music
               <br/>
               (1972)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              The early seventies was a world of pretentious prog rock and extrovert glam rock  Roxy Music landed combining the two in a new futuristic pop sound. Brian Enos otherworldy synth sounds and tape loops acted as the perfect foil to the crooning art-rock of Bryan Ferry and the rest of the band. Only Bowie had a more profound impact in bringing a new arty flamboyance to pop.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Re-make/Re-model, Ladytron, Virginia Plain
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/4KjUgJn22cmBRQC0AHcjI3" target="_blank" title="Roxy Music">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               The Libertines  Up The Bracket
               <br/>
               (2002)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              At the dawn of the new millennium British music was in the thralls a crappy pop music (Robbie Williams the biggest selling act!!!). This London band of misfits made guitars and mindless rock n roll abandon cool again. Forget the Pete Doherty circus since, at the time Up The Bracket was simply essential.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Time for Heroes, Boys in the Band, Up the Bracket, The Boy Looked at Johnny.
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/2prIc5Om2QPCGIjKVC5UQj" target="_blank" title="The Libertines - Up The Bracket">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               The Specials  The Specials
               <br/>
               (1979)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              The concrete jungle of Coventry seems an unlikely birthplace for 2-Tone, the energetic fusion of punk and ska that owned the charts in the late seventies and early eighties. The Beat, The Selector and Madness were all exceptional, but the daddies of the scene were its leaders The Specials. A multi-cultural gang The Specials revitalised old ska/reggae classics and added some of their own to the mix. Produced by one Elvis Costello, this is a must-own from the era.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  A Message to you Rudy, Concrete Jungle, Blank Expression, Too Much Too Young
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/3slMWt0RziAAsTyKO0v98W" target="_blank" title="The Specials">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Mos Def  Black on Both Sides
               <br/>
               (1998)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              He was incredible in Black Star, but this solo debut from Mos Def showed the sheer depth of his talent. Witty, outspoken and most of all smooth rhymes cut through a release with universal appeal  to both the underground and mainstream crowds. Forays into other genres like punk, soul and reggae create a beautiful aural feast for the ears.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Hip Hop, Ms Fat Booty, Knew That
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/4jZI8SruF5N6sKJoWItEhl" title="Mos Def - Black On Both Sides">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Massive Attack  Blue Lines
               <br/>
               (1991)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              An effortless fusion of electronic music, hip hop, dub, soul and reggae the Bristol collectives debut birthed the trip hop genre. Quintessentially British but sounding like it comes from another dimension, Blue Lines has rarely been bettered by any group for its melding of different sounds into one brilliant whole. Unfinished Sympathy still regularly features in best tracks ever list.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Safe From Harm, Unfinished Sympathy
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               <a href="http://open.spotify.com/album/5mAPk4qeNqVLtNydaWbWlf" title="Massive Attack - Blue Lines">
                LISTEN NOW
               </a>
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Television  Marquee Moon
               <br/>
               (1977)
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              Well before The Strokes were the epitome of New York guitar cool, Television rewrote the rule book for guitar music. Emerging from the citys punk scene their textured, instrumental interplay and extended improvisation set them apart from the three-chord thrash of other groups. Marquee Moon is an ambitious record in sound and lyrical content that echoes through indie music today.
             </span>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>
               Stand out tracks  Friction Marquee Moon
              </strong>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: medium;">
              <strong>

                LISTEN NOW
