var contentAdjust = function() {
    var leftContent = $(document).find('.left_content');
    var rightHeight = 0;
    $('.right_content').children().not('.content-list.region').each( function(index) { rightHeight += $(this).outerHeight(true); });
    var leftHeight = leftContent.height(); // Height of left pane (without margins)
    leftHeight  = leftHeight - rightHeight; // Subtract the height of any non-list items on the right from the total height of the left
    var listElementHeight = $('.right_content').find('.views-row').first().outerHeight(true);
    var listElementNumber = Math.floor(leftHeight / listElementHeight); // Get the max number of items that should be displayed in the list
    var rightElementCollection = $('.right_content').find('.views-row');
    for (var i = listElementNumber; i &lt; rightElementCollection.length; i++) {
        $(rightElementCollection[i]).remove();

    }
};

      <div class="article_copy">
       <p>
        Fan of Broken Social Scene?  Have you ever been curious about what the band sounded like
        <i>
         before
        </i>
        it was the full lineup?  We at InYourSpeakers are giving you the rare chance to do so.  Catch a glimpse inside K.C. Accidental, the project that came before Broken Social Scene, after the jump.
       </p>
       <p>
        Formed in 1998 by founding
        <a href="http://www.brokensocialscene.ca/">
         Broken Social Scene
        </a>
        members Charles Spearin and Kevin Drew,
        <a href="http://www.myspace.com/residentiallovesong">
         K.C. Accidental
        </a>
        was the lo-fi, post-rock outfit that put these two musicians on the Toronto music scene.  After two years and two albums, the duo decided to call it quits.  Since then, trying to obtain those two albums,
        <i>
         Captured Anthems for an Empty Bathtub
        </i>
        and
        <i>
         Anthems for the Could've Bin Pills
        </i>
        , has been nigh impossible for fans of Broken Social Scene and
        <a href="http://www.ilovemetric.com/">
         Metric
        </a>
        .  For a combined length of twelve songs, fans outside of the Canadian border have been dying to get their hands on a copy of these albums.  However, now, that is no longer an issue.
       </p>
       <p>
        Thanks to
        <a href="http://www.arts-crafts.ca/">
         Arts and Crafts
        </a>
        , you can pre-order a vinyl re-release of both albums.  The albums will be released in MP3, FLAC, double CD, and double LP vinyl versions, all of which you can pre-order
        <a href="http://galleryac.com/captured-anthems-for-an-empty-bathtub-anthems-for-the-could-ve-bin-pills.html">
         here
        </a>
        .  The albums are slated for an October 26 re-release, so be sure to have your fingers ready; they're sure to go fast!
       </p>
       <p>
        For now, catch a few tracks from K.C. Accidental below, and the tracklisting for the albums, as well.
       </p>
