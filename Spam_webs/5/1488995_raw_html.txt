            <h1 class="news-article-head">
             Vue books vs movies: From page turner to box office burner
            </h1>
           </div>
           <div class="news-article-content" id="nac">
            <!-- flexible layout in here -->
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              When a book-to-movie blockbuster works, theres nothing quite like it. Characters literally jump off the page and explode onto the big screen, satisfying even the most die-hard of followers and urging new fans to seek out the novel from which it was adapted.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Get it wrong, however, and you can expect to see movie blogs and social media blow up with a flurry of negative comments from disappointed fans, proving that you cant please all of the people all of the time.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Thousands of Harry Potter fanatics were distraught that the Quidditch World Cup was absent from the movie franchise, with many putting pen to paper, complaining to author J.K Rowling  in the end they had to make do with a video game, which appeased at least some of them.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Despite receiving universal acclaim from both critics and fans, the Lord of the Rings movie trilogy was forced to miss out several characters and sub-plots from Tolkiens literary masterpiece, which angered some fans. Director Peter Jackson has often defended his decisions, stating that time and budget restraints prevented him from making the movies 100% comprehensive.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Turning a best-selling page-turner into a big screen box office earner is nothing new, of course. Its easy to forget that many classics such as
              <em>
               The Godfather
              </em>
              (1972),
              <em>
               Jaws
              </em>
              (1975) and
              <em>
               The Shawshank Redemption
              </em>
              (1994)
              <em>
              </em>
              all began life in the bookstore, before raking in millions of pounds at the box office and in subsequent DVD releases.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Here at Vue, we decided to set the record straight and conducted a survey of 1,000 movie, book and comic fans to see what you love and hate about some of the biggest book-to-movie franchises in recent years.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Some of the results were surprising and, we have to say, respondents were not backward in coming forwards when it came to airing their views!
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
               <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/best%20or%20worst%20banner%202.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 292px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
               <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/fans%20of%20harry%20potter%20quote.jpg" style="float: right;"/>
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Fans of wizard wunderkind Harry Potter were the most divided. The popular J.K. Rowling books were voted the least favourite book-to-movie adaptations, with many citing favourite sub-plots being missed out as the reason. Oddly enough, Harry potter was also voted second best book-to-movie adaption, losing out to Lord of the Rings.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              A big surprise was the absence of the hugely popular Twilight franchise in the best list, whereas Bridget Jones Diary managed the number five spot, despite the fact that its 10 years since the hapless singleton hit the silver screen.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
               <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/50%20shades%20of%20disappointment%203.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 196px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/50%20shades%20quote.jpg" style="float: left;"/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              <a href="http://www.myvue.com/latest-movies/info/film/fifty-shades-of-grey">
               50 Shades of Grey
              </a>
              somehow managed to make number two on the worst list, despite the fact it isnt released until next year. Comments on social media seem to suggest many fans of the book have been disappointed by the fact the lead actor is not the Christian Grey they envisaged  talk about giving actor Jamie Dornan a complex!
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              We put four of the most successful book-to-movie adaptations under the microscope to try and determine the magic formula for striking bookstore to box office gold:
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
               <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/harry%20potter%20banner%204.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 246px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/12%20percent%20quote.jpg" style="float: right;"/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Few franchises reach the heights of success achieved by J.K. Rowlings best-selling childrens novels. Since
             </span>
             <em style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Harry Potter and the
             </em>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
             </span>
             <em style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Philosophers Stone
             </em>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              was released in 2001, the combined box office of all eight movies has hit $7,723,431,572 (4,731,479,873), not to mention tie-in memorabilia, box sets and a hugely successful theme park.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              This didnt stop 12% more of those surveyed across the UK saying that they preferred the books to the movies.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              The main gripe fans cited were major sub-plots missing from the movies  joint number one being the missing Peeves the Poltergeist character and the Quidditch World Cup storyline.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Hot on its heels was Hermiones elf rights group, the oddly titled SPEW. Interestingly enough, women were bigger fans of the books than the movies, whereas males preferred its big screen counterpart.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Chris Columbus, who directed the first two Potter movies and produced the third, recalls that the first movie was actually criticised for being
              <em>
               too
              </em>
              faithful to the book, which is why he believes it worked so well.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/Quote%20(3)%20-2011.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 175px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              <br/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
               <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/lord%20of%20the%20rings%205.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 232px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/50%20percent%20quote.jpg" style="float: right;"/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Director Peter Jackson scored a box office hat trick with his movie adaption of J.R.R. Tolkiens literary masterpiece, the Lord of the Rings.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              With ticket sales amassing $2,917,506,956 (1,787,304,818) globally, it came as no surprise that the majority of those surveyed preferred the movies to the books, with the exception of fans in East Anglia, the North West, Yorkshire and Humber districts.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Although both sexes chose the movies over the books, a larger percentage of males preferred the books. Over 50% of respondents that felt key moments from the books were missing from the movies  many fans agreed that the absence of Tom Bombadil left a major gap in the plot.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Peter Jackson has addressed this issue in the past, stating that they simply couldnt figure out a way to include Bombadil without losing momentum on the rest of the plot.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
               <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/Twilight%20banner%206.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 269px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/twilight%20fans%20prefer%20movie%20quote.jpg" style="float: left;"/>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              It doesnt seem two minutes since the Twilight phenomenon was dominating cinema, breaking as many records as it did hearts, igniting resurgence in the popularity of vampire related TV shows, books and movies.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Despite being absent from the best of list, most Twilight fans agreed that they preferred the movies to the books.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              That said, there were still several ingredients fans felt would have added extra bite to the movies. Many felt that Jacobs part was played down too much, as well as Alices back story.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Some grumbled that the big screen adaption couldnt quite convey the same level of emotion reached in the best-selling novels. Ireland seemed most passionate about the movies, with only 3% stating that the book was better!
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
               <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/Narnai%20banner%207.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 201px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              </span>
             </strong>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              The classic C.S. Lewis tales have been enjoyed by children for generations and the movie adaptions proved to be incredibly profitable, earning a cool $1.5bn (919,000,000) worldwide. That said, with the exception of the East Midlands and Wales, 10% more of Narnia fans preferred the books to the movies.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              Fans of the books complained that far too much character background information was missed out of the movie adaptation, giving the overall cinematic experience less substance for those who had not read the books first.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              So there you have it. If any lessons are to be learned from our survey its this; if you are a film director looking to take on a classic book, loved by millions across the globe, then it best be amazing, and true to the original, otherwise you could be in hot water. The End.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">
              <img alt="" src="/Portals/1/Footer-nolamppost.jpg" style="width: 640px; height: 87px; vertical-align: middle;"/>
              <br/>
             </span>
            </p>
           </div>
