     <h1>
      Travel guide to Idahos coolest small towns
     </h1>
     <div class="post-inner clearfix">
      <div class="post-meta">
       <div class="post-meta-author">
        by
        <a href="http://matadornetwork.com/author/kristenbor/">
         Kristen Bor
        </a>
       </div>
       <div class="post-meta-date">
        May 21, 2015
       </div>
       <div class="post-avatar">
        <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-100" height="100" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/authors/kristenbor.jpg" width="100"/>
       </div>
       <div class="post-share-wrapper post-share--vertical" data-post-id="355856" data-sticky="" data-sticky-max-width="991" data-sticky-offset-top="69" data-sticky-parent-contain="" data-sticky-parent-selector=".page-section">
       </div>

      <div class="post-content">
       <h2>
        Ketchum (Blaine County)
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_355985" style="width: 1510px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="A WALL OF WOOL copy (1)" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/A-WALL-OF-WOOL-copy-1.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-355985 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/A-WALL-OF-WOOL-copy-1.jpg" height="1200" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="1500"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="http://www.trailingofthesheep.org/">
          Michael Edminster
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Home to the worlds first chairlift and the birthplace of
        <i>
         Powder Magazine
        </i>
        , ski culture runs deep in this lively mountain town of 2,700. With more than 3,400 vertical feet, Sun Valley has become the training ground for dozens of Olympic skiers and snowboarders. But this premier American ski resort has plenty for all skill levels and ages as well.
       </p>
       <p>
        This part of Idaho sees 250 days of sun per year, and Ketchum certainly doesnt shut down when the snow melts. In 2014, Sun Valley opened up its lifts to mountain bikers, so now the downhill adrenaline rush can be experienced year round. If you prefer flat, paved terrain, the 18-mile Wood River bike path links Ketchum with its neighboring towns of Hailey and Bellevue, and hugs the beautiful cold mountain waterway that flows from the nearby Sawtooths. Hiking, hot springs, fly fishing, and horseback riding are also right on your doorstep.
       </p>
       <p>
        Ketchums main drag is lined with great restaurants and bars like the Sawtooth Club and the Pioneer Saloon, which serve up traditional Idaho fare (think elk and bison), and boutique shops selling everything from local art and outdoor gear to high-end fashion. Hemingway fans will want to spend some time walking the halls at the Sun Valley Lodge, where the author worked on
        <i>
         For Whom the Bell Tolls
        </i>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Definitely check out Sun Valleys events calendar when youre in town. Theres always a ton of  stuff going on: jazz festivals, wine auctions, and a summer concert series (this year featuring the John Butler Trio and Wilco). And every October, Sun Valley hosts the Trailing of the Sheep Festival, paying homage to the Wests ranching heritage with five days of cooking classes, sheepdog competitions, wool workshops, and  of course  a sheep parade. Theres nothing quite like seeing 1,500 woolies strut their stuff down Ketchums main drag.
       </p>
       <h2>
        McCall (Valley County)
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_355991" style="width: 970px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="SUP Payette River Games" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/51.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-355991 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/51.jpg" height="540" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="960"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="http://www.payetterivergames.com">
          Payette River Games
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Located on the shore of the 5,330-acre Payette Lake, McCall is all about the water. Boaters, kayakers, SUPers, and swimmers gravitate here to explore the nooks and crannies of the deep blue glacial lake, and the areas home to some of the best whitewater opportunities in the country. The
        <a href="http://18summers.us/adventures/main-payette-river/?utm_source=Matador&amp;utm_medium=Listicle&amp;utm_term=IdahoSmallTowns&amp;utm_content=PayetteRiver&amp;utm_campaign=ITCPrimeFY15">
         Payette River
        </a>
        offers everything from mellow float trips for the family to sections with class IV and V rapids. Kayakers can also work on improving their skills at Kellys Whitewater Park just south of town (SUPers should head to Kellys in June for the Payette River Games  theres a $50,000 prize!).
       </p>
       <p>
        But McCall is probably most famous for its Winter Carnival, which takes places every February. Originating in 1965, this massive 10-day festival features elaborate, life-sized ice sculptures, fireworks, a winter beer garden, live music, and a Mardi Gras parade. Youll also have your choice of two nearby ski resorts: Brundage Mountain is located right in town and is an intermediate mountain known for its wide groomers and consistently soft snow. Then theres Tamarack  Idahos newest  located 20 miles south of McCall on Lake Cascade, with an extensive Nordic trail system.
       </p>
       <p>
        Several commercially developed hot springs are easily accessible from McCall, including Gold Fork Hot Springs and Burgdorf, where you can stay overnight in one of their cabins. Both are open year round, although youll need a snowmobile to reach Burgdorf in winter.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Stanley (Custer County)
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_356055" style="width: 970px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="IMG_4233" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/IMG_4233.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-356055 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/IMG_4233.jpg" height="1440" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="960"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="http://www.stanleybakingco.com/">
          Kat Smith for Stanley Baking Co. &amp; Cafe
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Sitting on the
        <a href="http://18summers.us/adventures/middle-fork-salmon-river/?utm_source=Matador&amp;utm_medium=Listicle&amp;utm_term=IdahoSmallTowns&amp;utm_content=SalmonRiver&amp;utm_campaign=ITCPrimeFY15">
         Salmon River
        </a>
        and surrounded by the
        <a href="http://18summers.us/adventures/sawtooth-scenic-byway/?utm_source=Matador&amp;utm_medium=Listicle&amp;utm_term=IdahoSmallTowns&amp;utm_content=Sawtooth&amp;utm_campaign=ITCPrimeFY15">
         Sawtooth
        </a>
        and White Cloud Ranges, Stanley  population 69  is the perfect base for accessing Idahos beautiful backcountry. From easy day hikes to multi-day backpacking trips and summit expeditions, the trails around town lead to remote wilderness experiences.
       </p>
       <p>
        Dont forget your fishing pole, as many of the regions alpine lakes are stocked with trout species, and in the spring steelhead cruise through town on their 900-mile upstream journey. For lakefront accommodations that dont require a sleeping bag, the historic Redfish Lake Lodge, seven miles from town, has a range of options from simple rooms to large, stand-alone 10-person cabins big enough for the whole family.
       </p>
       <p>
        In winter, snowmobilers rally to Stanley and its 185+ miles of groomed trails and endless fields of fresh powder  you could spend days zipping around by sled. Most snowmobilers depart from Smiley Creek Lodge, 22 miles south of town, where in the summer you can glamp out in a decked-out tepee.
       </p>
       <p>
        While the mountains really are the draw in Stanley, the town itself has some spots you shouldnt miss. Stanley Baking Company does epic breakfasts and serves up enormous specialty pancakes, while the riverside Backcountry Bistro specializes in free-range meats and fresh seafood.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Sandpoint (Bonner County)
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_355988" style="width: 970px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Schweitzer Mountain Scenic Chairlift" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/Schweitzer-Mountain-Biking.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-full wp-image-355988 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/Schweitzer-Mountain-Biking.jpg" height="638" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="960"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="http://www.schweitzer.com/">
          Schweitzer Mountain Resort
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        Thick green forests, miles of singletrack, and a prime location on the shore of
        <a href="http://18summers.us/adventures/lake-pend-oreille/?utm_source=Matador&amp;utm_medium=Listicle&amp;utm_term=IdahoSmallTowns&amp;utm_content=LakePendOreille&amp;utm_campaign=ITCPrimeFY15">
         Lake Pend dOreille
        </a>
        make northern Idahos Sandpoint worth the trek. Think of it as the quieter, slower-paced neighbor to Coeur dAlene.
       </p>
       <p>
        Despite being home to fewer than 7,500 residents, Sandpoints eclectic dining scene could keep you busy for weeks. Taqueria-style Mexican at Joels, super fresh sushi at Thai Nigiri, and the famous key lime pie at Baxters on Cedar  a popular new American joint  are just a few of the spots to hit up. For drinks, check out the taproom at Laughing Dog Brewing, which serves 10+ locally brewed, award-winning beers. And the Pend dOreille Winery has a beautiful but relaxed tasting room with live music on Friday nights.
       </p>
       <p>
        Youll want to save some energy to play at Schweitzer Mountain, Idahos largest ski resort. Its open all year, and in the warm months you can play the 9-hole disc golf course, head out on a geocaching treasure hunt, go mountain biking and hiking, or try your hand at huckleberry picking (late July to late August).
       </p>
       <p>
        Theres a ton of hotels to choose from in town, including several lakefront options. The K2 Inn is a great budget choice just a couple of blocks from the water. For something more Wild West, check out the
        <a href="http://18summers.us/adventures/western-pleasure-guest-ranch/?utm_source=Matador&amp;utm_medium=Listicle&amp;utm_term=IdahoSmallTowns&amp;utm_content=WesternPleasureGuestRanch&amp;utm_campaign=ITCPrimeFY15">
         Western Pleasure Guest Ranch
        </a>
        : 16 miles from town, all-inclusive summer packages with cabin-style lodging, daily horseback rides, gourmet meals, and evening campfires.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Driggs (Teton County)
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_356242" style="width: 950px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Driggs, Idaho" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/driggs-idaho-940x627.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-large wp-image-356242 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/driggs-idaho-940x627.jpg" height="627" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/mmmavocado/">
          Malcolm Manners
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        As tourists flock to Jackson, Wyoming, every summer to experience the Tetons, Driggs, on the other side of the mountains, offers an alternative and equally incredible homebaseminus the crowds.  And with access to both Grand Targhee and Jackson Hole, Driggs is an amazing spot for skiers and snowboarders: Grand Targhee Resort, just 20 minutes from downtown, has a laid-back, family-friendly vibe and over 2,000 acres of varied terrain. Its also the first resort in the country to offer fat biking  winter mountain biking on snow-covered trails. And in the summer, the mountain hosts the three-day Annual Grand Targhee Bluegrass Festival.
       </p>
       <p>
        Driggs is also home to the headquarters of the National Outdoor Leadership School. One of the leading outdoor education centers in the country, NOLS has a massive range of Teton Valley adventure itineraries to choose from, including backcountry splitboarding, backpacking, and rafting.
       </p>
       <p>
        Downtown, pull up your rig and head back in time at the Spud Drive-in Theater, sample local cheeses at the Teton Valley Creamery, or sip on the worlds #1 potato vodka at the Grand Teton Distillery. For the best views of the region, take a scenic flight with Teton Aviation or a hot-air balloon ride with Teton Balloon Flights.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Idaho City (Boise County)
       </h2>
       <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_356243" style="width: 950px">
        <noscript>
         <img alt="Idaho City, ID" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/idaho-city-940x705.jpg" title=""/>
        </noscript>
        <img class="size-large wp-image-356243 lazy" data-original="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/blogs/1/2015/04/idaho-city-940x705.jpg" height="705" src="http://cdn1.matadornetwork.com/assets/images/blank1.png" width="940"/>
        <p class="wp-caption-text">
         Photo:
         <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/jitze1942/">
          Jitze Couperus
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>
       <p>
        This 500-person historic town sits directly on the Ponderosa Pine Scenic Byway, which winds up and over the Boise foothills, offering a quick escape with immediate access to hot springs, the Boise National Forest, and a taste of Idahos past. Founded in the 1860s as a booming gold mining town, Idaho City once had fancy theaters, dozens of saloons, a bowling alley, the states first Catholic church, and a county jail that housed the criminals of the Old West. And while four separate fires have hit the community since its founding, many of the original wooden plank buildings still stand today. Visit the Boise Basin Museum for a glimpse of what life during the boom was like.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Springs, a newly opened high-end commercial hot springs facility, is one of the towns biggest draws, where you can get your soak on in one of their large geothermal pools. They also offer massages, and theres a full-service cafe and bar onsite. Visit on Wednesdays for an adults-only environment; Sunday is family day at the Springs. Whichever day you choose to come, its a good idea to make reservations.
       </p>
       <p>
        If youre looking for more of an off-the-grid experience, there are several all-natural hot pools not too far north of Idaho City, including Pine Flats and Kirkham Hot Springs. Along Highway 21, Idaho Parks and Recreation also runs six furnished backcountry yurts that are available for rent year round.
       </p>
       <p>
        Oh, and one last thing  no one comes to Idaho City without getting a slice of the incredible homemade pie at Trudys Kitchen. Go for it.
       </p>
