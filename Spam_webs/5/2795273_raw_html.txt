                      <hgroup class="post-title fix">
                       <h1 class="entry-title">
                        Ricotta with Honey and Herbs
                       </h1>
                      </hgroup>
                      <div class="metabar">
                       <div class="metabar-pad">
                        <em>
                         <span class="post-comments sc">
                          <a href="http://bakedbree.com/ricotta-with-honey-and-herbs#wp-comments">
                           <span class="num">
                            19
                           </span>
                           Comments
                          </a>
                         </span>
                         <time class="date time published updated sc" datetime="2013-05-17T08:00:50+00:00">
                          May 17, 2013
                         </time>
                        </em>
                       </div>
                      </div>
                     </section>
                    </section>
                   </section>
                   <div class="entry_wrap fix">
                    <div class="entry_content">
                     <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_8.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12837" height="840" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_8.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      I have been spending a lot of time lately leafing through
                      <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/073229049X?ie=UTF8&amp;creativeASIN=073229049X&amp;tag=bakbre04-20" target="_blank">
                       Seasons by Donna Hay
                      </a>
                      . It is my favorite cookbook right now, the recipes are simple and the photos beyond gorgeous. I found a recipe for ricotta with toasted brioche with vanilla honey and thought that it would be perfect for my
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/nachos-with-tropical-fruit-haberno-salsa" target="_blank" title="Nachos with Tropical Fruit Habanero Salsa">
                       Sargento Flavor Journey
                      </a>
                      . I knew that the addition of fresh thyme and rosemary would be perfect with the honey, and I was right. I have always loved ricotta in savory things like
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/veggie-lasagna" target="_blank" title="Veggie Lasagna">
                       lasagna
                      </a>
                      , but am just starting to appreciate it in
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/strawberry-basil-ice-cream" target="_blank" title="Strawberry Basil Ice Cream">
                       sweet things
                      </a>
                      , and even on its own. It is creamy, has a delicate taste, and a great vehicle for other flavors. This might be a humble looking appetizer (or dessert, brunch item, you really could eat this any time, for any meal) but I guarantee that you will make it time and time again. It is incredibly easy, and so flavorful.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      I bought a loaf of brioche at Whole Foods. Brioche is a very rich bread made with loads of butter and eggs. It reminds me of
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/no-knead-challah" target="_blank" title="No Knead Challah">
                       challah
                      </a>
                      , but even more decadent. If you cannot find brioche, you could
                      <a href="http://www.twopeasandtheirpod.com/brioche/" target="_blank">
                       make a loaf
                      </a>
                      , (which sort of defeats the idea of easy appetizer, but you could if you wanted to) or just use a really crusty baguette. The bread needs to be really good. When the ingredients are this simple, each one really needs to be high quality and delicious.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12841" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      1 cup
                      <a href="http://www.sargento.com/products/85/sargento-whole-milk-ricotta-cheese/" target="_blank">
                       Sargento Whole Milk Ricotta
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      1 cup water
                      <br/>
                      1/2 cup honey
                      <br/>
                      1
                      <a href="http://astore.amazon.com/bakbre04-20/detail/B000CPZSC8" target="_blank">
                       vanilla bean
                      </a>
                      <br/>
                      fresh thyme
                      <br/>
                      fresh rosemary
                      <br/>
                      brioche (or other delicious bread)
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_1.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12830" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_1.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      You could easily just put some ricotta in a beautiful bowl, but I wanted to see if I could shape the ricotta into a ball. Wrap the ricotta in a piece of cheesecloth.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_2.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12831" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_2.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      Wrap the cheesecloth around the ricotta and chill in the fridge for an hour or so.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_3.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12832" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_3.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      Slice the vanilla bean in half. Use the back of your knife to scrape the seeds out.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_4.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12833" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_4.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      Add the vanilla bean, honey, and water to a saucepan.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_5.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12834" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_5.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      Bring to a boil over medium heat. Boil for 12 to 15 minutes, it will get thick and syrupy.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_6.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12835" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_6.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      Let the syrup cool and strain out the vanilla bean. Chop rosemary and thyme.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_10.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12839" height="429" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_10.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      Sprinkle herbs over ricotta. Drizzle honey over ricotta.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      <a href="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_9.jpg">
                       <img alt="ricotta with honey and herbs recipe" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-12838" height="840" src="http://bakedbree.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/honeyed-ricotta_9.jpg" width="600"/>
                      </a>
                      Toast some brioche, and spread the cheese over the bread.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                     </p>
