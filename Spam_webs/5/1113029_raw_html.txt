                 <h1 class="story-bedge" style="text-align: center; font-size: 20px; margin-top: 20px;">
                  Travel in Style
                 </h1>
                 <h2 class="story-title" style="text-transform: capitalize !important; font-size: 34px; ">
                  Boutique Hotels by the Worlds Top Fashion Designers
                 </h2>
                </div>
               </div>
              </div>
              <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
               <div class="field-items">
                <div class="field-item even">
                 <p>
                  Some of the worlds most glamorous hotels literally have the fashion industrys stamp of approval. They wear major
                  <a href="http://www.lifestylemirror.com/life/food-drink/2014/03/19/443628243/dine-in-style-The-Chic-Rise-of-Designer-Owned-Restaurants/">
                   designer labels
                  </a>
                  . It's no surprise that the jetsetters who create our favorite fashion collections are just as adept at designing fantastical suites and hotel villas. Feeling whimsical? Go on a fairy tale adventure and visit the Maison Moschino hotel or remote island tipi by Alice Temperley. The luxurious minded will bask in the luxury of a Christian Lacroix-designed boutique hotel that offers a respite from the crowded streets of Paris.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  Peruse the gallery above to see 17 hotels and suites that were decorated by top fashion designers from Diane von Furstenberg and Ralph Lauren to
                  <a href="http://www.lifestylemirror.com/woman/fashion/2014/04/09/1366159818/pvh-invests-karl-lagerfeld/">
                   Karl Lagerfeld
                  </a>
                  .
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Hotel Missoni
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Rosita Missoni, one of the original founders of the iconic Italian brand, designed this 136-room hotel that opened in Edinburgh in 2006. The classic black and white color palette has splashes of the label's signature bold colors throughout. Rosita also took inspiration from her own home by including several pieces from designers she admires such as Marcel Wandes, Eero Sarrinen and Arne Jacobson. An outpost in Kuwait later followed.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Diane von Furstenberg, Claridges and Hayman Island Resort
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Diane von Furstenberg leant her creative genius and recipe for success to not one, but two hotels. Furstenberg has been a 30-year regular at Claridges in London where she custom-designed cashmere blankets, furniture and fabrics from DvFs exclusive textile collection for the Piano Suite. Across the globe, a two-bedroom suite at luxury hotel Hayman Island Resort is full of unique furnishings upholstered in her
                  <a href="http://www.lifestylemirror.com/woman/fashion/2014/01/14/802685223/diane-von-furstenberg-wrap-dress-anniversary/">
                   signature wrap-dress prints
                  </a>
                  . For a special touch, the room includes personal butler service.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Maison Moschino
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Much like the bold and
                  <a href="http://www.lifestylemirror.com/woman/fashion/2014/04/24/370574524/spring-2014-fashion-trends-logo-mania/">
                   quirky designs that Moschino presents
                  </a>
                  on its runways, the four-year old Moschino hotel aims to create a sense of escape and fantasy.The hotel, situated in Milans first railway station,was designed by previous creative director of the house Rossella Jardini and art director JoAnn Tan.The rooms were created with 15 different fairytale themes, such as Alices Room (Alice in Wonderland) and Little Red Riding Hood, each outfitted with special touches to transport the guest. Expect to see beds that transform into velvet gowns, covered with a cascade of rose petals or surrounded by trees and lamps in the shape of owls.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Dior Suite, St. Regis New York
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Inspired by the Dior ateliers in Paris, the St. Regis New Yorks Dior Suite is an exquisite tribute to one of Frances most beloved fashion houses. The 1,700 square-foot suite is outfitted in a subtle palette of greys and baby pinks. Couture-style fabrics such as lace, tulle and metallic leather are used as window coverings and upholstery. And for the perfect finishing touch, a watercolor by New York illustrator Bill Donovan depicting Dior gowns through the ages hangs in the living room.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Armani Hotel
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  With outposts in Dubai and Milan, the Armani hotels are much like the minimalist yet luxurious fashion label that Giorgio Armani created. The most recent location in Milan contains 95 rooms filled with high-tech amenities and special features. The bathrooms include one-way glass showers, the walls and floors are clad in silk georgette and a 24-hour lifestyle manager is appointed to each guest so that every wish and command may be filled.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Bottega Veneta Suite, St. Regis Rome
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  The St. Regis Grand Hotel Rome opened in 1894, but houses a modern aesthetic in the Bottega Veneta suite. Designed by the brands creative director, Thomas Maier, the three-bedroom suite is outfitted with pieces from the BV furniture collection and includes linen pillows, hand-carved Venetian glassware, cashmere throws and even signature room fragrances.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Alice Temperley Tipi at the One &amp; Only Le Saint Geran
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Alice Temperley is known for her romantic and whimsical bridal and ready-to-wear collections, so its fitting that her contribution to the hotel world is a hand-stitched tipi complete with interior chandelier and custom-made wooden floor. The dream-like structure is located at the One &amp; Only Le Saint Gran resort; a secluded getaway perched on its own private peninsula on the edge of the Indian Ocean in Mauritius.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Ralph Lauren, Round Hill Hotel
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Ralph Lauren is a longtime fan of Jamaica. In fact, he has owned a property at Round Hill Hotels &amp; Villas for over 20 years. Opened in 1953, Round Hill is comprised of 27 individually owned villas. The 36-room Pineapple House was recently re-designed by Lauren who became a co-owner of the hotel. He also masterminded the newly renovated restaurant and cocktail bar.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Palazzo Versace
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Versace opened the very first fully branded fashion hotelwith 272 rooms, a spa, three restaurants and private marina on Australias Gold Coast. The hotel is completely furnished with custom-made pieces in order to best represent the Versace heritage. Another outpost is located in Dubai.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Christian Lacroix, Hotel du Petit Moulin
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  The 16-room Hotel du Petit Moulin transports guests from the busy Paris streets to a playground of adventure through Christian Lacroix-designed spaces. Each room is decorated with its own distinct theme, from baroque palaces to a cacophony of prints.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Karl Lagerfeld, Schlosshotel Im Grunewald and Macau
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  A 270-room Karl Lagerfeld Hotel is slated to open its doors in Macau in 2017, but the iconic designer has already leant his hand to the hotel's interior design. The Schlosshotel Im Grunewald in Berlin, Germany is full of Lagerfeld-style rooms in sumptuous colors like deep purple, ruby red and moody grays. The hotel has an air of grandeur befitting for a favorite haunt of Josephine Baker and Romy Schnieder.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Bulgari Hotels
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Bulgari is known more for its fine jewels than hotels, but the luxury brand boasts four hotels worldwide with outposts in Bali, London, Shanghai and Milan. Wherever they are in the world, the hotels showcase Italian craftsmanship through custom-made silk curtains and handcrafted furnishings. A stunning infinity pool is a standout feature in Bali, while a focus on silver accentsin Londonpay tribute to Bulgaris origins as a silversmith.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Philip Treacy, The G Hotel
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  The G hotel in Galway, Ireland is just what youd expect from the incredibly imaginative milliner Philip Treacy. Riotous color, plenty of prints and dozens of expressive accessories decorate thehotel, which includes a spa, conference center and bespoke wedding venue. The space is so fashionable that one suite is named after one of the 90s most iconic supermodels, Linda Evangelista.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Diesel, UXUA Casa Hotel &amp; Spa
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Former Diesel creative director,Wilbert Das, is the owner and designer behind UXUA. The remote Brazilian fishing village of Trancoso houses 11 casas, which incorporate materials from local artisans into the luxurious settings. Bathtubs are created from fallen, hollowed out tree trunks and a swimming pool is lined with aventurine crystals, thought by locals to have healing powers.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Badgley Mischka, The Breakers Palm Beach
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  The Breakers Palm Beach holds a special place in the heartsof design duo Mark Badgley and James Mischka. They set about redesigning the hotels Imperial Suite with color palettes and fabrics inspired by the Badgley Mischka labelfor the room complete with not one but
                  <em>
                   five
                  </em>
                  balconies.
                 </p>
                 <p>
                  <strong>
                   Maison Martin Margiela, Maison Champs Elyses
                   <br/>
                  </strong>
                  Maison Martin Margiela is infamous for its all-white, minimalist aethestic with unique design aspects. La Maison Champs Elyses is no different. Redesigned by Margiela in 2009, the 57-room hotel is a tribute to artistic design and the beauty of simplicity. Its exemplified by a silver hallway with diamond lighting that serves as a focal point. The interior decorating version of Margielas harmonious contrasts with a surrealistic slant.
                 </p>
                </div>
