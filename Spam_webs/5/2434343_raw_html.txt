           <div class="content">
            <p dir="ltr">
             <span>
              Digital news publisher Linda Solomon Wood used the spotlight of her news team's win at the Canadian Journalism Foundation Awards gala tonight in Toronto, to announce plans to launch a new journalistic endeavour -- the
             </span>
             <em>
              National Observer
             </em>
             <span>
              .
             </span>
            </p>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              "Were starting a new national site called the National Observer which will focus on what we have been focusing on at VO, which we really know how to do well, which is energy politics, and energy and its impact on people and places, said an ecstaticSolomon from Toronto.
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
             </span>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              [After the award] people came up to me, and said that is so great. People love what we do at the VO, and there was excitement in the crowd about our next step."
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <img alt="Linda Solomon Wood Matt Millar Canadian Journalism Foundation Awards 2014 - laun" height="323" src="/sites/vancouverobserver.com/files/resize/images/article/body/Linda%20Solomon%20Wood%20Matt%20Millar%20Canadian%20Journalism%20Foundation%20Awards%202014%20-%20launch%20of%20National%20Observer-500x323.jpg" title="Linda Solomon Wood Matt Millar Canadian Journalism Foundation Awards 2014 - launch of National Observer" width="500"/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <h5>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              Observer Media Group publisher Linda Solomon Wood, and Ottawa correspondent Matt Millar at the Canadian Journalism Foundation awards gala in Toronto Wednesday night.
             </span>
            </h5>
            <div>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              The National Observer will build from its Vancouver base, and will have additional corespondents across Canada, including Toronto and Ottawa.
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
             </span>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              We are looking to go live [with the National Observer] in the fall, and we are working now on the development and design, and looking to build partnerships with other news organizations potentially. We are looking for investment money, and people who can help advise us to make this just an outstanding news site, said Solomon Wood.
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
             </span>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              Citing a statistic from the Canadian Association of Journalists, Solomon said more than 2,000 people in journalism have lost their jobs since 2012.
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
             </span>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              "We're seeking to change that."
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              She added, there are many imperatives that led to her decision to go national:
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
             </span>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              One is, journalism is in decline, and another is were facing some of the largest energy proposals - pipelines, LNG projects -- they are happening at an enormously rapid pace. At the same time, democracy is not able to respond quickly enough. In fact, it hasnt responded that well, and citizens are not having their voices heard.
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
            <p>
             <span>
              <span>
               Were also going to tell the story of exciting sustainability projects that are happening across Canada.
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             <span>
              Solomon Wood accepted the CJFs Excellence in Journalism Award for regional media on behalf of the Vancouver Observer.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p dir="ltr">
             <span>
              Others nominated for this award were:
             </span>
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              <a href="http://www.cbc.ca/edmonton/" rel="nofollow" style="line-height: 1.5em;" target="_blank">
               CBC
               <span class="xn-location">
                Edmonton
               </span>
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              <a href="http://www.cbc.ca/nb/" rel="nofollow" style="line-height: 1.5em;" target="_blank">
               CBC
               <span class="xn-location">
                New Brunswick
               </span>
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              <a href="http://globalnews.ca/calgary/" rel="nofollow" style="line-height: 1.5em;" target="_blank">
               Global
               <span class="xn-location">
                Calgary
               </span>
              </a>
             </li>
             <li>
              <em style="line-height: 1.5em;">
               <a href="http://www.echodefrontenac.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
                L'cho de
                <span class="xn-location">
                 Frontenac
                </span>
               </a>
              </em>
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p dir="ltr">
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              The Vancouver Observer broke the story about Chuck Strahl, who was sitting as both the chair of Canada's spy watchdog -- the Security Intelligence Review Committee -- and as a registered lobbyist for Enbridge.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              The news resulted in Strahls resignation.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              The reporting was principally by Matt Millar, the Parliamentary bureau chief in Ottawa for the Vancouver Observer.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              Vancouver Observer's Managing Editor
              <span>
               Jenny Uechi said:
              </span>
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              "This is a huge win for independent media
             </span>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              . All of our writers work incredibly hard to bring stories to the public and to give visibility to voices that aren't often heard."
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              "Especially in this time when Canada is undergoing so many changes, the reporting that journalists do is so important and I hope our readers will
             </span>
             <a href="https://www.vancouverobserver.com/membership/monthly?amount=Enter+Amount&amp;subscribe=" style="line-height: 1.5em;" target="_blank">
              <strong>
               help support our coverage
              </strong>
             </a>
             <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
              as we go national," she added.
             </span>
            </p>
            <p>
             The illustration of the new National Observer website was developed by
             <a href="http://www.codenamedesign.com/" target="_blank" title="Code Named Design">
              Codename Design
             </a>
             .
            </p>

              Press release
