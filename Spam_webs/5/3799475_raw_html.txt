    <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-leftRailSpanner" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
     <h1 class="grub-topOfColumn" itemprop="name" style="font-size: 27px;">
      Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread
     </h1>
     <img height="535" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Broccoli-Gouda-Stuffed-Cornbread-Main-790x535.jpg" width="790"/>
     <div class="yui3-g">
      <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-rail-well">
       <div class="grub-byline" style="margin-top: 1em;">
        Recipe by
        <a href="https://plus.google.com/113274240511284571445" rel="author">
         Jerry James Stone
        </a>
         January 17, 2013
       </div>
       <div class="grub-mainPostContent" itemprop="recipeInstructions" not-style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 23px;">
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="678" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-main/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-main" rel="attachment wp-att-1053">
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         We could all use a little more broccoli on our plates, right? Now, the vegetable is wonderful just on its own  or wonderful with a little lemon and butter  but the more places I can sneak in broccoli, the better! This isnt the first thing Ive snuck into a corn muffin. For example, it is great with an egg inside. But today is about broccoli.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="338" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/W7ePZ46P-Gw" width="600">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <div class="yui3-g" id="grub-recipeSummary">
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-left">
          <h2>
           Ingredients
          </h2>
          <ul id="grub-ingredients">
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup Buttermilk
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Tablespoons Unsalted Butter, melted and cooled
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Tablespoons Canola Oil
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 large
            <a href="http://cookingstoned.tv/ingredient/egg/">
             Eggs
            </a>
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup all-purpose Flour
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup coarse-ground yellow Corn Meal
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            4 Tablespoons Brown Sugar
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            2 Teaspoons Baking Powder
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon Baking Soda
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
             Teaspoon Salt
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Cup shredded Gouda, more for topping
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            12 florets Broccoli
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Water, for blanching the broccoli
           </li>
           <li itemprop="ingredients">
            Ice Water, for stopping the broccoli from cooking
           </li>
          </ul>
         </div>
         <div class="yui3-u" id="grub-recipeSummary-right">
          <h2>
           <a class="grub-button" href="http://cookingstoned.tv/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/printable/" target="_blank">
            <span class="grub-printIcon">
            </span>
            Printable View
           </a>
          </h2>
          <h2>
           Yield:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            12 cornbread muffins
           </span>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT40M" itemprop="totalTime"/>
          <h2>
           Total Time
           <div style="float: right;">
            40 minutes
           </div>
          </h2>
          <meta content="PT20M" itemprop="prepTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Prep time
           <div style="float: right">
            20 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <meta content="PT20M" itemprop="cookTime"/>
          <div style="margin-bottom: 3px;">
           Cook time
           <div style="float: right">
            20 minutes
           </div>
          </div>
          <h2>
           Oven Temperature:
           <span>
            400 F
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Recipe Types:
           </h2>
           Breads, Muffins
          </div>
          <div class="grub-recipeTaxonomyList">
           <h2>
            Main Ingredient:
           </h2>
           Broccoli
          </div>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F and bring a pot of water to boil.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="918" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/cornbread-ingredients/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/cornbread-ingredients" rel="attachment wp-att-1051">
          <img alt="Cornbread Ingredients" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1051" height="551" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Cornbread-Ingredients-600x551.jpg" title="Cornbread Ingredients" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         2. In a medium bowl, whisk together 1 cup of buttermilk, 3 tablespoons of melted and cooled butter, 3 tablespoons of canola oil, and 2 large eggs until well blended. Set aside.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/making-cornbread/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/making-cornbread" rel="attachment wp-att-1058">
          <img alt="Making Cornbread" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1058" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Making-Cornbread-600x399.jpg" title="Making Cornbread" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         3. In a large bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour,1 cup of course-ground yellow cornmeal, 4 tablespoons of brown sugar, 2 1/2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/4 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/cornbread-dry-ingredients/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/cornbread-dry-ingredients" rel="attachment wp-att-1057">
          <img alt="Cornbread Dry Ingredients" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1057" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Cornbread-Dry-Ingredients-600x399.jpg" title="Cornbread Dry Ingredients" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Pour the liquid ingredients into the dry and combine fully, then fold in 1 cup of shredded gouda. It is important to always go from wet to dry to avoid overworking your flour and making lumps. The other way can result in a rubbery dough, and unless you are making chew toys, we just dont want that!
        </p>
        <p>
         5. The water should be boiling now. Add 12 broccoli florets and blanch them for just a minute, until they turn bright green. Quickly strain them and transfer them to the ice bath. Once the florets have completely cooled, strain them and let em dry.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/broccoli/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/broccoli" rel="attachment wp-att-1052">
          <img alt="Broccoli" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1052" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Broccoli-600x399.jpg" title="Broccoli" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Put a heaping tablespoonful of batter in the bottom of each muffin case, then give the pan a good shake to spread the batter evenly in the bottoms of the cases.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/broccoli-stuffed-cornbread/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/broccoli-stuffed-cornbread" rel="attachment wp-att-1056">
          <img alt="Broccoli Stuffed Cornbread" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1056" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Broccoli-Stuffed-Cornbread-600x399.jpg" title="Broccoli Stuffed Cornbread" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="1255" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-pan/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-pan" rel="attachment wp-att-1055">
          <img alt="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread Pan" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1055" height="753" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Broccoli-Gouda-Stuffed-Cornbread-Pan-600x753.jpg" title="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread Pan" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Place a single floret in each muffin case, upright. Top the florets with batter, dividing it evenly between the cases. Top with even more Gouda cheese.
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the tops are golden and the cheese is melted and somewhat crispy. An inserted toothpick into the center of one muffin should come out clean when they are finished baking.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread" rel="attachment wp-att-1054">
          <img alt="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1054" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Broccoli-Gouda-Stuffed-Cornbread-600x399.jpg" title="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         9. Remove the pan from the oven and let cool for 5 minutes before transferring the muffins to a cooling rack.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="666" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-sliced/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-sliced" rel="attachment wp-att-1059">
          <img alt="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread Sliced" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1059" height="399" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Broccoli-Gouda-Stuffed-Cornbread-Sliced-600x399.jpg" title="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread Sliced" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         How amazing do these look cut open? Enjoy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a class="grub-popupPhotoLink" grub-popupphotoheight="1339" grub-popupphotopath="/recipe/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-cooling/" grub-popupphotowidth="1000" href="#photo/broccoli-gouda-stuffed-cornbread-cooling" rel="attachment wp-att-1050">
          <img alt="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread Cooling" class="alignnone size-grub-wellWidth wp-image-1050" height="803" itemprop="image" src="http://cookingstoned.tv/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Broccoli-Gouda-Stuffed-Cornbread-Cooling-600x803.jpg" title="Broccoli &amp; Gouda Stuffed Cornbread Cooling" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
