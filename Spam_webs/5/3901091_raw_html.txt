       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Patriotic Pinwheel Wreath
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2014-06-12T08:30:30+00:00">
         June 12, 2014
        </span>
        by
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/author/ems1976/" rel="author">
           Emily
          </a>
         </span>
        </span>
        
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/2014/06/patriotic-pinwheel-wreath/#comments">

         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <div class="wwsgd" style="display:none;">
         <p style="border:thin dotted black; padding:3mm;">
          If you're new here, you may want to subscribe to my
          <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/feed/">
           RSS feed
          </a>
          . Thanks for visiting!
         </p>
        </div>
        <p>
         Its less than a month to the Fourth of July. Can you believe it? Do you have patriotic decorations ready? Honestly, I dont. Ive just gotten started. Ive got a couple of projects in the works, and the one that Im sharing today is my first completed patriotic craft of the season. Of course, its a wreath because as Mellow Man tells everyone we may not be completely decorated around here, but we have a wreath for every holiday! For this Fourth of July, I made a wreath with chevron burlap ribbon and felt pinwheels. It was a quick and easy way to add to my holiday wreath collection, and Im delighted with how it turned out. I think I need to make a few more pinwheels for decorations, but Im really enjoying my Patriotic Pinwheel Wreath.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-Title.jpg">
          <img alt="My Mom Made That: Patriotic Pinwheel Wreath" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3993" height="1024" src="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-Title-731x1024.jpg" width="731"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         <span id="more-3987">
         </span>
         I purchased the red chevron burlap ribbon at
         <a href="http://www.pickyourplum.com/" target="_blank">
          Pick Your Plum
         </a>
         , and I used it to wrap a Styrofoam wreath form, securing it with straight pins. I use the smallest pins, and pin the back around the edges of the ribbon, so that the pins are barely visible.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-1.jpg">
          <img alt="My Mom Made That: Patriotic Pinwheel Wreath" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3989" height="731" src="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-1-1024x731.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
         The next step was to make the pinwheels. I used stiffened felt that is available for approximately $1 at any local craft store. I wanted something that was going to maintain the sharp points of the pinwheel, so I felt the stiffened felt would work better than regular craft felt.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-3.jpg">
          <img alt="My Mom Made That: Patriotic Pinwheel Wreath" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3991" height="731" src="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-3-1024x731.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I started by cutting a square of stiffened felt. The stiffened felt helps the lines look crisp and the pinwheel look good. It doesnt have to be perfect, but try to cut as close to a square a possible. I made two 3-inch squares and one 5-inch square for this project. After I cut the square out, I drew two diagonal lines across the square. I then cut towards the center, stopping 1/4 to 1/2 from the center point.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-4.jpg">
          <img alt="My Mom Made That: Patriotic Pinwheel Wreath" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3992" height="731" src="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-Wreath-4-1024x731.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I folded the left-hand side of the cut to the center point, and secured them all in place with a glue dot. Once the four pieces were attached to the center, I used a red, adhesive, scrapbooking embellishment in the center to cover up the glue dots. Then I simply played with the 3 pinwheels until I positioned them in a way that was pleasing to me, and I attached them to the wreath with my hot glue gun.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-5.jpg">
          <img alt="My Mom Made That: Patriotic Pinwheel Wreath" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-3988" height="731" src="http://mymommadethat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Patriotic-Pinwheel-5-1024x731.jpg" width="1024"/>
         </a>
         Thats it! The project was so fast and fun. I love it when I can make a craft in under an hour. There is such a feeling of satisfaction and accomplishment when I do that! How about you?
        </p>
        <p>
