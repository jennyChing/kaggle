            <div class="early_access_review tooltip" data-tooltip-content="This review was written while Primal Carnage: Extinction was marked as Steam Early Access">
             Early Access Review
            </div>
            <div class="content">
             <b>
              Simply put, the game disappoints me.
             </b>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Primal Carnage, back in 2012. I played the **** out of that game.
             <br/>
             Loads of people playing meant loads of fun times.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Then I heard they were doing this massive update to their game sold as a whole new game. Fine by me.
             <br/>
             I even got this game cheap in Early Access for just 1 euro.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Now sadly, with Primal Carnage: Extinction... I fear those aforementioned fun times will never even come in Extinction.
             <br/>
             There's a couple of reasons I personally find to blame, so let's have a look:
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div class="bb_h1">
              Playerbase part 1: Sheer number
             </div>
             That's right, I'm partially downvoting an Early Access-game for having too few people playing. Because it shouldn't be
             <i>
              this
             </i>
             few people playing. I live in Western Europe, there should be plenty of servers and plenty of people playing, at least at prime hour.
             <br/>
             Instead, at whatever time I pick to go online, there's merely 4 servers below 100 ping and if I'm lucky, 1 of those will be slightly populated.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div class="bb_h1">
              Playerbase part 2: Fanboys
             </div>
             I don't even mean to call them
             <i>
              fanboys
             </i>
             . But there's just no proper term. Loyalists? Roleplayers?
             <br/>
             Whatever they're called, I'm talking about a serious problem concerning this game's longevity.
             <br/>
             Out of those few playable servers, 90% are 24/7 Free Roam servers. A game mode where literally nothing happens and players just troll and walk around.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I'll never understand why people would prefer this game mode over actual action but honestly preference isn't the issue. I wouldn't have cared if most players prefer Free Roam while I could still play Team Deathmatch or Get To The Chopper (objective-styled game mode) with the few other players.
             <u>
              But I can't.
             </u>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Because 4 out of those 4 playable servers are stuck on Free Roam. If I at all even want to play something else I'd have to join a laggy servers from a different continent.
             <br/>
             Primal Carnage: Extinction is one of those few games where the "core game mode" is the least played one, yet in this particular case I just don't know why.
             <br/>
             Personally? To hell with Free Roam.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div class="bb_h1">
              Playerbase part 3: Immaturity
             </div>
             Hey, what can I say? Young people love dinosaurs.
             <br/>
             Young adult gamers occasionally like them too; I've always adored them since my childhood.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             But this is different. The kids in this game, man. They get to you. It's the same as jumping into a McDonald's ball pit and expecting to encounter calm, relaxed people enjoying a beer and playing cards.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Nope. I hate to belittle the playerbase as a whole because of a select "few" but in this case... there is no "few", only "everyone".
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Another sense of immaturity shows itself in how far people are willing to go for cosmetic items. There's a ton of skins in this game for both Humans and Dinosaurs. As I'm sure you can guess, some are more difficult to come by than others, although they're all randomly obtained by just playing on particular servers.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             There's also marketplace-support for this game where people can sell their duplicate skins. They pick their own prices.
             <br/>
             Needless to say, these got ridiculous pretty fast. Guess how much some people ask for the rarest skins?
             <br/>
             <br/>
             No really, guess. It's fun.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             You want to know? I found something called a "Falcon Novaraptor" for sale for
             <b>
              <span class="bb_spoiler">
               <span>
                317,53
               </span>
              </span>
             </b>
             .
             <br/>
             <b>
              Are you kidding me?!
             </b>
             That's what happens when you let the community pick their prices. You get trolls and just plain idiots taking advantage of people either too young or too ignorant, willing to pay these kinds of cash for cosmetic items.
             <br/>
             Of course there's plenty of cheap stuff there, too. 16 cents for a Raptor skin. That's amazing compared to most overpriced DLC nonsense in 2015 games if you ask me. But there's also some rarer skins for 42 on average. Ridiculous.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Now keep in mind I'm fully blaming the community here, not the developers. Although admittedly, they are the ones encouraging it and are too selling bundles and crates for a tad too much money. Albeit not comparable to 42 bucks.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I haven't played LoL or TF2 to know anything about how it's handled in these games, but I'm just objectively looking at Extinction. And it sickens me.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div class="bb_h1">
              Little improvement
             </div>
             Primal Carnage: Extinction has very little to offer over the original Primal Carnage, both games I consider practically dead judging from the amount of player at peek time. This game divived the community with the actual differences Extinction has to offer hardly worth it.
             <br/>
             Extinction was, at Early Access launch, very different from the original Primal Carnage. Changed HP values, speeds, animation "improvements", etc.
             <br/>
             And with every patch that came along, the game slowly took a step back towards Primal Carnage.
             <br/>
             At launch, the Ptera's grab animation behaved differently. Now, it's the same as in the original game.
             <br/>
             At launch, Human stamina values were much lower. Now, they're almost on par with the original game.
             <br/>
             2 general examples, I assume the full list of comparisons to be rather long for in a Steam game review.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             The game tried to approach everything differently but instead ended up realizing the original Primal Carnage had most things just right from the start.
             <br/>
             <u>
              Extinction didn't
              <b>
               improve
              </b>
              on anything. It just implemented
              <b>
               changes
              </b>
              solely for the sake of change, nothing more.
             </u>
             <br/>
             And, oh yeah, it got skins. Too many of them. Read below.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div class="bb_h1">
              Wrong priorities
             </div>
             I get that game development companies have different sectors of people not all working at the same part. You got people who do the 3d modelling, people who draw the 2d textures, people who write the AI, etc.
             <br/>
             Nonetheless, some of those 3d modellers and texture drawers should've
             <b>
              stopped
             </b>
             what they're doing to occasionally help out the other developers.
             <br/>
             Because Extinction now suffers from a problem very common in today's gaming industries: A very,
             <b>
              very
             </b>
             poor first impression.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             "Hey, the team select HUD broke."
             <br/>
             "Hmm, my FPS takes a huge dip from this irrelevant setting."
             <br/>
             "Wait, why did I get stuck in the wall?"
             <br/>
             <br/>
             And then:
             <br/>
             <br/>
             "What the...? THE GAME IS RIDDLED WITH BUGS BUT YOU STILL FOUND TIME FOR ALL THIS DLC?"
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I know it sounds harsh but that's the way it seems (and
             <u>
              should
             </u>
             seem) to newcomers. The game has at least as many, probably more, bugs as your typical Early Access / newly released game and yet it offers cosmetic DLC as far as the eye can see.
             <br/>
             Let's pretend that doesn't seem greedy at all. Nah...
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <br/>
             <div class="bb_h1">
              Final "verdict"
             </div>
             Don't buy. While my review offers mostly personal opinion, it does offer truth about at least 1 thing very vital to playing a multiplayer game: lack of people actually playing.
             <br/>
             If you buy this game because you want a Humans VS Dinosaurs match then you very likely won't even be getting one. There's a lack of servers, lack of players and no offline play / bot support whatsoever.
             <br/>
             (Funnily enough, it's still the best Humans VS Dinosaurs game out there, but even if you desperately crave that sort of game I still strongly recommend saving your money.)
             <br/>
             <br/>
             As much as I loved the original Primal Carnage, I can't recommend Extinction. Both games can be declared practically dead following the standards of multiplayer-only games.
             <br/>
             I will look back at the ol' days in Primal Carnage with fondness. It's a shame it'll be just that; a memory.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Either way, I wish both Circle Five Studios and LukeWarm the best of luck on their next project. I genuinely hope they'll figure out the best way to approach marketing and gain more success that time.

            <div class="content">
             I would NOT recommend this game. There are so many things I want to cover. I know this game is still in the early stages and a lot can be/will be changed, this is my opinion of the game right now and where it stands. This review is mostly on the loot system, crates, and skins. I do not focus on the gameplay because there are so many issues with balancing and such.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Speaking of which about how this game feels like it's predecessor and maybe even worse. There isn't much difference to be honest, some may argue, "But they made a whole new game from SCRATCH." Yes, this is true but it feels the same only minor changes, and changes that may be for the worse depending on your opinions. Please, add in new skins, maps, loot system, secondary weapons, but in the end it is basically the same game. If you took all those items and put them into the old Primal Carnage that would basically be a sum of Primal Carnage Extinction. Honestly I feel somewhat ripped off. The devs claimed that loot would be free yet I have to go to the market and buy a key to open this crate. First off the keys are way too expensive, think about it, each key costs basically $2 (for me it's $2.30 CAN) so then you have ten crates, so 10x2=20, *Goes out and buys keys* So I've just spent $20 on keys ALONE, and for what? Another skin that I already have, or you are like me who opens four crates in a row and gets Sandstorm Ptera for each one. Some of you may say, "Well it's the factor of chance and you are not likely to get anything good." Well good sir if I open even three crates in a row wouldn't my chances change? I find it rather odd that I just got four Sandstorm Pteras in a row, don't you think?
             <br/>
             <br/>
             The loot system is ruining the game from its intended purpose. No, I'm not saying, "The loot system is terrible they should get rid of it!" I don't think they should get rid of it, I feel as if they should make it so keys are in the loot so that the younger players can be apart of opening crates as well. Also I wish that this game would give you the option to buy most if not all the skins like in the first game. I think it would be best honestly but this is my own opinion. Loot just seems to be the main focus in the game, sure other servers are fun too but if you look at how many people are in certain servers you will most likely see that loot servers seem to be the ones with most people. The loot isn't even that good, I have spent time on these "Loot Servers" it was terrible, it took about 5-8 matches with a server filled with 10 people to get any loot. Yes. I know that the more people on the server means more loot but seriously the odds are almost the same if you think about it. Every round about 2-4 items drop on a populated server but keep in mind that there are 20 other players who could get those items and the chances of you getting them are slim. I just wish this game would get back to it's purpose. Slowly I am getting bored with this game, why you may ask? All everyone talks about is the loot and it's sickening. I have had countless times where I would be on the server where I am an admin and someone would come on to say this, "Guys there is lots of loot on the one server *player leaves*" Then soon after 2 to 4 players would leave as well saying, "Ah yes loot, maybe I'll get something good this time!"
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Please bring back the option (from the old game) to buy skins maybe not all of them but at least some, it would still contribute to the creators and help pay for things. It just feels as if the devs aren't looking out for the younger crowd with the whole idea of buying keys to open crates. A lot of people would agree that the DLC option is good because then we would know what we were buying instead of taking these "chances."
             <br/>
             <br/>
             I went ahead and asked my friends who play Primal Carnage Extinction. My question was, "What do you think about the whole loot system?" (I have kept my contacts anonymous) To which one replied, "Oh my. Its a mess." The other said, "It's dumb, a waste of money." I could keep going on asking my friends, I could very well continue this. Obviously some people are happy with the way that it is, I don't see why when you have to pay for keys. The one who I asked the question and he said he liked the system, I then proceed to ask him about buying keys to which he replied, "That I don't like." I feel as if this is enough to make my point.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             Now I'm going to talk about the skins. They are terrible. It seems as if no effort was put into most of them, there are only a few that look at least somewhat effort was done on them. Honestly look at the Green Longhorn Carno, it's basically the Bloodhorn just coloured over in blue/green, nothing else. Most people can agree with me that most of the reskins are lousy. Just to prove myself I asked a few, I asked, "What do you think of the skins?" To which they replied, " Some are good but things like the snow ovi are just a bit plain." "Horrible" Another said this, " I think there kind of, Eh, I mean look at all the other skins they have pretty colors and stripes spikes all amazing, but the reskin of ovis just looks like they got jewled and rust ovi and made them green,grey, no excitment." The other friend that I know also had something to say about the skins which I agree on, "I think they're mostly just recolorations of the same skins. It just doesn't seem like they put much effort into most of them." I couldn't agree more, yes some skins do look nice but others were just painted over. I wish we wouldn't see anymore skins like the Snow Ovi, Green Longhorn Carno, Eagle Ovi, or the Sandstorm Ptera. Put a little effort into it? Yes, I know it costs money to make these skins but it just seems that they put in the minimum effort, they at least tried for the Flaming Dart Dilo.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             The game itself isn't bad, yes it's in the early stages and there will be bugs and glitches for some time. Some work needs to be done on the FPS drops from dilos and cryos but besides that it isn't that bad. Also on a side note I wish admining would be easier, sometimes I wish to kick a player and I then have to go to sign in, type in AdminPlayerList, then find their number and type AdminKick (players #). It's such a pain, also it would be nice if there was a timer after kicking someone, I do not wish to ban people since it really isn't allowed for the server I am admin on. What ends up happening is I will kick someone for being offensive, which in return they keep joining after I kick them. A timer would be an improvement to say the least. This is just something I wanted to point out.
             <br/>
             <br/>
             My final thought is that I wish they did not create a whole new game but keep the old one and update it. I know they had reasons to make a whole new game but I feel that the new game is just a money grabber, sucking people dry, yes they should have been careful with their money but look at it this way, "Oh my legendary falcon might be in this crate, well I could try once *opens, gets emerald ovi* one more try." It's almost as if the crates are false hope, it wouldn't be as bad if the keys were in the loot, but the fact is they aren't. Honestly the only reason I am playing Primal Carnage Extinction is because of the new maps and skins, if the old game had that I wouldn't be playing PCE. As always this is just my opinion, who knows maybe my thoughts will change on this game but this is my review as it stands. Take it or leave it, your choice.

The loot system has also been improving, especially now that their is a new member of the dev team to make cool skins.  Of course, there are still bugs and balancing issues, but these should be fixed over time, and are largely the results of a whiny community.  In the end, if you were a fan of the original, and you have patience, I would definitely reccomend Primal Carnage: Extinction.
