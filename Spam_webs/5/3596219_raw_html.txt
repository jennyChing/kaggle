       Abbott and Costello loafing script
      </h1>
      <div class="postAuthor">
       By
       <span class="vcard author post-author">
        <span class="fn">
         <a href="http://freeclownskits.info/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by Tom Raymond">
          Tom Raymond
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       on
       <span class="post-date updated">
        Tuesday, January 21st, 2014
       </span>
       |
       <a href="http://freeclownskits.info/abbott-and-costello-skits/abbott-costello-loafing-script/#respond">
        No Comments
       </a>
      </div>
      <div id="socratesinpostad" style="float:right; display:block; padding:5px; margin:0;">
       <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
       </script>
       <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-7025657422597955" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="8734563368" style="display:block">
       </ins>

        (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
       </script>

      <div class="entry">
       <p>
        A classic
        <a href="http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/', 'Abbott and Costello']);" target="_blank">
         Abbott and Costello
        </a>
        routine, where
        <a href="http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/lou-costello-biography/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/lou-costello-biography/', 'Lou Costello']);" target="_blank">
         Lou Costello
        </a>
        misunderstands that by loafing 
        <a href="http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/bud-abbott-biography/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/bud-abbott-biography/', 'Bud Abbott']);" target="_blank">
         Bud Abbott
        </a>
        means that hes making loaves of bread in a bakery; not loafing around the house like Costello! Taken from their classic television series,
        <a href="http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/the-abbott-costello-show-the-complete-series-collectors-edition/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://abbott-and-costello-whos-on-first.info/the-abbott-costello-show-the-complete-series-collectors-edition/', 'The Abbott and Costello Show']);" target="_blank" title="The Abbott and Costello Show">
         <em>
          The Abbott and Costello Show
         </em>
        </a>
        .
        <span id="more-514">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         <a href="http://freeclownskits.info/wp-content/uploads/bud-abbott-lou-costello-loafing.png">
          <img alt="Bud Abbott and Lou Costello - the loafing skit" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-520" height="209" src="http://freeclownskits.info/wp-content/uploads/bud-abbott-lou-costello-loafing-300x209.png" width="300"/>
         </a>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : I had to get a job! You dont want to work!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Whatre you doing?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : I got a job at a bakery.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Good! Whatre you doing there?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Loafing.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : [
        <em>
         reacts
        </em>
        ] Loafing?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Loafing!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Where?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : In a bakery.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : You working?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Well, certainly.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Doing what?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Loafing!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Thats what I was doing here, I was taking it easy 
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : No, no, no, not that kind of loafing. Youre just a lazy idiot.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : I was taking it easy, same as you.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : I work when I loaf!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : How can you work and loaf at the same time?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Why not?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Can they do that?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Thats what they pay me for!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : They pay your for what?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : To loaf!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : [aggravated] How do they pay you to loaf?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : I work for the baker, Im loafing there.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : You loaf, you dont do a thing! How much do they pay you at the baker store?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : A dollar and a half an hour.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : For loafing
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Im a union man! I belong to the union!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Im loafing here, and I dont get nothing for it!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Youre not supposed to get money for that kind of loafing!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Can I loaf there with you?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : I should say not; youve got to join the union.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : [
        <em>
         confused
        </em>
        ] I gotta join a union of loafers?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : You cant loaf without belonging to the union.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Well, what do you think of that? You mean I gotta join a union in order to loaf?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Well, certainly!
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Dont say nothin but I was loafing here the union knowin.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : No, you werent loafing here, you cant loaf here! You cant loaf here, youve got no dough.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : I got no dough? Thats why Im loafing, because I dont got no dough.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : No, no, you got dough you could loaf. Then youd have to get a card and join the union.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : You mean, youve gotta have dough to loaf?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Well sure, how can you loaf without dough?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : Thats very hard, Ive been trying to do it.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : You cant do anything.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : I cant retire right now, Ive got to have a job.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Some day come down to the bakery, Ill show you how to loaf. I do real loaf. In fact, you know, Ive never told you this, my whole family were loafers.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : No kidding.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Sure, my father was a bigger loafer than me.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : He was?
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : Yeah, he was one of the best loafers in the family.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : I think youre a better loafer than your father.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Bud Abbott
        </strong>
        : I know that now, because he taught me how to loaf.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         Lou Costello
        </strong>
        : You know youre a bigger loafer than your father?
       </p>
