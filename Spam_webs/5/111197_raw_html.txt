                <article about="/whats-on/exhibition/duchamp-man-ray-picabia" class="node node-exhibition node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-maggie-hills even clearfix" id="node-exhibition-237201" typeof="sioc:Item foaf:Document">

                  <div class="view view-exhibition-second-title view-id-exhibition_second_title view-display-id-entity_view_1 view-dom-id-b17781d9df33e726e2129ebd3647a8ed">
                   <div class="view-content">
                    <div class="views-row views-row-1 views-row-odd views-row-first views-row-last">
                     <div class="title">
                      <span class="field-content">
                       Duchamp, Man Ray, Picabia
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="field-subtitle">
                      <div class="field-content">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="field-rooms">
                      <div class="field-content">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="field-venues">
                      <div class="field-content">
                       Tate Modern: Exhibition
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="field-date-exhibition">
                      <div class="date-tate-render date-from-view">
                       21 February  26 May 2008
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="field-booking-advice">
                      <div class="field-content">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div class="field-annoucement">
                      <div class="field-content">
                      </div>
                     </div>
                    </div>
                   </div>
                  </div>
                  <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
                   <div class="field-items">
                    <div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded">
                     <p>
                      This revelatory exhibition brings together three of the greatest figures of twentieth-century art:
                      <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/artists/marcel-duchamp">
                       Marcel Duchamp
                      </a>
                      , the father of
                      <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/art/artworks?gm=421&amp;ap=1&amp;wp=1&amp;wv=grid">
                       Conceptual
                      </a>
                      art and creator of the readymade;
                      <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/artists/man-ray">
                       Man Ray
                      </a>
                      , an artist who produced some of the most celebrated photographs of the modern era; and
                      <a href="http://www.tate.org.uk/artists/francis-picabia">
                       Francis Picabia
                      </a>
                      , a flamboyant French painter famous for defying the conventions of goodtaste.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Duchamp, Man Ray and Picabia were the leading figures in the New York Dada movement, rebelling against the norms of traditional art. They became friends and, as the exhibition shows, their friendship influenced the direction of their lives and their art. A powerful intellectual chemistry allowed them to respond freely to each others ideas in an artistic dialogue filled with in-jokes andinnuendos.
                     </p>
                     <p>
                      Focusing on periods of collaboration and highlighting their common interests,
                      <em>
                       Duchamp, Man Ray, Picabia
                      </em>
                      explores the intriguing connections between these three pioneering figures. This major exhibition includes many icons of modern art. It features
                      <em>
                       Duchamps
                       <a href="/node/253723">
                        Nude Descending a Staircase (No 2)
                       </a>
                      </em>
                      1912, which caused a furore when displayed in New York, his famous
                      <a href="/node/186081">
                       <em>
                        Fountain
                       </em>
                      </a>
                      1917, and a parody of the
                      <em>
                       Mona Lisa
                      </em>
                      ,
                      <em>
                       <span class="caps">
                        L.H.O.O.Q.
                       </span>
                      </em>
                      1919. Also included are Man Rays innovative experiments with the camera, such as his Rayographs and early films, as well as Picabias early abstract paintings and late eroticnudes.
                     </p>
                    </div>
                   </div>
