         <h1 class="title single-title">
          How To Let It Be When Youre Upset
         </h1>
         <!-- End Title -->
         <!-- Start Post Meta -->
         <div class="post-info">
          <span class="theauthor">
           <a href="http://www.the-open-mind.com/author/jadesmall/" rel="nofollow" title="Posts by Jade Small">
            Jade Small

         <p>
          <a href="http://www.the-open-mind.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/angry-woman.jpg">
           <img alt="angry-woman" class="alignnone wp-image-1580" data-wp-pid="1580" height="332" src="http://www.the-open-mind.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/angry-woman.jpg" width="445"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Something has you upset. You cant get it off your mind. Youre troubled and you struggle with this ball of bad feelings. Whats the advice we frequently hear from others and even from ourselves? Just let it go. Well, thats easier said than done.
         </p>
         <p>
          Just let it go implies a sort of active forgetting. Youve probably tried the experiment, Dont think of a pink elephant. Once you have that in your mind, you simply cant forget it. The same is true with grief, anger, or other strong distressing emotions.
          <span id="more-530" style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong style="font-style: inherit;">
           Let It Be
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Another approach to consider is to Let It Be. What does this mean? Im sure this can mean different things to different people, but what it means to me is to stay present with your uncomfortable feelings instead of running or hiding from them. Try to really feel those difficult emotions. Observe them. Dont fear them or seek to run from them. Instead endeavor to live with them and learn from them, knowing that the time you spend doing this will help to heal you. When you do this with serenity, as much as you can muster, you will discover your strength and over time you will notice the bad feelings start to soften and melt.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong style="font-style: inherit;">
           Take Your Time
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Depending on the circumstances, this can take quite a bit of time or it can work in an afternoon. The main thing to remember is not to rush it. Have faith and know that things will get better. Be patient with yourself and with the process.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong style="font-style: inherit;">
           Guide to Being Present
          </strong>
          <br/>
          As you work to be present with your challenging emotions, here are some questions and affirmations to guide you:
         </p>
         <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
         <div style="float:right;margin:10px 0 10px 10px;">
          <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <!-- ToM Mid -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-1375666127335643" data-ad-slot="2123132213" style="display:inline-block;width:336px;height:280px">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <ul style="color: #000000;">
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           What am I really feeling right now? What is the essence of this feeling?
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           As an observer, this is interesting.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           How would an outsider describe this?
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Why do I want to run away from this?
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Why does this feel bad?
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           What can I do to change my perspective of this?
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           What can I learn from these feelings?
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           I can handle this.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           I can be at peace with this emotion passing through me.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Im not running from this and Im also not attached to it.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           There is something to be learned from this.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           There is purpose in this. What is that purpose?
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           I know the purpose will eventually be revealed.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           I am being strengthened by this.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           I am strong.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Its OK to feel this way. I dont need to run away.
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           This will morph into goodness in the long run.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong style="font-style: inherit;">
           Examples of When Let It Be Might Be Helpful
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ul style="color: #000000;">
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Quitting smoking or other addictive substance
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Feeling anger after a conflict or fight
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Feeling grief after losing a loved one
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Disappointment
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Jealousy
          </li>
          <li style="font-weight: inherit; font-style: inherit;">
           Sadness
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          <strong style="font-style: inherit;">
           Living Life
          </strong>
          <br/>
          Let It Be means being present with your difficult feelings, but it also means living life. A big part of healing is to live life, to be active, and engaged. If you are in the throes of grief, obviously you should take things slowly. Listen to what your body and soul tell you to do. But, do try, in small ways at first, to be active. This is not to drown out your feelings, but rather to give you the physical strength and the mental perspective that will help you be present with your feelings and heal. Its a balance. Take the time to analyze and also take the time to live life. Wishing you peace.
         </p>
         <p>
         </p>
