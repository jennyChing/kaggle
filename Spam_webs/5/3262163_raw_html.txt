          <h1 class="sports-postheader entry-title" itemprop="name">
           Top ten cities Tampa Bay Rays should consider for relocation
          </h1>
          <div class="metas roboto">
           <ul class="aut">
            <li class="left puplish">
             <i class="fa fa-clock-o">

             <div>
              <p>
               The Tampa Bay Rays are not a viable franchise on the Florida Gulf Coast. The Rays
               <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/leagues/MLB/2013-misc.shtml" target="_blank">
                attendance lags well behind the rest of the League
               </a>
               . They have the worst stadium in baseball and nobody is interested in building a new one. Commissioner Bud Selig seems to have no interest in addressing the issue as a lame duck commish, punting the decision to his successor. Also, there are probably more fans of the Rays Al East rival New York Yankees thanks to the preponderance of retirees from the northeast within the greater Tampa area in combination with proximity of the Yankees spring training home as well as its Single A minor league team, the Tampa Yankees. They need out.
              </p>
              <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_19703" style="width: 585px">
               <a href="http://i2.wp.com/sports-kings.com/readingbetweentheseams/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/110610tampabayrays.jpg">
                <img alt="Rays fans show no interest in watching a good team Credit: thegoodpoint.com" class="wp-image-19703" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/sports-kings.com/readingbetweentheseams/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/110610tampabayrays.jpg?resize=575%2C316"/>
               </a>
               <p class="wp-caption-text">
                Rays fans, sitting in a bad venue, show no interest in supporting a good team
                <br/>
                Credit: thegoodpoint.com
               </p>
              </div>
              <p>
               But there arent that many ideal locations left now that sports market hot-spots like Phoenix, Washington, and Denver all have successful franchises (where the league didnt have teams as late as 1990).
               <a href="http://sports-kings.com/readingbetweentheseams/" target="_blank">
                Reading Between the Seams
               </a>
               presents ten potential locations, some inside-the-box, some outside-the-box. All would make sense and most would open up a new avenue of fans to the league. And if its not the Rays, then any team should consult this list for pros and cons.
              </p>
             </div>
