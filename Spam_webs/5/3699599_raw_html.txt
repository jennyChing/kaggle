        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Vanilla Coconut  Ice Box Cake
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-03-20T06:30:00+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          March 20, 2015
         </time>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.lemonsforlulu.com/vanilla-coconut-ice-box-cake/#comments">
           23 Comments 
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          This simple
          <strong>
           ice box
          </strong>
          cake features light and creamy
          <strong>
           coconut
          </strong>
          filling that is resting between layers of graham crackers. This no-bake dessert couldnt be easier!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.lemonsforlulu.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Vanilla-Coconut-Ice-Box-Cake.jpg">
          <img alt="This vanilla coconut ice bake cake is such an easy no-bake treat!  Its rich and creamy too! lemonsforlulu.com #IDelight @InDelight" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18582" height="900" src="//www.lemonsforlulu.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Vanilla-Coconut-Ice-Box-Cake.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         I have been wanting to try my hand at an ice box cake for quite some time. Having a cake made with graham crackers that sets up in the refrigerator is quite intriguing. As I set out to make this simple cake however, it struck me how old-fashioned the word ice box actually is. I am so dependent upon my appliances and I cannot fathom that not so long ago,people used an ice box instead of a fridge.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         My own kids wont think twice about the term ice box. They cant even believe there was ever a time without cell phones and wi-fi. Rotary phones, phone booths, cassette tapes, dvds and typewriter ribbons are part of my own past, but dont even resonate with my kids. Things change so quickly, I cannot fathom what changes are yet to come.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <img alt="This vanilla coconut ice box cake is light and absolutley dreamy!  A no bake treat that tastes purely decadent! lemonsforlulu.com #IDelight @InDelight" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18585" height="900" src="//www.lemonsforlulu.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Vanilla-Coconut-Ice-Box-Cake-4.jpg" width="600"/>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         One thing that never changes is our love affair with dessert. Some days I crave
         <a href="http://www.lemonsforlulu.com/guinness-chocolate-bundt-cake/" target="_blank">
          dense cakes
         </a>
         with rich frosting,
         <a href="http://www.lemonsforlulu.com/molasses-cookies-maple-mascarpone-frosting/" target="_blank">
          buttery cookies
         </a>
         or thick
         <a href="http://www.lemonsforlulu.com/chocolate-peanut-butter-brownies/" target="_blank">
          brownies
         </a>
         . Sometimes all I want is an easy, light dessert. Today, I was craving the latter which is why I decided it was time tofinallygive an ice box cake a try. The ingredients inthese cakes can vary quite a bit. The cake layer can be made out of chocolate wafer cookies, chocolate chip cookies or graham crackers. The filling can be made with heavy cream, custard or pudding. As you can see the possibilities are endless. For this recipe I used graham crackers, pudding,
         <a href="http://www.internationaldelight.com/products/fat-free-sugar-free-french-vanilla" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          International Delights Fat Fee
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.internationaldelight.com/products/fat-free-sugar-free-french-vanilla" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          FrenchVanilla Creamer
         </a>
         , neufschatel, and lite whipped topping.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://www.lemonsforlulu.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Vanilla-Coconut-Ice-Box-Cake-3.jpg">
          <img alt="This vanilla coconut ice bake cake is such an easy no-bake treat!  Its rich and creamy too! lemonsforlulu.com #IDelight @InDelight" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-18584" height="900" src="//www.lemonsforlulu.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Vanilla-Coconut-Ice-Box-Cake-3.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         The graham crackers soften as this dessert sets up and forms a cake-like layer that yields slightly to the ultra creamy filling. Sweet shredded coconut adds a bit of surprise to each bite while the toasted coconut on top gives you a nutty taste and a crunchy texture.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         This ice box cake might be light but its definitely rich in taste! While the ice box may have gone the way of the record player, ice box cakes are certainly here to stay!
        </p>

         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Lemons for Lulu
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSCategory">
           Recipe type:
           <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
            dessert, pudding
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            8
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            45 graham cracker halves
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            4 oz neufchatel cheese, softened
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 3.4 oz package vanilla instant pudding mix
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1  cup International Delights Fat Free French Coffee Creamer
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup cream of coconut (found in the mixed drink section of your grocery store)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 8 oz tub of lite whipped topping, divided
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup shredded coconut
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             cup toasted coconut *
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Line a 8 x 8 inch square pan with two sheets of overlapping plastic wrap allowing about 4 inches to hang over all four sides.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            In the bowl of stand mixer fitted with a whisk attachment whip cream cheese until creamy. Mix in pudding mix, creamer and cream of coconut. Continue to whisk until mixture combines and thickens. This can take anywhere from 1 - 5 minutes. Fold in half of the whipped topping.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Arrange 9 graham crackers in your pan. Top with one cup of filling and  cup of shredded coconut. Repeat layers ending with 9 graham crackers. Fold plastic wrap over top of cake and freeze for at least 4 hours.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Before serving. invert cake onto a cake plate, top with remaining whipped topping and toasted coconut. Serve immediately or keep frozen.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            *To toast coconut:
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Preheat oven to 300 degrees. Arrange coconut in an even layer. Toast coconut in the oven, stirring every 5 minutes until coconut is golden in color. This could take 15-20 minutes. Be sure to keep a close eye as the coconut can burn quickly.
           </li>
          </ol>
