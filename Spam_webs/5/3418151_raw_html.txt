             <div class="itemIntroText">
              <p>
               The highlight of the Ballets de Monte-Carlo 2013-14 season will be Jean-Christophe Maillots new creation of Casse-Noisette et Compagnie.
              </p>
             </div>
             <!-- Item fulltext -->
             <div class="itemFullText">
              <p>
               The original production - known as 
               <em>
                Casse-Noisette Circus
               </em>
               - was presented beneath the canvas ceiling of the
               <a href="http://www.riviera-buzz.com/features/local-buzz/item/476-monaco-kermesse-2013.html" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Chapiteau de Fontvieille
                </strong>
               </a>
               in 2000 to celebrate the jubilee of
               <strong>
                HSH Prince Rainier III
               </strong>
               , who loved both the circus and dance.
              </p>
              <p>
               This new creation - performed by the Companys 50 dancers, and accompanied by the
               <strong>
                Monte-Carlo Philharmonic Orchestra
               </strong>
               - echoes the original, but also reflects Maillots own story since his arrival in the Principality in 1993, now celebrating his
               <a href="http://www.riviera-buzz.com/features/arts-culture/item/480-jean-christophe-maillot-20th-anniversary.html" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 20th anniversary
                </strong>
               </a>
               as
               <strong>
                Director and Choreographer of the Company
               </strong>
               this year.
              </p>
              <p>
               Like Clara - in the traditional story of 
               <em>
                The Nutcracker
               </em>
               - whose dreams magically come true,
               <strong>
                J-CH Maillot
               </strong>
               also had a dream - of creating an artistic world in
               <strong>
                Monaco
               </strong>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Les Ballets de Monte-Carlo and Casse Noisette 2103" src="/images/casse-noisette-002.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Jean-Christophe Maillot and Les Ballets de Monte-Carlo - Casse Noisette 2103" src="/images/casse-noisette-005.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               Like
               <strong>
                Clara
               </strong>
               , he too met a magician, in the person of
               <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caroline,_Princess_of_Hanover" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 HRH the Princess of Hanover
                </strong>
               </a>
               , patron and key supporter of the choreographic art in
               <strong>
                Monaco
               </strong>
               , who enabled him to create over 30 ballets for the
               <strong>
                Ballets de Monte-Carlo
               </strong>
               . 
               <em>
                Casse-Noisette et Compagnie
               </em>
               relates this story, paying tribute to the
               <strong>
                Royal patron
               </strong>
               who made it possible.
              </p>
              <p>
               The son of a painter who produced many theatre sets,
               <a href="http://www.balletsdemontecarlo.com/ballets-de-monte-carlo/page.php?id_menu=84&amp;lg=en" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Maillot
                </strong>
               </a>
               learned from an early age how easily fictional characters could slide into reality - as did
               <strong>
                Clara
               </strong>
               - so the ballet features some of the
               <strong>
                fascinating people
               </strong>
               with whom he has come into contact during the past 20 years, reflecting his personal experiences, and symbolizing the unique link between himself, the public and the
               <strong>
                Principality of Monaco
               </strong>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Les Ballets de Monte-Carlo perform a version of Casse Noisette 2103" src="/images/casse-noisette-003.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               <img alt="Les Ballets de Monte-Carlo perform Casse Noisette in December 2013" src="/images/casse-noisette-004.jpg"/>
              </p>
              <p>
               "'
               <em>
                Casse-Noisette Compagnie is a new choreography that puts the leading characters that I have created in Monaco on stage together,
               </em>
               he explains. 
               <em>
                They have all come from my box of memories and are preparing to dance for you and say a huge thank you!
               </em>
              </p>
              <p>
               Creating this new version of the ballet has, he says, been an
               <strong>
                emotional process
               </strong>
               . 
               <em>
                I realise how rare it is for a choreographer to be able to explore the same subject several years on, with this time, HSH Prince Albert II at its first performance.
               </em>
               
              </p>
              <p>
               
               <em>
                Casse-Noisette et Compagnie
               </em>
               , based on a theme by
               <strong>
                J-CH Maillot
               </strong>
               and
               <strong>
                Jean Rouaud
               </strong>
               , is set to Tchaikovskys much-loved score, and directed by
               <a href="http://www.iesanetwork.com/c.liepin/directionorchestre/brochot.html" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Nicolas Brochot
                </strong>
               </a>
               , with set design by
               <strong>
                Alain Lagarde
               </strong>
               and costumes by
               <strong>
                Philippe Guillotel
               </strong>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <!-- JoomlaWorks "AllVideos" Plugin (v4.4) starts here -->
                <div class="avPlayerWrapper avVideo">
                 <div class="avPlayerContainer" style="width:680px;">
                  <div class="avPlayerBlock" id="AVPlayerID_fe15ee3a_1914909667">
                   <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="383" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/Y9Tn8cJiW3w?rel=0&amp;rel=0&amp;fs=1&amp;wmode=transparent" title="JoomlaWorks AllVideos Player" width="680">
                   </iframe>
                  </div>
                 </div>
                </div>
                <!-- JoomlaWorks "AllVideos" Plugin (v4.4) ends here -->
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               It will be presented in the
               <strong>
                Salle des Princes
               </strong>
               in the
               <strong>
                Grimaldi Forum
               </strong>
               in
               <strong>
                Monaco
               </strong>
               , from Thursday December 26th to Sunday January 5th. Tickets prices start at 19 and may be
               <a href="http://www.monaco-spectacle.com/index.php?c=performance&amp;m=index&amp;show=509" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 reserved online
                </strong>
               </a>
               or by calling the Grimaldi Forum on +377 99 99 30 00. For further information please visit the
               <a href="http://www.balletsdemontecarlo.com" target="_blank">
                <strong>
                 Ballets de Monte-Carlo website
                </strong>
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                <span style="color:#ff0000;">
                 CONTACT DETAILS
                </span>
                <br/>
                Grimaldi Forum
                <br/>
                10, ave. Princesse Grace
                <br/>
                98000 Monaco
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Tel: +377 99 99 20 00
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               <a href="https://foursquare.com/v/grimaldi-forum/4b669a39f964a52042252be3" target="_blank">
