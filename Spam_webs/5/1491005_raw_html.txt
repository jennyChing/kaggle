          New Carriers Offering Hawaii Airfare
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <span class="date">
           <a href="http://www.airtohawaii.com/new-carriers-offering-hawaii-airfare/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to New Carriers Offering Hawaii Airfare">

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.airtohawaii.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/P1090023_edited-1.jpg">
           <img alt="P1090023_edited-1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1273" height="1940" src="http://www.airtohawaii.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/P1090023_edited-1.jpg" width="3448"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The April 7, 2015 announcement that Virgin America was intending to fly to the Aloha State was somewhat of a surprise. It seemed like more were expecting Southwest Airlines to be the next carrier to offer
          <a href="http://www.airtohawaii.com" target="_blank" title="airfare to Hawaii">
           airfare to Hawaii
          </a>
          rather than Virgin America. Back in 2013 Virgin America announced its plans to offer service to Hawaii sometime in 2015 and the carrier is now living up to that promise. Still, it seems like many were expecting Southwest to beat Virgin to punch.
         </p>
         <p>
          Pending FAA approval, starting in November 2, 2015, Virgin America will be flying to Honolulu from San Francisco and in December 3, 2015 from San Francisco to Kahului, Maui. The plan is to have two daily flights to Honolulu and 1 to Kahului from San Francisco.
         </p>
         <p>
          Virgin America is based out of the Bay Area so it makes sense for them to initially fly out of San Francisco. But will Virgin America also fly to Hawaii from other US mainland cities? Only time will tell. But dont expect Virgin American to compete on prices. Unlike other carriers, Virgin America competes on service. This is not a surprise because Virgin America has been consistently rated, service-wise, as the top airline in America as well as among the top-rated airlines internationally.
         </p>
         <p>
          One can speculate that Virgin America is going to do well in this route as there should be a market for those willing to pay somewhat more for a better flying experience. After all, a flight from the west coast is typically more than 5 hours and flying with the other carriers in coach seats that have increasingly less legroom with virtually no level of service certainly leaves a lot to be desired.
         </p>
         <p>
          In other news, Asian discount carrier, AirAsia X, announced on April 6, 2015 that they will be flying to Honolulu out of Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia starting November 1, 2015. This will be the first ever carrier offering direct flights out of Kuala Lumpur. There will be 4 weekly flights to Honolulu with refueling stops in Osaka, Japan.
         </p>
