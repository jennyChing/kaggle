     <p>
      <b>
       James Reynolds Bath
      </b>
      (born August 18, 1936)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      is a former director of
      <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Credit_and_Commerce_International" title="Bank of Credit and Commerce International">
       Bank of Credit and Commerce International
      </a>
      (BCCI), a former frontman for
      <a href="/wiki/Salem_bin_Laden" title="Salem bin Laden">
       Salem bin Laden
      </a>
      (
      <a href="/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden" title="Osama bin Laden">
       Osama bin Laden
      </a>
      's older half-brother),
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and also former part owner of
      <a href="/wiki/Arbusto_Energy" title="Arbusto Energy">
       Arbusto Energy
      </a>
      with
      <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">
       George W. Bush
      </a>
      , with whom Bath served as a member of the
      <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
       Texas
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Air_National_Guard" title="Air National Guard">
       Air National Guard
      </a>
      during the
      <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">
       Vietnam War
      </a>
      , protecting the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast" title="Gulf Coast">
       Gulf Coast
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He has business interests in the aircraft business and real estate.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">
       Biography
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=James_R._Bath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Bath, a native of
      <a href="/wiki/Natchitoches,_Louisiana" title="Natchitoches, Louisiana">
       Natchitoches, Louisiana
      </a>
      , moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">
       Houston
      </a>
      in 1965 at age 29 to join the
      <a href="/wiki/Texas_Air_National_Guard" title="Texas Air National Guard">
       Texas Air National Guard
      </a>
      , where he became friends with
      <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">
       George W. Bush
      </a>
      . In 1972, he and Bush were both suspended from flying for failing to show up for a scheduled physical exam.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In the late 1960s, after working for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_Aviation" title="Atlantic Aviation">
       Atlantic Aviation
      </a>
      , Bath moved to Houston and became an aircraft broker.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bath got his start in real estate in 1973 by forming a partnership with Texas Senator
      <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Bentsen" title="Lloyd Bentsen">
       Lloyd Bentsen
      </a>
      's son, Lan, in finding investments for the Senator's blind trust, Bath Bentsen Interests.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1978, Bath became a Director of the
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Main_Bank_of_Houston&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Main Bank of Houston (page does not exist)">
       Main Bank
      </a>
      , based in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">
       Houston, Texas
      </a>
      . His fellow investors were
      <a href="/wiki/John_Connally" title="John Connally">
       John Connally
      </a>
      ; Saudi financier
      <a href="/wiki/Ghaith_Pharaon" title="Ghaith Pharaon">
       Ghaith Pharaon
      </a>
      ; and
      <a href="/wiki/Khalid_bin_Mahfouz" title="Khalid bin Mahfouz">
       Khalid bin Mahfouz
      </a>
      . Also at this time, he founded
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=JB%26A_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="JB&amp;A Aviation (page does not exist)">
       JB&amp;A Aviation
      </a>
      , a corporate aviation brokerage firm, along with fellow businessmen Johnson Taylor and Jerry Smith.
     </p>
     <p>
      In 1980, Bath was named company president of
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cotopax_Investments&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cotopax Investments (page does not exist)">
       Cotopax Investments
      </a>
      , registered in the
      <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">
       Cayman Islands
      </a>
      . The name was changed to
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Skyway_Aircraft_Leasing_Ltd.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Skyway Aircraft Leasing Ltd. (page does not exist)">
       Skyway Aircraft Leasing Ltd.
      </a>
      The company board then resigned en masse, leaving Bath as a sole director. The company acted as a supplier of large passenger and air cargo jets.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Bath founded
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southwest_Airport_Services" title="Southwest Airport Services">
       Southwest Airport Services
      </a>
      to manage the
      <a href="/wiki/Houston_Gulf_Airport" title="Houston Gulf Airport">
       Houston Gulf Airport
      </a>
      , and to provide military fueling services at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ellington_Field" title="Ellington Field">
       Ellington Field
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Bath's
      <a href="/wiki/Military_discharge" title="Military discharge">
       discharge
      </a>
      from the National Guard and his relationships to the bin Laden family, the Bush family, and Arbusto were among the allegations discussed in
      <a href="/wiki/Michael_Moore" title="Michael Moore">
       Michael Moore
      </a>
      's 2004
      <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">
       documentary film
      </a>
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit_9/11" title="Fahrenheit 9/11">
        Fahrenheit 9/11
       </a>
      </i>
      , as well as his 2003 book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Dude,_Where%27s_My_Country%3F" title="Dude, Where's My Country?">
        Dude, Where's My Country?
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      These topics are also explored in
      <a href="/wiki/Greg_Palast" title="Greg Palast">
       Greg Palast
      </a>
      's 2002 book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Best_Democracy_Money_Can_Buy" title="The Best Democracy Money Can Buy">
        The Best Democracy Money Can Buy
       </a>
      </i>
      and 2004 film
      <i>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bush_Family_Fortunes" title="Bush Family Fortunes">
        Bush Family Fortunes
       </a>
      </i>
      , as well as
      <a href="/wiki/Russ_Baker" title="Russ Baker">
       Russ Baker
      </a>
      's 2009 book
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.familyofsecrets.com" rel="nofollow">
       <i>
        Family of Secrets
       </i>
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Jim Bath lived in
      <a href="/wiki/River_Oaks,_Houston" title="River Oaks, Houston">
       River Oaks, Houston
      </a>
      with his wife, Sandra. Their house was behind the house of
      <a href="/wiki/Khalid_bin_Mahfouz" title="Khalid bin Mahfouz">
       Khalid bin Mahfouz
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Southwest_Airport_Services">
       Southwest Airport Services
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=James_R._Bath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Southwest Airport Services">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defense_contractor" title="Defense contractor">
       defense
      </a>
      fuels contractor that operates out of a 12,500-square-foot (1,160m
      <sup>
       2
      </sup>
      ) facility at
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ellington_Field" title="Ellington Field">
       Ellington Field
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">
       U.S. state
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">
       Texas
      </a>
      providing on-site
      <a href="/wiki/Car_rental" title="Car rental">
       car rentals
      </a>
      , computerized weather systems and high-speed Internet access. It also provides fueling services for the military as well as training facilities.
     </p>
     <p>
      Southwest Airport Services was established in 1985 by James R. Bath to manage the
      <a href="/wiki/Houston_Gulf_Airport" title="Houston Gulf Airport">
       Houston Gulf Airport
      </a>
      until it closed in 2002.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2008)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
