       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Where Can I Sell My Baseball Cards?
       </h1>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         Its a popular question. Where can I sell my baseball cards? We hope to answer that for you through this page.
        </p>
        <p>
         The first question anyone trying to sell baseball and other sports cards has to answer is what do you have? That may decide the best way to liquidate your collectionor if its even a possibility. There are a lot of cards that simply arent marketable and youd be better off keeping them as a way to resurrect your youth when the mood strikes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.sportscollectorsdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/VintageCardMontage1.jpg">
          <img alt="Vintage Baseball Cards" class="alignright wp-image-33512" height="397" src="http://www.sportscollectorsdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/VintageCardMontage1.jpg" width="381"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.cardshopfinder.com" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          Card shops
         </a>
         are still out there, but there arent nearly as many as there once were. Local sports card dealers might be interested in your collection but only if they know they can turn it around for a profit quickly. Not every card or set is in demand and some arent in demand at all. Feel free to reach out to them or attend a card show (you may have to travel a bit but you can find card show listings
         <a href="http://www.beckett.com/venue_manager" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          here
         </a>
         ).
        </p>
        <p>
         However, selling your baseball, football, basketball or hockey cards
         <em>
          online
         </em>
         might be the way to go and there are several options to do that.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How to Sell Your Sports Cards
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are trying to sell small collection of old baseball cards(generally considered pre-1980s), you have several options. You should be able to listcomplete sets on
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574630358&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5337615254&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fcsr.ebay.com%2Fcse%2Fsell.jsf" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          eBays selling platform
         </a>
         . Be sure to describe them in detail and show photos of a few of the more valuable cards (eBay allows up to a dozen pictures per listing so be sure to take advantage of that option).
        </p>
        <p>
         The same holds true for lots, especially from the mid-1970s on back. Of course, the older they are and better condition theyre in, the better your return. If you have vintage cards that truly look like they just came out of the pack (NO corner wear, stains and well-centered), you may consider having your best cards graded. Its also wise to grade iconic cards in slightly lesser grades such as
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574630358&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5337615254&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2Fi.html%3F_odkw%3Dmickey%2Bmantle%26_sop%3D1%26Era%3DPost%25252DWWII%252520%2525281942%25252D1980%252529%26_from%3DR40%26_dcat%3D213%26_osacat%3D213%26_from%3DR40%26_trksid%3Dp2045573.m570.l1313%26_nkw%3Dmickey%2Bmantle%26_sacat%3D213" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          Mickey Mantle
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574630358&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5337615254&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2FBaseball-%2F213%2Fi.html%3F_sop%3D1%26_from%3DR40%257CR40%257CR40%26_nkw%3DWillie%2BMays%26_dcat%3D213%26Era%3DPost%25252DWWII%252520%2525281942%25252D1980%252529%26rt%3Dnc" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          Willie Mays
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574630358&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5337615254&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2FBaseball-%2F213%2Fi.html%3F_sop%3D1%26_from%3DR40%257CR40%257CR40%257CR40%26_nkw%3Dhank%2BAaron%26_dcat%3D213%26Era%3DPost%25252DWWII%252520%2525281942%25252D1980%252529%26rt%3Dnc" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          Hank Aaron
         </a>
         and other Hall of Famers from the 1960s on back.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.sportscollectorsdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/sold-on-eBay1.jpg">
           <img alt="sold on eBay" class="alignleft wp-image-47085 size-full" height="272" src="http://www.sportscollectorsdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/sold-on-eBay1.jpg" width="257"/>
          </a>
          Compare Your Cards
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Sports cardbuyers are a savvy bunch. They will use online price guide services and completed sales on eBay to see what cards have sold for and you should do the same. Compare the quality of your vintage cards to others that have sold in the past few months (look for the sold search option on the left side of eBay. That way youll know generally where the market is.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Auction House Option
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are looking to sell complete or near complete sets or pre-1960s stars in quantity from the mid-1960s on back, you might consider contacting an auction company that specializes in sports memorabilia. Look around the top, bottom and sides of this page and youll see their ads. All are reputable companies. The older, the better and high-grade material is of special interest, especially graded vintage. However, we cant guarantee theyll be interested in buying your sports card collection. It doesnt hurt to ask and their catalogs are great fun to look at. One of the countrys top buyers of vintage cards (generally pre-1975) is
         <a href="http://www.justcollect.com" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          Just Collect
         </a>
         . They, too, sell on consignment.
        </p>
        <p>
         Their fees for consigning cards vary but they will be glad to tell you what youll pay.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Your 1980s and 90s Cards Arent Likely Worth Much
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Maybe you still love sports but collect
         <a href="http://www.shareasale.com/r.cfm?u=193947&amp;b=242403&amp;m=28250&amp;afftrack=Where%20Can%20I%20sell&amp;urllink=www%2Esportsmemorabilia%2Ecom%2Fsports%2Dmemorabilia%2Fbaseball%2F" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          autographs and other sports memorabilia
         </a>
         instead of cards and were hoping to use the proceeds of your old cards on something else. Unfortunately, many baseball cards from the 1980s and 90s were printed in such high quantity to meet consumer demand that there arent very many buyers out there. If the bulk of your collection dates from the mid-1980s through the early 90s, you may have to simply sell them at a garage sale and take what you can geteven if its only a few dollars.
         <a href="http://www.sportscollectorsdaily.com/cards-worth-much-things-can/" target="_blank">
          Here are someother options
         </a>
         that might work for those, too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574630358&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5337615254&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2FBaseball-%2F213%2Fi.html%3FLH_Complete%3D1%26LH_Sold%3D1%26_from%3DR40%257CR40%257CR40%257CR40%257CR40%26_nkw%3D%2528Griffey%2BJr%252C%2BFrank%2BThomas%252C%2BDerek%2BJeter%252C%2BCal%2BRipken%2529%2Bbgs%26_sop%3D13" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          Higher end rookie cards
         </a>
         of Ken Griffey Jr., Frank Thomas, Derek Jeter and a couple of other high-profile players may have some valuebut unless you get them graded by a company like
         <a href="http://www.pntra.com/t/RUBLS0pFS0BMSUNFQ0BMQ0ZKRQ" rel="external nofollow">
          Professional Sports Authenticator
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://www.pntra.com/i/RUBLS0pFS0BMSUNFQ0BMQ0ZKRQ" width="1"/>
         (PSA) or
         <a href="http://www.sgccard.com" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          Sportscard Guaranty
         </a>
         (SGC) and are fortunate enough to score a 9 or 10, it may not be worth it. Again, seeking out other cards that have sold is a good way to get some idea of what they go for.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Beyond eBay
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         There are some other options, too. You can try
         <a href="http://comc.com/rt/14BFSCD" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          COMC.com
         </a>
         , a website that offers you the opportunity to simply put your cards inside penny sleeves, fill out a form and send them in. COMC then does the work of scanning the cards and placing them on their site. Once they tell you via email that your order has been processed, you go in and set prices. You can compare the cards listed by others and undercut or match them to give your selling probability a boost.
        </p>
        <p>
         There is a fee per card of around 25 cents, so be sure that you have cards that are worth selling (pre-1980s or popular sets from the modern era in all sports). Its a very easy way to get a lot of cards listed, but competition among sellers is strong so be sure you price your cards right. Its also a great place to buy cards if youve decided to become a buyer and fill out those old card sets instead of getting rid of them! And youd be surprised
         <a href="http://rover.ebay.com/rover/1/711-53200-19255-0/1?ff3=4&amp;pub=5574630358&amp;toolid=10001&amp;campid=5337615254&amp;customid=&amp;mpre=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.ebay.com%2Fsch%2Fi.html%3F_odkw%3D%26_sop%3D15%26_osacat%3D213%26_from%3DR40%26_trksid%3Dp2045573.m570.l1313.TR0.TRC0.H0.TRS0%26_nkw%3D%26_sacat%3D213" rel="external nofollow" target="_blank">
          how cheaply you can find some cards youre missing
         </a>
         on eBay as well.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you have any questions or youd like us to help you turn your vintage baseball card collection or sports memorabilia into cash, please dont hesitate to
         <a href="/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#cfaaaba6bba0bd8fbcbfa0bdbbbcaca0a3a3aaacbba0bdbcabaea6a3b6e1aca0a2">
          contact us
         </a>
         .
        </p>
