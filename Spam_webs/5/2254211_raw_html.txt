       <p>
        <img class="bordered" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/craphound.com/images/NGseanomadJPEGImage608404pixels.jpeg"/>
        <br/>
        In
        <em>
         National Geographic
        </em>
        , a rare moment of marine nomadism, as a sea nomad child called Enal hitches a ride by holding onto the tail of his friend, a tawny nurse shark. The picture is by James Morgan, submitted to
        <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/traveler-magazine/photo-contest/2012/">
         the National Geographic Traveler Photo Contest
        </a>
        (Top prize: Photo expedition for two to the Galapagos with a National Geographic photographer).
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         Enal, a young sea nomad, rides on the tail of a tawny nurse shark, in Sulawesi, Indonesia. Marine nomadism has almost completely disappeared in South East Asia as a result of severe marine degradation. I believe children such as Enal have stories that could prove pivotal in contemporary marine conservation.
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        <a href="http://travel.nationalgeographic.com/travel/traveler-magazine/photo-contest/2012/entries/gallery/spontaneous-moments-week-4/#/4">
         Spontaneous MomentsWeek 4 Gallery
        </a>
        (
        <i>
         Thanks,
         <a href="http://intelligenttravel.nationalgeographic.com/">
          Marilyn
         </a>

        </i>

             <p>
              http://www.guardian.co.uk/environment/2010/sep/18/last-sea-nomads

             <p>
              Diana Botutihe was born at sea. Now in her 50s, she has spent her entire life on boats that are typically just 5m long and 1.5m wide. She visits land only to trade fish for staples such as rice and water, and her boat is filled with the accoutrements of everyday living  jerry cans, blackened stockpots, plastic utensils, a kerosene lamp and a pair of pot plants.
             </p>
             <p>
              Diana is one of the worlds last marine nomads; a member of the Bajau ethnic group, a Malay people who have lived at sea for centuries, plying a tract of ocean between the Philippines, Malaysia and Indonesia.
