         <img alt="Sealing Window with Caulk" src="http://learn.livingdirect.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/sealing_window.jpg" style="width:100%;" title="Sealing Window with Caulk"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Considering the high cost of energy, it pays to do whatever you can to maximize your homes use of this precious and expensive commodity. Fortunately, there are small, simple steps you can take to increase energy efficiency that can save you a substantial amount of money.
        </p>
        <p>
         Try these six simple yet vital
         <a href="http://learn.livingdirect.com/homeowners-guide-infographic/">
          energy-saving tips
         </a>
         and watch your utility bill start to shrink.
        </p>
        <h3>
         1. Seal Windows &amp; Doors
        </h3>
        <p>
         Test the air-tightness of your home, and you might be astounded at how much energy passes through the cracks and crevices surrounding your windows and doors. Check the seal around your windows and the perimeter of your doors by holding a piece of tissue in front of them. If it moves, even slightly, you have a leak, which will be even more pronounced during windy weather. Rattling windows and doors also indicate air leaks, as does daylight peeking in from around door and window frames. Also check around switch plates and outlets for airflow leaks.
        </p>
        <p>
         Cut down substantially on energy leakage by sealing off leaks in stationary windows with caulking and using weather sealant around windows and doors that are movable. Also install door sweeps.
         <br/>
         <span id="more-1399">
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3>
         2. Flush the Water Heater
        </h3>
        <p>
         Water heaters use a surprising amount of energy heating and reheating water. Standard water heaters experience sediment buildup in their tanks over time, and that reduces their life expectancy and energy efficiency. The buildup can also clog water lines and even cause the appliance to leak.
        </p>
        <p>
         Flush your water heater by connecting a garden hose to the water-heater drain outlet, which is located at the base of the water heater. Put the other end of the hose at a floor drain or in a large bucket. Turn on the water to flush out the buildup at the bottom of the water heater. Keep flushing until the water that comes out is clear.
        </p>
        <h3>
         3. Spot Cool with a Portable Air Conditioner
        </h3>
        <div class="photofloat30">
         <img alt="Spot Cool with a Portable Air Conditioner" src="http://learn.livingdirect.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/portable_air_conditioner.jpg" title="Spot Cool with a Portable Air Conditioner"/>
        </div>
        <p>
         Instead of using your central air conditioner to cool down your entire house  including all the rooms nobody is even using  you should try using a
         <a href="http://www.livingdirect.com/Portable-Air-Conditioners/Home_Air_Quality-Portable_Air_Conditioners,default,sc.html">
          portable air conditioning unit
         </a>
         to cool off the area you are in.
        </p>
        <p>
         With energy rates soaring higher and higher every month, more homeowners are starting to figure out that paying to cooling off all of the unused rooms in their home isnt a wise use of their budget. Using a portable air conditioner will not only keep you and your family comfortable at your desired temperature, but it also
         <a href="http://learn.livingdirect.com/energy-bills-portable-air-conditioners/">
          cuts down on your overall energy use
         </a>
         and lessens the overall strain youre putting on your central A/C.  Its also worth noting that you can also accomplish this same task with a
         <a href="http://www.livingdirect.com/Window-Air-Conditioners/Home_Air_Quality-Window_Air_Conditioners,default,sc.html">
          window air conditioning unit
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Alternatively in the winter, you can use a
         <a href="http://www.livingdirect.com/efficient-space-heaters/Home_Air_Quality-Heaters-Space_Heaters,default,sc.html">
          space heater
         </a>
         or
         <a href="http://www.livingdirect.com/electric-fireplace-heaters/Home_Air_Quality-Heaters-Electric_Fireplaces,default,sc.html">
          electric fireplace
         </a>
         to keep you warm without paying to heat your entire home.  Same strategy, different appliance.
        </p>
        <h3>
         4. Add Attic Insulation
        </h3>
        <p>
         Insulating your attic helps prevent the exchange of indoor air with outdoor air, which keeps your home at an even temperature. Stabilizing the temperature in this way generally means a substantial energy savings. According to
         <a href="http://www.energystar.gov/index.cfm?c=home_sealing.hm_improvement_sealing">
          Energy Star
         </a>
         , most homes in the United States dont have sufficient insulation, and the air leaks in a typical home are equivalent to having a window open for an entire year.
        </p>
        <p>
         The
         <a href="http://energy.gov/energysaver/articles/adding-insulation-existing-home">
          U.S. Department of Energy
         </a>
         reports that you can most likely reduce energy bills by adding insulation, and doing so will generally pay for itself within a few years. To find out how much adding more insulation will save you over time, use the agencys
         <a href="http://energy.gov/energysaver/articles/estimating-payback-period-additional-insulation">
          estimation tool
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         Prior to adding insulation, its necessary to inspect your current insulation or have it inspected by a professional. This checkup should involve determining what type of insulation you have, including its thermal resistance (R-value), and discovering where more should be installed. According to Energy Star, indicators that you need to replace or replenish insulation include a drafty attic and when the insulation level is located at or below the attic floor joists.
        </p>
        <h3>
         5. Install a Programmable Thermostat
        </h3>
        <p>
         Take advantage of technology and put in a programmable thermostat, which will allow you to predetermine when you want the heater and air-conditioner to run and at what temperature you wish your home to remain. Such smart thermostats also allow you to program your heater and air-conditioner to be off during the hours you arent at home and to come on prior to your return. Some thermostat models even allow you to check in remotely to adjust the system, which is helpful when you are away on vacation or are returning earlier or later than expected.
        </p>
        <h3>
         6. Avoid Phantom Energy Absorbers
        </h3>
        <p>
         According to the U.S. Department of Energy, all of the common electronic devices we use today, such as cell phone chargers and cable boxes, generate small amounts of electricity even when not in use. Considering that the average person owns about 25 electronic devices, when added up, these phantom drains waste a substantial amount of energy.
        </p>
        <p>
         The solution to phantom energy loss is to unplug the various electronic devices when not in use. To make the chore easy on yourself, use surge protector power strips to turn off several electronic devices all at one time.
        </p>
        <p>
         Also avoid leaving your computer paused for an extended period of time, which can cost you more than $100 a year. Instead, put the computer in sleep mode, or power it down.
        </p>
        <p>
         Armed with these energy-saving home improvement tips, you can make some simple changes and watch your utility bill shrink.
        </p>
