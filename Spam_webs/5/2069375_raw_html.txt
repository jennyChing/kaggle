     Forgotten parts of life in Mexico
    </h1>
   </div>

     <div id="content">
      <div id="breadcrumbs">
       <span class="et_breadcrumbs_content">

        Forgotten parts of life in Mexico
       </span>
      </div>
      <!-- #breadcrumbs -->
      <div id="left-area">
       <article class="entry-content clearfix">
        <p class="post-meta">
         Posted By
         <a href="http://1dad1kid.com/author/admin/" rel="author" title="Posts by Talon Windwalker">
          Talon Windwalker
         </a>
         on 2 Jul 2014 |
         <span class="comments-number">
          <a href="http://1dad1kid.com/forgotten-parts-of-life-in-mexico/#respond">
           12 comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         When youve spent a lot of time somewhere, you naturally notice smaller details that others who are just passing through dont. Being away for a couple of years, there were some things I had forgotten about
         <a href="http://1dad1kid.com/the-sounds-of-cozumel/" target="_blank" title="The Sounds of Cozumel">
          life in Mexico
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1dad1kid.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/P5090002.jpg">
          <img alt="Mexico" border="0" height="375" src="http://1dad1kid.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/P5090002_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; margin: 5px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Beautiful Caribbean Sea" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Noise
        </h2>
        <p>
         I dont know how I had forgotten this, but Mexican culture has an affinity for noise. And lots of it. Being a people who like to share, music is never played at a level suitable for just the listener. Music must be shared which means blaring it so the whole neighborhood can enjoy it.
        </p>
        <p>
         And if they can get the bass setting so that your windows rattle appropriately, even better!
        </p>
        <p>
         Needless to say, this isnt one of my favorite parts of the culture. I love the Mexican zest for life, but Im very OK with not being forced to listen to my neighbors music all day long. Especially when its
         <em>
          ranchera
         </em>
         which has the same beat over and over and over again.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1dad1kid.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/P6140014.jpg">
          <img alt="Mexico" border="0" height="375" src="http://1dad1kid.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/P6140014_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; margin: 5px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Isla Holbox" width="500"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Freedom for children
        </h2>
        <p>
         Tiggers stomach was growling as we walked to the grocery store. When he spotted the large McDonalds next door, he immediately begged me to have our dinner there. Since its been a while since weve eaten in one of their restaurants, I let out a sigh of submission and smiled at his even bouncier steps.
        </p>
        <p>
         Once inside I saw several young children running around the restaurant. They were yelling and chasing each other while laughing. And barefoot.
        </p>
        <p>
         I laughed to myself as I imagined how this behavior would be received in some other places weve been.
        </p>
        <p>
         In the grocery store, I stood in the fish section carefully deliberating whether I should buy the salmon I had spotted. Out of the corner of my eye I spot
         <a href="http://1dad1kid.com/parenting-isnt-for-wimps/" target="_blank" title="Parenting a free spirit isnt for wimps">
          Tigger skipping back and forth
         </a>
         along the frozen food section. In Europe, his bouncy ways always elicited stares and odd looks. Im quite sure people wondered if he was mental. Especially since hes often laughing to himself as he remembers funny lines from videos.
        </p>
        <p>
         Tigger often lives in his own, happy little world. And in Mexico thats just fine.
        </p>
        <p>
         While hes hopping from corner to corner, other kids are chasing each other and making plenty of noise. One little girl bounced off my cart, her parents laughing and saying Im sorry as I stood there practically mortified that she might have hurt herself.
        </p>
        <p>
         Here kids are allowed to be kids in all their messy, noisy, happy glory. And I love it!
        </p>
        <p>
         Its also nice to not be the only parent with a kid bouncing around in the grocery store.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://1dad1kid.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/P5240010.jpg">
          <img alt="Mexico" border="0" height="500" src="http://1dad1kid.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/P5240010_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; margin: 5px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; padding-top: 0px; border: 0px;" title="Beach on Cozumel" width="375"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Friendliness
        </h2>
        <p>
         Now, I had not forgotten just how friendly the Mexican people can be, but it has been thoroughly enjoyable becoming reacquainted with it. A sweet old lady reminded me of it today when we were in the store and I asked her if she knew where they were hiding the sugar.
        </p>
        <p>
         We discussed which aisles I had already checked. She was dumbfounded it wasnt where we both figured it would be. I thanked her and decided to go in search of an employee, but she told me to wait. She made it her personal mission to find it.
        </p>
        <p>
         I also love the look of absolute relief from some people, especially cashiers in the stores, when they discover I speak Spanish. Their grin is huge, and their shoulders visibly relax. Its completely understandable, but its also really kind of cute.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its also lovely to be somewhere that has a cultural norm of greeting people. Its so enjoyable to walk around and be greeted with a Good [insert proper term for the time of day]! or to see someones face light up when you beat them to it.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its difficult to have a bad or grumpy day when everyone you meet is smiling and wishing you happiness.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What is your favorite part of the culture in Mexico?
         </strong>
        </p>
