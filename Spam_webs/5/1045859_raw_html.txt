    NGC 4676: When Mice Collide
   </b>
   <br/>
   <b>

  <p>
   <b>
    Explanation:
   </b>
   These two mighty galaxies are pulling each other apart.  

Known as the "
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mouse">
    Mice
   </a>
   " 
because they have such long tails, each
   <a href="spiral_galaxies.html">
    spiral galaxy
   </a>
   has likely already passed through the other.

The
   <a href="http://burro.astr.cwru.edu/NAS/NAS.html">
    long
tails
   </a>
   are created by the relative
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galactic_tide">
    difference between gravitational pulls
   </a>
   on the near and far parts of each galaxy. 

Because the distances are so large, the
   <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pjjRfOT87Wc">
    cosmic 
interaction
   </a>
   takes place in slow motion -- 
over hundreds of millions of years.
   <a href="http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1974ApJ...187..219S">
    NGC 4676
   </a>
   lies about 300 million
   <a href="http://chandra.harvard.edu/photo/cosmic_distance.html">
    light-years
   </a>
   away toward the constellation of Bernice's Hair 
(
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coma_Berenices">
    Coma Berenices
   </a>
   ) and are
   <a href="http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1961ApJ...133..726B">
    likely members
   </a>
   of the
   <a href="ap100502.html">
    Coma Cluster of Galaxies
   </a>
   . 

The
   <a href="http://hubblesite.org/newscenter/archive/releases/2002/11/image/h/">
    above picture
   </a>
   was taken with the
   <a href="http://www.nasa.gov/mission_pages/hubble/story/index.html">
    Hubble Space Telescope
   </a>
   's
   <a href="http://acs.pha.jhu.edu/">
    Advanced Camera for Surveys
   </a>
   in 2002.

These galactic mice will probably
   <a href="ap130514.html">
    collide again and again
   </a>
   over the next billion years until they coalesce to
   <a href="ap120604.html">
    form a single galaxy
   </a>
   .
  </p>
