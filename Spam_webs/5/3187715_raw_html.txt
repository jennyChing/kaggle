       <div class="bd">
        <div class="col6 ingredients">
         <h6>
          Ingredients
         </h6>
         <ul>
          <li class="subtitle">
           For the Dough:
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           1 1/3 cups Dutch-process cocoa powder
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           1 1/2 cups
           <a class="crosslink" debug="74 91" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/flour/index.html">
            all-purpose flour
           </a>
           , plus more for dusting
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           1/4 teaspoon salt
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           2 sticks
           <a class="crosslink" debug="152 167" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/butter/index.html">
            unsalted butter
           </a>
           , softened
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           2 cups
           <a class="crosslink" debug="190 206" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/sugar/index.html">
            granulated sugar
           </a>
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           2 large eggs
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           1 teaspoon
           <a class="crosslink" debug="241 256" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/extracts/index.html">
            vanilla extract
           </a>
          </li>
          <li class="subtitle">
           For the Filling:
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           1 stick unsalted butter, softened
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           1/2 cup
           <a class="crosslink" debug="343 363" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/vegetable-shortening/index.html">
            vegetable shortening
           </a>
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           3 cups confectioners' sugar, sifted
          </li>
          <li itemprop="ingredients">
           1 teaspoon vanilla extract
          </li>
         </ul>
        </div>
        <div class="col12 directions" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
         <h6>
          Directions
         </h6>
         <p>
          Prepare the dough:
          <a class="crosslink" debug="19 23" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/sift/index.html">
           Sift
          </a>
          together the
          <a class="crosslink" debug="37 49" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/cocoa-powder/index.html">
           cocoa powder
          </a>
          , flour and salt in a large bowl.
         </p>
         <p>
          Using a mixer, cream the butter and sugar. Add the eggs one at a time, then the vanilla, incorporating each ingredient before adding the next. Add the dry ingredients and mix just until incorporated, scraping the bottom of the bowl with a rubber
          <a class="crosslink" debug="334 341" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/spatula/index.html">
           spatula
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          Divide the
          <a class="crosslink" debug="359 364" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/dough/index.html">
           dough
          </a>
          into 2 pieces; place one piece between 2 lightly floured sheets of parchment paper and roll into a 1/4-inch-thick rectangle. Repeat with the other piece of dough. Refrigerate both rectangles, covered with the parchment sheets, until firm, at least 1 hour or up to several days.
         </p>
         <p>
          Using a 2-inch round cutter, cut the dough into 64 circles. (You can reroll the scraps once.) Place the cookies about 2 inches apart on ungreased baking sheets and chill for 20 minutes. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees.
         </p>
         <p>
          Bake the
          <a class="crosslink" debug="881 888" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/cookie/index.html">
           cookies
          </a>
          until they are set and slightly darker around the edges, about 20 minutes. Cool completely on wire racks.
         </p>
         <p>
          Meanwhile, prepare the filling: Using a
          <a class="crosslink" debug="1040 1045" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/mixer/index.html">
           mixer
          </a>
          ,
          <a class="crosslink" debug="1047 1052" href="http://www.foodterms.com/encyclopedia/cream/index.html">
           cream
          </a>
          the butter and shortening until fluffy. Beat in the confectioners' sugar and vanilla.
         </p>
         <p>
          Flip half of the cookies upside down and top each with 1 level tablespoon of filling. Press the remaining cookies on top to make sandwiches.
         </p>
