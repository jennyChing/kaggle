          <h3 itemprop="name">
           Webster, NY Police Wake Taxpayer Up at 2:30 In The Morning for a Report of a Loud Bird in a Tree
          </h3>
          <address>
           <a href="http://davyv.blogspot.com/2014/07/webster-ny-police-wake-taxpayer-up-at.html">
            <b>
             By Davy V.
            </b>
           </a>
          </address>
          <address>
           <strong>
            July 15, 2014
           </strong>
          </address>
          <p>
           <br/>
           If youre a Monroe County, NY taxpayer, especially if you live in the Town of Webster, NY, you may be interested to know just how the Webster Police department is using your tax dollars.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Looking for a bird in a tree.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Yes.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           You read that right.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Looking for a bird in a tree.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           At 2:30 in the morning.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Thats exactly what Webster, NY Police Sgt. Shaun Welch and officer Samuel States were doing early Tuesday morning when they went to Ryan Conklins home for a report of a loud bird in a tree, waking Conklin and his son up at 2:30 in the morning.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           The video below, which Ryan Conklin recorded begins with the cops telling Conklin to turn it off, thinking that Conklin had some sort of electronic bird soundscape recording eminating from a tree.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Later in the video the keystone cops confirm that its a real bird.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            WTF?
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            Really?
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           <b>
            You cant make this crap up!
           </b>
           <br/>
           <b>
            <br/>
           </b>
           I have been to Ryan Conklins home.He loves birds.Conklin has over 10 bird feeders in his back yard which he keeps full of bird food for several types of birds.He spends over $400.00 a month on bird food!
           <br/>
           <br/>
           By the way, if the name Ryan Conklin sounds familiar, it should.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           On July 4th, Conklin was arrested by Monroe County, NY Sheriffs deputy Philip Baretela, after he asked Baretela, why he, along with MCSO deputy Baker and Sgt. Edwards were all eating together at 2:00 a.m., instead of patrolling for DWIs, at a time when bars are letting out and intoxicated drivers are getting behind the wheel.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Conklins friend recorded the arrest, and documented it in a video which has gone viral.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           <b>
            Below, I have posted a link to my original piece on Ryan Conklins arrest by Monroe County, NY Sheriffs deputies.
           </b>
           <br/>
           <br/>
           As an advocate of the right to record, I always encourage taxpayers to video record each and every encounter with law enforcement.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           And thats what I love about Ryan Conklin.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           He video records each and every encounter with law enforcement.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           As he should.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           In fact, Conklins home, both inside and out, has more video cameras than a bank.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           Conklins home is under 24 hour video surveillance.
           <br/>
           <br/>
           And everything is backed up.
