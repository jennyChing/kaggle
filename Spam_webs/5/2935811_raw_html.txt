    <h6 class="underlined">
     I am manly
    </h6>
    <div class="post-31 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-memery tag-silly-meme">
     <div class="content clearfix">
      <p>
       I saw an interesting meme on
       <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/principles/2007/10/new_meme_manly_or_selfsufficie.php" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://scienceblogs.com/principles/2007/10/new_meme_manly_or_selfsufficie.php', 'Uncertain Principles']);" target="_blank">
        Uncertain Principles
       </a>
       (originated by
       <a href="http://chase.inthebasement.us/?p=720" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://chase.inthebasement.us/?p=720', 'Cut to the Chase']);">
        Cut to the Chase
       </a>
       ).  The first list comes from Popular Mechanics
       <a href="http://www.popularmechanics.com/blogs/technology_news/4221635.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.popularmechanics.com/blogs/technology_news/4221635.html', '25 Skills every man should know']);">
        25 Skills every
        <strong>
         man
        </strong>
        should know
       </a>
       .   The list below, my skills in bold:
      </p>
      <dt>
       <ol>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Patch a radiator hose
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Protect your computer
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         Rescue a boater who has capsized
        </li>
        <li>
         Frame a wall
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Retouch digital photos
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         Back up a trailer
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Build a campfire
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Fix a dead outlet
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Navigate with a map and compass
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Use a torque wrench
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Sharpen a knife
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         Perform CPR ((its been about 25 years since I took the CPR class.))
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Fillet a fish
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Maneuver a car out of a skid
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Get a car unstuck
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Back up data
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Paint a room
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Mix concrete
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         Clean a bolt-action rifle
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Change oil and filter
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Hook up an HDTV
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Bleed brakes
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Paddle a canoe
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Fix a bike flat
         </strong>
         ((I have too much experience with this.))
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Extend your wireless network
         </strong>
        </li>
       </ol>
      </dt>
      <p>
       I would describe Popular Mechanics as having some amusing stuff like
       <a href="http://www.popularmechanics.com/outdoors/boating/1277141.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.popularmechanics.com/outdoors/boating/1277141.html', 'build your own boat']);" target="_blank">
        build your own boat
       </a>
       (sadly, I couldnt find the DIY submarine) and interviews with
       <a href="http://www.popularmechanics.com/blogs/science_news/4226205.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.popularmechanics.com/blogs/science_news/4226205.html', 'Richard Garriott']);" target="_blank">
        Richard Garriott
       </a>
       (creator of the Ultima game and future space tourist), but it lacks depth.  The ads arent as bad as Popular Science ((I unfortunately received a subscription to PopSci as a benefit of purchasing a microscope.  I think its finally stopped coming.)) , but still feature the occasional herbal enhancement.  Consider the demographic.
      </p>
      <p>
       Obviously, few of these items would make
       <em>
        my
       </em>
       top 25 list.  Thus, I found it interesting that Chase created his own list twenty things he calls Practical skills every self-sufficient adult should have. Theyre more sensible:
      </p>
      <dt>
       <ol>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Know basic nutritional needs &amp; how to plan balanced meals
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Hone your sense of direction &amp; navigation so you dont need step-by-step turns to find a location
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Understand types of health insurance &amp; terminology such as OOP max &amp; co-insurance percentage
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Maintenance of a personal computer
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          In-depth knowledge of your employment benefits
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Change a flat tire
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Wash &amp; iron clothes
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Balance a checkbook &amp; manage your finances
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Patch holes in walls
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Fix a clogged toilet
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Jump start a car
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Use public transportation to get around
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Write an effective resume cover letter
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Professional oral &amp; written communication
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Basic math
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Stay calm in emergencies
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Know when to ask for help
          </strong>
         </em>
         ((I will spend a lot of time trying to work things out on my own.  I only get half-credit))
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Personal hygiene
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Do your own taxes
         </strong>
        </li>
        <li>
         <strong>
          Use internet search engines strategically
         </strong>
        </li>
       </ol>
      </dt>
      <dt>
       So Im 80% manly, 97.5% self-sufficient.  Woo!
      </dt>

        <div class="comment-body">
         <p>
          Along with all the information about love and sex the Kama Sutra contains, it also has a listing of the 64 things every educated person should know. Some of these things are suited to a low-tech society (like how to collect rain water in a cistern); some are specific to Indian culture (like how to wind a turban), and some are universal (how to play a musical instrument).
         </p>
         <p>
          As a parent, Ive often reflected on what skills I should make sure my kids know how to do. Back in 1998, I came up with this list:
         </p>
         <p>
          * Be able to read music, and either sight-sing or play an instrument
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know the basics of every major world religion
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know how to sew a button
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know how to use a knife, fork, spoon, and chopsticks
         </p>
         <p>
          * I hope for a substitute, but until it comes  know how to touch type a QWERTY keyboard
         </p>
         <p>
          * Be able to read and understand a basic bus/train/ferry/etc. schedule
         </p>
         <p>
          * Be able to swim
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know how to make and keep at least one really good friend
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know the flora and fauna of the natural world in the area in which one lives
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know how to tie basic knots: square, slip, clove hitch. (I wish I knew the bowline!)
         </p>
         <p>
          * Be able to read a map
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know basic meditation techniques
         </p>
         <p>
          * Know how to drive a car  stick shift even better
