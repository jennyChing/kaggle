     <p>
      <b>
       Robert Zubrin
      </b>
      (born April 9, 1952) is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Aerospace_engineering" title="Aerospace engineering">
       aerospace engineer
      </a>
      and author, best known for his advocacy of the manned exploration of
      <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">
       Mars
      </a>
      . He was the driving force behind
      <a href="/wiki/Mars_Direct" title="Mars Direct">
       Mars Direct
      </a>
      a proposal intended to produce significant reductions in the cost and complexity of such a mission. The key idea was to use the
      <a href="/wiki/Mars#Atmosphere" title="Mars">
       Martian atmosphere
      </a>
      to produce oxygen, water, and
      <a href="/wiki/Rocket_propellant" title="Rocket propellant">
       rocket propellant
      </a>
      for the surface stay and return journey. A modified version of the plan was subsequently adopted by
      <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">
       NASA
      </a>
      as their "design reference mission". He questions the delay and cost-to-benefit ratio of first establishing a base or outpost on an asteroid or another
      <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">
       Project Apollo
      </a>
      -like return to the Moon, as neither would be able to provide all of its own oxygen, water, or energy; these resources are producible on Mars, and he expects people would be there thereafter.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Disappointed with the lack of interest from government in
      <a href="/wiki/Exploration_of_Mars" title="Exploration of Mars">
       Mars exploration
      </a>
      and after the success of his book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Case_for_Mars" title="The Case for Mars">
        The Case for Mars
       </a>
      </i>
      as well as leadership experience at the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Space_Society" title="National Space Society">
       National Space Society
      </a>
      , Zubrin established the
      <a href="/wiki/Mars_Society" title="Mars Society">
       Mars Society
      </a>
      in 1998. This is an international organization advocating a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manned_mission_to_Mars" title="Manned mission to Mars">
       manned Mars mission
      </a>
      as a goal, by private funding if possible.
     </p>
     <p>
      Zubrin lives in
      <a href="/wiki/Lakewood,_Colorado" title="Lakewood, Colorado">
       Lakewood, Colorado
      </a>
      ; he has two daughters, Rachel and Sarah.
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifications_and_professional_experience">
       Qualifications and professional experience
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Zubrin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifications and professional experience">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Zubrin holds a B.A. in
      <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">
       Mathematics
      </a>
      from the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Rochester" title="University of Rochester">
       University of Rochester
      </a>
      (1974), a M.S. in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_Engineering" title="Nuclear Engineering">
       Nuclear Engineering
      </a>
      (1984), a M.S. in
      <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">
       Aeronautics
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Astronautics" title="Astronautics">
       Astronautics
      </a>
      (1986), and a Ph.D. in Nuclear Engineering (1992)  all from the
      <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington" title="University of Washington">
       University of Washington
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He has developed a number of concepts for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_propulsion" title="Space propulsion">
       space propulsion
      </a>
      and exploration, and is the author of over 200 technical and non-technical papers and five books. He was a member of
      <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin" title="Lockheed Martin">
       Lockheed Martin
      </a>
      's scenario development team charged with developing strategies for
      <a href="/wiki/Space_exploration" title="Space exploration">
       space exploration
      </a>
      . He was also "a senior engineer with the Martin Marietta Astronautics company, working as one of its leaders in development of advanced concepts for interplanetary missions".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He is also President of both the Mars Society and Pioneer Astronautics, a private company that does research and development on innovative aerospace technologies. Zubrin is the co-inventor on a U.S. design patent and a U.S. utility patent on a hybrid rocket/airplane, and on a U.S. utility patent on an oxygen supply system (see links below). He was awarded his first patent at age 20 in 1972 for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Three-handed_chess" title="Three-handed chess">
       Three Player Chess
      </a>
      . His inventions also include the
      <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_salt-water_rocket" title="Nuclear salt-water rocket">
       nuclear salt-water rocket
      </a>
      and co-inventor (with Dana Andrews) of the
      <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_sail" title="Magnetic sail">
       magnetic sail
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Pioneer_Energy">
       Pioneer Energy
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Zubrin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pioneer Energy">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      In 2008, Zubrin founded
      <a class="external text" href="http://www.pioneerenergy.co" rel="nofollow">
       Pioneer Energy
      </a>
      , a research and development firm headquartered in Lakewood, Colorado. The company's focus is to develop mobile
      <a href="/wiki/Enhanced_oil_recovery" title="Enhanced oil recovery">
       Enhanced Oil Recovery
      </a>
      (EOR) systems that can enable CO2-based EOR for both small and large oil producers in the United States. The company has also developed a number of new processes for manufacturing synthetic fuels.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Books">
       Books
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [

     </h2>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Case_for_Mars" title="The Case for Mars">

        </a>
       </i>
       (1996), with Richard Wagner. This outlines the
       <a href="/wiki/Mars_Direct" title="Mars Direct">
        Mars Direct
       </a>
       plan along with speculation about the economic, social and technical viability of future
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mars_colonization" title="Mars colonization">
        Mars colonization
       </a>
       . Revised and updated in 2011.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Entering_Space:_Creating_a_Spacefaring_Civilization" title="Entering Space: Creating a Spacefaring Civilization">
         Entering Space: Creating a Spacefaring Civilization
        </a>
       </i>
       (1999). This ranges from the current status quo and innovative startups of the aerospace industry, through exploration and colonization of Mars, to a more futuristic look at humanity's possible colonization of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">
        solar system
       </a>
       and the feasibility of
       <a href="/wiki/Interstellar_travel" title="Interstellar travel">
        interstellar travel
       </a>
       with known physics.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mars_On_Earth:_The_Adventures_of_Space_Pioneers_in_the_High_Arctic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mars On Earth: The Adventures of Space Pioneers in the High Arctic (page does not exist)">
         Mars On Earth: The Adventures of Space Pioneers in the High Arctic
        </a>
       </i>
       (2003). Zubrin recounts the origins and progress of the Mars Society's Mars Analog Research Station project, including a variety of perils, both mundane and adventurous, that were overcome in establishing the first analog Mars habitat on
       <a href="/wiki/Devon_Island" title="Devon Island">
        Devon Island
       </a>
       in the
       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=High_Arctic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="High Arctic (page does not exist)">
        high Arctic
       </a>
       . He offers highlights of what has been learned so far through that effort.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/First_Landing" title="First Landing">
         First Landing
        </a>
       </i>
       (2001), a
       <a href="/wiki/Hard_science_fiction" title="Hard science fiction">
        hard science fiction
       </a>
       novel about a future Mars flight using the
       <a href="/wiki/Mars_Direct" title="Mars Direct">
        Mars Direct
       </a>
       plan.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Holy_Land_Book&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Holy Land Book (page does not exist)">
         The Holy Land
        </a>
       </i>
       (2003). This is an "
       <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">
        SF
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Satire" title="Satire">
        satire
       </a>
       on the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_East_conflict#Arab-Israeli_conflict_or_Israeli-Palestinian_conflict" title="Middle East conflict">
        Middle East crisis
       </a>
       and the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_on_Terrorism" title="War on Terrorism">
        War on Terrorism
       </a>
       , and concerns what happens when the liberal
       <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">
        Western
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Galactic_empire" title="Galactic empire">
        Galactic Empire
       </a>
       relocates the oppressed Minervan
       <a href="/wiki/Sect" title="Sect">
        sect
       </a>
       to their ancient homeland of
       <a href="/wiki/Kennewick,_Washington" title="Kennewick, Washington">
        Kennewick
       </a>
       , Washington, in the midst of a United States ruled by
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_fundamentalist" title="Christian fundamentalist">
        Christian fundamentalist
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Fanaticism" title="Fanaticism">
        fanatics
       </a>
       ".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         5
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
       The story was partly inspired by
       <a href="/wiki/Karel_%C4%8Capek" title="Karel apek">
        apek
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/War_with_the_Newts" title="War with the Newts">
         War with the Newts
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         <span>
          [
         </span>
         6
         <span>
          ]
         </span>
        </a>
       </sup>
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Benedict_Arnold:_A_Drama_of_the_American_Revolution_in_Five_Acts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Benedict Arnold: A Drama of the American Revolution in Five Acts (page does not exist)">
         Benedict Arnold: A Drama of the American Revolution in Five Acts
        </a>
       </i>
       (2005). This is an effort to humanize and show the multiple dimensions of
       <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold" title="Benedict Arnold">
        Benedict Arnold
       </a>
       , and to contrast the democratic values embodied in the spirit of the Revolution with the socially bankrupt classism embodied in the British subjects who won Arnold to their side.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_Victory:_Winning_the_War_on_Terror_by_Breaking_Free_of_Oil" title="Energy Victory: Winning the War on Terror by Breaking Free of Oil">
         Energy Victory: Winning the War on Terror by Breaking Free of Oil
        </a>
       </i>
       (2007). An expansion on his recent articles criticizing both reliance on oil and the potential for
       <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_economy" title="Hydrogen economy">
        hydrogen energy
       </a>
       , while highlighting the benefits of switching more toward
       <a href="/wiki/Methanol" title="Methanol">
        methanol
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Ethanol" title="Ethanol">
        ethanol
       </a>
       . It carries an endorsement from former
       <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">
        CIA
       </a>
       director
       <a href="/wiki/R._James_Woolsey,_Jr." title="R. James Woolsey, Jr.">
        James Woolsey
       </a>
       .
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=How_to_Live_on_Mars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="How to Live on Mars (page does not exist)">
         How to Live on Mars
        </a>
       </i>
       (2008). A tongue-in-cheek technically accurate guidebook for Mars-bound Earthlings about how to survive and thrive on the Red Planet, written from the point of view of a 22nd-century Mars native.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Merchants_of_Despair:_Radical_Environmentalists,_Criminal_Pseudo-Scientists,_and_the_Fatal_Cult_of_Antihumanism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Merchants of Despair: Radical Environmentalists, Criminal Pseudo-Scientists, and the Fatal Cult of Antihumanism (page does not exist)">
         Merchants of Despair: Radical Environmentalists, Criminal Pseudo-Scientists, and the Fatal Cult of Antihumanism
        </a>
       </i>
       (2011). This traces the history of
       <a href="/wiki/Antihumanism" title="Antihumanism">
        antihumanism
       </a>
       over the last two centuries, from
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_Malthus" title="Thomas Malthus">
        Thomas Malthus
       </a>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Eugenics" title="Eugenics">
        eugenics
       </a>
       movement through to the
       <a href="/wiki/Anti-nuclear_movement" title="Anti-nuclear movement">
        anti-nuclear
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Global_warming_controversy" title="Global warming controversy">
        global warming
       </a>
       campaigns. Zubrin argues that these movements have all represented an attempt to gain oppressive political control through the restriction of human activities and freedom.
      </li>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mars_Direct:_Space_Exploration,_the_Red_Planet,_and_the_Human_Future&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mars Direct: Space Exploration, the Red Planet, and the Human Future (page does not exist)">
         Mars Direct: Space Exploration, the Red Planet, and the Human Future
        </a>
       </i>
       (2013). This e-book looks at the current situation of NASA and outlines a Mars Direct mission proposal with the
       <a href="/wiki/Red_Dragon_(spacecraft)" title="Red Dragon (spacecraft)">
        SpaceX Dragon
       </a>
       , and considers the implications of interplanetary exploration for humanity.
      </li>
     </ul>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Books_edited_or_co-authored">
       Books edited or co-authored
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [

     <p>
      Zubrin has also edited or co-edited the following books, most of which include contributions he wrote:
     </p>
     <ul>
      <li>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Islands_in_the_Sky:_Bold_New_Ideas_for_Colonizing_Space" title="Islands in the Sky: Bold New Ideas for Colonizing Space">
         Islands in the Sky: Bold New Ideas for Colonizing Space
        </a>
       </i>
       (1996), co-edited with Stanley Schmidt. This is a collection of fifteen selected non-fiction entries that had been published in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Analog_Science_Fiction_and_Science_Fact" title="Analog Science Fiction and Science Fact">
        Analog magazine
       </a>
       over the years; it includes five articles authored or co-authored by Zubrin, including "The Hypersonic Skyhook", "Mars Direct: A Proposal for the Rapid Exploration and Colonization of the Red Planet" (co-authored with David A. Baker), "Colonizing the Outer Solar System", "Terraforming Mars" (co-authored with
       <a href="/wiki/Christopher_McKay" title="Christopher McKay">
        Christopher McKay
       </a>
       ), and "The Magnetic Sail". Notable additional contributors include
       <a href="/wiki/Robert_L._Forward" title="Robert L. Forward">
        Robert L. Forward
       </a>
       and the godfather of terraforming,

        Martyn J. Fogg
       </a>
       , each of whom contributed two articles.

       <i>

       <a href="/wiki/Buzz_Aldrin" title="Buzz Aldrin">
        Buzz Aldrin

      <li>
       <i>
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=On_to_Mars:_Colonizing_a_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="On to Mars: Colonizing a New World (page does not exist)">

        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=On_to_Mars_2:_Exploring_and_Settling_a_New_World&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="On to Mars 2: Exploring and Settling a New World (page does not exist)">

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_ethics_of_terraforming">
       The ethics of terraforming
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Zubrin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The ethics of terraforming">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a href="/wiki/Ethics_of_terraforming" title="Ethics of terraforming">
       Ethics of terraforming
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Dr. Zubrin is known as an advocate of a moderately
      <a href="/wiki/Anthropocentrism" title="Anthropocentrism">
       anthropocentric
      </a>
      position in the ethics of terraforming. Discussions of the ethics of
      <a href="/wiki/Terraforming" title="Terraforming">
       terraforming
      </a>
      often
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      make reference to a series of public debates Zubrin has held with his friend
      <a href="/wiki/Christopher_McKay" title="Christopher McKay">
       Christopher McKay
      </a>
      , who advocates a moderately
      <a href="/wiki/Biocentrism_(ethics)" title="Biocentrism (ethics)">
       biocentric
      </a>
      position on the ethics of terraforming. For example, a written account of some of these debates is available in
      <i>
       On to Mars: Colonizing a New World
      </i>
      , as a joint article, "Do Indigenous Martian Bacteria have Precedence over Human Exploration?" (pp.177182)
     </p>
