        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life1.jpg">
          <img alt="Home for Life1 green" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13786" height="324" src="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life1.jpg" title="Home for Life1" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         The solar powered Home for Life, designed by Danish architecture firm
         <a class="broken_link" href="aart.dk/">
          AART A/S
         </a>
         is well designed for not just net-zero energy consumption (producing its own energy), but to actually be a
         <a href="http://www.activehouse.info/cases/home-life" target="_blank">
          net energy generator
         </a>
         , producing a surplus for the grid. Heres what we can learn from it.
        </p>
        <p>
         For states  (like New Jersey) and countries (like Germany) that pay  individuals for  energy generated from a home, here is how to design a  home to maximize solar energy production.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life2.jpg">
          <img alt="Home for Life2 green" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13787" height="291" src="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life2.jpg" title="Home for Life2" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Get at least 530 square feet facing south on the roof. No little tricky  hip roofs and cute gables going this way and that. Then put only one   to one and-a-half  stories under it. You want to ensure that you can  produce more solar power on the roof than you use under it. Keep some space for a solar water heater  for hot water and radiant heating.
         <span id="more-13785">
         </span>
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life3.jpg">
          <img alt="Home for Life3 green" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13788" height="333" src="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life3.jpg" title="Home for Life3" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Maximize passive solar gain for winter. This house is a good example of  passive design. A full 40% of the total floor area is replicated in  huge, (mostly South-facing windows). Deep overhangs keep summer sun out,  and allow low-in-the-sky winter sun in.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life5.jpg">
          <img alt="Home for Life5 green" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13790" height="338" src="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life5.jpg" title="Home for Life5" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Use sensors to monitor when you leave a room and automatically turn off the lights.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life4.jpg">
          <img alt="Home for Life4 green" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13789" height="343" src="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life4.jpg" title="Home for Life4" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Use sensors to monitor temperatures and automatically open clerestory  windows at the rooftop to let out hot air (which rises, remember) when  it is getting too hot, optimizing the flow of fresh air.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life6.jpg">
          <img alt="Home for Life6 green" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-13791" height="335" src="http://www.homedesignfind.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Home_for_Life6.jpg" title="Home for Life6" width="500"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         Put an office upstairs that allows for telecommuting. Put bedrooms are in the back. Keep the house simple, and youll be Home for Life.
        </p>
        <p>
         Source:
         <a href="http://inhabitat.com/2010/08/05/ultra-efficient-danish-home-produces-more-energy-than-it-needs/" target="_blank">
          Inhabitat
         </a>
        </p>
