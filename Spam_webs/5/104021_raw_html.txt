            var string_type = Object.prototype.toString.call(arguments[i]);

                case '[object Undefined]':

     <div class="article wysiwyg">
      <p class="byline">
       By Kyle Macdonald, 8th August 2014, 16:02
      </p>
      <p class="stand_first">
       Music history can be a complex and daunting subject - so we've enlisted the help of a few cats to explain every detail to you.
      </p>
      <img alt="Cat piano" class="main_image" height="298" src="http://assets1.classicfm.com/2014/31/cat-piano-1407506815-article-0.png" width="618"/>
      <div id="article_body_wrap">
       <div class="article_body grid_3">
        <p>
         <strong>
          c. 800 - Gregorian chant
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         In the beginning there was one voice, singing one line.
         <a href="/composers/pope/">
          Pope
         </a>
         Gregory, we're told, did it first (but he probably didn't).
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/tCcR0UX">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/tCcR0UX.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <div id="Teads_ad">
        </div>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          c. 1000 - The birth of polyphony
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         And then there were two lines, singing together harmoniously. It was beautiful.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/0xCeeJI">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/0xCeeJI.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1400-1600 - The Renaissance
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The choirs just kept getting bigger.
         <a href="/composers/tallis/">
          Thomas Tallis
         </a>
         ' masterpiece was written for 40 independentvoices. Here's a quintet, diligently watching the conductor.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/MfzeiZy">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/MfzeiZy.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1605 - The Baroque begins
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Monteverdi just wanted to be different. He thought music should have a bit more style and drama.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/D46tNX8">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/D46tNX8.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1650-1750 - The great German Baroque
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Great German Baroque composers like Schutz, Handel and Bach took ideas, styles and pieces from Italy, and used them as their own. Those big, musical genius bullies.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/uVpih5S">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/uVpih5S.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1717 - Water Music
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="/composers/handel/">
          Handel
         </a>
         's regal musical extravaganza happened on the River Thames. No thirsty cats were harmed.
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/tGzLqJ0">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/tGzLqJ0.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1750-1810 - The Classical period
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The Baroque and Classical periods saw a massive development in instrumental styles and performance ornamentation, including staccato, illustratedhere.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/0AlYPUl">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/0AlYPUl.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1756 - Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         And here he is: child prodigy, keyboard virtuoso, genius composer, wine-lover and all-round fun guy. We're sure he was fond of cats too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/76sIQHG">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/76sIQHG.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1770 - Beethoven
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         He revolutionised the symphony and chamber music, and changed the course of music forever. But he sure was grumpy.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/qIcbT">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/qIcbT.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1800 - 1850 - The rise of the virtuoso
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Now for a few extraordinary virtuosos.
         <a href="/composers/paganini/">
          Paganini
         </a>
         , Schumann and Liszt. All musical geniuses. All show-offs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/ZoY4QPT">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/ZoY4QPT.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          c. 1810 - The Romantic period
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         There was lush orchestration, overflowing emotion, tender melodies, and heart-breaking love stories set to music.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/eM78wP0">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/eM78wP0.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1813 - Wagner
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Along came Wagner, and things suddenly got very dramatic. There were now dwarves and scary giants on the
         <a href="/discover/opera/">
          opera
         </a>
         stage.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/ASdD2EN">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/ASdD2EN.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1860 - Italian opera
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         On the peninsula,Verdi was taking opera to new heights of expression and emotion.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/6iuCtLp">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/6iuCtLp.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1860 - Back at home
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Operacomposersfrom theBritishIsles at that time were somewhat less successful. Oh dear.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/OnQh04r">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/OnQh04r.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1890 - Mahler and Richard Strauss
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         New instruments, larger forces and freer expression were pushing symphonic music in new, sometimes unexpected directions.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/db867xJ">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/db867xJ.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          1913 - Rite of Spring
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Outrage and riots at the modern rhythms during the premiere of
         <a href="/composers/stravinsky/">
          Stravinsky
         </a>
         's ballet score. Everyone got a little over-emotional...
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/StkeNRd" title="Cat fight!">
          <img alt="Cat fight!" src="http://i.imgur.com/StkeNRd.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          c. 1940 - The avant-garde
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Composers were now doing quite individual things.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/XLH4Zyb">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/XLH4Zyb.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The late 20th Century
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Things are now getting rather silly, all making
         <a href="http://www.classicfm.com/discover/periods/modern/second-viennese-school-where-start/">
          Serialism
         </a>
         seem easy-listening in comparison. Stockhausen'sHelicopterQuartet anyone?
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/s1PKLuc">
          <img alt="" src="http://i.imgur.com/s1PKLuc.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <br/>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          c. 1920 Music for the big (and small) screen
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The last century has taken symphonicmusic in new directions and to new audiences - from massive cinematic scores, through to the new genre of orchestral video game music.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/OZQ3o7l" title="Happy cat">
          <img alt="Happy cat" src="http://i.imgur.com/OZQ3o7l.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          And to the present day
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         With
         <a href="http://www.classicfm.com">
          classical music
         </a>
         's thriving traditions now accessibleto so many through recordings, downloads, the online world and social media, all we need to do is find whatmakes us happy, and indulge in it.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://imgur.com/gNDwsbm" title="That is one happy cat">
          <img alt="That is one happy cat" src="http://i.imgur.com/gNDwsbm.gif" title="Hosted by imgur.com"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="/discover/music/cute-moments-classical-music/">
           More cats: 12 of the cutest moments in classical music
          </a>
         </strong>
        </p>
