           <h1>
            PETA is Worse than Michael Vick
           </h1>
           <ul class="navigation byline">
            <li>
             <a class="nolink">
              Posted by
             </a>
             <a href="/profile/Gaytor">
              Gaytor
             </a>
             <a class="nolink">
              on October 1, 2010 at 7:30pm
             </a>
            </li>
            <li>
             <a class="xg_sprite xg_sprite-view" href="http://www.thinkatheist.com/profiles/blog/list?user=0va2lmzf3mamz">
              View Blog
             </a>
            </li>
           </ul>

         <div class="xg_module_body">
          <div class="postbody">
           <div class="xg_user_generated">
            There are groups out there that care for animals. Ones that don't pay lip service to get attention, but actually stand behind their message. Take the
            <a href="http://www.animalleague.org/" rel="nofollow">
             NorthShore Animal League
            </a>
            . They say they care about animals, then they show it by being a no kill shelter. If you are overwhelmed by your pet, they will take it and find it a new home. They will feed or house that animal as long as the animal is alive if need be. PETA has a catchy name, People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals. Sounds good, who would be against that? Me for one.
            <div>
             <span class="Apple-style-span" style="color: rgb(51, 51, 51); font-family: 'Lucida Grande', 'Lucida Sans Unicode', sans-serif; line-height: 19px;">
              <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">
               <br/>
              </span>
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">
              PETA kills animals by the truck loads all while speaking out and protesting against agencies like the Humane Society. The Humane Society adopts out about
              <a href="http://www.humanesociety.org/animal_community/resources/qa/common_questions_on_shelters.html#How_many_animals_enter_animal_shelters_e" rel="nofollow">
               50%
              </a>
              of their 6-8 million pets a year. A sad number for sure. Consider the violent or injured or sick pets and it makes some sense. They are more of the factory of having to deal with unwanted pets. But you know what's interesting, PETA takes in pets too. That's great! I bet that they are the beacon of treating animals ethically! They are a Non-Profit so their records are public. Let's go see how they did.
             </span>
             <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">
              "According to public records from the Virginia Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services, PETA killed 2,124 pets last year and placed only
              <b style="font-size: 1em; xg-p: static !important;">
               seven
              </b>
              in adoptive homes. Since 1998, a total of 21,339 dogs and cats have died at the hands of PETA workers."
              <a href="http://www.petconnection.com/blog/2009/03/25/peta-kills-rates-for-2008-95-percent/" rel="nofollow">
               Source
              </a>
              I... I... Maybe it's for financial reasons?
             </span>
            </div>
            <div>
             <span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-weight: normal;">
              <br/>
             </span>
            </div>
