    function validateInterviewForm()
    {

            $('#int-form-message').show();

        }

       <h1 class="post-title">
        <span>
         Extreme Personal Finance: Eating Well on One Dollar a Day
        </span>
       </h1>
       <div class="post_author_div">
        by
        <span class="post_author_name">
         <a href="http://www.getrichslowly.org/blog/blogger/admin/" rel="author" title="About J.D. Roth">
          J.D. Roth
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="post_publish_div">
        <span class="clock_image">
        </span>
        <span class="post-date">
         Modified on June 13th, 2010
        </span>
        <div class="comment_count_div">
         <span class="comment_count">
          74
         </span>
         <span class="comment_label">
          <a href="http://www.getrichslowly.org/blog/2010/06/09/extreme-personal-finance-eating-well-on-one-dollar-a-day/#comments" title="Extreme Personal Finance: Eating Well on One Dollar a Day">
           Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="post-content post_content_article">
       <!-- google_ad_section_start -->
       <p>
        Last Thursday, Ron Lieber (who writes the
        <a href="http://topics.nytimes.com/topics/news/business/columns/your_money/index.html">
         Your Money
        </a>
        column for
        <i>
         The New York Times
        </i>
        ) posted
        <a href="http://twitter.com/ronlieber/status/15351469136">
         an innocuous little tweet
        </a>
        :
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         This person will have book deal &amp; Today show slot in 5 minutes. RT @marypilon Personal finance blogger eats on $1/day.
         <a href="http://bit.ly/aeGlmC">
          http://bit.ly/aeGlmC
         </a>
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        To translate into plain English, Jeffrey from the
        <a href="http://www.grocerycouponguide.com/">
         Grocery Coupon Guide
        </a>
        blog undertook a little experiment last month. In response to a challenge from his sister he ate well on just a buck a day, thanks largely to his awesome shopping skills and couponing prowess.
       </p>
       <p>
        Because I love stories of extreme personal finance, and because I havent highlighted one in a long time, I decided to take a closer look; I spent an hour reading about his project. Holy cats! This fellows shopping abilities are insane.
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.grocerycouponguide.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/05/safeway-day-1-100-percent-savings-396x600.jpg">
         <img align="right" alt="" height="250" hspace="5" src="http://www.getrichslowly.org/images/GRS/safewaycoupon.jpg" title="One of Jeffrey's coupons" vspace="3" width="198"/>
        </a>
        If you dont have time to read the entire series, at least check out
        <a href="http://www.grocerycouponguide.com/articles/eating-well-on-1-a-day-day-1-what-i-bought/">
         day one
        </a>
        , in which Jeffrey documents the things he bought to get the project started. He photographed
        <i>
         everything
        </i>
        he purchased and
        <i>
         every
        </i>
        receipt, and describes exactly how you might be able to find similar discounts. Its great stuff. (Near the end of his introductory post, he gives a sort of
        <a href="http://www.grocerycouponguide.com/articles/eating-well-on-1-a-day/">
         table of contents for the project
        </a>
        so that you can jump to the day you want.)
       </p>
       <p>
        On that first day, he picks up two boxes of instant oatmeal, some cream cheese and some sour cream, ten apples, four boxes of wheat thins, a jar of peanut butter, two cans of pork and beans, a bag of rice, and two packages of tortillas. And he spent just $4.49 for all of this.
       </p>
       <p>
        Heres Jeffreys list of the
        <a href="http://www.grocerycouponguide.com/articles/10-things-i-learned-eating-on-1-a-day-for-a-month/">
         ten thing he learned while eating on $1 a day
        </a>
        for a month:
       </p>
       <ol>
        <li>
         Grocery shopping is a game. If youre willing to learn the rules of the game, you can save big bucks.
        </li>
        <li>
         You can eat more than junk food on a dollar a day. Yes, more money lets you make healthier choices, but you can still make good choices on a tight budget.
        </li>
        <li>
         Drugstores can be a great place to get free food. Because its so easy to save money playing
         <a href="http://www.getrichslowly.org/blog/2008/05/21/how-to-save-hundreds-by-playing-the-drugstore-game/">
          the drugstore game
         </a>
         , you can often score food for free.
        </li>
        <li>
         If you dont know what a
         <a href="http://www.hotcouponworld.com/forums/other-coupons/18931-what-catalina-coupon-other-faqs.html">
          catalina coupon
         </a>
         is, you dont know about the most powerful discounts available. These coupons, which print at the cash register, can offer tremendous savings.
        </li>
        <li>
         Generic and
         <a href="http://www.getrichslowly.org/blog/2009/09/15/slash-your-grocery-bill-with-store-brand-products/">
          store-brand
         </a>
         items arent as cheap as youd think. Their regular price may be less, but youre not going to find coupons and discounts on them like you will on big national brands.
        </li>
        <li>
         Most people dont know how to shop to save. They decide what they want and then go to the grocery store to buy those things. To truly save, you need to build your meals around whats on sale (and what you have coupons for).
        </li>
        <li>
         Its possible to donate a lot of food while only eating on a buck a day.
        </li>
        <li>
         It doesnt take nearly as much time as youd imagine. Yes, theres a learning curve, but once you know what to do, its easy.
        </li>
        <li>
         Finding coupons is the key. The Sunday newspaper inserts are great, but Jeffrey says that the coupons you find in the stores themselves are often even better.
        </li>
        <li>
         Anyone can do it. And once you learn, youll save big bucks
         <i>
          every time you shop
         </i>
         .
        </li>
       </ol>
       <p>
        On
        <a href="http://www.grocerycouponguide.com/articles/eating-well-on-1-a-day-day-31-final-day/">
         the final day of his project
        </a>
        , Jeffrey summarized his purchases.
        <b>
         During the month of May, he spent $27.08 to purchase $597.96 worth of food.
        </b>
        And because of the goofy rules he agreed to with his sister, he ended up donating a bunch of stuff to his local food bank. (The last post is entertaining because of the mistake he makes and then corrects; it had me laughing out loud.)
       </p>
       <p>
        I like that Jeffrey has a realistic attitude about this project. While he thinks that anyone can do the same thing hes doing, he also recognizes that living on a tight food budget puts you in a precarious position: The problem with eating on a limited budget, when the unexpected happensis that you cant just stop into a fast food joint and grab a bite.
       </p>
       <p>
        Jeffrey was so pleased with the challenge that he decided to continue it. For the past week, hes been chronicling his continued efforts to eat well for less. (Though hes loosened some of the arbitrary restrictions he agreed to when he was trying to prove a point to his sister.) I think its fun that his readers have started to give him tips and suggestions for finding other great bargains, which helps him keep his costs even lower!
       </p>

         <p>
          I dont know if Id really go for thisI tend try to not let the perfect be the enemy of the good in budgeting, and Id be afraid that something like this could blow a whole budget. The sheer psychological effect of going over budget on one item can infect the whole budget (you suddenly have less respect for your plan, and break things more often). And lets face itthe vast majority of people are going to want a nice meal thats not on sale every so often, or get steaks for their 4th of July cookout or the like.
         </p>
         <p>
          Given, Im single, and I tend to buy often and in small amounts (if I stockpile, things always go bad, and I waste more money than Id save). But I buy store brand (or the cheapest national brand) on items I dont care about, and have adjusted some eating habits to reflect good deals at my local stores (99 cent store-brand cornflakes!)for me, staying sane and sticking to my budget requires regular deals, not one-off coupons.
         </p>
         <p>
          Im having a little trouble seeing how a lot of the lessons here could scale to a moderate food budgetafter all, you might not find store brands on deep discount, but you can rely on store-brand corn flakes being $0.99 every time you need cereal. You might not be able to get coupons for bulk vegetables or meat, but you can rely on those to be cheap, if not rock-bottom.
         </p>
         <p>
          For me, budgeting a decent amount for groceries is like buying index fundsI might not get the best possible returns, but I can guarantee Ill still be going strong and sticking to my plan years down the line. I can see myself buying store brands and fresh vegetables for years, I just cant say the same for whatever the local drugstore has on sale.
         </p>

          Thank you for all your comments as I always find what people think of this episode interesting. First and foremost, I would never suggest anyone do this as a way of life, but for me it was a good exercise to seeing what I could actually do and not do. I would hope that people that read it can look at what I was able to do on $1 a day and think about what they really could do with their current grocery budget with a bit of creativity while shopping.
          <p>
           I actually spent very little time on the couponing aspect of the challenge. I hate couponing and if I had to cut, sort and organize coupons, I wouldnt last a single day. Its just not how I would want to spend my time and would drive me absolutely crazy.
          </p>
          <p>
           Basically, I let others do all the work for me. There are plenty of free sites that match up coupons with the weekly deals and I basically only focused on the best of the best of those deals. My time spent couponing was dating the Sunday coupon inserts so that finding coupons when needed was easy and printing good ones from the Internet.
          </p>
          <p>
           Probably the most time consuming part of the challenge was trying to decide what to buy. When you have only $1 a day, spending $0.50 on something that you really want, but probably isnt a need becomes a huge internal struggle. No matter how much you know intellectually that you should buy something else, its hard to pass up when in any other instance you would be buying as many as you could for that price.
          </p>
          <p>
           I limited my shopping to the three grocery stores that I would normally shop at (except when traveling where I just went with what happened to be available) and one drug store chain because even though the other two drugstore chains do exist in my area, they are far enough away that I would not normally go to them.
          </p>
          <p>
           I also spent more time in the supermarkets than I would normally (that is not saying much since I hate shopping, so normally I wouldnt spend any time in them). I spent extra time wandering down aisles seeing if I could make deals work where normally I would be in and out as quickly as possible.
          </p>
          <p>
           Basically I wanted to show that anyone can probably do a bit better shopping with a reasonable time and effort and that can mean hundreds of dollars in savings on your grocery budget. Dont try to eat on $1 a day, but do try and think a bit more creatively with your current grocery budget so that you do get the most out of the money you spend.
