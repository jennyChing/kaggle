      <div class="body">
       <div class="body_inner">
        <p>
         Conflicts between hacker group Anonymous and Federal authorities keep escalating: now, Anonymous seems to have gotten their hands on a confidential phone call between the American FBI and the British Scotland Yard, and theyve put it on YouTube for all to see.
        </p>
        <p>
         Listen along, and laugh out loud at the law enforcement agents bumbling away, says the description on YouTube.
        </p>
        <iframe class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="365" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/pl3spwzUZfQ" type="text/html" width="485">
        </iframe>
        <p>
         The agents are a jovial lot, and spend a fair amount of time laughing and telling jokes. Though the call has been edited in some instances, it appears to concern tracking and prosecuting several members of Anonymous, including Ryan Cleary and Jack Davis, two British suspects linked to Anonymous.
        </p>
        <p>
         The FBI might be curious how were able to continuously read their internal comms for some time now, said Anonymous on Twitter.
        </p>
        <p>
         According to the
         <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/world/europe/hackers-claim-to-have-intercepted-leaked-sensitive-conference-call-between-fbi-scotland-yard/2012/02/03/gIQAyg8jmQ_story.html">
          Washington Post,
         </a>
         calls to either the FBI or Scotland Yard were not returned, though the call appears legit. Anonymous shut the FBI website down in the wake of the raid on file-sharing website Megaupload, and theyve been focusing their efforts on law enforcement ever since. They also posted an email sent by the FBI giving dial-in information for the call.
        </p>
        <p>
         Today, FBI director robert Mueller toldtold the U.S. House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence that cyber crime could be a biggerconcernfor thebureauthanterrorismin the future.
        </p>
        <p>
         There is very little we do in this day and age that is not on or somehow associated with the internet, he said, according to
         <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-3460_162-57370682/fbi-cyber-threat-might-surpass-terror-threat/">
          CNN.
         </a>
         The theft of intellectual property, the theft of research and development, the theft of the plans and programs of a corporation for the future, of all which are vulnerable to being exploited by attackers.
        </p>
        <p>
         As the FBI has decided that cyber crime is a bigger and bigger concern, so have its opponents decided that the FBI is a bigger and bigger concern. The conflict doesnt seem like it will de-escalate anytime soon.
        </p>
