         <h1>
          My Au Pair is Christian, We are Scientologists  Does that Work?
         </h1>
         <p class="post-category">
          Categories:
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/category/scientology-parenting/" rel="category tag">

         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_2252" style="width: 1034px">
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/my-au-pair-christian-in-scientology-family/8853050107_8c5ae3fca4_h/" rel="attachment wp-att-2252">
           <img alt="Our au pair spending some nature time with the kids on the Appalachian Trail." class="size-large wp-image-2252" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.scientologyparent.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/8853050107_8c5ae3fca4_h.jpg?resize=1024%2C681" title="Our au pair spending some nature time with the kids on the Appalachian Trail."/>

         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          We are a family of two working parents and two toddlers ages 4 and 2. With daycare being exorbitant and with the constant hassles of having to find babysitters for the myriad of reasons that come up, we
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/au-pairs-works-its-worked/">
           last year got our first au pair
          </a>
          .  We were so happy with how it worked out for our family that we decided to get another au pair after our first au pairs stay in the US was complete and she
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/dealing-with-toddler-grief-loss-use-of-assists/">
           had to return home to Brazil
          </a>
          .
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2249" style="width: 260px">
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/my-au-pair-christian-in-scientology-family/9216541378_094bec4fd4_h/" rel="attachment wp-att-2249">
           <img alt="Our au pair celebrating the 4th of July with us." class=" wp-image-2249 " data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.scientologyparent.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/9216541378_094bec4fd4_h.jpg?w=250" title="Our au pair celebrating the 4th of July with us."/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Our au pair celebrating the 4th of July with us.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Our new au pair is from South Africa, and she has just been a
          <strong>
           dream
          </strong>
          . She works outrageously well with the kids, the kids LOVE her, and she has enriched our whole quality of life to have her in the house.
          <em>
           [As a sidenote  if youre curious how it works to get an au pair, how they come to the USA, etc, etc,
           <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/au-pairs-works-its-worked/">
            read my first post about it here
           </a>
           , where I cover the topic in much detail.]
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          The one part of the experience that I wanted to take up is the aspect of our two cultures and how it relates to the care of our kids. Our au pair is a
          <a href="http://www.upci.org/about-us/beliefs" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.upci.org/about-us/beliefs', 'Pentecostal Christian']);">
           Pentecostal Christian
          </a>
          , and was quite active in her church back at home. Some might think that this would be a cause for conflicts or awkwardness when dealing with the task of the care of the children, but Ive really found it to be the reverse.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Personally, Ive found that I get along best with folks that have a religious background, whether or not theyre also of my own religion.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          We have spent actually many hours, my wife, my au pair &amp; I, talking about our various religious beliefs, and found that there are countless ways that they actually align  that the way we want the children treated line up extremely well with her own moral background with little, if any, differences between how we approach things. I think its fascinating really, and sometimes I feel that we share the same concepts, but just we use different languages to describe them.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_2251" style="width: 1034px">
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/my-au-pair-christian-in-scientology-family/9640363580_9036a6385d_h/" rel="attachment wp-att-2251">
           <img alt="Our daughter and our au pair out on a canoe ride." class="size-large wp-image-2251" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.scientologyparent.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/9640363580_9036a6385d_h.jpg?resize=1024%2C681" title="Our daughter and our au pair out on a canoe ride."/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Our daughter and our au pair out on a canoe ride.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          Obviously, there are things that differ greatly between our religions  but mainly those are areas where the philosophies simply dont overlap. There are parts of Scientology like the
          <a href="http://www.scientologycourses.org/courses-view/communication/step/10.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.scientologycourses.org/courses-view/communication/step/10.html', 'Cycle of Communication']);">
           <em>
            Cycle of Communication
           </em>
          </a>
          or
          <em>
           the Cycle of Action
          </em>
          or the
          <em>
           Tone Scale
          </em>
          which are applied portions of the philosophy, can be used by any religious or non-religious individual, and simply dont have a counterpart to compare with in a Christians faith.
         </p>
         <p>
          Similarly, Scientology makes no effort to define God or ones relationship to such. We have the concept of the
          <a href="http://www.volunteerministers.org/training/dynamics/overview.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.volunteerministers.org/training/dynamics/overview.html', '8th Dynamic']);">
           8th Dynamic
          </a>
          [see
          <a href="http://www.volunteerministers.org/training/dynamics/overview.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.volunteerministers.org/training/dynamics/overview.html', 'video here for description']);">
           video here for description
          </a>
          ] but that leaves the definition and ones approach to God or
          <em>
           infinity
          </em>
          entirely up to the individual.
         </p>
         <p>
          But as it pertains to parenting, the points that we hold dear, shes all too willing to help with. Things like
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/scientology-parent-video-blog-2-allowing-children-to-contribute-in-the-family/">
           allowing children to contribute
          </a>
          in the family, allowing them to finish cycles of action, teaching by example, etc  all of which she takes in stride.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_2250" style="width: 310px">
          <a href="http://www.scientologyparent.com/my-au-pair-christian-in-scientology-family/9370670349_6d7f57292a_h/" rel="attachment wp-att-2250">
           <img alt="Sharing a good laugh. " class="size-medium wp-image-2250" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/www.scientologyparent.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/9370670349_6d7f57292a_h.jpg?resize=300%2C199" title="Sharing a good laugh. "/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Sharing a good laugh.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          I also appreciate the fact that, as a practicing Christian, she does feel a very personal need to act morally  not just because well deport her or something  but because, with her background, acting well in the sight of God is
          <em>
           important
          </em>
          . So  she doesnt smoke, drink, party all night, etc. And even if some portions of her moral code may be different from mine, Ill take someone with morals pretty much any day over someone with a YOLO dude attitude where anything goes.
         </p>
         <p>
          In 1973,
          <a href="http://www.lronhubbard.org/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.lronhubbard.org/', 'L. Ron Hubbard']);">
           L. Ron Hubbard
          </a>
          wrote an essay entitled
          <a href="http://www.volunteerministers.org/l-ron-hubbard.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.volunteerministers.org/l-ron-hubbard.html', 'Religious Influence in Society']);">
           <em>
            Religious Influence in Society
           </em>
          </a>
           one I feel very strongly about, and which I think applies here.  I rather encourage you to read the whole essay, but a salient excerpt follows:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <em>
             The truth of the matter is that at no time is religion more necessary as a civilizing force than in the presence of huge forces in the hands of Man, who may have become very lacking in social abilities emphasized in religion.
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <em>
             The great religious civilizing forces of the past,Buddhism, Judaism, Christianity, and others, have all emphasized differentiation of good from evil and higher ethical values.
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <em>
             The lowering of church attendance in the United States coincided with a rise in pornography and general immorality, and an increase in crime which then caused a rise in numbers of police without a subsequent decline in actual moral aberration.
            </em>
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            <em>
             When religion is not influential in a society or has ceased to be, the state inherits the entire burden of public morality, crime and intolerance. It then must use punishment and police. Yet this is unsuccessful as morality, integrity and self-respect not already inherent in the individual, cannot be enforced with any great success. Only by a spiritual awareness and inculcation of the spiritual value of these attributes can they come about. There must be more reason and more emotional motivation to be moral, etc., than threat of human discipline.
            </em>
           </strong>
            L, Ron Hubbard
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          So, in short  it is possible, and highly enjoyable, to have someone of a different religious background in our house, and even working with that which is most precious to us  our children. It, to me, is a lesson that essentially every religious group on earth could actually get along if they took the time to understand and respect each others religious beliefs  and see where it is they agree, rather than focus on where they dont.
         </p>
