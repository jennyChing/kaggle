           Six Unforgettable Quotes from Horror Films
          </h2>
          <div class="post-calendar-date">

          <p>
           <img alt="exo" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6640" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/exo.jpg?resize=520%2C278"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           I think that people often mistakenly think of horror as a visual medium. While certain aspects of it may, indeed, be visual, we must never forget that most horror started out as words first. Also, at the heart of your story, if there is no story, there is no horror. It is with that in mind that I wanted to take a moment to point out some particularly wonderful, funny, disturbing, and unforgettable quotes from horror films that people may forget were once just words on a page. The following are quotes from horror films that really stuck with me (and in some cases, pop culture as a whole) since I first heard them. People are always so quick to quote comedy films and drama films and the funny lines from action films, but why do horror quotes get no love? This is me, trying to change that. Here are six unforgettable quotes from horror films. Please note, there will no lines from any Nightmare on Elm Street movies. Only because that dude has so many dope one-liners, he deserves his own list.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-6638">
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Jesus Wept from Hellraiser
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="jes" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-6641" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/jes.png?resize=520%2C325"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            That is what happens to me every time I go the gym. That is why I dont go the gym anymore.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           I dont recall there being a single line in horror that ever resonated with me and disturbed me quite like this line and this scene from Hellraiser did. After being splayed by hooks and slowly torn asunder, for a man to look at the screen (seriously, he is looking at us when he says it) and laughingly make a reference that Jesus wept is not only chilling and unforgettable, it is downright disturbing (which makes it kind of awesome).
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="323" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/MoA63WunEJ0?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="520">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Sorry, but it takes balls to put a line like that in horror, and here it works to great affect.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Bigger Boat line from Jaws
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="jaws" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-6642" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/jaws.jpg?resize=520%2C325"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Thank god sharks dont really do this. Oh wait, they do? F*ck.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           I know this is the one on the list that seems to get the most attention, but I had to shout it out anyway. Though I want to avoid the most clich lines from horror everyone already quotes ( Its Alive! Theyre Here! and so on), I had to put this line here or else I would look like a charlatan. Truth is, there are a good dozen other horror films that have managed to use the bigger boat line in some form or another as an homage to this film for decades now. As much as we all may be familiar with the line, I bet few of you knew that
           <a href="http://screenrant.com/greatest-unscripted-movie-scenes/">
            the line was actually unscripted
           </a>
           and made up on the spot by actor Roy Schneider?
          </p>
          <p>
           That makes it so much more epic.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="323" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/8gciFoEbOA8?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="520">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Now THAT, my friend, is how you do improv.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            God Is An Absentee Landlord from The Devils Advocate
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="pacino" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-6643" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/pacino.jpg?resize=520%2C347"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            This dude is so badass, he gets a bigger picture than anyone else on the page. Sorry guys, this is Pacino as Satan. He earned it.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           I realize that The Devils Advocate is not a full-on horror movie, but for me to not mention this line from Al Pacino in this film would be a fail on my part. It might actually be the greatest Devil speech ever given on film. The whole entire speech is just brilliant, and delivered with such gusto and passion by Pacino, you start buying that this guy really is the Devil, and that he really does have a grudge against the man upstairs.
          </p>
          <p>
           He says this line the way a bull would charge. Just f*cking brilliant. I cannot stress that enough. It made up for Keanus shitty on-again, off-again accent in the film.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="323" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/kdR_StE6Niw?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="520">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Sorry, but speeches in films really dont get much better.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Kick Ass For The Lord from Dead Alive
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="arse" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-6644" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/arse.png?resize=520%2C292"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            It always stood out to me that he looked like Peterman from Seinfeld.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           Remember when Peter Jackson used to make the most batshit insane horror films? There is no better example of that than Dead Alive. A movie well ahead of its time in terms of gore, humor, and just the overall tone of the film. Think of the pacing of Evil Dead, add even MORE humor and guts, and you have one of the greatest gore films of all time. Though there are dozens of quotable lines in the film (Thats my Mom your pissing on!), I had to go with this line, as it is one of the best character intros and lines of dialogue ever put on film:
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="323" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/MfkHkdu5IEI?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="520">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Yes, that,s a priest kicking the shit out of zombies. Yes, this was made by the same guy who made those 19 Lord of the Rings movies.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           If I was ever given the chance to beat up a Priest pedophile, this is the line I would yell during that assault.
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Whats In The Box from Se7en
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="Brad Pitt Se7en" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6645" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Brad-Pitt-Se7en.jpeg?resize=300%2C142"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            He is such a lovely man, he actually gets a smaller picture than anyone else.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           If you have seen Se7en, you know the scene I speak of. If you have not seen Se7en (all Se7en of you), then I dont want to ruin it for you here. I will simply say this scene is a perfect example of writing and acting, meeting up and creating something beautiful. The line all came down to the way (actually, ways) it was said. There is a certain desperation in Brad Pitts voice in this scene, and we all feel it with him as we all fear what he is about to find out.
          </p>
          <p>
           I will let you guys watch the whole four-minute scene, so the build up reaches a proper pay off. A simple quote, yes. But one my mind has never, ever forgotten.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="323" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/1giVzxyoclE?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="520">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            Such an emotionally devastating film moment, perfectly captured by Brad Pitt.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            The Best Your Mother Diss Ever from The Exorcist
           </strong>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="the_exorcist" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-6639" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/remycarreiro.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/the_exorcist.jpeg?resize=520%2C292"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            This movie still scares the fuck out of me.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           So your Mom has just died, and you feel guilty because of some shit that went down at the end. You find yourself in front of a little girl who is possessed by an incarnation of the Devil. She looks at you, and tells you that Your Mother Sucks Cocks in Hell. Well Ill be damned (literally). That is about as evil and vile (and any other version of those four letters you can mix up and make words out of) as it comes. Not only is that the most awful thing to hear about your Mom, but it is being told to you a child. Sort of. Either way, that shit is mad f*cked up.
          </p>
          <p>
           The thought of your Mom sucking c*cks is life shattering enough, but the thought of her doing it in Hell takes it to another level. I mean, come on. Dorothy Mantooth is a Saint. Wait a sec..
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="embed-youtube" style="text-align:center; display: block;">
            <iframe allowfullscreen="true" class="youtube-player" frameborder="0" height="323" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/rxofWN8BAoc?version=3&amp;rel=1&amp;fs=1&amp;showsearch=0&amp;showinfo=1&amp;iv_load_policy=1&amp;wmode=transparent" type="text/html" width="520">
            </iframe>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            I mean, could I end this list with any other entry? Nope.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           So what did we learn from this list? Essentially, the Devil talks a lot of shit.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             So there you have it. Six unforgettable quotes from horror films. Which ones did I miss? Which one is your favorite? Take to the comments and let me know. Or
             <a href="https://www.facebook.com/RemyCarreiroIsBetterThanBacon?ref=hl">
              pop over here and we can talk about it.
             </a>
            </strong>
           </em>
          </p>
