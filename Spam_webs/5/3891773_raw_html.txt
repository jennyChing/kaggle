     <p>
      <img alt="2490190_large2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15738 aligncenter" src="http://mbworld.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/2490190_large2.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      I am continually amazed at the level of lunacy thats actually let loose on public roads. Its not just Hellcats, Veyrons or even the new breed of hybrid hypercars that truly astonish me. It isnt even the tuner specials with 2,000 horsepower. Rather, its those select few homologated cars that international racing organizations require manufacturers to build to allow them to go racing.
     </p>
     <p>
      Case in point: the Mercedes-Benz CLK GTR.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="2490191_large2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15739 aligncenter" src="http://mbworld.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/2490191_large2.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      The CLK GTR was originally built as a racecar that would compete at the infamous 24 hours of Le Mans. However, to meet the ACOs rules, Mercedes was required to build a total of 25 road cars that mirrored the racecar. That meant using a 6.0L V12 engine that pumped out 604 horsepower and 572 lb-ft of torque, which was enough for the car to sprint to 62 mph in just 3.8 seconds.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="2490194_large2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15740 aligncenter" src="http://mbworld.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/2490194_large2.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      Upon launch, the car was an immediate success, not only on the public consumer level, but also on the racing level, which gave the CLK GTR even more presence as it was truly bred from racing.
     </p>
     <p>
      The CLK GTR is one of those cars that, evenat almost 20 years old, still looks like a car youd find posted on the wall of a young boys bedroom today.
     </p>
     <p>
      <img alt="2490213_large2" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-15742 aligncenter" src="http://mbworld.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/2490213_large2.jpg" width="600"/>
     </p>
     <p>
      The CLK GTR can still hang its hat with the Paganis and Ferraris of the world, and by all accounts it was a properly fast car. Additionally, it remains one of the last real racecars for the road. Nowadays, McLaren and Ferrari wont let you take their track-focused creations past the confines of a circuit. The CLK GTR lets you truly experience what a race car for the road would actually be like.
     </p>
     <p>
      The silver CLK GTRshown here, though, has led an even more interesting life; one that involved being destroyed only to risefrom the ashes back to the state its in. And guess what? Its
