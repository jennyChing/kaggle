      <h1>
       2014 Chevrolet Camaro Z/28 laps Nrburgring in 7 minutes, 37.4 seconds
      </h1>
      <p>
       October 14, 2013
      </p>
     </div>
    </div>
    <section class="main-body">
     <!-- if use thumb as main image -->
     <!-- or use first from content as featured -->
     <!-- node-aw_video-article-featured-top.tpl.php -->
     <article class="story featured-video first-featured featured video">
      <div class="article-wrap">
       <div class="royalSlider video-gallery rsDefault rsNoDrag">
        <a class="rsImg" data-rsvideo="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qj1h75vA1Jw" href="http://img2.autoweek.com/styles/gen-932-524/public/131019880_0.jpg?itok=k5Qv-nSa">

     <div class="wrap-content one">
      <article class="story contents">
       <!-- node-aw_paragraph-content-article.tpl.php -->
       <p>
        How about a nice video of a
        <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20130327/NEWYORK/130329827">
         2014 Chevrolet Camaro Z/28
        </a>
        tackling the Nrburgring to start your day? The run itself only takes 7:37.4, so it won't cut into your morning productivity
        <i>
         too
        </i>
        much. Be sure to crank up the volume.
       </p>
       <p>
        You might love watching automakers chase each other's best times around Germany's Nrburgring, or you might think that running production cars around the roughly 13-mile circuit is nothing but an irrelevant battle for bragging rights, but there's no denying that the 'Ring is an international performance-testing benchmark.
       </p>
       <p>
        American automakers trying to break into the world luxury market (see the
        <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20130829/CARNEWS/130829768">
         Cadillac CTS Vsport on the track
        </a>
        earlier this year) or looking to prove their performance chops (
        <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20111006/CARNEWS/111009935">
         Chevy ran a Camaro ZL1 on the 'Ring
        </a>
        a few years ago) are especially keen on gaining the cachet that comes along with a respectable Nrburgring time.
       </p>
       <p>
       </p>
       <div style="text-align:center;margin-top:30px;margin-bottom:22px;">
       </div>
       <p>
        Despite the rain, this Z/28 managed to shave a few seconds off the still-impressive 7:41.27 run a Camaro ZL1 laid down in 2011. Chevrolet is quick to point out that the Z/28's time is a bit better than the 991 Porsche 911 Carrera S and a
        <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20060703/free/60623011">
         Lamborghini Murcielago LP640
        </a>
        , and we can add the
        <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20130416/carreviews/130419882">
         Lexus LFA
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20130708/CARNEWS01/130709919">
         Ford GT
        </a>
        to the list of cars the Camaro bests.
       </p>
       <p>
        Since you're probably not a professional driver, you shouldn't push your 505-hp 7.0-liter V8-equipped as hard as Adam Dean did in the above video -- especially not when roads are wet. But at least you'll have something to hold over the heads of every
        <a href="http://www.autoweek.com/article/20130521/carreviews/130529975">
         Audi R8 V10
        </a>
        driver who dares to show up at your local show-and-shine.
       </p>

        <img alt="Graham Kozak" src="http://img2.autoweek.com/styles/article-author/public/GKozak_400x400_0.jpg?itok=0CHrPTsV" title="Graham Kozak"/>
        <p>
         <a href="/authors/graham-kozak">
          Graham Kozak
         </a>
         - Graham Kozak drove a 1951 Packard 200 sedan in high school because he wanted something that would be easy to find in a parking lot. He thinks all the things they're doing with fuel injection and seatbelts these days are pretty nifty too.

         </a>
        </p>
