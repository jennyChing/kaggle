         <h1 class="entry-title">
          How to: Delicate Beaded Necklace
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          <div class="meta clearfix">
           <span class="date">
            Jan 9, 2015
           </span>
           <span class="comments-links">
            <a href="http://thesarahjohnson.com/2015/01/09/how-to-delicate-beaded-necklace/#comments">
             2 Comments
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-meta -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content">
         <!-- Easy Plugin for AdSense V8.60 -->
         <!-- [leadin: 1 urCount: 1 urMax: 0] -->
         <div class="ezAdsense adsense adsense-leadin" style=";margin:12px;">
          <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
          </script>
          <!-- responsive leaderbard -->
          <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-9101928723407899" data-ad-format="auto" data-ad-slot="4529130362" style="display:block">
          </ins>
          <script>
           (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
          </script>
         </div>
         <!-- Easy Plugin for AdSense V8.60 -->
         <p>
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-7" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1339" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-7.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Wire wrapping is one of my favorite jewelry-making techniques. I learned how to wire wrap when I internedwith jewelry designer
          <a href="http://www.amandakeidan.com/" target="_blank">
           Amanda Keidan
          </a>
          back in 2004. Once I got the hang of it, it took my jewelry-making to a new level.Its a really simple technique that creates unlimited possibilities.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-1332">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          You will need:
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1347" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-16" class="wp-image-1347 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-16.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Beads of your choice. These are faceted aquachalcedony beads.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1346" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-15" class="wp-image-1346 size-full" height="494" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-15.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Chain: I recommend at least 16, more if you want your necklace to turn out longer.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <!--more-->
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1345" style="width: 767px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-14" class="wp-image-1345" height="1136" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-14-683x1024.jpg" title="" width="757"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Wire, depending on the size of the hole of your bead, probably around 20-24 gauge. (The higher the number the thinner the gauge.) A clasp (I like the lobster clasp!) and a jump ring.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1349" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-18" class="wp-image-1349 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-18.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Flat nose pliers, round nose pliers and wire cutters. As Aczeb always says, theright tool for the job will make your life a lot easier.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The steps:
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1344" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-13" class="wp-image-1344 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-13.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Take your round nose plier and make a loop at the end of a 3 piece of wire. Leave some excess wire at the end of the loop, you will wrap this around the base to secure the loops hole. Before wrapping the end, slip the chain into the loop. Hold the loop flat with the round nose plier and pinch the end of the wire around and around the base with the flat nose plier, until the end is completely wrapped and tight around the base (like below).
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1343" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-12" class="wp-image-1343 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-12.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Slip as many beads onto the wire, and bend the wire however you like. I always think odd numbers of things look best together and create nice balance.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1341" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-9" class="wp-image-1341 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-9.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Now that the beads are on the wire, you will make another loop at the unclosed end. Loop the wire around the round nose plier to form a circle close to the bead so there isnt any extra wire space. Leave excess wire to wrap around the base like you did before. Dont forget to slip the chain into the loop before you wrap the ends around the base!
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1340" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-8" class="wp-image-1340 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-8.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Now you should have one long chain attached to your row of beads on either end. Measure how long youd like the necklace and cut the ends. Next well add a clasp for easy on/off removal.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1333" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-1" class="wp-image-1333 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-1.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Take two 3 inch pieces of wire and attach to the newly cut chain via the loop method. Wrap the excess ends around the base to secure the chains to the wire.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1334" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-2" class="wp-image-1334 size-full" height="509" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-2.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Next I add another bead for a more decorative finish near the clasp. After you wrap the loops to the chain, but a bead on the wire before looping again. In the same way you slipped the chain into the loop before wrapping the ends, this time slip a clasp. Then wrap the excess end of wire in a circle around the base of the loop. Be sure to make the second loop close enough to the bead so there is not any extra room in between the loop and the bead.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1335" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-3" class="wp-image-1335 size-full" height="542" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-3.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Do the same for the other side, but inside of slipping a clasp into the unfinished loop, slip in a jump ring for the clasp to latch onto.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1337" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-5" class="wp-image-1337 size-full" height="502" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-5.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           You are done!
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_1338" style="width: 774px">
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-6" class="wp-image-1338 size-full" height="504" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-6.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The finished product! Looks great on its own, or makes a great layering piece.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <img alt="diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-11" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1342" height="534" src="http://thesarahjohnson.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/diy-beaded-gold-chain-necklace-11.jpg" title="" width="764"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Questions? Leave them in the comments below.
         </p>
         <p>
          Happy wire wrapping!
         </p>
