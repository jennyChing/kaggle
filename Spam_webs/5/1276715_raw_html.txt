     <p>
      <b>
       Lucian Pulvermacher
      </b>
      (born
      <b>
       Earl Pulvermacher
      </b>
      , April 20, 1918  November 30, 2009)
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      was a
      <a href="/wiki/Traditionalist_Catholic" title="Traditionalist Catholic">
       traditionalist
      </a>
      <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Schismatic" title="Schismatic">
       schismatic
      </a>
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" title="Roman Catholic Church">
       Roman Catholic
      </a>
      priest. He was the head of the "True Catholic Church," a small
      <a href="/wiki/Conclavism" title="Conclavism">
       conclavist
      </a>
      group that elected him
      <b>
       Pope Pius XIII
      </b>
      in October 1998. He resided in
      <a href="/wiki/Springdale,_Washington" title="Springdale, Washington">
       Springdale, Washington
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">
       Life and career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lucian_Pulvermacher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Born in 1918 near
      <a href="/wiki/Marshfield,_Wisconsin" title="Marshfield, Wisconsin">
       Marshfield, Wisconsin
      </a>
      in the town of
      <a href="/wiki/Rock,_Wood_County,_Wisconsin" title="Rock, Wood County, Wisconsin">
       Rock
      </a>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Wood_County,_Wisconsin" title="Wood County, Wisconsin">
       Wood County
      </a>
      , Pulvermacher was one of nine children of a farm family.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      His three brothers became priests. In 1942, at the age of 24, he joined the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capuchin_Order" title="Capuchin Order">
       Capuchin Order
      </a>
      , taking the religious name
      <i>
       Lucian.
      </i>
      He was subsequently ordained to the priesthood on June 5, 1946.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At first he was posted to a parish in
      <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">
       Milwaukee
      </a>
      , but in 1948 he was sent to
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He spent the greater part of his career as a Capuchin (from 1948 to 1970) as a missionary priest in the
      <a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_Islands" title="Ryukyu Islands">
       Ryukyu Islands
      </a>
      , including
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">
       Okinawa
      </a>
      . In 1970, he was transferred from Japan to
      <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
       Australia
      </a>
      , where he continued his missionary work until his disillusionment with the changes that followed the
      <a href="/wiki/Second_Vatican_Council" title="Second Vatican Council">
       Second Vatican Council
      </a>
      of 19621965.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_6-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In January 1976 he left the Capuchin Order, and returned to the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       United States
      </a>
      . "I was without money," he later remembered, "without a home or anything. The few things I brought along with me I could carry in two bags."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He left what he called "the
      <a href="/wiki/Mass_of_Paul_VI" title="Mass of Paul VI">
       Novus Ordo
      </a>
      , bogus Council
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vatican_II" title="Vatican II">
       Vatican II
      </a>
      Church" and spent eight months "with the general Latin Mass traditionalists until I saw there was no unity. Hence, I am alone on the job here in the States since August 1976."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_6-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      According to a Capuchin friar from Pulvermacher's former province, Pulvermacher died on November 30, 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-death_8-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-death-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-death2_9-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-death2-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
