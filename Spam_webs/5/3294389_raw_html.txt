       <p>
        They were dead set on changing the way you book your travel. In the building next to me on the Microsoft campus in 1996, there was a team that wasabsolutely convinced they would radically alter thetravel industry by allowing customers to directly accessa hugedatabase of flightsviathe web. It turns out they were right, and within a few years, Rich Barton, who led the team, convincedSteve Ballmer to
        <a href="http://blog.seattlepi.com/venture/2008/05/01/barton-microsoft-couldnt-do-another-expedia/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://blog.seattlepi.com/venture/2008/05/01/barton-microsoft-couldnt-do-another-expedia/', 'spin the project called Expedia out of Microsoft');" rel="nofollow">
         spin the project called Expedia out of Microsoft
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Expedia and its competitors did go on to change the world of travel. Theiruser-friendly interfaces to databases, once reserved for professionals, gave usa
        <em>
         self-serve
        </em>
        option for booking flights, hotels, and car rentals. In the process,they seriously shook upthe travel industry, knocking out a large portionof the travel agent profession.
       </p>
       <p>
        In the United States, there are currently around13,000 travel retail locations,
        <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/10/03/travel/travel-agent-survival/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.cnn.com/2013/10/03/travel/travel-agent-survival/', 'down from a peak of 34,000 in the mid-1990s');" rel="nofollow">
         down from a peak of 34,000 in the mid-1990s
        </a>
        when Internet travel websites were just getting started. The real toll, though, is mostvisible when you look at Department of Labor statistics on actual employment in the travel agent sector. There are now less than half the number of travel agent jobs than there were just two decades ago:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Travel-Agent-Employment.jpg">
         <img alt="Travel Agent Employment" class="aligncenter wp-image-7056" height="298" src="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Travel-Agent-Employment.jpg" width="482"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        Automation is never a simple story. What creates traumatic economic displacement for some, oftenbrings convenience and lower prices for others  and sure enough, the cost of air travel has dropped significantly over this same period. Of the top twenty markets, Las Vegas and Phoenix are the only two cities were prices have actually increased. For the remaining eighteen, prices have all fallen. On average, airfares in these top twenty markets last year were30% less than they were twenty years earlier:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Airfare-Declines.jpg">
         <img alt="Airfare-Declines" class="size-large wp-image-7322 aligncenter" height="360" src="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Airfare-Declines-1024x597.jpg" width="618"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2013/02/how-airline-ticket-prices-fell-50-in-30-years-and-why-nobody-noticed/273506/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2013/02/how-airline-ticket-prices-fell-50-in-30-years-and-why-nobody-noticed/273506/', 'Airline deregulation');" rel="nofollow">
         Airline deregulation
        </a>
        undoubtedlyplayed a criticalrole inthese airfare decreases, but there also seems little doubt that the easy comparison shopping thatservices like Expedia has enabled isalso an important factor.
       </p>
       <p>
        Technology givethand technology taketh away. Those travel agencies that survived had to move up market, away from purely transactional ticket-bookingto more consultative services, where domain expertise and the ability to provide useful advice really mattered. Iactually used a firm like this earlier this year on a familytrip to China and it was quite valuable.
       </p>
       <p>
        But now, even these firms may soon be at risk, thanks to new computing technologies that will gradually take over some of the domain expertise in travel.
       </p>
       <h2>
        Travel AgentSmith
       </h2>
       <p>
        The first wave ofautomation to hit the travel agency market was
        <em>
         transactional
        </em>
         the result of giving end users direct access to a reservations database. This next wave of automation builds on the first wave, but is
        <em>
         knowledge-based
        </em>
        and reliesona new layerofartificial intelligence.
       </p>
       <p>
        This next wave of automation willreplace travel agents with personal travel concierges, able to sift through mountains of information to give you tailored advice on how to plan your trip. That, at least, is the plan of the startup called
        <a href="http://wayblazer.com/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://wayblazer.com/', 'WayBlazer');" rel="nofollow">
         WayBlazer
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <blockquote>
        <p>
         When my colleagues and I created Travelocity, we reduced the need for live travel agents to handle routine arrangements. But we didnt address another role that agents fillas expert advisors. With cognitive computing, were putting the intelligence back into travel planning. People can spend less time searching and more time dreaming.
         <br/>
         <a href="http://asmarterplanet.com/blog/2014/10/cognitive-computing-transform-travel.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://asmarterplanet.com/blog/2014/10/cognitive-computing-transform-travel.html', 'Terry Jones, Chairman, WayBlazer');" rel="nofollow">
          <em>
           Terry Jones, Chairman, WayBlazer
          </em>
         </a>
        </p>
       </blockquote>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/F1-Racing.jpg">
         <img alt="F1-Racing" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-7327" height="167" src="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/F1-Racing-300x167.jpg" width="300"/>
        </a>
        The most interesting thing about WayBlazer is that ithasthe
        <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yJptrlCVDHI" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yJptrlCVDHI', 'Jeopardy-winning');">
         Jeopardy-winning
        </a>
        IBM Watson under its hood.WayBlazers not yet on the market: its first prototype application will befor the Austin Convention &amp; Visitors Bureau, but you can get a taste for what the user interface might look like on its
        <a href="http://austintexas.wayblazer.com/schedule/2014-10-31" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://austintexas.wayblazer.com/schedule/2014-10-31', 'Formula 1 racing app');" rel="nofollow">
         Formula 1 racing app
        </a>
        .
       </p>
       <p>
        Behind the scenes, WayBlazer willdraw on some ofthe
        <a href="http://www.ibm.com/smarterplanet/us/en/ibmwatson/developercloud/services-catalog.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ibm.com/smarterplanet/us/en/ibmwatson/developercloud/services-catalog.html', 'eight Watson services that IBM recently released');" rel="nofollow">
         eight Watson services that IBM recently released
        </a>
        through its Bluemix cloud services platform. These includeservices forlanguage processing, framing questions to more easily extract knowledge from large knowledge bases, language translation, user modeling and message tailoringbased on thosemodels, and forgraphic display of information:
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Screen-Shot-2014-10-12-at-8.11.35-PM.png">
         <img alt="8 IBM Watson Services" class="aligncenter wp-image-7065" height="354" src="http://www.the-vital-edge.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/Screen-Shot-2014-10-12-at-8.11.35-PM.png" width="600"/>
        </a>
       </p>
       <p>
        It looks as though WayBlazer will be largely drawing on thelanguage processinganduser modeling of Watson, and using these cognitive computing services to create meaning out oflarge bodies of structured and unstructured travel informationsitting in databases, websites and even on social network posts.Like other approaches to machine learning, Watson will get better as it learns from our interactions with it and as it digests more information. WayBlazers Terry Jones estimates that they will have learned what they need for the Austin city travel knowledge basein approximately two months.
       </p>
       <h2>
        <em>
         Vertical
        </em>
        and
        <em>
         Horizontal
        </em>
        Artificial Intelligence
       </h2>
       <p>
        One thing worth noting aboutIBM is that it is rolling out Watson services as a business-to-business offering, targeting vertical industries throughpartnershipsin
        <a href="http://www-03.ibm.com/press/us/en/pressrelease/43722.wss" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www-03.ibm.com/press/us/en/pressrelease/43722.wss', 'retail');">
         retail
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.healthcareitnews.com/news/watson-now-open-app-development" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.healthcareitnews.com/news/watson-now-open-app-development', 'several healthcare applications');" rel="nofollow">
         several healthcare applications
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="https://www.elance.com/q/blog/elance-partners-ibm-create-talent-apps-powered-ibm-watson" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.elance.com/q/blog/elance-partners-ibm-create-talent-apps-powered-ibm-watson', 'talent management');" rel="nofollow">
         talent management
        </a>
        , and now travel. The only way that you will get to experience IBM Watson artificial intelligence is throughan IBM partner. Even WayBlazer, a Watson partner itself, willonlybeavailable throughpartners, rather than its own consumer-facing website. This is what you might call avertical strategy for the artificial intelligence market.
       </p>
       <p>
        Contrast thatwith what companies like Google,Facebook, Twitter, Yahoo and LinkedIn are likelyup to, which is more of ahorizontal strategy for artificial intelligence. They wont deep-dive into specificvertical markets, but instead choose broader, more utility-like approaches for processingthe broad range of topics their end users are likely to need. Your interaction with the artificial intelligence mayfeel a bitdifferent across these services, but this horizontal approach will be part of the way you make sense of the world. It willbecomeanincreasingly central partof your relationships with thesecompanies and the value you derive from their services.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, when it comes to using artificial intelligence to help you plan your vacation in 2018, will you usea general purpose artificial intelligence withGoogle or Facebook, or will you work throughsome entity like anairline or localtourism bureau, using an industry-specific artificial intelligence like WayBlazer and its Watson engine?
       </p>
       <p>
        In the short-run, its very possible that a vertical approach like WayBlazer might produce superior results. Mybet, though, is that competitive advantage in the artificial intelligence market will depend on having ahighvolume of user interactions. Todaysmachine learning approaches to artificial intelligence learnand improve by interacting and getting feedback from people, and that means the technologies with greater scale inuser interactions are more likely to learn faster. Thats why, in the longer-run Im betting that Google, Facebook and the horizontal players will eventually have the edge over IBM.
       </p>
       <p>
        IBMsrecent
        <a href="http://www.wired.com/2014/10/ibm-twitter/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.wired.com/2014/10/ibm-twitter/', 'deal with Twitter');" rel="nofollow">
         deal with Twitter
        </a>
        suggests the companyunderstands the importance of volume when it comes toalgorithmic learning. But can either company rely on partnerships for something as critical to their future as artificial intelligence learning? Its far too early to tell.
       </p>
       <p>
        One things for sure though, if youre one of the remaining 60,000 a travel agents out there, you might want to start looking at some other lines of work. Travel Agent Smith has his eye on you.
       </p>
