        <h2>
         Spoiler-free review
        </h2>
        <p>
         <em>
          Jack the Giant Slayer
         </em>
         is quite different from the normal 
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_and_the_beanstalk">
          Jack and the Beanstalk
         </a>
          story weve heard. It mixes in some elements from the Cornish fairy tale 
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_the_Giant_Killer">
          Jack the Giant Killer
         </a>
         . But the end of
         <em>
          Jack the Giant Slayer
         </em>
         nicely illustrates how the story changed over time from what they show in the movie to what we know today.
        </p>
        <p>
         Even though the giants eat people, there isnt really gore. Theres plenty of violence, but
         <em>
          Jack the Giant Slayer
         </em>
         also doesnt take itself too seriously. If your kids are okay with the
         <em>
          Harry Potter
         </em>
         series, then
         <em>
          Jack the Giant Slayer
         </em>
         wont give them problems.
        </p>
        <p>
         But this remix also contains more magic and darkness than the original story. Monks use magic in search of God, humans try to use the magic to rule, and the giants are blood-thirsty ogre-like creatures who want revenge.
        </p>

        <h2>
         Using magic to find God
        </h2>
        <p>
         In
         <em>
          Jack the Giant Slayer,
         </em>
         monks wanted to find God and thus used magic to make beans that would take them to heaven. This reminded us of the Tower of Babel from Genesis 11.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          Now the whole earth had one language and the same words. And as people migrated from the east, they found a plain in the land of Shinar and settled there. And they said to one another, Come, let us make bricks, and burn them thoroughly. And they had brick for stone, and bitumen for mortar. Then they said, Come, let us build ourselves a city and a tower with its top in the heavens, and let us make a name for ourselves, lest we be dispersed over the face of the whole earth. And the Lord came down to see the city and the tower, which the children of man had built. And the Lord said, Behold, they are one people, and they have all one language, and this is only the beginning of what they will do. And nothing that they propose to do will now be impossible for them. Come, let us go down and there confuse their language, so that they may not understand one anothers speech. So the Lord dispersed them from there over the face of all the earth, and they left off building the city. Therefore its name was called Babel, because there the Lord confused the language of all the earth. And from there the Lord dispersed them over the face of all the earth. [Genesis 11:19, ESV]
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         Its crazy how many people are searching for their idea of god and ignore the clear directions in Scripture. When they did this with the Tower of Babel, God confused their languages. In
         <em>
          Jack the Giant Slayer
         </em>
         , the monks search brought them to a prison between heaven and earth that brought death and destruction to the land.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Its just a story
        </h2>
        <p>
         Young Jack and the princess Isabelle wereenamored with the stories of the giants from long ago. But Jacks dad reminded him, Its just a story.
        </p>
        <p>
         But when Jack asked, How do you know giants arent real? His dad replied very honestly, I dont.
        </p>
        <p>
         The same challenge is often raised against the Bible. How do we know the Bible is true? Always remember that proof outside of Scripture elevates that proof to a position of higher authority than the Bible. Instead, the world is full of things that
         <em>
          confirm
         </em>
         the Bibles historicity (historical authenticity).Archaeology, science, geology, astronomy, anthropology, and more all scream loudly that the Bible recounts actual historical events that literally happened.
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          If I have told you earthly things and you do not believe, how can you believe if I tell you heavenly things? [John 3:12, ESV]
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         So if the Bible is true on all physical counts, then we can also trust it on spiritual and future accounts!
        </p>

        <h2>
         Do you believe in God?
        </h2>
        <p>
         One of the giants asked this classic question, but it fizzled out into nothing. But it does raise an interesting connection.
        </p>
        <p>
         If
         <em>
          Jack the Giant Slayer
         </em>
         was true, what would the monks have believed about the giants? Did they think God created them to be the vile creatures that they were?
