     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        Whore of the Orient
       </b>
      </i>
      is an upcoming
      <a href="/wiki/Action-adventure_game" title="Action-adventure game">
       action-adventure
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">
       video game
      </a>
      for the
      <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">
       Microsoft Windows
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">
       PlayStation 4
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">
       Xbox One
      </a>
      being developed by
      <a href="/wiki/Team_Bondi" title="Team Bondi">
       Team Bondi
      </a>
      and published by
      <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Interactive_Entertainment" title="Warner Bros. Interactive Entertainment">
       Warner Bros. Interactive Entertainment
      </a>
      , due for a tentative 2015 release.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Release_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Release-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Setting">
       Setting
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Whore_of_the_Orient&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Setting">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Set in the year of 1936, the game is centered around the corrupt city of
      <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">
       Shanghai
      </a>
      in the hands of Western powers, filled with mob crime and political troubles. The
      <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">
       Kuomintang
      </a>
      ruthlessly puts down labor movements in an effort to suppress communism, whilst the International Police Force attempt to keep the peace.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kotaku1_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-kotaku1-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vault_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-vault-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Development">
       Development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Whore_of_the_Orient&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tleft">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="349" data-file-width="600" height="145" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2c/Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg/250px-Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2c/Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg/375px-Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/2c/Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg/500px-Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg 2x" width="250"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Whore_of_the_Orient_screenshot.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        The first official screenshot for the game, featuring the names of companies
        <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Interactive_Entertainment" title="Warner Bros. Interactive Entertainment">
         Warner Bros.
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Miller_Mitchell" title="Kennedy Miller Mitchell">
         KMM Interactive
        </a>
        .
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Following the release of Team Bondi's
      <a href="/wiki/2011_in_video_gaming" title="2011 in video gaming">
       2011
      </a>
      video game
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/L.A._Noire" title="L.A. Noire">
        L.A. Noire
       </a>
      </i>
      , the company was in talks with other studios to work with, like they had previously done with
      <a href="/wiki/Rockstar_Games" title="Rockstar Games">
       Rockstar Games
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It was reported that no companies actually wanted to work with Team Bondi, due to previous claims that their work ethic was unhealthy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      However, it was later stated that
      <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Miller_Mitchell" title="Kennedy Miller Mitchell">
       Kennedy Miller Mitchell
      </a>
      bought out Team Bondi, and that they're working together on
      <i>
       Whore of the Orient
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Various people involved in the development of the project suggest that the game will be a narrative action adventure title that is similar to Team Bondi's earlier title,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/L.A._Noire" title="L.A. Noire">
        L.A. Noire
       </a>
      </i>
      . It is suggested that the proprietary facial
      <a href="/wiki/MotionScan" title="MotionScan">
       MotionScan
      </a>
      technology used in
      <i>
       L.A. Noire
      </i>
      will be reused in the new game project.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ukpsmag1_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-ukpsmag1-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The project has received $200,000 worth of funding from Screen NSW, an Australian trade and investment board.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Early unfinished gameplay footage was leaked in mid-2013, depicting the combat-style action.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
