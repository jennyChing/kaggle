         <h1 class="entry-title">
          NYC exhibition showcases early American guitar designs
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-meta">
          By
          <a href="http://www.designfaves.com/author/steph" rel="author">
           Steph Guinan
          </a>
          /
          <a href="http://www.designfaves.com/category/design">
           Design

         <img alt="NYC exhibition showcases early American guitar designs" class="attachment-full wp-post-image" src="//www.designfaves.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/02-guitar-10102-1393-790x350.jpg"/>
         <div class="entry-content">
          <p>
           On display at the
           <a class="external" href="http://www.metmuseum.org/exhibitions/listings/2014/early-guitars" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Metropolitan Museum of Art
           </a>
           is an exhibition that looks at the history of American guitar design, centering on early 19
           <sup>
            th
           </sup>
           century Martin guitars. Christian Frederick Martin, a German immigrant, was influenced by the instruments of his own culture as well as Spanish guitars from the Andalusia region.
          </p>
          <p>
           The 35 guitar exhibition will showcase some of Martins earliest guitars including those where he used his famed X-braced construction. It will also showcase guitars from Austro-German and Spanish guitar makers, visually demonstrating the connection between Martins instruments and those that influenced him
          </p>
