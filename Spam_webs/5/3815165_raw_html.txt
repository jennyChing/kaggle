       External hard drive support for Xbox One wont be ready at launch

      <div class="article-content clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
       <figure class="intro-image image center full-width" style="width:602px">
        <img height="396" src="http://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/xbox-one-inside-the-console-640x426.jpg" width="602"/>
        <figcaption class="caption">
         <div class="caption-credit">
          <a href="http://www.wired.com/gadgetlab/2013/05/xbox-one-development-photos/" rel="nofollow">
           Wired
          </a>
         </div>
        </figcaption>
       </figure>
       <!-- cache miss 4119:single/related:40baa33be70b3108046cb2859201a123 -->
       <!-- empty -->
       <p>
        When the Xbox One was first revealed in May,
        <a href="http://arstechnica.com/gaming/2013/05/how-the-xbox-one-draws-more-processing-power-from-cloud-computing/">
         Microsoft said
        </a>
        that users would be able to expand the system's internal 500GB hard drive with external USB storage. It seems that feature won't initially be available to early adopters when the console is released in November, though.
       </p>
       <p>
        Speaking during
        <a href="http://majornelson.com/cast/2013/09/01/pax-podcast-2013/">
         a live podcast recorded at PAX this weekend
        </a>
        , Xbox Community Manager Larry "Major Nelson" Hryb said that, while the external storage feature is being worked on, "my understanding is that feature will not be there at launch because the team's working on other things. It's definitely on the list; I don't know when it will come in, though."
       </p>
       <p>
        As a practical matter, this probably won't mean much to early Xbox One owners; it will take time to fill up 500GB, after all. Still, it's never a good sign when a company is still working to add promised features to a product that's planned for release in only a few months.
       </p>
       <p>
        In comparison,
        <a href="https://twitter.com/yosp/statuses/373623812681523200">
         according to Sony Head of Worldwide Studios Shuhei Yoshida
        </a>
        , the PlayStation 4 will not support external USB storage for installed games, but PS4 users will be able to replace the 500GB internal hard drive in the system with expanded storage. (The Xbox One will not offer direct physical access to the internal hard drive for replacement.) Nintendo's Wii U supports external USB hard drives of up to 2TB in size.
       </p>
