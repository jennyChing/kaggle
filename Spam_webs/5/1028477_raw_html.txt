            Ford introduced the 240 I6 in 65. In van applications, it made 150 HP. The companion 300 (a stroked 240) made 170. Torque numbers almost matched HPs.
           </p>
           <p>
            Falcon vans weighed about 2700 pounds, had a payload of about 1600 lbs.
            <br/>
            In comparison, a VW Kombi from the same year (1965) had a 1500cc Beetle-derived four with 53 HP and weighed around 2130 lbs. So the much-loved VW was 20% lighter but had 1/3 the power.
           </p>
           <p>
            The Falcon was truck-geared, so people write them off as slugs. But it would pull and haul plenty and, after a few minutes to get there, cruise happily at highway speeds (in low-crosswind conditions,of course).
           </p>
           <p>
            And a good 240 pulls 30 MPG or better at cruising speeds. On top of that, these were among the most reliable engines Ford ever produced. You couldnt kill one unless you really worked at it. There are truckloads of hot-rod parts still available for them as well. Four-barrels, heads, etc. You can make serious power with one of these and the right tweaks. All of which would be transparent.
           </p>
           <p>
            So, with an automatic and a good frame hitch, no one should have any reservations about using this to pull 1200 or 1500 pounds of car and spares. Any cant pull its own weight criticism is misplaced.
           </p>
