       <h3>
        DIY Level: Beginner/Intermediate
       </h3>
       <ul>
        <li>
         Materials Needed:
        </li>
        <li>
         Plywood cut to desired size (19/32 thick used in example, and sized about 2.5 x 3.5)
        </li>
        <li>
         Paint (optional)
        </li>
        <li>
         Minwax Polycrylic (optional)1 or 1-1/2 nails
        </li>
        <li>
         Cotton string
        </li>
        <li>
         Butcher paper or newspaper (or any paper, really, thats large enough to make a template)
        </li>
        <li>
         Heavy duty wall mount
        </li>
       </ul>
       <h3>
        Step 1: Draw your map.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/draw-your-map/" rel="attachment wp-att-238906">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238906" height="532" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/draw-your-map.jpg" width="855"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Grab a piece of butcher paper, cut to the size of your plywood, and a map of your string art subject.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/map-ready/" rel="attachment wp-att-238905">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238905" height="513" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/map-ready.jpg" width="736"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Using the grid system (smaller grid on the smaller map that correlate to a larger grid on your butcher paper), draw the outline of your map on the butcher paper. Dont worry about the details too much  theyll be lost on the string art. Stick with the major silhouette points. When your butcher paper map is to your satisfaction, cut it out.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/create-a-paper-map/" rel="attachment wp-att-238904">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238904" height="549" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/create-a-paper-map.jpg" width="815"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <h3>
        Step 2: Prepare your plywood.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/prepare-your-plywood/" rel="attachment wp-att-238903">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238903" height="600" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/prepare-your-plywood.jpg" width="900"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        With your plywood cut to the size you want, sand the front surface down. To achieve an industrial or rustic look, dont worry about being too precious  sand the rough edges just enough to keep slivers at bay, but thats about all. In this example, I kept the factory printing on the plywood, just sanded a little to mute it a bit.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 3: Add industrial touches. (optional)
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/prepare-the-paint-for-plywood/" rel="attachment wp-att-238902">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238902" height="600" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/prepare-the-paint-for-plywood.jpg" width="900"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Paint a stripe or add some paint splatters to the front of your plywood to give it a roughened, industrial vibe
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/paint-the-plywood/" rel="attachment wp-att-238901">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238901" height="600" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/paint-the-plywood.jpg" width="551"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Some paint on the end of a stirring stick, for example, is a perfect vehicle for making a few discreet splatters, if you want.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/center-plywood/" rel="attachment wp-att-238900">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238900" height="600" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/center-plywood.jpg" width="900"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        An off-center stripe can make for a great touch that keeps things from being too predictable.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/prepare-the-plywood/" rel="attachment wp-att-238899">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238899" height="900" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/prepare-the-plywood.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Sand everything down. Remember that industrial style is anything but shiny-new-paint-ish.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 4: Seal the plywood. (optional)
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/seal-plywood/" rel="attachment wp-att-238898">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238898" height="600" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/seal-plywood.jpg" width="900"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Slather a coat of Minwax Polycrylic (or similar sealing agent) onto the plywood to give it a nice, finished look.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Let the sealing coat dry.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/coat-dry/" rel="attachment wp-att-238893">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238893" height="900" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/coat-dry.jpg" width="600"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <h3>
        Step 5: Position map onto plywood.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/map-on-plywood/" rel="attachment wp-att-238890">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238890" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/map-on-plywood-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        When sealing coat has dried, grab your butcher paper map, a hammer, your nails, a bit of painters tape, and your plywood. Its time to have some fun!
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/tape-string/" rel="attachment wp-att-238888">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238888" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/tape-string-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Loosely tape a few corners of your butcher paper map onto the plywood  just enough to hold it in place exactly where you want it.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 6: Hammer nails around map silhouette.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/hammer-nails/" rel="attachment wp-att-238886">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238886" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/hammer-nails-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        For nails, I used black 1 panel nails. For your reference: in total, I used probably 2/3 or 3/4 of a 6 oz. box.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/start-hammering/" rel="attachment wp-att-238885">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238885" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/start-hammering-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Pick a corner and start hammering. Decide on how far apart you want your nails, then be consistent as you work around the silhouette.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-materials-for-stringing-art-2/" rel="attachment wp-att-238881">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238881" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/diy-materials-for-stringing-art1-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        A couple of tips for best results: Keep the nails going in perpendicular to the plywood; if one gets off somehow, use your hammer on the side of the nail to gently maneuver it back into place.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/silhouette-design1/" rel="attachment wp-att-238880">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238880" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/silhouette-design1-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Also, do your best to keep the exposed nail height consistent throughout. I recommend hammering each nail into the plywood about 1/2 because thats enough to secure it into the board but not so much the nail pokes out the back of the plywood.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 7: Hammer focal heart.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/hammer-focal-point/" rel="attachment wp-att-238879">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238879" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/hammer-focal-point-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Choose the place you want to emphasize  where you live, or where you have fond memories  and hammer in a heart with nails. Here, youll want to hammer the nails fairly close together, because strings from all over the silhouette will be merging here. Its a pretty big ratio, of silhouette nails to heart nails, so insert as many as you comfortably can.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/paper-silhouette/" rel="attachment wp-att-238878">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238878" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/paper-silhouette-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        When silhouette and focal heart are complete, remove the butcher paper.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 8: Install mounting hardware.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/install-mounting-hardware/" rel="attachment wp-att-238877">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238877" height="1024" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/install-mounting-hardware-683x1024.jpg" width="683"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        It might feel like youre jumping the gun a bit, installing the mounting hardware before youve completed the string art project, but now is actually the easiest and most effective time to install the mounting hardware. Plywood is heavy, so I got a heavy-duty wall mount fixture (although you most likely wont need one that holds 200 lbs, like this one shows).
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-materials-for-stringing-art/" rel="attachment wp-att-238876">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238876" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/diy-materials-for-stringing-art-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Center, then install the smaller mounting piece on the back of your plywood, as shown on the packaging installation instructions.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/screwdrive-handle/" rel="attachment wp-att-238875">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238875" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/screwdrive-handle-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        I just used a handheld screwdriver, and it was easy. Takes about 2 minutes.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 9: String it up.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/string-it-up/" rel="attachment wp-att-238874">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238874" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/string-it-up-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Your nailed map silhouette will look something like this.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/cotton-rope/" rel="attachment wp-att-238873">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238873" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/cotton-rope-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Tip: Use cotton string because it will not stretch and lose its tautness like polyester or poly blend strings have a tendency to do. I had a ball of 475 feet of string and used probably half of it (my best guess) for this project.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/tie-string-on-nails/" rel="attachment wp-att-238872">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238872" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/tie-string-on-nails-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Tie off string onto a nail. Dont worry about keeping the string ends short at this time; you can trim them later. Just tie a square knot on a nail about halfway through the bottom section of your map.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/sting-start-process/" rel="attachment wp-att-238871">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238871" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/sting-start-process-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        You can see where I started my stringing in this photo. Its best to not start in the corner, because the halfway point will give you a better idea on how to allocate or distribute the nail usage in your heart location.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-string-art-process3/" rel="attachment wp-att-238870">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238870" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/diy-string-art-process3-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Since you started halfway through the lower section of your map, string this up to the lowest nail on your heart.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/string-it-the-heart/" rel="attachment wp-att-238869">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238869" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/string-it-the-heart-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        String it up to the heart and back to the same nail (only for the starting line). Wrap once around your perimeter nail, then go back toward the heart. String on the same nail, then come back to the nail next to your starting perimeter nail.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/kee-loose-track/" rel="attachment wp-att-238868">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238868" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/kee-loose-track-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Go up and back, from the perimeter to the heart, like this. Keep loose track of proportions of the number of nails used; for example, when I had strung the nails about 10 from my starting perimeter nail (these were all strung on the same single heart nail), then Id move onto the next heart nail. In other words, if you used 20 nails in your heart, for example, youll want to loosely divide your outer map into 20, and disperse the stringing accordingly.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-string-art-process2/" rel="attachment wp-att-238867">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238867" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/diy-string-art-process2-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Depending on the map youve chosen, you may come to a part where there is no possible straight line from the perimeter nails to the heart. This happened several times for me, with my map of the United States. To keep the flow of the string art, I simply strung these nails together in a way that maintained the line directions toward the heart, if that makes sense.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/tie-of-string-art/" rel="attachment wp-att-238866">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238866" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/tie-of-string-art-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        This often mean I had to tie off the string, in order to maintain the integrity of the line directions. Up close, this may seem like an odd move, but from a foot or more away, its the string directions that create the art here.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/dip-of-texas/" rel="attachment wp-att-238865">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238865" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/dip-of-Texas-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        The tip of Texas, for example, ended up looking like this. See how the western strings dont even go near the central heart, but they give the illusion that theyre headed that way? Thats what youre after.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/continue-stringing/" rel="attachment wp-att-238864">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238864" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/continue-stringing-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Continue stringing, making sure youre distributing the strings around the heart in proportion to your perimeter nails. Also, its critical that you pull each string pass very taut, so the completed map will retain its shape.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-string-art-process1/" rel="attachment wp-att-238863">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238863" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/diy-string-art-process1-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Here is another example of the tricky spaces in the northeast section, where the string on perimeter nails wouldnt have a chance of reaching the heart.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-string-art-process/" rel="attachment wp-att-238862">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238862" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DIY-String-Art-process-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        I established my line toward the central heart.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/closer-look-to-string-art/" rel="attachment wp-att-238861">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238861" height="683" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/closer-look-to-string-art-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Using this string line, I went back and forth on the nails, doing my best to maintain the line direction, always focused on the heart.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 10: Tie off the last string and give your fingers a break.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/finished-string-process/" rel="attachment wp-att-238860">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-238860" height="742" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/finished-string-process-1024x742.jpg" width="1024"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        When completed, your map might look something like this. I fell in love with mine and its raw, industrial vibe. (The string is much more apparent in real life.)
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 11: Mount the string art on the wall.
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/mount-on-the-wall/" rel="attachment wp-att-238859">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238859" height="1067" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/mount-on-the-wall.jpg" width="1600"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        Use a level to install the second piece of your heavy duty wall mount on the wall.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-wall-string-art-system-hanging/" rel="attachment wp-att-238858">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238858" height="1600" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/diy-wall-string-art-system-hanging.jpg" width="1067"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        As per the installation instructions, keep the angled side facing up. This might look odd or feel counterintuitive, but it is this angled edge that holds the piece up; the piece will slide down right behind this edge.
       </p>
       <h3>
        Step 12: Enjoy your new wall art!
       </h3>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-wall-string-art/" rel="attachment wp-att-238857">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238857" height="1067" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/diy-wall-string-art.jpg" width="1600"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        My USA string art is hung on the wall in our hallway. I love its
        <a href="http://www.homedit.com/accent-wall-ideas/" title="Accent walls">
         wall accent large scale
        </a>
        and the fact that, despite being a 3-dimensional piece of art, it doesnt stick out too far.
       </p>
       <figure class="post-image">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.homedit.com/diy-map-string-art/diy-wall-string-art-finished/" rel="attachment wp-att-238856">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-238856" height="1067" src="http://cdn.homedit.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/02/DIY-wall-String-Art-finished.jpg" width="1600"/>
          <span class="goto-gallery">
           View in gallery
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
       </figure>
       <p>
        We hope you enjoy customizing your own piece of string art and that you enjoy the low price tag on such a large piece of wall art as well.
       </p>
