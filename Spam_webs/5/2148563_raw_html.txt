                  Switch way too stiff; Unreliable
                 </span>
                 <span itemprop="description">
                  I took the Steripen Adventurer on a three-night backpack to the Olympic National Park coast with my son and his two children in July 2008.   I was anxious to try the Steripen after hearing so much about it.  After the first attempt to purify water, I was very unhappy with it.  However, to give it a fair trial I used it for two more rounds of purification (8 quarts each time) before becoming so disgusted that I put the Steripen away and used chemical disinfection (which I always carry as backup) for the rest of the trip. 

The switch is so stiff that most of the time I had to call my son to turn it on because I couldn't do it.  I've heard the same complaint from other woman hikers.  The Steripen was obviously not designed for women with small hands. 

The Steripen kept switching off in mid-treatment, forcing me to treat each quart 2 or 3 times.  I had the directions with me and read them carefully, as did my son (an electrical engineer), and we could find no explanation for this behavior.  The batteries were fresh and I was definitely getting the "aborted" signal, not the "low battery" signal.  

It's too slow for groups unless each person purifies his/her own water.  With a family party of 4, I had to sit by the buggy creek stirring and stirring for at least half an hour for a half-day's water.  

The Steripen does not purify the water that remains on the rim or outside threads of the bottle after dipping it into the water source.  This source of contamination remains.  The small amount may not be a concern for healthy adults, but I was very concerned about the young children in our party.  

The Steripen cannot be used with the lightweight, narrow mouth containers that most backpackers carry.  Basically, it requires a 1 quart, wide-mouth Nalgene bottle, which weighs nearly 4 times as much as the 1-liter containers I normally carry.  

Total weight of the Steripen system, with batteries, extra batteries (I'd take 2 extra sets for a week's trip) and the required Nalgene bottle is comparable to the weight of all but the heaviest pump filters and several ounces heavier than the gravity filter I've used for 2 years.  Do not be fooled by the low weight without batteries into thinking the Steripen is a way to reduce pack weight.  

If you do decide to use the Steripen, be sure to take plenty of chemical disinfection for backup.  Either you'll need it or, like me, you'll decide to forget the Steripen and use the chemicals.  

Needless to say, I returned the Steripen after this trip.  This product needs radical improvement before I would again consider using it.
