        <div class="entry-content clearfix">
         <p>
          <img alt="DIY Nail Tutorial - Snowflake Nails" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4830" height="684" src="http://lauralaurablog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/11.jpg" width="711"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Hi again! Serra here from
          <a href="http://peacockpie.com">
           peacockpie.com
          </a>
          with a winter snowflake design just in time for the last cold spell to kick in! (Bring on spring!)
         </p>
         <div>
          For this manicure I have used a blue called Serenity by Star Kin and Orly White Out for the details. My favourite base and top coats to use at the moment are Nail Tek Foundation III and HK Girl by Glisten &amp; Glow. You can use a tiny nail art brush if you have one, or a toothpick or something similar.
         </div>
         <h2>
          DIY Snowflake Nails
         </h2>
         <div>
          <img alt="Step One DIY Nail Tutorial - Snowflake Nails" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4828" height="600" src="http://lauralaurablog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/step1.jpg" width="711"/>
         </div>
         <div>
          1. A base coat is really important for this one because a lot of blue nail polishes will stain!
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <img alt="Step Two Easy Nail Tutorial - Snowflake Nails Tutorial" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4827" height="600" src="http://lauralaurablog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/step2.jpg" width="711"/>
         </div>
         <div>
          2. Paint white crosses where you would like your snowflakes. Mix it up with different sizes and placements.
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <img alt="Snowflake Nails Tutorial from Peacock Pie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4826" height="600" src="http://lauralaurablog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/step3.jpg" width="711"/>
         </div>
         <div>
          3. Paint Xs through each cross.
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <img alt="Snowflake Nails Tutorial from Peacock Pie" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4825" height="600" src="http://lauralaurablog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/step4.jpg" width="711"/>
         </div>
         <div>
          4. Add little V shapes to each branch of each flake where you can fit them. Some might be too small to add the lines, just go with what looks best
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <img alt="step5" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4824" height="600" src="http://lauralaurablog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/step5.jpg" width="711"/>
         </div>
         <div>
          5. Dot in some little dots for falling snow and if you like you can place on some gems to give it some bling. If you have gems that dont come with their own adhesive, a little dot of top coat under each one will hold tack them in place
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          <img alt="1" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4830" height="684" src="http://lauralaurablog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/11.jpg" width="711"/>
         </div>
         <div>
          6. Top coat over the finished nails, adding a second top coat where you have gems to help seal everything in place!
         </div>
         <div>
         </div>
         <div>
          Wrap up warm and Ill be back soon with some spring looks!
         </div>
