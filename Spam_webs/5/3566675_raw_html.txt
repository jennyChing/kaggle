           <strong>
            Erectile dysfunction
           </strong>
           , also being referred to as impotence, affects close to estimated 20 million men in the United States alone. Nevertheless, while it is definitely not a picnic and it causes significant stress in patients, it is not as dreadful nor as a long-term problem as it sounds or as is generally believed to be. All the shame and panic typically associated with erectile issues is only due to the fact that the condition is totally misunderstood most of the time.
           <p>
            FACT: National Institute of Health states that approximately 5% of all 40-year-old men and in between 15% and 25% of 65-year-old men experience
            <strong>
             ED
            </strong>
            on a long-lasting basis.
           </p>
           <p>
            A very common problem which impacts the majority of men at some time in their life is the periodic failure to get aroused, which can happen for a variety of factors, such as from drinking too much alcoholic beverages or being over-tired.
           </p>
           <p>
            So in an attempt to define the ways to control or get rid of it, lets first figure out exactly what erectile dysfunction causes are, and how it might be triggered. Then we will certainly talk about how does it impact the lives of men who struggle with it, and how it also affects people around them, for it does influence their partners, family, and friends too. Once we have a thorough understanding of the causes and exactly what it involves, it becomes much easier to see what possible treatment or remedies we can use.
           </p>

           <h2>
            How To Define the Problem of Erectile Dysfunction (ED)
           </h2>
           <p>
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erectile_dysfunction" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Erectile Dysfunction">
             Erectile Dysfunction
            </a>
            (ED) is generally defined as the inability to attain or to hold an erection long enough to participate in sexual intercourse with a partner. However, to be able to completely understand erectile dysfunction, we first have to investigate how the actual physiological process happens.
           </p>
           <p>
            Penis consists of 2 chambers called the
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corpus_cavernosum_penis" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Corpora Cavernosa">
             corpora cavernosa
            </a>
            , which are filled with spongy tissues, and which run whole the length of the organ. These two chambers include a network of blood rich vessels.
           </p>
           <p>
            An erection happens when the vessels of the corpora cavernosa relax and completely open  due to the fact that the nervous system sends appropriate messages that will send a proper signal to the penis. These impulses from the brain and regional nerves cause the blood to rush in, fill the arteries and maintain the high pressure, which expands the penis and causes an erection.
           </p>
           <div style="float: left; padding-right: 20px;">
            <img alt="Erectile Dysfunction ED Causes Stress" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-154" height="239" src="//www.edprotocols.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Erectile-Dysfunction-ED-Causes-Image-300x239.jpg" width="300"/>
           </div>
           <p>
            Under typical conditions, when you are sexually aroused, your brain will send a message through your spine and into the nerves of your penis. The nerve endings of the penis then release neurotransmitters (which are very sophisticated chemical messengers), which instruct the arteries that supply corpora cavernosa to relax and fill up with blood. As they expand, this causes the other veins, which would usually drain blood from the penis, to remain closed. As the penis ends up being filled with blood, it expands and stiffens, causing what we see as an erection. It is then obvious that any potential issues with capillaries, nerves, or tissues of the penis can interfere with a full and complete erection.
           </p>
           <p>
            You can see that an erectile dysfunction is clearly an issue with bodys blood flow, and hence, is pointing to the fact that it could be easily solved. There is really no need to be humiliated by this condition, to abandon your relationships be distressed or depressed, because it could affect men across all ages.
           </p>
           <p>
            In general, failure to get an erection less than 20% of the time is not unusual and treatment is seldom required. Failure to achieve arousal more than 50% of the time, however, typically indicates there is an issue needing a closer look.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Some Erectile Dysfunction Symptoms
           </h2>
           <p>
            In quite large number of cases, Erectile Dysfunction occurs only in particular scenarios. For instance, you have no problems while masturbating and often wake up in the morning with a normal erection. On the other hand, you may be not able to get an erection when you are with your sexual partner.
           </p>
           <p>
            It is most likely, then, that the underlying causes of ED are mainly mental. However, if you are unable to obtain an erection under any conditions at all, this may point towards causes that are purely of a physical nature.
           </p>
           <p>
            In order to attain an erection, these conditions will need to happen. If there is something disrupting any of them, a full arousal might not happen: circulation in the penis should be normal, the veins need to be able to react and trap the blood inside, and there needs to be a strong and correct stimulus from the brain.
           </p>
           <p>
            It is not surprising that usual causes of Erectile Dysfunction include conditions that directly impede blood circulation, including arteriosclerotic vascular disease (your blood vessels harden over time) or
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venous_leak" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Erectile Dysfunction - Venous Leak">
             Venogenic Erectile Dysfunction
            </a>
            (caused by drainage of the cavernosal tissue).
           </p>
           <p>
            In addition to that, mental factors, such as tension, stress or anxiety are also known as causes of erectile dysfunction (ED), along with any physical injuries you may have sustained. If you have any type of chronic disease, or are taking certain medications or have been diagnosed with
            <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peyronie%27s_disease" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Peyronie's Disease">
             Peyronies Disease
            </a>
            , you might also experience ED as part of secondary level symptoms.
           </p>

            Usual reactions when diagnosed with ED
           </h3>
           <p>
            Most guys fail to genuinely understand Erectile Dysfunction, its causes, and how it can be treated successfully. It is understandable why, but this usually creates a knee-jerk types of reactions, panic and fear. You have to stand back for a second and do a thorough erectile dysfunction protocol review for each of the options you are presented with.
           </p>
           <p>
            Make sure you do the research, learn as much as you can about the causes and reasons for this condition and start believing that erectile dysfunction truly isnt the end of your life and that you are certainly not stuck with it forever! Check out all available options (including the ED protocol review we prepared), keep on trying and youll find the cure, remedy or a system that works for you.
