         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          DIY Rubber Stamps
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-12T01:05:04+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">

         <div class="pf-content">
          <p>
           Make super cute dishtowels using DIY rubber stamps! An inexpensive stamp set and fabric paint will work well on just about any material, so dig out those solid colored shirts or tote bags and get started!
          </p>
          <p>
           We used kitchen towels and printables to get us started, but if youre feeling ambitious you can draw your own design and transfer it to the stamp block. Just remember to place it face down on the block and only cut out the negative space surrounding the design!
          </p>
          <p>
           Once youve transferred and cut out your design, youll want to practice stamping on scrap fabric a few times. Figure out how youd like your pattern to look and how much paint to use with each stamp then go for it! Youll end up with a one-of-a-kind design that suits your taste and dcor perfectly.
          </p>

           <h2 class="ERSName recipe-title" itemprop="name">
            How to make DIY rubber stamps
           </h2>
           <div class="divERSHeadItems">
           </div>
           <div class="row-fluid">
            <div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-4 ERSIngredients">
             <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
              what you'll need
             </div>
             <ul class="ingredients">
              <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               rubber stamps printable
              </li>
              <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               scissors
              </li>
              <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               pencil
              </li>
              <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B000BYXF4S/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B000BYXF4S&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=chery01-20" target="_blank" title="Speedball stamp making kit">
                stamp making kit
               </a>
              </li>
              <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               <a href="http://www.ilovetocreate.com/store/products/234-tulip-soft-paint.aspx" target="_blank">
                fabric paint
               </a>
              </li>
              <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               paintbrush
              </li>
              <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
               tea towels
              </li>
             </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="col-xs-12 col-sm-8 ERSInstructions instructions">
             <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
              let's do it
             </div>
             <ol>
              <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               Download the
               <a href="http://everydaydishes.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/diy-rubber-stamps-cherylstyle-printable.pdf" target="_blank" title="Everyday Dishes &amp; DIY Rubber Stamps">
                rubber stamp printable
               </a>
               and open in
               <a href="http://get.adobe.com/reader/otherversions/" target="_blank" title="Adobe Reader">
                Adobe Reader
               </a>
               .
              </li>
              <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               Print and cut out desired stamp template where indicated. Trace the graphic with pencil then place face down onto your stamp block or eraser. To transfer the traced graphic onto your block, rub the back of it using your fingernail.
              </li>
              <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               Once the design is transferred to your stamp block, begin to cut out the negative space surrounding the design using the cutter. Make sure not to cut the design itself.
              </li>
              <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               Next, add paint to your paintbrush and apply to the design on the stamp.
              </li>
              <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
               Practice stamping your pattern on a scrap piece of fabric to see how it turns out and to get a feel for the amount of paint you want to use. Stamp once and reapply paint every time. Once youre pleased with the results, carefully begin to stamp your towels or other fabric. Allow them to dry then hang and admire!
              </li>
             </ol>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSNotesDiv">
            <div class="ERSNotesHeader">
             Cheryl Says
            </div>
            <div class="ERNotes ERSNotes">
             You can use an eraser instead of a stamp block, but youll still need a Speedball cutter to cut out the design. Its cheaper to buy the set!
            </div>
