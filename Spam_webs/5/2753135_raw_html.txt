                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Gingers, Redheads and Freckles
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-8937466948932222041" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both;">
                      <a href="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-GbHIwdmdNiQ/VPvuO5S3YzI/AAAAAAAAAIw/sNBRvZ5oVCE/s640/blogger-image--414333433.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-GbHIwdmdNiQ/VPvuO5S3YzI/AAAAAAAAAIw/sNBRvZ5oVCE/s400/blogger-image--414333433.jpg" width="300"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       In January I won a Twitter competition with Border Biscuits (@BorderBiscuits). They were doing a campaign called #GingerJanuary on Twitter where many people were posting various pictures of all things ginger! I was one of their lucky winners of biscuits and this gorgeous bag. Being a fellow ginger/redhead myself I was over the moon and I'm so proud to be a ginger! This bag means a lot to me and I'll be proud to carry it. Thanks very much Border Biscuits!
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       Redheads have always got a lot of stick for their hair colouring. At school there was always the whispers and comments 'hey carrot top' or 'Ginge'. I have even heard of people getting asked in the street if they are a natural redhead, really! I mean would you go up to a brunette and start asking them if they are a natural brunette? It doesn't happen.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       I have been asked the question, can you go out in the sun? That is a bit on the offensive side for a redhead, as we are redheads not vampires! All we need is some factor 50 sun cream, to protect those gorgeous freckles!
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
                      <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">
                       Back in July 2012 I won a book from a Goodreads giveaway called 'Redhead', it is written by a fellow redhead called Ian Cook. It is a thriller with historical content and covers alot of mythology surround redheads. It's a very interesting book with great characters with many twists and turns building up to a great conclusion. The book has an amazing cover, showing how dark and dramatic the story actually is. It is a very exciting read for fellow redheads and I highly recommend it.
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
