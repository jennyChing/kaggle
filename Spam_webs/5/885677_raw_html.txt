         10 Things to do in Osaka
        </h1>

        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          The trip to Japan was a whirlwind of adventures. A few weeks of planning, reading online guides, tripadvisor and
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1742201164/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1742201164&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=cumcik-20&amp;linkId=NXV5LGAHG2XFFPMO" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1742201164/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1742201164&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=cumcik-20&amp;linkId=NXV5LGAHG2XFFPMO', 'travel books']);">
           travel books
          </a>
          <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=cumcik-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1742201164" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
          didnot really prepare us for the task that lay ahead.
         </p>
         <p>
          Our first destination in Japan was
          <a href="http://www.city.osaka.lg.jp/contents/wdu020/english/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.city.osaka.lg.jp/contents/wdu020/english/', 'Osaka city']);" target="_blank">
           Osaka city
          </a>
          , the capital city of the Osaka Prefecture in the Kansai region, located over 500km from Tokyo city and just a 3 hour bullet train ride away. The name is pronounced with an emphasis on O, therefore Oh-Saka. Once a former trade capital of Japan (before Tokyo), it holds 7% of the countrys population, outputs an economy bigger than that of Hong Kong and Thailand combined. Osaka is reputed to have the highest number of bridges in Japan. Bunraku, the Japanese Puppet theater, had its origins here. This second largest city of Japan was nicknamed the Nations Kitchen, not just because of the wide variety of food available but actually traces back to olden times where it was a gathering and distribution center of numerous raw food materials and commodities.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16482986860" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16482986860', '']);" title="IMG_20150213_225333 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="IMG_20150213_225333" height="360" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8673/16482986860_5f8743c252_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Osaka nights.. Dotonburi and Namba..
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-39370">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          Having said that, we had a great time sussing the system out for ourselves (read our guide on how
          <a href="http://ccfoodtravel.com/2015/03/how-to-navigate-japans-train-subway-system/" target="_blank">
           Navigate Japans Train &amp; Subway System plus getting Connected
          </a>
          here). Upon the morning of arrival
          <a href="http://ccfoodtravel.com/2015/03/flying-airasia-x-to-the-tokyo-marathon-2015/" target="_blank">
           by flight
          </a>
          to Tokyo,we would catch the Shinkansen to Osaka, where our adventures would begin.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16482827008" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16482827008', '']);" title="IMG_20150213_121421 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="IMG_20150213_121421" height="426" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8608/16482827008_ecf5af8d37_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Osaka bound..! These Shinkansen arrive like clockwork and leave just as punctually. If you miss it, thenit might be a mad scramble to get to the right platform for the next one. We are happy to report that for our entire trip, we did not miss any Shinkansen we were meant to be on. Thats because I was the time keeper, and I didnt let us be late for anything!
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16484245599" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16484245599', '']);" title="IMG_20150213_125619_1 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">

         <p>

         <p>
          Osaka was a massive cosmopolitan that didnt seem to sleep. Everywhere you turned, there were hip and happening locals milling about  from the cool Umeda neighborhood, with a praiseworthy, pulsating nightlife to the super packed Namba department stores it was hard to go home at midnight.. there was just so much to see and do.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16463099767" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16463099767', '']);" title="IMG_20150214_181456 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">

         <p>
          Loved the varied ethnic restaurants in the entertainment districts but mainly loved eating at the markets and off the hidden path restaurants. We didnt have much time as we arrived late in the evening  only a little less than 2 days at this first port of call, before heading to Kyoto, so heres our top 5 things to do in Osaka.
         </p>
         <h4>
          <strong>
           1. Stay at the New Otani Hotel, Osaka
          </strong>
         </h4>
         <p>
          Arriving via Shinkansen in Shin-Osaka, then changing for the JR line and alighting at sakajken Station, we were pleased to find our hotel, a mere 4 minute walk from the JR Train station.
         </p>
         <p>
          As I needed to have practice runs before the marathon, I read about the locals jogging around the 2 square kilometer Osaka-jo-koen (Osaka Castle Park) and thought this would be the ideal place to be. Not only would you get to jog around the castle lake and a baseball field, youd get to run within the castle grounds (60,000 square meters (15 acres)) and covertly use the super fast japanese as pacers. Through Agoda we found the best rates with
          <a href="http://www.agoda.com/hotel-new-otani-osaka/hotel/osaka-jp.html" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.agoda.com/hotel-new-otani-osaka/hotel/osaka-jp.html', 'New Otani']);" target="_blank">
           New Otani
          </a>
          . We use the
          <a href="http://www.agoda.com/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.agoda.com/', 'portal']);" target="_blank">
           portal
          </a>
          pretty extensively with our bookings as it has the most competitive rates where hotels are concerned.

         <h4>
          <strong>
           3. Visit theNational Bunraku Theater
          </strong>
         </h4>
         <p>
          Bunraku is the traditional puppet theatre of Japan, a high-level stage art of which Japan can be very proud. Bunraku was originally the name of the theatre in which this puppet drama was performed, but gradually it came to be used as the name of the art itself and is today used as the official name of the puppet theatre.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_39494" style="width: 560px">
          <a href="http://ccfoodtravel.com/2015/03/10-things-to-do-in-osaka/2092_01/" rel="attachment wp-att-39494">
           <img alt="photo credit : japan-guide.com" class=" wp-image-39494" height="305" src="http://ccfoodtravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/2092_01-590x327.jpg?1e5b2b" width="550"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           photo credit : japan-guide.com
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          The art only came to be known as Bunraku around the end of the Meiji era.Anyway, this theater is one of the best places to see it.Bunraku puppets are about 1.2 meters (4 feet) tall. Each puppet is controlled by three people  the main puppeteer, right puppeteer and left puppeteer. It can take 30 years of practice to become a main puppeteer.
         </p>
         <h4>
          <strong>
           4. Run Osaka Castle
          </strong>
         </h4>
         <p>
          Osaka Castle was built originally by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, Japans revolutionary leader in the late 16th century who rose from peasantry to become one of the three unifiers of Japan and put an end to a long, bloody period of feudal warfare. Completed in 1597, the castle was the largest, most intimidating castle in Japan at the time, and acted as a catalyst for rapid growth of Osaka.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16669640242" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16669640242', '']);" title="20150214_115337(0)-001 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="20150214_115337(0)-001" class=" aligncenter" height="640" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8636/16669640242_2fb769a260_z.jpg" width="360"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          This area is also a runners paradise I tell you, and since I had a marathon to run the following week, it was the ideal location for a test run, and warm up if you like.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16472036249" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16472036249', '']);" title="20150214_115855-001 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="20150214_115855-001" height="360" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8562/16472036249_0bf30ed346_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          The gorgeous Osaka Castle .. orOsaka-j as the locals refer to affectionately as..
         </p>
         <p>
          This castle was a realdisplay of power, using, its said, the labour of 100,000 workers. Although the present structure is a 1931 concrete reconstruction (refurbished 1997), its nonetheless quite a sight, looming dramatically over the surrounding park and moat. Inside is an excellent collection of art, armour, and day-to-day implements related to the castle, Hideyoshi and Osaka. An 8th-floor observation deck has 360-degree views.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16483250720" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16483250720', '']);" title="20150214_121134-001 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">

The entire Osaka Castle Park covers about two square kilometers with lots of green space, sport facilities, a multi-purpose arena (Osakajo Hall) and a shrine dedicated to Toyotomi Hideyoshi, a prominent political figure from the 16th century (Sengoku Period) who also completed the construction of the castle.

          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16691257885" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16691257885', '']);" title="P2156086 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="P2156086" height="427" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8676/16691257885_9e748d4b70_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Torafugu or Tiger Puffer Fish is the most popular choice. Poisoned remove and deskinned, its lips, gils, fins, flesh, liver and roe (shirako) is placed separately in plastic wrapped styrofoam plates for shoppers to purchase home. Some retailers would slice the flesh thin into translucent sashimi strips to be eaten onsite.
           <br/>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16690223502" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16690223502', '']);" title="P2156108 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="P2156108" height="427" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8604/16690223502_75caca03e7_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Variety of sweet and savoury mochis. Some made with shrimps and some with vegetable
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16689938221" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16689938221', '']);" title="P2156063 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="P2156063" height="480" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8600/16689938221_d356c66016_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Different fish parts
           <br/>
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16505111189" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16505111189', '']);" title="Osaka Kuromon Market3 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="Osaka Kuromon Market3" height="480" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8565/16505111189_980e48dcc3_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16691250495" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16691250495', '']);" title="Osaka Kuromon Market4 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="Osaka Kuromon Market4" height="480" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8662/16691250495_b76f0e005e_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16483985007" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16483985007', '']);" title="P2156165 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="P2156165" height="480" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8636/16483985007_ca7f3ecd94_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16068931374" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16068931374', '']);" title="P2156177 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="P2156177" height="427" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8615/16068931374_91bcbcd0f7_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Large scallops fresh from the grill. Lightly seasoned then straight into the mouth!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16505114249" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16505114249', '']);" title="P2156180 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="P2156180" height="427" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8616/16505114249_6b89d6fb62_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16503666428" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16503666428', '']);" title="Osaka Kuromon Market1 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="Osaka Kuromon Market1" height="480" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8564/16503666428_5b4936c275_z.jpg" width="640"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Wagyu very much!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16503863540" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'https://www.flickr.com/photos/cumidanciki/16503863540', '']);" title="Osaka Kuromon Market2 by @agentcikay, on Flickr">
           <img alt="Osaka Kuromon Market2" class=" aligncenter" height="640" src="https://farm9.staticflickr.com/8607/16503863540_9f61e17fa4_z.jpg" width="481"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <em>
           Oden a traditional Japanese winter hot pot consisting of several ingredients such as boiled eggs, daikon, konjac(konnyaku), atsuage (deep fried tofu), ganmodoki (tofu fritters), and fishcakes stewed in a light, soy-flavoured dashi broth.
          </em>
         </p>

           And those are our 10 Things to do in Osaka.. Next up.. Kyoto. Stay tuned!
