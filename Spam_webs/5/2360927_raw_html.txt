         <h2>
          Why Use Bandwidth Savers for VoIP Call Terminations
         </h2>
         <p>
          A Synchronous Bandwidth Optimizer (SBO), sometimes referred to as a bandwidth saver, is a complementary VoIP product. Its a tool that targets VoIP call terminationthe routing process between carriersreducing bandwidth usage by up to 80% without affecting call or service quality. Primarily, SBOs apply to data-restricted environments like homes and mobile hotspots.
         </p>
         <p>
          Third-party companies sell SBOs at varying prices. Nevertheless, most work universally with VoIP services and network components (i.e. NATs and firewalls). This is because SBOs support all
          <a href="http://www.mypbx.com/blog/definitions/understanding-voip-compression">
           compression protocols
          </a>
          . However, not all SBOs operate equally efficient. The difference between a free and paid tool, for instance, might be significant. Before purchasing or downloading, read reviews and specification sheets to determine the true efficacy of the software.
         </p>
         <h3>
          Synchronous Bandwidth Optimizer Incentives
         </h3>
         <p>
          Most SBOs promise roughly 80% bandwidth savings. Depending on your data habits, this could cut costs substantiallynot just for VoIP, but also activities like streaming and browsing. Thus, SBOs not only bolster telecom products, but myriad other hosted services too. In fact, they often work with all types of internet connections, including mobile networks like 3G, 4G and LTE.
         </p>
         <p>
          Not every user requires an SBO. Most VoIP solutions are lightweight enough that data consumption is not a concern. Largely, these tools apply to setups with limited or congested connectivity. Before purchasing a solution, investigate your network for programs draining resources or slowing down performance. This step is crucial before implementing any cloud-based solution. Always conduct a security check too!
         </p>
