       <div class="headline_area">
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Youth is a Womans Most Important Asset
        </h1>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         by
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn" href="http://chloeofthemountain.com/author/admin/">
           Chloe Jeffreys
          </a>
         </span>
         
         <span>
          <a href="http://chloeofthemountain.com/sex-love-marriage/is-youth-a-womans-most-important-asset/#comments" rel="nofollow">
           13 comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p class="headline_meta">
         in
         <span>
          <a href="http://chloeofthemountain.com/category/sex-love-marriage/" rel="category tag">
           Sex, Love and Marriage
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="format_text entry-content">
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Geraldo Rivera created a tempest in a teapot when he said during a recent interview on Fox News that women should not agree to short term trial marriages (
          <a href="http://time.com/3024606/millennials-marriage-sex-relationships-hook-ups/" target="_blank" title="What is a Beta Marriage?">
           called beta marriages
          </a>
          ) because youth is a womans most important asset. Heres what he said:
         </span>
        </p>
        <blockquote>
         <p>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           I think [what] a woman brings to a marriage more than anything else, to a relationship, is her youth. Her youth is a fragile and diminishing resource. So if a woman were to invest two years in one of these marriages, and then to be rejected by the man, I think that she has given up a valuable asset that is unequal  in other words, the man gets everything and the woman gets nothing from this arrangement.
          </span>
         </p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Of course, the headlines for the article made it sound like Geraldo has it in for ageing women.
         </span>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Thats what got me to click on it.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          I read the headline and was all ready to go in with guns ablazin and tell Geraldo Rivera what he could do with his stupid opinion until I actually listened to what he was saying. And you know what? Hes right.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Biology has not played a fair hand where women are concerned. We can stamp our feet and toss our pretty little curls all we like, but the truth is that biology is a cruel bitch, and women have drawn the short end of the Darwinian straw.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Lets ask Demi Moore
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Right this minute, my 52-year old husband could go out and and get himself a woman half his age to marry him. He wouldnt even have to try hard. It wouldnt matter that hes a little wrinkled, or that his hair is thinning a bit on top. He has a job, all of his teeth, and he knows how to commit and make a woman happy. Plenty of 25-year old women would be happy for just the good job and teeth, and would consider his ability to please a woman an add-on bonus.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Me? I couldnt get me a 25-year old husband if I was an heiress with a terminal illness. It wouldnt matter that I look younger than my age and can fuck like a minx. Sure, I could find me a younger lover. That I have no doubt. But a husband? Nope. Not a chance.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Why?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Biology.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          <img alt="Flower" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-7798" height="306" src="http://chloeofthemountain.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Flower-e1406695681290.jpg" width="350"/>
          My 52-year old husband can still father children. A 25-year old woman wouldnt be walking away from her mothering potential to be with him. But me? Im done for. The shops closed. Hell, Ive had a hysterectomy. The shops been torn down and turned into a pleasure palace.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          And while pleasure might be nice, and I might be darned good conversationalist after the lovings over, a 25-year old man can easily find his pleasure while not having to sacrifice potential fertility. Why should he give up one for the other?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          The best I could do if I were looking for a
          <strong>
           husband
          </strong>
          would be to find a man my own age. And from what I hear from my friends on the dating circuit, Id be damned lucky to get that. Nope. More than likely Id be looking at a man 10-20 years older, or what I like to call the pre-Depends years.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Fertility Determines Marriageability
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          The hard truth for a woman is that
          <a href="http://www.webmd.com/baby/features/fertility-101" target="_blank">
           fertility peaks at 27
          </a>
          . Yes, you read that right. 27. Not 40. Not even 35. 27.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Sure, women over 27 get pregnant every day, but women over 40 dont. Mens fertility declines with age as well, but not nearly as steeply. Within reasonable parametersfor instance, most women in their 20s and 30s wouldnt seek out a 70-year old husband unless big money was involvedage is simply not considered a factor for women when considering a spouse.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          But for women, age determines everything. All the Botox in the world cannot fool your ovaries. Just ask Demi Moore. Our ovaries have a date stamp that determines our marriageability, and thats just the way it is.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Boys will be Boys
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          Young men are biologically programmed to seek out young, fertile women. They cant help themselves. Just like women are biologically programmed to seek out men they think will be good providers. We cant help ourselves. This is one reason younger women are more attracted to older men, and older,
          <a href="http://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/apologies-freud/201210/why-women-want-married-men" target="_blank">
           married men in particular
          </a>
          .
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          This doesnt mean that younger men wont engage in a fling with an older woman. My friend, Erika Jagger, shows on her blog,
          <a href="http://asexywomanofacertainage.com/what-happens-when-youre-a-sex-positive-blogger-in-a-sex-negative-world/" target="_blank" title="A Sexy Woman of a Certain Age">
           A Sexy Woman of a Certain Age
          </a>
          , that they most certainly will. But when it comes down to time to commit theyll go younger every time.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          So if a woman spends two years in a trial marriage then thats really two years she cant get back if it doesnt work out. Shes aged herself two years (or more!) in the mating pool, while the man hasnt aged at all.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          For example, if two 25-year olds go in for a trial marriage, and after two years decide to call it quits, the now 27-year old man still has access to partners 5 or more years younger than himself. But the 27-year old woman is not so fortunate. She has wasted her two years on a relationship that went nowhere, and now shes limited to men at least 27, and more likely older.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          And the hard truth is that by their late 20s and 30s, most of the marriageable men are already taken. They wont be available again until their 40s or 50s when theyll be back on the market looking for, you guessed it, younger women.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Youth is a Womans Most Valuable Asset
        </h2>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          <img alt="sun-sets-on-a-womans-youth" class="alignright wp-image-7797 size-full" height="467" src="http://chloeofthemountain.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/The-Sun-Sets-e1406695462848.jpg" width="350"/>
          It sucks. But its true. We can be mad at the messenger, but the message is clear, shacking up, hooking up, and trial marriages benefit men to the detriment of the women who agree to these relationships out of denial that age matters. Age does matter. It matters a helluva lot when youre a woman. And anyone who tells you otherwise is trying to sell you something. Or, if its a man, trying to get you in bed.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          I cant believe Im going to say this, but I agree with Geraldo Rivera. Unless a woman is really onlyand I mean REALLY, not just pretending so she can appear all liberated and evolved, if even only in her own mindlooking to get laid and avoid commitment that could lead to children then shed be wise to avoid these temporary relationships and seek out men who are truly able to commit. Times a-wasting.
         </span>
        </p>
