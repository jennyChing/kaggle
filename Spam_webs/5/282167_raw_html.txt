     <p>
      <b>
       The Wesley Willis Fiasco
      </b>
      was an
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       American
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">
       punk rock
      </a>
      band based in Wicker Park,
      <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">
       Chicago
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">
       Illinois
      </a>
      .
     </p>

     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
       History
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wesley_Willis_Fiasco&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The band was formed by
      <a href="/wiki/Wesley_Willis" title="Wesley Willis">
       Wesley Willis
      </a>
      on
      <a href="/wiki/Lead_vocalist" title="Lead vocalist">
       vocals
      </a>
      , Pat Barnard on lead
      <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">
       guitar
      </a>
      , and his friend Dale Meiners on guitar and bass. Dale Meiners previously played guitar in
      <a href="/wiki/Billy_Corgan" title="Billy Corgan">
       Billy Corgan
      </a>
      's pre-
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smashing_Pumpkins" title="Smashing Pumpkins">
       Smashing Pumpkins
      </a>
      band,
      <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Marked&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Marked (page does not exist)">
       the Marked
      </a>
      . In 1992 Willis began writing songs, and soon after, Meiners took Willis into his studio to record them. After many drummer changes, Meiners, Barnard and Willis formed the Wesley Willis Fiasco with Dave Nooks on bass and Brendan Murphy on drums. Their name was coined by early second guitarist Michael Cates.
     </p>
     <p>
      They released only one studio album in 1996,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Spookydisharmoniousconflicthellride" title="Spookydisharmoniousconflicthellride">
        Spookydisharmoniousconflicthellride
       </a>
      </i>
      (a reference to Willis's description of his
      <a href="/wiki/Schizophrenia" title="Schizophrenia">
       schizophrenic
      </a>
      episodes), which is found on the Urban Legend label. Various live albums are available, though most are bootlegs. After the band broke up, Willis went on to record his own solo albums. One of his songs on his solo album "Rush Hour" is about the Fiasco.
     </p>
     <p>
      The Fiasco band covered the song "
      <a href="/wiki/Girls_on_Film" title="Girls on Film">
       Girls on Film
      </a>
      " by
      <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">
       Duran Duran
      </a>
      , which appeared on the Duran Duran Tribute Album in 1997. They also released a much sought after and hard to find 7" vinyl split with
      <a href="/wiki/Sublime_(band)" title="Sublime (band)">
       Sublime
      </a>
      , as well as a split with Milwaukee's
      <a href="/wiki/The_Frogs_(band)" title="The Frogs (band)">
       The Frogs
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      The Fiasco toured with many bands, most notably
      <a href="/wiki/Rocket_from_the_Crypt" title="Rocket from the Crypt">
       Rocket from the Crypt
      </a>
      , Sublime, and
      <a href="/wiki/Lordz_of_Brooklyn" title="Lordz of Brooklyn">
       Lordz of Brooklyn
      </a>
      . The band was also noted for charming
      <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Vedder" title="Eddie Vedder">
       Eddie Vedder
      </a>
      , lead singer of
      <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Jam" title="Pearl Jam">
       Pearl Jam
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      Due to conflicts between the Fiasco band's shows and Willis's solo keyboard shows, the band broke up while on tour in
      <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">
       Ohio
      </a>
      in 1996. Willis stated that the demons in his head worsened, causing him to constantly yell at the band unintentionally.
     </p>
     <p>
      After the breakup, Pat Barnard sold his equipment and moved to
      <a href="/wiki/Lake_Tahoe" title="Lake Tahoe">
       Lake Tahoe
      </a>
      , where he died in a drowning accident at the age of 27. Murphy, who briefly played with
      <a href="/wiki/The_Jesus_Lizard" title="The Jesus Lizard">
       The Jesus Lizard
      </a>
      , lives in Chicago, working for an indie label. Dale Meiners builds custom furniture.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Dave Nooks got married and was a member of the Chicago based punk and roll band The Symptoms for a time. Willis continued his solo career until his death in 2003, age 40.
     </p>
