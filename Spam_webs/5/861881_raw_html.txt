        <div class="post-headline">
         <h1>
          Happy Holidays? 28 Hard Questions It Would Be Great If We Could Get Some Real Answers To
         </h1>
        </div>
        <div class="post-byline">
         <img alt="" src="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/wp-content/themes/atahualpa/images/icons/user.gif"/>
         By Michael Snyder, on November 27th, 2010
         <p>
          <span class="st_plusone_buttons" displaytext="plusone" st_title="Happy Holidays? 28 Hard Questions It Would Be Great If We Could Get Some Real Answers To" st_url="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/happy-holidays-28-hard-questions-it-would-be-great-if-we-could-get-some-real-answers-to">
          </span>
          <span class="st_facebook_buttons" displaytext="facebook" st_title="Happy Holidays? 28 Hard Questions It Would Be Great If We Could Get Some Real Answers To" st_url="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/happy-holidays-28-hard-questions-it-would-be-great-if-we-could-get-some-real-answers-to">
          </span>
          <span class="st_twitter_buttons" displaytext="twitter" st_title="Happy Holidays? 28 Hard Questions It Would Be Great If We Could Get Some Real Answers To" st_url="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/happy-holidays-28-hard-questions-it-would-be-great-if-we-could-get-some-real-answers-to" st_username="Revelation1217" st_via="Revelation1217">
          </span>
          <span class="st_pinterest_buttons" displaytext="pinterest" st_title="Happy Holidays? 28 Hard Questions It Would Be Great If We Could Get Some Real Answers To" st_url="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/happy-holidays-28-hard-questions-it-would-be-great-if-we-could-get-some-real-answers-to">
          </span>
          <span class="st_email_buttons" displaytext="email" st_title="Happy Holidays? 28 Hard Questions It Would Be Great If We Could Get Some Real Answers To" st_url="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/happy-holidays-28-hard-questions-it-would-be-great-if-we-could-get-some-real-answers-to">
          </span>
          <span class="st_sharethis_buttons" displaytext="sharethis" st_title="Happy Holidays? 28 Hard Questions It Would Be Great If We Could Get Some Real Answers To" st_url="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/happy-holidays-28-hard-questions-it-would-be-great-if-we-could-get-some-real-answers-to">
          </span>
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="post-bodycopy clearfix">
         <p>
          <a href="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/happy-holidays-28-hard-questions-it-would-be-great-if-we-could-get-some-real-answers-to/hard-questions" rel="attachment wp-att-1448">
           <img alt="" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-1448" height="185" src="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/11/Hard-Questions-250x185.jpg" title="Hard Questions" width="250"/>
          </a>
          Over the coming weeks, Americans will be wishing each other happy holidays millions upon millions of times. But are these really happy times? Record numbers of Americans are going to be going hungry and cold this winter. Millions upon millions of our fellow citizens would gladly give up all holiday celebrations in exchange for a decent job. The vast majority of us have plenty of examples of horrible personal tragedy all around us this holiday season, and much of that tragedy has been brought on by the deteriorating economic conditions. Meanwhile, we have a control freak government that wants to establish an even tighter grip over our lives and that now insists on either viewing our exposed bodies or groping our private areas before we can get on an airplane. Once upon a time in America the holiday season was a time to rejoice because we lived in a prosperous land where liberty and freedom were respected, but today we live in a nation with a highly centralized economy dominated by a federal government that is becoming more totalitarian by the day.
         </p>
         <p>
          But we are told that centralized control by an overwhelmingly powerful national government is good in our case because they know what is best for us.
         </p>
         <p>
          Oh really? They sure have done a great job managing our economic system, havent they? Unfortunately, it seems as though anything that the federal government takes control over just gets more messed up.
         </p>
         <p>
          The following are 28 hard questions that you should ask anyone who believes that having a highly centralized economy and a highly centralized government is good for us.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #1
          </strong>
          Why is the U.S. government trying to put a choke hold on our food production system?
          <a href="http://theeconomiccollapseblog.com/archives/s-510-12-reasons-why-the-food-safety-bill-from-hell-could-be-very-dangerous-for-the-u-s-economy">
           S. 510
          </a>
          , The Food Safety Modernization Act, is being called one of the most dangerous bills in American history. This very vague and incredibly broad bill (
          <a href="http://frwebgate.access.gpo.gov/cgi-bin/getdoc.cgi?dbname=111_cong_bills&amp;docid=f:s510rs.txt.pdf">
           which you can read here
          </a>
          ) will give the U.S. government unprecedented control over the growing, storing and sale of food in the United States.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #2
          </strong>
          Approximately
          <a href="http://money.cnn.com/2010/11/24/news/economy/99ers_unemployment_benefits/">
           14.8 million Americans
          </a>
          are unemployed this holiday season. So why in the world is the greatest economy on earth not able to provide jobs for all of them?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #3
          </strong>
          Why are the U.S. and South Korea insisting on conducting
          <a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/idUSL3E6MN0SQ20101126">
           4 days of naval exercises
          </a>
          in the Yellow Sea when tensions in the reason are at an all-time high and when a single mistake could spark an all-out war? Wouldnt it be better to postpone these naval exercises until things have calmed down a bit?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #4
          </strong>
          What prompted Russia and China to suddenly decide
          <a href="http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/china/2010-11/24/content_11599087.htm">
           to quit using the U.S. dollar
          </a>
          and instead start using their own national currencies when trading with each other?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #5
          </strong>
          Why does it cost
          <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/politics/2010/11/24/military-pegs-hourly-air-force-cost-g-obama-sets-travel-record/">
           $181,757 per hour
          </a>
          for Barack Obama to travel on Air Force One?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #6
          </strong>
          Are we still a great nation when so many of our citizens are going hungry?
          <a href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-us-canada-11761970">
           According to a recent BBC report
          </a>
          , 15% of all U.S. households experienced a shortage of food at some point during 2009. One of our readers named Gary recently left a comment that indicated that he encountered a very big crowd during his recent visit to a local food pantry.
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           <em>
            The line at the food pantry was very long. There are a lot of folks who have little food and no money.
           </em>
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #7
          </strong>
          If the U.S. economy is recovering, why were new home sales for October down
          <a href="http://endoftheamericandream.com/archives/home-sales-drop-once-again">
           28.9 percent
          </a>
          from a year ago and why were existing home sales for October down
          <a href="http://endoftheamericandream.com/archives/home-sales-drop-once-again">
           25.9 percent
          </a>
          over the previous year?
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #8
          </strong>
          Why are there so many reports of unprofessional behavior by TSA agents? For example,
          <a href="http://thedebtweowe.com/tsa-agents-are-actively-profiling-air-travelers-they-are-specifically-targeting-attractive-women-for-enhanced-pat-downs">
           it is being reported
          </a>
          that some TSA agents have specifically targeted attractive young women for additional screening.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           #9
          </strong>
          Why are U.S. home builders only selling
          <a href="http://money.cnn.com/2010/11/24/real_estate/new_home_sales/index.htm?section=money_news_economy&amp;utm_source=feedburner&amp;utm_medium=feed&amp;utm_campaign=Feed%3A+rss%2Fmoney_news_economy+%28Economy+News%29&amp;utm_content=Google+Reader">
