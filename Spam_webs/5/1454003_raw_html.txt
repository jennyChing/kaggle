       <header>
        <h1 class="entry-title">
         11 Android features were glad are available in iOS 8
        </h1>
        <small class="postmetadata">
         Posted by Jason on Jun 04, 2014 |
         <a href="http://www.iphonehacks.com/2014/06/ios-8-features-android.html#comments">
          <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="69725 http://www.iphonehacks.com/?p=69725">
           Comments (23)
          </span>
         </a>
        </small>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content desktop">
        <figure>
         <img alt="iOS 8 logo" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69621" height="315" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ios-8-logo.png" width="600"/>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Apple unveiled
         <a href="http://www.iphonehacks.com/ios-8">
          iOS 8
         </a>
         yesterday, with a bunch of new features for end-users and developers that bring the iPhone in parity to Android when it comes to some advanced features.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span id="more-69725">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Here are the major Android features that were glad are available in iOS 8:
        </p>
        <div class="no-ads" style="float: left; margin-right: 15px;">
         <div id="div-gpt-ad-1362921649687-4">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           googletag.cmd.push(function() { googletag.display('div-gpt-ad-1362921649687-4'); });
          </script>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: large;">
          <strong>
           Widgets
          </strong>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Android has had widgets since quite sometime, and Apple finally added support for widgets in iOS 8. Widgets are a part of Apples effort to make iOS extensible, and live inside the Today tab of the Notification Center or the lock screen with the purpose of giving
         <span style="color: #414141;">
          quick access to information.
         </span>
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="iOS 8 Notification Center Widgets" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69718" height="571" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ios-8-nc-widgets.jpg" width="312"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Third-party Keyboards
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Keyboard input has seen a lot of innovation largely because Android allows companies like Swype, SwiftKey to provide their own keyboards that are available system wide. With iOS 8,users can install third-party keyboards on their iPhone or iPad, and popular third-party keyboards SwiftKey and Swype have said that theyll have iOS 8 keyboards ready on launch.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="iOS 8 Third-party Keyboards" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69720" height="578" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ios-8-thirdparty-keyboards.jpg" width="310"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Easier content sharing between apps
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Sharing content between apps has been a sore point for iOS, and a huge strength for Android. With iOS 8, Apple brings the two platforms level, by letting apps add their own share options system wide. Apps can also define actions thathelps users view or transform content within the context of another app. For instance, Bing could have a translate action in any app, that translates the selected text into a language of your choice.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="ios 8 actions share" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69727" height="367" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ios-8-actions-share.png" width="600"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Standard file picker
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Services like Dropbox, Google Drive etc. can now define themselves as Storage Providers so that apps that deal with files can store and retrieve files stored in these services without any hassles. This is of course present in Android since quite some time.
        </p>
        <p>
         These changes make it much easier to work on a single file from multiple apps without creating unnecessary copies.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="document providers" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69737" height="713" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/document-providers.png" width="354"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Built-in support for beta testing
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Google recently let developers beta test their apps through the Google Play Store itself, and Apple is doing something very similar with the new App Store
         <a href="http://www.iphonehacks.com/2014/02/apple-buys-burstly-testflight.html">
          through its recent acquisition of TestFlight
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="testflight apple" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-69728" height="356" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/testflight-apple-1024x571.jpg" width="640"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          App Store analytics
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Tracking the way users are discovering your app is quite important for developers, and Google was way ahead with these features on the Play Store. At yesterdays keynote, Apple made a significant step forward with a redesigned iTunes Connect that adds retention, and visits along with download and revenue numbers.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          App Store video demoes
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Google Play Store let developers add video demoes for their apps since quite some time, and Apple finally lets iOS developers do the same. Videos do a much better job at explaining the functionality of an app that screenshots, so this is a significant addition, not only from developers but even users point of view.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Interactive Notifications
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Another Android feature that let you take action on Notifications like reply to a message, respond to an event invite, snooze and alarm and so on. iOS 8 adds Interactive Notifications that third-party apps can take advantage of.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="interactive-notifications" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-69617" height="378" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/interactive-notifications1-1024x605.jpg" width="640"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Hey, Siri
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Google first brought hands-free Google Now voice search activation with the phrase OK, Google and
         <a href="http://www.iphonehacks.com/2014/06/new-siri-features-announced-bring-hands-free-activation.html">
          iOS 8 lets you similarly activate Siri
         </a>
         by simply saying Hey, Siri.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="iOS 8 - Siri features" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69724" height="355" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/ios-8-siri.jpg" width="600"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Intelligent Search
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Google is of course the leader when it comes to search, and has naturally integrated its great searching abilities in Android.
         <a href="http://www.iphonehacks.com/2014/06/apple-updates-spotlight-improved-search-options-ios-8.html">
          Spotlight in iOS 8
         </a>
         adds some intelligence in the way it shows results, by showing you iTunes and nearby theatres when you search for a movie, Maps results when you search for a place and so on.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="img_0008" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69674" height="618" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/img_00081.png" width="348"/>
        </figure>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          Home Kit
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Google famously tried to get into home automation with
         <a href="http://www.engadget.com/2011/05/10/google-announces-android-at-home-framework/" target="_blank">
          Android @ Home a few years back
         </a>
         , but didnt see much success.
         <a href="http://www.iphonehacks.com/2014/06/homekit-api-announced-ios-8.html">
          Apples HomeKit
         </a>
         is a platform to achieve a similar goal, and well see if this is a success or failure.
        </p>
        <figure>
         <img alt="homekit" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-69622" height="399" src="http://cdn.iphonehacks.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/homekit.jpg" width="600"/>
        </figure>
        <p>
         Before WWDC,
         <a href="http://www.iphonehacks.com/2014/05/android-ios-8.html">
          we had posted about the Android features we want to see in iOS 8
         </a>
         , and were quite pleased with what Apple showed us.If youre an Android user, we would love to know if you will switch to iOS with these new features.
        </p>
