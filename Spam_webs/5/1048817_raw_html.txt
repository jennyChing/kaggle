         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Google Glass Runs On Android, Obvious Isnt It?
         </h1>
         <div class="entry-byline">
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.360technosoft.com/blog/author/admin" itemprop="url" rel="author" title="Posts by 360 Degree Technosoft">
            <span itemprop="name">
             360 Degree Technosoft
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <time class="entry-published updated" datetime="2013-05-01T14:09:09+00:00" title="Wednesday, May 1, 2013, 2:09 pm">
           May 1, 2013
          </time>
          <span class="comments-link">
          </span>
         </div>
         <!-- .entry-byline -->
        </header>
        <!-- .entry-header -->
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
         <p>
          Google Glass seems to be hot topic these days; the tech media looks like focusing on Googles new Glass run by Android operating system.
         </p>
         <p>
          Truth: Glass requires an operating system; Android being Google owned operating system is an embedded OS, which is run on ARM processors and diminutive memory. What the device is required to do is delivering Bluetooth/Wi-Fi connectivity, control both video and audio &amp; that basically is Glass for you. Its not supernatural but reduction and design savvy Android device.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Google Glass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3267" height="311" src="http://www.360technosoft.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/610x311xgoogle-glass.jpg.pagespeed.ic.4AXlAjdW33.jpg" title="Google Glass" width="610"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          The benefit about using Android is that it simplifies the process of connecting it to any portable device be it iOS, Blackberry, Android, Windows. This is a sensible way of thinking  there is not much money in hardware and Google knows that as well so they are trying to reduce the cost of its Nexus devices and encourage app development.
         </p>
         <p>
          The other good thing about this is as the first device it may stop the other competitors from creating it. Google is getting many other top hardware developers on board and therefore it definitely looks like a winner. Android being open source Google also profits from app store not just the device.
         </p>
         <p>
          Actually I havent come across too many apps that run on the Glass processor at the moment. The heavy lifting is done by the Smartphone; some of that would be client cloud computing. Glass is just the interface like the watch and wrist-pad etc. To carry on developing Glass as a conception a lot more needs to be done within miniaturization, power and battery technology increasing the wearability of the product to at least 30 days.
         </p>
         <p>
          Android is already spread in cameras, smart TVs, video game consoles, fridges, portable media, etc. Android Key Lime Pie will be taking us further from mobile devices to desktops.
         </p>
         <p>
          The important thing is for it to remain open source and inspire the
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.360technosoft.com/services/android-application-development" title="Android Application Developers">
            android app developers
           </a>
          </strong>
          to imagine and create.
         </p>
