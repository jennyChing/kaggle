             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Locate wood.
              </b>
              The goal is to find the sturdiest Y-shaped piece of wood you can find. You can make a slingshot with a forked branch of just about any size, but a piece about 6 to 9 inches (15.2 to 22.9cm) long with a fairly uniform thickness of 1 to 2 inches (2.5 to 5.1cm) will make a strong, easy-to-use slingshot.

               </div>
               <div class="img-whvid" id="smlimgurl-whvid-ba67524f1d315e2a1d2fc43d714e8095" style="display:none">
                http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/b/b8/Make-a-Sling-Shot-Step-1-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Make-a-Sling-Shot-Step-1-preview-Version-2.jpg

               </div>
               <div class="player-whvid no-background" id="player-whvid-ba67524f1d315e2a1d2fc43d714e8095">
               </div>

              <ul>
               <li>
                Try to find a piece with as few imperfections as possible--even moderate cracks can render your slingshot dangerous or unusable. If there are knots or bumps you can cut or sand them off.
               </li>
               <li>
                You can peel off the bark to make the handle more comfortable to hold.
               </li>
              </ul>

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Dry out your wood.
              </b>
              If you're working with wood that has recently been cut, it will contain moisture that will not make for a very sturdy sling shot. To dry your wood fork out quickly, pop in in your microwave oven.
              <div class="whvid_cont whvid_cont_hideable">
               <div class="img-whvid" id="lrgimgurl-whvid-64d8f9157a53b2ad0b121fc6c50762d5" style="display:none">

               </div>
               <div class="vid-whvid" id="vidurl-whvid-64d8f9157a53b2ad0b121fc6c50762d5" style="display:none">
                http://d5kh2btv85w9n.cloudfront.net/3/30/Make a Sling Shot Step 2 Version 2.360p.mp4
               </div>
               <div class="player-whvid no-background" id="player-whvid-64d8f9157a53b2ad0b121fc6c50762d5">
               </div>
              </div>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Wrap your fork in a kitchen dish towel and pop it into the microwave. Heat it on high in a series of 30 second intervals.
               </li>
               <li>
                After every 30 second interval, allow the wood to rest for a few seconds and listen for hissing. Once the hissing has stopped, all the moisture has been removed from the wood, and you can proceed to the next step.
               </li>
              </ul>

               Carve some notches.
              </b>

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Cut your tubing.
              </b>
              " latex surgical tubing (found at hardware stores) is a good, solid option for a firing mechanism. Determine how long you want your bands to be. Shorter bands will produce more powerful shots, but you need some give to be able to pull the band back.
              <div class="whvid_cont whvid_cont_hideable">

               <li>
                When you have determined the desired tube length, double it and cut it in half, yielding two tubes of equal length.
               </li>

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Attach the tubing to your wooden fork at the notch.
              </b>
              Wrap one end of the tubing around a fork prong and back on itself to form a "C" shape. The tail end should approximately 1" long.
              <div class="whvid_cont whvid_cont_hideable">
               <div class="img-whvid" id="lrgimgurl-whvid-bb685aa5693d12c584b8292af552ab8e" style="display:none">

               </div>
               <div class="img-whvid" id="smlimgurl-whvid-bb685aa5693d12c584b8292af552ab8e" style="display:none">
                http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/8/8f/Make-a-Sling-Shot-Step-5-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Make-a-Sling-Shot-Step-5-preview-Version-2.jpg

               </div>

               </div>
              </div>
              <ul>
               <li>
                Tightly wind a 10" piece of dental floss around both lengths of the tubing so the tubing is firmly affixed to the wooden prong. Tie a square knot to secure the floss and trim off any excess.
               </li>
               <li>
                Repeat this tube attachment on the other wooden prong.
               </li>
              </ul>

             <div class="step">
              <b class="whb">
               Make your pouch.
              </b>

               </li>
               <li>
                You can opt to trim off " from all the corners of the rectangle. This octagonal shape cups and supports the rock or stone you are shooting with.

         <div class="section tips sticky ">
          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="Tips">
            Tips
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="section_text" id="tips">
           <ul>
            <li>
             Tightly wrap the handle with tape or twine to provide a surer, more comfortable grip.
            </li>
            <li>
             To protect your eyes, don't raise the slingshot to eye level. With practice you can be just as accurate (or more) by holding the loaded pocket in a fixed position close to your body (near the hip for example), and moving the body of the slingshot to aim.
            </li>
            <li>
             You can make slingshots of just about any size. Try varying sizes to find out which work best.
            </li>
            <li>
             Be careful when you are making the pouch. You may accidentally cut your finger.
            </li>
           </ul>
