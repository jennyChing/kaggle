          Laughing at my Nightmare by Shane Burcaw Review
         </a>
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="byline">
          by
          <span class="author vcard">
           <a class="fn" href="http://thebohemianhousewife.com/author/thebohemianhousewife/" rel="author" title="View all posts by thebohemianhousewife">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         What can I say about this book? It was funny and sombering. A few times I would laugh but soon put the book down so I could hug my son. He really gave us a glimpse into his life with Spinal muscular atrophy. A disease I knew very little about. I had never read his blog before this or even know who he was. I now follow LAMN on everything I can. If you dont, you really should. His story is great. He didnt let his disability keep him down. As someone with recent health problems this book really helped me put things in perspective. If he can stay positive so can I.
        </p>
        <p>
         I love how Shane didnt make himself out to be perfect. That is one thing that always bothers in memoirs. You cant make yourself out to be the perfect human and expect people to believe you. He judges people, even if they have the same disability he has. Its something we all do even if we dont want to admit it. He did stupid things. Chapter 11 Slam Dunk cracked me up because it would be something my siblings would do. He had his heart broken and probably broke hearts.
        </p>
        <p>
         I would give this book
         <span style="color: #339966;">
          4/5
         </span>
         mostly because I feel like it was pulled from Rated R to PG-13. Some of the words used felt stale. Like he had to change them out so he could market the book to a younger audience. I still enjoyed it. I caught myself laughing out loud more than once.
        </p>
