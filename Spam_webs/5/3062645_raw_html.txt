      </h1>
     </div>
     <div class="byline">
      by Dave Llorens
     </div>
     <div class="entry-content">
      <p class="no-break">
       <span class="st_email_large" st_title="Solar Info: List of Solar Lease and Solar PPA s" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/affordable-solar/solar-info-list-of-solar-lease-and/">

       <span class="st_facebook_large" st_title="Solar Info: List of Solar Lease and Solar PPA s" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/affordable-solar/solar-info-list-of-solar-lease-and/">

       <span class="st_googleplus_large" st_title="Solar Info: List of Solar Lease and Solar PPA s" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/affordable-solar/solar-info-list-of-solar-lease-and/">
       </span>
       <span class="st_twitter_large" st_title="Solar Info: List of Solar Lease and Solar PPA s" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/affordable-solar/solar-info-list-of-solar-lease-and/" st_username="solarpowerrocks">
       </span>
       <span class="st_linkedin_large" st_title="Solar Info: List of Solar Lease and Solar PPA s" st_url="http://www.solarpowerrocks.com/affordable-solar/solar-info-list-of-solar-lease-and/">
       </span>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center">
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/ppas.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-5046 aligncenter" height="230" src="http://solarpowerrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/ppas.jpg" width="275"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       So, weve been talking about Solar Leases and Solar PPAs for homes. As I mentioned in
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/los-angeles-solar/solar-ppa-vs-lease-solar/">
        <span style="font-weight: bold;font-style: italic">
         an earlier post
        </span>
       </a>
       <span style="font-weight: bold;font-style: italic">
        , the structures of these can vary greatly.
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Ive briefly reviewed the
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/affordable-solar/ct-solar-lease-basics/">
        CT Solar Lease
       </a>
       and briefly outlined the
       <a href="http://solarpowerrocks.com/los-angeles-solar/cash-poor-solar-rich-part-one/">
        Solar City Lease
       </a>
       and SunRun PPA model.
      </p>
      <p>
       In a quick (2 hour search) of the web, Ive found the following
       <span style="font-style: italic">
        seemingly
       </span>
       active residential solar lease or PPA providers, but other than the ones just mentioned, I have not done my homework yet to go over each company. So buyer beware, but if youre interested in going with a solar lease or solar PPA in your area, here are the websites of the residential ones Ive found so far:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        CT Solar
        <a href="http://www.solarcity.com/">
         www.ctsolarlease.com
        </a>
        . Connecticut only.
       </li>
       <li>
        Heliomu PPA
        <a href="www.heliomu.com">
         www.heliomu.com
        </a>
        California only
       </li>
       <li>
        Solar City Lease
        <a href="http://www.solarcity.com/">
         www.solarcity.com
        </a>
        Arizona, California, and Oregon
       </li>
       <li>
        SunRun PPA.
        <a href="http://www.sunrunhome.com/">
         www.sunrunhome.com
        </a>
        California, Massachusetts. In Arizona, SunRun provides a lease model similar to Solar Citys.
       </li>
       <li>
        freEner-g
        <a href="http://solarflowenergy.com">
         www.freener-g.com
        </a>
        Pilot Minnesota Solar Lease plan
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       In the Near Future: (As announced in the
       <a href="http://www.philly.com/philly/business/20090510_Leasing_a_path_to_solar_power.html?posted=y&amp;viewAll=y">
        Philidelphia Enquirer
       </a>
       ):
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="http://thegemstonegroup.com/">
         Gemstone Group Pennsylvani
        </a>
        a Solar Leasing Plan (details not yet announced)
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://thegemstonegroup.com/">
         Gemstone Group New Jersey
        </a>
        Solar Leasing Plan (details not yet announced)
       </li>
       <li>
        Urban Eco Electric
        <a href="http://www.urbanecoelectric.com/">
         www.urbanecoelectric.com
        </a>
        , Philadelphia, PA only, (coming soon)
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Here are mainly Commercial/Business/Government PPA companies:
      </p>

      <p>
       <!--[endif]-->
       In terms of what to expect from the residential list above:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        Solar PPAs and Solar Leases will be different from company to company and State to State.
       </li>
       <li>
        In fact, the same company offering a Solar Lease in California may only offer a Solar PPA in Arizona or visa-versa.
       </li>
       <li>
        Some pay for maintenance or contribute funds to the maintenance, while others dont.
       </li>
       <li>
        Some companies are the installer, while others offer their leasing or PPA program through pre-qualified installers.
       </li>
       <li>
        Some charge you a leasing fee for 15 years or longer with (or without) an annual increase, while with PPAs, you pay just for the energy you use with a yearly per watt rate increase.
       </li>
       <li>
        Some require a down payment or deposit of as little as $1000or more. Others cost you nothing upfront.
       </li>
       <li>
        Most require that you have a pretty good credit score in the high 600s or even 720 in the case of Solar Citys program.
       </li>
       <li>
        All are long term commitments of at least 15 years, with options to buy and/or transfer the agreement to a new owner.
       </li>
       <li>
        Mostif not all of these companieswill be happy to provide a commercial PPA for your school, church, business, or government agency as well.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Know of any more residential solar lease or Solar PPA programs? Please drop a comment below and share.
