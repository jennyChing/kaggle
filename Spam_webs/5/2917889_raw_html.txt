         Slow Cooker Marinara Sauce
        </h1>

        <p>
         It is soon to be Valentines Day and what could be more romantic than to share a bowl of pasta. To make your day easier all you have to do is put your ingredients in a slow cooker. That leaves you the whole day to explore and enjoy each others company. When you arrive home a big pot of delicious
         <strong>
          Slow Cooker Marinara Sauce
         </strong>
         is waiting for you.
        </p>
        <p>
         You can think past the pasta too and make pizza, soup, mussels, braised meats or poached eggs with this delicious versatile sauce. You can leave it naturally vegan or change it up to how you enjoy it. A good sauce is the start to many recipes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Marinara Sauce by Noshing With The Nolands" class="aligncenter wp-image-12990 size-full" height="768" src="//noshingwiththenolands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Slow-Cooker-Marinara-Sauce-by-Noshing-With-The-Nolands-3-Medium.jpg" width="512"/>
         Start with great ingredients and you will in turn, end up with a great sauce. I used our own canned tomatoes but there are increasingly better products out there now. I am in love with San Marzano tomatoes, some brands are better than other. For the pureed tomatoes you can buy them already like that or pop some great quality tomatoes in a blender. So easy and it makes a difference.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Slow Cooker Marinara Sauce by Noshing With The Nolands " class="aligncenter wp-image-12991 size-large" height="683" src="//noshingwiththenolands.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/Slow-Cooker-Marinara-Sauce-by-Noshing-With-The-Nolands-2-Medium-2-1024x683.jpg" width="1024"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         You can enjoy this sauce all year long in all of you favorite dishes!! But for right now think Valentines Day!! Hugs and kisses!!
         <br/>

         <div class="item ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Slow Cooker Marinara Sauce
         </div>
         <div class="ERSDetails">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Tara Noland
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSHead">
           Recipe type:
           <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
            Entree
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSHead">
           Cuisine:
           <span itemprop="recipeCuisine">
            Italian
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTimes">
           <div class="ERSHead">
            Prep time:
            <time datetime="PT15M" itemprop="prepTime">
             15 mins
            </time>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSHead">
            Cook time:
            <time datetime="PT7H" itemprop="cookTime">
             7 hours
            </time>
           </div>
           <div class="ERSHead">
            Total time:
            <time datetime="PT7H15M" itemprop="totalTime">
             7 hours 15 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSHead">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            6-8 servings
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          A delicious and versatile sauce that can be made into so many dishes, like pizza, pasta, poached eggs and used to braise meats and make soups.
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 medium onion, chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            6 tsp. garlic paste or 6 cloves garlic, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp. olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1500 ml. (50 oz.) pureed tomatoes, freshly pureed or purchased canned
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 large sprigs of fresh basil, pureed with the tomatoes or finely chopped
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            28 oz. can San Marzano tomatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 Tbsp. sugar. optional
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp. salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3-4 Tbsp. tomato paste
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Saute onions and garlic in the olive oil in a medium skillet, over medium heat, until translucent. Add to a large pot of a slow cooker. Add all the remaining ingredients. Add more tomato paste if desired for thickness.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Cook on low for 2 - 3 hours on high or 5-7 hours on low. Break up tomatoes at the end of cooking and then let cook for a half hour longer to thicken. Enjoy with your favorite recipe or on pasta or pizza.
           </li>
          </ol>
