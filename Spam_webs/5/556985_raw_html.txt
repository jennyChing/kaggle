                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     The best blueberry muffin recipe in the world
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-2257389760254228220" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-YkrxC7_LXRc/Te2PXccDM_I/AAAAAAAAB6A/pN7_smVmT0I/s1600/Blueberry+muffin+solo.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="">
                       <img border="0" height="186" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-YkrxC7_LXRc/Te2PXccDM_I/AAAAAAAAB6A/pN7_smVmT0I/s280/Blueberry+muffin+solo.jpg" width="280"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     Every so often Clemence Gossett, a fabulous baking instructor and co-founder of
                     <a href="http://www.thegourmandiseschool.com/">
                      The Gourmandise School of Sweets and Savories
                     </a>
                     in Santa Monica, sends one of her recipes out to her email list. Clemence's recipes are exactly my kinds of sweets: never fussy, always achievable and truly delicious.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Last year Clemence sent a message titled "The Muffin Method" detailing her general muffin philosophies and proportions. I make muffins all the time, so I kept it in my inbox. It took me a year, but I finally pulled it out this weekend to make some blueberry muffins.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gllUsxernyo/Te2SsJnoD5I/AAAAAAAAB6E/T7tO4SN3PmY/s1600/Blueberry+muffin+close.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="">
                       <img border="0" height="186" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-gllUsxernyo/Te2SsJnoD5I/AAAAAAAAB6E/T7tO4SN3PmY/s280/Blueberry+muffin+close.jpg" width="280"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     I read the recipe three times before I started. I was confused. No eggs? Two
                     <i>
                      tablespoons
                     </i>
                     of baking powder? I'd never seen a blueberry muffin recipe like that. I wondered if Clemence had erred. Hah! I should have known better than to doubt a French baker. Clemence's recipes are well-tested.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     These are the best blueberry muffins I've ever tasted. They're closer to cake than to health food - don't let the fruit fool you. But if you can't indulge in a sweet muffin or two (or seven, in the case of my nine-year-old) on a lazy Sunday, when can you?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_mska1FIsG0/Te2W9GRtgHI/AAAAAAAAB6Q/3rNGag9_ApI/s1600/Blueberry+muffins.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="">
                       <img border="0" height="186" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-_mska1FIsG0/Te2W9GRtgHI/AAAAAAAAB6Q/3rNGag9_ApI/s280/Blueberry+muffins.jpg" width="280"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     If you're in southern California, do stop by the new
                     <a href="http://www.thegourmandiseschool.com/index.php">
                      Gourmandise School
                     </a>
                     , in
                     <a href="http://www.facebook.com/TheMarketSMP">
                      The Market at Santa Monica Place
                     </a>
                     . The Market is Los Angeles's answer to San Francisco's Ferry Building, and Gourmandise is holding
                     <a href="http://www.thegourmandiseschool.com/classes.php">
                      cooking classes
                     </a>
                     just about every day in their gorgeous, brand-new, shiny demonstration kitchen. I'm trying to work up the nerve to ask Clemence if I can teach a class over the summer. Maybe a
                     <a href="http://www.inerikaskitchen.com/2010/09/trufflepalooza-2010-menu-and-reports.html">
                      Trufflepalooza
                     </a>
                     class, featuring
                     <a href="http://www.inerikaskitchen.com/2010/09/trufflepalooza-2010-menu-and-reports.html">
                      black summer truffle recipes
                     </a>
                     . What do you think? Would you sign up?

                         Instructions
                        </div>
                        <div itemprop="instructions">
                         <span class="instruction">
                          Preheat the oven to 375 degrees. Line a 24-cup standard muffin tin with paper liners.
                         </span>
                         <span class="instruction">
                          Whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt in a large bowl.
                         </span>
                         <span class="instruction">
                          In a glass or ceramic microwave-safe bowl, put the sugar, milk, yogurt or sour cream, and butter. Microwave the bowl 1 minute on high, or until the butter is starting to melt and the bowl is warm. Whisk the mixture until you see little bits of butter floating in the mixture.
                         </span>
                         <span class="instruction">
                          Add the blueberries to the flour mixture and toss to coat. Pour over the milk mixture and slowly, carefully, fold together with a large spoon or spatula. Do not overmix or the muffins will be tough. Streaks of flour are perfectly okay.
                         </span>
                         <span class="instruction">
                          Divide the batter among the muffin tins and sprinkle with sugar. Bake 30 minutes or until a toothpick comes out with a few crumbs clinging to it and the tops are starting to turn golden brown. Cool 5 minutes in the pan, then remove the muffins to a rack to cool completely.
