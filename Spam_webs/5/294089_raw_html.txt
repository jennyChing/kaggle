        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Top 10 U.S. Cities for Career Opportunities
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="date">
          <a href="http://www.collegefeed.com/blog/top-10-u-s-cities-career-opportunities/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Top 10 U.S. Cities for Career Opportunities">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2013-09-19T09:02:16+00:00">
            September 19, 2013
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="categories-links">
          <a href="http://www.collegefeed.com/blog/category/career-growth/" rel="category tag" title="View all posts in Career Growth">

       <div class="entry-content">
        <a id="dd_start">
        </a>
        <p>
         <img alt="Top 10 U.S. Cities for Career Opportunities" class="alignright size-full wp-image-400" height="232" src="http://www.collegefeed.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Top-10-U.S.-Cities-for-Career-Opportunities.jpg" width="350"/>
         When youre searching for jobs it can sometimes seem like no one is hiring, but perhaps youre simply looking in the wrong place. Many of us do not have the luxury of moving wherever work is, but if you are at a transition point in life, or a recent college grad with little established, it might be wise to seek out a new place to live based on job availability. Although unemployment is slowly inching its way downward, there are some cities, which provide more opportunities than others. Here are ten of the most promising cities for career opportunities in the United States.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Washington D.C.
        </h2>
        <p>
         The federal government is, unsurprisingly, the largest employer in the nations capitol. D.C. is home to over 200,000 employees in the metro area and boasts a 6 percent unemployment rate, which is more than three percentage points below the national figure. D.C.s plethora of jobs are mainly in programming and private-sector research, but there are also a number of opportunities with nonprofit organizations, government contractors, consulting firms, think tanks, and educational institutions. This is a great city for 20-somethings to live and work, in fact, 30% of the citys population is between the ages of 20 and 34.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Austin, Texas
        </h2>
        <p>
         Austin is becoming more and more known for being a great city to live in with a colorful culture. As more people make their way to the capitol city of Texas, more major companies have been willing to expand here such as Accenture, AT&amp;T, Time Warner Cable, Apple, Visa, and National Instruments (which is known for hiring employees straight out of college). Texas boasts a strong infrastructure and thriving energy market. Texas also has a low tax burden, predictable regulations, and a cost of living below the national average. Austin is seeing strong growth for jobs in manufacturing, technology-related employment, and business services and boasts one of the highest job postings per capita.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Salt Lake City, Utah
         <b>
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <b>
         </b>
         Salt Lake City shows great opportunity for jobs in high-tech industries including pharmaceutical, medical devices, transportation equipment, computer design, and data processing companies. Intermountain Health Care is the largest employer in the city with 180,000 employees, but Salt Lake City is also a hub for companies such as the Huntsman Corporation, Zions Bancorporation, Questar, AlphaGraphics, Sinclair Oil, Smiths Food and Drug, MonaVie, and Myraid Genetics. There are also many opportunities in public sector jobs: University of Utah, state of Utah, Salt Lake City International Airport.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Huntsville, Alabama
        </h2>
        <p>
         Huntsville
         <b>
         </b>
         offers a large number of high-tech and aerospace jobs as it is home to NASAs Marshall Space Flight Center and the United States Army Aviation and Missile Command. Huntsville is also home to the Cummings Research Park, which employs 25,000 people. This makes Huntsville a scientists and engineers dream city for employment. On top of that, Huntsville is also home to major employers such as the University of Alabama, ADTRAN, Intergraph, Avocent, Delatacom, and operations from 57 of the Fortune 500 companies.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Fort Collins, Colorado
         <b>
         </b>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <b>
         </b>
         Fort Collins
         <b>
         </b>
         is home to Colorado State University, which is the largest employer of the city. CSUs research facilities attracted major firms such as Hewlett-Packard Intel, AMD, Avago, Beckman Coulter, National Semiconductor, LSI, and Pelco. Apart from the university, Fort Collins also has plenty of jobs in clean energy, bioscience, agri-tech, manufacturing and service-related industries. Since Fort Collins is primarily a college town, it is home to a growing music scene as well as a hub for some of the countrys best microbreweries, making this city an excellent place for 20-somethings to live and work.
        </p>
        <h2>
         San Jose, California
        </h2>
        <p>
         As the capitol of techy Silicon Valley, San Jose is a tech-geeks dream. Many of the high-tech companies in the area such as Google, Cisco, Intel, Samsung, Facebook, Oracle, HP, Yahoo!, LinkedIn, Agilent Technologies, Intel, and Apple recruit and hire young professionals specifically those with degrees in math, science, engineering, software design, marketing, and analytics. This is also a great city for startups and entrepreneurs, and as such, employees in San Jose generally work in a more casual and friendly environment.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Seattle, Washington
        </h2>
        <p>
         Seattle
         <b>
         </b>
         is also a great city for the tech-savvy, home to major companies such as Microsoft, Amazon, and Boeing. Google, Facebook, and eBay are also stretching their reach to Seattle. The life sciences industry is also flourishing in Seattle with a solid research base in the University of Washington (which is also the highest employer of the city). Seattle is becoming a huge focal point in the global health initiative as well as clean tech.
         <b>
         </b>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Minneapolis-St. Paul, Minnesota
        </h2>
        <p>
         The Twin Cities
         <b>
         </b>
         has a huge diversity of industries such as finance, healthcare, retail, medical technology, and manufacturing as well as a need for specialty positions such as specialty tax and compliance, IT audit, and SEC-related experience. The Twin Cities is also home to major companies such as UnitedHealth, Target, Picis, General Mills, Best Buy, 3M, and Xcel Energy. But the Twin Cities is also a friendly environment for small business and boasts a low job competition rate.
         <b>
         </b>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Oklahoma City, Oklahoma
        </h2>
        <p>
         Oklahoma City
         <b>
         </b>
         employs mainly city and state government positions as well as a high number of military and health care employees. One of the worlds biggest companies, GE, announced in April 2013 that they wish to establish an oil and gas research center in Oklahoma City which will hire 125 people as well as collaborate with universities, government agencies, and companies within the area. Oklahoma city also hosts major companies such as AT&amp;T, Cox Communications, Devon, and Chesapeake Energy.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Milwaukee, Wisconsin
        </h2>
        <p>
         Milwaukee is home to
         <b>
         </b>
         five healthcare companies, each employing over 3,000 people as well was the Medical College of Wisconsin which employs 4,500 people. Healthcare is the leading industry in Milwaukee, but the city is also home to the Kohls department store headquarters (which employees 6,300 people locally) as well as Harley-Davidson motorcycle manufacturer (which employs 3,600 people).
        </p>
        <p>
         Which of these 10 destinations would you like to move to if you had a great job offer? Leave us a comment!
        </p>
