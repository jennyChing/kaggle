        <h1>
         Your child might have special needs but it does not give them the right to hurt mine.
        </h1>
        <time class="tm-page-pubdate" datetime="2015-05-17T08:24:59+00:00">
         May 17, 2015, at 8:24 am.

        <div class="tm-page-byline tm-has-avatar">

       <div class="tm-page-content">
        <p>
         <span>
          What would you do in this situation?
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span>
          My
         </span>
         <a href="http://www.themotherish.com/arguments-with-daughter/" style="line-height: 1.5em;" target="_blank">
          daughter
         </a>
         <span>
          , an outgoing three-year-old loves her daycare. She happily skips off with her
          <a href="http://www.themotherish.com/things-parents-should-never-say/" target="_blank" title="I swore I would never do this (and then I had kids).">
           Dora the Explorer
          </a>
          backpack weighing her down. She used to hardly take a second glance backwards.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         And then she did.
        </p>
        <p>
         Then she started crying, clinging, shaking when I tried to drop her off. At first I just assumed it was a stage. That it would pass. And then it didnt.
        </p>
        <p>
         Its heartbreaking when your child suddenly doesnt want to do something they previously loved. You start to wonder what went wrong, why the sudden about-shift. Something must have happened.
        </p>
        <p>
         Jackson hits me.
        </p>
        <p>
         So tell your teacher sweetie.
        </p>
        <p>
         I did but he still does.
        </p>
        <p>
         And then I began to notice the bruises and the scratches and one day a bite mark.
        </p>
        <p>
         My daughter is the youngest of our bunch so I have been through a whole heap of kid stuff.Ive been the mum of the child who gets hit and Ive been the mum of the child who hits and I know from both sides how tough it can be.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_298689" style="width: 720px;">
         <img alt="bad boy iv" class="size-full wp-image-298689 " height="477" src="http://ivillageau.s3.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/files/bad-boy-iv.jpg" width="720"/>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          He hits me Mama. Image via iStock.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         So I left it for a while encouraging her to speak out loudly and ask him not to hit her. I taught her to say STOP Jackson I dont like it. to speak out. I reminded her to tell her
         <a href="http://www.themotherish.com/sending-your-child-to-preschool/" target="_blank">
          teachers
         </a>
         and to move away if she felt concerned.
        </p>
        <p>
         I tried all the tricks I knew and a few I had just read about but it kept happening.
        </p>
        <p>
         The tears, the clinginess, the bruises.
        </p>
        <p>
         So I went to see her carers and was met with a challenge  the boy who was hitting her has special needs.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Here is a short video on how to talk to children about autism. Post continues after the video
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="641" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/rguyTZeeQv8?feature=oembed" width="1140">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         They have a special needs
         <a href="http://www.themotherish.com/sending-your-child-to-preschool/" target="_blank">
          teacher
         </a>
         at certain times of the day, they informed me, and they were aware of his behaviour but could I understand how difficult the situation was?
        </p>
        <p>
         They were managing things to the best of their ability.
        </p>
        <p>
         They had spoken to him.
        </p>
        <p>
         They were putting in place a system to try and stop this boy harming others.
        </p>
        <p>
         All very fair answers. But no solution.
        </p>
        <figure class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_298690" style="width: 766px;">
         <img alt="sad girl iv 2" class=" wp-image-298690 " height="508" src="http://ivillageau.s3.amazonaws.com/wp-content/uploads/files/sad-girl-iv-2.jpg" width="766"/>
         <figcaption class="wp-caption-text">
          I thought I had been understanding Image via iStock.
         </figcaption>
        </figure>
        <p>
         They asked me if I could be understanding.
        </p>
        <p>
         So heres the thing. I HAVE been understanding. I have been patient and thoughtful and tried to be wise but this kid is hurting my daughter.I am now stuck, she doesnt want to go. They are limited in what they can do.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know the benefits of having a child with s
         <a href="http://www.themotherish.com/special-needs-shopping-trolley/" target="_blank">
          pecial needs
         </a>
         cared for with children without special needs. In many ways this boy is no different than my kids, he just presents other challenges.And I feel for his parents so much so that I dont want to make them feel awkward so I dont feel like I can confront them about the situation.
        </p>
        <p>
         I feel like we are dancing around the issue here, this child isnt well managed, he isnt in control and he is making my childs life hell.
        </p>
        <p>
         I understand that this boy has emotions he cant control. I understand that every child at some stage has emotions that they cant control, and that in a child who is on the
         <a href="http://www.themotherish.com/brelfies-hashtag-new-in-2015/" target="_blank">
          autism spectrum
         </a>
         it is difficult to ascertain his triggers. I know this isnt just some uncontrolled kid assaulting my daughter.
        </p>
        <p>
         I know he is just a kid too, but I need it to stop.
        </p>
        <p>
         I just dont know how.
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           What would you do in this situation? Do you have any advice for this mother?
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
