          <h2 class="hide">
           How to Hack Netflix: Free DVDs and Money Saving Tips
          </h2>
          <p>
           I remember a time when I had to rent a VHS from the local video rental store. It wasn't all that bad, with the only real annoyances being the trip to and fro, having to rewind the movie and the possible malfunction of my VHS player, which liked to eat tape from time to time.
          </p>
          <p>
           When DVDs started replacing VHS tapes, I was one of the first to jump on the bandwagon, eliminating 2 out of 3 of my minor grievances with VHS rentals. Visiting the store again and again became the only real hassle from that point on. But then
           <a href="http://www.wonderhowto.com/topic/netflix/" title="http://www.wonderhowto.com/topic/netflix/">
            Netflix
           </a>
           came in on its white horse and gave me everything I could possible want DVD rentals without leaving my home. I was practically the first to sign up.
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="How to Hack Netflix: Free DVDs and Money Saving Tips" class="imgCenter imgCenterFull " height="257" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/38/81/63433650882160/0/hack-netflix-free-dvds-and-money-saving-tips.w654.jpg" width="554"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           And now movie rentals is even easier with instant streaming online, something Netflix is killing the competition in.
           <a href="http://www.slashfilm.com/infographic-netflix-destroyed-blockbuster/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://www.slashfilm.com/infographic-netflix-destroyed-blockbuster/">
            Blockbuster
           </a>
           went bankrupt and other services like Hulu Plus, Apple,
           <a href="http://www.zune.net/en-us/products/zuneonxbox/default.htm" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://www.zune.net/en-us/products/zuneonxbox/default.htm">
            Zune
           </a>
           and Amazon are trying to bring the mighty Netflix down. Even
           <a href="http://www.pcworld.com/article/219983/redbox_readies_streaming_netflix_rival_but_why.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://www.pcworld.com/article/219983/redbox_readies_streaming_netflix_rival_but_why.html">
            Redbox
           </a>
           has joined the battle over streaming media.
          </p>

           But Netflix is still far superior with over 20 million subscribers, mainly because they offer both DVD
           <img alt="How to Hack Netflix: Free DVDs and Money Saving Tips" class="hasLarge imgRight " src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/79/31/63433650828253/0/hack-netflix-free-dvds-and-money-saving-tips.w240.jpg" style="width: 262px; height: 151px;"/>
           rentals and streaming video. And you can stream movies from your Instant Queue on your computer, AppleTV, iPhone, iPad,
           <a href="http://techland.time.com/2011/02/15/netflix-finally-makes-it-to-boxee/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://techland.time.com/2011/02/15/netflix-finally-makes-it-to-boxee/">
            Boxee Box
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="http://blog.netflix.com/2010/11/netflix-on-android.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://blog.netflix.com/2010/11/netflix-on-android.html">
            Windows Phone 7
           </a>
           , Xbox 360, PlayStation 3, Nintendo Wii and most recently
           <a href="http://news.cnet.com/8301-31001_3-20031875-261.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://news.cnet.com/8301-31001_3-20031875-261.html">
            Android
           </a>
           devices. They're even about to tackle the
           <a href="http://www.hackingnetflix.com/2011/02/next-netflix-streaming-international-launch-in-south-america.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://www.hackingnetflix.com/2011/02/next-netflix-streaming-international-launch-in-south-america.html">

I know my Netflix DVDs have been sitting in a drawer latelyout of sight, out of mindsince I've taking a liking to streaming via my Xbox 360. Perhaps I only need one DVD now?
          </p>

Chances are, you're going to want a DVD plan, but just in case you only want to stream your movies and TV shows, Netflix now has that option available for you for the low subscription cost of $7.99 a month, right next to the Limited Plan, which only costs $4.99, with obviously limited options, including about one streaming movie a month.

         <section>
          <h2 class="step">
           <span class="stepNumber">
            <span>
             Step
            </span>
            2
           </span>
           <span class="stepText">
            Hack Your Subscription Plan
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           Okay, so you toughed it out and decided to go with one DVD at a time, with unlimited instant watching. If you had to think long and hard about those disappearing extra DVDs in the mail, then you'll be interested in this little, perfectly legal hack.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://www.unplggd.com/unplggd/hack-your-netflix-queue-to-upgrade-your-disc-plan-for-free-134754" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://www.unplggd.com/unplggd/hack-your-netflix-queue-to-upgrade-your-disc-plan-for-free-134754">
            Unplggd
           </a>
           claims this hack is completely legal in
           <a href="http://blog.netflix.com/2008/12/new-dvd-shipping-process.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://blog.netflix.com/2008/12/new-dvd-shipping-process.html">
            Netflix's terms of service
           </a>
           , so you're not risking total banishment from mail-order-DVD rentals.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            "You see, when the next DVD on your queue is a less-popular flick that's not stocked in every warehouse, Netflix often has to ship your choice from a different shipping facility than usualcausing a delay in you getting your sweet paws on those discs. (You can sometimes tell that's the case when the first DVD on your queue says its availability is "Short wait.")
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            "To make up for lost time, Netflix sends out the next available DVD lower on your queue so it arrives on schedule. You'll see it in your "DVDs at Home" list with a plus (+) sign next to it. In addition, your "Short wait" DVD is still on its way from the farther facility.
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="How to Hack Netflix: Free DVDs and Money Saving Tips" class="imgCenter imgCenterFull " src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/85/13/63433648262308/0/hack-netflix-free-dvds-and-money-saving-tips.w654.jpg" style="width: 376px; height: 209px;"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            "The result? An extra DVD in your hands. For free."
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           So, if you're only at 1 DVD at a time now, try this method out to squeeze an extra movie from time to time.
          </p>
         </section>

           <img alt="How to Hack Netflix: Free DVDs and Money Saving Tips" class="hasLarge imgCenter imgCenterFull " height="165" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/97/63/63433648798511/0/hack-netflix-free-dvds-and-money-saving-tips.w654.jpg" width="592"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This warning should also display in your Instant Queue on your Xbox 360 or other device. But sometimes you just don't notice itbut now you can,
           <a href="http://www.geeksugar.com/Automated-Twitter-Feed-Tells-You-When-Instant-Movies-Expire-14192775" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://www.geeksugar.com/Automated-Twitter-Feed-Tells-You-When-Instant-Movies-Expire-14192775">
            via Twitter
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            "The account,
           </em>
           <a href="http://twitter.com/queuenoodle" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://twitter.com/queuenoodle">
            <em>

           <em>

The automated feed alerts you to movies that will expire shortly, so if they're in your queue you can watch them before they're gone. Each tweet also includes the movie's star ranking, so you can know which movies you need to get on top of watching and which you can skip."
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <img alt="How to Hack Netflix: Free DVDs and Money Saving Tips" class="hasLarge imgCenter imgCenterFull " height="799" src="http://img.wonderhowto.com/img/35/13/63433649207246/0/hack-netflix-free-dvds-and-money-saving-tips.w654.jpg" width="592"/>
          </p>
          <p>
           This can definitely help you get more out of your Netflix subscriptionby making sure to watch everything you want!
          </p>

         <section>
          <h2 class="step">
           <span class="stepNumber">
            <span>
             Step
            </span>
            4
           </span>
           <span class="stepText">
            Save Time with Keyboard Shortcuts
           </span>
          </h2>
          <p>
           If you're trying to watch as many movies as you can squeeze into your instant viewing plan, then you're going to need to get a little quicker with your stop and play buttons. If you're primarily using your computer for movies, using
           <a href="http://markwarren.wordpress.com/2010/03/24/netflix-movie-player-keyboard-shortcuts/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="http://markwarren.wordpress.com/2010/03/24/netflix-movie-player-keyboard-shortcuts/">
            keyboard shortcuts
           </a>
           can drastically improve your speed between one movie and the next.
          </p>
          <p>
           Space = Toggle Play / Pause
           <br/>
           Enter = Toggle Play / Pause
           <br/>
           PgUp = Play
           <br/>
           PgDn = Pause
           <br/>
           F = Full-screen
           <br/>
           Esc = Exit full-screen
           <br/>
           Shift+Left arrow = Rewind
           <br/>
           Shift+Right arrow = Fast Forward
          </p>
          <p>
           In full-screen mode:
           <br/>
           Ctrl+Shift+Alt+M = Menu
           <br/>
           Ctrl+Shift+Alt+C = Codes; frame rate plus other info.
           <br/>
           Ctrl+Shift+Alt+D = Display A/V Stats on-screen
           <br/>
           Ctrl+Shift+Alt+L = Logging window
           <br/>
           Ctrl+Shift+Alt+P = Player Info
           <br/>
           Ctrl+Shift+Alt+S = Stream bit rate and manual rate selection
           <br/>
           Ctrl+Space = Frame forward / Backward mode
          </p>
          <p>
           Mac users, simply replace the Ctrl+Shift*Alt+* with Ctrl+Shift+Option+* to make the keyboard work for you.
          </p>
         </section>
