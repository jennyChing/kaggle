        <div class="background-entry-thumbnail" style="background-image:url( http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.20.42.png )">

        <div class="entry-thumbnail">
         <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.20.42" class="attachment-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" height="270" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.20.42-600x270.png" width="600"/>

        <h1 class="entry-title">
         The LFX Guide to: Cycling Gadgets
        </h1>
        <div class="entry-meta">
         <span class="date">
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/lfx-guide-cycling-gadgets/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to The LFX Guide to: Cycling Gadgets">
           <time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-07-10T08:00:29+00:00">
            July 10, 2014
           </time>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="categories-links">
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/category/trader-lifestyle/" rel="category tag">
           Trader Lifestyle
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="author vcard">
          <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/author/katie/" rel="author" title="View all posts by Katie Newton">
           Katie Newton
          </a>
         </span>
        </div>
        <!-- .entry-meta -->
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         The Tour de France is well underway, so make sure youre ready for the biggest cycling event of the year with The LFX Guide to: Cycling Gadgets. From indestructible inner tubes and invisible helmets to powered-by-cycle phone chargers, its all here
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="font-family: Bitter, Georgia, serif; font-size: 30px; line-height: 1.3;">
          The inner tube that wont burst
         </span>
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-08.51.23.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 08.51.23" class="alignright wp-image-55068 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-08.51.23-239x300.png" width="239"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         Slime Smart Tubes
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         These self-healing inner tubes are pre-filled with Slime sealant so they can automatically repair a puncture up to 3mm. SlimeLite tubes are the lightest self-healing tubes on the planet, so they wont weigh you down.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         The fact well never have to spend time kneeling at the side of the road changing a punctured inner tube. Any experienced cyclist will know what a chore this can be!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://www.slime.com/shop/lite-smart-tubes/" target="_blank">
          Slime.com
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         15
        </p>
        <h2>
         The cycling supercomputer
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-08.53.40.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 08.53.40" class="alignright wp-image-55069 size-full" height="268" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-08.53.40.png" width="186"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         Garmin Edge 810
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         Tracks every detail of your cycle, from your heart rate, speed and power to distance, ascent/descent and more. It tracks all your data and can send it direct to your smartphone so you can
         <span style="text-decoration: line-through;">
          show off to
         </span>
         share your progress with friends and family. They can even track your cycles (if they really want). It also gives you weather alerts so you wont get caught in the rain.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         The 17-hour battery life and waterproof casing. Itll never let you down.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="https://buy.garmin.com/en-GB/GB/prod112912.html?utm_source=GarminEMEA&amp;utm_medium=LandingPage&amp;utm_campaign=GarminTraining" target="_blank">
          Garmin.com
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         319.99
        </p>
        <h2>
         The keyless bike lock
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-08.56.50.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 08.56.50" class="alignright wp-image-55070 size-full" height="221" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-08.56.50.png" width="174"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         Bitlock
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         The Bitlock is a bike lock with a difference- no fiddly keys. All you have to do is walk up to your bike and press a button to open the lock. As long as you have the Bitlock app on your phone, the lock will know when you come within 3 feet of your bike.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         The fact that the Bitlock app also works as an activity tracker, and the Bitlock can be tracked via GPS in case you forget where youve left your bike.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://bitlock.co" target="_blank">
          Bitlock.co
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         $95 to pre-order (to be shipped in November 2014)
        </p>
        <h2>
         The laser bike light
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.01.26.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.01.26" class="alignright wp-image-55073 size-full" height="81" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.01.26.png" width="253"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         Blaze Laserlight
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         Projects a laser symbol of a bicycle 6-5m ahead of your bike to make it easier for vehicle drivers to spot you. Its designed to tackle the most common cause of cycling accidents- vehicles turning across an unseen bike. As well as the laser light for commuter cycling, the light has a 300+ lumen output for country lane cycling, so youll never be left in the dark.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         That youll never be invisible to drivers again. Its a must-have for safe cycling.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="https://www.blaze.cc/" target="_blank">
          https://www.blaze.cc/
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         125
        </p>
        <h2>
         The powered-by-bike phone charger
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.00.38.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.00.38" class="alignright wp-image-55072 size-full" height="274" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.00.38.png" width="232"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         Siva Cycle Atom
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         Worried all these gadgets and apps will drain your smartphone battery? Not to worry. This portable generator mounts on to any bike frame and uses kinetic power from your cycling to power your devices via a USB port.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         That it comes with a detachable battery pack so you can save up your cycling energy to charge up your devices later.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://sivacycle.com" target="_blank">
          SivaCycle.com
         </a>
         (next delivery is August/September)
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         $99
        </p>
        <h2>
         The crash sensor
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.05.15.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.05.15" class="alignright wp-image-55074 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.05.15-298x300.png" width="298"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         ICEdot Crash Sensor
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         We dont mean to be doom-mongers here, but cycling accidents do happen. This nifty little gadget attaches to your helmet and sends alerts to your contacts if you find yourself in trouble. A countdown is triggered on impact and if you dont disable it, your GPS coordinates and alerts are sent to the emergency services.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         That it might just save your bacon.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://icedot.org" target="_blank">
          ICEDot.org
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         $149, plus $10 a year for ICEdot membership
        </p>
        <h2>
         The cycling glasses with a built-in computer
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.07.44.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.07.44" class="alignright wp-image-55075 size-full" height="171" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.07.44.png" width="278"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         The Recon Jet
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         Combines protective glasses with polarized lenses with a high-tech cycling computer. It tracks, speed, distance and elevation and can be connected to your smartphone or heart rate monitor. You can even install apps for checking the weather and social media.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         The built-in HD camera with microphone and speaker, so you can record every second of your cycle.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://reconinstruments.com" target="_blank">
          ReconInstruments.com
         </a>
         this autumn
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         $599
        </p>
        <h2>
         The invisible cycle helmet
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.09.42.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.09.42" class="alignright wp-image-55077 size-medium" height="300" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.09.42-294x300.png" width="294"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         The Hovding Airbag for Cyclists
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         We know you know you should always wear a helmet when youre cycling. But we also know you like to feel the wind in your hair, and there are times when you just dont want to arrive with helmet-hair. This is the dilemma that this little gadget solves. Its a scarf-like collar that wont interfere with your hairdo, but if sensors detect youre heading for trouble an inflatable helmet puffs out in milliseconds to protect your head.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         What a clever idea it is.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://hovding.com" target="_blank">
          Hovding.com
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         299
        </p>
        <h2>
         The indicating handlebars
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.11.32.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.11.32" class="alignright wp-image-55078 size-full" height="143" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.11.32.png" width="279"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         BlinkerGrips
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         These little bicycle grips have integrated LEDs that act as steady side marker lights as well as direction indicators. Because hand signals are all well and good if the drivers around you recognize them. But thats not always the case.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         How easy they are to fit- all you have to do is slide them into your handlebars and youre ready to go.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://blinkergrips.com" target="_blank">
          BlinkerGrips.com
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         $69
        </p>
        <h2>
         The speaker that fits into your bottle holder
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <a href="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.13.47.png">
           <img alt="Screen Shot 2014-06-09 at 09.13.47" class="alignright wp-image-55081 size-medium" height="258" src="http://www.littlefishfx.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Screen-Shot-2014-06-09-at-09.13.47-300x258.png" width="300"/>
          </a>
          They call it:
         </strong>
         boomBOTTLE
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What it does:
         </strong>
         Allows you to listen to music while cycling without using headphones, which can be a dangerous game. The boomBOTTLE fits into the bottle holder on your bike and synchs to your smartphone to blast out your favourite tunes on the go.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          We love:
         </strong>
         Being able to cycle and listen to music at the same time. But you might want to delete One Direction from your playlist now that everyone else can hear your music choices, too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Get it from:
         </strong>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B00CHKRLQC/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1634&amp;creative=19450&amp;creativeASIN=B00CHKRLQC&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=litfx-21">
          Amazon.co.uk
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-uk.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=litfx-21&amp;l=as2&amp;o=2&amp;a=B00CHKRLQC" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Will set you back:
         </strong>
         90.95
        </p>
