  <p>
   <b>
    Explanation:
   </b>
   What will passenger airplanes be like in the future?

To help brain storm desirable and workable attributes, NASA sponsors
   <a href="http://www.nasa.gov/topics/aeronautics/features/future_airplanes.html">
    design competitions
   </a>
   .
   <a href="http://www.nasa.gov/multimedia/imagegallery/image_feature_1698.html">
    Shown here
   </a>
   is an artist's depiction of a concept plane
that has been recently suggested.

This futuristic plane would be expected to achieve
   <a href="ap070819.html">
    supersonic speeds
   </a>
   ,
possibly surpassing the
   <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QX04ySm4TTk">
    speeds
   </a>
   of the
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supersonic_transport">
    supersonic transport
   </a>
   planes that ran commercially in the late
   <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twentieth_century">
    twentieth century
   </a>
   .

In terms of noise reduction, the future aircraft has been drawn featuring an
   <a href="http://www.springerlink.com/content/q2u1414284747r2p/">
    inverted
V wing
   </a>
   stretched over its engines.

The structure is intended to
   <a href="http://www.space.com/businesstechnology/
supersonic-concept-plane-shush-sonic-booms-100630.html">
    reduce the sound
   </a>
   from annoying sonic booms.

Additionally,
   <a href="http://www.aeronautics.nasa.gov/nra_awardees_10_06_08_d.htm">
    future airplanes
   </a>
   would aim to have relatively little impact on
our environment, including
   <a href="http://www.thegreenguide.com/">
    green
   </a>
   limits
on pollution and fuel consumption.
   <a href="http://www.popularmechanics.com/technology/aviation/news/2932316">
    Aircraft utilizing similar design concepts
   </a>
   might well become operational by the 2030s.
  </p>
