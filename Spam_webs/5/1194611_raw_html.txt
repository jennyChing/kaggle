         <p>
          When my daughter gave me this
          <b>
           Tacky Toffee recipe
          </b>
          , she said
it is often referred to as Trailer Trash Toffee or White Trash Toffee. I am not
sure why the derogatory references, however, this is one easy delicious
dessert. If you are looking for something sweet and easy, try making
          <b>
           Tacky
Toffee
          </b>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Tacky Toffee" src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/-BOi2tFsHDC4/UOIK35wUv3I/AAAAAAAACYc/acUokLXCYdE/s640/tacky%2520toffee.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 0px 0px 10px 10px; width: 428.007px; height: 334px;"/>
          <b>
           Tacky Toffee
          </b>
          is sure to please everyone with a sweet tooth,
better yet; it is so simple to make even your kids will want to help.
         </p>
         <p>
          <br/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Ingredients for the tacky toffee recipe:
         </h2>
         <ul>
          <li>
           2 sticks real butter
          </li>
          <li>
           1 cup brown sugar
          </li>
          <li>
           1 stack Saltine crackers*
          </li>
          <li>
           1 12 ounce bag of semi-sweet chocolate chips
          </li>
          <li>
           Pecans; crushed, to taste
          </li>
          <li>
           Jellyroll pan or sheet pan
          </li>
          <li>
           Aluminum foil
          </li>
          <li>
           Non-stick cooking spray
          </li>
          <li>
           Wooden spoon
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          *Keebler original Club crackers work great too.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Instruction for making the tacky toffee recipe:
         </h2>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. If you do not
have a timer, 20-30 minutes will suffice.
          </li>
          <li>
           Using a jellyroll pan or sheet pan, line it with foil and
spray with non-stick spray. The non-stick grilling foil works fantastic using one
less step.
          </li>
          <li>
           Line the bottom of the pan with the crackers putting the
salt side down. The salt and sweet taste works well together.
          </li>
          <li>
           Taking a saucepan, melt the butter and brown sugar. Bring it
to a boil for 3-minutes stirring often with the wooden spoon.
          </li>
          <li>
           Pour the sweet delight over the crackers keeping the mixture
as even as possible. Place in the heated oven and bake for 6-8 minutes. Turn
the oven off.
          </li>
          <li>
           Remove the pan from the oven and sprinkle the semi-sweet
chocolate chips over the crackers. Spread the chocolate, as evenly as you are
able, and sprinkle the crushed pecans over the top of that. If for some reason
your chips are not melting, place the pan back in the oven for a minute or so.
          </li>
          <li>
           Allow your tacky toffee to cool before serving, therefore, using
crackers makes it so easy to break apart and serve your family.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
         </p>
         <p>
          Therefore, no matter if you call it white trash toffee,
trailer trash toffee or
          <b>
           tacky toffee recipe
          </b>
          , this is a sweet and easy recipe.
         </p>
