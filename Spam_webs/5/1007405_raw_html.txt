         <h2>
          Major League Baseball Considers Ban on Beer in Clubhouse
         </h2>
         <div class="meta">
          By Jim Cohen | 
									October 24, 2011 |
									1 Comments |
         </div>
         <div class="addthis">
          <a class="addthis_button" href="http://addthis.com/bookmark.php?v=250&amp;username=beeruniverse">
           <img alt="Bookmark and Share" height="16" src="http://s7.addthis.com/static/btn/v2/lg-share-en.gif" style="border:0" width="125"/>
          </a>
          <script src="http://s7.addthis.com/js/250/addthis_widget.js#username=beeruniverse" type="text/javascript">
          </script>
         </div>
        </div>
        <div class="image left">
         <img src="http://www.beer-universe.com/images/articles/375/beer banned mlb.png"/>
        </div>
        <p class="first">
         Youve probably seen the articles floating around discussing the debacle of the Boston Red Sox. It was reported by the Boston Globe that Jon Lester, John Lackey and Josh Becket  three big time pitchers for the team  generally drank beer, ate fried-chicken, and played games in the clubhouse while their teammates were playing a game. Well what-seemed to be a Boston specific problem has now escalated to the top of the organization  Major League Baseball (MLB) is considering a ban of beer in all team clubhouses.
        </p>
        <p>
         Bostons season was promising. They were scheduled to once again be a top-contender in their division. At the end of the day, the Red Sox came one game short of the post-season. Now, apparently the entire problem can be directed at one thing:
         <strong>
          <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
           beer
          </span>
         </strong>
         . The MLB has taken notice.
        </p>
        <p>
         "You've got to make them understand why there are rules,'' MLB's vice president told reporters. "I want people to understand the nature if we do ban alcohol from clubhouses. We're supposed to be role models for youngsters.''
        </p>
        <p>
         This is a pretty big deal since previously the rule on alcohol in the clubhouse was done on a team-by-team basis. Basically, the irresponsibility of three players could affect all of the players around the league  a few bad apples, eh?
        </p>
        <p>
         Wed like to point out some issues with the overall decision:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Banning beer from baseball will do very little to disassociate itself with the sport  think about how much Budweiser / Miller / Coors gets served at each game (to mom &amp; dad too)
         </li>
         <li>
          In fact, two of the final four teams played in ballparks named for beer companies
         </li>
         <li>
          Does the league realize that the Milwaukee Brewers are named after the
          <strong>
           brewing
          </strong>
          tradition on the city?
         </li>
         <li>
          Why not suspend players who actually abuse alcohol  for example those who receive DUIs outside of the game
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         By the way, for youngsters you may also want to remove tobacco products from the clubhouse too. Far too often do you see players packing dip, which makes kids think its cool, or chewing tobacco.
        </p>
        <p>
         Now on the other hand, most people cant drink at their place of business. In fact, most people have the common sense not to drink while on the job. Thats generally a hey, dont be stupid rule. Professional sports, however, is not your typical work environment.
        </p>
        <p>
         What do you think? Should they ban beer from the clubhouse? We can definitely understand why Lester, Lackey, and Becket drank in the clubhouse 
         <a href="../../../../../beer-education-article/2011-10-14/Most-and-Least-Expensive-Ball-Park-Beer-and-Hot-Dogs-in-2011/">
          the beer prices at Fenway Park
         </a>
         are some of the highest in the country!
        </p>
        <p>
         Your Choice. Your Beer. Drink Up.
         <br/>
         - Beer Universe
        </p>
