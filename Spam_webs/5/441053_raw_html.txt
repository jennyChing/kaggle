      <h1>
       How To Plan Your Week When You Have Tons To Do
      </h1>
      <div class="meta">
       <img alt="" class="avatar avatar-60 wp-user-avatar wp-user-avatar-60 photo avatar-default" height="60" src="http://1.gravatar.com/avatar/72914ea7ec84fddf5fcb41492f584593?s=60&amp;d=http%3A%2F%2F1.gravatar.com%2Favatar%2Fad516503a11cd5ca435acc9bb6523536%3Fs%3D60&amp;r=G" width="60"/>
       <p class="date-meta">
        By
        <a href="http://shetakesontheworld.com/author/natalie/" rel="author" title="Posts by Natalie MacNeil">
         Natalie MacNeil
        </a>
       </p>
       <div class="comments-link">

      <div addthis:title="How To Plan Your Week When You Have Tons To Do " addthis:url="http://shetakesontheworld.com/2013/05/how-to-plan-your-week/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style ">
       <a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:layout="button_count">

      <p>
       <a href="http://shetakesontheworld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/BASKETS-1.jpg">
        <img alt="How to Plan Your Week" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-9943" height="169" src="http://shetakesontheworld.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/BASKETS-1-300x169.jpg" width="300"/>
       </a>
       Have you ever been told you should just focus on one thing at a time? Its way easier said than done, and if you enjoy having multiple projects on the go you are certainly not alone. Im the same way, and today Im going to share how to plan your week.
      </p>
      <p>
       I have tried to focus on one thing at a time in the past, and it just doesnt work for me. I thrive on juggling a few different projects at a time, and I think thats perfectly okay as long as youre finishing what youre starting.
      </p>
      <p>
       Thats where my basket system comes in handy.
      </p>
      <p>
       My basket system is how I plan my week, and bring a bit of balance to the multiple projects I work on at any given time.
      </p>
      <p>
       In todays episode of She Takes on the World TV Im walking you through this basket system so it can help you better plan your week too.
      </p>
      <p>
       Watch the episode below (or click
       <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Styyq6q1MoU">
        here
       </a>
       ):
      </p>
      <div class="video-container">
       <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="506" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Styyq6q1MoU?rel=0" width="900">
       </iframe>
      </div>
      <p>
       <strong>
        Now I want to hear from you!
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       In a comment below, I want to hear from you! Whats your strategy for planning your week and how do you juggle multiple projects at once?
      </p>

           <p>
            Aw Libby I know exactly what you feel like. When I used to go through that, I realized that it was really all tied to my desire to decide what I would be working on. I naturally hate being told what to do, and doing work for school just felt like I was being pushed into doing something I really didnt want to do.
           </p>
           <p>
            I finally did something about it when I started having the same problem in my business  when I started just doing things because they had to get done, I lost enthusiasm and procrastinated by doing other things instead.
           </p>
           <p>
            I fixed this issue by asking myself why do I want to do this? and do I really want this? for everything I do. In the end, when I think about the pros and cons, I usually decide that yes, I do want to do this. That makes it look less like imposed work in my eyes and more like a personal choice that Im willing to follow through with. Its a trick that worked for me, along with some good old-fashioned mindfulness
            <img alt=";)" class="wp-smiley" src="http://shetakesontheworld.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_wink.gif"/>
            hope this helps you too!
           </p>
