       <header class="entry-header">
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-11-02T05:41:51+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          November 2, 2014
         </time>
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          by:
          <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
           DudeMom
          </span>

        </p>
        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         DudeFood: Oven Baked French Toast Breakfast For Dinner
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="16888665" data-link="http://dudemom.com/2014/11/baked-french-toast-breakfast.html" data-summary="" data-title="DudeFood: Oven Baked French Toast Breakfast For Dinner">
        </div>
        <p>
         At any given time, if you were to pop over (never do it, Im warning you, call first) and take a peek in my fridge youd likely find a number of mostly eaten loaves of bread.
        </p>
        <p>
         I have this thing for tossing them in the fridge, right before they get moldy, and forgetting them entirely. Because I dont like cold bread. And no one in my family does either.
        </p>
        <p>
         So they sit.
        </p>
        <p>
         Until I decide to make breakfast for dinner!
        </p>
        <p>
         Breakfast for dinner is always a winner at our house.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Dudes love it. DudeDad loves it. DudeDog loves smelling it and standing around under the table hoping someone randomly dribbles some onto her face.
        </p>
        <p>
         Theres not a being in this place who doesnt appreciate breakfast food at dinner time.
        </p>
        <p>
         French toast is one of their all time faves.
        </p>
        <p>
         Only, I hate making French toast.
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont like how my fingers get all wet and icky when Im dipping (please dont bother trying to fix this issue for me, Ive literally tried everything).
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive considered the overnight version before but that requires advanced planning, and yall know I do not excel in that.
        </p>
        <p>
         Last week, in a desperate attempt to provide my children with sustenance, I decided to do a fridge purge and, as per usual, all I had at my disposal was a bottle of spicy mustard, milk, eggs, string cheese, hazelnut coffee creamer, butter, and bread.
        </p>
        <p>
         So. Much. Bread.
        </p>
        <p>
         Thank goodness thats about all you need for oven baked French toast, well not the mustard and cheese, but the other stuff.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://dudemom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/bread-pudding.jpg">
          <img alt="french toast" border="0" height="431" src="http://dudemom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/bread-pudding_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="french toast" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          <span style="font-size: large;">
           Oven Baked French Toast
          </span>
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ingredients
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A bunch of oldish bread (I used a mix of cinnimon raisin bread and white bread), torn in chunks (just use enough to fill up a 139 in pan)
        </p>
        <p>
         7 eggs
        </p>
        <p>
         1 1/2 cups milk
        </p>
        <p>
         1/2 cup flavored coffee creamer (I used the hazelnut because thats what I had)
        </p>
        <p>
         3/4 tsp cinnamon
        </p>
        <p>
         1/2 cup brown sugar
        </p>
        <p>
         2 tablespoons vanilla
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          Topping
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          1/2 cup flour
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          1/2 cup brown sugar
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          1 teaspoon cinnamon
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-size: medium;">
          1 stick cold butter, sliced
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://dudemom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/bread-pudding2.jpg">
          <img alt="oven baked french toast" border="0" height="362" src="http://dudemom.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/bread-pudding2_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border: 0px;" title="oven baked french toast" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Directions
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         1. Preheat oven to 350 and grease up your pan. I buttered mine, you could use cooking spray if you want.
        </p>
        <p>
         2. Tear the bread into chunks and drop it in the pan.
        </p>
        <p>
         3. In a bowl, mix all the wet stuff. Make sure you beat the eggs good so there wont be any egg hunks in your bread. I dont know about you, but when I run into egg hunks in my French toast I gag a little.
        </p>
        <p>
         4. Add the sugar and mix it some more.
        </p>
        <p>
         5. Pour on the bread. Make sure all of your bread is nice and soaked in the stuff. Now, if you are a planner, this is the point where you could pop it in the fridge and bake it tomorrow and serve your family the pot roast youve been cooking in your crock pot all day. But, if youre like me, and you dont have a pot roast anywhere in your house, let alone in a crock pot nearly ready for grubbin, you should probably just move along to step 6.
        </p>
        <p>
         6. Mix all of the topping stuff in another bowl.
        </p>
        <p>
         7. Cut in the butter.
        </p>
        <p>
         8. Sprinkle over your wet bread mixture thingy.
        </p>
        <p>
         9. Bake for 45 minutes to an hour. I bake a little longer because, wet eggs, gag.
        </p>
        <p>
