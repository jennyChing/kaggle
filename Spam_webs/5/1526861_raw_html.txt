        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Visit to Blue Bird Gap Farm {Hampton, VA}
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-05-06T07:37:03+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          May 6, 2014
         </time>
         by
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.thetalkingsuitcase.com/author/dzeliff/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Dana Zeliff
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://www.thetalkingsuitcase.com/blue-bird-gap-farm-hampton-va/#comments">
           6 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <p>
        <em>
         <small>
          <a href="http://www.thetalkingsuitcase.com/disclosure-disclaimer-privacy-policies/" target="_blank">
           Disclosure:
          </a>
          This post may contain an affiliate link &amp; I may have been invited as a guest for free or a reduced rate.
         </small>
        </em>
       </p>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <h2 style="text-align: center;">
         <a data-rel="lightbox-0" href="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Bluebird-Gap-Farm.jpg">
          <img alt="Bluebird Gap Farm" class=" wp-image-550 aligncenter" height="333" src="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Bluebird-Gap-Farm-1024x562.jpg" width="607"/>
         </a>
         <br/>
         {
         <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
          <a href="http://www.pinterest.com/pin/497436721314861232/" target="_blank">
           Pin It
          </a>
         </span>
         }
        </h2>
        <p>
         We attended a birthday party at Blue Bird Gap Farm in Hampton, VA this weekend. It was such a fun afternoon for all the kiddos  from toddlers to my oldest. They ran around climbing, looking at animals and just pain enjoying being crazy kids for an afternoon.
        </p>
        <p>
         I used to go to Blue Bird Gap Farm with my aunt as a child, so it was nice to be back after all these years. Even though a lot has changed, it still brought back great memories. I enjoyed getting to share the farm with my children all these years later.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-rel="lightbox-1" href="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Blue-Bird-Gap-Farm-Animals.jpg">
          <img alt="Blue Bird Gap Farm Animals" class="wp-image-533 aligncenter" height="505" src="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Blue-Bird-Gap-Farm-Animals.jpg" width="624"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Where is Blue Bird Gap Farm?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Blue Bird Gap Farm is located just off I-64 near the Hampton Coliseum. If youre traveling along I-64 near the Hampton Roads Bridge Tunnel then its a great spot to let the kids out to stretch their legs and have a bite to eat. Its only about 3 minutes to the farm once you jump off the interstate.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What to See and Do at Blue Bird Gap Farm:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         The 60-acre farm has about 150 animals, both domestic and wild. Youll find deer, tortoises, racoons, goats, birds of prey and my personal favorite  PEACOCKS just to name a few.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-rel="lightbox-2" href="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Blue-Bird-Gap-Farm-Playground.jpg">
          <img alt="Blue Bird Gap Farm Playground" class="wp-image-534 aligncenter" height="533" src="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Blue-Bird-Gap-Farm-Playground-768x1024.jpg" width="400"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         There is a large playground with a big playset for big kids and a smaller set for little ones. Youll also find an ample amount of swings to accommodate lots of kids.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-rel="lightbox-3" href="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Bluebird-Gap-Farm-Collage.jpg">
          <img alt="Bluebird Gap Farm Collage" class=" wp-image-515 aligncenter" height="281" src="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Bluebird-Gap-Farm-Collage-1024x512.jpg" width="562"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         After you play, take a walk around the nature trail and make sure to visit the barn and feed the goats. Youll also want to take a peak at the Hampton Master Gardeners Display Garden and Arboretum. Its a pretty little area full of flowers and plants.
        </p>
        <p>
         Another nice feature is that the farm also has restrooms that are surprisingly well cared for and clean. Going to public restrooms on a farm can sometimes be pretty gross. Is it odd that the restrooms are one of the things I remember most?
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre hungry there are snack and drink vending machines, but I advise bringing your own food. Have a picnic (or just a few snacks) on one of the benches, picnic tables or simply spread a blanket of the grass near the Azalea trail or the pond.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <strong>
          The best part? Its FREE to enjoy the entire Blue Bird Gap Farm!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a data-rel="lightbox-4" href="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Bluebird-Gap-Farm-Picnic-Area.jpg">
          <img alt="Bluebird Gap Farm Picnic Area" class="wp-image-531 aligncenter" height="352" src="http://thetalkingsuitcase.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/Bluebird-Gap-Farm-Picnic-Area-1024x768.jpg" width="469"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Having a gathering such as a birthday party is fantastic at this location. There are so many things to keep the kids busy and you dont have to pay a rental fee! Since you cant reserve a spot, make sure to come early. Our party was held back near the tortoise and rabbit enclosures.  It really is the perfect spot for an event. Youre tucked away from most of the crowd, have picnic benches and a huge tree to provide shade. Plus its only about a minute walk to the playground and the barns.
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="kidtips">
          Bring lots of quarters to feed the animals. The goats will eat right out of your hands! Watch for peacocks  they run around everywhere. Theyre nice but its kinda scary seeing them staring at you from on top of the bathroom.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         Visit the
         <a href="http://www.hampton.gov/bbgf/" target="_blank">
          park website HERE
         </a>
         for information on hours (closed Tuesday).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <em>
          Have you been to Blue Bird Gap Farm? Share with us your favorite memories.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         Have you even gotten home and realized youre missing a ton of pictures? I took 10 (at least) of the kids feeding the goats. Where are they? I took a couple shots of the entire playground and a peacock on the restroom roof. Nope. Dont have those either. Im sticking with I have not lost my mind and my camera ate my pictures.
        </p>
