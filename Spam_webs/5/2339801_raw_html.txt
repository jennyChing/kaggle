            <h1 class="post-title entry-title">
             5 Job Types That Allow You to Travel the World While Working
            </h1>
            <div class="yit_post_meta first_block">
             BY
             <span class="vcard author">
              <span class="fn">
               <a href="http://iamaileen.com/about/">
                Aileen Adalid
               </a>
              </span>

             <span class="post-date date updated">

             </span>
             <span class="comments">

          <div class="yit_the_content">
           <p>
            It has been
            <strong>
             almost two years
            </strong>
            since I quit my corporate job in the Philippines so that I cantravel the world and work remotely. Looking back, the start of it all surelyinvolved a lot of changes and spurtsof fear on my part but right now?I dont regret the decision that Ive made and I never will,becauseIm having the time of my life and Im enjoying every second of it!
           </p>
           <div class="yit_post_quote " data-delay="0">
            <i class="fa fa-quote-left shade-1">
            </i>
            <h2 class="quote-title">
            </h2>
            <p>
             
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <strong>
               READ
              </strong>
             </span>
             :
             <a href="http://iamaileen.com/quit-job-5-steps-to-take-start-life-travel/" title="The 5 Steps I Took to Start a Life of Travel">
              The 5 Steps I Took to Start a Life of Travel
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Besides,
            <em>
             this
            </em>
            is exactly the kind of lifestylethat I have always wanted.And working a 9 to 5 job
            <em>
             often, even more hours!
            </em>
            cooped inside an office setting? Well
            <em>
             that
            </em>
            , wasnt really my cup of tea
           </p>
           <p>
            Well okay, I have to admit that
            <em>
             at first
            </em>
            it looked great because of the sense of security that it gave me as well asthe whole idea of achieving success in the corporate setting of an investment bank
            <strong>
             at the age of 19
            </strong>
            . But as time slowly passed,I realized that I wanted to
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             Take control of my time (who doesnt?) and not trade my 20s for a job that I didnt love
            </li>
            <li>
             Earn whatI actually deserve with my set of skills (fresh graduatesin my opinionwill always be undervalued anyways); and,
            </li>
            <li style="margin-bottom: 20px;">
             Get rid of unnecessary time-wasters (hello, horrible traffic and humongous stress!)
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p>
            in order todevelop the careerorbusiness that I have always wanted whileI venture out to see the world, meet different people, and experiencevarious things
            <em>
             all
            </em>
            at the same time.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>

           <p>
            Today, other than blogging aboutmy travels in Asia and Europe, I work as a freelance graphic designer, web designer, SEO manager, and marketing assistant (as needed)while managing my own Amazon business,
            <strong>
             <a href="http://AdalidGear.com" target="_blank" title="Adalid Gear (Amazon Shop) Website">
              Adalid Gear
             </a>
            </strong>
            (together with my partner in crime a.k.a. boyfriend, Jonas). Most days involve around4 hours of working
            <em>
             not consecutively
            </em>
             which leaves mea lot ofhours fortraveling, strolling, bumming *lol*, and doing the hobbies that Ive never managed to do before (like playing the piano, singing in a bossa nova band, etc.)
           </p>
           <p>
            With all of this
            <em>
             blah-blah
            </em>
            , I guess what Ijust want to say is that:
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <a href="http://iamaileen.com/quit-job-5-steps-to-take-start-life-travel/" title="The 5 Steps I Took to Start a Life of Travel">
               YOUcan do the same thing
              </a>
             </span>
             !
            </strong>
           </p>
           <p>
            With the help of this post, I will give you some ideas on how you can jumpstart your
            <em>
             life of travel
            </em>
            thats filled with financial + job location independence. Other than my own experiences, I will also share other jobs that my friends and travel bloggers have done or are doing.
            <br/>
            <sub>
             <em>
               To make the list easier, Ill group related jobs in one number/heading.
             </em>
            </sub>
           </p>
           <h3 style="margin-top: 40px;">
            <span id="DigitalNomad" style="color: #ed2665;">
             #1
            </span>
            DIGITAL NOMAD JOBS
           </h3>
           <p>
            This was how I started after I resigned in 2013. Being a 
            <strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              digital nomad
             </span>
            </strong>
             simply means making full use of the technology around you (ta-dah: Internet!) in order to work remotely and earn an income whenever and wherever you are
            <em>
             much like a nomadic lifestyle.
            </em>
            (Truth be told, if youre creative enough, you can convert any existing profession that you have into a digital nomad job!)
           </p>
           <div class="yit_post_quote " data-delay="0">
            <i class="fa fa-quote-left shade-1">
            </i>
            <h2 class="quote-title">
            </h2>
            <p>
             <strong>
              MY STORY:
             </strong>
             (My story)I first started out with oDesk to offer my services of:
             <em>
              graphic design, web design, SEO management, and marketing.
             </em>
             (Mind you that I didnt know much about these areas at firstmy course in college was about business anyways and none about marketing, design, or SEOI just learned these skills by myself online which you can do too; theres just
             <em>
              sooooo
             </em>
             many free resources on the internet that theres no need to really enroll yourself to a school). With the work Ive done in oDesk, I controlled my timeandI accepted/applied for paid projects that I wantedright in the comfort of my own apartment or in some beach or resort in Asia. [Working remotely throughthe internet = digital nomad life]
            </p>
            <p>
             <span style="color: #ededed;">
              .
             </span>
             <br/>
             Ieventually landed ona stable high-paying online job with a Swedish brand and thatwent on for almost a year (I first started as one of theirCustomer Service Staff, advanced to being their Product Line Supervisor, and eventually their Head Marketing + Social Media Manager). It lasted only for a year because come May2014, I decided to adapt their kind of business. So, after a bit of prep (and a bit ofguidance from the CEO of thee said Swedish company), Jonas and I set up our own online Amazon business:
             <strong>
              <a href="http://AdalidGear.com" target="_blank" title="Adalid Gear (Amazon Shop) Website">
               Adalid Gear
              </a>
             </strong>
             . Even after resigning from the Swedish company, it still made me me a digital nomadapart from being an entrepreneurbecause the kind of business that we had was still online and we can work anytime, anywhere.
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            <img alt="Aileen Nomad" class="aligncenter wp-image-7952 size-full" height="406" src="http://iamaileen.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/jobs-allow-travel-the-world-while-working-digital-nomad.jpg" title="Digital Nomad Jobs: Work whenever and wherever you want!" width="846"/>
           </p>
           <p>
            For moretypes ofdigitaljobs that you can do with the help of an internet connection(see the
            <a href="http://iamaileen.com/top-picks-best-websites-make-money-online-work-from-home/" title="My Top Picks: 33 Best Websites to Make Money Online &amp; Work from Home">
             top 33 helpful websites you can use
            </a>
            ), just read throughthe list below (because one or more could fit you!):
           </p>
           <ul>
            <li>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <strong>
               Consultation or Coaching
              </strong>
             </span>
             <br/>
             If you are a professional or an expert ona certain field (lets say: financial management, events management, real estate, relationship + dating
             <em>
              [pick up artists and so on]
             </em>
             , etc.) then this is something that you can offer online; this is because most companies often need the help without the need to permanently hire someone in their physical office. You can evensign up for sites like www.
             <strong>
              SkillShare
             </strong>
             .comwhere you can make passive income from prepared courses that you will make.
            </li>
            <li>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <strong>
               Online Entrepreneurship
              </strong>
             </span>
             <br/>
             For my case, we do (e-commerce) retail on www.
             <strong>
              Amazon
             </strong>
             .com but other than this kind of start-up (which you can also do on www.Etsy.com and www.
             <strong>
              eBay
             </strong>
             .com), you can try selling digital products like eBooks, games,and apps, or set up your own website and build a brand for your services (onprogramming, website development, tour organization/trips, etc.). Sure, it doesnt always have to be something online, you can stillbe a business owner of a different kind; but its just that most of the time, online-based businesses give you more freedom to move around. (To know more about other websites where you can sell digital products, see this
             <a href="http://iamaileen.com/top-picks-best-websites-make-money-online-work-from-home/" title="Top Picks: 33 Best Websites to Make Money Online &amp; Work from Home">
              article
             </a>
             .)
            </li>
            <li>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <strong>
               Online Poker Player
               <br/>
              </strong>
             </span>
             Aside from being an entrepreneur
             <em>
              waaaaay
             </em>
             before me, Jonas was also a professional online poker player ever since 2008 and that enabled him to travel to different places like the Maldives, Prague, U.S., etc. and eventually landing in Asia. This of course requirescertain level of skills likebankroll management, number crunching, probability forecasting, etc. so it might be a bit of a hard start. But once you learn the game, the return is
             <em>
              enormous
             </em>
             . Ive tried to learn it before but decided not to really commit because it was just not my thing LOL(I hate dealing with numbers and probabilities). Plus: the plays here are just intense. [If youre in the U.S., online poker laws are a bit cloudy thats why its best that you check www.
             <strong>
              LegalUSPokerSites
             </strong>
             .com. If youre interested in learning the game, www.
             <strong>
              PokerStrategy
             </strong>
             .com is a good website to start and www.
             <b>
              PokerStars
             </b>
             .com is one of the best places to start playing poker online.
            </li>
            <li>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <strong>
               Online Sports Betting
              </strong>
             </span>
             <br/>
             Its exactly what it says and it can definitely help in funding your work-and-travel lifestyle. Unfortunately, Jonas and I dont know much about this and have never tried it at all BUT we know a couple of friends who succeeded with it (one is on basketball and the other is on football) and they did say that there are a lot of resources online to help guide you. For a start, check by http://
             <strong>
              Sports.Bovada.lv
             </strong>
             /betting-guide/ that has a very comprehensive introductory guide for online sports betting.
            </li>
            <li>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <strong>
               Trading (Arbitrage, Foreign Exchange / Forex, Stocks, etc.)
              </strong>
             </span>
             <br/>
             If you have a strong background on these areas, make full use of it (otherwise, it can be learned). A LOT of money can be made through this, but then again, a LOT of money can be lost too; hence, its always best to really know what youre doing. I have a background on this since I worked in an investment bank before but I dont really enjoy it; but Jonas, on the other hand, enjoys it (since for him, its much like poker anyways plus he also holds a related degree that he got from university) so he does this on the side.
            </li>
            <li>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <strong>
               (Travel, Fashion, Lifestyle, etc.) Blogging + Vlogging (YouTube)
              </strong>
             </span>
             <br/>
             This takes work, but once youve established yourself online, paid opportunities can be really good. Companies or brands will pay you for features and reviews, or give you a free stay/flight/meal.You can even make use of advertising and affiliate marketing opportunities that can really rake in some money. As for vlogging, its one of popular things nowadays because the return can be enormous once you become a partner of YouTube. Most people do makeup and fashion videos or just make funny skits online. Whereas if youre into gaming, you can do commentaries or compilations (yes, people actually earn millions just by recording themselves while playing a game  yeah I know, its crazy, go to www.
             <strong>
              Twitch
             </strong>
             .tv and youll see what Im talking about), or record yourself singing/dancing, or your cute pet moments. Simply put, the choices are almost endless that its likely that one of your hobbies would make a perfect YouTube channel and following.
             <br/>
             <div class="yit_post_quote " data-delay="0">
              <i class="fa fa-quote-left shade-1">
              </i>
              <h2 class="quote-title">
              </h2>
              <p>
               
               <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                <strong>
                 READ
                </strong>
               </span>
               :
               <a href="http://iamaileen.com/how-to-start-a-travel-blog-from-scratch-ultimate-guide/" title="How to Start a Travel Blog from Scratch: Your Ultimate Guide!">
                How to Start a Travel Blog from Scratch: Your Ultimate Guide!
               </a>
              </p>
             </div>
            </li>
            <li>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <strong>
               Virtual Specialists or
              </strong>
             </span>
             <span style="color: #ed2665;">
              <b>
               Assistants
              </b>
             </span>
             <br/>
             With the help of online websites (like
             <a href="http://iamaileen.com/top-picks-best-websites-make-money-online-work-from-home/" title="My Top Picks: 33 Best Websites to Make Money Online &amp; Work from Home">
              these
             </a>
             ) and depending on your skills and interests, you can do:
             <ul style="margin-top: -15px;">
              <li>
               Administrative Work
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Customer Service, Data Entry, Data Processing, Management,Online Research, Secretarial, Transcription, etc.
                </li>
               </ul>
              </li>
              <li>
               Specialist Services
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Content Writing / Copywriting / Editing / Proofreading, Finance (Bookkeeping), Legal, Medical, Marketing/Marketer,Social MediaManagement, etc.
                </li>
               </ul>
              </li>
              <li>
               Technical Services
               <ul>
                <li>
                 Graphic Design, HTML, Programming, Web Design, Web Development, Video/Audio Editing, etc.
                </li>
               </ul>
              </li>
             </ul>

Yet,here you are, reading this article as a start-up advise, with me as one of your guide! Thereforeif you think about it, this is already a stepping stone for you: so take advantage of it ;)
            </em>
           </p>
           <blockquote>
            <p>
             <strong>
              
             </strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <strong>
               TIP
              </strong>
             </span>
             :Much like what you have learned before in school, its a wise choice to 
             <em>
              diversify your portfolio
             </em>
             meaning that when applied to this kind of lifestyle, make the most of what you can do or what you love to do. Dont just settle on one job for your earnings, go mix and match! Example, youre great on lettering and calligraphy, other than selling your services while on the road (to hostels, restaurants, galleries, etc.) also build your digital nomad opportunities by building a website of your own, selling your prints, and more. There are just so many opportunities out there! And once you find the best fit for you,
             <em>
              <span style="color: #ed2665;">
               start thinking long term
              </span>
             </em>
             because (for instance) you wouldnt to keep working for somebody else (bars, hostels, etc.) for all your life, right? So try to find a business or profession that will secure your future; may it be a stable online company, a tutoring school, a dance studio, etc.
            </p>
            <p>
             <strong>
              
             </strong>
             <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
              <strong>
               LAST TIP
              </strong>
             </span>
             :
             <em>
              Are you under 35 and planning to stay abroad for a year?
             </em>
             If yes, consider the possibility of getting a youth working holiday visa! Some countries allow travelersto work (and look for work) during their vacation which can lastup to one yearthis would actas some sort of residence permit. To know more about this, simplycheck
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Working_holiday_visa" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Countries and territories offering working holiday visas">
              this article
             </a>
             to see which countries have reciprocal agreements (if youre from the Philippines, we have an agreement with New Zealand so its worth checking out).
            </p>
            <p>
             Like Ive already mentioned above, always check the visa requirements first of the country that youre planning on visiting, as well as their conditions for non-onlineworking travelers. For example, Its usually possible to 
             <em>
              work under the table
             </em>
              and receive free lodging and pay in exchange for your services; but, some countries wont allow that. Just be mindful about these things and youre good to go!
            </p>
           </blockquote>
