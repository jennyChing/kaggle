         <header class="page-header">
          <h1 class="page-title">
           <a href="http://cinemasideshow.com/chris-seavers-evil-night-2014/">
            Chris Seavers Evil Night  2014
           </a>
          </h1>
         </header>
         <div class="page-content">
          <figure>
           <a href="http://cinemasideshow.com/chris-seavers-evil-night-2014/">
           </a>
          </figure>
          <p>
           <a href="http://cinemasideshow.com/wp-content/uploads/evilnightposter.jpg">
            <img alt="Evil Night 2014 Poster" class="aligncenter wp-image-400 size-full" height="296" src="http://cinemasideshow.com/wp-content/uploads/evilnightposter.jpg" width="200"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           <strong>
            Language
           </strong>
           : English
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Original Aspect Ratio
           </strong>
           : 16:9
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Directed by
           </strong>
           : Chris Seaver
           <br/>
           <strong>
            Cast
           </strong>
           : Josh Suire, Meredith Host, Marilyn Mayson
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Synopsis
             <br/>
            </strong>
           </span>
           A nerd goes on a killing spree with his new telekinetic superpowers after being the victim of a prank by a group of college kids.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Blu-ray Release
             <br/>
            </strong>
           </span>
           Evil Night is available in a limited VHS/DVD combo, DVD and a limited Blu-ray edition by
           <a href="http://www.warlockhomevideo.com/">
            Warlock Home Video
           </a>
           . As of this writing, both the VHS/DVD and the Blu-ray editions were limited to 50 pieces and are now currently sold out.
           <em>
            (Update: Looks like theyre printing more of the Blu-ray)
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           This review is for the Blu-ray edition which comes in a very fitting orange case. The cover art appears to be printed on normal printer paper and the disc is a BD-R. Im little disappointed in the fact its a burned copy but we have to remember that this is a low budget company were talking about.The film was shot on aBlackmagic Design 4k Production Camera and the transfer looks good. Audio is presented in 2.0 and sounds fineas well.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Extras
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Commentary withChris Seaver and a friend
            </strong>
             An interesting commentary on the history of Chris Seavers filmmaking experience for the first twenty minutes. The commentary then goes to talk about the making of the film and potential future projects. Sounds like theyre eating in the beginning and its a little distracting but overall its a fun listen.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Commentary with Chris Seaver
            </strong>
            <strong>
             and Clint Kelly
            </strong>
             First time listening to a Skype person on an audio commentary. This one seems a little slower than the first commentary. Theres a lot more technical information in this one for those who are interested in that kind of thing.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Todd Cook SOV Interview
            </strong>
            (16:09)Looks to be the same interview that you can find onthe
            <a href="http://www.sovhorror.com/" title="SOV Horror">
             sovhorror website
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Evil Night Behind the Scenes 
            </strong>
            (16:17) Watch Chris Seaver direct! Interesting if not a little slow.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Warlock Home Video Promo 
            </strong>
            (2:05) Promo for other shot on videos by the Chris Seaver crew. Whats weird is the trailer for Evil Night immediately follows the promo.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             Trailer 
            </strong>
            (1:29) Trailer for the film. I dig the music.
           </li>
           <li>
            <strong>
             12 Inches of Dangling Fury Short
            </strong>
             Low Budget Pictures short film. See a poorly dubbed big breasted Asian lady!
           </li>
          </ul>

          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Thoughts
             <br/>
            </strong>
           </span>
           If youve never seen a Chris Seaver film, you owe yourself to at least give one a watch. I stumbled across his former label called Low Budget Productions several years ago when I was on a zombie movie buying spree. I ended up picking up Mulva Zombie Ass Kicker and boy was I not prepared for what I was watching. The film was shot on consumer grade equipment with a couple of his friends. Add a nonsense plot and sprinkle it with a bunch of bad acting and you have a bona fide Chris Seaver film. But I have to say, its pretty damn interesting to watch.Evil Night is a remake of a1992 Shot on Video film of the same name directed by cult director  Todd Jason Cook. Full disclosure but I have never seen the original so I cannot comment on how the remake differs from the original. The remake feels a little short and clocks in under a hour. Its probably a positive considering the type of film it is.
          </p>
          <p>
           First off, let me just say that the movie is full of crude adult humor. Sex jokes run rampant throughout the entire thing and I know this will not be everybodyscup of tea.
          </p>
          <p>
           The acting in the film is pretty typical of a Chris Seaver film. If youve managed to watch a few of his films already, youll start to realize that he uses a lot of the same people. Whats typical acting you might say? Its a lot of intentional over-acting for comedic effect. Its an acquired taste of course. I personally dont mind it.Sometimes it works. Sometimes it comes off wrong. I thoroughly enjoyed the over the top performance of Jimmy Fisher, played wonderfully by Josh Suire. His character just clicks and works for me. Doing a little research, looks like the character Samantha was played by a porn star. Huh, learn something new everyday.
          </p>
          <p>
           The film was shot by Clint Kelly and looks good for a no budget film. The gore effects are just as bad as the acting. In one scene we have a dildo go through the back of a girls head. Okay, maybe its not that bad. Another thing I liked about the film was the choice of music performed byChan Walrus? Neat name.
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            A movie full of dildos, kitchen grade gore and some boobs sprinkled here and there. Bama Chris Seaver film!
           </em>
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Conclusions
             <br/>
            </strong>
           </span>
           Fans of adulthumor will love the film. Im a little mix on the film but I did find myself enjoyingit at times.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Rating
            </strong>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           2.5 out of 5
          </p>
