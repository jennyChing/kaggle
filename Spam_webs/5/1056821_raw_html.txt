                   By Jim Beard
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Writer Dan Slotts got plans.
                   <i>
                    Big
                   </i>
                   plans.
                  </p>

                   <tbody>
                    <tr>
                     <td>
                      <a href="/images/gallery/story/20411/superior_spider-man_big_changes/image/948223">

                      </a>
                     </td>
                    </tr>

                  <p>
                   Those plans involve setting Spider-Man up for a sizzling summer in an event we could only call Superior Spider-Month. It happens this July, but the festivities really begin right
                   <i>
                    now
                   </i>
                   , in April, in the pages of SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN
                   <a href="http://comicstore.marvel.com/Superior-Spider-Man-7/digital-comic/FEB130536" target="blank">
                    issue #7
                   </a>
                   and
                   <a href="http://comicstore.marvel.com/Superior-Spider-Man-8/digital-comic/FEB130537" target="blank">
                    #8
                   </a>
                   . Big ideas, big changes and the biggest storylines around.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   To get to the bottom of it all, we asked Slott some
                   <i>
                    big
                   </i>
                   questions: What should readers look for? What will the Superior Spider-Mans relationships be like when the dust clears? What about
                   <nolink>
                    Peter Parker
                   </nolink>
                   ? And did he answer them all? Read along and find out!
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marevl.com: Dans whats going on right
                    <i>
                     now
                    </i>
                    in
                    <a href="http://comicstore.marvel.com/Superior-Spider-Man/comics-series/9382" target="blank">
                     SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN
                    </a>
                    thats laying the ground work for this summer?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   <i>
                    Everything
                   </i>
                   lays ground work, everything. Dont miss a single issue.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: But anything in particular that readers should really concentrate on or look at and know that this its going to lead into Julys Superior Spider-Month?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Right now we are having a two-parter that involves Cardiac and the Avengers, after a couple things that Spidey has done have gone public. The Avengers are starting to go heeeeey, that doesnt feel like Spider-Man, whats going on here, what is this? And even other people are starting to tweak to it too. And weve seen Spider-Man act weird in the past, weve seen him lose his cool, and smash up people like Firelord, and when he thinks he is going to kill the Lizard. You know, weve seen him lose it at times, and at no point does anyone go Spider-Man youre losing it. Someone mustve swapped your brain. You know, its not the first thing you jump to. But people are starting to get a little, Eehhhhhhh, somethings weird. Somethings going on.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: Who else might be thinking somethings up with Spidey as we go into the summer?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="popupimage" style="float: left; width: 120px;">
                   <tbody>
                    <tr>
                     <td>
                      <a href="/images/gallery/story/20411/superior_spider-man_big_changes/image/949861">

                      </a>
                     </td>
                    </tr>
                    <tr>

                      <a href="/images/gallery/story/20411/superior_spider-man_big_changes/image/949861">
                       Superior Spider-Man #14 cover by Humberto Ramos
                      </a>
                     </td>
                    </tr>
                   </tbody>
                  </table>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Well, weve already seen [it in] the villain community and the criminal community. Words spreading fast that if you mess with Spider-Man, he messes you up. Weve already seen in
                   <a href="http://comicstore.marvel.com/Superior-Spider-Man-4/digital-comic/DEC120664" target="blank">
                    SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN #4
                   </a>
                   when Spider-Man is swinging over to stop some crooks, White Rabbit and their gang, and everybody just immediately throws up their hands. Because Well, weve heard you are going all Punisher and Wolverine on us. You know, we give up. So already the underground has taken notice.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   And we know that Carlie Cooper, she pretty much had it all laid out for her on a platter. Shes not out there going Spider-Mans weird, shes going I think he might be Doc Ock. So weve seen Carlie in the past when shes been trying to figure things out where she really dots her ts and crosses her is, [but] she cant just walk up to the Avengers or Mayor Jameson and go I think theres been a mind swap. Its just so ludicrous. She better have all her information, she better have a lock solid case.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: Glad you mentioned Jameson. Theres some big stuff coming up with Jonah in the book; how is that dynamic going to change, or evolve?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Right now, you know, Jonah has completely flipped over to Spider-Mans side. Black is white, white is black, cats and dogs living togetherits crazy. And with this more brutal Spider-Man, who is really cracking down on crime, Jonah is respecting him. Jonah is living in his New York as Mayor where hes lost his wife to the Spider Slayer. Hes seen a woman who is like a daughter to him, Betty Brant, mugged. Hes adopted a zero tolerance policy to crime. And now hes sees Spider-Man, who has all this great power and a very different sense of responsibility, who is very much in line with how he sees the world, how Jonah sees the world. And hes really warming to this guy.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: Thats going to come to a head in No Escape, beginning in Mays SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN #11, right?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="popupimage" style="float: right; width: 120px;">
                   <tbody>
                    <tr>
                     <td>

                      </a>
                     </td>

                    <tr>

                     </td>
                    </tr>
                   </tbody>
                  </table>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Thats when you are going to finally see the Spider Slayer answer for his crimes, for killing Marla Madison. J. Jonah has been waiting for this day to come.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: And Spider-Man and Jonah end up on the Raft?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Yes, Jonah in recent issueswhether or not he has the authority to do this as the mayorwants the Raft closed. Because the Raft, when you think about it, is this criminal dumping station right off of Manhattan. And guys break out all the time, and then
                   <i>
                    bam
                   </i>
                   , theyre in Manhattan. And all that makes it very easy for us Marvel writers. But its very silly, if you have any kind of political sway in New   York, to go yeah, we want this thing. We want this thing right here. Right off the tip of Manhattan. Thats great. So Jonah has been pushing real hard, calling in whatever favors he can to get the Raft closed down, locked up and just done. So we are going to see a story in the last days of the Raft.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com:
                   </b>
                   <b>
                    With people like Jonah Jameson in his corner, is Otto becoming more confident, more comfortable in what hes doing?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Otto is
                   <i>
                    always
                   </i>
                   comfortable with what hes doing.
                   <nolink>
                    Otto Octavius
                   </nolink>
                   is always correct. He knows exactly what hes doing. He is superior in every way.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: What can you say about the dynamic between Peter and Otto? About whats going to happen there?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Well, weve already seen a sneak preview in SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN #7 when Doc is asleep; Peter has been practicing and hes been gained control of the right hand. Uh-oh. Stuff is going on. Peter is ramping up the fight. Hes sick of just being the whiny ghost that no one can hear here. Hes starting to fight back.

                   <b>
                    Marvel.com:
                   </b>
                   <b>
                    So things will be escalating on that score?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   Everything ramps up. And come SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN #9 there will be a massive change in the status quo of the book. A massive change, a game-changing issue.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com:
                   </b>
                   <b>
                    Speaking of game-changers, looks like the Green Goblin returns in SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN #10...
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott
                   </b>
                   : Yes, we saw the Green Goblin in #4; we will see more of him. We have not yet seen who is under the mask.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: Will whoever he is be a part of Superior Spider-Month this summer?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   You are going to have to wait and see. But you will see more of the Goblin. And more of what the Goblin is doing.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com:
                   </b>
                   <b>
                    We know that there is so much that you cant say, but what in general are you really excited about, coming up to the summer?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Dan Slott:
                   </b>
                   In SUPERIOR SPIDER-MAN #14 there will be new allies, a new look, and an all-new agenda. What is Superior Spider-Man doing? Why is he doing
                   <i>
                    that
                   </i>
                   ? We have never seen a Spider-Man in any continuity ever do
                   <i>
                    that
                   </i>
                   . Yeah. Everyone just suddenly got very shocked in #5 and when Chris Eliopoulos was lettering it his son walked into the room and saw the art and he just yelled out, Spider-Man doesnt do that! Well, heres something else in #14 that Spider-Man doesnt do. And hes doing it.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   <b>
                    Marvel.com: There are no limits anymore?
                   </b>
                  </p>
                  <table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="popupimage" style="float: right; width: 120px;">
                   <tbody>
                    <tr>
                     <td>
                      <a href="/images/gallery/story/20411/superior_spider-man_big_changes/image/946826">

                      </a>
                     </td>
                    </tr>

                   </b>
                   Change your expectations. Everything that you think Spider-Man cant do? Thats off the table.
                  </p>

                   I could not stop smiling while I was writing [issue #14]. Its the issue where everything explodes, explosions and craziness and we literally change the face of the New   York of the Marvel Universe.
                  </p>
                  <p>
                   Going forward from there, expect to see more of the Goblin, and we promised
                   <nolink>
                    Spider-Man 2
                   </nolink>
                   099, so you will get
                   <nolink>
                    Spider-Man 2
                   </nolink>
                   099. And we have carefully created a world where the Flash Thompson Venom has yet to really meet and fight Spider-Man. There have been little tiny blips, like in the pages of
                   <a href="http://comicstore.marvel.com/Venom-Vol-2/comics-series/5659" target="blank">
                    VENOM
                   </a>
                   right when it was kicking off and in one of the Carnage minis. But for the most part these two characters have been kept at a distance. Even when both guys were on different Avengers teams, they didnt really cross paths. Even in Spider Island, where both greatly affected each others stories they never really met. We are going to put it front and center; the two will come face to face.
