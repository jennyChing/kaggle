         <h2 class="date-header">
          September 04, 2010
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-author-judy_aldridge entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a0105364db45b970c0133f3c3d56c970b">
          <div class="entry-inner">
           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://www.atlantishome.com/my_weblog/2010/09/carols-chocolate-disco-cookies.html">
             CAROL'S CHOCOLATE DISCO COOKIES
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f3c3bdaf970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Cookie 1" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0105364db45b970c0133f3c3bdaf970b image-full " src="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f3c3bdaf970b-800wi" title="Cookie 1"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Last week Carol made these fantastic chocolate cookies. The recipe called for sanding sugar, but we had only shimmering sugar on hand.
              <a href="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c013486e5b6ca970c-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Cookie 2" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0105364db45b970c013486e5b6ca970c image-full " src="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c013486e5b6ca970c-800wi" title="Cookie 2"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              We ended up with chocolate disco cookies, but the taste was not compromised. Here is the recipe. Taken from Martha Stewart's cookies. BTW--Carol is in heaven in her new kitchen. I think she may give me the boot.
             </p>
             <p>
              GRAMMY'S CHOCOLATE COOKIES (actual recipe name)
             </p>
             <p>
              2 cups, plus 2 tablespoons all purpose flour
             </p>
             <p>
              3/4 cup unsweetened Dutch-process cocoa powder
             </p>
             <p>
              1 teaspoon baking soda
             </p>
             <p>
              1/2 teaspoon salt
             </p>
             <p>
              1 1/4 cups (2 1/2 sticks) unsalted butter, room temperature
             </p>
             <p>
              2 cups sugar
             </p>
             <p>
              2 large eggs
             </p>
             <p>
              2 teaspoons vanilla extract
             </p>
             <p>
              Sanding sugar for rolling, or shimmering if you want disco cookies
             </p>
             <p>
              1-Sift together flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt into a bowl
             </p>
             <p>
              2-In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, beat butter and sugar on medium speed until light and fluffy, about 2 minutes. Add eggs and vanilla, and beat to combine. Reduce speed to low, and gradually add flour mixture; beat to combine. Form dough into a flattened disk, wrap with plastic wrap, and chill until firm, about 1 hour.
             </p>
             <p>
              3- Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Shape dough into 1 1/4" balls. Roll each ball in sanding sugar. Place on baking sheets lined with parchment paper, about 1 1/2 inches apart. Bake until set, 10 to 12 minutes, rotating halfway through. Transfer to a rack to cool for 5 minutes. Transfer cookies from baking sheet to wire rack. Cookies can be stored between layers of parchment paper in an airtight container at room temperature up to 1 week.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              Enjoy!!
              <br/>
             </p>

         <h2 class="date-header">
          August 24, 2010
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-author-judy_aldridge entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a0105364db45b970c0133f34ce7fd970b">
          <div class="entry-inner">
           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://www.atlantishome.com/my_weblog/2010/08/carols-banana-bread.html">
             CAROLS BANANA BREAD
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f34cd910970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Carols banana bread" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0105364db45b970c0133f34cd910970b image-full " src="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f34cd910970b-800wi" title="Carols banana bread"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              I hope everyone isn't tired of cooking posts. I promise to get back to other things--and, my cooking partner just went back to school. The cooking posts will definitely slow down, but we still plan on keeping busy in the kitchen. Carol even made the most delicious soup in the slow cooker before she went to school this morning! Here is Carol's banana bread recipe--it's very easy, and always turns out perfectly. Taken from Williams Sonoma kids baking.
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>
              BANANA BREAD
             </p>
             <p>
              1 2/3 cups all purpose flour
             </p>
             <p>
              2 teaspoons baking powder
             </p>
             <p>
              1/2 teaspoon salt
             </p>
             <p>
              1/4 teaspoon of baking soda
             </p>
             <p>
              3 medium, very ripe bananas
             </p>
             <p>
              2/3 cup sugar
             </p>
             <p>
              1/3 cup vegetable oil
             </p>
             <p>
              2 large eggs
             </p>
             <p>
              1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
             </p>
             <p>
              *We added 1/2 cup of broken walnuts
             </p>
             <p>
              Pre-heat oven to 350 degrees
             </p>
             <p>
              Grease a loaf pan with butter
             </p>
             <p>
              In a medium sized bowl, using a wooden spoon, stir together the flour, baking powder, salt and baking soda.
             </p>
             <p>
              In a large sized bowl, mash the peeled bananas with a fork. Add the sugar, oil, eggs, and vanilla and beat with a wooden spoon until well blended. Add the flour mixture to the banana mixture. Stir just until blended.
             </p>
             <p>
              Scrape the batter into the prepared loaf pan, spreading it evenly with a wooden spoon. Put the pan in the oven and bake for about 45 minutes, until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Remove from oven, and let cool 20 minutes. Gently run a table knife along the sides of the loaf pan, and turn finished loaf out.
             </p>
             <p>
              Enjoy!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
             <p>

            <a href="http://www.atlantishome.com/my_weblog/2010/08/more-kitchen-love.html">
             MORE KITCHEN LOVE
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f34cce26970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Desire to inspire" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0105364db45b970c0133f34cce26970b image-full " src="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f34cce26970b-800wi" title="Desire to inspire"/>

             <p>
              I absolutely love this kitchen! Borrowed from
              <a href="http://www.desiretoinspire.net/">

One of things I did this weekend was watch episodes of Curb Appeal on Hulu,--I'm completely addicted to this show. I love seeing what they can do to change the appearance of older homes. My home was built in the 70's, and it definitely needs a little curb appeal. The yard looks pretty okay, but I have yucky 70's sandy colored brick. I'm going to take the plunge and paint my brick. I think it will really help increase the value of my house overall--does anyone have any brick painting experiences they would like to share? Don't be shy--let's hear your thoughts!!

         <h2 class="date-header">
          August 19, 2010
         </h2>
         <div class="entry-author-judy_aldridge entry-type-post entry" id="entry-6a0105364db45b970c0133f32be93f970b">
          <div class="entry-inner">
           <h3 class="entry-header">
            <a href="http://www.atlantishome.com/my_weblog/2010/08/marthas-lemon-bars-versus-aunt-gails-lemon-bars.html">
             MARTHA'S LEMON BARS VERSUS AUNT GAIL'S LEMON BARS
            </a>
           </h3>
           <div class="entry-content">
            <input name="zemanta-related" type="hidden" val=""/>
            <div class="entry-body">
             <p>
              <a href="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bc87a970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Lemons" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bc87a970b image-full " src="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bc87a970b-800wi" title="Lemons"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              I love homemade lemon bars--who doesn't? Traditionally in my family, we have always used a tried and true recipe from my Aunt Gail. Recently, Carol challenged this tradition by using Martha Stewart's lemon bar recipe. She felt is was better. After much debate about the attributes of each recipe, we decided to have a lemon bar throw down--Martha vs. Aunt Gail.
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bd48c970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Aunt gails" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bd48c970b image-full " src="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bd48c970b-800wi" title="Aunt gails"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Aunt Gail's lemon bar. (my pan was a little large, so they should be a bit thicker)
             </p>
             <p>
              <a href="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bd53a970b-pi" style="display: inline;">
               <img alt="Marthas" border="0" class="asset asset-image at-xid-6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bd53a970b image-full " src="http://atlantishome.typepad.com/.a/6a0105364db45b970c0133f32bd53a970b-800wi" title="Marthas"/>
              </a>
             </p>
             <p>
              Marthas.
             </p>
             <p>
              We had several expert tasters on hand to try the end result. (mostly a group of Jane's friends)
             </p>
             <p>
              The winner hands down is--Aunt Gail. They were both good, but Martha's was a little rich for my taste, and much more complicated to make. Even Carol agreed that Aunt Gail's crust was far superior.
             </p>
             <p>
              AUNT GAIL'S LEMON BARS
             </p>
             <p>
              Preheat oven to 350 degrees
             </p>
             <p>
              CRUST
             </p>
             <p>
              1 cup all purpose flour
             </p>
             <p>
              1/2 cup of butter (1 stick)
             </p>
             <p>
              1/4 cup powered sugar
             </p>
             <p>
              Add sugar to butter, then flour. Mix well. Press evenly into 8" square pan. Bake 20 minutes.
             </p>
             <p>
              FILLING
             </p>
             <p>
              2 eggs
             </p>
             <p>
              1 cup sugar
             </p>
             <p>
              1/2 teaspoon baking powder
             </p>
             <p>
              1/4 teaspoon salt
             </p>
             <p>
              2 tablespoons of lemon juice
             </p>
             <p>
              a little lemon zest
             </p>
             <p>
              Beat all ingredients together, and pour into baked crust. Return to oven and bake 20 minutes longer. Do not over bake. Let cool, then dust with powered sugar and cut into squares.
             </p>
             <p>
              Sorry Martha!
             </p>
             <p>
             </p>
