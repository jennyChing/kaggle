       <h3>
        Which Brands Will Last?
       </h3>
       <em>
        Snob,
       </em>
       <p>
        <em>
         If you'd have to guess which new manufacturer today (making entry-level watches under $1,000-$4,000), or over the last few years, is making a watch that will appreciate rather than depreciate and still be in existence by 2050, what would your guess be? Xetum? Prometheus? Sinn (if it is "new")? Anyone else?
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        My guess is that in 2050, none of these entry-level brands will exist, no matter how clever their names are. But this is nothing new. The history of the watch industry is littered with forgotten names and broken dreams, with $20 watches on eBay as their legacy. Except nowadays there are simply more of them due to the prevalence of internet marketing and sales and access to cheap Chinese labor.
       </p>
       <p>
        For the record, Sinn should not be included in that list. The brand may have some entry-level watches  even some acceptable ones  but at the higher end, it has managed to add some value to its timepieces beyond just a cool logo and sea monster caseback engraving. Sure, it's not making
        <a href="http://www.askmen.com/fashion/trends/watch-movements-watch-fundamentals.html">
         movements
        </a>
        , but it has a history of stellar casework and durability innovations as well as thoughtful modifications to otherwise boring ETA calibres.
       </p>
       <p>
        Small brands can easily be started by some bored IT guy in a cube who wants the name of his favorite
        <em>
         Lord of the Rings
        </em>
        character on the dial. Think that guy cares what happens to his watches when hes tapping his 401k and golfing in 2050? Itll just be a story he tells his retirement community buddies.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h3>
        The Snob Approves NOMOS
       </h3>
       <em>
        Dear Snob,
       </em>
       <p>
        <em>
         Lately I have taken a bit of an interest in watches and decided to buy my first real watch. Ive narrowed it down to two pieces: the NOMOS Tangente with the date, power reserve and seconds, or a MeisterSinger automatic N. 03. Where the NOMOS is a bit more expensive, it is also a manufacture and has beautiful details. The MeisterSinger has a unique design with the single hand and is a bit bigger (but is bigger better in this case?) so it stands out a bit more. Could you please help me?
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Im tempted to take credit for the uptick in mentions of NOMOS lately, given my recent slightly-warmer-than-tepid endorsement of the brand. And at the risk of sounding like a broken record, or whatever the kids are playing music on these days, here again I recommend it over the MeisterSinger. I admit, the aesthete in me is drawn to the latter brands single-handed watches, but then I flip it over and see the unimaginative movement. The 43 mm case seals its fate as an also-ran.
       </p>
       <p>
        An in-house, German-made movement with
        <a href="http://www.askmen.com/entertainment/better_look/5_degrees-of-complication-the-chronograph.html">
         a useful complication
        </a>
        inside a 35-millimeter Bauhaus-design case with the bit of color in the Gangreserve  read that list of features a couple of times, and then go buy the NOMOS.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <h3>
        Question From A Watch Knob
       </h3>
       <em>
        What colognes do you wear? I think that's as important as a watch. Might I suggest Santa Maria Novella or similar?
       </em>
       <p>
        Though perhaps equally overpriced,
        <a href="http://www.askmen.com/grooming/appearance/21_cologne-dos-donts.html">
         cologne
        </a>
        has hardly anything in common with timepieces. One part whale bile, one part wolf urine, seven parts cardamom, cloves and motor oil, mix thoroughly and bottle in a phallic-shaped contained designed to be posed with a muscular, hairless man-child draped with a scantily clad supermodel sporting a come-hither expression. No, thank you. I dont need cologne to attract mates. The Watch Snobs own aura suffices.
       </p>
