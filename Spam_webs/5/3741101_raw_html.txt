      <h2 class="post-title">
       10 Brilliant Ways To Use Vinegar!
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta">
       <em>
        posted by
       </em>
       Desiree Fawn
       <em>
        on
       </em>
       August 31, 2014 //
       <a href="http://momspark.net/10-brilliant-ways-to-use-vinegar/#comments">
        2
        <em>
         comments 
        </em>
       </a>
      </div>
      <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       A few weeks ago, I shared a whole mess of ways to use
       <a href="http://momspark.net/coconut-oil-10-awesome-ways-to-use-it/" target="_blank" title="Coconut Oil: 10 Awesome Ways To Use It!">
        coconut oil
       </a>
        something simple that can be kept for years in your home without spoiling. Its basic, its awesome, and its SO useful. You know what else fits into that basic-awesome-useful category? VINEGAR! Of all kinds!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Honestly, the first things that comes to mind when I think of vinegar is either a) cleaning with white vinegar or b) eating malt vinegar on my fries! When I was pregnant, I
       <em>
        craved
       </em>
       malt vinegar like nobodys business. I actually considered drinking it straight out of the bottle, but thought better of it  instead I just ate a LOT of fries. Probably thats the lesser of two evils? Either way, vinegar is excellent, in all forms, and today Im going to share TEN brilliant ways to use vinegar in your daily life  lets do it!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <a href="http://momspark.net/10-brilliant-ways-to-use-vinegar/ways-to-use-vinegar/" rel="attachment wp-att-48065">
        <img alt="10 Brilliant Ways To Use Vinegar!" class="alignnone wp-image-48065 size-full" height="1200" src="http://momspark.net/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/ways-to-use-vinegar.png" title="10 Brilliant Ways To Use Vinegar!" width="800"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
       10 Brilliant Ways To Use Vinegar
      </h2>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        01. To disinfect your childs toys!
       </strong>
       Plastic toys can be soaked in a water-vinegar solution to get rid of lingering bacteria that kids really enjoy spreading around. Some daycare centers use this method  especially when toys end up in mouths so often!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        02. To clean your sink drain!
       </strong>
       Pour a 1/2 cup of white vinegar down your drain and let it stand for about 20 minutes. You can boost this cleaning power with baking soda if youd like, then follow it up with a few kettles of boiling water. If your drain was clogged, not just dirty, you can use a plunger (but probably not the one that use you in your toilet, for obvious reasons).
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        03. To kill fruit flies!
       </strong>
       My pal Maggie of
       <a href="http://www.sheletthemeatcake.com/" target="_blank">
        SheLetThemEatCake.com
       </a>
       swears by using vinegar to snuff out those pesky fruit flies. Heres what she does:
       <em>
        I add it to a bowl, say a 1/4 cup or so with a drop or two of dish soap. Cover it with saran and poke toothpick holes in it. Change every couple of days, its like they smell death and avoid it if you dont change it up. That sounds terrible, but theyre fruit flies!!!
       </em>
       Sounds good to me!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        04. To boost your laundry cleaning power!
       </strong>
       Toss in a cup or so of white vinegar to your next load for added freshness. Thisll get the funk out of your stale, summery beach-towels too!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        05. To wash your fruits and veggies!
       </strong>
       Get rid of icky pesticide residue by soaking your fruits and veggies in a vinegar solution before using them.
       <a href="http://goodgreenhabits.com/wash-your-fruits-veggies-with-vinegar/" target="_blank">
        Good Green Habits
       </a>
       has a great post on how to do just that.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        06. To clean your dishwasher!
       </strong>
       Get rid of stains and soap scum and stink by using vinegar to swish out your fancy dishwashing machine! Ifound
       <a href="http://youtu.be/jEiKS8ezIQo" target="_blank">
        this awesome video
       </a>
       via
       <a href="http://youtu.be/jEiKS8ezIQo" target="_blank">
        Clean My Space
       </a>
       that shows you how to use vinegar with baking soda to kick your stinky dishwasher in the butt! #karatechop
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        07. To cure hiccups!
       </strong>
       To this day, one of my BFFs carries tiny restaurant packets of white vinegar in her purse and swigs them as soon as her hiccups hit. She gets those mean, barking, painful sort of hiccups, and swears that one good swig of vinegar is all it takes to send those hiccups packing.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        08. As a safe flea repellentfor your pets!
       </strong>
       You can create a safe flea spray using apple cider vinegar that can be applied to your pets skin before heading outdoors.
       <a href="http://www.earthclinic.com/pets/flea-treatment-for-dogs-apple-cider-vinegar.html" target="_blank">
        Earth Clinic
       </a>
       suggests making a diluted solution of ACV and water in a sprayer to apply it easily. Just be sure to cover your pets eyes before spritzing.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        09. To kill the weeds between sidewalk cracks and cobblestone!
       </strong>
       Say goodbye to the pesky bits of greenery that like to show up between your paving stones by mixing up a concoction of vinegar, sea salt, and a drop of dish liquid, then spraying it on the unwanted shrubbery. Adios, weeds!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       <strong>
        10. As bugbite relief!
       </strong>
       Some swear by a dab of vinegar on your bug bites to take away that uncomfortable sting. Bring it with you the next time youre camping or out at the cottage  a little goes a long way!
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Time to stock up on every kind of vinegar you can find! Thankfully, despite its incredible versatility, vinegar is a cost effective way to do any of the 10 things Ive listed about, which means youll never have to worry about running out! Enjoy the virtue of vinegar, my friends  and feel free to add your OWN favorites uses to the comments! Wed love to hear what youve been using it for lately!
      </p>
