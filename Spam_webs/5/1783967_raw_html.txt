         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SVLL1IGFE28DM06" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/FMY/H3EL/FE379EQ5/FMYH3ELFE379EQ5.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/FMYH3ELFE379EQ5" data-notes='[{"noteID":"NBPEQZOFHPVYCSK","author":"MXB246UFB7FC2GD","screenName":"stinkymum","addedDate":1214131940556,"modifiedDate":1214131940556,"text":"tie dying on Sandy Island in New Hampshire (week 1)","top":0.51,"height":0.072,"left":0.691304347826087,"width":0.12173913043478261}]' href="/file/FMYH3ELFE379EQ5" id="photoset-link-FMFFTG4IAXRWFES-1" style="">
          <div class="photo-container" style="">
           <img alt="dye.jpg" class="photoset-photo id_FMYH3ELFE379EQ5 lazyphoto" data-notes='[{"noteID":"NBPEQZOFHPVYCSK","author":"MXB246UFB7FC2GD","screenName":"stinkymum","addedDate":1214131940556,"modifiedDate":1214131940556,"text":"tie dying on Sandy Island in New Hampshire (week 1)","top":0.51,"height":0.072,"left":0.691304347826087,"width":0.12173913043478261}]' data-original="http://cdn.instructables.com/FMY/H3EL/FE379EQ5/FMYH3ELFE379EQ5.MEDIUM.jpg" src="/static/img/pixel.png" style="height:600px;"/>

          <i class="notes-icon">
          </i>
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <div class="txt step-body">
        <p>
         You can buy dye kits very easily in the craft stores, which come with everything you need.  They include rubber gloves, rubber bands, soda ash, urea and complete, easy to follow instructions. The dyes are already in the squeeze bottles, all you have to add is warm water (not hot). Other alternatives include buying single packages of dyes.  These also come with the necessary urea, soda ash and instructions for mixing.  However, you would need to purchase some squeeze bottles separately if you don't have any.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Although I have used kits, I prefer to use the Procion dyes as supplied by Dharma Dye. They have a great website where you can buy all you need.
         <a href="http://www.dharmatrading.com/dyes/" rel="nofollow">
          http://www.dharmatrading.com/dyes/
         </a>
         Procion dyes are the best and brightest dyes, I thoroughly recommend them.   If you mix the dye powder yourself however, always wear a mask so that you don't inhale the powder into your lungs.
        </p>

        Step 3: The tools you will need for success
       </h2>
       <div class="photoset" data-entry-id="SU3Z9PQFE28DM1H" data-entry-url="/id/How-to-tie-dye-an-old-white-shirt-or-a-new-shirt-/step3/The-tools-you-will-need-for-success/" id="photoset-SU3Z9PQFE28DM1H">
        <div class="row" style="width:600px;">
         <a class="photoset-link" data-fancybox-group="photoset-gallery-SU3Z9PQFE28DM1H" data-fancybox-href="http://cdn.instructables.com/F0S/4G4S/FE28DM09/F0S4G4SFE28DM09.LARGE.jpg" data-href="/file/F0S4G4SFE28DM09" data-notes="" href="/file/F0S4G4SFE28DM09" id="photoset-link-F1IQQMQIAXRWFEU-0" style="">

           <img alt="Picture of The tools you will need for success" class="photoset-photo id_F0S4G4SFE28DM09 " data-notes="" src="http://cdn.instructables.com/F0S/4G4S/FE28DM09/F0S4G4SFE28DM09.MEDIUM.jpg" style="width:600px;"/>

       <div class="txt step-body">
        <p>
         If you are like me and not using a pre-packaged kit, you will need the following;   Procion dye, rubber bands, rubber gloves, squeeze bottles for the dye, urea and soda ash.  Oh yes, don't forget to have paper towels and old rags to mop up the spills.  Tie-dying does not have to be messy!

         Hello:
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Soda Ash isn't the same as baking soda.  you can buy it from a dye store or get it at a pool and spa store.  It is caustic so be sure to protect your hands and eyes.
         <br/>
         <br/>
         As for banding.  Once you twist your shirt into a bun.  (I use a fork and twirl like its pasta)  Just take your rubber band, hold it open and slide it under , and around the shirt while it's sitting flat.  Do that 2 more times and you'll have your triangles. (almost looks like a pie chart at this point.)
         <br/>
         <br/>
         Once you've allowed the garment to sit dyed for 24 hours, rinse, then wash with the hottest water for the garment in the machine...with Syntrapol or similar product.  The shirt is amazingly color fast.  I have one dress I bought about 5 years ago that the colors are still vibrant... the fabric is showing wear... but the colors are still awesome.
