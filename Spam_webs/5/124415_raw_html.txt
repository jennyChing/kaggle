              <span class="credit pull-right">

                John Spooner

              </span>
             </figcaption>
            </figure>
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="2" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articleReview" href="/od/sportstrek/ss/TorresGrCircuit.htm">
               1.
Torres del Paine Circuit
              </a>
             </h3>
             <p>
              In a national park of the same name in Chilean Patagonia there are several options for hikers. Beginner hikers may take a leisurely day hike to view the flora and fauna while most hike the 'W' route over five days. For the most adventurous it is possible to hike the full circle in 9 days to view the waterfalls, glacier lakes and dense forests.
             </p>
             <p>
              As it is a national park there are refugios throughout the hike to provide basic services and campsites.
             </p>
             <p>
              Hiking is best from December through February as these Summer months provide the best weather and longest daylight hours.
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="1" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articelReview" href="/od/sportstrek/ss/TorresGrCircuit.htm">
               More 
              </a>
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>

           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="3" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articleReview" href="/cs/southamerica/a/PerIncaTrail.htm">
               2.
Inca Trail to Machu Picchu
              </a>
             </h3>
             <p>
              In the heart of the Peruvian Andes the Inca trail is on many traveler's bucket lists. Created by the Incas this path begins outside
              <a href="/od/perucuzco/Cuzco_and_Surrounding_Area.htm">
               Cuzco
              </a>
              and takes three or four days to reach the Incan city.
             </p>
             <p>
              Travelers must book this tour a few months in advance to secure a trail permit. For those who have not planned there are several other hikes to Machu Picchu which do not require permits but similar views.
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="2" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articelReview" href="/cs/southamerica/a/PerIncaTrail.htm">
               More 
              </a>
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>

              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="4" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articleReview" href="/od/santamarta/Santa_Marta_Colombia.htm" source="outbound_list">

             <h3 class="item-heading">
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="5" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articleReview" href="/od/santamarta/Santa_Marta_Colombia.htm">
               3.
Ciudad Perdida
              </a>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Known in English as The Lost City, many travelers head North in
              <a href="/od/colombia/Colombia.htm">
               Colombia
              </a>
              specifically for this hike. Ciudad Perdida was discovered less than 50 years ago and is believed to have been created before Machu Picchu.
             </p>
             <p>
              This hike is for the adventurous as it takes three days walking through dense jungle before reaching the 1200 steps that take you to the city. It can be particularly tiresome during the rainy season as there are a number of rivers to walk through and accommodation along the way is very rustic.
             </p>
             <p>
              The land still belongs to the indigenous community who live in the area permitting only official guides through the area for tourism.
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="3" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articelReview" href="/od/santamarta/Santa_Marta_Colombia.htm">
               More 
              </a>
             </p>

           <div adsense-displaylabel="true" adsense-numlinks="2" class="adsense-slot adsense" id="adsense2">

           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              4.
Cotopaxi
             </h3>
             <p>
              Only a few hours away from Quito, Cotopaxi National Park is a popular hiking destination. Cotopaxi volcano is one of
              <a href="/od/ecuador/tp/Ecuadors-Ten-Highest-Mountains.htm">
               ten mountains in Ecuador over 5000m
              </a>
              and the highest active volcano in the world.
             </p>
             <p>
              The most popular hike includes a three day trek to the base of the mountain, for there it takes less than 8 hours to reach the peak. Interestingly while Cotopaxi historically has an eruption at the beginning of the century, there has not been one in over 100 years.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="6" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articleReview" href="/cs/southamerica/a/PeruColca.htm">
               5.
Colca Canyon
              </a>
             </h3>
             <p>
              Located in Southern Peru, Colca Canyon is a popular tour from
              <a href="/od/arequipa/Arequipa_Peru.htm">
               Arequipa
              </a>
              . The canyon is the world's deepest, incredibly twice as deep as the Grand Canyon however, because the walls aren't as steep it is possible to hike down to Colca River.
             </p>
             <p>
              A very manageble hike down in 2-3 hours, many hikers find the steep walls to be challenging, especially as some areas are quite unstable with lots of rocks and pebbles. Below there are two resorts that offer hikers a dip in the pool before their hike back up. Many of those hikers opt to stay overnight and for those who cannot fathom climbing the steep walls there are horses and mules available to take you back up.
              <a data-component="contentListPage" data-ordinal="4" data-source="outbound_list" data-type="link.articelReview" href="/cs/southamerica/a/PeruColca.htm">
               More 
              </a>
             </p>

            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              6.
Cerro Campanario
             </h3>
             <p>
              Head to
              <a href="/cs/argcities/p/bariloche.htm">
               Bariloche
              </a>
              to find one of the best views in Argentina,Cerro Campanario is only 17km outside of town but boasts a National Geographic rating of one of the 'Top 10 views in the World'.
             </p>
             <p>
              The hike is rather steep but thankfully short. Hikers are rewarded with a beautiful view of the Andes and lakes that dot the area. For those who do not feel like hiking, it is possible to take a chair lift to the top and enjoy the view from a cafe.
             </p>
            </div>
           </section>
           <section class="list-unit group">
            <div class="expert-content-text">
             <h3 class="item-heading">
              7.
Illampu Circuit
             </h3>
             <p>
              This climb in
              <a href="/cs/southamerica/a/BolBolivia_2.htm">
               Bolivia
              </a>
              is only for experienced hikers as it is very physically demanding and requires acclimatization.
             </p>
             <p>
              The views are stunning on this 66 mile trail but it is challenging and requires a llama or alpaca to carry treking gear as hikers ascend 1,000 feet each day in this remove area.
             </p>
