      <h1 class="omc-post-heading-standard">
       Treehouses All Grown Up in Stunning Architectural Designs
      </h1>
      <p>
       <span>
        November 15th, 2012
       </span>
       by
       <strong>
        <span style="color: #85d2eb;">
         <a href="http://greenbuildingelements.com/author/jenshock81/" rel="author">
          Jennifer Shockley
         </a>
        </span>
       </strong>
       <a id="dd_start">
       </a>
       Some of the best, most inspiring modern architectural designs that work with nature also are a remarkable play on childhood fantasies. An earlier article by Glenn Meyers discussed
       <a href="http://greenbuildingelements.com/2012/03/26/instead-of-a-treehouse-try-a-floating-home/" target="_blank">
        floating homes
       </a>
       . Other articles discuss different uses for materials used to make homes like
       <a href="http://planetsave.com/2009/08/05/could-trees-be-the-new-rubber/" target="_blank">
        trees as the replacement for rubber
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       This article highlights the use of trees in architectural brilliance as tree houses. Three different beautifulbuildings are
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The conference center
       </li>
       <li>
        The Redwoods Treehouse
       </li>
       <li>
        The Wilkinson Residence
       </li>
      </ul>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8547" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-1.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8547" height="150" src="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-1-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        The conference center
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8548" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-3.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8548" height="150" src="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-3-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Hechtel-Eksel
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8549" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-6.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8549" height="150" src="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-6-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Hechtel-Eksel Conference Room
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8550" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-11.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8550" height="150" src="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-11-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Hechtel-Eksels stair/skylight
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The conference center was designed by German architects, Baumraum Architects in the forests of Hechtel-Eksel, Belgium as a group commissioned project for four companies. The companies are Sappi, The Flemish Forest &amp; Nature Agency, Hechtel-Eksel and Proximity.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8551" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8551" height="150" src="http://greenbuildinge.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse-1250x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        The Redwoods Treehouse
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8553" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://greenbuildinge.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse-12.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8553" height="150" src="http://greenbuildinge.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse-12-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Walkway to Redwoods Treehouse
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8554" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse-7.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8554" height="150" src="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse-7-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Day photo of Redwoods Treehouse
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8555" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://greenbuildinge.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse-126.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8555" height="150" src="http://greenbuildinge.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/redwoodstreehouse-126-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Redwoods Interior
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Redwoods Treehouse was originally part of the marketing campaign for the Yellow Pages but now it has become a permanent diner in Auckland, New Zealand. It is built ten meters (33 feet) above the ground in the trees. It was designed by Pacific Environment Architects.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8556" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouseoshatzwilkinson.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8556" height="150" src="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouseoshatzwilkinson-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Wilkinson Residence
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_8557" style="width: 160px">
       <a href="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-oshatz-wilkinson-3.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-8557" height="150" src="http://2uec1mzw3qc42yelv1w8xtj13zp.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/files/2012/11/treehouse-oshatz-wilkinson-3-150x150.jpg" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Wilkinson Interior
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       The Wilkinson Residence built for owner Roy Wilkinson by architect Robert Harvey Oshatz in Portland, Oregon is designed to be almost inseparable from its surrounding.
      </p>
      <p>
       The materials of all the buildings are sustainable but the images that they present are magical.
      </p>
      <p>
       This is just a short introductory to three amazingly sustainable, imaginary-brought-to-life buildings that show how beautiful, nature-inspired places can be.
      </p>
