            <h4>
             With a full season of Thursday Night Football, Here is An Early Season Preview of the Remaining TNF NFL Games
            </h4>
            <p>
             With NFL betting weekends starting on Thursday night this season, I decided to give you a season long rundown of every remaining NFL Thursday Night Football game.
            </p>
            <p>
             Last night was the first NFL Thursday Night football game of the season and the Baltimore Ravens made a statement by whipping the Steelers 26-6.
             <a href="http://www.sportbet.com/lines/football_nfl">
              Click here for odds on Thursday Night Football
             </a>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/BlO_A7n9G2Y" width="560">
             </iframe>
            </p>
            <h2>
             <strong>
              2014 NFL Thursday Night Football  Picks
             </strong>
            </h2>
            <p align="center">
             Week 3  09/18
             <br/>
             Buccaneers at Falcons
             <br/>
             CBS/NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             The Buccaneers are rebuilding under respected head coach Lovie Smith while Atlanta tries to continue its rebound campaign after suffering from a severe case of Murphys Law a year ago.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Falcons.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 4  09/25
             <br/>
             Giants at Washington
             <br/>
             CBS/NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             New York and Washington will square off in the nations Capitol in this Thursday night affair with both of these NFC East rivals looking to rebound from respectively mediocre campaigns in 2013. Im thinking the outcome of this contest will come down to which starting quarterback, either Eli Manning or Robert Griffin III, turns the ball over the fewest amount of times.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet Washington.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 5  10/02
             <br/>
             Vikings at Packers
             <br/>
             CBS/NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             The Vikings and Packers will face off in one of their perennial NFC North matchups, which, generally, are tightly contested affairs. The bottom line for this Thursday night thriller will be whether or not veteran quarterback Matt Cassel can outplay Packers elite signal-caller Aaron Rodgers.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet the Packers.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 6  10/09
             <br/>
             Colts at Texans
             <br/>
             CBS/NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             Andrew Luck and the Colts will look to win this AFC South clash  while taking a big step toward keeping the Texans from going from worst to first after their flop of a season in 2013.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet the Colts.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 7  10/16
             <br/>
             Jets at Patriots
             <br/>
             CBS/NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             Sure, theres always a lot of media hoopla surrounding this perennial AFC East throw-down, but most of it is generally just a lot of hot air coming out of the mouth of Jets head coach rex Ryan.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Patriots
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 8  10/23
             <br/>
             Chargers at Broncos
             <br/>
             CBS/NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             Maybe its me, but I have a funny feeling that Philip Rivers and the San Diego Chargers are going to challenge Denver for division supremacy in the AFC West this season. Of course, I could feel a lot different once this Week 8 matchup is over.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Broncos.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 9  10/30
             <br/>
             Saints at Panthers
             <br/>
             NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             Carolina won a surprising 12 games last season, but Im thinking Saints future Hall of Fame signal-caller Drew Brees is going to give Panthers quarterback Cam Newton a lesson on how the position is really supposed to be played.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Panthers.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 10  11/06
             <br/>
             Browns at Bengals
             <br/>
             NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             While I believe its quite possible that exciting rookie Johnny Manziel could start for the Browns at this late point in the 2014 season, I dont think it really matters much whos under center for Cleveland as I fully expect Cincinnati to challenge for the AFC conference crown in 2014.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet the Bengals.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 11  11/13
             <br/>
             Bills at Dolphins
             <br/>
             NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             The Bills and Dolphins both kicked off the 2014 season with impressive victories despite being underdogs in their respective openers.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet the Bills.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 12  11/20
             <br/>
             Chiefs at Raiders
             <br/>
             NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             The Chiefs and Raiders will stage one of their perennial AFC West battles in this Week 12 showdown  and Im thinking the rebuilding Raiders will have a great shot to win this one if rookie quarterback Derek Carr continues to build on the impressive maturation hes shown early on.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Raiders.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Thanksgiving Day  11/27
             <br/>
             Bears at Lions
             <br/>
             CBS
            </p>
            <p>
             The Bears and Lions will square off in an NFC North divisional battle that could have playoff implications for one or both of these teams. Personally, Im betting on Matthew Stafford over Jay Cutler far more often than not.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Lions.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Thanksgiving Day  11/27
             <br/>
             Eagles at Cowboys
             <br/>
             FOX
            </p>
            <p>
             The high-scoring Eagles will look to exploit the mediocre Dallas Cowboys  who are looking to avoid becoming the first team in league history to go 8-8 in four straight seasons.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Cowboys.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Thanksgiving Day -11/27
             <br/>
             Seahawks at 49ers
             <br/>
             NBC
            </p>
            <p>
             Russell Wilson and Colin Kaepernick will go at it in this rematch of last seasons NFC title game. The outcome of this NFC West thriller could very well decide which team gets home-field advantage throughout the NFC portion of the playoffs at the very least.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Seahawks.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 14  12/04
             <br/>
             Cowboys at Bears
             <br/>
             NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             Maybe its me, but I can already see Chicagos Matt Forte scorching Dallas, both on the ground and out of the backfield as a receiver. I smell a possible 200 all purpose yards day waiting to happen for the versatile Forte.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Cowboys.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 15  12/11
             <br/>
             Cardinals at Rams
             <br/>
             NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             The Cardinals are one of my top picks to compete for a playoff berth despite playing in the same division as Seattle and Frisco. Theyll face a St. Louis Rams team that almost assuredly wont have much to play for at this point.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Cardinals.
            </p>
            <p align="center">
             Week 16  12/18
             <br/>
             Titans at Jaguars
             <br/>
             NFL Network
            </p>
            <p>
             While I suspect Jacksonville wont have much to play for in this contest, I suspect Jake Locker and the Tennessee Titans could have a potential playoff berth riding on the line.
            </p>
            <p>
             Bet The Titans.
            </p>
