             <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="name">
              Google prepares for one final Google+ push with Collections
             </h1>
             <div class="meta-info">
              <ul class="td-category">
               <li class="entry-category">
                <a href="http://www.filecluster.com/news/internet/" style="background-color:#4b94e2;">
                 Internet

              <time class="entry-date updated" datetime="2015-04-25T21:24:15+00:00" itemprop="dateCreated">
               Apr 25, 2015

              <div class="entry-comments-views">

             <a data-caption="" href="http://www.filecluster.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Google-Collections.png">
              <img alt="Google+ Collections" class="entry-thumb td-modal-image" height="357" itemprop="image" src="http://www.filecluster.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Google-Collections-668x357.png" title="Google+ Collections" width="668"/>
             </a>

            <div class="td-post-text-content">
             <p>
              Google+ is a very strange beast no matter which side you look at it from. For its part, Google has been extremely stubborn with the service and despite making considerable mistakes with it, it really wants to keep its social network going. On the side of the users, those who have stayed with Google+ for a long time have learned to love the service even with its many flaws.
             </p>
             <p>
              For the loyal ones that still use Google+, the company is now preparing a new Collections feature that will let users create curated content that anyone can follow. Think of it like Pinterest boards where the owner of the board adds interesting content and users can follow it to get updates whenever they please. This should help separate business and personal accounts or even ease your decisions over noteworthy content.
             </p>
             <p>
              This looks like one of Googles final attempts to promote Google+ as a viable social network where users can share interesting content and even spend some more time on its other services. I know I would definitely use Google+ more if I had more control over who can see what and I am sure others are the same. The company is apparently preparing Collections for a May release so we will see it soon enough.
             </p>
