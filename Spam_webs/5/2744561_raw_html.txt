           <div class="post-inner group">
            <h1 class="post-title">
             Facebook Should Now Merge FB Groups With WhatsApp
            </h1>
            <div class="clear">
            </div>
            <div class="entry ">
             <div class="entry-inner">
              <p>
               So
               <a href="http://www.nextbigwhat.com/whatsapp-web-client-297/" title="WhatsApp Launches Web Client For Desktops; Restricted To Chrome">
                WhatsApp has launched a web client
               </a>
               .
              </p>
              <p>
               What a move for the company which has time and again said that they are a mobile-only product and desktop makes no sense?
               <a href="http://cdn44.nextbigwhat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/whatsapp.jpg">
                <img alt="whatsapp" class="alignright size-full wp-image-133955" height="180" src="http://cdn44.nextbigwhat.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/whatsapp.jpg" width="230"/>
               </a>
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                WhatsApps Real Challenge..is?
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               The real challenge is not in the user base growth. But in growing the actual and engaged userbase.
              </p>
              <p>
               WhatsApp already has a huge engagement on its apps, but
               <a href="http://www.nextbigwhat.com/whatsapp-marketing-pricing-297/" title="How SMS Spammers Are Pricing WhatsApp Message Spams">
                spammers are taking over WhatsApp
               </a>
               andit almost
               <a href="http://www.nextbigwhat.com/orkut-product-lessons-297/" title="The Only Lesson Product Managers Can Learn From Orkut : Nobody Enters A Dirty Cafe">
                feels like Orkut
               </a>
               (to quickly share, NextBigWhat team has moved to Telegram for all internal chats/discussions  it is much cleaner and distraction free).
              </p>
              <p>
               The desktop client however, doesnt solve the spam issue  in fact, expect more bots on your way!
              </p>
              <p>
               Readfurther.
              </p>
              <h2>
               WhatsApp And Facebook Group:The Synergy
              </h2>
              <p>
               Both WhatsApp and Facebook are facing the same issues  i.e. lack of engaged and active/serious user base, which also translates to becoming the next Orkut/Myspace.
              </p>
              <p>
               They both can take separate strategy to grow the engaged userbase  but integrating FB groups and WhatsApp gives a huge leverage.
              </p>
              <p>
               I wont be surprised if Facebook announces an integration of Facebook group with WhatsApp. Its a two-way synergy and both need each other for growth. Facebook needs to drive more consumption of FB Groups
               <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
                <strong>
                 on mobile
                </strong>
               </span>
               so that they have enough room to play with when it comes to native ads and other monetizationoptions.
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Facebook GroupsAppIs A BIG Flop Show
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Facebook groups already have a good engagement score on the web, but the appsare a BIG flop show.
              </p>
              <p>
               For instance, Facebook groups has been
               <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.facebook.groups&amp;hl=en" target="_blank">
                installed
               </a>
               between 0.5  1 million times on Android,which is plain NOTHING (Third party and a really simple app, WhatsApp Wallpaper app
               <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.whatsapp.wallpaper&amp;hl=en" target="_blank">
                has
               </a>
               been downloaded over 50 million times!).
              </p>
              <p>
               If Facebook has to go big on native ads, Groups will play a significant role. Groups have a defined intent and are apt for targeted monetization.
              </p>
              <p>
               Facebook needs to stop focusing on Facebook groups app  the numbers are too small and insignificant. Convert these groups into WhatsApp groups and you are done! Most of the FB users are already on WhatsApp and the integration will simply drive more engagement.
              </p>
              <p>
               And before you do that, you need to get people used to
               <a href="http://www.nextbigwhat.com/whatsapp-web-client-297/" title="WhatsApp Launches Web Client For Desktops; Restricted To Chrome">
                WhatsApp web
               </a>
                for a seamless experience. I am guessing that within 6 months, an integration (between FB groups and WhatsApp) will be announced?
              </p>
              <p>
               <strong>
                Plus, Facebook At Work? SMEs ?
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               Imagine
               <em>
                <strong>
                 Facebook At Work
                </strong>
               </em>
               launching WhatsApp implementation!
              </p>
              <p>
               To me, its an ideal use-case forFacebook to get SMEs switch between Facebook and WhatsApp for conversations, engagement, and driving business.
              </p>
              <p>
               In fact,
               <a href="http://www.nextbigwhat.com/whatsapp-sales-tool-297/" title="Selling Via Text: WhatsApp An Emerging Marketing Tool">
                WhatsApp is an extremely popular tool among Indian SMEs
               </a>
               /
               <a href="http://www.nextbigwhat.com/delhi-police-whatsapp-helpline-297/" title="GovTech : Delhi Police Launches WhatsApp Helpline; Kanpur Police Launches SOS Security App [bigMobility]">
                government and police departments.
               </a>
               I am sure, its a global use-case to have organizations move to WhatsApp  but as a Facebook group (or
               <em>
                <strong>
                 Facebook At Work
                </strong>
               </em>
               group) driving signups across the two products : Facebook and WhatsApp.
              </p>
              <p>
               Facebook has done a terribly bad job of engaging with SMEs and maybe, WhatsApp is vehicle for them toget closer to $ME$. It is a successful product among SMEs, but needs to go beyond mobile forbroad adoption. And thats why you need the desktop client.
              </p>
              <p>
               At the end of the day, its all about engagement and capturing the OS mixes rather than sticking to mobile-only plan.
              </p>
              <p>
               After all,
               <strong>
                you got to do what you got to do.
               </strong>
              </p>
              <p>
               To those who have been talking/selling mobile-first and mobile-only paradigms, take a deep breath. The real worlddoesnt care so much.
              </p>
              <p>
               Thoughts?
              </p>
              <p>
               PS :
               <em>
                These are
                <strong>
                 Maybes
                </strong>
                and I could be 100% wrong!.I am no expert in social media, but these are perspectives and fun debates/discussions to have.
               </em>
               <em>
                If you have your own perspective and want to share with NextBigWhat audience, do connect with me (ashish@nextbigwhat.com).
               </em>
              </p>
