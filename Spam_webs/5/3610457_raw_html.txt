          <div class="entry-content hentry" id="ec-388951">
           <article>
            <span content="http://fansided.com/2013/12/05/steve-spurrier-defends-ohio-states-strength-schedule-takes-jab-florida-state/" itemprop="permalink">
            </span>
            <p>
            </p>
            <p>
             If one things has been proven throughout history in the great sport of college football, its that when you ask Steve Spurrier any sort of questionabout basically anythinghes going to give you an answer that will have you either talking, laughing, or both.
            </p>
            <p>
             Well, the Ol Ball Coach has done it again.
            </p>
            <p>
             The Coaches Poll is one factor that is used in the BCS system. Steve Spurrier, being the head coach of the South Carolina Gamecocks, obviously has a say in that poll. He was recently asked about ongoing debate about whether or not Ohio State should be able to go play for the national championship, even if they finish their season undefeated on Saturday, given how their Big Ten schedule is seen as weak.
            </p>
            <p>
             In Spurriers answer, he not only defended Ohio State,
             <a href="http://www.postandcourier.com/article/20131204/PC20/131209722">
              but he also took a jab at his former bitter rivals
             </a>
             and the current no. 1 team in the countrythe Florida State Seminoles:
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              You know what I think is interesting? Everybody is picking on Ohio State. Their schedule was ranked tougher than FSUs, Spurrier told The Post and Courier. Did you know that? Everybodys picking on how Ohio State hasnt played anybody. I said, Well, what about FSU? They beat Clemson, and thats about it.
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             This shouldnt be any surprise. This particular question was the perfect storm for Spurrier to rile everyone up, which he does better than not only any sports figure, but probably anyone in the history of humanity.
            </p>
