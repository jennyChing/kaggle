        <div class="article-content clearfix">
         <p>
          Back in February, Subway announced the good news that it would
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/214492/subway-pledges-to-stop-using-yoga-mat-material-in-its-bread/">
           stop using rubber yoga mat material
          </a>
          in its bread. On Friday, the
          <a href="http://bigstory.ap.org/article/subway-yoga-mat-chemical-almost-out-bread">
           AP
          </a>
          reported that the sandwich chain is making good on the promise, and that all its bread will be free of sneaker-sole, yoga-mat material next week.
         </p>
         <p>
          Good news, American fast food eaters! Unfortunately the not so good news is that if you eat basically anywhere elseBurger King, McDonalds, Stabucks, etcyoure still eating yoga mat.
         </p>
         <p>
          The chemical azodicarbonamidethe same rubbery substance used to make running sneaker soles and yoga matshas been sucked down the gullets of Americans en masse for years. Its how we get that fluffy, spongey bread.
         </p>
         <p>
          While it wasnt exactly new information food blogger
          <a href="http://www.deathandtaxesmag.com/214492/subway-pledges-to-stop-using-yoga-mat-material-in-its-bread/">
           Vani Hari
          </a>
          took the issue on inFebruary at her food blog Food Babe with a petition aimed at Subwaymaybe because they purport to be the healthy fast food option.
         </p>
         <p>
          Rather than trying to deny it or do some other kind of disingenuous damage control, Subway simply committed to getting the stuff out of its food.
         </p>
         <p>
          Subways marketing director acknowledged the power of the campaign and said the bread would be rubber free by net week. You see the social media traffic, and people are happy that were taking it out, but they want to know when were taking it out, Pace
          <a href="http://bigstory.ap.org/article/subway-yoga-mat-chemical-almost-out-bread">
           told Associated Press
          </a>
          . If there are people who have that hesitation, that hesitation is going to be removed.
         </p>
         <p>
          So there you have itif youre looking for a fast food option not literally made of sneakers, starting next week you can safely hit Subway.
         </p>
