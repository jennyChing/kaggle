          <p>
           <!--wsa:gooold-->
           Ever wonder what it would be like to live upside-down, sleep like a vampire or break out of prison? More and more hotels are breaking out of the tradition of cookie-cutter rooms and are moving toward unique one-of-a-kind artist-driven room designs. Here are sixteen such rooms that span the spectrum from clever and kitchy to sexy and surreal. Even though some may look uncomfortable, unusual or downright freakish at least they arent among the
           <a href="http://weburbanist.com/2007/12/20/7-of-the-smallest-hotels-and-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-pipe-rooms-to-capsule-hotels/" title="7 of the Worlds Smallest Hotels and Hotel Rooms">
            7 tiniest hotel rooms in the world
           </a>
           . See more here:
           <a href="http://weburbanist.com/themed-castle-and-art-hotels/" target="_blank">
            70+ Amazing Theme, Castle, Jail and Art Hotels
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-429">
           </span>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Vampire Hotel Room with Coffins" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/vampire-hotel-room-with-coffins.jpg"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/vampire-hotel-room-with-coffins.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Watching horror movies one has to be a little bit curious about whether it is comfortable for vampires to sleep in attic coffins. Now those who are so inclined (or who prefer partying at night and sleeping during the day) can experience this vampire room first hand at the
           <a href="http://www.propeller-island.com/" title="Propeller Island Hotel">
            Propeller Island Hotel
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Fully Mirrored Art Hotel Room" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/fully-mirrored-art-hotel-room.jpg"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/fully-mirrored-art-hotel-room.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Prefer something less kitchy and more romantic? Perhaps the mirror room of the same hotel is for you. Mirrors are typically considered an erotic addition to a hotel room but this example carries the principle to the extreme with wall-to-wall and floor-to-ceiling mirrors spanning the diamond-shaped space.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Art Concept Hotel Room with Erotic Dance Cages" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/art-concept-hotel-room-with-erotic-dance-cages.jpg"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/art-concept-hotel-room-with-erotic-dance-cages.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Mirrors too cliche and traditional for your tastes? How about a room with elegant furnishings and also complete with not one but two cages. Perhaps the most amazing (and strange) aspect of this particular room is the option to lift curtains and put on shows for ones neighboring guests.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Jail Cell Bizarre Art Hotel Room" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/jail-cell-bizarre-art-hotel-room.jpg"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/jail-cell-bizarre-art-hotel-room.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Up for more of an adventure? One room is outfitted as a surrealistically colored jail cell reminiscent of a cartoon lockup. At the end of the night, guests are encouraged to break out of the room by punching and escaping through a hole in one false wall.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Hotel Room with Crazy Symbols" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/hotel-room-with-crazy-symbols.jpg"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/hotel-room-with-crazy-symbols.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           The symbol room might be more appropriate for the reserved and intellectual clientele. With over 200 unique wood panels, this room provides endless black-and-white food for thought as well as arrangeable furniture objects that can be turned over and moved to create personalized configurations.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Kunsthotel Art Hotel in Berlin Germany" height="417" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/kunsthotel-art-hotel-in-berlin-germany.jpg" width="468"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/kunsthotel-art-hotel-in-berlin-germany.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>

          <p>
           The
           <a href="http://www.luise-berlin.com/" title="KunstHotel">
            Arte Luise Kunsthotel
           </a>
           (or Art Hotel) is also located in Berlin but takes less of a themed approach, with a variety of rooms that are the unique realization of singular artists visions. As you can see from the images above, some of them are abstract, others playful and still others simply bizarre. Best of all, each time someone stays in one of these creative rooms the artist gets a percentage of the payment.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Creative Canadian Artistic Hotel Rooms" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/creative-canadian-artistic-hotel-rooms.jpg"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/creative-canadian-artistic-hotel-rooms.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           Like the Kunsthotel, the
           <a href="http://www.gladstonehotel.com/artistdesignedrooms.htm" title="Gladstone Hotel">
            Gladstone Hotel
           </a>
           in Toronto, Canada relies on one artist per room with remarkably varied results. One room (left) purports to simulate a meeting place for a lesbian separatists commune, another celebrates Canadian culture and kitsch (upper right) while yet another is simply the artists meditation on the history of Victorian-era English culture (lower right).
          </p>
          <p>
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Seattle Max Art and Photography Hotel" src="http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/seattle-max-art-and-photography-hotel.jpg"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://weburbanist.com/2008/01/21/16-of-the-most-creative-and-sexy-hotel-rooms-in-the-world-from-upside-down-to-local-art/&amp;media=http://weburbanist.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/seattle-max-art-and-photography-hotel.jpg&amp;description=16 of the Most Creative &amp; Weird Hotel Rooms in the World')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Max Hotel in Seattle likewise boasts the work of artists but in this case emphasizes local art and is not limited to painting and sculpture. Shown above, local art is placed in rooms while photography and other media are applied both within rooms as well as in halls and other common spaces. A compilation of all of the art in the hotel is also available for purchase.
          </p>

           For those who enjoy art but arent sure about sleeping in it, the
           <a href="http://www.naoshima-is.co.jp/english/index.html" title="Benesse Art Site">
            Benesse Art Site Naoshima
           </a>
           in Japan provides a healthy compromise: a hotel attached to a beautifully constructed gallery of modern art. Visitors can interact in common eating and museum spaces while retiring to their own more conventional hotel rooms with sparse and minimalistic art. Looking for a more exotic destination?
