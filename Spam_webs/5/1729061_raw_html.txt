        <h1>

          Make a Dollar Store Fall Wreath
         </a>
        </h1>
       </div>
       <div class="post-date1">

         Oct

      </div>
      <div class="metadata">

       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/author/breanna/" rel="author" title="Posts by Breanna">

      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-finished/" rel="attachment wp-att-2294">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2294" height="399" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-finished-600x399.jpg" title="wreath finished" width="600"/>
       </a>
       When I came home with a few bags of dollar store supplies for fall decorating, I didnt have anything specific in mind. I mostly just laid it all out on the table and stared at it until I thought of a few things. This wreath was the first thing to come to mind. The packages of colorful autumn leaves were just begging me to put them into a wreath and hang them on my front door! This project took maybe 45 minutes total and was really very satisfying!
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-supplies/" rel="attachment wp-att-2295">
        <img alt="" class="size-medium wp-image-2295 alignleft" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-supplies-300x199.jpg" title="wreath supplies" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-cut-letter/" rel="attachment wp-att-2299">
        <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2299" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-cut-letter-300x199.jpg" title="wreath cut letter" width="300"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <p>
       Supplies:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        old pizza board or cardboard cut into circle- on hand
       </li>
       <li>
        fall leaves- $1 for a package of 10 in one color/shape. (I used about half of each package from all 4 varieties)
       </li>
       <li>
        container filler- $1 (very little was used in this project)
       </li>
       <li>
        hot glue gun/glue sticks- on hand
       </li>
       <li>
        manilla folder or cardstock- on hand
       </li>
       <li>
        scrapbook paper- on hand
       </li>
       <li>
        optional: electronic cutting machine
       </li>
      </ul>
      <div>
       Total cost- under $5
      </div>
      <div>
       Originally I planned to use a foam wreath form for this project, but after playing around with that a bit, I didnt like it at all! It was late at night (as you can see from the dimly lit photos) and I just stood there looking around my house hoping to find a solution. Slow motion AHA! moment as I saw the pizza board from dinner earlier that night and I was off and gluing!
      </div>
      <div>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-cut-leaves/" rel="attachment wp-att-2296">
        <img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-2296" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-cut-leaves-300x199.jpg" title="wreath cut leaves" width="300"/>
       </a>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-glue-leaves/" rel="attachment wp-att-2297">
        <img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-2297" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-glue-leaves-300x199.jpg" title="wreath glue leaves" width="300"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <div>
       I started by cutting the stems off of the leaves. This required slightly stronger than normal scissors as theres a bit of wire in there. Next I glued each leaf down around the outside of the circle, then another layer until the cardboard was covered. Heres how it looked at that point:
      </div>
      <div>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-leaves-done/" rel="attachment wp-att-2298">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2298" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-leaves-done-300x199.jpg" title="wreath leaves done" width="300"/>
       </a>
       I thought that was actually lovely on its own, but felt like it still needed something else. I decided to use my new cricut (hooray!!) to cut a large N (for our last name) out of cardstock and a piece of scrapbook paper that was similar in color to the cranberry container filler. I used a glue stick to adhere the two Ns together (for stability).
      </div>
      <div>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-glue-beads/" rel="attachment wp-att-2300">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2300" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-glue-beads-300x199.jpg" title="wreath glue beads" width="300"/>
       </a>
       Next I glued the little cranberries down onto the N using my hot glue gun. If I was more of a craft perfectionist, Id have chosen an N with all straight lines instead of curved corners to look a bit more crisp. I think it looks pretty great though, even slightly messy! The I put a bunch of hot glue on the back of the N and slapped it onto the wreath. I was worried that it wouldnt be quite secure enough or that the little berries would fall off, but when I was hanging it the next morning I dropped it and it hit the ground pretty hard and was completely in tact!
      </div>
      <div>
       <a href="http://dollarstoremom.com/2011/10/make-a-dollar-store-fall-wreath/wreath-finished/" rel="attachment wp-att-2294">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-2294" height="199" src="//dollarstoremom.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/wreath-finished-300x199.jpg" title="wreath finished" width="300"/>
