      <h1>
       Dell announces new Latitude Rugged Extreme series laptops
      </h1>
      <div class="clearfix">
       <span class="pull-left">
        By
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/author/nick-lavars/" rel="author">
         Nick Lavars
        </a>
        - April 2, 2014
       </span>
       <a class="picture-count" href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures">
        12 Pictures
       </a>
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="inline-item">
      <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures">
       <img alt="The Latitude Rugged Extreme devices are built to withstand harsh outdoor conditions" class="hero-img" data-at2x="http://images.gizmag.com/hero/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme@2x.jpg" src="http://images.gizmag.com/hero/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme.jpg"/>
      </a>
      <div class="caption caption--hero">
       <p>
        The Latitude Rugged Extreme devices are built to withstand harsh outdoor conditions
       </p>
       <a class="gallery-link" href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures">
        Image Gallery
       </a>
       (12 images)
      </div>
     </div>
     <div class="article-body">
      <p>
       Dell has today announced two ruggedized additions to its
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-e4310-business-laptop/15003/" target="_blank">
        Latitude
       </a>
       series. Comprising a 12-inch laptop-tablet convertible and a 14-inch notebook, the Latitude Rugged Extreme series is built to withstand harsh outdoor conditions and geared towards professionals working in military, public safety and first response.
      </p>
      <ul class="thumbnails">
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures#1">
         <img alt="The Latitude Rugged Extreme series is geared towards professionals working in military, public safety and first response" src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme-0.jpg" title="The Latitude Rugged Extreme series is geared towards professionals working in military, public safety and first response"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures#2">
         <img alt="The Latitude Rugged Extreme devices are constructed using impact-resistant ultra-polymers and magnesium alloy " src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme-1.jpg" title="The Latitude Rugged Extreme devices are constructed using impact-resistant ultra-polymers and magnesium alloy "/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures#3">
         <img alt="Both devices are powered by Intel's Haswell CPU with memory options ranging between 4 and 16 GB" src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme-2.jpg" title="Both devices are powered by Intel's Haswell CPU with memory options ranging between 4 and 16 GB"/>
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures#4">
         <img alt="Sealed ports combine with Dell's fourth-generation QuadCool thermal management system to enable use in temperatures ranging from -20 to 145 degrees Fahrenheit (-29 to 63C)" src="http://images.gizmag.com/gallery_tn/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme-5.jpg" title="Sealed ports combine with Dell's fourth-generation QuadCool thermal management system to enable use in temperatures ranging from -20 to 145 degrees Fahrenheit (-29 to 63C)"/>
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Both devices are constructed using impact-resistant ultra-polymers and magnesium alloy and are, according to Dell, drop-tested from a height of 6 ft (1.82 m). Sealed ports combine with Dell's fourth-generation QuadCool thermal management system to enable use in temperatures ranging from -20 to 145 degrees Fahrenheit (-29 to 63C).
      </p>
      <p>
       "These new rugged form factors have been developed with feedback from our user base in the field," Patrick Seidensticker, Director of Rugged Mobility Marketing at Dell tells Gizmag. "These devices are dust-proof and rated for use in wind and rain."
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="inline-item">
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures#8" target="_blank">
        <img alt="" src="http://images.gizmag.com/inline/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme-10.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       Both devices are powered by Intel's Haswell CPU, with memory options ranging between 4 and 16 GB and up to 512 GB of storage. They both feature a Full HD webcam, while the 12-inch convertible sports an 8-megapixel bottom camera with flash.
      </p>
      <p>
       Dell hasn't been afraid to get imaginative with its convertible design in the past. The
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-xt3/19455/" target="_blank">
        Latitude XT3
       </a>
       released back in 2011 used a bi-directional hinge to rotate the display and switch between tablet and laptop form. With the Latitude Rugged Extreme convertible, Dell is taking an equally unique, but perhaps a more straight-lined approach. The LCD display being held in a frame while a "turbine flip" side hinge inverts the screen as the notebook closes.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
      <div class="inline-item">
       <a href="http://www.gizmag.com/dell-latitude-rugged-extreme/31381/pictures#10" target="_blank">
        <img alt="" src="http://images.gizmag.com/inline/dell_latitude_rugged_extreme-13.jpg"/>
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The 12-inch display also features resistive multi-touch functionality, designed to recognize the user's gestures even when wearing thick gloves.
      </p>
      <p>
       Pricing for the Latitude 12 Rugged Extreme will start at US$3,649, with the device available from early May. The Latitude 14 will be available from mid-May with prices starting at $3,499.
      </p>
      <p>
       Source:
       <a href="http://www.dell.com/learn/us/en/555/rugged" target="_blank">
        Dell
       </a>
      </p>
