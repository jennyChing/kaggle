          <h2>
           <a href="http://dailytwocents.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/1024px-Kandersteg_area_in_summer_2011_7-300x2001.jpg">
            <img alt="autotrain Adventure Travel" class=" size-medium wp-image-179623 alignright" height="200" src="//dailytwocents.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/1024px-Kandersteg_area_in_summer_2011_7-300x2001-300x200.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
           Auto Train Adventure Travel in Switzerland
          </h2>
          <p>
           Another great rail travel experience that I have had was back in 1970 on an autotrain from Switzerland to Italy. What an auto train adventure I had that day.
          </p>
          <p>
           A friend and I had a four-day holiday and time to get away from our duties in the US Army stationed in West Germany during the cold war. We drove from Rheinland-Pfalz into Switzerland to the city of Bern. InBern, we found some other Americans that told us about this great small guesthouse in the city of Kandersteg. They also told us about the fun they had hiking in the Alps and visiting the city of Bern, Switzerland.
          </p>
          <p>
           <a href="http://dailytwocents.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/BLS_Autoverlad-300x2031.jpg">
            <img alt="Autotrain Adventure TRavel" class=" size-medium wp-image-179626 alignright" height="203" src="//dailytwocents.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/BLS_Autoverlad-300x2031-300x203.jpg" width="300"/>
           </a>
           They also told us about the fun autotrain adventure they had taking the train from Italy up into the Swiss Alps to the same city of Kandersteg. We drove, after lunch, from Bern up to Kandersteg and got a room in the hotel we had also heard about.
          </p>
          <p>
           We booked the autotrain for the next morning. Our planwas to get off the train near Lugano, to drive to Como and then back up into the Alps through the Brenner Pass in Austria. Then we would drive back west again through Lichtenstein, Switzerland and into Germany to return home.
          </p>
          <p>
           The trip truly was an adventure, to be able to see the beauty of the Swiss Alps without driving, yet having our car once we reached the other side. While on the autotrain I wished that there was transportation like this in America too.
          </p>

             Swiss Auto Train
            </strong>
           </a>
          </p>
