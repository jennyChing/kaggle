         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Quick SEO Wins: 31 Ideas From The Experts
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-07-09T09:33:42+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
           July 9, 2014
          </time>
           by
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.mattersolutions.com.au/blog/author/nemekn/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             Nemek
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-comments-link">

            14 Comments
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <p>
          When was the last time you started a new SEO campaign? New projects are often exciting, but theres one thing many SEOs dont look forward to. The mismatch between expectations and actual progress of many search campaigns.
         </p>
         <p>
          Even when clients are well informed about the long-term nature of most search strategies, secretly they hope
          <em>
           this new agency
          </em>
          will beat its own forecasts.
         </p>
         <p>
          This creates a gap between expectations and reality:
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11343" src="http://cdn.mattersolutions.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2014/05/seo-results-vs-expectations_600x400.jpg"/>
          <br/>
          <span id="contrib-1">
          </span>
          <br/>
          Managing expectations is crucial, but smart SEOs have learned how to bridge that gap in a different way. How? By having a list of quick wins that can be used early on in the campaign.
         </p>
         <p>
          We asked 31 experienced SEOs about quick wins they like to use at the start of a new campaign. This is what they said.
         </p>
         <h3>
          1. Reclaim link equity
         </h3>
         <p>
          What I love to do, aside from the obvious title tags, meta descriptions, and page content, is to
          <strong>
           look at 404 Not Found pages that have inbound links
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          I do this by using a tool like MajesticSEO to get a list of all the pages on the client site that have one or more incoming links, and then crawling this list with a tool like Screaming Frog. Then you can see which of those pages with inbound links serves a 404 error message, and youll of course want to 301-redirect those to the most relevant working page. A fast and easy way to reclaim some lost link juice.
         </p>

         <h3>
          2. Guest blogging with a twist
         </h3>
         <p>
          Quick wins in SEO are hard to come by. Especially when theyre actually not too good to be true. Having said that, I have a few up my sleeve for the start of each campaign to get things going.
         </p>
         <p>
          One in particular that isnt so much of a quick win, but it certainly gives you a ton more bang for your buck, is through
          <strong>
           guest blogging on websites that get heavily syndicated
          </strong>
          . When Im looking to raise the ratio of links vs. linking root domains, I always look at publishing content on websites that get their content syndicated across a few other domains so that I can cash in on a few branded links from a number of different domains. Perfect for a new domain, but just make sure youre sticking to branded links.
         </p>
         <p>
          One example in the SEO industry is Moz. Whenever I publish a post to Moz, I always get a ton of links from blogs that republish their content, which is great.
         </p>

         <h3>
          3. Activate social: ask for feedback
         </h3>
         <p>
          My favourite quick win for any SEO campaign is to ask for feedback.
         </p>
         <p>
          If theres a new site,
          <strong>
           ask what users think, and socialize the question, asking people to retweet it
          </strong>
          . If theres a redesign, the same thing applies.
         </p>
         <p>
          If nothings really new but youre starting something, just ask people a question about something that will enable them to socialize your site and get eyes on it.
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           Ask if they think its time for a redesign.
          </li>
          <li>
           Ask if the site looks OK for people all over the world.
          </li>
          <li>
           Ask if its slow on a mobile device.
          </li>
         </ul>
         <p>
          This is all assuming that part of your SEO campaign will involve social, of course (and hopefully it does) so its a great way to start getting more attention for the site.
         </p>

         <h3>
          4. Boost pages that are close to ranking
         </h3>
         <p>
          The best quick win is definitely internal linking. First, identify pages that are ranking on the bottom of the first page or on the second or third page. Then go to pages on your site that are authoritative and build internal links to those pages. If you cant build links directly to those pages, make sure they theyre only 2-3 clicks away from your homepage.
         </p>
         <p>
          Also remove any internal links coming FROM the pages you want to rank to pages that already have authority. Remember:
          <strong>
           you want authority to flow FROM your authoritative pages TO the pages you actually want to rank
          </strong>
          .
         </p>

         <h3>
          5. Track down duplication issues
         </h3>
         <p>
          My favourite checks at the start of the campaign are of the
          <strong>
           index
          </strong>
          and
          <strong>
           GWT
          </strong>
          .
         </p>
         <p>
          1. I do the usual site: search  look for unusual patterns or anything that stands out
          <br/>

         <h3>
          6. Make sure youre not working on a penalized site
         </h3>
         <p>
          Ive seen a number of business owners whose websites were never able to rank because right from the start they were dealing with penalty issues or problems with the Panda algorithm. If you have purchased a new domain, be sure to
          <strong>
           check Webmaster Tools for manual actions
          </strong>
          to make sure that the previous owner of the domain didnt get the site penalized. When you launch, be sure that your site doesnt have Panda issues. Here are a couple of things to look for:
         </p>
         <ul>
          <li>
           How much of your site contains content that is copied from elsewhere? Make sure that you have unique text on your homepage and
           <strong>
            dont use the same blurb that you have used in all of your local citations
           </strong>
           and other mentions of your business. If you are using content that is found on other sites of yours then use a canonical tag to tell Google which copy to index. Or, if you are using content that is found on other peoples sites then noindex it. If Google sees that the majority of your site is duplicate content this can be an indication of low quality in the eyes of Panda. For example, a lot of veterinary sites will have hundreds of articles on the site that are provided by a third party. Those articles exist on hundreds of other veterinarians sites. They need to be noindexed or else Google will see that the majority of your website is copied and this can cause Panda to lose trust in your entire site.
          </li>
          <li>
           Do you have thin pages on the site? An example would be a templated page that is the same for each one of hundreds of cities that you service with only a few words on each page that are unique. Those need to be either noindexed or a canonical tag should be used. Similarly, if you have an e-commerce site, be sure that you are not trying to get duplicate pages in the index. Google doesnt need to index every size and color variation of every product.
          </li>
         </ul>

         <h3>
          9. Check you havent shot yourself in the foot
         </h3>
         <p>
          While each site is different, and there are varying levels of complexity associated with audits, I have a few things that I always check that can provide quick wins right off the bat. They are to review:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           The robots.txt file
          </li>
          <li>
           Meta robots commands
          </li>
          <li>
           OSE Top Pages report
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          <strong>
           I always start by reviewing the robots.txt file
          </strong>
          . I make sure that were not blocking bots from accessing important parts of the site. It happens more than youd think and its an easy fix with big implications. If content isnt accessible to search engines, theres almost no way it will rank, unless youre a government entity and there are a ton of links pointing to your blocked pages.
         </p>
         <p>
          I then do a crawl of the site using
          <a href="http://www.screamingfrog.co.uk/seo-spider/">
           Screaming Frog
          </a>
          where Ill look for pages with the meta robots noindex/nofollow commands where they shouldnt be. It isnt surprising to find a noindex command accidentally carried over from a dev environment. Similar to the previous accessibility issue, if youre telling search engines to not index your page, youre going to have a hard time ranking well.
         </p>
         <p>
          A third quick win that I like to check for is to review the
          <a href="http://www.opensiteexplorer.org/pages.html?site=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.msn.com">
           top pages report
          </a>
          on Open Site Explorer and look for pages that have external links but report an error (such as a 4xx or 302). These are pages that already have equity and value associated with them, but that equity is being abandoned on a dead URL. These URLs can be 301 redirected to key pages to make sure we dont lose that link equity.
         </p>

         <h3>
          27. Combine keyword and landing page metrics for priceless insights
         </h3>
         <p>
          Its common for an SEO campaign to take a while through ideation, development, implementation, then for Google to ultimately find it and value it. Add more routine optimization on top of that, and you could be waiting months for results  either good or bad. But if you take what you already have developed and quickly optimize (the last step), you can get a nice, quick bump.
         </p>
         <p>
          I start many new clients this way, by
          <strong>
           searching out the low-hanging fruit like vital keywords that are just out of the 6-15 position
          </strong>
          . Run keyword research and competitive reports (
          <a href="http://semrush.com/">
           SEMrush
          </a>
          is great for this), and
          <strong>
           gather up keywords and metrics like estimated searches and competition
          </strong>
          . Then run a fresh rank report including currently associated landing pages (
          <a href="http://www.advancedwebranking.com/">
           Advanced Web Rankings
          </a>
          is my tool of choice for this quick pull). Also get
          <strong>
           landing page metrics like traffic, revenue, and backlink information
          </strong>
          to give you more insight into the page value (analytics and a tool Ive been using lately,
          <a href="http://urlprofiler.com/">
           URL Profiler
          </a>
          , is very good for this as well).
         </p>
         <p>
          Next, filter out any term thats not in the 6-15 position,
          <strong>
           review all the data, and make a judgement on what keywords and pages to focus on
          </strong>
          . Sometimes a simple content tweak or title tag update is enough to push you into the top 5 for a lot more qualified traffic. Total estimated time if youre drinking a lot of coffee: 1-2 hours.
         </p>

          One of the easiest quick wins I do at the start of every campaign is to
          <strong>
           find out if the client has any alternative or legacy domains that they own
          </strong>
          . A lot of the time if a client has re-branded or consolidated their website from multiple domains or even built microsites for individual campaigns that are no longer managed, there will be ranking signals associated with those legacy domains that can be valuable for our SEO efforts.
         </p>
         <p>
          Auditing any previous web assets and providing recommendations for redirecting or otherwise consolidating those relevancy signals is a quick, easy way to accomplish something tangible while youre doing your initial keyword research and other preliminary strategy development.
         </p>
