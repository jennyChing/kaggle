     <p>
      <b>
       The Used
      </b>
      is an American
      <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">
       rock
      </a>
      band from
      <a href="/wiki/Orem,_Utah" title="Orem, Utah">
       Orem, Utah
      </a>
      , formed in 2001. The group consists of lead vocalist
      <a href="/wiki/Bert_McCracken" title="Bert McCracken">
       Bert McCracken
      </a>
      , guitarist
      <a href="/wiki/Quinn_Allman" title="Quinn Allman">
       Quinn Allman
      </a>
      , bassist
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jepha_Howard" title="Jepha Howard">
       Jeph Howard
      </a>
      , and drummer
      <a href="/wiki/Dan_Whitesides" title="Dan Whitesides">
       Dan Whitesides
      </a>
      who replaced founding member
      <a href="/wiki/Branden_Steineckert" title="Branden Steineckert">
       Branden Steineckert
      </a>
      in 2006. Since early 2015, Allman has been on what is said to be a year long hiatus; the band recruited guitarist
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Justin_Shekoski" title="Justin Shekoski">
       Justin Shekoski
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Saosin" title="Saosin">
       Saosin
      </a>
      to perform with them on tour.
     </p>
     <p>
      The group signed to
      <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">
       Reprise Records
      </a>
      and rose to fame in June 2002 after releasing their
      <a href="/wiki/The_Used_(album)" title="The Used (album)">
       self-titled debut album
      </a>
      . They followed up with their second album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/In_Love_and_Death" title="In Love and Death">
        In Love and Death
       </a>
      </i>
      , in September 2004 and their third album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Lies_for_the_Liars" title="Lies for the Liars">
        Lies for the Liars
       </a>
      </i>
      , in May 2007.
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Shallow_Believer" title="Shallow Believer">
        Shallow Believer
       </a>
      </i>
      , an
      <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">
       EP
      </a>
      that featured most of the band's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-side" title="B-side">
       B-sides
      </a>
      , was released in February 2008. They spent 2008 working on their fourth studio album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Artwork_(album)" title="Artwork (album)">
        Artwork
       </a>
      </i>
      , which was released in August 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A fifth album, titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_(The_Used_album)" title="Vulnerable (The Used album)">
        Vulnerable
       </a>
      </i>
      , was released in March 2012 through the independent label
      <a href="/wiki/Hopeless_Records" title="Hopeless Records">
       Hopeless Records
      </a>
      . In summer 2013 they released another EP titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Ocean_of_the_Sky" title="The Ocean of the Sky">
        The Ocean of the Sky
       </a>
      </i>
      . Their sixth album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Imaginary_Enemy_(album)" title="Imaginary Enemy (album)">
        Imaginary Enemy
       </a>
      </i>
      , released in April 2014.
     </p>
     <p>
      Albums by The Used have achieved
      <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">
       gold and platinum
      </a>
      statuses in many countries worldwide. In 2008, the band has sold more than 3 million records worldwide.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-reuters-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_and_Demos_from_the_Basement_.282001.29">
       Formation and
       <i>
        Demos from the Basement
       </i>
       (2001)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Used&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation and Demos from the Basement (2001)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The Used was founded in January 2001 with the recruitment of members
      <a href="/wiki/Branden_Steineckert" title="Branden Steineckert">
       Branden Steineckert
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Quinn_Allman" title="Quinn Allman">
       Quinn Allman
      </a>
      and Jeph Howard.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-mtv-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      They had music already prepared, but did not have lyrics and required a vocalist. They held auditions at Howard's residence,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but the band was not satisfied with any of the vocalists, describing the auditions as "terrible". Allman then asked
      <a href="/wiki/Bert_McCracken" title="Bert McCracken">
       Bert McCracken
      </a>
      if he was interested in joining the band. McCracken was impressed with the material the band had created. After being given music that the band had written, McCracken wrote the lyrics to what would become "Maybe Memories" and returned the next day to record a demo version of the song. McCracken was then officially confirmed as being the band's vocalist. Throughout all of this, the band members suffered from poverty, homelessness, and substance abuse, panhandling for change in order to buy food.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The members decided upon the name "Used" after friends claimed they felt "used" when contact with the band members waned as they became more engrossed in the project.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_3-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-mtv-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The Used continued to write songs together and recorded a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demo_album" title="Demo album">
       demo album
      </a>
      in drummer Steineckert's bedroom titled
      <i>
       Demos from the Basement
      </i>
      . Steineckert sent their songs to producer
      <a href="/wiki/John_Feldmann" title="John Feldmann">
       John Feldmann
      </a>
      from the
      <a href="/wiki/Ska" title="Ska">
       ska
      </a>
      group
      <a href="/wiki/Goldfinger_(band)" title="Goldfinger (band)">
       Goldfinger
      </a>
      . On hearing the song "A Box Full of Sharp Objects", Feldman helped The Used find a record label. Feldmann flew the band to Los Angeles, taking them out of their home state for the first time in their lives. They played for various record executives, but received little interest. Eventually, after sending copies of their demo to many record companies, they began to receive offers. They decided to sign to Reprise Records in late 2001.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="The_Used_and_In_Love_and_Death_.282002.E2.80.932005.29">
       <i>
        The Used
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        In Love and Death
       </i>
       (20022005)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Used&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Used and In Love and Death (20022005)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In 2002, it was discovered that a
      <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">
       Boston
      </a>
      band had already trademarked the name "Used." The band decided to add "The" to their name, thus deciding "The Used". Their self-titled debut album, produced by John Feldmann, was released on June 25, 2002 to critical acclaim. The album featured four singles: "A Box Full of Sharp Objects", "
      <a href="/wiki/The_Taste_of_Ink" title="The Taste of Ink">
       The Taste of Ink
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/Buried_Myself_Alive" title="Buried Myself Alive">
       Buried Myself Alive
      </a>
      ", and "
      <a href="/wiki/Blue_and_Yellow" title="Blue and Yellow">
       Blue and Yellow
      </a>
      ", with the latter three entering the charts. The album has been certified gold.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_7-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-riaa-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Used went on to play
      <a href="/wiki/Warped_Tour" title="Warped Tour">
       Warped Tour
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ozzfest" title="Ozzfest">
       Ozzfest
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Projekt_Revolution" title="Projekt Revolution">
       Projekt Revolution
      </a>
      , as well as
      <a href="/wiki/Box_Car_Racer" title="Box Car Racer">
       Box Car Racer
      </a>
      's first and only tour. During these tours, they employed a touring guitarist named Greg Bester. He was later forced to return to his home country when he couldn't get a working
      <a href="/wiki/Visa_(document)" title="Visa (document)">
       visa
      </a>
      . The band received much recognition when Bert McCracken dated
      <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Osbourne" title="Kelly Osbourne">
       Kelly Osbourne
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In July 2003, The Used released a compilation CD/DVD combo package titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Maybe_Memories" title="Maybe Memories">
        Maybe Memories
       </a>
      </i>
      . The CD features live, rare, previously unreleased tracks, and demo material, while the DVD features the history of the band, member bios, and part of a live concert.
      <i>
       Maybe Memories
      </i>
      has been certified platinum.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_7-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-riaa-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      In 2004, lead vocalist McCracken's ex-girlfriend died of a drug overdose while pregnant with his child.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Their second studio album was thus named
      <i>
       In Love and Death
      </i>
      , released as a reference to the tragedy McCracken dealt with during the recording. The song "Hard to Say" was a reference to McCracken's ex-girlfriend; although the song was written years earlier, its dedication was only then declared.
      <i>
       In Love and Death
      </i>
      received very positive reviews
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and spawned three singles: "
      <a href="/wiki/Take_It_Away_(The_Used_song)" title="Take It Away (The Used song)">
       Take It Away
      </a>
      ", "
      <a href="/wiki/All_That_I%27ve_Got_(The_Used_song)" title="All That I've Got (The Used song)">
       All That I've Got
      </a>
      " (which earned them their first
      <a href="/wiki/2005_MuchMusic_Video_Awards" title="2005 MuchMusic Video Awards">
       MuchMusic Video Award
      </a>
      nomination), and "
      <a href="/wiki/I_Caught_Fire" title="I Caught Fire">
       I Caught Fire
      </a>
      ", before being re-released in 2005. "
      <a href="/wiki/Under_Pressure" title="Under Pressure">
       Under Pressure
      </a>
      " (a
      <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">
       Queen
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">
       David Bowie
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">
       cover song
      </a>
      performed with
      <a href="/wiki/My_Chemical_Romance" title="My Chemical Romance">
       My Chemical Romance
      </a>
      ) was featured as a bonus track on the re-release. The album has been certified gold.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_7-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-riaa-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After finishing up an international tour for the album, The Used took about eight months off before starting to work on their next album.
     </p>
     <h3>

      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

     <p>
      On September 12, 2006, The Used announced that Steineckert was no longer a member of the band.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-branden_11-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-branden-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-branden_11-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-branden-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Dan_Whitesides" title="Dan Whitesides">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/The_New_Transit_Direction" title="The New Transit Direction">
       The New Transit Direction
      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/Rancid_(band)" title="Rancid (band)">
       Rancid
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      On February 6, 2007, The Used released a live double-album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Berth_(album)" title="Berth (album)">
        Berth
       </a>
      </i>
      , which includes a DVD that features video of their performance at the
      <a href="/wiki/Taste_of_Chaos_2005" title="Taste of Chaos 2005">
       Taste of Chaos 2005
      </a>
      tour in
      <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">
       Vancouver
      </a>
      and a CD that features it on audio. The DVD also includes a biography of the band and four music videos. The biography depicts the band after the release of
      <i>
       Maybe Memories
      </i>
      , leading into the creation of
      <i>
       In Love and Death
      </i>
      . The sale of
      <i>
       Berth
      </i>
      lead it to be certified as gold.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-riaa_7-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-riaa-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Used spent most of the early part of 2007 on the
      <a href="/wiki/Taste_of_Chaos_2007" title="Taste of Chaos 2007">
       Taste of Chaos
      </a>
      tour before their third album,
      <i>
       Lies for the Liars
      </i>
      , was released May 22, 2007. They headlined the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Give_It_a_Name" title="Give It a Name">
       Give It a Name
      </a>
      festival. In June, they played the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MuchMusic_Video_Awards" title="MuchMusic Video Awards">

      </a>

      <a href="/wiki/2007_MuchMusic_Video_Awards" title="2007 MuchMusic Video Awards">
       second nomination
      </a>
      for Best International Group Video, for "
      <a href="/wiki/The_Bird_and_the_Worm" title="The Bird and the Worm">
       The Bird and the Worm
      </a>
      ". The Used were scheduled to play Warped Tour in June and July, in support of their album, but were forced to cancel due to McCracken's
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vocal_cord" title="Vocal cord">
       vocal cord
      </a>
      surgery.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reading_festival" title="Reading festival">
       Reading
      </a>
      and Leeds festivals in August, and went on to tour the United States in September.
      <i>
       Lies for the Liars
      </i>
      charted in the top five in more than seven countries,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-reuters-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]

      <a href="/wiki/Pretty_Handsome_Awkward" title="Pretty Handsome Awkward">
       Pretty Handsome Awkward
      </a>
      ", and "Paralyzed". Although Whitesides had been chosen as the new drummer,
      <a href="/wiki/Dean_Butterworth" title="Dean Butterworth">
       Dean Butterworth
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Good_Charlotte" title="Good Charlotte">
       Good Charlotte
      </a>
      recorded drums for the album. The six-month recording process was the longest The Used had ever undertaken.
      <i>
       Lies for the Liars
      </i>
      generally received mixed to positive reviews.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Originally,
      <i>
       Lies for the Liars
      </i>
      was expected to be a double-album. Nineteen songs were recorded, but they instead decided to save the songs for a future release.
     </p>

     <p>

      <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">

        15

      </i>

      Recording and officially releasing the new album was significantly delayed after The Used left Reprise Recordstheir record label group of ten years. According to Howard, "We were kind of waiting to see if we were going to do this record on Warner or not. We kind of had to wait and see what happened about that before we started recording."

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">
       Musical style
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Used&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Musical style">

        ]

     </h2>
     <p>
      The Used's music has been classified under many subgenres of rock. Their musical style has been described as
      <a href="/wiki/Post-hardcore" title="Post-hardcore">
       post-hardcore
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_36-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Screamo" title="Screamo">
       screamo
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic.com_36-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-allmusic.com-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Emo" title="Emo">
       emo
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Alternative_metal" title="Alternative metal">
       alternative metal
      </a>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
       <a href="#cite_note-37">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        37
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">
       metal
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nme.com_38-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-nme.com-38">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        38
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
       <a href="#cite_note-39">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        39
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Band bassist Jeph Howard has said, "We just don't consider ourselves '
      <a href="/wiki/Emo" title="Emo">
       emo
      </a>
      ' or 'screamo' and we never have. We're a rock band that's all we've ever been."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
       <a href="#cite_note-40">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        40
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      For
      <i>
       Artwork
      </i>
      , McCracken described their genre as "gross pop".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-altpress_15-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-altpress-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Allman described the album's style: "It's gonna be pretty raw. I think a lot of the stuff is very much more poetic and lyrical, and the sound is more retro and raw with less production".
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      On their fifth album
      <i>
       Vulnerable
      </i>
      , Bert McCracken stated that there is a lot of
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hip-hop" title="Hip-hop">
       hip-hop
      </a>
      influence, beats and
      <a href="/wiki/Drum_and_bass" title="Drum and bass">
       drum and bass
      </a>
      kind of stuff, but it is also still a The Used record with many soft and heavy, brutal, sharp, bright sounds, and the tempos are anywhere from ultra-slow to super fast and heavy.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
       <a href="#cite_note-41">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        41
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Anger_Music_Group">
       Anger Music Group
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Used&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Anger Music Group">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Angermusicgroup.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="141" data-file-width="400" height="53" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bd/Angermusicgroup.jpg/150px-Angermusicgroup.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bd/Angermusicgroup.jpg/225px-Angermusicgroup.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bd/Angermusicgroup.jpg/300px-Angermusicgroup.jpg 2x" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Angermusicgroup.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        AMG logo
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      Anger Music Group is an independent record label based out of Orange County, California founded in 2011 by
      <a href="/wiki/Bert_McCracken" title="Bert McCracken">
       Bert McCracken
      </a>
      and The Used. They are partners with
      <a href="/wiki/Hopeless_Records" title="Hopeless Records">
       Hopeless Records
      </a>
      who assist with worldwide distribution.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
       <a href="#cite_note-42">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        42
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
       <a href="#cite_note-43">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        43
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Used_partner_with_Hopless_44-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Used_partner_with_Hopless-44">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        44
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Used founded their own record label Anger Music Group in 2011 after parting ways with
      <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">
       Reprise Records
      </a>
      .
      <a href="/wiki/Bert_McCracken" title="Bert McCracken">
       Bert McCracken
      </a>
      describes the company as "a group that involves everything from managing to production to art design. The originally name for the label was going to be Dental Records but the name was already trademarked so The Used decided on Anger Music Group instead. They formed their own label for more musical freedom that Reprise held them back from and because they wanted to do everything on their own from putting it out and paying for it. They decided to partner with Hopeless Records after determining that the workers of Hopeless had the most similar mind set of how the music world should be working.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Used_partner_with_Hopless_44-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-The_Used_partner_with_Hopless-44">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        44
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
       <a href="#cite_note-45">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        45
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">
       <a href="#cite_note-46">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        46
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Bert is currently working with the Australian duo Corpus, which are the first band excluding The Used themselves to be signed to the label.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">
       <a href="#cite_note-47">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        47
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">
       <a href="#cite_note-48">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        48
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The Used album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_(The_Used_album)" title="Vulnerable (The Used album)">
        Vulnerable
       </a>
      </i>
      and EP
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Ocean_of_the_Sky" title="The Ocean of the Sky">
        The Ocean of the Sky
       </a>
      </i>
      were both released on Anger Music Group.

     <p>
      With the success of Anger Music Group, The Used have been working on the creation of a second record label called GAS Union and hope to have it operational by spring 2014. GAS is an abbreviation for "give a shit" and the label is self described as "revolutionary" because it is planned to be the first ever free music label where the musicians obtain all the money from the music they create and the people that don't have anything to do with the creation of music or production of music get filtered out. The motto of the label is "Giving Music Back To The People". The band's latest album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Imaginary_Enemy_(album)" title="Imaginary Enemy (album)">
        Imaginary Enemy
       </a>
      </i>
      is the first album to be released on GAS Union.
