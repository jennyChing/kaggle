       <h1 class="entry-title">
        Free Market Tote Crochet Pattern
       </h1>
       <div class="post-info">
        <span class="date published time" title="2012-08-11T01:17:00+00:00">
         Aug 11
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <span class="fn">
          Lauren
         </span>
        </span>
        
        <span class="post-comments">
         <a href="http://daisycottagedesigns.net/crochet/free-market-tote-crochet-pattern/#comments">
          103 Comments
         </a>
        </span>
       </div>
       <div class="entry-content">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p style="color: #444444; text-align: justify;">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="640" src="//farm8.staticflickr.com/7035/13729853524_d951dbde2a_z.jpg" width="640"/>
         Need a quick gift for a little girl in your life? This free market totecrochet pattern might be the perfect thing! Whip one up in a couple hours while listening to an ebook or spending time with your family, add a flower or other embellishment, and youre good to go! How cute would it be to add some felt or crocheted food inside the bag? Riley has already spent a good deal of time enjoying this bag and I know she would absolutely love having items to go in it! It would also make a good yarn storage bag  it holds a good number of cotton skeins.
        </p>
        <p>
         The finished dimensions of this crochet shopping tote patternis approximately 12 inches deep by 11 inches wide with a 9 inch deep handle (though thats easily adjustable).
        </p>
        <!-- Quick Adsense WordPress Plugin: http://quicksense.net/ -->
        <div style="float:none;margin:10px 0 10px 0;text-align:center;">
         <!--TBN 300x250 - pos - 4 STARTS-->
         <div id="tbn-c5a32fc0-6a84-4ddc-9ac3-3e3ec424ca7b">
          <script type="text/javascript">
           window.$tbn = window.$tbn || {"d":function(){}}; $tbn.d("c5a32fc0-6a84-4ddc-9ac3-3e3ec424ca7b");
          </script>
         </div>
         <!--TBN 300x250 - pos - 4 ENDS-->
        </div>
        <p style="color: #33a8a4; text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://www.daisycottagedesigns.net/2014/05/large-market-tote-crochet-pattern.html">
          <b>
           Click here for the adult size version of this pattern.
          </b>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         Before you get started, make sure you have the following:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li style="color: #444444;">
          About 250 yards of worsted weight yarn
         </li>
         <li style="color: #444444;">
          An I hook
         </li>
         <li style="color: #444444;">
          A safety pin for marking stitches.
         </li>
         <li style="color: #444444;">
          A flower or other embellishment to spice it up!
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <strong>
          NOTE:
         </strong>
         the chain 2 at the beginning of the first four rounds does NOT count as the first stitch  you will need to work in that stitch you would normally skip. You will be joining rounds in the first true DC stitch rather than the top of the turning chain.
        </p>
        <p>
         Ch 3.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Round 1:
         </b>
         Work 12 DC in the first chain from hook. Join in the top of the first DC. (12)
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Round 2:
         </b>
         ch 2. 2 DC in each stitch around. Join to the top of the first DC. (24)
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Round 3:
         </b>
         ch 2. *DC in the next stitch, 2 DC in the next* around. Join in the top of the first DC. (36)
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Round 4:
         </b>
         ch 2. *DC in the next 2 stitches, 2 DC in the next* around. Join in the top of the first DC.(48)
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <a href="http://daisycottagedesigns.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Market-Tote-Tutorial.jpg">
          <img alt="Market Tote Tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1874" height="480" src="//daisycottagedesigns.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/Market-Tote-Tutorial.jpg" width="640"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <b>
          Round 5:
         </b>
         Ch 3. *DC, ch 1* in each stitch. Do not slip stitch to the top of the starting chain. Instead, insert your hook in the first ch1 space of round 5 and continue with round 6 (see photo above).
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <b>
          NOTE:
         </b>
         Please understand that since youre working in spiral, there will be a gap when you first transition to working in spiral. If youd like to minimize this gap, make the last stitch of round 5 and the first stitch of round 6 a hdc.
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <b>
          Rounds 6-23:
         </b>
         DC, ch 1 in each ch 1 space.
        </p>
        <p>
         At the end of round 23, join with a slip stitch to the first DC of the previous round.
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <b>
          Round 24:
         </b>
         Ch 2. DC in each ch 1 space.
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <b>
          Round 25:
         </b>
         ch 2. DC BETWEEN each DC of the previous round.
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         <a href="http://daisycottagedesigns.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/market-tote-pattern.jpg">
          <img alt="market tote pattern" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1873" height="611" src="//daisycottagedesigns.net/wp-content/uploads/2012/08/market-tote-pattern.jpg" width="402"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         Handles will be worked in rows. The Ch 2 does count as a stitch here. I work between stitches, but you could work in the stitches, too.
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Row 1:
         </b>
         Ch 2. DC in the next 9 stitches. (10)
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Row 2:
         </b>
         ch 2, turn. DC decrease in the next two stitches. DC in the next 5. DC decrease in the last two stitches. (8)
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Row 3:
         </b>
         ch 2, turn. DC decrease in the next two stitches. DC in the next 3. DC decrease in the last two stitches. (6)
        </p>
        <p>
         <b>
          Rows 4-15:
         </b>
         ch 2, turn. DC in the next 5 stitches (6). (Continue on if youd like longer straps)
        </p>
        <p>
         Cut yarn and weave ends. Fold your bag in half as evenly as possible to determine where to start the other side of the handle (I skipped 15 stitches) and work the handle on the other side of the bag. Single crochet the two sides of the handle together on the wrong side.
        </p>
        <p style="color: #444444;">
         I hope you enjoy the pattern. Feel free to use it (and even sell the items youve made), but please remember to link back to this post so others can give it a try as well. And, as always, please use your own photos when selling items made from this pattern.
        </p>
