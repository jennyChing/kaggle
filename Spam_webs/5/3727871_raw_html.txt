    <h1>
     How Does Solar Power Work?
    </h1>
    <br/>
    <p>
    </p>
    <p>
     Did you know that the sun sends more energy to Earth in one hour, than the entire worlds population consumes in a year? Pretty impressive, right?
    </p>
    <p>
     Given the immense power of the sun, it makes sense that we make the most of it. And solar power is exactly that  solar energy converted into electricity to power properties and utilities.
    </p>
    <p>
     But how does solar power work exactly? Lets begin with the basics
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      The 4 parts of a solar power system
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     A basic household solar power system has four parts:
    </p>
    <ol>
     <li>
      Solar panels
     </li>
     <li>
      Regulator
     </li>
     <li>
      Battery
     </li>
     <li>
      Inverter
     </li>
    </ol>
    <p>
     When they work together, the components convert the suns energy into DC and AC electricity.
    </p>
    <p>
     Lets look at each part in more detail
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      How do solar panels work?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     When we think of solar power, most of us think of solar panels. Theyre the grey/blue celled sheets you see sitting on roofs across Australia. When the sun shines, the roof panels absorb the energy and convert it into DC electricity, which charges the battery.
    </p>
    <p>
     But
     <a href="http://www.3quotes.com.au/solar-power/are-all-solar-panels-the-same.html">
      not all solar panels are created equal
     </a>
     . They are rated depending on their efficiency and how much power they produce. In Australia, this rating ranges from 3 in southern regions to 6 in parts of Western Australia, Queensland and the Northern Territory.
    </p>
    <p>
     If you choose to connect the panels to the grid (known as grid-connect solar power), any excess electricity is sent to the mains grid. Grid connect also allows for a constant flow of electricity. So even if its a dark or rainy day, you can keep warm and power appliances. Off-grid solar power is the opposite; since the panels arent connected to the grid, they need to store energy for later use.
    </p>
    <p>
     <img alt="How solar power works" src="http://www.3quotes.com.au/content/images/how-does-solar-power-work.jpg" style="width: 400px; height: 106px;"/>
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      How do solar regulators work?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     The solar panels are connected to a regulator. The panels send the DC electricity to the regulator, which keeps the battery full of power. Also known as a charge controller, the solar regulator also regulates the electricity current to avoid battery damage due to overcharging.
    </p>
    <p>
     Your panels may feature a Low Voltage Disconnect attachment. This turns off the supply if the battery voltage goes too low. Again, this is to guard the battery and extend its life.
    </p>
    <p>
     Another handy feature of the regulator is that it stops the battery from sending electricity back to the panels, which would drain it.
    </p>
    <p>
     Like solar panels, not all regulators are the same. Its important that the regulator can support the solar panel output  and a certain amount above that level, since solar panels may produce more than their rated output on some days. A good guide is to use a regulator that can take on at least 125 percent of the panels short circuit current.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      How do solar inverters work?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     Once the DC energy is in the battery, it needs to be converted so that AC appliances in your home can use it. Thats the job of the
     <a href="http://www.3quotes.com.au/solar-power/what-is-a-solar-regulator-and-inverter.html">
      solar inverter
     </a>
     . It turns the DC power into a 240V alternating current (AC).
    </p>
    <p>
     The inverter will have either a pure sine wave or modified sine wave design. Bear in mind that some household appliances, lighting and motors dont perform as well with a modified inverter. And while they are a little more expensive, pure sine wave inverters produce power thats often better than that from the grid.
    </p>
    <p>
     When comparing converters, look at the rating. It tells you how much continuous power you can expect from the inverter.
    </p>
    <p>
     <strong>
      How do solar power batteries work?
     </strong>
    </p>
    <p>
     Solar power systems use deep cycle batteries. These can be discharged and recharged over and over again, without any issues. That being said, its a good idea to only discharge the battery to around 70 per cent.
    </p>
    <p>
     Youll notice the batteries have an Ah rating, which stands for Ampere Hours. This is how much current you can expect over a certain amount of time.
    </p>
    <p>
     If you have any questions about solar power parts and how to choose them, speak with your solar power installer. Theyll be only too happy to help.
    </p>
