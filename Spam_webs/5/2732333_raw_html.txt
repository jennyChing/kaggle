           Americans with Disabilities Act turns 21
          </a>
         </h1>
         <!--POST META-->

       <p>
        Earlier this week, the Americans with Disabilities Act ,(ADA), turned 21. It is almost unbelievable to me how much power this one law has. It offers persons with disabilities an equal opportunity to live, work and enjoy life by granting access. Access, not only to buildings, but technology and programs as well. Think of the ADA as a key card to the world, a world full of possibilities. Some may find this phrasing overblown or cliche but when one document presents millions of citizens with equal rights we cannot overlook its significance.
       </p>
       <p>
        In the past year, the ADA has played a major role in both local and national events. Locally, city and county officials continue to utilize the act as they move forward with accessibility improvements throughout the area.
       </p>
       <p>
        Local governments are forging ahead with efforts to install curb cuts, collaborate with agencies to build fully inclusive recreational facilities, and continuing to ensure that accessible transportation remains a priority. Additionally, university officials continue to make upgrades to the UNCW campus with an eye toward universal access. They have added exterior automatic doors where possible, continue to work with the city to adapt their portion of the cross-city trail and professors, (in my experience), are willing to work alongside students with disabilities to ensure their success.
       </p>
       <p>
        From a federal standpoint, the Obama Administration reaffirmed their commitment to serving all residents with disabilities. According to a White House press release officials are working to improve their Section 508 compliance. Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 relates to technology and access to information posted online.
       </p>
       <p>
        <em>
         Today,  following on other steps already taken, the Obama Administration  announced new efforts that will continue to support increased employment  opportunities for persons with disabilities and will also help make the  government more open and accessible to all citizens. Specifically, the  administration is working to release a draft comprehensive strategic  plan to improve compliance with Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act. Making  electronic and information technology, such as websites, 508 compliant  will ensure that applicants have equal access to apply for job  opportunities. 508 compliance also will promote increased retention, as  federal employees will be able to successfully utilize the technology   whether it be computers, telephones, fax machines, websites and many  other technological tools  necessary to perform their duties. Moreover,  it will make the government more open and accessible as people with  disabilities will be able to better access all the information the  federal government has placed online.
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        More detailed information regarding these efforts will be released in the coming days and weeks.
        <em>
        </em>
       </p>
       <p>
        Overall, the past year has been very fruitful in this community and beyond for those with disabilities. However, as those of us who live with , serve, and know the needs of the disabled understand, our work is never finished. To quote directly from the president:
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family: Calibri">
         Despite  these advancements, there is more work to be done, and my  Administration remains committed to ending all forms of discrimination  and upholding the rights of Americans with disabilities. The Department  of Justice continues to strengthen enforcement of the ADA by ensuring  that persons with disabilities have access to community-based services  that allow them to lead independent lives in the communities of their  choosing.
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family: Calibri">
         In Wilmington, I believe there is room for improvement as well. We must continue to collaborate, understand accessibility needs, and correct such needs with due diligence. UNCW should maintain their vows of an inclusive and diverse educational environment as well. Beyond prompt attention to as needed architectural upgrades, faculty and staff can constantly learn from students including those with special needs. In short, if everyone bands together we can create a better living and working life for the 36 million Americans with disabilities *
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family: Calibri">
         Until next year, Happy 21st Anniversary ADA! What improvements might you suggest in relation to disabilities? Share your thoughts on local, state and federal needs. Post your comments to my blog and continue the conversation.
        </span>
       </p>

    <aside class="sidebar_about">
     <h4>
      About This Blog
     </h4>
     <p>
      <img alt="david-150x1501" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-10279" height="150" src="http://disabilities.blogs.starnewsonline.com/files/2009/07/david-150x1501.jpg" width="150"/>
      My name is David Morrison. I am a graduate student living with cerebral palsy. As Chair of the Cape Fear Disability Commission, (CFDC), I will keep you informed of its activities, and act as your representative. Through this blog, I hope to improve disability awareness. With my first-hand knowledge and your help we can enhance the lives of those who have different abilities. This is meant to be an open discussion forum. You may wish to raise questions as well as learn about online resources, articles, meetings, events etc. This blog is open to everyone, and active participation is welcome! Check out snippets from my blog in every Saturdays Local section. Please feel free to comment on anything here via the blog, or by dropping me an email at

       david.morrison87@yahoo.com
