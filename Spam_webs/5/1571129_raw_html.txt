         <h1 class="entry-title">
          High-Speed Strings: Video Trickery Creates Wobbly Slow Motion Effect
          <span class="post-date">
           by
           <a href="http://thisiscolossal.com/about" rel="author external" title="Visit Christopher Jobsons website">
            Christopher Jobson
           </a>
           on December 1, 2013
          </span>
         </h1>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <p>
          <img alt="bass" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-38943" height="446" src="http://www.thisiscolossal.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/bass.jpg" width="800"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          <iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="450" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/MoUQdcLmz5g?rel=0" width="800">
          </iframe>
         </p>
         <p>
          This brief clip demonstrates what happens when you film an upright bass while synchronizing the vibration of the strings with the frame rate of the camera. The resulting video makes it appear as though the musician is playing in slow motion when the video is actually playing at normal speed, not unlike the effect of a strobe light. (via
          <a href="http://devour.com/video/high-speed-strings/" target="_blank">
           Devour
          </a>
          )
         </p>
         <p>
          <span class="update">
           Update:
          </span>
          According to
          <a href="http://createdigitalmusic.com/2011/07/fun-with-waves-as-videos-reveal-guitar-string-movement-and-iphone-shutters/" target="_blank">
           Create Digital Music
          </a>
          this phenomenon might have more to do with how a digital cameras capture an image by scanning images quickly from top to bottom. (thnx, Clepsydrae)
         </p>
