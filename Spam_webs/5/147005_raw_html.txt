         <p>
          <strong>
           Twitter Deals With Google | Twitter Tweets To Appear In Google Searches:
          </strong>
          Twitter recently struck a deal with Google by which Twitter tweets will be visible in Google searches more easily. This will help Twitters 284 million users sayings be seen by the world through Google search engine.
         </p>
         <p>
          Previously in 2009 both Twitter and Google made a similar kind of agreement but that didnt last long and the deal broke in 2 years after the agreement. Google then had to crawlTwitters site for information but now the Twitters 284m users tweets will be accessible to Google as soon they are posted on Twitter. Twitter Chief Executive Officer Dick Costolo is making very sincere efforts in making sure that tweets of the current users are reached to non users and so by seeing the tweets they can join Twitter, thus increasing the sites members and generating more revenue from larger members. Twitter which is already providing its tweets toMicrosoft Corp.s Bing search service and Yahoo! Inc., is aiming to get attention of more people and thus increase the microblogging sites popularity drastically which currently is very slow.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Promoted Twitter Tweets
         </h2>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           The deal will make tweets more searchable and should help to increase engagement and usage for Twitter. For Google this announcement will allow users to provide more real-time information about consumers/companies do/promote/discuss online. saidGartner analyst Annette Jump.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          Its a way of Twitter to get attention of people for increasing its growth which has slowed down in recent months.Twitter this week announced deals to show advertising in news sharing app Flipboard and with Yahoo Japan Corp to cartel its paid promoted tweets on their websites as featured content.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_3640" style="width: 649px">

         <h2>
          Twitter Earnings Report
         </h2>
         <p>
          Twitter by promoting its content to such websites and exposing it to more audience expects to gain some boost in their revenue. Twitter only made about $1.13 advertising revenue per user in comparison with Facebooks $2.2 in the third quarter lastyear. The last quarter report of Twitter showed 3.3% rise in their revenue to $42.05 at in New York.
         </p>
         <p>
          Conversely, Facebook shares had a rough beginning after their May 2012 presentation yet in this manner they had taken off to be just about 100% higher as the quantity of parts hit 1.35bn. It has made the dubious move from an administration used primarilyon a desktop program to one used on cell phones.
         </p>
         <p>
          Twitter included 13 million monthly active people  an ascent of 4.8%  between the second and third quarters in 2014, however the pace was slower than the 6.2% increment presented from the first on the second quarter.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Mutually Beneficial to both companies
         </h2>
         <p>
          As JP Morgan analyst said in a note to investors:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           The deal means more opportunities for Twitter to convert, and possibly monetize, logged-out users, JPsaid. It will also increase the frequency that people with Twitter accounts check the site, he further added.
          </p>
         </blockquote>
         <p>
          As Anmuth wrote:
         </p>
         <blockquote>
          <p>
           For Google, we believe search results will be enhanced by access to real-time tweets and a much broader amount of content.
          </p>
         </blockquote>

          All I can say is that its a good step by twitter to promote their microblogging site and in this way they will earn more popularity and along with this more revenue and profit for sure.
