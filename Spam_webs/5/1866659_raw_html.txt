         <h2 class="title icon">
          <img alt="Default" src="images/icons/icon1.png"/>
          Prodrive Open Day, Jul '07, Richard Burns day, 25 Sept '10. Factory tours...
         </h2>
         <div class="content">
          <div id="post_message_1596329">
           <blockquote class="postcontent restore ">
            infinity asked if I would put these up, so only too happy to
            <img alt="" border="0" class="inlineimg" src="images/smilies/smiley4b.gif" title="Big smile"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            I did a write up from the event in some slack time, which I'd never finished, thinking no one would be too bothered about it on a VW forum. Stuff in quotes I wrote mainly then.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            If people want more, I can post more. There's everything from Imprezas to Astons to 6R4s, to old skool M3s - and widgets by the truckload.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Better start with some hardcore p0rn then - the full phat tarmac-spec Impreza:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3526.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Arch fill and camber for the scene boys to die for:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3494.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3529.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div class="bbcode_container">
             <div class="bbcode_quote">
              <div class="quote_container">
               <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
               </div>
               I attended the Prodrive Open Day and thought I'd do a report on it for all us motorsporty types. It's a bit lengthy, but I enjoyed it so much they keys just tapped themselves
               <br/>
               <br/>
               It is rare to get an opportunity to look round a world class motorsport and automotive engineering house, even though Prodrive are known to open their doors once in a while. I once got a tour around the Ford Motorsport HQ at Boreham when they were doing the Escort Cosworths, and it's just the biggest buzz to stand there and take it all in, totally immersed in something so cutting edge, so important in a hobby like this.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               I last went to Prodrive evening tour in late 2003, before they got the Aston contract, so I had a few ideas of where things would be, and what would be on offer, except this time they'd scaled it all up. It was all glitz, as they rolled out the Le Mans Aston Martins, the latest Aston road cars, loads of James Bond stuff, and threw open the Subaru engine, gearbox, electrics, and bodyshell departments, aswell as machine shops - and put on a show of engineering to make Autosport International look a little unfocused. They then factored in the families, got the bouncy castles in, the band, the rally simulators, and then just waited as the Subaru hordes rolled in and packed the place out with the blue saloons. Banbury literally was burbling at 10am on Sunday. This was a genuine show of high credibility
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <br/>
            One of the last saloon shape Impreza WRC shells being built:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3630.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div class="bbcode_container">
             <div class="bbcode_quote">
              <div class="quote_container">
               <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
               </div>
               On original preparation, all shells go to Surface Preparation in Birmingham for acid dip, to clean the shells up of any zinc and coatings. Crashed shells are more of a nightmare, since they're covered in paint, nasty to weld. Depending on who I spoke to, opinion varied on whether you can acid dip cars which already have cages in them. One said no, whereas another elsewhere said that the cage feet must have 5mm holes drilled to allow them to drain in the drying process.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Prodrive quote 720 man hours to build. Team of two, each do one side, takes 4-5 weeks:
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3308.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div class="bbcode_container">
             <div class="bbcode_quote">
              <div class="quote_container">
               <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
               </div>
               Cage is TIG'd due to precision of mfr. Rest is MIG'd to allow for small tolerance diffs in shell. Cage beams vary in thickness from section to section, and there is some element of deformation factored in.
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3633.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3631.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3632.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Note in-house fabricated inner front arches / turrets:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3629.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3627.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            ^^
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div class="bbcode_container">
             <div class="bbcode_quote">
              <div class="quote_container">
               <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
               </div>
               They have to be on the ball for heat distortion. Amazingly once a shell is done, on MIRA shakedown it is not unheard of for the front turrets to move by 4mm, as the body de-stresses itself. Lately, they've started to build in a margin on prep as they know which way they're going to move. Previously they've had cars go out on shakedown which have moved outside tolerances, had to come back in, and have the top mounts cut off, moved and re-welded.
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            This is a jig they can park the shell on to measure it with also IIRC:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3301.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Front arch jig I saw when at a 2003 open evening:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/scan0011.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            And rear carbon rear arches (WRC &lt; stupid &gt; rules updated more recently to allow):
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3304.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3307.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Fabricated rear 3/4 of tunnel
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3628.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Tool in middle is a measuring device which they use to check on various points of the shell for tolerances.
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/IMG_3302.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div class="bbcode_container">
             <div class="bbcode_quote">
              <div class="quote_container">
               <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
               </div>
               Spoke to two guys who do this work, one of them in his lower 20s who evidently just loved it. "They're works of art" he said. Mentioned that he wasn't allowed near the welding on the actual cages for over a year, and just was doing seam welding work. Reckoned he'd done 15+ WRC shells in his 3 years there. As welders, they don't need to be coded apparently.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               WRC shells are 40k alone, basically a reflection of time costs. Stiffer than an F1 chassis apparently, measured on the basis of force required to twist the body by 1mm. Forget exact measurement - think it was 40,000 NM of force to twist by 1mm, and F1 is 32,000, but someone correct me if that sounds ridiculous - but those were the relative numerical amounts.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Apparently the designers of the new Impreza shell have asked the fabricators to weigh the MIG rolls before and after seam welding, so they can keep track of what weight is added in the seam welding process
               <img alt="" border="0" class="inlineimg" src="images/smilies/smiley3.gif" title="Shocked"/>
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            Also, some pics from the 2003 open evening:
            <br/>
            <br/>
            Sat on the above mentioned jig / measuring device
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/scan0005.jpg"/>
            <br/>

            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/scan0006.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            On the jig/measuring device again
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/scan0008.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://i74.photobucket.com/albums/i273/Chris_Eyre/prodrive/scan0009.jpg"/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <br/>
            <div class="bbcode_container">
             <div class="bbcode_quote">
              <div class="quote_container">
               <div class="bbcode_quote_container">
               </div>
               Not uncommon to need to jig 'uncrashed' shells apparently, engine bay box sections often being pushed up from landings, with crack giveaways, just ahead of inner arches.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Once done, they're painted at a bodyshop (STP) on the same estate in Banbury, so they don't have far to go.
              </div>
             </div>
            </div>
            <br/>
            So, not really hard to see why the WRC is f*ckin expensive!!!

All wire used is white - they gave up on coloured wires as they'd have so many that occasionally a mechanic would get them mixed up. Makes sure only the sparkies can deal with them
               <img alt="" border="0" class="inlineimg" src="images/smilies/smiley5.gif" title="Confused"/>
               . Wires are all tied with plastic ties. Cable ties not used for some reason - queried insulation damage risk which got a nod, but just mainly neatness  cant argue with that.
               <br/>
               <br/>
               Component costs of that centre console are around 2k. With the employee time costs to make them, they're 4k when sold out to a customer. Carbon a fairly expensive part of process.
