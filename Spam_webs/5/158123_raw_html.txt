         <div class="content">
          <p>
           While it is
           <a href="http://www.washingtonsblog.com/2014/07/president-obama-pentagon-dont-know-shot-malaysian-airlines-flight-mh-17-ukraine.html">
            too early to know
           </a>
           who shot down Malaysian Airlines flight MH-17 over Ukraine yesterday - and while it
           <a href="http://www.washingtonsblog.com/2014/07/shootdown-passenger-jet-ukraine-false-flag.html">
            could have been a false flag
           </a>
           - it would be good to remember that airlines have
           <em>
            accidentally
           </em>
           been shot down numerous times in the past.
          </p>
          <p>
           After all, the Malaysian airlines plane was
           <a href="http://www.zerohedge.com/news/2014-07-17/was-flight-mh-17-diverted-over-restricted-airspace" target="_blank" title="way off course">
            <em>
             way
            </em>
            off course
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           <a href="http://www.washingtonsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Flight-Path.jpg" target="_blank">
            <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-33710" height="397.0" src="http://www.washingtonsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Flight-Path.jpg" title="Flight Path" width="790"/>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           The plane flew over a war zone, which was well-known to be a no fly zone with restricted airspace.
          </p>
          <p>
           The Los Angeles Times
           <a href="http://www.latimes.com/world/europe/la-fg-ukraine-aviation-20140717-story.html" target="_blank" title="reports">
            reports
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            Malaysia Airlines Flight 17 was cruising
            <strong>
             just 1,000 feet above restricted airspace
            </strong>
            when it was struck by a missile in Ukraines Donetsk region, according to aviation and intelligence officials.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Despite ongoing violence in eastern Ukraine, including the recent downing of two military aircraft, Ukrainian aviation officials had closed the regions airspace only below 32,000 feet in altitude.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            The Boeing 777, en route from Amsterdam to Kuala Lumpur, the Malaysian capital, with 298 people aboard, was at 33,000 feet when it vanished from radar screens, according to European aviation authorities.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            ***
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            In recent days, though, military aircraft were downed by antiaircraft munitions, which should have concerned aviation authorities, he said.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Reuters
           <a href="http://www.reuters.com/article/2014/07/18/us-ukraine-crisis-flightplan-idUSKBN0FN1HR20140718" target="_blank" title="notes">
            notes
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            Malaysia Airlines filed a flight plan requesting to fly at 35,000 feet throughout Ukraine airspace but was instructed by Ukraine air traffic control to fly at 33,000 feet upon entry .
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           CBS
           <a href="http://sanfrancisco.cbslocal.com/2014/07/17/captain-sullenberger-big-question-is-why-plane-was-flying-over-war-zone/" target="_blank" title="reports">
            writes
           </a>
           in an article entitled Big Question Is Why Plane Was Flying Over WarZone:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            During a phone interview with CBS News Thursday morning, Captain Capt. Chesley Sully Sullenberger was questioned why Malaysia Airlines flight 17 would be flying over Ukraines border with Russia despite ongoing political unrest in the area.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            Sullenberger, the veteran of the 2009 Miracle on the Hudson landing, is an aviation expert for CBS News.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            That is one of the big questions right now, said Sully. The U.S. Federal Aviation Administration has barred U.S. Airlines from flying over this area for some time.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           The Atlantic
           <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/international/archive/2014/07/the-faas-notice-prohibiting-airline-flights-over-ukraine/374622/" target="_blank" title="points out">
            points out
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            Did aviation authorities know that this was a dangerous area?
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <blockquote id="yui_3_16_0_1_1405627852505_2059">
           <p>
            Yes, they most certainly did. Nearly three months ago, on the Special Rules section of its site, the U.S. Federal Aviation Administration put out an order prohibiting American pilots, airlines, charter carriers, and everyone else over whom the FAA has direct jurisdiction, from flying over parts of Ukraine.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           Bloomberg
           <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2014-07-18/malaysian-airline-took-flight-route-avoided-by-qantas-asiana.html" target="_blank" title="adds">
            adds
           </a>
           :
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            Qantas hasnt used the route for a few months, said Andrew McGinnes, a spokesman for the Australian carrier, while Hong Kong-based Cathay Pacific said it has been detouring for quite some time. Korean Air Lines Co. and Asiana Airlines Inc. said in statements they have been avoiding the area since March 3.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           This is not the first time such an incident has occurred 
          </p>
          <h3 style="color: #000099;">
           Numerous Previous Accidental Shootdowns ... By Russia, Ukraine, and Other Countries
          </h3>
          <p>
           In 1954, mainland China's Army shot down aCathay Pacific Airways airplane, killing 10 out of the 19 passengers. Cathay Pacific was the airline of Hong Kong, then under British control). In apologizing for the attack to Britain, the Chinese government
           <a data-mce-="" href="http://www.helianthus-productions.com/apology.html">
            said
           </a>
           that they had thought the plane was a military aircraft from the Taiwan on an attack mission.
          </p>
          <p>
           In 1973, a Libyan Arab Airlines Boeing 727 flying from Tripoli to Cairo
           <a data-mce-="" href="http://www.jpost.com/Features/In-Thespotlight/This-Week-in-History-IAF-shoots-down-Libyan-Flight-114">
            got lost
           </a>
           and flew over the Sinai peninsula, under Israeli control since the Six-Day War in 1967. After giving signals to land and firing warning shots, Israeli jets shot down the plane, killing 108 of the 113 people on board. The chief of staff of the Israeli armed forces
           <a data-mce-="" href="http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1915&amp;dat=19730224&amp;id=Bi8iAAAAIBAJ&amp;sjid=yHQFAAAAIBAJ&amp;pg=1614,3581538">
            took responsibility
           </a>
           for ordering the shoot-down, the Defense Minister called the event an "error of judgment", and the Israeli government
           <a data-mce-="" href="http://piersmorgan.blogs.cnn.com/2014/03/19/flight-370-puts-israel-on-high-alert-israel-has-close-to-zero-margin-for-error-in-countering-and-protecting-itself-against-a-hijacked-airplane/">
            compensated
           </a>
           the families of victims (Libya
           <a data-mce-="" href="http://www.nytimes.com/1983/09/21/opinion/l-testing-the-barometer-of-national-outrage-178370.html">
            condemned
           </a>
           the attack as "a criminal act" while the Soviets called it a "monstrous new crime").
          </p>
          <p>
           In 1983, a Korean airlines passenger airline was shot down by a Soviet pilot killing all
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Air_Lines_Flight_007" target="_blank" title="269 passengers">
            269 passengers
           </a>
           . The pilot had accidentally deviated from the normal flight path:
          </p>
          <div class="mw-mmv-image">
           <img alt="" class="aligncenter" height="807.7" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/KAL007.svg/640px-KAL007.svg.png" width="790"/>
          </div>
          <div style="text-align: center;">
           <em>
            A simplified CIA map showing divergence of planned and actual flight paths.
           </em>
          </div>
          <p>
          </p>
          <p>
           The Soviets believed it was likely a military aircraft.
           <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Air_Lines_Flight_007#Shootdown" target="_blank" title="Transcripts quote">
            Transcripts quote
           </a>
           the shootdown order from Soviet General Kornukov:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            What civilian? [It] has flown over Kamchatka! It [came] from the ocean without identification. I am giving the order to attack if it crosses the State border.
           </p>
          </blockquote>
          <p>
           The Washington Post
           <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/inatl/longterm/flight801/stories/july88crash.htm" target="_blank" title="reported">
            reported
           </a>
           in 1988:
          </p>
          <blockquote>
           <div class="quote_start">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <div class="quote_end">
            <div>
            </div>
           </div>
           <p>
            A U.S. warship fighting gunboats in the Persian Gulf yesterday mistook an Iranian civilian jetliner for an attacking Iranian F14 fighter plane and blew it out of the hazy sky with a heat-seeking missile, the Pentagon announced. Iran said 290 persons were aboard the European-made A300 Airbus and that all had perished.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            The U.S. government deeply regrets this incident, Adm. William J. Crowe Jr., chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, told a Pentagon news conference.
           </p>
           <p>
           </p>
           <p>
            ***
           </p>
           <p>
