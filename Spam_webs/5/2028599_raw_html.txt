      <h2 class="title">
       Beta Alanine: The Next Creatine to Bodybuilding
      </h2>
      <div class="postmeta-primary">
       <span class="meta_date">
        January 17, 2014
       </span>
       <span class="meta_categories">
        <a href="http://averagefitnessfreak.com/category/supplements-2/" rel="category tag">
         Supplements
        </a>
       </span>
       <span class="meta_comments">
        <a href="http://averagefitnessfreak.com/beta-alanine-the-next-creatine-to-bodybuilding/#respond">
         No comments
        </a>
       </span>
      </div>
      <div class="entry clearfix">
       <p>
        <a href="http://i2.wp.com/averagefitnessfreak.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/empty_cellucor_c4_1.jpg">
         <img alt="cellucor c4 empty" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-3342" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/averagefitnessfreak.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/empty_cellucor_c4_1.jpg?resize=300%2C300"/>
        </a>
        You know that tingling sensation that kicks in about 20 minutes after you ingest your pre-workout? That is your friend Beta Alanine. Beta Alanine is found in many of your favorite and most popular pre-workout formulas including:
        <a href="http://www.illpumpyouup.com/cellucor/c4-extreme.htm" target="_blank" title="cellucor c4">
         Cellucor C4
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.illpumpyouup.com/controlled-labs/white-flood-305-grams.htm" target="_blank" title="controlled labs white flood">
         Controlled Labs White Flood
        </a>
        , and
        <a href="http://www.illpumpyouup.com/muscletech/neurocore.htm" target="_blank" title="muscletech neurocore">
         Muscle Tech NeuroCore
        </a>
        . Very often today, we find supplement companies adding in all kinds of ingredient mixtures in hopes of creating the next best product. Sometimes this results in simply a culmination of junk ingredients and expensive marketing, other times however, there is actual scientific research to support the product claims and give reason for that ingredients presence in your pre-workout supplement. Beta Alanine happens to be one of those ingredients with actual scientific research to provide backup for its rightful home on your pre-workout nutrition label.
        <span id="more-3306">
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        Research by the
        <a href="http://kulturystyka.sklep.pl/inne/beta_alanina/beta_alanina_i_kreatyna_efekty.pdf" target="_blank" title="Effect of Creatine and -Alanine Supplementation on Performance and Endocrine Responses in Strength/Power Athletes">
         International Journal of Sport Nutrition and Exercise Metabolism
        </a>
        has shown that athletes supplementing with Beta Alanine along with Creatine had greater improvements and lean body mass, body fat and strength than Creatine alone or the placebo group. This double blind (neither the participants nor the researchers knew which person was receiving which supplementation) human study took 33 male collegiate football players and randomly assigned them to one of three groups: a placebo group, creatine group, and a creatine and Beta Alanine group. Following a 10 week resistance training program, subjects were assessed on strength, power and body composition. In addition to the creatine and Beta Alanine group showing the most improvements in lean tissue accruement and body fat composition, they also experienced elevated resting testosterone levels, together providing the validation for Beta Alanines place on many sports supplement nutrition labels.
       </p>
       <p>
        <i>
         <a href="http://i1.wp.com/averagefitnessfreak.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/beta-alanine_close_1.jpg">
          <img alt="beta alanine now sports" class="size-thumbnail wp-image-3347 alignleft" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/averagefitnessfreak.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/beta-alanine_close_1.jpg?resize=225%2C300"/>
         </a>
         Understanding that it has a positive effect when used by athletes, what exactly is Beta Alanine and what does it do to your body?
        </i>
        Beta Alanine is a non-essential amino acid. This means that it is an amino acid that can be made by your body and does not need to be obtained through diet. However, unlike most other amino acids, it is not used in the synthesis of any major proteins or enzymes but instead is metabolized into acetic acid.
        <i>
         But wait a minute, didnt you just read that your body already produces Beta Alanine and does NOT need to be obtained through diet? So why are we talking about supplementing with this non-essential amino acid?
        </i>
        Beta Alanine is therate-limiting precursor of carnosine, a dipeptide made from Beta Alanine and histidine.This means that carnosine levels are limited by the amount of available Beta Alanine and supplementation has been shown to increase the concentration of carnosine in muscles and in turn, decrease fatigue. Normally, Carnosine is quickly degraded in the body by the enzyme carosinase (which is why supplementing with carosine is inferior as opposed to Beta Alanine), however when supplementing dietary consumption, the carosinase enzyme is overwhelmed, allowing elevated blood levels of carnosine (
        <a href="http://www.lef.org/magazine/mag2011/jan2011_Carnosine-Exceeding-Scientific-Expectations_01.htm" target="_blank" title="LEF Carnosine Exceeding Scientific Expectations">
         Carnosine Exceeding Scientific Expectations
        </a>
        ).
        <i>
         So why should you care about your levels of carosine?
        </i>
        Because carnosine allows your type 2 muscle fibers (fast twitch, used in maximal power movements) to absorb more hydrogen (typically released at a more rapid pace during exercise). The increased absorption of hydrogen helps keep those muscle fibers in ideal pH ranges. Keeping your muscles in ideal pH ranges allows your body to sustain maximum endurance and strength for longer periods while you exercise (
        <a href="http://examine.com/supplements/Beta-Alanine/">
         Examine: Beta Alanine
        </a>
        ). And as you are well aware, the increased period of maximum endurance and strength allows you to increase your muscle gains!
       </p>
       <p>
        <a href="http://i0.wp.com/averagefitnessfreak.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/beta-alanine_nutrition_1.jpg">
         <img alt="beta alanine nutrition label now sports" class="alignright size-thumbnail wp-image-3346" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/averagefitnessfreak.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/01/beta-alanine_nutrition_1.jpg?resize=300%2C116"/>
        </a>
        When supplementing your diet from non-food sources, you should always be concerned with overdose. Fortunately, Beta Alanine has not been shown to have any side effects even at moderate doses, but may cause flushing and tingling at high doses (paresthesia) 
        <i>
         sound familiar?
        </i>
        The recommended daily dosage for improving physical performance is 3.2 to 6.4 grams (
        <a href="http://www.webmd.com/vitamins-supplements/ingredientmono-1222-BETA-ALANINE.aspx?activeIngredientId=1222&amp;activeIngredientName=BETA-ALANINE" target="_blank" title="webmd beta alanine">
         WebMD: Beta Alanine
        </a>
        ) per day. The research study reference earlier utilized a 3.2 gram daily dose.
       </p>
       <p>
        While Beta Alanine has been known to the scientific world for over 100 years, it is not as commonly known of a supplement as say creatine, although they were actually both discovered by Dr. Roger Harris. It is however found in the majority of the ever so popular pre-workout formulas, and for good reason. But remember, just because it is popular doesnt mean you shouldnt be well aware of the products you take and their effects on your body. Have you taken
        <a href="http://www.illpumpyouup.com/cellucor/c4-extreme.htm" target="_blank" title="cellucor c4">
         Cellucor C4
        </a>
        ,
        <a href="http://www.illpumpyouup.com/controlled-labs/white-flood-305-grams.htm" target="_blank" title="controlled labs white flood">
         Controlled Labs White Flood
        </a>
        , or maybe
        <a href="http://www.illpumpyouup.com/muscletech/neurocore.htm" target="_blank" title="muscletech neurocore">
         Muscle Tech NeuroCore
        </a>
        and felt the sensation of paresthesia? What was your first reaction?
       </p>
