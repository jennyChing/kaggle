        <h1 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
         <a href="http://lisamoorefield.com/lego-card-tutorial/" rel="bookmark">
          LEGO Card Tutorial
         </a>
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="post-content">
        <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
         <a data-pin-color="gray" data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Flisamoorefield.com%2Flego-card-tutorial%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Flisamoorefield.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2013%2F01%2FLego-card-tutorial.jpg&amp;description=LEGO%20Card%20Tutorial" rel="nobox">
          <img data-pib-button="true" src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pin_it_button.png?cef035" title="Pin It"/>
         </a>
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lisamoorefield.com/lego-card-tutorial/lego-card-tutorial/" rel="attachment wp-att-2419">
          <img alt="Lego card tutorial" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2419" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego-card-tutorial.jpg?resize=640%2C640"/>
         </a>
         This 3-D LEGO card is easy to make and perfect for the LEGO brick lover in your life. It can be used as a birthday card, party invitation, Valentines Day card and more.
        </p>
        <p>
         I made these for my son, a huge LEGOfan. My original plan was to create a card that resembled a minifigure, but that was
         <em>
          way
         </em>
         too complicated. I thought of a LEGO brick design after I remembered the 3-D foam dots I had. Its simple yet fun.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         LEGO Card Tutorial
        </h2>
        <p>
         Materials needed:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          cardstock (I used two 57 pieces)
         </li>
         <li>
          <a href="http://www.avantlink.com/click.php?tt=cl&amp;mi=10513&amp;pw=128657&amp;ctc=&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.twopeasinabucket.com%2Fshop%2Ftools-category%2Fpunches%2F_circle_%2F">
           circle punch
          </a>
          (I used a 13/16 punch)
         </li>
         <li>
          3-D adhesive
          <a href="http://www.avantlink.com/click.php?tt=cl&amp;mi=10513&amp;pw=128657&amp;ctc=&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.twopeasinabucket.com%2Fshop%2Famerican-crafts%2F91459-circles-foam-tabs%2F%3Ffilters%3Dtools-category%252Fadhesives">
           foam dots
          </a>
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <img alt="Lego1" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2392" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego1.jpg?resize=640%2C388"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Fold a piece of cardstock in half to make a card:
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Lego2" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2394" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego2.jpg?resize=640%2C310"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Punch holes in the remaining cardstock. The number of circles needed depends on your card size and punch size; just line up the circles on your card as you punch to determine how many will fit. I used 12 circles.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Lego3" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2395" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego3.jpg?resize=640%2C386"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Attach foam dots to the punched circles. (Hmm they look like monster eyeballs from a cookie.)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Lego4" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2396" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego4.jpg?resize=640%2C256"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Arrange the dots on the card  eyeball it (ha!) or measure and mark the placement spots with a pencil.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Lego5" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2397" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego5.jpg?resize=640%2C431"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Remove the remaining backing from the foam dots and stick to the card.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Lego6" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2398" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego6.jpg?resize=640%2C301"/>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Red card:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lisamoorefield.com/lego-card-tutorial/lego-red/" rel="attachment wp-att-2399">
          <img alt="Lego red" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2399" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego-red.jpg?resize=640%2C468"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yellow card:
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lisamoorefield.com/lego-card-tutorial/lego-yellow/" rel="attachment wp-att-2402">
          <img alt="Lego yellow" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2402" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego-yellow.jpg?resize=640%2C518"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <h2>
         Valentines Day LEGO Card
        </h2>
        <p>
         <a href="http://lisamoorefield.com/lego-card-tutorial/lego-white/" rel="attachment wp-att-2424">
          <img alt="Lego white" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2424" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i0.wp.com/lisamoorefield.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/Lego-white.jpg?resize=325%2C301"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         These little cards can be made quickly by using a store-bought pack of white cards with envelopes (the one shown is 2.53.5). Add a heart sticker to one of the circles, or a punched heart in place of one of the circles. Include a clever note inside, such as Ill never LEGO of you.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          Id love to see your LEGO card designs and ideas for what to write inside  leave a comment and include a link to your card!
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
