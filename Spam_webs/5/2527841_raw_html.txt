        <p>
         President Obama signed a law early in his administration to raise taxes from 39 cents to $1.01 per pack of cigarettes and from 19.5 cents to 50 cents per pound for chewing tobacco.
         <br/>
         This new tax will start on all tabacco sales on April 1, 2009.
        </p>
        <p>
         I am not a smoker, and I do not agree with this tax! Where do you draw the line for increasing taxes on certain categories? It seems to be borderline discrimination. Should fat people have to pay more for food? Furthermore, the revenue from the increase is supposed to go to children that do not have health insurance.
        </p>
        <p>
         What about people who have lost their jobs due to the poor economy?
        </p>
        <p>
         I also think about one of the many promises that Obama made to the American people.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you make less than $250k, your taxes will not go up. Not one penny! 
         <em>
          Barack Obama
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         With one pen stroke, tobacco taxes more than doubledwhats next?
        </p>
