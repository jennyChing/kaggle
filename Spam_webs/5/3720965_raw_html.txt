        <h1 class="entry-title">
         Curried Potatoes with Poached Eggs
        </h1>
        <div class="recipe-cost">
         $5.39 recipe / $1.35 serving
        </div>
        <div class="entry-content">
         <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
         <p>
          Jet lag from my vacation has been hitting me hard, but I found the time in between naps yesterday to make it to the grocery and cook up a good meal to last me through the end of the week. Ive had this idea for Curried Potatoes with Poached Eggs kicking around in the back of my head for a couple months now, so I decided it was the perfect no-brainer thing to make while Itry to shake my brain out of vacation mode.
          <span id="more-21802">
          </span>
         </p>
         <p>
          I love making quick tomato curry sauces because theyre super fast and provide tremendous flavor to anything (see
          <a href="http://www.budgetbytes.com/2011/02/quick-curried-chick-peas/">
           Quick Curried Chickpeas
          </a>
          ). This time around I used potatoes as my inexpensive bulk ingredient and added eggs to provide protein and keep me full. A little fresh cilantro on top gives the dish a freshnote and balances the spicy curry (you can use mild curry powder if preferred). I poached my eggs in the sauce just to make things easier, but if you want a prettier presentation or more control over how the yolks are cooked, you can always fry up your eggs in a separate skillet and just lay them on top of the curried potatoes. Either way, the creamy yolk is
          <em>
           awesome
          </em>
          with that spicy curry sauce. YUM! :D
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Curried Potatoes with Poached Eggs - BudgetBytes.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21806" height="853" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Curried-Potatoes-and-Eggs-text.jpg" width="640"/>
         </p>

            Ingredients
           </div>
           <ul>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 russet potatoes (about 2 lbs.) $2.09
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 inch fresh ginger $0.39
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 cloves garlic $0.16
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 Tbsp olive oil $0.16
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             2 Tbsp curry powder (hot or mild) $0.60
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 15oz. can tomato sauce $0.89
            </li>

          <div class="ERSInstructions">
           <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
            Instructions
           </div>
           <ol>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Wash the potatoes well, then cut into -inch cubes. Place the cubed potatoes in a large pot and cover with water. Cover the pot with a lid and bring it up to a boil over high heat. Boil the potatoes for 5-6 minutes, or until they're tender when pierced with a fork. Drain the cooked potatoes in a colander.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             While the potatoes are boiling, begin the sauce. Peel the ginger with a vegetable peeler or scrape the skin off with the side of a spoon. Use a small holed cheese grater to grate about one inch of ginger (less if you prefer a more subtle ginger flavor). Mince the garlic.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add the ginger, garlic, and olive oil to a large, deep skillet (or a wide based pot). Saut the ginger and garlic over medium low heat for 1-2 minutes, or just until soft and fragrant. Add the curry powder to the skillet and saut for about a minute more to toast the spices.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add the tomato sauce to the skillet and stir to combine. Turn the heat up to medium and heat the sauce through. Taste the sauce and add salt, if needed. Add the cooked and drained potatoes to the skillet and stir to coat in the sauce. Add a couple tablespoons of water if the mixture seems dry or pasty.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Create four small wells or dips in the potato mixture and crack an egg into each. Place a lid on the skillet and let it come up to a simmer. Simmer the eggs in the sauce for 6-10 minutes, or until cooked through (less time if runny yolks are desired). Top with chopped fresh cilantro.
            </li>
           </ol>

         <h2>
         </h2>
         <h2>
          Step by Step Photos
         </h2>
         <p>
          <img alt="Dice Potatoes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21809" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Dice-Potatoes.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Start by scrubbing two russet potatoes (about 2 lbs. total) to remove any dirt. Dice the potatoes into 3/4-inch cubes.(Mine were closer to one inch, but smaller is definitely better.)
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Boil Potatoes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21816" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Boil-Potatoes.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Place the diced potatoes in a large pot and cover with water. Put a lid on the pot and bring it up to a boil over high heat. Boil the potatoes for 5-6 minutes, or until theyre tender when pierced with a fork (the total cooking time will depend on the size of your cubes, so check them by poking with a fork. When the fork slides in easily, theyre done.) When theyre finished cooking, drain in a colander.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Ginger and Garlic" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21813" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Ginger-Garlic.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           While the potatoes are cooking, you can begin the sauce. The sauce starts with my favorite duo: ginger and garlic! Mince two cloves of garlic and peel about one inch of ginger. You can use a vegetable peeler or the side of a spoon to scrape the skin from the ginger. Once peeled, use a small holed cheese grater to grate the ginger. I like ginger a lot, so I used about one inch, but its flexible.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Saut ginger and garlic" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21817" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Saute-Ginger-and-Garlic.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Add the ginger and garlic to a deep skillet with one tablespoon of olive oil. Saut over medium-low heat for 1-2 minutes, or until the ginger and garlic are a little softened and smell fragrant. If you dont have a deep skillet like this, you can use a pot. Just remember, it needs to be big enough to hold all those potatoes later.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Sharwoods Curry Powder" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21815" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Sharwoods-Curry-Powder.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Next, add 2 Tbsp curry powder. This is the curry powder that I use and I like it quite a bit. You can use hot or mild. If you only have mild curry powder but want to make it hot, you can add a little cayenne pepper.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Toast Curry Powder" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21810" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Toast-Curry-Powder.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Add the curry powder to the skillet and continue to saute it with the ginger, garlic, and oil for another minute or so. This toasts the spices and amplifies their flavor.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Tomato Sauce" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21808" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Tomato-Sauce.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Next add one 15oz. can of tomato sauce. If you live in a country that doesnt have tomato sauce, you can use any pured tomato product, although you may have to add salt to the sauce later.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Heat Sauce Through" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21818" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Heat-Sauce-Through.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Add the tomato sauce to the skillet, turn the heat up to medium, and let it heat through. Taste the sauce and add salt if needed (I didnt add any, but it will depend on the tomato sauce you use).
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Add Boiled Potatoes" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21811" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Add-Boiled-Potatoes.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Add the boiled and drained potatoes, then stir to coat them in the sauce. If it seems really thick and pasty at this point, add a couple tablespoons of water.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Add Four Eggs" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21814" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Add-Four-Eggs.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Make four little dips or wells in the mixture, then crack a large egg into each one. Place a lid on the skillet and let it come to a simmer. Simmer the eggs for 6-10 minutes, or until cooked through (if you prefer a runny egg, let it simmer for less time). Alternately, you can fry the eggs in a separate skillet and just place them on top of the potatoes before serving.
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Poached Eggs and Cilantro" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21812" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Poached-Eggs-Cilantro.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Lastly, roughly chop some fresh cilantro and sprinkle over top. Perfection!
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Curried Potatoes with Poached Eggs - BudgetBytes.com" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-21805" height="480" src="http://static.budgetbytes.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Curried-Potatoes-and-Eggs-above.jpg" width="640"/>
          <em>
           Tons of flavor, very filling, simple, and cheap. Just my style. (
           <a href="http://www.budgetbytes.com/2010/09/naan/">
            Homemade naan
           </a>
           is awesome for dipping in the sauce.)
          </em>
         </p>
