                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <u>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         Five Disparities that the Bible Hates, and every Christian should
Also
                         <o:p>
                         </o:p>
                        </span>
                       </u>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DzIvJOdDbvo/Umxu7ArlGbI/AAAAAAAACxk/Tr3QS7XvvDo/s1600/no+equal.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-DzIvJOdDbvo/Umxu7ArlGbI/AAAAAAAACxk/Tr3QS7XvvDo/s320/no+equal.jpg" width="287"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       Introduction:
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      Often times we think
that it is sufficient to ignore the injustices around us. We become accustomed to using terms such as
someone ought to or somebody should which allows us to neglect our personal
responsibility. Some people even have a
live in the clouds theology that frees them from their human responsibility,
but they fail to realize that when God gave man and woman free will, he also
gave them responsibility. Thus, the
question of the ages becomes, Am I my brother or sisters keeper? Gods answer
would be a resounding yes. The second commandment is to love ones neighbor as
thyself. Therefore, disparities,
whether be it economic, generational, gender, racial, or even of accessibility issues, such disparities should be rejected by a Christian. Not only should Christians
reject such disparities, they must proactively work against them.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        Generational Disparities
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-p6cA4j6sEnI/UmxrphS3g0I/AAAAAAAACw4/FH9qD0wl-EI/s1600/generational.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="213" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-p6cA4j6sEnI/UmxrphS3g0I/AAAAAAAACw4/FH9qD0wl-EI/s320/generational.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      It is important to remember that
the church is a place to serve for all generations. The church is not just a place for old men,
but for everyone. The Bible has used
many young people such as Joseph, David, Solomon, a young Jesus teaching in the
temple, or a young John Mark Paul encouraged Timothy to not let anyone despise
his youth by telling Timothy to not become discouraged by generational disparities.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Jesus delivers some tough words in
Mark 9:42 were He states that if anyone causes a child to sin, it is better for
him to tie a millstone to his neck and jump into the sea. When you think about such a death, it seems
horrifying, and very scary. But, I
believe that the weight of the punishment can show how great the offense is
considered to be.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .25in;">
                      When the church fails to
proactively reach out to her young, to teach them, help them to develop, and
create an environment conducive to their spiritual growth are they not guilty
of being offensive? For instance, if I
invite someone to my home and my house is a mess, and the guest room I have
them sleeping in has dirty clothes all over the bed and I make no movement to
clean things up, is that not me being offensive, by not creating an environment
conducive for my company? Therefore, the church must step up and accept her
responsibility for the young generations because they are the church of the
future.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                      <span style="font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
                        Similarly, I remember working in a nursing home and
woman named Sister Miller told me about how she was completely neglected by
biological family, but also spiritual family.
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
                       </span>
                       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
                        For this reason, the church I was with at the time started a nursing
home
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Times, 'Times New Roman', serif; font-size: 16px;">
                       ministry
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;">
                       <span style="font-size: 12pt;">
                        to
                       </span>
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Times, 'Times New Roman', serif; font-size: 16px; text-indent: 0.25in;">
                       minister
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Times, 'Times New Roman', serif; font-size: 12pt; text-indent: 0.25in;">
                       to every saint in that facility.
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Times, 'Times New Roman', serif; font-size: 12pt; text-indent: 0.25in;">
                      </span>
                      <span style="font-family: Times, 'Times New Roman', serif; font-size: 12pt; text-indent: 0.25in;">
                       The church must not neglect her young, but
she also must not neglect her old, because the church is for everyone.
                      </span>
                     </div>

                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        Persons with Disabilities &amp; Accessibility Disparities
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-iiICkt0S0RU/UmxtIDzKjVI/AAAAAAAACxE/pumhYXZl6wQ/s1600/disability.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="245" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-iiICkt0S0RU/UmxtIDzKjVI/AAAAAAAACxE/pumhYXZl6wQ/s320/disability.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      In Matthew 11:2-6, we find that
John the Baptist was in prison having what many believe to have been a moment
of weakness, when he questioned Jesus because he wanted to know if Christ was
the real deal. Jesus responded with
this:
                      <span style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                       Go and tell John what you
hear and see:
                      </span>
                      <b>
                       <sup>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                         5
                        </span>
                       </sup>
                      </b>
                      <span style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                       the blind receive their sight and the
lame walk, lepers are cleansed and the deaf hear, and the dead are raised up,
and the poor have good news preached to them.
                      </span>
                      <b>
                       <sup>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                         6
                        </span>
                       </sup>
                      </b>
                      <span style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                       And blessed is the one who is not
offended by me.
                       <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
                       </span>
                       <a href="http://www.blogger.com/blogger.g?blogID=1288559888104826415#_ftn1" name="_ftnref1" title="">
                        <sup>
                         <!--[if !supportFootnotes]-->
                         <sup>
                          <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
                           [1]
                          </span>
                         </sup>
                         <!--[endif]-->
                        </sup>
                       </a>
                      </span>
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      When Jesus minister was questioned
by his dear cousin, John the Baptist, he responded with how he had ministered
to persons with disabilities. In light of this, we see the mighty importance in
Jesus ministry to offer care to those who have disabilities, and are handicapped.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Many people with disabilities do
not have the luxury of hand picking whatever denomination or church they want
to go to. Instead, they must settle for
whichever one is accessible to them.
This is unfortunate, because a major part of Jesus ministry was
ministering to persons with disabilities.
Jesus actually took time out to let those who had disabilities know that
He loved them and cared about them. I
wonder if the church has done the same.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .25in;">
                      This means that the church must be
aware of its surroundings, and the needs of people. People with disabilities who use wheelchairs
need ramps, and hearing impaired people need sign language interpreters. The visually impaired church member needs
accessible materials and perhaps guidance around the facility... The members with intellectual disabilities
needs a unique curriculum. God sees just
as much value in the person with a disability that He does in all of us,
therefore these worshippers should not be treated as second class citizens in
our church. We should minister to them, just as Jesus did, and just as we
minister to everyone else.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .25in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        Economic Disparities
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Y0ZqSES99Vs/Umxtdbfv1BI/AAAAAAAACxM/wQqi0w6Z4F0/s1600/homeless.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Y0ZqSES99Vs/Umxtdbfv1BI/AAAAAAAACxM/wQqi0w6Z4F0/s320/homeless.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      The Bible is quite clear about
taking care of the poor. The Old
Testament features such theories as the year of Jubilee, or of not harvesting
the corners of ones field so that the poor can have it, or the social justice
component in every last one of the Major, and Minor Prophets. Also, Jesus
himself was devoted to the cause of the have-nots: He once declared in Luke
4:18 that He had come to preach to the poor. During his ministry, Jesus
encourages giving a cold cup of water in His name; He also admonishes the
Samaritan who gave healthcare, and financial assets to the man beaten and left
for dead on the road. Jesus also creates
the criteria for the separating of the sheep from the goat based on acts of
social kindness. The 1
                      <sup>
                       st
                      </sup>
                      Century church seemed to understand this very well, because as Acts 2:42-47
shows, the early Christians were selling their goods and sharing them among
those who had need.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      I propose that the church does a
better job of taking care of her members, and also those in the community: It
is not optional as it is mandated.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      <i>
                       Also,
associated with Economics is Educational Disparities
                      </i>
                      the Bible states:
                      <b>
                       <sup>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                         13
                        </span>
                       </sup>
                      </b>
                      <span style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                       Now when they saw the boldness of Peter
and John, and perceived that they were uneducated, common men, they were
astonished. And they recognized that they had been with Jesus.
                      </span>
                      <b>
                       <sup>
                        <span style='font-family: Arial; mso-bidi-font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                         14
                        </span>
                       </sup>
                      </b>
                      <span style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                       But seeing the man who was healed
standing beside them, they had nothing to say in opposition.
                      </span>
                      <a href="http://www.blogger.com/blogger.g?blogID=1288559888104826415#_ftn2" name="_ftnref2" title="">
                       <sup>
                        <!--[if !supportFootnotes]-->
                        <sup>
                         <span style="font-family: Calibri;">
                          <span style="font-size: xx-small;">
                           [2]
                          </span>
                         </span>
                        </sup>
                        <!--[endif]-->
                       </sup>
                      </a>
                      <span style='font-family: "Times New Roman";'>
                       (Acts 4:13-14)
                      </span>
                      <i>
                       <o:p>
                       </o:p>
                      </i>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Often an institutional education is
used as a conduit of social mobility.
However, many do not have access to such opportunities. However, in the Kingdom of God, this does not
limit one from the service of God. It
might rightfully limit one from being a doctor, a lawyer, or a schoolteacher,
but it will not limit one from being a servant of God.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      In Acts 4:13-14, these men not
being educated to become a testimony that they had been with Jesus. Interestingly enough, there is quite a bit
you can learn from reading the word of God, and I would argue some of the most
important lessons can be learned from it.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      In our society today, many are
capable but simply cannot afford the blessing of a higher education, or even a
high school education. Therefore, in
Mark 9:42 when Jesus says that one He gives even a cold cup of water in His
name will certainly not lose His reward. How do we apply this to our 21
                      <sup>
                       st
                      </sup>
                      postmodern world? What does a cold cup
of water look like in our context? In some parts of the world, it may be a cold
cup of water, but other parts of the more industrialized world, it may be
social mobility. One of the greatest tools for social mobility is education.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .25in;">
                      Therefore, I proposed that the
church, in an effort to live out what Psalm 68:5 states about being a parent to
the parentless, we must make an effort to help high dropouts attain G.E.D.s,
and poor gifted students to find resources for seeking out higher education.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .25in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        Gender Disparities
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-S1vmIqKBYFU/Umxt4FFC80I/AAAAAAAACxU/fyWW0lcn6XE/s1600/ChurchWomenClipArt.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="258" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-S1vmIqKBYFU/Umxt4FFC80I/AAAAAAAACxU/fyWW0lcn6XE/s320/ChurchWomenClipArt.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Unfortunately, due to ignorance,
and/or malice, some Christians have even created disparities for women. However, one who sees God as a chauvinist and
therefore they behave chauvinistically by seeking to oppress women does not really
know God! One who knows God, would know
that God created man and women as equals. Man is not superior to woman, and
neither is woman superior to man. God
created man and woman as a chord that when struck together, creates a beautiful
sound.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Some have argued that as a result
of the fall of humanity, woman was placed under man, but even if one has such
an interpretation, it would have been crushed on the cross of Christ: where
Christ reversed the curse of the fall, and were Christ reversed the curse of
the law.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Without women, there would be no
Christianity. Women were the most
devoted followers of Christ. A woman was the first missionary in Samaria (John
4); it was the women at his cross (John 19:25), and at his tomb. It was a woman who first saw the resurrected
Jesus (Mark 16:9). It was a woman who
housed the Corinthian Church, and was so influential that in the letter to
Corinth Paul called a part of the church
in Corinth as Chloes people (1 Corinthian. 1:11). It was a woman, with the help of her husband,
that set straight the zealous but slightly inaccurate Apollos (Acts 18:26).In
Pauls farewell address in Romans 16, he thanks at least five women (Phoebe,
Prisca, Mary, Junia, Nereus sister) for their loyal service to his ministry
and to Christ. Even in the Old Testament
outside of the New Covenant, there were prophetesses such as Deborah (Judges
4:4), Miriam (Exodus 15:20), Huldah (2 Chronicles 34:22), Anna (Luke 2:36), or
Phillips Daughters (Acts 21:9). In the
Bible, we find God using everything to serve His people. He uses a donkey
(Numbers 22:30), a rock (Exodus 17:6), a cloud (Exodus 13:22), among
others. A chauvinist must ask himself,
if God can use a donkey, then certainly God can use a woman!
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .25in;">
                      Therefore, I propose that, out of
respect for our daughters, we create an environment that welcomes the gifts,
and the talents of both men and women so that the beautiful chord can be struck
that can define both sexes working together.
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .25in;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <span style="font-size: large;">
                        Racial Disparities
                        <o:p>
                        </o:p>
                       </span>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-9We9Pb3uvXw/UmxuU7kLK5I/AAAAAAAACxc/tlFbPOCbq9Y/s1600/MulticulturaliStock_000010892712small.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="240" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-9We9Pb3uvXw/UmxuU7kLK5I/AAAAAAAACxc/tlFbPOCbq9Y/s320/MulticulturaliStock_000010892712small.jpg" width="320"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align: center;">
                      <b>
                       <br/>
                      </b>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Christianity was born in the midst
of diversity. Acts 2 tells us of a
diverse audience from many nations and languages gathered together. Peter preached the first Gospel sermon, and
at least 3,000 were baptized. In both
the Old and New Testament, we find people from all different kinds of ethnic
backgrounds being used by God.
Furthermore, throughout history, we find all different kinds of people
from various backgrounds being used by God.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      Segregation is a work of the
Devil! God seeks to unite, not divide. As Paul wrote, Division makes the cross of
no effect. God is not the inventor of
race. Race is a Darwinian ideal that
seeks to dehumanize, categorize, and separate humanity among artificial
lines.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      The truth is that all humans DNA
are 99.9% similar. Therefore, racial
differences are primarily a matter of pigment, just as two poodles color
differences are only a matter of fur.
How silly it would be to see two poodles fighting because of the color
of each others fur?
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: .5in;">
                      One of the saddest issues that
racism causes is the fact that people miss out in the beauty of each others
cultures. I believe that everyones
culture, and ethnic heritage contains gems.
However, when one fails to realize this, they can miss the great joys of
diversity. Furthermore, the best
congregations I have ever been a part of are diverse congregations. The reasons
are because just like two heads are better than one, two cultures are better
than one: you get the best of both worlds.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpFirst" style="margin-left: 0in; mso-add-space: auto; text-indent: .5in;">
                      Finally, if a person hates diversity, they will hate
Heaven. They might as well begin
preparing themselves for Hell. Because
as Revelation 5:9 says, people from every nation and every tongue, will be in
Heaven. This means that Heaven will be
the most diverse place that many of us have ever experienced, and if diversity
bothers you that much, than I would suggest that you stop singing songs about
Heaven. As great it will be, for a racist, a bigot, or a segregationist, it
will not be that great.
                      <o:p>
                      </o:p>
                     </div>
                     <div class="MsoListParagraphCxSpLast" style="margin-left: 1.0in; mso-add-space: auto;">

                     </div>

function mce_success_cb(resp){
    $('#mce-success-response').hide();
    $('#mce-error-response').hide();
    if (resp.result=="success"){
        $('#mce-'+resp.result+'-response').show();
        $('#mce-'+resp.result+'-response').html(resp.msg);
        $('#mc-embedded-subscribe-form').each(function(){
            this.reset();
     });
    } else {
        var index = -1;
        var msg;
        try {
            var parts = resp.msg.split(' - ',2);
            if (parts[1]==undefined){
                msg = resp.msg;
            } else {
                i = parseInt(parts[0]);
                if (i.toString() == parts[0]){
                    index = parts[0];
                    msg = parts[1];
                } else {
                    index = -1;
                    msg = resp.msg;
                }
            }
        } catch(e){
            index = -1;
            msg = resp.msg;
        }
        try{
            if (index== -1){
                $('#mce-'+resp.result+'-response').show();
                $('#mce-'+resp.result+'-response').html(msg);

';

                    $(input_id).focus();

                    $('#mce-'+resp.result+'-response').show();
                    $('#mce-'+resp.result+'-response').html(msg);
                }
            }
        } catch(e){
            $('#mce-'+resp.result+'-response').show();
