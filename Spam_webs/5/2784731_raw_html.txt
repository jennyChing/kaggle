   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    United States of Africa
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>

        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1440" data-file-width="1525" height="283" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/African_continent-en.svg/300px-African_continent-en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/African_continent-en.svg/450px-African_continent-en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/African_continent-en.svg/600px-African_continent-en.svg.png 2x" width="300"/>

        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_African_countries" title="List of African countries">

     <p>
      The
      <b>
       United States of Africa
      </b>
      is a proposed concept for a
      <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">
       federation
      </a>
      of some or all of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_African_countries" title="List of African countries">
       54
      </a>
      <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">
       sovereign states
      </a>
      on the
      <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">
       African
      </a>
      continent. The concept takes its origin from
      <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Garvey" title="Marcus Garvey">
       Marcus Garvey
      </a>
      's 1924 poem,
      <i>
       Hail, United States of Africa
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hail_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hail-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Independent-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-African_Union_2002_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-African_Union_2002-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Former
      <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">
       Libyan
      </a>
      leader
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muammar_al-Gaddafi" title="Muammar al-Gaddafi">
       Muammar al-Gaddafi
      </a>
      , who was the 2009
      <a href="/wiki/Chairperson_of_the_African_Union" title="Chairperson of the African Union">
       Chairperson
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">
       African Union
      </a>
      (AU), advanced the idea of a United States of Africa at two regional African summits: first in June 2007 in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conakry,_Guinea" title="Conakry, Guinea">
       Conakry, Guinea
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      and again in February 2009 in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Addis_Ababa,_Ethiopia" title="Addis Ababa, Ethiopia">
       Addis Ababa, Ethiopia
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-divisions_5-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-divisions-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Gaddafi had previously pushed for its creation at a 2000 summit in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lom%C3%A9,_Togo" title="Lom, Togo">
       Lom, Togo
      </a>
      ,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      having described the AU as a failure on a number of occasions; Gaddafi asserted that only a true
      <a href="/wiki/Pan-Africanism" title="Pan-Africanism">
       pan-African
      </a>
      state can provide stability and wealth to Africa. A number of senior AU members also support the proposed federation, believing that it could bring peace to a 'new' Africa.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Oumar_Konar%C3%A9" title="Alpha Oumar Konar">
       Alpha Oumar Konar
      </a>
      , former President of
      <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">
       Mali
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Chairperson_of_the_African_Union_Commission" title="Chairperson of the African Union Commission">
       former Chairperson
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/African_Union_Commission" title="African Union Commission">
       African Union Commission
      </a>
      , spoke in favor of the concept at the commemoration of
      <a href="/wiki/Africa_Day" title="Africa Day">
       Africa Day
      </a>
      , on May 25, 2006.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">
       Origins
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_of_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4857" data-file-width="3170" height="230" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg/150px-Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg/225px-Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg/300px-Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg 2x" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Marcus_Garvey_1924-08-05.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Garvey" title="Marcus Garvey">
         Marcus Garvey
        </a>
        in 1924
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      The "United States of Africa" was mentioned first by
      <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Garvey" title="Marcus Garvey">
       Marcus Garvey
      </a>
      in his poem
      <i>
       Hail, United States of Africa
      </i>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hail_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Hail-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in 1924. Garvey's ideas deeply influenced the birth of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan-Africanist" title="Pan-Africanist">
       Pan-Africanist
      </a>
      movement which culminated in 1945 with the Fifth
      <a href="/wiki/Pan-African_Congress" title="Pan-African Congress">
       Pan-African Congress
      </a>
      in
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manchester,_United_Kingdom" title="Manchester, United Kingdom">
       Manchester, United Kingdom
      </a>
      , attended by
      <a href="/wiki/W._E._B._Du_Bois" title="W. E. B. Du Bois">
       W. E. B. Du Bois
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Patrice_Lumumba" title="Patrice Lumumba">
       Patrice Lumumba
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/George_Padmore" title="George Padmore">
       George Padmore
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jomo_Kenyatta" title="Jomo Kenyatta">
       Jomo Kenyatta
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Dudley_Thompson" title="Dudley Thompson">
       Dudley Thompson
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Kwame_Nkrumah" title="Kwame Nkrumah">
       Kwame Nkrumah
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Independent-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Later, Nkrumah and
      <a href="/wiki/Haile_Selassie" title="Haile Selassie">
       Haile Selassie
      </a>
      took the idea forward to form the 37 nation
      <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_African_Unity" title="Organisation of African Unity">
       Organisation of African Unity
      </a>
      , the precursor of the
      <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">
       African Union
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The idea of a multinational unifying African state was noted in the
      <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
       French
      </a>
      publication
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Le_Monde_diplomatique" title="Le Monde diplomatique">
        Le Monde diplomatique
       </a>
      </i>
      as a successor to the medieval African empires: the
      <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Empire" title="Ethiopian Empire">
       Ethiopian Empire
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Ghana_Empire" title="Ghana Empire">
       Ghana Empire
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Mali_Empire" title="Mali Empire">
       Mali Empire
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Songhai_Empire" title="Songhai Empire">
       Songhai Empire
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Benin_Empire" title="Benin Empire">
       Benin Empire
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Kanem_Empire" title="Kanem Empire">
       Kanem Empire
      </a>
      , and other historic nation states.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
       <a href="#cite_note-10">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        10
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">
       Demographics
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_of_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      The proposed federation would
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_countries_and_outlying_territories_by_total_area" title="List of countries and outlying territories by total area">
       have the largest total territory
      </a>
      of any state, exceeding the
      <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">
       Russian Federation
      </a>
      . It would also be the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population" title="List of countries by population">
       third most populous state
      </a>
      after
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">
       China
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">
       India
      </a>
      , and with a population speaking an estimated
      <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_Africa" title="Languages of Africa">
       2,000 languages
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Potential_development">
       Potential development
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_of_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Potential development">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <div class="thumb tright">
      <div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;">
       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg">
        <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="567" data-file-width="364" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg/150px-Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg/225px-Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg/300px-Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg 2x" width="150"/>
       </a>
       <div class="thumbcaption">
        <div class="magnify">
         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Muammar_al-Gaddafi-09122003.jpg" title="Enlarge">
         </a>
        </div>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muammar_al-Gaddafi" title="Muammar al-Gaddafi">
         Muammar al-Gaddafi
        </a>
        in 2003
       </div>
      </div>
     </div>
     <p>
      In February 2009, upon being elected chairman of the 53-nation African Union in Ethiopia, Gaddafi told the assembled African leaders: "I shall continue to insist that our sovereign countries work to achieve the United States of Africa."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">
       BBC
      </a>
      reported that Gaddafi had proposed "a single African military force, a single currency and a single passport for Africans to move freely around the continent". Other African leaders stated they would study the proposal's implications, and re-discuss it in May 2009.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-divisions_5-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-divisions-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The focus for developing the United States of Africa far has been on building subdivisions of Africa - the proposed
      <a href="/wiki/East_African_Federation" title="East African Federation">
       East African Federation
      </a>
      can be seen as an example of this. Former
      <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Senegal" title="List of Presidents of Senegal">
       President
      </a>
      of
      <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">
       Senegal
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Abdoulaye_Wade" title="Abdoulaye Wade">
       Abdoulaye Wade
      </a>
      , has indicated that the United States of Africa may exist from as early as 2017.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The African Union, by contrast, has set itself the task of building a "united and integrated" Africa by 2025.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Gaddafi had also indicated that the proposed federation may extend as far west as the
      <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">
       Caribbean
      </a>
      :
      <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">
       Haiti
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">
       Jamaica
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">
       Dominican Republic
      </a>
      , and other islands featuring a large
      <a href="/wiki/African_diaspora" title="African diaspora">
       African diaspora
      </a>
      , may be invited to join.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
       <a href="#cite_note-14">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        14
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="National_views">
       National views
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_of_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: National views">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      African integration generally has had a higher level of support among poorer, less developed, and smaller African countries versus less poor, more developed, and larger African countries.
     </p>
     <p>
      The nations of
      <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">
       Eritrea
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">
       Ghana
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">
       Senegal
      </a>
      , and
      <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">
       Zimbabwe
      </a>
      , have supported an African federation.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
       <a href="#cite_note-15">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        15
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Others such as
      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
       South Africa
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">
       Kenya
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">
       Nigeria
      </a>
      , and North African countries such as
      <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">
       Morocco
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">
       Egypt
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">
       Tunisia
      </a>
      , and post-revolution
      <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">
       Libya
      </a>
      have shown less interest in the idea.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_2-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-Independent-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Support appears to be inversely proportional to a nation's power and influence. Doubts have been raised about whether the goal of a unified Africa can ever be achieved while ongoing problems of conflict and poverty persist throughout the continent.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
       <a href="#cite_note-16">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        16
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Gaddafi">
       Gaddafi
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_of_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gaddafi">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Gaddafi also received criticism for his involvement in the movement, and lack of support for the idea from among other African leaders.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
       <a href="#cite_note-17">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        17
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      A week before Gaddafi's death during the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Libyan_Civil_War" title="2011 Libyan Civil War">
       Libyan Civil War
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">
       South African
      </a>
      President
      <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Zuma" title="Jacob Zuma">
       Jacob Zuma
      </a>
      expressed relief at the regime's downfall, complaining that Gaddafi had been "intimidating" many African heads of state in an effort to gain influence throughout the continent and suggesting that the African Union will function better without Gaddafi and his repeated proposals for a unitary African government.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
       <a href="#cite_note-18">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        18
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Cape_Verde">
       Cape Verde
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_of_Africa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cape Verde">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">
      Main article:
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde-European_Union_relations" title="Cape Verde-European Union relations">
       Cape Verde-European Union relations
      </a>
     </div>
     <p>
      Cape Verde was formerly in favour of an African federation, however as of 2014, it has been lessening its African integration. It is now focusing on
      <a href="/wiki/European_integration" title="European integration">
       European integration
      </a>
      , as all other countries in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macaronesian" title="Macaronesian">
       Macaronesian
      </a>
      region are in the EU (Canary Islands, Azores, and Madeira). Furthermore, the majority of the people of Cape Verde do not consider themselves African, and have many cultural and genetic links to Portugal and the rest of Europe. In addition, Cape Verde was part of Portugal for 515 years. Over 70% of people in Cabo Verde are mixed (African and European.)
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition, the vast majority of economic investment, aid, and tourism comes from Europe. The people of Cape Verde speak Portuguese, a European language. The leaders of Cape Verde have proposed withdrawing from
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ECOWAS" title="ECOWAS">
       ECOWAS
      </a>
      , a West African integration organization.
     </p>
