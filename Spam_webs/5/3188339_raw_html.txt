     <p>
      <i>
       <b>
        A Suitable Girl
       </b>
      </i>
      is an upcoming novel by
      <a href="/wiki/Vikram_Seth" title="Vikram Seth">
       Vikram Seth
      </a>
      , which is a sequel to his 1993 book
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Suitable_Boy" title="A Suitable Boy">
        A Suitable Boy
       </a>
       ,
      </i>
      due for publication in 2016.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_1-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flood_2-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Flood-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Seth has stated that the book will be set in the present, rather than in 1952 where
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Suitable_Boy" title="A Suitable Boy">
        A Suitable Boy
       </a>
      </i>
      finished, and will therefore be what Seth calls a "jump sequel".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_1-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-BBC-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flood_2-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-Flood-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After
      <a href="/wiki/Penguin_Group" title="Penguin Group">
       Penguin Publisher
      </a>
      deciding to back out,
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_3-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      the book now will be published in autumn of 2016 by
      <a href="/wiki/Orion_Publishing_Group" title="Orion Publishing Group">
       Orion Publishers
      </a>
      , who had published
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/A_Suitable_Boy" title="A Suitable Boy">
        A Suitable Boy
       </a>
      </i>
      too.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian2_4-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-Guardian2-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
