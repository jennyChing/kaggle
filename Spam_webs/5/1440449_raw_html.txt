         <div class="headline_area">
          <h1 class="entry-title">
           How to Make Homemade Ginger Ale
          </h1>
         </div>
         <div class="format_text entry-content">
          <div class="shareaholic-canvas" data-app="share_buttons" data-app-id="4627250" data-link="http://theNourishingCook.com/ginger-ale/" data-summary="" data-title="How to Make Homemade Ginger Ale">
          </div>
          <p>
           <a href="http://thenourishingcook.com.s96112.gridserver.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/Ginger-Ale-4-10-Web-Size.jpg">
            <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
             <img alt="" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1522" height="333" src="http://thenourishingcook.com.s96112.gridserver.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/Ginger-Ale-4-10-Web-Size.jpg" title="Ginger Ale 4-10 Web Size" width="500"/>
             <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://theNourishingCook.com/ginger-ale/&amp;media=http://thenourishingcook.com.s96112.gridserver.com/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/Ginger-Ale-4-10-Web-Size.jpg&amp;description=How to Make Homemade Ginger Ale')">
             </span>
            </span>
           </a>
          </p>
          <p>
           Do not mistake this recipe for the ginger ale that you get at the grocery store. It is much stronger, and actually tastes like ginger! Sally Fallon says in
           <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/nourishing-traditions" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Amazon - link to Nourishing Traditions cookbook">
            Nourishing Traditions
           </a>
           to drink it warm and dilute it with water or carbonated water if youd like. It is refreshing, and especially good for menstrual cramps or other tummy aches. I love this stuff, and will make it again! I used the full amount of ginger, but I am a ginger freakoid. It is not as sweet as many are used to, so you may want to use additional
           <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/rapadura" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Rapadura Sugar">
            Rapadura
           </a>
           if you want a treat.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span id="more-1429">
           </span>
          </p>
          <h2>
           Tips for making Ginger Ale
          </h2>
          <ul>
           <li>
            I used
            <a href="http://thenourishingcook.com/homemade-pickled-ginger/" target="_self" title="Fermented Ginger">
             fermented ginger
            </a>
            to replace the fresh ginger in this recipe, and the juice of it to replace half of the whey in this recipe.
           </li>
           <li>
            Use half the amount of ginger noted below if you want a lighter tasting ginger ale.
           </li>
           <li>
            One thing I would do for this is to add more
            <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/rapadura" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Rapadura Sugar">
             Rapadura
            </a>
            if serving to others who prefer a sweeter taste. I make a
            <a href="http://thenourishingcook.com/need-an-inexpensive-maple-syrup-replacement/">
             Rapadura simple syrup
            </a>
            and keep it in the fridge for guests to use in iced tea &amp; this &amp; other beverages. Its also fun to flavor simple syrup with mint, vanilla or other flavors.
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h1>
           Old Fashioned Traditional Ginger Ale
          </h1>
          <h3>
           <span style="font-size: 1.17em; line-height: 1.5em;">
            Difficulty:
           </span>
          </h3>
          <p>
           Easy
          </p>
          <h3>
           Page in
           <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0967089735?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=nouricook-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=0967089735" target="_blank" title="Buy Nourishing Traditions at Amazon.com">
            NT
           </a>
           : 586
          </h3>
          <h3>
           Equipment:
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            1/2 gallon
            <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/mason-jars" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Wide Mouth Mason Jars">
             mason jars
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/microplane" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Amazon - Microplane Fine Grater">
             Microplane
            </a>
            grater (for ginger)
           </li>
           <li>
            Fine
            <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/fine-strainer" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Stainless Steel Fine Strainer">
             strainer
            </a>
            &amp;
            <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/cheesecloth" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Cheesecloth ">
             cheesecloth
            </a>
            (to strain
            <a href="http://thenourishingcook.com/1-whey-cool/" target="_blank" title="The Nourishing Cook - Whey">
             whey
            </a>
            from yogurt)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3>
           Ingredients:
          </h3>
          <ul>
           <li>
            3/4 cup ginger, peeled and finely chopped or grated
           </li>
           <li>
            1/2 cup fresh lime juice
           </li>
           <li>
            1/4  1/2 cup
            <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/rapadura" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Rapadura Sugar">
             Rapadura
            </a>
            (See
            <a href="http://thenourishingcook.com/kitchen-cooking">
             Resources
            </a>
            )
           </li>
           <li>
            1/4 cup
            <a href="http://thenourishingcook.com/1-whey-cool/" target="_blank" title="The Nourishing Cook - Whey">
             whey
            </a>
           </li>
           <li>
            2 teaspoons
            <a class="pretty-link-keyword" href="http://theNourishingCook.com/sea-salt" rel="nofollow" target="_blank" title="Celtic Sea Salt">
             sea salt
            </a>
            (See
            <a href="http://thenourishingcook.com/kitchen-cooking">
             Resources
            </a>
            )
           </li>
           <li>
            2 quarts filtered water
           </li>
          </ul>
          <h3>
           Preparation:
          </h3>
          <p>
           This is a most refreshing drink, taken in small amounts with meals and as a pick-me-up after outside work in the sun.
          </p>
