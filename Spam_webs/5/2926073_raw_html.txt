    <h1 class="grid_9 omega" id="page-header">
     Garden features
    </h1>
    <div class="grid_7 suffix_2 omega" id="main-content">
     <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="349" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/Y5-0wXmz5Ac" width="560">
     </iframe>
     <p>
     </p>
     <p class="highlight">
      <em>
       "Benmore is one of the most powerful gardens I have seen and is a place of vital mystery and enchantment." - The Washington Post
      </em>
     </p>
     <blockquote>
     </blockquote>
     <p>
      <a href="assets/images/gardens/benmore/Benmore_Botanic_Garden__REdwoods_1.jpg" rel="lightbox" title="The Redwood Avenue">
       <img alt="The Redwood Avenue " class="rightpic" height="169" src="assets/images/gardens/benmore/Benmore_Botanic_Garden__REdwoods.jpg" title="The Redwood Avenue" width="170"/>
      </a>
      Benmore Botanic Garden in its majestic mountain setting
is a vital link to disappearing wilderness.
     </p>
     <p>
      Visitors enter the Garden through the great
      <a href="the-gardens/benmore/garden-features/sierra-redwood-avenue">
       Redwood Avenue
      </a>
      planted in 1863 by Piers
Patrick, a wealthy American who had bought the estate the year before.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="assets/images/gardens/benmore/Formal_Garden_1.jpg" rel="lightbox" title="Formal Garden">
       <img alt="Formal Garden" class="leftpic" height="116" src="assets/images/gardens/benmore/Formal_Garden.jpg" width="170"/>
      </a>
     </p>
     <p>
      Another popular feature of the Garden is
      <a href="the-gardens/benmore/garden-features/pucks-hut">
       Puck's
Hut
      </a>
      which celebrates the memory of Isaac Bayley
Balfour. He was Regius Keeper of the Royal Botanic Garden Edinburgh from 1888
to 1922 and it was he who first thought of creating a west coast botanic
garden. The wooden hut designed by
Robert Lorimer and tiled in red cedar can be found at the head of the
      <a href="the-gardens/benmore/garden-features/the-formal-garden">
       Formal Garden
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      A much photographed feature is the bronze statue 'A boy with two
dolphins' which dates back to 1875 and is a legacy of James Duncan, a
Greenock sugar refiner, who took over the house in 1870. He made numerous changes
to Benmore House and to the estate. He was responsible for the Fernery which is
in Glen Massan, about 30 metres above the valley floor. This steep area of
hillside has been developed to create plantings to represent the different
flora of Bhutan, Chile, Japan
and Tasmania.
     </p>
     <p>
      The cast iron footbridge over the River Eachaig was opened in August
1999 and was the replacement for a bridge which collapsed in February 1998
after ten days of torrential rain.
     </p>
     <p>
      <a href="assets/images/gardens/benmore/bhutanese_glade_winter_ben_2.jpg" rel="lightbox" title="Bhutanese Glade">
       <img alt="Bhutanese Glade" class="rightpic" height="113" src="assets/images/gardens/benmore/bhutanese_glade_winter_ben_1.jpg" title="Bhutanese Glade" width="170"/>
      </a>
     </p>
