        <h1>
         When to See Your Doctor for Atrial Fibrillation
        </h1>
        <ul class="list-inline inline">
         <li class="byline">
          By Chris Iliades, MD | Medically reviewed by Pat F. Bass III, MD, MPH
         </li>
        </ul>
       </header>
      </div>
     </div>
     <span class="ehshare share-inline clearfix" data-count="true" data-description="Living%2bwith%2batrial%2bfibrillation%2bincreases%2byour%2bstroke%2brisk.%2bFind%2bout%2bthe%2bdifference%2bbetween%2bafib%2bsymptoms%2band%2bstroke%2bsymptoms%2b%25e2%2580%2594%2band%2bwhen%2bto%2bsee%2ba%2bdoctor." data-migratedbyline="By%2bChris%2bIliades%252c%2bMD%2b%257c%2bMedically%2breviewed%2bby%2bPat%2bF.%2bBass%2bIII%252c%2bMD%252c%2bMPH" data-networks='["facebook", "twitter", "pinterest", "email"]' data-title="When%2bto%2bSee%2bYour%2bDoctor%2bfor%2bAtrial%2bFibrillation" data-track-self="false" data-url="http%3a%2f%2fwww.everydayhealth.com%2fheart-health%2fwhen-to-see-your-doctor-for-atrial-fibrillation.aspx">
     </span>
     <div class="row">
      <h2 class="eh-deck">
       Living with atrial fibrillation increases your stroke risk. Find out the difference between afib symptoms and stroke symptoms  and when to see a doctor.
      </h2>
     </div>
     <div class="row">
      <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-4 col-sm-4 col-xs-12 pull-right eh-module-unit">
       <div class="right-rail clearfix" data-adreplace="">
        <section class="pull-left eh-module eh-module-border eh-module-related js-core" data-esiurl="http://www.everydayhealth.com/conditions/recommendation/getrelatedhtml/259402_0?pageId=259402&amp;maskUrl=%7e%2fheart-health%2fwhen-to-see-your-doctor-for-atrial-fibrillation.aspx&amp;zone=%2fcardio%2fafib&amp;articleType=Article&amp;userId=" data-module=" related links" data-type="dynamic recommendation" data-url=" url=/heart-health/when-to-see-your-doctor-for-atrial-fibrillation.aspx&amp;zonename=/cardio/afib&amp;type=rx:2:0|nx:8:0 ">
         <header>
          <h4>
           Don't Miss This
          </h4>
         </header>
         <div class="media">
          <a class="media-object pull-left js-promoted" data-id="rx_4005635" href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/news/drugs-heart-disease-under-scrutiny/">
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/Drugs-for-Heart-Disease-Under-Scrutiny-th.jpg"/>
          </a>
          <div class="media-body">
           <a class="media-heading caps" href="">
           </a>
           <a class="js-promoted" data-id="rx_4005635" href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/news/drugs-heart-disease-under-scrutiny/">
            Drugs for Heart Disease Under Scrutiny
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="media">
          <a class="media-object pull-left js-promoted" data-id="rx_4005886" href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/news/atrial-fibrillation-caregiver-tips/">
           <img alt="" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/gcms/AFib-What-Caregivers-Need-to-Know-RM-th.jpg"/>
          </a>
          <div class="media-body">
           <a class="media-heading caps" href="">
           </a>
           <a class="js-promoted" data-id="rx_4005886" href="http://www.everydayhealth.com/news/atrial-fibrillation-caregiver-tips/">
            Atrial Fibrillation Caregiver Tips
           </a>
          </div>
         </div>
        </section>
        <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-4 col-sm-4 col-xs-12 ad-content">
         <div class="ad-container ad-standard">
          <div class="ad-placeholder adhesive" data-adsize="300x250,300x600,300x1050" data-pos="topsticky" data-tile="5" id="adDiv5" title="ADVERTISEMENT">
          </div>
          <noscript>
           <a href="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/jump/everydayhealth/cardio/afib;p=37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Heart Health;page=a66057964569488d9f09c4a648d7eabf;bn=;content=;;pos=topsticky;sz=300x250,300x600,300x1050;tile=5;ord=20150707142228?" target="_blank">
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/ad/everydayhealth/cardio/afib;37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Heart Health;page=a66057964569488d9f09c4a648d7eabf;bn=;content=;;pos=topsticky;sz=300x250,300x600,300x1050;tile=5;ord=20150707142228?"/>
           </a>
          </noscript>
         </div>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <figure class="col-lg-3 col-md-3 col-sm-3 col-xs-12 article-image pull-left clear-left">
       <img id="eh-share-img" src="http://images.agoramedia.com/everydayhealth/cms/atrial_fibrillation_prevention_article.jpg?width=200"/>
      </figure>
      <div class="article-content">
       <div class="article-body" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Article">
        <p>
         Living with atrial fibrillation, a common form of irregular heartbeat, can put a person on edge. But a close relationship with your doctor  including knowing when to call him or her and even knowing when you might need to go to the hospital  can be reassuring.
        </p>
        <p>
         "Many patients with atrial fibrillation have no symptoms at all, they are shocked to find out they have an arrhythmia," says A. Marc Gillinov, MD, a cardiovascular surgeon at the Cleveland Clinic. If you have been diagnosed with atrial fibrillation, you should call your atrial fibrillation doctor any time you have symptoms. In most cases, these symptoms can be prevented with treatment.
        </p>
        <p>
         Symptoms, when they do exist, are quite variable. Patients need to know what atrial fibrillation symptoms to watch out for, but they also need to watch out for symptoms of stroke, which is the most dangerous complication of atrial fibrillation," says Sandeep K. Jain, MD, a cardiologist and cardiac electrophysiology specialist (atrial fibrillation doctor) at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center.
        </p>
        <p>
         A recent study published in the
         <em>
          New England Journal of Medicine
         </em>
         found that atrial fibrillation puts you at risk for a stroke even if you don't necessarily have afib symptoms. The study followed 2,580 patients 65 and older who had a pacemaker but had not been diagnosed with atrial fibrillation. When the researchers checked pacemaker readings for atrial fibrillation over three months, they found that 10 percent had periods of atrial fibrillation without symptoms. The risk of stroke in these patients was 13 percent.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          When to See a Doctor for Atrial Fibrillation Symptoms
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Atrial fibrillation occurs when the upper chambers of your heart start to beat in a disorganized way because of abnormal electrical impulses. This causes the upper part of your heart to quiver or flutter. You may feel your heart is beating too fast, beating too hard, or skipping beats. Another word for these symptoms is "palpitations." Other symptoms of atrial fibrillation may include:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Shortness of breath
         </li>
         <li>
          Weakness or fatigue
         </li>
         <li>
          Fatigue or trouble breathing with exercise
         </li>
         <li>
          Dizziness or fainting
         </li>
         <li>
          Mental confusion
         </li>
         <li>
          Chest pain
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-4 col-sm-4 col-xs-12 pull-right clear-right ad-content">
         <div class="ad-container ad-standard">
          <div class="ad-placeholder" data-adsize="[[300,250],[300,600]]" data-pos="bottomsticky" data-tile="7" id="adDiv7" title="ADVERTISEMENT">
          </div>
          <noscript>
           <a href="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/jump/everydayhealth/cardio/afib;p=37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Heart Health;page=a66057964569488d9f09c4a648d7eabf;bn=;content=;;pos=bottomsticky;sz=[[300,250],[300,600]];tile=7;ord=20150707142228?" target="_blank">
            <img alt="" border="0" src="http://ad.doubleclick.net/N4213/ad/everydayhealth/cardio/afib;37;ugc=0;ct=;cat=Heart Health;page=a66057964569488d9f09c4a648d7eabf;bn=;content=;;pos=bottomsticky;sz=[[300,250],[300,600]];tile=7;ord=20150707142228?"/>
           </a>
          </noscript>
         </div>
        </div>
        <p>
         "Your doctor will want to know about any of these symptoms, says Dr. Jain. Let your doctor know if you have palpitations and let your doctor know if they are lasting longer and becoming more frequent."
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         Another set of symptoms that deserve a call to your atrial fibrillation doctor are symptoms of congestive heart failure (CHF), another common complication of atrial fibrillation. Atrial fibrillation can weaken your heart over time and cause heart failure, meaning your heart is not pumping enough blood throughout your body.
        </p>
        <p>
         Symptoms like fatigue and shortness of breath are common in both atrial fibrillation and CHF. But because blood is not moving through your body fast enough, CHF can cause fluid to leak out of your blood and into your tissues. Call your atrial fibrillation doctor if you have swelling in your feet, legs, ankles, or sudden weight gain.
        </p>
        <h2>
         <strong>
          When to Go Directly to an Emergency Room
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p>
         Sometimes you need to call 911 instead of your doctor. "If you have symptoms of fainting, chest pain, or any symptoms of a stroke, don't call your doctor. Call 911," says Dr. Gillinov.
        </p>
        <p>
         Stroke in atrial fibrillation occurs when a clot forms inside the upper chambers of your heart, breaks free, and travels to your brain. When the clot blocks blood flow to part of your brain, every second counts. Stroke can be treated but once brain cells die, brain damage can be permanent.
        </p>
        <p>
         A study published in the
         <em>
          Journal of the American Medical Society
         </em>
         reviewed 1,605 cases of people with stroke diagnosed in an emergency room and found that only 51 percent arrived by ambulance. This is about the same percentage as 11 years ago. The researchers concluded that too much time is lost during stroke. The public must be better educated about stroke symptoms and the need to call 911. An ambulance is always the best way to get to the hospital in an emergency.
        </p>
        <p>
         Symptoms of stroke are sudden and include:
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Weakness or numbness on one side of the body
         </li>
         <li>
          Confusion or trouble forming words
         </li>
         <li>
          Loss of vision
         </li>
         <li>
          Loss of balance and coordination
         </li>
         <li>
          Severe headache
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         Even if you haven't been diagnosed with atrial fibrillation, remember that you can have afib without having any symptoms and therefore can be at risk of a stroke. Atrial fibrillation increases with age and is more likely if you have high blood pressure or another type of heart condition. If you have not been checked for atrial fibrillation, talk to your doctor about it.
        </p>
        <p>
         If you are living with atrial fibrillation, call your doctor any time you have symptoms. But if you pass out, have chest pain, or any symptoms of a stroke, call 911.
        </p>
