       <div class="article-content post-3734 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-in-store-check-in category-location category-mobile-wallet category-offline-shopping category-payments category-personal-finance-management tag-europe tag-us">
        <div class="article-header">
         <h1>
          6 popular Mobile Payment Apps for Bars and Restaurants
         </h1>
         <span>
          <span>
           <a href="http://letstalkpayments.com/2013/12/">
            December 23, 2013
           </a>
          </span>
         </span>
        </div>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         You would agree that when it comes to restaurants suddenly we become a little more impatient. We cant wait in queue, cant wait to get our food and finally when we finish eating (and talking) we cant wait to get the check and pay the bill. The whole bill delivery, check, and tip process takes a minimum of 2 and sometime 3-4 trips of the waitress/waiter. It puts a lot of pressure on the overall functioning of the restaurant. To address this pain point, a number of apps have been launched over the last couple of years targeting Bars and Restaurants specifically.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Some of the popular payment apps for bars and restaurants have been described here:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          PayPal:
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://letstalkpayments.com/paypal-gets-better-at-paying-in-restaurants-new-app/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="paypal" class="alignnone wp-image-1335" height="80" src="http://n6zgo3se7pe2sazc62u1v9qe.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/paypal1.jpg" width="160"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         PayPal announced a major update to its iOS and android apps on 5th September 2013, revealing features designed to make it more convenient for customers to pay restaurant bills and take advantage of deals based on location. Some functions of the app, such as accessing and managing
         <strong>
          PayPal account
         </strong>
         and
         <strong>
          transferring money to a friend
         </strong>
         , remain the same.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         With this update, the
         <strong>
          PayPal
         </strong>
         pre order feature which was made possible through its partnership with Eat24 is available at more than
         <strong>
          1000 restaurants
         </strong>
         . When in action, nearby restaurants and shops offering its coupons and services are displayed on the phone. The app leads the customer to the full menu where the consumer can customize his order. Once the order has been placed, the customer does not have to wait in queue at the restaurant and can directly pick up his food or drink. After transaction is complete the receipt is sent to the customer via e-mail.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          PayPals partners
         </strong>
         for in store payment include  Advance Auto Parts, Abercrombie &amp; Fitch, Aropostale, Barnes &amp; Noble, American Eagle Outfitters, Foot Locker, the Home Depot, Guitar Center, JC Penney, Jamba Juice, Jos. A. Bank Clothiers, Office Depot, Nine West, Tiger Direct, Toys R Us and Rooms To Go among others.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Cover:
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://letstalkpayments.com/dont-like-waiting-for-the-cheque-in-restaurants-thats-why-cover-got-investors-like-naval-ravikant/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="cover" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3735" height="105" src="http://n6zgo3se7pe2sazc62u1v9qe.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/cover1.png" width="160"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Cover lets you
         <strong>
          pay at restaurants
         </strong>
         without waiting for the check. Its your house account, everywhere.Cover gives restaurants data to personalize service. Servers save time and turn tables faster. The result is better service and a better experience for everyone. Cover is also meant to solve the problem of splitting the bill. The usual conundrum who pays what, who doesnt pay for what and so on. So its pretty neat in that sense as well. Cover app can be downloaded for free through iTunes
         <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/cover-for-iphone/id671290905?ls=1&amp;mt=8" title="Download Cover App">
          here
         </a>
         . Cover was founded by Mark Egerman and Andrew Cove. The company has received a total funding of $1.6 Mn.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Cover was
         <strong>
          founded by Mark Egerman
         </strong>
         and Andrew Cove. Mark is the payments and legal expert while Andrew drives the tech and fundraising. The company has received a total funding of $1.6 Mn from OATV and other investors.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Tabbedout:
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://letstalkpayments.com/tabbedout-makers-of-a-mobile-payment-app-acquires-7-75-million-in-funding/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="tabbedout" class="alignnone wp-image-1493" height="105" src="http://n6zgo3se7pe2sazc62u1v9qe.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/tabbedout--150x110.png" width="160"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Tabbedout was co-founded in 2009 by Rick Orr (CEO) and David Lemley (CTO). The Texas based company has acquired a total of $13.5 Mn in funding till date. The
         <strong>
          free Tabbedout app
         </strong>
         foriPhoneandAndroidenables consumers to open a tab with their mobile phone, view their tab in real-time and pay the tab anytime, anywhere. Users can even split the check paying just their portion directly from the app.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Tabbedout says that its
         <strong>
          mobile payment solution
         </strong>
         integrates directly with the merchants point-of-sale (POS) system without the need of additional hardware. This enables Tabbedout merchants to serve more people and turn more tables, especially during peak hours.
         <strong>
          Tabbedout
         </strong>
         also provides validated payment information when a tab is first opened. Tabbedout is available at more than 1,000 locations nationwide. The
         <strong>
          free smartphone app
         </strong>
         is available for download from the Apple App Store or Google Play.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          MyCheck:
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://letstalkpayments.com/6-1-mn-funding-mycheck-improvises-checkout-experience-consumers/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="mycheck" class="alignnone wp-image-3617" height="105" src="http://n6zgo3se7pe2sazc62u1v9qe.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/mycheck3.png" width="160"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         MyCheck was launced In March 2011 in Israel. The company has received a total funding of $6.1 Mn through investors Werthaimer family, Saban Capital Group and Eli ElroyHow does it work? When they arrive at a participating restaurant, consumers can check in with MyCheck. A four-digital code on their
         <strong>
          smartphone
         </strong>
         screens is then shown to their servers. Once a server enters this code into the restaurants POS system, customers are able to view, split, and pay for their restaurant bill. Restaurants get paid within 24 to 48 hours via ACH credit. Restaurants are charged no setup costs or monthly fees.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         MyCheck says that it has over 100,000 direct subscribers and 1,000,000 indirect users in Israel. It is accepted by around 400 merchants including the largest full service caf chain (at 120 locations) and 2nd largest gas station chain (215 locations). MyCheck counts 20 persons in Israel, 5 in London and 9 in Manhattan, as employees.
        </p>
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          Dash:
         </strong>
        </h2>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://letstalkpayments.com/pay-bill-100000-bars-restaurants-u-s-dash-mobile-app/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="dash" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3328" height="102" src="http://n6zgo3se7pe2sazc62u1v9qe.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/dash2.png" width="160"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Dash is a
         <strong>
          mobile payments
         </strong>
         platform that enables users to pay the bill with their smartphone without having to wait for the check at restaurants, clubs and bars. Restaurant also gets reports, analytics and insights into customer behavior.The company was founded in January 2011 and has received a total funding of $700K through investors
         <a href="http://www.crunchbase.com/financial-organization/new-york-angels">
          New York Angels
         </a>
         ,
         <a href="http://www.crunchbase.com/financial-organization/brooklyn-incubator">
          Brooklyn Incubator
         </a>
         and
         <a href="http://www.crunchbase.com/financial-organization/caerus-ventures">
          Caerus Ventures
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         Dash enables users to open, view, and pay their restaurant or bar tab directly on their iPhone at some of the popular venues. Apart from being able to collect faster payments, restaurants get access to analytics based on the data captured through Dash. Dash will also be able to split bills and store gift card information in the coming months, according to the company. Dash users can even suggest new venues to be added to the application by making use of a feature called Request Venue. This will ping bars and restaurants to inform them that their customers are requesting them to enable payments via Dash.
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          E La Carte:
         </strong>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://letstalkpayments.com/mitians-bring-all-in-one-tablets-to-restaurants-get-series-b-funding-led-by-intel/" target="_blank">
          <img alt="elacarte" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3736" height="104" src="http://n6zgo3se7pe2sazc62u1v9qe.wpengine.netdna-cdn.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/elacarte1.jpg" width="160"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         E La Carte is a French term which means menu. E La Carte was founded by a group of MIT engineers. The CEO of the company is Rajat Suri and launched its first Presto tablet in 2011. E La Carte developed the tablet for restaurant and related
         <strong>
          hospitality industries
         </strong>
         . The company provides tablets on restaurants tables so guests can order food whenever they want to,
         <strong>
          children can play games
         </strong>

         <strong>

         </strong>

         <strong>

Its chain partners include Genghis Grill, global restaurateur, HMSHost, and Evergreen Restaurant group, a franchise of Outback Steakhouse.
