               <p>
                <img alt="Scariest and Best Horror Movies fo All Time" border="0" src="/images/stories/horror/horror-header-fitted.jpg" style="border: 0pt none;" title="Scariest and Best Horror Movies fo All Time" width="395"/>
               </p>
               <p>
                In 1896, pioneering French filmmaker Georges Mlis set out to scare the bejeebers out of audiences with a short film he called
                <em>
                 Le Manoir du diable
                </em>
                that is often credited as the first horror film. With his "
                <em>
                 House of the Devil
                </em>
                ," not only did he do just that, but the innovative filmmaker also unknowingly unleashed a whole new genre of filmmaking onto the world that would go on to become one of the most proliferative in the history of film.
               </p>
               <p>
                Ever since Mlis' creepy images flickered on a wall somewhere in Paris, audiences have been clamoring for the next thrill, the next fright or some new way of getting their "horror freak on." And fortunately for an ever-increasingly sophisticated and skeptical audience, horror filmmakers keep on delivering.
               </p>
               <p>
                Everyone has a different threshold of "horror," and everyone has a different way of having that threshold breached, so keep that in mind while perusing our list of the scariest horror movies of all time. You may not find your favorite on the list, and you may not think that every entry included is scary, but remember that each movie listed, at one time or another, scared the pants off someone, somewhere.
               </p>
               <p>
                If you don't see your favorite scary horror movie on the list, you can let us know about it by posting in the comments section at the end of the article.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Shining" border="0" height="138" src="/images/stories/horror/shining.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px;" title="The Shining" width="100"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Shining
                </strong>
                - Numerous memorable scenes come to mind, ("redrum", "all work and no play", "man in the bunny suit") - but they all add up to the scariest movie of all time.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Silence of the Lambs" border="0" height="137" src="/images/stories/horror/silenceofthelambs.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Silence of the Lambs" width="99"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Silence of the Lambs
                </strong>
                - The most visceral fear comes from one's own mind, and this one is the king of psychological horror. We recommend watching it slathered in lotion while listening to a Sparklehorse CD.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Seven" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/seven.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Se7en" width="95"/>
                </strong>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/se7en-blu-ray-review">
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/se7en-blu-ray-review">
                 <strong>
                  Se7en
                 </strong>
                </a>
                - Wonder if FedEx would really make that delivery? It's been reported that the studios balked at the ending, but Brad Pitt refused to complete filming with a different final scene. Good move, Brad!
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="King Kong" border="0" height="118" src="/images/stories/horror/kingkong.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="King Kong" width="100"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <a href="/dvd-reviews/king-kong-1933-blu-ray-review">
                  King Kong
                 </a>
                 (1933)
                </strong>
                - Despite the flickering gorilla fur, this one frightened a whole generation of new moviegoers. it's now
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/king-kong-1933-blu-ray-review">
                 available in sparkling blu-ray
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/psycho-blu-ray-50th-anniversary">
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Psycho" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/psycho.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Psycho" width="98"/>
                 </strong>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/psycho-blu-ray-50th-anniversary">
                 <strong>
                  Psycho
                 </strong>
                </a>
                - The most prominent representative of the genius of Hitchcock. Janet Leigh's shower scene is often mentioned as one of the most frightening scenes ever filmed for a movie.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Ring" border="0" src="/images/stories/horror/ring.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The  Ring"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Ring
                </strong>
                - A ringing phone will scare you for days after seeing this one. A clean change of underwear anyone?
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Halloween" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/halloween.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Halloween" width="98"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Halloween
                </strong>
                (1978) - So scary it spawned no fewer than 8 direct sequels, not to mention numerous acknowledgments in other Hollywood productions. William Shatner should be proud of that!
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Frankenstein" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/frankenstein.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Frankenstein" width="100"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Frankenstein
                </strong>
                (1931) - Combines a beautifully simplistic tale, with the potent topic of man vs. nature, to create a horrific fairy-tale that continues to mesmerize more than seven decades after its release.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Hills Have Eyes" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/hillshaveeyes.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Hills Have Eyes" width="100"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/reviews/2006/hillshaveeyes.htm">
                 <strong>
                  The Hills Have Eyes
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (2006) - with the threat of nuclear disaster and its effects on humans largely forgotten, how successful would a remake featuring villains mutated by radioactive fallout play to today's generation of horror fans? Well, it worked back then and it works today.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Carrie" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/carrie.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Carrie" width="93"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Carrie
                </strong>
                (1976) - Mixes telekinetic powers with our innate fear of high school ridicule to create a classic horror masterpiece. This was the first Stephen King novel to be adapted for the big screen.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Bad Ronald" border="0" height="136" src="/images/stories/horror/badronald.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Bad Ronald" width="95"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Bad Ronald
                </strong>
                (1974) - An ABC, made-for-TV movie. The premise alone is the star of the show and is guaranteed to keep you awake for a few nights. Hard to get your hands on it though, as it's not yet been distributed on DVD.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvdblu-ray-reviews/jaws-1975">
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Jaws" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/jaws.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Jaws" width="99"/>
                 </strong>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvdblu-ray-reviews/jaws-1975">
                 <strong>
                  Jaws
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (1975) - Forced a whole generation to stay out of the water. Is it safe to go back yet?
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Night of the Living Dead" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/nightofthelivingdead.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Night of the Living Dead" width="99"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Night of the Living Dead
                </strong>
                (1968) - The Royal King Mother of all zombie movies. Anyone hungry!
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/the-exorcist-directors-cut-dvd-review">
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Exorcist" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/exorcist.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Exorcist" width="91"/>
                 </strong>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/the-exorcist-directors-cut-dvd-review">
                 <strong>
                  Exorcist
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (1973) - Made an entire generation of filmgoers never order split pea soup again! Be sure to watch the
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/the-exorcist-directors-cut-dvd-review">
                 Extended director's cut
                </a>
                rather than the original theatrical release.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Birds" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/birds.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Birds" width="96"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Birds
                </strong>
                (1963) - The film's tagline alone says it all: "Suspense and Shock Beyond Anything You Have Seen or Imagined!" Ooooo, scary!
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Blair Witch Project" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/blairwitch.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Blair Witch Project" width="97"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Blair Witch Project
                </strong>
                (1999) - Although it turned out to be the biggest sham in filmmaking history, it was scary nonetheless.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Changeling" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/changeling.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Changeling" width="97"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Changeling
                </strong>
                (1980) - You'll never see a wheelchair the same again! The movie is based on events which supposedly took place at a house in Denver, Colorado, in the 1960s. The Chessman Park neighborhood in the movie is a reference to Cheesman Park in Denver, where the original haunting transpired.
                <a href="/reviews/2008/changeling.htm">
                 Not the 2008 movie of the same name
                </a>
                starring Angelina Jolie.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Friday the 13th" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/friday13th.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Friday the 13th" width="98"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Friday the 13th
                </strong>
                (1980) - Must be scarier than
                <em>
                 Halloween
                </em>
                because it spawned more sequels! By the way, not the
                <a href="/reviews/2009/friday13th.htm">
                 2009 remake
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Signs" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/signs.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Signs" width="99"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Signs
                </strong>
                (2002) - Crop circles, Shyamalan and Aliens, Oh My! Unfortunately, This was the beginning of the end for M. Night.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Paranormal Activity" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/paranormalactivity.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Paranormal Activity" width="94"/>
                </strong>
                <a href="/reviews/2009/paranormalactivity.htm">
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/reviews/2009/paranormalactivity.htm">
                 <strong>
                  Paranormal Activity
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (2007) - Works on the often overlooked idea that what you don't see is scarier than what you do. It's scary, and it's unsettling. And besides, you've been getting too much restful sleep lately anyway.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Alien" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/alien.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Alien" width="97"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Alien
                </strong>
                (1979) - Ever wonder about that indigestion you get from time to time?
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Descent" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/descent.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Descent" width="94"/>
                </strong>
                <a href="/reviews/2006/descent.htm">
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/reviews/2006/descent.htm">
                 <strong>
                  The Descent
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (2005) - The translucent-skinned humanoids resemble a creepy cross between Gollum, Spock, and Vladislaus Dracula's naked, flying-monkey devil-brides featured in
                <em>
                 Van Helsing
                </em>
                . A descent into madness in more ways than one.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Devil's Rejects" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/devilsrejects.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Devil's Rejects" width="94"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Devil's Rejects
                </strong>
                (2005) - Sequel to
                <em>
                 House of 1000 Corpses
                </em>
                . One of the few instances of a sequel surpassing the original. Also contains some of the best one-liners in a horror movie.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="A Clockwork Orange" border="0" height="124" src="/images/stories/horror/clockworkorange.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="A Clockwork Orange" width="91"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 A Clockwork Orange
                </strong>
                (1971) - Many don't classify it as a true horror movie, but it's terrifying nonetheless. Kubrick's best movie? Naughty, naughty, naughty! You filthy old soomka!
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Videodrome" border="0" height="137" src="/images/stories/horror/videodrome.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Videodrome" width="100"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Videodrome
                </strong>
                (1983) - Cronenberg horror that relates the troubles of society to television. Was he a visionary?
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="The Thing" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/thing.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Thing" width="100"/>
                </strong>
                <a href="/netflix-finds/the-thing-netflix-finds-review">
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/netflix-finds/the-thing-netflix-finds-review">
                 <strong>
                  The Thing a.k.a John Carpenter's The Thing
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (1982) - This remake of the 1951 original was largely dismissed by critics and audiences when it first came out, probably because Spielberg's E.T. had just been released two weeks prior. A great example of how characters should be written in horror movies.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <a href="/reviews/2008/quarantine.htm">
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Quarantine" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/quarantine.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Quarantine" width="94"/>
                 </strong>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/reviews/2008/quarantine.htm">
                 <strong>
                  Quarantine
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (2008) - Undoubtedly, one-hour martinizers and upholstery cleaning companies around the world will make a fortune laundering soiled trousers and wet theater seats. A scene-by-scene remake of the foreign-made
                <em>
                 [REC]
                </em>
                which was a better film, by the way.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Texas Chainsaw Massacre" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/horror/texaschainsawmassacre.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Texas Chainsaw Massacre" width="98"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Texas Chainsaw Massacre
                </strong>
                (1974) - The 1974 Tober Hooper version, not the 2003 version. Another film "based on" the life of real-life serial killer Ed Gein. What a popular serial killer he was!
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="Wait Until Dark" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/waituntildark.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Wait Until Dark" width="96"/>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Wait Until Dark
                </strong>
                (1967) - Yes, Audrey Hepburn can do "scary." A great example of how to leave it up to an audience to unravel the mystery themselves. Stephen King once declared this the scariest movie of all time.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <strong>
                   <strong>
                    <img alt="Rosemary's Baby" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/rosemarys-baby.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0px none;" title="Drag Me to Hell" width="98"/>
                   </strong>
                  </strong>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvdblu-ray-reviews/rosemarys-baby-the-criterion-collection">
                 <strong>
                  Rosemary's Baby
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (1968) - Filmed at The Dakota building, in Manhattan's Upper West Side where John Lennon was fatally shot some twelve years later. A coincidence?
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <strong>
                   <strong>
                    <img alt="Freaks" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/freaks.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0px none;" title="Drag Me to Hell" width="98"/>
                   </strong>
                  </strong>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Freaks
                </strong>
                (1932) - Not so much scary as it is disturbing. Ever seen a man with no arms and no legs roll a cigarette?
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <strong>
                   <strong>
                    <img alt="The Blob" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/blob.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0px none;" title="Drag Me to Hell" width="98"/>
                   </strong>
                  </strong>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Blob
                </strong>
                (1958) - When viewed with a nostalgic attitude and with one corner of your mind thinking back of sitting in the tuck-and-roll seats of a '57 Chevy parked at the drive-in theater, The Blob will endear itself to classic sci-fi fans more and more each time it is viewed.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Drag Me to Hell" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/dragmetohell.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Drag Me to Hell" width="98"/>
                 </strong>
                 <a href="/reviews/2009/dragmetohell.htm">
                 </a>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <a href="/reviews/2009/dragmetohell.htm">
                  Drag Me to Hell
                 </a>
                </strong>
                (2009) - Say what you will about the cheesey title, but Sam Raimi returns to form with this delightfully funny and refreshingly scary slasher flick. Welcome back, Sam!
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="ju-On" border="0" height="134" src="/images/stories/horror/ju-on.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="ju-On" width="95"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p style="text-align: left;">
                <strong>
                 Ju-on
                </strong>
                (2000) - The Japanese version. Don't bother with the Sarah Michelle Gellar American remake. When someone is killed in a bout of resentment, the resulting rage accumulates in the place where the dead once lived. He who comes in contact with the curse loses his life, and a new curse is born. The Japanese prefer to believe places become haunted, whereas American filmmakers think people are haunted.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Invasion of the Body Snatchers" border="0" height="122" src="/images/stories/2010/invasion-of-the-body-snatchers.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Invasion of the Body Snatchers" width="98"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <a href="/dvd-reviews/invasion-of-the-body-snatchers">
                  Invasion of the Body Snatchers
                 </a>
                </strong>
                (1978) - The rare instance of a remake that's better than the original. Must... stay... awake...
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="28 Days Later" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/28-days-later.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="28 Days Later" width="95"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 28 Days Later
                </strong>
                (2002) - Danny Boyle's breakout flick is a dystopian tale that opens four weeks after a mysterious, incurable virus spreads throughout the UK, leaving only a handful of non-infected survivors try to find sanctuary. Not a zombie movie... they're not zombies... don't call them zombies.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="The Mist" border="0" height="131" src="/images/stories/2007/mist.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Mist" width="100"/>
                 </strong>
                 <a href="/reviews/2007/mist.htm">
                 </a>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <a href="/reviews/2007/mist.htm">
                  The Mist
                 </a>
                </strong>
                (2007) - From a Stephen King Story. Hard to recall an ending to a movie causing such polarization of viewers. Say what you will about it, they couldn't have ended it in any other way.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="I Walked with a Zombie" border="0" height="120" src="/images/stories/zombies/i-walked-with-a-zombie.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="I Walked with a Zombie" width="100"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 I Walked With a Zombie
                </strong>
                (1943) - Made in 1943, this Val Lewton production, directed by Jacques Tourneur, features disturbing atmosphere that sees its female stars, Christine Gordon and Frances Dee, taking a long and lonely walk through some haunting fields of sugar cane.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="The Wicker Man 1973" border="0" height="139" src="/images/stories/horror/wicker-man.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Wicker Man 1973" width="95"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Wicker Man
                </strong>
                (1973) - A creepy-crawly little film that, despite its myriad production problems, went on to become a cult fave and one of Great Britain's most popular horror films. Don't confuse it with
                <a href="/reviews/2006/wickerman.htm">
                 Nic Cage's ridiculous 2006 version
                </a>
                . The soundtrack is equally freaky.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="Nosferatu - Count Orlock" border="0" height="135" src="/images/stories/horror/nosferatu.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="Nosferatu - Count Orlock" width="95"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Nosferatu, eine Symphonie des Grauens (Nosferatu, A Symphony of Horror)
                </strong>
                (1922) - Where the legend of Dracula began, with Max Schreck's Count Orlock, the phantom of the night that drinks the blood of his victims. This silent movie was the predecessor to the perhaps more easily "watchable" Bela Lugosi version of
                <em>
                 Dracula
                </em>
                . Count Orlock also appears on our
                <a href="/shorttakes/reelrant/rantmonster.htm">
                 list of the Best Movie Monster and Creatures of All Time.
                </a>
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <img alt="A Nightmare on Elm Street" border="0" height="140" src="/images/stories/2010/nightmareelmstreet1984.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="A Nightmare on Elm Street" width="91"/>
                </strong>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/a-nightmare-on-elm-street-1984-blu-ray-review">
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/a-nightmare-on-elm-street-1984-blu-ray-review">
                 A Nightmare on Elm Street
                </a>
                (1984) - Wes Cravens sweet dreamscape premise behind
                <em>
                 A Nightmare on Elm Street
                </em>
                is what continues to work in this little jewel of horrific fun from 1984. Terrifying and clever (even if that intelligence is somewhat dated),
                <em>
                 Nightmare
                </em>
                 trying to reign in on the success of John Carpenters Halloween series  offers a bit more humor and modern-like psychosis for fans of slasher films by introducing audiences to the wise-cracking psychotic nature of Freddy Krueger. By the way, it's
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/a-nightmare-on-elm-street-1984-blu-ray-review">
                 now on sparkling blu-ray
                </a>
                .
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="The Wolf Man" border="0" height="135" src="/images/stories/horror/wolf-man.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0pt none;" title="The Wolf Man" width="95"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Wolf Man
                </strong>
                (1941) - Lon Chaney Jr.'s rendition from Universal's vault of classic horror staples, not to be confused with
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/the-wolfman-blu-ray-review">
                 Joe Johnston's 2010 remake
                </a>
                . Interesting tidbit: The Wolfman battled a bear in one scene but unfortunately the bear ran away during filming. What few scenes were filmed, were included in the theatrical trailer.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 <strong>
                  <img alt="A Tale of Two Sisters - 2003" border="0" height="148" src="/images/stories/horror/a-atale-of-two-sisters.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0px none;" title="A Tale of Two Sisters - 2003" width="100"/>
                 </strong>
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 A Tale of Two Sisters
                </strong>
                (2003) - This South Korean horror masterpiece features one of the most chilling quotes ever: "As much as you hate it, I'm the only one in this world you can call mother, got it?"
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/insidious-blu-ray-review">
                 <strong>
                  <strong>
                   <img alt="Insidious - scary movie" border="0" height="148" src="/images/stories/trailers/insidious.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0px none;" title="Insidious - scary movie" width="100"/>
                  </strong>
                 </strong>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvd-reviews/insidious-blu-ray-review">
                 <strong>
                  Insidious
                 </strong>
                </a>
                (2010) - Don't let your son go into the further. Nothing good will come of it.
               </p>
               <hr/>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvdblu-ray-reviews/prometheus">
                 <strong>
                  <strong>
                   <img alt="Prometheus" border="0" height="148" src="/images/stories/2012/prometheus-100.jpg" style="margin-left: 6px; margin-right: 6px; border: 0px none;" title="Prometheus" width="100"/>
                  </strong>
                 </strong>
                </a>
               </p>
               <p>
                <a href="/dvdblu-ray-reviews/prometheus">
                 <strong>
                  Prometheus
                 </strong>
                </a>
                - Scott is onto something so close to being perfect that one cant help but let the film slide on its few shortcomings and sit back while its themes gestate.
               </p>
