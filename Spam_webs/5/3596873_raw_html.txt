         <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
         <input data-frizzlyhovercontainer="" data-frizzlypostcontainer="" data-frizzlyposttitle="Chocolate Chip Gluten Free Zucchini Bread" data-frizzlyposturl="http://glutenfreeonashoestring.com/chocolate-chip-zucchini-bread/" type="hidden" value=""/>
         <p>
          <img alt="Chocolate Chip Gluten Free Zucchini Bread" class="aligncenter wp-image-22851 size-full" height="906" src="//glutenfreeonashoestring.com/_main_site/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/CC-Zucchini-Bread-sliced.jpg?bbcd03" width="685"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          When the zucchini plants start to flower, you know youve got to get your recipes ready if you have any hope at all of staying ahead of things. Or at least not falling miserably behind. Like night follows day, its just the way things are. At least
          <strong>
           <a href="http://glutenfreeonashoestring.com/gluten-free-zucchini-recipes/" target="_blank">
            baking with zucchini
           </a>
          </strong>
          is truly a pleasure (so much moisture!), and this chocolate chip gluten free zucchini bread is a time-honored favorite. And it just so happens to be dairy-free, too.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Chocolate Chip Gluten Free Zucchini Bread" class="aligncenter wp-image-22853 size-full" height="972" src="//glutenfreeonashoestring.com/_main_site/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/slices-on-plates.jpg?bbcd03" width="685"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          Do you have a vegetable-hating child (or adult?) in your home? I have one. It would be a total drag, but her saving grace is that shes now old enough that she
          <em>
           wishes
          </em>
          she liked vegetables. Shes accepted that vegetables will be a part of every meal, and understands why. So when I can, I try to give her vegetables hiding in a healthy homemade popsicle or, say, baked into this lightly sweet, moist and tender zucchini bread. Its the least I can do.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Chocolate Chip Gluten Free Zucchini Bread" class="aligncenter wp-image-22854 size-full" height="450" src="//glutenfreeonashoestring.com/_main_site/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/Zucchini-Bread-raw.jpg?bbcd03" width="685"/>
         </p>
         <p>
          I originally posted this recipe back in 2012 (oh the humanity of those photos!), back when I used to add some mashed banana to my gluten free zucchini bread. Ive started replacing the banana with more zucchini, and pureeing the zucchini along with the eggs and oil in the recipe. The result is even more moist and delicious. Zucchini, you really are a wonder.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="Chocolate Chip Gluten Free Zucchini Bread" class="aligncenter wp-image-22852 size-full" height="916" src="//glutenfreeonashoestring.com/_main_site/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/in-pan.jpg?bbcd03" width="685"/>
         </p>
         <p style="text-align: left;">
          As a vegetable-lover myself, I dont detect the actual taste of zucchini in this quick bread. Ditto for my 13-year-old vegetable-hater. How do you do it, zucchini??
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="post-recipe">
         <div class="prep center white70">
          <span class="prep-label purple slab bold">
           Prep time:
          </span>
          <time datetime="PT10M" itemprop="prepTime">
           10 minutes
          </time>
          <span class="prep-sep">
          </span>
          <span class="prep-label purple slab bold">
           Cook time:
          </span>
          <time datetime="PT50M" itemprop="cookTime">
           &gt;50 minutes
          </time>
          <span class="prep-sep">
          </span>
          <span class="prep-label purple slab bold">
           Yield:
          </span>
          <span itemprop="yield">
           1 loaf
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="section-title ingredients slab xbold purple upper space">
          Ingredients
         </div>
         <div class="ingredient-text">
          <span itemprop="ingredient">
           <p>
            1 1/2cups (210g)
            <strong>
             <a href="http://glutenfreeonashoestring.com/all-purpose-gluten-free-flour-recipes/" target="_blank">
              all-purpose gluten-free flour
             </a>
            </strong>
            (I used Better Batter)
           </p>
           <p>
            1/2 teaspoon xanthan gum (omit if your blend already contains it)
           </p>
           <p>
            1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
           </p>
           <p>
            1/2 teaspoon baking soda
           </p>
           <p>
            1/2 teaspoon baking powder
           </p>
           <p>
            1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
           </p>
           <p>
            2/3 cup (4 ounces) semi-sweet chocolate chips (use dairy free chips to keep the recipe dairy free, if necessary)
           </p>
           <p>
            1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
           </p>
           <p>
            1/2 cup (109g) packed light brown sugar
           </p>
           <p>
            2 1/8 cups (215g) grated zucchini
           </p>
           <p>
            2 eggs (100 g, weighed out of shell) at room temperature
           </p>
           <p>
            1/4 cup (56 g)neutraloil (like grapeseed, canola or vegetable oil)
           </p>
           <p>
            1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
           </p>
          </span>
         </div>
         <div class="section-title post-steps slab xbold purple upper space">
          Directions
         </div>
         <ul class="directions-list clean" itemprop="instructions">
          <li>
           <p>
            Preheat your oven to 325F. Grease astandard 9-inch x 5-inch loaf pan and set itaside.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            In a large bowl, place flour, xanthan gum, salt, baking soda, baking powder and cinnamon, and whisk to combine well. Place the chocolate chips in a small bowl, add about 1 teaspoon of the dry ingredients to the chips and toss to combine. Set the chips aside. To the flour mixture, add the granulated sugar and light brown sugar, and whisk to combine (working out any lumps in the brown sugar). Add 1 1/4 cups (125 g) of the grated zucchini to the dry ingredients, and mix gently to combine. Set aside the dry ingredients.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Place the remaining 7/8 cup (88 g) of grated zucchini, the eggs, oil and vanilla in a blender, and pulse until well-blended and beginning to emulsify. Create a well in the center of the bowl of dry ingredients, and add the wet ingredient mixture.The batter will be very wet. Add about 1/2 cup of the chocolate chips and reserved dry ingredients to the batter and mix until the chips are evenly distributed throughout the batter. Scrape the batter into the prepared loaf pan,and smooth the top with a wet spatula. Scatter the remaining chocolate chips on top and press down lightly to adhere.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            Place the loaf pan in the center of the preheated oven. Bake, rotating once, until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the center of each loaf comes out with a few moist crumbs attached (about 50 minutes).Remove from the oven and allow to cool for at least 30 minutes in the loaf panbeforetransferring to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing and serving.
           </p>
          </li>
          <li>
           <p>
            <em>
             Originally published here on the blog in 2012. Recipe tweaked to increase eggs slightly and replace mashed banana with pureed zucchini, blended with eggs and oil.
            </em>
           </p>
          </li>
         </ul>
