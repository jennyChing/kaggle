       <div class="six units entry-content">
        <span class="date-container minor-meta meta-color">
         January 26, 2011
        </span>
        <p>
         I swear Im not selling Apple products out of my garage (or at AMS Pictures) but the software giant has recently been making our lives in the production department a lot easier. Ive recently found (and employed) a brand new product that actually incorporates the iPad in our Texas video productions. Production Assistants (PAs) and Associate Producers (APs) will be especiallyglad to know that Apple and
         <a href="http://www.pureblendsoftware.com" target="_blank">
          PureBlend Software
         </a>
         have something that will lighten the production workload and change the way they do things.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2569" height="202" src="http://cp.amspictures.com/files/2011/01/Apple-iPad-475x253.png" width="380"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Drum Roll Please
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left">
         Its a $20 application called
         <a href="http://www.pureblendsoftware.com/movieslate" target="_blank">
          MovieSlate
         </a>
         and it primarily functions like its name suggests. Yet, when you look at its functionality, the applications features are much deeper than just a slate for production. MovieSlate assists PAs and APs by offering the option of making script notes with multiple text, voice, image notes per shot. In other words, you can document each shot in a variety of different ways, depending on what you want to highlight in your script notes.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-2562" height="322" src="http://cp.amspictures.com/files/2011/01/splash_01_main.jpg" width="224"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres another feature that will blow you away. It can sync its time code with your cameras as well as with other people who have the application. No more looking back and forth from the monitor to the paper, hastily grabbing time code and scribbling in information, hoping you didnt miss anything. Just a quick tap on the screen and you can type, talk, or take pictures of what you want to document at that particular moment in time.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <a href="http://cp.amspictures.com/?attachment_id=2588" rel="attachment wp-att-2588">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2588" height="267" src="http://cp.amspictures.com/files/2011/01/slate_01_main1-356x267.jpg" width="356"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Are you an editor that needs to know what location a set of script notes were taken at? How about the frame rate of the camera? With MovieSlate, the application automatically logs the location addresses using GPS or Wifi technology. You also have the option of selecting which frame rate the camera is set at, letting the post production team know what theyre dealing with.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center">
         <a href="http://cp.amspictures.com/?attachment_id=2594" rel="attachment wp-att-2594">
          <img alt="" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-2594" height="267" src="http://cp.amspictures.com/files/2011/01/slate_03_jamtimecode2-356x267.jpg" width="356"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Editors everywhere will be happy to know that MovieSlate has the ability to store the productions project information with relative ease and in an organized fashion. All script notes can be exported to Excel, CSV, HTML, and Final Cut XML if need be.
        </p>
        <p>
         To sum it up, this is a scary good application for us at AMS Pictures, a Texas Video Production Company. It revolutionizes the way we can document our shooting and as a result, improves communication between the production and post production crew. Let me know your thoughts on this product, if youve tried it, and where you think this kind of technology is taking us.
        </p>
