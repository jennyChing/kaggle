     </h1>
     <time>

     <div class="linksalpha-email-button" data-desc="brought to you by Jones Oil - the home heating oil provider. 				&lt;p&gt;brought to you by Jones Oil &#8211; the &lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/home-heating/" title="home heating oil Ireland"&gt;home heating oil&lt;/a&gt; provider.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;&lt;span style="line-height: 1.6em;"&gt;I control the majority of the world&#8217;s wealth, I&#8217;m mainly found in Russia and everyone just WISHES they were my kid. What am I? Yep. You guessed it. I&#8217;m an oil tycoon.&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;You don&#8217;t need a degree to be an oil baron, in fact some of the most successful are college drop-outs or inherited their oil companies from their parents. What those at the top have in common however, is ambition, drive, a savvy business sense and at times, very expensive tastes. Here&#8217;s our top ten oil tycoons.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;10) Folorunsho Alaija&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Folorunsho-Alaija.png"&gt;&lt;img class="size-full wp-image-462 aligncenter" title="Folorunsho Alaija" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Folorunsho-Alaija.png" alt="Folorunsho Alaija" width="416" height="416" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.forbes.com/profile/folorunsho-alakija/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.forbes.com/profile/folorunsho-alakija/', 'Image Source');" title="Folorunsho Alaija"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;Image Source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;Nigerias first female billionaire Nigerian Folorunsho is relatively new to the billionaires club and can boast that her wealth is self-made. She dabbles in various industries and is the vice chair of Famfa Oil Limited. Forbes estimated her net worth at $2.5 billion. She gives back through philanthropic ventures include the Rose of Sharon Foundation that provides scholarships and business grants to widows and orphans.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;9) Mohammed Al Amoudi&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Mohammed-Al-Amoudi.png"&gt;&lt;img class=" wp-image-463 aligncenter" title="Mohammed Al Amoudi" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Mohammed-Al-Amoudi.png" alt="Mohammed Al Amoudi" width="374" height="374" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://adiszena.com/mohammed-al-amoudi-to-build-two-more-cement-factories-in-ethiopia/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://adiszena.com/mohammed-al-amoudi-to-build-two-more-cement-factories-in-ethiopia/', 'image source');" title="Mohammed Al Amoudi"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;This Saudi Arabian/Ethiopian businessman has oil refineries in Morocco, Saudi Arabia and Sweden. It was the building of Saudi Arabia&#8217;s nation-wide underground oil storage that gave Al Amoudi his big break &#8211; a $30 Billion project, quite the leg-up. He is the second-richest person in Saudi Arabia, after the country&#8217;s Prince of course, because that would be rude otherwise. His investments in Sweden saw him honoured with the Order of the Polar Star by King Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden. His net worth in 2012 was $12.5 billion.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;8) Roman Abramovich&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Roman-Abramovich.png"&gt;&lt;img class="size-full wp-image-464 aligncenter" title="Roman Abramovich" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Roman-Abramovich.png" alt="Roman Abramovich" width="520" height="370" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.plushasia.com/article/4028" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.plushasia.com/article/4028', 'image source');" title="Roman Abramovich"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;This Russian self-made billionaire was orphaned as a child and dropped out of college, but he didn&#8217;t let any of those things hold him back. Roman makes his riches from stakes in oil, steel, aluminium and gold. While his net worth of $13.6 billion doesn&#8217;t put him at the top of the rich list of oil tycoons, he can boast that he owns his very own football club (Chelsea) and, like, a really, really big yacht. The biggest in the world, in fact.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;7)Vagit Alekperov&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Vagit-Alekperov.png"&gt;&lt;img class="size-full wp-image-465 aligncenter" title="Vagit Alekperov" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Vagit-Alekperov.png" alt="Vagit Alekperov" width="330" height="450" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.themoscowtimes.com/people/article/vagit-alekperov/434253.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.themoscowtimes.com/people/article/vagit-alekperov/434253.html', 'image source');" title="Vagit Alekperov"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;This Russian businessman used to be Minister for Oil and Gas before the dissolution of the Soviet Union. The contacts no doubt came in handy in his during his subsequent rise to billionaire oil tycoon running LUKoil. In 2011 Forbes estimated his wealth at $13.9 billion&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;6) Viktor Vekselberg&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Viktor-Vekselberg.png"&gt;&lt;img class="size-full wp-image-466 aligncenter" title="Viktor Vekselberg" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Viktor-Vekselberg.png" alt="Viktor Vekselberg" width="179" height="150" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.mieks.com/intro/collectors.htm" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.mieks.com/intro/collectors.htm', 'image source');" title="Viktor Vekselberg"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;&lt;em&gt;image source&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;Okay it&#8217;s settled, we&#8217;re moving to Russia to make our fortune. Viktor is one of Russia&#8217;s top three richest people with Forbes putting his fortune at an estimated $17.2 billion. When he has some downtime from his investments in oil, telecoms and chemicals he can usually be found dusting his egg collection. Viktor boasts the world&#8217;s largest collection of Faberg eggs. Fifteen of them. Let&#8217;s be honest, fifteen eggs doesn&#8217;t exactly sound overly impressive does it? But we&#8217;re sure they were pretty pricey.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;5) Mikhail Fridman&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Mikhail-Fridman.png"&gt;&lt;img class="size-full wp-image-467 aligncenter" title="Mikhail Fridman" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Mikhail-Fridman.png" alt=" Mikhail Fridman" width="350" height="362" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://infonetworth.com/mikhail-fridman-net-worth/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://infonetworth.com/mikhail-fridman-net-worth/', 'image source');" title="Mikhail Fridman"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;Mikhail is one of the younger oil tycoons on this rich list, coming in at a youthful 49. He founded Alfa Group in 1989. He&#8217;s now worth $17.6 Billion. He is a supporter and instigator of Jewish initiatives in Russia and Europe, in particular the Genesis Philanthropy Group that promotes Jewish identity among Russian-speaking Jews world-wide. He also owns Alfa-Eco and Alfa-Bank. We bet he drives an Alfa Romeo too.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;4)Mukesh Ambani&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Mukesh-Ambani.png"&gt;&lt;img class=" wp-image-468 aligncenter" title="Mukesh Ambani" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Mukesh-Ambani.png" alt="Mukesh Ambani" width="434" height="329" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.celebritynetworth.com/articles/entertainment-articles/mukesh-ambani-became-richest-person-india/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.celebritynetworth.com/articles/entertainment-articles/mukesh-ambani-became-richest-person-india/', 'image source');" style="text-align: center; line-height: 1.6em;" title="Mukesh Ambani"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;India&#8217;s richest man, Mukesh, inherited his company, Reliance Industries from his father. He initially dropped out of college to help his father expand the growing company and expand it he sure did. His personal wealth comes in at $19.6 billion, not too shabby. He doesn&#8217;t own a massive yacht but he does live in a private 27-storey building in Mumbai. Does that count?&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;3)The Koch Brothers&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/The-Koch-Brothers.png"&gt;&lt;img class=" wp-image-469 aligncenter" title="The Koch Brothers" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/The-Koch-Brothers.png" alt="The Koch Brothers" width="540" height="364" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/politics/blogs/national-affairs/the-koch-brothers-exposed-20120420" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rollingstone.com/politics/blogs/national-affairs/the-koch-brothers-exposed-20120420', 'image source');" style="text-align: center; line-height: 1.6em;" title="The Koch Brothers"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;Charles and David Koch inherited Koch Industries from their father Fred, who founded the company in the 1940s. Koch Industries was named the second largest privately held company in the US last year, with an annual revenue of $115 billion. They bought out their other two brothers for $1.1 Billion which now puts their worth at a hefty $34 Billion each. Quite a sum and they like it that way. Charles famously said the company will offer public shares &#8220;literally over my dead body&#8221;. Aw c&#8217;mon Charlie! Sharing is caring.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;2) Rockefeller&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Rockefeller.png"&gt;&lt;img class=" wp-image-470 aligncenter" title="Rockefeller" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/Rockefeller.png" alt="Rockefeller" width="304" height="439" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_D._Rockefeller" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_D._Rockefeller', 'image source');" title="Rockefeller"  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;The richest of them all, and one of the most famous surnames in the world. Born in 1839 John Davison Rockefeller, Sr. was co-founder of the Standard Oil Company, which dominated the oil industry and domestic kerosene market. When he died, two months before his 98th birthday, Rockefellers net worth was $663.4 Billion. Making him the wealthiest oil tycoon ever. Standard Oil developed over 300 oil-based products including tar, paint, Vaseline petroleum jelly and chewing gum. Often under fire for his questionable business tactics, Rockefeller revolutionised the petroleum industry nonetheless.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;h3&gt;1) JR Ewing&lt;/h3&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: center;"&gt;&lt;a href="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/JR-Ewing.png"&gt;&lt;img class="size-full wp-image-471 aligncenter" title="JR Ewing" src="http://www.jonesoil.ie/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/JR-Ewing.png" alt="JR Ewing" width="328" height="422" /&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;em&gt;&lt;a href="http://blog.chron.com/txpotomac/2012/06/jr-is-back-and-dallas-is-a-melodramatic-destination-once-again/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://blog.chron.com/txpotomac/2012/06/jr-is-back-and-dallas-is-a-melodramatic-destination-once-again/', 'image source');" style="text-align: center; line-height: 1.6em;" title="JR Ewing "  rel="nofollow" target="_blank"&gt;image source&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p style="text-align: left;"&gt;Admit it. This is how you imagined all oil tycoons looked. It&#8217;s hard to put a net worth on this Dallas based oil baron&#8217;s actual fortune because he kept Ewing Oil&#8217;s books close to his chest. But if his wife Sue Ellen&#8217;s jewellery was anything to go by, they weren&#8217;t doing too badly. His rumoured dodgy deals and run-ins with his younger brother and partner Bobby threatened the future of the company many times. Yes you could argue that he&#8217;s not a real oil baron, but to many, he&#8217;s the only one that comes to mind. TV Guide voted him #1 in their 2013 list of The 60 Nastiest Villains of All Time and that&#8217;s quite the achievement.&lt;/p&gt;
