         15 Sci-Fi Spaceships That Rock
        </h1>

       <div class="entry-content">
        <h2 style="text-align: justify;">
         Check out the following 15 Rocket-ships that put your Honda Civic to shame!
        </h2>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          <span style="font-weight: bold;">
           The sky is no limit for a well built spaceship.
          </span>
         </span>
        </h3>
        <h4 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          Whether they are the size of a planet, or small, sleek and fierce, decrepit or shiny new, possessing hallucinating offensive weapons or simply indestructible, sci-fi spaceships made their way into any space lovers heart and imagination. Take a look at this list of 15 most memorable, ass-kicking space crafts and think which one would you like to command, which one is the most scary, the most technological advanced, the best designed, the most mind-blowing of them all.
         </span>
        </h4>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          1. The Borg Cube (Star Trek: Next Generation)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/borg-cube.jpg">
          <img alt="borg cube" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/borg-cube_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="borg cube" width="404"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         The Borg Collective Assimilation Cube. A cube shaped mass of technology, one of the most impressive known to the Federation.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         On stardate 42761.3 (2365), the Borg Cube was encountered near System J-25 in the Delta Quadrant, by the
         <em>
          U.S.S. Enterprise NCC-1701
         </em>
         -D, thanks to Q.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because its the greatest translation in interstellar terms of the new minimalism and less is more design concepts. The Cube is a colossus of over 3000 meters per edge, it hosts tens or hundreds thousands of drones and caries large sphere ships that can be launched for attack. The spaceship lacks any identifiable function spaces like bridge or personnel quarters. It is equipped with organic weapons that can analyse the opponents weaponry and adapt defences within seconds. Other capabilities include transwrap, self-regeneration and various beam and missile weapons.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.startrek.com/database_article/borg-cube">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          2. The Lexx (Lexx)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/lexx.jpg">
          <img alt="lexx" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/lexx_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="lexx" width="404"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A bio-engineered, gigantic-sized (10 000 meters), planet-destroying bioship in the shape of a giant wingless dragonfly.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         In the first episode of Lexx, I Worship His Shadow.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because it can instantly obliterate any planet with a single, high-power blast. The attack commend can be given by the captain only which triggers the release of huge amounts of orange particles from the Ocular Parabola. These are then focused in a devastating planar wave that moves ahead exponentially until colliding with a planet. Being alive (how cool is that?), The Lexx is animated and can be hungry, emotional or cranky. It actively enjoys bringing planets to pieces and can act independently for defence purposes.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lexx">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          3. The U.S.S. Enterprise NCC 1707-D (Star Trek: The Next Generation)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/USS_Enterprise-D.jpg">
          <img alt="USS_Enterprise-D" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/USS_Enterprise-D_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="USS_Enterprise-D" width="537"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A
         <em>
          Galaxy
         </em>
         -class spaceship, the fifth Federation ship to bear the name
         <em>
          Enterprise
         </em>
         . It was the flagship of Starfleet and was the first starship to visit another galaxy.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         Enterprise-D appears in the episode Encounter at Farpoint under the command of Captain Jean-Luc Picard. Was commissioned in 2363 and destroyed in 2371.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because its the emblematic Enterprise model of the already emblematic series. Dont forget that even NASA named its initial flight-test Space Shuttle
         <em>
          Enterprise, as the result of a successful fan letter campaign. With a total of 42 decks, the USS
          <em>
           Enterprise
          </em>
          -D was twice the length and had eight times the interior space of the
          <em>
           Constitution
          </em>
          -class ships of over a century earlier.
         </em>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://memory-alpha.org/wiki/USS_Enterprise_%28NCC-1701-D%29">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          4. The Nostromo (Alien)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/the-nostromo.jpg">
          <img alt="the nostromo" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/the-nostromo_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="the nostromo" width="334"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A M class starship that was repurposed and used to run machine-controlled oil and ore refineries between 20 Reticuli and the Solar system
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         in Alien movie, year 2116
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because it is nostro uomo, the man (ship) that does the job, always. I
         <span style="background-color: #ffff00;">
          <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
           ts an ugly, gritty and derelict flying colossus (towing a 2/1.5 miles refining platform), but thats exactly what it takes to do business into space in year 2116. Sturdy enough to withstand atmospheric re-entries and with a triple shock landing system that can endanger the crew, but never the cargo.
          </span>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://nostromo.joeh.org/content/nostromo/..html">
          <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
           Source
          </span>
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          5. The Star Destroyer (Star Wars)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/imperial-star-destroyer.jpg">
          <img alt="imperial-star-destroyer" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/imperial-star-destroyer_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="imperial-star-destroyer" width="537"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A fierce war vessel that can destroy entire star systems
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         <em>
          Star Wars Episode IV: A New Hope
         </em>
         (1977) referred to as Imperial Cruieser
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because its the war ship par excellence, the enemy ship with attitude. It has an overwhelming firepower and is packed with expensive equipment. It is so impressive and intimidating, that its mere presence is often enough to deter rebellion. No wonder the design of the Star Destroyer stands as influence for all space ships designs ever after.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          <a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Star_Destroyer">
           Source
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          6. The Battlestar Galactica (Battlestar Galactica)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/battlestar-Galatica.jpg">
          <img alt="battlestar Galatica" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/battlestar-Galatica_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="battlestar Galatica" width="404"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         One of the undisclosed number of battleships constructed by the
         <a name="rdb-footnote-link-10">
         </a>
         Twelve Colonies of Man. It represents the Colonial planet
         <a name="rdb-footnote-link-11">
         </a>
         Caprica
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         more than 500
         <a name="rdb-footnote-link-12">
         </a>
         yahren before the close of the
         <a name="rdb-footnote-link-13">
         </a>
         Thousand Yahren War
         <sup>
         </sup>
         (and the start of the pilot film)
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because its maybe the most fetishized and consistent ship design in SF history. Like JR Ewing, the Battelstelar cannot die, no matter what movie sequels are there to be launched. So you can call it a venerable classic  simple, practical and unique, matronly escorting its refugee fleet and ready to protect it with military gear.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battlestar_Galactica_%28ship%29">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          7. The Alien Mothership (Close Encounters of the Third Kind)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/alien-mothership-close-encounters.jpg">
          <img alt="alien mothership close encounters" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/alien-mothership-close-encounters_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="alien mothership close encounters" width="404"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/alien-mothership-details.jpg">
          <img alt="alien mothership details" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/alien-mothership-details_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="alien mothership details" width="404"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A gigantic craft ship that hosts the alien fleet.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         Rising up from behind Devils Tower in Wyoming.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         For the surprise effect. Everything about it is anticipation, the public and the characters are awestruck when it finally appears in all its glory surrounded by rotating colored lights. The cool and funny thing is that, if you look close at the prototype, you can spot playful details not seen in the movie: a Volkswagen Buss, a submarine, a U.S. mailbox and even the R2-D2 android from Star Wars!
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.mutara.net/Collective/mothership.html">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          8. The Shadow Vessel (Babylon 5)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/shadow-vassel.jpg">
          <img alt="shadow vassel" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/shadow-vassel_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="shadow vassel" width="404"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         Also called Battle Crab or The Ghost, the Shadow Vessel is the primary ship of the Shadows.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         Signs and Portents episode of the Babylon 5 TV Series.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because it is a devious masterpiece able inflict psychological terror. A spider, big as death and twice as ugly. When it flies past, its like you hear a scream in your mind.  to be exact. Mischievous as a hungry spider, Shadow vessels cloaked by drifting somewhere between real space and Hyperspace. This made them virtually invulnerable to attack in any of the two spaces while cloaked.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://babylon5.wikia.com/wiki/Shadow_Vessel">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         9. Cheyenne Dropship (Aliens)
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/dropship-aliens.jpg">
          <img alt="dropship aliens" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/dropship-aliens_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="dropship aliens" width="411"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it
         </strong>
         : The
         <strong>
          UD4L Cheyenne
         </strong>
         craft is a dropship used to carry troops and provisions to other planets, part of the USCMC Aerospace Division.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         Aliens Movie, 1986
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks
         </strong>
         : For its unique, kick-ass, flexibility. Is it a fighter jet? Is it an attack helicopter? Is it the Transformer? Designed as a transporter, the Cheyenne can easily become a gunship in case of need, with its weapons pods, fixed point rockets and the blasting internal cannon. It can land and take off from unprepared sites using solely its vertical takeoff capability (VTOL).
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.denofgeek.com/movies/287154/top_75_spaceships_in_movies_and_tv_part_6.html#dropship">

        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         10. Klingon Birds of Prey (Star Trek
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          )
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Klingon_bird-of-pray.jpg">
          <img alt="Klingon_bird of pray" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/Klingon_bird-of-pray_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="Klingon_bird of pray" width="530"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         One of the earliest class of starships encountered by Earth Starfleet during the early 2150s
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         on the outer perimeter of the Sol system in early 2153. (ENT: The Expanse)
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         For its dramatic aggressiveness. A serpentine green avian attacker that descends like a raptor upon its prey. Underneath its feathery looks lie an impressive arsenal of disruptor cannons mounted in the neck, wing-tips and belly. Also watch out for those dual photon torpedo launchers! The Bird of Prey is the first Klingon ship that incorporated a cloaking device.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://memory-alpha.org/wiki/Klingon_Bird-of-Prey_%2822nd_century%29">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         <span style="background-color: #ffffff;">
          11. The Millennium Falcon (Star Wars)
         </span>
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/millenium-falcon-2.jpg">
          <img alt="millenium falcon 2" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/millenium-falcon-2_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="millenium falcon 2" width="438"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         The personal smuggling starship of Han Solo.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         Star Wars Episode IV: A New Hope novel
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because its the ultimate bad ass pirate ship of space. Although it has been often catalogued as an overestimated old freighter, its name is the first one that comes to mind when you think about awesome ships. Insanely modified to fit its owners needs for fight and escape, the Falcon carries military-class laser turrets, concussion missile launchers and a speedy hyperdrive. Legendary for making the Kessel Run in less than 12 parsecs. 
         <em>
          Shes the fastest hunk of junk in the galaxy!
         </em>
          Lando Calrissian
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Millennium_Falcon">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         12. The Heart of Gold (The Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy)
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/heart-of-gold-hitchhiker-guide.jpg">
          <img alt="heart of gold hitchhiker guide" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/heart-of-gold-hitchhiker-guide_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="heart of gold hitchhiker guide" width="406"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it
         </strong>
         : the first prototype ship to successfully utilise the revolutionary Infinite Improbability Drive
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         Built as a secret government project on planet Damogran from where Zaphod Beeblebrox, the then-President of the Imperial Galactic Government, stole it at the launching ceremony.
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because its so damn sexy and smooth. Able to pass through every point in the multi-verse simultaneously thanks to its Infinite Improbability Drive (!), the Heart of Gold is number one in the special features category. The ship is also equipped with the latest in Sirius Cybernetics Corporation robots and computers with GPP technology.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://hitchhikers.wikia.com/wiki/Infinite_Improbability_Drive">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         13. Serenity (Firefly Series)
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/serenity-firefly.jpg">
          <img alt="serenity firefly" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/serenity-firefly_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="serenity firefly" width="404"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A
         <em>
          Firefly
         </em>
         class small transporter spaceship that provides a house for the nine characters of the movie in their legal and illegal adventures in space.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         In the pilot episode of Joss Whedons
         <em>
          Firefly
         </em>
         , which is set in the year 2517, in a star system humanity migrated to after using all of Earths resources. She is the property of Malcolm Mal Reynolds.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         For its lovable frailness. Like the Millennium Falcon, Serenity has the decrepit look of a chewing gum glued old piece of junk ready to fall into pieces. But its value comes from durability and ease of repair and once youve been in Serenity [Valley], you never leave. You just learn to live there.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serenity_%28Firefly_vessel%29">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         14. TARDIS  Time And Relative Dimensions In Space (Dr. Who)
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/tardis.jpg">
          <img alt="tardis" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/tardis_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="tardis" width="379"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A time machine and spacecraft in the British television series
         <em>
          Doctor Who
         </em>
         .
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         In the first
         <em>
          Doctor Who
         </em>
         episode, An Unearthly Child, when the TARDIS landed in London in 1963.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Because of its faulty chameleon circuit that was supposed to make it blend inconspicuously into whatever environment it turned up in, but made it a police box instead. Ha! Thats adorable for a well equipped spaceship, isnt it? Nonetheless, its interior is expandable and it hosts, among others, an ancillary power station disguised as an art gallery, a well organised study, a greenhouse, a library, a swimming pool and a medical bay.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TARDIS">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
        <h3 style="text-align: justify;">
         15. The Death Star (Star Wars)
        </h3>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/death-star-1.jpg">
          <img alt="death-star-1" border="0" height="304" src="http://www.deathstarpizza.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/death-star-1_thumb.jpg" style="background-image: none; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="death-star-1" width="364"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <strong>
          What is it:
         </strong>
         A moons sized battle station, a horrific weapon developed by the Empire, capable of destroying a planet in a single attack.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          First appearance:
         </strong>
         The original Death Star first appears in
         <em>
          A New Hope, c
         </em>
         ommanded by Grand Moff Tarkin.
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Why it rocks:
         </strong>
         Its an instrument of terror, the pinnacle of military space craft through history. Why? Not because its huge size, not necessarily because it takes more than a million military personnel to run it, but because it can simply destroy entire planets with one single terrifying beam. A dark grey, frightening sphere, resembling the moon, with a concave disk housing the stations main laser weapon. It also hosts legions of imperial troops and fightercraft and elaborate detention blocks and interrogation cells. Comparable to the nuclear bomb, in potential power and size of therat, the Death Star is the most prominent evidence of the evil that was the Galactic Empire.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: justify;">
         <a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Death_Star">
          Source
         </a>
        </p>
