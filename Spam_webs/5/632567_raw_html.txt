       <p>
        Chocolate ice cream and deep-dish pizza may be more appealing than kale and almonds, but we usually feel bloated and sluggish after eating fatty, greasy foods.
       </p>
       <p>
        What about foods that are both yummy
        <em>
         and
        </em>
        make us feel happier and more energetic?
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        In a recent
        <a href="http://wellandgood.com/2014/10/20/refrigerator-look-book-siggi-hilmarsson/" target="_blank">
         interview
        </a>
        in the blog Well + Good, Siggi Hilmarsson, founder of Siggi's Icelandic yogurt, talked about his favorite indulgences like dark chocolate said whole milk, saying, I like things that make you feel good an hour after youve eaten them."
       </p>
       <p>
        That's a brilliant concept.
       </p>
       <p>
        Feeling good after eating is behavioral, biological, and depends on our associations or food memories, says Madelyn Fernstrom, TODAY health and nutrition editor. We are hard wired to enjoy more palatable foods, said Fernstrom. My mantra is there are no bad foods, just bad portions.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Foods that are high in carbs provide a lot of satisfaction because they raise our serotonin, the feel-good chemical, said New York nutritionist Bonnie Taub-Dix, R.D. and author of "Read It before You Eat It."
       </p>
       <p>
        Theres the part of us that wants to have the carbs, but then rethink it because its high in calories. The ideal compromise is picking the right food in a group, like whole grain carbs, so we can feel good about it.
       </p>
       <p>
        So, here are a few super foods that you can enjoy while eating and  bonus! make you feel great afterwards.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         1.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Eggs
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="inline-img-container">
        <figure class="entry-img retina-enabled">
         <img alt="Chicken eggs and yolk" src="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2014/November/141121/1D274907272476-ss-141111-foods-feel-good-eggs-02.today-inline-large.jpg" title=""/>
         <small class="entry-img-credit">
          Igor Dutina/FeaturePics.com
         </small>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size: one whole egg.Eggs provide high quality protein. Theyre filling, delicious, and studies have shown that eggs can help you feel full when you include them in your meals versus carbohydrate-rich like starchy, or sweet  meals," said nutritionist Elisa Zied, R.D., New York.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         2.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Berries
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="inline-img-container">
        <figure class="entry-img small-image retina-enabled">
         <img alt="A bowl full of fresh blueberries and strawberries" src="http://media3.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2014/November/141121/1D274907272541-ss-141111-foods-feel-good-berries-02.today-inline-med.jpg" title=""/>
         <small class="entry-img-credit">
          STEVE CUKROV/FeaturePics.com
         </small>
         <div class="entry-img-caption">
          Fresh blueberries and strawberries
         </div>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size:  cup to 1 cup
        <strong>
         . 
        </strong>
        Fruit is natures candy, like fresh apples and fresh berries, said Fernstrom. These are two things that people love, because they have a lot of water, stimulate taste buds, and are easily digestible.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         3.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Canned tuna (white or light)
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size: 3 ounces, about one small can or half of a larger can.
       </p>
       <p>
        Canned tuna is a source of high quality protein that can fill you up and help keep blood sugar levels steady with omega-3-fatty acids, said Zied. Regular fish intake helps preserve body proteinsand that keeps you feeling strong and energized. Regular fish intake is also linked with lower risk of depression.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         4.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Chicken soup
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size: 8-12 ounces. People always feel good after eating chicken soup, said Fernstrom. She said people associate feel-good foods with the way their mouths feel while eating something creamy or warm, and chicken soup is a perfect example. Its the universal warm, healthy food.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         5.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Beans and peas, lentils, chickpeas
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size:  to  cup. These are rich sources of protein and also pack in complex carbohydrates, mainly in the form of resistant starch, said Zied. A study in Public Health Nutrition in 2010 found that moderate intake of legumes, one or two servings weekly, protected menopausal women against severely depressed moods.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         6.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Oats and oatmeal
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size:  cup to 1 cup cooked, or 1-2 packets instant oatmeal with no sodium added.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Oats provide complex carbohydrates that are slowly digested and provide the brain and entire central nervous system with their key source of fuel, said Zied. Carbohydrates also play a key role in creating serotonin, a neurotransmitter in the brain that helps regulate mood, appetite and sleep.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         7. Avocados
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="inline-img-container">
        <figure class="entry-img retina-enabled">
         <img alt="Avocado" src="http://media3.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2014/November/141121/1D274907272477-ss-141114-foods-feel-good-avo.today-inline-large.jpg" title=""/>
         <small class="entry-img-credit">
          FeaturePics.com
         </small>
         <div class="entry-img-caption">
          Avocado is high in omega-3 fatty acids and other essential vitamins and minerals. They're also higher in protein than other fruits.
         </div>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size:  of an avocado.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        Avocados are really creamy, and the texture is so inviting. A lot of people dont realize how healthy they are, said Taub-Dix. You can even swap it for butter in some recipes. Or, you can puree avocado and add to salad dressing.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         8. Hummus
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size: four tablespoons. Hummus is a great source of protein and fiber, but what are you eating it with? said Taub-Dix. If youre eating hummus with tons of pita bread, thats a problem. But try dipping jicama, carrots, zucchini strips; its a great carrier for veggies.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         9.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Yogurt
        </strong>
       </p>
       <div class="inline-img-container">
        <figure class="entry-img retina-enabled">
         <img alt="Raspberry and yogurt" src="http://media4.s-nbcnews.com/j/streams/2014/November/141121/1D274907272542-ss-141114-foods-feel-good-yogurt.today-inline-large.jpg" title=""/>
         <small class="entry-img-credit">
          Rob Stark/FeaturePics.com
         </small>
         <div class="entry-img-caption">
          Raspberry and yogurt.
         </div>
        </figure>
       </div>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size: about 5 ounces.Some yogurts like Greek yogurt are great sources of calcium, and people especially kids  love to dip, said Taub-Dix. Something like a flavored or Greek yogurt is great for dipping, too.
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         10. Nuts and seeds
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size: 1 ounce. Nuts, like almonds, give you that great overall feeling for your taste buds sweet, crunchy, creamy, said Taub-Dix. She also recommends adding almond butter in her oatmeal for a creamy boost of protein.
        <br/>
       </p>
       <p>
        <strong>
         11.
        </strong>
        <strong>
         Tea
        </strong>
       </p>
       <p>
        Suggested serving size: 1 cup. A cup of tea is low-calorie, gives you that comfort, and sometimes, its a speed bump to high-calorie foods, said Taub-Dix. You could wind up having a lot more calories if you hadnt had that cup of tea.
       </p>
