        <a href="http://www.cucumbersome.com/tutorial-leather-chain-necklace/" rel="bookmark" title="Tutorial: Leather Chain Necklace">
         Tutorial: Leather Chain Necklace
        </a>
       </h1>
       <p class="byline">
        <span class="text">
         By
        </span>
        <span class="author vcard">
         <a class="url fn n" href="http://www.cucumbersome.com/author/admin/" title="britt">
          britt
         </a>
        </span>
        <span class="text">
         on
        </span>
        <abbr class="published" title="Wednesday, June 17th, 2009, 1:27 pm">
         June 17, 2009
        </abbr>
       </p>
       <div class="entry-content entry">
        <p>
         <img alt="leather_necklace" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-816" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_necklace.jpg" title="leather_necklace" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         I must really have a thing for soft, fabric jewelry. First, the
         <a href="http://www.cucumbersome.com/diy-recycled-fabric-necklace/">
          t-shirt necklace
         </a>
         , now this. I have been admiring
         <a href="http://handlespout.bigcartel.com/category/necklaces">
          these
         </a>
         leather necklaces by Handle &amp; Spout for quite some time. Can I use the imitation is the sincerest form or flattery line
         <a href="http://www.cucumbersome.com/hi-lo/">
          again
         </a>
         ?
         <br/>
         <strong>
          What you need:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         Leather piece  approximately 12 x 12(if you are in Vancouver, check out
         <a href="http://maps.google.ca/maps?hl=en&amp;um=1&amp;q=dressew&amp;near=Vancouver,+BC&amp;fb=1&amp;view=text&amp;latlng=49282985,-123110280,7996288865381189604">
          Dressew
         </a>
         for cheap leather in various sizes. I got a 24 x 24 piece for $7)
         <br/>
         This
         <a href="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_template.gif">
          template
         </a>
         , printed out on heavy cardstock (The image is 300dpi, and when printed will be 3 wide)
         <br/>
         Utility Knife (like an x-acto knife)
         <br/>
         Scissors
         <br/>
         Needle &amp; thread (matching your leather)
         <br/>
         <strong>
          Instructions:
         </strong>
         <br/>
         After you have printed out
         <a href="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_template.gif">
          this template
         </a>
         on heavy cardstock, cut it out with your exacto knife. This is my paper template (as you can see, my utility knife is not so exact):
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_papertemplate" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-817" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_papertemplate.jpg" title="leather_papertemplate" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Now on the back side of the leather (the rough side) trace your template in pen, like so:
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_trace" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-812" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_trace.jpg" title="leather_trace" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Cut the shape out of the leather with scissors, making sure to cut just inside the pen marks so you dont have any lines showing. To cut out the center, fold it and make a little snip with your scissors and then cut (like the one below). I tried to use the utility knife and it looked really rough and found scissors to be more accurate.
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_cutout" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-813" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_cutout.jpg" title="leather_cutout" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Continue cutting out the leather shapes until you have a bunch. I used 36 pieces to make a longish necklace, but it totally depends on how long you want it to be.
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_stack" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-819" height="341" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_stack.jpg" title="leather_stack" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Fold one piece in half so the smooth side is on the outside. Then push half of the next piece through the hole as shown:
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_firstlink1" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-821" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_firstlink1.jpg" title="leather_firstlink1" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Now fold the second piece down and continue fitting them together:
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_severallinks" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-818" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_severallinks.jpg" title="leather_severallinks" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Keep going, holding it up to your neck until it gets to be your desired length.
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_done" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-814" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_done.jpg" title="leather_done" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         For the closure, you could do something fancy like a snap or a button. My necklace is long and I can just pull it over my head, so I folded the last loop over my first one and stitched it closed:
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_closure" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-829" height="333" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_closure.jpg" title="leather_closure" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         I feel (and look) pretty awkward about posting a picture of myself here, but so you can see the length of the necklace, and the size of the links, here it is:
         <br/>
         <img alt="leather_moi" class="alignleft size-full wp-image-828" height="435" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_moi.jpg" title="leather_moi" width="500"/>
         <br/>
         Notes: You could also buy a leather jacket from a thrift store and cut it up. If you didnt want to use leather, a good quality felt might look nice (just not the cheap stuff  I think it would end up looking like a kindergarten project) or you could use two fabrics fused together with double-sided fusible interfacing.
         <br/>
         Click the thumbnail below for a full-sized version of the template:
         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_template.gif">
          <img alt="leather_template" class="alignleft size-thumbnail wp-image-808" height="150" src="http://www.cucumbersome.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/leather_template-150x150.gif" title="leather_template" width="150"/>
         </a>
        </p>
