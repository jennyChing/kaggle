     <h2 class="dek">
      Dont pretend Bibi didnt mean to offend Obama. He is a bully and a liar.
     </h2>
     <div class="byline" id="main_byline">
      By
      <a href="http://www.slate.com/authors.william_saletan.html" rel="author">
       William Saletan
      </a>

      <div class="text text-1 parbase section">
       <p>
        Benjamin Netanyahu says his speech against President Obamas Iran policy, delivered on the floor of Congress, shouldnt be taken as an affront. 
        <a href="http://www.pmo.gov.il/English/MediaCenter/Speeches/Pages/speechAipac020315.aspx">
         My speech is not intended to show any disrespect to President Obama
        </a>
        , Netanyahu told AIPAC, the pro-Israel lobby group, on Monday. In his remarks to Congress on Tuesday morning, the prime minister brushed aside those who 
        <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2015/03/03/full-text-netanyahus-address-to-congress/">
         perceive my being here as political
        </a>
        .
       </p>
      </div>
      <section class="about-the-author fancy inline with-head">
       <a class="header author-toggle" href="http://www.slate.com/authors.william_saletan.html">
        <header>
         <img alt="William Saletan" class="author-photo" src="/content/dam/slate/Authors/will_saletan-authorbio.png"/>
         <span class="name">
          William Saletan
         </span>
        </header>
       </a>
       <div class="author-bio">
        <p>
         Will Saletan writes about politics, science, technology, and other stuff for
         <b>
          <i>
           Slate
          </i>
         </b>
         . Hes the author of
         <i>
          <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/0520243366/?tag=slatmaga-20" target="_blank">
           Bearing Right
          </a>
         </i>
         .
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/dp/0520243366/?tag=slatmaga-20">
          <i>
           <br/>
          </i>
         </a>
        </p>
       </div>

      <div class="text-2 text parbase section">
       <p>
        But back home, Netanyahu shows no such tolerance. He claims to represent not just all Israelis, but all Jews. When critics question his policies, he purges them from office, challenges their patriotism, and accuses them of serving foreign masters. If anyone were to do in Israel what Netanyahu has just done herewalk into the nations parliament at the unilateral invitation of an opposition party and deliver a speech against the governments foreign policyNetanyahu would have cried treason.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text-3 text parbase section">
       <p>
        Lets get a few excuses out of the way. First, the indisputable purpose of this speech was to enlist Congress as a weapon against Obama. Two weeks ago, according to
        <a href="http://www.haaretz.com/news/diplomacy-defense/.premium-1.642858">
         <em>
          Haaretz
         </em>
        </a>
        , Israels ambassador to the United Statesthe Netanyahu protg who negotiated the speaking engagementtold officials in Jerusalem that Netanyahu was going to Congress because Israel has almost no ability to influence the negotiations through other channels. Last Friday, campaigning in Israel, Netanyahu said he was coming here to lobby 
        <a href="http://www.israelnationalnews.com/News/News.aspx/191929">
         the only body that may prevent
        </a>
         the Iran deal. The gist of both statements is obvious: Netanyahu doesnt like Obamas policy, so hes trying to use Congress to block it.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="pullquotebox section">
       <aside class="pullquote">
        <p class="quote">
         Lets be clear: Netanyahu came to defy Obama.
        </p>
       </aside>
      </div>
      <div class="tablet-ad">
       Advertisement
       <br/>
       <div class="ad " data-ad-mode="tabletPortrait" data-ad-type="midarticleflex" id="slug_midarticleflex">
       </div>
       <div class="ad " data-ad-mode="mobile" data-ad-type="mob_bigbox" id="slug_mob_bigbox">
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-4 section">
       <p>
        Netanyahu says hes doing this only because Iranian nukes are an existential threat to Israel. But this
        <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/10/01/a-short-history-of-awkward-encounters-between-obama-and-netanyahu/">
         isnt the first time
        </a>
        Netanyahu has publicly challenged Obama. The last time he did itlecturing Obama in the Oval Office, in front of television cameras, for
        <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l28xJitnP78">
         seven minutes
        </a>
        in May 2011the subject wasnt Iran. It was peace talks with the Palestinians.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-5 section">
       <p>
        In Israel, Netanyahu is exploiting his fight with the administration. He accuses his rivals in the center and on the left of 
        <a href="http://www.israelnationalnews.com/News/News.aspx/190481">
         groveling to the international community
        </a>
         while he stands up to foreign pressure. A Likud
        <a href="http://jpupdates.com/2015/02/21/new-likud-ad-u-s-opposed-establishment-israel/">
         campaign ad
        </a>
        casts Netanyahu in the tradition of past Israeli leaders who, according to the ad, defied the American secretaryof state and the American State Department.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text text-6 parbase section">
       <p>
        So lets be clear: Netanyahu has come here to defy Obama. He has done so because confrontation is in his nature. And hes politicizing it. You can dismiss all his protestations that the speech shouldnt be taken as an assault on the authority of our head of state. Because thats exactly how Netanyahu treats criticism of his own policies back home.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-7 section">
       <p>
        Two years ago, Netanyahu formed a
        <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/world/middle_east/netanyahu-reaches-agreement-to-form-government/2013/03/14/18beaee8-8c99-11e2-9838-d62f083ba93f_story.html">
         coalition government
        </a>
        with several smaller parties. He got to be prime minister. In exchange, leaders of the other parties got jobs in the cabinet. Israel has a parliamentary system, so the other leaders are members of Israels congress. Two of them criticized some of Netanyahus policies. So, in December, Netanyahu fired them. 
        <a href="http://www.jpost.com/Breaking-News/Netanyahu-I-will-not-tolerate-opposition-in-the-government-383472">
         I will not tolerate opposition anymore within the government
        </a>
        , he declared.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-8 section">
       <p>
        Netanyahu didnt just dissolve the government and force new elections; he demanded greater authority. He announced plans to pass a new law that would
        <a href="http://www.israelnationalnews.com/News/News.aspx/189551">
         strip dissenting parties of their power to check the prime minister
        </a>
        by withdrawing from the government. Under the new law, said Netanyahu, theheadof the largest party will automatically be installed by the government, and will be ensured a four-year term, unless a supermajority of parliament votes to remove him.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-9 section">
       <p>
        In January, after the
        <em>
         Charlie Hebdo
        </em>
        massacre in Paris, France organized a massive march against terrorism. The French government asked Netanyahu and Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas
        <a href="http://www.haaretz.com/news/diplomacy-defense/.premium-1.636557">
         not to attend
        </a>
        . France didnt want Israeli-Palestinian issues or Netanyahus election campaign to cloud the message of the march. Netanyahu attended anyway. In a direct challenge to the national solidarity and pluralism France wanted to convey, Netanyahu urged 
        <a href="http://www.pmo.gov.il/English/MediaCenter/Spokesman/Pages/spokefrastate100115.aspx">
         all French Jews
        </a>
         to move to Israel. He worked his way up to the
        <a href="http://www.timesofisrael.com/report-claims-france-didnt-want-netanyahu-at-paris-march/">
         front row
        </a>
        of the march and plugged his own biography in a speech. I am personally familiar with the wounds of terror, he
        <a href="http://www.pmo.gov.il/English/MediaCenter/Speeches/Pages/speechparis110115.aspx">
         recalled
        </a>
        . As a soldier, I was wounded in an operation to free hostages who had been kidnapped on a Sabena airplane.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text parbase text-10 section">
       <p>
        Having sown division in France, Netanyahu used his trip to quash dissent in Israel. He portrayed his participation at the marchwhich he had decided to attend only after discovering that
        <a href="http://www.haaretz.com/news/diplomacy-defense/.premium-1.636557">
         two other Israeli politicians
        </a>
        would be thereas glory for Israel, since Netanyahu represented the nation. There is great significance in what the world saw, the prime minister of Israel marching with all the world leaders in a united effort against terrorism,
        <a href="http://www.pmo.gov.il/English/MediaCenter/Events/Pages/eventleaders120115.aspx">
         said Netanyahu
        </a>
        . In fact, he
        <a href="http://jpupdates.com/2015/02/09/netanyahu-speaks-french-olim-makes-bold-statements/">
         asserted
        </a>
        , I came to Paris not only as prime minister of Israel, but as a representative of the Jewish people.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text-11 text parbase section">
       <p>
        When Netanyahus point man negotiates a congressional speech behind Obamas back, its just a disagreement among friends. But when a former Obama campaign operative meddles in Israeli politics, Netanyahu treats it as an invasion. A month ago, Netanyahus political party, Likud, found out that an Israeli peace organization had hired a strategist who had previously worked for Obamas 2012 campaign. The strategist also worked for like-minded groups in other countries. Likud demanded that Israeli election officials prohibit the group from participating in the election. Likud
        <a href="http://www.haaretz.com/news/israel-election-2015/.premium-1.639821">
         accused the group
        </a>
        of using foreign funding and said its overseas connections raise a red flag ... regarding the true allegiance of Netanyahus rivals.
       </p>
      </div>

      <div class="text-12 text parbase section">
       <p>
        When Israelis question the wisdom of Netanyahus hard line on Iran, or his plan to speak in Congress, he dismisses them and purports to speak for the whole nation. Two weeks ago, he told Israelis that he would come to Congress 
        <a href="http://www.pmo.gov.il/English/MediaCenter/Speeches/Pages/speechpre160215.aspx">
         representing all the citizens of Israel
        </a>
        . On Sunday, he
        <a href="http://www.pmo.gov.il/English/MediaCenter/Spokesman/Pages/spokeDC010315.aspx">
         went further
        </a>
        : I am the emissary of all Israelis, even those who disagree with me, of the entire Jewish people. A statement from Likud accused dissenters of
        <a href="http://jpupdates.com/2015/02/21/new-likud-ad-u-s-opposed-establishment-israel/">
         betraying national security
        </a>
        : On such a crucial existential  issue for the citizens of Israel, opposition leaders should rise above political and personal considerations and stand alongside Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu.
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="text-13 text parbase section">
       <p>
        So please, Mr. Prime Minister, dont pretend we shouldnt take offense at what you just did. If anybody did the same to you, youd never stand for it.
       </p>
