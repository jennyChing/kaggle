           <br/>
           What is it about lasagna that makes me love it so much?!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           I came across this funny ecard and it cracked me up:
           <br/>
           <a href="http://media.sixsistersstuff.com/uploads/2013/11/Screen-Shot-2013-11-02-at-6.01.05-PM.png">
            <img alt="Screen Shot 2013-11-02 at 6.01.05 PM" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-10494" height="335" src="http://media.sixsistersstuff.com/uploads/2013/11/Screen-Shot-2013-11-02-at-6.01.05-PM.png" width="478"/>
           </a>
           To me, lasagna is comfort food at its finest! (Especially when its moms lasagna!)
          </p>
          <p>
           A couple of years ago, I shared a delicious
           <a href="http://www.sixsistersstuff.com/2011/11/slow-cooker-buffalo-chicken-lasagna.html">
            <strong>
             Slow Cooker Buffalo Chicken Lasagna
            </strong>
           </a>
           (please oh please dont judge it by its picture! That was when we had first started our blog and our only reader was our mom . . . I probably took that picture with my camera phone! Ha ha!). Its one of my favorites, but the hard thing with slow cooker meals is if you dont plan hours in advance, you are out of luck!
          </p>
          <p style="text-align: center;">
           So with an hour until dinner time, I decided that I would convert the recipe to go into my oven. The results were AWESOME! If you have a buffalo chicken lover around, you will definitely want to try this recipe.
          </p>
          <p>
           <span style="text-decoration: underline;">
            <strong>
             Buffalo Chicken Lasagna
            </strong>
           </span>
           <br/>
           <em>
            <strong>
             Ingredients:
            </strong>
           </em>
           <br/>
           10 lasagna noodles, cooked and cooled
           <br/>
           2-3 cups cooked chicken, shredded
           <br/>
           1 (26 oz) jar spaghetti sauce
           <br/>
           1 cup buffalo sauce (I love Franks)
           <br/>
           1 tablespoon olive oil
           <br/>
           2 green peppers, diced
           <br/>
           1 onion, diced
           <br/>
           1 clove garlic, minced
           <br/>
           1 zucchini, diced (optional- I am just always looking for ways to get more vegetables)
           <br/>
           2 celery stalks, diced
           <br/>
           1 (15 oz) container ricotta cheese (or you could use cottage cheese)
           <br/>
           1 (4 oz) container bleu cheese crumbles
           <br/>
           1/2 teaspoon dried parsley
           <br/>
           2 cups mozzarella cheese
           <br/>
           2 cups cheddar cheese
          </p>
          <p>
           <em>
            <strong>
             Directions:
            </strong>
           </em>
           <br/>
           Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
           <br/>
           In a large bowl, mix together the cooked chicken, spaghetti sauce, and buffalo sauce. Set aside.
           <br/>
           Add olive oil to a large skillet over medium high heat. Once oil is heated, add green peppers, onion, garlic, zucchini, and celery. Saute until vegetables are tender and add to chicken and sauce mixture.
           <br/>
           In another bowl, mix together the ricotta, bleu cheese and parsley.
           <br/>
           Now its time to layer! Spread about 1 cup of the chicken and sauce mixture into the bottom of a 913 baking pan sprayed with non-stick cooking spray. Place three noodles on top. Spread about 2/3 cup of the ricotta cheese mix on top, then 2/3 cup mozzarella cheese, and 2/3 cup cheddar cheese. Repeat layers 2 more times.
           <br/>
           Cover pan with aluminum foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove foil and bake for about 20 more minutes (until cheese is nice and bubbly).
           <br/>
           Makes about 12 servings.
          </p>
          <p>
           *In the picture, I topped the lasagna with some diced green peppers.
          </p>
