        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Alfredo Arugula Pizza
        </h1>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <div class="pf-content">
         <p>
          Happy Monday!! It is definitely a happy Monday around here because we are on vacation!!! We love being on vacation, especially when we get to spend time with family too. This week we are hanging out at Lake Norris in Tennessee with my husbands family. We are both excited to relax and enjoy life at the lake. This is my last trip before little miss makes her arrival in just two months! I cant believe September is so close (and next time we travel somewhere we will be family of three crazy!)
          <a href="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1710.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
            <img alt="DSC_1710" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-3694" height="680" src="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1710-1024x680.jpg" width="1024"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thescentoforanges.com/recipes-2/meals/alfredo-arugula-pizza/&amp;media=http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1710-1024x680.jpg&amp;description=Alfredo Arugula Pizza')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          Because we are in full vacation mode I decided to cut out one of my posts for this week, so there will be no post on Thursday, but rest assured, the rest of this week I will keep the recipes coming:-) We are going to start things off with a little pizza. I know my husband is pretty much always thrilled when I tell him we are having pizza for dinner and this is one I was really excited to try. We have another tomato free pizza for my non-tomato-eating friends
          <em>
           and
          </em>
          we get to use our Alfredo sauce again. Double win:-)
          <a href="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1700.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
            <img alt="DSC_1700" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-3802" height="680" src="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1700-1024x680.jpg" width="1024"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thescentoforanges.com/recipes-2/meals/alfredo-arugula-pizza/&amp;media=http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1700-1024x680.jpg&amp;description=Alfredo Arugula Pizza')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          After making the pasta, pizza seemed like the next must-make with my new Alfredo sauce. I used my tradition garlic herb crust. I actually left the herbs out this time because I really wanted the Alfredo flavor to shine through with minimal competition. After the crust and sauce were ready to go I got to work on my toppings. We like chicken on our pizza so I baked some of that with a little bit of mushrooms. I roasted them together in the oven, but you can cook them however you prefer. Even thought the sauce has no dairy in it, it still has that cheesy flavor thanks to our nutritional yeast. Since my husband doesnt love the flavor of cheese I left our actual cheese layer really thin to satisfy us both:-) The last topping was a large handful of fresh arugula. I discovered that cooked arugula looses all of its strong flavor so you can really pile this one on if you want. Fresh arugula is very potent, but cook arugula is mild as can be. The overall combination was pretty fantastic and I think it is safe to say we will be repeating this recipe in the near future:-)
          <a href="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1715.jpg">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest ">
            <img alt="DSC_1715" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-3803" height="680" src="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1715-1024x680.jpg" width="1024"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thescentoforanges.com/recipes-2/meals/alfredo-arugula-pizza/&amp;media=http://www.thescentoforanges.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/DSC_1715-1024x680.jpg&amp;description=Alfredo Arugula Pizza')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Alfredo Arugula Pizza
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/recipes-2/meals/tuscan-grilled-chicken-pizza/" target="_blank" title="Tuscan Grilled Chicken Pizza">
           GF pizza crust
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.thescentoforanges.com/recipes-2/meals/df-creamy-alfredo-sauce/" target="_blank" title="DF Creamy Alfredo Sauce">
           DF creamy Alfredo sauce
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          Grated mozzarella cheese, very light sprinkling
         </p>
         <p>
          4 oz sliced mushrooms
         </p>
         <p>
          1 chicken breast
         </p>
         <p>
          Handful of fresh arugula
         </p>
         <p>
          Sprinkle of black pepper
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Mix together pizza dough ingredients (see link above for the recipe I used). Preheat oven to 450 F. Form crust against the bottom of a pizza or pie pan or roll dough out flat.
          </li>
          <li>
           Soak cashews overnight, or at least for a few hours. Drain off the excess water and add all the Alfredo ingredients to blender and mix until smooth.
          </li>
          <li>
           Bake chicken and mushrooms in the oven (or on the stove) seasoned with a little salt and pepper.
          </li>
          <li>
           Pre-bake crust for 7 minutes (oven still at 450 F). Spread alfredo sauce over the crust and sprinkle black pepper over the top, followed by a very thin layer of cheese. (You can do it as thin or thick as you like, but we preferred less cheese since it is combined with the Alfredo sauce.) Layer chicken and mushrooms and arugula over the top. Bake for an additional 8-10 minutes.
          </li>
          <li>
           Slice, eat slowly and smile.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          * We added sliced tomatoes and fresh basil to the top of ours and it made the pizza even better. Because this is a tomato free pizza I didnt include this on the actual recipe, but for anyone who can eat tomatoes, we definitely recommend adding them in:-)
