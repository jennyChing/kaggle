        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Savory Riced Cauliflower  Low Carb Side Dish
        </h1>
        <a class="YUMMLY-YUM-BUTTON" href="//yummly.com" rel="nofollow">
         Yum
        </a>
        <script src="https://www.yummly.com/js/widget.js?wordpress">
        </script>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-01-20T11:41:34+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          January 20, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://wifemomgeek.com/author/Donella Crigger/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            Donella Crigger
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <a href="http://wifemomgeek.com/savory-riced-cauliflower-low-carb-side-dish/#comments">
           11 Comments
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <input class="jpibfi" type="hidden"/>
        <p>
         <a href="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower-pin.jpg">
          <img alt="Savory Riced Cauliflower - You can make this dish even if you only have frozen cauliflower on hand." class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter wp-image-1028" data-lazy-src="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower-pin.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="714" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="500"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Savory Riced Cauliflower - You can make this dish even if you only have frozen cauliflower on hand." class="aligncenter wp-image-1028" height="714" src="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower-pin.jpg" width="500"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Last week, I wanted to make riced cauliflower, but unfortunately, we didnt have any of the fresh stuff. We often by frozen bags of cauliflower for steaming in the microwave, so I wondered if I could still make riced cauliflower despite the moisture from the frozen stuff. I did, and it was delicious.
         <span id="more-1027">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         While Ill always prefer the fresh stuff because its drier and fries up better, this absolutely works in a pinch for any of your
         <a href="http://wifemomgeek.com/simple-low-carb-meals/">
          simple low carb meals
         </a>
         . Its also great if youre on a budget, because the bags of frozen cauliflower can often be bought for a buck, while a head of frozen cauliflower is around $3 or more here. Either way, its a win-win.
        </p>
        <p>
         I didnt snap a picture of the ingredients, because by this time, my husband was bemoaning the fact that he was starving. I just rushed through it and served it up with these
         <a href="http://wifemomgeek.com/low-carb-meatloaf-muffins/" target="_blank">
          low-carb meatloaf muffins
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower.jpg">
          <img alt="Savory Riced Cauliflower" class="lazy lazy-hidden aligncenter size-full wp-image-1030" data-lazy-src="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" height="800" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="1000"/>
          <noscript>
           <img alt="Savory Riced Cauliflower" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1030" height="800" src="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower.jpg" width="1000"/>
          </noscript>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres the recipe. Use it. Eat it. Yum.
         <br/>
        </p>
        <div class="easyrecipe" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
         <div class="ERSName" itemprop="name">
          Savory Riced Cauliflower - Low Carb Side Dish
         </div>
         <div class="ERSClear">
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTopRight">
          <img class="lazy lazy-hidden" data-lazy-src="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower-pin.jpg" data-lazy-type="image" itemprop="image" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODdhAQABAPAAAP///wAAACwAAAAAAQABAEACAkQBADs=" width="205"/>
          <noscript>
           <img itemprop="image" src="http://wifemomgeek.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/01/savory-riced-cauliflower-pin.jpg" width="205"/>
          </noscript>
          <div class="ERSSavePrint">
           <span class="ERSPrintBtnSpan">
            <a class="ERSPrintBtn" href="http://wifemomgeek.com/easyrecipe-print/1027-0/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
             Print
            </a>
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSTimes">
          <div class="ERSTime">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Prep time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT5M" itemprop="prepTime">
             5 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Cook time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT15M" itemprop="cookTime">
             15 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSTime ERSTimeRight">
           <div class="ERSTimeHeading">
            Total time
           </div>
           <div class="ERSTimeItem">
            <time datetime="PT20M" itemprop="totalTime">
             20 mins
            </time>
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSClearLeft">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          This low carb side dish can be made from frozen cauliflower. It has a savory flavor that pairs well with meatloaf and other comfort foods.
         </div>
         <div class="divERSHeadItems">
          <div class="ERSAuthor">
           Author:
           <span itemprop="author">
            Donella Crigger
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSCategory">
           Recipe type:
           <span itemprop="recipeCategory">
            Side Dish, Low Carb
           </span>
          </div>
          <div class="ERSServes">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            4 servings
           </span>
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 bag frozen cauliflower
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 carrots
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             medium onion
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tbsp. olive oil
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp. seasoned salt
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. black pepper
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Defrost the frozen cauliflower by running warm water over it. Drain well, and pat dry with paper towels.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Put the cauliflower in your food processor, or use your blender on the "chop" setting. Pulse a couple of times until the cauliflower is small like rice.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Heat 1 tbsp. olive oil in a skillet. Add cauliflower, onions and carrots. Cook until veggies are almost done.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Season with 1 tsp. seasoned salt and  tsp. black pepper. Cook for a few more minutes until the cauliflower starts to brown, stirring frequently.
           </li>
          </ol>
