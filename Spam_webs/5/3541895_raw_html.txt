      <h2>
       Black Moon Dead Sea
      </h2>
      <p class="meta">
       16 februari, 2014 door
       <a href="http://www.takeacharcoal.com/author/sarah/" rel="author" title="Berichten van Sarah Bijlsma">
        Sarah Bijlsma
       </a>
      </p>
      <p>
       When I saw
       <strong>
        Black Moon Dead Sea
       </strong>
       for the first time, I was attracted by the combination of dreamy images happening in the actual world. I couldnt see it as real fiction, because it was clear the scenery of the Dead Sea
       <span id="more-3005">
       </span>
       plays an elementary part in the video. Israeli artist
       <strong>
        Sharon Glazberg
       </strong>
       (Tel Aviv, 1976) made with this work a portrait of the Dead Sea, which is such a magical place in itself. When there is no room for living creatures, a place automatically appears to ba a little out of space. To let sci-fi meet with natural images Black Moon Dead Sea captures the ambiance of the lake in its form. The three different story lines are linked by their actions and round shapes, creating a very interesting interaction with one another.
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.krop.com/sglazberg/#/" target="_blank">
         Website Sharon Glazberg
        </a>
       </strong>
      </p>
