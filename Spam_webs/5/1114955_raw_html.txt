       <h1 itemprop="headline name">
        Bubba's new generation should worry Europe
       </h1>
       <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline description">
        Watson's battle with Spieth at Augusta shows the US have both the passion and 
  the personalities to win back the Ryder Cup at Gleneagles
       </h2>
       <style media="print">
        .newGigyaShare { display: none !important; }

         <div>
          <p class="bylineBody">
           <span itemprop="author">
            By James Corrigan
            <br/>
            Telegraph golf correspondent
           </span>
          </p>
         </div>
         <p class="publishedDate">
          9:01AM BST 17 Apr 2014
         </p>
         <meta content="2014-04-17" itemprop="datePublished"/>
         <div class="cl">
         </div>
        </div>
        <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
         <div class="firstPar">
          <p>
           There are those who believe the scoring for the
           <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sponsored/sport/standard-life-ryder-cup/">
            Ryder Cup
           </a>
           starts on the Friday morning and finishes on the Sunday evening. Those poor dolts really do not get it.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="secondPar">
          <p>
           For at least a year before the biennial dust-up there is an unofficial counter ticking furiously in the background. Granted, it is only based on opinion as to which team are looking the strongest and it means nought when the match tees off. But in terms of the build-up, of the anticipation, of the excitement, this mythical gauge is as crucial as it is irresistible.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="thirdPar">
          <p>
           It is prone to fluctuate wildly, especially after the majors. The 78th Masters was the perfect case in point. Ladbrokes reports that the United States were brought in to 5-4 from 6-4 and if that does not sound a great amount then when it is measured in pounds and pence, or dollars and cents, the fractions convert to millions.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fourthPar">
          <p>
           Bubba Watson won his second green jacket in three years and runner-up Jordan Spieth went closer than any 20-year-old in trying to triumph on his debut at the Augusta National. Those were the main headlines and it is no wonder that the Ryder Cup perception was thus covered in stars and stripes.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="fifthPar">
          <p>
           Bubba Watson did his bit, reacting to the tweet sent by Tom Watson, the US captain  see you on the plane to Gleneagles  by declaring that his biggest mission for the rest of the season is to win the Ryder Cup.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="body">
          <p>
           Bubba has been on two losing teams. Bubba does not want the hat-trick.
          </p>
          <p>
           The mercurial left-hander will see the likes of Spieth and the other youngsters coming through and feel confident that the US can win just their second Ryder Cup this century. Bubba Watson may have turned on all his creative powers, as well as his experience, to deny Spieth history in the final round at Augusta but he knew what he had witnessed. That boy is some competitor, Bubba Watson said. Jeez, hes a good player.
          </p>
          <p>
           And as impressed as his captain would have been with Spieths skill set, it would have been his attitude which really appealed.
          </p>
          <p>
           It stings right now, and the only thing Im thinking about is when am I getting back next year, Spieth said. Ive worked my whole life to lead Augusta on Sunday, and although I feel like its very early in my career and Ill have more chances, its a stinger.
          </p>
          <p>
           And I had it in my hands and I could have gone forward with it but I just didnt quite make the putts. It hurts.
          </p>
          <p>
           That is the sort of character Tom Watson is looking for at Gleneagles. A player to whom second best is no sort of best at all.
          </p>
          <p>
           There are others from Spieths brave new generation also promising to be in the Highlands in September. Harris English and Patrick Reed are proven winners on the PGA Tour and both, to varying degrees, are ultra confident. Should they make it  and it will be a surprise if they fail  they will not go in afraid.
          </p>
          <p>
           In fact, Tom Watson will scroll down the US standings and feel a surge of optimism. Inevitably, the focus has been on Tiger Woodss back operation and whether he will be fit to play. Tom Watson has said he will pick him if healthy and so we will argue the rights and wrongs of that decision. In truth, Woods is not crucial to the US cause as captain Paul Azinger proved in 2008 when inflicting on Europe their one defeat in the last six matches.
          </p>
          <p>
           Tom Watson has enough players in his armoury, many of whom appeared on the Augusta leaderboard. Jimmy Walker, a three-time winner on the PGA Tour this campaign, gave it a run on his debut, gaining a top 10, while Matt Kuchar was many experts idea of the winner on Sunday morning until tailing off to finish in a tie for fifth with Rickie Fowler, the forerunner to Spieth as the American wonderkid.
          </p>
          <p>
           If there is a worry, beyond Woods, it will centre around the partnership of Keegan Bradley and Phil Mickelson, which was so productive at Medinah.
          </p>
          <p>
           Mickelson is camped in the automatic positions as Tom Watson will feel fairly sure he will remain so, regardless of a few niggling injuries.
          </p>
          <p>
           Bradley, however, is down in 19th place and Tom Watson will be seeking improvement from the New Englander.
          </p>
          <p>
           Bradley displayed a fighting spirit at Medinah that America had been lacking for so long.
          </p>
          <p>
           Europe captain Paul McGinley, of course, has no such worries with Ian Poulter, his motivator-in-chief, firmly installed within the automatic berths, despite his slow start to the season. And he would have found much to fret about at the Masters. There are two sides to every scoreboard and he would have noted that there were six Europeans in the top 13 and nine in the top 20, the same as the US.
          </p>
          <p>
           Alongside Spieth in second, the Swede Jonas Blixt established himself as a genuine contender, while, in fourth, Miguel ngel Jimnez, 50, showed he means business in his mission to become Europes oldest player in Ryder Cup history.
          </p>
          <p>
           Thomas Bjorn impressed until the Sunday afternoon and Jamie Donaldson  another stick-on to make the team  chalked up his second top 15 in the majors.
          </p>
          <p>
           Yet perhaps most pleasing for McGinley was the return to form of Lee Westwood, in seventh. McGinley wants Westwood to make his ninth appearance, not just because of his golf but because of his presence in the team-room.
          </p>
          <p>
           That is what the Ryder Cup is all about; not simply about the numbers on the scoreboard but the camaraderie, the passion, the feeling. And at the moment, while the golf just about points to the US, now holding three out of the four major titles, in terms of the personalities it seems dead level. In the months ahead, however, the mood will change... and change... and change.
          </p>

             standardlifeinvestments.com/rydercup
            </a>
           </strong>
          </p>

        /*
         Read Next Widget
         */
        var readNextWidget = (function(){

            var settings = {
                articleImage: "",
                articleTitle: "",
                articleUrl: "#",
                articleId: "",
                currentArticleId: "",
                articleList: [],
                articleListToSkip: [],
                readNextCookieName: "tmg_read_next",
                openButton: "#readNextOpen a",
                closeButton: "#closeBtn a",
                articleContainer: "#readNextArticleContainer",
                widgetContainer: "#readNextWidget",
                displayThumbnail: false,
                panelOpen: false,
                autoOpen: true
            };

            var renderTemplate = function() {
                var template = '&lt;div id="readNextWidget" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextOpen"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;div id="readNextOpenInner"&gt;&lt;a href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;Next Story&lt;/span&gt;&lt;span class="icn"&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="readNextArticleContainer" style="display:none"&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticle"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;div id="nextArticleInnerWrapper"&gt;'+
                        '&lt;p id="readNextLabel"&gt;MOST VIEWED IN NEWS&lt;/p&gt;';
                        
				if(settings.articleImage &amp;&amp; settings.articleImage!="null" &amp;&amp; settings.displayThumbnail){
					template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageThumbnail" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;img src="'+ settings.articleImage +'"/&gt;&lt;/a&gt;';
				}
                        
				template = template + '&lt;a id="nextPageTitle" href="' + settings.articleUrl + '"&gt;&lt;span&gt;' + settings.articleTitle + '&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;div id="closeBtn"&gt;&lt;div class="topBar"&gt;&lt;/div&gt;&lt;a href="#"&gt;&lt;span&gt;&lt;/span&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;/div&gt;' +
                        '&lt;iframe style="border:none;position:absolute;top:0;left:0; height:100%;width:100%;z-index:-1;" id="iefix" src="about:blank"&gt;&lt;/iframe&gt;' + //fix for IE bug
                        '&lt;/div&gt;';
                return template;
            };


            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }

                        if($(window).scrollTop() + $(window).height() == $(document).height()) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {
                                openArticleContainer();
                            }
                        }
                    });
                }
            };

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();

    };
