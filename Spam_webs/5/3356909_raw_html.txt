     <h1>
      IRS contracted with email archiving  company in 2005
     </h1>
     <div class="author">
      By
      <a href="/thomas_lifson">
       Thomas Lifson
      </a>
     </div>
     <div class="article_body top">
      <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
       <span style="font-size: 14px;">
        Is that a smoking gun I smell? It turns out the IRS contracted with a company that provides email backup services starting in 2005. This first came to light in the Twitter feed of
        <a href="https://twitter.com/morgenr/status/480033982701371392">
         moregenr
        </a>
        , who noticed that the IRS appears on the client list of
        <a href="http://www.sonasoft.com/company/customers/">
         email archiving service provider Sonasoft
        </a>
        .
       </span>
      </span>
      <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" lang="en">
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         The IRS is listed as a customer of Sonasoft software - "Email Archiving Done Right"
         <a href="http://t.co/DoRYpk6n2R">
          http://t.co/DoRYpk6n2R
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          Morgen (@morgenr)
         <a href="https://twitter.com/morgenr/statuses/480033982701371392">
          June 20, 2014
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
      </blockquote>
     </div>

      <p>
       <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
        <span style="font-size: 14px;">
         This was was picked up by Peter Suderman of
         <a href="http://reason.com/blog/2014/06/20/the-irs-had-a-contract-with-an-email-bac?fb_action_ids=10203926514421456&amp;fb_action_types=og.likes">
          Reason,
         </a>
         who writes:
        </span>
       </span>
      </p>
      <blockquote>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          The IRS had a contract with email backup service vendor Sonasoft starting in 2005,
          <a href="http://www.fedspending.org/fpds/fpds.php?reptype=r&amp;database=fpds&amp;unique_transaction_id=6b1e1ac07603a819d7c7d1c22d9eea24&amp;detail=3&amp;datype=T&amp;sortp=i">
           according to FedSpending.org
          </a>
          , which lists the contract as being for "automatic data processing services."Sonasoft's motto is "email archiving done right," and the company
          <a href="http://www.sonasoft.com/company/customers/">
           lists
          </a>
          the IRS as a customer.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          In 2009, Sonasoft even
          <a href="https://twitter.com/Sonasoft/status/4738641591">
           sent out a Tweet
          </a>
          advertising its work for the IRS.
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p style="text-align: center;">
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          <img alt="" src="http://www.americanthinker.com/images/bucket/2014-06/192637_5_.png" style="width: 599px; height: 356px;"/>
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
       <p>
        <span style="font-family:times new roman,times,serif;">
         <span style="font-size: 14px;">
          The exact details of the service that Sonasoft provided to the IRS aren't clear. But the company
          <a href="http://www.sonasoft.com/products/email-archiving-promotions/">
           advertises
          </a>
          its email archiving solution as "ideal for small and medium businesses, government agencies, school districts, nonprofit organizations using Microsofts Exchange Server." And a document
          <a href="http://www.sonasoft.com/downloads/whitepapers/sonavault/Osterman-Research/Osterman-Research-Email-Archiving-Executive-Summary-Sonasoft.pdf">
           posted
          </a>
          on its website describing its services says that its system "archives all email content and so reduces the risk of non-compliancewith legal, regulatory and other obligations to preserve critical business content."
         </span>
        </span>
       </p>
      </blockquote>
