     <p>
      <b>
       Charlotte Maria Church
      </b>
      (born
      <b>
       Charlotte Maria Reed
      </b>
      ; 21 February 1986) is a
      <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">
       Welsh
      </a>
      singer-songwriter, actress and television presenter.
     </p>
     <p>
      She rose to fame in childhood as a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_classical_music" title="European classical music">
       classical singer
      </a>
      before branching into pop music in 2005. By 2007, she had sold more than 10 million records worldwide
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      including over 5 million in the United States.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In 2010 she was reported to be worth as much as
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C2%A3" title="">
       
      </a>
      11m
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      (though one 2003 report quoted her worth at 25m
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      ). She hosted a
      <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">
       Channel 4
      </a>
      chat show titled
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/The_Charlotte_Church_Show" title="The Charlotte Church Show">
        The Charlotte Church Show
       </a>
      </i>
      . Church released her first album in five years, titled
      <i>
       Back to Scratch
      </i>
      , on 17 October 2010. Church has a
      <a href="/wiki/Soprano" title="Soprano">
       soprano
      </a>
      vocal range.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">
       Early life and education
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life and education">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Charlotte Church was born
      <b>
       Charlotte Maria Reed
      </b>
      in
      <a href="/wiki/Llandaff" title="Llandaff">
       Llandaff
      </a>
      , a district of
      <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">
       Cardiff
      </a>
      , Wales. She was brought up as a
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">
       Roman Catholic
      </a>
      by her mother, Maria, who was separated from Church's biological father, Stephen Reed. Church was adopted by her mother's second husband, James Church, in 1999.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Her musical break came at age 11 when she sang
      <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Lloyd_Webber" title="Andrew Lloyd Webber">
       Andrew Lloyd Webber
      </a>
      's "
      <a href="/wiki/Pie_Jesu" title="Pie Jesu">
       Pie Jesu
      </a>
      " over the telephone on the television show
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/This_Morning_(TV_programme)" title="This Morning (TV programme)">
        This Morning
       </a>
      </i>
      in 1997, followed by her performance on
      <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">
       ITV
      </a>
      's
      <i>
       Big, Big Talent Show
      </i>
      in 1997.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      This led to concerts at
      <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_Arms_Park" title="Cardiff Arms Park">
       Cardiff Arms Park
      </a>
      , the
      <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">
       Royal Albert Hall
      </a>
      and opening for Dame
      <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Bassey" title="Shirley Bassey">
       Shirley Bassey
      </a>
      in Antwerp. She also received a vocal scholarship to
      <a href="/wiki/Howell%27s_School_Llandaff" title="Howell's School Llandaff">
       Howell's School Llandaff
      </a>
      in Cardiff where she started in 1998 after leaving
      <a href="/wiki/The_Cathedral_School,_Llandaff" title="The Cathedral School, Llandaff">
       The Cathedral School, Llandaff
      </a>
      . She balanced performing and school with help from tutors for when she was on the road and said in many interviews that she was "just like every other girl her age". Charlotte left school at age 16.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="1998.E2.80.932005:_Classical_career">
       19982005: Classical career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 19982005: Classical career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      As a classical music singer, Church used to sing in English,
      <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">
       Welsh
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">
       Latin
      </a>
      , Italian and French. She was then introduced to the Cardiff impresario Jonathan Shalit, who became her manager and negotiated a contract with
      <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">
       Sony Music
      </a>
      . Her first album,
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Voice_of_an_Angel" title="Voice of an Angel">
        Voice of an Angel
       </a>
      </i>
      , was a collection of
      <a href="/wiki/Aria" title="Aria">
       arias
      </a>
      , sacred songs, and traditional pieces that sold millions worldwide and made her the youngest artist with a No. 1 album on the British classical crossover charts.
     </p>
     <p>
      Church appeared on US
      <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">
       Public Broadcasting Service(PBS)
      </a>
      specials. Her
      <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Church_(album)" title="Charlotte Church (album)">
       self-titled second album
      </a>
      also included operatic, religious and traditional tracks. One, the soaring and inspirational
      <i>
       Just Wave Hello
      </i>
      , was the centre piece of a millennium-themed ad campaign for the
      <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">
       Ford Motor Company
      </a>
      . The song's full-length video, featuring Church, won acclaim at the

Her spokesperson released the following statement: "All I can really say, because of the confidentiality issues, is that it was in Charlotte's financial interests to do so before the agreement entered the second year of its term. This is typical for these type of deals, which are investment deals rather than record deals. I can also say that the decision to terminate the term early, which suited both parties, was made well before the commercial release of the album".      <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">
       Personal life
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Charlotte_Church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Church released an autobiography titled
      <i>
       Voice of an Angel (My Life So Far)
      </i>
      at 14.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
       <a href="#cite_note-29">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        29
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      She released a second autobiography titled
      <i>
       Keep Smiling
      </i>
      in late 2007.
     </p>
     <p>
      Church's personal life has often been portrayed in UK
      <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">
       tabloid
      </a>
      newspapers, inspiring the song "Let's Be Alone" on her album
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Tissues_and_Issues" title="Tissues and Issues">
        Tissues and Issues
       </a>
      </i>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      At age 15 she was criticised for remarks she made about the behaviour of people in the aftermath of the
      <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">
       11 September 2001 attacks
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
       <a href="#cite_note-30">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        30
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      At the
      <a href="/wiki/Leveson_Inquiry" title="Leveson Inquiry">
       Leveson Inquiry
      </a>
      she stated that her comments were taken vastly out of context.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">
       <a href="#cite_note-31">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        31
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In a 2006 interview with
      <a href="/wiki/Davina_McCall" title="Davina McCall">
       Davina McCall
      </a>
      , she agreed being diplomatic was "not in her nature".
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
       <a href="#cite_note-32">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        32
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      In November 2011, Church testified before the
      <a href="/wiki/Leveson_Inquiry" title="Leveson Inquiry">
       Leveson Inquiry
      </a>
      about the media intrusion into her personal life stating "I've been made a caricature for so long, and this person portrayed in the tabloids really isn't me. It's not the person I am, and it's had a massive impact on my career. As an artist, I find it hard to be taken seriously because my credibility has been blown to bits."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
       <a href="#cite_note-33">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        33
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On 27 February 2012 Church accepted 600,000 in damages and costs in settlement of a lawsuit arising out of the
      <a href="/wiki/News_International_phone_hacking_scandal" title="News International phone hacking scandal">
       News International phone hacking scandal
      </a>
      . She had claimed that 33 stories about her that appeared in the
      <a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World" title="News of the World">
       News of the World
      </a>
      were the product of illegal hacking into her family's voice mail. After the settlement was announced in open court she made a lengthy statement
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
       <a href="#cite_note-34">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        34
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      in which she said, "I have also discovered that despite the apology which the newspaper has just given in court, these people were prepared to go to any lengths to prevent me exposing their behaviour. They are not truly sorry. They are just sorry they got caught."
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
       <a href="#cite_note-35">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        35
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      The press devoted much attention to Church's relationship with boyfriend
      <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Henson" title="Gavin Henson">
       Gavin Henson
      </a>
      , a Welsh rugby player. At the end of 2005, she bought a property in her native
      <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">
       Cardiff
      </a>
      for a reported 500,000which she later sold for 900,000. The couple then bought a
      <a href="/wiki/Manor_house" title="Manor house">
       manor
      </a>
      with a 20-acre (8 hectare)
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Small_holding" title="Small holding">
       small holding
      </a>
      in the
      <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Glamorgan" title="Vale of Glamorgan">
       Vale of Glamorgan
      </a>
      in the village of
      <a href="/wiki/St_Brides_Major" title="St Brides Major">
       St. Bride's Major
      </a>
      . The couple mentioned marriage on talk shows and in the press
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hello_36-0">
       <a href="#cite_note-hello-36">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        36
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      but put off marriage while Church was pregnant.
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
      In 2007, Church made another appearance on a British young people's
      <a href="/wiki/Sunday_Times_Rich_List" title="Sunday Times Rich List">
       rich list
      </a>
      with Henson. They were ranked 49th-richest young people in Britain with an estimated joint wealth of 12 million.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
       <a href="#cite_note-37">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        37
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The couple bought a
      <a href="/wiki/Princess_Yachts" title="Princess Yachts">
       Princess yacht
      </a>
      in spring 2008 for 800,000, named
      <i>
       Sketchy,
      </i>
      which is berthed at
      <a href="/wiki/Swansea_docks" title="Swansea docks">
       Swansea marina
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
       [
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
         <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2015)">
          citation needed
         </span>
        </a>
       </i>
       ]
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On 20 September 2007, Church gave birth to a daughter.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
       <a href="#cite_note-38">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        38
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      On 11 January 2009, Church gave birth to a son.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
       <a href="#cite_note-39">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        39
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
       <a href="#cite_note-40">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        40
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The
      <i>
       <a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">
        Daily Mail
       </a>
      </i>
      reported on 31 May 2010 that Church and Henson had split up.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
       <a href="#cite_note-41">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
