     <p>
      <b>
       Carlos Francisco Delfino
      </b>
      (born August 29, 1982) is an Argentine-Italian professional
      <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">
       basketball
      </a>
      player who last played for the
      <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">
       Milwaukee Bucks
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">
       National Basketball Association
      </a>
      (NBA). He holds dual citizenship in both Italy and Argentina.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
       <a href="#cite_note-1">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        1
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Delfino plays at the
      <a href="/wiki/Small_forward" title="Small forward">
       small forward
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Shooting_guard" title="Shooting guard">
       shooting guard
      </a>
      positions. He stands 1.98m (6ft 6in) tall and weighs 104kg (229lb). He is also noted for his defense and three point shooting skills.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
       <a href="#cite_note-2">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        2
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      It is known by the nicknames "Cabeza" (in english "Head"), "Lancha" ("Boat") and "Queson" ("Big Cheese").
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
       <a href="#cite_note-3">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        3
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>

     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">
       Professional career
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional career">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">
       Early years
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      The son of Carlos and Cristina Delfino, he began his professional career playing in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liga_Nacional_de_B%C3%A1squetbol" title="Liga Nacional de Bsquetbol">
       Argentine Basketball League
      </a>
      for
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olimpia_de_Venado_Tuerto" title="Olimpia de Venado Tuerto">
       Olimpia de Venado Tuerto
      </a>
      in the 199899 season, and he then transferred to
      <a href="/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_de_Santa_Fe" title="Unin de Santa Fe">
       Unin de Santa Fe
      </a>
      in 19992000.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Italian_clubs">
       Italian clubs
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Italian clubs">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Delfino moved to Italy in 2000 and played four seasons in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lega_A" title="Lega A">
       Italian A-1 League
      </a>
      , the first two with
      <a href="/wiki/Viola_Reggio_Calabria" title="Viola Reggio Calabria">
       Reggio Calabria
      </a>
      and the other two seasons with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fortitudo_Bologna" title="Fortitudo Bologna">
       Skipper Bologna
      </a>
      .
     </p>
     <p>
      In his first season in Italy, he played for
      <a href="/wiki/Viola_Reggio_Calabria" title="Viola Reggio Calabria">
       Reggio Calabria
      </a>
      . In just under 21 minutes per game he averaged 8.8 points, 2.5 rebounds and 1.9 steals. He scored a season-high 25 points in his second game against
      <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Libertas_Pesaro" title="Victoria Libertas Pesaro">
       Scavolini Pesaro
      </a>
      , making five of eight three-point attempts. He scored 15 points in just 20 minutes against
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roseto_Basket" title="Roseto Basket">
       Cordivari Roseto
      </a>
      and tallied 14 points apiece against
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fortitudo_Bologna" title="Fortitudo Bologna">
       Paf Bologna
      </a>
      , Muller Verona and
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virtus_Bologna" title="Virtus Bologna">
       Kinder Bologna
      </a>
      . He hit at least one three-pointer in 19 of 24 games.
     </p>
     <p>
      In his first season with
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fortitudo_Bologna" title="Fortitudo Bologna">
       Skipper Bologna
      </a>
      , he moved into the starting lineup in the third game of the season and averaged about 26 minutes. He scored 18 points, including shooting 3-of-5 from three-point range, versus
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roseto_Basket" title="Roseto Basket">
       Euro Roseto
      </a>
      and posted double-doubles against
      <a href="/wiki/Pallacanestro_Treviso" title="Pallacanestro Treviso">
       Benetton Treviso
      </a>
      (13 points, 13 rebounds),
      <a href="/wiki/Pallacanestro_Cant%C3%B9" title="Pallacanestro Cant">
       Oregon Scientific Cant
      </a>
      (15 points, 11 rebounds) and
      <a href="/wiki/Olimpia_Milano" title="Olimpia Milano">
       Pippo Milano
      </a>
      (14 points, 11 rebounds). He suffered torn ligaments in his ankle late in the season, but returned in late May.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Detroit_Pistons_.282004.E2.80.932007.29">
       Detroit Pistons (20042007)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Detroit Pistons (20042007)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      Beginning in 2004, Delfino signed to play for the
      <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Pistons" title="Detroit Pistons">
       Detroit Pistons
      </a>
      of the
      <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">
       National Basketball Association
      </a>
      , who made him the 25th pick in the first round of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2003_NBA_Draft" title="2003 NBA Draft">
       2003 NBA Draft
      </a>
      , making him the first Argentine player ever to be selected in the first round of the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_Draft" title="NBA Draft">
       NBA Draft
      </a>
      . In November 2004, he suffered a knee injury that kept him on the injured list for over three months. He had an operation in the
      <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
       U.S.
      </a>
      and then another in Argentina, where he recovered. However, Delfino did not immediately return to form after his rehabilitation, and was left off the Pistons' 2005
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_Playoffs" title="NBA Playoffs">
       playoff
      </a>
      roster. After he recovered from the knee injury, Delfino averaged 15.3 minutes, 3.9 points, 1.8 rebounds, and 1.3 assists per game in 30 games under coach
      <a href="/wiki/Larry_Brown_(basketball)" title="Larry Brown (basketball)">
       Larry Brown
      </a>
      . Many regarded Brown as having limited space for Delfino's offensive creativity. It was perceived that under the Pistons upcoming new coach
      <a href="/wiki/Flip_Saunders" title="Flip Saunders">
       Flip Saunders
      </a>
      , that Delfino would thrive.
     </p>
     <p>
      In his second season on the Pistons' active roster, Carlos averaged 10.7 minutes, 3.6 points, 1.7 rebounds, and 0.6 assists per game. Delfino played off the bench substituting for either
      <a href="/wiki/Tayshaun_Prince" title="Tayshaun Prince">
       Tayshaun Prince
      </a>
      or
      <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hamilton_(basketball)" title="Richard Hamilton (basketball)">
       Richard Hamilton
      </a>
      . He had three straight games where he scored in double digits before being sidelined for the next 4 with the flu. Delfino became an important change-of-pace player in Flip Saunders' offensive scheme.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Toronto_Raptors">
       Toronto Raptors
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Toronto Raptors">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On June 15, 2007, the Detroit Pistons traded Delfino to the
      <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">
       Toronto Raptors
      </a>
      for 2nd round draft picks in both the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_NBA_Draft" title="2009 NBA Draft">
       2009 NBA Draft
      </a>
      and the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_NBA_Draft" title="2011 NBA Draft">
       2011 NBA Draft
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
       <a href="#cite_note-4">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        4
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      The 200708 season was his most productive in the
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA" title="NBA">
       NBA
      </a>
      , as he averaged 9 points, 4.4 rebounds and 1.8 assists per game during the regular season.
     </p>
     <p>
      On June 16, 2009, the
      <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">
       Toronto Raptors
      </a>
      extended a qualifying offer to Delfino.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
       <a href="#cite_note-5">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        5
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Khimki">
       Khimki
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Khimki">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      In the summer of 2008, Delfino signed a 3-year contract with the
      <a href="/wiki/Russian_Basketball_Super_League" title="Russian Basketball Super League">
       Russian Super League
      </a>
      club
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khimki_Moscow_Region" title="Khimki Moscow Region">
       Khimki Moscow Region
      </a>
      . Delfino was one of the highest paid basketball players in Europe, earning about $10 million US dollars per season, plus a house, a car and a driver, and savings on taxes.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
       <a href="#cite_note-6">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        6
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      He averaged 13.0 points, 3.6 rebounds, 2.6 assists, and 1.5 steals per game in Europe's second level competition, the
      <a href="/wiki/Eurocup_Basketball" title="Eurocup Basketball">
       ULEB Eurocup
      </a>
      during the 200809 season.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
       <a href="#cite_note-7">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        7
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Milwaukee_Bucks_.282009.E2.80.932012.29">
       Milwaukee Bucks (20092012)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Milwaukee Bucks (20092012)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On August 18, 2009, the Raptors signed and traded Delfino to the
      <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">
       Milwaukee Bucks
      </a>
      along with
      <a href="/wiki/Roko_Uki%C4%87" title="Roko Uki">
       Roko Uki
      </a>
      in exchange for
      <a href="/wiki/Amir_Johnson" title="Amir Johnson">
       Amir Johnson
      </a>
      and
      <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Weems" title="Sonny Weems">
       Sonny Weems
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
       <a href="#cite_note-8">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        8
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      Delfino had a breakout season with the Bucks, as he posted career highs in points per game, rebounds per game, assists per game, steals per game and blocks per game, while playing 30 minutes per game. He also played a major role in the playoffs, shooting a career high .405 from behind the 3-point line.
     </p>
     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Houston_Rockets_.282012.E2.80.932013.29">
       Houston Rockets (20122013)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Houston Rockets (20122013)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On August 20, 2012, Delfino signed with the
      <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">
       Houston Rockets
      </a>
      .
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
       <a href="#cite_note-9">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        9
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      After being a starter for three years in Milwaukee, Delfino became the
      <a href="/wiki/Sixth_man" title="Sixth man">
       sixth man
      </a>
      for the Rockets. On June 30, 2013, Delfino was waived by the Rockets.

     <h3>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_the_Milwaukee_Bucks_.282013.E2.80.932014.29">
       Return to the Milwaukee Bucks (20132014)
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Return to the Milwaukee Bucks (20132014)">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h3>
     <p>
      On July 17, 2013, Delfino signed with the Milwaukee Bucks.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
       <a href="#cite_note-11">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        11
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <p>
      On August 26, 2014, Delfino was traded, along with
      <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_Raduljica" title="Miroslav Raduljica">
       Miroslav Raduljica
      </a>
      and a 2015 second round draft pick, to the
      <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">
       Los Angeles Clippers
      </a>
      in exchange for
      <a href="/wiki/Jared_Dudley" title="Jared Dudley">
       Jared Dudley
      </a>
      and a 2017 conditional first round draft pick.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
       <a href="#cite_note-12">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        12
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
      Three days later, he was waived by the Clippers.
      <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
       <a href="#cite_note-13">
        <span>
         [
        </span>
        13
        <span>
         ]
        </span>
       </a>
      </sup>
     </p>
     <h2>
      <span class="mw-headline" id="Argentine_national_team">
       Argentine national team
      </span>
      <span class="mw-editsection">
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        [
       </span>
       <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Delfino&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Argentine national team">
        edit
       </a>
       <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
        ]
       </span>
      </span>
     </h2>
     <p>
      Delfino was a member of Argentina's junior national team that won the bronze medal at the 2001
      <a href="/wiki/FIBA_Under-21_World_Championship" title="FIBA Under-21 World Championship">
       FIBA Under-21 World Championship
      </a>
      that was held in
      <a href="/wiki/Saitama,_Saitama" title="Saitama, Saitama">
       Saitama
      </a>
      ,
      <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">
       Japan
      </a>
      . Delfino was also a part of the senior
      <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argentine_national_basketball_team" title="Argentine national basketball team">
       Argentine national basketball team
      </a>
      that won the gold at the
      <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Basketball at the 2004 Summer Olympics  Men's tournament">
       2004 Olympics basketball Tournament
      </a>
      . He also played with Argentina's senior national team at the
      <a href="/wiki/2006_FIBA_World_Championship" title="2006 FIBA World Championship">
       2006 FIBA World Championship
      </a>
      and at the
      <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Basketball at the 2008 Summer Olympics  Men's tournament">
       2008 Olympics basketball Tournament
      </a>
      , where he helped Argentina to win the bronze medal.
     </p>
