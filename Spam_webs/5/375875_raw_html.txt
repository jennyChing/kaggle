            <h1 itemprop="headline name">
             Mr Money: 33,000 off a new car
            </h1>
            <h2 itemprop="alternativeHeadline description">
             There are some amazing deals, but be wary of 'unbeatable offers' that involve 
  paying to get a discount.
            </h2>
            <style media="print">

                 <div class="artImageExtras">

                   <span class="caption">
                    It's possible to get 33,000 off an Aston Martin Rapide, but you won't get one built to order with that kind of discount

              <div>
               <div class="bylineImg">
                <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/motoring/columnists/mike-rutherford/">
                 <img alt="Mike Rutherford" border="0" height="60" src="http://i.telegraph.co.uk/multimedia/archive/01473/Mike_Rutherford_1473823j.jpg" width="60"/>
                </a>
               </div>
               <p class="bylineBody">
                By
                <a href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/motoring/columnists/mike-rutherford/" rel="author" title="Mike Rutherford">
                 Mike Rutherford
                </a>
               </p>
              </div>
              <p class="publishedDate">
               5:13PM GMT 10 Feb 2012
              </p>
              <meta content="2012-02-10" itemprop="datePublished"/>
              <p class="comments">
               <img alt="Comments" src="/template/ver1-0/i/share/comments.gif"/>
               <a dsqid="9074646" href="http://www.telegraph.co.uk/motoring/columnists/mike-rutherford/9074646/Mr-Money-33000-off-a-new-car.html#disqus_thread">
                Comments
               </a>
              </p>
              <div class="cl">
              </div>
             </div>
             <div id="mainBodyArea" itemprop="articleBody">
              <div class="firstPar">
               <p>
                A discount of about 22 per cent on a new car is well above the average, but it's only about half the size of the biggest and best (in percentage terms, at least) reductions available on some Hyundais, for example.
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="secondPar">
               <p>
                However, when it comes to the Aston Martin Rapide, percentages don't really matter. Pounds do  all 33,000 of them. That is the sort of price cut that's currently achievable if a potential buyer shops around inside and, crucially, outside the official dealer network. There is now clear evidence that non-franchised online retailers and others might be able to resort to the sort of record-breaking levels of price-slashing that more upmarket Aston franchisees wouldn't want to be seen to be associated with.
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="thirdPar">
               <p>
                A Rapide V12 Touchtronic Auto has a recommended price of 152,000, but can now be had for about 119,000 which has to be the new benchmark transaction price (as opposed to the theoretical list price) for a car that's in stock, although it's worth pointing out that one ordered to your spec is likely to cost more than the recommended price. There's no getting away from the fact that this and other unused Aston Martins are currently available with five figure discounts.
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="fourthPar">
               <p>
                Back in the real, affordable world those 40 per cent-plus discounts mentioned above apply to cars such as the Hyundai i30 estate, listed at about 17,500 but available pre-registered and with delivery mileage only through at least one independent, internet-based retailer for just over 10,000. For unused Fords, think of discounts as high as 33 per cent.
               </p>
              </div>
              <div class="fifthPar">
               <p>
                <strong>
                 * In view of the fact that price cuts of 20-something per cent are almost obligatory on many new Fords, and that some can be purchased with up to one third off, I'm less than impressed with a brand new "offer" from a leading comparison website which reckons its customers can save up to 25 per cent on showroom examples  while at the same time promising that "cars are not available cheaper anywhere else". I find that difficult to believe. Also puzzling is the condition that states customers "purchase the discount for 99".
                </strong>
               </p>
              </div>

              <div class="body">
               <p>
                <strong>
                 For the time being, my advice to would-be buyers is to be extremely cautious or, better still, give this allegedly "unbeatable offer" a wide berth because I reckon it definitely can be beaten  while paying hard cash simply in order to qualify for a price reduction seems like madness.
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 Currently, plenty of potential new car buyers are automatically granted discounts without asking for them, never mind paying for them. Skoda, for example, is knocking 20-25 per cent off its already attractively-priced cars. Watch this space for more.
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                * A few days ago I heard on my car radio that the Highways Agency couldn't understand why so many motorists were out driving after being warned that the roads would be blanketed in snow and ice. Does the HA honestly think we drive around in circles for fun at tricky times such as this? Can't it understand that folk who would really rather not be often have to be on the road when conditions are poor because they have everything from hospital appointments to jobs to attend?
               </p>
               <p>
                Instead of complaining about this, perhaps the Highways Agency, in conjunction with local authorities and other agencies, might concentrate on doing a better job of clearing the roads and pavements. Who pays? We do  via the hundreds of billions of pounds we pay annually in motoring tax, council tax and various other forms of tax.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 * I know from numerous winter driving expeditions to Sweden, Finland and Norway that at this time of year many Scandinavians stay at home and watch movies or resort to DIY work. While I'd never suggest trying the latter, I recommend that you catch up on your favourite car, road-trip or motorsport film  for free.
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 You can do so by signing up for Blockbusters' free films for 14 days promotion, which allows consumers to grab an up-to-the minute freebie movie for up to 24 hours, before returning it the following day/night and grabbing another one, also for nothing.
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 But you may first have to ask the Highways Agency if it's OK to drive to and from your local Blockbusters store between now and the end of February when winter, thankfully, comes to an end.
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                * Last week I stated that although some might deviously pretend otherwise, the truth is that the overwhelming majority of motoring offences (parking infringements, driving slightly over the posted speed limits, failing to spot fading road signs/markings etc) that take place in Britain are not criminal acts. The people responsible for these misdemeanours are not criminals. And it follows that they have not earned themselves criminal records which could impact on their employment prospects, ability to travel overseas and more.
               </p>
               <p>
                But serious motoring-related crimes such as passing on speeding points to a third party are in an entirely different league because perpetrators who are caught face charges of perverting the course of justice. If convicted they can lose licence, livelihood and liberty while earning genuine criminal status.
               </p>
               <p>
                After a long-running speeding points scandal which has seen him accused of covering up his wrongdoings, Chris Huhne will be hauled through the courts next week. He has already lost his job as Energy Secretary as well as his seat at the Cabinet table, although he rightly remains innocent unless proven guilty.
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 * Germany has more vehicles in the World Car of the Year and the related World Performance Car finals than the rest of the vehicle-producing nations put together.
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 The Audi A6 and Q3, BMW 1- and 3-series, Porsche 911 and Volkswagen Up are shortlisted for the World Car of the Year along with the Vauxhall Ampera, Citron DS5, Ford Focus and Range Rover Evoque.
                </strong>
               </p>
               <p>
                <strong>
                 In the Performance Car finals, the Porsche 911, Audi RS3, BMW 1-series M and M5, Mercedes-Benz CLS 63 AMG and C 63 Coup AMG are cited. The other contenders are the Chevrolet Camaro ZL1, Ferrari FF, Lamborghini Aventador and McLaren MP4-12C.
                </strong>
               </p>

            var settings = {

            var init = function(currentArticleId, articleList, displayThumbnail) {

                settings.articleList = articleList;
                settings.currentArticleId = currentArticleId;
                settings.displayThumbnail = displayThumbnail;

                settings.articleListToSkip = unescape($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName)).split(',');

                if ($.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName) != null) {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    updateCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                } else {
                    chooseNextArticle(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    createCookieBasedOnArticleList(settings.currentArticleId, settings.articleList, settings.articleListToSkip);
                }


                if (settings.articleId != "") {

                    $(renderTemplate()).appendTo("body");

                    $("#nextArticleWrapper a, #readNextOpenInner a").click(function () {
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_headline', 'version_1']);

                        getNavPos($(this).attr("href"), "wayfarerReadNext");
                    });

                    bindUIActions();

                    var displayed = false;

                    setTimeout(function () {
                        if (!displayed) {
                            displayed == true;
                            readNextWidget.display();
                        }

                    }, 10000);

                    $(window).scroll(function () {
                        if (isScrolledIntoView("#tmg-related-links")) {
                            if (!displayed) {
                                displayed == true;
                                readNextWidget.display();
                            }
                        }

                        if(isScrolledIntoView("#shareBottom") || isScrolledIntoView("#newGigyaShareBottom")) {
                            if(settings.autoOpen) {

                        }

            var display = function () {
                $(settings.widgetContainer).fadeIn(2000, function () {
                    //animation complete
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_closed', 'version_1']);
                });
            };

            var bindUIActions = function () {
                $(settings.openButton).mouseenter(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'hover_closed', 'version_1']);
                    openArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.widgetContainer).mouseleave(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    closeArticleContainer();
                });
                $(settings.closeButton).click(function (event) {
                    event.preventDefault();
                    _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'click_close', 'version_1']);
                    updateCookie(settings.articleId, settings.articleListToSkip);
                    closeArticleContainer();
                    settings.autoOpen = false;
                });
            };

            var openArticleContainer = function () {
                if (!settings.panelOpen) {
                	$("#readNextArticleContainer").show();//show/hide because height is calculated incorrectly for elements with display:none
                    $("#readNextArticleContainer").hide();
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                        _gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'wayfarer_read_next', 'display_open', 'version_1']);
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                    settings.autoOpen = false; //set auto open to false once user has viewed this once
                }
                return false;
            };

            var closeArticleContainer = function () {
                if (settings.panelOpen) {
                    $(settings.articleContainer).toggle('slide', {direction: 'left'}, function () {
                        //animation complete
                    });
                    settings.panelOpen = !settings.panelOpen;
                }
                return false;
            };


            var isScrolledIntoView = function(elem) {
                if($(elem).length) { //check if element exists
                    var docViewTop = $(window).scrollTop();
                    var docViewBottom = docViewTop + $(window).height();

                    var elemTop = $(elem).offset().top;
                    var elemBottom = elemTop + $(elem).height();

                    return ((elemBottom &lt;= docViewBottom) &amp;&amp; (elemTop &gt;= docViewTop));
                } else {
                    return false;
                }
            };

            var unique = function(array) {
                return array.filter(function (el, index, arr) {
                    return index == arr.indexOf(el);
                });
            }

            var createCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        articleListToSkip[0] = currentArticleId;
                        $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookieBasedOnArticleList = function(currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if (article.id == currentArticleId) {
                        updateCookie(currentArticleId, articleListToSkip);
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            var updateCookie = function(articleIdToSkip, articleListToSkip) {
                articleListToSkip.push(articleIdToSkip);
                articleListToSkip = unique(articleListToSkip);
                $.cookie(settings.readNextCookieName, escape(articleListToSkip.join(',')), {path: '/', expires: 1, domain: 'telegraph.co.uk'});
            }

            var chooseNextArticle = function (currentArticleId, articleList, articleListToSkip) {
                articleList.some(function (article) {
                    if ((article.id != currentArticleId) &amp;&amp; ($.inArray(article.id, articleListToSkip) === -1)) {

                        //set article
                        settings.articleTitle = article.title;
                        settings.articleUrl = article.url;
                        settings.articleId = article.id;
						settings.articleImage = article.imgUrl;
                        return true;
                    }
                });
            }

            //public API
            return {
                settings: settings,
                init: init,
                display: display,
                openArticleContainer: openArticleContainer,
                closeArticleContainer: closeArticleContainer
            }
        })();
