                   <div class="post hentry" itemprop="blogPost" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/BlogPosting">
                    <meta content="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-N2ztRaBS5Lk/Uu_a8SHz3FI/AAAAAAAACf0/cWBCCJnbj5w/s1600/painted+console+mim.jpg" itemprop="image_url"/>

                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Make It Monday: Painted Console {And My First Ever Linkup Party!}
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5129427771485025533" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-N2ztRaBS5Lk/Uu_a8SHz3FI/AAAAAAAACf0/cWBCCJnbj5w/s1600/painted+console+mim.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                      <img alt="painted console, electrohome phonograph, makeover" border="0" height="426" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-N2ztRaBS5Lk/Uu_a8SHz3FI/AAAAAAAACf0/cWBCCJnbj5w/s1600/painted+console+mim.jpg" title="" width="640"/>
                     </a>
                     Sometime in 1919, Mr. J. Harding placed the final screw in a new
                     <a href="http://doorsclosedwaterloo.wordpress.com/electrohome/" target="_blank">
                      Electrohome
                     </a>
                     Power Operated Phonograph Machine, never thinking that one day a mom of the twenty-first century would
                     <i>
                      desecrate
                     </i>
                     his art - this piece of Canadian history - with something so offensive as paint.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-p8a5-4Hlrh8/Uu_XmfhReqI/AAAAAAAACfg/iMIdmKp668k/s1600/painted+console+10.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;">
                      <img alt="electrohome phonograph, makeover, j harding, kitchener, 1919" border="0" height="150" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-p8a5-4Hlrh8/Uu_XmfhReqI/AAAAAAAACfg/iMIdmKp668k/s1600/painted+console+10.jpg" title="" width="400"/>
                     </a>
                     The record player came to me through a friend of a friend and was delivered on the very same day I came home from
                     <a href="http://selfbindingretrospect.alannarusnak.com/2010/06/market-tuesday-ode-to-keady.html" target="_blank">
                      Keady Market
                     </a>
                     with a new
                     <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00000JCFW/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=B00000JCFW&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=selfbretro-20">
                      Billy Idol
                     </a>
                     <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=selfbretro-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=B00000JCFW" style="border: none !important; margin: 0px !important;" width="1"/>
                     record. (And if you don't like Billy Idol, we probably can't be friends anymore!)
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     At the end of
                     <a href="http://selfbindingretrospect.alannarusnak.com/2014/01/how-to-work-from-home-without-losing.html" target="_blank">
                      my last post
                     </a>
                     I had a little teaser about this
                     <i>
                      'Joy 
Project'
                     </i>
                     and one of the comments was: "Do you ever run out of things to 
paint?"
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Seriously?
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Heavens no!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      There is something magical about watching a piece breath new life - about bringing freshness - about putting a bit of myself into something that makes me fall in love with it all over again.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     To be perfectly honest, there was ABSOLUTELY NOTHING WRONG WITH THIS CONSOLE! The finish didn't bother me - I love vintage things. What did bother me is that the one place in the house it fits really well is right beside the piano and THEY WERE EXACTLY THE SAME COLOUR!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Boring!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Truth is, I would LOVE to paint the piano but it was my mother's and she would NEVER paint it. Next best thing? Paint the console!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     It was almost unfortunate that it was in such good shape because a little piece of me felt guilty as I crept up with the sanding block - but then I squashed that fear and rolled up my sleeves - I mean, it wasn't
                     <i>
                      that
                     </i>
                     much fear!
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     Do you have a piece of tired old furniture begging for a make-over? Here's how I resurrected mine:
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="font-size: large;">
                      1. Sand it.
                     </span>
                     I used my trusty sanding block (that seems to last forever) to muss up that shiny finish. I was quick - not careful - and finished just as my daughter walked into the room and asked, "What Are You Doing, Mommy?" like I was breaking some cardinal house rule. "You want a job?" I asked her.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-54qzEk7ekhA/Uu_a5dGPLzI/AAAAAAAACfs/PZgIlCQhV3Q/s1600/Painted+Console+1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="electrohome phonograph, makeover, sanding, " border="0" height="320" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-54qzEk7ekhA/Uu_a5dGPLzI/AAAAAAAACfs/PZgIlCQhV3Q/s1600/Painted+Console+1.jpg" title="" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       2. Wipe it down.
                      </span>
                      With the help of a pretty assistant, we wiped up the sanding dust with a damp cloth and then ran over the surface again with a dry rag.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-o-NnEJKqyl4/Uu_gObXXdrI/AAAAAAAACgE/W2niR1w6MbQ/s1600/Painted+Console+2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="electrohome phonograph, makeover, sanding, washing" border="0" height="450" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-o-NnEJKqyl4/Uu_gObXXdrI/AAAAAAAACgE/W2niR1w6MbQ/s1600/Painted+Console+2.jpg" title="" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       3. Prime &amp; Paint.
                      </span>
                      I have a five gallon pail of self-priming Behr Premium Plus Ultra Pure White just for such important moments. I call my first coat a primer and my second coat the real deal.
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-KFoHi80xWcs/Uu_iBMqKnHI/AAAAAAAACgQ/nzbcT1dKkZg/s1600/Painted+Console+3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="electrohome phonograph, makeover, painting, " border="0" height="450" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-KFoHi80xWcs/Uu_iBMqKnHI/AAAAAAAACgQ/nzbcT1dKkZg/s1600/Painted+Console+3.jpg" title="" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-U97R8I1yj3c/Uu_iGUkBM_I/AAAAAAAACgc/qeBgIQhMl7g/s1600/Painted+Console+4.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="electrohome phonograph, makeover, painting, " border="0" height="320" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-U97R8I1yj3c/Uu_iGUkBM_I/AAAAAAAACgc/qeBgIQhMl7g/s1600/Painted+Console+4.jpg" title="" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      I had some concerns about the speakers and toyed with the idea of leaving them natural. I googled instructions but found that other people were either not interested in keeping their consoles as functioning music player, or they had speakers with removable fabric which they simply had to change in order to freshen them up. Mine are of a thick weave - much like a woven basket - not removable unless I disassembled the entire piece. Seeing how ugly and dirty the natural finish looked against the white, I decided to paint directly on them - dry brushing - being careful not to go too thick over the actual speaker spaces so as not to seal the 'sound space'. I regretted it as soon as I started. It looked messy and rough and just plain terrible. "I don't fink you should do dat!" Noa said. BUT, I didn't give up. By the end of the second coat I had dispelled my regret and celebrated my total awesomeness! (Most humbly, of course.)
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      I was pleased with the white finish. It was fresh. It was clean.
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       It was
                       <i>
                        boring
                       </i>
                       .
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div class="" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
                      So I pulled out the leftover paint from the feature wall in Liam's room -
                      <a href="http://www.homedepot.com/p/BEHR-Premium-Plus-Ultra-8-oz-520D-7-Mosaic-Tile-Interior-Exterior-Paint-Sample-520D-7U/202181991" target="_blank">
                       Mosaic Blue,
                      </a>
                      the prettiest blue in the universe - and laid that out on the top, as well as the underside of the lid that accesses the record player.
                     </div>
