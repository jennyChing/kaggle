        <h1 class="title titlenolink">
         Simple Lavender Shortbread {Powernap}
        </h1>
        <div class="content">
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.thenaptimechef.com/2011/08/simple-lavender-shortbread-powernap/lavender-shortbread/" rel="attachment wp-att-4274">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Lavender Shortbread" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-4274" height="447" src="http://www.thenaptimechef.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Lavender-Shortbread-490x447.jpg" title="Lavender Shortbread" width="490"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thenaptimechef.com/2011/08/simple-lavender-shortbread-powernap/&amp;media=http://www.thenaptimechef.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/Lavender-Shortbread-490x447.jpg&amp;description=Simple Lavender Shortbread {Powernap}')">
            </span>
           </span>

          <em>
           This is the latest installment
           <a href="../2011/02/2011/03/2011/01/powernap-express-recipe-double-egg-bacon-sandwich-breakfast-heaven/">
            of my Powernap column.
           </a>
           The series where I share quick, easy food that can be made for all kinds of situations. Powernaps are short and sweet, and so are these recipes. These are the things I make in a jiffy when I
           <a href="../2011/02/2011/03/2011/02/spiced-popcorn-for-a-quick-snack-powernap-recipe/">
            need a quick snack
           </a>
           , am
           <a href="../2011/02/2011/03/2011/01/the-broiled-clementines-experiment-powernap-recipe/">
            in the mood for a recipe experiment
           </a>
           , or simply need to
           <a href="../2011/02/2011/03/2011/03/quick-brownies-with-vanilla-yogurt-powernap-recipe/">
            clean out the pantry
           </a>
           .
          </em>
         </p>
         <p>
          When I visited my friend Katie two weeks ago I brought these cookies. It was just the two of us with three kids for the weekend and I knew lavender shortbread would be the perfect foil for the endless bowls of goldfish crackers and cheerios we would see throughout the most of each day. Of course, I let my daughter try a bite of the shortbread after she spotted them on the counter, but she wasnt a huge fan of the floral flavor, preferring her bag of classic Tates cookies instead.
         </p>
         <p>
          <span id="more-4272">
          </span>
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_4275" style="width: 490px">
          <a href="http://www.thenaptimechef.com/2011/08/simple-lavender-shortbread-powernap/lavender-cookies-hip/" rel="attachment wp-att-4275">
           <span class="pibfi_pinterest">
            <img alt="Lavender Shortbread 2" class="size-large wp-image-4275" height="490" src="http://www.thenaptimechef.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/lavender-cookies-HIP-490x490.jpg" title="lavender cookies HIP" width="490"/>
            <span class="xc_pin" onclick="pin_this(event, 'http://pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://www.thenaptimechef.com/2011/08/simple-lavender-shortbread-powernap/&amp;media=http://www.thenaptimechef.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/07/lavender-cookies-HIP-490x490.jpg&amp;description=Simple Lavender Shortbread {Powernap}')">
            </span>
           </span>
          </a>
          <div class="wp-caption-text">
           <p>
            Katie's photo, taken with her iPhone...
           </p>
          </div>
         </div>
         <p>
          Since the trip to Katies house wasnt at all long I decided to make my shortbread dough during naptime and transport it, wrapped in plastic, to her house to bake fresh. I packed the ball of dough in a snack cooler full of ice packs so I had no worries about it going bad. In fact, it still needed to thaw before baking when I got to the house. Upon arrival I baked up the small batch, it only made on trays worth, and they had time to cool while we put the kids to sleep. Then we settled in with a glass of wine, grilled chicken and these cookies for dessert. The perfect adult finish to an easy, breezy summer day.
         </p>

           <div class="rec-left">
            <h3>
             Instructions
            </h3>
           </div>
           <div class="rec-right">
            <p>
             1. Preheat the oven to 325F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone liner and set aside.
            </p>
            <p>
             2. Cream the butter and sugar together in an electric mixer until light and fluffy. Add the egg and salt and mix again.
            </p>
            <p>
             3. Use a spatula to stir the lavender flowers into the batter. Next, fold in the self-rising flour until just incorporated and no more flour is visible.
            </p>
            <p>
             4. Place the dough onto the baking sheet in heaping tablespoons and bake for 15 minutes, or until lightly golden around the edges.
            </p>
            <p>
            </p>
