         <h1 class="article__title entry-title" itemtype="name">
          5 snippets from Camps Bays history
         </h1>
         <hr class="separator"/>
         <div class="entry__meta--header">
          <div class="grid">
           <div class="grid__item ">
            <span class="article__author-name">
             By
             <address class="vcard author">
              <a class="url fn" href="http://www.holidaybug.co.za/author/kezia/" itemprop="author" title="Posts by Kezia Swanepoel">
               Kezia Swanepoel
              </a>
             </address>

            </span>
            <time class="article__time" datetime="2015-02-24T14:29:47+00:00">

        <section class="article__content entry-content js-post-gallery">
         <p>
          Camps Bay has always been a sought-after spot for fun in the sun and on the beach but before it was the haven of models on holiday and those gasping for a cocktail, it was a heavily forested paradise.
         </p>
         <h2>
          Vast wilderness
         </h2>
         <p>
          In 1652, the Dutch arrived. However, the area was originally inhabited by Khoisan and there were lush forests surrounding now developed areas and over the Twelve Apostles. In the 1600s, wildlife in Camps Bay included lions, leopard and antelope.
         </p>
         <p>
          <img alt="" src="http://www.villascampsbay.co.uk/camps-bay-news/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/camps-bay-overview.jpg"/>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Die Baai von Kamptz
         </h2>
         <p>
          Johan Wernich owned the area and married Anna Koekemoer. However, he died in 1778 and Anna married Fredrick Ernst von Kamptz, a sailor. The event was seen as scandalous as many believed von Kamptz had seen the marriage as advantageous for him, not helped by the fact that the area was changed to Die Baai von Kamptz. It was then translated to the English name we use today.
         </p>
         <h2>
          A fertile hunting ground
         </h2>
         <p>
          Lord Charles Somerset used the area of Camps Bay as a hunting ground. In the 1800s, there was very little development, both residential and industrial. His residence was The Roundhouse, which still stands today and now operates as a restaurant.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 614px">
          <img alt="camps bay guesthouse" height="440" src="http://theroundhouserestaurant.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/10/History-1.jpg" width="604"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Then.
          </p>
         </div>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 620px">
          <img alt="Camps Bay accommodation" height="407" src="http://www.lecreuset.co.za/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/08/The-Roundhouse-Camps-Bay.jpg" width="610"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Now.
          </p>
         </div>
         <h2>
          Picnic paradise
         </h2>
         <p>
          The use of the area as a hunting ground and the variety of smooth boulders, sandy beaches and grassy lawns, saw Camps Bay becoming the preferred picnic and recreation spot in Cape Town in the Victorian era. More people were able to access it in 1901, when a tramline was built. In 1913, it was officially made a residential part of Cape Town as more people started to build homes there.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 679px">
          <img alt="camps bay guesthouse " height="470" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/cc/Camps_Bay_tram,_terminus_-_ca._1902.jpg" width="669"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The tramline.
          </p>
         </div>
         <h2>
          The Rotunda
         </h2>
         <p>
          Like the beautiful white beaches and swaying palms, the dome-shaped Rotunda has played an integral part of in Camps Bay social and cultural history for more than a century. The dome was built in 1904 and was used as a venue to host the whos who of Victorian society and their calendar of high class affairs. Today, it is still used for anything from cocktail parties and weddings to fashion shows and conferences and you dont have to be a lord or lady to be invited!
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 681px">
          <img alt="Camps Bay accommodation" height="503" src="http://www.campsbayschoolreunion.com/memorabilia2/800x600/dsc05675.jpg" width="671"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Rotunda in the background as people socialise on the beach.
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          To stay in this classy and beautiful area, book with
          <a href="http://www.atlanticgold.co.za/tanzanite">
           Atlantic Gold accommodation
          </a>
          and enjoy the views that have transfixed residents and visitors for ages.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" style="width: 734px">
          <img alt="Camps Bay guesthouse " height="503" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c0/Victoria_Road,_Camps_Bay_-_ca._1903.jpg" width="724"/>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The main road then.
          </p>
         </div>
