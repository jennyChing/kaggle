            <p>
             <br/>
             <a href="http://www.richsoil.com/permaculture/paul-wheaton/">
              Paul Wheaton
             </a>
             , Jocelyn Campbell, and
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/forums/posts/watch/80/2230" target="_blank">
              Helen Atthowe
             </a>
             respond to listener questions. Helen talks about
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/t/6608/organic-sustainable-practices/Soil-testing" target="_blank">
              soil testing
             </a>
             and base saturation. Helen talks about "bad" compost, and the positive benefits of adding compost to your soil. They talk about some soils potentially being too rich. They discuss
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/t/682/permaculture/build-inches-topsoil-year-video" target="_blank">
              topsoil
             </a>
             , and what is good or bad. Paul talks about organic matter for the soil, and the work of
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/t/10360/organic-sustainable-practices/Ruth-Stout-Gardening-Without-Work" target="_blank">
              Ruth Stout
             </a>
             . Helen shares what she has learned from
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/forums/posts/list/160/2214" target="_blank">
              Masanobu Fukuoka
             </a>
             . Helen shares about Wes Jackson of the Land Institute, who has done work with
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/forums/posts/watch/12/4609" target="_blank">
              perennial grains
             </a>
             , and his analysis of human
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/forums/posts/list/13/7523" target="_blank">
              population
             </a>
             numbers and what the world can handle. Paul thinks we can double the population and be fine. Helen talks about the value of
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/t/5657/woodland-care/Forest-Gardens" target="_blank">
              forest gardens
             </a>
             , and of thinking about a healthy population not just in terms of humans, but all sentient beings. Paul quotes
             <a href="http://www.richsoil.com/sepp-holzer/sepp-holzer-permaculture.jsp">
              Sepp Holzer
             </a>
             saying that all the world's food could be raised in Russia. They talk about whether
             <a href="http://www.permies.com/t/11467/permaculture/Polyculture" target="_blank">
              polyculture
             </a>
             yields more or less food.
             <br/>
             <a href="http://www.richsoil.com/permaculture/discussing-the-podcasts/">
              Discussing the podcast
             </a>
             .
