         The Cart Before the Horse: Bouqs
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2015-04-28T08:26:08+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          April 28, 2015
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">

        <p>
         <span style="font-size: x-small;">
          <i>
           This post is sponsored by The
           <a href="http://bouqs./" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
            Bouqs.
           </a>
          </i>
         </span>
         <img alt="Tracking Pixel" src="http://ec2-54-244-107-86.us-west-2.compute.amazonaws.com/tracker.php?cid=00DE0000000eHXcMAM&amp;pixel=1E000000VnvDYIAZ"/>
         It is a little known fact in our family that when the house is overwhelmingly messy, the best way for a good thorough house cleaning to come about is from a triggeran inspiring reason to clean. This can come in two forms: inviting people over or buying something small and pretty that makes me want to clean. If you give a mouse a cookie, he will ask for a glass of milk. If you buy a new dish towel, you will clean your kitchen so the dish towel has a nice background from which to shine. Im going to Target to buy a candle, I tell Brett. I thought you were going to clean the house, he says. Thats why I need the candle, I answer. Arent you putting the cart before the horse? he challenges. The horse is tired and uninspired. He needs to see the cart to make him move. Im going to fetch a cart. Ill be back Yes, I clean my house without buying something new every time, but I do love finding and creating tiny rewards that brighten our home and inspire me to make efforts to keep our space as lovely as it can be. These rewards can be as simple as reading a magazine with afternoon coffee from the carafe after I clean the kitchen and vacuum the rug or something a little more specialsplurging on good bubble bath and pouring it in pretty jars to sit next to the bathtub I just scoured. The greatest of these little home inspirations is flowers.
         <a href="http://s259.photobucket.com/user/kascryder/media/Sponsor%20Photos/bouqs%203_zps9i9umlmg.jpg.html" target="_blank">
          <img alt=" photo bouqs 3_zps9i9umlmg.jpg" border="0" src="http://i259.photobucket.com/albums/hh296/kascryder/Sponsor%20Photos/bouqs%203_zps9i9umlmg.jpg"/>
         </a>
         Wouldnt it be lovely to have an entire field of wildflowers behind your home? And every few days all you have to do is grab a big straw hat and a pair of old scissors and set out back to clip bundles for your home and your neighbors and your friends? That is after twirling in the field, of course.
         <a href="http://s259.photobucket.com/user/kascryder/media/Sponsor%20Photos/bouqs%204_zpsfu5g6s4p.jpg.html" target="_blank">
          <img alt=" photo bouqs 4_zpsfu5g6s4p.jpg" border="0" src="http://i259.photobucket.com/albums/hh296/kascryder/Sponsor%20Photos/bouqs%204_zpsfu5g6s4p.jpg"/>
         </a>
         Well, we dont have a field. We did try
         <a href="http://xs.gy/7uzi" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          The Bouqs
         </a>
         last week thougha new flower delivery service thats easy, fresh, and beautiful. Flowers are cut the day you order and shipped the next day right from the farmsno waiting in a warehouseso you receive them sooner and they last longer. I bet the flower clippers twirl in the fields before they cut them. Im not sure about that, but Im going to follow up and check. Bouqs, do your clippers twirl? (just say yes.) All flowers from
         <a href="http://xs.gy/7uzi" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          The Bouqs
         </a>
         are grown at sustainable, eco-friendly farms that follow strict responsible farming practices. No upsells, no hidden pricing (ahemno cheap candy or cheesy teddy bears), and always free shipping.
         <a href="http://xs.gy/7uzi" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          The Bouqs
         </a>
         has got your Mothers Day covered. Send your mama these beauties and make sure you let her know shes worth farm-fresh.
