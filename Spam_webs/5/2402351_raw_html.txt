        <div class="mailmunch-forms-before-post" style="display: none !important;">

        <p>
         <a href="http://i2.wp.com/www.harlemparentxpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/31-Days-Without-My-Daughter.png">
          <img alt="tragic ending" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-470" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i2.wp.com/www.harlemparentxpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/31-Days-Without-My-Daughter.png?resize=600%2C901"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          The last attorney I had really messed my case up and was all about the money instead of helping me to help my daughter. In 2012, I received another call from my ex-husband to state that she ran away again. Like any other times, I look up another attorney and to get this ball rolling one more time. I found an amazing attorney that, I felt will be able to help me get my daughter, but I ran out of money, and they can no longer help me with my case. I didnt stop there; I represent myself against the demons. My ex-mother-in-law was trying to adopt my daughter, and I was like hell! She has a mother; that cares about her and love her too much to let that happen.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          I finally got to see my daughter; she was broken and pregnant at the age of 13 years old. My ex-mother-in-law told the judge that I was uninterested in my daughter; I didnt call or write or made any attempts to see her. I told the judge that was a lie and here were my records, and still no results were good on my side. I also told the judge if he doesnt remove her from her home, she will be just like her 16-year-old daughter that ran away from home and got murdered.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          We went back and forth to court; until my daughter was 14 years old and like any other times; it wasnt in my favor.
         </span>
        </p>
        <div class="mailmunch-forms-in-post-middle" style="display: none !important;">
        </div>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          I will never forget
          <span style="color: #000080;">
           <strong>
            September 4, 2013;
           </strong>
          </span>
          this is the day my ex-mother-in-law called me to say that my daughter committed suicide on August 25, 2013. I was in the middle of running for City Council, that year, and I had my niece who helped me stay together. When I heard that I was in shock. I immediately went to my finances job and told her that I thought I heard my daughter is dead but wasnt sure. My finance called to ask if I heard correctly. My ex-mother-in-law said yes, and the funeral is on that Saturday.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          <span style="color: #ff0000;">
           September 7, 2013
          </span>
          was my daughters funeral, and she didnt look like my precious little girl that I knew. She looks like she had a hard life and wasnt at peace at all. She still looks unkempt and unhappy. It took everything in my might to sit through a funeral of nothing but lies.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          My ex-mother-in-law and ex-husband were trying to make it like we were one happy family; which is far from the truth, and I wasnt playing that game. I told everyone at the funeral how she alienated my daughter from me, and she had someone that cared. Her sisters stated to me that they knew she didnt care for my daughter. I asked them why didnt they speak up. I didnt get an answer.
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          So, I followed them to the burial site; which they didnt have the courtesy to see my daughter get wheeled into the ground. I stayed, and that was the longest 5 minutes of my life. As she was being wheeled down in her final resting place, I just wanted to be there as well. My biggest nightmare has become true and now the hope of reuniting with my little girl is not going to happen.
         </span>
        </p>
        <h2>
         <span style="font-family: 'trebuchet ms', geneva; font-size: 12pt;">
          Now, the fight to get my granddaughter begins and she will never know her mother but her tragic story.
         </span>
        </h2>
        <h1>
         <span style="color: #ff0000;">
          Please dont say your sorry for my lost, just forward the story to someone who might need to hear it.
         </span>
        </h1>
