        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Reviewer September Romance Recommendations
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2012-10-01T10:00:35+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          October 1, 2012
         </time>
         By
         <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
          <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://fictionvixen.com/author/fiction-vixen/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
           <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
            FV Sophia
           </span>
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         <img alt="FV Monthly Recs" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-21959" data-recalc-dims="1" src="http://i1.wp.com/www.fictionvixen.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/09/FV-Monthly-Recs.png?resize=293%2C269" title="FV Monthly Recs"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Its time for our reviewer romance recommendations for September. These are books we read in September but not necessarily published in September.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Jens Recommendations
        </h2>
        <p>
         September was my best reading month in a long time. I wish every month could be September now. I had two books that will go into my Top Ten for 2012.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On Dublin Street by Samantha Young
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=On Dublin Street Samantha Young&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/On Dublin Street Samantha Young?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=On Dublin Street Samantha Young" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=On Dublin Street Samantha Young" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         I loved this book. It will be in my top ten for 2012. I loved the emotional depth and the hot sexiness. I think this book had it all. I just wish Ms. Young had a backlog of books for me to dive in to. See Amys review
         <a href="http://www.fictionvixen.com/taxi-take-me-to-samantha-young-on-dublin-street/" target="_blank" title="Taxi! Take me to Samantha Young, On Dublin Street.">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          My Liege of Dark Haven by Cherise Sinclair
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=My Liege of Dark Haven Cherise Sinclair&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/My Liege of Dark Haven Cherise Sinclair?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=My Liege of Dark Haven Cherise Sinclair" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=My Liege of Dark Haven Cherise Sinclair" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         Ms. Sinclair has done it again. Each books she writes is amazing and her heroes are some of the yummiest around. Xavier was calm and controlled. He made me shiver! See my review
         <a href="http://www.fictionvixen.com/review-my-liege-of-dark-haven-by-cherise-sinclair/" target="_blank" title="Review: My Liege of Dark Haven by Cherise Sinclair">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Celebrity in Death by J.D. Robb
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=Celebrity in Death J.D. Robb&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/Celebrity in Death J.D. Robb?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=Celebrity in Death J.D. Robb" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=Celebrity in Death J.D. Robb" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         Book 34 and it was a good one. New York to Dallas was so emotional and serious that Celebrity in Death was a welcome change. The gang was all back together again and Peabody/Eve/Nadine were hysterical. This book had some of the best one-liners in the series.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Creed by Kristen Ashley
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=Creed Kristen Ashley&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/Creed Kristen Ashley?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=Creed Kristen Ashley" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=Creed Kristen Ashley" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         Creed was sovery different from anything I have read by KA. Sylvie was harder and Creed was softer. Such a role reversal. See my review
         <a href="http://www.fictionvixen.com/review-creed-by-kristen-ashley/" target="_blank" title="Review: Creed by Kristen Ashley">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hostile Takeover by Joey W. Hill
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=Hostile Takeover Joey W. Hill&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/Hostile Takeover Joey W. Hill?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=Hostile Takeover Joey W. Hill" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=Hostile Takeover Joey W. Hill" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         This book was raunchy! It is the final book in the Knights of the Boardroom series by Joey W. Hill. Ben was always the most hardcore of all the guys and this book really played up to that. I love Ms. Hills writing. She makes no apologies for anything and I am always sucked in to her stories.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Captive in the Dark by C.J. Roberts
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=Captive in the Dark C.J. Roberts&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/Captive in the Dark C.J. Roberts?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=Captive in the Dark C.J. Roberts" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=Captive in the Dark C.J. Roberts" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         What to say about this book? It brought out every emotion you could think of. I ached for Livvie and Caleb, both so tortured in different ways. I know this is a dark subject and will put a lot of people of but I still highly recommend this book. It was so great and will be another top 10 for 2012. See my review
         <a href="http://www.fictionvixen.com/review-captive-in-the-dark-c-j-roberts/" target="_blank" title="Review: Captive in the Dark C.J. Roberts">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          If I Were You by Lisa Renee Jones
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=If I Were You  Lisa Renee Jones&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/If I Were You  Lisa Renee Jones?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=If I Were You  Lisa Renee Jones" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

         <br/>
         <strong>
         </strong>
         This is another new author I tried because the cover sucked me in as well as the blurb of a handsome British hero. Yes, it has a Bared/50 vibe but for me, the characters were on a completely different level that those of similar books. Ethan and Brynne are flawed but the author tackles their emotion and imperfections on a deeper level. AND, the couple have scorching chemistry.
         <em>
          Naked
         </em>
         ended with a cliffhanger and it is book one of a trilogy.
         <em>
          All In
         </em>
         is set to release in October which is not soon enough for me. Im anxious to see where Ms. Miller takes this couple. Check out my review
         <a href="http://www.fictionvixen.com/review-naked-raine-miller/" title="Review: Naked by Raine Miller">
          here.
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The Pirate Next Door by Jennifer Ashley by Jennifer Ashley
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=The Pirate Next Door by Jennifer Ashley Jennifer Ashley&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/The Pirate Next Door by Jennifer Ashley Jennifer Ashley?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=The Pirate Next Door by Jennifer Ashley Jennifer Ashley" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=The Pirate Next Door by Jennifer Ashley Jennifer Ashley" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         Since we celebrated
         <strong>
          Talk Like a Pirate Day
         </strong>
         this month I had to add a pirate romance to my reading list and scored big with
         <em>
          The Pirate Next Door
         </em>
         . This is book one in the Regency Pirate trilogy by Ms. Ashley and although this series is a re-release, it still delivers all the magic and sensuality I have come to love in her books. Grayson and Alexandra are such a fun and enchanting couple and the story had both light and dark moments which provided such an enjoyable read. Aye matey, this is one not to be missed. 
        </p>
        <h2>
         Catherine s Recommendations
        </h2>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Leave Me Breathless by Cherrie Lynn
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=Leave Me Breathless  Cherrie Lynn&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/Leave Me Breathless  Cherrie Lynn?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          BN
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://ebookstore.sony.com/search?keyword=Leave Me Breathless  Cherrie Lynn" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          S
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=Leave Me Breathless  Cherrie Lynn" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         I was thrilled with this book. I picked this on a whim and was pleasantly surprised to find that even though this isnt my usual type of book, I fell in love with it anyway. I cant wait to try more by this author.
         <em>
          *Review to come*
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A Week To Be Wicked by Tessa Dare
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=A Week To Be Wicked  Tessa Dare&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/A Week To Be Wicked  Tessa Dare?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

         <br/>
         <a href="http://www.fictionvixen.com/review-a-week-to-be-wicked-by-tessa-dare/" title="Review: A Week To Be Wicked by Tessa Dare">
          This was a reread
         </a>
         for me and I loved it just as much as I did the first time. The hero and heroine were perfect for each other, no matter how unlikely it looked on the surface. I closed this book with a grin and a warm feeling. Such a lovely romance.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On Dublin Street by Samantha Young
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=On Dublin Street Samantha Young&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

          K
         </a>

         I was one of the many to fall prey to the hype about this book. Luckily for me I loved it.  It was sexy, readable, and I loved the slow build of the romance. Amy wrote a great review of it
         <a href="http://www.fictionvixen.com/taxi-take-me-to-samantha-young-on-dublin-street/" title="Taxi! Take me to Samantha Young, On Dublin Street.">
          here
         </a>
         .
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Ride With Me by Ruthie Knox
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=Ride With Me Ruthie Knox&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/Ride With Me Ruthie Knox?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">

         <a href="http://www.kobobooks.com/search/search.html?q=Ride With Me Ruthie Knox" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          K
         </a>
         ]
         <br/>
         I finally took the plunge and read this book. After finishing it, all I could think was that I should have taken Amy and Samanthas advice and read it long ago. I had a blast watching the two leads butt heads and snipe on their journey across country.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Beauty and the Bounty Hunter by Lori Austin
         </strong>
         ** [
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?keywords=Beauty and the Bounty Hunter  Lori Austin&amp;index=books&amp;linkCode=qs&amp;tag=losiitaweiglo-20" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
          AMZ
         </a>
         
         <a href="http://www.barnesandnoble.com/s/Beauty and the Bounty Hunter  Lori Austin?store=ALLPRODUCTS" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">
