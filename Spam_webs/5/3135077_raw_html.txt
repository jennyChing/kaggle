        7 Ways to Save Energy in Your College Apartment
       </h1>

         <article class="post-5096 post type-post status-publish format-standard has-post-thumbnail hentry category-student-life tag-home tag-saving tag-students single-post list-post">
          <div class="entry-main">
           <div class="entry-header">
           </div>
          </div>
          <div class="entry-content">
           <p>
            Lets face it, sometimes the hardest part of college is your living situation. Whether youre
            <a href="http://onthehub.com/blog/should-i-live-in-residence-apartment-or-home" target="_blank">
             living in residency or off-campus
            </a>
            , you can be really lucky with finding new roommates or find yourself struggling to see eye-to-eye. If youre stuck with an energy-hogging roommate that drives up your electricity bills but doesnt want to pay for his or her fair share, its time to take charge and think of
            <a href="http://onthehub.com/blog/how-to-save-money-in-college" target="_blank">
             creative ways to reduce those costs
            </a>
            . After all, college is already expensive and nobody needs to be throwing away senseless money every month.
           </p>
           <p>
            Implement these seven strategies for saving energy, and both your roommate and your wallet will thank you for it.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Unplug Unused Electronics and Appliances
           </h2>
           <p>
            Did you know that when you keep an appliance plugged in, its actually using up electricity? For example, even when youre not using a microwave, keeping it plugged in keeps the clock running. That requires energy. As a result, even when youre not using an appliance, keeping it plugged in will still add to your monthly bill. To reduce your energy consumption,
            <a href="http://www.doityourself.com/stry/how-to-save-electricity-in-an-apartment#b">
             make it a habit to unplug
            </a>
            an appliance after you use it. If you use a power strip, simply turn it off.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Pick the Right Bulbs
           </h2>
           <p>
            We all know that turning the lights off when you leave a room is good practice, but what about the cost of lighting overall? Reduce your monthly energy bills by picking
            <a href="http://www3.dps.ny.gov/W/PSCWeb.nsf/All/0D31447BF74DA6F485257687006F3958?OpenDocument">
             energy-efficient light bulbs
            </a>
            . Use bulbs that have lower wattage, dimming lights, or lamps with various lighting levels so that you can appropriately adjust. Fluorescent bulbs are much more energy-efficient than incandescent bulbs. Not only do they last longer than the latter for the same amount of light, but they last longer too.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Insulate Your Apartment
           </h2>
           <p>
            Energy costs are quick to rise during the winter and its no wonder why. Our apartments are often not properly insulated, so any heat generated through home energy appliances or central heating can quickly escape . There are many
            <a href="http://energy.gov/energysaver/articles/types-insulation">
             types of insulation
            </a>
            that can be added in your home, and its best to first assess your needs before getting started on a project. One easy type of insulation you can do yourself is to cover windows with plastic to prevent cold air from coming in through the window cracks.
           </p>
           <h2>
            <span style="line-height: 1.5em;">
             Choose Air Condition Alternatives
            </span>
           </h2>
           <p>
            Everyone hates to admit it, but air conditioning is expensive. This is one of the major costs in your electricity bills every year. Enact small steps first: turn off the air conditioner when youre not in the room. Close your curtains or blinds to reduce the heat entering your home. For that extra, money-saving step, use a fan instead of air conditioning. If you must absolutely keep the air conditioner on, you should monitor a temperature that is in your comfortable without being too low. This will keep your apartment cool without keeping your pockets empty.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Load Your Dishwasher Efficiently
           </h2>
           <p>
            Pre-washing your dishes helps remove large chunks of food residue and keeps it out of the dishwasher. Use the design of your
            <a href="http://lifehacker.com/5953039/how-to-reduce-your-energy-bill-with-no-cost-or-sacrifice">
             dishwashers rack to its fullest advantage
            </a>
            to maximize efficiency. Place your plates on the bottom dish rack and have them facing inward toward the center of it. Placing your silverware sharp side up will concentrate the cleaning on the dirtier parts of the used silverware, rather than on the handle. Bowls and cups should go on the top row, facing down for the cleanest rinse. Large, flat objects should be placed far away from the dishwasher door to make sure that the detergent door will be able to fully open.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Find an Alternative Payment Plan
           </h2>
           <p>
            Most people do not know this, but some companies actually allow you to choose how you want to pay for your bill. The funny thing about it is although you may receive the same amount of energy you could end up paying more or less for it. Talk with your electric company and see what type of
            <a href="http://www.electric.com/energy-prices-and-products.html">
             payment plan works best for your apartment
            </a>
            . Whether you want a green energy plan or a fixed rate, there is likely to be an option that is tailored for you.
           </p>
           <h2>
            Cook the Right Way
           </h2>
           <p>
            To save energy while youre cooking, make sure the size of your pan isnt smaller than the burner under it. A larger burner will use up more energy to heat. If your pan is smaller, there will be a lot of wasted heat energy. Inaddition, reduce cooking time by thawing frozen food in the refrigerator overnight. These simple but easy-to-implement tips will help you reduce energy usage in no time.
           </p>
           <p>
            If you have an uncaring or unrelenting roommate, take charge in reducing your energy usage in the apartment. Change your energy consumption habits by implementing these seven easy steps to save energy, and it will be almost second nature in no time.
           </p>
