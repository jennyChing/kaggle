                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Lemon Chicken and Rice Summer Casserole
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-7046859365634579192" itemprop="articleBody">
                     <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wSmyBR1iB1I/U77NK5Q_2DI/AAAAAAAAgJY/DBO0quDEo50/s1600/Lemon+Chicken+and+Rice+CasseroleP.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img alt="Lemon Chicken and Rice Casserole ~ No-Fuss, one dish Chicken and Rice casserole with vegetables #ChickenCasserole #Casserole #OneDishMeal #NoFuss" border="0" height="640" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wSmyBR1iB1I/U77NK5Q_2DI/AAAAAAAAgJY/DBO0quDEo50/s1600/Lemon+Chicken+and+Rice+CasseroleP.jpg" title="Lemon Chicken and Rice Casserole ~ No-Fuss, one dish Chicken and Rice casserole with vegetables #ChickenCasserole #Casserole #OneDishMeal #NoFuss" width="480"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="color: #444444;">
                      Casseroles are most definitely one of the most convenient meals to prepare and cook or bake. I make this lemon Chicken and Rice Summer casserole at least once every two weeks.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <span style="color: #444444;">
                      You can skip the marinating part if you are in a hurry, but I do try to always marinate the Chicken, just to get that extra bit of zestiness.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="color: #444444;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="color: #444444;">
                      Other than the marinade bit, it is dump and bake and ready to be served with maybe one side salad. Also feel free to experiment with the vegetables, I use what I have available at the time, but my favorite combination is definitely the Carrots and Green Beans - not only because their flavors go so well together, but it makes the dish very colorful and appetizing as well.
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <span style="color: #444444;">
                      <br/>
                     </span>
                     <span style="color: #444444;">
                      Last thing - do not forget the Lemon wedges on the side, it makes the dish outstanding and ever so fresh in taste {I'd rather leave it out for little ones however!}
                     </span>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Recipe">
                     </div>
                     <span style="color: blue;">
                      <u>
                       <b>
                        <span style="font-size: large;">
                         LEMON CHICKEN AND RICE CASSEROLE
                        </span>
                       </b>
                      </u>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <span style="color: #444444;">
                       4 - 6 Servings
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       <u>
                        INGREDIENTS:
                       </u>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        6 - 8 Chicken pieces
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1 t Salt
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1/2 t Black Pepper
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        2 TB Olive Oil
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        Juice and Zest of 1 Lemon
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        2 TB Parsley, chopped, divided
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        1 1/2 cups Rice, long grain
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        3 Carrots, peeled and cut into rings
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        3 cups Chicken Stock
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        200g Green Beans
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       <span itemprop="ingredients">
                        Lemon Wedges, to serve
                       </span>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       <u>
                        DIRECTIONS:
                       </u>
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       1.) Pre-heat the oven to 200 deg C (400 deg F) - spray a large oven dish with cooking oil.
                       <br/>
                       2.) Combine the Salt, Pepper, Olive Oil, Lemon Juice and 1 TB Parsley in a small bowl.
                       <br/>
                       3.) Place the Chicken in a marinade container with a lid - pour the Lemon mixture over and marinate for 30 - 45 minutes, turning twice.
                       <br/>
                       4.) Sprinkle the Rice on the bottom of the oven dish - arrange the Chicken on top - add the Carrots.
                       <br/>
                       5.) Pour over the Chicken Stock and sprinkle the remaining Parsley and the Lemon Zest over - cover with a lid or aluminum foil - bake 30 minutes.
                       <br/>
                       6.) Remove from the oven - add the Green Beans, cover and return to the oven for another 30 minutes or until fully cooked {remove the cover for the last 10 minutes of baking}
                       <br/>
                       <br/>
                       Serve with the Lemon wedges on the side.
                      </span>
                      <br/>
