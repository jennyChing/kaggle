           <h2 class="entry-title">
            <a href="blog/2014/07/29/google-scandal-approving-dangerous-and-illegal-content/">
             Google Scandal: Approving Dangerous And Illegal Content
            </a>
           </h2>
           <em class="date published">
            Jul 29, 2014
           </em>
          </header>
          <div class="image-block">
           <img alt="Google Scandal: Approving Dangerous And Illegal Content image" src="media/google_sausa-blog.jpg"/>
          </div>
          <p>
           Most, if not all, of the population knows about Google, but recently the company has gained attention for negative reasons. Google has been accused of not properly going through illicit drug advertising that appears on their search engine. Mississippi is one state in particular that is going after the several billion dollar company. The state claims Google has not been careful about what they publicize for ads.
          </p>
          <h2>
           Action Against Google
          </h2>
          <p>
           Believe it or not, Google has a history of abusing the internet that dates back to a few years ago when made official in 2011  though started in 2003 -- where they owed half of a billion dollars toward a settlement over their search engines relation to unlawful sales of prescription drugs coming from Canada. Although Google claims it makes safety for viewers a top priority, the company has found itself in the hot seat for allegations of allowing illegal videos and public endorsements to remain on their site.
          </p>
          <p>
           This past December, multiple advocates for the law notified the search engine corporation that legal action may be taken pertaining to their dangerous and illegal content. Google was allegedly promoting potent drugs without the needed prescription and other illicit activity. Throughout the following months in the beginning of this year, discussions were held between representatives of both the corporation and accusing authorities.
          </p>
          <p>
           Pam Bondi, an attorney in Florida, stated that Google was providing a simple method of locating illegal drugs and displaying information for prostitution. Officials of the company spoke of nothing in relation to his comments. Bondi went on to say how the corporation would directly steer users toward illegal online content. With accusations rising and other states like New Mexico having attorneys consider partaking in additional action, a Google representative was quick to defend how they take drastic measures to prevent display of
           <a href="http://www.businessweek.com/news/2014-07-24/google-targeted-in-state-crackdown-on-illicit-drug-ads">
            corrupt advertisements
           </a>
           and even enforce policies to combat rogue online pharmacies and counterfeit drugs. The fact that the company took down over 350 million corrupt ploys in 2013 proves the womans defense to be true. To prove wrong about accusations for advertising child endangerment, the company was said to have given millions of dollars to specific associations that benefit childrens safety and take measures to prevent child exploitation.
          </p>
          <h3>
           Pressing Issue
          </h3>
          <p>
           Concerns are rising as seven states officially now have attorneys carefully looking at Google and its safety practices. Colorado attorney David Blake claimed that the issue remains active. The legality of online advertising ploys that promote illicit drug use and trade is a serious concern, especially in light of the drug rise in the country throughout the previous years. But when illegal activity is continuously increasing, how can officials of companies like Google be expected to keep up with the online aspect of unlawful actions? The corporation has already tightened up their policies and claims there are workers that put in diligent effort to taking down posted videos that display illegal activities. Its not as if Google is the online widely-known online leader that cant seem to keep up with illicit content. Youtube was just put in the spotlight this June for having videos that explain how to retrieve credit card account numbers. The Digital Citizens Alliance, which focuses on enforcing polices for online safety, would not tolerate such allowance. After hearing such, Youtube officials made a statement claiming they take as much as millions of videos down on their site if they contain anything against the law. It remains unknown what lies ahead in the future as far as Internet safety and its drug-related content.
          </p>
