             <p>
              VANCOUVER  You cant expect only congratulations.
             </p>
             <p>
              Not with professional athletes.
             </p>
             <p>
              Even being chosen for the NHL All-Star Game festivities for the first time comes with a price in the dressing room.
             </p>
             <p>
              A few chirps going on here and there, Calgary Flames captain Mark Giordano reported Saturday after he was named to the event later this month in Columbus. We have a big game so its pretty serious. Im sure Ill hear a bit throughout the week.
             </p>
             <p>
              Makes you wonder what kind of chiding one receives when theyre selected to be among the best in the league.
             </p>
             <p>
              Ask Deryk Engelland, hes the one throwing them out, Giordano said with a chuckle.
             </p>
             <p>
              Even still, Giordano is excited about the latest honour in his career, which still seems to be rocketing.
             </p>
             <p>
              And he wont be alone during the event which runs Jan. 23-25 in Ohio.
             </p>
             <p>
              Johnny Gaudreau is among the half-dozen rookies chosen to take part in the skills competition.
             </p>
             <p>
              Hes played so well, Giordano said of the clubs standout freshman. For most of the last couple of months, hes been piling up the points. Its gonna be fun for the fans because hes a really skilled guy. A lot of people will get a chance to see his skills.
             </p>
             <p>
              A lot of the guys are gonna be there Ive been watching for the last five, 10 years now, said Gaudreau, the 2011 fourth-round draft choice whos started his career with 31 points in his first 41 NHL games. Its really exciting for me to be around some of the guys Ive been watching.
             </p>
             <p>
              Plenty will get to know more about Giordano, whos one of the NHLs great stories. He set the wheels in motion to go to university when his junior days were done in the summer of 04, but was invited to Calgarys summer prospects camp. He soon signed a take-it-or-leave-it, bare-bones entry-level contract and worked his way to being a Norris Trophy candidate this season, having collected 10 goals and 36 points in 42 games going into Saturdays clash with the Vancouver Canucks.
             </p>
             <p>
              Hes even opened the eyes of people who thought they had him figured out.
             </p>
             <p>
              I knew he was a pretty good player, but I didnt know how much he means to this team in terms of the leadership and in terms of pulling and dragging the team along, said first-year Flames GM Brad Treliving.
             </p>
             <p>
              We say to young players, Watch him. Just watch him. A leader isnt just the guy who breaks a stick in the locker-room or yells and screams all the time. Just watch how he prepares. Hes in early, hes in the gym, he looks after himself, hes the hardest working guy on the ice, stays late. Thats not a bad cookbook.
             </p>
             <p>
              Giordano has been a model of consistency. He was held off the scoresheet in only 12 games this season heading into the clash with the Canucks, no small feat considering how he faces the top players every game.
             </p>
             <p>
              You try to be consistent and try to play the same way as much as I can, Giordano said. Numbers are funny. Sometimes they come in bunches and other times they dont, but Im trying to take a lot of pride in playing tough minutes and playing them well.
             </p>
             <p>
              The only disappointment is his good buddy TJ Brodie wasnt also selected.
             </p>
             <p>
              Playing with him all year, I think hes a guy who could have been there, for sure, Giordano said. Theres a lot of different players around the league and tough decisions. In our minds, he deserves to be there, but sometimes thats just the way it goes.
             </p>
             <p>
              As much as hes looking forward to the event, Giordano has a more pressing goal.
             </p>
             <p>
              The teams struggles over the last month has them outside a playoff spot, something he wants to change by the all-star break.
             </p>
             <p>
              We want to get into one of those wildcard spots, Giordano said. Its gonna be tough and we play a lot of good teams on the road here, but we have to find a way.
             </p>
             <p>
              You dont want to fall too far behind at that break.
             </p>

              <p>
               VANCOUVER  To the surprise of nobody, Calgary Flames captain Mark Giordano has been selected for the NHL All-Star Game festivities later this month.
              </p>
              <p>
               It's the first time the 31-year-old defenceman has been chosen for the event, which will take place Jan. 24-25 in Columbus.
              </p>
              <p>
               As well, Johnny Gaudreau is among the half-dozen rookies chosen to take part in the skills competition.
              </p>
              <p>
               Giordano, who's having the best season of his career and considered a Norris Trophy candidate as the league's top blueliner, is on pace to shatter his previous career highs in all offensive categories. Through 42 games, he's collected 10 goals and 36 points, and faces the top opposition every game. He's also a plus-16.
              </p>
