        <hgroup>
         <h1 class="title">
          Graffiti and Street Artists Take Over Former Police Precinct
         </h1>
         <br/>
         <h4 class="meta">
          By
          <a href="http://animalnewyork.com/author/aymann/" rel="author" title="Posts by Aymann Ismail">
           Aymann Ismail
          </a>
          |
          <time>
           August 15, 2014 - 12:51PM
          </time>
         </h4>
        </hgroup>
       </header>
       <div class="post-content">
        <p>
         Yesterday, ANIMAL stopped by to preview a massive, building-wide group showat the former 21st Precinct, a few blocks from Gramercy Park. Curated by
         <a href="http://outlaw-arts.wix.com/outlawarts">
          Outlaw Arts
         </a>
         , murals, tags, wheatpaste, stickers and installations filled all four floors of the building, including the stairwells and bathrooms, showcasing some incredible work. Standouts included work from ANIMAL favorites such asGIZ, GHOST, FAUST, DUEL, CASH4, PIXOTE, ELLE (see her video installation below), Coby Kennedy, the YOKand SHERYO, and NET, seen in the photo above administering a fiery touch to his contribution.
        </p>
        <p>
         The 1863 townhouselocated at 327 East 22nd Street (between 1st and 2nd Avenues) functioned as apolice precinct until 1914, and served as a halfway house for LGBTQ youth until
         <a href="http://www.greenchimneys.org/" target="_blank">
          last year
         </a>
         . Suzuki Capital, the developers who will be turning the location into
         <a href="http://observer.com/2014/04/suzuki-to-turn-old-21st-precinct-station-house-into-condos/" target="_blank">
          condos
         </a>
         , provided the space.
        </p>
        <p>
         Though thepop-up wasnt
         <a href="http://animalnewyork.com/2014/theres-secret-street-art-show-abandoned-building-tonight/" target="_blank">
          illegal
         </a>
         like the Surplus Candy showin an
         <a href="http://www.metro.us/newyork/news/local/2014/01/13/abandoned-east-village-building-turned-into-secret-art-show-for-one-night/" target="_blank">
          abandoned
         </a>
         space in the East Village, itfeatured some knock-out work. See it in the gallery above and check it out in person this weekend and the next, Saturday and Sunday, from 1PM-6PM.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="338" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/o9GcQh-R2F0" width="600">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>

          (Video/Photos: Aymann Ismail/ANIMALNewYork)
         </em>
        </p>
