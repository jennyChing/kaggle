        <p>

         <br/>
         Paula Biggs here writing from the party over at
         <a href="http://frogprincepaperie.com" target="_blank">
          Frog Prince Paperie.
         </a>
         I always love a good celebration, with cupcakes making parties all that more fun! There are so many things to celebrate in MayMothers Day and Teacher appreciation days to name a few. If youve got kids, having a few kids-can-make-them-too gifts up your sleeve is pretty handy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe.jpg">
          <img alt="Lush has nothing on these beautiful bath bombs. These fizzy cupcake bath bombs look good enough to eat!" class="alignnone wp-image-20474 size-full pib-hover-img" height="885" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         It goes without saying that little hands should be able to get involved in their gifts for mom and teachers. Making something to gift that could be a source of relaxation  like a fizzy cupcake bath bomb  is the perfect thing for kids to craft.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-8.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-8" class=" size-full wp-image-20466 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="784" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-8.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-9.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-9" class=" size-full wp-image-20467 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="900" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-9.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In making this post, Im going to admit to you I actually had a few Pinterest fails trying to find a recipe that worked. (Gasp!) And I consider myself pretty accomplished at crafting and in the kitchen. I was inspired by
         <a href="http://www.makeandtakes.com/fizzy-bath-bomb-cupcakes">
          this pin for Fizzy Bath Bomb Cupcakes
         </a>
         on MakeandTakes.com. Lovely little cupcake bath bombs there, no? Sweet and perfect for gift-giving I thought.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-1.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-1" class=" size-full wp-image-20460 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="451" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-1.jpg" width="634"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         For me, the execution didnt go so well. I think my point of failure was with the water spray. Was also a tad humid that day, so my bath fizzies completely took on a life of their own. Thought for a minute I was going to have to whack them with the spatula to keep them from crawling across the kitchen.
        </p>
        <p>
         So I looked on for another recipe. The second one didnt require a spray bottle, but they came out so crumbly, they were hard to handle let along package to give away.
        </p>
        <p>
         Luckily, third time was the charm.
         <a href="http://www.soapyhollow.com/how-to-make-bath-bombs/" target="_blank">
          This recipe from Soapy Hallow
         </a>
         was just perfect, and it was something the kids could easily do with a little supervision.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-12.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-12" class=" size-full wp-image-20469 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="820" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-12.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         I didnt give up completely on that first pretty cupcake pin, though. Once the bath fizzies came out of their molds, I tried out the royal icing frosting and it all set up just great and picture perfect.
        </p>
        <p>
         After tweaking a few Pinterest recipes and blending them together, heres how to make some sure-to-be appreciated
         <strong>
          Fizzy Cupcake Bath Bombs!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Vanilla Fizzy Cupcake Bath Bomb Recipe
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Makes 3 large round bombs or 7 cupcake bombs
        </p>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Cake ingredients:
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          8 oz baking soda
         </li>
         <li>
          4 oz citric acid
         </li>
         <li>
          4 oz corn starch
         </li>
         <li>
          4 oz Epsom Salts
         </li>
         <li>
          2  tablespoons coconut oil
         </li>
         <li>
           teaspoon water
         </li>
         <li>
          2 teaspoons vanilla extract
         </li>
         <li>
          4 drops food coloring
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
         <em>
          <strong>
           Frosting ingredients:
          </strong>
         </em>
        </p>
        <ul>
         <li>
          3 tablespoons meringue powder
         </li>
         <li>
          6 tablespoons warm water
         </li>
         <li>
          1 lb powdered sugar
         </li>
         <li>
          1 teaspoon vanilla extract
         </li>
        </ul>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-2.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-2" class=" size-full wp-image-20461 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="659" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-2.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step 1. Measure your ingredients.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Yes, youll want to use a little kitchen scale to measure your dry ingredients out. The good news about this is that kids are fascinated by this part and will be more than happy to measure the ingredients. Get them off to a good start making the bombs by mixing together the baking soda, citric acid, corn starch and salts into a large GLASS (its non-reactive!) bowl to create the bath bomb base.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-4.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-4" class=" size-full wp-image-20463 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="900" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-4.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         In a separate very small bowl, combine the coconut oil, water, vanilla extract and food coloring. Oil and water dont mix very well, so dont worry if they arent 100% combined.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-6.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-6" class=" size-full wp-image-20464 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="400" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-6.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step 2: Combine wet and dry ingredients.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Heres where this recipe really was different and what make it work: mixing the wet and the dry ingredients. The citric acid in the base fizzes up when it makes contact with water. You can see where there might be a problem combining the wet and the dry. Tell the kids theyre going to have to work really fast to get this project done!
        </p>
        <p>
         Add one teaspoon of the wet ingredients to the dry at a time. Youll have to work really quickly, and mix it around with your hands. Pinch at the little bits of stuck together pieces to get them to come apart. If your mixture is bubbling up, youre adding the wet to the dry too fast. When this happens, just be sure to mix the wet pieces to the non-reactive dry really fast and it should stop. At the end, your mixture should feel a lot like slightly wet sand. It will stick together, but its going to be crumbly. Mold the fizzy bath bombs immediately after youre done mixingthis doesnt keep!
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-7.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-7" class=" size-full wp-image-20465 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="400" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-7.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step 3: Molding your fizzy cupcake bath bombs.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Bath bombs can take pretty much any shape you want. If you want to make cupcakes out of these, line each cup in a muffin pan with plastic wrap. Hard pack the bath bomb mix into each of the cups. I mean, really REALLY pack that stuff in there, pushing it down as hard as you can. With this recipe, you only have to let them sit for about 10 minutes before you unmold them and let them dry for a few hours.
        </p>
        <p>
         If youre going to skip the frosting aspect of this recipe, use the clear plastic ornaments that split into two you can find at the craft store (for less than $1) for molds.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step 4: Making frosting.
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Basically, what were going to do is make a royal icing frosting. The kind that you put on sugar cookies that gets really hard. Personally, I can take or leave the frosting when it comes to putting it in the bathtub, but it does make the presentation pretty.
        </p>
        <p>
         In your mixing bowl, blend together the meringue powder and warm water until its frothy. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla. Beat on high until stiff peaks form. If the frosting is too stiff, add a teaspoon of water at a time until its a consistency you can pipe while still standing on its own. I liked adding a few sprinkles on top to make them pretty. Let the frosting dry for a few hours before you attempt to wrap them up.
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-stands.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-stands" class=" size-full wp-image-20473 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="900" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-stands.jpg" width="600"/>
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Step 5: Packaging!
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A cellophane bag and a piece of ribbon is really all you need to wrap up these beauties.
        </p>
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-18.jpg">
          <img alt="Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-18" class=" size-full wp-image-20472 aligncenter pib-hover-img" height="639" src="//www.madefrompinterest.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Fizzy-Cupcake-Bath-Bomb-Recipe-18.jpg" width="634"/>
         </a>
        </p>
