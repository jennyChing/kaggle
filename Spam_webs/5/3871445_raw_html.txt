        <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
         Whipped Coconut Cream
        </h1>
        <p class="entry-meta">
         <time class="entry-time" datetime="2014-06-24T07:00:10+00:00" itemprop="datePublished">
          June 24, 2014
         </time>
         <span class="sep">
          |
         </span>
         <span class="entry-comments-link">
          <i class="fa fa-comments">
          </i>
          <a href="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/whipped-coconut-cream/#comments">
           27
          </a>
         </span>
         <span class="entry-categories">
          <a href="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/category/paleo-primal/" rel="category tag">
           Paleo / Primal
          </a>
          <a href="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/category/paleo-primal/sweets-treats/" rel="category tag">
           Sweets &amp; Treats
          </a>
          <a href="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/category/paleo-primal/vegetarian-paleo-primal/" rel="category tag">
           Vegetarian
          </a>
         </span>
        </p>
       </header>
       <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
        <p>
         Whipped Cream Ooooh yes! Light, fluffy, airy, silky smooth and subtlysweet, heavenly whipped cream Who doesnt care for the stuff, right? The very wordsthemselves get me all excited!
        </p>
        <p>
         I really miss the stuff, sometimes.
        </p>
        <p>
         And if like me youve pretty much eliminated dairy from your diet, theres a fair chance that you too are missing it, or at least feelmelancholy once in a while when remembering how softly it used to caress your tongue and palate.
        </p>
        <p>
         Well, dry your tears my friend because Imight have just the perfect solution for you today. Chances are youve already seen this before, but Im thinking perhaps younever actually put it to work for you. If such is the case, I saynow would be a good time to indulge. Just because why not?
        </p>
        <p>
         This is just as quick and easy to make as the real thing providing that you have a can of chilled coconut milk on hand.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669144" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream.png" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         And that is precisely whyI always keep a can of coconut milk in the fridge that way I can whip up
         <em>
          (quite literally)
         </em>
         a batch of this heavenly treatwithin a few moments notice. Coconut milk is something that I tend to buy by the case anyway, so I always have quite a few cans on hand
        </p>
        <p>
         This is so easy to make, frankly, you dont even need a recipe, let alone a pictorial. But, I dunno. Im highly visual, you know, so I figured some of you might like to get an idea of what theyshould be looking at when making this for the first time. Others may just like to lick it off the screen
        </p>
        <p>
         Just dont you blink, alright?, cuz itll go quick!
        </p>
        <p>
         You ready? Lets get started.
         <span id="more-3436669139">
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669145" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-1.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         First, delicately flip your can of refrigerated coconut milk upside down and open it up.
        </p>
        <p>
         See how the liquid is obedientlysittingon top? Thats what were looking for!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669146" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-2.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Pour that liquid into a small bowl. Were not gonna be using it now, so you can either save it for another recipe, or drink it up if youd like.
        </p>
        <p>
         See that thick creamy stuff thats hiding under this layer of coconut water? Thats what were after!
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669147" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-3.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Transfer that thick cream to a large mixing bowl. I like to use a chilled stainless steel bowl, but thats not really necessary. Its just a little extra precaution that helps in keeping the cream nice and firm. If youhave the option todo it, too, go right ahead. But by all means, dont you go and keepyour guests waiting an extra hour for their whipped cream just so you can chill your bowl
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669148" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-5.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Beat the cream on high speed with a
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004MTAWEO/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004MTAWEO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theheafoo03-20" target="_blank">
          hand mixer
         </a>
         for about 3 to 4 minutes, or until it becomes light and fluffy.
        </p>
        <p>
         You could also use a
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B008AYS726/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B008AYS726&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theheafoo03-20" target="_blank">
          stand mixer
         </a>
         equipped with the whisk attachment, but for some reason, I find that the hand mixer yields better results.If you have both machines at your disposal, I say definitely go with the portable thing
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669149" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-6.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Once your coconut cream has gotten all light and fluffy, add the honey and vanilla powder (or vanilla extract, if you dont have any powder on hand) and resume whisking until everything is well incorporated, about one minute.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669150" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-7.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         This additional whisking action will make your whipped cream even lighter and fluffier (and much yummier, too!)
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669151" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-8.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         And there we have it. Your Whipped Coconut Cream (or is it Coconut Whipped Cream?) is now ready to throne proudly atop your favorite dessert! That wasnt complicated at all now, was it?
        </p>
        <p>
         Although this whipped cream will keep for a few days in the refrigerator, I strongly suggest that you use it right away. I find that it tends to firm up and get a tad grainyafter sitting in the fridge for an extended length of time. If you have to make it in advance, though, or if you have leftovers
         <em>
          (yeah like thats gonna happen!)
         </em>
         , you can give it another quick beating before serving to bring it right back to its original glory.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="Whipped Coconut Cream | thehealthyfoodie.com" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-3436669141" height="768" src="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Whipped-Coconut-Cream-11.jpg" width="512"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         Admitit,you want to dig right in, dont you?I know I do!
        </p>
        <p>
         So tell me, have you gotthat can of coconut milk chillin yet?
        </p>
        <p>
        </p>

           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-ingredients">
            INGREDIENTS
           </p>
           <ul id="zlrecipe-ingredients-list">
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-0" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 can
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B008CJ9UYS/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B008CJ9UYS&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theheafoo03-20" target="_blank">
              full fat coconut milk
             </a>
             , unshaken and refrigerated overnight
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-1" itemprop="ingredients">
             1 tablespoon
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00014JNI0/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00014JNI0&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theheafoo03-20" target="_blank">
              raw honey
             </a>
            </li>
            <li class="ingredient" id="zlrecipe-ingredient-2" itemprop="ingredients">
             1/4 teaspoon
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00IK8U8HO/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B00IK8U8HO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theheafoo03-20" target="_blank">
              vanilla powder
             </a>
             (or
             <a class="ingredient-link" href="http://thehealthyfoodie.com/vanilla-extract/" target="_blank">
              pure vanilla extract
             </a>
             )
            </li>
           </ul>
           <p class="h-4 strong" id="zlrecipe-instructions">
            INSTRUCTIONS
           </p>
           <ol class="instructions" id="zlrecipe-instructions-list">
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-0" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Gently flip the can of coconut milk upside down and open it up; pour the liquid that sits on top into a small bowl. You're not going to use this, so you can use it in another recipe or just plain drink it!
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-1" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Transfer the solid coconut cream to a large mixing bowl (bonus points if you can use a cold
             <a class="instruction-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004YZEO9K/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004YZEO9K&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theheafoo03-20" target="_blank">
              stainless steel bowl
             </a>
             ) and beat on high speed with a
             <a class="instruction-link" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B004MTAWEO/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B004MTAWEO&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=theheafoo03-20" target="_blank">
              hand mixer
             </a>
             until the cream gets light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-2" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Add honey and vanilla powder (or vanilla extract) and resume beating on high for another minute or so, until the ingredients are well combined and the cream gets even fluffier still.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-3" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             Use to garnish your favorite desserts without delay.
            </li>
            <li class="instruction" id="zlrecipe-instruction-4" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
             This whipped cream will keep for a few days in the fridge but will firm up some and may become a bit grainy, so it might be necessary to give it another quick beating just to "freshen it up" at the time of using.
            </li>
           </ol>
