      <p>
       Yesterday
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/27736/hiking-coastal-trail-pukaskwa-national-park/">
         I left you on the trail
        </a>
       </strong>
       at 9:30 pm, about a kilometer short of our first campsite. That was the first time in decades of backpacking that I have ever backpacked in the dark. At least we ended up right beside the water without breaking anything  and awoke to the view in the photo below.
      </p>
      <p>
       (
       <em>
        As a side note we had been told at our backcountry orientation that
        <strong>
         WE HAD
        </strong>
        to make the campsite we booked every night unless we had an emergency. We only ended up making our booked campsite on one out of four nights. But in ourdefensewe saw only ONE other person on the trail in five days once wed said goodbye to the group from Caledon so we never put anybody out and we never had a day of less than 8 hours of hiking.
       </em>
       )
      </p>
      <p>
       Before calling it a night we hadpacked all our food into one backpack and dragged it far away from our tents  just in case there were any bears. We hadnt seen any bear scat during the day so we were feeling pretty comfortable and frankly we were just too tired to do anything else. If there had been rocks we could have covered the pack with them  just like Id done on a
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/26674/backpacking-trip-auyuittuq-national-park-baffin-island/">
         backpacking trip to Auyuittuq National Park
        </a>
       </strong>
       this summer  but surprisingly rocks were few and far between.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27779" style="width: 670px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-100.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"The view of the coast we woke up to in the morning"' class="size-full wp-image-27779" height="437" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-100.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        The view of the coast we woke up to in the morning
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       Our plan for the second day was to backpack
       <strong>
        11 kilometers to Fish Harbour. Normally  without a pack on  I can knock off that distance in 2-3 hours.
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       <strong>
        <span style="font-size: medium;">
         That didnt happen on this trip!!
        </span>
       </strong>
      </p>
      <p>
       But a decent nights sleep, a cup of hot coffee and several slices of fresh banana bread at least put us in a positive frame of mind to continue.
      </p>
      <p>
       We knew ahead of time that most of the day would be in the woods. Its the section of trail that I would describe as the
       <strong>
        least interesting
       </strong>
       . Still, and especially because its fall, there is lots of colour, textures, mushrooms and lichens to liven up the hike.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27780" style="width: 670px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-103-copy.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"The view of the coast we woke up to in the morning"' class="size-full wp-image-27780" height="492" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-103-copy.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Loved the texture in the mushroom
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       It didnt take us long to realize that we made the right decision to stay put for the night on the trail. On the second day the first part of the hike took us over super slippery rocks and then up through a cleft in the rocks that I never would have found in the dark. Even in the daylight it took us about 90 minutes to reach
       <strong>
        White Spruce Harbour
       </strong>
       . Its a beautiful campsite  like all of them we saw on the trail.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27782" style="width: 670px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-123.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"beach at White Spruce Harbour"' class="size-full wp-image-27782" height="445" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-123.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        White Spruce Harbour  where we should have camped the first night
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27783" style="width: 670px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-115-copy.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"We became experts at finding comfortable rest spots"' class="size-full wp-image-27783" height="437" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-115-copy.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        We became experts at finding comfortable rest spots
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27785" style="width: 670px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-125-copy.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"Crossing the White Gravel River"' class="size-full wp-image-27785" height="466" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-125-copy.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Crossing the White Gravel River
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       After a lunch stop at
       <strong>
        White Gravel River
       </strong>
        a huge campsite thats great for large groups  we motored on  though
       <strong>
        motored might not be the best word to describe our pace
       </strong>
       . The trail in the afternoon was tough  though if the rocks had been dry it would have been a snap. They were slick and slippery. Every step required concentration and careful foot placement. Interestingly the Vibram soles on my leather boots didnt grip the rock but the rubber on my $15 Crocs did a fantastic job. Its too bad they didnt offer the support I needed to backpack in them.
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27786" style="width: 650px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-131.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"A great mushroom crop on this log"' class="size-full wp-image-27786" height="390" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-131.jpg" width="640"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        A great mushroom crop on this log
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27799" style="width: 650px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-508.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"pink mushrooms"' class="size-full wp-image-27799" height="627" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-508.jpg" width="640"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Mushrooms or ??? Ive never seen a plant like this before
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27790" style="width: 670px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-143-copy.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"Slippery walking on granite ridge tops"' class="size-full wp-image-27790" height="437" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-143-copy.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Slippery walking on granite ridge tops
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       About 2 in the afternoon the thunderstorm that had been forecast the day before shook the ground and deluged us with rain. It didnt stop us but it did make the going even slower. And it managed to soak our boots.
       <strong>
        I regret that I hadnt brought some short gaiters with me
       </strong>
       .
      </p>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27787" style="width: 670px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-146.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt='"There are very few red maples around in Pukaskwa NP"' class="size-full wp-image-27787" height="452" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-146.jpg" width="660"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        There are very few red maples around in Pukaskwa NP
       </p>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_27789" style="width: 650px">
       <a href="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-156.jpg" rel="lightbox[27778]">
        <img alt="&quot;Fisherman's Cove - the site of our 2nd campsite&quot;" class="size-full wp-image-27789" height="344" src="http://www.hikebiketravel.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Pukaskwa-Coastal-Trail-156.jpg" width="640"/>
       </a>
       <p class="wp-caption-text">
        Fishermans Cove  the site of our 2nd campsite
       </p>
      </div>
      <p>
       We didnt rolled into Fishermans Cove until 6 pm. It had been a nine hour day and we were all bagged.
      </p>
      <p>
       Fortunately the rain stopped before we got to camp. We were able to set up tents without getting anything else wet and cook and eat our spaghetti dinner with a view of Lake Superior. Id schlepped a Nalgene bottle full of red wine  so with a view like we had, a glass of wine, a warm tent and a full stomach, life at the moment seemed pretty good.
      </p>
      <p>
       <span style="font-size: medium;">
        <strong>
         Little did we know that the next day would be our day from hell  and I thought that had already happened.
        </strong>
       </span>
      </p>
      <p>
       Have you ever backpacked in
       <strong>
        <a href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/pn-np/on/pukaskwa/index.aspx">
         Pukaskwa National Park
        </a>
       </strong>
       ?
      </p>
      <p>
       Leigh McAdam
