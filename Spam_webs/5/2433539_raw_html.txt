      <h1 id="a-header" itemprop="headline">
       'Perks of Being a Wallflower' trailer released, Emma Watson stars in coming of age movie
      </h1>
      <h2 id="a-subheader" itemprop="alternativeHeadline">
       The book's author, Stephen Chbosky, also served as the screenwriter and director for the film

        <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">
         Emma Watson and Logan Lerman play the leads in upcoming coming-of-age flick 'The Perks of Being a Wallflower.'

        <a class="a-enlarge" href="#">
        </a>
       </figure>
      </div>
      <p>
       The perks of having a page-to-screen film directed by the original book's author is that little detail or tone is lost in the adaptation.
      </p>
      <p>
       And judging by the newly released trailer for
       <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/topics/Stephen+Chbosky" title="Stephen Chbosky">
        Stephen Chbosky
       </a>
       's "The Perks of Being a Wallflower," fans of the coming-of-age young adult novel won't be disappointed.
      </p>
      <p>
       The movie follows the lives of a group of friends - misfits who learn to navigate the treacherous waters of high school while leaning on one another for support.
      </p>
      <p>
       "Percy Jackson" star
       <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/topics/Logan+Lerman" title="Logan Lerman">
        Logan Lerman
       </a>
       takes the lead role, as mild-mannered high school freshman Charlie, and
       <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/topics/Emma+Watson" title="Emma Watson">
        Emma Watson
       </a>
       plays his new pal and psuedo love interest, Sam.
      </p>
      <p>
       This will mark the first major role that Watson adopts for the big screen since her turn as Hermione Granger in the "Harry Potter" series.
      </p>
      <div class="story-img">
       <img alt="WF1_WEB" src="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.1089583!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/landscape_635/image.jpg"/>
       <br/>
       <em>
        Author Stephen Chbosky (left) serves as both the screenwriter and the director of the film. (John Bramley/Summit Entertainment
        <span style="font-weight: bold;">
         )
        </span>
       </em>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the trailer, the 22-year-old actress dares to bare in risque black lingerie and a feather boa in one scene depicting the group's obsession with the "Rocky Horror Picture Show."
      </p>
      <p>
       During one of the more tender moments in the film, Watson's Sam asks Lerman's Charlie point-blank, "Why do I and everyone I love pick people who treat us like we're nothing?"
      </p>
      <p>
       Replies the shy freshman: "We accept the love we think we deserve."
      </p>
      <p>
       The film, which also stars Ezra Miller, Logan Lerman, Nina Dobrev and Paul Rudd, is due out on Sept. 14.
      </p>
