       <h2 class="futura condensed">
        The Power
        <span>
         of a promise
        </span>
       </h2>
       <h3 class="futura condensed">
        A message from Our executive director and ceo
       </h3>
       <p>
        A promise is a powerful thing. Not only does it represent a steadfast commitment, but it also speaks to the future and provides assurance to those who hear it.
       </p>
       <p>
        This is precisely why we chose to make a promise to educators in our 2013 Annual Report, The Promise of Leadership: Sustaining Learning, Transforming Teaching.
       </p>
       <p>
        During the past 70 years, ASCD has stood ready to assist educators in their quest to lead and serve students. We promise you that tomorrow and in the years ahead, we will be here ready to support you.
       </p>
       <p>
        Together, we will continue to change the lives of childrentoday, tomorrow, and for many years to come.
       </p>
       <p class="float-right">
        In service,
        <img src="http://ar2013.ascd.org/wp-content/themes/ascd2013/images/three/gene-signature.png"/>
       </p>

       <h2 class="futura condensed">
        A Foundation
        <span>
         for the future
        </span>
       </h2>
       <h3 class="futura condensed">
        A message from our president
       </h3>
       <div class="two-col">
        <p>
         I am humbled and honored to be the president of an organization with roots that stretch so deep and a history so storied.
        </p>
        <p>
         ASCDs founders had a vision of improving education worldwide that still stands today, and I am proud to help ASCD sustain learning and transform teaching.
        </p>
        <p>
         As you browse our 2013 Annual Report, I hope you discover or rediscover the amazing things that ASCD has to offer our profession. We have built a world-class organization that is well-known for high-quality professional development and unwavering support of educators, and together, we stand behind our 2013 Board of Directors Charter, 
         <a href="http://www.ascd.org/ASCD/pdf/siteASCD/governance/BOD_charter.pdf" target="_blank">
          Stewards of the Vision
         </a>
         , (PDF) which clearly defines our purpose, values, and action focus.
        </p>
        <p>
         ASCD has meant so many things to so many educators over the year. As I look to our future, I see incredible possibilities.
        </p>
        <p class="float-right">
         With hope,
         <img src="http://ar2013.ascd.org/wp-content/themes/ascd2013/images/three/becky-signature.png"/>
        </p>

       <h2 class="futura condensed">
        ASCD ARIAS
        <sup>
         
        </sup>
       </h2>
       <h3>
        <span class="firstlayer">
         Imprint Adds New Chapter to
        </span>
        <span class="secondlayer">
         ASCDs Publishing History
        </span>
       </h3>
       <p>
        In 1943, ASCD published the first-ever issue of
        <a href="http://www.ascd.org/el" target="_blank">
         <em>
          Educational Leadership
          <sup>
           
          </sup>
         </em>
        </a>
        magazine. The magazine is now on its 71st volume, and ASCD's publishing department is more prolific than ever. This year alone, we published more than 30 professional development
        <a href="http://www.ascd.org/books" target="_blank">
         titles
        </a>
        and
        <a href="http://www.ascd.org/news-media/Press-Room/News-Releases/ASCD-Arias.aspx" target="_blank">
         successfully launched
        </a>
        an innovative short-format imprint,
        <a href="http://www.ascd.org/ascdarias/index.html" target="_blank">
         ASCD Arias
        </a>
        . Each ASCD Arias publication answers a crucial and timely "How Do I...?" question and is available in low-cost print and e-book formats. These original publications deliver the high-quality content youve come to expect and can be read in one sitting and immediately applied to practice.
       </p>

       <p>
        Concerned with the personal element in education from the start, ASCD welcomed the humanist movement, and in 1962, we published
        <em>
         Perceiving, Behaving, Becoming
        </em>
        , a yearbook with contributions from influential psychologists and humanist thinkers Carl Rogers and A. H. Maslow. We have continued to build on this work; in 2007, with the launch of the
        <a href="http://www.ascd.org/whole-child.aspx" target="_blank">
         Whole Child Initiative
        </a>
        , we began advocating for a whole child approach to education, which ensures that each student is healthy, safe, engaged, supported, and challenged. In 2013 alone, successful ASCD advocacy efforts supported creation of a
        <a href="http://www.riascd.org/wp-content/uploads/Rhode_Island-2013-H5153-Introduced.pdf" target="_blank">
         joint resolution
        </a>
        (PDF) that supports a whole child approach in Rhode Island and the development of an Arkansas
        <a href="http://www.arkleg.state.ar.us/assembly/2013/2013R/Bills/SB1051.pdf" target="_blank">
         bill (PDF)
        </a>
         now law  that establishes a statewide recognition program for schools and communities that promote a whole child approach to education.

      <h2 class="futura condensed">
       Professional Reading
      </h2>
      <h3>
       Resources Go Digital, Mobile, and International
      </h3>
      <p>
       From
       <a href="http://www.ascd.org/about-ascd/ascd-apps.aspx" target="_blank">
        e-reading apps
       </a>
       to print media, weve got professional reading covered. Our first yearbook, published during World War II and edited by Gordon Mackenzie and J. Cecil Parker, marked the start of our print endeavors. Since then, our catalog has grown tremendously and so has the number of ways in which we deliver insightful and influential voices in education to you: in print, as
       <a href="http://shop.ascd.org/BOOKSEBOOKS.aspx" target="_blank">
        e-books
       </a>
       , and via apps and
       <a href="http://www.ascd.org/news-media/Press-Room/News-Releases/ASCD-Professional-E-Books-Available-International-Retailer-Kobo.aspx" target="_blank">
        international retailers
       </a>
       . Of the more than 450,000 books distributed as a member benefit this year  including
       <a href="http://www.ascd.org/Publications/Books/Overview/Essential-Questions.aspx" target="_blank">
        <em>
         Essential Questions
        </em>
       </a>
       and
       <a href="http://www.ascd.org/Publications/Books/Overview/Assessment-and-Student-Success-in-a-Differentiated-Classroom.aspx" target="_blank">
        <em>
         Assessment and Student Success in a Differentiated Classroom
        </em>
       </a>
        28,000 were e-books. Now, nearly every title published in print by ASCD is also available as a PDF e-book, and nearly 200
       <a href="http://www.ascd.org/publications/books/browse-by-title.aspx" target="_blank">
        titles
       </a>
       are available in e-reader and tablet-friendly formats.
      </p>
