       <h1>
        Perth Freight Link: Transport Minister apologises to residents over demolition plan
       </h1>
       <div class="byline">
        By
        <a href="/news/jacob-kagi/4421098" target="_self" title="">
         Jacob Kagi
        </a>
       </div>
       <p class="published">
        Posted
        <span class="timestamp">
         May 21, 2015 07:56:33
        </span>
       </p>
       <div class="attached-content">
        <div class="inline-content photo left">
         <a href="/news/2015-05-20/smirke-family-home-in-moody-glen-palmyrajpg/6485438">
          <img alt="The Smirke family home in Moody Glen, Palmyra." height="227" src="http://www.abc.net.au/news/image/6469342-3x2-340x227.jpg" title="The Smirke family home in Moody Glen, Palmyra." width="340"/>
         </a>
         <a class="inline-caption" href="/news/2015-05-20/smirke-family-home-in-moody-glen-palmyrajpg/6485438">
          <strong>
           Photo:
          </strong>
          This home in Palmyra is among nearly 80 slated for demolition.
          <span class="source">
           (Supplied: Tania Smirke)
          </span>
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="inline-content story left">
         <a href="/news/2015-05-14/palmyra-residents-fear-losing-homes-to-perth-freight-link/6469588">
          <strong>
           Related Story:
          </strong>
          'Like living The Castle': WA families face losing homes
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="inline-content story left">
         <a href="/news/2015-05-19/perth-freight-link-tunnel-option-may-save-homes-from-bulldozer/6480420">
          <strong>
           Related Story:
          </strong>
          Tunnel plan 'may save homes' from bulldozer
         </a>
        </div>
        <div class="inline-content map left">
         <div class="story-map">
         </div>
         <a class="inline-caption" href="http://maps.google.com/?q=-32.05,115.7833(Palmyra%206157)&amp;z=5">
          <strong>
           Map:
          </strong>
          Palmyra 6157
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        West Australian Transport Minister Dean Nalder has apologised to residents facing the prospect of losing their homes as part of the new Perth Freight Link project, as the Premier promised affected people would be treated generously.
       </p>
       <p>
        Nearly 80 properties in the southern suburb of Palmyra could be acquired by the Government and demolished, as part of the $1.6 billion project to connect Perth's eastern industrial areas to Fremantle.
       </p>
       <p>
        A tunnel option being investigated by Main Roads could offer a possible lifeline to those residents, but if that does not eventuate they stand to lose their homes.
       </p>
       <p>
        The Opposition stepped up its attack over the Government's handling of the road project in Parliament yesterday, accusing it of failing to properly communicate with the affected residents.
       </p>
       <p>
        Mr Nalder acknowledged that communication had not been good enough.
       </p>
       <p>
        "I apologise for the way and the type of communication that went out, as the minister, I have to take responsibility," he said.
       </p>
       <p>
        "I will meet with residents and talk them through.
       </p>
       <p>
        "We take the whole issue and impact on people and small businesses along Stock Road and High Street and Leach Highway very seriously."
       </p>
       <p>
        Earlier, Mr Barnett promised that residents would be treated "fairly and generously" if the Government had to resume properties.
       </p>
       <p>
        "I can understand their anguish but we are still at the early stages of finalising that route into the Port of Fremantle," he said.
       </p>
       <p>
        Mr Nalder said he was cautiously optimistic a tunnel would prove viable.
       </p>
       <p>
        "I want that fully explored because if we can do that, we will deliver a far better outcome for the community," he said.
       </p>
       <p>
        "But I cannot put my hand on my heart today and say 'that will be the outcome'."
       </p>
