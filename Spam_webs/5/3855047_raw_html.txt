     <h2 class="entry-title">
      <a href="http://bakerstreet.tv/2012/02/diy-vanilla-extract/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to DIY: Vanilla Extract">
       DIY: Vanilla Extract
      </a>
     </h2>
     <div class="entry-content">
      <div class="pin-it-btn-wrapper">
       <a data-pin-config="beside" data-pin-do="buttonBookmark" href="//www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fbakerstreet.tv%2F2012%2F02%2Fdiy-vanilla-extract%2F&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2Fbakerstreet.tv%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2012%2F02%2FHomemade-Vanilla-Extract2-600x1024.jpg&amp;description=DIY%3A%20Vanilla%20Extract" rel="nobox">

      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Its completely understandable if you would want to go out and buy yourself vanilla extract at the store. But if youre reading this blog, chances are youre comfortable experimenting in the kitchen and when youre going to bke your own cake, why not make the ingredients that go into it as well? This Im sure of: once youve made your own vanilla extract, there is just no going back to the store-bought one.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Branded vanilla extract is usually too sweet and uneven. If you opt for the purer, higher quality option your wallet will complain. Why bother when you could spend under 10 minutes and make your own using only 2 ingredients?
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       The process is as simple as they come: A sterilized bottle, some vodka which could be easily replaced by rum or bourbon and a few vanilla beans. Put them in a tight fitting lid jar and store for 8 weeks in a dry and cool place. Sounds easy right? You could also package these in a fancy bottle and gift to a food enthusiast friend.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: center;">
       <a href="http://bakerstreet.tv/2012/02/diy-vanilla-extract/homemade-vanilla-extract2/" rel="attachment wp-att-4726">
        <img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-4726" height="819" src="http://bakerstreet.tv/wp-content/uploads/2012/02/Homemade-Vanilla-Extract2-600x1024.jpg" title="Homemade Vanilla Extract2" width="480"/>
       </a>
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Homemade vanilla extract has a fountain-of-youth quality about it. Once youve used, say, 30% of your extract, you can replace the diminished amount with vodka (just make sure you use the same liquor you used earlier) and voila, its like the extract never ran out! You can add in a fresh bunch of beans every once in a while and remove the old ones if the jar becomes too crowded.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Now you dont want to go throwing those empty pods. They can be bunged into a jar of sugar or your tea tin to flavor them.
      </p>
      <p style="text-align: justify;">
       Try it. Once I started using my own vanilla extract in all my baked goods, like I said  there was no going back to the store for it.
      </p>

          Split the vanilla beans using a sharp knife. The idea is to expose the seeds but leaving the ends as it is. Put them in your jar.

         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Pour the vodka over the vanilla beans.
         </li>
         <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
          Put the lid on the bottle, and store for a 6-8 weeks. Give the bottle a stir once or twice a week.
         </li>

      <div class="comment-body">
       <p>
        Hello,
       </p>
       <p>
        Yes, vanilla extract is so easy to make at home, but it is extremely important to use the correct ratio of vanilla beans to alcohol. It is also important to use good quality vanilla beans. Most stores, especially online, sell very weak vanilla beans that do not contain much vanillin (the active ingredient of vanilla).
       </p>
       <p>
        First and foremost, you MUST use at least 5 vanilla beans per 8 ounces alcohol. For 4 cups, at least 20 vanilla beans should be used.
       </p>
       <p>
        A great source for vanilla is Beanilla Trading Co. They specialize in vanilla and always have really large vanilla beans. They sell online for $1 per bean.
       </p>
