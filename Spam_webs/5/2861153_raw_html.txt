         <h1 class="entry-title" itemprop="headline">
          Customer obsession and intelligence in the digital mailroom
         </h1>
         <p class="entry-meta">
          By
          <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
           <a class="entry-author-link" href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/author/conversionation/" itemprop="url" rel="author">
            <span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">
             J-P De Clerck
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
          <span class="entry-comments-link">
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/customer-obsession-intelligence-digital-mailroom/#respond">
            <span class="dsq-postid" data-dsqidentifier="412 http://www.informationdynamix.com/?p=412">
             Leave a Comment
            </span>
           </a>
          </span>
         </p>
        </header>
        <div class="entry-content" itemprop="text">
         <div class="at-above-post addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide" data-title="Customer obsession and intelligence in the digital mailroom" data-url="http://www.informationdynamix.com/customer-obsession-intelligence-digital-mailroom/">
         </div>
         <div class="at-above-post-recommended addthis_default_style addthis_toolbox at-wordpress-hide">
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           The idea and practice of the digital mailroom exists since quite some time now. However, despite the efforts to go paperless and digitize mailroom processes, it has been adopted in relatively few organizations (approximately 10%).
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          This is quite remarkable as one of the most direct benefits of the digital
          <a href="http://uk.knowledgeshare.kodakalaris.com/solutions/single-source-mailroom/" target="_blank">
           mailroom
          </a>
          is reducing costs, something virtually all businesses try to do. There are several reasons why there are still so many organizations with traditional (non-digital) mailrooms. From resistance to change to alternative solutions that have lowered costs in mailroom operations.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, things are about to change, again for several reasons. The technologies enabling the move to a digital mailroom exist. Yet, they also evolve and with the increasing use of artificial intelligence in a more connected and outcome-oriented
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/intelligent-information-management-knowledge-engage/" title="Intelligent information management: knowledge to engage">
           intelligent information management
          </a>
          approach, new possibilities arise, among others in better customer service and business process optimization.
          <span id="more-412">
          </span>
         </p>
         <h2>
          Drivers in digital mailroom adoption
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           Even if the evolutions regarding the digital mailroom are strongly related with cost efficiencies and going paperless, there are several other drivers that might step up the speed at which organizations move to digital mailrooms.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           A first driver has everything today with the changing dynamics of BPM (Business Process Management).
          </li>
          <li>
           A second reason is related to  what else could it be  the increasing
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/customer-experience-optimization-management/" title="Customer Experience Optimization: a holistic approach">
            customer experience (optimization)
           </a>
           priority.
          </li>
          <li>
           Driver number three is the increase of unstructured communications, beyond paper and email.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          These drivers arent new but the reality behind them is quickly becoming more important (also see the recent Forrester research below).
          <strong>
           <a href="http://graphics.kodak.com/DocImaging/GB/en/Industry_Solutions/Position_Papers/Position_Papers/Intelligent_Information_Improving_the_Customer_Experience/index.htm" target="_blank" title="Intelligent Information: Improving the Customer Experience">
            As found in research by AIIM, in collaboration with Kodak Alaris Information Management
           </a>
           , most organizations today receive inbound communications over at least 4 channels
          </strong>
          , there is a disconnect between paper and electronic content and the majority of respondents feel that the speed of response to customers should be drastically improved overall. That word overall (read: from capturing to outcome across the specific business process) is important here, certainly also in a digital mailroom context.
         </p>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_414" style="width: 560px">
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/The-customer-and-customer-response-key-in-future-business-success-and-in-the-digital-mailroom.gif">
           <img alt="The customer and customer response - key in future business success - and in the digital mailroom" class="wp-image-414 size-full" height="372" src="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/The-customer-and-customer-response-key-in-future-business-success-and-in-the-digital-mailroom.gif" width="550"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           The customer and customer response  key in future business success  and in the digital mailroom (
           <a href="http://graphics.kodak.com/DocImaging/GB/en/Industry_Solutions/Position_Papers/Position_Papers/Intelligent_Information_Improving_the_Customer_Experience/index.htm" title="Intelligent Information: Improving the Customer Experience">
            source: Intelligent Information: Improving the Customer Experience
           </a>
           )
          </p>
         </div>
         <p>
          <strong>
           In 
           <a href="http://www.aiim.org/Research-and-Publications/Research/Industry-Watch/Paper-Wars-2013" target="_blank" title="Winning the Paper Wars  capture the content and mobilize the process troops - See more at: http://www.aiim.org/Research-and-Publications/Research/Industry-Watch/Paper-Wars-2013#sthash.Z9GfQjw7.dpuf">
            Winning the Paper Wars
           </a>
           , AIIM summed up some reported benefits of implementing a digital mailroom as follows:
          </strong>
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Improved mail traceability and compliance.
          </li>
          <li>
           Enhanced customer service.
          </li>
          <li>
           Improved data capture quality into downstream processes.
          </li>
          <li>
           The availability of mail for remote access by staff immediately.
          </li>
          <li>
           Consolidation of separate scanning operations.
          </li>
          <li>
           Paybacks on labor and space savings alone.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_413" style="width: 560px">
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Reported-benefits-of-digital-mailroom-implementations-source-AIIM-Winning-the-Paper-Wars.gif">
           <img alt="Reported benefits of digital mailroom implementations - source AIIM Winning the Paper Wars" class="size-full wp-image-413" height="306" src="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Reported-benefits-of-digital-mailroom-implementations-source-AIIM-Winning-the-Paper-Wars.gif" width="550"/>
          </a>
          <p class="wp-caption-text">
           Reported benefits of digital mailroom implementations 
           <a href="http://www.aiim.org/Research-and-Publications/Research/Industry-Watch/Paper-Wars-2013" target="_blank" title="Winning the Paper Wars  capture the content and mobilize the process troops - See more at: http://www.aiim.org/Research-and-Publications/Research/Industry-Watch/Paper-Wars-2013#sthash.Z9GfQjw7.dpuf">
            source: AIIM Winning the Paper Wars
           </a>
          </p>
         </div>
         <h2>
          From going paperless to improving business processes and customer experiences
         </h2>
         <p>
          <strong>
           While
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/document-imaging-and-capture/" title="Document imaging and capture: evolutions and solutions">
            capturing/imaging
           </a>
           , which is also evolving, of course remains an essential part of the whole digital mailroom equation, there is an increasing focus on what happens with the mails (and, as mentioned, other forms of communication), once capturing and conversion into digital documents has happened.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Despite industry efforts and a clear will among several organizations to reduce the volume of paper in and outside the mailroom, paper wont go away anytime soon. In many circumstances, its simply required to maintain paper documents and lets not forget that many of us still have the habit of printing electronic documents, to read them later or send them upon request of a company. A simple example: you make an invoice electronically but still have to print it and send it using snail mail to your customer. We all know examples like this (again, sometimes required).
         </p>
         <p>
          The drivers that existed to improve and digitize the mailroom process on the capturing side, still exist and will continue to becoming more important, not in the least because customers in the broadest sense, want to be served faster than ever before.
         </p>
         <p>
          However, the mentioned other drivers also play a significant role on the output, follow-up process and workflow side.
          <strong>
           With electronic workflows and the use of artificial intelligence in the digital mailroom, within one single process documents from multiple sources in the mailroom can be captured, centralized, handled using intelligent processing, identified and connected to various business processes.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <h2>
          BPM: increasingly about and around the customer (experience)
         </h2>
         <p>
          As a result, the information of letters can be extracted using intelligent document recognition, matched with data in other systems (e.g. customer contact data and histories) and sent to the proper department and even agent for follow-up. This routing, based on digitized and validated information, along with the possibilities to connect with business rules and business processes, is not only possible today, its also becoming more important.
         </p>
         <p>
          <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/One-integrated-process-source-infographic-Business-Process-Outsourcing-BPO.gif">
           <img alt="One integrated process- source infographic Business Process Outsourcing - BPO" class="alignnone wp-image-415 size-full" height="246" src="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/One-integrated-process-source-infographic-Business-Process-Outsourcing-BPO.gif" width="550"/>
          </a>
         </p>
         <p>
          As mentioned, the customer experience, has a lot to do with it. In that sense its interesting to note, that
          <strong>
           according to
           <a href="http://blogs.forrester.com/clay_richardson/14-11-07-customer_obsession_set_to_disrupt_bpm_market_in_2015" target="_blank" title="Customer Obsession Set To Disrupt BPM Market In 2015">
            new research from Forrester
           </a>
           , the evolutions in business process management (BPM) will be dominated by what the company calls customer obsession
          </strong>
          in 2015. The result? An increasing number of BPM projects will be run by customer experience teams, just as BPM teams have started to adopt customer experience practices such as customer journey mapping.
         </p>
         <p>
          Knowing that improved customer service ranks second on the list of digital mailroom benefits according to AIIM and that in customer service, as well as in business process optimization, the customer experience and that so-called customer obsession plays a key role, its more than probable that the  intelligent  digital mailroom is becoming more important in this context too.
         </p>
         <p>
          <strong>
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/intelligent-information-management-knowledge-engage/intelligent-information-management-activation/" title="Intelligent information management and activation  an overview">
            Intelligent information activation
           </a>
           and data are essential in it and in the connected processes, as the AIIM research shows as well, confirming the importance of the downstream processes and the need for
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/actionable-data-big-data/" title="Actionable Data: from Big Data to smart processes and results">
            actionable data
           </a>
           , whereby
           <a href="http://www.informationdynamix.com/artificial-intelligence-harness-unstructured-disconnected-data/" title="Artificial intelligence to harness unstructured and disconnected data">
            artificial intelligence
           </a>
           is never far away.
          </strong>
         </p>
         <p>
          Furthermore, there always remains a link with going paperless. To quote AIIM from the mentioned paper: almost all business improvement initiatives can be augmented by a paper-free project, even if the end of paper is still far away. Starting by asking what paper exactly is really needed for legal compliance is one way to start. Focusing on the overall processes another one.
         </p>
         <div class="omsc-box omsc-with-title omsc-with-border omsc-with-bg-color" style="border-width:1px;background-color:#ffffff;text-align:left">
          <div class="omsc-box-inner">
           <div class="omsc-box-title">
            The digital mailroom and complaint handling
           </div>
           <p>
           </p>
           <div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_420" style="width: 260px">
            <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UI_fTHfWd1w" target="_blank">
             <img alt="Rod Hughes - Kodak Alaris left - interviewed by Ralph Gammon" class="size-full wp-image-420" height="153" src="http://www.informationdynamix.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Rod-Hughes-Kodak-Alaris-left-interviewed-by-Ralph-Gammon.jpg" width="250"/>
            </a>
            <p class="wp-caption-text">
             <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UI_fTHfWd1w" target="_blank" title="DIR Editor Ralph Gammon Interviews Kodak Alaris' Rod Hughes at Global Directions Conference ">
              Rod Hughes (left), interviewed by Ralph Gammon
             </a>
            </p>
           </div>
           <p>
            Firms have become very aware of the cost of getting new customers, very aware of how important customer experience is to retaining customers, and therefore closely examine traditional mailroom applications and the way they manage receiving complaints. The need to serve customers more quickly and more effectively has become really, really important to the long term viability of the firm
