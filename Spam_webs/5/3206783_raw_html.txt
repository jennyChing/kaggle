      <p class="clear ItemCenter Caption">
       Mount Rogers Wild Pony
       <br/>
       <img alt="Mount Rogers Wild Pony, appalachian trail" height="334" src="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/image-files/mount_rogers_pony_appalachian_trail.jpg" title="Mount Rogers Wild Pony" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       When you finish this hike, you wish it could go on. You will have great memories of wide open sky, jagged rock outcrops, and a cool dip below the falls on Comers Creek. The Damascus area has scenery unlike any you've seen in other parts of Virginia. Herds of wild horses roam expansive mountain meadows in Mount Rogers National Recreation Area.
      </p>
      <p>
       The tree capped Mount Rogers at 5,729 feet, Virginia's tallest peak, has a strong spruce fir scent lingering around. Mushrooms and wild berries are found on Iron Mountain. Rhododendron's blooms seem ever present in late spring, early summer. Turks cap lily is seen spring through fall. Using trails and abandoned roads, trek along rocky peaks, cliffs, and steep ridges, through deep valleys, and across high meadows.
      </p>
      <h3>
       Appalachian Trail, Mount Rogers
       <br/>
       National Recreation Area
      </h3>
      <p class="ItemCenter Caption">
       Mount Rogers
       <br/>
       <img alt="Mount Rogers, appalachian trail" height="334" src="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/image-files/mount_rogers_appalachian_trail.jpg" title="Mount Rogers" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       Mount Rogers is the highest mountain in Virginia. While quite a few mountains in the Appalachians outside of Virginia are higher, Mount Rogers can claim to be the highest east of South Dakota that lacks a road to its summit. The summit is covered by a thick spruce fir forest and offers no viewpoint, but the trails to the summit pass through open meadows and cross bald ridgetops with great vistas.
      </p>
      <p>
       Mount Rogers is the showcase of a National Recreation Area. The summit is reached by a 0.5 mile trail off of the Appalachian Trail. The trailheads are at Massie Gap in Grayson Highlands State Park and at Elk Garden on Virginia Secondary Route 600.
      </p>
      <p>
       Those wanting solitude should use the area on weekdays or during low use seasons. The following trails are heavily used: Appalachian Trail, Mount Rogers Trail, Virginia Highlands Trail, and the Mount Rogers Spur Trail.
      </p>
      <p>
       Wild ponies can be seen roaming the open crest zone. Rhododendron bloom in June.
      </p>
      <h3>
       Iron Mountain
      </h3>
      <p class="ItemCenter Caption">
       Iron Mountain
       <br/>
       <img alt="Iron Mountain, appalachian trail" height="375" src="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/image-files/iron_mountain_appalachian_trail.jpg" title="Iron Mountain" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <p>
       The AT runs over 2,100 miles from Georgia to Maine. It enters Johnson County on top of Iron Mountain and runs north to Cross Mountain, where Iron Mountain and Holston Mountain come together. From there it crosses over to Holston Mountain and follows it to the Virginia state line.
      </p>
      <p class="ItemCenter Caption">
       Water Fall
       <br/>
       <img alt="Water Fall, appalachian trail" height="375" src="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/image-files/water_fall_appalachian_trail.jpg" title="Water Fall" width="500"/>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align:center;">
       More Great AT Articles
      </h3>
      <p>
       Many
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/appalachian-trail.html">
        Appalachian Trail
       </a>
       hikers claim the views at the Pinnacle are the best views on the Pennsylvania section. Walk the Appalachian Trail for spectacular views of Hawk Mountain, the Lehigh Valley, and Blue Rocks.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/appalachian-trail-pennsylvania.html">
        The AT traverses ridges and dips through gaps
       </a>
       , logging some of its most rugged miles within this state. The Pennsylvania leg takes the better part of a month to complete.
      </p>
      <p>
       The 2,179 mile
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/appalachian-trail-ct-ma.html">
        AT, journeying from Springer Mountain, Georgia to Mount Katahdin, Maine
       </a>
       , traverses the northwest corner of Connecticut and western Massachusetts, touring forest, field, and mountain top.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/appalachian-trail-va.html">
        The northern Virginia leg of the AT
       </a>
       is one exhausting climb after another. On open mountain balds, views drop west onto the Great Valley.
      </p>
      <p>
       The less traveled western section of the
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/appalachian-trail-ny.html">
        Bear Mountain, AT, Harriman State Park
       </a>
       offers an ideal introduction to the history, flora, and fauna of the Hudson Highlands.
      </p>
      <p>
       This loop starts at the highest trailhead in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park,
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/appalachian-trail-tenn.html">
        taking the AT
       </a>
       past myriad views of the park and beyond. It then drops into Tennessee.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/springer-mountain.html">
        Springer Mountain
       </a>
       in southern Fannin County is the start of a two thousand+ mile trek to Maine's Mount Katahdin known as the Appalachian Trail.
      </p>
      <p>
       On
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/women-backpacking.html">
        Women Hiking trips
       </a>
       the stress of every day life disappear, simplicity replaces busyness, and gaining perspective on what is truly important in life slowly occurs.
      </p>
      <p>
       The 9.8 mile section of
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/harpers-ferry-wv.html">
        Appalachian Trail leading from Crampton Gap to Harpers Ferry WV
       </a>
       is one of the most popular and rewarding Appalachian Trail day hikes in this region.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/sunfish-pond.html">
        Sunfish Pond
       </a>
       is one of the most popular hiking destinations in New Jersey with good reason. The rocky shores of this crystal clear glacial lake are postcard perfect.
      </p>
      <p>
       Wish you could backpack tall mountains with ease? Here's the
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/best-strength-training-program.html">
        best strength training program designed for backpackers.
       </a>
      </p>
      <h3 style="text-align:center;">
       More Great Backpacking Articles
      </h3>
      <p>
       This is
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/backpack-food.html">
        backpack food
       </a>
       for any seven day trip. Longer trips require different planning and more caloric intake, in order to maintain strength and stamina over the long haul.
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/havasu-canyon.html">
        Havasu Canyon
       </a>
       is one of those places where a spectacular natural setting combines with the fascination of native peoples to create a uniquely memorable backcountry excursion.
      </p>
      <p>
       This area includes three hike options,
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/mt-pisgah.html">
        Mt Pisgah Trail
       </a>
       and Picnic Area Connector, Frying Pan Mountain Trail, and Buck Spring Trail. Driving the Parkway from Asheville to Mt Pisgah is a humbling experience.
      </p>
      <p>
       Located scarcely sixty miles northwest of Washington, DC,
       <a href="http://www.backpack-and-gear.com/cunningham-falls-state-park.html">
        Catoctin Mountain Park and Cunningham Falls State Park
       </a>
       let arriving visitors know immediately that they're far removed from the urban plains.
      </p>
