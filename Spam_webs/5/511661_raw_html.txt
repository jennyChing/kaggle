        <p>
         <span class="recipe-note">
          <strong>
           Author Notes:
          </strong>
          Is there a better street food than falafel? You can keep your sketchy taco trucks. The smell of the balls frying, the cool, creamy tzatziki on a muggy New York afternoon. The idea that you are eating something good for you that's on  the fried food pyramid. It's really got it all.
          <br/>
          And it even comes with it's own slideshow:)
         </span>
         
         <a class="username" href="/users/28766-marisab67">
          marisab67
         </a>
        </p>
        <p>
         <span class="recipe-note">
          <strong>
           Food52 Review:
          </strong>
          Call anything "world's easiest" and we're skeptical, but marisab67 is on to something. We'd never made falafel from scratch before and were astonished at how easy -- and fun -- it could be. The food processor does double duty for the herbed chickpea mixture and then the garlic-scented tzatziki, saving us from both fine-chopping and washing extra dishes. The falafel patties are easily formed (a good time to put children or other passersby to work) and well-behaved in the frying pan. One bite into a pita stuffed with freshly crisped falafel, doused in your perfect tzatziki and a few shakes of hot sauce, and ordering take-out will seem like too much trouble. - A&amp;M
         </span>
         
         <abbr title="The Editors">
          The Editors
         </abbr>
        </p>

         <ol>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Drain chickpeas and let air dry for 2 hours, or more.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Process chickpeas, onion, 2 cloves garlic, half bunch mint, cilantro, egg, bread, salt and pepper, cumin and half the lemon juice on low speed until a thick paste forms. No chunks or your balls will fall apart.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Form into patties and let rest while tzatziki is made.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Rinse processor and pulse cucumber, yogurt, rest of mint and lemon juice and the last garlic clove on low just until blended. salt and pepper to taste.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Fry patties in canola on medium high heat for 3 minutes each side or until golden brown.
          </li>
          <li itemprop="recipeInstructions">
           Serve with warm pita or flatbread.
          </li>
