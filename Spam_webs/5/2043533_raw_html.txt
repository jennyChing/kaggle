         <h1>
          <a href="http://phenomena.nationalgeographic.com/2013/08/17/ive-got-your-missing-links-right-here-17-august-2013/">
           Ive got your missing links right here (17 August 2013)
          </a>
         </h1>
         <div class="post-author">
          <span class="by">
           by
          </span>
          Ed Yong
         </div>
        </header>
        <div class="article-text">
         <p>
          <b>
           Top picks
          </b>
         </p>
         <p>
          How to Build an
          <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2013/08/how-to-build-an-ice-wall-around-a-leaking-nuclear-reactor/278634/">
           Ice Wall
          </a>
          Around a Leaking Nuclear Reactor. By Alexis Madrigal, featuring an interview with a guy who is Director of Ground Freezing.
         </p>
         <p>
          An insightful read: What the Science Tells Us About 
          <a href="http://compassblogs.org/blog/2013/08/12/trust-in-science/">
           Trust in Science
          </a>
          , by Liz Neeley.
         </p>
         <p>
          
          <a href="http://www.nature.com/news/evidence-based-justice-corrupted-memory-1.13543">
           Elizabeth Loftus
          </a>
          has spent decades exposing flaws in eyewitness testimony.  Great profile by Mo Costandi.
         </p>
         <p>
          
          <a href="http://www.nature.com/news/the-hidden-threat-that-could-prevent-polio-s-global-eradication-1.13557">
           Chronic excreters
          </a>
            not a description of Twitter users, but a threat to polio eradication. By Helen Branswell.
         </p>
         <p>
          On the trail of a
          <a href="http://www.sfchronicle.com/health/article/Local-doctor-on-trail-of-new-snakebite-treatment-4719507.php">
           new snakebite treatment
          </a>
          , an unidentified scientist volunteers to be injected with a simulated cobra bite.
         </p>
         <p>
          What makes the
          <a href="http://mindhacks.com/2013/08/15/what-makes-the-ouija-board-move/">
           ouija board
          </a>
          move, and what it says about your brain. Fascinating read by Tom Stafford.
         </p>
         <p>
          A new test for
          <a href="http://news.sciencemag.org/brain-behavior/2013/08/magnetic-trick-define-consciousness?rss=1">
           gauging consciousness
          </a>
          in an unresponsive patient that requires no participation by the individual
         </p>
         <p>
          Tim de Chant pulls the hype out of the
          <a href="http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/nova/next/tech/hyperloop-and-high-speed-trains/">
           Hyperloop
          </a>
          , leaving only an rloop.
         </p>
         <p>
          A virus laid waste to
          <a href="http://www.wired.com/wiredscience/2013/08/the-fall-and-rise-of-gene-therapy/">
           James Wilsons career
          </a>
          ; another could offer redemption. Carl Zimmer on rise/fall of gene therapy
         </p>
         <p>
          Before denoting a type of machine, 
          <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/technology/archive/2013/08/engine-the-history-of-a-concept-from-14th-century-poetry-to-google/278611/">
           engine
          </a>
           referred to the abstract concept of ingenuity. By Becca Rosen.
         </p>
         <p>
          This Ian Sample piece on a new species of
          <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/science/2013/aug/15/teddy-bear-olinguito-ecuador-carnivore">
           raccoon-like carnivore
          </a>
          (wait for it) discovered in Ecuador is wonderful.
         </p>
         <p>
          Data everywhere!
          <a href="http://blogs.smithsonianmag.com/science/2013/08/what-can-old-menus-from-hawaii-tell-us-about-changing-ocean-health/">
           Old Hawaiian restaurant menus
