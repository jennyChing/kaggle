        <h1 class="full_story_title">
         Sharan-Raja lose in Wimbledon 1st round
        </h1>
        <p class="DateAndAuthor">
         Jun 25 2013, 7:01am CDT | by
         <a href="/latest_stories/all/all/8" rel="author">
          Luigi Lugmayr
         </a>
        </p>
        <h2 class="StoryIntro">
         London, June 25 (IANS) Indian tennis pair Divij Sharan and Purav Raja squandered a two-set lead to end up losers in the men's doubles competition at Wimbledon in a first round clash against American-German duo Nicholas Monroe and Simon Stadler here.
        </h2>
        <div style="float:left;margin:6px;">
         <script async="" src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js">
         </script>
         <!-- Balla Story -->
         <ins class="adsbygoogle" data-ad-client="ca-pub-8330330673974283" data-ad-slot="6654334554" style="display:inline-block;width:300px;height:250px">
         </ins>
         <script>
          (adsbygoogle = window.adsbygoogle || []).push({});
         </script>
        </div>
        <p>
         <a href="/category/london">
          London
         </a>
         , June 25  Indian tennis pair
         <a href="/category/divij-sharan">
          Divij Sharan
         </a>
         and
         <a href="/category/purav-raja">
          Purav Raja
         </a>
         squandered a two-set lead to end up losers in the men's doubles competition at
         <a href="/category/wimbledon">
          Wimbledon
         </a>
         in a first round clash against American-
         <a href="/category/german">
          German
         </a>
         duo Nicholas
         <a href="/category/monroe">
          Monroe
         </a>
         and
         <a href="/category/simon-stadler">
          Simon Stadler
         </a>
         here.
        </p>
        <p>
         Monroe-Stadler defeated the Indian pair, making their Grand Slam debut, 6-7(4), 2-6, 6-3, 6-4, 6-4 in three hours and 15 minutes Monday in the only
         <a href="/category/grass">
          grass
         </a>
         court major.
        </p>
        <p>
         The Indian pair did well to bag the first set, in a tie-break, and the second set quickly but the tide turned when Monroe and Stadler broke in the third set to extend the match.
        </p>
        <p>
         The American-German duo broke the Indians once more than the latter in the fourth set to push the match into the decider which was also taken by the more experienced pair.
        </p>
        <p>
         Despite playing well, lack of experience at such a stage cost the Indians crucial points at significant junctures of the match.
        </p>
        <p>
         IANS
