       <div class="post-content main">
        <p>
         <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1936493861/ref=as_li_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=1936493861&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=atkfeed-20&amp;linkId=NYA4HNQ6ZZGTA4EA" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.amazon.com']);">
          <img align="left" border="0" src="http://ws-na.amazon-adsystem.com/widgets/q?_encoding=UTF8&amp;ASIN=1936493861&amp;Format=_SL110_&amp;ID=AsinImage&amp;MarketPlace=US&amp;ServiceVersion=20070822&amp;WS=1&amp;tag=atkfeed-20" style="margin-right: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;"/>
         </a>
         <img alt="" border="0" height="1" src="http://ir-na.amazon-adsystem.com/e/ir?t=atkfeed-20&amp;l=as2&amp;o=1&amp;a=1936493861" style="border:none !important; margin:0px !important;" width="1"/>
         <em>
          With the publication of
          <em>
           <a href="http://amzn.to/1m0mXBr" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://amzn.to']);">
            The Cooks Illustrated Meat Book
           </a>
          </em>
          , were celebrating all things meat on The Feed. Over the next week, well be running interviews with world-famous chefs who are known for their meat-centric dishes. Follow along for tales of their meat successes, meat failures, and advice for home cooks when it comes to cooking meat at home.
         </em>
        </p>
        <hr/>
        <p>
         Jonny Hunter is the Vice President of Research at
         <a href="http://www.undergroundfoodcollective.org/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.undergroundfoodcollective.org']);">
          Underground Food Collective
         </a>
         in Madison, Wisconsin. He and his business partner Mel Trudeau head up the team that envisioned the restaurant
         <a href="http://forequartermadison.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://forequartermadison.com']);">
          Forequarter
         </a>
         , the butcher shop
         <a href="http://undergroundbutcher.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://undergroundbutcher.com']);">
          Underground Butcher
         </a>
         , the meat-production company
         <a href="http://www.undergroundfoodcollective.org/meats" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.undergroundfoodcollective.org']);">
          Underground Meats
         </a>
         , and the catering business
         <a href="http://ufccatering.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://ufccatering.com']);">
          Underground Catering
         </a>
         , then made them all a reality. And as if those projects werent enough, Jonny is is currently working on opening up another restaurant, Middlewest, behind their butcher shop,
         <a href="http://www.madisonareachefsnetwork.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.madisonareachefsnetwork.com']);">
          putting on food festivals in Madison
         </a>
         , and opening a store in Minneapolis, all while constantly thinking up new ways to make, produce, and talk about food.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_8226b" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-46135" height="387" src="http://static.americastestkitchenfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_8226b.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         In 2013,
         <em>
          Bon Appetit
         </em>
         <a href="http://www.bonappetit.com/restaurants-travel/best-new-restaurants/article/top-50-new-restaurants-forequarter-madison-wi" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.bonappetit.com']);">
          listed Forequarter as one of the 50 best new restaurants in America
         </a>
         . They were right to do so. I was recently in Madison with
         <a href="http://www.americastestkitchenfeed.com/author/bryan-roof/">
          Bryan Roof
         </a>
         looking for regional recipes for
         <em>
          Cooks Country
         </em>
         magazine (
         <a href="http://iconosquare.com/viewer.php#/tag/cookscountrywisconsin/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://iconosquare.com']);">
          check out the Instagrams of the trip
         </a>
         ), and the house charcuterie board was a study in how to cure meat: The coppa was tender yet just dry enough, the thinly sliced mortadella with pistachios tasted earthy yet bright, and the goat sausage boasted hints of goat cheese behind its deep, gamy flavor. Meat sweats be damned, I ate every last piece on that beautiful wooden tray.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_8283" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-46119" height="720" src="http://static.americastestkitchenfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_8283.jpg" width="480"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         I could go on and on about the beets with cherry compote and rye croutons at Forequarter. I could wax poetic about the fermented corn, Jonnys midwestern riff on miso, that I tasted at Underground Meats. I could write paragraphs about the landjaegers (think artisanal Slim Jim-like sausages) from Underground Butcher. But instead, Im going to let Jonny tell you about his philosophy when it comes to buying meat, his favorite cooking demonstration of all time, and how he feels about sous vide. Because thats really why were here.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_8866" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-46127" height="387" src="http://static.americastestkitchenfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_8866.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On the current meat moment:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im over meat worship.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          More about the current meat moment:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         But at the same time Im pretty passionate about serving it in a way thats sustainable and delicious.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How he approaches meat:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I think of meat as adding to a dish, rather than being the main attraction.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On being an ex-vegetarian:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Not to be clich, but I was a vegetarian for five years. I think thats a normal thing, a healthy and good process to go through. But when I stopped, I really wanted to cook meat in a way that was possible to sustain, and I think thats why I was so drawn to sourcing the whole animals. It solves the waste of packaged meat products.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="710" scrolling="no" src="//instagram.com/p/m0jsDFN-Rt/embed/" width="612">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On buying meat:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         We never order in prime cuts by themselves. Im so sick of the pork chop special made with ordered-in chops at restaurants. Well sell them because we have them, not because I specially ordered them. I will order in off-cuts, though. We work with a slaughterhouse and take what they cant sell. Id rather let other people buy the prime parts and then work with whats left over.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On pig roasts:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I dont really believe in doing whole, big-animal cookingI think the whole-pig thing is ridiculous. The smaller the animal, the better it will be when you cook it whole.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hes obsessed with the perfect chicken:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I kind of have an obsession with poultry and cooking it perfectly. You know, roasting a one-pound bird and having everything temped in where it needs to be, making sure the breast isnt overcooked and the thighs are cooked correctly.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On wanting to be like Jacques Ppin:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Im also obsessed with Chicken Galantine. Theres Jacques Ppin video where he galantines a chicken out. Its so elegant and perfect. I want to be a cook who can show ease and technique like that.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="360" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/kAekQ5fzfGM" width="480">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          On vertical integration:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Underground Food Collective is at the perfect size where we have our standard animal orders every week for our butcher shop, and it meets the needs of our catering business and our restaurant. Its exciting to be able to see that vertical integration work. We save money by doing that but it also makes us stretch to use different ingredientsits fun and engaging to learn how to cook those things. Im excited about figuring out how to use things that dont have the commercial draw, but without making that the whole point of a dish. The point is delicious food.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          A childhood meat memory:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I remember once when I was young we went to Lawrys. They had this prime rib carving station. We grew up pretty poor, so going to a fine dining restaurant and having this huge rib station, that was a pretty crazy experience.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="testkitchen (1)" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-46143" height="387" src="http://static.americastestkitchenfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/testkitchen-1.png" width="580"/>
         <br/>
         <em>
          Photo by Jonny Hunter
         </em>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How he raised pigs:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A friend of mine is a farmer and has an orchard, and about eight years ago he told me he was going to raise some pigs. So I did it with himand the thought was that if we could raise two pigs, we might as well raise eight pigs. We built the fences, we fed them apples from the orchard, we thought about what kind of land they were on, what kind of movement they had, what kind of diet they had. They also ate our catering trash that year. We really got to know the process from start to finish. I thought that was cool and important.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How he killed pigs:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         We did the slaughter ourselves, and wed never done it before. This was pre-YouTubewell, maybe not pre-YouTube, but there wasnt a ton of info on how to slaughter a pig on the internet. My friend Henry had this book about being a farmer and it covered everythingit even had a diagram about how to kill a pig. So we used that. We slaughtered them, eviscerated them, butchered them, made sausages, and cooked the meat.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How he cooked pigs:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         We did a series of dinners; we called them The Pre-Industrial Pig Dinners. And I want to say that this was before pork was cool. This was my entryway into meatlearning and understanding the animals. We made sausage, and that was awesome, even though since then Ive learned a lot more about sausage-making.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          How he felt about cooking pigs:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Doing those dishes was my real first moment of feeling like, Okay, this is a highly involved and interesting process and something that I want to know how to do well.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="710" scrolling="no" src="//instagram.com/p/pxQAkst-cW/embed/" width="612">
         </iframe>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hes over sous vide:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Ive been thinking about it a lot latelyI embraced it pretty early on as a cooking technique because it guarded against human mistakes, like overcooking. But weve moved away from it more at Forequarter because I think theres a lot of evaporation that happens in a traditional cooking environment, and thats good because it concentrates protein flavor. Sous vide can be too watery. But not always.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          Hes not over sous vide (and hes not crazy):
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         I sometimes work the butcher counter at Underground Butcher with Matt. He gives straightforward advice to people. But when I work there Ill tell people to put their steak in a plastic bag and run it under hot water for three-and-a-half hours. And Matts like, Dont listen to him. And Im like, No, just google it! Youll see that Im not crazy!
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          The most important ingredient is:
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Salt. For chicken and pork, get it on there early. Beef? I always hit the steaks with salt after I sear them.
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What is cooking?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         Cooking a rib-eye is just heating something up; making a terrine or more composed dish, using organ meat, that is cooking. Well, no. Theres a lot to think about in terms of cooking a steakI could talk for hours about how I do it. But what I mean is that other dishes are more complicated.
        </p>
        <p>
         <img alt="IMG_8552" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-46125" height="387" src="http://static.americastestkitchenfeed.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/IMG_8552.jpg" width="580"/>
        </p>
        <p>
         <strong>
          What is beautiful?
         </strong>
        </p>
        <p>
         A perfectly made and cooked sausage is so awesome and beautiful.
        </p>
        <p>
         <iframe allowtransparency="true" frameborder="0" height="710" scrolling="no" src="//instagram.com/p/qhgMMet-e1/embed/" width="612">
         </iframe>
        </p>
