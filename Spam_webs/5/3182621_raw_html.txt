                    <h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
                     Apple Pie
                    </h3>
                    <div class="post-header">
                     <div class="post-header-line-1">
                     </div>
                    </div>
                    <div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5332031229092902298" itemprop="description articleBody">
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-2yvGKTEs_t0/UziCZJvtDhI/AAAAAAAAF6I/mRM32earUdI/s1600/animation+(18).gif" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="360" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-2yvGKTEs_t0/UziCZJvtDhI/AAAAAAAAF6I/mRM32earUdI/s1600/animation+(18).gif" width="640"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <a data-pin-config="above" data-pin-do="buttonPin" href="https://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.cookingwithgifs.com%2F2014%2F03%2Fapple-pie.html&amp;media=http%3A%2F%2F2.bp.blogspot.com%2F-KbbyC3f6bRk%2FUzh-E88tTcI%2FAAAAAAAAF5w%2FOkqIJzMJhoM%2Fs1600%2Fsliceapplepie.JPG&amp;description=Dutch%20Apple%20Pie">
                      <img src="//assets.pinterest.com/images/pidgets/pinit_fg_en_rect_gray_20.png"/>
                     </a>
                     <!-- Please call pinit.js only once per page -->
                     <script async="" src="//assets.pinterest.com/js/pinit.js" type="text/javascript">
                     </script>
                     <br/>
                     Every country that grows apples has some variety of apple pie. The French make a mean tarte tatin, the English love a good apple crumble, German speaking countries indulge in lovely strudel. So
                     <a href="http://www.cookingwithgifs.com/2014/02/call-for-recipes-apples.html" target="_blank">
                      when ER suggested I bake an apple pie
                     </a>
                     for my spotlight on the humble apple I decided to make a traditional Dutch apple pie. The crust is not quite short crust pastry and a simple trick keeps the bottom nice and firm. It is important to use the right apple for the pie. It needs a little tartness and it should stay firm.Jonagold, Cox Orange, Elstar and Goudrenet are the traditional options. It is easiest to bake the pie in a tin with a removable ring so you can easily remove the pie when it is done.
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <a name="more">
                     </a>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                      <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-KbbyC3f6bRk/Uzh-E88tTcI/AAAAAAAAF5w/OkqIJzMJhoM/s1600/sliceapplepie.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                       <img border="0" height="400" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-KbbyC3f6bRk/Uzh-E88tTcI/AAAAAAAAF5w/OkqIJzMJhoM/s1600/sliceapplepie.JPG" width="400"/>
                      </a>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <br/>
                     <br/>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       Ingredients
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      <span style="font-size: large;">
                       <br/>
                      </span>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                     </div>
                     <ul>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       5 big apples
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       300 g flour
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       200 g cold butter
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       150 g sugar
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       2 eggs
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       1 tsp baking powder
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       1 tsp cinnamon
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       pinch of salt
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       big handful raisins
                      </li>
                      <li style="text-align: left;">
                       2 tblsp custard powder
                      </li>
                     </ul>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: left;">
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-RSkl-pVRmgM/UzhzEsUAwsI/AAAAAAAAF4g/TYs4K3gLX34/s1600/animation+(11).gif" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="225" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-RSkl-pVRmgM/UzhzEsUAwsI/AAAAAAAAF4g/TYs4K3gLX34/s1600/animation+(11).gif" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <div style="text-align: center;">
                       <br/>
                      </div>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      First you make the pastry by putting 300 g flour, 200 g, cold butter, 100 g sugar, 1 tsp baking powder, 1 egg and a pinch of salt in a kitchen machine. Use the blades not the dough hooks. Pulse a couple of times until everything is well mixed and is coming together a bit. At this point you can push it together into roughly a ball shape, wrap it in clingfilm and let it cool down again in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3MlLF2x9II0/Uzh0fEDJ8wI/AAAAAAAAF4s/UPCkziLMVgc/s1600/animation+(12).gif" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="225" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3MlLF2x9II0/Uzh0fEDJ8wI/AAAAAAAAF4s/UPCkziLMVgc/s1600/animation+(12).gif" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                     </div>
                     <div style="text-align: center;">
                      Meanwhile peel and chop the apples and mix with 50 g sugar, the cinnamon and the raisins.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-W05qRxRdPhA/Uzh1oMa5X7I/AAAAAAAAF44/c6BrYux-LNM/s1600/animation+(13).gif" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="180" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-W05qRxRdPhA/Uzh1oMa5X7I/AAAAAAAAF44/c6BrYux-LNM/s1600/animation+(13).gif" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Butter the tin. Take the dough out and reserve about a third for the topping. You probably won't be able to roll it in one big piece, but it is super easy just to use your fingers to flatten the dough in the tin.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-1GFPCJX8XVE/Uzh7WhgVbmI/AAAAAAAAF5I/I7NuPJ71T5E/s1600/animation+(15).gif" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="180" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-1GFPCJX8XVE/Uzh7WhgVbmI/AAAAAAAAF5I/I7NuPJ71T5E/s1600/animation+(15).gif" width="320"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Now for my mum's sneaky little secret. Sprinkle two tablespoons of custard powder on the bottom of the dough before adding the apples. This will keep the bottom from getting soggy and creates a surprisingly delicious apple jelly.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cwOih98_EiU/Uzh8dPaAXBI/AAAAAAAAF5U/2xu5KGClbZU/s1600/animation+(16).gif" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="225" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-cwOih98_EiU/Uzh8dPaAXBI/AAAAAAAAF5U/2xu5KGClbZU/s1600/animation+(16).gif" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      Now make rolls form the left over dough to create a lattice work. Brush this with some eggwash and bake in the oven for an hour at 160 c.
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <br/>
                      <div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
                       <a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-uLhIV7An70w/Uzh8qLghIMI/AAAAAAAAF5c/rwFHAR1rfHI/s1600/wholeapplepie.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;">
                        <img border="0" height="400" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-uLhIV7An70w/Uzh8qLghIMI/AAAAAAAAF5c/rwFHAR1rfHI/s1600/wholeapplepie.JPG" width="400"/>
                       </a>
                      </div>
                      <br/>
                      Let the pie cool a little while before you remove the ring. Enjoy with a good helping of whipped cream for extra authenticity.
