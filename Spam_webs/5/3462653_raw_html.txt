         Bros Can't Stop, Won't Stop Wearing Shorts In Freezing Weather
        </h1>

       <div class="entry-body">
        <p>
         <span class="mt-enclosure mt-enclosure-image" style="display: inline;">
          <div class="image-none">
           <img alt="22115shorts.jpg" height="480" src="http://gothamist.com/attachments/byakas/22115shorts.jpg" width="640"/>
           <br/>
           <i>
            <a href="https://instagram.com/jennsweeney/">
             Jenn Sweeney
            </a>
           </i>
          </div>
         </span>
        </p>
        <p>
         If we learned anything from The Dark Knight trilogy, it's this: you either die of hypothermia a hero, or you live long enough to see yourself become the kind of person who wears shorts in below freezing weather. It's been so cold out lately, we've
         <a href="http://gothamist.com/2015/02/20/see_you_in_hell.php">
          broken temperature records
         </a>
         ; we've seen all of our rivers
         <a href="http://gothamist.com/2015/02/21/photos_nyc_frozen_corpse.php#photo-1">
          become ice barges
         </a>
         ; it's gotten so bad, we've even
         <a href="http://gothamist.com/2015/02/20/wicked_boreal_up_there.php">
          had to acknowledge that Boston exists
         </a>
         just to feel a little better about ourselves. And still, some people insist on shoving their superior body temperatures into our facesbecause some Bros just want to watch the world burn (or, in this case, shiver).
        </p>
        <p>
         The Bro above was spotted by
         <a href="https://instagram.com/jennsweeney/">
          Jenn Sweeney
         </a>
         on the Uptown C train at 34th Street around 9:30 a.m. on Friday, about an hour or so after temperatures hit 2 degrees in Central Park (not counting wind chill, mind you), making it the coldest February 20th on record in NYC history. "I don't understand," she noted. Considering that we were wearing thermal underwear underneath our regular long underwear underneath our over-underwear ("pants"), neither do we.
        </p>
        <p>
         And this Bro is not alone. There are plenty of people who want to live everyday like it's the
         <a href="http://gothamist.com/tags/nopantssubwayride">
          No Pants Subway Ride.
         </a>
        </p>
