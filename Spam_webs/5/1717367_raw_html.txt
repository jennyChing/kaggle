        <p>
         We have a Chili Cook-Off today for #SundaySupper. I really wanted to make a winning chili today for you, so I started off with a great name and then had to come up with a chili that was worthy!! I think I succeeded nicely. I really would like your votes for it to, so
         <a href="http://sundaysuppermovement.com/the-sundaysupper-chili-cook-off-vote-for-your-favorite/" target="_blank">
          VOTE HERE!!!
         </a>
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: left;">
         <a href="http://noshingwiththenolands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Best-Damn-Chili-Ever-by-Noshing-With-The-Nolands-3-Small.jpg">
          <img alt="Best Damn Chili Ever by Noshing With The Nolands " class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8900" height="720" src="//noshingwiththenolands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Best-Damn-Chili-Ever-by-Noshing-With-The-Nolands-3-Small.jpg" width="480"/>
         </a>
         I wanted this chili to have lots of deep layered smokey flavor, so I charred the peppers, added smokey paprika, dried smoked chipolte peppers, roasted tomatoes and chorizo, all to add that incredible taste. Never mind all of the other 15 ingredients!!
        </p>
        <p>
         This is spicy but not blow your head off spicy, I cant handle that. You can always adjust the heat with the amount of chipotle pepper you add.
        </p>
        <p style="text-align: center;">
         <a href="http://noshingwiththenolands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Best-Damn-Chili-Ever-by-Noshing-With-The-Nolands-Small.jpg">
          <img alt="Best Damn Chili Ever by Noshing With The Nolands " class="aligncenter wp-image-8901" height="720" src="//noshingwiththenolands.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Best-Damn-Chili-Ever-by-Noshing-With-The-Nolands-Small.jpg" width="480"/>

          </div>

           <div class="ERSHead">
            Prep time:
            <time datetime="PT40M" itemprop="prepTime">
             40 mins
            </time>
           </div>

            <time datetime="PT8H" itemprop="cookTime">
             8 hours

            <time datetime="PT8H40M" itemprop="totalTime">

          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
          <div class="ERSHead">
           Serves:
           <span itemprop="recipeYield">
            8
           </span>

          </div>

         <div class="ERSSummary" itemprop="description">
          A smokey chili layered with incredible flavors and outstanding ingredients.
         </div>
         <div class="ERSIngredients">
          <div class="ERSIngredientsHeader ERSHeading">
           Ingredients
          </div>
          <ul>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 lbs. extra lean ground beef, browned
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 chorizo sausages, cooked and diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 dried chipolte peppers (small or 1 large)
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 sweet onions, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            5 cloves garlic, minced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 red pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 yellow pepper
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 green pepper, diced
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1-28 oz. can San Marzano tomatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2-14 oz. cans fire roasted diced tomatoes
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup red wine
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            3 Tbsp. chili powder
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            2 tsp. cumin
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 tsp. smoked paprika
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
             tsp. cinnamon
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Salt to taste
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1-28 oz. kidney beans, drained and rinsed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1-14 oz. can Aduki Beans, drained and rinsed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            1 cup frozen corn, thawed
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Red and green jalapeno peppers, sliced for garnish
           </li>
           <li class="ingredient" itemprop="ingredients">
            Cheddar cheese, grated (optional)
           </li>
          </ul>
          <div class="ERSClear">
          </div>
         </div>
         <div class="ERSInstructions">
          <div class="ERSInstructionsHeader ERSHeading">
           Instructions
          </div>
          <ol>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Start by browning the ground beef in a large skillet.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Meanwhile soak the chipotle peppers in boiling water to rehydrate for 20 min.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Place the red and orange peppers under a broiler to char. Once charred place in a bowl and cover with a cloth to steam and cool.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Once the beef is browned add to a large bowl of a slow cooker. In the same pan brown the chorizo. Once cooked, dice into small pieces and add to the beef.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add the onions and garlic now to the pan with a little oil if needed. Saute until translucent and add to the beef mixture.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Chop the chipotle peppers once rehydrated. Skin and seed the red and orange peppers and dice. Add all the peppers to the beef mixture.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Add in the green peppers, both kinds of tomatoes, red wine, chili powder, cumin, cinnamon and salt. Cook on low 6-8 hours. In the last hour add the beans and corn.
           </li>
           <li class="instruction" itemprop="recipeInstructions">
            Garnish with the jalapenos and cheese if using.
           </li>
          </ol>
